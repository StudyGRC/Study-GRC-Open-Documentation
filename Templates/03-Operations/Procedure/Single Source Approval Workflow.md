### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a rigorous, compliant workflow for authorizing noncompetitive procurements (Single Source or Sole Source) at Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures that bypassing standard competitive bidding requirements is restricted to specific, justifiable circumstances in accordance with **2 CFR § 200.320(c)**. It is designed to prevent fraud, waste, and abuse while ensuring that costs incurred are reasonable and necessary for the Organization's charitable and educational mission.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees, contractors, and officers involved in the procurement of goods or services on behalf of Study GRC Inc.

*   **In Scope:** All procurements exceeding the Micro-Purchase Threshold (currently $10,000, or as adjusted by self-certification) where the purchaser intends to solicit a proposal from only one source.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Micro-purchases (where price is determined reasonable based on market research).
    *   Employment contracts (governed by HR policies).
    *   Standard competitive procurements (Sealed Bids or Competitive Proposals).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Single Source** | A procurement decision to solicit a proposal from only one source, even though other sources may be available. |
| **Sole Source** | A specific type of single source procurement where the good or service is available from **only one** vendor in the entire marketplace (e.g., patent holder, utility monopoly). |
| **Public Exigency** | An emergency situation where the urgency for the requirement will not permit a delay resulting from competitive solicitation (e.g., immediate disaster response). |
| **Cost Analysis** | The review and evaluation of separate cost elements and profit or fee in an offeror's or contractor's proposal, as needed to determine a fair and reasonable price. |
| **Conflict of Interest** | As defined in **Bylaws Article IX**, a situation where a personal, professional, or financial interest might interfere with an individual's loyalty to the Organization. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Prerequisites for Noncompetitive Procurement
Before initiating a Single Source request, the **Requester** must verify that the procurement meets at least **one** of the following criteria mandated by **2 CFR § 200.320(c)**:
1.  The item is available only from a single source (Sole Source).
2.  The public exigency or emergency for the requirement will not permit a delay resulting from competitive solicitation.
3.  The Federal awarding agency (or pass-through entity) expressly authorizes a noncompetitive procurement in response to a written request.
4.  After solicitation of a number of sources, competition is determined inadequate.

#### 4.2 Step 1: Conflict of Interest Check
Prior to engaging the vendor, the Requester must verify that no Conflict of Interest exists.
*   **Action:** Review the vendor against the *Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statements* (per **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1**).
*   **Restriction:** If the Requester or Approver has a financial interest in the selected vendor, they must recuse themselves, and the decision must be elevated to the Board of Directors.

#### 4.3 Step 2: Drafting the Justification Form
The Requester must complete the **Noncompetitive (Sole Source) Justification Form (FIN-FRM-030)**. This form must include:
1.  **Description of Requirement:** Detailed specifications of the goods/services.
2.  **Market Research:** Evidence that other vendors were surveyed and deemed incapable (e.g., lack of proprietary technology, inability to meet the schedule).
3.  **Rationale:** Specific citation of the criteria in Section 4.1.
    *   *Note:* "We have used them before" or "They are already onboarded" are **not** valid justifications.
4.  **Cost Reasonableness:** Since there is no price competition, the Requester must demonstrate the price is fair (e.g., comparison to historical prices, published price lists, or similar items).

#### 4.4 Step 3: Review and Cost Analysis
The completed Justification Form and draft contract/invoice are submitted to the **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)**.
*   **CFO Action:**
    *   Verify the justification aligns with Federal Agency Rules.
    *   Perform an independent Cost Analysis if the procurement exceeds the Simplified Acquisition Threshold ($250,000).
    *   Check for suspension/debarment status on SAM.gov.

#### 4.5 Step 4: Approval Hierarchy
Approvals must be obtained in writing (digital signature or secure email approval) based on the total contract value:

| Contract Value | Required Approvers |
| :--- | :--- |
| **$10,001 - $50,000** | Department Head + CFO |
| **$50,001 - $250,000** | Department Head + CFO + Executive Director |
| **$250,000+** | CFO + Executive Director + Board Treasurer (or Finance Committee) |

*   *Note:* If the procurement is >$250,000, the Organization must formally request authorization from the Federal Awarding Agency unless the agency has waived this requirement (per **2 CFR § 200.324(b)(2)**).

#### 4.6 Step 5: Execution and Documentation
Once approved:
1.  **Contract Issuance:** The contract is issued to the vendor.
2.  **Record Retention:** The approved Justification Form, Cost Analysis, and Proof of Price Reasonableness must be attached to the purchase record in the financial system.
3.  **Audit Trail:** These records must be retained for a minimum of three (3) years after the submission of the final expenditure report, in accordance with the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy**.

#### 4.7 Emergency Override (Public Exigency)
In cases of genuine emergency (e.g., security breach requiring immediate remediation by a specific firm):
1.  The **Executive Director** may verbally authorize the procurement.
2.  The Justification Form must be completed retroactively within **48 hours**.
3.  The Board of Directors must be notified of the emergency procurement at the next scheduled meeting.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requester** | Conducts market research, drafts the Justification Form, and provides proof of price reasonableness. |
| **Department Head** | Validates that the goods/services are mission-critical and budget is available. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Acts as the compliance gatekeeper; verifies SAM.gov status; performs Cost Analysis; maintains the audit trail. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides final operational approval for high-value single source requests; authorizes emergency overrides. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves single source procurements exceeding $250,000 or involving potential conflicts of interest. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure may result in **disallowed costs**, requiring Study GRC Inc. to reimburse the federal government using non-federal funds.

Internal violations of this procedure may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Deliberate attempts to steer contracts to favored vendors without justification will be treated as fraud and referred for legal action.
