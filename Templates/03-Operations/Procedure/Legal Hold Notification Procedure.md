### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for the issuance, management, and release of a "Legal Hold" (also known as a Litigation Hold) within Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures the Organization complies with its legal duty to preserve all forms of relevant information—including Electronically Stored Information (ESI) and physical records—when litigation, an audit, or a government investigation is reasonably anticipated.

Failure to preserve records under these circumstances constitutes "spoliation of evidence," which can result in severe legal sanctions against the Organization. This procedure aligns with **Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** of the Bylaws and the Organization's *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, contractors, and Advisory Board members.
*   **Assets:** All Organization-owned devices, networks, and accounts (e.g., Google Workspace, Slack, GitHub, AWS).
*   **BYOD:** Personal devices used for Organization business, insofar as they contain relevant Organization data.
*   **Records:** All formats, including emails, instant messages, code repositories, databases, financial logs, and physical paper files.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Legal Hold** | A directive requiring the immediate suspension of document destruction protocols for specific records relevant to a legal matter. |
| **Custodian** | An individual who possesses, controls, or has access to records or data relevant to the legal matter. |
| **ESI** | Electronically Stored Information (e.g., emails, Slack messages, voicemails, source code, metadata). |
| **Trigger Event** | An event that creates a "reasonable anticipation of litigation" (e.g., receipt of a complaint, a credible threat of a lawsuit, a regulatory inquiry, or a significant accident). |
| **Spoliation** | The destruction, alteration, or failure to preserve evidence that is relevant to pending or reasonably foreseeable litigation. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Identification of Trigger Event
The duty to preserve evidence arises immediately upon the occurrence of a Trigger Event.
1.  Any individual who receives a legal notice, subpoena, or credible threat of litigation must immediately notify the **Executive Director** and/or **General Counsel**.
2.  The Executive Director/General Counsel must assess whether the event triggers the legal duty to preserve records under Federal Rules of Civil Procedure (FRCP) or Texas Rules of Civil Procedure.

#### 4.2 Scope Assessment and Custodian Identification
Once a hold is deemed necessary, the General Counsel (or designee) must:
1.  **Identify the Scope:** Determine the date range, subject matter, and types of records relevant to the dispute.
2.  **Identify Custodians:** Create a list of all individuals (Board members, volunteers, staff) likely to possess relevant information.
3.  **Identify Systems:** List all IT systems containing relevant data (e.g., "Slack channels related to Project X," "Finance Drive," "Email accounts of the Treasurer").

#### 4.3 Issuance of Legal Hold Notification
The General Counsel must issue a written **Legal Hold Notice** to all identified Custodians and the IT Department. The notice must include:
1.  **Subject:** Clearly state "URGENT: LEGAL HOLD NOTICE – DO NOT DELETE."
2.  **Matter Description:** A brief, non-privileged description of the matter (enough to identify relevant documents).
3.  **Preservation Instructions:** Explicit instructions to stop deleting, overwriting, or modifying relevant records.
4.  **Suspension of Auto-Delete:** A directive to suspend any automated document destruction policies for the relevant data.
5.  **Acknowledgment Requirement:** A deadline for the Custodian to confirm receipt and compliance.

#### 4.4 IT System Suspension (Technical Preservation)
Upon receipt of the Legal Hold Notice, the **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** or IT Administrator must:
1.  **Suspend Retention Policies:** Disable auto-deletion rules for identified Custodian email accounts (e.g., Google Vault Legal Hold) and Slack retention settings.
2.  **Secure Backups:** Ensure backup tapes or cloud snapshots containing relevant historical data are segregated and protected from being overwritten.
3.  **Device Imaging:** If necessary, image the hard drives of key Custodians to preserve metadata.
4.  **Confirm Action:** Report back to the General Counsel confirming that technical holds are active.

#### 4.5 Custodian Acknowledgment
1.  Custodians must sign (electronically or physically) an **Acknowledgment of Legal Hold** within **three (3) business days** of receipt.
2.  The General Counsel must track acknowledgments in a **Legal Hold Register**.
3.  If a Custodian fails to respond, the General Counsel must escalate to the Custodian’s manager or the Board President.

#### 4.6 Ongoing Monitoring and Reminders
Litigation can span years. To ensure continued compliance:
1.  **Quarterly Reminders:** The General Counsel shall send a reminder notice to all active Custodians every 90 days re-affirming the hold.
2.  **New Personnel:** If new staff/volunteers join a project under hold, they must be issued the notice immediately.
3.  **Departing Personnel:** If a Custodian leaves the Organization, their devices and accounts must be sequestered and **not** wiped/re-imaged until the hold is released.

#### 4.7 Release of Legal Hold
A Legal Hold may only be lifted upon the final resolution of the matter (including the expiration of all appeal periods).
1.  **Authorization:** The General Counsel issues a formal **Release of Legal Hold** notice.
2.  **Notification:** Custodians and IT are notified that normal document retention/destruction policies may resume.
3.  **Resumption:** IT re-enables auto-delete policies. *Note: Data that would have been deleted during the hold period should be reviewed before mass deletion.*

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **General Counsel / Executive Director** | Determines necessity of hold; defines scope; issues notices; tracks compliance; authorizes release. |
| **CISO / IT Department** | Implements technical holds (Google Vault, Slack exports); suspends auto-delete policies; secures hardware of departing custodians. |
| **Custodians (Staff/Volunteers)** | Acknowledge receipt of hold; preserve all relevant data; cease deletion of relevant emails/files; surrender devices for imaging if requested. |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensures the Organization has resources for legal counsel; complies with holds regarding Board communications/minutes. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Strict Compliance is Mandatory.**
Failure to comply with a Legal Hold Notification—whether intentional or due to negligence—may result in:
1.  **Disciplinary Action:** Up to and including immediate termination of employment, volunteer status, or Board removal for cause (per Bylaws Section 3.6).
2.  **Legal Consequences:** Personal civil or criminal liability for obstruction of justice.
3.  **Organizational Harm:** Severe financial penalties and "adverse inference" instructions in court (where a judge instructs a jury to assume the missing evidence would have hurt the Organization's case).
