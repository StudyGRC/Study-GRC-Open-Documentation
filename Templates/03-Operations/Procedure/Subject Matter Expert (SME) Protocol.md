### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish a rigorous framework for the selection, engagement, and utilization of Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) within Study GRC Inc. As an organization dedicated to advancing Governance, Risk, and Compliance knowledge (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3), the accuracy and integrity of our educational resources are paramount. This procedure mitigates reputational and legal risk by ensuring that all technical content is authored or vetted by qualified individuals and validated for factual accuracy prior to publication.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees, volunteers, contractors, and Advisory Board members who contribute technical content, curriculum, or professional guidance on behalf of the Organization.

*   **In Scope:** Curriculum development, webinar presentations, technical blog posts, policy templates, and certification study guides.
*   **Out of Scope:** General administrative volunteers, community moderators (non-technical), and operational staff not involved in educational content creation.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Subject Matter Expert (SME)** | An individual with demonstrated authority, certification (e.g., CISSP, CISA, CPA, JD), or significant professional experience in a specific domain of GRC. |
| **Content Artifact** | Any material created for public or member consumption, including slides, documents, code, or video scripts. |
| **Peer Review** | The process by which a second qualified SME reviews a Content Artifact for accuracy, relevance, and safety. |
| **Work Product** | As defined in Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3, content created by the SME which becomes the property of the Organization. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 SME Qualification and Vetting
Before an individual is authorized to produce or approve technical content, they must undergo a qualification process.

1.  **Credential Verification:** The VP of Education & Training (or designee) must verify the candidate's professional credentials. This includes validating active status of certifications (e.g., ISC2, ISACA, IIA) or reviewing academic transcripts.
2.  **Experience Assessment:** The candidate must demonstrate a minimum of three (3) years of practical experience in the specific domain they intend to cover.
3.  **Conflict of Interest Check:** Pursuant to Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1, the candidate must complete a Conflict of Interest Disclosure to ensure their contributions are not covert marketing for a specific vendor or product.
4.  **Youth Protection Screening:** If the SME will interact directly with K-12 participants (e.g., live workshops), they must pass a background check in accordance with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.

#### 4.2 Engagement and Intellectual Property
To ensure the Organization retains the right to keep resources open-source and free (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3), the legal relationship must be established prior to work commencing.

1.  **Agreement Execution:** The SME must sign a Volunteer Service Agreement or Independent Contractor Agreement.
2.  **IP Assignment:** The agreement must explicitly reference Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3, confirming that all created materials are "works made for hire" and assigned to Study GRC Inc.
3.  **Non-Disclosure:** The SME must execute an NDA if they will have access to proprietary member data or internal security configurations.

#### 4.3 Content Creation Standards
SMEs must adhere to the following standards when drafting content:

1.  **Source Citation:** All claims regarding laws, regulations, or standards (e.g., "NIST requires...") must be cited with a direct reference to the specific control or statute.
2.  **Vendor Neutrality:** Content must remain vendor-agnostic unless the specific purpose of the training is tool-specific.
3.  **Plain Language:** In alignment with the mission to "simplify the GRC domain," content should avoid unnecessary jargon and define complex terms upon first use.

#### 4.4 Technical Peer Review (The "Accuracy" Check)
No technical content may be published without a documented Peer Review.

1.  **Reviewer Assignment:** The VP of Education & Training assigns a secondary SME (the "Reviewer") who was not involved in the initial drafting.
2.  **Validation Scope:** The Reviewer assesses:
    *   **Factual Accuracy:** Are the interpretations of regulations correct?
    *   **Currency:** Is the content based on the latest version of the standard (e.g., NIST CSF 2.0 vs 1.1)?
    *   **Safety:** Does the content recommend dangerous practices (e.g., disabling firewalls for testing)?
3.  **Remediation:** If errors are found, the content is returned to the primary SME for correction.
4.  **Approval:** Both the Primary SME and the Reviewer must sign off (digitally) on the final version.

#### 4.5 Content Lifecycle and Obsolescence
GRC is a rapidly changing field. Content must be maintained to remain accurate.

1.  **Expiration Dating:** All published content must be tagged with a "Next Review Date" not exceeding 12 months from publication.
2.  **Triggered Reviews:** If a major standard changes (e.g., a new PCI DSS version is released), the VP of Education & Training must trigger an immediate review of all related artifacts.
3.  **Deprecation:** Content deemed inaccurate or obsolete must be immediately archived or clearly labeled as "Legacy/Historical" to prevent users from relying on outdated guidance.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Owns the SME Protocol; approves SME qualifications; assigns Peer Reviewers; maintains the Content Review Schedule. |
| **Subject Matter Expert (SME)** | Creates content; ensures initial accuracy; cites sources; adheres to IP and COI policies. |
| **Peer Reviewer** | Validates content for accuracy and safety; provides constructive feedback; serves as the "Gatekeeper" for quality. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides final approval for high-risk content or content involving Fundamental Actions; resolves disputes between SMEs and Reviewers. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this protocol may result in the rejection of content, revocation of SME status, or termination of the volunteer/contractor relationship.
*   **Plagiarism:** Any SME found plagiarizing content will be immediately terminated and permanently banned from contributing.
*   **Negligence:** Gross negligence in providing accurate information that results in liability for a user may result in legal action against the SME, subject to the Indemnification clause in Bylaws Article VII.
