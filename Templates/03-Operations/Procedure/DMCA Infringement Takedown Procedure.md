### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant process for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") to respond to notifications of alleged copyright infringement. This procedure ensures the Organization maintains its "Safe Harbor" status under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA), specifically 17 U.S.C. § 512, by acting expeditiously to remove or disable access to infringing material posted by users, members, or third parties on Organization-controlled platforms.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all digital properties, websites, repositories, community forums, and learning management systems owned or operated by Study GRC Inc.

*   **In Scope:** User-Generated Content (UGC), community forum posts, open-source contributions, and member-uploaded resources.
*   **Out of Scope:** Content owned strictly by Study GRC Inc. where no third-party claim is involved (handled via internal content governance).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **DMCA** | The Digital Millennium Copyright Act, a US copyright law that provides a "Safe Harbor" for online service providers if they adhere to specific takedown procedures. |
| **Designated Agent** | The individual or role registered by the Organization with the U.S. Copyright Office to receive notifications of claimed infringement. |
| **Takedown Notice** | A formal written communication from a copyright owner (or their agent) requesting the removal of infringing material. |
| **Counter-Notice** | A legal response from the user who posted the material, disputing the infringement claim and requesting restoration of the content. |
| **Subscriber** | The user, member, or entity that uploaded the material alleged to be infringing. |
| **Repeat Infringer** | A user who has been the subject of multiple valid Takedown Notices. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Designation of Agent
To qualify for Safe Harbor protection, the Organization must maintain a current registration with the U.S. Copyright Office.

1.  The Executive Director shall appoint a **DMCA Designated Agent**.
2.  The Organization must publish the Agent’s contact information on the "Transparency" or "Legal" page of the website, including: Name (or title), Address, Phone Number, and Email Address.
3.  The Organization must file an Interim Designation of Agent with the U.S. Copyright Office and pay the required fee.

#### 4.2 Receipt and Review of Takedown Notice
Upon receipt of a Takedown Notice (via email or mail), the Designated Agent must validate that the notice is "substantially compliant" with 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3).

**A valid notice must include:**
1.  **Signature:** Physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent.
2.  **Work Identification:** Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed.
3.  **Material Identification:** Identification of the infringing material and information reasonably sufficient to locate it (e.g., specific URL).
4.  **Contact Info:** Address, telephone number, and email of the complaining party.
5.  **Good Faith Statement:** A statement that the complaining party has a good faith belief that the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
6.  **Accuracy Statement:** A statement that the information is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that the complaining party is authorized to act on behalf of the owner.

*If the notice is missing elements, the Designated Agent shall attempt to contact the complainant to rectify the omission before processing.*

#### 4.3 Expeditious Removal (The "Takedown")
If the Takedown Notice is valid, the Organization must act "expeditiously" (target: within 24 hours).

1.  **Verify:** The Designated Agent confirms the URL/material exists.
2.  **Disable:** The Designated Agent instructs the IT/Security team to remove or disable access to the material.
    *   *Note:* Do not delete the data permanently yet; disable public access (hide/quarantine) to allow for the counter-notice process.
3.  **Log:** The incident is recorded in the **DMCA Infringement Log** for tracking repeat infringers.

#### 4.4 Notification to Subscriber
Promptly after removing the material, the Organization must notify the user who posted it.

1.  The Designated Agent sends a **Notice of Removal** to the Subscriber's email address on file.
2.  The email must include:
    *   A statement that material has been removed due to a copyright claim.
    *   A copy of the original Takedown Notice (redacting personal phone numbers if necessary, but keeping the claimant's identity clear).
    *   Instructions on how to file a **Counter-Notice** if the Subscriber believes the removal was a mistake or misidentification.

#### 4.5 Receipt of Counter-Notice
If the Subscriber believes the material was removed in error (e.g., they own the rights or it is Fair Use), they may file a Counter-Notice.

**A valid Counter-Notice must include (per 17 U.S.C. § 512(g)(3)):**
1.  **Signature:** Physical or electronic signature of the Subscriber.
2.  **Identification:** Description of the material removed and its location (URL) before removal.
3.  **Perjury Statement:** A statement under penalty of perjury that the Subscriber has a good faith belief the material was removed by mistake or misidentification.
4.  **Consent to Jurisdiction:** Name, address, and phone number of the Subscriber, and a statement consenting to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for their address (or the Organization's district if the user is outside the US).

#### 4.6 Restoration of Material (The "Put-Back")
Upon receipt of a valid Counter-Notice:

1.  **Notify Claimant:** The Designated Agent promptly sends a copy of the Counter-Notice to the original complaining party.
2.  **Wait Period:** The Organization informs the claimant that the material will be replaced in **10 to 14 business days** unless the claimant notifies the Organization that they have filed a court action seeking a restraining order.
3.  **Restoration:** If the Designated Agent does not receive notice of a filed lawsuit within 14 business days, the IT/Security team must restore access to the material.

#### 4.7 Repeat Infringer Policy
In accordance with 17 U.S.C. § 512(i)(1)(A) and **Bylaws Article II (Membership)** regarding termination of rights:

1.  **Tracking:** The Designated Agent tracks "strikes" against users in the DMCA Infringement Log.
2.  **Threshold:**
    *   **Strike 1:** Warning and removal of content.
    *   **Strike 2:** Final Warning and removal of content.
    *   **Strike 3:** Termination of account and ban from the community.
3.  **Termination:** If a user reaches the Repeat Infringer threshold, the Designated Agent shall direct the revocation of membership and technical access. This action is final and not subject to the standard Member Grievance Dispute Procedure, as it is a legal compliance mandate.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **DMCA Designated Agent** | Primary point of contact for all claims; reviews legal validity of notices; maintains the DMCA Log; communicates with claimants and subscribers. |
| **CISO / IT Dept** | Executes the technical removal/disabling of content upon instruction from the Agent; restores content if required. |
| **Executive Director** | Oversees the Designated Agent; authorizes the termination of accounts for Repeat Infringers. |
| **Subscriber (User)** | Responsible for ensuring they have rights to content they upload; responsible for filing valid Counter-Notices. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure puts Study GRC Inc. at risk of losing Safe Harbor protection, potentially exposing the Organization to significant financial liability for copyright infringement.

*   **Staff:** Failure to act expeditiously on a valid notice may result in disciplinary action.
*   **Users:** Users found to be Repeat Infringers will have their accounts terminated and membership revoked pursuant to **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1** and the **Terms of Service**.
