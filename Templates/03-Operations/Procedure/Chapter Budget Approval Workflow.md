### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, rigorous workflow for the submission, review, and approval of Local Chapter annual budgets. This ensures that all Study GRC Inc. chapters operate within financial means, align resources with the Organization’s charitable mission, and maintain strict compliance with IRS 501(c)(3) regulations and 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance) regarding allowable costs. This procedure operationalizes the financial oversight mandate defined in **Article VIII** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   All chartered Local Chapters of Study GRC Inc.
*   Chapter Officers (Presidents and Treasurers).
*   HQ Finance Department and Operational Leadership.

**Exclusions:**
*   HQ Departmental Budgets (governed by *FIN-POL-005 Budget Development Policy*).
*   Discretionary spending below the petty cash threshold defined in *FIN-POL-008*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fiscal Year (FY)** | The financial operating period of the Organization, commencing January 1 and ending December 31, per **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3**. |
| **Zero-Based Budgeting** | A method of budgeting in which all expenses must be justified for each new period; budgets do not automatically roll over from the previous year. |
| **Allowable Cost** | A cost that is reasonable, necessary, and allocable to the federal award or non-profit mission, compliant with **2 CFR 200 Subpart E**. |
| **UBIT** | Unrelated Business Income Tax. Income generated from trade or business activities not substantially related to the charitable purpose. |
| **Budget Variance** | The difference between the budgeted amount of expense or revenue and the actual amount. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Annual Budget Cycle Timeline
To ensure consolidated reporting to the Board of Directors, Chapters must adhere to the following schedule:

1.  **September 1:** HQ Finance distributes the *Annual Chapter Budget Template (OPS-FRM-010a)* and *Strategic Priorities Memo* to Chapter Leaders.
2.  **October 15:** Deadline for Chapters to submit draft budgets to HQ Finance.
3.  **November 1 – November 15:** HQ Finance review and revision period.
4.  **December 1:** Final Budget approval notifications issued to Chapters.
5.  **January 1:** Approved budgets are loaded into the central accounting system; spending authority is active.

#### 4.2 Budget Preparation Requirements
Chapter Treasurers must prepare the budget using **Zero-Based Budgeting** principles.

1.  **Revenue Projections:**
    *   Must be conservative. Sponsorships may only be budgeted if a signed Letter of Intent (LOI) or multi-year agreement is on file.
    *   Membership dues revenue projections will be provided by HQ based on current allocation formulas.
2.  **Expense Allocation:**
    *   Expenses must be categorized according to the HQ **Chart of Accounts (COA)**.
    *   **Prohibited Expenses:** The following must **never** be included in a Chapter budget:
        *   Political contributions or lobbying activities.
        *   Purchase of alcohol (unless explicitly pre-approved via *Event Liability Protocol* and covered by non-federal unrestricted funds).
        *   Loans to officers or members.
3.  **Deficit Prohibition:**
    *   Chapters are prohibited from submitting a deficit budget. Total expenses cannot exceed Total Projected Revenue + Existing Net Assets.

#### 4.3 Submission Process
1.  The Chapter Treasurer completes the *Annual Chapter Budget Template*.
2.  The Chapter Board must vote to approve the draft budget. This vote must be recorded in the Chapter Meeting Minutes.
3.  The Chapter President submits the budget file and a copy of the meeting minutes via the **HQ Secure Finance Portal**. Email submissions are not accepted for security and version control reasons.

#### 4.4 HQ Review and Compliance Check
Upon receipt, the HQ Finance Team (reporting to the CFO) performs a compliance review:

1.  **Mission Alignment Test:** Verifies that allocated funds support educational and community goals (e.g., training events, student outreach) rather than purely social activities.
2.  **Allowability Review:** Scans for costs unallowable under **2 CFR 200** (e.g., entertainment costs without programmatic purpose, fines/penalties).
3.  **UBIT Analysis:** Reviews revenue streams (e.g., merchandise sales, advertising) to flag potential Unrelated Business Income Tax liabilities.
4.  **Math and Logic Check:** Verifies formulas and COA coding accuracy.

*If the budget fails any check, it is returned to the Chapter Treasurer with specific "Required Remediation Actions" and a 5-business-day deadline for resubmission.*

#### 4.5 Approval Authority Matrix
Budgets are approved based on total expense volume:

| Total Budgeted Expense | Approving Authority |
| :--- | :--- |
| **< $10,000** | HQ Finance Manager |
| **$10,001 - $50,000** | Chief Financial Officer (CFO) |
| **> $50,000** | Executive Director (CEO) & Treasurer (Board) |

#### 4.6 Budget Loading and Execution
1.  Upon final approval, the CFO issues a **Budget Authority Letter** to the Chapter.
2.  HQ Finance loads the approved line items into the central accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks Online/NetSuite).
3.  Chapter corporate cards and spending limits are adjusted to match the approved quarterly run rate.

#### 4.7 Mid-Year Budget Amendments
If a Chapter anticipates a variance of greater than **10%** in any major category or receives unexpected funding (e.g., a new grant):

1.  The Chapter must submit a *Budget Amendment Request Form (OPS-FRM-012)*.
2.  The request must detail the source of new funds or the reason for increased costs.
3.  Amendments follow the same approval workflow as the annual budget (Section 4.5).
4.  **Unauthorized Overspending:** Spending beyond the approved budget without an amendment may result in the suspension of Chapter financial privileges.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chapter Treasurer** | Prepares the budget draft; ensures mathematical accuracy; maps costs to the Chart of Accounts; submits to HQ. |
| **Chapter President** | Reviews the budget for strategic alignment; convenes the Chapter Board for local approval; certifies the submission. |
| **HQ Finance Manager** | Conducts initial compliance review; verifies COA coding; approves budgets under $10k. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews UBIT and high-risk expenditures; approves budgets up to $50k; advises the Board on aggregate financial risk. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Approves high-value budgets (> $50k); resolves disputes regarding budget rejections. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this workflow poses a significant risk to the Organization's 501(c)(3) status and federal grant eligibility.

*   **Non-Submission:** Chapters that fail to submit a budget by the deadline will have their financial accounts **frozen** effective January 1 until a budget is approved.
*   **Unallowable Spending:** Chapter Officers found spending funds on unapproved or prohibited items may be personally liable for reimbursement and subject to removal under **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.3**.
*   **Charter Revocation:** Persistent financial non-compliance is grounds for Chapter Charter revocation.
