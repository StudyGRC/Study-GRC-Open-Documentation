### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory steps for identifying, evaluating, and reporting foreign financial accounts held by Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") to the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN). This procedure ensures compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) and mitigates the risk of severe civil and criminal penalties associated with failure to file FinCEN Form 114 (FBAR).

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **The Organization:** Study GRC Inc. as a legal entity.
*   **Individuals:** Any Officer, Director, or employee with signature authority over, or financial interest in, foreign financial accounts owned by the Organization.
*   **Assets:** All bank accounts, securities accounts, mutual funds, and applicable cryptocurrency exchange accounts domiciled outside of the United States.

**Exclusions:** Accounts held with a U.S. branch of a foreign bank are generally excluded, but must be verified against current FinCEN guidance.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **FBAR** | Report of Foreign Bank and Financial Accounts (FinCEN Form 114). |
| **FinCEN** | Financial Crimes Enforcement Network, a bureau of the U.S. Department of the Treasury. |
| **Foreign Financial Account** | A financial account maintained with a financial institution located outside of the United States. This includes checking, savings, securities, brokerage, and certain foreign-domiciled cryptocurrency exchanges. |
| **Aggregate Value** | The total value of all foreign financial accounts combined. If the aggregate value exceeds $10,000 at *any time* during the calendar year, reporting is required. |
| **Signature Authority** | The authority of an individual (alone or in conjunction with another) to control the disposition of money, funds, or other assets held in a financial account by direct communication with the financial institution. |
| **Maximum Account Value** | The highest value of currency, funds, or assets in the account during the calendar year, based on periodic statements or transaction history. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Annual Assessment and Identification
**Responsible Role:** Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
**Timeline:** January 1 – January 31 (following the reporting year)

1.  **Account Inventory:** The CFO must review the General Ledger and the Chart of Accounts to identify all financial accounts utilized during the previous calendar year.
2.  **Jurisdiction Verification:** For every account identified, the CFO must verify the physical location of the financial institution.
    *   *Note:* Particular attention must be paid to cryptocurrency exchanges. If the exchange is domiciled outside the U.S. (e.g., entities not registered as US MSBs), it must be flagged for potential FBAR reporting.
3.  **Signature Authority Review:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IV (Board Officers)**, the CFO must identify which Officers (President, Treasurer, Secretary) held signature authority over identified foreign accounts.

#### 4.2 Valuation and Threshold Calculation
**Responsible Role:** CFO / Accounting Team
**Timeline:** February 1 – February 28

1.  **Determine Maximum Value:** For each identified foreign account, the CFO must determine the maximum value reached during the calendar year. This is **not** the year-end balance, but the highest balance at any point.
2.  **Currency Conversion:** If the account is denominated in foreign currency, the maximum value must be converted to U.S. dollars using the Treasury Department's Financial Management Service rate (Treasury Reporting Rates of Exchange) for the last day of the calendar year.
3.  **Aggregate Calculation:** Sum the maximum values of *all* foreign accounts.
    *   **If Total > $10,000:** FBAR filing is **MANDATORY**. Proceed to Section 4.3.
    *   **If Total ≤ $10,000:** No filing is required for the entity. The CFO must document this determination in a memo to the Treasurer for the audit trail.

#### 4.3 Data Collection for Filing
**Responsible Role:** CFO
**Timeline:** March 1 – March 31

If the threshold in 4.2 is met, the CFO must compile the following data for FinCEN Form 114:
1.  Maximum value of each account (in USD).
2.  Type of account (Bank, Securities, Other).
3.  Name of the Financial Institution.
4.  Account Number.
5.  Address of the Financial Institution.
6.  Names of all joint owners (if applicable).
7.  Names/Titles of all individuals with signature authority.

#### 4.4 Filing FinCEN Form 114
**Responsible Role:** CFO or Designated Tax Professional
**Timeline:** Due April 15 (Automatic extension to October 15 is generally available)

1.  **Access BSA E-Filing:** Access the BSA E-Filing System (http://bsaefiling.fincen.treas.gov).
2.  **Prepare Form:** Complete FinCEN Form 114 using the data collected in Section 4.3.
3.  **Review:** The Treasurer must review the prepared form for accuracy prior to submission, aligning with oversight duties defined in **Bylaws Article IV, Section 4.2**.
4.  **Submission:** Submit the form electronically. Paper filings are not accepted.
5.  **Confirmation:** Save the electronic acceptance receipt/confirmation number.

#### 4.5 Individual Officer Reporting Notification
**Responsible Role:** CFO / Legal Counsel
**Timeline:** Concurrent with Entity Filing

1.  **Notification:** If the Organization files an FBAR, the CFO must notify any Officer with signature authority over those accounts.
2.  **Personal Liability:** Officers with signature authority but no financial interest *may* have a separate obligation to file an FBAR personally, or may be eligible for a filing exception if the Organization files. The Organization shall provide necessary data to Officers to facilitate their personal compliance, but personal tax advice remains the responsibility of the individual.

#### 4.6 Record Retention
**Responsible Role:** CFO
**Timeline:** Ongoing

1.  **Statutory Requirement:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** and the **Document Retention Policy**, copies of all filed FBARs and the supporting documentation (statements, conversion rate sources, calculation worksheets) must be retained for a minimum of **five (5) years** from the due date of the report.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Primary owner of the FBAR process; performs identification, valuation, and filing. Ensures the "Transparency" page (Bylaws Art. VIII) reflects compliance status if required. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides Board-level oversight; reviews threshold calculations and final FBAR prior to submission. |
| **Board of Directors** | Maintains ultimate fiduciary responsibility for regulatory compliance. |
| **Officers (President/Secretary)** | Must disclose all foreign accounts they open or manage on behalf of the Organization during the fiscal year. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with FBAR reporting requirements can result in catastrophic financial and legal consequences for the Organization and its Officers.

*   **Non-Willful Violation:** Civil penalties up to $16,117 per violation (adjusted for inflation).
*   **Willful Violation:** Civil penalties up to the greater of $134,806 or 50% of the account balance per violation, plus potential criminal prosecution.

Any employee or Officer found to be willfully concealing foreign assets or obstructing this procedure will be subject to immediate termination and reported to legal authorities.
