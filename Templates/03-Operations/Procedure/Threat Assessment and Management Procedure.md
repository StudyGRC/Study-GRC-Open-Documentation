### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, systematic process for identifying, assessing, and managing behaviors that may pose a threat of violence or harm to Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"), its employees, volunteers, members, or the community. This procedure fulfills the Organization's "Duty of Care" and aligns with the OSHA General Duty Clause by providing a mechanism to intervene before a threat escalates into violence.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all "Covered Persons," defined as employees, Board Directors, officers, volunteers, contractors, and Voting Members.

**Applicability:**
*   **Physical Environments:** Official events, conferences, meetups, and rented facilities.
*   **Digital Environments:** Official communication channels (Slack, Discord, Email), social media platforms where the individual is associated with the Organization, and remote work interactions.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Active Emergencies:** Situations involving immediate, life-threatening violence (e.g., active shooter, physical assault in progress) must be referred immediately to local law enforcement (911) per the *Emergency Action Plan*, with this procedure applied post-incident.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Threat** | Any expression of intent to cause physical or mental harm. Threats may be direct ("I will hurt you"), conditional ("If you don't do X, I will hurt you"), or veiled ("You better watch your back"). |
| **Concerning Behavior** | Actions that do not constitute a direct threat but raise anxiety or fear, such as stalking, obsessive communication, fascination with violence, or sudden/erratic behavioral changes. |
| **TAT (Threat Assessment Team)** | A multidisciplinary group responsible for evaluating reported threats and determining the appropriate management strategy. |
| **Doxxing** | The act of publicly revealing previously private personal information about an individual (e.g., home address, personal phone number) with malicious intent. |
| **Imminent Danger** | A situation where a threat of death or serious physical harm is immediate or virtually certain to occur within a short time frame. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Identification and Reporting
All Covered Persons have a mandatory duty to report potential threats.

1.  **Immediate Reporting:** Any individual who witnesses or receives a threat must report it immediately.
    *   **Imminent Danger:** Call 911 (or local emergency services) immediately.
    *   **Non-Imminent:** Report to the immediate supervisor, the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG), or via the Whistleblower Complaint Intake Form.
2.  **Preservation of Evidence:** The reporter must preserve all evidence, including:
    *   Screenshots of digital communications (Slack, Discord, Social Media).
    *   Emails or voicemails (do not delete).
    *   Written notes regarding physical interactions (time, date, witnesses, exact words used).

#### 4.2 Phase II: Triage and TAT Activation
Upon receipt of a report, the Executive Director or their designee must activate the Threat Assessment Team (TAT).

1.  **TAT Composition:** The TAT shall consist of:
    *   **Executive Director (Chair)**
    *   **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** (for digital forensics/access control).
    *   **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** (for community context).
    *   **Legal Counsel** (internal or external).
2.  **Initial Triage:** The TAT must convene (virtually or physically) within **2 hours** of a high-priority report to determine if the threat is imminent.
    *   If **Imminent**: Contact law enforcement immediately; suspend subject's access to all systems.
    *   If **Non-Imminent**: Proceed to Phase III (Investigation).

#### 4.3 Phase III: Investigation and Information Gathering
The TAT will gather specific data to evaluate the context and credibility of the threat.

1.  **Subject Interview:** If deemed safe, two TAT members may interview the subject to assess intent and mental state. This should be done carefully to avoid escalation.
2.  **Witness Interviews:** Interview recipients of the threat and potential witnesses.
3.  **Digital Forensics:** The CISO will review system logs, chat history, and public social media activity to identify patterns of escalation or planning.
4.  **Background Review:** Review personnel files, volunteer history, or membership records for prior incidents or disciplinary actions.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Assessment and Classification
The TAT will classify the risk level using the following rubric:

*   **Level 1: Low Risk (Vague/Indirect)**
    *   *Characteristics:* Vague statements, venting, no history of violence, no specific target, no plan.
    *   *Example:* "I could just kill someone for making this mistake." (Said in frustration).
*   **Level 2: Moderate Risk (Specific but Conditional)**
    *   *Characteristics:* Specific target, conditional threats, history of conflict, but no evidence of a concrete plan or immediate means.
    *   *Example:* "If the Board doesn't approve this, they'll regret it."
*   **Level 3: High Risk (Targeted and Planned)**
    *   *Characteristics:* Specific target, specific method, evidence of planning, access to means (weapons/tech), intense fixation or grievance.
    *   *Example:* "I know where the CEO lives and I'm going to pay a visit."
*   **Level 4: Imminent/Critical Risk**
    *   *Characteristics:* Weapon displayed, physical action taken, or clear indication that violence is occurring or about to occur.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Management and Intervention
Based on the classification, the TAT will implement a management plan.

*   **For Level 1 (Low):**
    *   Document the incident.
    *   Supervisor/Manager coaching or verbal warning.
    *   Referral to Employee Assistance Program (EAP) or mental health resources.
*   **For Level 2 (Moderate):**
    *   Written warning or temporary suspension.
    *   Mandatory conflict resolution or anger management counseling.
    *   Implementation of a "No Contact" directive regarding the target.
*   **For Level 3 (High):**
    *   **Immediate Suspension/Termination:** Initiate termination of employment or volunteer status.
    *   **Membership Expulsion:** Initiate expulsion proceedings per Bylaws Article II.
    *   **Legal Action:** Issue Criminal Trespass Warning (for physical locations) or Cease and Desist letters.
    *   **Law Enforcement:** File a formal police report.
    *   **Target Protection:** Notify the target and assist with safety planning (e.g., changing work numbers, scrubbing public data).
*   **For Level 4 (Imminent):**
    *   Law Enforcement intervention is the primary mechanism.

#### 4.6 Phase VI: Documentation and Closure
1.  **Case File:** The TAT must maintain a confidential case file separate from personnel records, protected by Attorney-Client Privilege where applicable.
2.  **Closure:** A case is considered closed when the threat is resolved, the subject is no longer affiliated with the Organization, or the risk has been mitigated to an acceptable level.
3.  **Post-Incident Review:** For Level 3 or 4 incidents, the TAT must conduct an "After Action Review" within 14 days to update policies and security measures.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Personnel** | Immediately report any threats or concerning behavior; do not attempt to investigate personally. |
| **Supervisors / Managers** | Take all reports seriously; immediately escalate to the TAT; ensure the safety of the reporting individual. |
| **Threat Assessment Team (TAT)** | Conduct investigations, assess risk levels, and determine the management plan. Maintain confidentiality. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Secure digital evidence; revoke system access for subjects under investigation; monitor for digital retaliation (doxxing). |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final authority on expulsions and terminations resulting from threat assessments; primary liaison with Law Enforcement. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Zero Tolerance:** Study GRC Inc. maintains a zero-tolerance policy for violence and threats of violence.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Any employee, volunteer, or member found to have made a credible threat will be subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (per Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6), or expulsion from membership (per Bylaws Article II).
*   **Legal Prosecution:** The Organization reserves the right to pursue criminal prosecution and civil damages against any individual threatening the safety of the community.
*   **Non-Retaliation:** Retaliation against anyone who reports a threat in good faith is strictly prohibited and constitutes a separate violation subject to termination.
