### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant, and fair process for the removal of a Director from the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. for "Cause." This procedure ensures that any removal action adheres to the principles of due process, protects the Organization from liability, and complies with **Article III, Section 3.6** of the Bylaws and **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.211**.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies exclusively to the removal of a Director **by the Board of Directors**.
*   **In Scope:** Removal proceedings initiated by a current Director against another current Director for specific "Cause."
*   **Out of Scope:** Removal of Directors by the Voting Membership (refer to *GOV-PRC-038*); voluntary resignation of a Director; removal of Operational Officers who are not Directors.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cause** | Grounds for removal including, but not limited to: violation of Bylaws, failure to attend meetings (absenteeism), violation of the Code of Ethics, breach of fiduciary duty, conviction of a felony, or actions detrimental to the Organization’s non-profit status. |
| **Petitioner** | The Director(s) initiating the removal motion. |
| **Respondent** | The Director subject to the removal proceedings. |
| **Remaining Directors** | All current Directors excluding the Respondent. |
| **Executive Session** | A meeting or portion of a meeting open only to the Board members, where minutes are kept confidential. |
| **Special Meeting** | A meeting called for a specific purpose outside of the regularly scheduled Board cadence. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Initiation of Removal Proceedings
4.1.1 **Filing a Complaint:** Any Director (the "Petitioner") believing "Cause" exists for the removal of another Director must submit a formal **Written Complaint** to the President (or the Secretary, if the President is the Respondent).
4.1.2 **Complaint Contents:** The Written Complaint must include:
*   The specific "Cause" for removal (citing specific Bylaw sections or Ethical Codes violated).
*   Factual evidence supporting the allegations (e.g., attendance logs, emails, financial records).
*   A request for a Special Meeting to consider removal.

#### 4.2 Preliminary Review and Notice
4.2.1 **Review for Sufficiency:** Upon receipt, the President (or Secretary) must review the complaint to ensure it meets the definition of "Cause" and is not frivolous.
4.2.2 **Issuance of Notice:** If the complaint is valid, the President must issue a **Notice of Removal Hearing** to all Directors, including the Respondent, at least **ten (10) days** prior to the meeting date.
4.2.3 **Notice Contents:** The Notice must include:
*   Date, time, and virtual location (Zoom/Teams) of the Special Meeting.
*   A copy of the Written Complaint and supporting evidence.
*   A statement informing the Respondent of their right to be heard and to present a defense.

#### 4.3 The Removal Hearing (Special Meeting)
4.3.1 **Quorum Requirement:** A quorum of the **Remaining Directors** must be present to conduct the hearing.
4.3.2 **Executive Session:** The meeting must be conducted in Executive Session to protect the privacy of all parties. No non-Directors (staff, members) may attend unless explicitly invited by the Board to provide testimony.
4.3.3 **Presentation of Case:**
*   The Petitioner is granted up to twenty (20) minutes to present the case for removal.
*   The Respondent is granted up to twenty (20) minutes to present a defense, including witnesses or counter-evidence.
4.3.4 **Board Inquiry:** The Remaining Directors may ask questions of both the Petitioner and the Respondent.
4.3.5 **Deliberation:** The Respondent must leave the virtual meeting room (or be placed in a waiting room) during the Board's final deliberation.

#### 4.4 Voting and Resolution
4.4.1 **Voting Standard:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Section 3.6**, removal requires the affirmative vote of a **majority of the Remaining Directors**.
*   *Example:* If the Board has 5 members and 1 is the Respondent, the "Remaining Directors" count is 4. A majority (more than 50%) of 4 is 3. Therefore, 3 votes are required for removal.
4.4.2 **Method of Voting:** Voting must be conducted via a secure, auditable electronic ballot or roll call within the Executive Session.
4.4.3 **Immediate Effect:** If the vote passes, the removal is effective immediately.

#### 4.5 Post-Removal Actions
4.5.1 **Notification:** The President must formally notify the Respondent of the Board's decision in writing within 24 hours.
4.5.2 **Asset and Access Revocation:** The Secretary must immediately notify the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) to:
*   Revoke all system access (email, Slack, Drive, bank accounts).
*   Initiate a remote wipe of any Organization-owned devices (if applicable).
4.5.3 **Corporate Filings:** The Secretary must update the Organization’s internal roster and file any necessary updates with the Texas Secretary of State (e.g., Form 401) if the removed Director was a named governing person on public record.
4.5.4 **Member Transparency:** The Board may, at its discretion, issue a brief statement to the Membership regarding the vacancy, ensuring no defamatory details regarding the "Cause" are released publicly.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **President** | Receives complaints, determines sufficiency, issues Notice of Hearing, and presides over the Special Meeting. |
| **Secretary** | Acts as the presiding officer if the President is the Respondent; records minutes of the Executive Session; ensures regulatory filings are updated. |
| **Petitioner** | Compiles evidence and presents the case for removal. |
| **Respondent** | Responds to allegations and presents a defense. |
| **Remaining Directors** | Review evidence, maintain confidentiality, and cast a vote based on the best interests of the Organization. |
| **CISO** | Executes immediate deprovisioning of access upon notification of removal. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in the invalidation of the removal action, legal liability for **Wrongful Removal** or **Defamation**, and potential personal liability for Directors acting outside the scope of their authority.

Any Director who breaches the confidentiality of the Executive Session regarding removal proceedings is subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including their own removal.
