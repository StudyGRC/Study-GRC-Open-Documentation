### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for the preservation, collection, and production of Electronically Stored Information (ESI) and physical records when Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") is involved in, or reasonably anticipates, litigation, government investigations, or audits.

This procedure ensures compliance with the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure (FRCP), Texas Rules of Civil Procedure, and **Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** of the Organization’s Bylaws. It is designed to prevent "spoliation of evidence," which can result in severe legal sanctions, financial penalties, and reputational damage.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all "Custodians" of Organization records, including:
*   **Personnel:** Board Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and interns.
*   **Third Parties:** Contractors and vendors with access to Organization data.
*   **Data Sources:** All Organization-owned devices, servers, and cloud environments (e.g., Google Workspace, Slack, Discord, AWS), as well as personal devices (BYOD) used for Organization business.

**Exclusions:** This procedure supersedes the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy* for any records subject to a Hold. Routine destruction schedules must be suspended immediately for affected records.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Litigation Hold (Legal Hold)** | A directive requiring the preservation of all data that may relate to a legal action involving the Organization. |
| **ESI** | Electronically Stored Information. Includes emails, voicemails, text messages, Slack/Discord chats, documents, databases, and metadata. |
| **Spoliation** | The destruction, alteration, or mutilation of evidence that is relevant to pending or reasonably foreseeable litigation. |
| **Custodian** | Any individual who possesses, controls, or has access to data relevant to the legal matter. |
| **Discovery** | The pre-trial phase in a lawsuit in which each party investigates the facts of a case by obtaining evidence from the opposing party. |
| **Trigger Event** | An event that creates a "reasonable anticipation of litigation," such as receiving a complaint, a subpoena, a demand letter, or a credible threat of legal action. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Identification of Trigger Event
1.  **Reporting:** Any Director, Officer, or volunteer who receives a subpoena, summons, complaint, or credible threat of litigation must immediately notify the Executive Director and/or the Board President.
2.  **Assessment:** The Executive Director, in consultation with Legal Counsel, will determine if the event triggers the legal duty to preserve evidence.
3.  **Scope Determination:** Legal Counsel will identify:
    *   The specific issues relevant to the litigation.
    *   The date range for the records to be preserved.
    *   The list of Key Custodians (individuals likely to have relevant info).
    *   The relevant systems (e.g., "All Slack channels related to Project X").

#### 4.2 Issuance of Litigation Hold Notice
1.  **Drafting:** Legal Counsel will draft a **Litigation Hold Notice**. This document is confidential and protected by attorney-client privilege.
2.  **Distribution:** The Notice will be sent via email to all identified Custodians and the IT/Security department.
3.  **Contents:** The Notice must clearly state:
    *   The legal duty to preserve records.
    *   The types of records to be preserved (e.g., "All emails with Vendor Y").
    *   An explicit instruction **NOT** to delete, modify, or hide any relevant data.
    *   Instructions to suspend any auto-delete or shredding practices.
4.  **Acknowledgment:** All recipients must sign (electronically) an acknowledgment confirming they have read, understood, and will comply with the Hold.

#### 4.3 Suspension of Routine Destruction (IT Actions)
Upon receipt of the Litigation Hold Notice, the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) or designated IT Administrator must:
1.  **Suspend Auto-Deletion:** Immediately disable auto-retention/deletion policies for identified Custodians in systems including but not limited to:
    *   Email (Google Workspace/Office 365).
    *   Collaboration Tools (Slack/Discord retention settings).
    *   Cloud Storage (Google Drive, Dropbox).
2.  **Preserve Backups:** Ensure backup tapes or snapshots containing relevant data are isolated and protected from being overwritten.
3.  **Device Imaging:** If required by Legal Counsel, create forensic images of laptops or mobile devices belonging to Key Custodians.

#### 4.4 Custodian Responsibilities
Upon receipt of a Litigation Hold Notice, Custodians must:
1.  **Stop Deletion:** Immediately cease deleting any emails, documents, or messages related to the subject matter.
2.  **Preserve Drafts:** Keep all drafts, duplicates, and non-final versions of relevant documents.
3.  **Organize (Do Not Alter):** Create a specific folder (e.g., "Legal Hold - [Case Name]") to segregate relevant files, but **do not** modify the metadata (e.g., "Date Modified") of the original files.
4.  **Disclose Locations:** Inform Legal Counsel of any non-standard locations where data might exist (e.g., personal USB drives, personal email accounts used for work, handwritten notes).

#### 4.5 Monitoring and Reminders
1.  **Quarterly Reminders:** Legal Counsel will issue quarterly reminders to all Custodians to ensure continued compliance.
2.  **New Personnel:** If new personnel join the Organization and assume a role relevant to the litigation, they must be issued the Hold Notice immediately.
3.  **Departing Personnel:** If a Custodian leaves the Organization:
    *   HR must notify Legal Counsel/IT prior to the exit interview.
    *   IT must seize and preserve the departing employee’s devices and accounts indefinitely until the Hold is released. **Re-imaging or wiping the device is strictly prohibited.**

#### 4.6 Data Collection and Review (E-Discovery)
1.  **Collection:** IT and Legal Counsel will collect relevant ESI using forensically sound methods to ensure data integrity. Custodians should not attempt to "self-collect" (emailing files to themselves) unless explicitly instructed, as this alters metadata.
2.  **Processing:** Collected data will be processed for review (deduplication, keyword searching).
3.  **Privilege Review:** Legal Counsel will review documents to identify and withhold communications protected by attorney-client privilege.
4.  **Production:** Non-privileged, responsive documents will be produced to the opposing party in the format requested (e.g., PDF, Native).

#### 4.7 Release of Litigation Hold
1.  **Conclusion of Matter:** The Hold remains in effect until the litigation is fully resolved, including all appeals, or the statute of limitations has expired.
2.  **Release Notice:** Only Legal Counsel or the Board may authorize the lifting of a Hold. A written **Release of Litigation Hold Notice** will be sent to all Custodians and IT.
3.  **Resumption of Retention Policy:** Upon release, data subject to the Hold reverts to the standard *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*. Records that have exceeded their retention period during the Hold may then be destroyed.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director / General Counsel** | Acts as the Hold Administrator; determines scope; issues notices; oversees the legal strategy. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Implements technical holds (suspends auto-delete); manages forensic collection; secures departing employee data. |
| **Custodians (Staff/Volunteers)** | Acknowledge the Hold; preserve data without alteration; disclose data locations. |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensures resources are available for compliance; oversees Holds involving Executive leadership. |
| **HR Department** | Notifies Legal/IT of departing employees subject to a Hold to prevent asset recycling. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Strict Compliance Required:** Failure to comply with a Litigation Hold is a violation of federal/state law and Organization policy.

**Consequences:**
1.  **Internal:** Violation of this procedure may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board/Volunteer corps.
2.  **Legal:** Spoliation of evidence can lead to:
    *   Adverse Inference Instructions (the judge tells the jury to assume the missing evidence showed guilt).
    *   Monetary sanctions against the Organization and individual Directors.
    *   Dismissal of the Organization’s claims or defenses.
    *   Criminal obstruction of justice charges.
