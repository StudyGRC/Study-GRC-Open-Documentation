### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, objective, and non-discriminatory process for reviewing, verifying, and approving applications for **Supporting Membership** within Study GRC Inc. This workflow ensures compliance with **Article II of the Bylaws**, maintains the integrity of the Voting Member registry, and ensures that all applicants are treated with consistency and transparency regarding their rights and responsibilities.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all individuals seeking "Supporting Member" status.
*   **In Scope:** New applications based on financial contribution; new applications based on volunteer service; annual renewal verification.
*   **Out of Scope:** "Honorary Members" (appointed by the Board per Bylaws Section 2.1); "Community Participants" (non-voting); Corporate sponsors (unless an individual representative is designated).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Supporting Member** | A voting class of membership for individuals who have demonstrated commitment through financial contribution or volunteer service (Bylaws § 2.1). |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a member who is current on dues or has fulfilled volunteer service requirements within the rolling 12-month period. |
| **Voting Registry** | The official alphabetical list of members entitled to vote, maintained in accordance with Texas BOC § 22.158. |
| **DKG** | Director of Kindness and Generosity; the Operational Officer responsible for community culture and membership experience. |
| **Qualifying Contribution** | The minimum financial donation or volunteer hours required to achieve Supporting Member status, as defined in the *Membership Policy Manual*. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Application Submission
4.1.1 **Initiation:** Individuals may initiate the process by:
*   Submitting the *Supporting Member Application Form* via the Organization’s secure portal; or
*   Completing a qualifying financial donation via the official payment processor, which automatically triggers a membership invitation.

4.1.2 **Data Collection:** The Organization must collect the following minimum data points to satisfy Texas BOC § 22.158 (Voting List requirements):
*   Full Legal Name.
*   Primary Email Address (for Notice of Meetings).
*   Basis of Qualification (Financial or Volunteer).
*   Attestation to the *Code of Ethics and Conduct*.

#### 4.2 Verification of Eligibility
Upon receipt of an application, the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or their designee must verify eligibility within five (5) business days.

4.2.1 **Financial Qualification Path:**
*   The DKG must cross-reference the applicant's name with the *Donation Receipt Log* or verify with the Treasurer.
*   **Criteria:** The donation must meet the minimum threshold defined in the current *Membership Dues Structure* and must have cleared the banking system.

4.2.2 **Volunteer Qualification Path:**
*   The DKG must review the *Volunteer Hours Tracking Log*.
*   **Criteria:** The applicant must have completed the minimum required service hours within the preceding 12 months, verified by a Committee Chair or Project Lead.

#### 4.3 Review and Determination
4.3.1 **Consistency Check:** To ensure non-discrimination, the DKG must ensure the applicant meets the objective criteria. Subjective evaluation of the applicant's "character" is not permitted unless there is a documented violation of the *Code of Ethics*.

4.3.2 **Approval:**
*   If criteria are met, the application is marked "Approved."
*   The DKG updates the status in the Membership Database (CRM).

4.3.3 **Denial:**
*   If criteria are *not* met, the application is marked "Pending Information" or "Denied."
*   **Prohibited Reasons:** Denial cannot be based on race, religion, gender, political affiliation, or any protected class.
*   **Ethical Denial:** If an applicant is denied due to a known violation of the *Code of Ethics* or *Community Guidelines*, the denial must be reviewed and countersigned by the Executive Director.

#### 4.4 Onboarding and Registry Update
4.4.1 **Notification:** The DKG must send the *New Member Welcome Packet* via email within 48 hours of approval. This packet must include:
*   Confirmation of Voting Rights.
*   Link to the Bylaws and *Code of Ethics*.
*   Access credentials for the Member-only voting portal.

4.4.2 **Registry Maintenance:**
*   The DKG must transmit the new member's details to the Secretary.
*   The Secretary (or designee) must add the member to the official **Voting Registry**.
*   **Audit Trail:** The date of entry into the registry is the effective date for voting eligibility.

#### 4.5 Annual Renewal and Purging
4.5.1 **Expiration:** Supporting Membership is valid for 12 months from the date of qualification.
4.5.2 **Grace Period:** Members are granted a 30-day grace period to renew their financial contribution or volunteer hours.
4.5.3 **Removal:** If the grace period expires without renewal, the DKG must change the status to "Community Participant" (Non-Voting) and remove the individual from the Voting Registry to ensure quorum calculations remain accurate (Bylaws § 2.4).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Applicant** | Provides accurate information and proof of qualification (payment or service logs). |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Process Owner. Reviews applications, verifies eligibility, and manages the onboarding experience. |
| **Treasurer** | Validates receipt of financial dues/donations upon request. |
| **Secretary** | Oversees the integrity of the Voting Registry; ensures new members are added to the official roll in compliance with Texas BOC. |
| **Executive Director** | Final arbiter for denials based on ethical/conduct violations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Consistency Mandate:** Failure to follow this procedure—specifically regarding the objective verification of criteria—may result in claims of discrimination or invalidation of election results.
*   **Audit:** The Membership Committee shall conduct a random audit of 10% of new approvals annually to ensure criteria were applied consistently.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Staff or volunteers found to be manipulating the approval process to influence voting outcomes will be subject to immediate termination and removal from the Organization.
