### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a compliant, standardized process for verifying the identity and employment authorization of employees hired by Study GRC Inc. who work remotely. This procedure ensures compliance with the Immigration Reform and Control Act of 1986 (IRCA) and U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) regulations, specifically addressing the challenges of physical document inspection in a remote-first organizational structure.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all newly hired employees of Study GRC Inc., regardless of their physical location.
*   **Inclusions:** All W-2 employees (full-time, part-time, temporary).
*   **Exclusions:** Independent Contractors (1099), unpaid volunteers, and Community Participants as defined in Bylaws Article II.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Form I-9** | Employment Eligibility Verification form required by the USCIS for all paid employees in the U.S. |
| **Section 1** | The portion of Form I-9 completed by the employee (attestation of status). Must be completed by the first day of employment. |
| **Section 2** | The portion of Form I-9 completed by the employer or their authorized representative (document review). Must be completed within three (3) business days of the start date. |
| **Authorized Representative** | A person designated by the Organization to act on its behalf to complete Section 2. This person must physically examine the employee's documents. |
| **E-Verify** | A web-based system that allows enrolled employers to confirm the eligibility of their employees to work in the United States. |
| **DHS Alternative Procedure** | A permanent option for E-Verify employers in good standing to examine documents via live video interaction instead of physical presence (effective August 1, 2023). |

### 4. Procedures

Study GRC Inc. utilizes two methods for remote verification depending on the organization's current E-Verify enrollment status. **Method A (Authorized Representative)** is the standard default. **Method B (DHS Alternative Procedure)** may only be used if explicitly authorized by the CFO/HR Lead.

#### 4.1 General Requirements (All Methods)
4.1.1 **Timeline:**
*   **Day 1:** The employee **must** complete and sign Section 1 of Form I-9 no later than the close of business on their first day of employment (hire date).
*   **Day 3:** The employer (or representative) **must** complete Section 2 no later than three (3) business days after the employee's first day of employment.

4.1.2 **List of Acceptable Documents:**
The employee must present unexpired original documentation from the [List of Acceptable Documents](https://www.uscis.gov/i-9-central/form-i-9-acceptable-documents) (List A, or a combination of List B and List C).

#### 4.2 Method A: Authorized Representative (Physical Inspection Proxy)
*Use this method if the Organization is NOT currently enrolled in E-Verify or chooses not to use the video alternative.*

4.2.1 **Designation of Representative:**
Study GRC Inc. authorizes the new hire to select a trusted individual to act as the Organization’s "Authorized Representative." This individual may be:
*   A notary public (acting as a private citizen, not notarizing the document).
*   A personnel officer from a local university or partner organization.
*   A trusted adult (friend, neighbor, or family member) capable of reviewing documents and signing a legal form.

4.2.2 **Employee Instructions:**
The Hiring Manager or HR Lead must send the "Remote I-9 Instructions Email" to the new hire, containing:
*   A blank Form I-9 (or link to the electronic onboarding system).
*   Instructions to locate an Authorized Representative.
*   A warning that Study GRC Inc. remains liable for any errors made by the representative.

4.2.3 **Action by Authorized Representative:**
The designated representative must:
1.  Meet with the employee **in person**.
2.  Physically examine the original documents presented by the employee.
3.  Ensure the documents reasonably appear to be genuine and relate to the person presenting them.
4.  Complete Section 2 of Form I-9.
5.  In the "Title of Employer or Authorized Representative" field, enter "Authorized Representative."
6.  In the "Organization Name" field, enter "Study GRC Inc."
7.  Sign and date the form.

4.2.4 **Submission and Audit:**
1.  The employee must upload the completed Form I-9 to the Organization’s secure HR portal or encrypted email channel immediately upon completion.
2.  The HR Lead must review the form for errors (e.g., missing dates, incorrect document numbers) within 24 hours of receipt.
3.  If errors are found, the HR Lead must coordinate with the employee to rectify them immediately.

#### 4.3 Method B: DHS Alternative Procedure (Video Verification)
*Use this method ONLY if Study GRC Inc. is an E-Verify participant in good standing.*

4.3.1 **Pre-Conditions:**
The HR Lead must confirm the employee has voluntarily chosen this method. If the employee refuses, Method A must be used.

4.3.2 **Document Transmission:**
The employee must transmit a clear copy (front and back, if applicable) of the identity/employment authorization documents to the HR Lead via secure file transfer **before** the video call.

4.3.3 **Live Video Interaction:**
The HR Lead (or specific designee) must conduct a live video call (e.g., Zoom, Teams) with the employee. During the call:
1.  The employee must present the **original** documents to the camera.
2.  The HR Lead must compare the original documents on video to the transmitted copies and to the employee's face to ensure authenticity.

4.3.4 **Form Completion:**
1.  The HR Lead completes Section 2.
2.  The HR Lead **must** check the box in the Additional Information field (or the specific checkbox on the 2023 edition form) indicating "Alternative Procedure" was used.

4.3.5 **Document Retention:**
Unlike standard I-9s, clear and legible copies of **all** documents presented (List A, or List B and C) must be retained with the Form I-9 in the employee’s personnel file.

#### 4.4 Storage and Retention
4.4.1 **Security:**
Completed Forms I-9 must be stored separately from general personnel files in a secure, access-controlled digital repository (e.g., encrypted HRIS or restricted cloud folder) in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

4.4.2 **Retention Period:**
Forms must be retained for three (3) years after the date of hire or one (1) year after the date employment ends, whichever is later.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Initiates the onboarding process and ensures the new hire is aware of the I-9 deadline. |
| **New Hire** | Completes Section 1 by Day 1; secures an Authorized Representative (Method A) or transmits docs (Method B); presents original documents. |
| **Authorized Representative** | Physically examines documents and signs Section 2 on behalf of Study GRC Inc. (Method A). |
| **HR Lead / CFO** | Audits completed forms; conducts video verification (Method B); manages E-Verify submissions; ensures secure storage. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure exposes Study GRC Inc. to significant civil and criminal penalties from the Department of Homeland Security and Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE).

*   **Late Completion:** Fines range from $272 to $2,701 per form.
*   **Substantive Errors:** Fines range from $272 to $2,701 per form.
*   **Knowingly Hiring Unauthorized Aliens:** Fines range from $676 to $5,404 per worker (first offense).

Employees who fail to provide required documentation within three (3) business days of their start date are subject to immediate termination of employment. Staff members responsible for processing I-9s who negligently fail to follow this procedure may face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
