### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for requesting, reviewing, and approving Paid Time Off (PTO). This procedure ensures that Study GRC Inc. maintains adequate staffing levels to meet operational goals and grant deliverables (**Workforce Planning**) while supporting employee well-being and work-life balance. This document operationalizes the authority granted to Operational Leadership under **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.1** to manage day-to-day execution.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Employees** (full-time and part-time) eligible for PTO benefits.
*   **Exclusions:** Independent Contractors, Volunteers, and temporary staff not eligible for benefits.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **PTO (Paid Time Off)** | A bank of hours that employees may use for vacation, personal business, or illness, as defined in the Employee Handbook. |
| **HRIS** | Human Resources Information System; the digital platform used for time tracking and leave management. |
| **Blackout Period** | Designated dates during which PTO requests may be restricted or denied due to critical business needs (e.g., major conferences, grant deadlines). |
| **Unscheduled Absence** | Absence due to illness, injury, or emergency that prevents adherence to standard notice requirements. |
| **Workforce Planning** | The process of analyzing current and future staffing needs to ensure the organization has the right resources available to meet objectives. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Advance Notice Requirements
To facilitate effective **Workforce Planning**, employees must submit PTO requests in advance based on the duration of the requested leave.

| Duration of Leave | Required Notice Period |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Less than 1 day** | 24 Hours (or by manager agreement) |
| **1 to 2 days** | 3 Business Days |
| **3 to 5 days** | 2 Weeks |
| **More than 5 days** | 4 Weeks |

*   **Exception:** Emergency situations or sudden illness (see Section 4.4).

#### 4.2 Submission of Requests
4.2.1 The Employee must log into the designated **HRIS**.
4.2.2 The Employee must select the specific dates and total hours requested.
4.2.3 If the leave is for a partial day, the Employee must specify the hours they will be unavailable (e.g., "1:00 PM to 5:00 PM").
4.2.4 The Employee must verify they have a sufficient accrued PTO balance to cover the request.
4.2.5 Upon submission, the HRIS will automatically notify the Employee's Direct Manager.

#### 4.3 Managerial Review and Approval
4.3.1 **Timeline:** Managers must review and act upon PTO requests within **three (3) business days** of submission.
4.3.2 **Review Criteria:** Before approving, the Manager must assess:
*   **Accrual Balance:** Does the employee have sufficient time accrued?
*   **Team Coverage:** Will there be sufficient staff remaining to handle daily operations?
*   **Deadlines:** Does the leave conflict with critical grant reporting deadlines or project milestones?
*   **Blackout Periods:** Does the request fall within a designated Blackout Period?
4.3.3 **Conflict Resolution:** If multiple employees on the same team request the same dates, and operational requirements cannot accommodate all requests, priority will be given based on:
1.  Date of submission (First-come, first-served).
2.  Criticality of the employee's role during that specific period.
4.3.4 **Decision:** The Manager will mark the request as "Approved" or "Denied" in the HRIS. If denied, the Manager must provide a written reason in the system comments.

#### 4.4 Unscheduled Absences (Sick/Emergency)
4.4.1 In the event of illness or emergency, the Employee must notify their Direct Manager via email or the organization's instant messaging platform (e.g., Slack/Teams) no later than **one (1) hour** before their scheduled start time.
4.4.2 The Employee must submit the PTO request in the HRIS retroactively upon their return to work, marking the reason as "Sick/Emergency."
4.4.3 If an employee is incapacitated, the Manager or HR may enter the time on their behalf.

#### 4.5 Modifications and Cancellations
4.5.1 If an Employee needs to change dates or cancel an approved request, they must modify the entry in the HRIS immediately.
4.5.2 If the modification extends the leave, it is treated as a new request subject to approval.
4.5.3 If the leave is cancelled, the hours will be returned to the Employee's PTO bank.

#### 4.6 Unpaid Leave
4.6.1 Requests for time off exceeding the Employee's accrued balance (Unpaid Leave) require approval from both the Direct Manager and the **Executive Director**.
4.6.2 Unpaid leave generally will not be granted unless required by law (e.g., FMLA, ADA).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Submits requests in accordance with notice periods; ensures sufficient accrual balance; communicates coverage plans for their absence. |
| **Direct Manager** | Reviews requests against workforce planning needs; approves/denies within 3 business days; ensures team coverage. |
| **Human Resources** | Maintains the HRIS; defines Blackout Periods; audits PTO usage for compliance with federal grant timekeeping rules. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves exceptions to policy and requests for Unpaid Leave. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in the denial of PTO requests. Excessive unscheduled absences or taking leave without approval ("Job Abandonment") may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

For employees funded by federal grants, accurate recording of leave is mandatory for compliance with **2 CFR 200.430 (Compensation - Personal Services)**. Falsification of leave records constitutes fraud.
