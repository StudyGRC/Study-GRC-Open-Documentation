### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized methodology for the periodic self-evaluation of internal controls within Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with **2 CFR § 200.303 (Internal Controls)** by shifting the responsibility for risk monitoring to the operational process owners. The objective is to proactively identify control deficiencies, operational inefficiencies, and compliance gaps before they are detected by external auditors or result in material non-compliance.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all departments and functional areas of Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to Finance, Human Resources, Information Technology, and Program Operations.

**Inclusions:**
*   Financial reporting controls.
*   Federal grant compliance (Uniform Guidance).
*   Data privacy and security controls (GDPR/TDPSA).
*   Youth protection and safeguarding protocols.

**Exclusions:**
*   External financial audits (conducted by independent CPAs).
*   Legal strategy and privileged litigation assessments.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Control Self-Assessment (CSA)** | A methodology used to review key business objectives, risks involved in achieving those objectives, and internal controls designed to manage those risks. |
| **Internal Control** | A process, effected by the Board of Directors, management, and other personnel, designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives relating to operations, reporting, and compliance (COSO Framework). |
| **Process Owner** | The Operational Officer or Department Head responsible for the execution and management of a specific business process (e.g., the CISO is the Process Owner for IT Security). |
| **Test of Design (TOD)** | Verifying that a control, if operating as prescribed, would effectively prevent or detect an error or irregularity. |
| **Test of Operating Effectiveness (TOE)** | Verifying that a control is operating as designed and is being performed by a qualified person or system. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Annual CSA Planning
The Audit & Risk Committee, in coordination with the CFO, must establish the CSA Schedule for the fiscal year.

1.  **Risk Prioritization:** The CFO must review the **Strategic Plan** and **Risk Register** to identify high-risk areas requiring assessment.
2.  **Schedule Issuance:** By January 31st of each fiscal year, the CFO must issue a CSA Calendar to all Process Owners, designating which quarters specific departments will undergo assessment.
3.  **Tool Distribution:** The CFO must provide the standardized **CSA Questionnaire/Checklist** (Form INT-FRM-003a) to the Process Owner at least 14 days prior to the scheduled assessment start date.

#### 4.2 Conducting the Self-Assessment
Process Owners must complete the assessment within the designated timeframe (typically 10 business days).

1.  **Process Walkthrough:** The Process Owner must review current written policies and procedures against actual daily operations.
2.  **Control Verification (Design):** The Process Owner must answer: "Does the documented procedure adequately mitigate the risk?"
    *   *Example:* Does the procurement policy require three bids for purchases over the Simplified Acquisition Threshold?
3.  **Control Verification (Effectiveness):** The Process Owner must select a random sample of transactions (minimum of 5) from the prior quarter to verify the control was executed.
    *   *Example:* Pulling 5 expense reports to verify digital approval signatures and receipt attachments.
4.  **Documentation:** Evidence of testing (e.g., screenshots, transaction logs, checklists) must be linked or attached to the CSA Questionnaire.

#### 4.3 Evaluation and Grading
Upon completion of the questionnaire, the Process Owner must rate the maturity of their controls:

*   **Effective:** Controls are designed correctly and operating consistently.
*   **Needs Improvement:** Controls are designed correctly but executed inconsistently (human error).
*   **Deficient:** Controls are missing, poorly designed, or consistently failed.

#### 4.4 Review and Validation
1.  **Submission:** The completed CSA is submitted to the CFO (or Internal Audit function).
2.  **Independent Validation:** The CFO or a designated peer reviewer must perform a "spot check" validation on at least 20% of the submitted CSAs to ensure accuracy and prevent "rubber stamping."
3.  **Gap Analysis:** If a rating of "Needs Improvement" or "Deficient" is recorded, the Process Owner must draft a **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** immediately.

#### 4.5 Reporting
1.  **Summary Report:** The CFO must compile all CSA results into a quarterly **Internal Control Status Report**.
2.  **Board Oversight:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI (Committees)**, this report must be presented to the Audit & Risk Committee. The report must highlight:
    *   New deficiencies identified.
    *   Status of open Corrective Action Plans.
    *   Systemic trends (e.g., widespread failure to adhere to remote work security protocols).

#### 4.6 Remediation Monitoring
1.  **Tracking:** All identified deficiencies are logged in the **Master Risk Register**.
2.  **Follow-Up:** The CFO must schedule a follow-up review (30, 60, or 90 days depending on severity) to verify the CAP has been implemented.
3.  **Closure:** A deficiency can only be marked "Closed" after a re-test confirms the control is now effective.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Approves the CSA methodology; reviews aggregated results; ensures resources are available for remediation. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns the CSA process; maintains the schedule; trains Process Owners; aggregates reporting. |
| **Process Owners (Dept Heads)** | Conducts the self-assessment honestly; retains evidence; develops and executes Corrective Action Plans. |
| **Internal Audit (if applicable)** | Performs independent validation of CSA results to ensure integrity of the process. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
This procedure is mandatory for compliance with federal grant regulations. Falsification of CSA results (e.g., marking a control as "Effective" when it is known to be failing) constitutes a violation of the **Code of Ethics** and may result in immediate termination of employment and potential legal action under the False Claims Act.

Failure to complete the CSA by the assigned deadline will be reported to the Executive Director and reflected in the Process Owner's annual performance review.
