### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized framework for the lifecycle management of grants awarded to Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). This procedure ensures compliance with federal regulations, specifically **2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance)**, state laws, and private donor requirements. It defines the steps for application, acceptance, financial administration, programmatic monitoring, and closeout to mitigate the risk of audit findings, clawbacks, or reputational damage.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all grants, cooperative agreements, and restricted funding awards received by the Organization from federal, state, local, or private sources.
*   **Inclusions:** Federal pass-through funds, direct federal awards, and private foundation grants with specific deliverables or budget restrictions.
*   **Exclusions:** Unrestricted general operating donations and individual gifts, which are governed by the *Gift Acceptance Policy*.
*   **Personnel:** Applies to the Board of Directors, Operational Officers (CFO, VP of Education), Program Managers, and any staff (employees or contractors) whose salaries are funded by grant sources.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **2 CFR Part 200** | The "Uniform Guidance" – Federal regulations consolidating grant requirements for cost principles, audit, and administrative requirements. |
| **Allocable Cost** | A cost that is chargeable to a specific grant because it provides a direct benefit to that grant's objectives (2 CFR §200.405). |
| **Allowable Cost** | A cost that is necessary, reasonable, and compliant with the terms of the award and federal cost principles (2 CFR §200.403). |
| **Period of Performance** | The time during which the Organization may incur new obligations to carry out the work authorized under the federal award. |
| **Program Manager / PI** | The individual responsible for the technical and programmatic execution of the grant project (e.g., VP of Education). |
| **Drawdown** | The process of requesting payment from the funding agency to reimburse the Organization for allowable expenses incurred. |
| **Indirect Costs (F&A)** | Facilities and Administrative costs that are incurred for common or joint objectives and cannot be readily identified with a particular final cost objective. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Pre-Award and Proposal Development
Before applying for funding, the Organization must ensure the opportunity aligns with the Mission defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**.

1.  **Opportunity Assessment:** The Program Manager must present a "Go/No-Go" analysis to the Executive Director, evaluating alignment with the strategic plan and resource capacity.
2.  **Budget Development:**
    *   The CFO must draft the budget to ensure all costs are **Allowable**, **Reasonable**, and **Allocable** per 2 CFR §200 Subpart E.
    *   **Indirect Costs:** The budget must include the Organization's Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement (NICRA) rate or the *de minimis* rate of 10% of Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC) if no NICRA exists (2 CFR §200.414).
    *   **Matching Funds:** If cost-sharing is required, the source of non-federal matching funds must be identified and verified as available per the *Cost Sharing and Matching Policy*.
3.  **Board Notification:** For grant proposals exceeding $50,000 or requiring new permanent staff hires, the Executive Director must notify the Board of Directors prior to submission.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Award Acceptance and Setup
Upon receipt of a Notice of Award (NoA):

1.  **Review of Terms:** The CFO and Executive Director must review the NoA for special conditions, reporting schedules, and prohibitions.
2.  **Execution:** The Executive Director signs the agreement. Per **Bylaws Article V**, the Executive Director has operational authority, but significant contracts may require Board ratification depending on the *Delegation of Authority Matrix*.
3.  **Financial System Setup:**
    *   The CFO must establish a unique **Grant Code** or **Cost Center** in the accounting system (2 CFR §200.302(b)(1)).
    *   Budget line items must be loaded into the accounting system to enable budget-to-actual monitoring.
    *   Access rights to the grant code are restricted to authorized personnel.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Post-Award Financial Management
All spending must adhere to the *Internal Controls Framework*.

1.  **Expenditure Authorization:**
    *   Program Managers initiate spending requests.
    *   The CFO approves requests only after verifying:
        *   Funds are available in the specific budget line.
        *   The expense is allowable under the specific grant terms.
        *   The expense is incurred within the Period of Performance.
2.  **Procurement:** All purchases using grant funds must follow the *Procurement Policy (Federal Standards)*, specifically adhering to the micro-purchase, small purchase, and competitive bid thresholds defined in 2 CFR §200.320.
3.  **Time and Effort Reporting:**
    *   All employees paid via grant funds must complete **Personnel Activity Reports (PARs)** or time sheets that reflect the actual distribution of activity, not just the budgeted percentage (2 CFR §200.430).
    *   These reports must be signed by the employee and supervisor monthly.
4.  **Cash Management (Drawdowns):**
    *   The Organization operates on a reimbursement basis unless otherwise authorized.
    *   The CFO prepares drawdowns (e.g., via PMS, ASAP) based on *actual* immediate cash requirements or reimbursement of expenses already paid.
    *   Supporting documentation (general ledger detail) must be retained for every drawdown.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Monitoring and Reporting
1.  **Financial Reporting (FFR):**
    *   The CFO is responsible for filing the Federal Financial Report (SF-425) by the deadline (usually quarterly or annually).
    *   Reports must reconcile exactly with the Organization’s general ledger.
2.  **Programmatic Reporting (PPR):**
    *   The Program Manager is responsible for drafting the Performance Progress Report.
    *   The report must compare actual accomplishments to the objectives established in the award (2 CFR §200.328).
3.  **Budget Variance Analysis:**
    *   The CFO and Program Manager must meet monthly to review budget-to-actuals.
    *   **Re-budgeting:** If a budget transfer exceeds the threshold allowed by the donor (often 10% of the total budget), the CFO must submit a prior approval request to the granting agency (2 CFR §200.308).

#### 4.5 Phase V: Subrecipient Management
If the Organization passes funds through to another entity (Subrecipient):

1.  **Classification:** The CFO must determine if the partner is a Subrecipient or a Contractor using the checklist in 2 CFR §200.331.
2.  **Risk Assessment:** Before awarding funds, the Organization must evaluate the subrecipient's risk of non-compliance (2 CFR §200.332(b)).
3.  **Monitoring:** The Program Manager must monitor the subrecipient's activities to ensure the subaward is used for authorized purposes.

#### 4.6 Phase VI: Closeout
Closeout must occur within 120 days of the end of the period of performance (2 CFR §200.344).

1.  **Final Reconciliation:** The CFO ensures all allowable expenses are posted and all obligations are liquidated.
2.  **Equipment Disposition:** For equipment >$5,000 acquired with grant funds, the Organization must request disposition instructions from the awarding agency unless title is vested in the Organization (2 CFR §200.313).
3.  **Record Retention:** All financial records, supporting documents, and statistical records must be retained for **three (3) years** from the date of submission of the final expenditure report, in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final signatory on grant agreements; ensures organizational capacity to meet grant objectives; reports major grant activity to the Board. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | **Document Owner.** Responsible for financial compliance, drawdowns, FFR reporting, audit readiness, and enforcing cost principles. |
| **Program Manager / PI** | Responsible for technical execution, drafting PPRs, initiating allowable spend, and monitoring subrecipients. |
| **Board of Directors** | Provides oversight via the *Annual Financial Report* (Bylaws Art. VIII); approves high-risk or high-value grant applications. |
| **Staff/Volunteers** | Must accurately record time and effort if compensated by grant funds; must adhere to procurement policies. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure may result in:
*   **Disallowed Costs:** The Organization being forced to repay funds to the government using unrestricted assets.
*   **Enforcement Action:** Suspension or debarment from receiving future federal funds.
*   **Legal Action:** Liability under the **False Claims Act** for submitting inaccurate financial reports.

Violations of this procedure by employees or officers will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, as outlined in the *Employee Handbook*.
