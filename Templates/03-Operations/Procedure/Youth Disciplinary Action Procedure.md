### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, fair, and transparent process for addressing behavioral infractions by youth participants (minors under age 18) within Study GRC Inc. programs. This document ensures that the Organization balances its duty to maintain a safe, inclusive environment with the principles of **Due Process**, ensuring that no youth participant is unfairly deprived of access to educational resources without notice and an opportunity to be heard.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all youth participants engaging in Study GRC Inc. activities, including but not limited to:
*   **Virtual Environments:** Discord servers, Zoom webinars, learning management systems (LMS), and collaborative coding platforms.
*   **In-Person Events:** Workshops, conferences, and meetups.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to adult volunteers, employees, or contractors, whose conduct is governed by the *Employee Handbook* or *Volunteer Code of Conduct*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Youth Participant** | Any individual under the age of 18 participating in Organization activities. |
| **Minor Infraction** | Behavior that disrupts the learning environment but poses no safety risk (e.g., spamming chat, mild disrespect, off-topic posting). |
| **Major Infraction** | Behavior that threatens safety, violates laws, or constitutes harassment (e.g., bullying, hate speech, sharing inappropriate content, doxxing). |
| **Due Process** | The guarantee of fairness in decision-making, specifically requiring Notice of the allegation and an Opportunity to be Heard before a final decision is made. |
| **Suspension** | Temporary removal of access to Organization resources and community spaces pending investigation or as a time-bound sanction. |
| **Expulsion** | Permanent removal of the participant from the Organization and revocation of all access rights. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Categorization of Infractions
Upon observation or report of misconduct, the designated Moderator or Staff Member must categorize the behavior to determine the immediate response.

*   **Level 1 (Minor):** Unintentional or low-impact disruption (e.g., microphone noise, spamming emojis, "trolling").
*   **Level 2 (Moderate):** Repeated Level 1 offenses, use of profanity, minor insults, or cheating in gamified learning.
*   **Level 3 (Severe):** Hate speech, sexual harassment, threats of violence, sharing pornography, "doxxing" (sharing private info), or illegal acts.

#### 4.2 Immediate Response and Stabilization
*   **Level 1:** Verbal or written warning via private message. No formal log required unless repeated.
*   **Level 2:** Temporary "mute" or removal from the specific session/channel for the remainder of the day. Incident must be logged in the *Youth Protection Incident Log*.
*   **Level 3:** Immediate suspension of all access (ban from Discord/Zoom). The staff member must preserve evidence (screenshots, chat logs, recordings) immediately.

#### 4.3 Investigation (Fact-Finding)
For Level 2 (repeated) and Level 3 infractions requiring potential long-term suspension or expulsion:
1.  The **Youth Protection Officer (YPO)** is notified within 2 hours of the incident.
2.  The YPO reviews logs, interviews witnesses (if applicable), and secures evidence.
3.  The YPO creates an *Incident Case File*.

#### 4.4 Notice to Parents/Guardians (Due Process Step 1)
Within 24 hours of a suspension or investigation initiation, the Organization must provide written notice to the parent/guardian on file.
*   **Method:** Email to the registered address.
*   **Content:** The notice must include:
    *   Description of the alleged infraction (time, place, nature of conduct).
    *   Specific rule(s) violated (citing the *Youth Code of Conduct*).
    *   Current status (e.g., "Suspended pending review").
    *   Invitation to submit a written statement or attend a virtual review meeting.

#### 4.5 Disciplinary Review Meeting (Due Process Step 2)
For potential expulsions or suspensions longer than 7 days, a Review Meeting is mandatory unless waived by the parent.
1.  **Attendees:** Youth Protection Officer, Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG), Parent/Guardian, and Youth Participant.
2.  **Format:** Virtual meeting (video required for adults).
3.  **Agenda:**
    *   Organization presents the evidence.
    *   Youth/Parent provides their perspective or defense.
    *   Discussion of mitigating factors (age, intent, prior history).

#### 4.6 Determination and Sanctions
Following the review (or after 48 hours if the parent fails to respond to the Notice), the DKG will issue a decision based on the "Preponderance of Evidence" standard.

**Sanction Matrix:**
*   **Written Reprimand:** Formal warning in the file.
*   **Behavioral Contract:** Continued participation contingent on specific behavioral improvements signed by youth and parent.
*   **Restorative Justice:** Requirement to apologize or repair harm (e.g., community service within the context of the program), if appropriate and agreed upon.
*   **Fixed-Term Suspension:** Removal for 1 week to 6 months.
*   **Expulsion:** Permanent termination of Community Participant status.

#### 4.7 Notification of Decision
A formal *Decision Letter* is sent to the parent/guardian within 48 hours of the Review Meeting. This letter details the sanction, the effective dates, and the appeal process.

#### 4.8 Appeals Process
1.  **Right to Appeal:** Parents may appeal a decision of **Expulsion** only.
2.  **Submission:** Appeals must be submitted in writing to the Board Secretary within 5 business days of the Decision Letter.
3.  **Grounds for Appeal:**
    *   New evidence not available during the investigation.
    *   Procedural error (e.g., lack of notice).
    *   Sanction is grossly disproportionate to the offense.
4.  **Board Review:** A sub-committee of the Board (excluding any staff involved in the original decision) will review the record.
5.  **Final Ruling:** The Board’s decision is final and binding.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Moderator / Staff** | Observe behavior, issue Level 1 warnings, preserve evidence, and report Level 2/3 infractions. |
| **Youth Protection Officer (YPO)** | Conduct impartial investigations, maintain case files, and ensure evidence is preserved. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Adjudicate disciplinary reviews, issue sanctions, and communicate with parents. |
| **Board of Directors** | Review appeals regarding Expulsion to ensure Due Process was followed. |
| **Parent / Guardian** | Ensure youth compliance with sanctions and participate in the review process. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure by staff to follow this procedure (e.g., suspending a minor without notifying parents, or failing to preserve evidence) may result in disciplinary action against the staff member, up to and including termination.

Failure by a youth participant to abide by the terms of a suspension (e.g., evading a ban using an alternate account/VPN) will result in immediate, non-appealable Expulsion.
