### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a strict, standardized protocol for the safe release of minors participating in Study GRC Inc. in-person events, workshops, and educational programs. This procedure mitigates the risks of custodial interference, kidnapping, and unauthorized access to minors, ensuring compliance with Texas Family Code requirements regarding child custody and safety.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   All Study GRC Inc. staff, volunteers, and contractors (collectively "Personnel") managing in-person events where minors (individuals under 18 years of age) are present without their parents/guardians remaining on-site.
*   All parents, legal guardians, and authorized designees.

**Exclusions:**
*   Virtual/Online events (governed by *Digital Interaction with Minors Policy*).
*   Events explicitly designated as "Family Attendance Required" where parents maintain supervision of their children at all times.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authorized Guardian** | A parent, legal guardian, or individual explicitly named in writing by the registering parent/guardian as eligible to pick up the minor. |
| **Check-Out Station** | A designated physical location at an event venue where the transfer of custody occurs. |
| **Custodial Order** | A certified court document defining legal custody and visitation rights, used to restrict access to a minor. |
| **Self-Release** | The act of a minor leaving the event premises unaccompanied (e.g., walking home or driving), permitted only for certain age groups with prior written consent. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The requirement that a minimum of two authorized adult personnel must be present with minors at all times. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Pre-Event Registration Requirements
4.1.1 **Mandatory Designation:** During the registration process for any drop-off event, the registering parent/guardian must provide the full legal names and contact numbers of all individuals authorized to pick up the minor.
4.1.2 **Emergency Contacts:** At least one emergency contact who is distinct from the primary guardian must be provided.
4.1.3 **Custody Restrictions:** If a biological parent is legally prohibited from picking up a child, the registering guardian must provide a copy of the active Court Order/Decree. Study GRC Inc. personnel will abide by the most recent court order on file.

#### 4.2 Check-In Protocol
4.2.1 **Verification:** Upon arrival, staff must verify the minor's registration and the identity of the person dropping them off.
4.2.2 **Claim Ticket/Wristband System:**
*   **Wristbands:** If utilized, the minor and the guardian receive matching wristbands with a unique security code.
*   **Digital Check-In:** If utilizing an electronic system, the guardian receives a unique digital QR code or PIN required for release.
4.2.3 **Confirmation of Pick-Up Plan:** Staff must verbally confirm with the dropper-off who will be picking up the child and at what time.

#### 4.3 Standard Release (Check-Out) Protocol
4.3.1 **Location:** Release must only occur at the designated Check-Out Station. Staff must not release minors from side doors, parking lots, or restrooms.
4.3.2 **Identification Check:**
*   The individual requesting the minor must present a valid government-issued photo ID (Driver’s License, Passport, State ID).
*   **NO ID, NO RELEASE:** Staff are strictly prohibited from releasing a minor to anyone without ID, even if the staff member recognizes the parent personally.
4.3.3 **Authorization Match:** Staff must cross-reference the name on the ID with the "Authorized Guardian" list in the event registry.
4.3.4 **Physical Handover:** Once identity and authorization are verified, the minor is called to the station and released to the custody of the guardian.
4.3.5 **Documentation:** The time of release and the name of the person picking up the minor must be logged (digitally or on paper).

#### 4.4 Self-Release (Teenagers)
4.4.1 **Eligibility:** Self-release is only permitted for minors aged 16 and older.
4.4.2 **Written Consent:** The parent/guardian must sign a specific "Self-Release Waiver" prior to the event. Verbal permission over the phone is **not** accepted.
4.4.3 **Sign-Out:** The minor must physically sign themselves out at the Check-Out Station before leaving the premises.

#### 4.5 Exception and Emergency Protocols
4.5.1 **Unauthorized Individual Attempting Pick-Up:**
*   If an individual not on the list attempts to pick up a minor, staff must detain the release.
*   Staff must contact the primary registering guardian immediately for verbal authorization.
*   If the primary guardian cannot be reached, the child **must remain** in Study GRC custody.
4.5.2 **Intoxicated or Impaired Guardian:**
*   If the authorized guardian appears to be under the influence of drugs or alcohol, staff must **not** release the minor.
*   Staff must contact the secondary emergency contact.
*   If the impaired individual becomes aggressive, staff must immediately contact local law enforcement and the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).
4.5.3 **Late Pick-Up:**
*   Two staff members (adhering to Two-Deep Leadership) must remain with the child at the venue.
*   Staff will attempt to contact all authorized guardians.
*   If the child is not picked up within 60 minutes of the event end time and no contact has been made, staff are authorized to contact local Child Protective Services (CPS) or law enforcement, per Texas Family Code.

#### 4.6 Custody Disputes
4.6.1 **Neutrality:** Study GRC Inc. personnel do not mediate custody disputes.
4.6.2 **Presumption of Rights:** Without a court order on file, Study GRC Inc. cannot deny access to a biological parent who provides proof of identity/parentage, unless the child is in immediate physical danger.
4.6.3 **Conflict Escalation:** If a conflict arises between parents at the venue, staff must move the child to a secure, private area away from the altercation and call law enforcement immediately.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Manager** | Overall accountability for the security of the Check-In/Check-Out process; holds the master list of authorized guardians and court orders. |
| **Check-In/Out Staff** | Physically verifies IDs, manages the roster, and executes the physical release of minors. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Acts as the primary point of contact for de-escalating conflicts with parents or handling sensitive custody discussions (per Bylaws Art. V §5.2). |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all event staff are trained on this procedure prior to any youth-focused event. |
| **Registering Parent** | Responsible for providing accurate, up-to-date information regarding authorized pick-up persons and legal custody changes. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Zero Tolerance:** Releasing a minor to an unauthorized individual is a critical safety violation.
*   **Staff/Volunteers:** Failure to strictly adhere to the ID verification and list-checking steps will result in immediate removal from the event and potential termination of employment or volunteer service.
*   **Legal Action:** Negligence resulting in the endangerment of a minor may result in civil or criminal liability under Texas law.
