### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, transparent, and compliant process for filling vacancies on the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. that occur outside of the standard Annual Meeting cycle. This procedure ensures continuity of governance, adherence to the democratic rights of the Voting Membership, and compliance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.212** and **Article III, Section 3.8** of the Organization's Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all vacancies on the Board of Directors created by resignation, removal, death, incapacitation, or an increase in the authorized number of Directors.

**Exclusions:**
*   This procedure does not apply to the re-election of Directors whose terms are expiring naturally at an Annual Meeting (refer to *GOV-PRC-046 Officer Election Procedure* and *GOV-PRC-029 Voting Membership Roll Maintenance*).
*   This procedure does not apply to the appointment of Non-Voting Advisory Board members.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Vacancy** | A seat on the Board of Directors that is unoccupied due to resignation, removal, death, or the creation of a new seat. |
| **Unexpired Term** | The period of time remaining in the vacating Director's designated term, measured from the effective date of the vacancy to the date the term was originally scheduled to end. |
| **Special Election** | An election held outside of the Annual Meeting cycle for the specific purpose of filling a vacancy. |
| **Voting Member** | A Supporting Member or Honorary Member in good standing who holds voting rights as defined in Bylaws Article II. |
| **Doomsday Clause** | A provision in Bylaws Section 3.10 triggered if the number of Directors falls to zero (0). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Declaration of Vacancy and Assessment
Upon receipt of a resignation notice, confirmation of death, or finalization of a removal vote:

1.  **Effective Date Determination:** The Secretary must record the effective date of the vacancy.
    *   For resignations, this is the date specified in the written notice or the date of receipt if unspecified.
    *   For removals, this is the date the removal vote was certified.
2.  **Term Assessment:** The Governance Committee must calculate the **Unexpired Term**.
3.  **Procedural Path Determination:** The Board must determine the required action based on Bylaws Section 3.8:
    *   **Path A (Short Term Exception):** If the Unexpired Term is **less than three (3) months** AND the remaining Board has at least three (3) Directors, the seat may remain vacant until the next Annual Election.
    *   **Path B (Immediate Fill - Minimum Override):** If the vacancy reduces the Board to **fewer than three (3) Directors**, a Special Election must be initiated immediately, regardless of the Unexpired Term length.
    *   **Path C (Standard Special Election):** In all other cases, a Special Election must be initiated to fill the seat for the remainder of the Unexpired Term.

#### 4.2 Nomination Process
If a Special Election is required (Path B or C), the Organization must replicate the nomination standards defined in Bylaws Section 2.5:

1.  **Call for Nominations:** The Secretary must issue a "Call for Nominations" to the Voting Membership via email and the Organization’s official communication channels.
2.  **Nomination Window:** The nomination period must remain open for a minimum of **fourteen (14) days**.
3.  **Candidate Submission:** Nominees must submit a "Nomination Statement" via the designated secure form, including:
    *   Professional background/biography.
    *   Position statement (vision for the role).
    *   **Mandatory Conflict of Interest Disclosure** (refer to *GOV-POL-005 Conflict of Interest Policy*).
4.  **Vetting:** The Governance Committee must review all nominees to ensure they meet the qualifications set forth in Bylaws Section 3.3 (Voting Member in good standing; adherence to Ethics).

#### 4.3 Notice of Special Election
Once candidates are vetted:

1.  **Meeting Scheduling:** The Board must schedule a Special Meeting of the Members (virtual) or establish a standalone electronic voting period.
2.  **Notice Delivery:** Written notice must be delivered to all Voting Members no fewer than **10 days** and no more than **60 days** prior to the election date.
3.  **Content of Notice:** The notice must explicitly state:
    *   The purpose of the meeting/vote (Filling a Board Vacancy).
    *   The name(s) of the vacating Director(s) (for record accuracy).
    *   The list of nominees and their statements.
    *   Instructions for electronic voting.

#### 4.4 Candidate Presentations and Voting
To ensure transparency and informed decision-making:

1.  **Presentations:** Nominees shall be offered a five (5) minute window to present their qualifications to the membership. This may be conducted via:
    *   A live virtual Special Meeting (recorded for asynchronous viewing).
    *   Pre-recorded video statements distributed with the ballot.
2.  **Electronic Voting:**
    *   Voting must be conducted via the Organization's secure electronic voting system.
    *   The voting window must remain open for **seven (7) days**.
    *   **Proxy Voting is strictly prohibited.**
3.  **Quorum:** A quorum is established if more than 50% of Voting Members cast a vote or attend the virtual meeting.

#### 4.5 Certification and Seating
1.  **Tabulation:** The Secretary (or an independent third party if the Secretary is a candidate) must tabulate the votes.
2.  **Victory Threshold:** The candidate receiving the highest number of votes is elected.
3.  **Certification:** The results must be certified in writing and recorded in the corporate minutes.
4.  **Immediate Seating:** The elected Director assumes office immediately upon certification.
5.  **Term Limit:** The newly elected Director serves **only for the remainder of the Unexpired Term**. They must run for re-election at the conclusion of that term if they wish to continue.

#### 4.6 Emergency Contingency (Zero Directors)
In the event the Organization has zero (0) Directors (Bylaws Section 3.10):
1.  Any Voting Member may call a **Special Emergency Meeting** with **10 days' notice**.
2.  The sole purpose of this meeting is to elect an Interim Board.
3.  This election shall follow the procedures in Section 4.4 to the extent feasible given the emergency nature.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Declares the vacancy; determines the procedural path (A, B, or C); certifies election results. |
| **Secretary** | Calculates unexpired term; issues notices; manages the electronic voting platform; records minutes. |
| **Governance Committee** | Vets nominees for eligibility and conflicts of interest. |
| **Voting Members** | Review candidate materials; cast votes personally (no proxies). |
| **Nominees** | Submit nomination statements; disclose conflicts; present qualifications to the membership. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in the invalidation of the election results, potential legal challenges under the Texas Business Organizations Code, and a breach of fiduciary duty by the Board.

Any Director appointed or elected in violation of this procedure (e.g., by Board appointment rather than Member election, unless authorized by a future Bylaw amendment) shall not be considered a valid Director, and their votes on Board matters shall be null and void.
