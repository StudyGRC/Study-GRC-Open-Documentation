### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish a rigorous, standardized process for screening all individuals seeking positions that involve access to minors within Study GRC Inc.’s ecosystem. This procedure operationalizes the Organization’s commitment to "Duty of Care" and satisfies the "Enhanced Checks" requirement mandated by our *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*. It is designed to prevent individuals with a history of abuse, violence, or inappropriate conduct from interacting with youth participants in our K-12 programs, whether virtually or in person.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Youth-Serving Personnel** candidates prior to the confirmation of their role.

*   **In Scope:**
    *   Paid Employees (Full-time, Part-time, Temporary).
    *   Board Directors and Officers (if participating in youth-facing events).
    *   Volunteers (e.g., Mentors, Tutors, Moderators).
    *   Independent Contractors and Consultants with access to youth data or direct interaction.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Third-party vendors with no access to youth data or direct contact (e.g., software licensors).
    *   One-time guest speakers who are never left unsupervised with minors (subject to *Visitor Management Procedures*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Youth-Serving Personnel** | Any individual who has the opportunity for direct contact (physical or virtual) with a minor, or access to the personally identifiable information (PII) of a minor. |
| **Enhanced Check** | A multi-layered screening process that exceeds standard criminal history searches, including sex offender registries, county-level court records, and behavioral reference checks. |
| **Direct Contact** | Face-to-face interaction or virtual interaction (via Zoom, Discord, Slack, etc.) with a minor. |
| **Adjudication** | The review process to determine if a candidate’s background check results disqualify them from service based on defined risk criteria. |
| **NSOPW** | The U.S. Department of Justice National Sex Offender Public Website. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Application and Self-Disclosure
To ensure transparency and early detection of potential risks, screening begins at the application stage.

1.  **Job Description Tagging:** The Hiring Manager must tag the position description as "Youth-Serving" and "Safety-Sensitive," explicitly stating that an Enhanced Background Check is a condition of employment/service.
2.  **Application Disclosure:** All applicants must complete a self-disclosure questionnaire asking:
    *   "Have you ever been convicted of, or pled no contest to, a crime involving a minor?"
    *   "Have you ever been investigated by Child Protective Services (or equivalent) for abuse or neglect?"
3.  **Review of Disclosure:** If an applicant answers "Yes," the HR Manager must conduct a preliminary risk assessment before proceeding to the interview stage.

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Behavioral Interviewing
Interviews must assess the candidate’s suitability for working with children, not just technical GRC skills.

1.  **Mandatory Questions:** The interviewer must ask at least two (2) behavioral questions specifically related to youth interactions. Examples:
    *   "Describe a time you had to enforce a boundary with a student or youth participant."
    *   "What would you do if you suspected a child was being bullied in an online forum?"
2.  **Red Flag Monitoring:** Interviewers must document any "Red Flags," such as:
    *   Reluctance to follow safety rules (e.g., "Two-Deep Leadership").
    *   Desire for unsupervised, one-on-one access to children.
    *   Over-emphasis on "special friendships" with youth.

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Enhanced Reference Checks
Reference checks must specifically verify the candidate's safety history.

1.  **Minimum Requirement:** A minimum of two (2) professional references are required. At least one must be from a previous role involving work with children (if applicable).
2.  **Specific Inquiry:** The Reference Checker must ask:
    *   "Is this individual eligible for re-hire?"
    *   "To your knowledge, has this individual ever been the subject of an allegation of misconduct involving a minor?"
    *   "Would you have any reservations about this person supervising your own child?"
3.  **Documentation:** Responses must be documented in the *Reference Check Log*.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Technical Background Screening (The "Enhanced Check")
Following a conditional offer, the Organization will initiate the formal background check via an FCRA-compliant third-party vendor.

1.  **Consent:** Obtain a signed *Background Check Disclosure and Authorization Form* from the candidate.
2.  **Screening Components:** The check must include, at a minimum:
    *   **SSN Trace & Address History:** To identify all jurisdictions of residence in the last 7 years.
    *   **Multi-Jurisdictional Criminal Database Search:** National scope.
    *   **National Sex Offender Registry Search (NSOPW):** Must cover all 50 states, territories, and tribal lands.
    *   **County Criminal Search:** Physical court record search in all counties of residence for the past 7 years.
    *   **Federal Criminal Search:** For federal crimes not listed in county courts.
    *   **Texas DPS Computerized Criminal History (CCH):** Specific search for Texas residents/operations.
    *   **Global Watchlist:** Terrorist and sanctions lists.
3.  **Social Media/Online Presence Review:** For roles with moderation duties (e.g., Discord Mods), a review of public online behavior (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Reddit) will be conducted to identify hate speech or grooming behaviors.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Adjudication and Decision Making
Results are reviewed by the *Background Check Adjudication Committee* (typically HR + Legal/Executive Director).

1.  **Automatic Disqualifiers:** Candidates with convictions (or pending charges) for the following are automatically disqualified:
    *   Any sex offense (regardless of time passed).
    *   Murder or homicide.
    *   Crimes involving violence against children or vulnerable adults.
    *   Felony assault or battery within the last 10 years.
    *   Distribution of pornography.
2.  **Individualized Assessment:** For other offenses (e.g., non-violent drug offenses from 10+ years ago), the Committee will consider:
    *   Nature and gravity of the offense.
    *   Time passed since the offense.
    *   Relevance to the specific job duties.
3.  **Adverse Action Process:** If the decision is made to rescind the offer based on the report:
    *   **Pre-Adverse Action Notice:** Send the candidate a copy of the report and a summary of rights under FCRA.
    *   **Waiting Period:** Allow 5 business days for the candidate to dispute accuracy.
    *   **Final Adverse Action Notice:** If no dispute or dispute is unresolved, send final notice of revocation.

#### 4.6 Phase 6: Continuous Monitoring and Re-Screening
Screening is not a one-time event.

1.  **Annual Re-Screen:** All Youth-Serving Personnel must undergo a refreshed National Sex Offender Registry check annually.
2.  **Full Re-Check:** A full criminal background check is repeated every 24 months.
3.  **Duty to Report:** Per the *Employee Handbook*, all staff must self-report any new arrests or convictions within 48 hours. Failure to report is grounds for immediate termination.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Identifies "Safety-Sensitive" roles; conducts behavioral interviews; flags concerns. |
| **HR Manager** | Administers the background check vendor process; ensures FCRA compliance; maintains confidential records. |
| **Adjudication Committee** | Reviews "hits" on background checks; makes final hiring decisions based on risk matrix. |
| **Candidate** | Provides accurate information; signs consent forms; responds to inquiries during the process. |
| **Executive Director** | Final authority on appeals regarding disqualification decisions (excluding Automatic Disqualifiers). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Zero Tolerance:** Study GRC Inc. maintains a zero-tolerance policy regarding the employment or engagement of individuals with a history of sexual misconduct or violence against children.

**Confidentiality:** All background check reports are confidential and will be stored separately from the general personnel file, in a secure, access-controlled repository.

**Violations:** Failure by a Hiring Manager to initiate a check, or allowing an individual to begin work prior to clearance, will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
