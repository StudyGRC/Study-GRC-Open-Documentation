### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for reconciling **Study GRC Inc.**’s bank accounts with the General Ledger (GL) on a monthly basis. Regular reconciliation is a critical internal control required to:
1.  Ensure the accuracy of financial reporting in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP).
2.  Detect and prevent fraud, unauthorized transactions, or bank errors.
3.  Maintain "correct and complete books of account" as mandated by **Article VIII, Section 8.1** of the Organization’s Bylaws.
4.  Satisfy federal grant requirements for internal controls under **2 CFR § 200.303**.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all demand deposit accounts (checking), savings accounts, and money market accounts held by Study GRC Inc.

**Inclusions:**
*   Primary Operating Accounts.
*   Grant-specific bank accounts (if segregated).
*   Merchant clearing accounts (e.g., Stripe, PayPal) where funds are held prior to transfer.

**Exclusions:**
*   Petty Cash funds (Covered in *FIN-POL-005 Cash Handling Policy*).
*   Investment portfolios (Covered in *FIN-POL-008 Investment Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reconciliation** | The process of comparing the Organization's internal financial records (General Ledger) against the records provided by the bank (Bank Statement) to ensure they match. |
| **General Ledger (GL)** | The master set of accounts that summarize all transactions occurring within the Organization. |
| **Outstanding Check** | A check written and recorded by the Organization that has not yet cleared the bank. |
| **Deposit in Transit** | Funds received and recorded by the Organization that have been sent to the bank but have not yet appeared on the bank statement. |
| **Segregation of Duties** | An internal control principle ensuring that no single individual has control over all aspects of a financial transaction (e.g., the person writing checks cannot be the sole person reconciling the bank account). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Segregation of Duties
To prevent fraud and ensure objective review, the following separation of roles must be maintained:
1.  **The Reconciler:** The individual performing the reconciliation (e.g., Bookkeeper or CFO) **must not** have check-signing authority or the ability to authorize wire transfers for the account being reconciled.
2.  **The Reviewer:** The reconciliation must be reviewed and approved by a separate individual (e.g., Treasurer or Executive Director) who did not perform the data entry.

#### 4.2 Preparation
**Timing:** Reconciliation must be completed within **ten (10) business days** of the bank statement becoming available.

1.  **Obtain Bank Statement:** The CFO or designated staff downloads the official PDF bank statement from the secure online banking portal.
2.  **Access General Ledger:** Open the accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks, Xero) to the reconciliation module for the corresponding period.
3.  **Gather Supporting Docs:** Have access to the check register, deposit slips (digital or physical), and credit card receipts for the period.

#### 4.3 Reconciliation Process

**4.3.1 Clearing Deposits (Credits)**
1.  Compare each deposit listed on the Bank Statement against the deposits recorded in the GL.
2.  Mark matched transactions as "Cleared."
3.  Identify **Deposits in Transit** (recorded in GL but not on Bank Statement). Ensure these are valid and date-appropriate.
4.  Identify any **Unrecorded Deposits** (on Bank Statement but not in GL). These may include:
    *   Interest earned.
    *   Direct deposits/ACH grants not yet entered.
    *   Enter these into the GL immediately.

**4.3.2 Clearing Withdrawals (Debits)**
1.  Compare each check, ACH transfer, and wire withdrawal on the Bank Statement against the GL.
2.  Mark matched transactions as "Cleared."
3.  Identify **Outstanding Checks** (recorded in GL but not on Bank Statement).
    *   *Note:* If a check remains outstanding for more than 90 days, initiate the *Stale-Dated Check Procedure*.
4.  Identify **Unrecorded Withdrawals** (on Bank Statement but not in GL). These may include:
    *   Bank service fees.
    *   Wire transfer fees.
    *   Automatic bill payments not yet entered.
    *   NSF (Non-Sufficient Funds) returns.
    *   Enter these into the GL immediately.

**4.3.3 Investigating Discrepancies**
1.  If the "Adjusted Bank Balance" does not match the "Adjusted Book Balance," the Reconciler must identify the variance.
2.  Common errors to check:
    *   Transposition errors (e.g., recording $540 as $450).
    *   Duplicate data entry.
    *   Bank errors (rare, but possible).
3.  If a bank error is suspected, the CFO must contact the bank within 48 hours.

#### 4.4 Finalizing and Reporting
1.  Once the difference is zero ($0.00), generate the **Reconciliation Report**.
2.  The Report must include:
    *   Summary of cleared balance.
    *   List of outstanding checks and deposits in transit.
    *   Copy of the Bank Statement.
3.  The Reconciler signs and dates the report (electronic signature is acceptable).

#### 4.5 Review and Approval
1.  The Reconciler submits the Reconciliation Report and Bank Statement to the **Treasurer** (or Executive Director if the Treasurer is unavailable, provided the ED did not authorize the transactions).
2.  The Reviewer verifies:
    *   The balances match.
    *   There are no unusual or unexplained adjustments.
    *   Old outstanding checks are being managed.
    *   There is no evidence of check tampering (e.g., gaps in check numbers).
3.  The Reviewer signs/approves the report within **five (5) business days** of receipt.

#### 4.6 Record Retention
In accordance with *FIN-STD-002 Records Retention Schedule*:
1.  The approved Reconciliation Report and attached Bank Statement must be saved in the Organization’s secure financial repository (e.g., SharePoint/Box - Restricted Access).
2.  Records must be retained for a minimum of **seven (7) years**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bookkeeper / Staff Accountant** | Performs the detailed matching of transactions; identifies discrepancies; prepares the initial Reconciliation Report. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Oversees the process; ensures segregation of duties; investigates complex discrepancies; serves as Reconciler if no Bookkeeper exists (provided CFO is not a signer). |
| **Treasurer** | Acts as the primary Reviewer; provides Board-level oversight of cash assets; approves the final Reconciliation Report. |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures the procedure is enforced; acts as backup Reviewer if Treasurer is conflicted or unavailable. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure puts the Organization at risk of financial misstatement, fraud, and loss of tax-exempt status.

*   **Late Reconciliations:** Reconciliations more than 30 days overdue must be reported to the Finance Committee.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees found falsifying reconciliation reports or intentionally bypassing segregation of duties will be subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination and legal prosecution.
