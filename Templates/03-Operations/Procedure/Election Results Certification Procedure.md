### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, transparent, and auditable process for validating, tabulating, and certifying the results of votes conducted by the Voting Membership of Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures strict compliance with **Bylaws Article II (Membership)** and **Article III (Board of Directors)**, guaranteeing that all elections and Fundamental Actions reflect the true will of the community without error, fraud, or manipulation.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all matters submitted to a vote of the Membership, including but not limited to:
*   Annual Election of Directors.
*   Removal of Directors or Officers by Member Petition.
*   Amendments to the Bylaws or Certificate of Formation.
*   Fundamental Actions (Mergers, Dissolution, Asset Sales).

This procedure **does not** apply to internal votes taken within the Board of Directors meetings, which are recorded via meeting minutes.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Teller’s Committee** | A temporary group appointed by the Board (comprising at least two individuals who are not candidates on the ballot) responsible for verifying the integrity of the vote. |
| **Quorum** | As defined in **Bylaws Section 2.4**, a majority (more than 50%) of the total number of Voting Members entitled to vote. |
| **Electronic Voting System** | The Board-approved third-party platform used to cast ballots (e.g., ElectionBuddy, OpaVote) as required by **Bylaws Section 2.3**. |
| **Certificate of Election** | A formal document signed by the Secretary and the Teller’s Committee attesting to the accuracy of the results. |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a Supporting or Honorary Member who has met all financial or service requirements as of the Record Date. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. Pre-Certification: Closing the Polls
1.  **Voting Window Termination:** Per **Bylaws Section 2.5**, the electronic voting window must remain open for exactly seven (7) days following the Annual Meeting.
2.  **System Lock:** At the precise expiration of the seven-day period, the Secretary must log into the Electronic Voting System and formally close the poll to prevent any further submissions.
3.  **Data Export:** The Secretary must export the raw voting data, including the audit trail (timestamps, IP addresses, and unique voter identifiers), into a secure, read-only format (e.g., PDF or locked Excel).

#### 4.2. Quorum Verification
Before any votes are counted, the validity of the election must be established.
1.  **Total Voter Count:** The Teller's Committee must determine the total number of unique ballots cast.
2.  **Total Eligible Voters:** Compare this against the official Membership Roll as of the Record Date.
3.  **Calculation:**
    *   If `(Total Votes Cast) / (Total Eligible Voters) > 50%`, Quorum is established (**Bylaws Section 2.4**). Proceed to Section 4.3.
    *   If Quorum is **not** met, the election is void. The Board must schedule a Special Meeting to re-initiate the voting process.

#### 4.3. Integrity Audit and Tabulation
1.  **Duplicate Check:** The Teller's Committee must review the audit logs for irregularities, such as multiple votes originating from the same IP address without reasonable explanation (e.g., members of the same household).
2.  **Eligibility Verification:** Ensure all cast ballots correspond to a Voting Member in Good Standing. Any ballot cast by a non-voting participant or a member not in good standing must be disqualified.
3.  **Proxy Check:** Confirm that no proxy votes were utilized, as strictly prohibited by **Bylaws Section 2.3**.
4.  **Tabulation:**
    *   **Director Elections:** Candidates are ranked by total votes received. The top candidates corresponding to the number of open seats are designated as winners.
    *   **Fundamental Actions:** Verify if the affirmative votes meet the two-thirds (2/3) supermajority threshold required by **Bylaws Article X**.

#### 4.4. Handling Ties
In the event of a tie vote for a Board seat or a pass/fail measure:
1.  **Notification:** The candidates (or membership) must be notified immediately of the tie.
2.  **Run-Off Election:** A special 48-hour electronic run-off election will be scheduled solely between the tied candidates or regarding the specific tied measure.
3.  **Recertification:** The results of the run-off are subject to this same certification procedure.

#### 4.5. Certification of Results
1.  **Drafting the Certificate:** The Secretary prepares the **Certificate of Election**, which must include:
    *   Date of Election.
    *   Total Eligible Voters.
    *   Total Ballots Cast (Quorum Verification).
    *   Number of Invalid/Disqualified Ballots (if any).
    *   Final Tabulation per Candidate/Issue.
    *   Signature block for the Secretary and Teller’s Committee.
2.  **Signing:** The Secretary and at least one member of the Teller’s Committee must digitally sign the Certificate.

#### 4.6. Publication and Transparency
1.  **Member Notification:** Within 48 hours of certification, the results must be emailed to all Members.
2.  **Website Posting:** The Certificate of Election (redacted of any personal voter data) must be posted on the Organization’s "Transparency" page.
3.  **Records Retention:** The Certificate, the raw data export, and the audit trail must be stored in the Organization’s secure document repository for a minimum of five (5) years, in accordance with the **Records Retention Schedule**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Oversees the election system, secures data exports, drafts the Certificate of Election, and publishes results. |
| **Teller’s Committee** | Independent volunteers who audit the voting logs, verify Quorum, and co-sign the Certificate of Election to validate integrity. |
| **Board President** | Receives the final report; announces the results to the public if necessary. |
| **IT/CISO** | Ensures the security of the Electronic Voting System and provides technical logs if forensic audit is required. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Election Fraud:** Any attempt to manipulate voting results, hack the voting system, or cast ballots on behalf of others (proxy voting) constitutes a severe violation of the **Code of Ethics**.
*   **Consequences:** Violations will result in immediate termination of Membership and/or removal from the Board/Office, and may be referred to legal authorities if constituting fraud under Texas law.
*   **Contest of Election:** Any Voting Member wishing to contest the results must file a formal grievance within five (5) business days of publication, detailing specific evidence of procedural failure or fraud.
