### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") to notify Data Controllers (such as corporate partners, chapters, or enterprise users) and relevant Data Subjects when the Organization intends to engage a new **Subprocessor** or replace an existing one.

This procedure ensures compliance with **Article 28(2) of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**, which mandates that a Processor shall not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorization of the Controller and must inform the Controller of any intended changes. This transparency is vital to maintaining the trust of our community and adhering to the ethical standards outlined in **Bylaws Article IX**.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **All Operational Officers** and staff members involved in the procurement or implementation of third-party software, cloud services, or contractors.
*   **All Third-Party Vendors** who process Personal Data on behalf of Study GRC Inc.
*   **All Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)** where Study GRC Inc. acts as a Data Processor.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to vendors who do not have access to, store, or process Personal Data (e.g., hardware suppliers, janitorial services).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Subprocessor** | A third-party data processor engaged by Study GRC Inc., who has or will have access to or process Personal Data from a Controller. |
| **Controller** | The entity that determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data. In B2B contexts, this is the client/partner; in B2C contexts, Study GRC acts as the Controller. |
| **General Authorization** | A clause in a DPA where the Controller gives Study GRC Inc. permission to change Subprocessors, provided notice is given and an opportunity to object is provided. |
| **Specific Authorization** | A requirement where the Controller must explicitly sign off on a specific Subprocessor before data is shared. |
| **DPA** | Data Processing Agreement; a legally binding contract regulating the processing of personal data. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. Pre-Notification Assessment
Before a notification can be sent, the new Subprocessor must be vetted in accordance with the **Vendor Security Risk Assessment (VSRA)**.

1.  **Initiation:** The Requesting Officer (e.g., VP of Education) submits a request to the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) to onboard a new vendor.
2.  **Security Review:** The CISO evaluates the vendor’s security posture to ensure it meets or exceeds the Organization's standards.
3.  **Legal Review:** The DPO or Legal Counsel reviews the vendor’s DPA to ensure Article 28 GDPR compliance (e.g., confidentiality, security measures, audit rights).
4.  **Approval:** Only upon successful completion of the VSRA and DPA execution may the notification process begin.

#### 4.2. Updating the Public Subprocessor List
To facilitate "General Authorization," Study GRC Inc. maintains a publicly accessible list of active Subprocessors.

1.  **Repository:** The DPO shall maintain the "Transparency" page on the Organization’s website (referenced in **Bylaws Section 8.2**).
2.  **Content:** The list must include:
    *   Name of the Subprocessor.
    *   Nature of the services provided (e.g., "Cloud Hosting," "Email Marketing").
    *   Country/Location of processing (to identify Cross-Border transfers).
    *   Link to the Subprocessor's Privacy Policy.
3.  **Timing:** The list must be updated **at least 30 days prior** to the new Subprocessor commencing the processing of live data, barring emergency situations (see Section 4.6).

#### 4.3. Notification Workflow (General Authorization)
For Controllers relying on General Authorization (standard for most Study GRC Inc. agreements):

1.  **Drafting Notice:** The DPO shall draft a "Notice of Subprocessor Update."
2.  **Distribution Channel:**
    *   **Direct Email:** Sent to the primary administrative contact of all active Controllers (e.g., Chapter Heads, Corporate Partners).
    *   **Subscription Feed:** Sent to all subscribers of the "Trust & Safety" RSS/Email feed.
3.  **Timeline:** The notification must be sent **30 days** before the Subprocessor is granted access to data.
4.  **Content Requirements:** The notice must explicitly state:
    *   The name of the new Subprocessor.
    *   The function they will perform.
    *   The effective date of the change.
    *   Instructions on how to object (see Section 4.4).

#### 4.4. Handling Objections
Controllers have the right to object to a new Subprocessor on reasonable grounds related to data protection.

1.  **Receipt of Objection:** Objections must be submitted in writing to `privacy@studygrc.org` within **14 days** of the notification.
2.  **Validity Assessment:** The DPO and CISO will review the objection to determine if it is based on legitimate data protection risks (e.g., "Vendor X has a known history of breaches") versus commercial competition.
3.  **Resolution Steps:**
    *   **Mitigation:** Study GRC Inc. may propose additional security controls to satisfy the Controller.
    *   **Alternative:** Study GRC Inc. may offer not to use that specific Subprocessor for that specific Controller's data (if technically feasible).
    *   **Termination:** If no resolution is found and the Subprocessor is essential to the service, Study GRC Inc. or the Controller may terminate the specific service agreement without penalty.

#### 4.5. Notification Workflow (Specific Authorization)
For Controllers with contracts requiring **Specific Authorization**:

1.  **Direct Request:** The Account Manager must send a formal "Request for Approval" to the Controller.
2.  **Hold on Data:** No data belonging to that Controller may be transferred to the new Subprocessor until **written approval** (signature or email confirmation) is received.
3.  **Logging:** The approval must be saved in the Organization’s Contract Management System.

#### 4.6. Emergency Replacements
In the event a critical Subprocessor fails (e.g., bankruptcy, critical security failure) and immediate replacement is necessary to preserve business continuity:

1.  **Immediate Action:** The CISO may authorize the immediate engagement of a replacement Subprocessor.
2.  **Retroactive Notice:** The DPO must send the notification to Controllers within **24 hours** of the change.
3.  **Justification:** The notice must explain the emergency nature of the replacement and the reason the standard 30-day window could not be honored.
4.  **Right to Object:** Controllers retain the right to object post-facto.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Owns this procedure; drafts notifications; maintains the public Subprocessor list; adjudicates objections. |
| **Chief Info. Security Officer (CISO)** | Vets Subprocessors via VSRA; authorizes emergency replacements; assesses validity of security-based objections. |
| **Operational Officers** | Identify the need for new vendors; ensure no data is shared prior to the completion of this procedure. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews Subprocessor DPAs to ensure liability flows down appropriately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this procedure constitutes a violation of the Organization's contractual obligations and **Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)**.

*   **Internal:** Employees who share data with unapproved or un-notified Subprocessors may face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **External:** Failure to notify Controllers puts Study GRC Inc. in breach of contract and GDPR, potentially leading to significant fines and reputational damage.
