### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the official response protocol for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") in the event of an active shooter or hostile intruder situation. This document is designed to minimize the risk of loss of life and injury by implementing the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and FBI standard "Run-Hide-Fight" methodology. This procedure fulfills the Organization's obligation under the OSHA General Duty Clause to provide a workplace free from recognized hazards that are causing or are likely to cause death or serious physical harm.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees, volunteers, contractors, and participants present at any physical location operated or occupied by the Organization.

*   **Inclusions:**
    *   Corporate administrative offices (if applicable).
    *   Rented co-working spaces.
    *   **Events:** Conferences, workshops, and training sessions hosted by the Organization at third-party venues (hotels, convention centers, schools).
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   This procedure does not dictate the security protocols of employees working from their private residences (Remote Work), though the principles remain valid for personal safety.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Active Shooter** | An individual actively engaged in killing or attempting to kill people in a confined and populated area, typically using firearms. |
| **Hostile Intruder** | A person who enters a facility with the intent to cause harm, theft, or disruption, who may or may not be armed with a firearm. |
| **Run-Hide-Fight** | The federally recommended survival strategy: Evacuate (Run), Barricade (Hide), or Defend (Fight). |
| **Event Lead** | The senior-most Study GRC staff member present at a specific physical event or location. |
| **Safe Room** | A pre-designated room with a locking door, limited visibility from the outside, and ballistic cover (e.g., concrete walls). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Pre-Event/Pre-Occupancy Preparation
Prior to the commencement of any in-person event or the occupation of a physical office, the Event Lead or Director of Operations must:
1.  **Identify Exits:** Locate at least two emergency exits in every room or facility area.
2.  **Designate Safe Rooms:** Identify rooms that can be locked and barricaded.
3.  **Communication Check:** Ensure the venue has a working PA system or that staff have a dedicated communication channel (e.g., Signal group, radios).
4.  **Briefing:** Conduct a safety briefing for all staff and volunteers, explicitly identifying the "Run" routes and "Hide" locations.

#### 4.2 Immediate Response: Run-Hide-Fight
In the event of an active shooter or hostile intruder, all personnel must immediately assess the situation and execute one of the following actions based on their proximity to the threat. **Do not wait for official instructions.**

**4.2.1 RUN (Evacuate)**
If there is an accessible escape path, attempt to evacuate the premises.
1.  **Leave Belongings:** Do not attempt to gather laptops, bags, or equipment.
2.  **Visualize the Route:** Move quickly toward the nearest exit away from the sound of gunfire.
3.  **Help Others:** Assist others (especially youth participants or those with disabilities) to escape, if possible, but do not allow their hesitation to slow your evacuation.
4.  **Prevent Entry:** Prevent individuals from entering an area where the active shooter may be.
5.  **Call 911:** Once safe, call 911. Do not assume someone else has called.

**4.2.2 HIDE (Lockdown)**
If evacuation is not possible, find a place to hide where the active shooter is less likely to find you.
1.  **Out of View:** Your hiding place should be out of the active shooter’s view.
2.  **Protection:** Select a location that provides protection if shots are fired in your direction (e.g., an office with a closed and locked door).
3.  **Secure the Space:**
    *   Lock the door.
    *   Blockade the door with heavy furniture (tables, chairs).
    *   Close, cover, and move away from windows.
4.  **Silence:**
    *   Silence all cell phones (including vibration mode).
    *   Turn off any source of noise (radios, TVs).
    *   Remain absolutely quiet.

**4.2.3 FIGHT (Defend)**
As a last resort, and **only when your life is in imminent danger**, attempt to disrupt and/or incapacitate the active shooter.
1.  **Commitment:** Act as aggressively as possible against the shooter.
2.  **Improvised Weapons:** Throw items and improvise weapons (fire extinguishers, chairs, laptops, hot coffee).
3.  **Teamwork:** If with others, ambush the intruder together.
4.  **Do Not Stop:** Continue fighting until the shooter is incapacitated or disarmed.

#### 4.3 Protection of Minors (Youth Participants)
Given the Organization's focus on K-12 education and youth programs, staff and volunteers have a heightened duty of care.
1.  **Guidance:** Staff must immediately direct minors to Run or Hide.
2.  **Supervision:** Do not leave minors unattended during the crisis if it can be avoided without immediate death.
3.  **Silence Enforcement:** Staff must actively enforce silence among youth participants during a "Hide" scenario to prevent detection.

#### 4.4 Interaction with Law Enforcement
When law enforcement arrives, their primary focus is to stop the active shooter.
1.  **Empty Hands:** Keep hands visible and empty. Do not hold phones, bags, or improvised weapons.
2.  **No Sudden Moves:** Do not run towards officers or grab onto them.
3.  **Follow Commands:** Immediately comply with all police commands (e.g., "Get down," "Hands up").
4.  **Information:** If asked, provide:
    *   Location of the shooter.
    *   Number of shooters.
    *   Physical description.
    *   Number and type of weapons.
    *   Number of potential victims.

#### 4.5 Post-Incident Protocol
Once the threat has been neutralized and the area is declared safe by Law Enforcement:
1.  **Accountability:** The Event Lead must conduct a headcount of all staff, volunteers, and registered participants.
2.  **Medical Aid:** Provide First Aid/CPR to injured persons only after the scene is secure.
3.  **Notification:** The Executive Director or Board President must be notified immediately.
4.  **Media Blackout:** Staff and volunteers must not speak to the media. Refer all inquiries to the designated spokesperson per the *Crisis Communications Plan*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures this protocol is integrated into all event planning and that staff are trained annually. |
| **Event Lead / Manager** | Acts as the on-site safety coordinator; conducts pre-event safety walkthroughs; accounts for personnel post-incident. |
| **Staff & Volunteers** | Responsible for knowing the Run-Hide-Fight protocol and guiding participants (especially minors) to safety. |
| **HR / Operations** | Maintains emergency contact lists and facilitates post-incident counseling (EAP). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Participation in safety briefings prior to major events is mandatory for all on-site staff and volunteers. Failure to adhere to safety protocols or obstructing an evacuation may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
