### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized protocol for conducting fire drills and emergency evacuation simulations for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This procedure ensures that all employees, volunteers, and event participants are familiar with emergency egress routes and assembly protocols to minimize risk of injury or loss of life during a fire or similar emergency. This document aligns with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.38 (Emergency Action Plans) and 1910.39 (Fire Prevention Plans).

### 2. Scope
Due to the Organization's hybrid nature, this procedure applies to three distinct operational environments:
1.  **Registered Office:** The physical registered office maintained in the State of Texas (per Bylaws Article I, Section 1.2).
2.  **In-Person Events:** Any temporary venue (hotels, conference centers, schools) rented or utilized by the Organization for workshops, conferences, or community gatherings.
3.  **Remote Work Environments:** Advisory guidelines for employees and volunteers working from home offices.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not supersede the specific fire safety regulations of a rented venue (e.g., a hotel's internal fire policy) but acts as a supplemental requirement for Organization staff.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Assembly Point** | A designated safe area outside a building where personnel must gather for a headcount following an evacuation. |
| **Event Fire Warden** | The Study GRC staff member (usually the Event Manager) responsible for coordinating evacuation and accounting for personnel at a specific event. |
| **Simulated Drill** | A walkthrough or tabletop exercise used when activating physical alarms is not permitted by a venue owner. |
| **Egress Route** | A continuous and unobstructed path of exit travel from any point within a workplace to a place of safety. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Frequency of Drills
To ensure preparedness, drills must be conducted according to the following schedule:

*   **Registered Office:** A physical evacuation drill must be conducted **annually**.
*   **In-Person Events:** A "Simulated Drill/Walkthrough" must be conducted by staff **prior to the start** of every distinct event (e.g., during setup day).
*   **Remote Employees:** Fire safety awareness reminders are issued **annually** during National Fire Prevention Week (October).

#### 4.2 Procedure: Registered Office Evacuation
1.  **Initiation:** The Director of Operations or designee will activate the alarm or announce "Drill, Drill, Drill" over the communication system.
2.  **Evacuation:** All occupants must immediately cease work, secure sensitive data (lock screens) if time permits, and proceed to the nearest emergency exit. Elevators must not be used.
3.  **Assembly:** Personnel must gather at the designated Assembly Point (defined in the office specific EAP).
4.  **Headcount:** The designated Fire Warden will conduct a roll call against the daily attendance log.
5.  **Clearance:** Personnel may only re-enter the building when the "All Clear" signal is given by the Fire Warden.
6.  **Documentation:** The drill must be logged in the *Fire Drill Record Log* (EHS-REG-002).

#### 4.3 Procedure: In-Person Events (Simulated Drill)
Given that Study GRC Inc. often utilizes third-party venues, we cannot independently activate alarms. The following procedure applies:

1.  **Pre-Event Walkthrough (Staff Only):**
    *   The Event Manager must identify all primary and secondary exits in the rented space.
    *   The Event Manager must identify the venue's designated Assembly Point.
    *   All volunteers and staff must physically walk the route from the main event hall to the emergency exit during setup.
2.  **Youth Protection Protocol:**
    *   For events involving minors (K-12 focus), specific staff members must be assigned as "Sweepers" to check restrooms and breakout rooms to ensure no minor is left behind.
    *   Staff must maintain possession of the participant roster at all times for emergency headcounts.
3.  **Participant Briefing:**
    *   At the start of the event (Opening Remarks), the Event Manager or Emcee must verbally inform attendees of the location of emergency exits and the assembly point.
4.  **Documentation:** The completion of the pre-event walkthrough and participant briefing must be noted in the *Event Safety Checklist*.

#### 4.4 Procedure: Remote Work (Advisory)
While the Organization cannot enforce drills in private residences, it mandates the following for employee safety:
1.  **Onboarding:** New hires receive the *Home Office Safety Checklist* (HR-FRM-015) which includes verifying the presence of functional smoke detectors.
2.  **Annual Reminder:** The Director of Operations will send a communication requiring remote staff to test their smoke detectors and review their personal home evacuation plans.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ultimate accountability for the safety of staff and event participants. Approves the annual drill schedule. |
| **Director of Operations** | Acts as the primary Fire Warden for the Registered Office. Maintains drill records. |
| **Event Manager** | Acts as the Fire Warden for specific events. Conducts pre-event safety walkthroughs. |
| **Youth Program Lead** | Responsible for the specific evacuation protocols regarding minors, including "Sweeper" designation. |
| **Employees/Volunteers** | Required to participate in all drills and familiarize themselves with exits upon arriving at any work location. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to participate in mandatory fire drills (for office staff) or failure to conduct the required pre-event safety walkthrough (for Event Managers) will result in disciplinary action.

For events involving youth, failure to adhere to the evacuation supervision protocols ("Sweeper" duties) is considered a critical safety violation and may result in immediate termination of volunteer assignment or employment.
