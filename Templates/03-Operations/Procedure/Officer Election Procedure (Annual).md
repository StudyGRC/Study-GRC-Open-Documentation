### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, transparent, and compliant process for the annual election of Board Officers (President, Secretary, and Treasurer) for Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures continuity of governance, adherence to the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC), and compliance with **Article IV** of the Organization’s Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the **Board of Directors** during the "Organizational Meeting," which occurs immediately following the Annual Meeting of Members.

*   **In Scope:** Election of the President, Secretary, Treasurer, and any optional Board Officers (e.g., Vice President).
*   **Out of Scope:** Appointment of Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director/CEO, CISO), which is governed by the *Operational Officer Appointment Policy* (GOV-POL-020), although these appointments may occur during the same meeting.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Organizational Meeting** | The first meeting of the Board of Directors held immediately following the Annual Meeting of Members and the election of Directors. |
| **Board Officer** | A Director elected to a specific governance role (President, Secretary, Treasurer) as defined in Bylaws Article IV. |
| **Acclamation** | A method of election where a candidate is elected by unanimous consent without a formal ballot, typically used when there is only one nominee for a position. |
| **Presiding Officer** | The individual chairing the meeting. Typically the outgoing President until the new President is elected. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Timing and Notice
1.  **Schedule:** In accordance with **Bylaws Section 4.3**, the election of officers must take place at the first Board meeting following the completion of the Director election (the "Organizational Meeting").
2.  **Agenda:** The Secretary must include "Election of Officers" as a distinct line item on the agenda for the Organizational Meeting.

#### 4.2 Order of Elections
To ensure the orderly transfer of authority, elections shall proceed in the following strict order:
1.  President
2.  Vice President (if applicable/desired by the Board)
3.  Secretary
4.  Treasurer

#### 4.3 Nomination Process
1.  **Floor Nominations:** The Presiding Officer shall open the floor for nominations for the specific office currently being contested.
2.  **Self-Nomination:** Directors are permitted to nominate themselves.
3.  **Seconding:** In accordance with the *Board Meeting Rules of Order*, nominations do not require a second.
4.  **Acceptance:** The nominee must verbally accept the nomination. If a nominee is absent, their written consent to serve if elected must be presented to the Secretary prior to the meeting.
5.  **Closing Nominations:** The Presiding Officer asks, "Are there any further nominations?" If none are offered, the Presiding Officer declares nominations closed.

#### 4.4 Conduct of Voting (Remote/Virtual Context)
As Study GRC Inc. operates primarily as a remote entity, voting shall be conducted as follows:

**4.4.1 Uncontested Elections (Single Nominee)**
1.  If only one candidate is nominated for a position, the Presiding Officer may declare the candidate elected by **Acclamation** (General Consent).
2.  The Secretary records the election as unanimous in the minutes.

**4.4.2 Contested Elections (Multiple Nominees)**
1.  **Speeches:** Each candidate is afforded two (2) minutes to speak regarding their qualifications and vision for the role.
2.  **Balloting:**
    *   Voting must be conducted via a secure electronic method (e.g., private direct message to a designated non-voting teller, or a secure polling tool like ElectionRunner or Zoom Polls) to ensure the integrity of the vote.
    *   If a non-voting teller (e.g., General Counsel or Executive Director) is not present, the Board may agree to a roll-call vote (voice vote), provided no Director objects.
3.  **Majority Requirement:** To be elected, a candidate must receive a majority of votes cast by the Directors present (Quorum required per **Bylaws Section 3.5**).
4.  **Run-off:** If no candidate receives a majority (e.g., a 3-way split), the candidate with the lowest number of votes is dropped, and a re-vote is conducted immediately.

#### 4.5 Validation of Roles (TBOC Compliance)
1.  **Separation of Duties:** Before certifying the election of the Secretary, the Presiding Officer must verify that the nominee is not the same individual just elected as President.
    *   *Regulatory Note:* Per **Bylaws Section 4.1** and **TBOC § 22.231**, the President and Secretary **cannot** be the same person.
2.  **Dual Roles:** The same person *may* hold any other combination of offices (e.g., President and Treasurer), provided they are not President and Secretary.

#### 4.6 Assumption of Office
1.  **Immediate Effect:** Officers take office immediately upon the announcement of the election results.
2.  **Gavel Pass:** If a new President is elected, the outgoing President immediately yields the chair (the "virtual gavel") to the new President, who presides over the remainder of the meeting (including the elections of Secretary and Treasurer).

#### 4.7 Post-Election Administration
1.  **Minutes:** The Secretary (or acting Secretary) must record the specific vote counts (if roll call) or the declaration of results in the official minutes.
2.  **Bank Signatories:** The Treasurer is responsible for updating the *Signature Authority Matrix* and initiating the update of bank signature cards within five (5) business days.
3.  **State Filings:** The Secretary is responsible for filing any necessary updates with the Texas Secretary of State (Form 802 - Periodic Report) if the registered agent or officer information on file requires updating.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Outgoing President** | Presides over the election of the new President; ensures order during nominations. |
| **Incoming President** | Assumes the chair immediately upon election; presides over remaining officer elections. |
| **Secretary** | Records nominations, votes, and results in the minutes; validates TBOC separation of duties compliance. |
| **Treasurer** | Updates financial institution records and the *Signature Authority Policy* post-election. |
| **Directors** | Participate in nominations and cast votes for officer candidates. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in:
*   **Invalidation of Acts:** Officer actions taken by improperly elected individuals may be voidable.
*   **Regulatory Penalties:** Failure to maintain distinct President and Secretary roles violates Texas law.
*   **Loss of Good Standing:** Inaccurate officer records may impact the organization's standing with the IRS or State of Texas.

Disputes regarding the election process shall be resolved by a majority vote of the Directors not involved in the dispute, referencing the current edition of *Robert's Rules of Order Newly Revised*.
