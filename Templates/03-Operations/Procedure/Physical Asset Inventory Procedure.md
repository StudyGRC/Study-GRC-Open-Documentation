### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a systematic method for the physical verification, tracking, and reconciliation of Study GRC Inc.'s tangible assets. This procedure ensures compliance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** regarding the maintenance of correct books and records, satisfies federal grant requirements under **2 CFR § 200.313(d)** for equipment management, and mitigates the risk of theft, loss, or misuse of organizational property in a remote-first environment.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all physical assets owned by Study GRC Inc., regardless of funding source.

**In Scope:**
*   **Capital Assets:** Tangible property with a useful life of more than one year and a per-unit acquisition cost exceeding the capitalization threshold (currently $5,000).
*   **Sensitive/Attractive Assets:** Items with a per-unit cost under $5,000 that are portable and prone to theft (e.g., laptops, tablets, cameras, projectors), referred to as "Walkable Assets."
*   **Federally Funded Equipment:** All equipment purchased in whole or in part with federal grant funds.

**Out of Scope:**
*   Consumable office supplies (e.g., paper, toner).
*   Intangible assets (software licenses, trademarks).
*   Real estate (Land/Buildings).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Asset Tag** | A unique identification label (physical barcode or etched number) permanently affixed to an asset for tracking purposes. |
| **Custodian** | The specific employee, volunteer, or contractor who has physical possession of the asset and is responsible for its safety. |
| **Master Asset Register** | The central database (spreadsheet or software) containing details of all tracked assets. |
| **Physical Inventory** | The process of verifying the existence, location, and condition of assets through visual inspection. |
| **Reconciliation** | The accounting process of comparing the physical inventory results against the General Ledger (GL) and Master Asset Register to identify discrepancies. |
| **2 CFR 200.313** | Federal regulation governing the management, use, and disposal of equipment acquired under a federal award. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Asset Tagging and Initial Recording
Upon receipt of a new asset, the following steps must occur within three (3) business days:

1.  **Receipt Verification:** The receiving party (Custodian) must verify the item matches the purchase order and is free of damage.
2.  **Tagging:**
    *   If shipped to a central location: The Asset Manager affixes a Study GRC Asset Tag.
    *   If shipped directly to a remote Custodian: The Asset Manager mails an Asset Tag to the Custodian. The Custodian must affix the tag to the chassis of the device (not a removable case) and upload a photo of the tagged item to the Asset Management System.
3.  **Registration:** The Asset Manager enters the following mandatory data points into the Master Asset Register (per 2 CFR 200.313(d)(1)):
    *   Description of the property.
    *   Serial number or other identification number.
    *   Source of funding (including the Federal Award Identification Number [FAIN], if applicable).
    *   Who holds title (Study GRC Inc. or Federal Govt).
    *   Acquisition date.
    *   Cost of the property.
    *   Percentage of federal participation in the project costs for the federal award under which the property was acquired.
    *   Location (Remote address of Custodian).
    *   Use and condition of the property.

#### 4.2 Annual Physical Inventory Schedule
A complete physical inventory of all assets must be taken and the results reconciled with the property records at least once every two (2) years. However, Study GRC Inc. mandates an **Annual** cycle to ensure financial accuracy.

*   **Inventory Period:** November 1st – November 30th.
*   **Reconciliation Deadline:** December 15th (Prior to Fiscal Year-End Close).

#### 4.3 Remote Verification Protocol
Due to the Organization's remote-first structure, physical verification is conducted virtually.

1.  **Notification:** The CFO sends a "Notice of Inventory" to all Custodians by November 1st, listing assets assigned to them.
2.  **Verification Method:** Custodians must perform one of the following verification methods for each assigned asset:
    *   **Method A (Live Video):** Schedule a 5-minute video call with the Asset Manager to display the item, its condition, and the Asset Tag.
    *   **Method B (Timestamped Evidence):** Upload a photo of the asset clearly showing the Asset Tag and Serial Number, alongside a digital timestamp (e.g., a phone screen showing the current date/time or a current news website header).
3.  **Condition Reporting:** The Custodian must explicitly state the condition of the asset (Excellent, Good, Fair, Poor) during verification.

#### 4.4 Reconciliation and Discrepancy Investigation
1.  **Comparison:** The Asset Manager compares the verified inventory against the Master Asset Register and the General Ledger.
2.  **Discrepancies:**
    *   **Unlocated Items:** If an item cannot be located, the Custodian has five (5) business days to locate it.
    *   **Missing Tags:** If a tag is missing or unreadable, a replacement tag is issued immediately.
    *   **Location Changes:** If an asset is found in a location different from the record, the record is updated immediately.
3.  **Lost/Stolen Assets:**
    *   If an item is confirmed lost or stolen, the Custodian must file an Incident Report immediately per the *Incident Response Plan*.
    *   For stolen items, a police report must be filed.
    *   The CFO determines if the asset must be written off.

#### 4.5 Final Reporting
1.  **Audit Report:** The CFO prepares a "Physical Inventory Report" summarizing:
    *   Total value of assets verified.
    *   List of missing/written-off assets.
    *   Condition assessment summary.
2.  **Board Submission:** This report is submitted to the Audit Committee and Board of Directors as part of the Annual Financial Report preparation (Bylaws Section 8.2).

#### 4.6 Asset Transfers and Disposal
*   **Transfers:** Custodians may not transfer assets to another employee without submitting an "Asset Transfer Form" to the Asset Manager.
*   **Disposal:** No asset may be sold, scrapped, or donated without prior approval via the *Fixed Asset Disposal Authorization Form*. For federally funded assets, disposition instructions must be requested from the awarding agency if the current fair market value exceeds $5,000.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns the inventory process; authorizes write-offs; reports results to the Board/Audit Committee. |
| **Asset Manager** | Maintains the Master Asset Register; issues tags; conducts remote verifications; performs reconciliation. |
| **Custodian** | Safeguards assigned assets; performs physical verification; reports loss/damage immediately. |
| **IT Department** | Assists in verifying technical specifications and serial numbers for IT hardware. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure—including failure to produce assets for inventory or negligence resulting in the loss of assets—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

For assets purchased with federal funds, failure to account for equipment may result in disallowed costs, requiring the Organization to reimburse the federal government.
