### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally defensible, and respectful process for ending the relationship between Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") and a volunteer. This procedure ensures that dismissals are conducted fairly, without discrimination, and in a manner that protects the Organization’s assets, reputation, and community safety while minimizing legal risk. It provides clear documentation protocols to satisfy risk management and employment law principles applied to the volunteer workforce.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Operational Volunteers**, including committee members, interns, and general volunteers.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Board of Directors:** Removal of Directors is strictly governed by **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**.
*   **Voting Members:** Revocation of Voting Membership rights is governed by **Bylaws Article II** and the *Member Expulsion Due Process Policy*.
*   **Note:** An individual may be dismissed from their *volunteer role* (e.g., Event Coordinator) under this procedure while retaining their status as a *Voting Member*, unless separate action is taken under Bylaws Article II.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **At-Will Volunteer** | The relationship is voluntary and may be terminated by either the volunteer or the Organization at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice. |
| **For Cause** | Dismissal resulting from a violation of policy, code of conduct, failure to perform duties, or ethical breaches. |
| **Job Abandonment** | Also known as "Ghosting"; when a volunteer fails to communicate or perform duties for 30 consecutive days without notice. |
| **Digital Offboarding** | The process of revoking access to Organization systems (Slack, Google Workspace, GitHub, etc.) to protect data security. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Grounds for Dismissal
While volunteering is at-will, the Organization generally initiates dismissal for the following reasons:
1.  **Policy Violation:** Breach of the *Code of Conduct*, *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, or *Data Privacy Policy*.
2.  **Non-Performance:** Failure to meet the requirements of the role description or repeated failure to complete assigned tasks.
3.  **Attendance:** Repeated unexcused absences from scheduled meetings or shifts.
4.  **Conflict of Interest:** Undisclosed conflicts as defined in **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1**.
5.  **Conduct:** Behavior incompatible with the Organization’s mission or values (e.g., harassment, insubordination).

#### 4.2 Immediate Dismissal (Zero Tolerance)
Certain actions require immediate removal pending investigation. In these cases, the **Executive Director** or **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** must:
1.  Immediately suspend the volunteer's activities.
2.  Instruct the **CISO** to revoke all system access (Slack, Email, Drive) immediately.
3.  Notify the volunteer via email that their service is suspended pending review.

**Triggers for Immediate Dismissal:**
*   Allegations of abuse, harassment, or endangerment of a minor (See *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*).
*   Theft, fraud, or misappropriation of funds.
*   Intentional data breach or IP theft.
*   Violence or credible threats of violence.

#### 4.3 Progressive Discipline (Performance Issues)
For non-egregious issues (e.g., missed deadlines, minor interpersonal conflict), the supervisor must follow these steps:
1.  **Verbal Coaching:** Discuss the issue with the volunteer. Clarify expectations. Document the conversation in the volunteer's file.
2.  **Written Warning:** If behavior persists, send a formal email outlining the gap between expectations and performance. Set a timeline for improvement.
3.  **Dismissal:** If no improvement occurs within the timeline, proceed to Section 4.4.

#### 4.4 Standard Dismissal Process
1.  **Preparation:** The supervisor compiles documentation (emails, incident reports, warning logs).
2.  **Approval:** Dismissal of a volunteer requires approval from the Department Head. Dismissal of a "Key Volunteer" (e.g., Committee Chair) requires Executive Director approval.
3.  **The Meeting:**
    *   Conduct a video call (Zoom/Teams) whenever possible.
    *   State the decision clearly: "We have decided to end your volunteer assignment effective immediately."
    *   Keep the explanation brief and professional. Avoid argumentation.
    *   Thank them for their past contributions (unless "For Cause" severity precludes this).
4.  **The Letter:** Follow up immediately with a *Notice of Volunteer Service Termination* email. This serves as the official record.

#### 4.5 Handling Job Abandonment ("Ghosting")
If a volunteer stops responding:
1.  **Attempt 1 (Day 7):** Check-in email via DKG or Supervisor.
2.  **Attempt 2 (Day 14):** Follow-up email asking if they wish to resign.
3.  **Final Notice (Day 30):** Send a "Presumed Resignation" email stating: *"Since we have not heard from you, we are processing your file as a voluntary resignation effective today. Thank you for your service."*
4.  **Action:** Proceed to Offboarding (Section 4.6).

#### 4.6 Offboarding and Asset Recovery
Upon confirmation of dismissal or resignation:
1.  **IT Revocation:** The CISO or IT Admin must revoke access to all platforms within 24 hours (Immediate for high-risk terminations).
2.  **Intellectual Property:** Remind the volunteer that per **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all work product created during their tenure remains the property of the Organization.
3.  **Data Deletion:** Request written confirmation that the volunteer has deleted any Organization data stored on personal devices (BYOD).
4.  **Physical Assets:** If the volunteer possesses physical keys or equipment, arrange for immediate return via prepaid shipping label.

#### 4.7 Documentation and Record Retention
1.  File the *Termination Notice* and any *Incident Reports* in the secure Volunteer Personnel File.
2.  Update the Volunteer Registry to "Inactive" status.
3.  Retain records for **7 years** post-termination in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer Supervisor** | Identifies performance issues; conducts coaching; initiates dismissal requests; documents incidents. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Reviews dismissal requests for consistency; conducts termination meetings; manages "ghosting" communications. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves dismissals of high-level volunteers; handles appeals; ensures alignment with Bylaws. |
| **CISO / IT Admin** | Executes Digital Offboarding; secures data; manages access logs. |
| **Board of Directors** | Notified only if the dismissal carries significant legal risk or involves a high-profile community member. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Non-Retaliation:** The Organization strictly prohibits retaliation against any volunteer who is dismissed, unless the dismissal was the result of a malicious false report filed by that volunteer. Volunteers dismissed for reporting misconduct in good faith have rights under the *Whistleblower Protection Policy*.

**Legal Review:** Any dismissal involving allegations of discrimination, harassment, or potential litigation must be reviewed by Legal Counsel prior to the termination meeting.

Failure to follow this procedure may result in disciplinary action against the staff member responsible for the deviation, up to and including termination of employment.
