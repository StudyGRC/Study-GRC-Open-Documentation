### 1. Purpose
This procedure establishes the formal mechanism by which the Voting Membership of Study GRC Inc. may exercise their right to remove a serving Director for "Cause," pursuant to Article III, Section 3.6 of the Bylaws. This document ensures that such actions are conducted with transparency, adherence to Due Process, and strict compliance with the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC).

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **Voting Members:** Supporting Members and Honorary Members in good standing.
*   **Board of Directors:** All current Directors and Officers.
*   **The Respondent:** The Director subject to the removal proceedings.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to the removal of Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director) unless they simultaneously hold a seat on the Board of Directors. Removal of Directors by the *Board* is covered under a separate procedure (GOV-PRC-037).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cause** | Specific grounds for removal as defined in Bylaws §3.6, including but not limited to: material violation of Bylaws, failure to attend meetings (absenteeism), ethical violations (Code of Conduct/Conflict of Interest), or conduct detrimental to the Organization. |
| **Respondent** | The Director against whom the removal petition is filed. |
| **Petitioner** | The Voting Member(s) initiating the removal request. |
| **Special Meeting** | A meeting of the membership called specifically for the purpose of considering the removal of a Director, distinct from the Annual Meeting. |
| **Quorum** | A majority (more than 50%) of the total number of Voting Members entitled to vote (Bylaws §2.4). |
| **Supermajority** | A vote requiring at least two-thirds (2/3) approval to pass. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Initiation of Removal Proceedings
1.  **Submission of Petition:** A Voting Member must submit a "Petition for Removal" to the Secretary of the Board (or the President, if the Secretary is the Respondent).
2.  **Petition Requirements:** The petition must be in writing (electronic submission via official channels is acceptable) and must include:
    *   The name of the Respondent (Director).
    *   A clear statement of the "Cause" for removal.
    *   Evidence or documentation supporting the claim of Cause.
    *   Signatures or digital endorsements of at least 10% of the Voting Membership (pursuant to TBOC § 22.155 regarding calling Special Meetings), unless the motion is to be heard at a previously scheduled Annual Meeting.

#### 4.2 Validation and Notification
1.  **Validation:** Within five (5) business days of receipt, the Secretary (or disinterested Officer) must validate that:
    *   The Petitioners are Members in Good Standing.
    *   The stated grounds constitute "Cause" under the Bylaws.
    *   The petition meets the minimum signature threshold (if applicable).
2.  **Notice to Respondent:** Upon validation, the Secretary must immediately notify the Respondent in writing. This notice must include a copy of the petition and all supporting evidence.
3.  **Notice to Membership:** The Board must schedule a meeting (Annual or Special) to hear the motion. Written notice must be delivered to all Voting Members at least 10 days, but no more than 60 days, prior to the meeting date (Bylaws §2.5). The notice must explicitly state that the purpose of the meeting is to consider the removal of the Director.

#### 4.3 The Removal Hearing (Meeting Protocol)
1.  **Presiding Officer:** The President shall preside over the meeting. If the President is the Respondent, the Secretary or Treasurer shall preside.
2.  **Quorum Verification:** Before proceedings begin, the Secretary must verify that a Quorum of Voting Members is present (physically or virtually). If no Quorum is established, the motion cannot be heard.
3.  **Presentation of Cause:** The Petitioner (or their designee) is granted a specific time limit (e.g., 15 minutes) to present the case for removal to the Membership.
4.  **Defense (Due Process):** The Respondent is granted an equal time limit to present a defense, offer rebuttals, and call witnesses if applicable.
5.  **Member Q&A:** The Presiding Officer will facilitate a Question and Answer period where Voting Members may question both the Petitioner and the Respondent.

#### 4.4 Voting Process
1.  **Ballot Method:** To ensure integrity and prevent intimidation, the vote must be conducted via a secret ballot or a secure, anonymized electronic voting system (Bylaws §2.3).
2.  **Proxy Prohibition:** Proxy voting is strictly prohibited. Members must cast their own votes.
3.  **Recusal:** The Respondent may not vote on their own removal, though they may be counted for purposes of establishing a quorum if permitted by law.

#### 4.5 Threshold and Certification
1.  **Vote Count:** The Secretary (and a witness, if available) shall tally the votes.
2.  **Threshold:** Removal requires the affirmative vote of **two-thirds (2/3)** of the Voting Members participating in the meeting (Bylaws §3.6).
3.  **Certification:** The results must be certified immediately and recorded in the minutes.

#### 4.6 Post-Vote Actions
1.  **Immediate Effect:** If the vote passes, the removal is effective immediately. The individual ceases to be a Director and loses all associated authority and access.
2.  **Access Revocation:** The CISO and IT staff must immediately revoke the removed Director's access to all organizational systems, data, and accounts (Procedure IS-PRC-012).
3.  **Vacancy Filling:** The vacancy created by the removal shall be filled according to Bylaws §3.8 (Vacancies).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Voting Member** | Initiates petition; attends meeting; casts vote based on evidence. |
| **Secretary** | Validates petition standing; issues notices; manages voting process; records minutes. |
| **Respondent** | Provides defense against allegations; cooperates with the hearing process. |
| **Presiding Officer** | Maintains order during the hearing; ensures equal time for both parties. |
| **CISO** | Executes immediate account deprovisioning upon successful removal vote. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Legal Compliance:** Failure to follow this procedure strictly may result in legal challenges regarding the validity of the removal, potentially exposing the Organization to liability for wrongful termination of a directorship.
*   **Bylaws Supremacy:** In any conflict between this procedure and the Bylaws, the Bylaws shall govern.
*   **Retaliation Prohibited:** Retaliation against Members for filing a petition in good faith is strictly prohibited and constitutes a violation of the Code of Ethics.
