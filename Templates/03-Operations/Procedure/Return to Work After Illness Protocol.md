### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish clear, science-based criteria for employees, volunteers, and contractors returning to work following a communicable illness. This procedure aims to:
1.  Protect the health and safety of the Study GRC Inc. workforce, community members, and event participants.
2.  Prevent the transmission of infectious diseases (including COVID-19, Influenza, RSV, and Norovirus) within in-person settings.
3.  Provide clarity on the distinction between "medically fit to work remotely" and "safe to return to in-person environments."
4.  Ensure compliance with current Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) guidelines and Texas state health regulations.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Personnel**, defined as employees, independent contractors, Board Directors, and volunteers acting on behalf of Study GRC Inc.

*   **Remote Work Context:** Applies to the resumption of digital duties.
*   **In-Person Context:** Applies strictly to physical presence at Study GRC Inc. offices, off-site meetings, conferences, training workshops, and youth programs.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not override specific medical advice provided to an individual by their primary care physician. In cases of conflict, the physician’s written directive prevails.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Communicable Disease** | An illness caused by an infectious agent or its toxins that occurs through the direct or indirect transmission of the infectious agent or its products from an infected individual. Examples: COVID-19, Influenza, Norovirus, Strep Throat. |
| **Fever** | A measured body temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher. |
| **Fever-Free** | A period of time during which a person has a body temperature below 100.4°F (38°C) without the use of fever-reducing medications (e.g., acetaminophen, ibuprofen). |
| **Symptoms Improving** | The overall sense of feeling better and the reduction in severity of symptoms (e.g., cough is less frequent, energy is returning). |
| **Vulnerable Population** | Individuals at higher risk for severe illness, including the elderly, immunocompromised individuals, and, for the purposes of this organization, minors participating in youth programs. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Notification and Reporting
1.  **Immediate Notification:** Personnel unable to perform their duties due to illness must notify their direct supervisor via the designated communication channel (e.g., Slack, Email) prior to the start of their scheduled shift or volunteer commitment.
2.  **Privacy:** Personnel are not required to disclose a specific medical diagnosis (e.g., "I have COVID") but must disclose if they have a **Communicable Disease** if they have recently been (within 48 hours) or plan to be in physical contact with other staff or community members.

#### 4.2 Return to Remote Work (Work-From-Home)
As a remote-first organization, Study GRC Inc. distinguishes between being *contagious* and being *too sick to work*.

1.  **Self-Assessment:** Personnel may resume remote work duties when they feel physically and mentally capable of performing their essential job functions.
2.  **Symptom Management:** Personnel should not attempt to work remotely if experiencing severe "brain fog," fatigue, or symptoms that prevent professional conduct during video calls.
3.  **Flexibility:** Supervisors must allow for flexible hours or reduced workload during the recovery phase to prevent burnout and relapse.

#### 4.3 Return to In-Person Work (Respiratory Viruses)
In alignment with **CDC Respiratory Virus Guidance (March 2024)**, covering COVID-19, Influenza, and RSV, the following steps are mandatory for returning to physical events or meetings:

**Step 1: Isolation Phase**
Personnel must stay home and away from others until **BOTH** of the following criteria are met:
1.  They have been **Fever-Free** for at least 24 hours (without the use of fever-reducing medication); **AND**
2.  Their symptoms are getting better overall.

**Step 2: Added Precaution Phase (5 Days)**
Upon returning to in-person activities after meeting the criteria in Step 1, Personnel must take added precautions for the next **five (5) days**:
1.  **Masking:** Wear a well-fitting, high-quality mask (N95/KN95) when indoors around others.
2.  **Distancing:** Maintain physical distance from others where feasible.
3.  **Testing:** If the illness was confirmed COVID-19, a negative antigen test is recommended (but not strictly required) before removing a mask in close-quarters.

#### 4.4 Return to In-Person Work (Gastrointestinal Illness)
Due to the high transmissibility of Norovirus and similar ailments:
1.  Personnel experiencing vomiting or diarrhea must not attend in-person events.
2.  Return is permitted only after being **symptom-free for a full 48 hours**.

#### 4.5 Special Protocol: Youth Programs
Pursuant to the organization's commitment to **Youth Protection (Section VI of the Master Registry)**, stricter standards apply to staff interacting with minors.

1.  **Precautionary Exclusion:** Personnel currently in the "Added Precaution Phase" (Step 2 of Section 4.3) are **prohibited** from staffing in-person youth workshops or events where close contact (instructional proximity) is required.
2.  **Substitution:** Supervisors must arrange for a substitute instructor or volunteer if the primary assignee is within the 5-day precaution window.

#### 4.6 Medical Clearance Requirements
A written note from a healthcare provider stating the individual is "cleared to return to work" is required if:
1.  The absence exceeds three (3) consecutive business days (for employees).
2.  The individual was hospitalized.
3.  The individual has a communicable disease subject to specific state quarantine orders (e.g., Tuberculosis, Measles).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Personnel (Employees/Volunteers)** | Monitor own health status; honestly report inability to work; adhere to isolation timelines; use PPE during the precautionary phase. |
| **Direct Supervisors** | Enforce this protocol without pressuring sick staff to work; arrange coverage for duties; ensure no retaliation occurs for taking sick leave. |
| **Executive Director** | Authorize exceptions or stricter measures based on local community transmission levels (e.g., declaring a "Mask Mandate" for a specific event). |
| **HR / Operations** | Maintain confidentiality of medical information (HIPAA/ADA compliance); track sick leave balances (for employees). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**6.1 Disciplinary Action**
Failure to comply with this policy—specifically, knowingly attending an in-person event while contagious or symptomatic with a communicable disease—is considered a violation of the **Code of Ethics** and the **Safety Policy**. Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or permanent removal from volunteer service.

**6.2 Non-Retaliation**
Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who stays home due to illness or who reports safety concerns regarding a sick colleague.

**6.3 Authority**
This procedure is established under the operational authority granted to the Executive Director in **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.2**, to manage the day-to-day operations and ensure the safety of the Organization's community.
