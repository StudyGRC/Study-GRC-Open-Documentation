### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, repeatable process for closing the financial books of Study GRC Inc. at the conclusion of the Fiscal Year (defined in **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3** as the calendar year). This procedure ensures the accuracy of financial data, compliance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), adherence to **2 CFR 200** (Uniform Guidance) for federal awards, and readiness for the annual independent audit and IRS Form 990 filing.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all financial transactions, accounts, and reporting units of the Organization.
*   **Applies to:** Finance Department, Executive Director, Grant Managers, and the Board Treasurer.
*   **Exclusions:** This procedure does not cover the specific *audit fieldwork* process, but rather the internal preparation *for* the audit.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fiscal Year** | The accounting period for the Organization, running from January 1 to December 31, per Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3. |
| **Cut-Off** | The point in time separating transactions between the current fiscal year and the next. |
| **Accrual** | An accounting adjustment to record revenues earned or expenses incurred that have not yet been exchanged in cash. |
| **SEFA** | Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards; a mandatory schedule for federal grantees under 2 CFR 200. |
| **Sub-Ledger** | Detailed records for specific items (e.g., Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable) that roll up to the General Ledger. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Pre-Close Planning (December 1 – December 31)
*   **4.1.1 Timeline Establishment:** The CFO must issue a "Year-End Closing Calendar" to all department heads by December 10, detailing deadlines for expense submission, invoice approval, and time entry.
*   **4.1.2 Vendor Notification:** Notify vendors to submit all outstanding invoices for the current year by January 5 of the following year.
*   **4.1.3 Grant Review:** Grant Managers must review all active federal and private grants to ensure all allowable costs for the period have been obligated.
*   **4.1.4 Physical Inventory:** If applicable (e.g., merchandise, hardware), conduct a physical count of inventory on December 31.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Sub-Ledger Close (January 1 – January 10)
*   **4.2.1 Accounts Payable (AP) Cut-Off:**
    *   Ensure all invoices for goods/services received on or before December 31 are entered into the accounting system with a December 31 date.
    *   Invoices received in January for December services must be accrued (see Section 4.4).
*   **4.2.2 Accounts Receivable (AR) Cut-Off:**
    *   Ensure all invoices for services rendered or grants earned through December 31 are generated and posted.
    *   Review "Aged Receivables" report; write off uncollectible bad debts upon written authorization from the Executive Director.
*   **4.2.3 Payroll Reconciliation:**
    *   Reconcile quarterly Form 941s to the General Ledger (GL) wage expense accounts.
    *   Calculate and record the vacation/PTO liability accrual based on the "Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy."

#### 4.3 Phase III: General Ledger & Asset Reconciliation (January 11 – January 20)
*   **4.3.1 Bank Reconciliations:** Reconcile all bank and credit card accounts to the GL as of December 31. Any uncleared checks older than 90 days must be investigated.
*   **4.3.2 Fixed Assets:**
    *   Update the Fixed Asset Register with all acquisitions and disposals made during the year.
    *   Calculate and post depreciation expense for the year.
    *   Verify compliance with **2 CFR 200.313** regarding equipment purchased with federal funds.
*   **4.3.3 Prepaid Expenses:** Review the Prepaid Expense account. Amortize expenses (e.g., insurance premiums, software subscriptions) that were utilized during the fiscal year.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Accruals and Grant Compliance (January 21 – January 25)
*   **4.4.1 Expense Accruals:** Create journal entries for expenses incurred but not yet billed (e.g., legal fees, audit fees, December utilities).
*   **4.4.2 Revenue Recognition:**
    *   Review all restricted contributions. Ensure funds are released from restriction (Net Assets with Donor Restrictions $\to$ Net Assets without Donor Restrictions) if the purpose or time restriction was met.
    *   Defer revenue for grants/sponsorships received in the current year for activities scheduled in the *next* fiscal year.
*   **4.4.3 SEFA Preparation:** Draft the Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA). Reconcile SEFA totals to the GL revenue and expense accounts to ensure 2 CFR 200 compliance.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Review and Final Reporting (January 26 – January 31)
*   **4.5.1 Variance Analysis:** The CFO must run a "Budget vs. Actual" report. Any variance exceeding 10% or $5,000 (whichever is lower) requires a written explanation.
*   **4.5.2 Net Asset Roll-Forward:** Reconcile beginning Net Assets + Net Income (Loss) = Ending Net Assets.
*   **4.5.3 Draft Financial Statements:** Generate the preliminary Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet) and Statement of Activities (Income Statement).
*   **4.5.4 Treasurer Review:** Submit preliminary statements and the Variance Analysis to the Treasurer for review.

#### 4.6 Phase VI: Period Lock (February 1)
*   **4.6.1 System Lock:** Once the Treasurer approves the preliminary figures, the CFO must "Lock" the closing period in the accounting software to prevent accidental posting of entries to the prior year.
*   **4.6.2 Board Presentation:** Present the unaudited year-end financial report to the Board of Directors in accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns the closing process; approves all adjusting journal entries; ensures GAAP/Federal compliance; locks the period. |
| **Bookkeeper / Accountant** | Performs data entry, bank reconciliations, and initial sub-ledger reviews. |
| **Grant Managers** | Verify grant expenditures and assist in SEFA preparation. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides oversight; reviews preliminary statements; presents final report to the Board. |
| **Board of Directors** | Receives the Annual Financial Report; appoints the external auditor. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this procedure may result in inaccurate financial reporting, audit findings, loss of federal funding eligibility, and potential legal liability.
*   **Internal:** Staff failing to meet cut-off deadlines may be subject to disciplinary action per the Employee Handbook.
*   **External:** Material misstatements resulting from negligence in this process may trigger a "Material Weakness" finding in the Single Audit.
