### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a rigorous system for tracking, calculating, and reporting lobbying expenditures incurred by Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). As a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt entity, the Organization is strictly limited in the amount of lobbying it may conduct. Furthermore, as a recipient of federal grant funds, the Organization is prohibited from using federal funds for lobbying activities under the Byrd Amendment and 2 CFR § 200.450.

This procedure ensures compliance with Internal Revenue Code (IRC) restrictions, facilitates accurate filing of IRS Form 990 Schedule C, and protects the Organization’s tax-exempt status and eligibility for federal funding.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, and volunteers acting on behalf of the Organization.

**In Scope:**
*   Any communication with legislators, government officials, or the general public intended to influence specific legislation.
*   Allocation of staff time, overhead, and direct expenses related to such activities.

**Exclusions:**
*   Non-partisan analysis, study, or research.
*   Technical advice or assistance provided to a governmental body in response to a written request by that body.
*   Self-defense communications (contacting legislators regarding legislation that would affect the Organization’s existence, powers, or tax-exempt status).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Direct Lobbying** | Communication with a legislator (federal, state, or local) or legislative staff member that refers to specific legislation and reflects a view on that legislation. |
| **Grassroots Lobbying** | Communication with the general public that refers to specific legislation, reflects a view on it, and encourages the recipient to take action (e.g., "Call your Senator"). |
| **Legislation** | Action by Congress, a state legislature, a local council, or a similar legislative body, or by the public in a referendum, ballot initiative, or constitutional amendment. It does not include actions by executive, judicial, or administrative bodies (e.g., school boards, zoning boards). |
| **501(h) Election** | An IRS provision allowing 501(c)(3) organizations to measure lobbying limits based on a sliding scale of expenditures rather than the vague "substantial part" test. |
| **Unallowable Cost** | Costs that cannot be charged to federal awards, either directly or indirectly. Lobbying is strictly unallowable under 2 CFR § 200.450. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Pre-Activity Authorization
Before engaging in any activity that meets the definition of Direct or Grassroots Lobbying, the initiator must obtain approval.

1.  **Proposal Submission:** The requesting staff member must submit a brief "Lobbying Activity Proposal" to the Executive Director (CEO).
2.  **Review:** The CEO and CFO review the proposal to ensure:
    *   The activity aligns with the Organization’s charitable purpose (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3).
    *   Sufficient **unrestricted, non-federal** funds are available.
    *   The activity will not cause the Organization to exceed its annual lobbying expenditure limits (calculated per the 501(h) election thresholds).
3.  **Approval:** Written authorization (email or memo) must be granted by the CEO before activity begins.

#### 4.2 Personnel Time Tracking
Since staff time often constitutes the largest portion of lobbying expenses, accurate timekeeping is mandatory.

1.  **Timesheet Coding:** Employees engaging in lobbying must record their time in the Organization’s timekeeping system using specific codes:
    *   `LOBBY-DIR` (Direct Lobbying)
    *   `LOBBY-GRASS` (Grassroots Lobbying)
2.  **Preparation Time:** Time spent researching, drafting, and preparing materials for lobbying purposes must be coded as lobbying, even if the communication is not yet sent.
3.  **Verification:** The CFO or designee reviews timesheets monthly to ensure lobbying time is accurately categorized and separated from programmatic or administrative time.

#### 4.3 Direct Expense Tracking
All non-labor expenses related to lobbying must be isolated in the financial records.

1.  **General Ledger (GL) Codes:** The CFO shall maintain distinct GL accounts for lobbying expenses:
    *   `5000-LOBBY`: Direct Lobbying Expenses (Travel, printing, postage, consultant fees).
    *   `5100-LOBBY`: Grassroots Lobbying Expenses (Advertising, mass mailings, social media boosts).
2.  **Expense Reports:** Employees submitting reimbursement requests for lobbying-related travel or meals must explicitly flag these expenses as "Lobbying" in the expense management system.
3.  **Allocation of Mixed Costs:** If an expense covers both lobbying and non-lobbying activities (e.g., a newsletter with one article urging legislative action), the cost must be allocated reasonably between the two categories based on column inches or page count.

#### 4.4 Segregation of Funds (Federal Grant Compliance)
To comply with 2 CFR § 200.450, the Organization must strictly segregate funding sources.

1.  **Prohibition:** No federal grant funds (direct or indirect) may be used for lobbying.
2.  **Source Identification:** The CFO must identify a source of **unrestricted private funds** (e.g., individual donations, unrestricted foundation grants) to cover all costs recorded in the `LOBBY` GL accounts.
3.  **Reconciliation:** During the monthly financial close, the CFO verifies that the total lobbying expenditure does not exceed available unrestricted funds.

#### 4.5 Quarterly Compliance Review
1.  **Calculation:** The CFO calculates the year-to-date lobbying expenditures (Direct + Grassroots).
2.  **Threshold Check:** The CFO compares the total against the Organization’s 501(h) expenditure limit (generally 20% of the first $500,000 of exempt purpose expenditures).
3.  **Reporting:** The CFO provides a "Lobbying Expenditure Report" to the Board of Directors quarterly, highlighting the percentage of the limit utilized.

#### 4.6 Annual Reporting (IRS Form 990)
1.  **Data Aggregation:** At the close of the fiscal year (Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3), the CFO aggregates all time and direct expenses.
2.  **Schedule C Preparation:** The CFO (or external tax preparer) completes **Schedule C (Form 990)**, Part II-A (if 501(h) elected) or Part II-B.
3.  **Transparency:** Per Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2, the completed Form 990, including Schedule C, is made available for public inspection.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews quarterly lobbying reports; determines whether to make/maintain the 501(h) election; ensures lobbying remains insubstantial. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Approves lobbying initiatives; ensures alignment with mission; ensures no federal funds are committed. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Maintains GL codes; monitors timekeeping; performs cost allocations; prepares Schedule C data; enforces segregation of funds. |
| **Employees/Volunteers** | Obtain prior approval for lobbying; accurately record time and expenses using designated codes. |
| **External Tax Preparer** | Reviews Schedule C data for compliance with current IRS regulations during the annual audit/filing process. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure puts the Organization at risk of losing its 501(c)(3) status and facing significant excise taxes under IRC § 4911.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees who engage in unauthorized lobbying or fail to accurately record lobbying time/expenses are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Grant Violation:** Use of federal funds for lobbying is a violation of federal law and may result in the termination of grant awards and debarment from future federal funding.
