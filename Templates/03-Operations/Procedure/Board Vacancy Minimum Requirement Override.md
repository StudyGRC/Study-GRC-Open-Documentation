### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory, expedited protocol for filling Board of Director vacancies when the total number of active Directors falls below the statutory and Bylaw-mandated minimum of three (3). This procedure operationalizes the "Minimum Requirement Override" clause found in Bylaws Article III, Section 3.8, ensuring Study GRC Inc. maintains its legal standing as a Texas Nonprofit Corporation and prevents the activation of the "Doomsday Clause" (Section 3.10).

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the **Board of Directors** and **Officers** of Study GRC Inc.

*   **Trigger Condition:** This procedure is automatically triggered strictly when the number of active, voting Directors is reduced to **one (1) or two (2)**.
*   **Exclusion:** If the number of Directors reaches **zero (0)**, this procedure is null and void, and the Organization must proceed immediately to the *Emergency Succession Plan (Zero Directors)* pursuant to Bylaws Section 3.10.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Critical Vacancy** | A state in which the Board of Directors consists of fewer than three (3) active members, violating Bylaws Section 3.2. |
| **Standard Election Protocol** | The normal process of nominations, vetting, and membership voting described in Bylaws Section 2.5. |
| **Emergency Appointment** | The expedited selection of a Director by the remaining Board members to restore the minimum legal composition of the Board. |
| **Remaining Directors** | The Director(s) still in office after resignations, removals, or deaths have occurred, even if they constitute less than a quorum. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Declaration of Critical Vacancy
Upon receipt of a resignation, removal order, or notice of death that reduces the Board size to fewer than three (3) members:
1.  The **Secretary** (or remaining Officer) must immediately issue a **Notice of Critical Vacancy** to the remaining Directors and the Executive Director.
2.  The remaining Directors must convene an **Emergency Meeting** within 48 hours via video conference.
    *   *Note:* Per Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.212, the remaining Directors may fill a vacancy even if they constitute less than a quorum.

#### 4.2 Suspension of Standard Protocols
To ensure business continuity and legal compliance:
1.  The **Standard Election Protocol** (Bylaws Section 2.5) is immediately suspended.
2.  The requirement for a general membership vote is temporarily waived until the next Annual Meeting.
3.  The requirement for a 14-day nomination period is waived.

#### 4.3 Candidate Identification and Vetting
Due to the urgency of the Critical Vacancy, the Remaining Directors must identify candidates who can be appointed immediately.
1.  **Priority Pool:** The Remaining Directors shall prioritize candidates in the following order:
    *   Current members of the **Advisory Board**.
    *   Former **Board Directors** who left in good standing.
    *   **Operational Officers** (subject to Conflict of Interest checks).
    *   **Supporting Members** with a demonstrated history of service.
2.  **Vetting:** The Remaining Directors must verify that the candidate is a Voting Member in good standing and has no disqualifying conflicts of interest.

#### 4.4 Emergency Appointment Vote
1.  The Remaining Directors shall vote to appoint individuals to fill the vacant seat(s) until the total number of Directors reaches at least three (3).
2.  **Voting Threshold:** An affirmative vote of the majority of the Remaining Directors is required.
    *   *Scenario A (2 Directors Remaining):* Both must agree, or a majority (2 of 2) is required. If split, they must compromise on a candidate.
    *   *Scenario B (1 Director Remaining):* The sole Director has the unilateral authority to appoint the necessary Directors to restore the Board to three members (TBOC § 22.212).

#### 4.5 Term of Emergency Appointee
1.  Directors appointed under this procedure shall serve only for the **unexpired term** of the predecessor they are replacing.
2.  If the unexpired term concludes within 90 days, the appointee must stand for formal election by the Membership at the next Annual Meeting to continue service.

#### 4.6 Notification and Ratification
1.  Once the Board is restored to three (3) or more members, the **Secretary** must record the appointments in the minutes of the Emergency Meeting.
2.  The Board must notify the **Voting Membership** within five (5) business days regarding the emergency appointments and the restoration of the Board's legal status.
3.  The Board must file any necessary updates with the Texas Secretary of State (e.g., Form 802) if the Registered Agent or key governing persons have changed.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Remaining Directors** | Sole authority to identify, vet, and appoint emergency replacements to restore the Board to legal minimums. |
| **Secretary** | Responsible for certifying the "Critical Vacancy" status and recording the official minutes of the emergency appointment. |
| **Executive Director** | Assists in identifying candidates from the Advisory Board or Membership but has no voting power in the appointment. |
| **Emergency Appointee** | Must immediately sign the *Conflict of Interest Disclosure* and *Board Code of Ethics* upon acceptance. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to execute this procedure when the Board falls below three (3) members places the Organization in violation of Texas Law and its own Bylaws.
*   **Risk:** Inability to legally transact business, loss of liability protection for remaining Directors, and potential revocation of 501(c)(3) status.
*   **Escalation:** If the Remaining Directors fail to act within 14 days of the vacancy, any Voting Member may petition for a Special Emergency Meeting under Bylaws Section 3.10 to elect a new Board.
