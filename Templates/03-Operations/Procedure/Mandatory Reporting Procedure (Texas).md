### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory steps for reporting suspected child abuse or neglect within Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). This document ensures compliance with **Texas Family Code § 261.101**, which imposes a universal duty to report, and aligns with the Organization’s commitment to providing a safe, zero-cost educational ecosystem for all learners, including minors.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to **all** individuals associated with the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   Members of the Board of Directors.
*   Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director, DKG).
*   Employees and Contractors.
*   Volunteers (including Community Moderators and Instructors).
*   Supporting and Honorary Members.

This scope encompasses all interactions, whether physical or virtual (e.g., Zoom, Discord, Learning Management Systems), occurring under the auspices of Study GRC Inc.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Abuse** | Mental, emotional, physical, or sexual injury to a child, or failure to prevent such injury, as defined in Texas Family Code § 261.001. |
| **Neglect** | The leaving of a child in a situation where the child would be exposed to a substantial risk of physical or mental harm, or the failure to seek, obtain, or provide necessary medical care, food, or shelter. |
| **Mandated Reporter** | Under Texas law, **any person** who has cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect. |
| **DFPS** | Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (also known as Child Protective Services or CPS). |
| **The 48-Hour Rule** | The statutory requirement that a report must be made not later than the 48th hour after the hour the professional first suspects that the child has been or may be abused or neglected. |
| **Immunity** | Protection from civil or criminal liability for reporting in "good faith," pursuant to Texas Family Code § 261.106 and Bylaws Article VII. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Immediate Danger Assessment
If a child is in immediate, life-threatening danger:
1.  **Call 911 immediately.**
2.  Provide the location of the child (if known) and the nature of the emergency.
3.  Proceed to Section 4.2 only after emergency services have been contacted.

#### 4.2 The Duty to Report (External)
**WARNING:** Reporting your suspicion to a supervisor, the Executive Director, or the Board **DOES NOT** satisfy your legal obligation under Texas law. You must report directly to the State of Texas.

1.  **Timeline:** The report must be made **immediately**, but no later than 48 hours after the suspicion arises.
2.  **Reporting Channels:**
    *   **Online (Non-Emergency):** [Texas Abuse Hotline Website](https://www.txabusehotline.org) (Preferred for non-emergency situations as it provides a confirmation number).
    *   **Phone (Urgent/Emergency):** Call **1-800-252-5400**.
3.  **Information to Provide:**
    *   Name, age, and address of the child (if known).
    *   Name and address of the person responsible for the care of the child.
    *   Nature and extent of the child’s condition/injuries.
    *   Any evidence of previous injuries.
    *   Your name and contact information (reports are confidential).

#### 4.3 Internal Notification
After the legal obligation to report to the State has been fulfilled, the reporter must notify the Organization to allow for internal safety measures.

1.  **Notify the DKG:** Submit a confidential notification to the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).
    *   *Exception:* If the DKG is the alleged perpetrator, notify the President of the Board of Directors.
2.  **Content of Internal Notice:**
    *   Date and time the report was made to DFPS.
    *   The Case Reference Number provided by DFPS.
    *   **Do not** investigate or interview the child yourself. This may contaminate evidence required by law enforcement.

#### 4.4 Preservation of Digital Evidence
As a remote-first organization, abuse or neglect may be witnessed via digital channels.
1.  **Capture:** Take screenshots or export chat logs immediately if the suspicion arises from online interaction (e.g., Discord messages, video call conduct).
2.  **Secure:** Upload these files to the secure "Legal Hold" repository as directed by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).
3.  **Do Not Delete:** Do not ban the user or delete the content until authorized by the DKG or Law Enforcement, as this may destroy evidence.

#### 4.5 Suspension of Accused Personnel
Upon receipt of an internal notification regarding an allegation against a Staff Member, Volunteer, or Director:
1.  The accused individual shall be placed on **immediate administrative leave** or temporary suspension from all duties involving interaction with the community.
2.  Access to organizational systems (email, Discord admin, LMS) will be revoked by the CISO pending investigation.
3.  This action is neutral and precautionary, not an admission of guilt.

#### 4.6 Confidentiality and Non-Retaliation
1.  **Confidentiality:** The identity of the reporter is protected by law. Internal discussions regarding the report must be limited strictly to the DKG, the Executive Director, and Legal Counsel.
2.  **Non-Retaliation:** Pursuant to the **Whistleblower Protection Policy**, no person who reports child abuse or neglect in good faith will suffer harassment, retaliation, or adverse employment consequences.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Personnel** | Legally required to report suspected abuse/neglect directly to DFPS immediately. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Acts as the internal Compliance Officer for youth protection; coordinates with legal counsel; manages internal suspensions. |
| **CISO** | Secures digital evidence and manages access revocation for accused individuals. |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensures the organization maintains liability insurance and adheres to Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification) for good faith reporters. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

*   **Legal Penalties:** Failure to report suspected child abuse or neglect is a Class A Misdemeanor in Texas, punishable by up to one year in jail and/or a fine of up to $4,000. If the intent is to conceal the abuse, it is a State Jail Felony.
*   **Organizational Discipline:** Failure to comply with this procedure, including failure to report or interfering with a report, will result in immediate termination of employment, volunteer status, or Board membership.
