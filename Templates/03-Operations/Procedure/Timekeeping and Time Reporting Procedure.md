### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory protocols for recording, reviewing, and approving work hours and leave usage at Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), the Texas Payday Law, and Federal Uniform Guidance (2 CFR § 200.430) regarding compensation for personal services. Accurate timekeeping is essential to prevent wage theft, ensure accurate grant reporting, and maintain financial integrity.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **All Employees:** Both Non-Exempt (hourly) and Exempt (salaried).
*   **Volunteers:** Specifically those whose hours are tracked for grant "in-kind" matching or community impact reporting.
*   **Contractors:** For the purpose of invoice verification against service logs.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not supersede specific terms within independent contractor agreements regarding flat-fee deliverables.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Non-Exempt Employee** | An employee entitled to overtime pay under the FLSA. They are paid for all hours worked. |
| **Exempt Employee** | An employee paid a salary basis who is not eligible for overtime pay under the FLSA. |
| **Off-the-Clock Work** | Work performed by a Non-Exempt employee that is not recorded in the timekeeping system. This is strictly prohibited. |
| **Cost Center/Grant Code** | A specific accounting code used to allocate labor costs to a specific project, department, or federal award. |
| **Personnel Activity Report (PAR)** | A document certifying that the distribution of activity represents a reasonable estimate of the work performed by the employee during the period. |
| **Pay Period** | The recurring schedule on which time is recorded and paid (e.g., Bi-Weekly). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 General Principles of Timekeeping
4.1.1 **Accuracy:** All time reported must reflect the actual time spent working. Recording time for another employee ("buddy punching") or falsifying time records is fraud and grounds for immediate termination.
4.1.2 **System of Record:** All time must be recorded in the Organization’s designated Human Resources Information System (HRIS) or electronic timekeeping platform.
4.1.3 **Federal Compliance:** As a federal grantee, Study GRC Inc. requires that time records reflect **total activity** for which the employee is compensated, not exceeding 100% of compensated activities.

#### 4.2 Non-Exempt (Hourly) Employee Protocols
4.2.1 **Recording Time:**
*   Non-Exempt employees must record their starting time, ending time, and the beginning and ending time of any unpaid meal break.
*   Time must be recorded to the minute.
*   Employees must clock in no earlier than 7 minutes before their shift and clock out no later than 7 minutes after their shift, unless overtime has been authorized.

4.2.2 **Remote Work Timekeeping:**
*   Due to the Organization's remote-first nature, "clocking in" is defined as logging into the Organization’s systems and being available for work.
*   Employees must log out during significant personal interruptions (e.g., picking up children, medical appointments) and log back in upon return.

4.2.3 **Meal and Rest Breaks:**
*   **Rest Breaks:** Paid 15-minute rest breaks (if taken) do not need to be clocked out.
*   **Meal Breaks:** Unpaid meal periods (typically 30-60 minutes) must be recorded by clocking out and clocking back in. Employees must be fully relieved of duties during this time.

4.2.4 **Overtime:**
*   All hours worked in excess of 40 hours in a single workweek must be recorded.
*   Employees must obtain prior written approval (email or Slack) from their manager before working overtime, per `HR-POL-038 Overtime Authorization Policy`.
*   **Prohibition on Off-the-Clock Work:** Employees are strictly prohibited from performing work without recording it to "save budget" or avoid overtime limits. If work is performed, it must be recorded and paid.

#### 4.3 Exempt (Salaried) Employee Protocols
4.3.1 **Exception Reporting:**
*   Exempt employees are not required to clock in/out for daily shifts but must record "Exceptions" to their standard workweek, such as:
    *   Sick Leave
    *   Paid Time Off (PTO)
    *   Jury Duty
    *   Bereavement
4.3.2 **Functional Allocation (Grant Compliance):**
*   Exempt employees working on multiple cost objectives (e.g., 50% on Federal Grant A, 50% on General Admin) must record their time distribution based on **after-the-fact determination of actual activity**, not just budget estimates.

#### 4.4 Volunteer Time Tracking
4.4.1 **In-Kind Match:**
*   Volunteers performing professional services or labor that serves as a "match" for grant funding must log hours via the Volunteer Portal.
*   Logs must include the date, duration, and specific description of the activity performed.

#### 4.5 Time Sheet Submission and Approval
4.5.1 **Submission Deadline:**
*   All employees must submit their finalized time sheets by **10:00 AM CST** on the Monday following the close of the pay period.
4.5.2 **Manager Review:**
*   Managers must review time sheets for accuracy, ensuring:
    *   Total hours match expected output.
    *   Overtime is accurate and was pre-approved (if applicable).
    *   Time is allocated to the correct Grant/Project codes.
    *   PTO/Leave is correctly categorized.
4.5.3 **Approval Deadline:**
*   Managers must approve time sheets by **5:00 PM CST** on the Monday following the close of the pay period to ensure timely payroll processing.

#### 4.6 Corrections and Adjustments
4.6.1 **Missed Punches:**
*   If a Non-Exempt employee forgets to clock in or out, they must submit a "Time Correction Request" within the HRIS, including the reason for the error.
4.6.2 **Retroactive Adjustments:**
*   If an error is discovered after payroll has run, the employee must notify the CFO immediately. Adjustments will be processed in the subsequent pay period in compliance with Texas Payday Law.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Record time accurately and truthfully on a daily basis. Submit time sheets by the deadline. Notify management immediately of any discrepancies. |
| **Manager** | Verify the accuracy of hours worked and project codes charged. Monitor overtime usage. Approve time sheets by the deadline. |
| **CFO / Payroll** | Audit time sheets for compliance with 2 CFR 200. Process payroll. Maintain records for the retention period defined in `GOV-STD-013`. |
| **Executive Director** | Review and approve time sheets for direct reports (e.g., CFO, VP of Education). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Falsification:** Intentionally recording time not worked, or recording time for another employee, is considered theft and will result in immediate termination.
*   **Wage Theft:** Managers who instruct employees to work "off-the-clock" or who alter employee time records to reduce hours paid will be subject to immediate termination and potential legal action.
*   **Grant Fraud:** Inaccurate allocation of time to federal grants constitutes a violation of federal law and may result in personal liability under the False Claims Act.
