### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, non-discriminatory method for evaluating criminal history information obtained during background checks for prospective employees, volunteers, and Board Directors. This procedure ensures Study GRC Inc. complies with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) Enforcement Guidance on the Consideration of Arrest and Conviction Records ("Fair Chance" guidance), the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), and Texas state laws regarding youth protection, while maintaining the safety and integrity of the Organization’s community and assets.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all candidates for:
*   **Employment** (Full-time, part-time, and temporary);
*   **Board of Directors** (Pursuant to Bylaws Article III, Section 3.3 regarding qualifications and ethics);
*   **Operational Officers** (Pursuant to Bylaws Article V);
*   **Volunteers** with access to sensitive data, financial assets, or direct interaction with minors;
*   **Independent Contractors** performing duties equivalent to the roles above.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to general Community Participants who do not hold a position of trust or authority.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Green Factors** | The three criteria defined by the EEOC to assess criminal history: (1) The nature and gravity of the offense; (2) The time that has passed since the offense/completion of sentence; and (3) The nature of the job held or sought. |
| **Individualized Assessment** | A process that provides the candidate an opportunity to demonstrate that the exclusion based on criminal history should not apply to them due to specific circumstances or rehabilitation. |
| **Targeted Screen** | A screening process that isolates specific criminal offenses that have a demonstrable relationship to the position in question. |
| **Adverse Action** | A denial of employment or volunteer status based, in whole or in part, on information contained in a consumer report (background check). |
| **Youth-Serving Role** | Any position involving unsupervised access to minors or decision-making authority regarding youth programming. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Timing of Assessment
To minimize unconscious bias and comply with best practices:
1.  Criminal history checks must only be initiated **after** a conditional offer of employment or volunteer appointment has been made.
2.  No decision regarding criminal history shall be made until the official report is received from the Organization's background check vendor.

#### 4.2 Initial Review and Filtering
Upon receipt of the background check report, the Human Resources Director (or designated Compliance Officer) must review the results.
1.  **Clear Report:** If the report contains no criminal history, the candidate is cleared for onboarding.
2.  **Arrest Records:** Records of arrest *without* conviction must not be used as the sole basis for denying employment. The Organization must investigate whether the conduct underlying the arrest actually occurred and if it is relevant to the role.
3.  **Conviction Records:** If a conviction is present, the reviewer must proceed to Section 4.3.

#### 4.3 The "Green Factors" Relevance Test
The reviewer must apply the EEOC "Green Factors" to determine if the conviction is relevant to the specific position.

**4.3.1 Nature and Gravity of the Offense**
*   Was the offense violent?
*   Did it involve fraud, theft, or breach of trust?
*   Did it involve a minor or vulnerable population?

**4.3.2 Time Elapsed**
*   How much time has passed since the offense or the completion of the sentence?
*   *Standard:* Older offenses carry less weight than recent ones, unless the offense is a permanent disqualifier (see 4.4).

**4.3.3 Nature of the Job**
*   **Fiduciary Roles (Treasurer, CFO):** Financial crimes (embezzlement, fraud) are highly relevant.
*   **Youth Roles:** Crimes against persons, sexual offenses, and drug distribution are highly relevant.
*   **IT/Security Roles (CISO):** Cybercrimes, identity theft, and hacking offenses are highly relevant.
*   **General Roles:** A driving offense (e.g., DUI) may not be relevant for a remote developer but is critical for a volunteer driver.

#### 4.4 Automatic Disqualifiers (High Risk)
Due to the Organization's work with youth and fiduciary responsibilities, the following offenses generally result in disqualification, subject to the Individualized Assessment in 4.5:
1.  **Youth Protection:** Any conviction for sexual abuse, child pornography, or violent crimes against children is an automatic, permanent disqualifier for any role.
2.  **Governance/Finance:** Any conviction for embezzlement, money laundering, or felony fraud within the last 10 years is a disqualifier for Board Directors, the Treasurer, and the CFO.

#### 4.5 Individualized Assessment Protocol
If a conviction is deemed "relevant" under Section 4.3, the Organization must conduct an Individualized Assessment before making a final decision.

1.  **Notification:** The HR Director must contact the candidate (prior to issuing a Pre-Adverse Action notice) to inform them that a relevant record has been found.
2.  **Evidence Collection:** The candidate is invited to submit evidence of rehabilitation or context, including:
    *   Errors in the criminal history report.
    *   Facts surrounding the offense (e.g., age at time of conviction).
    *   Evidence of rehabilitation efforts (education, training).
    *   Employment history before and after the offense.
    *   Character references.
3.  **Review Meeting:** The Adjudication Committee (comprised of the HR Director and one Board Member or Executive) reviews the submitted evidence.
4.  **Determination:** The Committee decides if the candidate's specific history poses an unreasonable risk to the Organization.

#### 4.6 Adjudication and Documentation
1.  **Approval:** If the assessment determines the candidate is suitable, the HR Director documents the rationale (referencing the Green Factors) in the *Background Check Adjudication Matrix* and clears the candidate.
2.  **Denial:** If the assessment determines the candidate is unsuitable, the HR Director must proceed to Section 4.7.

#### 4.7 Adverse Action Compliance (FCRA)
If the decision is made to rescind the offer based on the background check:

1.  **Pre-Adverse Action Notice:** Send a written notice to the candidate containing:
    *   A statement that a decision is pending based on the report.
    *   A copy of the background check report.
    *   A copy of "A Summary of Your Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act."
2.  **Waiting Period:** The Organization must wait a minimum of **five (5) business days** to allow the candidate to dispute the accuracy of the report with the vendor.
3.  **Final Adverse Action Notice:** If the candidate does not successfully dispute the report within the waiting period, send a final written notice containing:
    *   Statement that the adverse action has been taken.
    *   Name/contact info of the background check vendor.
    *   Statement that the vendor did not make the decision and cannot explain it.
    *   Notice of the candidate's right to a free copy of the report within 60 days.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Makes selection decisions based on merit; does *not* review detailed criminal history reports. Receives only "Cleared" or "Not Cleared" status. |
| **HR Director / Compliance Officer** | conducting the initial review, applying Green Factors, facilitating Individualized Assessments, and ensuring FCRA compliance. |
| **Adjudication Committee** | Reviews complex cases or appeals during the Individualized Assessment phase to ensure consistency and fairness. |
| **Candidate** | Responsible for providing accurate information and, if necessary, evidence of rehabilitation or context regarding criminal history. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in legal liability for the Organization under the FCRA and EEOC regulations.
*   **Internal:** Staff or volunteers found violating this procedure (e.g., sharing confidential criminal history data) are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **External:** Failure to adhere to youth protection disqualifiers (Section 4.4) constitutes a violation of the Organization's *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* and may result in immediate removal and reporting to authorities.
