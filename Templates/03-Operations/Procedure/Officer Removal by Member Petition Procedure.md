### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a transparent, fair, and orderly process for Voting Members to exercise their right to remove an Operational Officer as granted by **Article V, Section 5.3** of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc. This document ensures that such significant actions are conducted with due process, integrity, and strict adherence to the high voting threshold mandated by the Organization's governing documents.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All Voting Members (Supporting and Honorary) in good standing and all Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director/CEO, CISO, VP of Education).
*   **Exclusions:** This procedure does **not** apply to the removal of members of the Board of Directors (Governance), which is covered under Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6.
*   **Dual Roles:** If an individual serves as both a Director and an Operational Officer, this procedure applies *only* to their removal from the Operational Officer role.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Operational Officer** | An individual appointed by the Board to manage day-to-day execution of the mission (e.g., CEO, CFO, CISO), as defined in Bylaws Art. V. |
| **Petition** | A formal written request submitted to the Board of Directors initiating the removal process. |
| **Absolute Supermajority** | A vote requiring approval by at least two-thirds (2/3) of the **total** number of Voting Members listed in the registry, not merely 2/3 of those participating in the vote. |
| **Good Standing** | A status indicating a member has paid all required dues and is not currently suspended due to disciplinary action. |
| **Record Date** | The specific date established to determine which members are eligible to sign the petition and vote. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. Petition Initiation and Submission
To initiate the removal of an Operational Officer, Voting Members must submit a formal petition.
1.  **Drafting:** The petition must include:
    *   The full name and title of the Operational Officer subject to the petition.
    *   A "Statement of Cause" (not exceeding 1,000 words) detailing the reasons for the requested removal.
    *   The names, email addresses, and digital signatures of the Petition Sponsors.
2.  **Sponsorship Threshold:** To prevent frivolous disruptions, the petition must be sponsored (signed) by at least **5%** of the total Voting Membership or **five (5)** Voting Members, whichever is greater.
3.  **Submission:** The Lead Petitioner must submit the document via email to the Board Secretary (or the President, if the Secretary is the subject of the petition).

#### 4.2. Validation of Petition
Upon receipt, the Board Secretary (or designee) has **five (5) business days** to validate the petition.
1.  **Verification:** The Secretary validates that all signatories are Voting Members in Good Standing as of the submission date.
2.  **Notification:**
    *   **Valid:** If the threshold is met, the Secretary notifies the Board, the Lead Petitioner, and the Officer in question within 24 hours.
    *   **Invalid:** If the threshold is not met, the petition is returned to the Lead Petitioner with a specific explanation of the deficiency.

#### 4.3. Due Process and Rebuttal
The Organization adheres to principles of fairness. The Officer subject to removal is afforded an opportunity to respond.
1.  **Rebuttal Statement:** The Officer may submit a written "Statement of Defense" (not exceeding 1,000 words) to the Board within **five (5) business days** of receiving the validation notice.
2.  **Distribution:** The Board must distribute the Petition's "Statement of Cause" and the Officer's "Statement of Defense" (if provided) to the entire Voting Membership simultaneously.

#### 4.4. Scheduling the Vote
1.  **Timeline:** The vote must commence no later than **twenty (20) days** after the petition is validated.
2.  **Method:** In accordance with the Organization's remote-first nature and Bylaws Art. II §2.3 (No Proxy Voting), the vote shall be conducted via the Organization's designated secure electronic voting system.
3.  **Voting Window:** The electronic ballot shall remain open for a period of **seven (7) days** to maximize participation.

#### 4.5. The Vote and Tabulation
1.  **Ballot Question:** The ballot shall clearly state: *"Shall [Officer Name] be removed from the office of [Title]?"* with options for "Yes" and "No."
2.  **Threshold Calculation (Bylaws Art. V §5.3):**
    *   The denominator for the calculation is the **Total Number of Voting Members** on the Organization's registry as of the Record Date.
    *   The numerator is the number of "Yes" votes cast.
    *   **Abstentions/Non-Votes:** Because the Bylaws require an affirmative vote of 2/3 of *all* Voting Members, a failure to vote has the same mathematical effect as a "No" vote.
3.  **Example:** If there are 100 Voting Members:
    *   Removal requires 67 "Yes" votes.
    *   If only 60 members vote, and all 60 vote "Yes," the motion **fails** (60 < 67).

#### 4.6. Certification and Execution
1.  **Certification:** Upon close of the voting window, the Secretary and one other Director shall certify the results.
2.  **Communication:** Results are communicated to the Membership and the Officer within 24 hours.
3.  **Effect of Removal:**
    *   **Pass:** If the 2/3 threshold is met, the Officer is immediately removed from their operational role. Access to systems and data is revoked per the *Offboarding/Account Deprovisioning Checklist*.
    *   **Fail:** If the threshold is not met, the Officer remains in their position.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Lead Petitioner** | Drafts the petition, gathers initial signatures, and serves as the primary point of contact for the Board. |
| **Board Secretary** | Validates member standing, manages the electronic voting process, and certifies results. |
| **Board of Directors** | Oversees the process to ensure neutrality and adherence to Bylaws; appoints interim leadership if removal is successful. |
| **Voting Members** | Review the statements of Cause and Defense; cast votes within the designated window. |
| **Subject Officer** | Continues to perform duties during the petition process unless placed on administrative leave by the Board for immediate risk mitigation; provides Rebuttal Statement. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Retaliation Prohibited:** Retaliation against members who sign a petition or vote for removal is strictly prohibited and constitutes a violation of the *Whistleblower Protection Policy*.
*   **Interference:** Any attempt to manipulate the voting system, coerce votes, or obstruct the distribution of the Rebuttal Statement will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of membership or legal action.
*   **Record Keeping:** All petitions, statements, and certified voting records must be retained for a minimum of seven (7) years in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.
