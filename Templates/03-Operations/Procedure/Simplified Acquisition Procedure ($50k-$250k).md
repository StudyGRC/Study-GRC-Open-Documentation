### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory steps for procuring goods and services that fall within the **Simplified Acquisition Threshold (SAT)**. This procedure ensures Study GRC Inc. complies with **2 CFR 200.320(b)** by promoting free and open competition, preventing waste and fraud, and ensuring the Organization obtains the best value for mid-sized expenditures without the administrative burden of formal sealed bidding.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all purchases of supplies, services, or property where the aggregate dollar amount exceeds the Organization’s Micro-Purchase Threshold ($50,000) but does not exceed the Federal Simplified Acquisition Threshold ($250,000).

*   **Applies to:** All employees, Board members, and contractors initiating purchases using Organization funds (including federal grant funds).
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Purchases under $50,000 (See *FIN-PRC-024 Micro-Purchase Procedure*).
    *   Purchases over $250,000 (See *FIN-PRC-026 Sealed Bid Competitive Procurement*).
    *   Sole Source procurements (Must follow *FIN-PRC-028 Noncompetitive Justification*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Simplified Acquisition Threshold (SAT)** | The dollar amount below which a non-Federal entity may purchase property or services using small purchase methods. Currently set at **$250,000** under 2 CFR 200. |
| **Quotation** | A written statement of price and terms submitted by a vendor. Unlike a formal "Bid," a quotation is not a binding offer until accepted by the issuance of a Purchase Order or contract. |
| **Responsive Vendor** | A vendor who has submitted a quotation that fully conforms to the requirements of the solicitation. |
| **Responsible Vendor** | A vendor who has the capability, integrity, and reliability to perform the contract. |
| **SAM.gov** | The System for Award Management; the official U.S. government system used to verify if a vendor is suspended or debarred from receiving federal funds. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Determination of Need and Budget Verification
Before initiating any solicitation, the Requestor must:
1.  Define the clear business need and scope of work (SOW) or specifications.
2.  Verify that funds are available in the approved budget.
3.  Confirm the total estimated cost falls between **$50,000.01 and $250,000.00**.
    *   *Note:* Breaking down a large purchase into smaller amounts to avoid the $250k threshold (splitting) is strictly prohibited.

#### 4.2 Preparation of Solicitation
The Requestor must prepare a written solicitation (Request for Quotation - RFQ) to ensure all vendors are quoting on the same requirements. The RFQ must include:
1.  Description of goods/services.
2.  Required delivery dates or performance period.
3.  Evaluation criteria (e.g., "Lowest Price Technically Acceptable" or "Best Value").
4.  Any required federal provisions (e.g., Davis-Bacon Act for construction).

#### 4.3 Solicitation of Quotations (The "Rule of Three")
Pursuant to 2 CFR 200.320(b), the Organization must obtain price or rate quotations from an **adequate number of qualified sources**.
1.  The Requestor must solicit quotes from a minimum of **three (3)** qualified vendors.
2.  Solicitations may be sent via email or obtained from vendor websites (if the website provides specific pricing for the specific scope).
3.  **MWBE Inclusion:** The Requestor must make a good faith effort to include Minority and Women-owned Business Enterprises (MWBE) in the solicitation list, per *FIN-POL-032*.

#### 4.4 Evaluation of Quotations
1.  **Responsiveness Check:** Ensure the quote meets all technical specifications defined in Step 4.2.
2.  **Price Analysis:** Compare the prices received.
3.  **Selection:**
    *   If using **Lowest Price:** Select the lowest priced responsive and responsible vendor.
    *   If using **Best Value:** Select the vendor offering the best trade-off between price and non-price factors (e.g., past performance, technical quality). The rationale for selecting a vendor other than the lowest price must be documented in writing.

#### 4.5 Vendor Responsibility Check (SAM.gov)
Before awarding the contract, the Compliance Officer or CFO must:
1.  Search the vendor’s legal name on **SAM.gov**.
2.  Verify the vendor is **not** listed with an active "Exclusion" (Suspension or Debarment).
3.  Print/Save the search result (timestamped) to the procurement file.
    *   *Critical:* No contract may be awarded to a party listed on the government-wide exclusions in the System for Award Management (SAM).

#### 4.6 Approval and Award
1.  **Conflict of Interest:** The Requestor and Approver must certify no Conflict of Interest exists regarding the selected vendor, pursuant to *GOV-POL-005*.
2.  **Authorization:**
    *   Purchases up to $100,000: Require approval by the **CFO**.
    *   Purchases $100,001 - $250,000: Require approval by the **Executive Director**.
3.  **Contract Issuance:** Once approved, a Purchase Order or Contract is issued. This document must include the *FIN-CHK-035 Required Contract Provisions Checklist* (Appendix II to Part 200).

#### 4.7 Documentation (The Procurement File)
The Requestor must compile a Procurement File containing:
1.  The original solicitation/scope.
2.  Copies of **all** quotes received (winning and losing).
3.  If fewer than three quotes were obtained, a written justification detailing the efforts made to locate sources.
4.  The SAM.gov verification printout.
5.  The final selection memo/justification.
6.  The signed contract/Purchase Order.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requestor** | Defines scope, solicits minimum of 3 quotes, performs initial evaluation, and compiles the procurement file. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Verifies budget availability, reviews procurement file for compliance, and approves awards up to $100k. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Provides final approval for awards between $100k and $250k; ensures alignment with strategic goals. |
| **Compliance Officer** | Performs independent SAM.gov verification and reviews contract terms for federal compliance (Appendix II). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure constitutes a violation of federal grant regulations and Organization policy.
*   **Internal:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **External:** Non-compliance may result in **disallowed costs** (the Organization must repay the grant funds), loss of federal funding, or legal action under the False Claims Act.
