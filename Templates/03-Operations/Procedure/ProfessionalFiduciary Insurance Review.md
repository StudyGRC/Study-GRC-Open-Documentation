### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a systematic framework for the annual review, evaluation, and renewal of Study GRC Inc.’s professional and fiduciary insurance portfolio. This procedure ensures the Organization maintains adequate financial protection against operational liabilities, regulatory penalties, and litigation risks.

Furthermore, this procedure operationalizes **Article VII (Indemnification)** of the Bylaws, ensuring that the Organization has the financial capacity to indemnify Directors, Officers, and volunteers through Directors & Officers (D&O) liability insurance, rather than relying solely on the Organization's net assets.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all commercial insurance lines held by the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   Directors & Officers (D&O) Liability.
*   Employment Practices Liability (EPLI).
*   Professional Liability (Errors & Omissions / E&O).
*   Cyber Liability and Data Breach Response.
*   Commercial General Liability (CGL) including Abuse & Molestation coverage.
*   Fidelity Bonds (Crime Insurance) covering employee dishonesty.

This procedure involves the Board of Directors, the Finance Committee, the Executive Director, and external Insurance Brokers.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **D&O Insurance** | Directors and Officers Liability Insurance; protects individuals from personal losses if they are sued as a result of serving as a director or an officer of the Organization. |
| **EPLI** | Employment Practices Liability Insurance; covers wrongful acts arising from the employment process (e.g., wrongful termination, discrimination). |
| **Fiduciary Liability** | Insurance protecting the Organization against claims of mismanagement of employee benefit plans or organizational funds. |
| **Tail Coverage** | An endorsement extending the reporting period for claims made after a policy has expired (crucial for "Claims-Made" policies). |
| **2 CFR 200** | Uniform Guidance for Federal Awards; specifically regarding requirements for bonding and insurance to protect federal interest. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Annual Review Timeline
The insurance review process must commence no later than **90 days prior** to the expiration of current policies.

1.  **T-Minus 90 Days:** Executive Director initiates data gathering for the renewal application.
2.  **T-Minus 60 Days:** Submission of applications to the Insurance Broker for market quoting.
3.  **T-Minus 30 Days:** Finance Committee reviews quotes, coverage changes, and premium costs.
4.  **T-Minus 15 Days:** Board of Directors approves the insurance package.
5.  **Renewal Date:** Policies are bound and certificates of insurance (COI) are filed.

#### 4.2 Operational Risk Inventory
Prior to soliciting quotes, the Executive Director must update the **Operational Risk Inventory** to reflect changes in the Organization’s profile. This update must address:
1.  **Asset Growth:** Significant increases in net assets or grant funding (triggering higher D&O limits).
2.  **Program Expansion:** New youth programs (triggering review of Abuse & Molestation limits).
3.  **Data Volume:** Increases in PII/PHI storage (triggering review of Cyber Liability limits).
4.  **Geographic Footprint:** New remote employees in states with specific Workers' Compensation requirements.
5.  **Contractual Obligations:** Review of active grant agreements and vendor contracts for specific insurance minimums.

#### 4.3 Coverage Gap Analysis
The Treasurer and Executive Director must perform a gap analysis against the following minimum standards:

*   **Directors & Officers (D&O):** Must include "Side A" (individual protection), "Side B" (corporate reimbursement), and "Side C" (entity coverage). Limits must be benchmarked against non-profits of similar size and revenue.
*   **Cyber Liability:** Must cover first-party costs (forensics, notification, credit monitoring) and third-party liability (lawsuits).
*   **Fidelity/Crime:** Must meet or exceed the minimum bonding requirements mandated by 2 CFR 200 for entities handling federal funds.
*   **Abuse & Molestation:** Must be a dedicated limit (not a sub-limit of General Liability) due to the Organization's involvement with K-12 populations.

#### 4.4 Broker Engagement and Quoting
1.  **Broker Selection:** The Organization shall utilize a licensed insurance broker specializing in the non-profit and technology sectors.
2.  **Market Check:** Every three (3) years, the Organization must competitively bid the brokerage service or require the current broker to market the program to at least three (3) distinct carriers to ensure competitive pricing.
3.  **Application Accuracy:** The Executive Director must ensure all representations made in insurance applications (especially regarding financial health and cybersecurity controls) are accurate. Material misrepresentation can void coverage.

#### 4.5 Board Review and Approval
The Treasurer shall present the renewal proposal to the Board. The presentation must include:
1.  **Premium Comparison:** Year-over-year cost analysis.
2.  **Limit Adequacy:** Confirmation that limits meet the Organization's risk tolerance.
3.  **Exclusion Review:** Highlighting any new exclusions (e.g., specific cyber exclusions or communicable disease exclusions).
4.  **Retroactive Date Check:** Verification that "Prior Acts" dates remain intact to ensure continuous coverage for past actions.

#### 4.6 Binding and Documentation
1.  Upon Board approval, the Executive Director is authorized to sign binding documents and remit premium payments.
2.  **Policy Archival:** Full policy documents (not just declaration pages) must be stored in the Organization’s secure record repository indefinitely (see *Document Retention Policy*).
3.  **COI Distribution:** Updated Certificates of Insurance must be distributed to grantors, landlords, or partners as required by contract.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Completes renewal applications, maintains the Operational Risk Inventory, and acts as primary liaison with the Broker. |
| **Treasurer** | Leads the financial analysis of premiums, reviews coverage adequacy, and presents recommendations to the Board. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves the final insurance portfolio; ensures D&O coverage is sufficient to protect the Board. |
| **Finance Committee** | Conducts the detailed review of quotes and exclusions prior to full Board presentation. |
| **Insurance Broker** | Advises on market trends, solicits quotes from carriers, and advocates for the Organization during claims. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain adequate insurance coverage is a breach of the Board’s fiduciary duty of care and places the Organization’s assets and the personal assets of Directors at risk.

*   **Lapse in Coverage:** Any lapse in D&O or General Liability coverage must be reported to the Board immediately.
*   **Claims Reporting:** All potential claims or "circumstances likely to give rise to a claim" must be reported to the carrier within the policy's specified reporting window. Failure to report timely may result in denial of coverage.
