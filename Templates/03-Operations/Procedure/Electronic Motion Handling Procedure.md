### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant method for the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. to propose, debate, and vote on motions using electronic means outside of scheduled synchronous meetings. This procedure ensures that "Action Without Meeting" adheres to the requirements of **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.9** and the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC), maintaining a clear audit trail for governance decisions.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Directors serving on the Board of Study GRC Inc.
*   **In Scope:** Motions regarding operational approvals, policy adoptions, and financial authorizations conducted via email, dedicated communication platforms (e.g., Slack/Teams), or secure board management software.
*   **Out of Scope:**
    *   Voting by the General Membership (covered under Bylaws Article II).
    *   "Fundamental Actions" (Mergers, Dissolution, Amending Certificate of Formation) which require specific supermajority thresholds and are recommended for synchronous meetings.
    *   Election of Officers (conducted annually per Bylaws Article IV).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Action Without Meeting** | A legal provision allowing the Board to take action without physically or virtually gathering, provided specific consent thresholds are met (Bylaws § 3.9). |
| **Asynchronous Voting** | Voting that occurs over a defined period where participants do not need to be present simultaneously. |
| **Unanimous Written Consent** | The requirement that **all** Directors entitled to vote must affirmatively approve an action taken without a meeting for it to be valid. |
| **The Chair** | The President or the presiding officer managing the motion. |
| **Quorum** | For the purposes of electronic voting under Bylaws § 3.9, the concept of a "quorum" is superseded by the requirement for unanimous participation/consent. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Authorized Channels
To ensure an immutable audit trail, electronic motions must only be conducted through the following approved channels:
1.  **Primary:** The Organization’s official Board Management Software (if applicable).
2.  **Secondary:** The Organization’s dedicated, private Board channel on the corporate messaging platform (e.g., Slack channel `#board-official-business`).
3.  **Tertiary:** Official organizational email addresses. Personal email addresses should not be used for voting.

#### 4.2 Initiating a Motion
Any Director (the "Mover") may introduce a motion electronically.
1.  The Mover must post the motion in the Authorized Channel with the header: **"MOTION: [Subject]"**.
2.  The motion text must be clear, concise, and affirmative (e.g., *"I move to approve the Q3 Budget as attached..."*).
3.  Any relevant supporting documentation (PDFs, links) must be attached or linked directly in the motion post.

#### 4.3 Seconding the Motion
1.  Another Director must "Second" the motion to proceed to discussion.
2.  The Second must be indicated by a clear reply (e.g., *"I second the motion"*).
3.  If no Second is received within 24 hours, the motion dies and is removed from consideration.

#### 4.4 Discussion Period
1.  Once seconded, the Chair (President) opens the floor for electronic discussion.
2.  The Chair sets a **Discussion Window** (standard: 24 to 48 hours), unless the matter is designated "Urgent" (minimum 4 hours).
3.  Directors must post questions, debate, or amendments as "Replies" or "Threads" to the original motion post to keep the conversation consolidated.
4.  If a substantive amendment is proposed and accepted by the Mover, the revised motion becomes the "Main Motion."

#### 4.5 Call to Vote
1.  Upon conclusion of the Discussion Window, or when discussion ceases, the Chair will formally "Call the Question."
2.  The Chair posts: **"VOTING OPEN: [Restatement of Motion]. Please cast your vote by [Time/Date]."**
3.  The Voting Window shall remain open for a minimum of 24 hours unless all Directors have cast their votes prior to that time.

#### 4.6 Casting Votes
1.  Directors must cast their vote using one of the following clear, unambiguous methods:
    *   **Affirmative:** "Yes," "Aye," or "Approve."
    *   **Negative:** "No," "Nay," or "Reject."
    *   **Abstention:** "Abstain."
2.  **Prohibition on Implicit Voting:** "Silence" or failure to respond does **not** constitute a vote.
3.  **Prohibition on Proxy:** Per Bylaws Section 2.3, Directors must vote personally; proxy voting is prohibited.

#### 4.7 Threshold for Approval (Crucial)
Pursuant to **Bylaws Section 3.9 (Action Without Meeting)**:
1.  **Unanimous Consent:** For the motion to pass electronically without a meeting, **100% of Directors entitled to vote** must vote in the affirmative.
2.  **Failure to Achieve Unanimity:** If a single Director votes "Nay," "Abstains," or fails to vote within the window, the motion **fails** as an electronic action.
3.  **Remedy:** If the motion fails due to lack of unanimity (but not lack of majority support), the Chair must place the item on the agenda for the next synchronous Board Meeting, where standard majority quorum rules (Bylaws § 3.5) will apply.

#### 4.8 Recording and Ratification
1.  **Tally:** The Secretary must tally the votes upon closure of the Voting Window.
2.  **Announcement:** The Secretary or Chair posts the result (e.g., *"Motion Passes via Unanimous Consent"* or *"Motion Fails - Moved to Next Agenda"*).
3.  **Minutes:** The Secretary must record the full text of the motion, the method of voting, and the result in the minutes of the **next** scheduled Board meeting. The electronic log (email thread or chat export) must be saved to the corporate records repository.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **President (Chair)** | Moderates the electronic channel, calls the question, and enforces the Discussion/Voting windows. |
| **Secretary** | Verifies the eligibility of voters, tallies results, ensures the Unanimous Consent threshold is met, and archives the record. |
| **Directors** | Responsible for monitoring the Authorized Channel, reviewing attached materials, and casting votes within the designated window. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly adhere to this procedure—specifically the requirement for **Unanimous Written Consent** per Bylaws § 3.9—renders the electronic vote **void and legally non-binding**.

Any Director found to be manipulating electronic records or voting on behalf of another Director (spoofing) will be subject to immediate removal for cause under Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6.
