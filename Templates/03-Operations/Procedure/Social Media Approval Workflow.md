### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, quality-controlled workflow for the creation, review, and publication of content on Study GRC Inc.'s official social media channels. This workflow is designed to:
1.  **Mitigate Risk:** Prevent the publication of legally compromising, factually incorrect, or reputation-damaging material.
2.  **Ensure Compliance:** Verify adherence to 501(c)(3) restrictions (e.g., no political campaigning), COPPA regulations regarding youth privacy, and Intellectual Property laws.
3.  **Maintain Brand Consistency:** Ensure all communications align with the Organization’s mission to foster a community-driven GRC ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Employees, Board Members, and Volunteers** ("Contributors") who create, schedule, or publish content on behalf of Study GRC Inc.

**In Scope:**
*   All official social media platforms (LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Discord Announcements, YouTube, Instagram, etc.).
*   All content formats (text, images, video, audio, infographics).
*   Replies to public comments that constitute official organizational statements.

**Out of Scope:**
*   Personal social media accounts of Contributors, provided they do not purport to speak on behalf of the Organization (refer to *COM-POL-004 Social Media Policy*).
*   Real-time community moderation within the Discord server (refer to *OPS-GUD-002 Community Moderation Guidelines*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Content Creator** | The individual drafting the post, image, or video. |
| **Approver** | The designated authority (usually the Comms Lead or Executive Director) responsible for green-lighting content. |
| **Sensitive Content** | Content involving minors, crisis communications, legal settlements, major policy changes, or financial solicitations. |
| **Evergreen Content** | Pre-approved, timeless content that can be reposted without re-approval (e.g., "Join our Discord" posts). |
| **COPPA** | Children's Online Privacy Protection Act; federal law regulating data collection/images of children under 13. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Content Creation and Self-Review
The Content Creator must draft content using the Organization's approved scheduling tool or project management system. Before submission, the Creator must perform a **Self-Review**:

1.  **Brand Voice Check:** Is the tone professional, inclusive, and aligned with the "Director of Kindness and Generosity" (DKG) ethos?
2.  **Visual Compliance:** Do images use the correct hex codes and logo usage per the *Brand Identity Guide*?
3.  **Link Verification:** Do all hyperlinks function and lead to secure (HTTPS), relevant destinations?

#### 4.2 Risk Assessment (Mandatory Check)
The Content Creator must verify the content against the following Risk Matrix. If any "Yes" answers occur, the content is flagged as **High Risk**.

*   **Youth Protection:** Does the image/video contain the face of a minor?
    *   *Requirement:* A signed *Parental Media Release Form* must be on file. If not, the face must be blurred or the content rejected.
*   **Intellectual Property:** Does the post use third-party music, art, or text?
    *   *Requirement:* Verify license or Fair Use. Per **Bylaws Article IX (Intellectual Property)**, ensure we are not infringing on others' rights while maintaining our open-source commitment.
*   **Political/Lobbying:** Does the post mention legislation or political candidates?
    *   *Requirement:* Must be reviewed by the Executive Director for 501(c)(3) compliance.
*   **Financial Ask:** Is this a solicitation for donations?
    *   *Requirement:* Must align with current fundraising campaigns approved by the Treasurer.

#### 4.3 Submission and Approval Tiers
Content is submitted to the approval queue. The workflow path depends on the content tier:

**Tier 1: Standard Content (Routine)**
*   *Examples:* Educational tips, event reminders, holiday greetings, Evergreen Content.
*   *Approval:* Requires **one (1)** approval from the Communications Lead or DKG.
*   *SLA:* 24 hours.

**Tier 2: High-Risk / Strategic Content**
*   *Examples:* Press releases, crisis response, partnership announcements, posts involving minors, financial reports.
*   *Approval:* Requires **two (2)** approvals:
    1.  Communications Lead
    2.  Executive Director (CEO) or Board President.
*   *SLA:* 48 hours (unless emergency).

#### 4.4 Review Process
The Approver shall:
1.  Review the draft for grammar, accuracy, and tone.
2.  Verify the Risk Assessment (Section 4.2) was completed accurately.
3.  **Action:**
    *   **Approve:** Content moves to "Scheduled" status.
    *   **Request Changes:** Feedback provided to Creator; workflow resets to Section 4.1.
    *   **Reject:** Content is archived with a reason for rejection (e.g., "Violates Non-Partisanship Policy").

#### 4.5 Scheduling and Publication
1.  Once approved, the content is locked in the scheduling tool.
2.  No edits may be made to approved content without restarting the approval process, with the exception of minor typo corrections that do not alter meaning.
3.  The Social Media Manager ensures the post goes live at the optimal time for the target audience.

#### 4.6 Post-Publication Monitoring
1.  **Sentiment Analysis:** The Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or designee monitors immediate reactions for the first hour post-publication.
2.  **Takedown Protocol:** If a post generates unexpected negative backlash or is found to contain an error:
    *   **Immediate Action:** The Executive Director or CISO has the authority to delete the post immediately to protect the Organization.
    *   **Review:** A "Post-Mortem" review is conducted within 24 hours to determine the cause and update this procedure if necessary.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Content Creator** | Drafts content, performs initial risk self-assessment, and secures necessary media releases. |
| **Communications Lead** | Primary Approver for Tier 1 content; ensures brand style adherence. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Reviews content for community culture fit; monitors post-publication sentiment. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final Approver for Tier 2 (High Risk) content; authorizes emergency takedowns. |
| **CISO** | Reviews content related to cybersecurity news to ensure technical accuracy; authorizes takedowns if security risks arise (e.g., social engineering links). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this workflow, particularly regarding the unauthorized posting of unapproved content or content violating Youth Protection standards, may result in disciplinary action.
*   **Volunteers:** Immediate revocation of social media access and potential removal from the volunteer program.
*   **Employees:** Disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Legal:** Posting copyrighted material without license or minors without consent may result in personal liability and legal action against the Organization.
