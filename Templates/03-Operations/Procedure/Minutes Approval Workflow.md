### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant process for the drafting, review, approval, and archiving of meeting minutes for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This workflow ensures that the Organization maintains contemporaneous documentation of its governing body's actions, satisfying Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) requirements and IRS Form 990 governance standards. Accurate minutes serve as the definitive legal record of corporate decisions, protecting the Organization's liability shield and ensuring historical continuity.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all formal meetings where binding votes or governance decisions may occur, including:
*   **Board of Directors Meetings** (Regular and Special).
*   **Committee Meetings** for committees with Board-delegated authority (e.g., Finance, Audit).
*   **Annual or Special Meetings of the Membership**.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to the Advisory Board (which holds no voting power per Bylaws Article VI) or informal working group sessions where no binding votes are taken.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Draft Minutes** | The preliminary record of proceedings prepared by the Secretary, subject to correction and amendment. |
| **Approved Minutes** | The final, official legal record of the meeting, formally adopted by a vote of the body. |
| **Contemporaneous** | Documentation created at the time of the event or shortly thereafter (specifically, by the next scheduled meeting or within 60 days). |
| **Executive Session** | A portion of a meeting closed to non-voting members/staff to discuss confidential matters (legal, personnel, security). |
| **Circularization** | The process of distributing documents to all relevant parties for review. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Drafting and Preparation
**4.1.1 Timing**
The Secretary (or designated minute-taker) must prepare the Draft Minutes within **seven (7) calendar days** following the conclusion of the meeting.

**4.1.2 Content Requirements**
Per *Bylaws Article VIII (Records)* and *Robert’s Rules of Order*, minutes must record **what was done**, not strictly what was said. Draft minutes must include:
*   **Header:** Organization name, meeting type (Regular/Special), date, time, and location (or virtual platform).
*   **Attendance:** List of Directors present, Directors absent, and guests/staff present.
*   **Quorum:** Explicit statement confirming a quorum was present (per *Bylaws Section 3.5*).
*   **Actions:** Exact wording of all motions, the name of the mover (seconder optional), and the outcome of the vote (Pass/Fail/Tabled).
*   **Conflicts:** Notation of any Director recusing themselves due to a Conflict of Interest (per *Bylaws Section 9.1*).
*   **Adjournment:** Time of adjournment.

**4.1.3 Executive Session Handling**
If the Board enters Executive Session:
*   The general minutes must note the time the Board entered and exited Executive Session and the general topic (e.g., "Personnel Matter").
*   **Separate** Executive Session minutes must be kept confidential, stored securely with restricted access, and approved only by those who were present.

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Review and Circularization
**4.2.1 Initial Review**
The Secretary submits the Draft Minutes to the Presiding Officer (President/Chair) for a strictly editorial review (checking for factual errors, not changing the substance of debate) within **10 days** of the meeting.

**4.2.2 Distribution**
Once cleared by the Chair, the Secretary must circulate the Draft Minutes to all Directors (or Committee Members) via the Organization’s secure communication channel (e.g., Board Portal or secure email).
*   **Deadline:** Draft minutes must be distributed no later than **three (3) business days** prior to the *next* scheduled meeting to allow for review.
*   **Watermark:** Documents should be clearly marked "DRAFT."

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Approval
Minutes may be approved via one of two methods:

**Method A: Approval at Next Meeting (Standard)**
1.  **Agenda Item:** "Approval of Minutes" must be a standing item on the Consent Agenda or Regular Agenda.
2.  **Corrections:** The Chair asks, "Are there any corrections to the minutes?"
    *   *Minor Typos:* Can be corrected by unanimous consent without a vote.
    *   *Substantive Changes:* If a dispute arises regarding the accuracy of a motion or vote, the Board may vote to amend the draft.
3.  **Vote:** A motion to "Approve the minutes [as submitted / as corrected]" is made and voted upon.

**Method B: Electronic Approval (Expedited)**
Per *Bylaws Section 3.9 (Action Without Meeting)*:
1.  The Secretary circulates the Draft Minutes via the electronic voting system.
2.  Directors vote to approve.
3.  Unanimous written consent (or electronic equivalent) is required for this method.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Finalization and Archiving
**4.4.1 Signature**
Upon approval, the Secretary must:
*   Remove the "DRAFT" watermark.
*   Update the footer to read "Approved on [Date]."
*   Affix their electronic or physical signature to the document.

**4.4.2 Storage**
*   **Master File:** The signed PDF is uploaded to the Organization’s central secure repository (e.g., Google Workspace Shared Drive > Governance > Minutes > [Year]).
*   **Naming Convention:** Files must follow the standard: `YYYY-MM-DD_Board_Meeting_Minutes_APPROVED.pdf`.
*   **Backup:** The repository must be backed up in accordance with the *IT Backup Policy*.

**4.4.3 Transparency (Public Version)**
In accordance with *Bylaws Section 8.2 (Transparency)*, if the Board designates minutes for public release:
*   A "Public Redacted" version must be created removing any sensitive personal data, security credentials, or proprietary financial details not intended for public disclosure.
*   The Public Version is uploaded to the Transparency page of the website.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Corporate Secretary** | Primary responsibility for drafting, distributing, and archiving minutes. Ensures compliance with Bylaws. |
| **Board President (Chair)** | Reviews initial drafts for factual accuracy. Presides over the approval process during meetings. |
| **Directors** | Responsible for reviewing Draft Minutes prior to meetings and proposing corrections to ensure the record is accurate. |
| **Committee Chairs** | Responsible for appointing a minute-taker for their specific committee and following this workflow for committee records. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain accurate minutes is a violation of Texas law and puts the Organization’s tax-exempt status at risk.
*   **Legal Compliance:** Failure to approve minutes in a timely manner may result in the inability to defend corporate decisions in court or during an IRS audit.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Officers who consistently fail to produce or archive minutes may be subject to removal from office per *Bylaws Section 4.3*.
