### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a systematic process for monitoring Study GRC Inc.'s "Public Support Test" status throughout the fiscal year. As a 501(c)(3) organization, Study GRC Inc. must demonstrate that it receives a substantial part of its support from the general public or government units to maintain its status as a **Public Charity** rather than a **Private Foundation**.

Failure to meet the Public Support Test requirements (generally 33 1/3% public support) results in "tipping" into Private Foundation status, which imposes stricter operating rules, excise taxes, and administrative burdens. This procedure ensures proactive management of the support ratio to prevent inadvertent reclassification.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the Finance Department, the Fundraising/Development Team (including the Director of Kindness and Generosity), and the Board of Directors. It specifically governs the classification of revenue, the calculation of the support fraction, and the identification of "Disqualified Persons."

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Public Support Test** | The IRS calculation used on Form 990, Schedule A, to determine if an organization is publicly supported. Generally, public support must equal at least 33 1/3% of total support over a 5-year rolling period. |
| **Disqualified Person** | Individuals or entities in a position to exercise substantial influence over the organization (e.g., Directors, Officers), their family members, and "Substantial Contributors." |
| **Substantial Contributor** | Any person who contributed an aggregate amount of more than $5,000, if that amount is more than 2% of the total contributions and bequests received by the organization since its inception. |
| **The 2% Limit** | Under IRC § 170(b)(1)(A)(vi), contributions from any single individual, corporation, or trust are counted as "public support" only to the extent that the total amount does not exceed 2% of the organization's *total* support for the period. |
| **Unusual Grant** | A substantial contribution or bequest that is unexpected, attracted by the public nature of the organization, and would adversely affect the status of the organization if included in the calculation. These may be excluded from the test entirely. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Revenue Classification and Tagging
To ensure accurate calculation, all incoming funds must be correctly classified in the accounting system upon receipt.

1.  **Source Identification:** The CFO or designee must tag every deposit with one of the following Source Types:
    *   General Public (Individual < $5,000).
    *   Government Unit (Federal, State, Local grants).
    *   Public Charity (Donations from other 501(c)(3) entities).
    *   Private Foundation/Corporate Grant.
    *   Board Member/Officer (Related Party).
    *   Program Service Revenue (Tuition, ticket sales, etc.).
2.  **Donor Aggregation:** The accounting system must track cumulative giving by donor ID to identify when a donor crosses the "Substantial Contributor" threshold.

#### 4.2 Quarterly Support Ratio Calculation
The CFO shall perform a preliminary Public Support Test calculation within 15 days of the close of each fiscal quarter.

1.  **Determine Total Support (Denominator):**
    *   Sum all contributions, grants, membership fees, and gross investment income for the rolling 5-year period (current year-to-date + previous 4 tax years).
    *   *Exclude:* Capital gains/losses and unusual grants (see Section 4.4).
2.  **Calculate the 2% Threshold:**
    *   Multiply the **Total Support** figure by 0.02 (2%). This is the maximum amount of funding from any single private donor that can count toward the numerator.
3.  **Determine Public Support (Numerator):**
    *   Sum all contributions from Government Units and other Public Charities (these count 100%).
    *   Sum contributions from individuals/corporations/private foundations, **capped at the 2% Threshold calculated above**.
    *   *Note:* Contributions from Disqualified Persons are subject to specific limitations based on the applicable IRS test (509(a)(1) vs 509(a)(2)).
4.  **Calculate Percentage:**
    *   Divide **Public Support** (Numerator) by **Total Support** (Denominator).

#### 4.3 Monitoring and Reporting
1.  **Green Status (> 35%):** If the ratio is comfortably above 33 1/3%, the CFO reports the percentage in the quarterly financial packet to the Treasurer.
2.  **Yellow Status (33.34% - 35%):** If the ratio is close to the minimum:
    *   The CFO must notify the Executive Director and Treasurer immediately.
    *   The Fundraising team must prioritize "broad-based" fundraising (small dollar donations) over major gifts from existing major donors for the remainder of the quarter.
3.  **Red Status (< 33.33%):** If the ratio falls below the threshold:
    *   The Organization must rely on the "10% Facts and Circumstances Test" (a secondary safety net).
    *   The Board must convene to review the fundraising strategy.
    *   Acceptance of large checks from non-public sources (e.g., a single large private foundation grant) requires Board approval to ensure it does not permanently damage the ratio.

#### 4.4 Unusual Grant Determination
If the Organization receives a large, unexpected grant that would negatively impact the Public Support Test (by inflating the denominator without fully counting in the numerator due to the 2% limit):

1.  **Assessment:** The CFO and Treasurer must evaluate if the contribution meets the IRS criteria for an "Unusual Grant" (Treas. Reg. § 1.509(a)-3(c)(3)). Factors include:
    *   Was the grant unexpected?
    *   Is the amount large relative to normal contributions?
    *   Is the donor a person with no prior relationship to the organization?
2.  **Exclusion:** If deemed an Unusual Grant, the amount is excluded from **both** the numerator and the denominator of the calculation.
3.  **Documentation:** The CFO must prepare a memo justifying the classification to be kept with the tax records for that fiscal year.

#### 4.5 Annual Review and Form 990 Preparation
1.  **Draft Review:** Prior to filing IRS Form 990, the external tax preparer or auditor must review the Schedule A calculation.
2.  **Transparency:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, the final Schedule A data is part of the Form 990 which must be made available for public inspection.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Performs quarterly calculations; tags revenue sources; identifies substantial contributors; maintains the 5-year rolling data set. |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews quarterly reports; reports "Yellow" or "Red" status to the full Board; approves "Unusual Grant" classifications. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Adjusts fundraising strategies based on the support ratio (e.g., shifting focus to crowd-funding vs. major donors). |
| **Board of Directors** | Holds ultimate fiduciary responsibility for maintaining tax-exempt status; approves corrective action plans if the ratio falls below requirements. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly monitor the Public Support Test may result in the Organization being reclassified as a Private Foundation by the IRS. This would result in:
*   Liability for excise taxes on investment income.
*   Restrictions on self-dealing and taxable expenditures.
*   Loss of donor tax deductibility limits (30% vs 50/60%).

Any Operational Officer found to be falsifying donor classification data to manipulate the ratio will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination and legal action for fraud.
