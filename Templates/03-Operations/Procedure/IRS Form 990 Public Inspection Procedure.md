### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the specific protocols for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") to comply with Internal Revenue Code (IRC) §6104 regarding the public inspection and disclosure of its tax-exempt status and annual information returns. This procedure ensures the Organization maintains its commitment to transparency as mandated by **Article VIII, Section 8.2** of the Bylaws while protecting the privacy of its donors as permitted by law.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the Board of Directors, the Treasurer, the Chief Financial Officer (CFO), and any staff member or volunteer responsible for managing the Organization’s website or responding to public inquiries.

**Documents Covered:**
*   **IRS Form 990 series** (Annual Information Return) for the three most recent years.
*   **IRS Form 1023** (Application for Recognition of Exemption).
*   **IRS Determination Letter** (Granting 501(c)(3) status).

**Exclusions:**
*   **Schedule B (Form 990):** Names and addresses of contributors are strictly confidential and **must** be redacted prior to public disclosure.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Public Inspection** | The right of the general public to view and request copies of specific tax documents filed by a tax-exempt organization. |
| **Widely Available** | An IRS standard met by posting documents on the internet in a format that exactly reproduces the image of the original document (e.g., PDF) and allows for downloading and printing without a fee. |
| **Redaction** | The process of obscuring or removing sensitive information (specifically donor names on Schedule B) from a document before it is released to the public. |
| **Harassment Campaign** | A single coordinated effort to disrupt the operations of the Organization by flooding it with requests for copies of documents, as defined under Treasury Regulations §301.6104(d)-3. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Preparation and Redaction of Documents
Upon the filing of the Annual Form 990 with the IRS, the Treasurer or CFO must prepare a "Public Disclosure Copy" within five (5) business days.

1.  **Generate PDF:** Create a high-resolution PDF of the filed Form 990, including all schedules and attachments.
2.  **Mandatory Redaction:**
    *   Locate **Schedule B** (Schedule of Contributors).
    *   **Redact (Black out)** the names and addresses of all contributors listed.
    *   **Do not redact** the amounts of the contributions or the description of non-cash property (unless the description would reveal the donor's identity).
    *   **Do not redact** compensation information for Officers, Directors, or Key Employees in Part VII.
3.  **Verification:** The Secretary or a designated Compliance Officer must review the Public Disclosure Copy to ensure no donor names are visible and that the document is otherwise complete.

#### 4.2 The "Widely Available" Standard (Website Posting)
To satisfy the inspection requirement without maintaining a physical public office for walk-in requests (due to the Organization's remote-first nature), the Organization shall make documents "Widely Available."

1.  **Upload:** The Public Disclosure Copy must be uploaded to the Organization’s dedicated "Transparency" webpage (as required by **Bylaws Section 8.2**) within 30 days of filing with the IRS.
2.  **Format:** The file must be in a widely used format (PDF) that allows free downloading, viewing, and printing.
3.  **Permanence:**
    *   The Form 1023 and Determination Letter must remain on the site indefinitely.
    *   Form 990s must remain on the site for a rolling three-year period (the current filing plus the prior two years).

#### 4.3 Responding to Written or Email Requests
If the Organization receives a written or email request for a copy of the covered documents:

1.  **Check Availability:** If the documents are posted on the Organization's website in compliance with Section 4.2, the Organization is **not** required to provide physical copies.
2.  **Response Protocol:**
    *   Respond to the requester within seven (7) days.
    *   Provide the direct URL (web link) where the documents can be accessed.
    *   Inform the requester that the documents are available for immediate download at no cost.
3.  **Physical Copy Exception:** If the Organization chooses to provide a physical copy (at its discretion, or if the website is down):
    *   The Organization may charge a reasonable fee for copying (not to exceed IRS guidelines, currently $0.20 per page) and actual postage costs.
    *   Copies must be provided within 30 days of receiving the request and payment.

#### 4.4 Responding to In-Person Requests
As a remote-first entity, the Organization does not maintain a traditional walk-in office. However, if a request is made in person at a registered event or the Registered Office:

1.  **Immediate Inspection:** If a member of the public demands inspection at a location where the books are legally held, the Organization must allow inspection during regular business hours.
2.  **Alternative:** Staff should direct the individual to the "Transparency" webpage for immediate, free access.

#### 4.5 Handling Harassment Campaigns
If the Organization determines it is the subject of a harassment campaign (e.g., receiving 50+ requests from a single source or coordinated group within a short period to disrupt operations):

1.  **Suspension:** The Treasurer may suspend compliance with the requests.
2.  **Application:** The Organization must file an application with the IRS for a determination that the requests are part of a harassment campaign.
3.  **Notification:** The Organization generally does not need to respond to the requests while the IRS application is pending.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Ensures Form 990 is filed; prepares the Public Disclosure Copy; ensures Schedule B is redacted. |
| **Secretary** | Verifies redaction accuracy; ensures the "Transparency" webpage is updated; maintains the permanent file of Form 1023 and Determination Letter. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves the Form 990 prior to filing; oversees compliance with this procedure. |
| **Webmaster / IT** | Technical execution of uploading documents and ensuring 99.9% uptime for the Transparency page. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with IRC §6104 can result in significant financial penalties for both the Organization and the responsible individuals ($20 per day for each day the failure continues, up to a maximum of $10,000 per return).

**Internal Enforcement:**
Any Officer or Director who willfully refuses to allow public inspection or fails to post the required documents in accordance with this procedure may be subject to removal for cause under **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**.
