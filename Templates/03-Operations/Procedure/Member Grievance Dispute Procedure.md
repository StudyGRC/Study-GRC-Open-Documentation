### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a structured, fair, and transparent process for resolving disputes, complaints, and grievances involving members of Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures that all Voting Members and Community Participants have access to due process regarding allegations of Bylaw violations, ethical breaches, or conduct detrimental to the Organization, in alignment with the Organization's commitment to a healthy, inclusive community ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all classes of membership as defined in **Bylaws Article II** (Community Participants, Supporting Members, and Honorary Members).

**Inclusions:**
*   Disputes between Members.
*   Grievances filed by a Member against the Organization’s leadership or operational staff regarding governance matters.
*   Allegations of violations of the *Code of Ethics and Conduct* or *Community Guidelines*.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Employment Disputes:** Grievances related to paid employment are governed by the *Employee Handbook* and HR policies.
*   **Youth Protection:** Any grievance alleging abuse, neglect, or inappropriate interaction with a minor must **immediately** be routed through the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* and reported to the appropriate authorities pursuant to Texas Family Code Chapter 261.
*   **Whistleblower Reports:** Reports regarding financial fraud or legal violations are governed by the *Whistleblower Protection Policy*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Grievance** | A formal written complaint submitting an allegation of wrongdoing, policy violation, or unfair treatment. |
| **Complainant** | The member(s) filing the grievance. |
| **Respondent** | The individual(s) or entity against whom the grievance is filed. |
| **Grievance Committee** | An ad-hoc committee appointed by the Board to investigate and adjudicate a specific grievance. |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a member who has paid all required dues and is not currently under disciplinary suspension. |
| **Mediation** | A voluntary, informal process where a neutral third party assists disputants in reaching a mutually acceptable resolution. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Informal Resolution (Mediation)
Prior to filing a formal grievance, members are encouraged to resolve disputes amicably.

1.  **Direct Communication:** The Complainant should attempt to resolve the issue directly with the Respondent, provided safety and civility can be maintained.
2.  **DKG Intervention:** If direct communication fails, the Complainant may request informal mediation from the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)**.
3.  **Mediation Outcome:**
    *   If resolved, the DKG notes the resolution in the *Member Engagement Activity Tracking Log*.
    *   If unresolved, or if the DKG determines the matter is too severe for mediation, the Complainant may proceed to Phase II.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Formal Grievance Submission
1.  **Submission Timeline:** Grievances must be filed within **thirty (30) days** of the alleged incident or discovery of the violation.
2.  **Format:** The Complainant must complete the *Member Grievance Form* and submit it to the Secretary of the Board via the secure governance portal.
3.  **Required Information:**
    *   Name of Respondent.
    *   Specific Bylaw, Policy, or Ethical Standard alleged to have been violated.
    *   Date(s) and description of the incident(s).
    *   Evidence (screenshots, emails, witness statements).
    *   Desired remedy or outcome.
4.  **Conflict Check:** If the Secretary is involved in the grievance, the form must be submitted to the President.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Intake and Screening
1.  **Initial Review:** Within **five (5) business days** of receipt, the Secretary (or designee) reviews the grievance to determine:
    *   Is the Complainant a member in good standing?
    *   Does the allegation, if true, constitute a violation of Organization policy?
    *   Is the grievance frivolous, malicious, or retaliatory?
2.  **Dismissal:** If the grievance does not meet the criteria, it may be dismissed with a written explanation to the Complainant.
3.  **Acceptance:** If accepted, the Secretary notifies the Board and initiates the formation of a Grievance Committee.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Investigation
1.  **Committee Appointment:** The Board appoints an ad-hoc Grievance Committee consisting of three (3) neutral individuals (Directors or respected Voting Members).
    *   *Note:* No member of the Grievance Committee may have a Conflict of Interest as defined in **Bylaws Article IX**.
2.  **Notification:** The Respondent is notified in writing of the grievance, provided a copy of the complaint (redacted if necessary for safety), and given **ten (10) days** to submit a written response.
3.  **Fact-Finding:** The Committee may:
    *   Interview the Complainant, Respondent, and witnesses via secure video conference.
    *   Review digital logs, community posts, or financial records.
    *   Request additional documentation.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Hearing (Virtual)
1.  **Scheduling:** If the Committee deems a hearing necessary, it shall be scheduled via a secure video conferencing platform.
2.  **Procedure:**
    *   The hearing is closed to the public.
    *   Both parties may present a brief opening statement.
    *   The Committee questions both parties.
    *   Cross-examination is not permitted unless authorized by the Committee Chair.
3.  **Record:** A summary record of the hearing shall be kept. Recording is permitted only with the consent of all parties, pursuant to Texas wiretapping laws (one-party consent state, but Organization policy requires all-party consent for governance meetings).

#### 4.6 Phase VI: Determination and Sanctions
1.  **Deliberation:** The Committee deliberates in a closed Executive Session.
2.  **Standard of Proof:** The standard of proof shall be a "Preponderance of the Evidence" (more likely than not).
3.  **Outcomes:** The Committee may recommend:
    *   **Exoneration:** No violation found.
    *   **Warning:** Written reprimand placed in the member's record.
    *   **Probation:** Suspension of certain privileges (e.g., posting rights, committee service) for a set period.
    *   **Suspension:** Temporary removal of membership rights.
    *   **Expulsion:** Termination of membership.
4.  **Board Ratification:** Recommendations for Suspension or Expulsion require a **two-thirds (2/3) vote** of the Board of Directors to be finalized, pursuant to **Bylaws Article III §3.6** (applied analogously to members).

#### 4.7 Phase VII: Appeal
1.  **Right to Appeal:** Either party may appeal the decision to the full Board of Directors within **seven (7) days** of receiving the determination.
2.  **Grounds for Appeal:** Appeals are limited to:
    *   Procedural errors that significantly affected the outcome.
    *   New evidence not previously available.
    *   Sanctions that are disproportionate to the offense.
3.  **Final Decision:** The Board reviews the record (no new hearing) and issues a final, binding decision within **fourteen (14) days**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Complainant** | Submit truthful, evidence-based grievances; maintain confidentiality during the process. |
| **Respondent** | Cooperate with the investigation; refrain from retaliation against the Complainant. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Facilitate informal mediation (Phase I); promote restorative justice where appropriate. |
| **Secretary** | Receive grievances; conduct initial screening; maintain official records of the proceedings. |
| **Grievance Committee** | Conduct impartial investigations; hold hearings; issue recommendations for resolution. |
| **Board of Directors** | Appoint Grievance Committees; ratify sanctions; hear final appeals. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Confidentiality:** All parties involved in a grievance must maintain strict confidentiality regarding the proceedings. A breach of confidentiality may result in independent disciplinary action.
*   **Retaliation Prohibited:** Retaliation against a member for filing a grievance or participating as a witness is strictly prohibited and constitutes grounds for immediate expulsion.
*   **False Accusations:** Filing a grievance known to be false or malicious is a violation of the *Code of Ethics* and may result in disciplinary action against the Complainant.
*   **Record Retention:** All records related to grievances shall be retained for a minimum of **seven (7) years** in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.
