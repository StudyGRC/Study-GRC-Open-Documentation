### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for managing physical access to Study GRC Inc. facilities and temporary event venues. This procedure is designed to prevent unauthorized access to organizational assets, ensure the safety of personnel and youth participants, and maintain a physical audit trail. This aligns with **NIST SP 800-53 (PE-3 Physical Access Control)** and supports the Organization’s commitment to youth protection as outlined in the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, contractors, and visitors.
*   **Locations:** This applies to any physical administrative office (leased or owned) and any temporary venue (conference centers, schools, hotels) where Study GRC Inc. is hosting an event and exercises control over the perimeter.
*   **Exclusions:** Public areas of third-party venues (e.g., hotel lobbies) outside of the Organization’s designated event perimeter are excluded, though staff vigilance is required.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Visitor** | Any individual who is not a current employee, Board Director, or active volunteer with a cleared background check. This includes vendors, delivery personnel, and family members of staff. |
| **Escort** | A Study GRC Inc. employee or authorized volunteer responsible for accompanying a visitor at all times. |
| **Restricted Area** | Physical locations containing sensitive data, infrastructure (server closets), or vulnerable populations (youth changing areas). |
| **Badge** | A physical credential (card, sticker, or lanyard) providing visual authorization to be in a specific area. |
| **Tailgating** | The act of an unauthorized person following an authorized person through a secured entry point without proper validation. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 General Principles of Access
1.  **Zero Trust Physical Access:** No individual shall be granted access to Study GRC Inc. controlled spaces without positive identification and a valid business purpose.
2.  **Mandatory Display:** All persons within controlled perimeters must display a valid badge or identification credential on their outer garment, above the waist, at all times.
3.  **Challenge Protocol:** All staff are authorized and required to professionally challenge any individual not displaying a badge or unaccompanied in a restricted area (e.g., "May I help you find your escort?").

#### 4.2 Administrative Office Visitor Protocol
*Note: As a remote-first organization, this applies to any co-working space or leased office where Study GRC maintains a physical footprint.*

1.  **Entry Log:** Upon arrival, the Visitor must sign the **Visitor Log (IS-REG-005)**, providing:
    *   Full Name.
    *   Company/Affiliation.
    *   Date and Time In.
    *   Name of Study GRC Escort.
2.  **Identity Verification:** The Receptionist or Escort must verify the Visitor's identity using a government-issued photo ID (Driver’s License or Passport).
3.  **Badge Issuance:**
    *   Visitors must be issued a "VISITOR" badge with a red lanyard (or color designated for non-cleared individuals).
    *   The badge must be numbered to correspond with the Visitor Log entry.
4.  **Escort Requirement:** Visitors must be escorted **100% of the time** while inside the facility. Visitors may not be left alone in conference rooms or offices where unlocked computers or physical files are present.
5.  **Departure:** Upon departure, the Visitor must return the badge and sign out in the Visitor Log.

#### 4.3 Event and Conference Venue Protocol
*Given the Organization's focus on K-12 education and youth workshops, strict perimeter control at events is mandatory.*

1.  **Perimeter Establishment:** Prior to the start of any event, the Event Director must define the "Controlled Perimeter" and establish Check-In/Registration desks at all entry points.
2.  **Badge Classification System:** Badges must be color-coded to visually distinguish roles and clearance levels:
    *   **Staff/Board:** (e.g., Black Lanyard) – Full Access.
    *   **Volunteer (Cleared):** (e.g., Green Lanyard) – Access to youth areas.
    *   **Attendee (Adult):** (e.g., Blue Lanyard) – General sessions only.
    *   **Attendee (Minor):** (e.g., Orange Lanyard) – **Must never be photographed without checking the "Media Release" indicator on the badge.**
    *   **Vendor/Visitor:** (e.g., Red Lanyard) – No access to youth areas without escort.
3.  **Youth Protection Enforcement:**
    *   Individuals with "Visitor" or "Vendor" badges are strictly prohibited from entering designated Youth Zones (classrooms, changing rooms) unless accompanied by a background-checked Staff member.
    *   See **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy** for "Two-Deep Leadership" rules.
4.  **Lost Badge Protocol:**
    *   Lost badges must be reported immediately to the Registration Desk.
    *   The previous badge ID must be voided in the registration system.
    *   Identity must be re-verified via government ID before a replacement is printed.

#### 4.4 Delivery and Maintenance Personnel
1.  **Short-Term Access:** Delivery personnel (e.g., FedEx, Catering) remaining in the reception/loading area do not require a badge but must not proceed past the reception point.
2.  **Long-Term Access:** Maintenance workers requiring access to facility infrastructure must be badged as Visitors and escorted by Operations or IT staff.

#### 4.5 Badge Inventory and Audit
1.  **Inventory Control:** The Office Manager or Event Director must maintain an inventory of reusable visitor badges.
2.  **Daily Reconciliation:** At the end of each business day (office) or event day, the Visitor Log must be reviewed to ensure all visitors have signed out and badges have been returned.
3.  **Unreturned Badges:** If a badge is not returned, it must be marked as "Lost" in the inventory logs. If the badge controls electronic access (RFID), it must be deactivated immediately.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Receptionist / Registration Lead** | Enforces entry logging, verifies ID, issues badges, and ensures no visitor enters without an escort. |
| **Study GRC Escort** | Remains with the visitor at all times, ensures the visitor does not access unauthorized data or areas, and ensures youth protection standards are met. |
| **All Employees/Volunteers** | Responsible for wearing their own badges and challenging unbadged individuals ("See something, say something"). |
| **Event Director** | Defines the physical perimeter for events and establishes the badge color-coding system. |
| **CISO** | Conducts periodic audits of visitor logs and physical security procedures. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure puts the Organization and its community at risk.
*   **Employees:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, particularly if the violation compromises **Youth Protection** standards.
*   **Visitors:** Visitors refusing to comply with badging or escort requirements will be denied entry or escorted off the premises immediately.
*   **Legal:** Unauthorized entry may be prosecuted under applicable Texas penal codes regarding trespassing.
