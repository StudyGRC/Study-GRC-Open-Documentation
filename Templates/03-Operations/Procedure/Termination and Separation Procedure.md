### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant, and respectful process for the separation of employees and volunteers from Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with the Texas Labor Code and the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), mitigates legal and security risks, ensures the return of organizational assets, and facilitates accurate final compensation.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees (full-time, part-time, temporary), independent contractors, and volunteers of Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not supersede the specific removal processes for Board Directors (Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6) or Operational Officers elected by the Board (Bylaws Article V, Section 5.3), which require specific voting thresholds.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **At-Will Employment** | The legal doctrine in Texas whereby either the employer or the employee may terminate the employment relationship at any time, for any reason (provided it is not illegal), or for no reason at all. |
| **Involuntary Termination** | Separation initiated by the Organization (e.g., discharge for cause, layoff, reduction in force). |
| **Voluntary Resignation** | Separation initiated by the employee (e.g., resignation, retirement, job abandonment). |
| **Offboarding** | The administrative and technical process of removing access, recovering assets, and finalizing records for a departing individual. |
| **Separation Date** | The final day the employee is physically present at work or approved for remote work. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. Classification of Separation
The Human Resources (HR) department or the Executive Director must classify the separation immediately upon notice to determine final pay timelines under Texas law.

1.  **Voluntary:** Written or verbal resignation provided by the employee.
2.  **Involuntary:** Termination initiated by management.
3.  **Job Abandonment:** Failure to report to work for three (3) consecutive days without notification is considered a voluntary resignation.

#### 4.2. Voluntary Resignation Process
1.  **Notice:** Employees are requested to provide at least two (2) weeks' written notice of resignation to their immediate supervisor.
2.  **Acceptance:** The supervisor must forward the resignation notice to HR/Operations immediately.
3.  **Counter-Offers:** Study GRC Inc. generally does not extend counter-offers.
4.  **Early Release:** The Organization reserves the right to accept a resignation effective immediately. In such cases, the employee will be paid through the intended notice period (up to 2 weeks) unless the early release is due to misconduct.

#### 4.3. Involuntary Termination Process
*Note: Consult `HR-POL-048` (Progressive Discipline Policy) prior to termination for performance issues.*

1.  **Pre-Termination Review:** Before terminating an employee, the manager must review the decision with HR and/or the Executive Director to ensure:
    *   Consistency with past actions.
    *   Documentation supports the decision.
    *   No violation of anti-discrimination or anti-retaliation laws (e.g., Whistleblower Protection).
2.  **Termination Meeting:**
    *   Must be conducted by the direct manager and a witness (HR or another manager).
    *   For remote employees, this meeting occurs via video conference.
    *   State the decision clearly. Do not debate or argue.
    *   Provide the **Notice of Separation** document.

#### 4.4. Final Wage Payment (Texas Payday Law Compliance)
Strict adherence to Texas Labor Code Chapter 61 is mandatory.

1.  **Involuntary Termination:** Final wages must be paid no later than the **6th calendar day** after the date of termination.
2.  **Voluntary Resignation:** Final wages must be paid no later than the **next regularly scheduled payday**.
3.  **Accrued Leave:** Payout of accrued but unused PTO is governed by `HR-POL-058` (Paid Time Off Policy). If the policy states PTO is forfeited upon termination for cause, it shall not be paid.
4.  **Deductions:** No deductions for unreturned equipment may be made from the final paycheck unless the employee has signed a specific written authorization allowing such deduction, in accordance with Texas Labor Code § 61.018.

#### 4.5. Asset Recovery and Security (Remote-First Protocol)
Due to the Organization's remote nature and focus on Information Security, immediate action is required to protect data.

1.  **Digital Access Revocation:**
    *   **Involuntary:** The CISO or IT Administrator must revoke all system access (Email, Slack, CRM, GitHub, Cloud Storage) **immediately** prior to or during the termination meeting.
    *   **Voluntary:** Access is revoked at the close of business on the Separation Date.
2.  **Hardware Return:**
    *   HR/Operations will provide a pre-paid shipping label to the employee for the return of laptops, monitors, and security keys.
    *   The employee must ship equipment within three (3) business days of the Separation Date.
3.  **Intellectual Property:**
    *   Departing staff are reminded of their obligations under **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, confirming that all Work Product created during service belongs to the Organization.
    *   Staff must confirm deletion of any proprietary data stored on personal devices (BYOD).

#### 4.6. Benefits Continuation
1.  **Health Insurance:** If applicable, coverage ends on the last day of the month in which employment terminates.
2.  **COBRA/State Continuation:** HR will notify the benefits administrator to send required COBRA or State Continuation election notices to the employee's home address within legally mandated timelines.

#### 4.7. Exit Interview
1.  For voluntary resignations, HR may schedule an optional Exit Interview to gather feedback on culture and operations.
2.  Exit Interviews are generally not conducted for involuntary terminations.

#### 4.8. Reference Checks
1.  All requests for employment verification must be directed to HR.
2.  Study GRC Inc. maintains a **neutral reference policy**: We confirm only dates of employment and job title. We do not discuss reasons for separation or eligibility for rehire unless required by law.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Manager** | Initiates termination request; conducts termination meeting; collects immediate physical assets (if in-person). |
| **HR / Operations** | Calculates final pay; generates shipping labels; conducts exit interviews; administers COBRA notices. |
| **CISO / IT Admin** | Executes the "Kill Switch" (access revocation); audits logs for data exfiltration prior to departure. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves all involuntary terminations to ensure strategic and legal alignment. |
| **Employee** | Returns all company property; clears personal data from company devices; submits final timesheet. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure, particularly regarding the timing of final pay and the revocation of digital access, poses significant legal and security risks to the Organization.

*   **Managers** who fail to notify HR of a termination immediately may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination, as this may cause a violation of Texas Payday Law.
*   **Departing Employees** who fail to return equipment or attempt to steal data may be subject to civil litigation and/or criminal prosecution.
