### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, rigorous process for Study GRC Inc. management to respond to findings arising from internal audits, external financial audits, and federal grantor monitoring visits. This procedure ensures that the Organization complies with **2 CFR § 200.511 (Audit findings follow-up)**, demonstrates accountability to stakeholders, and effectively remediates identified deficiencies to prevent recurrence.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **Audits:** Annual Financial Audits, Single Audits (Uniform Guidance), Internal Audits, and Grantor Site Visits/Monitoring.
*   **Personnel:** The Executive Director, Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Department Heads, and the Board of Directors (specifically the Audit Committee).
*   **Entities:** All operational departments and programs within Study GRC Inc.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit Finding** | A deficiency in internal control, non-compliance with laws/regulations, or a questioned cost identified by an auditor. |
| **Management Response** | A formal written reply to the auditor acknowledging the finding and detailing the Organization's position and plan for resolution. |
| **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** | A specific, time-bound plan required by 2 CFR § 200.511(c) detailing how the Organization will resolve a finding. |
| **Questioned Cost** | A cost that is questioned by the auditor because of an alleged violation of a provision of a law, regulation, contract, grant, or other agreement or document governing the expenditure of funds. |
| **Root Cause Analysis** | The process of discovering the underlying causes of a problem in order to identify appropriate solutions. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Receipt and Initial Assessment
1.  **Receipt of Draft Report:** Upon receipt of a draft audit report or "Notification of Findings," the **CFO** must immediately log the receipt in the *Audit Finding Tracking Log* and notify the **Executive Director** and the **Chair of the Audit Committee** within 24 hours.
2.  **Distribution:** The CFO must distribute the relevant sections of the draft report to the Department Head responsible for the area where the finding occurred (e.g., CISO for IT findings, VP of Education for programmatic findings).
3.  **Fact-Checking:** The responsible Department Head and CFO must review the finding for factual accuracy.
    *   If the finding is factually incorrect, the CFO must provide evidence to the auditor to dispute the finding prior to the issuance of the final report.

#### 4.2 Root Cause Analysis
1.  For every valid finding, the responsible Department Head must conduct a **Root Cause Analysis**.
2.  The analysis must answer:
    *   *Why* did the deficiency occur? (e.g., lack of policy, failure to follow policy, lack of training, system error).
    *   *Why* was it not detected by internal controls?
3.  The Root Cause Analysis must be documented and retained in the audit work papers.

#### 4.3 Drafting the Management Response
The Management Response must be drafted by the CFO in collaboration with the responsible Department Head. The response must include three distinct components:

1.  **Concurrence or Non-Concurrence:**
    *   Statement clearly indicating whether management agrees or disagrees with the finding.
    *   *If disagreeing:* A detailed legal or accounting justification must be provided, citing specific evidence or regulation.
2.  **Corrective Action Plan (CAP):**
    *   Specific steps the Organization will take to correct the deficiency.
    *   Specific steps to prevent recurrence (systemic fix).
    *   **Timeline:** An anticipated completion date (must be realistic but prompt).
    *   **Responsible Person:** Title of the individual accountable for implementation.
3.  **Retroactive Correction (if applicable):**
    *   If the finding involves Questioned Costs, the response must address how funds will be returned or reallocated in compliance with 2 CFR 200.

#### 4.4 Review and Approval
1.  **Executive Review:** The Executive Director must review and approve all Management Responses to ensure alignment with organizational risk tolerance and strategic goals.
2.  **Governance Review:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI (Committees)**, the Audit Committee must review any finding classified as a "Material Weakness" or "Significant Deficiency" before the response is finalized.
    *   For findings involving fraud or ethical violations by senior management, the Board of Directors must draft the response, excluding the implicated officer.

#### 4.5 Submission to Auditor
1.  The CFO is responsible for submitting the finalized Management Response and Corrective Action Plan to the external auditor or granting agency within the deadline specified by the auditor (typically 5–10 business days after receipt of the draft).
2.  The Management Response will be included in the Final Audit Report which, pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, may be made publicly available.

#### 4.6 Implementation and Tracking
1.  **Logging:** Upon submission, the CAP details must be entered into the *Audit Finding Tracking and Closure* system.
2.  **Monitoring:** The Internal Audit function (or CFO) must verify the implementation of the CAP on a monthly basis until closed.
3.  **Closure:** A finding is considered "Closed" only when independent verification confirms the corrective action is fully implemented and operating effectively.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit Committee** | Provides oversight of the audit process; reviews responses to Material Weaknesses; ensures management is held accountable for remediation. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ultimate operational responsibility for the accuracy of the response and the commitment of resources to fix deficiencies. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Manages the audit relationship; drafts technical responses; maintains the *Audit Finding Tracking Log*. |
| **Department Heads** | Conduct root cause analysis for findings in their domain; implement corrective actions; report progress to CFO. |
| **Internal Audit** | Independently verifies that Corrective Action Plans have been implemented before closing findings. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adequately respond to audit findings or to implement Corrective Action Plans constitutes a breach of fiduciary duty and operational negligence.
*   **Federal Grants:** Under 2 CFR 200, failure to remedy audit findings may result in "High Risk" designation, special conditions on awards, suspension of payments, or debarment from federal funding.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Employees responsible for findings resulting from willful negligence or failure to implement agreed-upon CAPs are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination, in accordance with the *Progressive Discipline Policy*.
