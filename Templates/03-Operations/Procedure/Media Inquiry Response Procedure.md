### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, time-sensitive workflow for handling inquiries from media outlets, journalists, bloggers, and external content creators. This procedure ensures that Study GRC Inc. maintains a consistent brand voice, provides accurate information, protects the privacy of its members and youth participants, and mitigates reputational risk through timely and professional engagement.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Authorized Personnel**, including the Board of Directors, Operational Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors.

**Inclusions:**
*   Requests for interviews (phone, video, email, or in-person).
*   Requests for official statements, quotes, or comments.
*   Requests for filming or photography at Organization events.
*   Inquiries regarding financial status, governance, or crisis events.

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal social media interactions where the individual is clearly not speaking on behalf of the Organization (governed by *HR-POL-060 Social Media Policy*).
*   Routine marketing or advertising procurement.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Media Inquiry** | Any request for information, comment, or interview from a representative of a news organization, trade publication, blog, podcast, or similar public-facing entity. |
| **Spokesperson** | An individual explicitly authorized by the Executive Director or Board of Directors to speak on the record on behalf of the Organization. |
| **Deadline** | The specific time and date by which the media representative requires a response to meet their publication schedule. |
| **Holding Statement** | A pre-approved, temporary statement acknowledging the inquiry and promising a follow-up, used to buy time for fact-checking. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Intake and Immediate Action
**Strict Prohibition:** Unauthorized personnel must **never** answer questions "off the cuff," provide "off the record" background, or speculate on Organization business.

1.  **Receive the Inquiry:** When a media inquiry is received (via email, phone, or social media), the recipient must remain polite but non-committal.
2.  **Gather Information:** The recipient must attempt to collect the following details using the *Media Inquiry Intake Form*:
    *   Name of reporter and outlet.
    *   Topic/Subject of the story.
    *   Specific questions or angle.
    *   **Deadline** (Date and Time/Time Zone).
    *   Contact information (Email/Phone).
3.  **Standard Response:** Use the following script:
    > "Thank you for reaching out to Study GRC. I will forward your request to our Communications team immediately, and they will be in touch with you shortly to discuss your deadline."
4.  **Log and Forward:** Immediately (within 30 minutes) forward the details to the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** and the **Executive Director (CEO)** via the designated internal communication channel (e.g., Slack `#media-requests` or email).

#### 4.2 Triage and Assessment
Upon receipt, the DKG and Executive Director must categorize the inquiry within **one (1) hour**:

*   **Category A: Routine/Positive:** Feature stories, educational resource promotion, event coverage.
*   **Category B: Operational/Technical:** Questions regarding GRC subject matter, IT security, or specific curriculum.
*   **Category C: Sensitive/Crisis:** Inquiries regarding financial audits, legal disputes, youth protection incidents, or negative allegations.

#### 4.3 Spokesperson Designation
Based on the Triage Category, the appropriate Spokesperson is assigned:

*   **Routine:** Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or Subject Matter Expert (SME) appointed by the DKG.
*   **Operational:** Executive Director (CEO) or VP of Education & Training.
*   **Crisis/Governance:**
    *   **Operational Crisis:** Executive Director (CEO).
    *   **Board/Governance Crisis:** President of the Board (pursuant to Bylaws Article IV).

#### 4.4 Response Formulation and Approval
1.  **Drafting:** The DKG or designee drafts the response or talking points.
    *   *Note:* For Category C (Crisis), Legal Counsel must review the draft prior to approval.
2.  **Youth Protection Check:** If the inquiry involves images, names, or stories of youth participants, the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy** applies.
    *   **Requirement:** No information regarding a minor may be released without a signed *Parental Consent and Media Release Form* on file.
3.  **Approval:**
    *   **Category A:** Approved by DKG.
    *   **Category B:** Approved by Executive Director.
    *   **Category C:** Approved by Executive Director and Board President.

#### 4.5 Execution and Timeliness
1.  **Acknowledgment:** If a full response cannot be drafted immediately, the DKG must send a **Holding Statement** within **two (2) hours** of the initial inquiry to acknowledge receipt and confirm the deadline.
2.  **Delivery:** The designated Spokesperson delivers the response via the requested channel (email/phone) prior to the reporter's deadline.
3.  **Interview Support:** If an interview is scheduled:
    *   The DKG must brief the Spokesperson on the reporter’s background and potential "gotcha" questions.
    *   A second staff member should sit in on the interview to take notes or record the conversation (with consent).

#### 4.6 Post-Inquiry Monitoring
1.  **Tracking:** The DKG logs the final outcome (published article link, broadcast time) in the *Media Relations Register*.
2.  **Correction:** If the published story contains factual errors regarding Study GRC Inc., the DKG must contact the reporter immediately to request a correction.
3.  **Review:** Significant media interactions are reviewed during the weekly Operational Leadership meeting.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Act as the initial intake point; strictly adhere to the "no comment" rule; forward inquiries immediately. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Primary media contact; manages the intake log; drafts initial responses; coordinates interviews. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Approves responses for operational and sensitive matters; serves as the primary Spokesperson for Organization strategy. |
| **Board President** | Serves as the primary Spokesperson for governance, Board-level, or existential crisis matters. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews responses regarding litigation, liability, or regulatory compliance prior to release. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Timeliness is mandatory.** Failure to acknowledge media inquiries promptly can result in "No Comment" appearing in stories, which damages the Organization's reputation for transparency.

**Unauthorized Communication:** Any employee, volunteer, or contractor found to be speaking to the media on behalf of the Organization without authorization, or releasing confidential member/youth data to the press, will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including **termination of employment or volunteer assignment** and potential legal action for breach of the Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA).
