### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant process for the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") to take action without a formal meeting via electronic means. This procedure ensures compliance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.220** and **Article III, Section 3.9** of the Organization's Bylaws, while maintaining a verifiable audit trail of corporate decisions.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Voting Directors of Study GRC Inc. It governs any "Action Without a Meeting" where a vote is conducted via email, secure messaging platform, or dedicated electronic voting software.

**Exclusions:**
*   This procedure does not apply to votes taken during a live, synchronous video or telephonic meeting (e.g., a vote taken verbally during a Zoom call).
*   This procedure does not apply to the election of Directors by the General Membership (governed separately by *GOV-PRC-027 Electronic Ballot Integrity Protocol*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Action Without Meeting** | A corporate action taken by the Board without a physical or virtual gathering, authorized by written consent of the Directors. |
| **Unanimous Consent** | The requirement under Bylaws Section 3.9 that **all** Directors entitled to vote must affirmatively approve the action for it to pass without a meeting. |
| **Electronic Transmission** | A form of communication that does not involve the physical transmission of paper, creates a record that can be retained, retrieved, and reviewed, and may be directly reproduced in paper form (e.g., email). |
| **Voting Window** | The specific duration of time during which Directors may cast their electronic vote. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Initiation of Electronic Vote
4.1.1 **Authority:** An electronic vote may be initiated by the President, the Chair of the Board, or by a written request of at least two (2) Directors submitted to the Secretary.
4.1.2 **Format:** The initiating party must draft a clear, unambiguous motion. The motion must be phrased so that a "Yes" or "No" response is sufficient to indicate intent.
4.1.3 **Distribution:** The Secretary (or President, in the absence of the Secretary) must transmit the motion to **all** current Directors via their registered email addresses or the Organization’s designated secure governance platform.

#### 4.2 Content of the Notice
The electronic transmission initiating the vote must contain:
1.  The exact wording of the resolution or motion to be adopted.
2.  Any supporting documentation or context required for informed decision-making.
3.  The deadline for the vote (the "Voting Window"), which shall be no less than 24 hours unless an emergency is declared by the President.
4.  A statement clarifying that pursuant to Bylaws Section 3.9, **unanimous approval** is required for the motion to pass via this method.

#### 4.3 Voting Process
4.3.1 **Casting Votes:** Directors must reply to the electronic transmission (e.g., "Reply All" to an email thread or select an option in voting software).
4.3.2 **Authentication:** The response must clearly originate from the Director’s authorized account to serve as a valid electronic signature under TBOC § 1.002.
4.3.3 **Required Threshold:**
*   **Approval:** To pass, the motion must receive an affirmative vote ("Yes", "Approve", "Consent") from **100%** of the Directors entitled to vote.
*   **Failure:** If a single Director votes "No," "Abstain," or fails to respond within the Voting Window, the motion **fails** to carry as an Action Without Meeting.
*   **Remedy for Failure:** If the motion fails due to lack of unanimity, the matter must be deferred to the next regular or special meeting of the Board for discussion and a standard majority vote.

#### 4.4 Verification and Recording
4.4.1 **Tallying:** Upon the close of the Voting Window or receipt of the final vote (whichever occurs first), the Secretary must tally the results.
4.4.2 **Certification:** The Secretary shall draft a "Certificate of Action Taken by Unanimous Written Consent," attaching the email thread or voting logs as evidence.
4.4.3 **Filing:** The Certificate and the evidence of voting must be filed in the Organization’s Minute Book (digital repository) alongside the minutes of proceedings.
4.4.4 **Notification:** The Secretary must notify the Board of the final result within 24 hours of the vote closing.

#### 4.5 Ratification
While the action is legally effective immediately upon the final required signature/vote, the Secretary shall list all Actions Without Meeting taken since the last session on the agenda of the next scheduled Board meeting for formal ratification and entry into the minutes of that meeting.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **President / Chair** | Authorizes the initiation of an electronic vote and determines if a matter is suitable for asynchronous decision-making. |
| **Secretary** | Administers the voting process, verifies the identity of senders, ensures unanimous consent is achieved, and archives the records in the Minute Book. |
| **Directors** | Review the motion and supporting materials; cast a vote within the designated Voting Window; ensure security of their email/voting account credentials. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly adhere to this procedure—specifically the requirement for unanimous consent and proper record-keeping—may render the corporate action **void or voidable** under Texas law.

*   **Invalid Actions:** Any action taken electronically that does not receive approval from **all** Directors shall be considered null and void until such time as it is passed at a validly convened meeting.
*   **Record Integrity:** Failure by the Secretary to maintain the audit trail of electronic votes constitutes a violation of the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy* and may result in removal from the Officer role.
