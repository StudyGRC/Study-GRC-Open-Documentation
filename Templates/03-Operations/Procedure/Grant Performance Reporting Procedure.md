### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized methodology for monitoring, documenting, and reporting program performance for all grants awarded to Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with **2 CFR § 200.329 (Monitoring and reporting program performance)**, ensuring that the Organization compares actual accomplishments to established objectives and provides transparent explanations for any deviations.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Federal, State, and private foundation grants managed by Study GRC Inc. that contain performance reporting requirements.
*   **In Scope:** All Program Managers, the Finance Department, and Operational Officers involved in grant execution.
*   **Exclusions:** Unrestricted donations or gifts that do not require specific performance deliverables (though internal tracking is still encouraged).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **2 CFR 200.329** | The specific section of the Uniform Guidance requiring non-Federal entities to monitor grant activities to ensure performance goals are achieved. |
| **PPR** | Performance Progress Report. The standard terminology for the narrative report submitted to the awarding agency. |
| **Milestone** | A scheduled event signifying the completion of a major deliverable or a set of related deliverables (e.g., "Launch of GRC 101 Course"). |
| **KPI** | Key Performance Indicator. Quantifiable measure used to evaluate success (e.g., "Number of students certified"). |
| **Slippage** | A delay in the schedule or a failure to meet a performance target within the reporting period. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Pre-Award Metric Definition
To ensure reporting is possible, metrics must be defined prior to the start of the performance period.
1.  **Proposal Alignment:** During the grant proposal phase, the **VP of Education & Training** must collaborate with the **CFO** to ensure that proposed performance goals are realistic, measurable, and financially viable.
2.  **Data Source Identification:** For every objective listed in a grant agreement, the Program Manager must identify the specific data source (e.g., LMS Analytics, GitHub Commit Logs, Event Attendance Rosters) that will serve as the "Source of Truth."
3.  **Baseline Establishment:** Prior to spending funds, the Program Manager must record baseline data to allow for accurate "Pre/Post" comparison.

#### 4.2 Continuous Monitoring
In accordance with 2 CFR 200.328, monitoring must be continuous, not just performed at reporting deadlines.
1.  **Monthly Reviews:** The **VP of Education & Training** shall conduct monthly "Health Checks" with Program Managers to review:
    *   Progress against the approved timeline.
    *   Current burn rate of funds vs. completion of deliverables.
    *   Any emerging risks that may prevent meeting goals.
2.  **Data Collection:** Program Managers must aggregate performance data into the central Grant Management System (GMS) or designated secure repository by the 5th business day of each month.

#### 4.3 Drafting the Performance Progress Report (PPR)
When a reporting cycle (Quarterly, Semi-Annual, or Annual) concludes, the following steps must be taken:

**4.3.1 Comparison of Actuals vs. Objectives**
The Program Manager must draft the narrative section, which must include:
*   A comparison of **actual accomplishments** to the **objectives** established for the period.
*   Quantifiable data (e.g., "Target: 500 downloads; Actual: 650 downloads").
*   *Note:* Where the accomplishments can be quantified, a computation of the cost (e.g., unit cost) may be required if specified in the award terms.

**4.3.2 Explanation of Slippage**
If established goals were **not** met, the report must include a transparent narrative explaining:
*   **Why** the goal was missed (e.g., "Delay in vendor procurement," "Lower than expected community enrollment").
*   **Corrective Actions** taken to get back on track.
*   **Revised Timeline** for completion (if applicable).

**4.3.3 Financial Alignment Check**
The **CFO** must review the draft PPR to ensure the narrative aligns with the Financial Federal Rights (FFR/SF-425) report.
*   *Risk Check:* If the report claims 90% project completion but only 20% of funds have been drawn down (or vice versa), a detailed explanation must be included to prevent audit flags.

#### 4.4 Review and Submission
1.  **Internal Approval:** The draft PPR must be approved by the **VP of Education & Training**.
2.  **Executive Sign-Off:** For Federal awards exceeding $100,000, the **Executive Director** must review and sign off on the report.
3.  **Submission:** The designated Authorized Organization Representative (AOR) submits the report via the grantor’s portal (e.g., Grants.gov, GrantSolutions, or email) **no later than** the deadline (typically 30, 60, or 90 days post-period).
4.  **Archival:** The final submitted report and proof of submission (email receipt or screenshot) must be saved in the Master Grant File in accordance with the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy**.

#### 4.5 Significant Developments (Ad-Hoc Reporting)
Per 2 CFR 200.329(e), events may occur between scheduled reports that have a significant impact on the activity.
1.  **Immediate Notification:** If problems, delays, or adverse conditions will materially impair the ability to meet the objective of the award, the Program Manager must notify the Executive Director immediately.
2.  **Agency Disclosure:** The Executive Director or AOR must inform the Federal awarding agency as soon as the following conditions become known:
    *   Problems/delays materially impairing the project.
    *   Favorable developments enabling meeting time schedules and objectives sooner or at less cost than anticipated.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | **(Owner)** Ensures all educational programs have measurable KPIs; reviews and approves all narrative performance reports prior to submission. |
| **Program Manager** | Collects raw data from LMS/Community platforms; drafts the initial PPR; identifies reasons for slippage. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Verifies that performance narratives align with financial expenditure reports; calculates unit costs if required. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides final sign-off for high-value awards; manages high-level relationships with Grant Officers regarding significant developments. |
| **Authorized Org. Rep (AOR)** | Physically submits the report to the federal agency and logs the submission receipt. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure constitutes a violation of Federal grant terms and may result in the awarding agency withholding cash payments, disallowing costs, suspending or terminating the award, or initiating debarment proceedings (2 CFR 200.339).

Internal disciplinary action for failure to submit accurate or timely reports may include performance improvement plans or termination of employment, particularly if negligence results in the loss of funding.
