### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, risk-based process for the initiation, review, negotiation, approval, and execution of all contracts and legal agreements entered into by Study GRC Inc. This workflow ensures that the Organization:
1.  Mitigates legal, financial, and reputational risks.
2.  Complies with internal Bylaws, specifically regarding signature authority and Fundamental Actions.
3.  Adheres to federal grant requirements (2 CFR 200) regarding procurement and sub-awards.
4.  Protects Intellectual Property (IP) and Data Privacy rights.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to **all** employees, officers, Board members, and volunteers acting on behalf of the Organization.

**Inclusions:**
*   Vendor Service Agreements (MSAs, SOWs).
*   Software License Agreements (SaaS, EULAs) exceeding $500/year.
*   Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs).
*   Memorandums of Understanding (MOUs) or Partnership Agreements.
*   Grant Agreements (Incoming) and Sub-award Agreements (Outgoing).
*   Leases (Equipment or Real Estate).
*   Speaking or Sponsorship Agreements.

**Exclusions:**
*   Standard employment contracts (governed by HR-POL-040).
*   Routine retail purchases (e.g., office supplies) made via corporate credit card under the micro-purchase threshold, provided no signature is required.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Contract Owner** | The individual initiating the request for goods/services or partnership. Responsible for business terms and budget availability. |
| **Signatory** | An Officer authorized by the Bylaws or Board Resolution to legally bind the Organization. |
| **Fundamental Action** | As defined in Bylaws Article X, actions requiring Member vote (e.g., mergers, sale of substantially all assets). |
| **2 CFR 200 Appendix II** | Required contract provisions for non-Federal entities under Federal awards (e.g., termination for cause, anti-lobbying). |
| **Redlining** | The process of editing a contract where changes are tracked for negotiation. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Initiation and Business Review
**4.1.1 Budget Verification**
Before requesting a contract, the **Contract Owner** must verify with the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) that funds are allocated in the approved budget.

**4.1.2 Conflict of Interest Check**
The Contract Owner must determine if the proposed counterparty presents a conflict of interest. If a conflict exists, the *Interested Party Transaction Review Protocol* (GOV-PRC-007) must be triggered immediately, pursuant to Bylaws Article IX.

**4.1.3 Vendor Due Diligence**
For contracts involving federal funds or access to sensitive data:
*   **SAM.gov Check:** The Contract Owner must verify the vendor is not suspended or debarred.
*   **Security Review:** If the vendor will process PII or student data, the CISO must perform a *Vendor Security Risk Assessment* (IS-FRM-059).

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Legal and Compliance Review
**4.2.1 Submission**
The Contract Owner submits the draft agreement to the Legal/Compliance Department via the Central Document Repository.

**4.2.2 Triage and Risk Assessment**
The Reviewer categorizes the contract:
*   **Tier 1 (Low Risk):** Standard NDAs, low-value SaaS (<$5k), renewals with no changes. *Target Turnaround: 2 Business Days.*
*   **Tier 2 (Medium Risk):** Professional services, event contracts, data processing agreements. *Target Turnaround: 5 Business Days.*
*   **Tier 3 (High Risk):** Federal sub-awards, IP licensing, multi-year commitments >$50k, Fundamental Actions. *Target Turnaround: 10+ Business Days.*

**4.2.3 Substantive Review**
The Reviewer analyzes the contract for:
*   **Indemnification:** Ensuring Study GRC Inc. is indemnified against third-party claims (Bylaws Article VII alignment).
*   **Intellectual Property:** Ensuring "Work Made for Hire" clauses assign ownership to the Organization (Bylaws Article IX §9.3).
*   **Termination:** Ensuring the Organization has the right to terminate for convenience or cause.
*   **Federal Compliance:** Inserting required 2 CFR 200 clauses if grant funds are used.
*   **Jurisdiction:** Venue must be Texas (or neutral), avoiding foreign law governance.

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Negotiation (Redlining)
**4.3.1 Negotiation**
The Reviewer or General Counsel negotiates terms with the counterparty. The Contract Owner is kept informed of business-impact changes but does not negotiate legal terms directly.

**4.3.2 Finalization**
Once terms are agreed upon, a "Clean Execution Version" is generated.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Approval and Execution
**4.4.1 Authority Verification**
The Reviewer identifies the correct Signatory based on the *Delegation of Authority Matrix* (FIN-POL-060) and Bylaws Article V:
*   **Operational Officers (CEO/CFO):** Day-to-day operations within budget.
*   **Board President:** Matters exceeding CEO authority or specific governance issues.
*   **Voting Members:** **STOP.** If the contract involves a merger, dissolution, or sale of substantially all assets, it is a **Fundamental Action** (Bylaws Article X) and requires a 2/3 vote of the Membership before signing.

**4.4.2 Signature**
The contract is sent for electronic signature (e.g., DocuSign).
*   **Note:** The Organization always prefers to sign *last* to ensure the counterparty has accepted all terms.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Post-Execution Management
**4.5.1 Archival**
The fully executed PDF is stored in the *Central Contract Repository*.

**4.5.2 Metadata Entry**
The Contract Owner must enter key data into the Contract Management System:
*   Effective Date and Expiration Date.
*   Renewal Notice Deadline (to prevent unwanted auto-renewals).
*   Total Contract Value.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Contract Owner** | Initiates request, confirms budget, manages vendor relationship, ensures deliverables are met. |
| **General Counsel / Compliance** | Reviews legal terms, negotiates redlines, ensures regulatory compliance (2 CFR 200, GDPR, COPPA). |
| **CFO** | Validates budget availability and tax implications (UBIT). |
| **CISO** | Reviews data security addendums and vendor security posture. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Primary Signatory for operational contracts (Bylaws Art. V). |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves contracts exceeding Executive Director authority or involving conflicts of interest. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Unauthorized Commitments:** Any individual who signs a contract or verbally commits the Organization to an agreement without proper authority under this procedure may be personally liable for the obligation.

**Disciplinary Action:** Failure to follow this workflow, particularly regarding Conflict of Interest disclosures or Federal Grant compliance, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board/Volunteer position.
