### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized framework for the proposal, development, review, and approval of all educational materials produced by Study GRC Inc. This ensures that all content:
1.  Strictly aligns with the charitable and educational purposes defined in **Article 1.3** of the Bylaws.
2.  Maintains high standards of technical accuracy and pedagogical quality.
3.  Adheres to the Organization’s open-source commitments and Intellectual Property requirements (**Article 9.3**).
4.  Is safe, inclusive, and appropriate for diverse audiences, including K-12 participants.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Educational Assets** created for or on behalf of Study GRC Inc.

*   **Applies to:**
    *   Employees, Board Members, Operational Officers, Volunteers, and Contractors.
    *   Formal courses, certification study guides, webinar presentations, workshops, and exam banks.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Informal community forum discussions.
    *   Third-party content linked for reference only (not hosted/owned by the Organization).
    *   Administrative or operational internal training (e.g., HR onboarding).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Educational Asset** | Any structured content designed to teach Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) concepts, including text, video, slides, and code. |
| **Mission Alignment** | The verification that content directly supports "fostering a community-driven ecosystem," "removing financial barriers," or "simplifying the GRC domain" per Bylaws Art. 1.3. |
| **SME** | Subject Matter Expert; the individual(s) drafting the technical content. |
| **Work Product** | As defined in Bylaws Art. 9.3, content created by leaders/volunteers which is considered "work made for hire" and owned by the Organization. |
| **Gate Review** | A mandatory pause in the process where approval must be granted before moving to the next phase. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Proposal and Mission Alignment (Gate 1)
Before significant resources are invested, the concept must be validated.

1.  **Submission:** The Proposer submits a *Curriculum Proposal Form* to the VP of Education & Training.
2.  **Mission Alignment Check:** The VP of Education reviews the proposal against **Bylaws Article 1.3**.
    *   *Does this advance GRC knowledge?*
    *   *Is this resource planned to be zero-cost?*
    *   *Does it simplify the domain?*
3.  **Conflict of Interest Check:** The Proposer must disclose any potential conflicts (e.g., promoting a specific commercial vendor tool for personal gain) per the *Conflict of Interest Policy*.
4.  **Gate 1 Decision:**
    *   **Approved:** Proceed to Phase 2.
    *   **Rejected:** Proposal does not meet mission or ethical standards.

#### 4.2 Phase 2: IP Assignment and Development
To ensure the Organization can keep resources open-source, ownership must be clear before writing begins.

1.  **IP Agreement Execution:** If not already on file, the SME/Author must sign the *Volunteer Service Agreement* or *Contractor Agreement* containing the "Work Made for Hire" assignment clause (**Bylaws Art. 9.3**).
2.  **Drafting:** The SME develops the content using the Organization’s standard templates (Slide Decks, Markdown files, etc.).
3.  **Accessibility Integration:** Content must be drafted in accordance with the *Digital Accessibility Policy* (e.g., Alt Text for images, proper heading structures for screen readers).

#### 4.3 Phase 3: The Review Cycle (Gate 2)
Draft content must undergo three distinct reviews.

1.  **Technical Accuracy Review:**
    *   Assigned to a peer SME.
    *   Verifies correctness of GRC frameworks (e.g., NIST, ISO) and technical claims.
2.  **Pedagogical Review:**
    *   Assigned to the Education Committee.
    *   Evaluates learning objectives, clarity, and instructional design.
3.  **Safety & Suitability Review (Youth Protection):**
    *   Assigned to the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or designate.
    *   Ensures content is free of grooming language, hate speech, or inappropriate material, specifically considering K-12 learners.
    *   Verifies compliance with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Final Approval and Licensing (Gate 3)
1.  **Final Sign-Off:** The VP of Education & Training reviews the consolidated feedback and final draft.
2.  **Licensing Application:** The Organization applies the standard Open Source License (e.g., Creative Commons BY 4.0 or MIT License for code) to the asset.
    *   *Note:* Per Bylaws Art. 9.3, the Organization shall **never** grant exclusive rights to third parties that prevent public access.
3.  **Release Authorization:** The VP of Education authorizes the upload to the Learning Management System (LMS) or public repository.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Maintenance and Retirement
1.  **Annual Audit:** The Education Committee must review active curricula annually to ensure continued accuracy (e.g., checking for updated NIST versions).
2.  **Deprecation:** Content deemed obsolete or factually incorrect must be archived or updated immediately.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Process Owner. Final authority on Mission Alignment (Gate 1) and Final Release (Gate 3). |
| **Subject Matter Expert (SME)** | Creates content; ensures technical accuracy; executes IP assignment agreements. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Conducts Safety & Suitability reviews to ensure inclusive and safe culture. |
| **Education Committee** | Conducts pedagogical reviews and annual audits of existing material. |
| **Executive Director** | Arbitrates disputes regarding content rejection or strategic direction. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Mission Criticality:** Any content released that violates the nonprofit purpose (e.g., charging fees for access without Board approval, or commercial solicitation) is a violation of the Organization’s Charter.

Failure to follow this procedure may result in:
*   Immediate removal of the unauthorized content.
*   Revocation of publishing privileges.
*   Disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer status.
*   Legal action regarding Intellectual Property disputes if IP assignments were not executed.
