### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish mandatory operational standards for the protection of minors participating in virtual events hosted by Study GRC Inc. This procedure operationalizes the Organization’s commitment to "Remote Visibility" and "Two-Deep Leadership" to mitigate risks associated with digital grooming, cyberbullying, and unmonitored interactions. It ensures compliance with the Texas Family Code regarding child safety and aligns with the ethical standards mandated in **Article III, Section 3.3** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Board Directors, Operational Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors (collectively "Personnel") who organize, host, or moderate virtual events where minors (individuals under the age of 18) are present.

**In Scope:**
*   Webinars and workshops.
*   Mentoring sessions.
*   Online community gatherings (e.g., Discord stages, Zoom calls).
*   Hackathons or capture-the-flag (CTF) competitions.

**Exclusions:**
*   Internal administrative meetings where no minors are present.
*   Asynchronous communication (covered under *Digital Interaction with Minors Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18 years. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The mandatory presence of at least two background-checked, authorized adults in any digital space where minors are present. |
| **Authorized Platform** | A video conferencing or collaboration tool approved by the CISO that supports necessary security controls (e.g., Waiting Rooms, Chat Restrictions). |
| **Breakout Room** | A sub-session within a virtual event. |
| **Floater** | An authorized adult designated to move between Breakout Rooms to ensure compliance and safety. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Pre-Event Configuration and Staffing
To ensure safety by design, the Event Lead must configure the Authorized Platform with the following settings prior to the event start:

1.  **Mandatory Registration:** All participants must register in advance. "Drop-in" links posted publicly on social media without a registration gate are prohibited.
2.  **Waiting Room Enabled:** The Waiting Room feature must be active to allow the Host to vet participants before they enter the main session.
3.  **Chat Restrictions:**
    *   **Public Chat:** Permitted (visible to everyone).
    *   **Direct Message (DM) to Host:** Permitted.
    *   **Participant-to-Participant DM:** **MUST BE DISABLED.** Minors must not be able to privately message other participants or adults during the session.
4.  **Screen Sharing:** Restricted to "Host Only" by default. Participant screen sharing may be granted temporarily by the Host for educational purposes only.
5.  **Staffing Ratios:**
    *   A minimum of **two (2)** background-checked adults must be present for the duration of the event (Two-Deep Leadership).
    *   The ratio of adults to minors shall not exceed 1:10 for interactive workshops or 1:20 for lecture-style webinars.

#### 4.2 Admission and Identity Verification
1.  **Roster Check:** The Host or Moderator must verify that the display name in the Waiting Room matches the registration list.
2.  **Unknown Users:** Individuals with unrecognizable names (e.g., "iPhone", "User123") must be messaged in the Waiting Room to verify identity before admission. If identity cannot be verified, access is denied.
3.  **Visual Verification:** Upon entry, if the event is interactive, participants may be asked to briefly turn on their camera to verify they are a minor and match their registration, after which they may turn the camera off for privacy.

#### 4.3 Conduct During the Event
1.  **Recording Transparency:**
    *   If the session is recorded, the Host must verbally announce this at the start.
    *   Visual indicators (e.g., the red "Recording" icon) must be visible to all participants.
    *   Recordings are stored securely according to the *Data Retention Policy*.
2.  **Breakout Room Supervision:**
    *   Breakout rooms must not be used unless there are enough authorized adults to maintain Two-Deep Leadership in *every* room, OR;
    *   If static supervision is not possible, designated "Floaters" must randomly rotate through breakout rooms at intervals no longer than 5 minutes.
    *   **Zero Tolerance:** An adult must never be alone in a breakout room with a single minor.
3.  **Content Moderation:**
    *   Moderators must actively monitor the public chat log for bullying, inappropriate language, or sharing of personal identifiable information (PII).
    *   Violators must be immediately muted and moved to the Waiting Room for a behavioral assessment.

#### 4.4 Incident Response and Removal
1.  **Immediate Removal:** If a participant (adult or minor) engages in grooming behavior, sexual harassment, or severe bullying, the Host must immediately remove the individual from the meeting and permanently ban their re-entry.
2.  **Documentation:** The Moderator must timestamp the incident and preserve chat logs or screenshots immediately.
3.  **Reporting:** The Event Lead must file a *Suspected Abuse Reporting Form* or *Incident Report* within 24 hours to the Director of Kindness and Generosity.

#### 4.5 Post-Event Protocols
1.  **Debrief:** Staff and volunteers shall conduct a brief "hot wash" immediately following the event to identify any safety concerns or near-misses.
2.  **Log Retention:** Chat logs and attendance reports must be exported and saved to the secure drive designated for Youth Protection records.
3.  **One-on-One Follow-Up Prohibition:** Staff/Volunteers are prohibited from contacting minors individually after the event via DM or email. All follow-up communication must include the minor's parent/guardian or a second staff member on the copy line.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Lead** | Accountable for overall safety, staffing ratios, and ensuring Two-Deep Leadership is maintained throughout the event. |
| **Moderator (Tech Host)** | Responsible for platform configuration, admitting users from the Waiting Room, monitoring chat, and ejecting disruptive participants. |
| **Volunteer / Floater** | Provides supervision in main sessions and breakout rooms; reports suspicious behavior to the Event Lead. |
| **CISO** | Validates that the Authorized Platform meets security and privacy standards (e.g., encryption, data residency). |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Receives incident reports and manages communication with parents/guardians regarding safety issues. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure puts minors and the Organization at significant risk.
*   **Staff/Volunteers:** Violations may result in immediate removal from youth-facing duties, termination of employment or volunteer status, and potential legal action.
*   **Mandatory Reporting:** Any observed abuse or neglect must be reported to Texas authorities as required by the *Mandatory Reporting Procedure (Texas)*.
