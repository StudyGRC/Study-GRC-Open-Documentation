### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to outline the specific steps Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") will take when there is reasonable suspicion that an employee, volunteer, or contractor is under the influence of drugs or alcohol while performing work-related duties. This procedure is designed to ensure the safety of all community participants (specifically youth under the Organization's care), maintain compliance with the Federal Drug-Free Workplace Act of 1988 (required for federal grantees), and mitigate liability risks associated with impaired judgment or physical coordination.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Covered Individuals**, defined as employees, volunteers, and independent contractors acting on behalf of the Organization.

**Applicability:**
*   **In-Person Events:** Strictly enforced during conferences, meetups, and youth-facing events.
*   **Remote Work:** Applicable during scheduled work hours if impairment is visibly evident via video communication or work output.
*   **Vehicle Operation:** Strictly enforced for any individual operating a vehicle on behalf of the Organization or transporting other members/participants.

**Exclusions:**
*   Off-duty conduct that does not affect the Organization’s reputation or safety, provided no Organization equipment is involved.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reasonable Suspicion** | A belief based on articulable, specific, and contemporaneous observations concerning the appearance, behavior, speech, or body odors of the individual. It is not based on a hunch, rumor, or "gut feeling." |
| **Safety-Sensitive Position** | A role in which impairment could result in immediate physical harm to the individual or others (e.g., Youth Program Leaders, Drivers, Event Security). |
| **Prohibited Substance** | Illegal drugs, controlled substances used without a valid prescription, or alcohol consumed during work hours or immediately prior to reporting for duty. |
| **Refusal to Test** | Failure to appear for testing within the allotted time, failure to remain at the testing site, failure to provide a specimen, or tampering with a specimen. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Observation and Verification
**4.1.1** When a Manager, Event Lead, or Officer suspects an individual is impaired, they must immediately remove the individual from any safety-sensitive task (e.g., driving, supervising youth).
**4.1.2** The observing leader must secure a **Second Observer** (another Manager or Board Member) to corroborate the observation, if feasible. This aligns with the Organization's "Two-Deep Leadership" safety protocols.
**4.1.3** Both observers must look for and document specific indicators, including but not limited to:
*   **Physical:** Bloodshot eyes, dilated pupils, slurred speech, unsteady gait, smell of alcohol or marijuana.
*   **Behavioral:** Unexplained lethargy, hyperactivity, aggression, paranoia, or inability to focus.
*   **Performance:** Significant, unexplained decline in quality of work or safety violations.

#### 4.2 Documentation
**4.2.1** The observing leader must complete the **Reasonable Suspicion Observation Checklist (HR-FRM-066)** immediately.
**4.2.2** Documentation must be specific (e.g., write "Individual stumbled three times while walking 10 feet," not "Individual looked drunk").
**4.2.3** Both observers must sign and date the checklist within 24 hours of the incident.

#### 4.3 The Interview
**4.3.1** The Manager/Lead must escort the individual to a private area.
**4.3.2** The Manager shall inform the individual: "We have observed specific behaviors [cite examples] that give us reasonable suspicion to believe you may be in violation of our Drug-Free Workplace Policy."
**4.3.3** The individual shall be given an opportunity to explain the behavior (e.g., medical emergency, reaction to prescribed medication).
**4.3.4** If the explanation is insufficient or the safety risk remains, the Manager will inform the individual that they are required to undergo a drug/alcohol test immediately.

#### 4.4 Authorization to Test
**4.4.1** The Manager must contact the Executive Director or HR Lead to obtain verbal or written authorization to proceed with testing.
**4.4.2** If the individual is a Volunteer, the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) should be notified, though safety remains the priority.

#### 4.5 Transportation and Testing
**4.5.1** **Strict Safety Rule:** Under no circumstances shall the suspected individual be allowed to drive themselves to the testing facility or home.
**4.5.2** The Manager or a designated staff member must transport the individual to the Organization’s designated testing facility (e.g., Quest Diagnostics, LabCorp, or local urgent care).
**4.5.3** If the individual refuses transportation and attempts to drive, the Manager must explicitly warn them not to drive and, if they proceed, notify local law enforcement immediately.
**4.5.4** The test ordered shall be a standard NIDA-5 panel (or expanded panel if specific substances are suspected) and/or a breath alcohol test.

#### 4.6 Post-Testing Status
**4.6.1** After the specimen is provided, the Organization must arrange transportation for the individual to their home or a safe location.
**4.6.2** The individual is placed on **Administrative Leave** (paid for employees; suspended status for volunteers) pending the receipt of test results.
**4.6.3** The individual is prohibited from accessing Organization systems, data, or youth programs during this interim period.

#### 4.7 Test Results and Adjudication
**4.7.1 Negative Result:** If the test is negative, the individual shall be reinstated immediately. For employees, lost time will be compensated. A discussion regarding the behavior that triggered the suspicion (e.g., fatigue, illness) may still occur.
**4.7.2 Positive Result:**
*   The Medical Review Officer (MRO) will contact the individual to determine if there is a valid medical explanation (e.g., prescription).
*   If the MRO confirms a positive result without valid medical justification, the individual is subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or permanent ban from volunteer service.
**4.7.3** All test results are confidential health records and must be stored separately from the personnel file, accessible only to the Executive Director and HR Lead.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Manager / Event Lead** | Identifying signs of impairment, removing the individual from safety-sensitive duties, documenting observations, and arranging transportation. |
| **Second Observer** | Corroborating the signs of impairment to ensure objectivity and prevent bias. |
| **Executive Director / HR** | Authorizing the test, managing the relationship with the testing vendor, and determining final disciplinary action. |
| **Covered Individual** | Complying with the testing procedure and refraining from operating vehicles if suspected of impairment. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**6.1 Refusal to Test:** Refusal to submit to a test, failure to cooperate with testing procedures, or tampering with a specimen constitutes insubordination and acts as an admission of a policy violation. This will result in immediate termination of employment or volunteer service.
**6.2 Confidentiality:** Failure by managers to maintain the confidentiality of the observation or test results is a violation of this procedure and may result in disciplinary action.
**6.3 False Accusations:** Maliciously reporting an individual for drug testing without genuine reasonable suspicion is a violation of the Code of Ethics and will result in disciplinary action.
