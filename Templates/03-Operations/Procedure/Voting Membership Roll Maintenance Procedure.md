### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for maintaining, verifying, and producing the Official List of Voting Members (the "Roll") for Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.158** and **Article II** of the Organization’s Bylaws. It mitigates the risks of election fraud, disenfranchisement of eligible members, and legal challenges regarding quorum or election validity.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the Board Secretary, Treasurer, and any staff or volunteers with administrative access to the Organization’s membership database.

**Inclusions:**
*   **Supporting Members** (Voting)
*   **Honorary Members** (Voting)

**Exclusions:**
*   **Community Participants** (Non-Voting) are excluded from the Voting Roll but maintained in the general community database for communication purposes.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Voting Member** | A Supporting Member or Honorary Member in "Good Standing" as defined by the Membership Policy Manual. |
| **Good Standing** | A status indicating the member is current on all financial obligations (dues) and is not currently suspended due to disciplinary action. |
| **Record Date** | The specific date established by the Board (or by default under TBOC) to determine which members are eligible to receive notice and vote at a meeting. |
| **The Roll** | The alphabetical list of voting members entitled to notice and vote, containing names, addresses/emails, and number of votes held. |
| **TBOC** | Texas Business Organizations Code. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Routine Data Hygiene
To ensure the Roll is accurate at any given moment, the following maintenance steps must occur on a rolling basis:

1.  **New Member Processing:** Upon receipt of dues or Board appointment (Honorary), the Membership Database Administrator must classify the account as "Voting" within 48 hours.
2.  **Lapsed Membership Purge:** On the 1st of each month, the system shall automatically flag members whose dues are more than 30 days past due. These members are temporarily tagged as "Not in Good Standing" and removed from the active voting pool until arrears are cleared.
3.  **Contact Information Updates:** The Organization shall prompt members quarterly to verify their primary email address, which serves as the official channel for electronic balloting.

#### 4.2 Establishing the Record Date
Pursuant to **Bylaws Section 2.5**, the Record Date determines eligibility.

1.  **Board Determination:** At the Board meeting where the Annual or Special Meeting is scheduled, the Board must formally vote to set the Record Date.
2.  **Default Rule:** If the Board does not explicitly set a date, the Record Date shall be the date on which notice of the meeting is sent (typically 10 to 60 days prior to the meeting).
3.  **System Lock:** Once the Record Date passes, a "snapshot" of the membership database must be taken. Members joining *after* this date are not eligible to vote in the immediate upcoming election.

#### 4.3 Preparation of the Official Roll
**Responsible Role:** Secretary (assisted by Treasurer)
**Timeline:** Must be completed no later than two (2) business days after notice of the meeting is given.

1.  **Data Query:** The Secretary shall export a list from the Membership Database filtering for:
    *   Class = Supporting OR Honorary
    *   Status = Active / Good Standing
    *   Join Date <= Record Date
2.  **Financial Verification:** The Treasurer must review the exported list to certify that all listed Supporting Members have satisfied financial obligations as of the Record Date.
3.  **Formatting:** In compliance with **TBOC § 22.158(b)** and **Bylaws Section 2.6**, the list must be formatted alphabetically and include:
    *   Member Full Name
    *   Primary Email Address (as the registered voting address)
    *   Number of Votes Entitled (Standard: 1 vote per member)
4.  **Certification:** The Secretary must sign (digitally) a cover sheet certifying the list is accurate and complete as of the Record Date.

#### 4.4 Availability and Inspection
**Regulatory Requirement:** **TBOC § 22.158(c)** and **Bylaws Section 2.6**.

1.  **Electronic Availability:** Beginning two (2) business days after meeting notice is sent and continuing through the meeting, the Roll must be available for inspection.
2.  **Request Protocol:**
    *   A Voting Member must submit a **Member Record Inspection Request Form** (GOV-FRM-012).
    *   The request must state a proper purpose related to the meeting (e.g., verifying quorum).
3.  **Access Method:**
    *   Due to the remote nature of the Organization, access is provided via a secure, read-only view (e.g., screen share session or watermarked PDF via secure portal).
    *   **Prohibition:** The Organization shall not provide raw data files (CSV/Excel) to members to prevent unauthorized commercial solicitation or data privacy breaches.
4.  **Meeting Availability:** The list must be available at the virtual meeting (via the Secretary) for inspection by any voting member present.

#### 4.5 Post-Election Archival
1.  **Retention:** The certified Roll used for the election must be saved as an attachment to the Meeting Minutes.
2.  **Storage:** The file must be stored in the Organization’s secure governance repository (e.g., Google Workspace Shared Drive > Governance > Annual Meetings > [Year]).
3.  **Destruction:** Non-archived working copies or drafts of the list must be securely deleted immediately following the certification of election results.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Ultimate accountability for the accuracy of the Roll; certifies the list; manages member inspection requests. |
| **Treasurer** | Verifies financial "Good Standing" status of members prior to Roll finalization. |
| **Membership Admin** | Executes technical database queries; ensures day-to-day data integrity. |
| **Board of Directors** | Sets the Record Date via resolution. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain an accurate Voting Roll is a violation of Texas law and may result in the invalidation of election results, requiring a new meeting and vote at significant expense to the Organization.

**Internal Enforcement:**
*   Officers found to be intentionally manipulating the Roll to influence election outcomes will be subject to immediate removal for cause under **Bylaws Section 3.6** and potential legal action.
*   Members who misuse the Roll for commercial purposes or harassment will face immediate termination of membership.
