### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized method for identifying, reviewing, and formally retiring organizational policies, standards, and procedures that are no longer relevant, accurate, or legally compliant. This process mitigates the legal and operational risks associated with "zombie policies"—outdated documents that employees or volunteers might inadvertently follow, leading to non-compliance with current laws (e.g., Texas Business Organizations Code, 2 CFR 200) or conflict with the Organization’s Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Controlled Documents** (Policies, Standards, Procedures, Guidelines, and Forms) listed in the Master Documentation Registry (MDR) of Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:**
*   Transactional records (e.g., invoices, personnel files), which are governed by the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.
*   Board Meeting Minutes, which are permanent records under Bylaws Article VIII.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Obsolete** | A document status indicating the content is no longer required due to changes in operations, regulations, or technology, but has not yet been formally retired. |
| **Retired** | A document status indicating the content has been formally withdrawn from active use and is no longer authoritative. |
| **Superseded** | A specific type of retirement where a document is replaced by a newer version or a different document entirely. |
| **Archived** | The state of a retired document being stored securely for audit, litigation, or historical purposes for a defined retention period. |
| **MDR** | Master Documentation Registry; the central inventory of all active governance documents. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Identification of Candidates for Retirement
A document may be flagged for retirement/obsolescence under the following conditions:
1.  **Regulatory Change:** A change in law (e.g., Texas state law, IRS regulations) renders the policy non-compliant or unnecessary.
2.  **Operational Shift:** The Organization no longer performs the specific function or uses the technology governed by the document.
3.  **Redundancy:** The content is fully absorbed into another document (e.g., merging a "Password Policy" into a broader "Access Control Standard").
4.  **Sunset Clause:** The document has reached a pre-defined expiration date without renewal.

#### 4.2 Retirement Proposal and Impact Assessment
The **Document Owner** must initiate the request.

1.  **Submit Request:** The Document Owner submits a "Document Change Request" (DCR) to the Governance Committee or Executive Director, explicitly stating the reason for retirement.
2.  **Impact Analysis:** The Document Owner must verify that retiring the document will not:
    *   Create a gap in compliance with 2 CFR 200 (for federal grants).
    *   Violate the Bylaws (specifically Article IX regarding Conflicts of Interest or IP).
    *   Break references in other active policies (e.g., if Policy A references Procedure B, and Procedure B is retired, Policy A must be updated).

#### 4.3 Approval
Retirement requires the same level of authority as the original approval, unless delegated.

*   **Level 1 (Governance Policies):** Must be approved by the **Board of Directors**.
*   **Level 2 (Standards/Procedures):** Must be approved by the **Executive Director**.
*   **Level 3 (Guidelines):** Must be approved by the **Department Head**.

*Note: Approval must be recorded in the meeting minutes (for Board actions) or via written/electronic signature (for Operational actions).*

#### 4.4 Decommissioning and Watermarking
Upon approval, the **Document Controller** (or Secretary) must perform the following technical steps within 48 hours:

1.  **Remove from Circulation:** Remove the document from the active Intranet, Employee Handbook, and public Transparency Page (if applicable).
2.  **Watermark:** Apply a conspicuous watermark to the electronic file stating: **"RETIRED - DO NOT USE - [Date]"**.
3.  **Update MDR:** Update the Master Documentation Registry status from "Active" to "Retired."
4.  **Broken Link Remediation:** Scan the internal knowledge base to remove or update hyperlinks pointing to the retired document.

#### 4.5 Communication
To prevent confusion, the retirement must be communicated to relevant stakeholders:

1.  **Staff/Volunteers:** Notification via internal communication channels (Slack/Email) summarizing *why* the policy was retired and what (if anything) replaces it.
2.  **External Stakeholders:** If the policy was public (e.g., Privacy Policy), a notice or the new version must be posted immediately to maintain transparency in accordance with Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2.

#### 4.6 Archival and Retention
Retired documents are **not** deleted immediately. They serve as evidence of the rules in force at a specific point in time (crucial for legal defense or audits).

1.  **Storage:** Move the watermarked file to the "Legal Archive/Retired Policies" secure folder.
2.  **Retention Period:** Retain the retired document for **seven (7) years** from the date of retirement, or as defined in the *Records Retention Schedule*, whichever is longer.
3.  **Destruction:** Upon expiration of the retention period, the document may be permanently destroyed in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Document Owner** | Identifies obsolete documents and conducts impact analysis to ensure no compliance gaps are created by removal. |
| **Approving Authority** | Reviews the justification for retirement and provides formal authorization to decommission the document. |
| **Document Controller** | Executes the physical removal, watermarking, MDR updates, and archival of the document. |
| **Compliance Officer** | Ensures retired policies are retained for the statutory period to satisfy audit requirements (e.g., IRS, Grantors). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in the continued use of invalid rules, leading to operational errors, audit findings, or legal liability.
*   **Audit Risk:** Using a retired version of a financial procedure during a federal grant audit (Single Audit) can result in questioned costs.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Staff members who knowingly circulate or enforce retired policies after notification may be subject to disciplinary action.
