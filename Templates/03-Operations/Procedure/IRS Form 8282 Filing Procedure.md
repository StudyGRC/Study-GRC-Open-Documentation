### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory steps for preparing and filing IRS Form 8282 (*Donee Information Return*). This procedure ensures Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") complies with Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 6050L, which requires the reporting of information to the IRS and the donor when the Organization sells, exchanges, consumes, or otherwise disposes of charitable deduction property within three (3) years of the date of receipt.

Failure to file this form accurately and on time may result in significant financial penalties for the Organization and potential legal liability for Officers under the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the Finance Department, Development/Fundraising Team, and any Operational Officer (e.g., CISO, VP of Education) responsible for the management or liquidation of non-cash assets.

**Inclusions:**
*   Disposition of any non-cash property (e.g., cryptocurrency, real estate, vehicles, artwork, hardware) for which the Organization signed an IRS Form 8283 (*Noncash Charitable Contributions*) upon receipt.
*   Dispositions occurring within three (3) years of the original donation date.

**Exclusions:**
*   Cash donations.
*   Publicly traded securities.
*   Items consumed or distributed for charitable purposes in furtherance of the Organization's exempt purpose (subject to specific IRS criteria).
*   Property held for more than three (3) years from the date of receipt.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Form 8282** | IRS form titled "Donee Information Return," used to report the sale or disposition of charitable deduction property. |
| **Form 8283** | IRS form titled "Noncash Charitable Contributions," filed by the donor and signed by the Organization to substantiate the value of a gift >$5,000. |
| **Charitable Deduction Property** | Any property (other than money or publicly traded securities) contributed to the Organization where the claimed value exceeds $5,000. |
| **Disposition** | The sale, exchange, consumption, or destruction of property. This includes the immediate liquidation of cryptocurrency. |
| **Donee** | Study GRC Inc. (the recipient of the charitable gift). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Identification and Tracking of Restricted Assets
To ensure compliance, assets subject to Form 8282 reporting must be flagged immediately upon receipt.

1.  **Intake Flagging:** Upon signing Part IV of a donor's **Form 8283**, the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or authorized signatory must mark the asset in the Organization’s Fixed Asset Register or Crypto Wallet Ledger as "8282-Restricted."
2.  **Data Recording:** The asset record must include:
    *   Donor Name and Tax ID (SSN/EIN).
    *   Date of Contribution.
    *   Description of Property.
    *   A calendar alert set for three (3) years from the contribution date.

#### 4.2 Triggering Event (Disposition)
Whenever a "8282-Restricted" asset is processed for disposition (e.g., selling donated hardware, liquidating cryptocurrency, or auctioning items):

1.  **Notification:** The operational lead handling the asset (e.g., CISO decommissioning hardware or CFO liquidating crypto) must verify the acquisition date.
2.  **Three-Year Test:**
    *   **If > 3 Years:** No Form 8282 is required. Proceed with standard asset disposal procedures.
    *   **If < 3 Years:** The disposition triggers the Form 8282 requirement. The operational lead must notify the Finance Department immediately.

#### 4.3 Preparation of Form 8282
The CFO or designated accountant must prepare the form.

1.  **Information Gathering:** Collect the following data points:
    *   Original Form 8283 (from donor files).
    *   Date of Disposition.
    *   Amount received upon disposition (Gross Sale Price).
    *   Description of the property.
2.  **Completing the Form:**
    *   **Part I:** Enter Study GRC Inc. information (Name, EIN, Address).
    *   **Part II:** Enter the Original Donor’s information.
    *   **Part III:** Enter the Property Information (Description, Date of Contribution, Date of Disposition, Amount Received).
3.  **Cryptocurrency Specifics:** If the disposition involves cryptocurrency liquidated immediately upon receipt (per *FIN-POL-060 Cryptocurrency Liquidation Policy*), the "Date of Contribution" and "Date of Disposition" may be the same or consecutive days. This must be accurately recorded to prevent discrepancies in the donor's tax deduction claims.

#### 4.4 Review and Signature
1.  **Verification:** The Treasurer or a designated Board Director must review the completed Form 8282 against the financial records of the sale/liquidation to ensure the "Amount Received" matches the bank deposit or ledger entry.
2.  **Signature:** The form must be signed by an Officer authorized in **Bylaws Article IV** (President, Treasurer, or Secretary).

#### 4.5 Filing and Notification (The 125-Day Rule)
Strict deadlines apply to this filing.

1.  **IRS Filing:** The original signed Form 8282 must be filed with the IRS **within 125 days** after the date of disposition.
    *   *Filing Address:* Department of the Treasury, Internal Revenue Service Center, Ogden, UT 84201-0027 (or current address per IRS instructions).
2.  **Donor Notification:** A copy of the completed Form 8282 must be provided to the original donor.
    *   **Deadline:** Must be sent to the donor within **15 days** of filing with the IRS.
    *   **Method:** Certified Mail or secure electronic delivery (if consented) to ensure proof of delivery.

#### 4.6 Record Retention
1.  A copy of the filed Form 8282, proof of mailing to the IRS, and proof of delivery to the donor must be attached to the original asset record.
2.  These records must be retained in accordance with *GOV-POL-012 Document Retention and Destruction Policy* (Permanent Retention recommended for tax filings).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns the process; identifies "8282-Restricted" assets; prepares the form; ensures filing within 125 days. |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews Form 8282 for accuracy regarding sale price; provides Board-level oversight of asset disposition. |
| **Operational Officers (e.g., CISO)** | Notifies Finance immediately upon the disposal, destruction, or sale of any physical asset to check for 8282 restrictions. |
| **Development Director** | Ensures the copy of Form 8282 is delivered to the donor to maintain transparency and trust. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to file Form 8282 is a violation of federal tax law.

*   **IRS Penalties:** The Organization may be subject to a penalty of $50 per form for failure to file by the due date, up to higher amounts for intentional disregard of filing requirements.
*   **Donor Impact:** Failure to file may trigger IRS audits for the donor, severely damaging the Organization's reputation and future fundraising capability.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Employees found to have willfully neglected this procedure, resulting in penalties or loss of donor trust, are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.
