### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish a secure, verifiable, and legally compliant process for conducting membership votes via electronic transmission. This procedure is designed to satisfy **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.160(b)**, which requires that any electronic vote must be verifiable as having originated from the specific member entitled to vote. Furthermore, this protocol enforces **Article II, Section 2.3** of the Bylaws, which strictly prohibits proxy voting, ensuring that every vote cast is a direct act of the member.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   All **Voting Members** (Supporting Members and Honorary Members) in good standing.
*   The **Board Secretary** and **Governance Committee** responsible for election administration.
*   All binding votes submitted to the membership, including the Annual Election of Directors, Fundamental Actions (mergers/dissolution), and Bylaw amendments.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to non-binding polls, straw polls conducted during workshops, or Board of Directors meetings (which are covered under *GOV-PRC-026 Electronic Voting Consent Procedure*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Unique Authentication Token** | A cryptographic hash or unique link generated by the voting platform that allows only one specific user to access a ballot. |
| **Record Date** | The specific date established by the Board for determining which members are eligible to receive notice and vote (per Bylaws Art. II, § 2.6). |
| **Electronic Transmission** | A form of communication that is not directly involving the physical transmission of paper, creates a record that can be retained, retrieved, and reviewed, and may be directly reproduced in paper form (TBOC § 1.002). |
| **Spoiled Ballot** | A ballot that has been declared invalid due to technical error, identity verification failure, or duplicity. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 System Selection and Configuration
To comply with the "No Proxy" mandate (Bylaws § 2.3), the Organization must utilize a third-party electronic voting platform (e.g., ElectionBuddy, OpaVote) that meets the following security criteria:
1.  **Single-Vote Enforcement:** The system must technically prevent a single credential from casting more than one ballot.
2.  **Encryption:** All data in transit and at rest must be encrypted (TLS 1.2 or higher).
3.  **Audit Trail:** The system must generate a timestamped log of when the ballot was viewed and when the vote was cast, associated with the member's unique ID.
4.  **Anonymity (Optional but Recommended):** While the system must track *who* voted to ensure validity, the specific *choices* made should be anonymized from administrators unless the vote is by open roll call.

#### 4.2 Voter Roll Certification (The "Record Date" Snapshot)
1.  **Establish Record Date:** The Board must set a Record Date in accordance with Bylaws § 2.6.
2.  **Generate List:** The Secretary (or designee) shall export the list of Voting Members from the CRM/Membership Database as of the Record Date.
3.  **Data Validation:** The list must contain:
    *   Full Legal Name.
    *   Primary Email Address (this serves as the unique identifier for TBOC verification).
    *   Membership Class (Supporting vs. Honorary).
4.  **Certification:** The Secretary must sign (digitally or physically) a statement certifying this list as the "Official Voting Roll" for the specific election.

#### 4.3 Ballot Distribution and Identity Verification
1.  **Direct Transmission:** Ballots must be sent directly to the email address on record. Sending ballots to secondary emails or via general communication channels (e.g., Slack public channels) is prohibited.
2.  **Unique Credentials:** Each email must contain a **Unique Authentication Token** (link or code).
    *   *Note:* Generic links (e.g., a SurveyMonkey link open to anyone with the URL) are strictly prohibited as they fail the TBOC § 22.160(b) verification requirement.
3.  **Anti-Proxy Warning:** The ballot email must include the following disclaimer:
    > *"Warning: In accordance with Bylaws Article II, Section 2.3, proxy voting is prohibited. This ballot link is assigned specifically to you. Forwarding this link to another individual to vote on your behalf is a violation of the Organization's Code of Ethics and will result in the invalidation of the vote."*

#### 4.4 The Voting Window
1.  **Opening:** As per Bylaws § 2.5, the electronic voting window opens immediately following the adjournment of the Annual Meeting (or as specified in the Notice for Special Meetings).
2.  **Duration:** The window shall remain open for exactly seven (7) days.
3.  **Reminders:** The system may be configured to send automated reminders to members who have not yet cast a vote.

#### 4.5 Tabulation and Verification
1.  **Quorum Check:** Upon close of the voting window, the Secretary must verify that the total number of votes cast (plus those attending in person, if a hybrid meeting) exceeds 50% of the Voting Members (Bylaws § 2.4).
2.  **Integrity Check:** The Governance Committee shall review the system logs for anomalies, such as:
    *   Multiple access attempts from suspicious IP addresses.
    *   Votes cast within seconds of ballot distribution (bot behavior).
3.  **Results Certification:** The Secretary and one other Officer (President or Treasurer) must certify the final results report.

#### 4.6 Record Retention
In compliance with TBOC § 22.353 and the *Document Retention Policy*:
1.  The **Official Voting Roll** must be retained for 3 years.
2.  The **System Audit Trail** (proving who voted) must be retained for 3 years.
3.  The **Final Tally Report** must be retained permanently in the Corporate Minute Book.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Acts as the Chief Election Officer; certifies the voting roll; configures the electronic ballot; certifies final results. |
| **Governance Committee** | Audits the election process for compliance with Bylaws; investigates reports of voting irregularities. |
| **CISO / IT Dept** | Vets the third-party voting vendor for security compliance; ensures email deliverability (DKIM/SPF). |
| **Voting Member** | Responsible for maintaining a current email address with the Organization; must cast their own vote personally. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Invalidation of Election:** Failure to adhere to the verification steps (e.g., using a non-secure survey tool) renders the election results voidable under Texas law.
*   **Member Discipline:** Any member found to have shared their authentication token to allow another person to vote (Proxy Violation) may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of membership, as defined in the *Member Expulsion Due Process Policy*.
*   **System Failure:** In the event of a catastrophic technical failure of the voting system, the Board may extend the voting window by up to 48 hours to resolve the issue, provided notice is sent to all members.
