### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, compliant, and accurate process for the calculation, verification, and distribution of employee compensation. This procedure ensures Study GRC Inc. complies with the **Texas Payday Law**, **Internal Revenue Service (IRS)** withholding requirements, **Texas Workforce Commission (TWC)** regulations, and **2 CFR § 200.430** regarding compensation charged to federal awards. It mandates the segregation of duties to prevent fraud and error in financial disbursements.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **W-2 Employees** (full-time, part-time, and temporary) of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Inclusions:** Salary, hourly wages, overtime, bonuses, and benefit deductions.
*   **Exclusions:** Payments to Independent Contractors (1099-NEC), which are processed via the *Accounts Payable Procedure* (FIN-PRC-020), and volunteer reimbursements.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Gross Pay** | Total compensation earned by an employee before any deductions or taxes. |
| **Net Pay** | The amount remaining after all withholdings (taxes, benefits, garnishments) are deducted from Gross Pay. |
| **FICA** | Federal Insurance Contributions Act taxes, comprising Social Security and Medicare. |
| **SUTA** | State Unemployment Tax Act. In Texas, this is paid to the Texas Workforce Commission (TWC). |
| **Garnishment** | A legal order requiring the employer to withhold a portion of an employee's wages to pay a debt (e.g., child support, tax levy). |
| **PAR** | Personnel Activity Report. A document certifying the distribution of time across specific cost objectives (grants), required by 2 CFR § 200.430. |
| **Pay Period** | The recurring schedule for payroll. Study GRC Inc. operates on a **Bi-Weekly** pay period (26 pay periods per year). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Pay Cycle and Deadlines
4.1.1 **Pay Frequency:** Employees are paid bi-weekly every other Friday. If a pay date falls on a federal banking holiday, payment is distributed on the preceding business day.
4.1.2 **Cut-Off:** The pay period ends at 11:59 PM on the Sunday prior to the pay date. All time and time-off requests must be submitted by employees no later than 10:00 AM on the following Monday.

#### 4.2 Timekeeping and Data Collection
4.2.1 **Time Submission:**
*   **Non-Exempt Employees:** Must record actual start and stop times for all hours worked using the designated Timekeeping System.
*   **Exempt Employees:** Must record "Exceptions Only" (e.g., PTO, Sick Leave) unless working on multiple federal grants, in which case positive time reporting is required per *Time and Effort Reporting Policy* (FIN-POL-045).
4.2.2 **Grant Allocation:** All employees must code their hours to the specific project, grant, or administrative code supported during that time block to ensure compliance with 2 CFR § 200.430.
4.2.3 **Manager Approval:** Supervisors must review and approve timecards by 5:00 PM on the Monday following the close of the pay period. Approvals constitute certification that the work was performed.

#### 4.3 Payroll Calculation and Processing
4.3.1 **Data Import:** The Payroll Administrator imports approved time data into the Payroll System.
4.3.2 **Gross-to-Net Calculation:** The system calculates:
*   **Gross Pay:** Base rate × hours (plus overtime at 1.5x for non-exempt hours > 40).
*   **Pre-Tax Deductions:** Health insurance, 401(k), FSA.
*   **Tax Withholdings:**
    *   **Federal Income Tax:** Based on current W-4 status.
    *   **FICA:** 6.2% Social Security (up to wage base) + 1.45% Medicare.
    *   **State Tax:** $0.00 for Texas residents. For remote employees residing outside Texas, the specific state income tax (SIT) for their nexus must be withheld.
*   **Post-Tax Deductions:** Garnishments, Roth 401(k), post-tax benefits.
4.3.3 **Employer Contributions:** The system calculates the employer's share of FICA, FUTA, and SUTA.

#### 4.4 Verification and Approval (Segregation of Duties)
4.4.1 **Preliminary Register Review:** The Payroll Administrator generates a "Preliminary Payroll Register" and "Variance Report" (comparing current vs. prior payroll).
4.4.2 **Variance Analysis:** Any variance in Gross Pay exceeding 5% for an individual or the organization must be investigated and justified.
4.4.3 **Final Approval:**
*   The Payroll Administrator submits the register to the **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)**.
*   Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V**, the CFO (or Treasurer in CFO's absence) must review and digitally sign off on the payroll batch before funds are released.
*   **Prohibition:** The individual who edits employee master data (e.g., pay rates, bank accounts) cannot be the sole releaser of the payroll batch.

#### 4.5 Distribution
4.5.1 **ACH Transmission:** Upon CFO approval, the ACH file is transmitted to the bank no later than Wednesday at 4:00 PM (2 days prior to pay date).
4.5.2 **Pay Stubs:** Electronic pay stubs are made available to employees via the Employee Self-Service (ESS) portal on the pay date, complying with **Texas Payday Law** requirements for earnings statements.

#### 4.6 Tax Remittance and Reporting
4.6.1 **Federal Deposit:** The Payroll Administrator must ensure federal tax deposits (Income Tax + Employee FICA + Employer FICA) are remitted via EFTPS in accordance with the IRS semi-weekly or monthly deposit schedule assigned to the Organization.
4.6.2 **Quarterly Filings:**
*   **IRS Form 941:** Filed by the last day of the month following the quarter end.
*   **TWC C-3:** Texas Employer's Quarterly Report filed with the Texas Workforce Commission.
4.6.3 **Annual Filings:**
*   **IRS Form 940:** FUTA reconciliation.
*   **W-2s:** Distributed to employees and filed with the SSA by January 31st.

#### 4.7 Record Retention
4.7.1 In accordance with the *Document Retention Policy* (GOV-POL-012) and **2 CFR § 200.334**, all payroll records, including timecards, PARs, tax filings, and registers, must be retained for a minimum of **three (3) years** from the date of submission of the final expenditure report for the relevant federal grant, or **four (4) years** for IRS tax records, whichever is longer.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Accurately record time, allocate hours to correct grant codes, and submit timecards by the deadline. Maintain current W-4 and Direct Deposit information. |
| **Supervisor** | Verify accuracy of hours and grant allocation; approve timecards by the deadline. |
| **Payroll Administrator** | Process payroll calculations, manage deductions, generate reports, remit taxes, and handle garnishments. |
| **Chief Financial Officer** | Review payroll variances, authorize final release of funds (ACH), and ensure liquidity for payroll funding. |
| **HR Manager** | Maintain employee master data (pay rates, status changes, new hires) in the HRIS to ensure payroll accuracy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this procedure may result in:
*   **Employees:** Delayed payment or disciplinary action for timecard fraud.
*   **Organization:** IRS penalties for late deposits (up to 15%), TWC fines, or disallowance of costs by federal grantors due to improper time accounting.
*   **Legal:** Personal liability for "Responsible Persons" (Officers) for unpaid trust fund taxes (Trust Fund Recovery Penalty).

Any intentional falsification of payroll records is grounds for immediate termination and potential legal prosecution.
