### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally defensible process for disciplining, suspending, or terminating the charter of a Local Chapter affiliated with Study GRC Inc. This procedure mitigates organizational risk regarding brand reputation, financial liability, and community safety by defining the steps for **Probation** (remediation) and **Revocation** (severance).

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all officially chartered Local Chapters of Study GRC Inc. and their respective leadership teams. It governs actions taken in response to violations of the *Local Chapter Affiliation Agreement*, *Bylaws*, *Code of Conduct*, or applicable local, state, and federal laws.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Probation** | A temporary status wherein a Chapter is placed under heightened supervision with specific restrictions while completing a remediation plan. |
| **Revocation** | The permanent termination of the *Local Chapter Affiliation Agreement*, resulting in the dissolution of the Chapter's relationship with Study GRC Inc. |
| **Cure Period** | A specific timeframe (typically 30-90 days) granted to a Chapter to rectify identified deficiencies. |
| **Asset Repatriation** | The process of returning Chapter funds, data, and intellectual property to Study GRC Inc. HQ upon revocation. |
| **Egregious Violation** | An act posing immediate threat to safety, financial integrity, or legal standing (e.g., fraud, abuse of minors, criminal activity). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Identification of Deficiencies
Disciplinary action may be initiated based on, but not limited to, the following triggers:
1.  **Operational Failure:** Failure to submit annual reports, maintain minimum membership, or hold required meetings.
2.  **Financial Irregularity:** Failure to submit financial reports, unauthorized use of funds, or failure to comply with 501(c)(3) restrictions.
3.  **Conduct Violation:** Breach of the *Code of Conduct*, *Youth Protection Policy*, or *Conflict of Interest Policy*.
4.  **Brand Damage:** Unauthorized use of Intellectual Property (IP) or public communications that severely damage the Organization's reputation.

#### 4.2 Emergency Suspension (Immediate Action)
In cases involving **Egregious Violations** (specifically those involving youth safety, active financial fraud, or criminal investigations):
1.  The **Executive Director (CEO)** has the authority to immediately suspend Chapter operations pending an investigation.
2.  **Notice:** The Chapter President must be notified immediately via email and certified mail.
3.  **Asset Freeze:** The **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** must immediately revoke Chapter access to bank accounts, payment processors, and CRM systems.
4.  **Investigation:** An internal or third-party investigation must commence within 48 hours of suspension.

#### 4.3 Probationary Status (Remediation)
If a violation is remediable (e.g., administrative backlog, minor policy breach), the Chapter will be placed on Probation.

**4.3.1 Notification of Probation**
The VP of Education & Training must issue a formal **Notice of Probation** to the Chapter leadership containing:
*   Specific citations of the violated policies or agreement clauses.
*   The duration of the Probationary Period (typically 90 days).
*   Restrictions imposed (e.g., inability to spend funds without HQ approval, suspension of event hosting).

**4.3.2 Remediation Plan (The "Cure")**
1.  HQ will provide a **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** outlining specific deliverables required to lift probation.
2.  Chapter leadership must acknowledge receipt and agreement to the CAP within five (5) business days.
3.  Failure to sign the CAP constitutes grounds for immediate Revocation.

**4.3.3 Monitoring**
During Probation, the Chapter must report progress to the VP of Education & Training on a bi-weekly basis.

#### 4.4 Charter Revocation
Revocation is the final measure taken when a Chapter fails to complete the CAP, commits an Egregious Violation, or is deemed insolvent.

**4.4.1 Recommendation for Revocation**
The Executive Director must submit a formal recommendation for revocation to the Board of Directors, including the investigation file and evidence of failed remediation.

**4.4.2 Board Vote**
Pursuant to the *Local Chapter Affiliation Agreement*, the Board of Directors must approve the revocation by a majority vote.

**4.4.3 Notice of Revocation**
Upon Board approval, a **Notice of Revocation** is sent to the Chapter. This notice serves as the formal termination of the Affiliation Agreement.

#### 4.5 Winding Up and Asset Repatriation
Upon issuance of the Notice of Revocation, the following steps must be executed within ten (10) business days:

**4.5.1 Financial Assets**
1.  The Chapter must transfer all remaining funds to Study GRC Inc. HQ.
2.  If the Chapter fails to comply, the CFO is authorized to work directly with the banking institution to seize funds based on the master banking agreement.

**4.5.2 Intellectual Property & Digital Assets**
1.  **Cease and Desist:** The former Chapter must immediately cease using the name "Study GRC," the logo, and all associated branding.
2.  **Domain/Social Media:** Administrative control of all social media accounts and domain names containing the Organization's marks must be transferred to HQ IT.
3.  **Data:** All member data (PII) must be securely transferred to HQ and purged from local devices in compliance with the *Data Retention and Destruction Policy*.

**4.5.3 Member Notification**
HQ will notify the Chapter's general membership of the dissolution and provide options for transferring their affiliation to the "At-Large" community or another nearby Chapter.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Final authority on Charter Revocation votes. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Authorizes Emergency Suspensions; recommends Revocation to the Board. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Manages the Probation process; drafts Corrective Action Plans (CAP); monitors compliance. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Executes financial freezes; manages asset repatriation; audits Chapter financials during investigations. |
| **Chapter President** | Responsible for communicating with HQ and executing the CAP or Winding Up procedures. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure by Chapter leadership to comply with the Revocation procedures, specifically regarding Asset Repatriation and IP cessation, will result in immediate legal action, including but not limited to civil litigation for trademark infringement and conversion of funds.

Study GRC Inc. reserves the right to report financial improprieties to the IRS and relevant state Attorneys General.
