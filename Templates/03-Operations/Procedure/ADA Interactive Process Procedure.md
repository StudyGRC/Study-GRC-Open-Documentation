### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, compliant workflow for the "Interactive Process" required by the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), the ADA Amendments Act (ADAAA), and Texas Labor Code Chapter 21. This document ensures that Study GRC Inc. engages in a timely, good-faith dialogue with qualified individuals with disabilities to identify and implement reasonable accommodations that enable them to perform essential job functions, barring undue hardship to the Organization.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **Individuals:** All current employees (full-time, part-time, temporary), job applicants, and volunteers acting in an operational capacity.
*   **Context:** Requests for accommodation related to physical or mental disabilities, including pregnancy-related conditions.
*   **Exclusions:** This procedure does not cover requests for religious accommodations (see *HR-POL-012 Religious Accommodation Policy*) or purely personal preferences not related to a disability.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Interactive Process** | A collaborative, good-faith dialogue between the employer and the individual to clarify what the individual needs and identify the appropriate reasonable accommodation. |
| **Reasonable Accommodation** | A modification or adjustment to a job, the work environment, or the way things are usually done that enables a qualified individual with a disability to enjoy an equal employment opportunity. |
| **Essential Functions** | The fundamental job duties that an individual must be able to perform, with or without reasonable accommodation. |
| **Undue Hardship** | An action requiring significant difficulty or expense when considered in light of factors such as the Organization's size, financial resources, and the nature and structure of its operation. |
| **Qualified Individual** | An individual who satisfies the requisite skill, experience, education, and other job-related requirements of the position and who, with or without reasonable accommodation, can perform the essential functions of the position. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Initiation of Request
The Interactive Process is triggered when an employee or applicant indicates a need for an adjustment or change at work for a reason related to a medical condition.
1.  **Notification:** The individual may make the request orally or in writing to their immediate supervisor, the VP of Operations, or the Executive Director. No specific legal language (e.g., "ADA") is required.
2.  **Managerial Duty:** If a manager receives a request, they **must** forward it to the VP of Operations (or designated HR representative) within **two (2) business days**. Managers are strictly prohibited from summarily denying a request or asking for detailed medical history.
3.  **Initial Acknowledgment:** The VP of Operations will contact the individual within **three (3) business days** of receipt to acknowledge the request and explain the Interactive Process.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Information Gathering & Documentation
1.  **Request Form:** The individual will be asked to complete the *Reasonable Accommodation Request Form (HR-FRM-018a)* to document the specific limitation and the suggested accommodation.
2.  **Medical Inquiry (If applicable):**
    *   If the disability or need for accommodation is not obvious, Study GRC Inc. may request limited medical documentation from the individual’s healthcare provider.
    *   The VP of Operations will provide the *Medical Inquiry Form (HR-FRM-018b)*, which attaches the employee's Job Description.
    *   **Privacy Rule:** Medical information must **never** be placed in the employee’s general personnel file. It must be stored in a separate, secure, confidential medical file (physical or encrypted digital folder) accessible only to authorized HR/Legal personnel.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Exploration of Options
1.  **Collaborative Meeting:** The VP of Operations, the Manager (for operational context only), and the Employee will meet (virtually) to discuss:
    *   The specific limitations imposed by the disability.
    *   How these limitations affect the performance of *Essential Functions*.
    *   Potential accommodations.
2.  **Remote-First Considerations:** Given the Organization's remote nature, exploration should include:
    *   Assistive technology (screen readers, speech-to-text).
    *   Hardware modifications (ergonomic equipment).
    *   Schedule flexibility (for medical appointments).
    *   Communication method adjustments (e.g., text-based vs. video).
3.  **Alternative Suggestions:** If the requested accommodation is not feasible, the Organization will propose alternative accommodations that would be effective.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Selection and Decision
1.  **Employer Discretion:** Study GRC Inc. has the ultimate discretion to choose between effective accommodations. The Organization is not required to provide the *specific* accommodation requested by the employee if an alternative is equally effective and less burdensome.
2.  **Undue Hardship Analysis:** If an accommodation poses an Undue Hardship, the VP of Operations must document the specific reasons (financial, operational disruption) and obtain approval from the Executive Director and Legal Counsel before denial.
3.  **Formal Notice:** The decision will be communicated in writing via the *Accommodation Determination Letter (HR-FRM-018c)*, indicating approval, modification, or denial.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Implementation
1.  **Procurement:** If equipment or software is required, the VP of Operations will coordinate with the CFO and CISO to procure items in compliance with the *Procurement Policy (FIN-POL-022)*.
2.  **Installation/Training:** The CISO or IT Support will assist in installing software or configuring systems, ensuring compatibility with the Organization’s security standards.
3.  **Timeline:** Implementation should occur as expeditiously as possible. Delays must be communicated to the employee with an estimated completion date.

#### 4.6 Phase VI: Monitoring and Review
1.  **Check-In:** The VP of Operations will schedule a follow-up meeting **30 days** after implementation to ensure the accommodation is effective.
2.  **Ongoing Adjustment:** If the accommodation is no longer effective or if the employee’s medical condition changes, the Interactive Process may be reopened.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee / Applicant** | Initiate the request; provide truthful and timely medical documentation; engage in the dialogue in good faith; test proposed accommodations. |
| **Manager** | Recognize accommodation requests and immediately escalate to HR/Ops; participate in defining "Essential Functions"; implement approved operational changes; maintain strict confidentiality. |
| **VP of Operations (HR)** | Own the Interactive Process; maintain confidential medical files; analyze undue hardship; issue formal determination letters. |
| **Executive Director** | Provide final approval for denials based on Undue Hardship; ensure budgetary resources are available for compliance. |
| **CISO** | Vet assistive technology for security compliance; assist in the deployment of digital accessibility tools. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Legal Liability:** Failure to engage in the Interactive Process is a violation of federal and state law and exposes the Organization to significant liability, regardless of whether a reasonable accommodation actually existed.
*   **Retaliation Prohibited:** Retaliation against an individual for requesting an accommodation is strictly prohibited. Any employee or manager found engaging in retaliation will face immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Confidentiality:** All medical information obtained during this process is confidential. Breaching this confidentiality is a violation of the ADA and Organization policy.
