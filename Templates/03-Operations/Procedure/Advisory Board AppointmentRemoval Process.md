### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, transparent, and compliant process for the nomination, vetting, appointment, management, and removal of members of the Study GRC Inc. Advisory Board.

This document ensures compliance with **Article VI, Section 6.2** of the Bylaws, specifically regarding the separation of governance authority from advisory influence, and enforces the strict communication protocols required between Operational Officers and Advisors to prevent "shadow governance."

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **The Board of Directors:** Who hold sole authority to appoint and remove Advisors.
*   **Operational Officers:** Who must adhere to strict communication protocols when interacting with Advisors.
*   **Prospective and Current Advisory Board Members.**

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to statutory Board Committees (e.g., Finance Committee, Audit Committee) defined in Bylaws Section 6.1, which must be chaired by a voting Director.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Advisory Board** | A body of volunteers appointed to provide non-binding strategic advice, technical expertise, or industry insights. They possess **no voting rights** and no legal authority to bind the Organization. |
| **Voting Director** | A member of the governing Board of Directors with fiduciary duties and voting power. |
| **Operational Officer** | Individuals managing day-to-day execution (e.g., Executive Director, CISO, VP of Education) as defined in Bylaws Article V. |
| **Facilitated Communication** | The mandatory requirement that all formal interactions between Operational Officers and Advisors be arranged, overseen, or explicitly authorized by a Voting Director. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Nomination and Eligibility
1.  **Eligibility:**
    *   Candidates must possess specific expertise relevant to the Organization’s mission (e.g., GRC subject matter experts, fundraising professionals, education specialists).
    *   **Prohibition:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Section 6.2**, individuals currently serving as Voting Directors are **not** eligible for appointment to the Advisory Board.
2.  **Nomination:**
    *   Any Voting Director in good standing may nominate a candidate.
    *   Nominations must be submitted to the Secretary in writing (email or secure portal) using the *Advisory Board Nomination Form*.
    *   The nomination must include the candidate’s CV/Resume and a brief statement justifying the strategic value of the appointment.

#### 4.2 Vetting and Due Diligence
1.  **Conflict of Interest Review:**
    *   The candidate must complete a *Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statement* prior to appointment.
    *   The Board (or Governance Committee) reviews the disclosure to ensure no conflicts exist that would jeopardize the Organization’s 501(c)(3) status or reputation.
2.  **Background Check:**
    *   If the Advisor will have access to internal systems or attend events with youth participants, they must undergo a background check consistent with the *Background Check Policy (HR-POL-024)*.
3.  **Interview:**
    *   At least one Voting Director (preferably the President or the nominator) must interview the candidate to align expectations regarding time commitment and the non-binding nature of the role.

#### 4.3 Appointment
1.  **Board Vote:**
    *   Appointment requires a majority vote of the Board of Directors at a meeting where a quorum is present.
2.  **Term of Service:**
    *   Advisors are appointed for a term of **one (1) year**, renewable annually at the discretion of the Board.
3.  **Appointment Letter:**
    *   Upon approval, the Secretary issues an *Advisory Board Appointment Letter*.
    *   The candidate must sign and return the letter, acknowledging:
        *   They have no voting rights.
        *   They have no authority to incur expenses on behalf of the Organization.
        *   Adherence to the *Code of Ethics* and *Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)*.

#### 4.4 Operational Communication Protocols (Mandatory)
Pursuant to **Bylaws Section 6.2**, the following strict protocols apply to prevent operational interference:

1.  **Facilitation Requirement:**
    *   Operational Officers (including the Executive Director) are prohibited from formally soliciting advice or direction from the Advisory Board without prior approval.
    *   Requests for Advisory Board input must be routed through the Board President or a designated Director liaison.
2.  **Meeting Attendance:**
    *   Advisors may only attend Board meetings or Operational meetings by specific invitation.
3.  **Mandatory Reporting:**
    *   If an Operational Officer engages in substantive communication with an Advisor (e.g., a scheduled call regarding curriculum strategy), the Officer **must** provide a briefing to the Board of Directors.
    *   This briefing must be logged in the *Officer-Advisory Board Communication Log (GOV-REC-053)* and submitted via email or at the next Board meeting.

#### 4.5 Resignation and Removal
1.  **Resignation:**
    *   An Advisor may resign at any time by providing written notice to the Board Secretary.
2.  **Removal:**
    *   **At-Will Status:** Advisors serve at the pleasure of the Board. The Board may remove an Advisor at any time, with or without cause, by a majority vote.
    *   **Automatic Removal:** An Advisor is automatically removed if they:
        *   Become a Voting Director.
        *   Violate the NDA or Code of Ethics.
        *   Fail to disclose a material Conflict of Interest.
3.  **Offboarding:**
    *   Upon termination of service, the Secretary ensures all access to Organization systems (if any) is revoked immediately in accordance with *User Account Deprovisioning Procedure (IS-PRC-012)*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Sole authority to nominate, appoint, and remove Advisors. Reviews COI disclosures. |
| **Secretary** | Manages the nomination workflow, issues appointment letters, and maintains the *Advisory Board Roster*. |
| **Operational Officers** | Must seek Board approval before engaging Advisors and report all interactions to the Board. |
| **Advisory Board Member** | Provides strategic advice when requested; adheres to NDA and communication protocols; abstains from operational decision-making. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Operational Officers:** Failure to adhere to the communication protocols defined in Section 4.4 constitutes a violation of Board directives and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Advisors:** Attempting to exert binding authority, incurring unauthorized expenses, or violating confidentiality will result in immediate removal and potential legal action.
