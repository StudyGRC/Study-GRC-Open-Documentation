### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to operationalize the right of Voting Members to inspect the books and records of Study GRC Inc. in accordance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.351** and **Article II, Section 2.6** of the Organization’s Bylaws. This procedure establishes a standardized, secure, and transparent method for fulfilling inspection requests while strictly protecting the privacy of members, the security of the Organization’s data, and preventing the unauthorized use of contact information for solicitation.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All Voting Members (Supporting and Honorary) in good standing, the Board of Directors, and the Corporate Secretary.
*   **In Scope:**
    *   Minutes of proceedings of the members or Board.
    *   Books of account (financial records).
    *   Membership lists (names and email addresses only).
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Personnel records (HR), medical information, or background check results.
    *   Attorney-client privileged communications.
    *   Sensitive security configurations or CISO-restricted data (e.g., vulnerability reports).
    *   Requests made by non-voting Community Participants.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Proper Purpose** | A purpose reasonably related to the member's interest as a member of the Organization. Examples include ascertaining the financial condition of the entity or determining the propriety of management. Marketing, commercial solicitation, or harassment are **not** proper purposes. |
| **Good Standing** | A status wherein a member is current on all dues (if applicable), has no pending disciplinary actions, and is not suspended. |
| **Virtual Reading Room** | A secure, supervised video conferencing session (e.g., Zoom, Teams) where documents are shared via screen-sharing technology without file transfer, to facilitate inspection in a remote-first environment. |
| **Redaction** | The process of obscuring sensitive information (e.g., SSNs, bank account numbers, home addresses of minors) from documents prior to inspection. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Request Intake and Validation
1.  **Submission:** The Requesting Member must submit a completed **Member Record Inspection Request Form (GOV-FRM-031)** to the Corporate Secretary via the official governance email address.
2.  **Mandatory Disclosures:** Per Bylaws Section 2.6, the request must state the **specific purpose** of the inspection.
3.  **Validation:** Within five (5) business days of receipt, the Secretary must:
    *   Verify the member is in **Good Standing**.
    *   Validate that the stated purpose constitutes a **Proper Purpose** under TBOC § 22.351.
    *   Confirm the request is not for commercial, marketing, or solicitation purposes.
4.  **Denial:** If the request lacks a proper purpose or the member is not in good standing, the Secretary shall issue a written denial citing the specific deficiency.

#### 4.2 Preparation and Cost Estimation
1.  **Scope of Production:** The Secretary, in coordination with the Treasurer (for financial records) or CISO (for data security), will identify the records responsive to the request.
2.  **Redaction:** All records must be reviewed. The following data **must** be redacted prior to inspection:
    *   Social Security Numbers or Tax IDs of individuals.
    *   Bank account or credit card numbers.
    *   Home addresses or phone numbers of volunteers/staff (unless legally required).
    *   Any data pertaining to minors (K-12 participants) other than aggregate statistics.
3.  **Cost Estimate:** If the member requests physical copies or digital file transfers (rather than just inspection), the Organization may charge a reasonable fee for costs of labor and materials as permitted by **TBOC § 22.351**.
    *   The Secretary will provide a **Cost Estimate** to the member.
    *   Payment must be received prior to the release of copies.

#### 4.3 Inspection Execution (Remote-First Protocol)
Due to the remote-first nature of Study GRC Inc., inspections are primarily conducted virtually to ensure security.

1.  **Scheduling:** The Secretary and Member shall agree on a reasonable time for the inspection, typically within ten (10) business days of validation.
2.  **The Virtual Reading Room:**
    *   The inspection will occur via a secure video conference link controlled by the Organization.
    *   **Supervision:** The Secretary or a designated Board Director must be present on the call at all times.
    *   **Method:** Records will be displayed via **Screen Share**.
    *   **Recording Prohibition:** The Requesting Member is strictly prohibited from recording the session (video or audio).
3.  **Digital Copies (If Requested):**
    *   If the member demands copies, files will be provided via a secure, time-limited download link.
    *   **Watermarking:** All digital copies must be watermarked with the Requesting Member’s name and the date to deter unauthorized distribution.
    *   **NDA:** The member may be required to sign a **Non-Disclosure Agreement (LEG-AGR-005)** regarding proprietary operational data.

#### 4.4 Membership List Specifics
1.  **Restricted Use:** Per Bylaws Section 2.6, membership lists provided for inspection must only include Name, Primary Email, and Voting Class.
2.  **Anti-Solicitation:** The Requesting Member must sign a **Member Inspection Access Restriction Use Agreement** confirming the list will not be used for marketing, commercial purposes, or unauthorized solicitation.

#### 4.5 Transparency Notification
1.  **Community Notice:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.6**, upon granting access to the membership list, the Secretary must notify **all** Voting Members that:
    *   A request was made (identifying the Requesting Member).
    *   The stated purpose of the request.
    *   That the request was fulfilled in compliance with the Bylaws.
2.  **Timing:** This notification should be sent via the Organization’s official communication channel within 48 hours of the inspection.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requesting Member** | Submits written demand with proper purpose; pays applicable costs; adheres to non-solicitation and confidentiality rules. |
| **Corporate Secretary** | Receives requests; validates standing/purpose; supervises inspections; manages redaction; issues community notifications. |
| **Treasurer** | Assists in retrieving and preparing financial records for inspection. |
| **CISO** | Ensures the Virtual Reading Room technology is secure; approves watermarking and file transfer methods. |
| **Board of Directors** | Arbitrates disputes regarding "Proper Purpose" denials. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Misuse of Information:** Any member found using inspected records (specifically membership lists) for commercial purposes, marketing, or harassment will be subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including **termination of membership** (Bylaws Art. II) and potential legal action for damages.
*   **Unauthorized Recording:** Recording a Virtual Reading Room session without consent is a violation of this procedure and the Code of Conduct.
*   **Statutory Compliance:** Failure by the Organization to allow inspection for a proper purpose may subject the Organization to legal penalties under TBOC.
