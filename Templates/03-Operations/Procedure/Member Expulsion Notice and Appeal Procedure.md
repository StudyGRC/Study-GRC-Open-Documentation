### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally defensible, and equitable process for the involuntary termination (expulsion) of a Member from Study GRC Inc. This document ensures that the Organization adheres to the principles of **Due Process**—specifically providing adequate notice and a fair opportunity to be heard—before depriving any individual of membership rights or privileges. This procedure mitigates legal risks associated with wrongful termination claims and preserves the integrity of the community ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all classes of membership defined in **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1**:
*   Community Participants (Non-Voting)
*   Supporting Members (Voting)
*   Honorary Members (Voting)

**Exclusions:**
*   **Removal of Directors:** Governed specifically by **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**.
*   **Employment Termination:** Governed by the Employee Handbook and HR policies.
*   **Voluntary Resignation:** Governed by the Membership Policy Manual.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Respondent** | The Member against whom a complaint has been lodged or disciplinary action is being considered. |
| **Cause** | Justification for expulsion, including but not limited to: violation of the Bylaws, Code of Ethics, or Community Guidelines; conduct injurious to the Organization's reputation; or failure to pay dues (if applicable). |
| **Summary Suspension** | Immediate, temporary suspension of membership rights pending a full investigation, utilized only when there is an imminent threat to safety, assets, or compliance. |
| **Hearing Body** | The group designated to review evidence and make a determination. By default, this is the Board of Directors, unless delegated to an Ethics Committee. |
| **Notice of Intent** | A formal written communication detailing the allegations and the Organization's intent to consider expulsion. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Initiation and Investigation
1.  **Complaint Filing:** Any Voting Member, Director, or Officer may submit a formal complaint regarding a Member’s conduct to the Board Secretary or the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).
2.  **Initial Review:** The Board (or designated Committee) shall conduct a preliminary review within five (5) business days to determine if the complaint, if true, constitutes **Cause** for expulsion.
3.  **Summary Suspension (Optional):** If the alleged conduct involves:
    *   Sexual harassment or misconduct;
    *   Threats to the safety of minors (Youth Protection);
    *   Theft or fraud; or
    *   Active compromise of IT security;
    The President or Executive Director may issue a **Summary Suspension**, immediately revoking access to Organization systems pending the outcome of this procedure.

#### 4.2 Notice of Intent to Expel
If the initial review validates the complaint, the Secretary must issue a **Notice of Intent to Expel** to the Respondent via the email address on record (per **Bylaws Section 2.6**). The Notice must include:
1.  **Specific Charges:** A clear statement of the alleged conduct and the specific Bylaw, Policy, or Ethical Standard violated.
2.  **Evidence Summary:** A summary of the evidence supporting the allegations (redacted as necessary to protect whistleblowers).
3.  **Right to Hearing:** A statement informing the Respondent of their right to respond in writing or request a virtual hearing.
4.  **Deadline:** The Respondent must reply within **fifteen (15) calendar days** of the Notice transmission. Failure to respond constitutes a waiver of the right to a hearing and acceptance of the expulsion.

#### 4.3 Response and Hearing
1.  **Written Response:** The Respondent may submit a written defense, witness statements, or mitigating evidence to the Board Secretary prior to the deadline.
2.  **Hearing Request:** If the Respondent requests a hearing, it shall be scheduled to occur via a secure virtual meeting platform (e.g., Zoom/Teams) no less than 10 days and no more than 30 days after the request is received.
3.  **Hearing Conduct:**
    *   The hearing shall be conducted in **Executive Session** (confidential).
    *   The Respondent shall have up to thirty (30) minutes to present their defense.
    *   The Respondent may not have legal counsel present during the internal hearing unless approved by the Board in advance; however, they may consult counsel outside the meeting.
    *   The Hearing Body may ask clarifying questions.

#### 4.4 Deliberation and Decision
1.  **Standard of Proof:** The Hearing Body shall determine if it is "more likely than not" (preponderance of the evidence) that the violation occurred.
2.  **Voting Requirement:**
    *   Expulsion requires an affirmative vote of **two-thirds (2/3)** of the Directors present at a meeting where a quorum is established.
    *   The Board may opt for lesser sanctions (e.g., censure, probation, suspension) by a simple majority vote.
3.  **Finality:** The decision of the Board is final and binding.

#### 4.5 Notification of Decision
1.  **Written Notice:** Within five (5) business days of the decision, the Secretary shall send a **Notice of Determination** to the Respondent.
2.  **Content:** The notice shall state the final decision, the effective date of expulsion (if applicable), and confirmation that all access to Organization resources has been revoked.
3.  **Record Keeping:** All notices, evidence, minutes of the hearing, and the final decision must be securely archived in the Organization’s legal records for a period defined in the **Records Retention Schedule** (typically 7 years).

#### 4.6 Post-Expulsion Actions
1.  **System Access:** The CISO shall ensure all digital access (Slack, Email, GitHub, Voting Systems) is terminated immediately upon the effective date.
2.  **Dues:** Expelled members are not entitled to a refund of dues or contributions previously paid, as per the **Membership Policy Manual**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Serves as the Hearing Body; makes final determination on expulsion via 2/3 vote. |
| **Secretary** | Manages the procedural timeline, issues formal Notices (Intent/Determination), and maintains the official record. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | May attempt mediation prior to formal expulsion proceedings (for non-egregious offenses) and assists in the investigation. |
| **CISO** | Executes technical revocation of access upon Summary Suspension or Final Expulsion. |
| **Respondent** | Responsible for maintaining a valid email address for notice and responding within the designated timelines. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly adhere to this procedure—particularly regarding Notice and the Opportunity to be Heard—may render an expulsion decision void and expose the Organization to legal liability. Directors or Officers who willfully bypass this Due Process procedure may be subject to removal for cause under **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**.
