### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized methodology for recording, tracking, remediating, and formally closing audit findings identified during internal audits, external financial audits, federal Single Audits, and regulatory examinations. This procedure ensures Study GRC Inc. complies with **2 CFR § 200.511** (Audit findings follow-up) and maintains a robust internal control environment by guaranteeing that identified deficiencies are promptly and effectively corrected.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Departments, Operational Officers, and employees of Study GRC Inc. It covers all findings, observations, and recommendations arising from:
*   Annual Financial Statement Audits.
*   Federal Single Audits (Uniform Guidance).
*   Internal Audits and Compliance Reviews.
*   Grantor Monitoring Visits.
*   IT Security Assessments (e.g., Penetration Tests).
*   Regulatory Agency Examinations (e.g., IRS, DOL).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit Finding** | A deficiency, significant deficiency, or material weakness in internal controls, or an instance of non-compliance with laws, regulations, or grant terms identified by an auditor. |
| **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** | A documented plan detailing the specific steps the Organization will take to remedy a finding, including the person responsible and the anticipated completion date. |
| **Questioned Cost** | A cost that is questioned by the auditor because of an alleged violation of a provision of a law, regulation, contract, grant, or other agreement or document governing the expenditure of funds. |
| **Audit Finding Register** | The centralized digital log (e.g., secure database or spreadsheet) used to track the lifecycle of all findings. |
| **Process Owner** | The Operational Officer or Manager responsible for the specific business area where the finding occurred. |
| **Management Decision** | The evaluation by the Federal awarding agency or pass-through entity of the audit findings and corrective action plan and the issuance of a written decision to the auditee. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Intake and Recording
Upon receipt of a draft or final audit report, the following steps must occur within **five (5) business days**:

1.  **Receipt & Review:** The **CFO** or designated **Audit Liaison** receives the report and identifies all distinct findings.
2.  **Risk Rating:** Each finding is assigned a risk rating based on the Organization's Risk Matrix:
    *   **High:** Material weakness, significant financial impact, legal violation, or immediate security threat.
    *   **Medium:** Significant deficiency, policy violation, or operational inefficiency.
    *   **Low:** Minor control gap or housekeeping item.
3.  **Registration:** The Audit Liaison enters the finding into the **Audit Finding Register**. Required data fields include:
    *   Audit Source & Date.
    *   Finding Reference Number (from the report).
    *   Description of Deficiency.
    *   Risk Rating.
    *   Assigned Process Owner.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Root Cause Analysis and CAP Development
Within **ten (10) business days** of registration:

1.  **Root Cause Analysis:** The **Process Owner** must investigate *why* the issue occurred (e.g., lack of policy, human error, software failure) rather than just addressing the symptom.
2.  **Drafting the CAP:** The Process Owner drafts a Corrective Action Plan (CAP) in accordance with **2 CFR § 200.511(c)**. The CAP must include:
    *   Specific action steps to correct the deficiency.
    *   Name(s) of the contact person responsible for corrective action.
    *   Anticipated completion date.
    *   If the Organization disagrees with the finding, a detailed explanation and specific reasons for the disagreement.
3.  **Approval:**
    *   **Low/Medium Risk:** Approved by the Executive Director (CEO).
    *   **High Risk:** Must be reviewed and approved by the **Audit & Risk Committee**.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Remediation Implementation
1.  **Execution:** The Process Owner executes the steps defined in the CAP.
2.  **Documentation:** Evidence of remediation (e.g., new policy documents, screenshots of configuration changes, training logs, journal entries) must be collected and saved to the secure Audit Evidence Repository.
3.  **Status Updates:** The Process Owner must update the Audit Finding Register status to "In Progress" and provide monthly progress notes for the Audit & Risk Committee.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Verification and Testing
Once the Process Owner marks a finding as "Complete":

1.  **Internal Validation:** The **Internal Auditor** or **Compliance Officer** (independent of the process owner) reviews the evidence.
2.  **Testing:**
    *   **Document Review:** Verifying that required documents exist and are approved.
    *   **Re-testing:** For High-Risk findings, the Internal Auditor must perform a walkthrough or sample test to ensure the control is operating effectively.
3.  **Outcome:**
    *   **Pass:** Finding moves to Phase V.
    *   **Fail:** Finding is returned to Phase III with notes on why the remediation was insufficient.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Closure
1.  **Formal Closure:**
    *   **Internal/Low Risk:** Closed by the CFO/Compliance Officer.
    *   **External/High Risk:** Requires formal sign-off by the **Audit & Risk Committee** during a scheduled meeting.
2.  **External Validation (If applicable):** For findings from external bodies (e.g., Single Audit), the finding remains "Open - Pending External Validation" until the external auditor reviews the prior year's findings during the *next* audit cycle and marks them as "Corrected" in the Summary Schedule of Prior Audit Findings.

#### 4.6 Phase VI: Reporting
1.  **Quarterly Reporting:** The CFO presents a "State of Audit Compliance" report to the Board of Directors, summarizing:
    *   Total Open Findings vs. Closed Findings.
    *   Aging of Open Findings (30/60/90+ days).
    *   Status of High-Risk items.
2.  **Transparency:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Transparency)**, if findings relate to public financial statements or Form 990, the resolution status may be summarized for the Transparency Page if deemed necessary by the Board to maintain public trust.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Provides oversight of the audit process; approves CAPs for High-Risk findings; authorizes final closure of material weaknesses. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures operational resources are available to remediate findings; holds Process Owners accountable for CAP deadlines. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns the Audit Finding Register; acts as the primary liaison with external auditors; ensures 2 CFR 200 compliance. |
| **Process Owner** | Performs root cause analysis; drafts the CAP; implements the fix; provides evidence of remediation. |
| **Internal Auditor / Compliance Officer** | Validates that remediation is effective before closing the finding; conducts independent testing. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to promptly remediate audit findings, particularly those related to federal awards, constitutes a violation of **2 CFR § 200.303 (Internal Controls)** and may result in the suspension of grant funding, "High Risk" auditee status, or termination of employment for negligence in fiduciary duties.

**Bylaws Reference:** Per **Article VI**, the Audit & Risk Committee has the authority to investigate any matter brought to its attention with full access to all books, records, facilities, and personnel of the Organization.
