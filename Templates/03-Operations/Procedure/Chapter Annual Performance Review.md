### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, objective framework for evaluating the operational health, mission alignment, and regulatory compliance of all Study GRC Inc. chartered chapters. This review process ensures that every chapter contributes effectively to the Organization’s purpose of fostering a GRC ecosystem and removing financial barriers (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3), while strictly adhering to financial integrity and youth protection standards. This process is designed not merely for oversight, but to identify chapters requiring additional support and to drive continuous improvement across the network.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all officially chartered Local Chapters of Study GRC Inc. regardless of geographic location or size.
*   **Inclusions:** Chapter Boards, Chapter Officers, and HQ Operations Staff.
*   **Exclusions:** Informal study groups or unchartered community meetups.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Annual Chapter Report (ACR)** | The mandatory data submission packet required from every chapter, detailing financials, activities, and membership data. |
| **Good Standing** | A status signifying a chapter has met all financial, legal, and operational requirements and is authorized to use the Study GRC brand. |
| **PIP** | Performance Improvement Plan; a formal remediation strategy for chapters falling below minimum standards. |
| **Youth Protection Compliance** | Verification that all chapter volunteers interacting with minors have passed background checks and adhered to the "Two-Deep Leadership" policy. |
| **Fiscal Year** | The calendar year (January 1 – December 31) as defined in Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Review Cycle Timeline
The Annual Performance Review follows the Organization's Fiscal Year.
1.  **November 15:** HQ distributes the Annual Chapter Report (ACR) template and self-assessment rubric to Chapter Presidents.
2.  **January 31:** Deadline for Chapters to submit the ACR and all required supporting documentation.
3.  **February 1 – 28:** HQ Operations and Finance teams conduct the review and scoring process.
4.  **March 15:** Performance determination letters and (if applicable) PIPs are issued to Chapters.

#### 4.2 Submission Requirements (The ACR)
Chapter Presidents must submit the following via the secure Chapter Portal. Failure to submit by the deadline results in an automatic "Probationary" status.

**4.2.1 Financial Integrity Artifacts**
*   Year-end Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet).
*   Statement of Activity (Income/Expense), specifically highlighting any funds raised or spent.
*   Bank statements for December 31st to verify reported balances.
*   *Note:* Per Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3, chapters must demonstrate they have not erected financial barriers to entry (e.g., charging mandatory high fees for core resources).

**4.2.2 Operational & Mission Artifacts**
*   **Activity Log:** List of events held, including topic, attendance count, and format (virtual/in-person).
*   **Membership Roster:** Current list of active participants.
*   **Youth Protection Log:** Confirmation of background check completion for all volunteers involved in K-12 outreach, and signed acknowledgments of the Code of Conduct.

#### 4.3 HQ Review and Scoring Methodology
HQ Staff will evaluate submissions against the **Chapter Health Matrix**.

**4.3.1 Compliance Pass/Fail Gate**
Before scoring, the chapter must pass the "Critical Compliance Gate." A failure in any of the following triggers immediate intervention:
*   Evidence of financial impropriety or failure to report funds.
*   Violation of Youth Protection policies (e.g., allowing unchecked adults unsupervised access to minors).
*   Violation of Non-Profit status (e.g., political lobbying, private inurement).

**4.3.2 Performance Scoring (100 Point Scale)**
*   **Mission Impact (40 points):** Frequency of events, quality of educational content, alignment with "Zero-Cost" resource mandate.
*   **Community Engagement (30 points):** Growth in membership, active participation rates, diversity of attendees.
*   **Governance & Admin (30 points):** Timeliness of reporting, proper use of Brand/IP, succession planning for local officers.

#### 4.4 Performance Classifications
Based on the review, chapters will be assigned one of the following statuses:

**4.4.1 Exemplary (Score 90-100)**
*   **Status:** Good Standing.
*   **Outcome:** Eligible for "Chapter of the Year" awards, priority access to HQ funding/grants, and invitation to mentor other chapters.

**4.4.2 Satisfactory (Score 70-89)**
*   **Status:** Good Standing.
*   **Outcome:** Charter renewed for one year. Standard support from HQ.

**4.4.3 Needs Improvement (Score 50-69)**
*   **Status:** Probationary (Level 1).
*   **Outcome:** Charter renewed provisionally. The Chapter must execute a **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** with specific goals to be met within 6 months. Voting rights in organizational matters may be suspended.

**4.4.4 Unsatisfactory / Non-Compliant (Score <50 or Critical Fail)**
*   **Status:** Probationary (Level 2) or Revocation.
*   **Outcome:** Immediate suspension of operations.
    *   If remediation is possible: A strict 90-day PIP is implemented with direct HQ oversight.
    *   If remediation is unlikely or risk is high: Initiation of the **Chapter Charter Revocation Procedure**.

#### 4.5 Feedback and Remediation
1.  **Notification:** HQ will send a formal "Letter of Determination" to the Chapter Board detailing the score, status, and specific feedback.
2.  **PIP Meeting:** For chapters classified as "Needs Improvement" or lower, a mandatory meeting between the Chapter President and the VP of Education & Training must occur within 14 days of notification to sign the PIP.
3.  **Appeals:** A Chapter may appeal a classification of "Unsatisfactory" by submitting a written petition to the Executive Director within 10 days of receipt, providing new evidence to refute the findings.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chapter President** | Compiles and submits the Annual Chapter Report (ACR) accurately and on time. Disseminates feedback to local officers. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Owns the review process. Evaluates mission alignment and educational output. Approves PIPs. |
| **Treasurer (HQ)** | Reviews chapter financial submissions for irregularities, fraud indicators, and 501(c)(3) compliance. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Reviews community engagement metrics and culture/inclusivity feedback. |
| **Executive Director** | Final approving authority for Charter Revocations or denials of appeal. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Falsification of Data:** Any chapter found to have intentionally falsified data in the ACR (financials, attendance, or safety checks) will face immediate Charter Revocation and the individual officers responsible may face legal action and permanent ban from the Organization.
*   **Failure to Submit:** Non-submission by the January 31st deadline results in an automatic suspension of Chapter funding and brand usage rights until the report is received and processed.
