### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish a standardized method for obtaining the "floor" (the exclusive right to speak) during virtual meetings of Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures equitable participation, prevents chaotic audio overlap, and maintains strict adherence to *Robert’s Rules of Order Newly Revised* (RONR) in a remote environment, as authorized by **Article II, Section 2.5** and **Article III, Section 3.4** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all formal business meetings conducted via electronic communications technology, including:
*   Regular and Special Meetings of the Board of Directors.
*   Annual and Special Meetings of the Voting Membership.
*   Committee meetings where formal business is being transacted.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not strictly apply to informal working sessions, brainstorming workshops, or social events, unless invoked by the Presiding Officer to restore order.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **The Floor** | The exclusive right of a member to be heard at a specific time. Only one person may hold the floor at a time. |
| **Presiding Officer** | The individual chairing the meeting (usually the President or Committee Chair). |
| **Digital Hand** | The software feature within the meeting platform (e.g., Zoom, Teams) that signals a request to speak. |
| **Incidental Motion** | A motion that arises out of the business at hand and must be decided immediately (e.g., Point of Order), often allowing for interruption. |
| **The Queue** | The ordered list of members waiting to speak, managed by the Presiding Officer or Secretary. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 General Audio/Video Standards
1.  **Mute on Entry:** All participants must join the meeting with microphones muted.
2.  **Mute Default:** Participants must remain muted at all times unless they have been explicitly recognized by the Presiding Officer.
3.  **Video Preference:** To facilitate visual cues, Directors and Members are encouraged to keep video cameras enabled when speaking, bandwidth permitting.

#### 4.2 Seeking Recognition (Standard Queue)
To obtain the floor for standard debate, questions, or main motions:
1.  **Digital Hand Raise:** The member must utilize the "Raise Hand" feature in the meeting platform. This is the primary and official method of seeking recognition.
2.  **Chat Backup:** If the "Raise Hand" feature is technically unavailable, the member may type "Request to Speak" or "Hand Up" in the meeting chat window.
3.  **Physical Hand:** Raising a physical hand on video is **discouraged** due to latency and screen visibility limits. It should only be used as a last resort if technical features fail.
4.  **Wait for Recognition:** The member must not unmute until the Presiding Officer calls their name.

#### 4.3 Order of Precedence (The Queue)
The Presiding Officer shall recognize speakers in the following order of priority, consistent with *Robert's Rules*:
1.  **Maker of the Motion:** The member who made the motion currently under debate has the right to speak first.
2.  **Alternating Debate:** To the extent possible, the Presiding Officer shall alternate between proponents (speaking *for* the motion) and opponents (speaking *against* the motion).
3.  **Unheard Voices:** A member who has not yet spoken on the pending question shall be recognized before a member who has already spoken twice on the same question.
4.  **Chronological Order:** Among those with equal priority, recognition is granted in the order the Digital Hands were raised.

#### 4.4 Interrupting the Floor (Priority Motions)
Certain conditions allow a member to interrupt a speaker. In a virtual setting, this must be handled carefully to avoid chaos.
1.  **Valid Interruptions:** Points of Order, Points of Personal Privilege (e.g., "I cannot hear the speaker"), or Appeal from the Decision of the Chair.
2.  **Method of Interruption:**
    *   **Primary:** Type "POINT OF ORDER" or "POINT OF PRIVILEGE" in all caps in the chat window.
    *   **Secondary (Audio):** If the chat is not immediately seen, the member may unmute and state clearly and briefly: *"Mr./Madam President, Point of Order."* The member must then immediately mute and await recognition.
3.  **Resolution:** The Presiding Officer must immediately stop the current speaker and address the priority motion before returning to the main queue.

#### 4.5 Managing the Floor
1.  **Announcement:** The Presiding Officer will announce the current speaker and the next person in the queue (e.g., *"Director Smith has the floor; Director Jones is on deck."*).
2.  **Time Limits:** If debate time limits are in effect (e.g., 2 minutes per speaker), the Secretary or designated Timekeeper shall announce "30 seconds remaining" and "Time" via audio or chat.
3.  **Lowering Hands:** Upon being recognized, the member (or the meeting host) should lower the Digital Hand to keep the queue accurate.

#### 4.6 Yielding the Floor
1.  **Conclusion:** When a member has finished speaking, they shall state *"I yield the floor"* or *"I yield back"* and immediately mute their microphone.
2.  **Yielding to Questions:** If a member yields for a question, they retain the floor. The questioner asks the question and mutes. The holding member answers and continues.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Presiding Officer** | Monitors the Digital Hand queue and Chat; recognizes members in the correct order of precedence; rules on Points of Order. |
| **Secretary** | Records the names of members who speak for the minutes; assists the Chair in monitoring the Chat for priority interruptions. |
| **Meeting Moderator (Host)** | Technical oversight; authorized to force-mute participants who speak without recognition or transmit disruptive background noise. |
| **Voting Members / Directors** | Adhere to the protocol; utilize digital tools correctly; respect the rights of others to be heard. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Failure to comply** with this protocol disrupts the business of the Organization and violates the decorum required by the Bylaws.

*   **First Violation:** The Presiding Officer will issue a verbal warning and the Moderator may mute the offender.
*   **Repeated Violations:** The Presiding Officer may rule the member "out of order."
*   **Gross Misconduct:** In extreme cases of intentional disruption (e.g., "Zoombombing" or refusal to yield), the Presiding Officer may direct the Moderator to remove the participant from the virtual meeting room, pursuant to the authority granted to maintain order in **Bylaws Article III**.
