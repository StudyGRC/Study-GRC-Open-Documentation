### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish mandatory safety protocols for the use of electrical equipment and temporary wiring within Study GRC Inc. operations. This document is designed to prevent electrical shock, burns, fires, and electrocution hazards. It ensures organizational compliance with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910 Subpart S (Electrical)** and fulfills the Organization's duty of care to staff, volunteers, and event participants.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, contractors, and temporary staff.

**In Scope:**
*   **Physical Events:** Setup and operation of temporary computer labs, audio-visual equipment, and server racks at conferences, workshops, or training events.
*   **Storage & Logistics:** Handling of equipment in organizational storage facilities.
*   **Remote Work Guidance:** Baseline safety standards for home office environments where Organization-owned equipment is utilized.

**Exclusions:**
*   Maintenance of permanent building wiring or infrastructure at third-party venues (e.g., hotels, conference centers), which remains the responsibility of the venue owner.
*   High-voltage electrical work (Study GRC personnel are prohibited from performing work on circuits >600 volts).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Daisy-Chaining** | The prohibited practice of plugging one power strip or extension cord into another to extend reach or add outlets. |
| **GFCI** | Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter. A device that shuts off an electric power circuit when it detects that current is flowing along an unintended path, such as through water or a person. |
| **Qualified Person** | An individual who has received training in and has demonstrated skills and knowledge in the construction and operation of electric equipment and installations and the hazards involved (per OSHA 1910.399). |
| **Temporary Wiring** | Electrical wiring and connections used for a short duration (e.g., event setup) that are not part of the permanent structure. |
| **LOTO** | Lockout/Tagout. A safety procedure used to ensure that dangerous machines are properly shut off and not able to be started up again prior to the completion of maintenance. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. General Equipment Inspection
**Regulatory Reference:** OSHA 1910.303(b)(1)
All personnel must visually inspect electrical equipment before use.

1.  **Visual Check:** Inspect power cords, plugs, and casings for defects. Look for:
    *   Frayed, cut, or brittle insulation.
    *   Exposed wiring.
    *   Missing ground prongs (the third round pin).
    *   Cracked or damaged casings on power strips or laptop bricks.
2.  **Tag and Remove:** If equipment is damaged:
    *   **Do not use it.**
    *   Apply a "DO NOT USE" tag or label.
    *   Report the defect to the Event Lead or Director of Operations immediately for repair or disposal.

#### 4.2. Flexible Cords and Extension Cords
**Regulatory Reference:** OSHA 1910.305(g)
Improper use of extension cords is the leading cause of electrical citations and fires in temporary setups.

1.  **Prohibited Uses:**
    *   Do not use extension cords as a substitute for permanent wiring.
    *   Do not run cords through holes in walls, ceilings, or floors.
    *   Do not run cords through doorways or windows where they can be pinched.
    *   Do not attach cords to building surfaces using staples or nails.
2.  **Daisy-Chaining Ban:** Never plug a power strip (Relocatable Power Tap) into an extension cord, or one power strip into another. Power strips must be plugged directly into a wall outlet.
3.  **Trip Hazard Management:**
    *   Cords running across walkways must be secured using gaffer tape or rigid cable bridges/ramps.
    *   Cords must not be covered by rugs or carpets (this traps heat and prevents visual inspection).

#### 4.3. Event and Lab Setup (Temporary Wiring)
**Regulatory Reference:** OSHA 1910.304
During the setup of "Capture the Flag" (CTF) events, training labs, or booth displays:

1.  **Load Management:**
    *   Calculate the amperage load before plugging in equipment.
    *   Do not overload circuits. A standard 15-amp circuit should generally not exceed 12 amps of continuous load (80% rule).
    *   *Guideline:* High-performance gaming laptops and servers draw significant power. Distribute these devices across multiple distinct wall circuits, not just different outlets on the same circuit.
2.  **Grounding:**
    *   All equipment with a grounding pin must be plugged into a grounded outlet.
    *   **Never** remove or bypass the ground pin on a plug.
    *   **Never** use "cheater plugs" (3-prong to 2-prong adapters) without a verified ground connection.
3.  **Wet Locations:**
    *   If equipment is set up near water sources (e.g., beverage stations, outdoor areas), **GFCI protection is mandatory**.
    *   Portable GFCI adapters must be used if the venue outlets are not GFCI protected.

#### 4.4. Work on Energized Equipment
**Regulatory Reference:** OSHA 1910.333(a)(1)

1.  **De-energize First:** Study GRC personnel generally do not perform electrical repairs. However, if minor troubleshooting is required (e.g., swapping a server component), the equipment must be unplugged (de-energized) completely before opening the case.
2.  **Capacitor Safety:** Be aware that some equipment (UPS units, power supplies) retain a charge even when unplugged. Only Qualified Persons may service internal components of high-voltage electronics.

#### 4.5. Emergency Response to Electrical Incidents

1.  **Do Not Touch:** If a person is being shocked, **do not touch them** while they are in contact with the electrical source.
2.  **Cut Power:** Immediately unplug the device or turn off the circuit breaker if accessible.
3.  **Separate:** If power cannot be turned off, use a non-conductive object (dry wood, plastic) to push the person away from the source.
4.  **Medical Aid:** Call 911 immediately. Administer CPR/AED if qualified and the scene is safe.
5.  **Report:** File an Incident Report via the *Incident Investigation Report Template* (OPS-FRM-035) within 24 hours.

#### 4.6. Remote Work (Home Office) Guidance
While OSHA regulations for home offices are limited, Study GRC encourages the following for all remote staff:

1.  Ensure home office outlets are grounded.
2.  Use surge protectors for all Organization-issued IT equipment.
3.  Avoid overloading home circuits with space heaters or AC units on the same line as IT equipment.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensure sufficient budget for safe electrical equipment (e.g., cable ramps, industrial power strips) and enforce this procedure. |
| **Director of Operations** | Oversee electrical safety during event planning; ensure venue power capabilities match event requirements. |
| **Event Lead** | Act as the on-site "Competent Person" to inspect setups, enforce the "No Daisy-Chaining" rule, and manage cable trip hazards. |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Inspect their own equipment prior to use; report damaged cords immediately; adhere to load limits. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure puts the Organization and its community at risk of fire, injury, and regulatory fines.

*   **Internal:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **External:** Negligence regarding electrical safety at public events may result in civil liability or criminal charges.
