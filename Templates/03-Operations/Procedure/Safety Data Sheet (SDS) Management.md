### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for the acquisition, maintenance, and accessibility of Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for all hazardous chemicals used or stored by Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200) and ensures that all employees, volunteers, and event participants have immediate access to critical safety information regarding chemical hazards.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Study GRC Inc. operations where hazardous chemicals are present.

*   **Applies to:**
    *   All employees, contractors, and volunteers.
    *   **Physical Locations:** Administrative offices, storage units, and temporary event venues (e.g., workshops, hackathons, training labs).
    *   **Materials:** Cleaning agents, workshop consumables (e.g., soldering flux, isopropyl alcohol), and science/educational kit components.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Consumer products used for personal use in the same duration and frequency as a normal consumer (e.g., a volunteer bringing their own hand sanitizer).
    *   Articles (manufactured items that do not release hazardous chemicals under normal conditions of use).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)** | A standardized document (formerly MSDS) containing information on the potential health effects of exposure to chemicals and safe working procedures. |
| **Hazardous Chemical** | Any chemical which is classified as a physical hazard or a health hazard, a simple asphyxiant, combustible dust, pyrophoric gas, or hazard not otherwise classified. |
| **GHS** | The Globally Harmonized System of Classification and Labeling of Chemicals. |
| **Secondary Container** | Any container (spray bottle, jar, beaker) used to hold a chemical transferred from its original manufacturer's container. |
| **Event Lead** | The designated Study GRC Inc. representative responsible for oversight at a specific physical event or workshop. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Procurement and Acquisition
To ensure an up-to-date inventory, SDS acquisition must occur **prior** to or simultaneously with the purchase of any chemical.

1.  **Pre-Purchase Verification:** The individual requesting the purchase (the "Requestor") must verify if the chemical is already in the Master Chemical Inventory.
2.  **Vendor Request:** If the chemical is new to the Organization, the Requestor must obtain a current (GHS-compliant) SDS from the manufacturer or distributor at the time of purchase.
3.  **Submission:** The Requestor must submit the digital PDF of the SDS to the Director of Operations *before* the chemical is brought onto Organization property or into an event venue.

#### 4.2 Master Digital Repository
Given the Organization's remote-first nature, the primary repository for SDSs is digital.

1.  **Central Storage:** All SDSs must be stored in the designated "Health & Safety" folder within the Organization’s secure cloud storage (e.g., Google Drive/SharePoint).
2.  **Naming Convention:** Files must be named using the format: `[Product Name] - [Manufacturer] - [Revision Date YYYY-MM-DD].pdf`.
3.  **Indexing:** The Director of Operations must maintain a **Master Chemical Inventory List** (spreadsheet) in the same folder, hyperlinking to the specific SDS files.
4.  **Accessibility:** The folder must be set to "View All" permissions for all staff and active volunteers. Access must not require a password that is unavailable to staff during an emergency.

#### 4.3 Event-Specific SDS Protocols
As Study GRC Inc. hosts pop-up events and workshops, specific protocols apply to temporary venues.

1.  **Event Inventory Assessment:** 14 days prior to an event, the Event Lead must review the packing list to identify all chemicals being transported to the venue (e.g., soldering stations, cleaning supplies).
2.  **Event SDS Kit:** The Event Lead must compile an **Event SDS Kit** specific to that inventory. This Kit must be:
    *   **Physical:** A printed binder kept at the registration desk or safety station; OR
    *   **Offline Digital:** A tablet or USB drive containing the files, ensuring access is possible even if venue Wi-Fi fails.
3.  **On-Site Briefing:** During the volunteer safety briefing, the Event Lead must demonstrate the location of the Event SDS Kit.

#### 4.4 Secondary Container Labeling
When chemicals are transferred from original containers to secondary containers (e.g., pouring alcohol into a pump dispenser for a fiber optic workshop):

1.  **Immediate Labeling:** The secondary container must be labeled *immediately* upon transfer.
2.  **Label Requirements:** The label must include:
    *   Product Identifier (Name matching the SDS).
    *   Signal Word (Danger or Warning).
    *   Hazard Statements or Pictograms (e.g., Flammable, Corrosive).
3.  **Prohibition:** Use of food or beverage containers for chemical storage is strictly prohibited.

#### 4.5 Review and Archiving
1.  **Annual Review:** The Director of Operations shall conduct an annual audit of the Master Chemical Inventory to ensure all SDSs are current (within the last 3-5 years).
2.  **Archiving:** When a chemical is no longer used, its SDS must be moved to an "Archived SDS" folder.
3.  **Retention Period:** In compliance with 29 CFR 1910.1020, archived SDSs must be retained for **30 years** to track potential employee exposure history.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Operations** | Maintains the Master Chemical Inventory and Digital Repository. Conducts annual audits. |
| **Event Lead** | Creates and maintains the Event SDS Kit for specific workshops/events. Enforces secondary container labeling on-site. |
| **Requestor (Purchaser)** | Obtains the SDS from the vendor during the procurement process. |
| **Staff / Volunteers** | Review SDSs for chemicals they handle. Report missing SDSs or damaged labels immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure puts the Organization at risk of regulatory fines and staff at risk of injury.

*   **Regulatory Risk:** Failure to maintain accessible SDSs is a violation of OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1200 and can result in significant civil penalties.
*   **Internal Enforcement:** Violations of this procedure, particularly regarding the transport of chemicals to events without accompanying safety documentation, may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
