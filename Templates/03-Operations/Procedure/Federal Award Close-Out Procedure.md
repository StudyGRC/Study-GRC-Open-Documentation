### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory steps for the orderly and compliant close-out of federal grants and cooperative agreements awarded to Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with **2 CFR § 200.344**, requiring that all financial, performance, and other reports be submitted no later than **120 calendar days** after the end date of the period of performance. Proper close-out mitigates the risk of de-obligation of funds, audit findings, and negative impacts on future funding eligibility.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all federal awards received by Study GRC Inc., whether as a direct recipient or a subrecipient.
*   **Applies to:** The Board Treasurer, Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Executive Director, and all Program Managers (e.g., VP of Education & Training) managing federally funded projects.
*   **Exclusions:** Private foundation grants or unrestricted donations, unless the donor agreement explicitly references federal close-out standards.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Period of Performance (PoP)** | The time during which Study GRC Inc. may incur new obligations to carry out the work authorized under the federal award. |
| **Liquidation** | The process of paying all incurred costs (obligations) associated with the award. |
| **Unobligated Balance** | The portion of the funds authorized by the federal agency that has not been obligated by the recipient and is determined to be unspent at the end of the project. |
| **SF-425 (FFR)** | The Federal Financial Report, a standard form used to report financial activity to the federal agency. |
| **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)** | Federal regulations governing the management of federal grant awards. |
| **De-obligation** | The withdrawal of authorized federal funds from the recipient by the awarding agency. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Pre-Closeout Review (90 to 30 Days Prior to PoP End)
To ensure a smooth transition, the Program Manager and CFO must initiate the following steps before the grant ends:

1.  **Spending Forecast Review:** The CFO must review the budget vs. actuals to identify any significant unspent balances.
2.  **Stop-Work Orders:** The VP of Education & Training must notify all contractors and vendors of the upcoming contract end date.
3.  **Procurement Halt:** No new equipment or supply orders may be placed in the final 30 days of the grant unless explicitly required for final deliverables and approved by the Executive Director.
4.  **Subrecipient Notice:** If Study GRC Inc. has issued subawards, the CFO must notify subrecipients that their final invoices are due no later than **60 days** after the PoP end date to allow Study GRC Inc. time to consolidate reports.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Liquidation and Reconciliation (Days 1–90 Post-PoP)
Upon the expiration of the Period of Performance, the following financial actions must be taken:

1.  **Incurred Cost Verification:** The CFO must verify that all costs charged to the grant were incurred *within* the Period of Performance. Any costs incurred after the end date must be moved to unrestricted operating funds.
2.  **Liquidation of Obligations:** All financial obligations (unpaid invoices, staff payroll for the final period) must be paid (liquidated) no later than **120 calendar days** after the PoP end date.
3.  **Cash Drawdown Reconciliation:** The CFO must reconcile cash drawn from the Payment Management System (PMS) with recorded expenditures in the General Ledger.
    *   *Excess Cash:* Must be remitted to the payment system immediately.
    *   *Under-drawn Cash:* Final drawdowns must be processed before the 120-day window closes.
4.  **Inventory Reconciliation:** In accordance with **FIN-POL-040 (Fixed Asset Capitalization Policy)**, the CFO must conduct a physical inventory of all equipment (>$5,000 unit value) and residual supplies (aggregate >$5,000) purchased with grant funds to determine disposition requirements.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Final Reporting (Days 91–120 Post-PoP)
All final reports must be submitted to the Federal Awarding Agency strictly within the 120-day statutory window.

1.  **Final Federal Financial Report (SF-425):**
    *   Prepared by the CFO.
    *   Must reflect "Final" status (not Quarterly or Semi-Annual).
    *   Must show zero unliquidated obligations.
2.  **Final Performance/Technical Report (PPR):**
    *   Prepared by the Program Manager (e.g., VP of Education & Training).
    *   Must summarize the achievement of project goals and objectives.
3.  **Tangible Personal Property Report (SF-428):**
    *   Required if equipment was purchased.
    *   Must request disposition instructions (e.g., retain for other federal programs, transfer, or sell) if applicable.
4.  **Certification:** The Executive Director must certify all reports, attesting that the data is true, complete, and accurate to the best of their knowledge, acknowledging the penalties for False Claims (U.S. Code Title 18, Section 1001).

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Submission and Record Retention
1.  **Submission:** Reports are submitted via the agency’s designated portal (e.g., Grants.gov, eRA Commons, GrantSolutions).
2.  **Proof of Submission:** Screenshots or email confirmations of successful submission must be saved in the grant master file.
3.  **Refunds:** If there is a positive Unobligated Balance (money left over), Study GRC Inc. must refund the agency via the PMS or by check as directed by the agency, referencing the Grant Number.
4.  **Archiving:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII (Records)** and **FIN-STD-013 (Records Retention Schedule)**, all close-out documents must be retained for a minimum of **three (3) years** from the date of submission of the final expenditure report.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Program Manager (VP Education)** | Ensures all programmatic deliverables are met; drafts the Final Performance Report; notifies vendors to stop work. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reconciles GL; liquidates all obligations; prepares SF-425; manages inventory disposition; processes refunds. |
| **Executive Director** | Serves as the Authorized Organizational Representative (AOR); reviews and certifies final reports; approves final drawdowns. |
| **Board Treasurer** | Provides oversight of the close-out process to ensure no financial liabilities remain; reports successful close-out to the Board. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to submit close-out reports within the **120-day deadline** constitutes a material violation of the terms of the federal award.
*   **Consequences:** Under 2 CFR § 200.344(i), if the recipient does not submit all reports within one year of the period of performance end date, the federal agency **must** report the failure to the OMB-designated integrity and performance system (currently FAPIIS). This may result in Study GRC Inc. being debarred or suspended from receiving future federal funding.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Employees responsible for late submissions resulting in financial loss or compliance flags are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
