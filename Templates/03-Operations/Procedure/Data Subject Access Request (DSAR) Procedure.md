### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant workflow for handling Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) submitted to Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures the Organization fulfills its obligations under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) Article 15, the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), and other applicable privacy laws. It operationalizes the commitment to transparency and data protection outlined in **Article IX** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all "Personal Data" processed by Study GRC Inc. regarding any natural person, including Community Participants, Supporting Members, volunteers, employees, and donors.

*   **In Scope:** Requests for confirmation of processing, access to specific data, and copies of personal data.
*   **Exclusions:** Requests from law enforcement (refer to *LE-PRC-002 Law Enforcement Data Request Procedure*) or internal discovery requests related to litigation (refer to *LEG-PRC-001 Litigation Hold Procedure*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Data Subject** | The identified or identifiable natural person to whom the personal data relates (e.g., a member or student). |
| **DSAR** | Data Subject Access Request. A request by a Data Subject to exercise their right to access their personal data. |
| **Personal Data** | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (e.g., name, email, IP address, donation history). |
| **Verifiable Request** | A request where the Organization can reasonably verify that the person making the request is the person about whom the Organization has collected information. |
| **Authorized Agent** | A natural person or business entity registered with the Secretary of State that a consumer has authorized to act on their behalf. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Intake and Recognition
**4.1.1** DSARs may be received via email (privacy@studygrc.org), the website "Transparency" page contact form, or physical mail.
**4.1.2** Any Study GRC Inc. volunteer, officer, or employee who receives a request that sounds like a request for data (e.g., "I want to see what info you have on me") must forward it to the **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** within **24 hours**.
**4.1.3** The CISO must log the request in the *DSAR Tracking Register* (PRIV-REG-001) immediately upon receipt to start the regulatory clock (30 days for GDPR; 45 days for TDPSA).

#### 4.2 Identity Verification
To prevent unauthorized data disclosure (social engineering), the requester's identity must be verified before any data is released.

**4.2.1 Standard Verification:**
*   If the requester has a password-protected account (e.g., Learning Management System or Member Portal), verify identity via authentication into that account.
*   If no account exists, request verification via the email address on file.

**4.2.2 High-Sensitivity Verification:**
*   If the request involves sensitive data (financial data, health/accessibility data), the CISO may require a signed declaration or a secondary form of ID.

**4.2.3 Requests Regarding Minors:**
*   If the Data Subject is a minor (under 18), the requester must prove they are the parent or legal guardian.
*   Refer to *YTH-PRC-005 Verifiable Parental Consent Form* procedures.

#### 4.3 Triage and Validity Check
**4.3.1** The CISO reviews the request to determine validity.
*   **Manifestly Unfounded/Excessive:** If a request is repetitive or intended to harass, the Organization may charge a reasonable fee or refuse to act (per GDPR Art. 12(5)). This decision requires Executive Director approval.
*   **Adverse Rights:** If fulfilling the request would adversely affect the rights and freedoms of others (e.g., exposing another member's private data), the scope must be limited.

#### 4.4 Data Collection and Aggregation
**4.4.1** The CISO coordinates with relevant departments to search for the Data Subject's information across all repositories, including but not limited to:
*   Donor Management System (Finance/Treasurer)
*   Learning Management System (Education)
*   Email Marketing Platforms (Operations)
*   Volunteer Records (HR)
*   Slack/Discord Community Logs (Community)

**4.4.2** All data must be exported in a common, machine-readable format (e.g., CSV, JSON, or PDF).

#### 4.5 Review and Redaction
**4.5.1** The CISO must review the collected data prior to release.
**4.5.2** **Third-Party Data:** Redact any information identifying other individuals (e.g., in a shared email thread or community chat log).
**4.5.3** **Intellectual Property:** Ensure no trade secrets or proprietary "Work Product" (as defined in **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**) are inadvertently released, provided this does not prevent the user from seeing their own personal data.
**4.5.4** **Confidentiality:** Ensure no internal notes protected by legal privilege are included.

#### 4.6 Response Generation
**4.6.1** The response package must include:
*   A copy of the personal data.
*   The purposes of processing.
*   The categories of personal data concerned.
*   The recipients or categories of recipients to whom the personal data have been or will be disclosed.
*   The envisaged period for which the personal data will be stored.
*   Information regarding the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

#### 4.7 Delivery and Closure
**4.7.1** The data package must be delivered securely (e.g., via a secure download link or encrypted email attachment). **Never** send unencrypted sensitive data via plain text email.
**4.7.2** The CISO updates the *DSAR Tracking Register* to "Closed" and records the completion date.
**4.7.3** If the deadline cannot be met, the CISO must notify the Data Subject prior to the deadline, explaining the reason for the delay and the extension period (maximum 2 additional months for complex requests under GDPR).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requester (Data Subject)** | Must provide sufficient information to verify their identity and locate their records. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Process owner. Responsible for verification, data collection, redaction, and final delivery. Maintains the DSAR Register. |
| **Department Heads** | Responsible for retrieving data from their specific systems (e.g., Treasurer retrieves donor records) upon CISO request within 3 business days. |
| **Executive Director** | Approving authority for denials of requests deemed "manifestly unfounded" or "excessive." |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Responsible for recognizing a DSAR and forwarding it to the CISO immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure puts Study GRC Inc. at risk of significant regulatory fines (up to €20 million under GDPR) and reputational damage.

*   **Internal:** Employees or volunteers who willfully ignore a DSAR or destroy data subject to a pending request may face disciplinary action, up to and including termination and removal from the Organization.
*   **External:** Violations may result in complaints filed with the Texas Attorney General or European Data Protection Authorities.
