### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for verifying the identity and employment authorization of all individuals hired for employment by Study GRC Inc. in the United States. This procedure ensures compliance with the **Immigration Reform and Control Act of 1986 (IRCA)** and regulations enforced by the **U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS)**.

As a federal grantee and a 501(c)(3) organization, Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits the unlawful employment of individuals not authorized to work in the United States and prohibits discrimination based on citizenship status or national origin during the verification process.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to **all** newly hired employees (W-2 status) of Study GRC Inc., regardless of citizenship, remote work location, or duration of employment.

**Exclusions:**
*   Independent Contractors (1099 status).
*   Volunteers (unless they transition to paid employment).
*   Employees hired on or before November 6, 1986, who have been continuously employed by the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Form I-9** | The USCIS document titled "Employment Eligibility Verification" used to verify the identity and legal authorization to work of all paid employees in the U.S. |
| **Section 1** | The portion of Form I-9 completed by the **employee** regarding their personal information and citizenship/immigration status. |
| **Section 2** | The portion of Form I-9 completed by the **employer** (or authorized representative) after physically examining the employee's documents. |
| **Authorized Representative** | An individual designated by Study GRC Inc. to act on its behalf to complete Section 2, particularly for remote employees. This person physically examines the employee's documentation. |
| **List A Documents** | Documents that establish **both** identity and employment authorization (e.g., U.S. Passport, Permanent Resident Card). |
| **List B Documents** | Documents that establish **identity only** (e.g., Driver's License). |
| **List C Documents** | Documents that establish **employment authorization only** (e.g., Social Security Card, Birth Certificate). |
| **E-Verify** | An internet-based system that compares information from Form I-9 to DHS and SSA records to confirm employment eligibility. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Timing and Anti-Discrimination
4.1.1 **Post-Offer Requirement:** Study GRC Inc. must not request Form I-9 documentation until **after** an offer of employment has been extended and accepted.
4.1.2 **Employee Choice:** Hiring managers and HR personnel must **not** specify which documents an employee must present. The employee may choose any valid documentation from the USCIS List of Acceptable Documents (List A OR List B + List C).

#### 4.2 Section 1: Employee Information and Attestation
4.2.1 **Deadline:** The employee must complete and sign Section 1 of Form I-9 no later than the **first day of employment** (the date the employee begins performing labor or services in return for pay).
4.2.2 **Preparation:** The Hiring Manager or HR representative must provide the employee with the current version of Form I-9 (and instructions) prior to their start date to ensure timely completion.
4.2.3 **Review:** The HR representative must review Section 1 to ensure all mandatory fields are populated and the form is signed and dated.

#### 4.3 Section 2: Employer Review and Verification
4.3.1 **Deadline:** Section 2 must be completed and signed within **three (3) business days** of the employee's first day of employment.
4.3.2 **Physical Inspection Requirement:** In accordance with USCIS regulations, the employer (or authorized representative) must **physically examine** the original documents presented by the employee in person. Video calls (Zoom/Teams) are **not** permitted for document inspection unless Study GRC Inc. is enrolled in E-Verify and utilizing the specific "Alternative Procedure" authorized by DHS.

#### 4.4 Remote Hire Verification (Authorized Representatives)
Given Study GRC Inc.'s "Remote-First" operating model, physical inspection often requires an Authorized Representative.
4.4.1 **Designation:** Study GRC Inc. may designate an Authorized Representative (e.g., a notary public acting in a non-notarial capacity, a trusted colleague, or a family member) to physically inspect documents and sign Section 2 on the Organization's behalf.
4.4.2 **Liability:** Study GRC Inc. remains liable for any violations in connection with the form or the verification process, even if an Authorized Representative completes the form.
4.4.3 **Process:**
1.  The employee locates a suitable representative.
2.  The employee meets the representative in person with their original documents.
3.  The representative reviews the documents to ensure they appear genuine and relate to the person presenting them.
4.  The representative completes Section 2, entering "Study GRC Inc." as the Employer, and signs the form.
5.  The completed form is securely transmitted to Study GRC Inc. HR via the encrypted file upload portal (refer to *IS-POL-005 Data Classification Standard*).

#### 4.5 Document Retention
4.5.1 **Storage:** Completed Forms I-9 must be stored **separately** from the employee's general personnel file to facilitate audits and protect privacy.
4.5.2 **Retention Period:** Forms I-9 must be retained for **three (3) years** after the date of hire, or **one (1) year** after the date employment ends, whichever is later.
4.5.3 **Destruction:** Upon expiration of the retention period, Forms I-9 must be destroyed in accordance with *HR-POL-012 Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

#### 4.6 Re-verification (Section 3)
4.6.1 **Expiring Authorization:** Study GRC Inc. must re-verify employment authorization for employees with temporary work authorization (e.g., Employment Authorization Document) prior to the expiration date.
4.6.2 **Process:** The employee must present a new document from List A or List C. The HR representative completes Section 3 of the current I-9 or a new I-9.
4.6.3 **Exclusions:** U.S. Citizens and Permanent Residents (Green Card holders) generally do not require re-verification, even if their passports or Green Cards expire.

#### 4.7 E-Verify Integration (If Applicable)
4.7.1 If Study GRC Inc. is required by a federal contract (FAR clause) to use E-Verify:
1.  Information from Form I-9 must be entered into E-Verify within three (3) business days of the start date.
2.  If a "Tentative Non-Confirmation" (TNC) is received, the employee must be notified immediately and allowed to contest the finding.
3.  No adverse action may be taken against an employee while a TNC is being resolved.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Ensures the new hire is aware of the I-9 requirement and deadlines prior to the start date. |
| **New Hire (Employee)** | Completes Section 1 by Day 1; presents original, valid documents to the reviewer by Day 3. |
| **Authorized Representative** | Acts on behalf of Study GRC Inc. to physically examine documents and sign Section 2 for remote employees. |
| **HR / Operations** | Audits incoming I-9s for errors; manages the retention schedule; initiates E-Verify cases (if applicable). |
| **Chief Financial Officer** | Oversees the compliance program and serves as the final authority on complex eligibility questions. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to properly complete and retain Form I-9 may result in significant civil and criminal penalties from the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and the Department of Justice (DOJ).

*   **Employee Non-Compliance:** Employees who fail to provide required documentation within three (3) business days of their start date are subject to immediate termination of employment.
*   **Staff Non-Compliance:** Hiring managers or HR staff who knowingly accept fraudulent documents or fail to adhere to verification timelines may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
