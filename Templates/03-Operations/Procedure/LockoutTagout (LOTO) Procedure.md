### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the minimum requirements for the control of hazardous energy in accordance with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147**. This procedure ensures that machines, equipment, and systems are isolated from all potentially hazardous energy sources and locked out/tagged out before employees, volunteers, or contractors perform any servicing or maintenance where the unexpected energization, start-up, or release of stored energy could cause injury.

This procedure supports the Organization's commitment to safety as mandated by the **Master EHS Policy** and aligns with the Board of Directors' duty to manage the affairs of the Organization responsibly (Bylaws Article III, Section 3.1).

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Study GRC Inc.** employees, volunteers, and contractors engaging in the installation, service, maintenance, or modification of equipment where hazardous energy exists.

**Applicability Scenarios:**
*   **Educational Labs:** Robotics workshops, "Hardware Hacking" villages, and server repair labs.
*   **Event Operations:** Setup/teardown of heavy Audio/Visual equipment, lighting rigs, or temporary power distribution systems.
*   **Facilities:** Maintenance of HVAC, electrical, or mechanical systems in leased or owned spaces.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Cord and Plug Connected Equipment:** This procedure does not apply to work on cord and plug connected electric equipment (e.g., laptops, soldering irons, desktop monitors) for which exposure to the hazards of unexpected energization is controlled by the unplugging of the equipment from the energy source and by the plug being under the exclusive control of the employee performing the servicing or maintenance.
*   **Hot Tap Operations:** Not authorized under any circumstances.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Affected Employee** | An employee/volunteer whose job requires them to operate or use a machine/equipment on which servicing is being performed under lockout or tagout, or whose job requires them to work in an area in which such servicing is being performed. |
| **Authorized Employee** | A person who locks out or tags out machines or equipment in order to perform servicing or maintenance on that machine or equipment. |
| **Energy Source** | Any source of electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, chemical, thermal, or other energy. |
| **Lockout Device** | A device that utilizes a positive means such as a lock, either key or combination type, to hold an energy isolating device in the safe position and prevent the energizing of a machine or equipment. |
| **Tagout Device** | A prominent warning device, such as a tag and a means of attachment, which can be securely fastened to an energy isolating device in accordance with an established procedure, to indicate that the energy isolating device and the equipment being controlled may not be operated until the tagout device is removed. |
| **Zero Energy State** | The condition where all energy sources have been removed or neutralized. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 General Rules
1.  **Standardized Devices:** Only locks and tags provided by Study GRC Inc. may be used for LOTO purposes. These devices must be:
    *   **Durable:** Capable of withstanding the environment.
    *   **Standardized:** Distinctive in color, shape, or size (e.g., Red locks for LOTO only).
    *   **Substantial:** Prevent removal without the use of excessive force.
    *   **Identifiable:** Indicate the identity of the employee applying the device.
2.  **One Key, One Person:** Each Authorized Employee shall have their own lock and key. No person may apply a lock for another, nor may they share keys.
3.  **Prohibition:** It is strictly prohibited to bypass, ignore, or forcibly remove a LOTO device applied by another person, except under the specific Emergency Removal provisions (Section 4.3.2).

#### 4.2 The Six Steps of Lockout/Tagout (Execution)
Authorized Employees must follow these six steps before servicing equipment:

**Step 1: Preparation and Notification**
*   Identify the type and magnitude of the energy that the machine or equipment utilizes.
*   Understand the hazards of the energy.
*   Notify all Affected Employees that servicing is required and that the machine or equipment must be shut down and locked out.

**Step 2: Shutdown**
*   Shut down the machine or equipment by the normal stopping procedure (e.g., depress the stop button, open switch, close valve).
*   Ensure the controls are in the "OFF" or "NEUTRAL" position.

**Step 3: Isolation**
*   De-activate the energy isolating device(s) (e.g., circuit breaker, disconnect switch, line valve) so that the machine or equipment is isolated from the energy source.
*   **Note:** Control circuit devices (e.g., push buttons, selector switches) are **not** energy isolating devices.

**Step 4: Application of LOTO Devices**
*   Lock out the energy isolating device(s) with assigned individual lock(s).
*   Attach a "DANGER - DO NOT OPERATE" tag to the lock shackle. The tag must include the Authorized Employee’s name, contact info, and date.

**Step 5: Stored Energy Release**
*   Dissipate or restrain stored or residual energy (e.g., capacitors, springs, elevated machine members, rotating flywheels, hydraulic systems, and air, gas, steam, or water pressure).
*   If there is a possibility of re-accumulation of stored energy, verification of isolation must be continued until the servicing or maintenance is completed.

**Step 6: Verification (The "Try" Step)**
*   Ensure that no personnel are exposed.
*   Verify that the equipment is disconnected from the energy source by first checking that no personnel are exposed, then verify the isolation of equipment by operating the push button or other normal operating control(s) or by testing to make certain the equipment will not operate.
*   **Caution:** Return operating control(s) to "neutral" or "off" position after verifying the isolation.

#### 4.3 Release from Lockout/Tagout (Restoration)
Before lockout or tagout devices are removed and energy is restored:

1.  **Inspection:** Inspect the work area to ensure that non-essential items (tools, spare parts) have been removed and that machine or equipment components are operationally intact.
2.  **Employee Safety:** Check the work area to ensure that all employees have been safely positioned or removed.
3.  **Removal:** The LOTO devices must be removed **only** by the employee who applied them.
4.  **Notification:** Notify Affected Employees that the lockout or tagout devices have been removed and the equipment is ready for use.

#### 4.3.1 Emergency Removal of LOTO Devices
If the Authorized Employee who applied the lock is not available to remove it, the device may be removed under the direction of the **Director of Operations** only if the following specific procedure is followed:
1.  Verification by the Director of Operations that the Authorized Employee is not at the facility/site.
2.  Making all reasonable efforts to contact the Authorized Employee to inform them that their device has been removed.
3.  Ensuring that the Authorized Employee has this knowledge before they resume work at that facility.

#### 4.4 Group Lockout (Multiple Personnel)
When servicing is performed by a crew, craft, department, or other group, they must utilize a procedure which affords the employees a level of protection equivalent to that provided by the implementation of a personal lockout or tagout device.
1.  A **Primary Authorized Employee** will lock out the equipment.
2.  The key to the primary lock is placed in a lockbox.
3.  Each Authorized Employee working on the equipment places their *own* personal lock on the lockbox.
4.  The primary key cannot be retrieved (and equipment cannot be re-energized) until *every* individual has removed their personal lock from the box.

#### 4.5 Shift or Personnel Changes
To ensure the continuity of lockout or tagout protection, orderly transfer of lockout or tagout device protection between off-going and oncoming employees is required.
1.  The oncoming Authorized Employee applies their lock/tag immediately as the off-going employee removes theirs.
2.  At no time should the equipment be left unsecured if work is still in progress.

#### 4.6 Contractors and Vendors
1.  Whenever outside servicing personnel (contractors) are to be engaged in activities covered by the scope and application of this standard, the Organization and the outside employer shall inform each other of their respective lockout or tagout procedures.
2.  The Organization's Authorized Employees must understand and comply with the restrictions and prohibitions of the outside employer's energy control program.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensure resources (locks, tags, training) are available. Authorize Emergency Removal of locks in the absence of the Director of Operations. |
| **Director of Operations / Safety Officer** | Maintain the LOTO program. Conduct annual inspections of the procedure. Maintain training records. Authorize Emergency Removal of locks. |
| **Authorized Employees** | Perform LOTO in strict accordance with this procedure. Maintain control of their personal keys. Report any equipment defects immediately. |
| **Affected Employees** | Comply with instructions regarding LOTO. Never attempt to start equipment that is locked/tagged out. |
| **Contractors** | Submit their LOTO program to the Safety Officer for review prior to commencing work. Adhere to Study GRC Inc. safety standards. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure puts lives at risk.
*   **Zero Tolerance:** Bypassing, cutting, or ignoring a LOTO device without authorization is a critical safety violation.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action.
*   **Training:** Training must be completed prior to initial assignment and retrained whenever there is a change in job assignments, machines, equipment, or processes that present a new hazard.
