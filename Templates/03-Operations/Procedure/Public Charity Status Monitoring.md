### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a mandatory, recurring process for monitoring Study GRC Inc.'s qualification as a **Public Charity** under Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 509(a). This procedure ensures the Organization avoids inadvertent reclassification as a **Private Foundation** by proactively tracking the "Public Support Test" ratios. This aligns with **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, ensuring the Organization operates exclusively for charitable and educational purposes, and **Article VIII**, regarding financial transparency.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the Finance Department, the Treasurer, the Executive Director, and the Fundraising/Development team. It covers the classification of all incoming revenue (donations, grants, membership dues, and gross receipts) and the calculation of support fractions required for IRS Form 990, Schedule A.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Public Charity** | A 501(c)(3) organization that receives a substantial portion of its support from the general public or government units, rather than from a few private sources. |
| **Private Foundation** | A 501(c)(3) organization that is funded primarily by a single source, family, or corporation. Private Foundations are subject to stricter excise taxes and operating restrictions. |
| **Public Support Test** | The IRS calculation (Schedule A) used to determine if an organization meets the 33 1/3% support threshold to remain a Public Charity. |
| **Disqualified Person** | Substantial contributors (donors of >$5,000 if that amount is >2% of total contributions), Directors, Officers, and their family members. |
| **The 2% Limit** | A rule in the Public Support Test where contributions from a single donor (other than a government unit or another public charity) count toward "Public Support" only up to 2% of the organization's *total* support. |
| **Unusual Grant** | A substantial contribution from a disinterested party that is unexpected and would adversely affect the organization's ability to meet the Public Support Test if included in the calculation. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Revenue Classification and Tagging
To accurately calculate the support test, all revenue must be correctly categorized in the accounting system upon receipt.

1.  **Donation vs. Exchange Transaction:** The CFO or designee must determine if revenue is a contribution (gift) or a payment for services (exchange).
    *   *Note:* Per **Bylaws Article II**, "Supporting Members" provide financial contributions. These must be analyzed to determine if they are pure donations (deductible) or dues for services (exchange).
2.  **Donor Identification:** All transactions must be tagged with the donor's identity to track cumulative giving over the rolling 5-year period.
3.  **Source Type Tagging:** Revenue must be tagged as:
    *   **Public Contribution:** Individual, Corporation, or Private Foundation.
    *   **Government Grant:** Funding from federal, state, or local units (counts 100% toward public support).
    *   **Public Charity Grant:** Funding from another 501(c)(3) Public Charity (counts 100% toward public support).
    *   **Gross Receipts:** Income from mission-related activities (e.g., training fees).

#### 4.2 The 2% Threshold Calculation (The Denominator)
The Organization must determine the "Total Support" base to calculate the 2% limitation for individual donors.

1.  **Calculate Total Support:** Sum the following over the rolling 5-year period (current year + previous 4 years):
    *   Gifts, grants, contributions, and membership fees received.
    *   Gross investment income.
    *   Tax revenues levied for the organization's benefit.
    *   *Exclude:* Capital gains and income from unrelated business activities.
2.  **Determine the 2% Limit:** Multiply the **Total Support** figure by **0.02**. This figure is the maximum amount of funding from any single private donor that can be counted in the *numerator* (Public Support).

#### 4.3 The Public Support Calculation (The Numerator)
1.  **Aggregate Public Support:** Sum the following:
    *   100% of contributions from Government Units.
    *   100% of contributions from other Public Charities.
    *   Contributions from individual donors/corporations/foundations **capped at the 2% Limit** calculated in Step 4.2.
2.  **Excess Contributions:** Any amount contributed by a single private donor *above* the 2% Limit is considered "Excess" and is included in the Denominator (Total Support) but **excluded** from the Numerator (Public Support).

#### 4.4 Performing the Test (Quarterly Simulation)
The CFO shall perform a "Mock Schedule A" calculation quarterly.

1.  **Formula:** `(Public Support / Total Support) x 100`
2.  **Threshold Analysis:**
    *   **> 33.3%:** Safe. The Organization qualifies as a Public Charity.
    *   **10% - 33.3%:** "Facts and Circumstances" Zone. The Organization *may* qualify but must document active fundraising from the general public and a representative governing body.
    *   **< 10%:** Critical Risk. The Organization is in danger of tipping into Private Foundation status.

#### 4.5 Managing Unusual Grants
If a large, unexpected donation is received that would dilute the Public Support percentage below 33.3%:

1.  **Assessment:** The CFO and Treasurer must evaluate if the gift qualifies as an "Unusual Grant" under IRS regulations (unexpected, from a disinterested party, attracted by the public nature of the organization).
2.  **Exclusion:** If qualified, the grant is excluded from **both** the Numerator and the Denominator, preserving the Public Support ratio.
3.  **Documentation:** A memo justifying the "Unusual Grant" classification must be retained in the grant file for audit purposes.

#### 4.6 Reporting and Remediation
1.  **Board Reporting:** The Public Support Percentage must be reported to the Board of Directors as part of the Annual Financial Report required by **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2**.
2.  **Remediation Triggers:** If the rolling average drops below **35%** (providing a safety buffer above 33.3%):
    *   The Treasurer must alert the Board immediately.
    *   The Fundraising Committee must prioritize small-dollar public solicitation campaigns.
    *   The Organization must restrict the acceptance of large, non-grant checks from single sources unless they are treated as "Unusual Grants."

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Responsible for accurate revenue tagging, performing the quarterly Schedule A simulation, and identifying potential "Unusual Grants." |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews the quarterly calculation; reports status to the Board; approves "Unusual Grant" classifications. |
| **Board of Directors** | Maintains fiduciary oversight; approves strategic shifts in fundraising if the Public Support ratio is threatened. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Ensures fundraising strategies prioritize a broad base of public support rather than reliance on a single donor. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to monitor and maintain Public Charity status may result in reclassification as a Private Foundation. This triggers:
*   Excise taxes on investment income.
*   Restrictions on self-dealing and lobbying.
*   Potential loss of donor trust.

Operational Officers failing to adhere to revenue classification procedures or failing to report "tipping" risks may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination, per the **Employee Handbook**.
