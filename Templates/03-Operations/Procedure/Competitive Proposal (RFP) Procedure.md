### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, transparent, and compliant method for procuring goods and services through Competitive Proposals (Requests for Proposals or RFPs). This procedure ensures Study GRC Inc. obtains the best value ("most advantageous to the program") while strictly adhering to **2 CFR 200.320(d)** requirements for federal grant recipients. It is designed to prevent fraud, waste, and abuse by mandating open competition and objective evaluation.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all procurement actions where:
1.  The estimated aggregate value of the purchase exceeds the **Simplified Acquisition Threshold (SAT)** (currently **$250,000** under federal guidelines, or lower if specified by specific grant conditions); **OR**
2.  The nature of the procurement is complex, and price is not the sole determining factor (e.g., consulting services, complex IT systems, curriculum development); **AND**
3.  Sealed Bids (formal advertising) are not appropriate.

**Exclusions:**
*   Micro-purchases (below $10,000).
*   Small Purchases (below the SAT, utilizing price quotes).
*   Sole Source procurements (requires *Noncompetitive Justification Form* FIN-FRM-030).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **RFP (Request for Proposal)** | A solicitation document used to obtain offers and proposals for goods or services where factors other than price (e.g., technical capability, past performance) are weighted in the decision. |
| **Evaluation Committee** | A group of at least three (3) individuals appointed to review and score proposals based on pre-established criteria. |
| **Responsive Proposal** | A proposal that complies with all material aspects of the RFP (e.g., submitted on time, includes all required forms). |
| **Responsible Offeror** | A vendor that has the capability, integrity, and reliability to perform the contract work. |
| **Best and Final Offer (BAFO)** | A final stage of negotiation where top-ranked vendors are given an opportunity to adjust their technical or price proposals. |
| **SOW (Statement of Work)** | A document detailing the specific tasks, deliverables, and timelines required from the vendor. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Preparation and Drafting
**4.1.1 Needs Assessment & SOW Creation**
The Requesting Department must draft a clear Statement of Work (SOW) or Specification. The SOW must not contain features that unduly restrict competition (e.g., specifying a brand name without allowing "or equal").

**4.1.2 Establishing Evaluation Criteria**
Prior to solicitation, the Requesting Department and Finance must establish weighted evaluation criteria. In accordance with **2 CFR 200.320(d)(1)**, the RFP must identify all evaluation factors and their relative importance.
*   *Example:* Technical Approach (35%), Past Performance (25%), Qualifications (20%), Price (20%).

**4.1.3 Independent Cost Estimate (ICE)**
For procurements expected to exceed the SAT, an Independent Cost Estimate must be generated internally before receiving proposals to determine reasonableness.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Public Notice and Solicitation
**4.2.1 Publicizing the RFP**
Pursuant to **2 CFR 200.320(d)(1)**, requests for proposals must be publicized.
*   The RFP must be posted on the "Transparency" or "Procurement" section of the Study GRC Inc. website.
*   For specialized services (e.g., IT Security, EdTech), the RFP should be advertised in relevant industry forums or trade publications.

**4.2.2 Solicitation of Sources**
The Organization must solicit proposals from an **adequate number of qualified sources** (minimum of three).
*   Solicitations must be sent simultaneously to ensure all vendors have the same preparation time.
*   **MWBE Inclusion:** Reasonable effort must be made to solicit Minority, Women-owned, and Small Business Enterprises (per *MWBE Preference Policy* FIN-POL-032).

#### 4.3 Phase III: Proposal Receipt and Screening
**4.3.1 Receipt and Logging**
Proposals must be received via the designated secure submission channel (e.g., dedicated procurement email or portal) by the date and time specified. Late proposals are strictly rejected and returned unopened/unread.

**4.3.2 Initial Compliance Review**
The CFO or designee reviews submissions for "Responsiveness":
*   Was it on time?
*   Are all required forms signed (e.g., Anti-Lobbying, Debarment certification)?
*   Does it meet the mandatory minimum technical requirements?
*   *Non-responsive proposals are disqualified before technical evaluation.*

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Technical Evaluation
**4.4.1 Evaluation Committee Formation**
An Evaluation Committee of at least three (3) subject matter experts is formed. All members must sign a *Conflict of Interest Disclosure* specific to the vendors involved.

**4.4.2 Scoring Methodology**
In compliance with **2 CFR 200.320(d)(3)**, the Organization must have a written method for conducting technical evaluations.
*   Evaluators score proposals individually using the *Vendor Selection Evaluation Matrix* (FIN-FRM-035).
*   Price proposals should ideally be evaluated separately from technical proposals to avoid bias.
*   The Committee meets to consolidate scores and discuss discrepancies.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Selection and Award
**4.5.1 Best and Final Offer (BAFO)**
The Organization may engage in discussions with offerors deemed within the "competitive range." If changes are required, the Organization may request a BAFO from all remaining qualified vendors.

**4.5.2 Final Selection**
The contract must be awarded to the responsible firm whose proposal is **most advantageous to the program**, with price and other factors considered (**2 CFR 200.320(d)(4)**).
*   *Note:* The lowest price is not required to be selected if a higher-priced proposal offers significantly better technical value (Best Value trade-off), provided the trade-off is documented.

**4.5.3 Suspension and Debarment Check**
Before awarding, the CFO must verify the vendor is not suspended or debarred from federal contracts by checking **SAM.gov**. A screenshot of the result must be saved to the procurement file.

#### 4.6 Phase VI: Documentation and Contracting
**4.6.1 Contract Execution**
A contract is drafted incorporating the RFP, the Vendor’s Proposal, and mandatory federal provisions (Appendix II to Part 200).
*   If the value exceeds the Executive Director’s signature authority (as defined in the *Delegation of Authority Matrix*), Board approval is required.

**4.6.2 Notification to Unsuccessful Bidders**
Unsuccessful bidders should be notified promptly in writing.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requesting Department** | Drafts SOW; defines technical requirements; participates in Evaluation Committee. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Manages the RFP process; ensures 2 CFR 200 compliance; performs SAM.gov checks; maintains the audit file. |
| **Evaluation Committee** | Reviews proposals objectively; scores based *only* on published criteria; signs COI disclosures. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Reviews final selection report; signs contracts within delegated authority limits. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves contracts exceeding the Executive Director's signature authority threshold. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in "questioned costs" during a Single Audit, potentially requiring Study GRC Inc. to repay federal funds.

Internal violations of this procedure (e.g., splitting purchases to avoid the RFP threshold, sharing inside information with a preferred vendor, or failing to disclose a conflict of interest) will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment and legal prosecution.
