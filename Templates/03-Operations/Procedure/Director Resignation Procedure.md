### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant process for the voluntary resignation of a member of the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures:
1.  Clear transfer of fiduciary responsibilities.
2.  Preservation of organizational continuity and quorum requirements.
3.  Secure revocation of access to organizational assets and data.
4.  Timely initiation of the vacancy filling process in accordance with **Bylaws Article III**.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Voting Directors** currently serving on the Board of Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Advisory Board Members:** Resignations for advisors are handled via the *Advisory Board Appointment/Removal Process*.
*   **Operational Officers:** Unless the Officer is also a Director, their resignation is handled via the *Employee Handbook* or *Volunteer Handbook* separation procedures.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Written Notice** | A formal communication (physical letter or electronic mail) stating the intent to resign. Verbal resignations are not valid until memorialized in writing. |
| **Effective Date** | The date and time at which the resignation becomes active. This is determined by the date specified in the notice or, if unspecified, the date of receipt. |
| **Doomsday Clause** | A provision in **Bylaws Section 3.10** triggered if the number of Directors falls to zero (0). |
| **Minimum Composition** | The requirement in **Bylaws Section 3.2** that the Board consist of no fewer than three (3) Directors. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Submission of Notice
To resign in good standing, a Director must provide **Written Notice** of their resignation.
1.  The resigning Director must draft a formal notice addressed to the **Board of Directors**, the **President**, or the **Secretary**.
2.  The notice must include:
    *   A clear statement of resignation.
    *   The intended **Effective Date**.
    *   (Optional) The reason for resignation, though this is not legally required.
3.  The notice must be delivered via email to the official Board correspondence address or the Secretary's official email.

#### 4.2 Determination of Effectiveness
1.  **Immediate Effect:** Unless the notice specifies a later date, the resignation is effective immediately upon receipt by the Organization (TBOC § 22.211).
2.  **No Vote Required:** The Board does not need to vote to "accept" a resignation for it to be valid. The resignation is a unilateral right of the Director.
3.  **Revocability:** A resignation is irrevocable once delivered, unless the Board agrees to allow a retraction prior to the Effective Date.

#### 4.3 Quorum and Composition Check (Critical Control)
Upon receipt of the notice, the **Secretary** (or remaining Officers) must immediately calculate the remaining Board composition to determine if emergency bylaws apply.

1.  **Standard Vacancy:** If the remaining number of Directors is **three (3) or more**, the Board proceeds to **Section 4.4**.
2.  **Minimum Requirement Breach:** If the resignation reduces the Board to **fewer than three (3)** Directors:
    *   The *Board Vacancy Minimum Requirement Override* (Bylaws § 3.8) is triggered.
    *   The vacancy must be filled immediately; the "Short Term Exception" for elections does not apply.
3.  **Zero Director Scenario:** If the resignation leaves **zero (0)** Directors:
    *   The *Contingency for "No Board" (Doomsday Clause)* (Bylaws § 3.10) is triggered.
    *   The resigning Director is requested (but not legally compelled) to delay the Effective Date until a Special Emergency Meeting of the Membership can be called.

#### 4.4 Board Acknowledgment and Recording
1.  The Secretary must circulate the resignation notice to all remaining Directors within **24 hours** of receipt.
2.  The resignation must be formally recorded in the minutes of the next scheduled Board meeting. The minutes must state:
    *   The name of the resigning Director.
    *   The date notice was received.
    *   The Effective Date of the resignation.

#### 4.5 Offboarding and Access Revocation
Due to the remote-first nature of Study GRC Inc., immediate digital offboarding is required to protect data integrity.

1.  **Identity Management:** The **CISO** or IT Administrator must disable the Director’s organizational accounts (Google Workspace, Slack, GitHub, etc.) by close of business on the Effective Date.
2.  **Financial Authority:** If the Director was a signatory on bank accounts or had access to payment platforms (Stripe, PayPal, Bank), the **Treasurer** must remove their authorization immediately.
3.  **State Filings:** If the Director served as the Registered Agent, the Board must immediately appoint a new agent and file the appropriate change form with the Texas Secretary of State.

#### 4.6 Return of Property
1.  The resigning Director must return or destroy all confidential information, physical assets, or intellectual property in their possession within **five (5) business days**.
2.  The Director must affirm compliance with the *Intellectual Property Policy* (Bylaws Art. IX), confirming that all work product created during their tenure remains the property of Study GRC Inc.

#### 4.7 Initiation of Vacancy Filling
1.  Following the acknowledgment of resignation, the Board must initiate the *Board Vacancy Filling Procedure* (GOV-PRC-040).
2.  If the unexpired term is less than three (3) months **AND** the Board has at least three (3) remaining members, the Board may vote to leave the seat vacant until the next Annual Meeting (Bylaws § 3.8).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Resigning Director** | Submit written notice; return all assets; maintain confidentiality of sensitive data post-resignation. |
| **Secretary** | Receive notice; calculate quorum impact; record in minutes; notify the CISO/IT. |
| **President** | Manage the transition; initiate the vacancy filling process; communicate change to the Membership (if appropriate). |
| **CISO / IT Admin** | Revoke digital access and privileges immediately upon the Effective Date. |
| **Treasurer** | Revoke financial signature authority and update banking records. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Fiduciary Survival:** Resignation terminates the Director's authority to act on behalf of the Organization but does not terminate the duty of confidentiality regarding non-public information acquired during service.
*   **Abandonment:** A Director who ceases to perform duties without providing Written Notice may be subject to *Removal for Cause* (Bylaws § 3.6) rather than a standard resignation, which may affect future eligibility for membership or volunteer roles.
*   **Failure to Update Records:** Failure to update the Registered Agent or Officer information with the State of Texas upon resignation may result in statutory penalties for the Organization.
