### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant mechanism for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") to ratify defective corporate acts and validate putative corporate acts. This procedure ensures that any corporate actions taken without full compliance with the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) or the Organization’s Bylaws—due to procedural errors, lack of quorum, or notice failures—can be retroactively validated to ensure the Organization's legal integrity.

This procedure is drafted in strict accordance with **TBOC Chapter 22, Subchapter F (§ 22.501 – § 22.516)**.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **The Board of Directors:** Regarding acts taken by the Board that may have been procedurally defective.
*   **Voting Members:** Regarding acts requiring member approval (e.g., Election of Directors, Fundamental Actions).
*   **Officers:** Regarding the execution of documents or contracts authorized by defective acts.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not cure acts that would have been illegal or prohibited by law at the time they were committed, nor does it absolve Directors or Officers of liability for breach of fiduciary duty or fraud.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Defective Corporate Act** | An election or act (or alleged act) by the Organization that is void or voidable due to a failure of authorization. |
| **Failure of Authorization** | The failure to authorize or effect an act in compliance with the TBOC, the Certificate of Formation, or the Bylaws (e.g., lack of quorum, improper notice, expired Board terms). |
| **Putative Corporate Act** | An act that was purportedly taken but was defective. |
| **Resolution of Ratification** | A formal resolution adopted by the Board (and Members, if required) specifically describing and validating the defective act. |
| **Validation Effective Time** | The date and time at which the ratification becomes valid, which is retroactive to the time the original defective act was purportedly taken. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Identification of Defective Act
Upon discovery of a potential procedural error (e.g., a Board meeting held without proper notice per Bylaws Article III, Section 3.4, or a Member vote taken without a quorum per Bylaws Article II, Section 2.4), the discovering party must notify the **Secretary** and the **President**.

1.  **Legal Review:** The Board must consult with legal counsel to determine if the error constitutes a "Defective Corporate Act" under TBOC § 22.501.
2.  **Scope Determination:** Determine if the act required only Board approval or if it also required Voting Member approval at the time of the defect.

#### 4.2 Adoption of Resolution of Ratification (Board Action)
To ratify a defective act, the Board of Directors must adopt a **Resolution of Ratification**. Regardless of whether the current Board consists of the same individuals as the Board at the time of the defect, the current Board acts as the governing authority.

The Resolution **must** contain the following specific information (TBOC § 22.503):
1.  **Description of the Act:** A clear statement of the defective corporate act to be ratified.
2.  **Date of Act:** The date the defective corporate act was purportedly taken.
3.  **Nature of Failure:** A statement describing why the act is defective (e.g., "Failure to provide the required 10-day notice to Voting Members pursuant to Bylaws Section 2.5").
4.  **Statement of Ratification:** A statement that the Board of Directors ratifies the defective corporate act.

**Quorum Requirement:** The quorum for this meeting is determined by the governing documents applicable *at the time the resolution is adopted*, not the time of the defect.

#### 4.3 Member Approval (If Required)
If the defective corporate act originally required approval by Voting Members (e.g., Election of Directors under Bylaws Section 2.2, or Fundamental Actions under Bylaws Article X), or if the ratification changes the election of directors:

1.  **Submission to Members:** The Board must submit the Resolution of Ratification to the Voting Members for approval.
2.  **Notice Requirements:** Notice must be given to:
    *   Each holder of valid voting membership as of the record date for the ratification meeting; AND
    *   Each holder of putative voting membership (individuals who *thought* they were members) as of the time of the defective act.
3.  **Content of Notice:** The notice must include a copy of the Resolution of Ratification and a statement that the purpose of the meeting is to consider ratification of a defective corporate act.
4.  **Voting Threshold:** The resolution must be approved by the number of votes required by the Bylaws (e.g., majority for standard business, or two-thirds for Fundamental Actions per Article X).

#### 4.4 Filing Certificate of Validation (If Required)
If the defective corporate act involved a filing with the Texas Secretary of State (e.g., an Amendment to the Certificate of Formation that was filed despite a voting defect), the Organization must file a **Certificate of Validation**.

1.  **Preparation:** The Secretary shall prepare the Certificate of Validation in accordance with TBOC § 22.512.
2.  **Attachments:** The certificate must include the Resolution of Ratification and the date of adoption.
3.  **Filing:** The document is filed with the Texas Secretary of State along with the applicable filing fee.

#### 4.5 Notice of Ratification
Unless notice was already provided under Section 4.3, notice of the ratification must be given to all Voting Members and putative members no later than the **60th day** after the date of adoption of the Resolution of Ratification.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Identifies defective acts; adopts Resolutions of Ratification; authorizes filings. |
| **Secretary** | Prepares Resolutions; manages notice distribution to current and putative members; maintains records of ratification in the corporate minute book. |
| **Voting Members** | Vote on Resolutions of Ratification for acts falling under Bylaws Article II (Elections) or Article X (Fundamental Actions). |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews defects to determine applicability of TBOC Subchapter F; drafts Resolutions to ensure statutory compliance. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly follow this procedure when curing a corporate defect may result in the act remaining void or voidable. This exposes the Organization to legal challenges regarding the validity of contracts, the authority of Directors, or the tax-exempt status of the entity.

**Judicial Review:** Under TBOC § 22.515, the District Court in the county where the Organization’s registered office is located may hear petitions regarding the validity of any ratification. Strict adherence to this procedure minimizes the risk of adverse judicial rulings.
