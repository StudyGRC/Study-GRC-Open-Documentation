### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a clear, legally binding protocol for the general membership to restore governance in the event of a catastrophic loss of leadership. Specifically, this document operationalizes the "Doomsday Clause" (Article III, Section 3.10) of the Bylaws, ensuring that Study GRC Inc. can elect an Interim Board of Directors and maintain business continuity if the number of active Directors falls to zero (0).

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Voting Members** (Supporting Members and Honorary Members) of Study GRC Inc.

**Triggers:** This procedure is **only** activated when:
1.  The number of active Directors on the Board reaches zero (0) due to death, resignation, removal, or incapacity; AND
2.  No remaining Directors exist to fill vacancies via standard Bylaw procedures (Article III, Section 3.8).

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply if at least one (1) Director remains in office. In such cases, the remaining Director must fill vacancies per Bylaws Section 3.8.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Doomsday Event** | A specific crisis scenario where the Organization has zero (0) serving Directors. |
| **Initiating Member** | The Voting Member who first takes affirmative action to call the Special Emergency Meeting. |
| **Interim Board** | The Board of Directors elected during the Special Emergency Meeting to serve until the next Annual Meeting or standard election cycle. |
| **Quorum** | More than 50% of the total Voting Members, as defined in Bylaws Section 2.4. |
| **Registered Agent** | The entity designated to receive service of process and official notices for the Organization in the State of Texas. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Verification of Doomsday Event
Before initiating this procedure, the potential Initiating Member must verify the status of the Board.
1.  **Evidence Collection:** The Initiating Member must gather written evidence that no Directors remain (e.g., resignation emails, public obituaries, or minutes from a removal hearing).
2.  **Registered Agent Confirmation:** If internal records are unavailable, the Initiating Member should contact the Organization’s Registered Agent to confirm no current officer filings contradict the assessment.

#### 4.2 Call to Meeting (The "Initiation")
Any Voting Member in good standing may act as the Initiating Member.
1.  **Notice Drafting:** The Initiating Member must draft a "Notice of Special Emergency Meeting." This notice must include:
    *   **Statement of Purpose:** "To elect an Interim Board of Directors following the vacancy of all Board positions pursuant to Bylaws Section 3.10."
    *   **Date and Time:** Must be at least 10 days from the date of distribution.
    *   **Location:** Virtual meeting link (e.g., Zoom, Teams) or physical location.
2.  **Access to Member List:**
    *   The Initiating Member shall request the alphabetical list of Voting Members and email addresses from the Executive Director, CISO, or remaining Operational Staff.
    *   If Operational Staff are unavailable or uncooperative, the Initiating Member exercises their right under **TBOC § 22.351** and **Bylaws Section 2.6** to inspect and copy the membership roll for the specific purpose of calling this meeting.

#### 4.3 Distribution of Notice
1.  **Transmission:** The Notice must be sent to **all** Voting Members via the email address on record.
2.  **Timeline:** The Notice must be sent no fewer than 10 days and no more than 60 days prior to the meeting date.
3.  **Proof of Notice:** The Initiating Member must retain a copy of the sent email and the recipient list as proof of due process.

#### 4.4 Conduct of the Special Emergency Meeting
1.  **Call to Order:** The Initiating Member calls the meeting to order.
2.  **Quorum Verification:**
    *   A roll call or digital attendance report must be generated.
    *   **Mandatory Threshold:** A majority (>50%) of all Voting Members must be present (virtually or in person).
    *   *Constraint:* If a quorum is not present, the meeting must be adjourned and rescheduled with new notice; no business can be conducted.
3.  **Election of Meeting Chair:** The Voting Members present shall elect a temporary Meeting Chair and Meeting Secretary by simple majority vote to facilitate the proceedings.
4.  **Nominations from the Floor:**
    *   The Meeting Chair opens the floor for nominations for the Interim Board.
    *   Nominees must be Voting Members in good standing.
    *   Nominees must verbally accept the nomination and confirm they have no disqualifying Conflicts of Interest.
5.  **The Vote:**
    *   Voting shall be conducted via open roll call or secure electronic poll.
    *   **Proxy Voting is Prohibited** (Bylaws Section 2.3).
    *   Members may vote for as many candidates as there are seats to fill (minimum 3, maximum 10).
6.  **Certification:** The temporary Meeting Secretary records the vote counts and declares the winners.

#### 4.5 Post-Meeting Transition
1.  **Immediate Effect:** The elected Interim Directors assume office immediately upon the adjournment of the meeting.
2.  **Officer Appointment:** The Interim Board must immediately convene (a "Board Meeting") to elect a President, Secretary, and Treasurer (Bylaws Article IV).
3.  **Bank/State Notification:** The new Secretary must file an update with the Texas Secretary of State (Form 401 or equivalent) and update authorized signers with the Organization's financial institutions.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Initiating Member** | Verifies the lack of Directors, obtains the member list, sends the Notice, and calls the meeting to order. |
| **Voting Members** | Required to attend to establish a quorum and vote for the Interim Board. |
| **Operational Officers (CEO/CISO)** | If still employed/active, must assist the Initiating Member by providing the Membership Roll and facilitating IT resources (Zoom/Email) for the meeting. |
| **Meeting Secretary (Temp)** | Records the minutes of the Special Emergency Meeting and certifies the election results for legal records. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Legal Validity:** Failure to strictly adhere to the notice period (10 days) or Quorum requirements (>50%) renders the election of the Interim Board **void** under Texas Law. This puts the Organization at risk of involuntary dissolution by the State or receivership.

**Documentation:** Minutes of the Special Emergency Meeting, including the proof of notice and the exact vote count, must be preserved permanently in the Organization’s Corporate Records.
