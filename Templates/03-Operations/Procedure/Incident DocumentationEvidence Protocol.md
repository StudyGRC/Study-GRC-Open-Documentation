### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish a standardized methodology for the identification, collection, preservation, and documentation of digital evidence during a cybersecurity incident or internal investigation. This procedure ensures **Forensic Readiness**, guaranteeing that evidence collected is legally admissible, accurate, and sufficient to support root cause analysis, insurance claims, and potential litigation. This protocol aligns with the Organization's commitment to data protection as outlined in **Bylaws Article IX**.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Information Systems, networks, cloud environments (SaaS/IaaS), and hardware owned or operated by Study GRC Inc. It applies to all employees, contractors, and volunteers ("Personnel") who may act as First Responders or members of the Incident Response Team (IRT).

**Exclusions:** Routine system troubleshooting logs that are not part of a declared security incident are excluded until such time as an incident is suspected.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chain of Custody (CoC)** | The chronological documentation or paper trail that records the sequence of custody, control, transfer, analysis, and disposition of physical or electronic evidence. |
| **Forensic Image** | A bit-for-bit copy of a digital storage device (e.g., hard drive, memory) that captures all data, including hidden and deleted files. |
| **Hash Value** | A unique numerical string (digital fingerprint) generated by a cryptographic algorithm (e.g., SHA-256) used to verify that data has not been altered. |
| **Order of Volatility** | The order in which evidence should be collected based on the life expectancy of the data (e.g., CPU cache is more volatile than a hard drive). |
| **Write Blocker** | A hardware or software tool that prevents any data from being written to a storage device during the forensic acquisition process. |
| **Artifact** | A piece of data that may serve as evidence (e.g., log entry, registry key, file metadata). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. Immediate Preservation (First Responder Duties)
Upon the detection or suspicion of a security incident, the First Responder must:
1.  **Stop and Think:** Do not rush. Hasty actions can destroy volatile evidence.
2.  **Do Not Reboot:** Never power down or reboot a compromised system unless explicitly instructed by the CISO to prevent immediate, catastrophic data loss. Rebooting destroys RAM (volatile memory).
3.  **Isolate, Don't Delete:** Disconnect the system from the network (pull the network cable or disable Wi-Fi) to prevent further spread, but keep the machine powered on.
4.  **Secure the Scene:** If physical hardware is involved, restrict physical access to the device.

#### 4.2. Order of Volatility
Evidence must be collected in the following order to capture data before it degrades or is overwritten:
1.  **CPU Cache and Registers** (Most Volatile).
2.  **Routing Table, ARP Cache, Process Table, Kernel Statistics, and RAM.**
3.  **Temporary File Systems.**
4.  **Disk/Storage Media** (Hard Drives, SSDs).
5.  **Remote Logging and Monitoring Data** (SIEM logs, CloudTrail).
6.  **Physical Configuration and Network Topology** (Least Volatile).

#### 4.3. Evidence Collection and Acquisition
Only authorized IRT members or external forensic consultants may perform active collection.

**4.3.1 Volatile Data Collection (Live Response)**
1.  Insert a sanitized, trusted external USB drive containing a toolkit of statically linked binaries (e.g., FTK Imager Lite, dd).
2.  Execute tools from the external drive, not the compromised system's drive.
3.  Capture RAM contents to the external drive.
4.  Capture active network connections (e.g., `netstat`), running processes, and open files.

**4.3.2 Non-Volatile Data Collection (Disk Imaging)**
1.  If the system is powered down, remove the drive and connect it to a forensic workstation using a hardware **Write Blocker**.
2.  Create a forensic image (E01 or raw dd format). **Never** work on the original drive.
3.  Immediately generate a **Hash Value** (SHA-256) of the original drive and the new image. Verify they match.

**4.3.3 Cloud and SaaS Evidence**
1.  **Snapshot:** Create a snapshot of the compromised virtual machine (VM) or container immediately.
2.  **Logs:** Export logs from the cloud provider (e.g., AWS CloudTrail, Azure Monitor, Google Workspace Audit Logs) to a secure, immutable storage bucket (WORM - Write Once Read Many).
3.  **Metadata:** Document the exact time (UTC) of the snapshot and log export.

#### 4.4. Chain of Custody (CoC) Documentation
A Chain of Custody form must be initiated for every piece of evidence collected.
1.  **Documentation:** The CoC form must include:
    *   Case Number/Incident ID.
    *   Description of evidence (Make, Model, Serial #, or Filename/Hash).
    *   Date, Time, and Location of collection.
    *   Name and Signature of the collector.
    *   Reason for collection.
2.  **Transfer:** Every time evidence changes hands (e.g., from Collector to Secure Storage, or Storage to Analyst), both parties must sign and date the CoC form.
3.  **Gaps:** Any gap in the CoC renders the evidence inadmissible in legal proceedings.

#### 4.5. Evidence Storage and Security
1.  **Digital Locker:** Digital evidence must be stored in a dedicated, access-controlled repository (e.g., an encrypted S3 bucket with restricted IAM roles or a secure on-premise server).
2.  **Physical Locker:** Physical media (hard drives, USBs) must be stored in a locked, fire-resistant safe accessible only to the CISO and designated IRT members.
3.  **Encryption:** All stored evidence must be encrypted at rest.

#### 4.6. Analysis and Examination
1.  **Work on Copies:** Forensic analysis must **always** be performed on a verified copy of the evidence, never the original.
2.  **Tool Validation:** Use industry-standard tools (e.g., EnCase, Autopsy, Volatility).
3.  **Notes:** The analyst must maintain a contemporaneous bench note log detailing every step taken, tool used (including version), and finding discovered.

#### 4.7. Final Reporting
1.  The Lead Forensic Analyst must compile a "Forensic Report" summarizing findings.
2.  The report must be reviewed by the CISO and, if necessary, Legal Counsel.
3.  Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX Section 9.2**, all findings are confidential and subject to NDA protections.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Ultimate authority for evidence handling. Authorizes the seizure of hardware or accounts. Ensures alignment with Bylaws Article V. |
| **Incident Response Team (IRT)** | Performs technical collection and analysis. Maintains the Chain of Custody. |
| **First Responder** | Any staff member who identifies an incident. Responsible for initial preservation (Do Not Reboot). |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews evidence for privilege and legal admissibility. Advises on notification obligations (e.g., Texas Business Code §521.053). |
| **IT Operations** | Assists in providing access to logs, backups, and cloud consoles under IRT supervision. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this protocol, particularly regarding the **Chain of Custody** or **Preservation of Evidence**, may result in the inadmissibility of evidence in legal proceedings and disciplinary action against the responsible employee or volunteer, up to and including termination and legal action.

Intentional destruction of evidence during an active investigation is a criminal offense and will be referred to law enforcement.
