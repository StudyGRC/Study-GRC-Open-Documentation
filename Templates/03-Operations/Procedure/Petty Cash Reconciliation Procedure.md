### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, secure, and auditable process for the maintenance, disbursement, and reconciliation of Petty Cash funds within Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with **2 CFR § 200.303 (Internal Controls)** by implementing an "Imprest System" to mitigate the risks of fraud, theft, and financial mismanagement. It aligns with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** regarding the maintenance of correct and complete books of account.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees, volunteers, and Board members authorized to handle physical cash on behalf of the Organization.

*   **Primary Scope:** Temporary cash funds established for physical events (e.g., conferences, meetups) and any permanent minor office funds maintained by the Executive Director.
*   **Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to corporate credit card transactions or expense reimbursements processed via payroll/accounts payable.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Custodian** | The single individual authorized to hold, disburse, and safeguard the physical cash fund. |
| **Imprest System** | An accounting system where a fixed sum is maintained. The total of cash on hand plus receipts for expenditures must always equal the fixed authorized amount. |
| **Reconciler** | An individual, independent of the Custodian, responsible for verifying the accuracy of the fund (typically the CFO or Treasurer). |
| **Voucher** | A written record (receipt or log entry) documenting a specific disbursement of cash. |
| **Overage/Shortage** | The difference between the recorded balance and the actual physical cash on hand. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Establishment of Fund
1.  **Authorization:** A Petty Cash Fund may only be established upon written approval by the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or Treasurer.
2.  **Limit:** The standard limit for a permanent fund is **$250.00**. Temporary event funds may be authorized up to **$1,000.00** depending on event size.
3.  **Assignment:** A single Custodian must be assigned responsibility for the fund. Transfer of custody requires a formal sign-off by both parties.

#### 4.2 Security and Custody
1.  **Physical Security:** The cash must be kept in a locked box, safe, or secure drawer. The key or combination must be known **only** to the Custodian and the CFO (for emergency access).
2.  **Commingling Prohibited:** Personal funds or other organizational revenue (e.g., donation checks) must never be mixed with the Petty Cash Fund.

#### 4.3 Disbursement Rules
1.  **Allowable Expenses:** Funds are strictly for minor business expenses (e.g., postage, emergency event supplies) where credit card use is not feasible.
2.  **Prohibitions:** Petty cash must **not** be used for:
    *   Salaries or wages.
    *   Personal loans or advances (IOUs).
    *   Cashing personal checks.
    *   Travel expenses (per diem/hotels).
3.  **Documentation:** Every disbursement must be supported by an original receipt or a Petty Cash Voucher signed by the recipient.

#### 4.4 Reconciliation Process
The Custodian must perform a reconciliation **monthly** (for permanent funds) or **within 5 business days** of the conclusion of an event (for temporary funds).

**Step 1: Count Cash on Hand**
*   Physically count all currency and coins.
*   Record the total on the *Petty Cash Reconciliation Form*.

**Step 2: Sum Expenditures**
*   Total all receipts and vouchers currently in the box.
*   Ensure all receipts are dated within the current period and align with the Organization's mission.

**Step 3: Calculate Balance**
*   Formula: `(Cash on Hand) + (Total Receipts) = (Authorized Fund Amount)`
*   Example: `$50.00 (Cash) + $200.00 (Receipts) = $250.00 (Total Fund)`

**Step 4: Identify Variances**
*   If the total does not match the Authorized Fund Amount, the difference is recorded as "Cash Over/Short."
*   Variances exceeding **$10.00** require a written explanation attached to the reconciliation form.
*   Variances exceeding **$50.00** must be immediately reported to the Treasurer and may trigger an internal audit.

#### 4.5 Replenishment
1.  **Request:** When cash runs low (or at month-end), the Custodian submits the *Petty Cash Reconciliation Form* and all original receipts to the CFO.
2.  **Review:** The CFO (or Treasurer) reviews the receipts for compliance with the **Grant Allowable Costs Policy**.
3.  **Payment:** A check or ACH is issued to the Custodian for the *exact amount of the receipts*, bringing the cash on hand back to the original Authorized Fund Amount.
4.  **Booking:** Expenses are recorded in the General Ledger against the appropriate expense categories (e.g., Office Supplies, Postage).

#### 4.6 Surprise Audits
1.  To ensure integrity, the Treasurer or an appointed auditor must conduct a "surprise count" of the Petty Cash Fund at least **once annually**.
2.  The auditor must verify the cash and receipts in the presence of the Custodian.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Custodian** | Safeguards the cash; ensures all disbursements have receipts; performs initial reconciliation; reports shortages immediately. |
| **Reconciler (CFO)** | Reviews reconciliation forms; verifies receipts against Allowable Costs Policy; authorizes replenishment; ensures segregation of duties (cannot be Custodian). |
| **Treasurer** | Provides Board-level oversight; authorizes the establishment of new funds; conducts surprise audits; investigates significant shortages. |
| **Staff/Volunteers** | Provides valid receipts for all cash requests; signs vouchers acknowledging receipt of funds. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure compromises the Organization's internal controls and tax-exempt status.
*   **Minor Variances:** Repeated unexplained shortages may result in the revocation of Custodian privileges.
*   **Theft/Fraud:** Any evidence of theft, falsification of receipts, or personal use of funds will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment/volunteer status and referral to law enforcement.
