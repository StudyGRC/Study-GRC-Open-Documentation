### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the strict protocol for proposing, noticing, and voting on "Fundamental Actions" as defined by the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc. Because these actions alter the core legal structure or existence of the Organization, they require a higher threshold of consensus (Supermajority) than ordinary business. This procedure ensures compliance with Article X of the Bylaws and the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC).

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the Board of Directors and all Voting Members (Supporting and Honorary).

**Inclusions:**
*   Amendments to the Certificate of Formation.
*   Voluntary dissolution or winding up of the Organization.
*   Sale, lease, exchange, or other disposition of all, or substantially all, of the Organization's property and assets.
*   Merger or consolidation with another entity.

**Exclusions:**
*   Routine amendments to Bylaws (except those affecting Member rights, Director removal, or Purpose, which are treated with similar severity per Article XII).
*   Election of Directors (governed by *GOV-PRC-008*).
*   Ordinary operational decisions or budget approvals.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fundamental Action** | A significant event defined in Article X, Section 10.1 of the Bylaws that alters the Organization's core structure (e.g., merger, dissolution). |
| **Supermajority (2/3)** | The affirmative vote of at least two-thirds (66.67%) of the **total number of Voting Members** entitled to vote, regardless of the number of members present at the meeting. |
| **Record Date** | The specific date established by the Board to determine which members are eligible to receive notice and vote. |
| **Plan of Action** | The formal legal document detailing the Fundamental Action (e.g., Plan of Merger, Plan of Dissolution). |
| **Total Voting Power** | The total count of Supporting Members and Honorary Members in good standing as of the Record Date. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Initiation and Board Resolution
1.  **Proposal:** A Fundamental Action must be initiated by a resolution adopted by the Board of Directors.
2.  **Board Approval:** The Board must approve the proposed Fundamental Action and direct that the matter be submitted to a vote of the members.
3.  **Plan Preparation:** The Board, in consultation with Legal Counsel, must prepare a detailed "Plan of Action" (e.g., Plan of Merger or Dissolution) compliant with TBOC Chapter 22.

#### 4.2 Establishing the Record Date
1.  The Board must set a Record Date for determining the members entitled to notice and to vote.
2.  The Record Date must be at least 10 days but not more than 60 days before the meeting date.
3.  The Secretary must freeze the membership roster on the Record Date to establish the "Total Voting Power" (the denominator for the 2/3 calculation).

#### 4.3 Notice to Members
1.  **Timing:** Written notice must be delivered to each Voting Member no fewer than 10 days and no more than 60 days before the meeting.
2.  **Content:** The notice must expressly state that the purpose (or one of the purposes) of the meeting is to consider the Fundamental Action.
3.  **Attachments:** The notice must include a copy or a summary of the Plan of Action (e.g., the proposed amendment or plan of merger).
4.  **Delivery:** Notice shall be delivered via the primary email address on file, consistent with the *Member Data Privacy Notice*.

#### 4.4 The Voting Process
1.  **No Proxy Voting:** Pursuant to Bylaws Section 2.3, proxy voting is strictly prohibited. Members must cast votes personally via the designated secure electronic voting system.
2.  **Quorum vs. Threshold:** While a quorum (50% + 1) is required to open the meeting, the approval of a Fundamental Action requires a higher threshold.
3.  **Calculation of Votes:**
    *   To pass, the "Yes" votes must equal or exceed two-thirds (2/3) of the **Total Voting Power** established in Step 4.2.
    *   *Example:* If there are 100 Voting Members total, 67 "Yes" votes are required. If only 60 members attend the meeting and all vote "Yes," the motion **fails** because it did not reach 2/3 of the total membership.
4.  **Electronic Balloting:** The Organization shall utilize a secure electronic voting platform that provides an audit trail of all cast votes.

#### 4.5 Tabulation and Certification
1.  **Tabulation:** The Secretary (or a designated Inspector of Election) shall tabulate the votes immediately following the close of the voting window.
2.  **Verification:** The Secretary must verify that the number of affirmative votes meets the Supermajority requirement based on the roster as of the Record Date.
3.  **Certification:** The Secretary shall execute a "Certificate of Vote" documenting:
    *   The Total Voting Power (Total Members).
    *   The number of members present/participating.
    *   The specific count of "Yes," "No," and "Abstain" votes.
    *   The final result (Pass/Fail).

#### 4.6 Post-Approval Filings
1.  **State Filing:** If the Fundamental Action is approved, the President or authorized Officer must execute and file the appropriate documents (e.g., Certificate of Amendment, Certificate of Merger) with the Texas Secretary of State.
2.  **Effective Date:** The action becomes effective upon the filing of the certificate or at a later date specified in the certificate, not to exceed 90 days from filing.

#### 4.7 Notification of Outcome
1.  The Board must notify all members of the result of the vote within five (5) business days of certification.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Initiates the Fundamental Action, approves the Plan, and sets the Record Date. |
| **Secretary** | Prepares the voting list, issues notice, manages the electronic voting system, and certifies the results. |
| **Voting Members** | Review the Plan of Action and cast a vote (Yes/No/Abstain). |
| **Legal Counsel** | Drafts the Plan of Action and reviews all notices for compliance with TBOC and IRS regulations. |
| **President** | Executes filings with the Texas Secretary of State upon approval. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Strict Adherence:** Failure to strictly adhere to the notice, quorum, and supermajority voting requirements defined in this procedure renders the Fundamental Action void or voidable under Texas law.

**Liability:** Directors who knowingly permit a Fundamental Action to proceed without the requisite member approval may be held personally liable for breach of fiduciary duty.

**Record Retention:** All notices, plans, voting rosters, and certificates of vote must be retained permanently in the Organization's corporate records.
