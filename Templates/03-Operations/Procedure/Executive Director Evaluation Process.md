### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a formal, objective, and consistent framework for evaluating the performance of the Executive Director (CEO) of Study GRC Inc. This process ensures:
1.  **Fiduciary Oversight:** The Board fulfills its duty to manage the affairs of the Organization pursuant to Bylaws Article III.
2.  **Strategic Alignment:** The Executive Director’s goals are directly tied to the Organization’s mission of fostering a GRC community and providing open-source resources.
3.  **Regulatory Compliance:** Documentation supports the Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness regarding executive compensation under IRS regulations (Intermediate Sanctions).

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   **The Board of Directors:** Specifically the Officers and the Governance Committee responsible for administering the evaluation.
*   **The Executive Director (CEO):** The primary subject of the evaluation.

**Exclusions:**
*   Evaluations of other Operational Officers (e.g., CISO, CFO), which are conducted by the Executive Director, unless otherwise directed by the Board.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (ED)** | The primary operational leader of the Organization as defined in Bylaws Article V, Section 5.2. |
| **KPI** | Key Performance Indicator; a quantifiable measure used to evaluate the success of the Organization in meeting objectives for performance. |
| **360-Degree Review** | A feedback process where input is solicited from the ED's direct reports, peers, and external stakeholders, not just the Board. |
| **Executive Session** | A meeting of the Board of Directors from which the Executive Director and other staff are excluded to allow for confidential discussion. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Annual Goal Setting (January)
The evaluation cycle begins concurrently with the fiscal year.
1.  **Drafting:** The ED must submit a draft of "Annual Strategic Goals" to the Board President by January 15th. These goals must align with the Board-approved Strategic Plan and Budget.
2.  **Categories:** Goals must cover:
    *   **Financial Health:** Fundraising, grant compliance (2 CFR 200), and budget management.
    *   **Programmatic Impact:** Growth of the open-source GRC resource library and community engagement.
    *   **Operational Excellence:** Staff retention, remote culture management, and IT security (working with the CISO).
3.  **Approval:** The Board reviews, refines, and formally approves the goals by January 31st.

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Mid-Year Check-in (July)
To prevent surprises at the annual review, a formal check-in is required.
1.  **Report:** The ED submits a brief status report on the Annual Strategic Goals.
2.  **Meeting:** The Board President (and optionally the Governance Committee) meets with the ED to discuss progress, obstacles, and any necessary adjustments to goals due to unforeseen circumstances.
3.  **Documentation:** A summary of this meeting is filed in the ED’s personnel file.

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Data Collection (November)
The formal review process initiates 60 days prior to the end of the fiscal year.
1.  **Self-Assessment:** The ED completes a self-evaluation form, rating performance against the approved goals and core competencies.
2.  **360-Degree Feedback:** The Governance Committee distributes anonymous survey links to:
    *   All Direct Reports (e.g., VP of Education, CISO, CFO).
    *   A selection of Advisory Board members.
    *   Key external partners or major donors (if applicable).
3.  **Board Survey:** All Voting Directors complete an evaluation form focusing on Board relations, fiscal integrity, and strategic vision.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Board Review and Synthesis (December)
1.  **Aggregation:** The Governance Committee aggregates all data (Self-Assessment, 360 Feedback, Board Surveys) into a single "Performance Report."
2.  **Executive Session:** The Board meets in Executive Session (without the ED present) to review the Performance Report.
3.  **Consensus:** The Board discusses the findings and agrees on a final rating and key messages to be delivered to the ED.
4.  **Compensation Review:** If the ED receives a salary, the Board reviews comparability data (salary surveys for similar non-profits) to ensure any proposed increase is reasonable and compliant with IRS regulations.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: The Review Meeting (January)
1.  **Delivery:** The Board President and one other Officer meet with the ED to deliver the review.
2.  **Discussion:** The meeting covers:
    *   Achievements and areas of excellence.
    *   Areas requiring improvement.
    *   Feedback on the "remote-first" culture and community health.
3.  **Signature:** Both the President and the ED sign the final written evaluation. The ED may attach a written response if they disagree with the findings.

#### 4.6 Phase 6: Performance Improvement (Conditional)
If the ED’s performance is deemed "Unsatisfactory" or "Needs Improvement":
1.  **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP):** The Board must draft a PIP detailing specific deficiencies, required corrective actions, and a timeline for re-evaluation (typically 30-90 days).
2.  **Consequences:** Failure to meet the requirements of the PIP may result in termination of employment/service pursuant to Bylaws Article V, Section 5.1.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board President** | Leads the evaluation process, facilitates the Executive Session, and delivers the final review to the ED. |
| **Governance Committee** | Administers the survey tools, aggregates data, and ensures the process adheres to the timeline. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides specific input regarding the ED’s financial management and adherence to the budget. |
| **Executive Director** | Drafts initial goals, completes self-assessment, and participates in review meetings constructively. |
| **Board of Directors** | Provides objective feedback via the Board Survey and votes on final compensation adjustments. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Failure to Conduct Evaluation:** Failure by the Board to conduct this annual evaluation constitutes a lapse in governance oversight and may expose the Organization to risks regarding executive compensation compliance (IRS Form 990 reporting).

**Non-Compliance by ED:** Failure by the Executive Director to participate in the process (e.g., refusing to submit goals or self-assessment) will be documented as insubordination and reflected negatively in the final performance rating.
