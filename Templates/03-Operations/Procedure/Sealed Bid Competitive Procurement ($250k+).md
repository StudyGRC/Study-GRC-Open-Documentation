### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory steps for conducting **Sealed Bid** procurements for goods, services, or construction projects exceeding the Simplified Acquisition Threshold (currently $250,000). This procedure ensures compliance with **2 CFR 200.320(c)**, promotes open and free competition, prevents fraud and favoritism, and ensures Study GRC Inc. obtains the lowest price from a responsive and responsible bidder.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all procurement actions where the aggregate cost is expected to exceed **$250,000** (or the current Federal Simplified Acquisition Threshold, whichever is lower) and where the conditions for sealed bidding are met.

**Applicability Conditions:**
*   A complete, adequate, and realistic specification or purchase description is available;
*   Two or more responsible bidders are willing and able to compete effectively for the business; and
*   The procurement lends itself to a firm fixed price contract and the selection of the successful bidder can be made principally on the basis of price.

**Exclusions:**
*   Procurements under $250,000 (See *FIN-PRC-024 Simplified Acquisition*).
*   Procurements where price is not the primary factor (e.g., architectural/engineering services), which require Competitive Proposals (RFP) (See *FIN-PRC-027*).
*   Sole Source procurements (See *FIN-PRC-028*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Sealed Bid** | A formal procurement method where bids are publicly solicited and a firm fixed price contract is awarded to the responsible bidder whose bid, conforming with all the material terms and conditions of the invitation for bids, is the lowest in price. |
| **Invitation for Bids (IFB)** | The solicitation document used in sealed bidding. |
| **Responsive Bidder** | A bidder who has submitted a bid that complies with all the requirements of the solicitation (specs, delivery, terms). |
| **Responsible Bidder** | A bidder who has the technical and financial capacity to perform the work. |
| **Public Opening** | A scheduled event where all bids are opened and read aloud to ensure transparency. |
| **Non-Collusion Affidavit** | A sworn statement that the bidder has not conspired with others to fix prices. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. Pre-Solicitation Phase
1.  **Needs Assessment & Independent Cost Estimate (ICE):**
    *   The Requesting Department must define the scope of work or specifications.
    *   An Independent Cost Estimate (ICE) must be generated *before* receiving bids to determine reasonableness, per 2 CFR 200.323.
2.  **Board Authorization:**
    *   Pursuant to the *Delegation of Authority Matrix*, expenditures exceeding $250,000 require prior Board approval to initiate the solicitation process.
3.  **Preparation of Invitation for Bids (IFB):**
    *   The CFO or designee prepares the IFB.
    *   **Specifications:** Must be clear and not unduly restrictive (e.g., avoiding "brand name only" unless "or equal" is included).
    *   **Terms:** Must include all applicable federal clauses (e.g., Davis-Bacon Act for construction, Byrd Anti-Lobbying Amendment).

#### 4.2. Solicitation and Advertising
1.  **Public Advertising:**
    *   The IFB must be publicly advertised to ensure adequate competition.
    *   **Channels:** Study GRC "Transparency" webpage, SAM.gov (if federal funds involved), and at least one relevant industry publication or broad-circulation newspaper.
2.  **Notice Period:**
    *   Bidders must be provided sufficient time to prepare bids prior to the date set for opening. The standard minimum period is **thirty (30) calendar days**, unless an emergency is declared under *FIN-PRC-036*.
3.  **Q&A Period:**
    *   All questions from potential bidders must be submitted in writing.
    *   Answers must be provided as an official Addendum to the IFB and sent to *all* known prospective bidders simultaneously to ensure a level playing field.

#### 4.3. Bid Submission (Remote/Digital Protocol)
1.  **Submission Format:**
    *   Due to the remote nature of Study GRC Inc., physical sealed envelopes are generally not practical.
    *   Bids must be submitted electronically via a designated secure portal or as **password-protected PDF files** sent to a dedicated procurement email address (e.g., `bids@studygrc.org`).
2.  **Security:**
    *   The password for the PDF must **not** be included in the email. It must be provided only during the Public Opening session.
    *   Bids received after the exact time specified in the IFB will be rejected and returned unopened (or deleted).

#### 4.4. Public Bid Opening
1.  **Scheduling:**
    *   The IFB must state the exact date, time, and virtual location (e.g., Zoom link) of the bid opening.
2.  **Conducting the Opening:**
    *   The CFO (or designee) serves as the Bid Officer.
    *   The meeting must be recorded.
    *   Bidders attending virtually will provide their document passwords via private chat or separate email at the start of the meeting.
    *   The Bid Officer opens each file, verifies the presence of required documents (Non-Collusion Affidavit, Bid Bond), and **reads the bid amount aloud**.
3.  **Tabulation:**
    *   A "Bid Tabulation Sheet" is created in real-time, recording the bidder's name and total price.

#### 4.5. Evaluation and Award
1.  **Responsiveness Review:**
    *   The Evaluation Committee reviews the lowest bid to ensure it conforms to all specifications (Pass/Fail).
2.  **Responsibility Review:**
    *   The Committee verifies the lowest bidder's capacity (financial health, references) and checks for suspension/debarment on SAM.gov.
3.  **Rejection of Bids:**
    *   Any or all bids may be rejected if there is a sound, documented reason (e.g., all bids exceed the budget, evidence of collusion).
4.  **Award Recommendation:**
    *   The contract must be awarded to the **lowest responsive and responsible bidder**.
    *   If the lowest bidder is disqualified, the Committee moves to the next lowest bidder.

#### 4.6. Final Approval and Contracting
1.  **Board Approval:**
    *   The CFO presents the Bid Tabulation and recommendation to the Board of Directors for final approval of the expenditure.
2.  **Contract Execution:**
    *   A firm fixed-price contract is drafted.
    *   The Executive Director signs the contract only after Board approval is minuted.
3.  **Notification:**
    *   Unsuccessful bidders are notified in writing promptly.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requesting Department** | Develops clear specifications; assists in technical review of responsiveness. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Acts as the Procurement Officer; manages the IFB process; ensures 2 CFR 200 compliance; conducts the public bid opening. |
| **Evaluation Committee** | Ad hoc group (minimum 3 staff/Board members) that reviews bids for responsiveness and responsibility. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the initiation of the solicitation; approves the final contract award based on the lowest responsive/responsible bid. |
| **Executive Director** | Executes the final contract; ensures operational alignment. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Conflict of Interest:** All staff and Board members involved in the selection, award, or administration of the contract must sign a specific Conflict of Interest disclosure for the procurement. No employee, officer, or agent may participate if a real or apparent conflict exists (See *GOV-POL-005*).
*   **Federal Clawback:** Failure to strictly adhere to this procedure when using federal funds may result in the disallowance of costs by the grantor agency, requiring Study GRC Inc. to repay the funds from non-federal sources.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees found violating this procedure, particularly regarding the confidentiality of bids or collusion, are subject to immediate termination and potential legal prosecution.
