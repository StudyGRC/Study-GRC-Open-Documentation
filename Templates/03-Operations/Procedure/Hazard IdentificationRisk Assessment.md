### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized methodology for the proactive identification of hazards and the assessment of associated risks across all Study GRC Inc. operations. This procedure ensures compliance with the "General Duty Clause" of the OSH Act of 1970 and aligns with Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) best practices. It is designed to prevent injury, illness, financial loss, and reputational damage by prioritizing the safety of our staff, volunteers, and community members—specifically including youth participants in our educational programs.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all activities, locations, and assets under the control of Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to:
*   **Physical Environments:** Rented event venues, temporary workspaces, and off-site meeting locations.
*   **Remote Work Environments:** Home offices of employees and contractors (limited to ergonomic and information security hazards).
*   **Digital Environments:** Online community platforms, learning management systems (LMS), and digital communication channels used by youth and adult members.
*   **Operational Activities:** Financial transactions, grant management, and volunteer coordination.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not cover strategic business risks (e.g., market competition) unless such risks pose a direct threat to the organization's charitable status or financial solvency.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hazard** | A potential source of harm or adverse health effect on a person, or damage to property or the environment. |
| **Risk** | The likelihood that a person may be harmed or suffers adverse health effects if exposed to a hazard, combined with the severity of that harm. |
| **Control Measure** | An action or system taken to eliminate or reduce a risk. |
| **Risk Register** | A central repository (database or spreadsheet) where all identified risks, their scores, and mitigation plans are recorded. |
| **Residual Risk** | The level of risk remaining after control measures have been implemented. |
| **Vulnerable Person** | Individuals requiring additional protection, including minors (youth), persons with disabilities, and pregnant persons. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Preparation and Team Assembly
4.1.1 The Executive Director or designee must appoint a Risk Assessment Team (RAT) appropriate for the activity being assessed.
4.1.2 For physical events involving minors, the RAT must include the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or a designated Youth Protection Lead.
4.1.3 The RAT must review historical incident reports, near-miss logs, and relevant legal standards (e.g., Texas Family Code for youth interactions, OSHA standards for physical safety) prior to assessment.

#### 4.2 Hazard Identification
The RAT shall identify hazards using the following methods, categorized by domain:

**4.2.1 Physical Hazards (Events & Venues)**
*   Inspect venues for slip/trip hazards, electrical safety, and emergency exit accessibility.
*   Evaluate environmental factors (heat/cold stress, noise levels).
*   Assess security vulnerabilities (unauthorized access points).

**4.2.2 Digital & Content Hazards**
*   Identify potential for cyberbullying, harassment, or grooming within community platforms.
*   Review educational content for accuracy and age-appropriateness.
*   Assess data privacy risks regarding member PII (Personally Identifiable Information).

**4.2.3 Operational & Financial Hazards**
*   Identify segregation of duties failures (fraud risk).
*   Assess single points of failure in key personnel or technology.

#### 4.3 Risk Analysis (Scoring)
4.3.1 Risks are analyzed by determining **Likelihood** and **Severity** to calculate a Risk Score.
4.3.2 The RAT must use the following Risk Matrix:

**Likelihood Scale:**
1.  **Rare:** May occur only in exceptional circumstances.
2.  **Unlikely:** Could occur at some time.
3.  **Possible:** Might occur at some time.
4.  **Likely:** Will probably occur in most circumstances.
5.  **Almost Certain:** Expected to occur in most circumstances.

**Severity Scale:**
1.  **Insignificant:** No injury, low financial loss (<$100).
2.  **Minor:** First aid required, medium financial loss.
3.  **Moderate:** Medical treatment required, high financial loss, localized reputational damage.
4.  **Major:** Extensive injuries, major financial loss, loss of grant funding capability.
5.  **Catastrophic:** Death, permanent disability, dissolution of the organization.

**Risk Score Formula:** Risk Score = Likelihood x Severity

#### 4.4 Risk Evaluation and Tolerance
4.4.1 Compare the Risk Score against the Tolerance Thresholds to determine required action:

| Score | Risk Level | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-4** | **Low** | Acceptable. Monitor periodically. |
| **5-9** | **Medium** | ALARP (As Low As Reasonably Practicable). Implement controls within 30 days. |
| **10-16** | **High** | **Unacceptable.** Activity must not proceed until controls reduce risk to Medium or Low. Immediate Board notification required. |
| **17-25** | **Critical** | **STOP WORK.** Immediate cessation of activity. Executive Director and Board intervention required. |

#### 4.5 Risk Treatment (Control Implementation)
4.5.1 The RAT must apply the **Hierarchy of Controls** to mitigate identified risks, in the following order of preference:
1.  **Elimination:** Physically remove the hazard (e.g., canceling a high-risk activity).
2.  **Substitution:** Replace the hazard (e.g., using non-toxic materials).
3.  **Engineering Controls:** Isolate people from the hazard (e.g., guardrails, software firewalls).
4.  **Administrative Controls:** Change the way people work (e.g., training, rotation, policies).
5.  **PPE:** Protect the worker/participant with Personal Protective Equipment.

4.5.2 For risks involving Youth Protection, "Two-Deep Leadership" (Administrative Control) is mandatory regardless of the risk score.

#### 4.6 Recording and Reporting
4.6.1 All identified hazards, risk scores, and selected controls must be documented in the **Master Risk Register**.
4.6.2 The Risk Register must be reviewed and updated:
*   Annually (General Operations).
*   Prior to any major event (Event-Specific).
*   Following any significant incident or near-miss.
*   When there are significant changes to regulations or Bylaws.

4.6.3 In accordance with Bylaws Article VIII (Records), risk assessments are corporate records and must be retained for a minimum of 7 years.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Provides oversight of the organization's risk profile; reviews Critical risks (Score 17+) pursuant to Bylaws Article III duties. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ultimate accountability for the implementation of this procedure; approves Risk Assessments for major events. |
| **Risk Owners (Dept Heads)** | Conducts assessments within their domain; ensures controls are implemented and effective. |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Duty to report new hazards immediately; complies with all established control measures. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Specific oversight for hazards related to community culture, youth safety, and volunteer well-being. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure, including the falsification of risk assessments or the willful ignoring of identified hazards, may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

For licensed professionals or Board members, negligence in risk management may result in removal for cause as outlined in Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6.
