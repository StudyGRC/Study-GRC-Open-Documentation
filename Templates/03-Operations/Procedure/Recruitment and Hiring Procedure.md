### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, non-discriminatory, and merit-based framework for recruiting and selecting employees for Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) guidelines, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), the Texas Labor Code, and the Organization’s commitment to diversity and inclusion. It mitigates legal risks associated with hiring bias and ensures alignment with the Organization’s budgetary constraints.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the recruitment and hiring of all **employees** (full-time, part-time, temporary, and seasonal) of Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Board of Directors:** Election of Directors is governed by **Article III** of the Bylaws.
*   **Volunteers:** Recruitment of unpaid volunteers is governed by the *Volunteer Recruitment Policy (HR-POL-070)*.
*   **Independent Contractors:** Engagement of 1099 contractors is governed by the *Procurement Policy (FIN-POL-022)*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | The individual to whom the new hire will directly report; responsible for defining the role and selecting the candidate. |
| **Essential Functions** | The fundamental job duties that an employee must be able to perform, with or without reasonable accommodation (ADA requirement). |
| **Selection Committee** | A panel of at least two (2) individuals designated to interview and score candidates to minimize individual bias. |
| **Nepotism** | The practice of those with power or influence favoring relatives or friends, especially by giving them jobs. |
| **Protected Class** | Groups protected from employment discrimination by law, including race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, gender identity, and sexual orientation), national origin, age (40 or older), disability, or genetic information. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Position Requisition and Approval
Before any recruitment activity begins, the position must be formally approved to ensure financial viability.

1.  **Requisition Submission:** The Hiring Manager must submit a *Personnel Requisition Form* to the Executive Director and CFO.
2.  **Budget Verification:** The CFO must verify that the position is accounted for in the current fiscal year budget (per *Budget Development Policy FIN-POL-005*).
3.  **Job Description Creation:** The Hiring Manager must draft a Job Description using the *ADA-Compliant Job Description Template (HR-TMP-020)*. This document must clearly distinguish between "Essential Functions" and "Preferred Qualifications."
4.  **Final Approval:** The Executive Director must sign off on the requisition.

#### 4.2 Sourcing and Posting
To ensure Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO), positions must be advertised to reach a diverse pool of candidates.

1.  **Internal Posting:** Positions must be posted internally (via Slack or email) for a minimum of three (3) business days to allow current employees to apply.
2.  **External Posting:** Positions must be posted on the Study GRC Careers page and at least two (2) external job boards (e.g., LinkedIn, Indeed, or industry-specific boards).
3.  **Remote Designation:** As a remote-first organization, postings must specify if the role is "Remote - Anywhere in US" or "Remote - Texas Resident Required."
4.  **EEO Statement:** All job postings must include the Organization’s standard Equal Opportunity Employer statement.

#### 4.3 Candidate Screening
1.  **Resume Review:** The Hiring Manager or HR designee reviews applications against the "Required Qualifications" listed in the Job Description.
2.  **Blind Review (Recommended):** Where feasible, identifying information (name, address) should be redacted during the initial screen to reduce unconscious bias.
3.  **Phone Screening:** A preliminary 15-30 minute call to verify:
    *   Availability and start date.
    *   Salary expectations (to ensure alignment with budget).
    *   Basic technical qualifications.
    *   *Note: Questions regarding salary history are prohibited.*

#### 4.4 Interview Process
1.  **Selection Committee:** A Selection Committee of at least two (2) members must be formed. One member must be from outside the hiring department to ensure objectivity.
2.  **Structured Interview Guide:** The Hiring Manager must prepare a standard set of questions relevant to the *Essential Functions* of the job. **All candidates must be asked the same core questions.**
3.  **Prohibited Questions:** Interviewers are strictly prohibited from asking questions related to a candidate’s:
    *   Age, Race, Religion, or National Origin.
    *   Marital status, children, or childcare arrangements.
    *   Health, disabilities, or workers' compensation history.
    *   Political affiliation.
4.  **Scoring:** Interviewers must complete an *Interview Evaluation Scorecard* immediately following the interview.

#### 4.5 Selection and Offer
1.  **Reference Checks:** A minimum of two (2) professional references must be contacted for the finalist candidate.
2.  **Background Check Initiation:**
    *   Pursuant to the *Background Check Policy (HR-POL-024)*, a conditional offer of employment is contingent upon the successful completion of a background check.
    *   **Youth Protection:** Because Study GRC Inc. serves K-12 populations, **no employee may commence work** until the criminal history check is adjudicated as "Clear."
3.  **Conflict of Interest Check:** The candidate must disclose any potential conflicts (e.g., relationships with current Board members or vendors) in accordance with *Bylaws Article IX*.
4.  **Offer Approval:** The final compensation package must be approved by the Executive Director and CFO.
5.  **Offer Letter:** An *Employment Offer Letter* is generated. It must state that employment is "At-Will" under Texas law and include the start date, salary, and reporting structure.

#### 4.6 Post-Hire Handoff
1.  **Acceptance:** Once the signed Offer Letter is received, the recruitment phase ends.
2.  **Notification:** Unsuccessful candidates who were interviewed must be notified via email within five (5) business days of the offer acceptance.
3.  **Record Retention:** All applications, resumes, interview notes, and scorecards must be retained for a minimum of one (1) year from the date of the hiring decision, in compliance with *EEOC Recordkeeping Requirements* and the *Document Retention Policy (GOV-POL-012)*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Defines role requirements, conducts interviews, selects finalist, and ensures technical fit. |
| **Human Resources / Ops** | Manages job postings, ensures EEO compliance, conducts background checks, and issues offer letters. |
| **Selection Committee** | Participates in interviews and provides objective scoring of candidates. |
| **CFO** | Verifies budget availability for the position and approves final compensation offer. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides final authority on all hiring decisions and ensures alignment with organizational strategy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure—specifically regarding discriminatory practices or failure to conduct mandatory background checks—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

Violations of EEO laws may subject the Organization to federal and state legal action. Any employee who suspects discrimination in the hiring process is encouraged to report it via the *Whistleblower Complaint Intake Form (GOV-FRM-010)*.
