### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the specific steps for administering leaves of absence under the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 (FMLA). This document ensures Study GRC Inc. complies with federal law by providing eligible employees with up to 12 weeks of job-protected, unpaid leave for qualified medical and family reasons, while defining the responsibilities of both the employee and the Organization during the leave period.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Employees** of Study GRC Inc.

*   **Inclusions:** Full-time, part-time, and remote employees who meet the eligibility criteria defined in Section 4.1.
*   **Exclusions:** Independent Contractors (1099), Volunteers, Board Directors, and Advisory Board members are not eligible for FMLA leave.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **FMLA** | The Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993, a federal law providing eligible employees with job-protected, unpaid leave for specified family and medical reasons. |
| **Serious Health Condition** | An illness, injury, impairment, or physical or mental condition that involves inpatient care or continuing treatment by a health care provider. |
| **Rolling 12-Month Period** | The method used by Study GRC Inc. to calculate leave availability. The 12-month period is measured backward from the date an employee uses any FMLA leave. |
| **Intermittent Leave** | Leave taken in separate blocks of time due to a single qualifying reason, rather than one continuous period. |
| **Remote Worksite** | For FMLA eligibility purposes, the "worksite" for remote employees is the office to which they report and from which assignments are made (typically the Texas Registered Office). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Eligibility Determination
To be eligible for FMLA leave, an employee must meet all three of the following criteria as of the date the leave is to start:
1.  Have worked for Study GRC Inc. for at least **12 months** (need not be consecutive).
2.  Have worked at least **1,250 hours** during the 12-month period immediately preceding the start of the leave.
3.  Work at a location where the Organization employs 50 or more employees within 75 miles.
    *   *Note on Remote Employees:* Per 29 C.F.R. § 825.111(a)(2), a remote employee's personal residence is not a worksite. The worksite is the office to which they report. Eligibility is based on the employee count at the reporting hub (Texas HQ).

#### 4.2 Qualifying Reasons for Leave
Eligible employees may take up to **12 workweeks** of leave in a 12-month period for:
1.  The birth of a child and to bond with the newborn child within one year of birth.
2.  The placement with the employee of a child for adoption or foster care and to bond with the newly placed child within one year of placement.
3.  A serious health condition that makes the employee unable to perform the functions of their job.
4.  To care for the employee’s spouse, son, daughter, or parent who has a serious health condition.
5.  Any qualifying exigency arising out of the fact that the employee’s spouse, son, daughter, or parent is a military member on covered active duty.

*Military Caregiver Leave:* An eligible employee may take up to **26 workweeks** of leave during a single 12-month period to care for a covered servicemember with a serious injury or illness.

#### 4.3 Request and Notice Process
Employees must provide notice to the **Chief Financial Officer (HR Lead)** or their designee.

1.  **Foreseeable Leave:** If the need for leave is foreseeable (e.g., expected birth, planned surgery), the employee must provide at least **30 days' advance notice**. If 30 days is not practicable, notice must be given as soon as possible.
2.  **Unforeseeable Leave:** The employee must provide notice as soon as practicable, generally following the Organization's usual call-in procedures for reporting an absence.
3.  **Submission:** Employees must submit the *Leave of Absence Request Form*.

#### 4.4 Medical Certification
1.  **Requirement:** For leaves related to a serious health condition (self or family), Study GRC Inc. requires medical certification from a health care provider.
2.  **Timeline:** The employee must provide the certification within **15 calendar days** of the Organization's request, unless it is not practicable under the particular circumstances to do so despite the employee's diligent, good faith efforts.
3.  **Incomplete Certification:** If the certification is incomplete or insufficient, the Organization will state in writing what additional information is necessary and allow the employee **7 calendar days** to cure the deficiency.
4.  **Second/Third Opinions:** The Organization reserves the right to require a second or third medical opinion at the Organization's expense.

#### 4.5 Designation of Leave
Within **5 business days** of having enough information to determine whether the leave is being taken for a FMLA-qualifying reason, HR will provide the employee with a *Designation Notice* (DOL Form WH-382) indicating whether the leave will be designated and counted as FMLA leave.

#### 4.6 Concurrent Use of Paid Leave
FMLA leave is unpaid. However, Study GRC Inc. requires employees to use accrued paid leave concurrently with FMLA leave.
1.  **Substitution:** Employees must use accrued Paid Time Off (PTO) or Sick Leave during FMLA leave.
2.  **Status:** The use of paid leave does not extend the 12-week (or 26-week) FMLA entitlement. The paid leave and FMLA leave run concurrently.
3.  **Exhaustion:** Once accrued paid leave is exhausted, the remainder of the FMLA leave will be unpaid.

#### 4.7 Benefits Continuation
1.  **Health Insurance:** During FMLA leave, Study GRC Inc. will maintain the employee’s health coverage under any "group health plan" on the same terms as if the employee had continued to work.
2.  **Employee Contributions:** Employees must continue to pay their share of health insurance premiums. If the employee is on paid leave (using PTO), premiums will be deducted from their paycheck. If on unpaid leave, the employee must make payment arrangements with the CFO.
3.  **Lapse of Coverage:** Coverage may be cancelled if the employee's premium payment is more than 30 days late, provided the Organization has given 15 days' written notice.

#### 4.8 Job Restoration
Upon return from FMLA leave, an employee will be restored to their original job or to an equivalent job with equivalent pay, benefits, and other employment terms and conditions.
*   **Fitness-for-Duty:** For leaves taken due to the employee's own serious health condition, the employee must present a fitness-for-duty certification from their health care provider before returning to work.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Provide timely notice of the need for leave; submit complete and sufficient medical certification; maintain communication with manager regarding return-to-work status; pay insurance premiums during unpaid leave. |
| **Manager** | Notify HR/CFO immediately upon learning an employee may need leave for a medical/family reason; **do not** ask for specific medical details or diagnoses; ensure no retaliation occurs against the employee. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (HR Lead)** | Determine eligibility; issue required DOL notices (Rights & Responsibilities, Designation); track FMLA hours usage; maintain medical records confidentially and separate from personnel files. |
| **Executive Director** | Ensure organizational compliance with FMLA regulations; approve policy exceptions where legally permissible and non-discriminatory. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Non-Retaliation:** Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits interference with, restraint of, or denial of the exercise of any right provided under the FMLA. Retaliation against any employee for requesting leave, taking leave, or filing a complaint regarding FMLA violations is grounds for immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

**Confidentiality:** All medical information provided in connection with an FMLA request will be kept confidential and maintained in a file separate from the employee’s general personnel file, in accordance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and HIPAA privacy standards.

Failure to comply with the procedural requirements of this document (e.g., failure to provide certification) may result in the delay or denial of FMLA protection.
