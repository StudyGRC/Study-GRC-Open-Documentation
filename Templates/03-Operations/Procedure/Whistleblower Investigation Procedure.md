### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, confidential, and rigorous process for the receipt, retention, and treatment of complaints regarding accounting, internal accounting controls, auditing matters, or violations of the Code of Ethics and Conduct. This procedure ensures Study GRC Inc. complies with the **Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 (SOX)** regarding whistleblower protection and the **Texas Whistleblower Act**, fostering an environment where concerns can be raised without fear of retaliation.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all "Protected Disclosures" made by **Reporting Persons**.

*   **Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, Employees, Volunteers, Contractors, and Vendors of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Subject Matter:**
    *   Financial impropriety, fraud, or theft.
    *   Violations of federal or state laws (including Grant Compliance/2 CFR 200).
    *   Gross mismanagement or abuse of authority.
    *   Ethical violations as defined in the *Board Code of Ethics*.
    *   Retaliation against an individual for making a report.
*   **Exclusions:** Standard employment grievances (e.g., performance evaluations, interpersonal conflicts) that do not involve illegal acts or policy violations should be handled via the *HR Grievance Dispute Procedure*, unless the reporter reasonably believes the grievance is a result of whistleblower retaliation.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reporting Person** | The individual (whistleblower) who submits a complaint or concern in Good Faith. |
| **Good Faith** | A reasonable belief that the information disclosed indicates a violation, even if the belief turns out to be mistaken. Reports made with malicious intent or knowing falsity are not made in Good Faith. |
| **Protected Disclosure** | Communication about actual or suspected misconduct that is protected by law and internal policy. |
| **Compliance Officer** | The individual designated by the Board (often the Chair of the Audit & Risk Committee or General Counsel) to oversee the investigation. |
| **The Subject** | The individual(s) accused of misconduct in the Protected Disclosure. |
| **Retaliation** | Any adverse action (termination, demotion, harassment, exclusion) taken against a Reporting Person because they made a Protected Disclosure. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Complaint Intake
1.  **Submission Channels:** Reporting Persons may submit concerns via:
    *   **Email:** Dedicated secure inbox (e.g., `whistleblower@studygrc.org`) accessible only by the Audit Committee Chair.
    *   **Web Form:** Anonymized submission form on the internal portal.
    *   **Direct Mail:** Sealed envelope marked "Confidential - To be opened by Audit Chair only."
2.  **Anonymous Reporting:** Anonymous reports are accepted. However, the Reporting Person must understand that anonymity may limit the investigator's ability to gather evidence or verify allegations.
3.  **Acknowledgment:** If contact information is provided, the Compliance Officer must acknowledge receipt within **five (5) business days**.

#### 4.2 Preliminary Review (Triage)
1.  **Initial Assessment:** Within **ten (10) business days** of receipt, the Compliance Officer (or designee) will review the complaint to determine:
    *   Does the allegation fall within the Scope of this procedure?
    *   Is there sufficient information to proceed?
    *   Does the complaint involve the Compliance Officer or a Board Director?
2.  **Conflict of Interest Check:**
    *   If the complaint involves the **Executive Director**, the investigation must be led by the Board of Directors.
    *   If the complaint involves a **Board Director**, that Director is recused from all proceedings per *Bylaws Article IX (Conflict of Interest)*.
3.  **Disposition:**
    *   **Proceed:** Credible allegation requiring investigation.
    *   **Refer:** Issue is an HR/Management matter; refer to Executive Director (unless ED is the Subject).
    *   **Dismiss:** Frivolous, lacks specific detail to investigate, or previously resolved.

#### 4.3 Investigation Team Formation
1.  **Investigator Selection:** The Compliance Officer appoints an Investigator or Investigation Team. This may include:
    *   Internal independent personnel (e.g., HR Director, non-conflicted Board Member).
    *   External Legal Counsel (required for allegations of criminal conduct or significant financial fraud).
    *   Forensic Accountants.
2.  **Legal Hold:** If the allegation involves documentation or data, the Compliance Officer must immediately trigger the *Legal Hold Notification Procedure* to prevent the destruction of evidence (compliance with *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*).

#### 4.4 Investigation Execution
1.  **Fact-Finding:** The Investigator shall:
    *   Interview the Reporting Person (if known) to gather specific details.
    *   Secure and review relevant documents, financial records, emails, and logs.
    *   Interview witnesses.
    *   Interview the Subject, providing them an opportunity to respond to the allegations (while maintaining the confidentiality of the Reporting Person).
2.  **Remote Investigation Protocols:**
    *   Interviews conducted via video conference must use approved, encrypted platforms.
    *   Recording of interviews requires the consent of all parties, in compliance with Texas "One-Party Consent" laws (though organizational policy may require Two-Party consent for transparency).
3.  **Confidentiality:** The identity of the Reporting Person will be protected to the fullest extent possible, consistent with the need to conduct a fair investigation and legal requirements.

#### 4.5 Findings and Report
1.  **Standard of Proof:** The investigation shall determine findings based on a "preponderance of the evidence" (i.e., it is more likely than not that the violation occurred).
2.  **Written Report:** The Investigator must draft a confidential report containing:
    *   Summary of allegations.
    *   Investigative methodology.
    *   Summary of evidence and witness statements.
    *   Findings of fact.
    *   Conclusion (Substantiated, Unsubstantiated, or Inconclusive).
    *   Recommended remedial actions.

#### 4.6 Adjudication and Resolution
1.  **Review Authority:**
    *   **Operational Issues:** The Executive Director reviews findings and determines discipline.
    *   **Executive/Board Issues:** The Audit & Risk Committee (or full Board) reviews findings.
2.  **Corrective Action:** If substantiated, the Organization will take appropriate action, which may include:
    *   Disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board (per *Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6*).
    *   Restitution.
    *   Self-reporting to government agencies (e.g., IRS, grantors) if legally required.
    *   Modification of internal controls to prevent recurrence.

#### 4.7 Closure and Feedback
1.  **Notification:** The Reporting Person will be notified that the investigation has concluded.
    *   *Note:* Due to privacy laws, specific details of disciplinary actions against other employees may not be disclosed to the Reporting Person.
2.  **Record Retention:** All records relating to the investigation shall be kept in a secure, encrypted file for a minimum of **seven (7) years**, in compliance with the *Records Retention Schedule*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reporting Person** | Report violations in Good Faith; maintain confidentiality of the investigation; cooperate with investigators. |
| **Compliance Officer / Audit Chair** | Receive complaints; determine scope; appoint investigators; ensure non-retaliation; report summary to the Board. |
| **Investigator** | Conduct impartial fact-finding; secure evidence; draft the final report. |
| **Board of Directors** | Provide oversight; adjudicate complaints involving the Executive Director or other Directors; ensure resources for external counsel if needed. |
| **The Subject** | Cooperate fully with the investigation; refrain from contacting or retaliating against witnesses or the Reporting Person. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Zero Tolerance for Retaliation:** In accordance with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act and Study GRC Inc. policy, retaliation against any individual who reports a violation in Good Faith is strictly prohibited.

*   Any employee, officer, or director found to have engaged in retaliation will be subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination and potential legal action.
*   Any Reporting Person who knowingly makes a false allegation (Malicious Intent) may be subject to disciplinary action.
