### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the specific protocols for detecting, reporting, investigating, and resolving instances of suspected fraud, waste, and abuse within Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization").

As a Federal Grantee and 501(c)(3) entity, the Organization is legally mandated under **2 CFR § 200.113** to disclose violations of Federal criminal law involving fraud, bribery, or gratuity violations potentially affecting the Federal award. This procedure operationalizes the Organization's commitment to financial integrity as outlined in **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** and ensures compliance with the **False Claims Act**.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all:
*   **Personnel:** Board Directors, Officers, Employees, and Volunteers.
*   **External Parties:** Contractors, Subrecipients, Vendors, and Consultants.
*   **Assets:** All financial resources, tangible property, intellectual property (as defined in **Bylaws Article IX**), and data.

**Exclusions:** Minor personnel grievances that do not involve financial misconduct or legal violations (refer to *HR-POL-045 Employee Grievance Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fraud** | Any intentional act or omission designed to deceive others, resulting in the victim suffering a loss and/or the perpetrator achieving a gain. |
| **Waste** | The act of using or expending resources carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose. |
| **Abuse** | Behavior that is deficient or improper when compared with behavior that a prudent person would consider reasonable and necessary business practice. |
| **Whistleblower** | An individual who, in good faith, reports a suspected violation of law or policy. |
| **Investigating Authority** | The designated individual or committee (usually the Treasurer or Audit Committee) responsible for overseeing the inquiry. |
| **2 CFR § 200.113** | Federal regulation requiring written disclosure of all violations of Federal criminal law involving fraud, bribery, or gratuity violations. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Fraud Detection (Red Flags)
All personnel must remain vigilant for "Red Flags" indicating potential fraud. Common indicators include, but are not limited to:
1.  **Document Anomalies:** Missing receipts, photocopied invoices where originals are expected, or alterations to dates/amounts.
2.  **Lifestyle Changes:** An employee living clearly beyond their means.
3.  **Control Overrides:** Management frequently bypassing established internal controls (e.g., skipping the 3-way match in procurement).
4.  **Vendor Irregularities:** Vendors with P.O. Box addresses only, or vendors with names similar to established companies.
5.  **Unusual Timing:** Transactions processed at odd hours, on holidays, or immediately prior to financial reporting deadlines.

#### 4.2 Reporting Mechanisms
Any individual who suspects fraud **must** report it immediately. Ignoring suspicion is a violation of the Code of Ethics.

**4.2.1 Reporting Channels**
1.  **Direct Reporting:** Report to the immediate supervisor.
2.  **Escalated Reporting:** If the supervisor is suspected, or if the reporter is uncomfortable, report directly to the **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** or the **Executive Director**.
3.  **Board Level Reporting:** If the Executive Director or a Board Member is suspected, the report must be made directly to the **Board Treasurer** or the **Chair of the Audit Committee**.
4.  **Anonymous Reporting:** Reports may be submitted via the Organization’s designated secure Whistleblower Hotline/Web Portal [Insert Link].

**4.2.2 Content of Report**
Reporters should provide as much factual detail as possible:
*   Description of the alleged misconduct.
*   Names of individuals involved.
*   Dates and locations (virtual or physical).
*   Evidence (emails, invoices, screenshots). *Note: Do not attempt to investigate or gather evidence if doing so alerts the suspect.*

#### 4.3 Triage and Preliminary Review
Upon receipt of a report, the Investigating Authority (CFO, Treasurer, or Audit Committee) must:
1.  **Acknowledge:** If the reporter is known, acknowledge receipt within **five (5) business days**.
2.  **Assess Conflict:** Ensure the Investigating Authority has no conflict of interest per **Bylaws Article IX**.
3.  **Initial Assessment:** Determine if the allegation, if true, constitutes fraud, waste, or abuse.
    *   *Frivolous:* If the report is clearly baseless, document the closure and notify the reporter (if known).
    *   *Credible:* If the report has merit, initiate a formal investigation.

#### 4.4 Investigation Protocol
**4.4.1 Preservation of Evidence (Legal Hold)**
Immediately upon launching an investigation:
1.  The **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** must be notified to secure relevant digital assets.
2.  Access logs, email archives, and cloud storage accounts of the suspect must be preserved.
3.  Physical access to sensitive areas (if applicable) may be suspended.

**4.4.2 Investigation Team**
The Investigating Authority will form a team which may include:
*   Internal Audit / Finance staff.
*   Legal Counsel (Internal or External).
*   IT Forensics experts.

**4.4.3 Interviews**
Interviews must be conducted confidentially. In a remote-first environment, interviews should be recorded (with consent where required by law) via secure video conferencing platforms.

#### 4.5 Mandatory Federal Reporting (2 CFR § 200.113)
If the investigation confirms credible evidence of a violation of Federal criminal law involving fraud, bribery, or gratuity violations potentially affecting a Federal award:
1.  The **Executive Director** (or Board Chair) must disclose the violation in writing to the Federal Awarding Agency or Pass-Through Entity.
2.  This disclosure must occur in a "timely manner" (typically within 2 weeks of confirmation).
3.  Failure to report is grounds for suspension and debarment.

#### 4.6 Resolution and Disciplinary Action
Based on the findings:
1.  **Disciplinary Action:** Violators are subject to discipline up to and including termination of employment and removal from the Board (per **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**).
2.  **Legal Action:** The Organization will refer criminal acts to appropriate law enforcement agencies (FBI, State Police) and may pursue civil litigation to recover lost assets.
3.  **Control Remediation:** The CFO must update the *Internal Controls Framework* to close the gap that allowed the fraud to occur.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Personnel** | Vigilantly monitor for red flags and report suspicions immediately via authorized channels. |
| **Managers** | Forward all reports of fraud to the CFO/Treasurer immediately without conducting a personal investigation. |
| **CFO** | Serve as the primary Investigating Authority for operational fraud; ensure evidence preservation. |
| **Treasurer / Audit Committee** | Serve as the Investigating Authority for fraud involving Senior Management or Directors; oversee external audit coordination. |
| **CISO** | Execute Legal Holds on digital data; provide forensic support during investigations. |
| **Board of Directors** | Review final investigation reports; authorize legal action; ensure compliance with Federal reporting requirements. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Zero Tolerance:** Study GRC Inc. maintains a zero-tolerance policy regarding fraud.

**Whistleblower Protection:** In accordance with the *Whistleblower Protection Policy* and **Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification)**, the Organization strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who reports a concern in good faith. Any employee found to have retaliated against a whistleblower will face immediate termination.

**False Claims Act:** Employees are reminded that the Federal False Claims Act allows private citizens to file suits on behalf of the government (qui tam) against those who have defrauded the government.
