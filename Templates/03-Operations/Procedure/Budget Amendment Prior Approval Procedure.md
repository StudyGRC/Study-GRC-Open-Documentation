### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory workflow for requesting, reviewing, and approving changes to the Organization’s approved budget. This procedure ensures compliance with **2 CFR 200.308 (Revision of budget and program plans)** regarding Federal awards and aligns with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** regarding the Board’s fiduciary oversight. Strict adherence to this procedure mitigates the risk of disallowed costs, audit findings, and unauthorized deviations from the Organization’s mission.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all financial resources managed by Study GRC Inc., with specific emphasis on:
*   **Federal Awards:** Grants and cooperative agreements subject to Uniform Guidance (2 CFR 200).
*   **Board-Designated Funds:** Unrestricted funds previously allocated by the Board.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to minor variances within the Executive Director’s discretionary spending limit as defined in the *Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix*, provided such variances do not trigger Federal prior approval requirements.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Budget Amendment** | A formal modification to the approved budget that increases the total budget, shifts funds between line items beyond allowable thresholds, or alters the scope of the project. |
| **Prior Approval** | Written permission from an authorized official (Internal or Federal) obtained *before* the financial obligation is incurred or the change is implemented. |
| **Key Personnel** | Individuals who contribute to the scientific or execution development of the project in a substantive, measurable way (e.g., Project Director, Principal Investigator) as defined in the grant award. |
| **Participant Support Costs** | Direct costs for items such as stipends or subsistence allowances, travel allowances, and registration fees paid to or on behalf of participants or trainees (but not employees) in connection with conferences or training projects. |
| **AOR** | Authorized Organization Representative. The individual (typically the Executive Director) authorized to sign and submit official requests to Federal agencies. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Identification of Need
The Program Manager or Department Head must identify the need for a budget amendment. A request is required if:
1.  **Internal Threshold:** The proposed change exceeds the Executive Director’s authority limit (e.g., >$5,000 transfer between major categories) per the *Delegation of Authority Matrix*.
2.  **Federal Trigger:** The change involves any criteria listed in Section 4.2 below.

#### 4.2 Determination of Federal Prior Approval Requirements (2 CFR 200.308)
The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) must review the request against 2 CFR 200.308. **Federal Agency Prior Approval** is mandatory for:
1.  **Change in Scope:** Any change in the scope or the objective of the project or program (even if there is no associated budget change).
2.  **Key Personnel:** Change in a key person specified in the application or the Federal award.
3.  **Leadership Absence:** The disengagement from the project for more than three months, or a 25% reduction in time devoted to the project, by the approved Project Director or Principal Investigator.
4.  **Participant Support Costs:** The transfer of funds budgeted for participant support costs to other categories of expense.
5.  **Subawarding:** The subawarding, transferring, or contracting out of any work under a Federal award (unless described in the application and funded in the approved Federal award).
6.  **Cost Sharing:** Changes in the amount of approved cost-sharing or matching provided.
7.  **Capital Expenditures:** General purpose equipment purchases not previously approved.

#### 4.3 Internal Review and Approval Workflow
Before any request is sent to a Federal Agency, it must clear internal governance:

1.  **Submission:** The Requestor submits a *Budget Amendment Request Form* to the CFO, detailing the justification, the "From" and "To" budget lines, and impact on program deliverables.
2.  **Financial Analysis:** The CFO verifies fund availability and allowability.
3.  **Executive Approval:**
    *   If the amendment is **Operational** (no Federal trigger, within ED limit): The Executive Director approves.
    *   If the amendment is **Strategic** (Federal trigger OR exceeds ED limit): The Executive Director reviews and escalates to the Board of Directors (or Finance Committee).
4.  **Board Action:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII**, the Board (or Finance Committee) must approve the amendment via electronic vote or meeting resolution.

#### 4.4 Federal Submission and Negotiation
If Federal Prior Approval is required (per Section 4.2):
1.  **Preparation:** The CFO prepares the formal request, including the revised budget narrative and justification.
2.  **Submission:** The AOR (Executive Director) submits the request via the appropriate portal (e.g., Grants.gov, ERA Commons, GrantSolutions).
3.  **Status Tracking:** The CFO tracks the request. **No funds may be obligated against the new category until the Agency Grants Officer issues a formal modification.**

#### 4.5 Execution and Documentation
Upon receipt of approval (Internal or Federal):
1.  **System Update:** The CFO updates the accounting system (General Ledger) to reflect the new budget allocations.
2.  **Notification:** The CFO notifies the Program Manager that spending may proceed.
3.  **Record Retention:** All correspondence, Board minutes, and Agency approval notices must be saved in the Grant Master File in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Program Manager** | Identifies the need for changes; ensures changes align with program goals; drafts initial justification. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Acts as the gatekeeper for compliance; determines if 2 CFR 200.308 applies; updates the General Ledger; maintains audit trails. |
| **Executive Director (AOR)** | Reviews strategic impact; holds signature authority for Federal submissions; approves operational amendments within DoA limits. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves significant budget deviations, changes in scope, or amendments exceeding the Executive Director's authority pursuant to Bylaws Article VIII. |
| **Finance Committee** | Reviews detailed amendment requests prior to full Board vote (if applicable). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to obtain prior approval before incurring costs may result in **Disallowed Costs**, requiring the Organization to repay funds to the Federal government from non-federal sources.

Violations of this procedure by staff may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Intentional circumvention of Board or Federal oversight will be treated as a violation of the *Board of Directors Code of Ethics and Conduct*.
