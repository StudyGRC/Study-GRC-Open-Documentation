### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized methodology for the initiation, documentation, implementation, verification, and closure of Corrective Action Plans (CAPs) within Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures that identified non-conformances—whether arising from safety incidents, financial audits, security breaches, or regulatory inspections—are addressed systematically to prevent recurrence, thereby satisfying the organization's commitment to continuous improvement and compliance with 2 CFR 200.511 (Audit Findings) and OSHA standards.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all departments, employees, volunteers, and contractors of Study GRC Inc. It covers all corrective actions resulting from:
*   Internal or External Audits (Financial, IT, Operational).
*   Incident Investigations (Safety, Security, Data Privacy).
*   Regulatory Inspections (OSHA, IRS, Grantors).
*   Whistleblower Complaints (substantiated).
*   Management Reviews and Self-Assessments.

**Exclusions:** Minor operational adjustments that do not pose a compliance, safety, or financial risk and can be resolved immediately (e.g., fixing a typo on a website) do not require a formal CAP unless a systemic pattern is identified.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** | A documented plan outlining specific steps to rectify a non-conformance and prevent its recurrence. |
| **Non-Conformance** | Failure to fulfill a requirement (legal, regulatory, internal policy, or standard). |
| **Root Cause Analysis (RCA)** | A systematic process for identifying the fundamental reason for an event or non-conformance. |
| **CAP Owner** | The individual assigned responsibility for executing the corrective action. |
| **Verification** | Independent confirmation that the corrective action has been implemented and is effective. |
| **Closure** | The formal status applied to a CAP once verification is complete and the risk is mitigated. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Initiation of Corrective Action
4.1.1 A CAP must be initiated within **three (3) business days** of the validation of a non-conformance or the issuance of a final investigation/audit report.
4.1.2 The finding must be logged in the **Central Corrective Action Register (CCAR)** with a unique tracking ID (e.g., `CAP-2023-001`).
4.1.3 The Department Head responsible for the area where the finding occurred is designated as the **CAP Owner**.

#### 4.2 Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
4.2.1 Before determining the solution, the CAP Owner must perform a Root Cause Analysis to ensure the underlying issue is addressed, not just the symptom.
4.2.2 Acceptable RCA methods include:
*   **The 5 Whys:** Asking "why" at least five times to drill down to the cause.
*   **Fishbone (Ishikawa) Diagram:** Categorizing causes (People, Methods, Machines, Materials, Environment).
4.2.3 For high-severity findings (e.g., data breaches, significant financial variances, safety incidents requiring hospitalization), the RCA must be reviewed by the **Audit and Risk Committee** or the **CISO/Safety Officer** prior to plan development.

#### 4.3 Plan Development
4.3.1 The CAP Owner must draft a plan that includes:
*   **Immediate Correction:** Steps to contain the issue (e.g., revoking access, freezing a bank account).
*   **Long-term Corrective Action:** Steps to prevent recurrence (e.g., rewriting policy, installing new software, training staff).
*   **Deadlines:** Specific due dates for each step.
*   **Resources Required:** Budget, personnel, or tools needed.
4.3.2 All CAP actions must be **SMART** (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).

#### 4.4 Approval and Implementation
4.4.1 The proposed CAP must be approved by the Executive Director (or the Board of Directors for Executive-level findings).
4.4.2 Upon approval, the CAP Owner initiates the work.
4.4.3 The CAP Owner must update the CCAR status to "In Progress."
4.4.4 If a deadline cannot be met, an **Extension Request** must be submitted to the Approving Authority *before* the due date, accompanied by a justification and a risk assessment of the delay.

#### 4.5 Verification of Effectiveness
4.5.1 Once the CAP Owner deems the action complete, they must mark the item as "Pending Verification" in the CCAR and submit evidence (e.g., training logs, screenshots, updated policy documents).
4.5.2 An independent **Verifier** (Internal Auditor, Compliance Officer, or a Manager from a different department) must review the evidence.
4.5.3 Verification methods include:
*   **Document Review:** Checking logs, invoices, or policy updates.
*   **Observation:** Watching a process to ensure the new procedure is followed.
*   **Testing:** Attempting to bypass a control to ensure it holds.
4.5.4 If the action is deemed ineffective, the CAP is returned to "In Progress" status, and the CAP Owner must revise the plan (Return to Step 4.2).

#### 4.6 Closure
4.6.1 A CAP is considered "Closed" only when:
*   All action items are complete.
*   Verification has confirmed effectiveness.
*   No recurrence of the specific non-conformance has been observed during the verification period.
4.6.2 The Executive Director (or designee) formally signs off on the closure in the CCAR.

#### 4.7 Reporting
4.7.1 A **CAP Status Report** must be generated monthly and presented to the Operational Leadership team.
4.7.2 Per Bylaws Article VI (Committees), the **Audit and Risk Committee** must receive a quarterly report detailing all High-Risk CAPs, including any that are overdue by more than 30 days.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Final approving authority for CAPs; ensures resources are available for implementation; reports systemic issues to the Board. |
| **Department Heads (CAP Owners)** | Conduct RCA; develop SMART action plans; execute implementation; provide evidence of completion. |
| **Compliance/Safety Officer** | Maintains the Central Corrective Action Register (CCAR); acts as the primary Verifier; monitors deadlines. |
| **Audit and Risk Committee** | Reviews high-risk CAPs and ensures executive accountability for closure. |
| **Employees/Volunteers** | Report non-conformances; participate in RCA interviews; adhere to new processes resulting from CAPs. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to initiate, execute, or close a Corrective Action Plan within the agreed-upon timeframe constitutes a performance failure.
*   **Grant Compliance:** Failure to resolve audit findings (CAPs) related to federal funds may result in the loss of funding eligibility under 2 CFR 200.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees who falsify completion evidence or negligently ignore assigned CAPs are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination, in accordance with the *Progressive Discipline Policy*.
