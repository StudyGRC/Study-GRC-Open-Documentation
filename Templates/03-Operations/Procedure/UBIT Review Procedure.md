### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a systematic process for identifying, analyzing, and reporting Unrelated Business Income (UBI) in compliance with Internal Revenue Code (IRC) §511-514. As a tax-exempt 501(c)(3) organization, Study GRC Inc. must ensure that revenue-generating activities do not jeopardize its tax-exempt status and that any tax liability resulting from unrelated business activities is accurately calculated and paid. This procedure operationalizes the financial oversight mandates established in **Article VIII** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all departments, committees, and personnel involved in generating revenue for Study GRC Inc. It covers all income streams **excluding** standard charitable contributions, government grants, and membership dues (unless such dues provide significant commercial benefits).

**Specific Inclusions:**
*   Sale of merchandise or educational materials.
*   Advertising or corporate sponsorship arrangements.
*   Rental of real or personal property.
*   Service fees for consulting or training.
*   Debt-financed income.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **UBIT** | Unrelated Business Income Tax. The tax imposed on the unrelated business taxable income of a tax-exempt organization. |
| **Unrelated Business Income (UBI)** | Income from a trade or business, regularly carried on, that is not substantially related to the charitable, educational, or scientific purpose of the organization. |
| **Trade or Business** | Any activity which is carried on for the production of income from the sale of goods or the performance of services (IRC §513(c)). |
| **Regularly Carried On** | Business activities that show a frequency and continuity, and are pursued in a manner similar to comparable commercial activities of non-exempt organizations. |
| **Substantially Related** | The activity contributes importantly to the accomplishment of the exempt purpose (other than through the production of funds). |
| **Qualified Sponsorship Payment** | A payment where there is no arrangement or expectation that the payor will receive any substantial return benefit other than the use or acknowledgment of the name or logo (IRC §513(i)). |
| **Debt-Financed Property** | Any property held to produce income and for which there is an acquisition indebtedness at any time during the tax year (IRC §514). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 New Revenue Stream Screening (Pre-Launch)
Before launching any new revenue-generating activity, the initiating Department Head must submit a **Revenue Activity Proposal** to the CFO.

1.  **Submission:** The proposal must detail the nature of the activity, projected frequency, use of volunteer vs. paid labor, and marketing strategy.
2.  **The Three-Prong Test:** The CFO shall evaluate the activity against the three IRS criteria for UBI:
    *   **Prong 1:** Is it a trade or business? (i.e., selling goods/services with intent to profit).
    *   **Prong 2:** Is it regularly carried on? (e.g., a daily coffee shop vs. an annual gala).
    *   **Prong 3:** Is it *not* substantially related to the exempt purpose?
    *   *Decision:* If the answer to **all three** is "Yes," the income is potentially taxable UBI unless an exception applies.

#### 4.2 Analysis of Statutory Exceptions
If the activity meets the Three-Prong Test, the CFO must determine if a statutory exception applies under IRC §513.

1.  **Volunteer Labor Exception (IRC §513(a)(1)):** Determine if substantially all (generally interpreted as ≥85%) of the work in carrying on the trade or business is performed for the organization without compensation.
    *   *Note:* Reimbursement for expenses is not compensation.
2.  **Convenience Exception (IRC §513(a)(2)):** Determine if the activity is carried on primarily for the convenience of members, students, or employees (e.g., a cafeteria).
3.  **Selling Donated Merchandise (IRC §513(a)(3)):** Determine if the activity consists of selling merchandise, substantially all of which has been received by the organization as gifts or contributions (e.g., a thrift shop).
4.  **Qualified Sponsorships (IRC §513(i)):**
    *   Review sponsorship agreements to ensure they provide **acknowledgment** (neutral description, logo, location) rather than **advertising** (qualitative language, price information, endorsement).
    *   *Action:* If the agreement includes advertising, the portion of the payment exceeding fair market value of the benefit may be UBI.

#### 4.3 Debt-Financed Income Review (IRC §514)
Even if an activity is passive (e.g., rental income, investment income), it may be taxable if the underlying property is debt-financed.

1.  **Asset Review:** The CFO must review the Balance Sheet annually to identify any property (real estate or securities) acquired with debt (e.g., a mortgage or margin account).
2.  **Neighborhood Land Rule:** Verify if the "Neighborhood Land Rule" exception applies (property acquired for future exempt use within 10 years).
3.  **Calculation:** If no exception applies, a percentage of the income is taxable based on the "Average Acquisition Indebtedness" divided by the "Average Adjusted Basis."

#### 4.4 Expense Allocation and Netting
If an activity is determined to be taxable UBI, the CFO must calculate the *Net* UBI.

1.  **Direct Expenses:** Deduct expenses directly connected with the carrying on of the unrelated trade or business.
2.  **Dual-Use Allocation:** For assets or personnel used for both exempt and unrelated purposes (e.g., the Executive Director's salary or office rent), allocate expenses on a reasonable basis (e.g., time tracking, square footage).
    *   *Requirement:* The allocation method must be documented and consistent.

#### 4.5 Reporting and Payment
1.  **Threshold Monitoring:** The CFO shall maintain a **UBIT Activity Tracking Log** (FIN-REG-069).
2.  **Filing Requirement:** If gross UBI exceeds **$1,000** in a fiscal year, the Organization must file **IRS Form 990-T**.
3.  **Estimated Taxes:** If the expected tax liability is $500 or more, the CFO must arrange for quarterly estimated tax payments using **Form 990-W**.
4.  **Board Notification:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, the CFO must report any UBIT liability to the Treasurer and the Finance Committee prior to filing.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Program/Department Heads** | Identify potential revenue streams and submit proposals for screening prior to implementation. Ensure sponsorship agreements do not contain advertising language without CFO review. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Conduct the Three-Prong Test. Apply statutory exceptions. Calculate expense allocations. Prepare Form 990-T. Maintain the UBIT Activity Tracking Log. |
| **Treasurer** | Provide Board-level oversight of UBIT exposure. Review Form 990-T prior to filing. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Approve final revenue generation strategies based on CFO's risk assessment. |
| **External Tax Counsel** | Provide legal opinions on complex "substantially related" determinations or debt-financed property calculations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to properly identify and report UBI can result in substantial penalties, interest, and potential revocation of Study GRC Inc.'s 501(c)(3) status.
*   **Internal:** Personnel who authorize revenue activities without following this screening procedure may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **External:** The Organization is liable for all unpaid taxes and penalties assessed by the IRS due to non-compliance with this procedure.
