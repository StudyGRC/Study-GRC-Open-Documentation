### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish a rigorous, legally defensible process for verifying quorum during remote meetings of the Board of Directors and Voting Members of Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.002** regarding meetings held by remote communications technology and validates that all organizational acts, elections, and resolutions are legally binding.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **Annual and Special Meetings of the Membership.**
*   **Regular and Special Meetings of the Board of Directors.**
*   **Committee Meetings** where the Board has delegated specific authority requiring a formal vote.

This procedure is executed primarily by the **Secretary** (or their designee) and the **Presiding Officer** (typically the President).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Quorum** | The minimum number of voting members or directors who must be present at a meeting to validly transact business. |
| **Remote Communication Technology** | Electronic methods (e.g., Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet) that allow participants to hear and communicate with each other simultaneously. |
| **Voting Member** | A "Supporting Member" or "Honorary Member" in good standing, as defined in Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1. |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a member who has fulfilled all financial and conduct requirements and is eligible to vote. |
| **Roll Call** | The process of calling out names from a list to verify attendance and identity. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Pre-Meeting Preparation
To ensure an accurate count, the Secretary must prepare the official roster prior to the meeting start time.

1.  **Roster Generation:**
    *   **For Board Meetings:** The Secretary must generate a list of all currently serving Directors.
    *   **For Member Meetings:** In accordance with **Bylaws Section 2.6**, the Secretary must prepare an alphabetical list of Voting Members entitled to notice, generated as of the Record Date.
2.  **Calculation of Quorum Threshold:**
    *   **Board:** Calculate the majority (>50%) of Directors currently in office (e.g., if 5 Directors, Quorum is 3).
    *   **Members:** Calculate the majority (>50%) of the total Voting Members (e.g., if 100 Voting Members, Quorum is 51).
3.  **Technology Check:** The Presiding Officer must verify that the remote meeting platform settings allow for the identification of attendees (e.g., "Waiting Room" enabled, requiring display names).

#### 4.2 Identity Verification (TBOC § 22.002 Compliance)
Texas law requires the organization to verify that a person participating remotely is who they claim to be.

1.  **Admission Control:** Participants should be admitted from the "Waiting Room" individually or in small batches.
2.  **Visual/Audio Confirmation:**
    *   **Directors:** Must have video enabled or confirm identity via voice if video is technically impossible.
    *   **Members:** Must log in using the email address on file in the membership records.
3.  **Display Name Standardization:** The Secretary shall request that all voting participants rename their digital presence to their legal name (e.g., change "iPhone 13" to "Jane Doe").
4.  **Proxy Check:** The Secretary must confirm that no individual is attempting to vote by proxy. **Bylaws Section 2.3** strictly prohibits proxy voting; the member must be personally present via the electronic connection.

#### 4.3 Establishing Quorum
Quorum must be established *before* the meeting is called to order.

1.  **The Count:** The Secretary counts the verified attendees.
2.  **The Comparison:** The Secretary compares the attendee count against the Threshold calculated in Step 4.1.2.
3.  **The Declaration:**
    *   **If Quorum is Present:** The Secretary announces, "Mr./Madam President, a quorum is present." The Presiding Officer then calls the meeting to order.
    *   **If Quorum is Absent:** The Secretary announces, "A quorum is not present." The Presiding Officer may **not** call the meeting to order for the transaction of business. The meeting may only be adjourned or recessed until a quorum is obtained.

#### 4.4 Continuous Quorum Monitoring
Quorum is not a one-time check; it must be maintained throughout the meeting.

1.  **Drop-off Monitoring:** The Secretary must monitor the participant list for technical disconnects.
2.  **Loss of Quorum:**
    *   If the number of active participants drops below the required threshold, the Secretary must immediately interrupt proceedings to announce the loss of quorum.
    *   No further business or votes may be conducted until quorum is re-established.
3.  **Recusal Impact:** If a Director or Member recuses themselves due to a Conflict of Interest (**Bylaws Section 9.1**), they are still counted toward the quorum for the meeting, but not for the vote on that specific issue.

#### 4.5 Documentation
The verification of quorum must be formally recorded.

1.  **Minutes Entry:** The minutes must explicitly state:
    *   The total number of Directors/Members entitled to vote.
    *   The number of Directors/Members present.
    *   The explicit statement: "A quorum was established."
    *   The names of Directors present (for Board meetings).
2.  **Electronic Logs:** The Secretary shall retain the platform-generated attendance report (e.g., Zoom Usage Report) as a supporting record in the organization's archives.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Responsible for maintaining the roster, calculating the threshold, verifying identity, monitoring attendance, and recording quorum in the minutes. |
| **Presiding Officer** | Responsible for ensuring the meeting technology allows for participation and for ruling the meeting "in order" only after quorum is confirmed. |
| **Voting Member / Director** | Responsible for ensuring their technology functions, identifying themselves clearly, and maintaining connectivity. |
| **Governance Committee** | Responsible for auditing this procedure annually to ensure alignment with state law. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly adhere to this procedure renders any business transacted, resolutions passed, or elections held **voidable or void** under Texas law. Conducting business without a quorum is a breach of fiduciary duty.

*   **Invalidation:** Votes taken without a verified quorum will be nullified.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Officers knowingly proceeding without a quorum may face removal from office under **Bylaws Section 4.3**.
