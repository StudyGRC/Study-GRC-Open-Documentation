### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the specific steps required to prepare, review, and submit the Federal Financial Report (Standard Form 425 or "SF-425") in compliance with 2 CFR § 200.327. Accurate financial reporting is essential to maintain public trust, ensure the transparency mandated by **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, and prevent the suspension or termination of federal funding.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all federal awards received by Study GRC Inc., whether received directly from a federal awarding agency or as a subrecipient through a pass-through entity. It applies to the Chief Financial Officer (CFO), the Executive Director, and any staff involved in grant accounting.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not cover performance/programmatic reporting (see *OPS-PRC-030 Grant Performance Reporting*), though financial data must align with programmatic progress.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **SF-425** | The Federal Financial Report; a standard form used by recipients to report financial status of grant funds. |
| **Unliquidated Obligations** | Obligations incurred by the Organization that have not been paid (e.g., accounts payable, accrued salaries, signed contracts for services not yet fully invoiced). |
| **Program Income** | Gross income earned by the Organization that is directly generated by a supported activity or earned as a result of the Federal award. |
| **Drawdown** | The process of requesting and receiving payment from the Federal government via systems like PMS (Payment Management System) or ASAP. |
| **Period of Performance** | The time during which the Organization may incur new obligations to carry out the work authorized under the Federal award. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Reporting Schedule and Deadlines
The CFO must maintain a **Compliance Calendar** (see *LEG-TOL-005*) tracking the specific reporting frequency for each award.
1.  **Quarterly Reports:** Due 30 days after the end of each reporting quarter (Jan 30, Apr 30, Jul 30, Oct 30).
2.  **Semi-Annual/Annual Reports:** Due 90 days after the end of the reporting period.
3.  **Final Reports:** Due no later than 120 days after the project end date.

#### 4.2 Preparation and Reconciliation
**Five (5) business days prior to the deadline**, the CFO must initiate the following reconciliation process:

1.  **General Ledger (GL) Review:** Run a "Statement of Financial Position" and "Statement of Activity" (P&L) filtered by the specific Grant Class/Customer code in the accounting system.
2.  **Drawdown Reconciliation:** Compare total expenses recorded in the GL against total cash drawn down from the Federal Payment Management System.
    *   *Note:* Cash Receipts (Line 10a on SF-425) must match the agency's records of disbursement.
3.  **Accrual Calculation (Unliquidated Obligations):**
    *   Identify all valid obligations incurred but not paid as of the period end date.
    *   Include: Unpaid invoices entered in Accounts Payable, accrued staff time/benefits, and executed subaward obligations.
    *   *Exclusion:* Do not include planned future expenses or budget allocations that are not legally binding obligations.

#### 4.3 Drafting the SF-425
The CFO shall populate the SF-425 (or the online equivalent in systems like eRA Commons or GrantSolutions) using the following logic:

*   **Line 10a (Cash Receipts):** Enter total federal cash received.
*   **Line 10b (Cash Disbursements):** Enter total federal cash spent.
*   **Line 10c (Cash on Hand):** Calculate (10a minus 10b).
    *   *Requirement:* If Cash on Hand is positive, provide an explanation in Box 12 (Remarks). Excess cash must be minimized per *FIN-POL-008 Cash Management*.
*   **Line 10d (Total Federal Funds Authorized):** Enter the total award amount.
*   **Line 10e (Federal Share of Expenditures):** Enter total cumulative expenses charged to the grant (Cash Disbursements + Unliquidated Obligations).
*   **Line 10f (Federal Share of Unliquidated Obligations):** Enter the total calculated in Step 4.2.3.
*   **Line 10i (Total Recipient Share Required):** Enter the mandatory cost-sharing/matching amount (if applicable).
*   **Line 10j (Recipient Share of Expenditures):** Enter the actual match provided (must be supported by *FIN-POL-050 Cost Sharing Policy*).

#### 4.4 Program Income Reporting
If the award generates Program Income (e.g., conference fees, sale of educational materials):
1.  Verify the income type (Additive, Deductive, or Matching) per the Notice of Award.
2.  Enter the cumulative amount in **Line 10l**.
3.  Ensure the use of income aligns with *FIN-POL-049 Program Income Policy*.

#### 4.5 Review and Approval
Prior to submission, a "Four-Eyes" review is mandatory to ensure data integrity and segregation of duties.

1.  **Draft Submission:** The CFO generates a draft PDF of the report.
2.  **Executive Review:** The Executive Director (or Treasurer, if the ED is unavailable) reviews the draft against the GL summary.
3.  **Certification:** The reviewer confirms that:
    *   Expenses are allowable under 2 CFR 200 Subpart E.
    *   Matching funds are verifiable.
    *   The report is mathematically accurate.
4.  **Approval:** The Executive Director provides written or digital approval (email or ticketing system) to proceed with submission.

#### 4.6 Submission and Archiving
1.  **Submission:** The CFO submits the report via the designated Federal portal (e.g., PMS, ASAP, Grants.gov).
2.  **Confirmation:** Save the "Submission Confirmation" or "Agency Receipt" notification.
3.  **Archiving:** In accordance with *GOV-POL-012 Document Retention*, save the following packet to the secure Grant Management Drive:
    *   Final SF-425 (PDF).
    *   Submission Confirmation.
    *   Supporting GL Detail Report.
    *   Reconciliation Worksheet (showing Unliquidated Obligations).
    *   Approval evidence from the Executive Director.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Primary responsibility for GL reconciliation, accrual calculation, drafting the SF-425, and technical submission. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Reviews SF-425 for alignment with program progress; provides final approval for submission. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides Board-level oversight; reviews annual financial reports for consistency with audit figures per Bylaws Article IV. |
| **Program Managers** | Provide data regarding subrecipient obligations and verify program income generation sources. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to submit timely and accurate FFRs is a violation of the grant terms and conditions.
*   **Late Submission:** May result in the Federal agency placing a temporary "stop payment" on the Organization's drawdowns.
*   **Inaccurate Reporting:** May result in the Organization being designated "High Risk," requiring detailed reimbursement requests for every expense.
*   **False Claims:** Intentional misrepresentation on an SF-425 constitutes a violation of the False Claims Act and may result in criminal prosecution, fines, and debarment.
