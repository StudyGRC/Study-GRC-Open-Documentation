### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the guidelines and steps for making **Micro-Purchases** at Study GRC Inc. This procedure is designed to expedite the acquisition of essential supplies and services while ensuring compliance with **2 CFR § 200.320(a)** (Uniform Guidance). It ensures that costs are reasonable, purchases are distributed equitably among qualified suppliers, and procurement actions are free from conflicts of interest.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees, officers, and volunteers of Study GRC Inc. initiating purchases of goods or services where the aggregate dollar amount does not exceed the Organization's self-certified Micro-Purchase Threshold (MPT).

*   **In Scope:** Purchases of supplies, materials, software licenses, and minor services under the MPT.
*   **Out of Scope:**
    *   Purchases exceeding the MPT (Refer to *FIN-PRC-025 Simplified Acquisition Procedure*).
    *   Employment contracts or payroll.
    *   Capital expenditures (Refer to *FIN-POL-013 Fixed Asset Capitalization Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Micro-Purchase** | A purchase of supplies or services where the aggregate dollar amount does not exceed the Micro-Purchase Threshold. Soliciting competitive price quotations is not required if the price is considered reasonable. |
| **Micro-Purchase Threshold (MPT)** | The maximum dollar amount for a micro-purchase. Study GRC Inc. sets this limit annually based on 2 CFR 200.320(a)(1). Currently set at **$50,000** (contingent on annual self-certification). |
| **Aggregate Dollar Amount** | The total cost of a single transaction, including taxes, shipping, and handling. |
| **Equitable Distribution** | The requirement to spread purchases among qualified suppliers to the extent practicable, rather than relying on a single vendor. |
| **Splitting** | The prohibited practice of breaking down a larger purchase into smaller components to avoid procurement thresholds (e.g., buying 10 laptops in 10 separate transactions to avoid getting quotes). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Threshold Verification and Self-Certification
Before utilizing the $50,000 threshold, the Finance Department must verify the Organization's status.
1.  **Standard Threshold:** By default, the federal MPT is **$10,000**.
2.  **Higher Threshold ($50,000):** Study GRC Inc. may utilize a threshold up to $50,000 **only if** the Organization is a "low-risk auditee" (as defined in 2 CFR 200.520) and conducts an annual self-certification.
3.  **Verification Step:** The Requester must verify with the CFO that the current fiscal year's self-certification is active before processing a micro-purchase between $10,001 and $50,000.

#### 4.2 Prohibition on Transaction Splitting
**Strict Prohibition:** It is a violation of federal law and Organization policy to split a larger purchase into smaller amounts to avoid the requirements of the Simplified Acquisition Threshold (purchases over $50k).
*   *Example:* If a project requires $60,000 worth of servers, the Requester **must not** execute two separate $30,000 purchases. This requires a competitive bid process.

#### 4.3 Vendor Selection and Equitable Distribution
1.  **No Quotes Required:** For micro-purchases, the Requester is **not** required to solicit competitive quotations (bids) if the price is considered reasonable.
2.  **Rotation of Vendors:** To the extent practicable, the Requester must rotate vendors.
    *   *Action:* If Study GRC Inc. frequently buys office supplies, the Requester should alternate between Vendor A, Vendor B, and Vendor C, provided their pricing remains reasonable.

#### 4.4 Determination of Price Reasonableness
Although quotes are not required, the Requester must determine that the price is fair and reasonable. This can be based on:
1.  **Market Research:** Comparison to current commercial catalog prices or online retailers.
2.  **Historical Pricing:** Comparison to recent purchases of the same item.
3.  **Personal Knowledge:** The Requester's professional knowledge of the item's value.
*   *Documentation:* If the purchase exceeds $3,000, a brief note regarding price reasonableness must be included in the purchase request (e.g., "Price checked against Amazon and Dell.com; this vendor is lowest").

#### 4.5 Conflict of Interest Check
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX (Section 9.1)** and **2 CFR 200.318(c)(1)**:
1.  No employee, officer, or agent may participate in the selection, award, or administration of a contract if they have a real or apparent conflict of interest.
2.  If the Requester has a financial interest in the selected vendor, they must recuse themselves and escalate the purchase to the CFO or Executive Director.

#### 4.6 Purchase Execution Workflow

**Step 1: Requisition**
*   The Requester identifies the need and confirms funds are available in the grant/department budget.
*   The Requester ensures the total aggregate amount is under the MPT.

**Step 2: Approval**
*   **$0 - $3,000:** May be approved by the Department Head or purchased via Corporate Credit Card (if authorized).
*   **$3,001 - $10,000:** Requires approval from the CFO.
*   **$10,001 - $50,000:** Requires approval from the Executive Director and verification of "Low-Risk Auditee" status.

**Step 3: Purchase**
*   The purchase is made via Corporate Credit Card, Purchase Order, or Invoice Payment Request.

**Step 4: Documentation & Reconciliation**
*   The Requester must upload the following to the financial system (e.g., QuickBooks/Expensify) within 3 business days:
    1.  **Itemized Receipt/Invoice.**
    2.  **Business Purpose Description.**
    3.  **Grant/Project Code.**
    4.  **Price Reasonableness Note** (for items >$3,000).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requester** | Identifies need, ensures price reasonableness, verifies budget availability, and avoids transaction splitting. |
| **Department Head** | Approves purchases up to $3,000; ensures equitable distribution of vendors within their department. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Approves purchases >$3,000; monitors for transaction splitting; maintains annual MPT self-certification records. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves purchases >$10,000; authorizes the annual self-certification of the higher Micro-Purchase Threshold. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure—specifically the splitting of transactions to avoid competitive bidding or failure to disclose conflicts of interest—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

Furthermore, non-compliance may result in **disallowed costs** by the federal grantor, requiring Study GRC Inc. to repay funds from non-federal sources.
