### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a mandatory, time-sensitive protocol for reporting lost, stolen, or compromised corporate credit cards. This procedure is designed to minimize financial liability, prevent fraudulent transactions, and ensure compliance with **2 CFR § 200.303 (Internal Controls)** and the Organization’s financial integrity standards. Rapid reporting is essential to protect Study GRC Inc.'s assets and maintain donor trust.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Authorized Cardholders**, including employees, Board Directors, Operational Officers, and volunteers who have been issued a corporate credit card or purchasing card (P-Card) in the name of Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to personal credit cards used for reimbursable expenses, although the compromise of personal data on Organization devices should be reported to the CISO.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authorized Cardholder** | An individual who has signed the *Corporate Credit Card User Agreement* and has been issued a card. |
| **Issuer** | The financial institution or bank that issued the credit card (e.g., Chase, Amex, Brex). |
| **Compromise** | The known or suspected exposure of credit card details (number, expiration, CVV) to unauthorized parties, including physical theft, loss, or digital skimming. |
| **Unauthorized Transaction** | A charge applied to the corporate card that was not made or authorized by the Cardholder. |
| **Zero Liability** | Protection provided by card issuers where the organization is not held responsible for unauthorized charges if reported promptly. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Immediate Action (The "Stop the Bleeding" Phase)
Upon discovering that a corporate credit card is lost, stolen, or that unauthorized transactions have occurred, the Cardholder **must** take the following actions immediately (within 1 hour of discovery).

1.  **Contact the Issuer:**
    *   Call the customer service number located on the back of the card (if available) or access the Issuer’s mobile app/website immediately.
    *   Request that the card be **permanently blocked/cancelled** immediately.
    *   If unauthorized charges are visible, formally initiate a **Fraud Dispute** with the Issuer during this call.
2.  **Record the Reference Number:**
    *   Note the time of the call, the name of the bank representative, and the Case/Ticket Reference Number provided by the Issuer.

#### 4.2 Internal Notification
After securing the account with the Issuer, the Cardholder must notify internal leadership.

1.  **Notify the CFO:**
    *   Send an email to the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) with the subject line: **URGENT: Corporate Card Compromise - [Last Name]**.
    *   Include the Case Reference Number from the bank.
2.  **Notify the CISO (If Applicable):**
    *   If the card was lost due to the theft of a physical device (laptop/phone) or a suspected digital breach (phishing), the Cardholder must also notify the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) immediately per the *Incident Response Plan*.

#### 4.3 Documentation and Investigation
Within 24 hours of the initial report, the Cardholder must submit a formal written account.

1.  **Submit Incident Report:**
    *   Complete the *Lost/Stolen Asset Report Form*.
    *   Detail the circumstances of the loss (e.g., "Wallet stolen on Metro," "Noticed suspicious charge from Vendor X").
    *   List the last **legitimate** transaction made by the Cardholder to assist finance in reconciliation.
2.  **Review Pending Charges:**
    *   The CFO or Finance Department will review the card ledger. The Cardholder must assist in identifying any transactions that posted between the time of loss and the time of cancellation.

#### 4.4 Replacement and Continuity
Once the immediate threat is neutralized, the Organization will move to restore operational capacity.

1.  **Card Re-Issuance:**
    *   The CFO will authorize the Issuer to send a replacement card.
    *   For remote staff, the card will be shipped directly to the Cardholder’s home address on file.
2.  **Updating Recurring Expenses:**
    *   If the compromised card was used for SaaS subscriptions or recurring vendor payments (e.g., AWS, Zoom, GitHub), the Cardholder is responsible for logging into those portals and updating the payment method immediately upon receipt of the new card to prevent service interruption.

#### 4.5 Security Post-Mortem
1.  **Root Cause Analysis:**
    *   If the loss was due to negligence (e.g., leaving a card visible in a public place) or a violation of the *Acceptable Use Policy*, the Cardholder may be required to undergo remedial security training.
    *   Repeated loss of cards may result in the permanent revocation of spending privileges.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authorized Cardholder** | Primary responsibility for physical and digital security of the card. Must report loss to the Issuer and CFO immediately. Responsible for updating recurring payment vendors. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Oversees the relationship with the Issuer. Reviews the ledger for fraudulent charges. Authorizes replacement cards. Reports material losses to the Board Treasurer. |
| **Chief Info. Security Officer (CISO)** | Investigates if the card loss is part of a larger data breach or identity theft incident. |
| **Finance Department** | Reconciles the final statement of the compromised card and ensures credits for disputed charges are applied correctly. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to report a lost or stolen card immediately may result in the Organization being held liable for fraudulent charges.

**Disciplinary Action:**
*   **Negligence:** Failure to comply with this procedure, resulting in financial loss to Study GRC Inc., may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Personal Liability:** In accordance with the *Corporate Credit Card User Agreement*, Cardholders may be held personally liable for charges incurred due to gross negligence or failure to report a loss within a reasonable timeframe.
