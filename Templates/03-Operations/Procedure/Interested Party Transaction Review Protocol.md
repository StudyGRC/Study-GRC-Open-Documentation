### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish a rigorous, transparent, and legally compliant process for reviewing, approving, or rejecting transactions between Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") and any Interested Party. This procedure operationalizes **Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)** of the Bylaws and ensures compliance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.230**, protecting the Organization’s tax-exempt status by preventing private inurement and ensuring all transactions occur at Fair Market Value (FMV).

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all "Interested Party Transactions" proposed or entered into by the Organization.

*   **Applies to:** Directors, Board Officers, Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director, CISO), Key Employees, and their Family Members or Affiliated Entities.
*   **Exclusions:** Reimbursement of reasonable business expenses incurred in the ordinary course of duties, processed under the *Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy (FIN-POL-040)*, provided no conflict exists regarding the vendor selected.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Interested Party** | Any Director, Officer, or member of a committee with Board-delegated powers, who has a direct or indirect Financial Interest in a proposed transaction. |
| **Financial Interest** | A person has a Financial Interest if they have, directly or indirectly: (a) An ownership or investment interest in any entity with which the Organization has a transaction or arrangement; (b) A compensation arrangement with the Organization or with any entity or individual with which the Organization has a transaction or arrangement; or (c) A potential ownership or investment interest in, or compensation arrangement with, any entity or individual with which the Organization is negotiating a transaction or arrangement. |
| **Interested Party Transaction** | Any contract, transaction, or arrangement (including grants, leases, and service agreements) in which an Interested Party has a Financial Interest. |
| **Disinterested Director** | A Director who has no Financial Interest in the transaction under review and no relationship that would interfere with the exercise of independent judgment. |
| **Comparability Data** | Data used to determine Fair Market Value, such as independent appraisals, written offers from other vendors, or salary surveys for comparable positions. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Duty to Disclose
In accordance with **Bylaws Section 9.1**, any individual who believes they may have a Financial Interest in a proposed transaction must disclose the existence of the interest and be given the opportunity to disclose all material facts to the Board or the delegated Committee.

1.  **Notification:** The Interested Party must submit a written disclosure (email is acceptable) to the Board President and Secretary prior to the discussion or execution of the transaction.
2.  **Material Facts:** The disclosure must include the nature of the interest, the financial value (if known), and any other relevant details required for the Board to make an informed decision.

#### 4.2 Preliminary Review and Comparability Data
Before the Board deliberates, the Organization must gather data to ensure the transaction is fair and reasonable.

1.  **Data Collection:** The Treasurer or Executive Director (if not the Interested Party) must obtain **Comparability Data**. This may include:
    *   At least two (2) competitive bids or quotes from unrelated vendors for similar goods/services.
    *   Independent compensation surveys (for employment contracts).
    *   Independent appraisals (for real property or intellectual property).
2.  **Fairness Analysis:** The collected data must demonstrate that the cost to the Organization is not greater than Fair Market Value and that the transaction is in the best interest of the Organization.

#### 4.3 Board Deliberation (Recusal)
The review of the transaction must occur during a formal meeting of the Board or authorized Committee.

1.  **Presentation:** The Interested Party may make a presentation at the Board or Committee meeting to explain the transaction and answer questions.
2.  **Recusal:** After the presentation and Q&A, the Interested Party **must leave the meeting** (physically leave the room or disconnect from the virtual conference) during the discussion of, and the vote on, the transaction. They may not be present for the deliberation.
3.  **Quorum Adjustment:** Per **TBOC § 22.230(d)**, the Interested Party may be counted in determining the presence of a quorum at the meeting, but they cannot vote.

#### 4.4 Voting and Approval Standards
The Board or Committee shall decide whether to approve the transaction.

1.  **Standard of Review:** The Disinterested Directors must determine:
    *   Whether the transaction is fair to the Organization.
    *   Whether the Organization could obtain a more advantageous arrangement with reasonable effort from a person or entity that would not give rise to a conflict of interest.
2.  **Voting:** Approval requires the affirmative vote of a majority of the **Disinterested Directors** present.
    *   *Note:* If the number of Disinterested Directors is less than a standard quorum, the vote of the majority of Disinterested Directors is sufficient to constitute an act of the Board regarding this specific transaction, provided the transaction is fair to the Organization.

#### 4.5 Documentation (Minutes)
The Secretary must document the proceedings in the meeting minutes with high specificity. The minutes must contain:

1.  **Names:** The names of the persons who disclosed or otherwise were found to have a Financial Interest.
2.  **Nature of Interest:** A description of the Financial Interest and the transaction.
3.  **Recusal Confirmation:** A statement confirming the Interested Party left the meeting during the discussion and vote.
4.  **Comparability Data:** A summary of the Comparability Data relied upon (e.g., "The Board reviewed three quotes from Vendors A, B, and C...").
5.  **Decision:** The names of the persons who were present for discussions and votes, the content of the discussion (including any alternatives considered), and a record of any votes taken.

#### 4.6 Violations of the Protocol
If the Board or Committee has reasonable cause to believe a member has failed to disclose an actual or possible conflict of interest:

1.  **Notification:** The Board shall inform the member of the basis for such belief and afford the member an opportunity to explain the alleged failure to disclose.
2.  **Corrective Action:** If, after hearing the member's response and making further investigation as warranted by the circumstances, the Board determines the member has failed to disclose an actual or possible conflict of interest, it shall take appropriate disciplinary and corrective action.
    *   **Voidability:** Per **TBOC § 22.230**, a transaction may be void or voidable if this protocol is not followed and the transaction is found to be unfair to the Organization.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Interested Party** | Proactively disclose Financial Interests; recuse self from deliberations and voting; provide necessary information to the Board. |
| **Board President** | Ensure the Interested Party leaves the meeting during deliberation; facilitate the discussion among Disinterested Directors. |
| **Secretary** | Record detailed minutes including the disclosure, recusal, data relied upon, and the final vote count. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Gather and present Comparability Data (bids, surveys, appraisals) to ensure the transaction is at Fair Market Value. |
| **Disinterested Directors** | Review the transaction objectively; vote based solely on the best interests of the Organization. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure may result in the transaction being voided by the Organization or challenged by the IRS as an "Excess Benefit Transaction," potentially subjecting the Interested Party and Board members to personal excise taxes (intermediate sanctions).

Internal disciplinary action for failure to disclose may include removal from the Board or termination of employment, in accordance with **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6** (Removal of Directors) or relevant employment policies.
