### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized methodology for collecting, maintaining, and retaining records related to the recruitment and hiring process at Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with the recordkeeping requirements of the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC), the Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs (OFCCP), and the Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures (UGESP). Furthermore, it mitigates legal risks associated with discrimination claims and ensures audit readiness for federal grant compliance under 2 CFR 200.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all recruitment activities conducted by Study GRC Inc., including:
*   **Positions:** Full-time employees, part-time employees, temporary staff, and long-term independent contractors.
*   **Personnel:** Hiring Managers, Interview Panels, HR/Operations staff, and the Board of Directors (when acting as a hiring body for Officers per Bylaws Article IV).
*   **Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to unsolicited resumes submitted when no specific job opening exists, provided those resumes are not entered into the Applicant Tracking System (ATS) or considered for future openings.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Applicant** | An individual who has expressed interest in a specific position, meets the basic qualifications, is considered by the Organization, and does not withdraw prior to a job offer. |
| **Internet Applicant** | As defined by OFCCP: An individual who submits an expression of interest electronically, is considered for a particular position, possesses the basic qualifications, and does not withdraw from the process. |
| **Disposition Code** | A standardized reason assigned to a candidate explaining why they were not selected for a position or did not advance to the next stage. |
| **Personnel Record** | Any record made or kept that is used to determine an employee's qualifications for promotion, compensation, termination, or disciplinary action. |
| **UGESP** | Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures; federal standards that provide a framework for determining the proper use of tests and other selection procedures. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Applicant Tracking System (ATS) Usage
To ensure data integrity and remote-first accessibility, Study GRC Inc. mandates the use of a centralized electronic Applicant Tracking System.

1.  **Mandatory Entry:** All applications must be processed through the approved ATS. Email submissions or physical resumes received by Hiring Managers must be redirected to the ATS or manually uploaded by HR staff within 24 hours of receipt.
2.  **Job Requisition:** A job requisition must be created in the ATS for every open position prior to posting. This requisition must include the approved Job Description and "Basic Qualifications" (objective, non-comparative criteria).
3.  **Demographic Data:** The ATS must be configured to solicit voluntary self-identification data (race, gender, veteran status, disability) from applicants. This data must be stored separately from the application and remain inaccessible to Hiring Managers and Interview Panels to prevent bias.

#### 4.2 Applicant Definition and Logging
To satisfy EEOC requirements, the Organization must distinguish between "Job Seekers" and "Applicants."

1.  **Expression of Interest:** An individual is logged as an Applicant only if they submit a resume or application for a specific, open requisition.
2.  **Basic Qualifications Filter:** The ATS or HR Lead shall conduct an initial screen. Individuals who do not meet the *Basic Qualifications* (e.g., "Must possess a CPA license") are coded immediately as "Does Not Meet Basic Qualifications" and are not subject to further retention requirements beyond the initial log.
3.  **Withdrawal:** If an applicant explicitly indicates they are no longer interested (e.g., "I accepted another offer"), they must be coded as "Voluntary Withdrawal" immediately.

#### 4.3 Disposition Coding
Every applicant who is not hired must be assigned a specific, factual Disposition Code in the ATS at the time they are removed from consideration. Generic codes such as "Not a fit" are prohibited.

**Approved Disposition Codes:**
*   **DNBQ:** Does Not Meet Basic Qualifications (Must specify which qualification).
*   **DNMQ:** Meets Basic, but Does Not Meet Preferred Qualifications.
*   **LME:** Less Qualified than Selected Candidate (Experience).
*   **LME-S:** Less Qualified than Selected Candidate (Skills/Technical).
*   **INT-P:** Poor Interview Performance (Must have interview notes attached).
*   **DNR:** Did Not Respond to inquiries/scheduling attempts (after 3 attempts).
*   **COI:** Conflict of Interest (per Bylaws Article IX or Policy GOV-POL-005).
*   **BGC:** Failed Background Check (per Policy HR-POL-024).
*   **WDR:** Voluntary Withdrawal.

#### 4.4 Interview Documentation
1.  **Note-Taking:** Interviewers must take notes focusing strictly on the candidate's ability to perform the job duties.
2.  **Prohibited Content:** Notes must not reference protected characteristics (age, race, religion, family status, etc.) or subjective personality assessments ("weird vibe").
3.  **Upload Requirement:** All interview notes, scoring matrices, and coding test results must be digitized and uploaded to the candidate's profile in the ATS within 48 hours of the interview.
4.  **Destruction of Physical Copies:** Once uploaded, any physical paper notes must be shredded.

#### 4.5 Record Retention Schedule
In compliance with 29 CFR § 1602.14 and to support federal grant audits (2 CFR 200.334), recruitment records must be retained as follows:

1.  **Retention Period:** All records related to the hiring process (job postings, applications, resumes, interview notes, tests, and disposition codes) must be retained for a minimum of **three (3) years** from the date of the personnel action (hiring or rejection).
2.  **Scope of Retention:**
    *   Job descriptions and postings.
    *   All applications and resumes received.
    *   Screening questions and answers.
    *   Interview notes and evaluation forms.
    *   Reference checks and background check results (stored securely/separately).
    *   Offer letters and employment contracts.
3.  **Litigation Hold:** If a charge of discrimination is filed or a lawsuit is initiated, all records relevant to the charge must be retained until the final disposition of the charge or lawsuit, regardless of the 3-year expiration.

#### 4.6 Adverse Impact Analysis
1.  **Annual Review:** The HR Lead or designated Officer shall conduct an annual review of hiring data to calculate the selection rate for each demographic group.
2.  **4/5ths Rule:** If the selection rate for any protected group is less than 80% (4/5ths) of the rate for the group with the highest rate, the Organization will investigate the selection procedures for potential bias and take corrective action.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Ensures all candidates are directed to the ATS; conducts interviews using objective criteria; uploads all interview notes; selects accurate Disposition Codes. |
| **HR Lead / Ops Officer** | Configures the ATS; audits Disposition Codes for accuracy; manages the voluntary self-identification data; conducts Adverse Impact Analysis. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves final hiring decisions; authorizes any exceptions to the standard process; ensures resources are available for ATS maintenance. |
| **All Employees** | Adheres to the *Conflict of Interest Policy* (Bylaws Art. IX) during the hiring process; maintains confidentiality of applicant data. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure—specifically the failure to document disposition reasons or the destruction of records prior to the retention period expiration—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

Intentional falsification of applicant tracking data or destruction of evidence during a Litigation Hold will result in immediate termination and potential legal action.
