### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, rigorous process for the recruitment, assessment, and clearance of instructors for Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures that all educational personnel possess the necessary subject matter expertise, pedagogical skills, and ethical standing to serve the Organization’s community. Furthermore, this procedure mandates strict background screening to mitigate safety risks, specifically regarding interactions with youth participants (K-12), in compliance with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all individuals seeking to serve as an **Instructor**, **Facilitator**, **Mentor**, or **Curriculum Developer**, regardless of whether the role is:
*   **Volunteer:** Unpaid service.
*   **Contractor:** Paid via 1099 Independent Contractor agreement.
*   **Employee:** W-2 status (if applicable).

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to one-time guest speakers who will be supervised at all times by a fully vetted Study GRC Inc. representative, provided they do not have direct, unsupervised access to minors or sensitive data.

| Term | Definition |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Application and Credential Review
1.  **Submission:** Candidates must submit a formal application via the Organization’s designated portal. The application must include:
    *   Current Curriculum Vitae (CV) or Resume.
    *   Statement of Teaching Philosophy.
    *   List of relevant certifications (e.g., CISSP, CISA, CPA) with active license numbers for verification.
2.  **Initial Screening:** The **VP of Education & Training** reviews the application to ensure the candidate meets the minimum *Instructor Qualification Standard*.
    *   *Criteria:* Demonstrated subject matter expertise (3+ years experience) and alignment with the Organization’s mission.
3.  **Credential Verification:** The **HR/Operations Team** verifies the status of professional certifications listed by the candidate. Expired or revoked certifications may be grounds for immediate disqualification.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Competency Assessment
1.  **Behavioral Interview:** Candidates passing the initial screen must attend a virtual interview with the Education Team. Questions will focus on:
    *   Communication style and inclusivity.
    *   Experience working with diverse learners (including youth, if applicable).
    *   Conflict resolution in a classroom setting.
2.  **Technical Demonstration (Teach-Back):**
    *   The candidate must prepare and deliver a 15-minute "mini-lesson" on a GRC topic assigned by the VP of Education.
    *   *Evaluation:* The lesson is scored on technical accuracy, clarity, engagement, and adherence to the *Brand Identity and Style Guide*.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Background Check and Safety Screening
**Mandatory Requirement:** No instructor may be scheduled to teach or access community systems until this phase is fully cleared.

1.  **Consent:** The candidate must complete the *Background Check Consent Form* authorizing Study GRC Inc. to conduct a consumer report investigation.
2.  **Investigation Scope:** The **HR/Operations Team** initiates the check via an accredited third-party vendor. The check must include, at a minimum:
    *   **Identity Verification** (SSN Trace).
    *   **National Criminal Database Search**.
    *   **County Criminal Search** (7-year history).
    *   **National Sex Offender Registry Search** (Mandatory for Youth Protection).
    *   **Global Watchlist Search** (Terrorism/Sanctions).
3.  **Adjudication:**
    *   **Clear:** Candidate proceeds to Phase IV.
    *   **Flagged:** Results are reviewed by the *Background Check Adjudication Committee* per the *Background Check Policy*.
    *   **Disqualification:** Any record of violent crime, sexual misconduct, or crimes against children results in automatic, permanent disqualification.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Reference Checks
1.  The Organization must contact at least two (2) professional references provided by the candidate.
2.  Inquiries must verify:
    *   Dates of employment/service.
    *   Reliability and professionalism.
    *   Suitability for working with students/minors.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Final Approval and Onboarding
1.  **Approval:** Upon successful completion of all prior phases, the VP of Education & Training submits a "Request to Hire/Onboard" to the Executive Director.
2.  **Contract Execution:**
    *   **IP Assignment:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX (Section 9.3)**, the instructor must sign the *Instructor Agreement* which includes a "Work Made for Hire" clause, assigning all course materials to Study GRC Inc.
    *   **NDA:** The instructor must sign the *Non-Disclosure Agreement*.
    *   **Code of Conduct:** The instructor must sign the *Instructor Code of Ethics* and *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* acknowledgment.
3.  **System Access:** Only after all documents are signed may the **CISO** or IT Team provision access to the Learning Management System (LMS) and internal communication channels.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Applicant** | Provides accurate information, consents to screening, and demonstrates teaching competency. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Owns the vetting process; evaluates technical and pedagogical skills; makes final recommendation. |
| **HR/Operations Team** | Administers background checks; verifies credentials; manages contract documentation. |
| **Background Check Adjudication Committee** | Reviews "flagged" background checks to determine eligibility based on risk assessment. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides final financial and operational approval for all instructor appointments. |
| **CISO** | Provisions system access only upon receipt of "Clearance" notification from HR. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this procedure—specifically the bypassing of background checks prior to allowing an instructor access to students—is a critical violation of the Organization's risk management protocols.

*   **Staff:** Employees found circumventing this procedure are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Applicants:** Falsification of credentials or application data will result in immediate disqualification and a permanent ban from future consideration.
