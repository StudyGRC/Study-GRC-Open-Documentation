### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for reviewing and signing IRS Form 8283 (Noncash Charitable Contributions) when requested by a donor. This procedure ensures that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") fulfills its obligations to donors to substantiate charitable deductions while strictly adhering to IRS regulations regarding the acknowledgment of property receipt. Crucially, this procedure mitigates the risk of the Organization inadvertently validating an inaccurate appraisal or valuation of donated property.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Officers, Directors, and Operational Leaders authorized to accept donations and execute tax documents on behalf of the Organization.

*   **In Scope:** Noncash donations (property, crypto-assets, hardware, intellectual property) where the claimed value exceeds $5,000 and the donor requests a Donee Acknowledgment.
*   **Out of Scope:** Cash donations, donations under $5,000 (unless grouped), and the actual appraisal of the property (which is the sole responsibility of the donor).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **IRS Form 8283** | The tax form filed by donors to report information about noncash charitable contributions. Section B, Part IV requires the Donee's signature for items valued over $5,000. |
| **Donee Acknowledgment** | The specific section of Form 8283 where the Organization confirms receipt of the property. |
| **Qualified Appraisal** | An appraisal document prepared by a qualified appraiser in accordance with generally accepted appraisal standards. The Organization **never** prepares this. |
| **IRS Form 8282** | The "Donee Information Return" the Organization must file if it sells, exchanges, or disposes of the donated property within three (3) years of receipt. |
| **Authorized Signer** | An individual designated by the Board of Directors (typically the Treasurer, CFO, or Executive Director) with the authority to sign tax documents. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. Receipt and Physical Verification
Before reviewing any tax forms, the Organization must verify possession of the asset.
1.  **Physical/Digital Verification:** The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or designee must confirm that the property described has been physically received or legally transferred (e.g., title transfer, crypto wallet transfer) to the Organization.
2.  **Date of Contribution:** Confirm the specific date the Organization took control of the asset. This date must match the Organization's internal *Gift Acceptance Log*.
3.  **Description Match:** Ensure the detailed description of the property on the *Gift Acceptance Form* matches the physical/digital asset received.

#### 4.2. Review of Form 8283 (Section B)
The Authorized Signer must review the Form 8283 provided by the donor for completeness and accuracy regarding the **receipt** of goods only.
1.  **Check Donor Information:** Verify the donor's name and ID number are present.
2.  **Verify Property Description (Part I):** Ensure the description of the donated property (Line 3a) accurately reflects the item received.
3.  **Review Appraised Value (Line 3c):**
    *   The Organization **does not** verify the value. However, the Authorized Signer must review this line to ensure the asset is not being grossly misrepresented based on obvious observation (e.g., a broken laptop listed as $50,000).
    *   *Note:* If the value appears fraudulent, the Authorized Signer must pause the process and consult the Board Treasurer and Legal Counsel immediately.
4.  **Check Appraiser Signature (Part III):** Ensure the Appraiser has signed Part III. The Organization should generally sign **after** the Appraiser to ensure the document is complete, though IRS rules allow concurrent execution.

#### 4.3. Prohibited Actions
To protect the Organization's 501(c)(3) status and avoid penalties:
1.  **No Blank Forms:** Never sign a blank or incomplete Form 8283. The property description must be filled in before signature.
2.  **No Valuation Validation:** The Organization acknowledges **receipt only**. Staff must never verbally or in writing agree to the *value* of the donation, only the *nature* of the donation.
3.  **No "Quid Pro Quo" Omission:** If the donor received goods or services in exchange for the donation (e.g., gala tickets), this must be noted.

#### 4.4. Execution of Donee Acknowledgment (Part IV)
Once verified, the Authorized Signer completes Part IV:
1.  **Organization Info:** Enter the full legal name (Study GRC Inc.) and Employer Identification Number (EIN).
2.  **Address:** Use the Registered Office address or the primary administrative address as filed with the IRS.
3.  **Signature:** The Authorized Signer executes the document.
    *   *Digital Signatures:* Per IRS Notice 2021-66, the Organization may use a secure digital signature (e.g., DocuSign) if it includes identity verification and an audit trail.
4.  **Date:** Enter the date the form is signed (not necessarily the date of the donation).

#### 4.5. Mandatory Asset Tracking (The "Three-Year Rule")
Upon signing Form 8283, the asset enters a restricted tracking category.
1.  **Asset Tagging:** The CFO must tag the asset record in the *Fixed Asset Register* or *Inventory Log* as "Form 8283 Property."
2.  **Tracking Period:** The record must be flagged for a period of three (3) years from the date of contribution.
3.  **Disposal Alert:** If this specific asset is sold, exchanged, consumed, or disposed of within three years, the Finance Department must trigger the **IRS Form 8282 Filing Procedure** (FIN-PRC-066) to report the sale price to the IRS within 125 days of disposition.

#### 4.6. Return to Donor
1.  Return the signed copy to the donor via secure electronic transmission (encrypted email or secure portal).
2.  Retain a copy of the fully executed Form 8283 in the Organization’s permanent financial records for a minimum of seven (7) years.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Donor** | Responsible for obtaining a qualified appraisal, completing Section B Parts I-III, and presenting the form to the Organization. |
| **CFO / Treasurer** | Acts as the Authorized Signer. Verifies receipt of goods, reviews the form for completeness, and executes the Donee Acknowledgment. |
| **Finance Department** | Logs the asset into the financial system with a "Form 8283" flag to ensure compliance with future Form 8282 filing requirements. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Consulted if the donation appears fraudulent or if the appraisal value is manifestly unreasonable. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in:
*   **IRS Penalties:** Under IRC § 6701, penalties may apply for aiding and abetting an understatement of tax liability if the Organization knowingly signs a fraudulent form.
*   **Loss of Status:** Severe violations regarding donation substantiation can jeopardize the Organization's 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees or Officers who sign blank forms or collude with donors to inflate values will be subject to immediate termination and potential legal action.
