### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory legal and governance steps required for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") to sell, lease, exchange, or otherwise dispose of all, or substantially all, of its property and assets.

This procedure ensures compliance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.164**, protects the Organization’s tax-exempt status under IRS 501(c)(3) by preventing private inurement, and adheres to the strict voting thresholds defined in **Article X** of the Organization's Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** The Board of Directors, Officers, and Voting Members of Study GRC Inc.
*   **In Scope:** Any transaction involving the sale, lease, exchange, or disposal of assets that constitutes a "Fundamental Action" as defined in the Bylaws, specifically when such transaction occurs **outside the usual and regular course of business**.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Disposal of obsolete equipment or inventory in the regular course of business (governed by the *Fixed Asset Disposal Policy*).
    *   Sales of educational materials, merchandise, or tickets as part of standard program operations.
    *   Granting of non-exclusive licenses for intellectual property where the Organization retains ownership.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fundamental Action** | As defined in Bylaws Article X, a significant event altering the core structure, including the sale of all or substantially all assets. |
| **Substantially All Assets** | A transaction involving assets that are quantitatively vital to the operation of the corporation, or a transaction that fundamentally changes the nature of the Organization's business or makes it impossible to continue current operations. |
| **Regular Course of Business** | Routine activities consistent with the Organization's charitable mission (e.g., selling training courses, disposing of broken laptops). |
| **Voting Members** | Supporting Members and Honorary Members in good standing who hold voting rights under Bylaws Article II. |
| **Interested Party** | Any Director, Officer, or Member who has a financial interest in the entity purchasing the assets. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Preliminary Assessment and Valuation
1.  **Proposal Initiation:** The Executive Director or a Board Director must submit a written proposal for the asset sale to the Board.
2.  **Legal Classification:** The Secretary and/or Legal Counsel must determine if the proposed transaction constitutes a sale of "substantially all assets" under TBOC § 22.164.
3.  **Valuation:** The Treasurer must obtain a qualified, independent valuation or appraisal of the assets to establish **Fair Market Value (FMV)**.
    *   *Note:* Selling assets below FMV to a private individual or entity may constitute an "Excess Benefit Transaction" under IRS rules and is strictly prohibited.
4.  **Conflict of Interest Review:** The Board must review the potential buyer against the *Conflict of Interest Policy*. If the buyer is an Interested Party, the strict protocols of the *Interested Party Transaction Review Protocol* apply in addition to this procedure.

#### 4.2 Board of Directors Resolution
1.  **Meeting Call:** The President must call a Board meeting to consider the proposal.
2.  **Board Vote:** The Board must adopt a resolution recommending the sale.
    *   The resolution must direct that the proposed transaction be submitted to a vote of the Voting Members.
    *   Approval requires a majority vote of Directors in office (Bylaws § 3.5).
3.  **Documentation:** The Secretary must record the resolution, the valuation data relied upon, and the Board's rationale in the meeting minutes.

#### 4.3 Notice to Voting Members
1.  **Notice Issuance:** Upon Board approval, the Organization must provide written notice to each Voting Member entitled to vote.
2.  **Timing:** Notice must be delivered at least **10 days** but no more than **60 days** before the meeting date (Bylaws § 2.5).
3.  **Content Requirements:** Per TBOC § 22.164(b)(2), the notice must expressly state that the **purpose** (or one of the purposes) of the meeting is to consider the sale, lease, exchange, or other disposition of all or substantially all of the property and assets of the corporation.

#### 4.4 Membership Vote (The Supermajority)
1.  **Quorum Verification:** The Secretary must verify a quorum is present (electronically or in-person) per Bylaws § 2.4.
2.  **Voting Threshold:** Per **Bylaws Section 10.2**, approval requires the affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3) of ALL Voting Members**.
    *   *Critical Distinction:* This is not merely 2/3 of those *present*; it is 2/3 of the *total* voting membership roster.
3.  **Proxy Prohibition:** No proxy voting is permitted (Bylaws § 2.3). Votes must be cast personally or via the Organization's secure electronic voting system.

#### 4.5 Execution and Disposition
1.  **Authorization:** Upon successful Member approval, the Board may authorize the President or Executive Director to execute the necessary legal instruments (contracts, deeds, bills of sale).
2.  **Abandonment:** Per TBOC § 22.164(d), the Board may abandon the sale—even after Member approval—subject to the rights of third parties under any contracts relating to the sale, without further action by the members.
3.  **Proceeds Management:** Proceeds from the sale must be deposited into the Organization’s accounts and used exclusively for exempt purposes.
    *   If the sale is part of a dissolution, proceeds must be distributed according to **Bylaws Article XI** (Distribution of Assets).

#### 4.6 Regulatory Reporting
1.  **IRS Form 990:** The Treasurer must ensure the transaction is reported on the next annual IRS Form 990, specifically **Schedule N** (Liquidation, Termination, Dissolution, or Significant Disposition of Assets).
2.  **State Filings:** Legal Counsel must determine if any filings with the Texas Secretary of State or Attorney General are required based on the specific nature of the assets (e.g., charitable trusts).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews valuation, approves the initial resolution, and ensures the transaction aligns with the 501(c)(3) mission. |
| **Voting Members** | Provide final authorization via a 2/3 supermajority vote of the entire membership. |
| **Executive Director** | Manages the operational aspects of the valuation and negotiation; executes documents upon final approval. |
| **Treasurer** | Oversees the independent valuation process and ensures proceeds are handled per financial policies. |
| **Secretary** | Issues compliant notices to members and certifies the voting results. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Strict Adherence:** Failure to follow this procedure renders the asset sale **voidable** under Texas law and may subject Directors to personal liability for breach of fiduciary duty.

**Ultra Vires Acts:** Any Officer or Director attempting to sell substantially all assets without this specific authorization is acting *ultra vires* (beyond their legal power). Such actions may result in immediate removal from office, termination of employment, and legal action for damages.
