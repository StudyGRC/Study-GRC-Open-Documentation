### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory steps for reporting, documenting, and investigating work-related accidents, injuries, and illnesses within Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures compliance with the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) Recordkeeping Standard (29 CFR 1904) and Texas Workers' Compensation laws. It aims to facilitate timely medical treatment, accurate recordkeeping, and the identification of root causes to prevent recurrence.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all accidents, injuries, illnesses, and "near misses" occurring within the scope of Study GRC Inc. operations.

*   **Applies to:** All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary), contractors, volunteers, and Board Directors.
*   **Locations:** Includes the Organization’s registered office, remote home offices (telework), and off-site locations where Organization-sponsored events or business is conducted.
*   **Exclusions:** Injuries occurring to employees during a voluntary commute or injuries in a home office that are unrelated to the performance of job duties (see Section 4.4).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Work-Related** | An event or exposure in the work environment that either caused or contributed to the resulting condition or significantly aggravated a pre-existing injury or illness. |
| **Recordable Injury** | An injury/illness resulting in death, days away from work, restricted work or transfer to another job, medical treatment beyond first aid, loss of consciousness, or significant injury diagnosed by a physician (29 CFR 1904.7). |
| **First Aid** | Limited medical care as defined by OSHA (e.g., using non-prescription meds at non-prescription strength, cleaning wounds, using bandages, using hot/cold therapy). First aid incidents are *not* recordable on the OSHA 300 Log. |
| **Remote Worksite** | A designated area within an employee's home used for the performance of job duties, as defined in the *Remote Work Agreement*. |
| **Near Miss** | An unplanned event that did not result in injury, illness, or damage but had the potential to do so. |
| **OSHA 300 Log** | The Log of Work-Related Injuries and Illnesses required by 29 CFR 1904. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Immediate Response and Medical Attention
1.  **Emergency:** If the injury is life-threatening or requires immediate professional intervention, the affected individual or witness must call **911** immediately.
2.  **Scene Safety:** The senior-most employee or volunteer present must secure the scene to prevent further injury.
3.  **Non-Emergency:** If the injury is not life-threatening, the individual should seek appropriate medical attention (e.g., Urgent Care) if necessary.
4.  **Notification:** The injured party must notify their immediate supervisor or the Director of Operations as soon as the situation is stabilized, but no later than **end of shift** on the day of the incident.

#### 4.2 Reporting Timelines (Internal)
All incidents must be reported internally within the following strict timelines:

1.  **Fatality or Mass Hospitalization:** Immediate notification to the Executive Director and Director of Operations (within 1 hour).
2.  **Serious Injury (Amputation, Loss of Eye, In-patient Hospitalization):** Immediate notification to the Director of Operations (within 2 hours).
3.  **General Injury/Illness:** Written report submitted within **24 hours** of the incident.
4.  **Near Miss:** Written report submitted within **48 hours**.

#### 4.3 Reporting Timelines (External / OSHA)
Pursuant to 29 CFR 1904.39, the Director of Operations must report specific severe incidents to OSHA:

1.  **Fatality:** Must be reported to OSHA within **8 hours**.
2.  **In-Patient Hospitalization, Amputation, or Loss of an Eye:** Must be reported to OSHA within **24 hours**.
    *   *Reporting Method:* Call 1-800-321-OSHA (6742) or use the reporting application on www.osha.gov.

#### 4.4 Remote Work (Home Office) Determination
Given the Organization's remote-first nature, determining work-relatedness in a home office follows OSHA Directive CPL 02-00-125:

1.  **Work-Related:** Injuries are recordable if they occur while the employee is performing paid work duties in the designated work area (e.g., dropping a box of work files on a foot; ergonomic injury from typing).
2.  **Not Work-Related:** Injuries are *not* recordable if they occur in the home environment outside of work tasks (e.g., tripping over a family pet while getting coffee; injury while doing laundry during a break).
3.  **Assessment:** The Director of Operations will review the *Remote Work Agreement* and the specific circumstances to determine OSHA recordability.

#### 4.5 Documentation Steps
1.  **Initial Report (Internal):** The injured party (or supervisor) must complete the **Internal Incident Report Form** (Form EHS-FRM-001).
2.  **OSHA Form 301 (Injury and Illness Incident Report):** For every recordable injury, the Director of Operations must complete Form 301 (or an equivalent) within **7 calendar days** of receiving information that a recordable injury occurred.
3.  **OSHA Form 300 (Log):** The Director of Operations must classify the injury and enter it onto the OSHA 300 Log within **7 calendar days**.
    *   *Privacy Cases:* If the injury involves sensitive medical information (e.g., sexual assault, mental illness, needle stick), the employee's name must be omitted and replaced with "Privacy Case."
4.  **OSHA Form 300A (Summary):**
    *   The Director of Operations must tabulate the annual summary based on the Form 300 Log.
    *   A company executive (Executive Director) must certify the summary.
    *   The Form 300A must be posted electronically (on the internal intranet/portal) from **February 1 to April 30** of the year following the covered year.

#### 4.6 Investigation and Corrective Action
1.  **Investigation:** Within 3 business days of a reported incident, the Director of Operations (or designated Safety Committee member) must conduct an investigation to determine the root cause.
2.  **Root Cause Analysis:** The investigation must look beyond "human error" to identify systemic issues (e.g., lack of training, faulty equipment, unclear policy).
3.  **Corrective Action Plan (CAP):** A CAP must be developed to mitigate the risk of recurrence. Implementation of the CAP will be tracked in the *Corrective Action Tracking Log*.

#### 4.7 Volunteer and Participant Injuries
While OSHA 1904 applies primarily to employees:
1.  Injuries to volunteers or event participants must be documented using the **General Incident Report Form**.
2.  These incidents are **not** entered into the OSHA 300 Log unless the individual is reclassified as an employee under specific legal tests.
3.  These reports must be retained for insurance liability purposes in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employees / Volunteers** | Immediately report all injuries, illnesses, or near misses. Cooperate with investigations. |
| **Supervisors** | Ensure injured staff receive medical attention. Submit the initial incident report within 24 hours. Secure the scene of on-site accidents. |
| **Director of Operations** | Manage the OSHA 300 Log, Form 301, and Form 300A. Determine work-relatedness. Lead investigations. Report severe injuries to OSHA within statutory deadlines. |
| **Executive Director** | Review and certify the Annual OSHA 300A Summary. Allocate resources for Corrective Action Plans. |
| **HR Department** | Coordinate Workers' Compensation claims with the insurance carrier. Maintain medical records confidentially (separate from personnel files). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to report a work-related injury immediately, or falsification of accident reports, constitutes a violation of this procedure.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Non-Retaliation:** In accordance with the *Whistleblower Protection Policy* and OSHA regulations, Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual for reporting an injury, illness, or safety concern.
*   **Legal Penalties:** Failure to maintain accurate OSHA logs or report fatalities/severe injuries within the required timeframe may result in federal civil penalties against the Organization.
