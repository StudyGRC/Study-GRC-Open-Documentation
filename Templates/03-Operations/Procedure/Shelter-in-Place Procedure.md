### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory steps for **Shelter-in-Place** protocols to protect the lives and safety of Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, members, and program participants (specifically minors) during environmental or external security threats. This procedure satisfies the Organization's "Duty of Care" and aligns with OSHA General Duty Clause requirements regarding emergency preparedness.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **Physical Events:** All in-person gatherings, workshops, conferences, or Board meetings hosted or managed by Study GRC Inc., regardless of venue.
*   **Remote Workforce:** Guidance for home-based employees and volunteers during local emergencies.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not cover **Evacuation** (e.g., fire inside the building). Refer to the *Emergency Action Plan (EAP)* for evacuation protocols.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Shelter-in-Place** | The act of seeking immediate safety within the building one already occupies, rather than evacuating the area or the building. |
| **Event Lead** | The highest-ranking Study GRC Inc. officer or designated staff member present at a physical location. |
| **Lockout** | A specific type of Shelter-in-Place used when there is a threat *outside* the building (e.g., civil unrest, police activity). The perimeter is secured, but normal activity may continue inside away from windows. |
| **Seal the Room** | A procedure used during chemical or radiological emergencies involving closing vents and sealing gaps to prevent air exchange. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The mandate that at least two background-checked adults must be present with minors at all times, even during emergency scenarios. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Activation Criteria
The **Event Lead** must immediately activate this procedure upon:
1.  **Notification:** Receipt of an emergency alert (EAS, Siren, Police, Venue Management) indicating a threat such as a tornado, chemical spill, or external violence.
2.  **Observation:** Direct observation of threatening weather or hazardous conditions.

#### 4.2 General Shelter-in-Place Steps (Physical Events)
Upon activation, all staff and volunteers must execute the following:

1.  **Announce:** The Event Lead must clearly announce: *"Attention. We are initiating Shelter-in-Place procedures due to [Threat]. Please remain calm and follow staff instructions. Do not leave the building."*
2.  **Clear Areas:** Move all participants from outdoor areas, hallways with glass, and lobbies into designated interior rooms or auditoriums.
3.  **Secure Perimeter:** Designated Safety Officers must close and lock all external doors and windows immediately.
4.  **Accountability:** Once settled, staff must conduct a headcount of all participants, with specific priority on verifying the location of all minors.

#### 4.3 Scenario-Specific Protocols

##### 4.3.1 Severe Weather (Tornado / High Winds)
1.  **Location:** Move to the lowest floor possible, into interior rooms, hallways, or restrooms without windows.
2.  **Posture:** Instruct participants to kneel facing the wall and cover their heads with their hands.
3.  **Avoid:** Stay clear of auditoriums with wide-span roofs, glass atriums, and elevators.

##### 4.3.2 Chemical / Biological / Radiological (Hazmat)
1.  **Location:** Move to an interior room above ground level (some chemicals are heavier than air).
2.  **HVAC Control:** The Event Lead must coordinate with venue management to immediately shut down air handling systems (HVAC).
3.  **Seal:** If instructed by emergency services, use available materials (tape, plastic, clothing) to seal gaps under doors and around vents.

##### 4.3.3 External Violence / Civil Unrest (Lockout)
1.  **Secure:** Lock all external doors immediately. Cover windows if blinds are available.
2.  **Visibility:** Turn off lights if the threat is in close proximity to the building.
3.  **Silence:** Instruct participants to silence mobile phones.
4.  **Movement:** Move participants away from line-of-sight of windows and doors.

#### 4.4 Youth Protection During Shelter-in-Place
Given the Organization's K-12 focus, the following strict safeguards apply during the chaos of an emergency:

1.  **Supervision:** **Two-Deep Leadership** must be maintained. No adult may be isolated alone with a minor in a closet, restroom, or office during sheltering.
2.  **Reunification:** Minors must **not** be released to parents/guardians during an active Shelter-in-Place order unless directed by Law Enforcement. Opening doors to parents compromises the safety of the entire group.
3.  **Calm:** Staff must prioritize managing the anxiety of youth participants through calm, clear communication.

#### 4.5 Remote Workforce Protocol
For employees and volunteers working from home (Remote-First context):

1.  **Priority:** Personal safety supersedes all work duties. If a local siren or alert sounds, the individual must cease work immediately and shelter.
2.  **Notification:** *Only once safe to do so*, the individual should notify their supervisor or the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** via Slack/Text that they are offline due to an emergency.
3.  **Check-In:** The Organization will initiate a welfare check if a remote employee in a known disaster zone is unresponsive for more than 4 hours after the "All Clear" is given by local authorities.

#### 4.6 All-Clear and Termination
1.  **Authority:** The Shelter-in-Place may only be lifted by the **Event Lead** after receiving an official "All Clear" from emergency services or venue management.
2.  **Reporting:** An *Incident Report Form (OPS-FRM-015)* must be filed by the Event Lead within 24 hours of the event.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Lead** | Authority to activate the procedure; primary liaison with venue and emergency services. |
| **Safety Officer** | Physically secures doors/windows; manages HVAC shutdown requests; directs crowd movement. |
| **Volunteers/Staff** | Maintain order; ensure Two-Deep Leadership; assist participants with mobility impairments. |
| **Executive Director** | Reviews Incident Reports post-event; authorizes updates to this procedure. |
| **Remote Staff** | Responsible for maintaining their own home emergency plan; notifying leadership of status when safe. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure, particularly regarding the **Two-Deep Leadership** requirement during emergencies or the failure to secure a venue perimeter, may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment. Negligence resulting in injury may lead to legal liability for the individual and the Organization.
