### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized workflow for the creation, review, and publication of the Study GRC Inc. Annual Report. This procedure ensures compliance with **Article VIII, Section 8.2** of the Bylaws and **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.352**, which mandates the preparation of an annual financial report. Furthermore, this document serves to maintain transparency with the community, donors, and regulatory bodies by accurately summarizing the Organization’s impact, financial health, and strategic direction.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the Executive Director, Operational Officers (specifically the CFO and DKG), and the Board of Directors. It covers the entire lifecycle of the Annual Report from data collection to public dissemination.

*   **In Scope:** Narrative development, financial data aggregation, graphic design, Board review, and digital publication.
*   **Out of Scope:** The preparation of the IRS Form 990 tax filing (covered in *FIN-PRC-065 Form 990 Prep and Review Checklist*), although data must be consistent between both documents.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Annual Report** | A comprehensive public document describing the Organization's activities, impact, and financial performance during the preceding Fiscal Year. |
| **Fiscal Year** | The period from January 1 to December 31, as defined in **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3**. |
| **Statement of Support** | A required financial component detailing revenue, expenses, and changes in fund balances, per **TBOC § 22.352**. |
| **Impact Metrics** | Quantifiable data points (e.g., number of students certified, hours of open-source content created) demonstrating mission success. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Planning and Timeline (January 15 – January 31)
The production cycle begins immediately following the close of the Fiscal Year.
1.  **Kickoff Meeting:** The Executive Director must convene a meeting with the CFO and DKG to establish the production schedule.
2.  **Theme Selection:** The team must select a central theme for the report that aligns with the Organization’s strategic goals for the upcoming year.
3.  **Asset Inventory:** The DKG must identify necessary visual assets (photos from events, screenshots of resources, volunteer testimonials) required for the layout.

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Data Collection and Content Drafting (February 1 – March 15)
1.  **Financial Data Aggregation:**
    *   The CFO must prepare the "Statement of Support, Revenue, and Expenses" and the "Statement of Changes in Fund Balances" as required by **Bylaws Section 8.2**.
    *   Financial data must be reconciled with the preliminary year-end books prior to the final audit.
2.  **Impact Data Collection:**
    *   The VP of Education & Training must provide metrics regarding course completions, resource downloads, and student engagement.
    *   The DKG must provide metrics regarding community growth, volunteer hours, and diversity/inclusion initiatives.
3.  **Narrative Drafting:**
    *   The Executive Director must draft the "Letter from the CEO" and the "Strategic Outlook."
    *   The Board President must draft the "Letter from the Board."

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Design and Layout (March 16 – April 15)
1.  **Draft Layout:** The DKG (or designated designer) must compile the text and data into the Organization’s standard Annual Report template, ensuring adherence to the *Brand Identity and Style Guide (COM-GUD-012)*.
2.  **Visual Compliance:** All photos of minors (if any) must be cross-referenced against *Youth Protection Photo Release Forms* to ensure valid consent exists.
3.  **Transparency Page Integration:** The design must include a specific section or QR code directing readers to the "Transparency" page on the website for IRS Form 990 access, per **Bylaws Section 8.2**.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Review and Approval (April 16 – April 30)
1.  **Financial Accuracy Review:** The Treasurer and CFO must review the draft to ensure the financial visualizations (charts/graphs) accurately reflect the accounting records.
2.  **Legal & Compliance Review:** The Secretary must review the draft to ensure no confidential or proprietary information is inadvertently disclosed and that the report satisfies **TBOC § 22.352**.
3.  **Board Approval:**
    *   The final draft must be circulated to the Board of Directors at least five (5) days prior to the May Board meeting.
    *   The Board must vote to approve the Annual Report for publication.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Publication and Distribution (May 1 – May 15)
1.  **Digital Publication:**
    *   The approved PDF must be uploaded to the Organization’s website under the "Transparency" or "Impact" section.
    *   The report must be optimized for web accessibility (WCAG 2.2 AA) per *Digital Accessibility Policy (IT-POL-090)*.
2.  **Stakeholder Notification:**
    *   An email announcement must be sent to all Voting Members, donors, and volunteers.
    *   Social media posts must be scheduled to highlight key statistics from the report.
3.  **Physical Copies:** If physical copies are required for donor meetings, they must be printed on demand to minimize waste and cost.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ultimate accountability for the report's completion; authors the strategic narrative; ensures alignment with mission. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Prepares all financial statements; verifies accuracy of financial data; ensures alignment with GAAP and tax filings. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Manages the design, layout, and collection of community stories/testimonials; oversees distribution. |
| **Board Treasurer** | Provides oversight of the financial section to ensure it meets fiduciary standards. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves the final document before public release. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to produce an Annual Financial Report is a violation of **Texas Business Organizations Code § 22.352** and **Bylaws Article VIII**, which may jeopardize the Organization's corporate status and public trust.

Staff members who falsify data within the Annual Report may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination, and potential legal action for fraud.
