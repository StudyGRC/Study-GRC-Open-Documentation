### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish a standardized process for announcing strategic partnerships, sponsorships, and alliances involving Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures that all public announcements align with the Organization’s charitable mission as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, comply with IRS regulations regarding non-profit endorsements and Unrelated Business Income Tax (UBIT), and protect the Organization’s brand integrity.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees, Board Directors, and volunteers involved in external relations, marketing, or business development.

**In Scope:**
*   Strategic Alliances (non-financial collaboration).
*   Corporate Sponsorships (financial contributions).
*   Grant Awards (Foundation or Government).
*   Joint Ventures or Co-Branded Events.

**Out of Scope:**
*   Routine vendor procurement (e.g., announcing the purchase of a new software tool for internal use).
*   Individual donor acknowledgments (covered under *Donor Acknowledgment Letter Template*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Strategic Partnership** | A formal agreement between Study GRC Inc. and an external entity to jointly pursue a specific goal that advances the Organization's mission. |
| **Qualified Sponsorship** | A payment where there is no arrangement or expectation that the payor will receive any substantial return benefit other than the use or acknowledgment of the name or logo. |
| **Endorsement** | A public statement recommending a product or service. **Strictly prohibited** for 501(c)(3) organizations regarding for-profit products. |
| **Call to Action (CTA)** | Language urging the consumer to buy, use, or patronize a product (e.g., "Buy now," "Best price"). Prohibited in tax-exempt acknowledgments. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Pre-Announcement Due Diligence
Before any announcement is drafted, the underlying relationship must be vetted.

1.  **Contract Execution:** No announcement may be planned until a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU), Sponsorship Agreement, or Contract is fully executed by authorized signatories per the *Signature Authority Policy*.
2.  **Risk Assessment:** The Relationship Manager must confirm that the partner has cleared the *Vendor Security Risk Assessment* (if data is shared) and the *Conflict of Interest* review.
3.  **Mission Alignment Check:** The Executive Director must certify that the partnership directly supports the purposes outlined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3** (Advancing GRC knowledge, removing financial barriers, or empowering learners).

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Classification and Messaging Strategy
The Director of Communications determines the announcement tier and permissible language to avoid IRS penalties.

1.  **Determine Tier:**
    *   **Tier 1 (Major):** Press Release, Dedicated Blog Post, Newsletter Feature, Social Media Campaign. (e.g., New Founding Sponsor, Federal Grant).
    *   **Tier 2 (Mid-Level):** Social Media Post, Logo on Website. (e.g., Event Sponsor).
    *   **Tier 3 (Minor):** Logo on Website only.
2.  **Drafting Constraints (IRS Compliance):**
    *   **Permissible:** "Study GRC Inc. is proud to partner with [Company] to expand access to GRC training." (Focus on Mission).
    *   **Permissible:** "Supported by a generous grant from [Company]." (Focus on Gratitude).
    *   **Prohibited (Advertising):** "[Company] offers the best GRC software on the market. Click here to buy." (Qualitative/Comparative language).
    *   **Prohibited (Endorsement):** "We recommend all our members use [Company] for their security needs."

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Drafting and Review
1.  **Draft Creation:** The Communications team drafts the content (Press Release, Social Copy, Blog) based on the approved messaging strategy.
2.  **Partner Review:** The draft is sent to the Partner’s marketing point-of-contact for review.
    *   *Note:* Study GRC Inc. retains final editorial control over all content published on its owned channels.
3.  **Internal Approval:**
    *   **Legal/Compliance Review:** Must review for "Endorsement" risks and IP usage.
    *   **Executive Director Review:** Final approval for Tier 1 and Tier 2 announcements.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Asset Exchange and Brand Safety
1.  **Logo Usage:**
    *   Send the Partner the *Logo and Brand Asset Usage Terms* along with official vector files.
    *   Request the Partner’s high-resolution vector logos.
2.  **Placement Verification:**
    *   Verify where the Partner intends to place the Study GRC logo.
    *   **Restriction:** The Study GRC logo must not be placed on pages primarily selling age-restricted products (alcohol, tobacco, gambling) or adjacent to content violating the *Code of Ethics*.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Distribution and Monitoring
1.  **Embargo Management:** If the announcement is time-sensitive, clearly mark all communications "EMBARGOED UNTIL [DATE/TIME] CST."
2.  **Publication:** Publish content across agreed channels (Website, LinkedIn, Email).
3.  **Social Monitoring:** The Social Media Manager must monitor comments for 48 hours post-announcement.
    *   If the Partner misrepresents the relationship (e.g., implies an endorsement where none exists), the Relationship Manager must request an immediate correction.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Relationship Manager** | Initiates the partnership, ensures contracts are signed, and acts as the primary liaison with the partner. |
| **Director of Communications** | Drafts content, ensures brand voice alignment, and manages the distribution channels. |
| **Legal / Compliance** | Reviews content for IRS compliance (UBIT/Advertising vs. Sponsorship) and IP protection. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides final approval for all Tier 1 partnerships and verifies mission alignment. |
| **Social Media Manager** | Executes the posting schedule and monitors public sentiment/comments. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this protocol may result in:
*   **Legal Risk:** Inadvertent creation of taxable Unrelated Business Income (UBIT) or loss of tax-exempt status due to improper private benefit.
*   **Reputational Damage:** Dilution of the Organization's independence and neutrality.

Employees found violating this procedure, particularly regarding unauthorized endorsements or releasing confidential partnership details prior to execution, are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.
