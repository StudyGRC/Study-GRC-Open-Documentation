### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for the identification, evaluation, and formal registration of Intellectual Property (IP) assets owned by Study GRC Inc.

While Study GRC Inc. is dedicated to open-source resources (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3), formal registration of copyrights and trademarks is essential to:
1.  Establish clear legal ownership of the "Work Product" as defined in Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3.
2.  Prevent unauthorized commercialization or misappropriation of the Organization’s open-source materials by third parties.
3.  Protect the Organization’s brand identity and reputation from confusion or fraud.
4.  Secure statutory damages and attorney's fees in the event of infringement litigation.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all:
*   **Trademarks:** Names, logos, slogans, and service marks used to identify Study GRC Inc. services (e.g., the Organization's name, certification marks, program titles).
*   **Copyrights:** Original works of authorship fixed in a tangible medium, including but not limited to educational curriculum, software code, website content, white papers, and training videos.

**Exclusions:**
*   Content created by third parties and hosted on Study GRC Inc. platforms under a non-exclusive license.
*   Minor administrative documents or transient communications (e.g., emails, slack messages) unless deemed of significant strategic value.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **USPTO** | United States Patent and Trademark Office; the federal agency for granting U.S. patents and registering trademarks. |
| **USCO** | United States Copyright Office; the federal agency responsible for administering a complex and dynamic national copyright system. |
| **Work Made for Hire** | A legal doctrine whereby the employer (Study GRC Inc.) is legally considered the author of work created by an employee within the scope of their employment, or by a contractor under a written agreement specifying such. |
| **Specimen** | A real-world example of how the trademark is used in commerce (e.g., a screenshot of the website offering services, a brochure). |
| **TEAS** | Trademark Electronic Application System; the USPTO's online filing system. |
| **eCO** | Electronic Copyright Office; the USCO's online registration system. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 IP Asset Identification and Assessment
**Responsibility:** Executive Director / Department Heads

1.  **Trigger Event:** Upon the creation of a new major program, logo, curriculum module, or software release (v1.0 or higher), the responsible Department Head must notify the Executive Director.
2.  **Materiality Assessment:** The Executive Director and Legal Counsel (or qualified Board designee) shall evaluate the asset based on the following criteria:
    *   **Strategic Value:** Is this asset central to the Organization's mission?
    *   **Commercial Risk:** Is there a high risk of third parties attempting to sell this free resource?
    *   **Longevity:** Will this asset be used for more than 2 years?
3.  **Decision:**
    *   **Register:** Proceed to Section 4.2 (Trademark) or 4.3 (Copyright).
    *   **Do Not Register:** Rely on common law rights and open-source licensing notices. Log the decision in the *IP Asset Inventory*.

#### 4.2 Trademark Clearance and Registration (USPTO)
**Responsibility:** Legal Counsel / Executive Director

1.  **Clearance Search:**
    *   Prior to adopting a new name or logo, conduct a "Knockout Search" using the USPTO TESS database to identify direct conflicts.
    *   For high-value marks, engage an external vendor to conduct a comprehensive common law search.
2.  **Application Preparation:**
    *   Identify the correct **International Class** (e.g., Class 041 for Education/Training; Class 042 for SaaS/Technology).
    *   Select the filing basis:
        *   **Section 1(a) Use in Commerce:** The mark is already being used.
        *   **Section 1(b) Intent to Use:** The mark will be used in the future (requires follow-up filing).
3.  **Specimen Collection:**
    *   Collect a digital image (JPG) showing the mark used in connection with the services (e.g., website header, course catalog).
4.  **Filing (TEAS):**
    *   Submit the application via USPTO TEAS Standard or TEAS Plus.
    *   Pay the required government filing fee.
5.  **Prosecution:**
    *   Monitor the status every 3-6 months.
    *   Respond to any Office Actions (refusals or requests for information) within the statutory deadline (usually 3 months).

#### 4.3 Copyright Registration (USCO)
**Responsibility:** VP of Education / Legal Counsel

1.  **Authorship Verification:**
    *   Confirm that all contributors have signed the *Work Made for Hire Assignment* or *Volunteer Service Agreement* as required by Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3.
    *   If third-party content is included, verify valid licenses are on file.
2.  **Application Submission (eCO):**
    *   Log in to the eCO system.
    *   Select "Register a Work" (Standard Application).
    *   **Claimant:** List "Study GRC Inc." as the claimant (not the individual author).
    *   **Author:** List "Study GRC Inc." (for work made for hire) or the individual employee with "Work Made for Hire" checked.
3.  **Deposit Material:**
    *   Upload the "Best Edition" of the work (e.g., the PDF of the curriculum, the source code of the software).
    *   For software with trade secrets, follow USCO redaction guidelines (e.g., first 25 and last 25 pages of source code).
4.  **Licensing Declaration:**
    *   Ensure the deposited material clearly displays the Organization’s chosen open-source license (e.g., Creative Commons BY-SA 4.0 or MIT License) to prevent ambiguity regarding public usage rights.

#### 4.4 Maintenance and Renewals
**Responsibility:** Secretary / Legal Counsel

1.  **Docketing:**
    *   All registration numbers and deadlines must be entered into the Organization’s *Compliance Calendar*.
2.  **Trademark Maintenance:**
    *   **Section 8 Declaration:** File between the 5th and 6th year after registration to prove continued use.
    *   **Section 15 Declaration:** File after 5 years of continuous use to achieve "Incontestability."
    *   **Section 9 Renewal:** File every 10 years.
3.  **Copyright Maintenance:**
    *   U.S. Copyrights for works made for hire generally last 95 years from publication; no active renewal is required for works created after 1978, but contact information in the public record should be kept current.

#### 4.5 Enforcement and Policing
**Responsibility:** Board of Directors

1.  **Monitoring:** The Organization shall periodically review the market for unauthorized use of its IP, specifically looking for:
    *   Entities selling Study GRC Inc. open-source materials in violation of the "Non-Commercial" or "ShareAlike" clauses (if applicable).
    *   Entities using the "Study GRC" name to imply false endorsement.
2.  **Cease and Desist:**
    *   Upon discovery of infringement, the Board shall authorize the issuance of a Cease and Desist letter.
    *   Legal action shall be pursued only if the infringement significantly threatens the Organization's mission or 501(c)(3) status.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the IP budget; authorizes litigation or enforcement actions; reviews high-level IP strategy. |
| **Executive Director** | operational owner of the brand; approves "Materiality Assessments" for registration; ensures budget allocation for filing fees. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Conducts clearance searches; prepares and files applications with USPTO/USCO; responds to Office Actions. |
| **VP of Education** | Ensures curriculum contains proper copyright notices and open-source license text before publication; provides specimens for filing. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains the *IP Asset Inventory* and ensures renewal deadlines are tracked in the corporate records. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in the loss of proprietary rights, inability to enforce open-source licenses, and dilution of the Organization's brand.

**Internal Enforcement:** Employees or volunteers who release Organization IP without proper vetting, or who fail to secure "Work Made for Hire" agreements from contributors, may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer service.

**External Enforcement:** The Organization reserves the right to pursue all legal remedies, including statutory damages and injunctive relief, against third parties who infringe upon its registered Intellectual Property.
