### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a mandatory, standardized framework for the prompt, thorough, and impartial investigation of sexual harassment complaints within Study GRC Inc. This procedure is designed to protect the rights and dignity of all parties involved, ensure compliance with Texas Labor Code Chapter 21 and EEOC guidelines, and establish an affirmative defense against liability by demonstrating the Organization's commitment to correcting prohibited conduct.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all **Protected Individuals**, defined as employees, independent contractors, volunteers, Board Directors, and Voting Members.

It covers alleged conduct occurring:
*   In the workplace (including home offices during work hours).
*   On Organization-managed digital platforms (e.g., Slack, Discord, Zoom, Email).
*   At Organization-sponsored events, conferences, or social gatherings.

**Exclusions:** Allegations of sexual abuse involving minors fall under the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* and strictly require immediate reporting to law enforcement and the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Complainant** | The individual alleging that they have been subjected to sexual harassment. |
| **Respondent** | The individual accused of engaging in sexual harassment. |
| **Preponderance of the Evidence** | The standard of proof used in these investigations, meaning it is "more likely than not" (more than 50%) that the incident occurred. |
| **Retaliation** | Any adverse action (firing, demotion, banning, shunning) taken against an individual for filing a complaint or participating in an investigation. |
| **Interim Measures** | Temporary administrative actions taken to protect the Complainant and the integrity of the investigation before a final decision is made. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Complaint Intake and Immediate Assessment
1.  **Reporting Channels:** Complaints may be received via the Whistleblower Hotline, direct email to the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG), or report to any Board Director.
2.  **Conflict Check:** Upon receipt, the recipient must determine if the DKG or Executive Director is implicated.
    *   If the **Executive Director** is the Respondent, the complaint must be immediately escalated to the **Board President**.
    *   If a **Board Director** is the Respondent, the complaint goes to the **Governance Committee**.
3.  **Initial Safety Assessment:** Within **24 hours** of receipt, the receiving officer must assess if there is an immediate physical threat or risk of data destruction.

#### 4.2 Implementation of Interim Measures
To prevent further harassment or retaliation during the investigation, the Organization may take the following non-disciplinary administrative actions:
1.  **Digital Separation:** Temporarily suspending the Respondent’s access to specific Slack channels, Discord servers, or shared drives where the Complainant operates.
2.  **Administrative Leave:** Placing an employee Respondent on paid administrative leave.
3.  **Volunteer Suspension:** Temporarily pausing a volunteer Respondent’s duties.
4.  **No-Contact Directive:** Issuing a written directive to the Respondent prohibiting direct or indirect contact with the Complainant.

#### 4.3 Selection of Investigator
1.  The Organization shall designate a **Qualified Investigator**. This may be:
    *   An internal HR professional (e.g., DKG) provided they are neutral and trained.
    *   External legal counsel.
    *   A third-party HR consultant.
2.  **Neutrality Requirement:** The Investigator must have no personal relationship with either party and no stake in the outcome.

#### 4.4 Investigation Planning and Evidence Preservation
The Investigator must create an Investigation Plan including:
1.  **Evidence Collection:** Securing digital logs, chat history (Slack/Discord exports), emails, video recordings (Zoom), and social media screenshots immediately to prevent deletion.
2.  **Witness List:** Identifying individuals with direct knowledge.
3.  **Timeline:** Establishing a target completion date (typically within 30 days).

#### 4.5 Interview Process
Interviews must be conducted privately (via secure video conference) and documented.

1.  **Complainant Interview:**
    *   Obtain a detailed account (Who, What, When, Where, Witnesses).
    *   Ask for specific evidence (screenshots, texts).
    *   Ask what outcome the Complainant desires (though the Organization is not bound by this).
2.  **Witness Interviews:**
    *   Interview witnesses identified by the Complainant.
    *   Ask open-ended questions.
    *   Remind witnesses of the strict **Non-Retaliation Policy**.
3.  **Respondent Interview:**
    *   Provide the Respondent with enough information about the allegations to allow them a fair opportunity to respond.
    *   Allow the Respondent to provide their version of events, witnesses, and evidence.

#### 4.6 Credibility Assessment
If there is no direct evidence (e.g., "he said/she said"), the Investigator must assess credibility based on EEOC factors:
1.  **Inherent Plausibility:** Is the testimony believable on its face?
2.  **Demeanor:** Did the person seem to be telling the truth or lying?
3.  **Motive to Falsify:** Does the person have a reason to lie?
4.  **Corroboration:** Is there witness testimony or physical evidence?
5.  **Past Record:** Does the Respondent have a history of similar behavior?

#### 4.7 Investigation Report
The Investigator shall draft a confidential report containing:
1.  Summary of allegations.
2.  Summary of witness statements and evidence.
3.  **Findings of Fact:** A determination of what likely happened based on the Preponderance of the Evidence.
4.  **Conclusion:** Whether the facts constitute a violation of the *Anti-Harassment Policy* or *Code of Ethics*.

#### 4.8 Adjudication and Disciplinary Action
1.  **Review Authority:**
    *   For Staff/Volunteers: The Executive Director reviews the report.
    *   For Executive Director/Board: The Board of Directors reviews the report.
2.  **Determination:** The Review Authority accepts or rejects the findings.
3.  **Disciplinary Action:** If a violation is found, discipline is imposed commensurate with the severity, including:
    *   Written Reprimand.
    *   Mandatory Harassment Training.
    *   Demotion or Pay Reduction.
    *   **Termination of Employment.**
    *   **Expulsion of Membership** (Pursuant to Bylaws Article II).
    *   **Removal from Board** (Pursuant to Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6).

#### 4.9 Closure and Notification
1.  **Notification:** Both the Complainant and Respondent must be notified in writing of the investigation's conclusion.
    *   *Note:* Privacy laws may limit the detail shared regarding specific disciplinary actions taken against the Respondent, but the Complainant must be informed that corrective action has been taken.
2.  **Follow-Up:** The DKG must check in with the Complainant 30 days and 90 days post-investigation to ensure no retaliation has occurred.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Acts as the primary intake officer; coordinates the selection of the Investigator; manages the timeline. |
| **Investigator** | Conducts interviews, gathers evidence, assesses credibility, and authors the Fact-Finding Report. |
| **Executive Director** | Acts as the adjudicator for staff and volunteer cases; enforces disciplinary actions. |
| **Board of Directors** | Acts as the adjudicator for cases involving the Executive Director or fellow Directors; handles appeals. |
| **All Personnel** | Required to cooperate fully with investigations; failure to cooperate is grounds for discipline. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure, including failure to report known harassment or failure to cooperate with an investigation, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Organization.

**False Accusations:** While the Organization encourages reporting, an individual who is found via investigation to have knowingly and maliciously filed a false claim (not merely a claim that could not be proven) will be subject to disciplinary action.
