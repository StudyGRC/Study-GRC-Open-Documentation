### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a rigorous, transparent, and merit-based process for the nomination, vetting, and election of **Honorary Members** to Study GRC Inc.

As defined in **Article II, Section 2.1** of the Bylaws, Honorary Members possess full voting rights. Therefore, this process is designed not only to recognize "lifetime achievement" but also to ensure that individuals granted this status possess the ethical standing, independence, and alignment with the Organization’s 501(c)(3) mission required to exercise governance authority.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   Current members of the Board of Directors (as Nominators and Approvers).
*    The Governance Committee (if established) or the Executive Committee acting in a vetting capacity.
*   Prospective nominees for Honorary Membership.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to "Supporting Members" (who gain status via financial contribution/volunteering) or "Community Participants" (non-voting).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Honorary Member** | A voting class of membership recognized by the Board for lifetime achievement in the GRC field or service to the Organization. |
| **Lifetime Achievement** | Sustained, high-impact contributions to the GRC domain, open-source education, or the Organization’s specific mission, extending beyond standard volunteerism. |
| **Voting Member** | A member entitled to vote on Fundamental Actions and Board Elections (Bylaws Art. II, §2.2). |
| **Vetting Dossier** | A compiled record of the nominee's qualifications, background check results, and alignment with the Organization's Code of Ethics. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Nomination Phase
4.1.1 **Eligibility to Nominate:** Nominations for Honorary Membership may only be submitted by a current Director or Officer of the Board. Self-nominations are prohibited.
4.1.2 **Nomination Window:** Nominations may be submitted at any time, but will only be reviewed during quarterly Board meetings or a Special Meeting called for this purpose.
4.1.3 **Submission Requirements:** The Nominator must submit a written "Nomination Statement" to the Secretary containing:
*   Candidate’s professional biography.
*   Detailed evidence of "Lifetime Achievement" in Governance, Risk, and Compliance.
*   Specific examples of alignment with Study GRC Inc.’s mission (e.g., open-source advocacy, educational empowerment).
*   Proposed Term (Life or Fixed Term).

#### 4.2 Vetting and Due Diligence
4.2.1 **Initial Review:** The Secretary (or Governance Committee) reviews the Nomination Statement for completeness and basic eligibility.
4.2.2 **Conflict of Interest (COI) Screen:**
*   The candidate is screened against the Organization’s **Conflict of Interest Policy**.
*   Any potential conflicts (e.g., employment with a direct competitor, vendor relationships) must be documented.
4.2.3 **Ethical Standing Check:** The candidate must have a clean record regarding professional ethics. A background check may be requested at the discretion of the Board, consistent with the **Background Check Policy**.
4.2.4 **Report Generation:** The Secretary compiles a "Vetting Dossier" and distributes it to the Board at least seven (7) days prior to the voting meeting.

#### 4.3 Board Review and Election
4.3.1 **Presentation:** During a valid meeting (Quorum established per Bylaws §3.5), the Nominator presents the candidate's case (maximum 10 minutes).
4.3.2 **Deliberation:** The Board deliberates in Executive Session (without the candidate or non-Board staff present).
4.3.3 **Term Definition:** Prior to the vote, the Board must explicitly define the term of the Honorary Membership in the motion:
*   **Life Term:** Permanent voting rights (revocable only for cause).
*   **Designated Term:** A specific period (e.g., 5 years).
4.3.4 **Voting:**
*   Approval requires an affirmative vote of a **majority of Directors then in office**.
*   Votes must be recorded in the minutes.

#### 4.4 Onboarding and Activation
Because Honorary Members are **Voting Members**, they are subject to the same compliance requirements as Directors regarding governance integrity.

4.4.1 **Notification:** The President issues a formal Letter of Conferral to the appointee.
4.4.2 **Mandatory Documentation:** To activate voting rights, the Honorary Member must sign and return:
*   **Code of Ethics and Conduct Acknowledgment.**
*   **Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statement.**
*   **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)** (per Bylaws Art. IX, §9.2).
4.4.3 **System Access:** Upon receipt of signed documents, the Secretary adds the Honorary Member to the "Voting Membership Roll" (Bylaws §2.6) and provisions access to the secure electronic voting system.

#### 4.5 Revocation of Status
4.5.1 **For Cause:** Honorary Membership may be revoked by the Board if the member violates the Code of Ethics, Bylaws, or acts in a manner detrimental to the Organization.
4.5.2 **Process:** Revocation follows the **Member Expulsion Due Process Policy**, ensuring notice and an opportunity to be heard.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Nominator (Director/Officer)** | Prepares the Nomination Statement and advocates for the candidate's lifetime achievement credentials. |
| **Secretary** | Conducts due diligence, prepares the Vetting Dossier, and maintains the Voting Membership Roll. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews qualifications, defines the membership term, and votes on the appointment. |
| **Honorary Member (Appointee)** | Signs mandatory compliance documents (COI, NDA) to activate voting rights. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in the invalidation of the Honorary Member's appointment and any subsequent votes cast by that individual.

**Regulatory Note:** Per **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.4**, once appointed, Honorary Members count toward the quorum of the Membership. Failure to properly onboard them or maintain their contact information may jeopardize the validity of Annual Member Meetings.
