### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized methodology for soliciting, reviewing, and integrating feedback from relevant parties prior to the finalization of significant organizational decisions, policies, or standards. This process ensures "Buy-In" across the Organization, mitigates risk by leveraging Subject Matter Expert (SME) knowledge, and aligns with Study GRC Inc.'s mission to foster a community-driven ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the development or significant revision of:
*   **Level 1 Governance Policies** (e.g., Ethics, Finance, HR).
*   **Level 2 Standards and Procedures** that impact cross-functional operations.
*   **Strategic Initiatives** requiring Board approval.
*   **Fundamental Actions** as defined in Bylaws Article X.

**Exclusions:**
*   Level 3 Guidelines (discretionary advice).
*   Minor administrative updates (e.g., correcting typographical errors or updating hyperlinks).
*   Emergency actions taken to protect life or property, provided a retroactive consultation occurs within 30 days.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Stakeholder** | Any individual, group, or entity that can affect or be affected by the Organization’s actions, objectives, and policies. |
| **RACI Model** | A matrix used to clarify roles: **R**esponsible (does the work), **A**ccountable (approves the work), **C**onsulted (provides input), **I**nformed (notified of outcome). |
| **SME** | Subject Matter Expert; an individual with deep technical or domain knowledge relevant to the document (e.g., CISO for security policies). |
| **Public Comment Period** | A designated window of time during which Voting Members and Community Participants may review and offer feedback on proposed changes. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Stakeholder Identification (The RACI Assessment)
Before drafting or revising a document, the Document Owner must identify who needs to be involved using the RACI model.

1.  **Identify the Impact Radius:** Determine which departments, roles, or member classes will be affected by the change.
2.  **Assign Roles:**
    *   **Consulted (C):** Must include relevant SMEs (e.g., HR for personnel policies, CISO for IT policies) and the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) for community impact.
    *   **Informed (I):** Departments that must change workflows based on the new rule.
3.  **Document the List:** The proposed stakeholder list must be recorded in the *Policy Impact Assessment* (GOV-FRM-005).

#### 4.2 Internal Consultation (Drafting Phase)
1.  **SME Review:** The Document Owner circulates the initial draft to identified SMEs via the Organization’s collaboration platform (e.g., Google Workspace/Slack).
2.  **Legal & Compliance Review:** All Level 1 Policies and documents citing federal regulations (e.g., 2 CFR 200) must be reviewed by the Compliance Officer or Legal Counsel to ensure statutory alignment.
3.  **Feedback Integration:** The Document Owner must address all SME comments. If an SME's critical advice is rejected, the Document Owner must document the rationale for the rejection.

#### 4.3 Broad Consultation (The "Buy-In" Phase)
Depending on the significance of the document, one of the following consultation tiers is applied:

**Tier 1: Operational/Technical (Internal Only)**
*   *Applicability:* Procedures affecting specific staff/volunteers (e.g., *Password Rotation Procedure*).
*   *Action:* Draft is posted to the internal "Request for Comments" (RFC) channel for 5 business days.
*   *Target:* Staff, Operational Officers, and active Volunteers.

**Tier 2: Governance/Community (Member Facing)**
*   *Applicability:* Bylaws amendments, Code of Conduct changes, Membership Policy updates.
*   *Action:*
    1.  **Notice:** In accordance with Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5, notice is sent to Voting Members regarding the proposed change.
    2.  **Town Hall/Q&A:** For high-impact changes, the Board or Executive Director may host a virtual Town Hall to explain the rationale.
    3.  **Feedback Window:** A secure feedback form is opened for a minimum of 10 days.

**Tier 3: Fundamental Actions**
*   *Applicability:* Mergers, Dissolution, Asset Sales (Bylaws Article X).
*   *Action:* Strict adherence to Bylaws Article X, Section 10.2, requiring a formal vote of the Supporting Members. Consultation is mandatory prior to the vote.

#### 4.4 Feedback Resolution and Finalization
1.  **Collation:** The Document Owner aggregates all feedback into a *Consultation Summary Report*.
2.  **Disposition:** Each unique piece of feedback is marked as:
    *   *Accepted:* Change incorporated.
    *   *Accepted with Modification:* Intent incorporated, wording adjusted.
    *   *Rejected:* Feedback noted but not applied (rationale required).
3.  **Final Polish:** The document is updated to the final draft version (e.g., v0.9) and submitted to the Approving Authority.

#### 4.5 Closing the Loop
1.  **Notification:** Once approved, the "Informed" stakeholders are notified.
2.  **Transparency:** For Tier 2 and Tier 3 consultations, a summary of how member feedback was addressed must be published on the Organization’s Transparency page, fostering trust and demonstrating that member voices were heard.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Document Owner** | Initiates the procedure, identifies stakeholders, manages the feedback cycle, and incorporates changes. |
| **Subject Matter Expert (SME)** | Provides technical accuracy review and identifies operational risks within the draft. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Reviews documents for alignment with community culture and inclusivity standards. |
| **Compliance Officer** | Reviews for regulatory alignment (IRS, Texas BOC, 2 CFR 200) and ensures the consultation process was followed. |
| **Executive Director** | Serves as the gatekeeper, ensuring no policy is presented to the Board without proper stakeholder consultation. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure may result in the resulting policy or decision being declared void or unenforceable by the Board of Directors due to lack of due process.

Employees or volunteers who repeatedly bypass stakeholder consultation requirements may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of role, for failing to adhere to the Organization's governance standards.
