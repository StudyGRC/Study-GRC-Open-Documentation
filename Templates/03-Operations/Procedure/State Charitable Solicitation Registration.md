### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a systematic process for monitoring, registering, and maintaining compliance with state charitable solicitation statutes. While Study GRC Inc. is incorporated in Texas, the remote nature of the Organization and its online fundraising activities may trigger registration requirements in up to 40 other jurisdictions. This procedure mitigates the risks of state-levied fines, "cease and desist" orders, and reputational damage associated with unauthorized fundraising.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all fundraising activities conducted by Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to:
*   **Online Solicitation:** "Donate Now" buttons, crowdfunding campaigns, and email appeals.
*   **Direct Solicitation:** Direct mail, telephone calls, and grant proposals.
*   **Events:** Fundraising tickets or auctions held physically in specific states.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not cover federal tax exemption maintenance (Form 990), which is covered under *FIN-POL-020 (Single Audit Compliance Policy)*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Solicitation** | Any direct or indirect request for a contribution of money or property on the plea that the contribution will be used for a charitable purpose. |
| **Nexus** | A sufficient physical or economic connection to a state that subjects the Organization to that state's jurisdiction and laws. |
| **Charleston Principles** | Non-binding guidelines adopted by the National Association of State Charity Officials (NASCO) suggesting that online solicitation only requires registration if the entity specifically targets persons in the state or receives substantial contributions from the state on a repeated and ongoing basis. |
| **Registered Agent** | A third-party individual or business designated to receive service of process (legal notices) in a state where the Organization is registered to do business. |
| **URS** | The Unified Registration Statement, a standardized form accepted by many (but not all) states for charitable registration. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Jurisdiction Assessment and Nexus Determination
The CFO must conduct a "Solicitation Nexus Assessment" quarterly to determine where registration is required.

1.  **Home State (Mandatory):** As a Texas Non-Profit Corporation, the Organization must maintain active registration with the Texas Secretary of State and comply with Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) requirements regarding solicitation (if applicable under Texas law for law enforcement/veteran groups, otherwise general consumer protection applies).
2.  **Physical Presence:** Registration is mandatory in any state where the Organization holds a physical fundraising event or maintains an office/employee conducting fundraising duties.
3.  **Online Solicitation (Charleston Principles Analysis):** The Organization adopts a risk-based approach to online fundraising. Registration is triggered in a specific state if:
    *   The Organization sends targeted emails or direct mail to residents of that state.
    *   The Organization receives contributions from that state exceeding the state’s statutory threshold (often $25,000, but varies by state) or receives a high volume of individual donations (e.g., 50+ donors).

#### 4.2 Donation Monitoring and Threshold Alerts
To prevent non-compliance, the Finance Department must configure the donor management system (CRM) to track donation origins.

1.  **State-by-State Reporting:** The CFO must generate a "Donation by State" report monthly.
2.  **Threshold Warnings:** The CFO must set alerts when donations from a non-registered state reach 75% of that state’s registration threshold (e.g., if a state requires registration at $25,000, an alert triggers at $18,750).
3.  **Cease Solicitation:** If a threshold is imminent and the Board has not authorized registration fees for that state, the Organization must technically cease *active* solicitation in that state (e.g., excluding that state from email blasts).

#### 4.3 Registration Filing Process
Upon determination that registration is required in a new jurisdiction:

1.  **Registered Agent Appointment:** If the state requires an in-state Registered Agent, the CFO must engage the Organization’s national registered agent service provider to add the jurisdiction.
2.  **Form Selection:**
    *   **Online Portals:** Prioritize states with online filing portals (e.g., Colorado, New York).
    *   **URS:** Use the Unified Registration Statement (URS) for states that accept it to streamline data entry.
3.  **Documentation Compilation:** Prepare the standard packet required by most states:
    *   IRS Determination Letter.
    *   Most recent IRS Form 990.
    *   Articles of Incorporation and Bylaws.
    *   List of Officers and Directors (per Bylaws Article III).
    *   Audited Financial Statements (if revenue exceeds state-specific audit thresholds).
4.  **Fee Payment:** Submit filing fees via the corporate credit card or check, allocated to the "Licenses and Permits" budget line item.

#### 4.4 Disclosure Statement Implementation
Many states require specific disclosure language to be included on all solicitation materials (website footer, donation receipts, email footers).

1.  **Master Disclosure Block:** The Webmaster must maintain a "State Nonprofit Disclosures" page on the website (linked from the footer).
2.  **Required Text:** This page must contain the specific statutory language for every state where the Organization is registered (e.g., *"Florida: A COPY OF THE OFFICIAL REGISTRATION AND FINANCIAL INFORMATION MAY BE OBTAINED FROM THE DIVISION OF CONSUMER SERVICES BY CALLING TOLL-FREE..."*).
3.  **Receipts:** The automated donation receipt system must include a link to the Disclosure page.

#### 4.5 Annual Renewals and Reporting
State registrations expire annually, often based on the fiscal year-end or the initial registration date.

1.  **Calendar Tracking:** All renewal deadlines must be entered into the Organization’s Compliance Calendar (*GOV-TOOL-001*).
2.  **Extension Filing:** If the IRS Form 990 is on extension, the CFO must file corresponding state extensions where allowed to avoid late fees.
3.  **Financial Report Submission:** Submit the previous fiscal year’s Form 990 and/or audited financials to the state charity bureaus annually as required by *Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owner of this procedure. Monitors donation thresholds, manages the Registered Agent relationship, and executes filings. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the annual budget for state registration fees. Reviews compliance status during the annual audit review. |
| **Webmaster / IT** | Ensures "Donate" pages and email templates contain legally required disclosure links and text. |
| **Fundraising/Development Team** | Consults with the CFO prior to launching targeted campaigns in new states to ensure registration is active. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to register in a state where solicitation occurs can result in:
*   Civil fines ranging from $500 to $25,000 per violation.
*   Administrative orders to return all donations collected from that state.
*   Public listing on state "Bad Actor" lists, damaging donor trust.
*   **Internal Enforcement:** Staff members who knowingly conduct targeted solicitation in unregistered jurisdictions in violation of a "Cease Solicitation" directive may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
