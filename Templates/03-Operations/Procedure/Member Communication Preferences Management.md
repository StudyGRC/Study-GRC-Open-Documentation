### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized mechanism for capturing, recording, and honoring the communication preferences of Study GRC Inc. members and community participants. This procedure ensures compliance with **GDPR Article 21 (Right to Object)**, the CAN-SPAM Act, and the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) regarding notice requirements. It balances the Organization's legal obligation to provide governance notices with the individual's right to control their personal data and limit direct marketing.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all outbound communications generated by Study GRC Inc., including email, instant messaging (Slack/Discord), and SMS.

*   **In Scope:**
    *   Marketing newsletters, event invitations, and fundraising solicitations.
    *   Community updates and educational resource announcements.
    *   Voting Member governance notices (AGM notifications, ballot access).
*   **Target Audience:**
    *   Voting Members (Supporting and Honorary).
    *   Community Participants (Non-Voting).
    *   Donors and Subscribers.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Transactional Communication** | Messages required to fulfill a contract or legal obligation, such as donation receipts, password resets, or mandatory governance notices defined in the Bylaws (e.g., Notice of Annual Meeting). |
| **Marketing Communication** | Any communication where the primary purpose is the commercial advertisement or promotion of a product, service, or request for donation. |
| **Right to Object (GDPR Art. 21)** | The right of a data subject to object, on grounds relating to their particular situation, to the processing of personal data concerning them, specifically including processing for direct marketing purposes. |
| **Preference Center** | A user-facing interface allowing individuals to granularly select which types of communications they wish to receive. |
| **Global Unsubscribe** | A request to cease *all* discretionary communications. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. Initial Collection of Preferences (Opt-In)
To ensure valid consent and data minimization:
1.  **Granular Options:** Upon registration or membership application, the Organization must present checkboxes separating "Essential/Transactional" updates from "Marketing/Community" updates.
2.  **Default State:** Marketing checkboxes must not be pre-checked (Privacy by Default), except where "Soft Opt-In" is legally permissible for existing customers/donors under specific jurisdictions (e.g., PECR), though explicit opt-in is the Organization's standard.
3.  **Bylaws Acknowledgement:** Applicants for Voting Membership must acknowledge that they cannot opt out of **Legal/Governance Notices** (as required by Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5) without resigning their voting status, as these notices are a statutory requirement for the validity of corporate actions.

#### 4.2. The Preference Center
The Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) must maintain a centralized "Preference Center" accessible via a link in the footer of all automated emails.
1.  **Accessibility:** The center must not require a login password to manage basic email preferences (tokenized access is preferred).
2.  **Categories:** Users must be able to toggle the following categories independently:
    *   *Governance & Voting* (Locked "On" for Voting Members).
    *   *Events & Webinars.*
    *   *Fundraising & Appeals.*
    *   *Weekly/Monthly Newsletters.*
    *   *Partner Offers* (if applicable).

#### 4.3. Processing the Right to Object (Opt-Out)
Pursuant to GDPR Art. 21(2) and (3), if a member objects to processing for direct marketing:
1.  **Immediate Cessation:** The Organization must cease processing the individual's data for marketing purposes immediately.
2.  **Technical Execution:**
    *   **Automated:** Clicking "Unsubscribe" in an email footer must automatically suppress the address in the sending platform within **24 hours**.
    *   **Manual Requests:** Requests received via reply email, Slack DM, or support ticket must be processed by the DKG or delegate within **48 hours**.
3.  **No Fee/No Hindrance:** The process to opt out must be free and require no more than two clicks.
4.  **Suppression List:** The email address must be moved to a "Suppression List" rather than deleted entirely, to ensure the system knows *not* to contact this individual again in the future (Compliance Record).

#### 4.4. Handling Governance Notices vs. Marketing
A critical distinction exists between "Marketing" and "Governance."
1.  **Bylaws Mandate:** Per Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5, the Organization *must* deliver notice of meetings to Voting Members.
2.  **Procedure for "Global Unsubscribe" by Voting Members:**
    *   If a Voting Member requests a "Global Unsubscribe," the DKG must manually contact the member to clarify: *"To remain a Voting Member, you must receive legal notices regarding elections and AGMs. Do you wish to resign your voting membership, or can we keep you subscribed to the 'Governance Only' list?"*
    *   If the member insists on zero contact, they must be processed as a resignation of Voting Membership, as the Organization cannot fulfill its legal duty to notify them of corporate acts.

#### 4.5. Cross-Platform Synchronization
To prevent "Zombie Emails" (where a user unsubscribes on one platform but is emailed from another):
1.  **Master Source of Truth:** The CRM (e.g., Salesforce/HubSpot) is the master record for consent.
2.  **Sync Frequency:** The CISO and IT team must ensure that the email marketing platform, the donor management system, and the CRM synchronize unsubscribe status at least **daily**.
3.  **Manual Lists:** Staff are prohibited from exporting email lists to Excel/CSV for mass emailing via personal Outlook/Gmail accounts, as this bypasses the suppression list check.

#### 4.6. Audit Trail and Record Keeping
1.  **Consent Log:** The system must record:
    *   Date/Time of consent or withdrawal.
    *   IP Address (if digital).
    *   Method (e.g., "Web Form," "Written Request").
2.  **Retention:** These records must be retained for the duration of the relationship plus 2 years, in accordance with the *Records Retention Schedule*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Process Owner. Responsible for managing the Preference Center, handling manual opt-out requests, and clarifying governance notice requirements with members. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Ensures the security of the subscriber database and validates that cross-platform synchronization is functioning to prevent privacy breaches. |
| **IT / Web Administrator** | Technical implementation of the Preference Center and API integrations between marketing platforms and the CRM. |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Strictly prohibited from emailing individuals on the Suppression List. Must forward any manual unsubscribe requests to the DKG immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure—specifically sending marketing communications to an individual who has exercised their Right to Object—constitutes a violation of GDPR and/or the CAN-SPAM Act.

*   **Internal Discipline:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Legal Risk:** Non-compliance exposes the Organization to significant regulatory fines (up to €20 million under GDPR) and reputational damage.
