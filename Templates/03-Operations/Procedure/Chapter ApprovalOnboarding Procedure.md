### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, risk-based framework for evaluating, approving, and onboarding new Local Chapters of Study GRC Inc. This process ensures that all affiliated chapters align with the Organization’s charitable mission (Bylaws Article I), adhere to strict quality control standards, and comply with legal requirements regarding intellectual property, youth protection, and financial integrity prior to commencing operations.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   All individuals or groups applying to form a Study GRC Inc. Local Chapter.
*   Operational Officers involved in the vetting and onboarding process (DKG, CISO, CFO).
*   The Board of Directors regarding final charter authorization.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to informal study groups or online-only forums that do not utilize the "Study GRC" trademark or request formal chapter status.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chapter Lead** | The primary volunteer applicant responsible for organizing the local chapter and serving as the liaison to Study GRC Inc. HQ. |
| **Affiliation Agreement** | The binding legal contract granting the Chapter the right to use the Organization’s name, logo, and 501(c)(3) status (if applicable). |
| **Provisional Status** | A probationary period (typically 90 days) where a prospective chapter operates under limited scope to demonstrate viability. |
| **DKG** | Director of Kindness and Generosity; the Operational Officer responsible for community culture and chapter oversight. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Application and Initial Screening
**4.1.1 Submission of Intent**
The prospective Chapter Lead must submit the *Chapter Charter Application Form* (OPS-FRM-012) via the official Study GRC portal. The application must include:
*   Proposed Chapter location/region.
*   Names and resumes of at least two (2) founding leaders (to satisfy "Two-Deep Leadership" requirements).
*   A statement of intent aligning with the Organization’s purpose (Bylaws § 1.3).

**4.1.2 Initial Review**
Within ten (10) business days of receipt, the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** must review the application to verify:
*   No geographic conflict with existing chapters.
*   Alignment with the Organization's open-source and zero-cost mission.
*   Completeness of the leadership team roster.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Due Diligence and Vetting
**4.2.1 Background Checks**
Pursuant to the *Background Check Policy* (HR-POL-024) and the Organization's focus on K-12 education, all proposed Chapter Leads must undergo a comprehensive background check.
*   **Action:** HR issues the *Background Check Consent Form*.
*   **Criteria:** Any history of violent crime, sexual offenses, or financial fraud results in immediate disqualification.

**4.2.2 Interview and Culture Fit**
The DKG and one other Operational Officer (e.g., VP of Education) must conduct a video interview with the applicants to assess:
*   Understanding of the *Code of Ethics and Conduct*.
*   Commitment to the "Zero-Cost" resource model.
*   Technical competence in the GRC domain.

**4.2.3 Social Media and Public Profile Review**
The DKG performs a review of the applicant's public professional profiles (e.g., LinkedIn) to ensure no conflicts of interest or reputational risks exist that could harm the Organization.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Provisional Authorization & Training
**4.3.1 Mandatory Training**
Upon passing vetting, Chapter Leads must complete the *Chapter Leader Onboarding Curriculum*, which includes:
*   **Youth Protection Training:** Mandatory for all leaders (See *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*).
*   **Brand Guidelines:** Proper use of the Study GRC logo and assets.
*   **Financial Compliance:** Rules regarding prohibition of bank accounts outside of HQ control (unless specifically authorized).

**4.3.2 Provisional Charter Issuance**
The Executive Director issues a "Provisional Charter" allowing the group to hold up to three (3) initial meetings under the Study GRC brand.
*   **Restriction:** Provisional chapters may not sign contracts, collect funds, or represent the Organization in legal matters.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Formal Chartering and Launch
**4.4.1 Execution of Affiliation Agreement**
Once the provisional period requirements are met, the **Affiliation Agreement** is prepared.
*   **Signatories:** Must be signed by the Chapter Lead and the Study GRC Inc. Executive Director.
*   **Content:** Explicitly assigns all IP created by the chapter to Study GRC Inc. (Bylaws § 9.3) and mandates adherence to the *Conflict of Interest Policy*.

**4.4.2 IT Provisioning**
Upon contract execution, the **CISO** or IT Delegate will:
*   Provision official `@studygrc.org` email addresses for Chapter leadership.
*   Create the Chapter’s space within the official community platform (e.g., Discord/Slack).
*   Grant access to the *Chapter Leader Resource Repository*.

**4.4.3 Public Announcement**
The DKG coordinates with the Communications team to announce the new Chapter on the official website and newsletter.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Post-Launch Monitoring
**4.5.1 90-Day Review**
Three months post-launch, the DKG reviews the Chapter’s activity:
*   Meeting attendance logs.
*   Adherence to the *Code of Conduct*.
*   Fiscal compliance (if applicable).

**4.5.2 Annual Renewal**
Chapter status is not permanent. It is subject to the *Chapter Annual Performance Review* (OPS-PRC-008) to ensure continued "Good Standing."

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Prospective Chapter Lead** | Submits application, consents to background checks, completes mandatory training, and signs the Affiliation Agreement. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Primary process owner; conducts interviews, reviews applications, and serves as the main point of contact for the Chapter. |
| **Human Resources (HR)** | Administers background checks and adjudicates results based on the *Background Check Policy*. |
| **CISO** | Provisions secure IT accounts and ensures Chapter Leads utilize MFA and secure communication channels. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final approving authority for the Affiliation Agreement and issuance of the Charter. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**6.1 Unauthorized Operation**
Any group operating under the name "Study GRC" or utilizing the Organization’s intellectual property without a signed Affiliation Agreement is in violation of trademark laws and the Organization's *Intellectual Property Policy*.

**6.2 Revocation**
Failure to complete the onboarding steps, failure to pass the background check, or violation of the *Provisional Status* restrictions will result in the immediate termination of the application process.
