### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a mandatory, systematic response mechanism for the immediate location and recovery of any minor or vulnerable adult reported missing during Study GRC Inc. sanctioned events. This protocol is designed to minimize the risk of abduction, injury, or trauma by ensuring rapid mobilization of staff, coordination with local law enforcement, and clear communication with guardians.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to **all** Study GRC Inc. personnel, including Board Directors, Operational Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers (collectively "Staff") operating at:
*   Physical events (conferences, workshops, meetups).
*   Hybrid events where Study GRC Inc. maintains physical custody or supervision of minors.

**Exclusions:** This procedure distinguishes between physical disappearance (immediate physical danger) and "Virtual Loss of Contact" (dropping off a video call), which is addressed in Section 4.5.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Missing Person** | Any participant (minor or vulnerable adult) who cannot be located within their designated area for a period exceeding 5 minutes, or immediately upon suspicion of abduction. |
| **Incident Commander (IC)** | The most senior Study GRC Inc. representative present at the event (usually the Event Lead or Executive Director) responsible for managing the emergency response. |
| **Code Adam** | A widely recognized safety protocol for missing children in public buildings, adapted here for organizational use. |
| **Soft Lockdown** | A security state where exits are monitored and restricted to prevent a potential abductor from leaving with a victim, while allowing emergency services entry. |
| **Guardians** | Parents, legal guardians, or authorized chaperones responsible for the minor. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Immediate Report and Verification (0–5 Minutes)
Upon receiving a report of a missing child or participant:

1.  **Obtain Description:** The Staff member receiving the report must immediately obtain a detailed description from the reporting party (Guardian), including:
    *   Name, Age, and Gender.
    *   Height, weight, hair color, and eye color.
    *   **Clothing description** (most critical visual identifier).
    *   Last known location and time.
2.  **Notify Incident Commander:** Transmit this information immediately to the Incident Commander (IC) via radio, internal chat, or in person.
3.  **Initiate "Code Search":** The IC will issue a "Code Search" alert to all Staff.
    *   *Note:* Do **not** announce the child's name over a public address system, as this may alert a potential abductor to flee or conceal the victim. Announce a code (e.g., "Staff, we have a Code Search") and distribute the description via internal secure channels.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Lockdown and Search (5–10 Minutes)
Once "Code Search" is initiated:

1.  **Secure Exits (Soft Lockdown):** Designated Staff must immediately move to all facility exits (doors, elevators, stairwells).
    *   Monitor all persons leaving the facility.
    *   Politely delay anyone matching the description or accompanying a child matching the description.
    *   **Do not** physically restrain an adult unless immediate harm to the child is visible; instead, delay them by engaging in conversation and signal for help/police.
2.  **Zone Search:** Available Staff must sweep specific zones:
    *   Restrooms and changing areas (Staff must announce themselves before entering).
    *   Storage rooms, closets, and under tables/stages.
    *   Parking lots and immediate exterior perimeters.
3.  **Guardian Isolation:** One Staff member must remain with the reporting Guardian in a quiet, secure area to provide support and gather further information (e.g., recent photos on a phone).

#### 4.3 Phase III: Law Enforcement Escalation (10+ Minutes)
If the participant is not located within 10 minutes of the initial report, or immediately if abduction is suspected:

1.  **Call 911:** The Incident Commander **must** call 911.
    *   State clearly: "We have a missing child at [Location]."
    *   Provide the physical description and last known location.
    *   Advise if the child has any medical conditions or disabilities (e.g., non-verbal autism).
2.  **Handover to Police:** Upon police arrival, the IC serves as the primary liaison.
    *   Provide a printed or digital copy of the participant's registration info (if available).
    *   Provide the list of Staff and attendees if requested.
3.  **Amber Alert Data:** Be prepared to provide information required for an Amber Alert if law enforcement determines criteria are met (abduction suspected).

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Resolution and Documentation
1.  **Recovery:**
    *   **If found by Staff:** Verify identity. Do not physically touch the child more than necessary for comfort/safety. Escort immediately to the IC and Guardian.
    *   **If found by Police:** Follow police instructions regarding reunification.
2.  **Stand Down:** The IC will issue an "All Clear" to Staff to resume normal operations.
3.  **Documentation:**
    *   The IC must complete an **Incident Report Form (OPS-FRM-009)** within 2 hours of the event conclusion.
    *   Statements must be collected from the Staff member who took the initial report and the Staff member who found the child (if applicable).

#### 4.5 Virtual Loss of Contact (Remote Events)
In a remote-first context, if a minor participating in a supervised virtual session drops offline unexpectedly:

1.  **Wait and Verify (5 Minutes):** Allow time for technical reconnection.
2.  **Contact Guardian:** Staff must attempt to contact the parent/guardian via the phone number on file.
3.  **Emergency Contact:** If the Guardian is unreachable, attempt the secondary emergency contact.
4.  **Welfare Check:** If contact cannot be established within 30 minutes and there was a preceding sign of distress or suspicious behavior on camera:
    *   The VP of Education or Executive Director may authorize a call to non-emergency police dispatch in the child's jurisdiction to request a Welfare Check.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Incident Commander (IC)** | Acts as the central point of contact; authorizes the 911 call; manages the Soft Lockdown; liaises with Law Enforcement. |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Responsible for knowing the "Code Search" signal; monitoring assigned zones/exits; refraining from spreading rumors or posting on social media. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Primary support liaison for the distressed family/Guardian during the search; shields the family from media or bystander interference. |
| **Executive Director** | Responsible for post-incident legal review and media statements (if the incident becomes public). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Mandatory Reporting:** Failure to report a missing child immediately upon suspicion is a violation of this procedure and potentially Texas Family Code.
*   **Non-Interference:** Staff must not interfere with Law Enforcement operations once officers arrive on scene.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Failure to comply with this protocol may result in immediate termination of employment or volunteer service.
*   **Indemnification:** Staff acting in good faith and in accordance with this procedure are indemnified against liability to the fullest extent permitted by **Bylaws Article VII**.
