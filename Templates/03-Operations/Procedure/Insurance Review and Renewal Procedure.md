### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, rigorous timeline and methodology for the annual review, renewal, and procurement of insurance coverage for Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures the Organization maintains adequate protection against liability, property loss, and operational risks in alignment with its remote-first structure, youth-focused programming, and federal grant obligations. Furthermore, this procedure supports the indemnification provisions outlined in **Article VII** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all commercial insurance policies held by the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   Directors & Officers (D&O) Liability;
*   General Liability (GL) and Sexual Abuse/Molestation Coverage;
*   Cyber Liability and Data Breach Response;
*   Professional Liability (Errors & Omissions);
*   Employment Practices Liability (EPLI); and
*   Worker’s Compensation (if applicable).

This procedure applies to the Board of Directors, the Treasurer, the Chief Financial Officer (CFO), and the Executive Director.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Broker of Record** | The licensed insurance professional or agency designated by the Organization to negotiate with insurance carriers on its behalf. |
| **Claims-Made Policy** | A policy that covers claims made during the policy period, regardless of when the wrongful act occurred (common in D&O and Cyber). |
| **Tail Coverage** | An endorsement extending the reporting period for a claims-made policy after it has been cancelled or non-renewed. |
| **D&O** | Directors and Officers Liability Insurance, protecting personal assets of board members and officers against suits alleging wrongful acts in managing the organization. |
| **COI** | Certificate of Insurance; a document used to verify the existence of insurance coverage to third parties (e.g., grantors, venues). |
| **Exclusion** | Specific conditions or circumstances listed in the policy for which the insurer will not provide coverage. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Annual Review Timeline
The insurance renewal process must commence no later than **90 days** prior to the expiration of current policies.

1.  **T-Minus 90 Days:** CFO initiates the "Risk Exposure Update" (See Section 4.2).
2.  **T-Minus 60 Days:** Submission of updated applications to the Broker of Record.
3.  **T-Minus 30 Days:** Receipt and review of binding quotes from carriers.
4.  **T-Minus 15 Days:** Board or Executive Committee approval of premiums and coverage levels.
5.  **T-Minus 5 Days:** Payment of premiums and issuance of binders.

#### 4.2 Step 1: Risk Exposure Update (Internal Assessment)
Before soliciting quotes, the CFO must compile an updated risk profile to ensure coverage matches current operations.
1.  **Programmatic Review:** Identify new programs, specifically those involving minors (K-12), to ensure compliance with Sexual Abuse/Molestation coverage limits.
2.  **Financial Review:** Update total assets, annual revenue, and grant funding levels (specifically 2 CFR 200 requirements).
3.  **Data & Cyber Review:** Consult with the CISO to update record counts (PII), data storage locations, and remote work infrastructure changes for Cyber Liability applications.
4.  **Geographic Review:** Confirm locations of all remote employees for Workers' Compensation compliance in respective states.

#### 4.3 Step 2: Broker Engagement and Application
1.  The CFO must provide the Risk Exposure Update to the Broker of Record.
2.  The CFO must complete all carrier applications.
    *   *Note:* All answers regarding internal controls (e.g., dual signatures, wire transfer verification) must be verified against current **Financial Policies and Procedures**.
3.  If the current Broker has underperformed, the CFO may issue a Broker of Record Letter to a new agency, provided this is done at least 60 days before renewal.

#### 4.4 Step 3: Coverage Analysis and Quote Review
Upon receipt of quotes, the CFO and Treasurer must analyze the proposals against the following criteria:

**4.4.1 Directors & Officers (D&O) Review**
*   **Bylaws Alignment:** Ensure coverage limits are sufficient to support the indemnification promise in **Article VII, Section 7.1** of the Bylaws.
*   **Side A/B/C Coverage:** Verify the policy covers non-indemnifiable losses (Side A), indemnifiable losses (Side B), and entity securities coverage (Side C).
*   **Exclusions:** Scrutinize "Insured vs. Insured" and "Conduct" exclusions.

**4.4.2 General Liability & Abuse Review**
*   **Youth Protection:** Confirm that Sexual Abuse and Molestation coverage is defined explicitly and not subject to a sub-limit lower than required by partner MOUs or grant agreements.
*   **Event Coverage:** Ensure virtual and physical events are covered.

**4.4.3 Cyber Liability Review**
*   **Ransomware:** Verify sub-limits for ransomware payments and extortion.
*   **Social Engineering:** Ensure coverage exists for phishing-induced wire fraud (often a separate insuring agreement).

#### 4.5 Step 4: Approval and Binding
1.  **Threshold for Board Vote:** If the total premium increase exceeds 10% of the previous year, or if coverage limits are reduced, the renewal package must be presented to the Board of Directors for a vote.
2.  **Routine Renewal:** If premiums and coverage are consistent (within 10% variance), the Treasurer and Executive Director may authorize binding.
3.  **Binding:** The CFO must sign the "Subjectivities" and "Bind Orders" and process payment via the Accounts Payable workflow.

#### 4.6 Step 5: Post-Renewal Administration
1.  **COI Distribution:** The CFO must generate and distribute new Certificates of Insurance (COI) to all active grantors, landlords, and partners requiring proof of insurance within 5 business days of renewal.
2.  **Policy Archival:** Electronic copies of the full policies (not just declaration pages) must be stored in the secure Legal/Finance repository.
3.  **Tail Coverage Assessment:** If a claims-made policy is moved to a new carrier, the CFO must purchase "Tail Coverage" (Extended Reporting Period) for the expiring policy to cover past acts, unless "Prior Acts" coverage is secured retroactively on the new policy.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Process owner. Manages broker relationship, completes applications, and ensures premiums are paid on time. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides oversight. Reviews quotes for fiscal feasibility and alignment with risk tolerance. Authorizes binding within delegated limits. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves significant changes in coverage or premium. Fiduciaries responsible for ensuring D&O coverage is adequate. |
| **CISO** | Provides technical data for Cyber Liability applications and verifies that security controls listed in the application are actually in place. |
| **Executive Director** | Signs legal applications and binds coverage. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this procedure, particularly regarding the timely renewal of D&O and Cyber Liability policies, constitutes a severe breach of fiduciary duty.
*   **Lapse in Coverage:** Any lapse in D&O coverage exposes the personal assets of the Board of Directors. An employee responsible for a lapse due to negligence may face immediate termination.
*   **Misrepresentation:** Providing false information on an insurance application (e.g., claiming MFA is enforced when it is not) allows the carrier to deny claims. This is grounds for immediate termination and potential legal action.
