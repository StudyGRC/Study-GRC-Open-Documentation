### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for verifying insurance coverage prior to any Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") sanctioned event. This procedure ensures that the Organization mitigates financial and legal liability associated with bodily injury, property damage, cyber risks, and professional errors. It operationalizes the risk management intent behind **Article VII (Indemnification)** of the Bylaws by ensuring financial backing exists for potential claims.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **All Events:** Physical, hybrid, and fully virtual events hosted, co-hosted, or sanctioned by Study GRC Inc.
*   **All Personnel:** Employees, Board Directors, Officers, and Volunteers (including Local Chapter Leads) involved in event planning.
*   **Third Parties:** Venues, vendors, contractors, and speakers engaging with the Organization for events.

**Exclusions:**
*   Informal, non-sanctioned gatherings of members that are not funded, promoted, or branded by Study GRC Inc.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **COI (Certificate of Insurance)** | A document issued by an insurance company/broker that verifies the existence of an insurance policy and summarizes the coverage aspects and limits. |
| **Additional Insured** | A status on a liability insurance policy that provides coverage to other individuals/groups (Study GRC Inc.) that were not originally named. |
| **General Liability (GL)** | Insurance coverage that protects against claims of bodily injury and property damage. |
| **Endorsement** | An amendment to an insurance policy that changes the terms or scope of the original policy (e.g., adding an Additional Insured). |
| **Sanctioned Event** | Any event approved by the Board or Operational Leadership that utilizes the Organization’s brand, funds, or platforms. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Event Risk Classification
Prior to soliciting insurance documents, the Event Lead must classify the event risk level to determine coverage requirements.

1.  **Low Risk:** Virtual webinars, online workshops (No physical presence).
    *   *Requirement:* Cyber Liability / Media Liability (Internal Policy).
2.  **Medium Risk:** Meetups in rented rooms, classroom training, dinners (No alcohol, no minors).
    *   *Requirement:* General Liability (Venue & Org), Hired/Non-Owned Auto (if driving required).
3.  **High Risk:** Large conferences, events with alcohol service, events with physical activity.
    *   *Requirement:* GL, Liquor Liability (Host or Vendor), Umbrella/Excess Liability.
4.  **Special Risk (Youth):** Any event where minors (K-12) are present.
    *   *Requirement:* Sexual Abuse and Molestation (SAM) Coverage is **mandatory**.

#### 4.2 Internal Coverage Verification
Before contracting with external venues, the Event Lead must verify the Organization's own coverage.

1.  **Check Master Policy:** Confirm the Organization’s current General Liability policy is active and covers the specific type of event proposed.
2.  **Geographic Scope:** For Local Chapters, confirm the Master Policy extends to the specific state/location of the chapter event.
3.  **Exclusions Check:** Review policy exclusions (e.g., specific exclusion for fireworks, athletic events, or communicable diseases) to ensure the event activity is not excluded.

#### 4.3 Third-Party Vendor/Venue Verification
For physical events, the Organization must obtain proof of insurance from all vendors (venues, caterers, A/V providers).

1.  **Request COI:** The Event Lead must request a Certificate of Insurance (COI) from the vendor *before* signing any contract.
2.  **Minimum Limits:** Verify the COI meets the following minimums (unless a waiver is approved by the CFO):
    *   **Commercial General Liability:** $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate.
    *   **Workers' Compensation:** Statutory Limits.
    *   **Liquor Liability (if serving):** $1,000,000 per occurrence.
    *   **Sexual Abuse/Molestation (if Youth involved):** $1,000,000 per occurrence.
3.  **Additional Insured Status:** The COI must explicitly name **"Study GRC Inc., its directors, officers, employees, and volunteers"** as Additional Insureds.
4.  **Waiver of Subrogation:** The vendor's insurer must agree to waive their right to recover payments from Study GRC Inc.

#### 4.4 Review and Approval Workflow
1.  **Submission:** Event Lead uploads the draft contract and the Vendor’s COI to the Central Document Repository (Folder: `Legal/Contracts/[Year]/[EventName]`).
2.  **Compliance Review:** The CFO or designated Risk Manager reviews the COI against the requirements in Section 4.3.
    *   *Pass:* COI is current, limits are sufficient, endorsement is present.
    *   *Fail:* COI is expired, limits are too low, or "Study GRC Inc." is not named.
3.  **Remediation:** If the review fails, the Event Lead must contact the vendor to request a corrected COI. **No payments may be issued until a valid COI is on file.**

#### 4.5 Special Protocol: Youth Participation
Pursuant to the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**, strictly rigorous verification is required for events involving minors.

1.  **Zero Tolerance:** No vendor may be used for a youth event if their policy contains a "Sexual Abuse and Molestation Exclusion."
2.  **Background Check Alignment:** Confirm that the vendor's insurance requires them to perform background checks on their staff. If their insurance does not require it, Study GRC Inc. must contractually mandate it.

#### 4.6 Document Retention
1.  **Storage:** Validated COIs must be retained for a minimum of **five (5) years** past the event date, in accordance with the **Records Retention Schedule**.
2.  **Audit Trail:** The approval email or digital signature verifying the COI must be saved with the document.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Lead / Chapter Lead** | Primary responsible party for requesting COIs from vendors and uploading them for review. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Final authority on insurance sufficiency. Approves waivers for lower limits if risk is deemed acceptable. |
| **General Counsel (External)** | Consulted for complex high-risk events or when interpreting ambiguous policy exclusions. |
| **Executive Director** | Signs contracts only after the CFO has confirmed insurance verification is complete. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Strict Enforcement:** The Executive Director and Treasurer are prohibited from releasing funds for venue deposits or vendor payments until the Insurance Verification process is complete.

Failure to comply with this procedure puts the Organization's 501(c)(3) assets at risk. Employees or volunteers who authorize events without proper insurance verification may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from volunteer leadership positions.
