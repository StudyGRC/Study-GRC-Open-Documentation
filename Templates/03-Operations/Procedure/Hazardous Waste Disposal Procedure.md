### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory protocols for the identification, handling, storage, and disposal of hazardous waste generated by Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). This procedure ensures compliance with the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA), Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ) regulations, and applicable local laws. It aims to prevent environmental contamination, protect the health of staff and volunteers, and mitigate legal and financial liability associated with improper waste management.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees, volunteers, contractors, and officers of the Organization.

*   **Inclusions:**
    *   **Physical Events:** Waste generated at conferences, workshops, or training sessions hosted by the Organization.
    *   **Remote Operations:** Disposal of Organization-owned assets (e.g., laptops, batteries) by remote staff.
    *   **Storage Facilities:** Any physical storage units or offices maintained by the Organization.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   General municipal solid waste (trash) and recycling (paper/plastic) that is not contaminated with hazardous materials.
    *   Household hazardous waste generated by remote employees that is unrelated to Organization business or assets.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hazardous Waste** | Waste with properties that make it dangerous or capable of having a harmful effect on human health or the environment. This includes waste that is Ignitable, Corrosive, Reactive, or Toxic (EPA "Characteristic" waste) or specifically listed by the EPA. |
| **Universal Waste** | A specific category of hazardous waste that includes batteries, pesticides, mercury-containing equipment (e.g., thermostats), and lamps (e.g., fluorescent bulbs). |
| **E-Waste** | Electronic products that are unwanted, not working, and nearing or at the end of their "useful life" (e.g., computers, monitors, printers). |
| **VSQG** | **Very Small Quantity Generator**. A generator status for facilities generating less than 100 kg of hazardous waste per month. Study GRC Inc. operates primarily as a VSQG. |
| **SDS** | **Safety Data Sheet**. A document provided by chemical manufacturers containing information on physical data, toxicity, health effects, first aid, storage, and disposal. |
| **Manifest** | A shipping document (EPA Form 8700-22) that travels with hazardous waste from the point of generation, through transportation, to the final treatment, storage, and disposal facility (TSDF). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1. Waste Identification and Determination
Before disposal, the generator (employee or volunteer) must determine if the waste is hazardous.

1.  **Check the Label:** Review the original product label for keywords such as "Danger," "Warning," "Toxic," "Flammable," or "Corrosive."
2.  **Review the SDS:** Consult Section 13 (Disposal Considerations) of the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for the specific material.
3.  **Assess "Universal" Status:** Determine if the item is a battery (Lithium-Ion, Ni-Cd), fluorescent lamp, or electronic device.
4.  **Unknown Substances:** If a substance is unknown or unlabeled, it must be treated as hazardous waste until testing confirms otherwise. Do not guess. Contact the Director of Operations immediately.

#### 4.2. Handling and Storage (On-Site Events & Storage)
For physical locations managed by the Organization (e.g., event venues, storage lockers):

1.  **Container Compatibility:** Waste must be placed in containers compatible with the material (e.g., no corrosives in metal drums).
2.  **Closure:** Containers must remain closed and latched/sealed at all times except when adding or removing waste.
3.  **Labeling:**
    *   Every container must be clearly labeled with the words **"HAZARDOUS WASTE"**.
    *   The label must include the specific contents (e.g., "Used Solvents," "Broken Fluorescent Lamps").
    *   The label must include the **Accumulation Start Date** (the date the first drop of waste was placed in the container).
4.  **Secondary Containment:** Liquid hazardous waste must be stored in secondary containment (e.g., spill pallets) capable of holding 110% of the volume of the largest container.
5.  **Segregation:** Incompatible wastes (e.g., acids and bases) must be stored physically separate from one another to prevent reactions.

#### 4.3. Universal Waste and E-Waste (Remote & IT Assets)
Given the Organization's remote-first nature, the most common hazardous waste streams are Lithium-Ion batteries and IT equipment.

1.  **Prohibition:** Under no circumstances may Organization-owned electronic equipment, batteries, or monitors be disposed of in general trash or municipal landfills.
2.  **Remote Employee Disposal Protocol:**
    *   **Return to HQ:** Employees should utilize the Organization's asset return process (shipping label provided by IT) to return end-of-life hardware to the central inventory for consolidated disposal.
    *   **Local Authorized Disposal:** With prior written approval from the CISO or Director of Operations, a remote employee may utilize a certified local e-steward or R2-certified recycler. The employee must obtain a receipt or certificate of destruction/recycling and submit it via the expense reimbursement system.
3.  **Damaged Batteries:** Swollen or damaged Lithium-Ion batteries must **not** be shipped via standard air mail.
    *   The employee must contact the Director of Operations for specific packaging instructions (e.g., sand/vermiculite packing) and ground-transport shipping labels.

#### 4.4. Disposal and Transportation
1.  **Authorized Transporters:** Hazardous waste must only be transported by a licensed hazardous waste transporter with a valid EPA ID number.
2.  **Disposal Facilities:** Waste must be sent to a permitted Treatment, Storage, and Disposal Facility (TSDF).
3.  **Drain Disposal Prohibition:** No chemical, solvent, or hazardous substance may be poured down sinks, toilets, or storm drains.
4.  **Manifesting:**
    *   For any shipment of hazardous waste, a Uniform Hazardous Waste Manifest must be prepared.
    *   The Organization must retain the "Generator Copy" signed by the transporter immediately upon shipment.
    *   The "Designated Facility to Generator" copy (signed by the receiving facility) must be received within 45 days.

#### 4.5. Emergency Response (Spills)
In the event of a spill of hazardous material at an event or facility:

1.  **Evacuate:** If the spill poses a respiratory or fire threat, evacuate the immediate area.
2.  **Contain:** If safe to do so, stop the source of the spill and use spill kit materials (absorbent pads/snakes) to prevent the spread to drains or soil.
3.  **Notify:** Contact the Event Manager and Director of Operations immediately.
4.  **Clean-up:** Only trained personnel may clean up hazardous spills. For significant spills, engage a professional environmental remediation contractor.

#### 4.6. Record Keeping
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** and EPA regulations:

1.  **Retention Period:** All waste determination records, manifests, bills of lading, and certificates of recycling/destruction must be retained for a minimum of **three (3) years** (five years recommended for best practice).
2.  **Storage:** Records shall be digitized and stored in the Organization’s secure central document repository (e.g., `EHS/Compliance/Waste_Manifests`).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensure adequate budget and resources are available for proper waste disposal. Signatory authority for environmental permits if required. |
| **Director of Operations** | Oversee the hazardous waste program. Approve local disposal requests. Maintain waste manifests and records. Act as the primary point of contact for regulatory agencies (EPA/TCEQ). |
| **Event Managers** | Ensure on-site compliance at physical events. Verify venue waste disposal capabilities. Manage immediate spill response at events. |
| **CISO / IT Dept** | Manage the lifecycle of IT assets. Ensure all e-waste is wiped of data (per IS-POL-XXX) prior to physical disposal/recycling. |
| **All Employees/Volunteers** | Properly label waste. Never dispose of batteries or electronics in the trash. Report spills or unidentified containers immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this procedure may result in:
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Legal Penalties:** Civil and criminal penalties imposed by the EPA or TCEQ, including fines up to $70,000+ per day per violation.
*   **Civil Liability:** Personal liability for Directors or Officers in cases of gross negligence regarding environmental hazards.
