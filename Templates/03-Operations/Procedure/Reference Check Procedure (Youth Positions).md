### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a rigorous, standardized process for verifying the professional history and behavioral suitability of individuals applying for **Youth-Serving Positions** at Study GRC Inc.

While criminal background checks identify documented legal offenses, they do not capture unreported behavioral red flags, boundary violations, or "grooming" behaviors. This procedure serves as a critical layer of **Due Diligence** to prevent negligent hiring and ensures the Organization fulfills its moral and legal obligation to protect minors, consistent with the safety mandates in **Article V** of the Bylaws and the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to **all** candidates seeking roles that involve direct contact with minors (children under 18), whether in-person or virtual.

*   **Applies to:**
    *   Employees (Full-time, Part-time, Temporary).
    *   Independent Contractors.
    *   Volunteers (including Board Members and Advisory Board members if they participate in youth programs).
    *   Interns.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Vendors with no direct access to minors (e.g., IT backend support with no community interaction).
    *   One-time guest speakers who are never left unsupervised with minors (subject to *Two-Deep Leadership Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Youth-Serving Position** | Any role that involves supervision, instruction, communication, or unsupervised access (physical or digital) to minors. |
| **Professional Reference** | A former supervisor, manager, teacher, or organizational leader who has directly observed the candidate's work or volunteer service. |
| **Ineligible Reference** | Family members, partners, roommates, or peers/friends without a supervisory relationship to the candidate. |
| **Boundary Violation** | Behaviors that infringe on the physical, emotional, or psychological safety of a child, even if not explicitly illegal (e.g., favoritism, secret communications, excessive gift-giving). |
| **Digital Grooming** | The process of building an emotional connection with a child online to lower their inhibitions for the purpose of sexual abuse or exploitation. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Candidate Authorization
1.  Prior to initiating any reference checks, the Hiring Manager must ensure the candidate has signed the **Employment/Volunteer Application**, which includes a release authorizing Study GRC Inc. to contact past employers and references.
2.  Candidates must provide a minimum of **three (3)** references.
    *   At least **two (2)** must be professional/supervisory.
    *   At least **one (1)** should be from a context where the candidate worked with children (if applicable based on resume).

#### 4.2 Timing of Checks
1.  Reference checks must be completed **after** a successful initial interview but **before** a final offer of employment or volunteer placement is confirmed.
2.  No candidate may begin work or have access to Study GRC Inc. platforms (Slack, Discord, LMS) until this procedure is closed.

#### 4.3 Conducting the Reference Check
Reference checks must be conducted via telephone or video call whenever possible to gauge tone and hesitation. Email questionnaires are a secondary option only if a call is refused.

**The Hiring Manager must follow this protocol:**

1.  **Verify Identity:** Confirm who you are speaking with and their relationship to the candidate.
2.  **Verify Employment:** Confirm dates of service and role title.
3.  **Mandatory Safety Questions:** The following questions **must** be asked for all Youth-Serving Positions:
    *   *"Is the candidate eligible for re-hire?"*
    *   *"Did the candidate ever face disciplinary action regarding their conduct with children, clients, or colleagues?"*
    *   *"To your knowledge, was the candidate ever investigated for sexual misconduct, harassment, or boundary violations?"*
    *   *"Did you ever observe the candidate engaging in behavior with minors that made you or others uncomfortable (e.g., being alone with a child, secretive communication)?"*
    *   *"Would you have any reservations about this person supervising a child alone?"*

#### 4.4 Specific Inquiry: Remote/Digital Conduct
Given Study GRC Inc.'s remote-first nature, the Hiring Manager must ask:
*   *"Did the candidate have access to sensitive data or direct digital communication channels with clients/students? If so, were there any concerns regarding their adherence to communication policies?"*

#### 4.5 Documentation
1.  The Hiring Manager must document the conversation using the **Reference Check Form (HR-FRM-025)**.
2.  Notes must be factual and objective (e.g., write *"Reference paused for 10 seconds before answering no,"* rather than *"Reference seemed suspicious"*).
3.  Completed forms must be uploaded to the candidate's secure personnel file.

#### 4.6 Handling Red Flags and Adverse Information
If a reference provides negative information regarding safety, boundaries, or integrity:
1.  **Stop:** Do not proceed with an offer.
2.  **Escalate:** Immediately notify the Director of Human Resources and the Executive Director.
3.  **Review:** The Executive Director will determine if the information disqualifies the candidate.
    *   *Note:* Any report of past sexual misconduct or abuse is an automatic disqualification.
4.  **Confidentiality:** Do not discuss the specific details with the candidate unless authorized by Legal Counsel.

#### 4.7 Inability to Contact References
If references do not respond after three (3) attempts over five (5) business days:
1.  Notify the candidate.
2.  Request alternative professional references.
3.  If a candidate cannot provide responsive professional references, they are ineligible for a Youth-Serving Position.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager / Volunteer Coordinator** | Executes this procedure; conducts calls; documents responses; ensures no "off-the-record" checks are used as primary validation. |
| **Candidate** | Provides accurate contact information for eligible references; signs release forms. |
| **Director of HR** | Reviews completed Reference Check Forms for compliance; advises on "Red Flag" situations. |
| **Executive Director** | Final authority on hiring decisions involving adverse reference information; ensures alignment with Bylaws Article V (Operational Leadership). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Zero Tolerance:** Falsification of reference information by a candidate (e.g., providing a friend's number disguised as a supervisor) will result in immediate rejection of the application or termination of employment/service if discovered later.

Failure by a Hiring Manager to conduct and document these checks for a Youth-Serving Position constitutes a violation of the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy** and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
