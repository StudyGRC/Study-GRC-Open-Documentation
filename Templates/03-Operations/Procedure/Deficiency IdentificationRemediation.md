### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized framework for identifying, classifying, documenting, and remediating control deficiencies within Study GRC Inc. This procedure ensures that issues identified through internal audits, external financial audits, regulatory reviews, or self-assessments are addressed promptly and effectively to mitigate risk. This document specifically satisfies the requirements for **Audit Response** and **Corrective Action** as mandated by **2 CFR § 200.511** for federal award compliance.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all operational, financial, and technical departments within Study GRC Inc. It covers all deficiencies identified regarding:
*   Internal controls over financial reporting (ICFR).
*   Compliance with federal statutes, regulations, and the terms and conditions of federal awards.
*   Information security and data privacy protocols.
*   Operational inefficiencies or policy violations.

**Exclusions:** Minor administrative errors that do not represent a systemic failure or control gap are handled via standard management supervision, though they may be logged for trend analysis.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Deficiency** | A design or operation flaw in a control that does not allow management or employees to prevent, or detect and correct, misstatements or non-compliance on a timely basis. |
| **Material Weakness** | A deficiency, or combination of deficiencies, such that there is a reasonable possibility that a material misstatement of the entity's financial statements or material non-compliance will not be prevented, or detected and corrected, on a timely basis. |
| **Significant Deficiency** | A deficiency that is less severe than a material weakness but important enough to merit attention by those charged with governance. |
| **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** | A formal document detailing the specific steps, responsible parties, and timelines required to resolve a finding, as required by 2 CFR § 200.511(c). |
| **Process Owner** | The individual with direct operational authority over the area where the deficiency was identified. |
| **Remediation** | The act of correcting a deficiency and verifying that the correction is sustainable. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Identification and Classification
Upon the discovery of a potential control gap or non-compliance issue:

1.  **Source Documentation:** The identifier (Auditor, Staff, or External Agency) must document the observation in the **Audit Finding Tracking Log** (`INT-REG-001`).
2.  **Initial Validation:** The CFO or designated Compliance Officer must validate the finding with the Process Owner to ensure factual accuracy.
3.  **Classification:** The Audit and Risk Committee (or their designee) must classify the deficiency based on severity:
    *   **Level 1 (Critical/Material Weakness):** Immediate threat to license, tax status, or financial integrity. Requires Board notification within 48 hours.
    *   **Level 2 (High/Significant Deficiency):** High risk of fraud or error. Requires Audit Committee oversight.
    *   **Level 3 (Moderate/Control Deficiency):** Operational gap. Managed by Department Head.
    *   **Level 4 (Low/Observation):** Best practice improvement. Discretionary implementation.

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Root Cause Analysis
Before a solution is proposed, the Process Owner must perform a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) to prevent recurrence.

1.  **Analysis Method:** Use the "5 Whys" or "Fishbone Diagram" methodology to determine if the failure was due to:
    *   **Design Failure:** The policy/procedure was missing or inadequate.
    *   **Operating Failure:** The policy existed but was not followed (training issue, negligence, or resource constraint).
    *   **IT/System Failure:** Technology controls failed or were bypassed.
2.  **Documentation:** The RCA must be documented within the **Corrective Action Plan (CAP) Template** (`INT-FRM-005`).

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Corrective Action Plan (CAP) Development
Pursuant to **2 CFR § 200.511(c)**, a CAP must be developed for every audit finding.

1.  **Drafting:** The Process Owner must draft the CAP within **ten (10) business days** of the final finding report.
2.  **Required Elements:** The CAP must include:
    *   Name(s) of the contact person responsible for corrective action.
    *   Specific steps to be taken to remediate the deficiency.
    *   Anticipated completion date.
    *   If the auditee does not agree with the audit findings or believes corrective action is not required, the CAP must include an explanation and specific reasons.
3.  **Review:**
    *   **Level 3 & 4:** Reviewed by the Executive Director.
    *   **Level 1 & 2:** Reviewed and approved by the Audit and Risk Committee.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Implementation and Interim Monitoring
1.  **Execution:** The Process Owner executes the steps defined in the CAP.
2.  **Evidence Collection:** As Study GRC is a remote-first organization, all evidence of remediation (screenshots, updated policy documents, training logs, system configuration exports) must be uploaded to the secure **Central Document Repository** under the specific Audit Case ID.
3.  **Status Reporting:**
    *   Open CAPs are reviewed monthly by the Executive Director.
    *   Summary reports of Open CAPs are presented quarterly to the Board of Directors in accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Validation and Closure
A deficiency cannot be marked "Closed" simply because the Process Owner says it is done.

1.  **Retesting:** The Internal Audit function or an independent manager must perform a "Test of Design" and "Test of Operating Effectiveness" on the new control.
    *   *Note:* For Material Weaknesses, retesting must occur over a sufficient period (minimum 30 days of operation) to prove sustainability.
2.  **Closure Authority:**
    *   **Level 3 & 4:** May be closed by the CFO.
    *   **Level 1 & 2:** Must be closed by vote of the Audit and Risk Committee.
3.  **Extension Requests:** If a deadline cannot be met, a formal **Extension Request Form** must be submitted to the Approving Authority *prior* to the due date, detailing the reason for delay and the interim mitigating controls in place.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit and Risk Committee** | Provides oversight of the remediation process; approves CAPs for Material Weaknesses; reviews quarterly status reports. (Per **Bylaws Article VI**) |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Accountable for the overall control environment; ensures resources are available for remediation. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns the Deficiency Remediation process; maintains the Audit Finding Tracking Log; validates closure evidence. |
| **Process Owner** | Performs Root Cause Analysis; drafts the CAP; implements changes; provides evidence of cure. |
| **Internal Audit / Compliance** | Identifies deficiencies; performs independent retesting/validation of remediation. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to remediate identified deficiencies—particularly those related to federal grant compliance or data security—places the Organization at significant legal and financial risk.

*   **Federal Grants:** Failure to implement a CAP as required by **2 CFR § 200.511** may result in the withholding of cash payments, disallowance of costs, suspension, or termination of the federal award.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Process Owners who fail to execute approved CAPs within the agreed timeline without valid justification may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, in accordance with the **Progressive Discipline Policy**.
