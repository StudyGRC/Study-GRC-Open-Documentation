### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized process for reviewing, updating, and publishing the **Study GRC Inc.** (the "Organization") external Privacy Policy. This procedure ensures that the Organization maintains transparency with its members and users regarding data processing activities, in strict compliance with **GDPR Article 13**, the **Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA)**, and **COPPA**. It defines how changes are identified, drafted, approved, and communicated to data subjects to ensure ongoing informed consent and regulatory compliance.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to the Organization’s external-facing Privacy Policy and any supplemental privacy notices (e.g., for specific events or youth programs). It applies to all Operational Officers, the Board of Directors, and third-party vendors who assist in website management or data processing.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not cover the update process for internal *Employee* privacy notices, which are governed by HR-PRC-012.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Material Change** | A revision that fundamentally alters how personal data is collected, used, shared, or retained (e.g., adding a new category of data collection, sharing data with a new third-party vendor, or changing the legal basis for processing). |
| **Non-Material Change** | Minor revisions that do not impact user rights or data processing practices, such as correcting typographical errors, updating contact information, or clarifying existing language without changing meaning. |
| **Data Subject** | The individual (member, donor, website visitor, or student) whose personal data is being processed. |
| **GDPR Art. 13** | Requirement to provide specific information to data subjects at the time personal data is collected. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Triggering a Review
A review of the Privacy Policy must be initiated under any of the following conditions:
1.  **Annual Scheduled Review:** The CISO must initiate a review every 12 months.
2.  **Operational Change:** Prior to the deployment of any new technology, vendor, or program that alters data collection practices (e.g., implementing a new Learning Management System).
3.  **Regulatory Change:** Upon the enactment of new state (Texas) or federal/international privacy laws.
4.  **Incident Response:** Following a data breach or security incident, if a policy gap is identified as a root cause.

#### 4.2 Impact Assessment and Drafting
Upon triggering a review, the Document Owner must:
1.  **Conduct a Gap Analysis:** Compare current data processing practices against the existing Privacy Policy.
2.  **Determine Materiality:** Classify the necessary changes as "Material" or "Non-Material."
3.  **Draft Revisions:**
    *   Ensure the draft explicitly states the identity of the Data Controller (Study GRC Inc.).
    *   Ensure the purposes of processing and legal basis are clearly defined (GDPR Art. 13(1)(c)).
    *   If youth data is involved, ensure language meets **COPPA** readability standards (clear, plain English).
    *   Update the "Effective Date" to the proposed publication date.

#### 4.3 Review and Approval
1.  **Stakeholder Review:** The draft must be circulated to the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** (for community impact assessment) and the **VP of Education** (if student data is involved).
2.  **Legal/Compliance Review:** The CISO or designated legal counsel must verify compliance with GDPR, TDPSA, and Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection).
3.  **Final Approval:**
    *   **Non-Material Changes:** May be approved by the CISO.
    *   **Material Changes:** Must be approved by the **Executive Director**.
    *   **Fundamental Changes:** If the change impacts the Organization's core tax-exempt purpose or involves selling data (prohibited by Bylaws), it must be referred to the **Board of Directors** for review.

#### 4.4 Notification and Communication (GDPR Art. 13 Compliance)
The method of notification depends on the classification of the change:

**4.4.1 Non-Material Changes**
1.  Update the "Last Updated" date on the website.
2.  No active notification to users is required.

**4.4.2 Material Changes**
1.  **Active Notification:** Users must be notified *before* the changes take effect. This must be done via:
    *   **Email Blast:** Sent to all active Members and Community Participants (using the email address defined in Bylaws Section 2.6).
    *   **Website Banner:** A prominent notice on the landing page and login screen.
2.  **Re-Consent (If Applicable):** If the change involves a new processing activity relying on *Consent* (e.g., sharing data with sponsors), the Organization must implement a mechanism to capture affirmative consent (opt-in) again.

#### 4.5 Publication and Archiving
1.  **Web Publication:** The Webmaster shall replace the old policy text with the approved version.
2.  **Version Control:** The superseded version must be saved in the Organization’s secure document repository as a PDF, named `Privacy_Policy_v[X.X]_[YYYYMMDD].pdf`.
3.  **Retention:** Superseded policies must be retained indefinitely to handle potential legal claims regarding past processing activities, in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Owns the Privacy Policy; monitors regulatory changes; drafts updates; determines materiality of changes. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Provides final approval for Material Changes; ensures alignment with the Organization's mission. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Reviews language for tone and clarity; manages community communication regarding updates. |
| **Webmaster / IT Admin** | Implements technical updates to the website; manages cookie banners and consent mechanisms; archives old versions. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews changes that fundamentally alter the Organization’s risk profile or ethical stance on data usage. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain an accurate Privacy Policy constitutes a violation of the Organization's **Professional Ethics and Community Guidelines** and may expose Study GRC Inc. to significant regulatory fines under GDPR and TDPSA.

Operational Officers who implement new data processing activities without triggering this update procedure may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination, as outlined in the **Employee Handbook**.
