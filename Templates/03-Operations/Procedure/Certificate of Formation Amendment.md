### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the strict legal and operational steps required to amend the Certificate of Formation (formerly Articles of Incorporation) of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). As the Organization’s chartering document filed with the Texas Secretary of State (SOS), amendments are classified as "Fundamental Actions" under the Bylaws and require heightened scrutiny, specific voting thresholds, and formal state filings to ensure compliance with the Texas Business Organizations Code (TX BOC).

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to any proposed change to the Organization's Certificate of Formation, including but not limited to:
*   Changing the Organization’s legal name.
*   Altering the specific purpose(s) of the Organization.
*   Modifying the duration of the Organization.
*   Changing the structure of the Board of Directors if fixed in the Certificate.
*   Modifying provisions regarding the distribution of assets upon dissolution.

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to amendments of the **Bylaws** unless such amendments conflict with the Certificate of Formation, in which case the Certificate must be amended first.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Certificate of Formation** | The legal document filed with the Texas Secretary of State that created the Organization as a legal entity. |
| **Fundamental Action** | Significant events defined in Bylaws Article X (e.g., mergers, dissolution, charter amendments) requiring a supermajority vote. |
| **Form 424** | The specific form prescribed by the Texas Secretary of State for filing a Certificate of Amendment. |
| **Voting Members** | Supporting Members and Honorary Members in good standing who hold voting rights as defined in Bylaws Article II. |
| **Supermajority** | The specific voting threshold required for Fundamental Actions: affirmative vote of at least two-thirds (2/3) of **all** Voting Members (not just those present). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Proposal and Board Resolution
1.  **Initiation:** An amendment may be proposed by the Board of Directors or by a petition signed by at least 10% of the Voting Members.
2.  **Board Review:** The Governance Committee and Legal Counsel must review the proposed amendment for compliance with TX BOC Chapter 22 and IRS 501(c)(3) regulations.
3.  **Board Resolution:** The Board must adopt a resolution directing that the proposed amendment be submitted to a vote at an Annual or Special Meeting of the members.
    *   The resolution must set forth the proposed amendment and direct that it be submitted to a vote.

#### 4.2 Member Notice Requirements
1.  **Written Notice:** The Secretary must deliver written notice to each Voting Member of record entitled to vote at the meeting.
2.  **Timing:** Notice must be delivered not less than 10 days and not more than 60 days before the date of the meeting, in accordance with Bylaws Section 2.5.
3.  **Content of Notice:** The notice **must** expressly state that the purpose (or one of the purposes) of the meeting is to consider the proposed amendment.
4.  **Documentation:** The notice must contain a copy of the proposed amendment or a summary of the changes to be effected by the amendment.

#### 4.3 Member Voting Process (Fundamental Action)
1.  **Quorum Verification:** The Secretary must verify that a quorum is present (physically or virtually) to convene the meeting.
2.  **Vote Tabulation:** The amendment is adopted **only** upon receiving the affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3) of the total number of Voting Members** (per Bylaws Article X, Section 10.2).
    *   *Note:* This threshold is higher than a simple majority of a quorum. If the Organization has 100 Voting Members, 67 affirmative votes are required, regardless of how many attend the meeting.
3.  **No Proxy Voting:** In accordance with Bylaws Section 2.3 and TX BOC § 22.160(b), no proxy votes are permitted. Votes must be cast in person or via the Organization's secure electronic voting system.

#### 4.4 Filing the Certificate of Amendment
1.  **Preparation:** Upon approval by the Members, the Secretary or Legal Counsel shall prepare **Texas SOS Form 424 (Certificate of Amendment)**.
2.  **Content of Form 424:**
    *   The name of the entity.
    *   A statement that the amendment has been approved in the manner required by the TX BOC and the entity's governing documents.
    *   The text of each amendment adopted.
    *   The date of adoption.
3.  **Execution:** The form must be signed by an authorized officer (President or Secretary).
4.  **Filing:** The form must be filed with the Texas Secretary of State, accompanied by the required filing fee (currently $25.00, subject to change).
5.  **Expedited Filing:** If the amendment is time-sensitive, the Organization may pay the additional fee for expedited processing.

#### 4.5 Post-Filing Notifications
1.  **Effective Date:** The amendment becomes effective upon filing with the SOS, or on a later date specified in the Certificate of Amendment (not to exceed 90 days).
2.  **IRS Notification:**
    *   **Name Change:** If the name changed, the Organization must report this to the IRS EO Determinations office or on the next Form 990.
    *   **Purpose Change:** Significant changes to the purpose must be reported on the next Form 990 (Part III).
3.  **Stakeholder Updates:** The Secretary must update the Organization’s records, website, bank accounts, and vendor contracts to reflect the amendment (e.g., new name or scope).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews proposed amendments for legal/strategic alignment; adopts resolution to submit amendment to members. |
| **Corporate Secretary** | Prepares notices; ensures accurate counting of the "2/3 of all members" threshold; executes Form 424. |
| **Voting Members** | Review proposed amendments; cast votes to approve or reject Fundamental Actions. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Drafts the amendment language; reviews Form 424 prior to filing; advises on IRS implications. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly adhere to this procedure—specifically the voting threshold defined in Bylaws Article X—renders the amendment **void ab initio** (invalid from the start). Filing a false or unauthorized certificate with the Secretary of State is a Class A misdemeanor under Texas law.

Any Director or Officer who knowingly authorizes a filing without the requisite Member approval may be subject to removal for cause and personal liability for damages incurred by the Organization.
