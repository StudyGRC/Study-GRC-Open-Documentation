### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a compliant, streamlined process for the procurement of goods and services during a public exigency or emergency. This procedure ensures that Study GRC Inc. can respond immediately to threats to life, safety, property, or mission-critical operations while maintaining compliance with federal regulations (specifically **2 CFR 200.320(c)**) and preserving fiscal accountability. This procedure provides the mechanism to bypass standard competitive bidding requirements when delay is unacceptable.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all employees, officers, and authorized volunteers of Study GRC Inc. It applies to all funding sources, including federal grants, private donations, and general operating funds.

**Exclusions:**
*   This procedure **does not** apply to urgent needs resulting from poor planning, administrative delays, or failure to forecast requirements. Such situations do not qualify as "emergencies" under federal grant guidance.
*   This procedure does not override the **Conflict of Interest Policy** (Bylaws Article IX); conflicts must still be disclosed and managed, even in emergencies.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Emergency** | A sudden, unexpected occurrence that poses an immediate threat to life, health, safety, property, or the continuation of essential services (e.g., natural disaster, cyberattack, facility failure). |
| **Public Exigency** | A situation that requires immediate aid or action to avoid a negative impact on the public welfare or the Organization's beneficiaries. |
| **Noncompetitive Procurement** | Procurement through solicitation of a proposal from only one source (Sole Source), bypassing the standard requirement for full and open competition. |
| **Reasonableness of Cost** | A cost is reasonable if, in its nature and amount, it does not exceed that which would be incurred by a prudent person under the circumstances prevailing at the time the decision was made. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Emergency Declaration and Authorization
Before initiating an emergency procurement, the situation must be formally recognized.

1.  **Identification:** The Requesting Officer identifies a critical need where the standard procurement timeline (e.g., obtaining 3 quotes) would cause unacceptable damage or risk.
2.  **Notification:** The Requesting Officer must immediately notify the **Executive Director (CEO)** or, in their absence, the **CFO**.
3.  **Verbal Approval:** Given the nature of emergencies, the Executive Director may grant verbal approval to proceed with the purchase immediately.
4.  **Threshold Escalation:**
    *   **Under $10,000:** Approval by Department Head.
    *   **$10,000 - $50,000:** Approval by Executive Director or CFO.
    *   **Over $50,000:** Approval by Executive Director *and* notification sent to the Board Treasurer within 24 hours.

#### 4.2 Vendor Selection and Negotiation
Under **2 CFR 200.320(c)(3)**, the Organization may solicit a proposal from a single source when the emergency will not permit a delay resulting from competitive solicitation.

1.  **Vendor Identification:** Select a vendor capable of providing the goods/services within the required timeframe. Preference should be given to existing, vetted vendors if possible.
2.  **Suspension and Debarment Check:** Even in an emergency, the Organization **must not** contract with parties suspended or debarred from federal programs. The CFO or designee must check [SAM.gov](https://sam.gov) unless the immediate threat to life prohibits the time to do so (in which case, check immediately post-purchase).
3.  **Price Negotiation:** The Requesting Officer must negotiate the best possible price and terms.
    *   *Note:* While competition is waived, the cost must still be **reasonable**. Price gouging should be documented and reported if no other option exists.

#### 4.3 Execution of Purchase
1.  **Commitment:** The authorized officer may use a corporate credit card, issue a verbal purchase order (to be confirmed in writing), or sign a contract.
2.  **Contract Provisions:** If a contract is signed, it should be limited in scope and duration to the emergency period only. Long-term contracts should not be entered into under emergency procedures; they must be bid out once the immediate crisis passes.

#### 4.4 Documentation and Justification (Mandatory)
To withstand audit scrutiny, the "Emergency Procurement Justification Form" must be completed within **48 hours** of the event.

1.  **Nature of Emergency:** Describe the specific threat to life, property, or operations.
2.  **Impact of Delay:** Explain explicitly why standard competitive bidding would have caused harm (e.g., "Waiting 2 weeks for bids would have resulted in total server data loss").
3.  **Vendor Selection:** Explain why this specific vendor was chosen (e.g., "Only vendor with generators in stock within 50 miles").
4.  **Cost Reasonableness:** Document how the price was determined to be fair under the circumstances (e.g., "Price compared to online averages," or "Acknowledged premium paid for 2-hour delivery").

#### 4.5 Post-Emergency Transition
1.  **Review:** Once the immediate emergency has passed, the CFO must review all emergency expenditures.
2.  **Re-Solicitation:** If the goods/services are still needed on an ongoing basis (e.g., temporary repairs need to become permanent), the remaining work must be subjected to standard competitive procurement procedures defined in the **Procurement Policy**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requesting Officer** | Identifies emergency, requests authorization, executes purchase, and completes justification documentation. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Authorizes the declaration of emergency status and approves high-value emergency procurements pursuant to Bylaws Article V. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Verifies budget availability (if possible), performs SAM.gov checks, reviews justification forms for audit compliance, and reports to the Treasurer. |
| **Board Treasurer** | Provides oversight for emergency expenditures exceeding $50,000 to ensure alignment with Bylaws Article VIII. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly adhere to this procedure—specifically the requirement to document the *justification* for bypassing competition—may result in **disallowed costs** (meaning the Organization must repay federal grant funds).

Misuse of this procedure to bypass standard procurement rules due to lack of planning is a violation of the **Code of Ethics** and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
