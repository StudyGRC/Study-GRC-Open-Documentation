### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized methodology for monitoring the financial performance of Study GRC Inc. against its approved operating budget and grant-specific budgets. This procedure ensures early detection of financial deviations, facilitates data-driven decision-making, and satisfies the monitoring requirements of **2 CFR § 200.308** (Revision of budget and program plans) and **Bylaws Article VIII** (Records, Transparency & Finance). Regular variance analysis is critical to maintaining the Organization’s tax-exempt status and fiscal health.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **All Financial Funds:** Including unrestricted operating funds, restricted grant funds, and project-specific accounts.
*   **Personnel:** The Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Treasurer, Executive Director (CEO), and all designated Budget Owners (e.g., VP of Education, Director of Kindness and Generosity).
*   **Reporting Period:** Monthly and Quarterly financial close cycles.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Budget Variance** | The mathematical difference between the budgeted amount and the actual amount incurred during a specific period. |
| **Material Variance** | A variance that exceeds the defined threshold (see Section 4.2) requiring formal explanation and potential corrective action. |
| **Favorable Variance** | A variance where actual revenue is higher than budgeted, or actual expenses are lower than budgeted. |
| **Unfavorable Variance** | A variance where actual revenue is lower than budgeted, or actual expenses are higher than budgeted. |
| **Budget Owner** | The Operational Officer or Department Head responsible for managing the revenue and expenses of a specific cost center or grant. |
| **2 CFR 200** | Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards (Uniform Guidance). |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Data Preparation and Timing
1.  **Monthly Close:** The CFO must close the books for the preceding month by the 10th business day of the current month.
2.  **Report Generation:** Following the close, the CFO shall generate a "Budget vs. Actuals" report from the accounting system. This report must include:
    *   Current Month (Actual vs. Budget).
    *   Year-to-Date (Actual vs. Budget).
    *   Variance in absolute dollars ($).
    *   Variance in percentage (%).

#### 4.2 Materiality Thresholds
To ensure efficiency, formal investigation is only required for "Material Variances." Study GRC Inc. defines a Material Variance as any line item that meets **BOTH** of the following criteria:
1.  **Percentage:** A deviation of **+/- 10%** from the budgeted amount.
2.  **Absolute Value:** A deviation exceeding **$500.00**.

*Note: For Federal Awards, strict adherence to the specific grant agreement thresholds takes precedence. If a grant requires reporting on a 5% variance, that lower threshold applies to that specific fund.*

#### 4.3 Investigation and Explanation
1.  **Distribution:** The CFO distributes the Budget vs. Actuals report to the relevant Budget Owners (e.g., VP of Education & Training for curriculum costs; CISO for IT infrastructure costs) by the 12th business day.
2.  **Analysis:** Budget Owners must review their respective cost centers.
3.  **Commentary:** For every Material Variance (as defined in 4.2), the Budget Owner must provide a written explanation to the CFO within three (3) business days. The explanation must categorize the variance as:
    *   **Timing Difference:** Expense occurred earlier/later than planned but total will remain unchanged.
    *   **Permanent Difference:** Unforeseen cost increase or savings that will impact the year-end result.
    *   **Coding Error:** Transaction was booked to the wrong account (requires CFO correction).

#### 4.4 Federal Grant Specific Monitoring (2 CFR 200.308)
1.  **Scope Change Detection:** The CFO must specifically analyze variances in federal grant funds to detect potential changes in the scope or objective of the project.
2.  **Prior Approval Triggers:** If the analysis reveals a need to transfer funds among direct cost categories in a federal grant where the cumulative amount of such transfers exceeds 10% of the total budget (or as specified in the award), the CFO must immediately notify the Executive Director to seek prior written approval from the Federal Awarding Agency.

#### 4.5 Reporting to Leadership and Board
1.  **Management Review:** The CFO compiles the explanations into a "Monthly Financial Performance Packet" for review by the Executive Director.
2.  **Treasurer Review:** The Packet is submitted to the Board Treasurer for preliminary review at least five (5) days prior to the Board meeting.
3.  **Board Presentation:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.4**, the Treasurer (or CFO) presents the variance analysis at the monthly Board meeting. The report must highlight:
    *   Significant Unfavorable Variances.
    *   Impact on cash flow and year-end projections.
    *   Status of Federal Grant burn rates.

#### 4.6 Corrective Action Plans
If an Unfavorable Variance is deemed "Permanent" and threatens the Organization's financial stability or grant compliance:
1.  **Action Plan:** The Executive Director and relevant Budget Owner must draft a Corrective Action Plan (e.g., hiring freeze, reduction in discretionary spending, or requesting a formal budget amendment).
2.  **Approval:**
    *   Operational adjustments within the CEO's authority are implemented immediately.
    *   Formal Budget Amendments requiring use of reserves must be voted on by the Board.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Generates reports, identifies material variances, monitors federal compliance thresholds, and compiles the final Board packet. |
| **Budget Owners** | Review departmental actuals, investigate root causes of variances, and provide written explanations for material deviations. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Reviews organization-wide performance, authorizes operational corrective actions, and ensures alignment with strategic goals. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides oversight on behalf of the Board, reviews monthly reports for accuracy, and presents findings to the Board. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews financial health monthly, questions significant variances, and approves formal budget amendments or corrective actions requiring governance intervention. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly adhere to this procedure—specifically the failure to report material variances or the concealment of overspending—constitutes a violation of the Organization’s **Code of Ethics** and **Financial Integrity Policy**.

*   **Federal Grants:** Failure to monitor and report deviations in federal funds may result in the disallowance of costs, suspension of the grant, or debarment from future federal funding under **2 CFR 200**.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment and removal from Board/Officer positions.
