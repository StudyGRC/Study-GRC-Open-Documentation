### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized, legally compliant method for identifying, investigating, and correcting payroll errors for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This procedure ensures that employees are paid accurately and timely in accordance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and the **Texas Payday Law (Texas Labor Code Chapter 61)**, while maintaining the financial integrity of the Organization’s funds, including federal grant awards.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all W-2 employees (full-time, part-time, and temporary) of the Organization.
*   **Inclusions:** Base salary, hourly wages, overtime calculations, and benefit deductions.
*   **Exclusions:** Independent Contractors (1099), whose payment disputes are handled via the Accounts Payable / Vendor Dispute process.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Overpayment** | Wages paid to an employee in excess of the amount earned or legally due (e.g., administrative error, incorrect rate, unearned time off). |
| **Underpayment** | Wages paid to an employee that are less than the amount earned or legally due. |
| **Written Authorization** | A document signed by the employee explicitly authorizing a deduction from their wages, as strictly required by **Texas Labor Code § 61.018**. |
| **Off-Cycle Payment** | A manual check or direct deposit issued outside of the standard payroll schedule. |
| **Grant Allocability** | The requirement under **2 CFR 200** that costs charged to federal awards must be accurate and incurred solely for the benefit of the award. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Reporting and Identification
4.1.1 **Employee Reporting:** Employees who suspect an error in their pay stub must submit a written inquiry (email or helpdesk ticket) to the Payroll Department (or CFO) within **30 days** of the pay date.
4.1.2 **Internal Discovery:** If the Payroll Department discovers an error during audits or reconciliation, they must document the discrepancy immediately and notify the affected employee and their direct supervisor.

#### 4.2 Investigation and Verification
4.2.1 The CFO or designated Payroll Administrator must verify the error by reviewing:
*   Approved time sheets (Time & Effort reports).
*   Employment contracts and offer letters.
*   Leave balances and PTO requests.
*   Applicable state and federal tax withholding tables.
4.2.2 **Grant Impact Analysis:** If the employee’s salary is funded by a federal grant, the CFO must determine if the error resulted in an incorrect charge to the grant.
*   *Note:* Grant accounts must be adjusted to reflect actual allowable costs immediately, regardless of the timeline for recovering funds from an employee.

#### 4.3 Correcting Underpayments (Organization Owes Employee)
4.3.1 **Minor Amounts (<$100):** With the employee's written consent (email is acceptable), the shortage will be added to the next scheduled payroll cycle as a retroactive adjustment.
4.3.2 **Significant Amounts (>$100 or causing hardship):** The Organization shall issue an off-cycle payment (check or direct deposit) within **three (3) business days** of verifying the error.
4.3.3 **Tax Reporting:** All retroactive payments will be taxed in the period they are paid, not the period they were earned, in accordance with IRS Constructive Receipt regulations.

#### 4.4 Correcting Overpayments (Employee Owes Organization)
*Strict adherence to Texas Labor Code § 61.018 is required. The Organization **cannot** unilaterally deduct overpayments from a future paycheck without specific written authorization.*

4.4.1 **Notification:** The CFO must notify the employee in writing detailing:
*   The nature of the error.
*   The dates the error occurred.
*   The total amount overpaid.
*   Proposed repayment options.

4.4.2 **Repayment Options:**
*   **Option A (Lump Sum):** Employee writes a personal check or authorizes a one-time payroll deduction for the full amount.
*   **Option B (Installments):** Employee authorizes a set deduction amount over a specific number of pay periods.

4.4.3 **Authorization Requirement:**
*   The employee must sign a **Payroll Deduction Authorization Form**.
*   The deduction must not reduce the employee's effective hourly rate below the federal minimum wage for the pay period in which the deduction is made (FLSA requirement).

4.4.4 **Disputes:** If an employee disputes the overpayment and refuses to sign an authorization:
*   The Organization may **not** deduct the money from the paycheck.
*   The Organization reserves the right to pursue civil legal action to recover the funds as a debt owed.

#### 4.5 Tax Adjustments for Overpayments
4.5.1 **Same Tax Year:** The Organization will void the incorrect payment record and process the repayment as a negative adjustment, correcting W-2 wages and tax withholdings.
4.5.2 **Cross-Tax Year:** If an overpayment is discovered after the tax year has closed:
*   The employee must repay the **gross** amount of the overpayment.
*   The Organization will issue a **W-2c** (Corrected Wage and Tax Statement) only for Social Security and Medicare wages. Federal Income Tax withheld in a prior year cannot be adjusted; the employee may claim a credit on their personal tax return (IRC § 1341).

#### 4.6 Record Keeping
4.6.1 All documentation regarding the error, investigation, communication, and signed authorizations must be retained in the employee’s payroll file for a minimum of **four (4) years** (per IRS and TWC requirements).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Review pay stubs regularly; report errors immediately; sign necessary authorization forms for overpayment recovery. |
| **Manager** | Assist in verifying time and attendance records during the investigation phase. |
| **CFO / Payroll Admin** | Investigate errors; calculate adjustments; ensure compliance with Texas Labor Code regarding deductions; adjust General Ledger for grant compliance. |
| **Executive Director** | Approve off-cycle payments; serve as the final escalation point for disputes regarding overpayment validity. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this procedure—specifically regarding the unauthorized deduction of wages—may subject the Organization to administrative penalties from the Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) and potential civil liability.

Employees who knowingly retain overpayments without reporting them, or who falsify records to create overpayments, are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal prosecution for theft.
