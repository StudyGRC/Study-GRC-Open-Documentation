### 1. Purpose
As a remote-first organization, Study GRC Inc. does not maintain a central physical office. However, pursuant to the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) General Duty Clause and Directive CPL 02-00-125 regarding home-based worksites, the Organization is committed to ensuring that all employees and volunteers maintain a safe, hazard-free, and ergonomically sound work environment.

This document serves as a mandatory self-assessment tool to:
1.  Identify and mitigate physical hazards in the Designated Workspace.
2.  Ensure compliance with workers' compensation insurance requirements.
3.  Promote long-term health through proper ergonomic setup.

### 2. Scope
This assessment applies to all **Operational Officers**, **Employees**, and **Volunteers** (collectively "Remote Workers") who perform duties for Study GRC Inc. from a home office or remote location for more than 20 hours per week.

**Exclusions:** This assessment does not apply to temporary workspaces such as coffee shops, libraries, or co-working spaces not leased by the Organization, though general safety awareness in these locations is encouraged.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Designated Workspace** | A specific area within the Remote Worker's home designated for the performance of official duties. Workers' compensation coverage is strictly limited to this defined area during work hours. |
| **Ergonomics** | The applied science of equipment design intended to maximize productivity by reducing operator fatigue and discomfort. |
| **MSD** | Musculoskeletal Disorders (e.g., carpal tunnel syndrome, tendinitis) often caused by repetitive strain or poor posture. |
| **Daisy-Chaining** | The unsafe practice of plugging one extension cord or power strip into another, creating a fire hazard. |

### 4. Assessment Checklist
All Remote Workers must verify the following conditions within their Designated Workspace. Any "No" answers require a remediation plan before the workspace is approved.

#### 4.1 General Environment & Fire Safety
| Item | Requirement | Status (Y/N) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4.1.1** | The workspace is free of tripping hazards (e.g., loose rugs, cabling across walkways, clutter). | [ ] |
| **4.1.2** | Floors are clear, dry, and level. | [ ] |
| **4.1.3** | A working smoke detector is installed within or immediately adjacent to the workspace and is tested monthly. | [ ] |
| **4.1.4** | A working carbon monoxide detector is present in the home (if applicable/gas appliances present). | [ ] |
| **4.1.5** | There is a clear, unobstructed exit path from the workspace in case of emergency. | [ ] |
| **4.1.6** | A functional fire extinguisher is easily accessible from the workspace. | [ ] |

#### 4.2 Electrical Safety
| Item | Requirement | Status (Y/N) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4.2.1** | All electrical equipment is plugged into grounded (3-prong) outlets or surge protectors. | [ ] |
| **4.2.2** | Surge protectors are UL-approved and not overloaded. | [ ] |
| **4.2.3** | There is **no** "daisy-chaining" of extension cords or power strips. | [ ] |
| **4.2.4** | Electrical cords are in good condition (no fraying or exposed wires) and are routed to prevent tripping. | [ ] |
| **4.2.5** | Adequate ventilation is provided for electronic equipment (laptops, monitors) to prevent overheating. | [ ] |

#### 4.3 Ergonomics & Workstation Setup
| Item | Requirement | Status (Y/N) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4.3.1** | **Chair:** The chair provides lumbar (lower back) support and is adjustable. | [ ] |
| **4.3.2** | **Posture:** When seated, feet are flat on the floor or on a footrest; thighs are parallel to the floor. | [ ] |
| **4.3.3** | **Keyboard/Mouse:** Located at elbow height; wrists are straight and not bent while typing. | [ ] |
| **4.3.4** | **Monitor:** The top of the screen is at or slightly below eye level to prevent neck strain. | [ ] |
| **4.3.5** | **Distance:** The monitor is approximately an arm's length (18-24 inches) away from the face. | [ ] |
| **4.3.6** | **Lighting:** Lighting is sufficient for reading without glare on the monitor screen. | [ ] |

#### 4.4 Information Security (Physical)
*Pursuant to Bylaws Article V (Role of CISO) and Article IX (Confidentiality).*

| Item | Requirement | Status (Y/N) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4.4.1** | The workspace allows for privacy during sensitive calls/meetings (e.g., a door that closes). | [ ] |
| **4.4.2** | Work screens are positioned away from windows or public views to prevent visual eavesdropping. | [ ] |
| **4.4.3** | Sensitive physical documents (if any) are stored in a locking drawer or cabinet. | [ ] |
| **4.4.4** | Work devices (laptops) are physically secured or locked away when not in use. | [ ] |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Remote Worker** | Conduct this self-assessment annually or upon moving to a new home. Truthfully report conditions. Remediate hazards immediately. |
| **Manager / Supervisor** | Review the self-assessment. Authorize equipment purchases (e.g., ergonomic chairs) if budget permits and need is demonstrated. |
| **Human Resources** | Retain completed assessments in the personnel file. Coordinate workers' compensation claims if an injury occurs in the Designated Workspace. |
| **Executive Director** | Ensure the organization provides reasonable resources to maintain safe remote work environments. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**6.1 Mandatory Submission:** This form must be completed within 14 days of the start date and annually thereafter.
**6.2 Remediation:** If a safety hazard is identified, the Remote Worker must correct it immediately. Work may be suspended until the environment is deemed safe.
**6.3 False Reporting:** Falsifying information on this assessment regarding the safety of the workspace may result in the denial of workers' compensation claims and disciplinary action, up to and including termination, in accordance with the **Employee Handbook**.
**6.4 Indemnification:** Per **Bylaws Article VII**, indemnification is contingent upon the individual acting in good faith. Knowingly maintaining an unsafe workspace violates this standard.
