### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to mandate the inclusion of specific legal clauses in contracts awarded by **Study GRC Inc.** (the "Organization"), particularly those funded in whole or in part by federal grants. This standard ensures compliance with **2 CFR Part 200, Appendix II** ("Contract Provisions for Non-Federal Entity Contracts Under Federal Awards"). Failure to include these provisions may result in audit findings, disallowed costs, and the loss of federal funding eligibility.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all procurement contracts, subcontracts, and purchase orders issued by the Organization.
*   **Mandatory Application:** All contracts funded by federal awards (direct or pass-through).
*   **Recommended Application:** All vendor contracts, regardless of funding source, to maintain uniform best practices and operational flexibility.
*   **Exclusions:** Employment contracts (W-2 employees), which are governed by the *Employee Handbook* and state labor laws.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Simplified Acquisition Threshold (SAT)** | The dollar amount below which a non-Federal entity may purchase property or services using small purchase methods. Currently set at **$250,000** (48 CFR 2.101). |
| **Non-Federal Entity** | Study GRC Inc., acting as the recipient or sub-recipient of a federal award. |
| **Termination for Cause** | The right to end a contract due to the vendor's failure to perform. |
| **Termination for Convenience** | The right to end a contract without a specific reason (e.g., loss of funding), usually requiring payment only for work already performed. |
| **Covered Transaction** | A non-procurement or procurement transaction that is subject to the prohibitions of federal suspension and debarment regulations. |

### 4. Standards and Checklist Procedures

All contracts must be reviewed against the following checklist prior to execution. The specific clauses required depend on the **contract value** and the **nature of the work**.

#### 4.1 Usage Instructions
1.  The **Requesting Manager** must identify the estimated total value of the contract.
2.  The **CFO or Designee** must verify the funding source (Federal vs. Non-Federal).
3.  If Federal funds are involved, the following provisions are **mandatory** based on the thresholds defined below.

#### 4.2 Required Provisions Checklist

**Category A: All Contracts (Regardless of Size)**

*   **[ ] Debarment and Suspension (Executive Orders 12549 and 12689):**
    *   *Requirement:* A contract award (see 2 CFR 180.220) must not be made to parties listed on the government-wide exclusions in the System for Award Management (SAM).
    *   *Action:* Vendor must certify they are not debarred. Study GRC must verify via SAM.gov search.
*   **[ ] Prohibition on Certain Telecommunications and Video Surveillance Services (2 CFR § 200.216):**
    *   *Requirement:* Prohibition on procuring equipment, services, or systems that use covered telecommunications equipment or services as a substantial or essential component (specifically Huawei, ZTE, Hytera, Hikvision, and Dahua).
    *   *Context:* Critical for Study GRC IT/Security procurement.
*   **[ ] Domestic Preferences for Procurements (2 CFR § 200.322):**
    *   *Requirement:* To the greatest extent practicable, the Organization should provide a preference for the purchase of goods, products, or materials produced in the United States (including but not limited to iron, aluminum, steel, cement, and other manufactured products).

**Category B: Contracts > $10,000**

*   **[ ] Termination Clauses (2 CFR 200 Appendix II (B)):**
    *   *Requirement:* Must address termination for **cause** and for **convenience** by the non-Federal entity, including the manner by which it will be effected and the basis for settlement.

**Category C: Contracts > Simplified Acquisition Threshold ($250,000)**

*   **[ ] Administrative, Contractual, or Legal Remedies (2 CFR 200 Appendix II (A)):**
    *   *Requirement:* Contracts must address administrative, contractual, or legal remedies in instances where contractors violate or breach contract terms, and provide for such sanctions and penalties as appropriate.

**Category D: Contracts > $100,000**

*   **[ ] Byrd Anti-Lobbying Amendment (31 U.S.C. 1352):**
    *   *Requirement:* Contractors that apply or bid for an award exceeding $100,000 must file the required certification. Each tier certifies to the tier above that it will not and has not used Federal appropriated funds to pay any person or organization for influencing or attempting to influence an officer or employee of any agency, a member of Congress, or an employee of a member of Congress in connection with obtaining any Federal contract, grant or any other award covered by 31 U.S.C. 1352.

**Category E: Contracts > $150,000**

*   **[ ] Clean Air Act and Federal Water Pollution Control Act:**
    *   *Requirement:* Contractor agrees to comply with all applicable standards, orders, or regulations issued pursuant to the Clean Air Act (42 U.S.C. 7401-7671q) and the Federal Water Pollution Control Act as amended (33 U.S.C. 1251-1387). Violations must be reported to the Federal awarding agency and the Regional Office of the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).

**Category F: Special Construction / Labor Categories (If Applicable)**

*   **[ ] Equal Employment Opportunity (41 CFR Part 60):**
    *   *Applicability:* All "federally assisted construction contracts."
    *   *Requirement:* Must include the equal opportunity clause provided under 41 CFR 60-1.4(b).
*   **[ ] Davis-Bacon Act (40 U.S.C. 3141-3148):**
    *   *Applicability:* Prime construction contracts > $2,000.
    *   *Requirement:* Contractors must pay wages to laborers and mechanics at a rate not less than the prevailing wages specified in a wage determination made by the Secretary of Labor.
*   **[ ] Contract Work Hours and Safety Standards Act (40 U.S.C. 3701-3708):**
    *   *Applicability:* Contracts involving mechanics or laborers > $100,000.
    *   *Requirement:* Compute wages on a 40-hour work week; excess hours paid at 1.5x rate. Prohibits unsanitary/hazardous working conditions.

**Category G: Research & Development / Intellectual Property**

*   **[ ] Rights to Inventions Made Under a Contract or Agreement (37 CFR Part 401):**
    *   *Applicability:* If the Federal award meets the definition of "funding agreement" and the Organization enters into a contract with a small business firm or nonprofit organization regarding the substitution of parties, assignment, or performance of experimental, developmental, or research work.
    *   *Requirement:* Must comply with 37 CFR Part 401 and any implementing regulations issued by the awarding agency.

**Category H: Environmental / Waste Management**

*   **[ ] Procurement of Recovered Materials (2 CFR § 200.323):**
    *   *Applicability:* A non-Federal entity that is a state agency or agency of a political subdivision of a state and its contractors.
    *   *Requirement:* Compliance with section 6002 of the Solid Waste Disposal Act. (Note: While Study GRC is a nonprofit, adherence is recommended best practice for federal alignment).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requesting Manager** | Identifies the scope of work and estimated value. Flags if the contract involves construction, R&D, or access to sensitive data. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews the contract draft against this checklist. Confirms funding source (Federal vs. Private). Ensures SAM.gov verification is performed and documented. |
| **Legal Counsel / Compliance** | Drafts the specific legal language for the required clauses to ensure enforceability under Texas Law and Federal Regulation. |
| **Executive Director** | Final signatory authority; ensures no contract is executed without the CFO's compliance validation. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to include these mandatory provisions in federally funded contracts renders the costs associated with that contract **unallowable**.
*   **Financial Impact:** The Organization may be required to repay federal funds used for non-compliant contracts.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees who willfully bypass this review process or execute contracts without required clauses are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination, per the *Employee Handbook*.
*   **Audit:** This checklist serves as a primary artifact for Single Audit (2 CFR 200 Subpart F) testing.
