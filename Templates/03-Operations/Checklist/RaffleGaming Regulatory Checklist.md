### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this checklist is to ensure that **Study GRC Inc.** complies strictly with the **Texas Charitable Raffle Enabling Act (Texas Occupations Code, Chapter 2002)** and IRS regulations regarding gaming events. As a Texas non-profit, failure to adhere to these laws constitutes illegal gambling, which is a criminal offense (Class C Misdemeanor) and grounds for revocation of the Organization’s 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to all fundraising activities involving the distribution of prizes by chance in exchange for consideration (money), including raffles, door prizes where tickets are purchased, and "50/50" draws.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Sweepstakes:** Promotions where entry is free (no purchase necessary).
*   **Silent Auctions:** Where prizes are awarded to the highest bidder (skill/financial capacity, not chance).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Raffle** | The award of one or more prizes by chance at a single occasion among a single pool or group of persons who have paid or promised a thing of value for a ticket that represents a chance to win a prize. |
| **Qualified Organization** | A non-profit that has been in existence for at least three (3) years and meets the criteria of the Texas Occupations Code. |
| **Consideration** | Money or anything of value paid for the chance to win. |
| **Prohibited Internet Sale** | The sale of raffle tickets via a website, email, or digital payment processor where the ticket is delivered digitally. |

### 4. Regulatory Checklist

All items must be marked **YES** or **N/A** for a raffle to proceed. Any **NO** response indicates a violation of Texas law or Organization policy.

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Organizational Eligibility (The "Stop" Gate)
*Before planning a raffle, verify the Organization's legal standing.*

| # | Requirement | Status | Regulatory Reference |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1.1** | Has Study GRC Inc. been in existence and actively operating for at least **three (3) continuous years**? *(Note: If the Organization is less than 3 years old, holding a raffle is illegal in Texas.)* | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.003 |
| **1.2** | Is the Organization currently in good standing with the Texas Secretary of State and the IRS (501(c)(3) status active)? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.003 |
| **1.3** | Does the Organization hold no more than **two (2)** raffles per calendar year? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.052 |

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Operational Restrictions
*Strict rules on how the raffle is conducted.*

| # | Requirement | Status | Regulatory Reference |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **2.1** | Is the raffle being conducted **exclusively** by volunteer members or authorized unpaid volunteers? *(Paid staff or professional fundraisers cannot organize or sell tickets.)* | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.054 |
| **2.2** | Have we verified that **no** compensation is being paid to anyone for organizing or conducting the raffle? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.054 |
| **2.3** | **Internet Prohibition:** Is the sale of tickets restricted to face-to-face or physical mail transactions? *(Note: You may advertise online, but you CANNOT sell the ticket directly via a website/shopping cart).* | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX AG Opinion KP-0110 |
| **2.4** | Are we ensuring that no tickets are sold statewide or via statewide advertising (unless the prize is a residence)? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.054 |
| **2.5** | Are all ticket sellers at least 18 years of age? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | Youth Protection Policy |

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Prize Limitations
*Restrictions on what can be awarded.*

| # | Requirement | Status | Regulatory Reference |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **3.1** | Is the value of the purchased prize less than **$50,000**? *(Or $250,000 if the prize is a residence).* | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.056 |
| **3.2** | If the prize is cash, is the amount strictly limited to the statutory cap (currently prohibited unless a "50/50" adhering to strict rules, generally merchandise is required)? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.056 |
| **3.3** | Has the Organization verified that the prize is in its possession or ownership, or posted a bond for the full value of the prize? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.056 |

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Ticket Content Requirements
*Every printed ticket must contain specific legal disclosures.*

| # | Requirement | Status | Regulatory Reference |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4.1** | Does the ticket state the **Name and Address** of Study GRC Inc.? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.055 |
| **4.2** | Does the ticket state the **price** of the ticket? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.055 |
| **4.3** | Does the ticket provide a **general description** of the prize(s) to be won? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.055 |
| **4.4** | Does the ticket state the **date** on which the prize will be awarded? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.055 |

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Financial & Tax Reporting
*IRS and Accounting compliance.*

| # | Requirement | Status | Regulatory Reference |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **5.1** | If the prize value is **$600 or more** and at least 300 times the wager, is the Organization prepared to file **IRS Form W-2G**? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | IRS Pub 3079 |
| **5.2** | If the prize value is **$5,000 or more**, has the Organization arranged to withhold 24% federal income tax from the winnings? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | IRS Notice 1340 |
| **5.3** | Are raffle proceeds segregated in the accounting books to prove they are used for charitable purposes? | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | TX Occ. Code § 2002.053 |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fundraising Chair** | Completes this checklist prior to announcing any raffle event. Ensures ticket design complies with Section 4.4. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews the checklist for accuracy. Manages IRS tax withholding and W-2G filing if prizes exceed thresholds. |
| **Executive Director** | Grants final approval for the event only after this checklist is fully validated. |
| **Volunteers** | Sell tickets in accordance with the "No Internet Sales" and "No Compensation" rules. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Warning:** Conducting an unauthorized raffle is a **Class C Misdemeanor** under Texas law.

*   **Internal:** Failure to complete this checklist prior to a gaming event will result in the immediate cancellation of the event and disciplinary action against the organizer.
*   **External:** Violations may result in civil penalties, criminal charges against officers, and the revocation of Study GRC Inc.'s corporate charter and tax-exempt status.
