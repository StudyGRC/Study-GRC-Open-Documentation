### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a mandatory, standardized framework for evaluating potential mergers, consolidations, or acquisitions of substantially all assets of another entity. This checklist ensures the Board of Directors fulfills its fiduciary **Duty of Care** and **Duty of Obedience** before recommending a "Fundamental Action" to the Voting Members, as required by **Article X** of the Bylaws.

This process is designed to identify legal, financial, operational, and reputational risks associated with a Target Entity to prevent the assumption of undisclosed liabilities and ensure alignment with Study GRC Inc.’s 501(c)(3) charitable mission.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to:
*   **The Board of Directors** and any Special Committee appointed to evaluate a potential transaction.
*   **Executive Leadership** (CEO, CFO) assisting in the evaluation.
*   **External Legal Counsel** and **Auditors** engaged for the transaction.

This document must be completed **prior** to the drafting of a definitive *Plan of Merger* and **prior** to the scheduling of a vote by the Voting Members under Bylaws Section 10.2.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Target Entity** | The organization proposed to merge into, consolidate with, or sell assets to Study GRC Inc. |
| **Fundamental Action** | As defined in Bylaws Article X, a significant event including merger, consolidation, or sale of substantially all assets, requiring a two-thirds (2/3) vote of Voting Members. |
| **Plan of Merger** | The legal document detailing the terms and conditions of the merger, as required by the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC). |
| **Restricted Assets** | Funds or property held by the Target Entity that are subject to donor-imposed restrictions or charitable trust laws. |
| **Change of Control** | A contractual provision that triggers specific rights (e.g., termination, repayment) upon a merger or change in governance. |

### 4. Due Diligence Checklist

The Special Committee or designated officers must obtain, review, and assess the following documents and information from the Target Entity.

#### 4.1 Corporate & Governance Structure
| Status | Item | Risk Factor / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ☐ | **Certificate of Formation (Articles)** | Verify legal existence and purpose. Check for dissolution clauses incompatible with Study GRC. |
| ☐ | **Bylaws (Current & Historic)** | Review member voting rights, indemnification clauses, and governance structure. |
| ☐ | **Good Standing Certificate** | Must be current from the Texas Secretary of State (and other operating states). |
| ☐ | **Board Minutes (Last 3 Years)** | Identify authorized actions, dissension, and potential undisclosed liabilities. |
| ☐ | **List of Directors & Officers** | Check for conflicts of interest, reputation risks, and overlapping board service. |
| ☐ | **Membership Roster** | If Target has voting members, verify exact count for quorum/voting requirements. |

#### 4.2 Tax & Regulatory Compliance
| Status | Item | Risk Factor / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ☐ | **IRS Determination Letter** | Verify 501(c)(3) status is active and not revoked. |
| ☐ | **IRS Form 990 (Last 3 Years)** | Review for UBIT, excess benefit transactions, and public support test ratios. |
| ☐ | **State Tax Exemptions** | Verify Texas Franchise Tax and Sales Tax exemption status. |
| ☐ | **Charitable Solicitations** | Verify registration in all states where the Target solicits funds. |
| ☐ | **Audit Inquiries** | Disclose any past or pending audits by the IRS, DOL, or State Attorney General. |

#### 4.3 Financial Integrity & Assets
| Status | Item | Risk Factor / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ☐ | **Audited Financials (3 Years)** | Analyze trends in revenue, expenses, and net assets. |
| ☐ | **Current Interim Financials** | Review YTD performance against budget. |
| ☐ | **Debt Schedule** | List all loans, lines of credit, mortgages, and guarantees. Check for "Change of Control" defaults. |
| ☐ | **Restricted Fund Analysis** | **Critical:** Identify permanently restricted (endowment) vs. temporarily restricted funds. Can Study GRC legally honor these restrictions? |
| ☐ | **Fixed Asset Register** | Verify ownership and condition of physical assets. |
| ☐ | **Insurance Policies** | Review D&O, General Liability, and Cyber coverage. Check "Tail Coverage" requirements. |

#### 4.4 Legal & Litigation
| Status | Item | Risk Factor / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ☐ | **Litigation Schedule** | List of all pending, threatened, or settled lawsuits in the last 5 years. |
| ☐ | **Regulatory Orders** | Any consent decrees, injunctions, or cease-and-desist orders. |
| ☐ | **Settlement Agreements** | Review for ongoing confidentiality or payment obligations. |
| ☐ | **Environmental Reports** | If real estate is involved, Phase I Environmental Site Assessment. |

#### 4.5 Contracts & Federal Grants
| Status | Item | Risk Factor / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ☐ | **Material Contracts** | Review leases, vendor agreements, and software licenses for assignability. |
| ☐ | **Federal Grant Awards** | **Critical:** Review Notice of Award (NoA) terms. Does the merger require prior approval from the Federal Awarding Agency (2 CFR 200)? |
| ☐ | **Subaward Agreements** | Review obligations to subrecipients. |
| ☐ | **Partnership Agreements** | Review MOUs and Joint Venture agreements. |

#### 4.6 Human Resources & Benefits
| Status | Item | Risk Factor / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ☐ | **Employee Census** | List of employees, titles, salaries, and tenure. |
| ☐ | **Employment Contracts** | Check for severance packages, "Golden Parachutes," or change-of-control bonuses. |
| ☐ | **Benefits Plans** | Review retirement (403b), health, and PTO accruals. Assess liability for unfunded benefits. |
| ☐ | **Independent Contractors** | Review for misclassification risks (1099 vs. W-2). |
| ☐ | **Workers' Comp Claims** | Review loss history. |

#### 4.7 Intellectual Property & IT
| Status | Item | Risk Factor / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ☐ | **IP Inventory** | Trademarks, copyrights, domains, and patents owned by Target. |
| ☐ | **Open Source Compliance** | **Critical:** Does Target's IP align with Study GRC's open-source mission (Bylaws Art. IX)? |
| ☐ | **Data Privacy Compliance** | Review Target's compliance with GDPR/CCPA/TDPSA. Assess risk of inheriting non-compliant data. |
| ☐ | **Cybersecurity Incidents** | Disclosure of any data breaches in the last 3 years. |

#### 4.8 Cultural & Mission Fit
| Status | Item | Risk Factor / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ☐ | **Mission Alignment** | Does the Target's mission legally fit within Study GRC’s Certificate of Formation? |
| ☐ | **Program Impact** | Evaluation of the effectiveness of Target's programs. |
| ☐ | **Reputation Analysis** | Search for negative press or social media sentiment regarding the Target. |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Special Committee** | Primary responsibility for executing this checklist and compiling the Due Diligence Report. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews all legal documents, assesses liability, and drafts the Plan of Merger. |
| **CFO / Treasurer** | Conducts financial analysis, reviews audits, and assesses restricted fund portability. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews the final Due Diligence Report to determine if the transaction is in the best interest of the Organization. |
| **Voting Members** | Vote to approve or reject the Fundamental Action based on Board recommendation (requires 2/3 vote). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

*   **Fiduciary Duty:** Failure to conduct adequate due diligence may constitute a breach of the Duty of Care, potentially exposing Directors to personal liability.
*   **Voting Threshold:** Per **Bylaws Article X Section 10.2**, the results of this due diligence must be presented to the membership to inform their vote. A Fundamental Action cannot proceed without the affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3)** of all Voting Members.
*   **Record Retention:** The completed checklist and all supporting documentation must be retained permanently in the Corporate Records as evidence of the Board's prudent decision-making process.
