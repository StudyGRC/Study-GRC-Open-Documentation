### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this checklist is to ensure Study GRC Inc. complies with Federal regulations regarding the prohibition of contracting with parties that are suspended or debarred from doing business with the Federal Government. This document serves as the primary mechanism to prevent "waste, fraud, and abuse" and to validate vendor eligibility prior to the commitment of funds, in accordance with **2 CFR § 200.214** and **2 CFR Part 180**.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to all procurement actions and subawards made by Study GRC Inc. involving Federal funds, specifically:
*   **Covered Transactions:** All contracts for goods or services expected to equal or exceed **$25,000**.
*   **Subawards:** All subawards to subrecipients, regardless of dollar value.
*   **Key Personnel:** Contracts for critical influence roles (e.g., consultants, principal investigators), regardless of dollar value.

**Exclusions:** This process is optional (though recommended) for micro-purchases (under $10,000) unless the vendor is a known risk.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **SAM.gov** | The System for Award Management; the official U.S. government system that consolidates the capabilities of CCR/FedReg, ORCA, and EPLS. |
| **Exclusion** | A status in SAM.gov indicating that an entity is suspended, debarred, or proposed for debarment and is ineligible for federal contracts or assistance. |
| **Covered Transaction** | A non-procurement transaction (grant, cooperative agreement) or a lower-tier covered transaction (contract for goods/services >$25k). |
| **UEI** | Unique Entity Identifier; the official alphanumeric identifier used in SAM.gov (replaced the DUNS number). |
| **Disallowed Cost** | A charge to a Federal award that the Federal awarding agency or pass-through entity determines to be unallowable, often resulting in repayment. |

### 4. Procedures and Checklist

This checklist must be completed **prior** to signing a contract or issuing a Purchase Order.

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Applicability Determination
*To be completed by the Requesting Department.*

- [ ] **Threshold Verification:** Is the expected value of the contract/transaction $\ge$ $25,000?
- [ ] **Subaward Verification:** Is this a subaward of Federal financial assistance (regardless of value)?
- [ ] **Role Verification:** Does the contractor hold a position of critical influence (e.g., Auditor, Legal Counsel, Grant Manager)?

*If **YES** to any of the above, proceed to Phase 2.*

#### 4.2 Phase 2: SAM.gov Verification Search
*To be completed by the Procurement Officer or CFO.*

- [ ] **Access System:** Navigate to [https://sam.gov/content/exclusions](https://sam.gov/content/exclusions).
- [ ] **Search Method:** Select "Search" and choose the domain "Entity Information" $\rightarrow$ "Exclusions".
- [ ] **Input Data:** Enter the entity's **Unique Entity ID (UEI)** (Preferred) or exact Legal Business Name.
    *   *Note: If searching by name, ensure the address matches the vendor invoice/quote exactly.*
- [ ] **Review Results:**
    *   **No Records Found:** The entity is eligible. Proceed to Phase 3.
    *   **Record Found (Active Exclusion):** **STOP.** The entity is ineligible. Do not proceed with procurement. Notify the CFO immediately.

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Documentation and Evidence
*Strict adherence to Bylaws Article VIII (Records) is required for audit defense.*

- [ ] **Capture Evidence:** Take a screenshot or "Print to PDF" of the SAM.gov search result.
- [ ] **Verification Elements:** Ensure the screenshot clearly displays:
    *   [ ] The Date of the search (System timestamp).
    *   [ ] The Search Term used (UEI or Name).
    *   [ ] The statement "No matches found" or "Active Exclusion: No".
- [ ] **File Retention:** Save the PDF to the vendor's permanent file in the central repository (e.g., `[VendorName]_SAM_Check_[YYYY-MM-DD].pdf`).

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Contractual Certification
*To be completed by the Contract Manager.*

- [ ] **Clause Inclusion:** Ensure the contract includes the mandatory Suspension and Debarment clause (Appendix II to 2 CFR Part 200).
    *   *Sample Language:* "The Contractor certifies that neither it nor its principals are presently debarred, suspended, proposed for debarment, declared ineligible, or voluntarily excluded from participation in this transaction by any Federal department or agency."
- [ ] **Vendor Signature:** Verify the contract is signed by an authorized representative attesting to this certification.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Ongoing Monitoring (Multi-Year Contracts)
*To be completed annually for active vendors.*

- [ ] **Annual Re-Verification:** For contracts spanning multiple fiscal years, repeat Phase 2 and Phase 3 prior to the renewal or start of the new fiscal year.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requesting Department** | Determines if the procurement meets the threshold for verification ($25k+ or Subaward). Provides correct Vendor Name and UEI. |
| **Procurement Officer** | Performs the physical search on SAM.gov. Generates the proof of compliance (PDF/Screenshot). |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews the verification documentation prior to authorizing payment or contract signature. |
| **Internal Auditor** | Periodically samples vendor files to ensure checklists are present and dated *prior* to contract execution. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**Failure to Comply:**
Entering into a covered transaction with a suspended or debarred entity will result in the immediate classification of all associated payments as **Disallowed Costs**. Study GRC Inc. would be required to repay these funds to the Federal government from non-federal sources.

**Disciplinary Action:**
Employees found falsifying this checklist or intentionally bypassing the verification process are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, in accordance with the **Employee Handbook** and **Fraud Detection and Reporting Procedure**.
