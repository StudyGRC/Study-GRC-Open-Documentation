### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a rigorous, standardized process for the preparation, review, and filing of IRS Form 990 (Return of Organization Exempt From Income Tax). This checklist ensures **Study GRC Inc.** maintains its federal tax-exempt status, complies with IRS regulations, and adheres to **Article VIII, Section 8.2** of the Bylaws regarding financial transparency. It serves to mitigate the risk of filing errors, omissions, or governance discrepancies that could trigger an audit or reputational damage.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to the **Finance Committee**, **Treasurer**, **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)**, and the **Board of Directors**. It covers the entire lifecycle of the filing process, from data aggregation to public disclosure.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Form 990** | The annual information return that federally tax-exempt organizations must file with the IRS. It provides information on the filing organization's mission, programs, and finances. |
| **Schedule A** | A supplementary schedule used to calculate the organization's "Public Support Test" percentage to ensure it qualifies as a public charity rather than a private foundation. |
| **Schedule L** | A supplementary schedule reporting "Transactions with Interested Persons," including loans, grants, or business transactions with Directors or Officers. |
| **UBIT** | Unrelated Business Income Tax. Income from a trade or business, regularly carried on, that is not substantially related to the charitable, educational, or other purpose that is the basis of the organization's exemption. |
| **Key Employee** | An employee with gross compensation over $150,000 who has significant responsibility or influence over the organization, as defined by IRS instructions. |

### 4. Procedures and Checklist

The following steps must be executed in chronological order.

#### 4.1 Phase I: Data Aggregation & Governance Review
*Responsible Party: CFO / Treasurer*

*   [ ] **Fiscal Year Verification:** Confirm the return covers the correct fiscal year as defined in **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3** (Calendar Year, unless changed).
*   [ ] **Entity Details:** Verify the Registered Office and Agent match the current filings with the Texas Secretary of State (**Bylaws Article I, Section 1.2**).
*   [ ] **Mission Statement:** Ensure the mission statement in Part I and Part III aligns verbatim or substantively with **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3** (Fostering GRC knowledge, removing financial barriers, empowering learners).
*   [ ] **Program Service Accomplishments:** Draft clear, quantitative descriptions of the three largest program services (e.g., "Provided zero-cost open-source GRC resources to X number of learners").
*   [ ] **Board Roster Reconciliation:**
    *   [ ] List all Directors who served at *any* point during the tax year.
    *   [ ] Verify titles (President, Secretary, Treasurer) match elections held per **Bylaws Article IV**.
    *   [ ] Ensure the total number of voting members reported matches the official minutes.
*   [ ] **Independent Contractor Review:** Identify the five highest-compensated independent contractors receiving >$100,000.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Financial Reconciliation
*Responsible Party: CFO / External CPA*

*   [ ] **Book-to-Tax Reconciliation:** Reconcile the Statement of Revenue and Expenses on Form 990 with the organization's internal financial reports (and audited financial statements, if applicable).
*   [ ] **Public Support Test (Schedule A):**
    *   [ ] Calculate the public support percentage.
    *   [ ] Verify the organization meets the 33 1/3% support test or the 10% facts and circumstances test.
*   [ ] **Donors (Schedule B):**
    *   [ ] Compile the list of contributors who gave $5,000 or more.
    *   [ ] **Note:** Schedule B names and addresses are *confidential* and must not be included in the public inspection copy.
*   [ ] **Non-Cash Contributions (Schedule M):**
    *   [ ] Did the organization receive non-cash assets (crypto, hardware, software licenses)?
    *   [ ] Ensure valuation methodology aligns with the *Non-Cash Contribution Valuation Policy*.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Governance & Policy Compliance (Part VI)
*Responsible Party: Secretary / Governance Committee*

*   [ ] **Board Review Question:** Ensure the box is checked "Yes" for "Has the organization provided a complete copy of this Form 990 to all members of its governing body before filing?"
*   [ ] **Conflict of Interest (COI):**
    *   [ ] Confirm the organization has a written COI policy (**Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1**).
    *   [ ] Confirm annual disclosure statements were signed by all Directors/Officers.
    *   [ ] If "Yes" to "Did the organization become aware of a material diversion of assets?", ensure the *Fraud Detection and Reporting Procedure* was followed.
*   [ ] **Whistleblower Policy:** Confirm the policy was in effect during the tax year.
*   [ ] **Document Retention:** Confirm the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy* was in effect.
*   [ ] **Compensation Process:** Confirm that compensation for the Executive Director (CEO) and Key Employees was reviewed by independent persons using comparability data (**Bylaws Article V, Section 5.1**).

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Transactions with Interested Persons (Schedule L)
*Responsible Party: Treasurer / Legal Counsel*

*   [ ] **Excess Benefit Transactions:** Verify no transactions occurred that would constitute an excess benefit under IRC §4958.
*   [ ] **Loans:** Verify no loans exist between the Organization and any Director/Officer (generally prohibited for Texas Nonprofits, but must be reported if they exist).
*   [ ] **Business Transactions:** Disclose any transaction >$100,000 with a Director/Officer, or any transaction where a Director/Officer had a direct financial interest.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Board Review & Approval
*Responsible Party: Board of Directors*

*   [ ] **Distribution:** Distribute the complete draft Form 990 (including all schedules) to all voting Directors via secure electronic means.
*   [ ] **Review Period:** Allow a minimum of 5 business days for Board review and questions.
*   [ ] **Q&A Session:** The Treasurer or CFO must be available to answer questions regarding specific line items.
*   [ ] **Approval:** Secure a formal vote or documented consent to file the return. This must be recorded in the Board Minutes.

#### 4.6 Phase VI: Filing & Transparency
*Responsible Party: CFO / Secretary*

*   [ ] **Signature:** The return must be signed by a current Officer (President, Treasurer, or CEO).
*   [ ] **Electronic Filing:** File the return with the IRS via an authorized e-file provider.
*   [ ] **State Filing:** Determine if a copy must be filed with the Texas Comptroller or other state charity officials (if soliciting outside Texas).
*   [ ] **Public Inspection Copy Creation:**
    *   [ ] Create a PDF version of the filed return.
    *   [ ] **REDACT** the names and addresses of contributors on Schedule B.
*   [ ] **Website Posting:** Upload the Public Inspection Copy to the "Transparency" page of the Study GRC Inc. website, in compliance with **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Primary owner of the Form 990 process; coordinates with external CPA; ensures financial data accuracy. |
| **Treasurer** | Conducts detailed review of the return; presents the return to the Board; ensures Schedule A (Public Support) is accurate. |
| **Secretary** | Verifies governance responses (Part VI); ensures Board minutes reflect the review process; manages Conflict of Interest disclosures. |
| **Board of Directors** | Fiduciary duty to review the return prior to filing; asks clarifying questions; authorizes filing. |
| **External CPA (if engaged)** | Prepares the return based on provided data; signs as Paid Preparer; advises on complex tax positions. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

*   **Late Filing Penalties:** Failure to file Form 990 by the due date (15th day of the 5th month after the fiscal year ends) may result in IRS penalties of $20 to $100 per day.
*   **Loss of Exemption:** Failure to file for three consecutive years will result in the automatic revocation of the Organization's 501(c)(3) status.
*   **Public Inspection:** Failure to provide the Form 990 for public inspection upon request can result in statutory penalties.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Falsification of data provided for the Form 990 is a violation of the *Code of Ethics* and may result in termination of employment or removal from the Board.
