### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a standardized, repeatable process for preparing Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") for its annual external financial audit. This checklist ensures compliance with **2 CFR 200.501** (Audit Requirements) regarding federal awards, satisfies the transparency requirements of **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, and minimizes audit adjustments or findings.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to the Finance Department, Executive Director, and Board Treasurer. It covers all financial activities, governance records, and grant compliance documentation for the fiscal year (January 1 – December 31).

**Exclusions:** This checklist does not replace the specific "Provided by Client" (PBC) list issued by the external auditor but serves as a foundational preparation standard to ensure readiness *before* the PBC list is received.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **SEFA** | Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards. A mandatory schedule for non-profits expending $750,000 or more in federal funds (2 CFR 200.510). |
| **Single Audit** | An audit that includes both the entity's financial statements and its federal awards, required if federal expenditures exceed $750,000. |
| **PBC List** | "Provided by Client" list. A request list sent by the external auditor detailing specific documents needed for testing. |
| **Management Representation Letter** | A letter from the Organization's management to the auditor confirming the accuracy of financial statements. |
| **Functional Expenses** | The classification of expenses by function (Program Services, Management & General, Fundraising) as required by GAAP for non-profits. |

### 4. Preparation Checklist Procedures

The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) must ensure the following items are reconciled, organized, and available in the Organization’s secure digital repository prior to the start of fieldwork.

#### 4.1 Phase I: Planning & Governance
*   [ ] **Engagement Letter:** Signed copy of the audit engagement letter on file.
*   [ ] **Board Minutes:** Compile all Board of Directors and Committee meeting minutes for the fiscal year (per **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.1**).
*   [ ] **Bylaws & Amendments:** Ensure the auditor has the current version of the Bylaws (specifically **Article X** if any Fundamental Actions occurred).
*   [ ] **Conflict of Interest:** Compile signed Annual Disclosure Statements for all Directors and Officers (per **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1**).
*   [ ] **Legal Representation:** Prepare a list of all legal counsel retained during the year for the attorney confirmation letter.
*   [ ] **Whistleblower Logs:** Summary of any reports filed under the Whistleblower Protection Policy (or confirmation of none).

#### 4.2 Phase II: Cash & Cash Equivalents
*   [ ] **Bank Reconciliations:** Final reconciliations for all bank accounts as of December 31.
*   [ ] **Bank Statements:** PDF copies of all 12 months of statements for all accounts.
*   [ ] **Cut-off Testing:** List of the last checks written/EFTs processed in the fiscal year and the first checks/EFTs of the new year.
*   [ ] **Petty Cash:** Year-end count sheet signed by the custodian.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Grants & Federal Compliance (2 CFR 200)
*   [ ] **SEFA Preparation:** Draft the Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA).
    *   *Must include:* Assistance Listing Number (ALN), Pass-through entity name, Award number, and Total federal expenditures.
*   [ ] **Grant Agreements:** Copies of all new grant agreements, amendments, and No-Cost Extensions (NCE) executed in the fiscal year.
*   [ ] **NICRA:** Copy of the current Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement (or election of the 10% de minimis rate).
*   [ ] **Subrecipient Monitoring:** Evidence of risk assessments and monitoring performed on any sub-grantees (if applicable).
*   [ ] **Time & Effort:** Certifications/Personnel Activity Reports (PARs) for all employees paid via federal funds.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Revenue & Support
*   [ ] **Contributions Schedule:** List of all donors contributing >$5,000 (Schedule B data).
*   [ ] **Restrictions Analysis:** Schedule of Net Assets with Donor Restrictions (Temporary vs. Perpetual) and releases from restriction.
*   [ ] **In-Kind Donations:** Valuation documentation for contributed services or goods (specifically related to open-source resources per **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**).
*   [ ] **Accounts Receivable:** Aged receivable listing and analysis of allowance for doubtful accounts.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Expenses & Accounts Payable
*   [ ] **Accounts Payable:** Aged AP listing as of December 31.
*   [ ] **Accrued Expenses:** Schedule of expenses incurred but not paid (e.g., accrued vacation, bonuses).
*   [ ] **Functional Expense Allocation:** Spreadsheet allocating expenses to:
    *   Program Services (Education/Training, Community).
    *   Management & General.
    *   Fundraising.
*   [ ] **Credit Cards:** Year-end credit card statements and reconciliations.
*   [ ] **Procurement Files:** For federal purchases >$10,000, ensure quotes/bids and suspension/debarment checks are attached to the voucher.

#### 4.6 Phase VI: Payroll & Human Resources
*   [ ] **Payroll Register:** Annual summary matching the General Ledger.
*   [ ] **Tax Filings:** Copies of filed Forms 941 (Quarterly), 940 (FUTA), and state unemployment filings.
*   [ ] **W-2/1099 Reconciliation:** Reconciliation of W-3 and 1096 transmittals to the General Ledger.
*   [ ] **Compensation Review:** Board minutes approving Officer compensation (per **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.1**).

#### 4.7 Phase VII: Fixed Assets
*   [ ] **Asset Roll-forward:** Schedule showing Beginning Balance + Additions - Disposals - Depreciation = Ending Balance.
*   [ ] **Depreciation Schedule:** Calculation of depreciation expense for the year.
*   [ ] **Inventory:** If applicable (e.g., swag, training materials), year-end inventory count sheets.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Primary owner of the audit relationship. Responsible for completing this checklist and generating the SEFA. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides Board-level oversight. Reviews the draft audit report and SEFA prior to final issuance. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Signs the Management Representation Letter and ensures staff availability for auditor inquiries. |
| **Audit Committee** | Selects the auditor and formally accepts the final audit report. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adequately prepare for the annual audit may result in:
1.  **Increased Audit Fees:** Due to additional hours required by the external firm to clean up records.
2.  **Compliance Findings:** Potential "Material Weakness" or "Significant Deficiency" findings in the Single Audit report.
3.  **Loss of Funding:** Non-compliance with **2 CFR 200.501** may result in the suspension or termination of federal grant awards.

Employees found to be negligent in maintaining financial records required for this checklist may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
