### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a standardized mechanism for determining the nature of a relationship between **Study GRC Inc.** (the "Organization") and a third-party entity when expending federal grant funds.

Pursuant to **2 CFR § 200.331** (Subrecipient and contractor determinations), the Organization must make case-by-case determinations whether the party receiving the funds is cast in the role of a **Subrecipient** (carrying out a portion of a federal award) or a **Contractor** (providing goods or services for the Organization's own use). Misclassification can lead to significant audit findings, disallowed costs, and failure to comply with federal monitoring requirements.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to **all** proposed agreements, contracts, or memorandums of understanding (MOUs) where the funding source originates from a Federal Award.

*   **Applies to:** All Program Managers, the Finance Department, and Operational Officers involved in procurement or grant administration.
*   **Exclusions:** Procurement actions funded entirely by unrestricted, non-federal donations (e.g., private member dues), unless the Organization voluntarily chooses to apply federal standards.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pass-Through Entity (PTE)** | Study GRC Inc., when it provides a subaward to a subrecipient to carry out part of a federal program. |
| **Subrecipient** | A non-federal entity that receives a subaward from a PTE to carry out part of a federal program. Subrecipients have their own performance measured against the objectives of the federal program. |
| **Contractor** | An entity that receives a contract for the purpose of obtaining goods and services for the Organization's own use and creates a procurement relationship. |
| **Subaward** | An award provided by a PTE to a subrecipient for the subrecipient to carry out part of a Federal award received by the PTE. |
| **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)** | Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards. |

### 4. Procedures and Determination Checklist

#### 4.1 Determination Protocol
1.  **Timing:** This determination must be completed **before** any agreement is signed or funds are encumbered.
2.  **Responsibility:** The Requesting Program Manager must complete the checklist; the CFO or Grants Manager must review and approve the final classification.
3.  **Substance Over Form:** In accordance with 2 CFR § 200.331(c), the substance of the relationship is more important than the form of the agreement. Calling a document a "Contract" does not make the entity a Contractor if they meet the definition of a Subrecipient.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Program Manager** | Initiates the relationship, defines the Scope of Work, and completes the initial draft of this checklist based on technical requirements. |
| **Grants Manager** | Reviews the checklist for accuracy against 2 CFR 200 definitions. Ensures the correct legal instrument (Subaward vs. Contract) is drafted. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Provides final approval on the classification. Ensures that if a Subrecipient is selected, the entity is not suspended or debarred (SAM.gov check). |
| **Board Treasurer** | Reviews determinations for high-value agreements (>$250,000) as part of the internal audit function. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to correctly classify a relationship may result in the repayment of funds to the Federal agency (disallowed costs).

*   **Internal:** Employees found to be willfully bypassing this determination process or falsifying checklist responses may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **External:** Misclassification does not absolve the Organization of its responsibilities as a Pass-Through Entity. If a Contractor is later deemed a Subrecipient by an auditor, Study GRC Inc. is liable for ensuring that entity complied with all federal regulations retroactively.
