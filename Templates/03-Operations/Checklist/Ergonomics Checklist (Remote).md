### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to provide a standardized self-assessment tool for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") personnel operating in a remote capacity. This checklist is designed to:
1.  Identify potential ergonomic hazards in home office environments to prevent Musculoskeletal Disorders (MSDs).
2.  Ensure compliance with the Organization's duty of care regarding workplace safety, aligning with general OSHA guidance for computer workstations.
3.  Facilitate the request process for necessary equipment adjustments under the *Remote Work Equipment Provision Policy*.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   All full-time and part-time employees working remotely.
*   Long-term contractors (serving >3 months) who utilize Organization-provided equipment.
*   Operational Officers and Board Directors performing significant administrative duties.

**Exclusions:**
*   Occasional volunteers or community participants.
*   Short-term tasks performed on mobile devices (e.g., checking email while traveling).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Neutral Posture** | A comfortable working posture where joints are naturally aligned, reducing stress and strain on the muscles, tendons, and skeletal system. |
| **MSD** | Musculoskeletal Disorder. Injuries or disorders of the muscles, nerves, tendons, joints, cartilage, and spinal discs (e.g., Carpal Tunnel Syndrome, Tendonitis). |
| **Remote Worksite** | The specific area within the individual's home designated for the performance of official Organization duties. |
| **20-20-20 Rule** | A visual fatigue reduction technique: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds. |

### 4. Self-Assessment Checklist

Personnel must complete this checklist upon hire, upon moving to a new home office location, and annually thereafter.

#### 4.1 Seating (The Chair)
*   **[ ]** **Back Support:** The chair provides adequate lumbar (lower back) support. The backrest maintains the natural curve of the spine.
*   **[ ]** **Seat Height:** The chair height allows feet to rest flat on the floor or on a footrest.
*   **[ ]** **Leg Clearance:** There is no pressure on the back of the knees. There is a 2-3 finger gap between the edge of the seat and the back of the knees.
*   **[ ]** **Armrests:** If present, armrests allow shoulders to be relaxed and elbows to stay close to the body (not shrugged or flared). They do not interfere with the desk.

#### 4.2 Work Surface (The Desk)
*   **[ ]** **Height:** The desk height allows typing with forearms parallel to the floor and wrists straight.
*   **[ ]** **Clearance:** There is sufficient clearance under the desk for legs and feet (no storage boxes or clutter restricting movement).
*   **[ ]** **Reach Zones:** Frequently used items (phone, notepad) are within easy reach (primary zone) to avoid excessive stretching or twisting.

#### 4.3 Monitor & Visual Setup
*   **[ ]** **Height:** The top of the monitor screen is at or slightly below eye level. (Bifocal wearers may need the screen lower).
*   **[ ]** **Distance:** The monitor is approximately an arm’s length (18–24 inches) away from the face.
*   **[ ]** **Alignment:** The monitor is centered directly in front of the user (no twisting of the neck).
*   **[ ]** **Dual Monitors:** If using two monitors, they are placed side-by-side in a slight "V" shape, meeting directly in front of the user.
*   **[ ]** **Glare:** The screen is positioned to minimize glare from windows or overhead lights.

#### 4.4 Input Devices (Keyboard & Mouse)
*   **[ ]** **Keyboard Position:** The keyboard is directly in front of the user.
*   **[ ]** **Wrist Posture:** Wrists are straight and in line with the forearms while typing (not bent up, down, or to the side).
*   **[ ]** **Mouse Location:** The mouse is positioned immediately next to the keyboard to prevent overreaching.
*   **[ ]** **Grip:** The mouse is held loosely; the user does not squeeze the mouse.

#### 4.5 Laptop Usage
*   **[ ]** **External Devices:** If using a laptop as a primary computer for >2 hours per day, an external keyboard and mouse are used.
*   **[ ]** **Elevation:** The laptop screen is raised (via a stand or stack of books) to eye level, or an external monitor is utilized.

#### 4.6 Environment & Safety
*   **[ ]** **Lighting:** The room has sufficient lighting for reading documents without straining.
*   **[ ]** **Trip Hazards:** Electrical cords, cables, and loose rugs are secured and out of walkways.
*   **[ ]** **Fire Safety:** The workspace has a functioning smoke detector nearby and a clear path to an exit.
*   **[ ]** **Temperature/Air:** The workspace is well-ventilated and maintained at a comfortable temperature.

#### 4.7 Work Habits & Recovery
*   **[ ]** **Micro-Breaks:** I take short breaks (standing, stretching) at least once per hour.
*   **[ ]** **Eye Rest:** I practice the 20-20-20 rule to reduce eye strain.
*   **[ ]** **Hydration:** I maintain hydration throughout the workday.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Remote Employee / Contractor** | Conduct this self-assessment honestly. Adjust the workspace to meet these standards. Report persistent pain or discomfort to HR immediately. |
| **Manager** | Review completed checklists. Approve reasonable requests for ergonomic equipment (e.g., laptop stands, external keyboards) within the department budget. |
| **HR / Operations** | Maintain completed checklists in the personnel file. Provide guidance on equipment procurement in compliance with *2 CFR 200* (for grant-funded positions). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Certification of Truthfulness:** By submitting this checklist, the individual certifies that the information provided is accurate and that their remote worksite meets the safety standards required by Study GRC Inc.

**Corrective Action:** If a workspace fails to meet critical safety standards (e.g., fire hazards, electrical risks) and cannot be remediated, the Organization reserves the right to revoke the Remote Work Agreement.

**Equipment Provision:** The Organization may provide specific ergonomic equipment based on the results of this assessment, subject to the *Remote Work Equipment Provision Policy* and budget availability.
