### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a mandatory standardized process for determining the correct classification of workers as either **Employees (W-2)** or **Independent Contractors (1099)** prior to the commencement of services.

Misclassification poses severe risks to Study GRC Inc., including liability for unpaid taxes, penalties, interest, denial of workers' compensation claims, and potential disqualification of tax-exempt status. This checklist aligns with the **IRS Common Law "Reality Test"** and the Department of Labor’s economic reality standards to ensure compliance with federal and state labor laws.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all Hiring Managers, Department Heads, and Operational Officers seeking to engage an individual to perform services for Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:**
*   This checklist does not apply to the engagement of registered corporations (C-Corps) or LLCs taxed as C-Corps/S-Corps where a formal B2B Master Service Agreement (MSA) exists, provided the entity has multiple clients and distinct business operations.
*   This checklist does not apply to unpaid Volunteers (refer to *HR-POL-065 Volunteer Recruitment Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee (W-2)** | An individual who performs services for the Organization where the Organization has the right to control and direct **what** will be done and **how** it will be done. |
| **Independent Contractor (1099)** | An individual or entity in business for themselves who provides services to the public. The Organization has the right to control or direct only the **result** of the work, not what will be done and how it will be done. |
| **Behavioral Control** | Facts that show whether the business has a right to direct and control how the worker does the task for which the worker is hired. |
| **Financial Control** | Facts that show whether the business has a right to control the business aspects of the worker's job. |
| **SS-8 Determination** | A formal request to the IRS to determine the status of a worker for purposes of federal employment taxes and income tax withholding. |

### 4. Classification Assessment Checklist

Hiring Managers must complete this assessment **before** an offer is made. If the distinction is unclear, the worker must be classified as an Employee (W-2) to minimize risk.

#### 4.1 Section A: Behavioral Control (The "How")
*Instructions: A "Yes" answer suggests Employee status.*

| # | Question | Yes | No |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A1** | Will Study GRC Inc. provide specific instructions on **when, where, and how** the work is to be performed (e.g., mandatory working hours, specific software workflow steps)? | ☐ | ☐ |
| **A2** | Will the Organization provide training to the worker? (Contractors are hired for existing expertise; Employees are trained to perform tasks a specific way). | ☐ | ☐ |
| **A3** | Is the worker required to perform the services personally? (Contractors can often subcontract or hire their own assistants). | ☐ | ☐ |
| **A4** | Does the Organization determine the order or sequence in which tasks must be performed? | ☐ | ☐ |
| **A5** | Is the worker required to submit regular oral or written reports (e.g., timesheets, status updates) accounting for their time (rather than just deliverables)? | ☐ | ☐ |

#### 4.2 Section B: Financial Control (The "Money")
*Instructions: A "Yes" answer suggests Employee status.*

| # | Question | Yes | No |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **B1** | Is the worker paid by the hour, week, or month? (Contractors are typically paid a flat fee per job or project milestone). | ☐ | ☐ |
| **B2** | Will Study GRC Inc. reimburse the worker for business/travel expenses? (Contractors typically cover their own overhead). | ☐ | ☐ |
| **B3** | Does the Organization provide the equipment, tools, or materials (e.g., laptop, software licenses) required to do the work? | ☐ | ☐ |
| **B4** | Is the worker guaranteed a regular wage amount regardless of the quality/outcome of the work? | ☐ | ☐ |
| **B5** | Is the worker restricted from seeking other business opportunities or working for other clients during this engagement? | ☐ | ☐ |

#### 4.3 Section C: Type of Relationship
*Instructions: A "Yes" answer suggests Employee status.*

| # | Question | Yes | No |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **C1** | Is the work performed a key aspect of Study GRC Inc.’s regular business/mission? (e.g., Developing GRC curriculum vs. fixing the office plumbing). | ☐ | ☐ |
| **C2** | Will the worker receive employee-type benefits (e.g., insurance, pension plan, paid vacation, sick pay)? | ☐ | ☐ |
| **C3** | Is there an expectation that the relationship will continue indefinitely, rather than for a specific project period? | ☐ | ☐ |
| **C4** | Does the worker lack a distinct business identity (e.g., no business website, no business cards, no EIN)? | ☐ | ☐ |

#### 4.4 Scoring and Determination
*   **Preponderance of "Yes":** The worker is likely an **Employee (W-2)**.
    *   *Action:* Process through standard Payroll/HR onboarding.
*   **Preponderance of "No":** The worker is likely an **Independent Contractor (1099)**.
    *   *Action:* Execute an Independent Contractor Agreement (ICA). Obtain Form W-9. Ensure no taxes are withheld.
*   **Mixed Results:**
    *   *Action:* Escalate to the CFO and Legal Counsel for review. If ambiguity remains, default to **Employee** status to ensure compliance.

### 5. Procedures

#### 5.1 Pre-Engagement Review
1.  The Hiring Manager completes the checklist in Section 4.
2.  The completed checklist is attached to the *Personnel Requisition Form* or *Contract Request Form*.
3.  The CFO reviews the checklist against the proposed Scope of Work.

#### 5.2 Documentation and Retention
1.  **For Contractors:** This checklist must be retained in the Vendor File for a minimum of **seven (7) years** (per *GOV-STD-013 Records Retention Schedule*) to defend against potential IRS or TWC audits.
2.  **For Employees:** This checklist may be discarded once the employee is successfully onboarded into the payroll system.

#### 5.3 Intellectual Property Implications
1.  **Employees:** Per Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3, work created by employees is automatically "work made for hire" and owned by the Organization.
2.  **Contractors:** The Independent Contractor Agreement **must** contain specific assignment language transferring IP rights to Study GRC Inc. Failure to classify correctly may result in the contractor retaining ownership of the work product.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Accurately completes the checklist based on the actual expected working relationship, not budgetary preference. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews all checklists for 1099 determinations; authorizes the engagement; ensures W-9 collection. |
| **Human Resources (HR)** | Onboards W-2 employees; ensures labor law compliance (posters, notices, I-9s). |
| **Executive Director** | Final arbiter for "grey area" classifications; authorizes filing of IRS Form SS-8 if a formal determination is required. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this standard may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

**Legal Consequence Warning:**
If the IRS or Texas Workforce Commission reclassifies a contractor as an employee, Study GRC Inc. may be liable for:
*   Unpaid federal and state income taxes (retroactive).
*   Unpaid Social Security and Medicare taxes (employer and employee share).
*   Unpaid unemployment insurance taxes.
*   Unpaid workers' compensation premiums.
*   Penalties for failure to file correct information returns.
