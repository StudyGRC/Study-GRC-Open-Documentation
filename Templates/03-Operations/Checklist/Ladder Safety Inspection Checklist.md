### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a mandatory inspection protocol for all portable ladders used in Study GRC Inc. operations. This checklist ensures compliance with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.23 (Ladders)**, mitigates the risk of falls—one of the leading causes of workplace injury—and ensures that all equipment provided to employees and volunteers is structurally sound and safe for use.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to all portable ladders (stepladders, extension ladders, and multi-purpose ladders) owned, leased, or utilized by Study GRC Inc. personnel.

*   **Applies to:** All employees, contractors, and volunteers performing tasks on behalf of the Organization (e.g., event setup, storage unit management, facility maintenance).
*   **Exclusions:** Permanent fixed stairs, step stools less than 32 inches in height, and ladders not used for organizational business.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Competent Person** | An individual capable of identifying existing and predictable hazards in the surroundings or working conditions and who has authorization to take prompt corrective measures to eliminate them. |
| **Stepladder** | A self-supporting portable ladder, non-adjustable in length, having flat steps and a hinged back. |
| **Extension Ladder** | A non-self-supporting portable ladder adjustable in length. |
| **Tagout** | The process of placing a warning tag (e.g., "DANGER - DO NOT USE") on a piece of equipment to prevent its use until it is repaired or destroyed. |

### 4. Inspection Standards & Checklist procedures

Pursuant to **OSHA 1910.23(b)(9)**, ladders must be inspected before the initial use in each work shift, and more frequently as necessary, to identify any visible defects that could compromise their safe use.

#### 4.1 General Inspection Criteria (All Ladders)
*   **Labels/Markings:** Safety labels and weight capacity ratings must be legible.
*   **Cleanliness:** Ladder must be free of oil, grease, wet paint, or other slipping hazards.
*   **Structural Integrity:** No cracks, splits, corrosion, or bends in side rails.
*   **Hardware:** All bolts, rivets, and fasteners must be tight and secure.

#### 4.2 Stepladder Specifics
*   **Spreaders:** Metal spreaders must lock securely in the open position.
*   **Steps:** Steps must be stable, not loose, and have slip-resistant surfaces.
*   **Feet:** Anti-slip safety feet must be present and in good condition.

#### 4.3 Extension Ladder Specifics
*   **Rungs:** Rungs must be tight and not rotate.
*   **Locks/Pawls:** Rung locks must engage and hold sections securely.
*   **Rope/Pulley:** Rope must not be frayed; pulley must operate smoothly.
*   **Shoes/Spurs:** Safety shoes must pivot correctly and have sufficient tread.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Ladder User (Staff/Volunteer)** | Perform a visual inspection using this checklist prior to every use. Report defects immediately. |
| **Event Manager / Site Lead** | Ensure ladders provided at events are inspected prior to setup. Verify that volunteers are not using personal, unverified ladders. |
| **Operations Manager** | Maintain the inventory of ladders. Arrange for the destruction and disposal of ladders deemed unsafe. |
| **Safety Officer** | Conduct annual audits of ladder inventory and inspection logs. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

#### 6.1 Defective Equipment Protocol
If a ladder fails inspection:
1.  **Immediately** remove it from service.
2.  Attach a "DANGER - DO NOT USE" tag (or similar visual warning) to the ladder.
3.  Notify the Operations Manager or Site Lead.
4.  The ladder must be repaired by a certified professional or destroyed (cut in half) to prevent future use. **Field repairs (e.g., duct tape, wire) are strictly prohibited.**

#### 6.2 Disciplinary Action
Failure to inspect equipment or the intentional use of tagged-out/defective equipment constitutes a violation of the **Master EHS Policy**. This may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or dismissal from volunteer service.
