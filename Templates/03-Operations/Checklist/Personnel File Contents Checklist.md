### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this checklist is to standardize the creation, maintenance, and auditing of employee personnel files at Study GRC Inc. Adherence to this checklist ensures compliance with federal and state recordkeeping requirements (including EEOC, FLSA, and IRS regulations), facilitates efficient audits, and mitigates legal risks associated with employment disputes. This document further mandates the strict segregation of medical and immigration records from general personnel files to comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and anti-discrimination laws.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to all **W-2 Employees** (full-time, part-time, and temporary) of Study GRC Inc.

*   **Exclusions:**
    *   **Independent Contractors (1099):** Contractor agreements and records must be stored in the *Vendor/Procurement* file system to avoid worker misclassification risks.
    *   **Volunteers/Board Members:** Volunteer records are maintained separately under the *Volunteer Management* system.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **General Personnel File** | The primary folder containing documents related to an employee's hiring, performance, and employment history. Accessible by HR and the employee's direct supervisor (on a need-to-know basis). |
| **Confidential Medical File** | A strictly segregated file containing any health-related information. Access is restricted to HR and the Executive Director only. |
| **I-9 File** | A separate master file (or digital repository) containing Form I-9s for all active employees. Kept separate to facilitate government inspections without revealing other personnel data. |
| **Background Check File** | A separate confidential file containing Consumer Reports and investigative results, kept separate to comply with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA). |

### 4. Checklist Requirements

The following documents must be collected and filed within the appropriate category. **Do not commingle categories.**

#### 4.1 Category A: General Personnel File (Basic Employment History)
*This file contains the core employment history. It is subject to inspection by the employee upon request per Texas Labor Code § 52.053.*

**Hiring & Onboarding**
*   [ ] **Employment Application / Resume:** The final version submitted by the candidate.
*   [ ] **Job Description:** Signed by the employee acknowledging responsibilities.
*   [ ] **Offer Letter:** Signed and dated by both parties.
*   [ ] **Emergency Contact Form:** Current contact details.
*   [ ] **Employee Handbook Acknowledgment:** Signed receipt of the current version.
*   [ ] **Policy Acknowledgments:** Signed acknowledgments for specific policies (e.g., Acceptable Use Policy, Conflict of Interest, Remote Work Agreement).
*   [ ] **Relocation Agreements:** If applicable.

**Performance & Development**
*   [ ] **Performance Evaluations:** Annual and mid-year reviews signed by employee and manager.
*   [ ] **Goal Setting Documents:** OKRs or KPI sheets.
*   [ ] **Commendations/Awards:** Copies of recognition letters or certificates.
*   [ ] **Training Records:** Certificates of completion for mandatory training (e.g., Sexual Harassment Prevention, Cybersecurity Awareness).
*   [ ] **Disciplinary Actions:** Written warnings, Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs), and notes from disciplinary meetings.
*   [ ] **Attendance Records:** Documentation of unexcused absences or tardiness (excluding medical reasons).

**Compensation & Status Changes**
*   [ ] **Compensation History:** Records of pay raises, bonuses, and salary adjustments.
*   [ ] **Promotion/Transfer Letters:** Documentation of role changes.
*   [ ] **Remote Work Authorization:** Approved requests for telecommuting.

**Termination/Separation (Post-Employment)**
*   [ ] **Resignation Letter:** Original written notice from employee.
*   [ ] **Termination Notice:** Copy of letter provided to employee (if involuntary).
*   [ ] **Exit Interview Notes:** Documentation from the exit interview.
*   [ ] **Property Return Checklist:** Confirmation that laptop, keys, and data were returned.
*   [ ] **COBRA Notice:** Proof of receipt of benefits continuation notice.
*   [ ] **Final Wage Payment Calculation:** Proof of final payout including accrued PTO (if applicable by policy).

#### 4.2 Category B: Confidential Medical File (RESTRICTED ACCESS)
*Must be stored separately from the General Personnel File. Access is strictly limited.*

*   [ ] **Medical Leave Requests:** FMLA or ADA leave application forms.
*   [ ] **Doctor’s Notes:** Work release forms or restrictions/accommodations notes.
*   [ ] **ADA Accommodation Requests:** Documentation of the interactive process and agreed accommodations.
*   [ ] **Workers’ Compensation Claims:** First Report of Injury and related correspondence.
*   [ ] **Drug Testing Results:** Reports from pre-employment or reasonable suspicion testing.
*   [ ] **Health Insurance Enrollment:** Forms revealing specific health selections or dependent health info.

#### 4.3 Category C: Payroll & Tax Records
*Often maintained by the Finance Department, but accessible to HR.*

*   [ ] **Form W-4:** Federal tax withholding certificate (current and historical).
*   [ ] **Direct Deposit Authorization:** Bank account information.
*   [ ] **Garnishment Orders:** Court orders for wage garnishments or child support.
*   [ ] **Time Sheets:** Weekly/Bi-weekly records of hours worked (Non-Exempt employees).
*   [ ] **Payroll Change Forms:** Authorizations for deduction changes.

#### 4.4 Category D: I-9 Compliance File
*Maintained in a single master binder or isolated digital folder for all employees.*

*   [ ] **Form I-9:** Employment Eligibility Verification.
*   [ ] **Supporting Documentation:** Copies of ID documents (e.g., Passport, Driver’s License) *only if* the organization has a policy to retain these copies consistently for all employees.

#### 4.5 Category E: Background Check File (Confidential)
*Maintained separately to comply with FCRA.*

*   [ ] **Disclosure and Authorization Form:** Signed consent for the background check.
*   [ ] **Background Check Results:** The actual report from the consumer reporting agency.
*   [ ] **Adverse Action Notices:** Copies of Pre-Adverse and Final Adverse Action letters (if hiring decision was impacted).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **HR Manager / Ops Lead** | Responsible for the creation, maintenance, and security of all personnel files. Ensures files are locked (physical) or encrypted (digital) and that medical files are segregated. |
| **Executive Director** | Conducts annual audits of personnel files to ensure completeness and compliance with document retention policies. |
| **Employee** | Responsible for notifying HR of changes to personal information (address, emergency contact) and signing required acknowledgments. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews the checklist annually to ensure alignment with changing state and federal labor laws. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain accurate and complete personnel files constitutes a violation of Study GRC Inc. policy and may result in disciplinary action for the responsible manager.

**Unauthorized Access:** Any employee found accessing **Confidential Medical Files** or **Background Check Files** without express authorization and a legitimate business need will be subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment and potential legal action.

**Record Retention:** All records listed in this checklist must be retained in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy* (HR-POL-012) and *Bylaws Article VIII*. Generally, personnel records are retained for the duration of employment plus 7 years.
