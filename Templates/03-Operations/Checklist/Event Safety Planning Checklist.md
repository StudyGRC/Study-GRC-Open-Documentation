### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a mandatory, standardized framework for identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks associated with events hosted by Study GRC Inc. This checklist ensures compliance with the Organization's duty of care, minimizes legal liability, and prioritizes the physical and digital safety of all participants, with specific emphasis on youth protection and accessibility.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to **all** events organized, sponsored, or officially endorsed by Study GRC Inc., including:
*   **Physical Events:** Meetups, workshops, conferences, and fundraisers.
*   **Virtual Events:** Webinars, live streams, and online training sessions.
*   **Hybrid Events:** Combinations of physical and virtual participation.

This checklist must be completed by the designated Event Lead and reviewed by the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) prior to the event date.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Lead** | The individual (staff or volunteer) primarily responsible for the planning and execution of a specific event. |
| **Duty of Care** | The legal obligation to adhere to a standard of reasonable care while performing any acts that could foreseeably harm others. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | A youth protection policy requiring a minimum of two authorized, background-checked adults to be present during any interaction with minors. |
| **Incident** | An occurrence that results in, or could have resulted in, injury, property damage, reputational harm, or legal liability. |

### 4. Safety Planning Checklist

The Event Lead must verify all applicable items below. Items marked **[M]** are Mandatory for all events. Items marked **[P]** are for Physical Events only. Items marked **[V]** are for Virtual Events only.

#### 4.1 Phase I: Risk Assessment & Administration (4-8 Weeks Out)
*   **[M] Event Classification:** Determine if the event is Low Risk (virtual, adults only) or High Risk (physical, minors present, alcohol served).
*   **[M] Budget Approval:** Ensure safety costs (security, insurance riders, EMTs) are included in the budget per **Financial Policies**.
*   **[M] Insurance Verification:** Confirm General Liability coverage extends to the specific venue and activity type.
    *   *Action:* If alcohol is served, verify Host Liquor Liability coverage.
*   **[M] Youth Protection Assessment:**
    *   Will minors be present? [ ] Yes [ ] No
    *   If **Yes**, trigger **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy** protocols immediately.
*   **[P] Contract Review:** Submit venue contracts to the Executive Director for review regarding indemnification clauses (Ref: **Bylaws Article VII**).

#### 4.2 Phase II: Physical Venue Safety (Physical Events Only)
*   **[P] Accessibility Audit (ADA):**
    *   [ ] Wheelchair accessible entrance, restrooms, and seating.
    *   [ ] Acoustics/lighting suitable for sensory sensitivities.
*   **[P] Emergency Egress:**
    *   [ ] Identify all fire exits.
    *   [ ] Ensure exits are unlocked and unobstructed.
*   **[P] Medical Preparedness:**
    *   [ ] First Aid Kit verified and stocked (ANSI Z308.1 compliant).
    *   [ ] AED (Automated External Defibrillator) location identified.
    *   [ ] Designated CPR/First Aid certified staff member identified.
*   **[P] Capacity Control:**
    *   [ ] Verify maximum occupancy load with Fire Marshal/Venue.
    *   [ ] Establish a method for counting attendees (e.g., clicker, badge scan).
*   **[P] Hygiene & Sanitation:**
    *   [ ] Hand sanitizer stations available.
    *   [ ] Restrooms clean and stocked.
    *   [ ] Communicable disease protocols (if applicable) posted.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Virtual Environment Safety (Virtual Events Only)
*   **[V] Platform Security:**
    *   [ ] Meeting password/passcode enabled.
    *   [ ] "Waiting Room" feature enabled to screen entrants.
    *   [ ] Screen sharing restricted to Host/Co-hosts only.
*   **[V] Moderation Plan:**
    *   [ ] Designated Moderator assigned (separate from the Speaker).
    *   [ ] Protocol for removing disruptive participants (Zoom-bombing response) reviewed.
*   **[V] Privacy & Recording:**
    *   [ ] Notify attendees if the session is being recorded (Ref: **Data Privacy Policy**).
    *   [ ] Ensure no PII of minors is visible on screen or in chat.
*   **[V] Accessibility:**
    *   [ ] Live captioning (auto or manual) enabled.
    *   [ ] Presentation materials compatible with screen readers.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Youth Protection (If Minors Present)
*   **[M] Background Checks:** Verify all staff/volunteers interacting with youth have passed a background check within the last 12 months.
*   **[M] Two-Deep Leadership:** Confirm schedule ensures no adult is ever alone with a minor (1-on-1) in a private physical or digital space.
*   **[M] Parental Consent:**
    *   [ ] Signed Liability Waivers collected for all minors.
    *   [ ] Emergency contact information collected and accessible on-site.
    *   [ ] Photo/Video Release forms signed by parents/guardians.
*   **[P] Pick-Up/Drop-Off:** Establish a secure sign-in/sign-out procedure for custodial guardians.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Crisis Response & Communication
*   **[M] Incident Reporting:** Ensure **Incident Report Forms** are printed (or digitally accessible) and staff knows how to use them.
*   **[M] Code of Conduct:**
    *   [ ] Study GRC Code of Conduct displayed or linked in registration.
    *   [ ] Anti-Harassment Policy explicitly communicated to attendees.
*   **[P] Emergency Briefing:**
    *   [ ] Brief staff/volunteers on "Run, Hide, Fight" (Active Shooter) and severe weather shelter locations.
    *   [ ] Designate a rally point for evacuations.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Lead** | Completes this checklist; serves as the primary Point of Contact (POC) for safety during the event. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Reviews completed checklist; ensures community culture and Code of Conduct are integrated into safety planning. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves high-risk events; signs venue contracts and insurance riders. |
| **Volunteers** | Adhere to safety briefings; report hazards or suspicious behavior to the Event Lead immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to complete this checklist prior to an event may result in the cancellation of the event by the Board of Directors or Executive Director.

Staff or volunteers found to be negligent in enforcing safety protocols—particularly those regarding Youth Protection or Fire Safety—will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer service, and potential legal action.
