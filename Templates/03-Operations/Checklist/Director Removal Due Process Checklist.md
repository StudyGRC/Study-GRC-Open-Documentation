### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this checklist is to ensure that any action taken to remove a Director from the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. adheres strictly to the Organization’s Bylaws and the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC). Adherence to this checklist is mandatory to guarantee **procedural due process**, mitigate liability, prevent wrongful termination claims, and ensure the legal validity of the Board’s composition.

### 2. Scope
This document applies to the removal of a Director **for cause** by the Board of Directors or the Voting Members.
*   **Inclusions:** Investigations, notice requirements, hearing procedures, voting thresholds, and post-removal documentation.
*   **Exclusions:** Voluntary resignation (see *Director Resignation Procedure*); removal of Operational Officers who are not Directors (see *Operational Officer Removal Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cause** | Grounds for removal including, but not limited to, violation of Bylaws, failure to attend meetings, ethical violations, or breach of fiduciary duty (Bylaws § 3.6). |
| **Interested Director** | The Director subject to the removal proceedings. |
| **Remaining Directors** | All Directors currently in office, excluding the Interested Director. |
| **Due Process** | The legal requirement that the Organization respects all legal rights owed to a person, specifically providing notice of charges and an opportunity to be heard before a decision is made. |

### 4. Checklist Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Preliminary Assessment & Investigation
*Before a removal vote is scheduled, the Board must establish that "Cause" exists.*

*   [ ] **Verify "Cause" Threshold:** Confirm the alleged behavior constitutes "Cause" under Bylaws § 3.6 (e.g., Bylaw violation, absenteeism, ethical breach).
*   [ ] **Document Evidence:** Compile written documentation of the infraction(s) (e.g., meeting minutes showing absence, emails demonstrating ethical breach, witness statements).
*   [ ] **Legal Review:** (Recommended) Consult with Legal Counsel to review the evidence and ensure it meets the legal standard for removal under Texas law.
*   [ ] **Conflict Check:** Ensure the Directors initiating the investigation do not have a personal conflict of interest regarding the Interested Director (outside of the professional governance issue).

#### 4.2 Phase II: Notice of Meeting
*Strict adherence to notice timelines is critical. Failure to provide proper notice can void the removal.*

*   [ ] **Draft Notice of Meeting:** Prepare a formal notice for a Regular or Special Meeting of the Board.
*   [ ] **State Specific Purpose:** The notice **must** explicitly state that "Removal of a Director" is on the agenda (TBOC § 22.211).
*   [ ] **Include Statement of Charges:** Attach a summary of the "Cause" (charges) to the notice sent to the Interested Director, ensuring they know exactly why removal is being considered.
*   [ ] **Verify Timeline:** Ensure notice is delivered at least **10 days** but no more than **60 days** prior to the meeting (Bylaws § 2.5 applied to Special Meetings).
*   [ ] **Delivery Method:** Send notice via the method prescribed in the Bylaws (usually email to the address on record) and retain proof of delivery.

#### 4.3 Phase III: The Hearing (Meeting Proceedings)
*The meeting must provide the Interested Director a fair opportunity to defend themselves.*

*   [ ] **Establish Quorum:** Verify a quorum is present.
    *   *Note:* The Interested Director counts toward the quorum for the meeting generally, but special voting rules apply to the removal itself.
*   [ ] **Executive Session:** Move the meeting into Executive Session (Board Members and Legal Counsel only) to discuss the personnel matter.
*   [ ] **Presentation of Charges:** The Chair or designated Director presents the evidence of "Cause."
*   [ ] **Opportunity to be Heard:** The Interested Director must be given a reasonable time limit (e.g., 15-30 minutes) to present a defense, explanation, or rebuttal.
*   [ ] **Q&A:** Remaining Directors may ask clarifying questions of the Interested Director.
*   [ ] **Deliberation (Exclusion):** The Interested Director must leave the meeting (virtual or physical) to allow the Remaining Directors to deliberate freely.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: The Vote
*Voting thresholds must follow Bylaws § 3.6 exactly.*

*   [ ] **Call for Vote:** The Chair calls for a vote on the motion to remove the Director.
*   [ ] **Recusal:** The Interested Director **does not vote** on their own removal (Conflict of Interest Policy § 9.1).
*   [ ] **Verify Threshold (Board Removal):**
    *   Requires an affirmative vote of the **Majority of the Remaining Directors**.
    *   *Example:* If there are 5 Directors total, and 1 is being removed, there are 4 Remaining Directors. A majority of 4 is 3.
*   [ ] **Verify Threshold (Member Removal - If applicable):**
    *   If removal is by Members, requires a **Two-Thirds (2/3)** vote of Voting Members present (Bylaws § 3.6).
*   [ ] **Record the Vote:** The Secretary must record the numerical vote count (For, Against, Abstain) in the minutes. Do not record "passed unanimously" if it was not.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Post-Removal Actions
*Immediate action is required to secure the organization.*

*   [ ] **Notify the Individual:** The Chair sends formal written notice of the removal decision to the former Director immediately following the meeting.
*   [ ] **Revoke Access (IT/Security):**
    *   [ ] CISO disables email accounts, Slack/Discord access, and cloud storage immediately.
    *   [ ] Revoke access to bank accounts, credit cards, and financial platforms (Stripe, PayPal).
    *   [ ] Change passwords for any shared accounts the individual possessed.
*   [ ] **Update Registry:** Update the corporate books and the "Transparency" page on the website.
*   [ ] **Regulatory Filings:**
    *   [ ] File an update with the Texas Secretary of State (Form 401 - Change of Management) if the Director was a named governing person on file.
    *   [ ] Update the Registered Agent filing if the removed Director served in that capacity.
*   [ ] **Asset Recovery:** Request return of any physical hardware (laptops, keys) or intellectual property.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board Chair** | Oversees the process, ensures order during the hearing, and delivers the final notice of removal. |
| **Secretary** | Drafts the meeting notice, ensures proof of delivery, and records detailed minutes of the hearing and vote. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews evidence for "Cause" sufficiency and advises on procedural compliance to minimize litigation risk. |
| **CISO** | Executes the immediate revocation of digital access and credentials upon confirmation of the vote. |
| **Remaining Directors** | Review evidence objectively, listen to the defense, and cast a vote based on the best interests of the Organization. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Strict Liability:** Failure to follow the steps in this checklist, particularly regarding **Notice** and **Quorum**, may render the removal "void ab initio" (invalid from the start). This could result in the removed Director being reinstated by a court, along with damages for legal fees.

**Documentation:** All notices, evidence, and minutes generated during this process are "Litigation Hold" documents and must be retained indefinitely under the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.
