### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a standardized, repeatable process for closing the Organization’s financial books on a monthly basis. This checklist ensures that financial data is accurate, complete, and recorded in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and federal grant requirements (2 CFR 200). Timely closing of books is essential to provide the Board of Directors with the financial visibility required by **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.1**.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to all financial activities of Study GRC Inc., including banking, accounts payable, accounts receivable, grant management, and payroll.
*   **Applies to:** Finance Department, CFO, Treasurer, and Budget Owners.
*   **Exclusions:** Year-end specific audit adjustments (covered in *FIN-PRC-018 Year-End Close Procedure*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **GAAP** | Generally Accepted Accounting Principles; the standard framework of guidelines for financial accounting. |
| **General Ledger (GL)** | The master set of accounts that summarize all transactions occurring within the entity. |
| **Sub-ledger** | A detailed subset of accounts (e.g., Accounts Payable) that rolls up into the General Ledger. |
| **Accrual Basis** | Accounting method where revenue/expenses are recorded when earned/incurred, regardless of when cash changes hands. |
| **Restricted Funds** | Assets (usually cash) that are limited by a donor-imposed restriction (time or purpose). |
| **PAR** | Personnel Activity Report; required for time and effort reporting on federal grants. |

### 4. Procedures (Checklist)

The Month-End Close process must be completed within **ten (10) business days** following the end of the month.

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Preparation (Days -2 to +1)
*   [ ] **4.1.1 Close Sub-ledgers:** Ensure all invoices and bills for the prior month are entered into the accounting system.
*   [ ] **4.1.2 Expense Report Deadline:** Notify all staff and volunteers that expense reimbursements and corporate credit card receipts are due.
*   [ ] **4.1.3 Recurring Entries:** Post all automated recurring journal entries (e.g., software subscriptions, depreciation).

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Cash and Banking (Days 2-4)
*   [ ] **4.2.1 Bank Reconciliations:** Reconcile all operating and savings bank accounts against bank statements.
    *   *Verify:* No uncleared checks older than 90 days.
*   [ ] **4.2.2 Credit Card Reconciliations:** Reconcile corporate credit card accounts.
    *   *Verify:* All transactions have attached digital receipts per *FIN-POL-040 Travel and Expense Policy*.
*   [ ] **4.2.3 PayPal/Stripe Reconciliation:** Reconcile payment processor holding accounts against the GL.
*   [ ] **4.2.4 Petty Cash:** Verify petty cash counts (if applicable) and record replenishment entries.

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Revenue and Grants (Days 3-5)
*   [ ] **4.3.1 Donation Revenue:** Reconcile CRM (donor database) records with the General Ledger to ensure all donations are captured.
*   [ ] **4.3.2 Grant Revenue Recognition:** Record revenue for cost-reimbursable federal grants based on actual allowable expenses incurred during the month.
*   [ ] **4.3.3 Release of Restrictions:** Journalize the release of temporarily restricted net assets for funds spent in accordance with donor intent.
*   [ ] **4.3.4 In-Kind Contributions:** Record value of donated services or goods per *FIN-POL-060 Non-Cash Contribution Valuation Policy*.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Payroll and Allocations (Days 4-6)
*   [ ] **4.4.1 Payroll Journal Entry:** Import payroll data from the third-party provider.
*   [ ] **4.4.2 Functional Allocation:** Allocate salaries and taxes to appropriate functional categories (Program, Management, Fundraising) based on actual time worked.
*   [ ] **4.4.3 Grant Allocation:** Verify that payroll charged to federal awards matches approved Personnel Activity Reports (PARs) or time sheets.
*   [ ] **4.4.4 Accrue PTO:** Update the liability for Paid Time Off (PTO) earned but not used.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Balance Sheet Review (Days 6-8)
*   [ ] **4.5.1 Prepaid Expenses:** Amortize prepaid items (e.g., annual insurance premiums) for the current month.
*   [ ] **4.5.2 Fixed Assets:** Record depreciation and amortization for capitalized assets.
*   [ ] **4.5.3 Accrued Liabilities:** Record expenses incurred but not yet invoiced (e.g., legal fees, contractor hours).
*   [ ] **4.5.4 Intercompany/Inter-fund:** Ensure all due-to/due-from accounts balance to zero.

#### 4.6 Phase 6: Final Review and Reporting (Days 8-10)
*   [ ] **4.6.1 Variance Analysis:** Compare actuals vs. budget. Investigate variances >10% or >$1,000.
*   [ ] **4.6.2 Financial Statement Generation:** Produce the standard package:
    *   Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet).
    *   Statement of Activities (P&L) - Month and YTD.
    *   Statement of Cash Flows.
*   [ ] **4.6.3 CFO Review:** Chief Financial Officer reviews the trial balance and signs off on the close.
*   [ ] **4.6.4 Lock the Period:** "Close" the period in the accounting software to prevent back-dated changes.
*   [ ] **4.6.5 Treasurer Notification:** Transmit financial package to the Treasurer for Board presentation.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bookkeeper / Accountant** | Performs daily transaction entry, bank reconciliations, and initial sub-ledger closing. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Oversees the close process, reviews journal entries, investigates variances, and performs final sign-off. |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews the final monthly financial reports for irregularities and presents them to the Board of Directors per **Bylaws Art. IV**. |
| **Budget Owners** | Submit expenses and receipts timely; explain budget variances when queried. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this checklist may result in inaccurate financial reporting, leading to potential loss of 501(c)(3) status, audit findings, or revocation of federal grant funding. Repeated failure to close books within the designated timeline will result in performance management action against responsible parties.
