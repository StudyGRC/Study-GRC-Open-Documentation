### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a standardized procedure for the inspection and maintenance of first aid supplies within Study GRC Inc. facilities and event venues. This checklist ensures compliance with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.151(b)**, which mandates that adequate first aid supplies must be readily available. Maintaining fully stocked and unexpired kits is a critical component of the Organization's duty of care toward employees, volunteers, and community participants.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to:
*   **Physical Headquarters:** Any permanent office or storage facility maintained by the Organization.
*   **Event Venues:** Temporary locations where Study GRC Inc. hosts workshops, conferences, or meetups.
*   **Company Vehicles:** Any vehicle owned or leased by the Organization for business use.

**Exclusions:** While Study GRC Inc. encourages safety in remote work environments, this mandatory inspection regime does not legally extend to the private home offices of remote employees or volunteers, unless explicitly designated as a "satellite office" for public business.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ANSI/ISEA Z308.1** | The American National Standard for Minimum Requirements for Workplace First Aid Kits and Supplies. |
| **Class A Kit** | A basic kit designed to deal with the most common types of workplace injuries (cuts, grazes, abrasions, minor burns). Suitable for general office/event use. |
| **Expiration Date** | The date after which a medical supply (e.g., ointment, eyewash) is no longer guaranteed to be effective or sterile. |
| **PPE** | Personal Protective Equipment (e.g., medical gloves, CPR barriers). |

### 4. Inspection Standards & Procedures

**Frequency:**
*   **Permanent Facilities:** Monthly.
*   **Events:** Pre-event (during setup) and Post-event (during teardown).

**Instructions:**
1.  Locate the First Aid Kit.
2.  Verify accessibility and signage.
3.  Open the kit and remove contents to inspect for damage, soil, or moisture.
4.  Check expiration dates on all sterile items, ointments, and washes. **Discard and replace expired items immediately.**
5.  Count inventory against the "Required Minimum" list below.
6.  Sign and date the physical inspection tag on the kit (if applicable) and log the digital record.

#### 4.1 General Inspection Criteria
| Status | Item | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| [ ] | **Accessibility** | Kit is easily accessible, not blocked by equipment or boxes. |
| [ ] | **Signage** | Location is clearly marked with a visible "First Aid" sign. |
| [ ] | **Case Integrity** | The container is clean, dry, and latches securely. Handle is intact. |
| [ ] | **Manual** | A current First Aid Guide/Booklet is present inside the kit. |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Manager** | Responsible for inspecting the kit prior to the start of any physical event and ensuring it is accessible to attendees. |
| **Office Manager / Ops Lead** | Responsible for the monthly inspection of kits located in permanent facilities. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Ensures that first aid availability is communicated to event attendees during "Housekeeping" announcements. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves budget for immediate replenishment of expired or used supplies. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain adequate first aid supplies is a violation of OSHA regulations and Organization policy.
*   **Missing Items:** Must be restocked within 48 hours of discovery (or prior to the next event).
*   **Expired Items:** Must be disposed of and replaced immediately.
*   **Negligence:** Failure to perform inspections that results in the unavailability of aid during an injury incident may result in disciplinary action and exposes the Organization to significant liability.
