### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a mandatory due diligence framework for the selection and operation of physical venues hosted by Study GRC Inc. This standard ensures that all Organization events comply with safety regulations, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), and internal Youth Protection mandates.

By rigorously inspecting venues prior to contractual commitment and event execution, the Organization demonstrates its fiduciary **Duty of Care**, mitigates liability regarding negligence, and satisfies the "good faith" requirements for indemnification outlined in **Bylaws Article VII, Section 7.1**.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all employees, Board Directors, and authorized volunteers ("Event Organizers") responsible for selecting, contracting, or managing physical spaces for Study GRC Inc. events.

*   **Inclusions:** Hotels, conference centers, schools, community centers, outdoor parks, and rented corporate offices used for workshops, hackathons, or meetups.
*   **Exclusions:** Purely virtual events or private residences (use of private residences for official events is prohibited per the *Youth Protection Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Egress** | A continuous and unobstructed path of travel from any point in a building or structure to a public way (exit). |
| **Mustering Point** | A designated safe area outside the venue where attendees assemble during an evacuation to be accounted for. |
| **Sightlines** | The ability to visually observe interactions between adults and minors without obstruction; critical for Youth Protection. |
| **ADA** | Americans with Disabilities Act; federal civil rights law that prohibits discrimination against people with disabilities in everyday activities. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | A supervision policy requiring a minimum of two authorized adults to be present with youth participants at all times. |

### 4. Inspection Standards and Procedures

The Event Venue Safety Inspection must be conducted in two phases:
1.  **Phase 1 (Selection):** Prior to signing any venue contract or rental agreement.
2.  **Phase 2 (Pre-Event):** On the day of the event, prior to admitting attendees.

#### 4.1 Fire and Life Safety
*   **Exits:** All emergency exits must be clearly marked, illuminated, and unlocked from the inside.
*   **Obstructions:** Corridors and aisles must be free of tripping hazards, cables, or storage items.
*   **Fire Suppression:** Fire extinguishers must be visible, accessible, and show a current inspection tag (within the last 12 months).
*   **Capacity:** The venue must not be overcrowded; attendance must remain within the posted occupancy load.
*   **Alarms:** Identify the location of manual fire alarm pull stations.

#### 4.2 Accessibility (ADA Compliance)
*   **Entrances:** Main entrances must be accessible via ramp or flat ground. If the main entrance is not accessible, clear signage must direct attendees to an accessible alternative.
*   **Elevators:** If the event is on an upper floor, functioning elevators must be available.
*   **Restrooms:** At least one restroom must be ADA-compliant (grab bars, turning radius).
*   **Seating:** Designated spaces for wheelchair users must be integrated into the seating plan, not segregated to the back.

#### 4.3 Youth Protection and Safeguarding
*   **Sightlines:** Breakout rooms and workshop areas must have windows or open doors to allow for visual monitoring from hallways.
*   **Restroom Configuration:** Restrooms should allow for monitoring of the *entrance* without violating privacy inside. Single-stall, gender-neutral restrooms are preferred to minimize unmonitored isolation.
*   **Isolation Risks:** Identify and lock/block access to unnecessary secluded areas (e.g., unused storage closets, basements, roof access) where a minor could be isolated.
*   **Public Access:** If the venue is shared with the public (e.g., a hotel lobby), define a perimeter that separates Study GRC participants from the general public.

#### 4.4 General Health and Environment
*   **Ventilation:** HVAC systems must be functioning to ensure adequate air exchange.
*   **Lighting:** All areas, including parking lots and walkways, must be adequately lit.
*   **Sanitation:** Restrooms and food service areas must be clean and stocked with soap/sanitizer.
*   **Hazards:** Check for loose carpeting, wet floors, exposed wiring, or broken furniture.

#### 4.5 Emergency Preparedness
*   **First Aid:** A stocked First Aid kit must be accessible at the registration desk.
*   **AED:** Locate the nearest Automated External Defibrillator (AED).
*   **Communications:** Verify that cell phone service or Wi-Fi is available for emergency calls.
*   **Evacuation Plan:** A floor plan showing emergency routes must be posted or available to the Event Organizer.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Organizer** | Conducts the physical inspection using this checklist; ensures remediation of "Fail" items before the event begins. |
| **Executive Director** | Reviews Phase 1 inspections for high-risk events; holds final authority to reject a venue based on safety concerns. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Reviews inspection reports specifically for ADA and inclusion barriers; advocates for accommodation solutions. |
| **Volunteer Staff** | Responsible for maintaining clear egress paths and monitoring safety during the event execution. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to conduct or document this inspection constitutes a breach of operational procedure and may result in the suspension of the event. Negligence in identifying obvious hazards that result in injury may lead to disciplinary action against the responsible Event Organizer, up to and including termination or removal from volunteer service.

In the event of a legal claim, this completed checklist serves as primary evidence of the Organization's due diligence and adherence to safety standards.
