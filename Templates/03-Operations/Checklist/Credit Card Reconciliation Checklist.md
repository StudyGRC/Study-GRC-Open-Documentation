### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a mandatory, standardized protocol for the monthly reconciliation of Study GRC Inc. corporate credit cards. This checklist is designed to detect errors, identify fraudulent activity, ensure compliance with IRS substantiation requirements, and verify that all expenditures align with the Organization’s 501(c)(3) charitable purpose and federal grant allowability rules (2 CFR 200).

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to:
*   **Cardholders:** All Directors, Officers, employees, and volunteers issued a corporate credit card.
*   **Finance Team:** Personnel responsible for bookkeeping and financial review.
*   **Approvers:** The CFO and Treasurer responsible for final oversight.

**Exclusions:** This checklist does not cover reimbursement of out-of-pocket expenses (refer to *Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Substantiation** | Documentary evidence (receipt, invoice) proving the amount, date, place, and essential character of the expense. |
| **Split Transaction** | The prohibited practice of dividing a single purchase into multiple smaller transactions to bypass spending limits or approval thresholds. |
| **Unallowable Cost** | An expense that cannot be charged to a federal grant or contract (e.g., alcohol, lobbying, entertainment) per 2 CFR 200 Subpart E. |
| **Personal Use** | Any charge not directly related to the business operations of Study GRC Inc. |

### 4. Reconciliation Standards & Checklist

The following steps must be completed within five (5) business days of the credit card statement closing date.

#### 4.1 Phase I: Documentation Gathering (Cardholder Responsibility)
*   [ ] **Receipt Collection:** Verify that a digital receipt exists for **every** transaction, regardless of amount.
*   [ ] **Missing Receipt Affidavit:** If a receipt is lost for an amount over $25, ensure a *Missing Receipt Affidavit* is attached and signed.
*   [ ] **Business Purpose:** Verify that every transaction includes a written note explaining the specific business reason (e.g., "Hosting fees for GRC Web Server," not just "Hosting").

#### 4.2 Phase II: Transaction Verification (Finance Team)
*   [ ] **Three-Way Match:** Confirm that the **Date**, **Amount**, and **Vendor** on the receipt match the credit card statement exactly.
*   [ ] **Sales Tax Exemption:** As a Texas Non-Profit, verify that Texas Sales Tax was **not** charged on qualifying purchases. If charged, flag for recovery.
*   [ ] **Currency Conversion:** For international transactions, verify the exchange rate and ensure foreign transaction fees are recorded separately if applicable.
*   [ ] **Duplicate Check:** Scan for duplicate charges (same amount, same vendor, same day).

#### 4.3 Phase III: Fraud & Compliance Detection (Mandatory)
*   [ ] **Split Transaction Review:** Look for multiple charges to the same vendor within a short timeframe that total just over a specific approval limit.
*   [ ] **Restricted Merchant Codes:** Flag any transactions from blocked categories (e.g., casinos, jewelry stores, liquor stores, escort services).
*   [ ] **Weekend/Holiday Activity:** Scrutinize purchases made on weekends or holidays to ensure they are legitimate business needs and not personal entertainment.
*   [ ] **Rounded Amounts:** Investigate flat-dollar transactions (e.g., $500.00) as these are often indicators of gift card purchases or fraud.
*   [ ] **Subscription Creep:** Identify recurring subscriptions that may no longer be in use or were not cancelled after a trial period.
*   [ ] **Grant Compliance (2 CFR 200):**
    *   [ ] Are costs **Allowable**? (No alcohol, no lobbying).
    *   [ ] Are costs **Allocable**? (Charged to the correct grant/program code).
    *   [ ] Are costs **Reasonable**? (Price is prudent and necessary).

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Coding and Approval
*   [ ] **GL Coding:** Ensure every transaction is assigned to the correct General Ledger account code per the *Chart of Accounts*.
*   [ ] **Class/Program Tracking:** Ensure expenses are tagged to the correct Program (e.g., "Education," "Community Outreach") or Grant ID.
*   [ ] **Manager Approval:** Verify that the Cardholder's direct supervisor has electronically approved the expense report.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cardholder** | Submits receipts and business justifications within 3 days of statement close. Reports lost/stolen cards immediately. |
| **Bookkeeper** | Performs the initial detailed review using this checklist. Flags discrepancies. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews the reconciled report. Investigates flagged "Fraud & Compliance" items. Approves the final reconciliation. |
| **Treasurer** | Conducts spot-checks of reconciliations quarterly to ensure Board-level oversight as required by Bylaws Article IV. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this checklist or the *Corporate Credit Card User Agreement* may result in:
1.  Immediate suspension or revocation of credit card privileges.
2.  Requirement for the Cardholder to personally reimburse the Organization for unsubstantiated or unallowable charges.
3.  Disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer service.
4.  Legal action and referral to law enforcement in cases of intentional fraud or theft.
