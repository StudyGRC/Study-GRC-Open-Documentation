### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this checklist is to ensure that the Annual Meeting of the Voting Members of Study GRC Inc. is conducted in strict compliance with **Article II** of the Organization's Bylaws and the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC). This document mitigates the risk of procedural challenges to election results, ensures valid quorum establishment, and guarantees transparency for the membership.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to the Board of Directors, the Secretary, and Operational Officers responsible for planning, executing, and certifying the Annual Meeting.
*   **In Scope:** Logistics, notice requirements, candidate nominations, meeting conduct, and electronic voting administration.
*   **Out of Scope:** Regular monthly Board of Directors meetings or committee meetings.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Voting Member** | A Supporting Member or Honorary Member in good standing as defined in Bylaws Section 2.1. |
| **Record Date** | The specific date established by the Board to determine which members are eligible to receive notice and vote. |
| **Quorum** | A majority (more than 50%) of the total number of Voting Members entitled to vote (Bylaws Section 2.4). |
| **Nomination Statement** | A mandatory disclosure by Board candidates including background, vision, and potential conflicts of interest. |

### 4. Procedures and Checklist

#### 4.1 Phase I: Strategic Planning (90–60 Days Prior)
*   **4.1.1 Set Meeting Date:** Confirm the meeting date falls within **January** as required by Bylaws Section 2.5.
*   **4.1.2 Platform Selection:** Select a virtual meeting platform (e.g., Zoom, Teams) capable of handling the expected attendance and verifying identity.
*   **4.1.3 Electronic Voting System Setup:** Select and configure the secure electronic voting system. Ensure the system prohibits proxy voting (Bylaws Section 2.3) and prevents cumulative voting (Bylaws Section 2.2).

#### 4.2 Phase II: Notice and Nominations (60–14 Days Prior)
*   **4.2.1 Establish Record Date:** Board votes to set the Record Date for determining voting eligibility.
*   **4.2.2 Distribute Meeting Notice:** Send written notice (email) to all Voting Members.
    *   [ ] Sent at least **10 days** but no more than **60 days** before the meeting.
    *   [ ] Includes Date, Time, and Virtual Access Link.
    *   [ ] States the specific purpose (Election of Directors).
*   **4.2.3 Open Nominations:** Announce the opening of Board nominations.
    *   [ ] Ensure window opens at least **14 days** before the meeting.
    *   [ ] Distribute "Nomination Statement" template to all interested candidates.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Transparency and Compliance (10–2 Days Prior)
*   **4.3.1 Prepare Voting List:** In compliance with TBOC § 22.158 and Bylaws Section 2.6:
    *   [ ] Generate an alphabetical list of all members entitled to vote.
    *   [ ] Include name, email address, and number of votes (1 per member).
    *   [ ] Make list available for electronic inspection **2 business days** after notice is sent.
*   **4.3.2 Candidate Vetting:**
    *   [ ] Collect all Nomination Statements.
    *   [ ] Verify "Good Standing" status of all nominees.
    *   [ ] Review Conflict of Interest disclosures.
*   **4.3.3 Distribute Candidate Packet:** Send the compiled Nomination Statements to the membership to allow for informed decision-making.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: The Annual Meeting (Day 0)
*   **4.4.1 Identity Verification:** Verify the identity of attendees against the Voting List upon entry to the virtual meeting.
*   **4.4.2 Establish Quorum:**
    *   [ ] Count attending Voting Members.
    *   [ ] Verify attendance exceeds 50% of the total Voting Membership.
    *   [ ] **Stop:** If quorum is not met, business cannot be transacted.
*   **4.4.3 Candidate Presentations:**
    *   [ ] Enforce the **5-minute** presentation window for each nominee (Bylaws Section 2.5).
    *   [ ] Facilitate the Member Q&A session.
*   **4.4.4 Open Voting Window:** Announce the opening of the 7-day electronic voting period immediately following the meeting adjournment.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Election and Certification (Day 1–8)
*   **4.5.1 Monitor Voting:** Ensure the electronic system remains open for exactly **seven (7) days**.
*   **4.5.2 Close Voting:** Terminate the voting window at the designated time.
*   **4.5.3 Certify Results:**
    *   [ ] Secretary reviews the electronic audit trail.
    *   [ ] Confirm no proxy votes were cast.
    *   [ ] Tabulate results (highest vote-getters fill the open seats).
*   **4.5.4 Notify Membership:** Announce the election results to the community.
*   **4.5.5 Officer Election:** Schedule the first meeting of the *new* Board to elect Officers (President, Secretary, Treasurer) per Bylaws Article IV.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Responsible for the Voting List, verifying Quorum, certifying election results, and ensuring Notice compliance. |
| **Board President** | Presides over the Annual Meeting and facilitates candidate Q&A. |
| **Governance Committee** | Vets candidates for eligibility and ensures Nomination Statements are complete. |
| **CISO / IT Dept** | Secures the electronic voting platform and manages virtual meeting access controls. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this checklist, particularly regarding **Notice** (Section 4.2.2) and **Quorum** (Section 4.4.2), may render the meeting proceedings and election results **void or voidable** under Texas law.

Any attempt to manipulate the Voting List or the Electronic Voting System will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of membership and legal action.
