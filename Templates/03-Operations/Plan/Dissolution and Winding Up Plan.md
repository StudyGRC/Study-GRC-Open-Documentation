### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this plan is to establish a legally compliant, orderly, and transparent framework for the voluntary dissolution and winding up of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This document ensures that in the event the Organization ceases operations, it satisfies all obligations under the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC), Internal Revenue Service (IRS) regulations, and the Organization’s Bylaws, while minimizing liability for Directors and Officers and honoring the intent of donors and the community.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to all assets, liabilities, operations, personnel, and intellectual property of the Organization. It is triggered only upon the initiation of a "Fundamental Action" to dissolve as defined in Article X of the Bylaws.

**Exclusions:** This plan does not apply to involuntary dissolution initiated by the State of Texas or judicial decree, although the operational winding-up steps may still be utilized as a guideline.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dissolution** | The formal legal termination of the Organization’s corporate existence. |
| **Winding Up** | The process of ceasing operations, settling debts, and distributing remaining assets after the decision to dissolve has been made. |
| **Fundamental Action** | As defined in Bylaws Article X, a significant event (including dissolution) requiring a supermajority vote of the Membership. |
| **Plan of Distribution** | A specific document required by TBOC § 22.305 detailing exactly how remaining assets will be transferred to other tax-exempt entities. |
| **Claimant** | Any person or entity having a known, present, or contingent claim against the Organization (e.g., vendors, creditors). |

### 4. Plan Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Authorization (The Decision)
Strict adherence to the Bylaws is required to validate the decision to dissolve.

1.  **Board Resolution:** The Board of Directors must adopt a resolution recommending that the Organization be dissolved and directing that the question of such dissolution be submitted to a vote at a meeting of the Voting Members (TBOC § 22.302).
2.  **Notice to Members:** Written notice must be sent to each Voting Member entitled to vote, stating that the purpose of the meeting is to consider the advisability of dissolving the Organization.
3.  **Membership Vote:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article X, Section 10.2**, the resolution to dissolve must be approved by the affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3)** of **all** Voting Members (not merely those present at the meeting).
4.  **Adoption of Plan:** Concurrent with the vote to dissolve, the Board and Members shall approve this "Dissolution and Winding Up Plan" to govern the process.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Cessation of Business (Winding Up)
Upon approval of the resolution, the Organization must cease all business activities except those necessary for the winding up of affairs (TBOC § 22.302).

1.  **Notice to Claimants:** The Organization shall send a registered letter to all known claimants notifying them of the dissolution and providing a deadline (not less than 120 days) to submit claims.
2.  **Contract Termination:** The Executive Director (or Liquidating Trustee) must review all contracts (software, vendors, insurance) and issue termination notices in accordance with specific agreement terms.
3.  **Personnel:** All employees and contractors must be notified of termination dates. Final payroll, including any accrued benefits required by law or policy, must be calculated and set aside immediately.
4.  **Intellectual Property (Open Source Preservation):** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all proprietary Work Product and educational resources must be prepared for transfer to another 501(c)(3) or public repository to ensure continued open-source availability.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Financial Settlement
The Organization must apply its assets in the following priority order (TBOC § 22.304):

1.  **Liabilities and Costs:** Payment of all costs and expenses of the winding up (legal fees, accounting fees).
2.  **Debts:** Discharge or make adequate provision for the discharge of all liabilities and obligations of the Organization.
3.  **Conditional Assets:** Return any assets held upon condition requiring return, transfer, or conveyance because of the dissolution.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Distribution of Remaining Assets
After all debts are satisfied, remaining assets must be distributed.

1.  **Plan of Distribution:** The Board must adopt a specific "Plan of Distribution" identifying the recipient(s) of the remaining assets.
2.  **Eligible Recipients:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article XI**, assets may **only** be distributed to:
    *   One or more organizations exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code;
    *   The federal government; or
    *   A state or local government for a public purpose.
3.  **Prohibition on Private Inurement:** Under no circumstances shall assets be distributed to any Director, Officer, Member, or private individual.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Regulatory Filings
1.  **IRS Filings:**
    *   File final Form 990, checking the "Terminated" box.
    *   File Schedule N (Liquidation, Termination, Dissolution, or Significant Disposition of Assets).
2.  **Texas Secretary of State:**
    *   File **Form 652 (Certificate of Termination)**.
    *   Note: The Certificate of Termination cannot be filed until the Texas Comptroller issues a certificate showing all taxes have been paid (if applicable).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Initiates the resolution, oversees the Liquidating Trustee, and holds fiduciary responsibility until the Certificate of Termination is filed. |
| **Voting Members** | Must approve the dissolution by a 2/3 supermajority vote of the entire membership roster. |
| **Executive Director / Liquidating Trustee** | Executes the winding-up tasks, manages asset inventory, settles debts, and handles administrative closure. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Prepares the Certificate of Termination, Plan of Distribution, and advises on creditor notifications to minimize liability. |
| **Treasurer** | Ensures all final tax filings (990, payroll taxes) are accurate and paid prior to asset distribution. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Director Liability:** Directors who vote for or assent to a distribution of assets to members or other persons during the liquidation of the corporation without paying and discharging all known debts, liabilities, and obligations of the corporation may be **jointly and severally liable** to the corporation for the value of the distributed assets (TBOC § 22.225).

Failure to follow this plan may result in the piercing of the corporate veil, personal liability for Officers and Directors, and revocation of tax-exempt status retroactively.
