### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Emergency Action Plan (EAP) is to establish a systematic approach to preventing, preparing for, and responding to emergencies during events hosted by Study GRC Inc. This plan ensures the safety of all attendees, volunteers, employees, and minors, and minimizes legal liability and reputational damage. This document satisfies the requirements of OSHA 29 CFR 1910.38 (Emergency Action Plans) and general duty of care obligations for non-profit entities.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to **all** events organized, hosted, or managed by Study GRC Inc., regardless of size or location.

*   **Physical Events:** In-person gatherings, conferences, workshops, and meetups.
*   **Virtual Events:** Online webinars, training sessions, and community calls where participant safety (e.g., seizures, threats) or platform security is at risk.
*   **Exclusions:** Events where Study GRC Inc. is solely a sponsor or attendee and does not exercise operational control over the venue or platform; in such cases, the host organization's EAP takes precedence, though Study GRC staff must remain vigilant.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Incident Commander (IC)** | The designated individual (usually the Event Lead) responsible for all decision-making during an emergency until professional first responders arrive. |
| **Muster Point** | A pre-designated safe location outside a physical venue where attendees gather after an evacuation. |
| **Code Adam** | A protocol for a missing child in a public building. |
| **Virtual Incident** | An emergency occurring in a digital space, including "Zoom-bombing," visible medical distress of a remote participant, or credible threats of violence made via chat/video. |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18. |

### 4. Emergency Procedures

#### 4.1 Pre-Event Planning and Risk Assessment
Prior to any physical event, the Event Lead must:
1.  **Venue Inspection:** Verify exit routes are unlocked and unobstructed.
2.  **Muster Point Designation:** Identify a primary and secondary assembly area.
3.  **Medical Resources:** Locate the nearest AED (Automated External Defibrillator) and First Aid Kit.
4.  **Communication Plan:** Establish a primary communication channel for staff (e.g., dedicated Signal group or radio channel).

#### 4.2 Chain of Command
In accordance with **Bylaws Article V (Operational Leadership)**, the Executive Director holds ultimate authority. However, during an event, authority is delegated as follows:
1.  **Incident Commander (IC):** The on-site Event Lead.
2.  **Safety Officer:** Designated volunteer/staff member assisting the IC.
3.  **Staff/Volunteers:** Must follow directives from the IC immediately.

#### 4.3 Physical Event Emergencies

**4.3.1 Medical Emergency**
1.  **Assess:** Ensure the scene is safe. Do not move the victim unless they are in immediate danger.
2.  **Call:** Designate a specific person to call 911.
3.  **Render Aid:** Only trained personnel (CPR/First Aid certified) should provide care.
4.  **Clear:** Staff must clear bystanders to provide privacy and access for EMS.
5.  **Report:** Complete an *Incident Investigation Report* (EHS-FRM-003) immediately after the event.

**4.3.2 Fire or Structural Evacuation**
1.  **Activate:** Pull the nearest fire alarm if not already sounding.
2.  **Evacuate:** Staff must direct attendees to the nearest exit, ensuring no one uses elevators.
3.  **Assist:** Designated staff must assist individuals with mobility impairments.
4.  **Account:** The IC must conduct a headcount of staff and volunteers at the Muster Point.
5.  **Do Not Re-enter:** No one may re-enter the building until cleared by the Fire Department.

**4.3.3 Missing Minor (Code Adam)**
Given the Organization's K-12 focus, this protocol is mandatory if a minor is reported missing:
1.  **Alert:** Staff notifies the IC immediately.
2.  **Secure:** Staff monitors all exits to ensure the child does not leave the facility (do not physically restrain adults, but observe).
3.  **Search:** Staff conducts a sweep of restrooms, common areas, and hiding spots.
4.  **Escalate:** If the child is not found within 10 minutes, call 911.

**4.3.4 Active Threat / Violence**
1.  **Run:** If there is an accessible escape path, attempt to evacuate the premises.
2.  **Hide:** If evacuation is not possible, find a place to hide where you are less likely to be found. Lock or blockade the door.
3.  **Fight:** As a last resort, and only when your life is in imminent danger, attempt to disrupt and/or incapacitate the active shooter.

#### 4.4 Virtual Event Emergencies

**4.4.1 Platform Intrusion (e.g., "Zoom-bombing")**
1.  **Lockdown:** The Host must immediately "Suspend Participant Activities" (mute all, stop video, lock meeting).
2.  **Eject:** Remove the offending participant(s) and report them to the platform.
3.  **Resume/Reschedule:** If the room is secure, resume. If compromised, end the meeting and send a new link to verified attendees via email.

**4.4.2 Participant Medical Distress (Visible on Camera)**
1.  **Identify:** Attempt to identify the user via registration data.
2.  **Contact:** If the user is unresponsive but visible, attempt to contact their emergency contact (if on file) or local authorities if their location is known.
3.  **Privacy:** Stop the recording and move other participants to a Breakout Room or end the session to preserve the victim's dignity.

#### 4.5 Post-Incident Protocol
1.  **Documentation:** The IC must complete an Incident Report within 24 hours.
2.  **Debrief:** The Operational Leadership team will review the incident to update policies.
3.  **Legal Review:** In cases of injury or significant disruption, the Board of Directors must be notified to assess liability and insurance implications.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures resources are available for safety planning; reviews all post-incident reports. |
| **Incident Commander (Event Lead)** | Acts as the primary decision-maker during an emergency; liaises with First Responders. |
| **Safety Officer** | Conducts pre-event checks; manages the First Aid Kit; ensures exits are clear. |
| **Volunteers/Staff** | Assist in evacuation; guide attendees to safety; report hazards to the IC. |
| **Board of Directors** | Provides oversight on risk management policies; reviews high-level liability incidents per **Bylaws Article III**. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this plan, particularly regarding the reporting of hazards or failure to evacuate, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

**Indemnification:** Per **Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification)**, the Organization shall indemnify Directors, Officers, and volunteers against claims and liabilities incurred in the performance of their duties under this plan, provided they acted in good faith and without gross negligence.
