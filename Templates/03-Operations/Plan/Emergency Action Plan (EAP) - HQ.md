### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Emergency Action Plan (EAP) is to facilitate and organize employer and employee actions during workplace emergencies. This plan is established to ensure the safety of all employees, volunteers, and visitors at the Study GRC Inc. Registered Office, in strict compliance with **OSHA Standard 29 CFR 1910.38**.

This plan establishes the procedures for reporting emergencies, evacuating the facility, and accounting for personnel to minimize the risk of injury or loss of life.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   The physical Registered Office of Study GRC Inc. as defined in **Bylaws Article 1.2**.
*   All employees, contractors, volunteers, and visitors present at the physical Registered Office.
*   Any temporary physical administrative sites rented or leased by the Organization.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Remote Home Offices:** While Study GRC Inc. encourages safety in remote work environments, this specific OSHA-mandated plan applies strictly to the Organization's controlled physical premises. Remote employees should refer to the *Remote Worksite Safety Self-Assessment (HR-FRM-005)*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Emergency Coordinator (EC)** | The primary point of contact (usually the Executive Director or designee) responsible for managing emergency situations and liaising with first responders. |
| **Rally Point** | The designated safe location outside the building where staff must assemble following an evacuation. |
| **Shelter-in-Place** | A protocol for staying inside the building in a secure room during environmental threats (e.g., tornadoes) or external chemical hazards. |
| **Route of Egress** | A continuous and unobstructed path of exit travel from any point within the workplace to a place of safety. |

### 4. Emergency Procedures (OSHA 1910.38 Mandates)

#### 4.1 Emergency Reporting Procedures (29 CFR 1910.38(c)(1))
In the event of a fire, medical emergency, or immediate threat:
1.  **Call 911 immediately.** Do not assume someone else has made the call.
2.  Provide the dispatcher with:
    *   **Nature of Emergency:** (Fire, Medical, Intruder, etc.)
    *   **Address:** [Insert Registered Office Address]
    *   **Location:** [Insert Suite Number/Floor]
    *   **Phone Number:** [Insert Callback Number]
3.  Notify the **Emergency Coordinator (EC)** immediately after calling 911.
4.  Activate the building fire alarm system (if available and not automatically activated).

#### 4.2 Evacuation Procedures and Emergency Escape Routes (29 CFR 1910.38(c)(2))
*   **Map:** Evacuation maps are posted at [Insert Locations, e.g., "all exits and the main breakroom"].
*   **Action:** Upon hearing a fire alarm or receiving an evacuation order:
    1.  Cease all work immediately.
    2.  Do not delay to pack personal belongings (laptops, purses, etc.).
    3.  Proceed to the nearest safe Emergency Exit.
    4.  Walk, do not run. Do not use elevators.
    5.  Assist visitors or individuals with mobility impairments if safe to do so.
    6.  Proceed directly to the designated **Rally Point**.

**Designated Rally Point:** [Insert Specific Location, e.g., "Northwest corner of the parking lot by the oak tree"].

#### 4.3 Critical Operations Shutdown (29 CFR 1910.38(c)(3))
Study GRC Inc. operates as a non-profit with cloud-based infrastructure. There are **no** critical physical plant operations (e.g., chemical pipes, high-voltage machinery) that require an employee to remain behind during an evacuation.
*   **Rule:** All employees must evacuate immediately. No staff member is permitted to delay evacuation to secure IT equipment or shut down servers.

#### 4.4 Accounting for Employees (29 CFR 1910.38(c)(4))
1.  Once at the Rally Point, the **Emergency Coordinator (EC)** must conduct a headcount.
2.  The EC will verify the headcount against the daily attendance log or visitor register.
3.  **Missing Persons:** If an employee or visitor is missing, the EC must report this immediately to the arriving Fire Department/Police.
    *   **Prohibition:** Under no circumstances shall an employee re-enter the building to search for missing persons.

#### 4.5 Rescue and Medical Duties (29 CFR 1910.38(c)(5))
*   Study GRC Inc. does **not** designate employees to perform rescue or advanced medical duties.
*   Rescue and medical treatment are to be left to professional first responders.
*   **Good Samaritan Exception:** Employees trained in CPR/First Aid may voluntarily assist within the scope of their training, but this is not a condition of employment.

#### 4.6 Reporting Fires and Emergencies (29 CFR 1910.38(c)(6))
For internal reporting purposes (post-emergency), all incidents must be documented using the *OSHA 301 Incident Report Form* and submitted to the Board of Directors within 24 hours.

### 5. Specific Scenario Protocols

#### 5.1 Fire
*   If the fire is small (wastebasket size) and you have been trained, you may use a fire extinguisher (PASS: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep).
*   If the fire is large or spreading, evacuate immediately.
*   Close doors behind you as you leave to slow the spread of smoke/fire.

#### 5.2 Severe Weather (Tornado/Hurricane)
*   **Alert:** Monitor NOAA weather radio or local alerts.
*   **Action:** Proceed to the designated **Shelter-in-Place Area**: [Insert Location, e.g., "Interior hallway away from windows"].
*   Assume the "duck and cover" position to protect head and neck.
*   Remain in the shelter until the "All Clear" is given by the EC.

#### 5.3 Workplace Violence / Active Shooter
*   **Run:** If there is an accessible escape path, attempt to evacuate the premises.
*   **Hide:** If evacuation is not possible, find a place to hide where the active shooter is less likely to find you. Lock the door and blockade it with heavy furniture. Silence cell phones.
*   **Fight:** As a last resort, and only when your life is in imminent danger, attempt to disrupt and/or incapacitate the active shooter.

### 6. Remote Workforce Welfare Check
*Note: While OSHA 1910.38 applies to the physical office, Study GRC Inc. acknowledges its remote-first nature.*

In the event of a widespread regional disaster (e.g., hurricane, grid failure) affecting the region where the Registered Office or a cluster of remote employees resides:
1.  The Executive Director will initiate a **"Safety Check-In"** via the primary communication channel (e.g., Slack/Teams) or emergency SMS.
2.  All employees in the affected region are expected to mark themselves "Safe" as soon as they are able.

### 7. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Emergency Coordinator (EC)** | Directs evacuation, conducts headcount, liaises with First Responders. Usually the Executive Director or most senior on-site officer. |
| **Evacuation Warden** | (Appointed if staff > 10) Assists in clearing specific zones/rooms and ensures visitors are guided to exits. |
| **Employees/Volunteers** | Must know the location of exits and the Rally Point. Must follow EC instructions without delay. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves the EAP annually. Ensures resources are available for training. |

### 8. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Training:** All new employees must be trained on this EAP upon hire. Annual drills will be conducted to ensure familiarity with the Rally Point and procedures.
*   **Discipline:** Failure to participate in drills or failure to evacuate during an alarm may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, as defined in the *Employee Handbook*.
