### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Severe Weather/Disaster Response Plan is to establish a structured framework for the protection of human life, the preservation of organizational assets, and the continuity of operations for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") during emergency situations. This plan fulfills the Organization's legal "Duty of Care" to its employees, volunteers, and program participants, particularly minors, by defining protocols for responding to natural disasters and severe weather events in compliance with OSHA 1910.38 and Texas safety regulations.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to:
*   **Remote Operations:** Protocols for distributed staff and volunteers working from home offices, specifically regarding communication and work stoppage during local disasters.
*   **Physical Events:** All in-person gatherings, conferences, training sessions, or meetups organized or sponsored by the Organization, regardless of venue.
*   **Personnel:** All Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, volunteers, and event attendees.

**Exclusions:**
*   Cybersecurity incidents (Refer to *IS-POL-001 Master Information Security Policy* and *IS-PLN-002 Incident Response Plan*).
*   Minor medical incidents not related to a disaster (Refer to *EHS-PRC-001 Accident/Injury Reporting Procedure*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Incident Commander (IC)** | The individual designated to take charge of the emergency response at a specific location or for the organization globally. |
| **Shelter-in-Place** | A safety protocol requiring individuals to seek immediate shelter inside a building, away from windows and doors, typically used during tornadoes or chemical releases. |
| **Muster Point** | A pre-designated safe location outside a building where personnel and participants gather after an evacuation to account for all individuals. |
| **NOAA** | National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (primary source for weather alerts). |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The mandate that a minimum of two authorized adults must be present with minors at all times, even during emergency evacuations. |

### 4. Plan Procedures

#### 4.1 Remote Workforce Response (Distributed Teams)
As a remote-first Texas Non-Profit, the Organization acknowledges that staff may be affected by regional disasters (e.g., Gulf Coast hurricanes, winter grid failures) while operations elsewhere remain unaffected.

**4.1.1 Welfare Checks**
1.  Upon issuance of a "State of Emergency" or severe weather warning in a region where staff reside, the Executive Director (or designee) must initiate a Welfare Check via the primary communication channel (e.g., Slack/Teams).
2.  Affected staff must mark themselves "Safe" or report their status as soon as it is safe to do so.
3.  If a staff member in an impact zone is unresponsive for more than 24 hours, the Organization may attempt to contact emergency contacts listed in the HR file.

**4.1.2 Work Stoppage and Safety Priority**
1.  **Safety First:** No deadline, meeting, or deliverable takes precedence over personal safety. Staff in the path of severe weather are authorized to cease work immediately to secure their homes or evacuate.
2.  **Power/Internet Outages:** Staff are not expected to work during power or internet outages caused by severe weather.
3.  **Equipment:** Staff should not attempt to save Organization-issued IT assets if doing so puts their physical safety at risk.

#### 4.2 Physical Event Response (Conferences & Meetups)
For any in-person event, the Event Lead serves as the on-site Incident Commander (IC) unless relieved by emergency services.

**4.2.1 Pre-Event Due Diligence**
1.  **Venue Assessment:** Prior to signing a venue contract, the Event Lead must verify the existence of emergency exits, fire alarms, and storm shelters.
2.  **Weather Monitoring:** For events exceeding 50 participants, a designated "Weather Watcher" must monitor local NOAA alerts throughout the duration of the event.

**4.2.2 Severe Weather (Tornado/High Winds)**
1.  **Notification:** Upon receipt of a Tornado Warning, the IC must immediately halt proceedings via the public address system.
2.  **Action:** Direct all attendees to the pre-designated storm shelter or interior hallways on the lowest floor, away from glass atriums or windows.
3.  **Youth Protection:** Volunteers must ensure all minors are accounted for. In accordance with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, Two-Deep Leadership must be maintained in the shelter area to the extent possible without delaying safety.
4.  **All Clear:** Attendees must remain in the shelter until the "All Clear" is given by the IC or local authorities.

**4.2.3 Fire or Structural Threat**
1.  **Evacuation:** Upon activation of a fire alarm or detection of smoke/fire, immediate evacuation is mandatory.
2.  **Egress:** Attendees must be directed to the nearest safe exit. Elevators must not be used.
3.  **Muster Point:** All attendees must gather at the pre-designated Muster Point (defined in the event briefing).
4.  **Headcount:** The IC and volunteers must perform a headcount, prioritizing the verification of any minors or attendees with disabilities (per *ADA Accommodation Policy*).

**4.2.4 Medical Emergency During Disaster**
1.  Call 911 immediately.
2.  Do not move the victim unless they are in immediate danger (e.g., approaching fire).
3.  Administer First Aid/CPR only if certified and safe to do so.

#### 4.3 Crisis Communications
1.  **External Statements:** Only the Executive Director or Board President is authorized to speak to the media regarding an emergency affecting the Organization.
2.  **Family Notification:** In the event of injury to a participant, the Organization will notify the emergency contact listed on the *Liability Waiver* immediately after emergency services have been contacted.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensure adequate resources and insurance coverage (General Liability/D&O) are in place for disaster response. |
| **Executive Director** | Serves as the primary Crisis Manager; authorizes office closures or event cancellations due to weather. |
| **Event Lead (Incident Commander)** | Responsible for on-site safety decisions, venue assessment, and executing evacuation/shelter protocols during events. |
| **Volunteers/Staff** | Assist in the orderly movement of participants; maintain supervision of minors; follow IC instructions. |
| **Weather Watcher** | Designated volunteer responsible for monitoring NOAA radio/apps during large events. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**6.1 Regulatory Compliance**
This plan is designed to comply with OSHA 1910.38 (Emergency Action Plans) and the Texas Business Organizations Code regarding the fiduciary duty of care.

**6.2 Indemnification**
Pursuant to **Article VII (Indemnification)** of the Bylaws, any Director, Officer, or volunteer acting in good faith to protect life and safety during a disaster shall be indemnified by the Organization against claims or liability, provided their actions were not grossly negligent.

**6.3 Enforcement**
Failure to follow safety directives during an emergency, or failure by leadership to execute this plan (e.g., refusing to evacuate), may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
