### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Exposure Control Plan (ECP) is to eliminate or minimize occupational exposure to bloodborne pathogens in accordance with **OSHA Standard 29 CFR 1910.1030**. While Study GRC Inc. operates primarily as a remote-first organization, specific operational activities—including in-person conferences, workshops, and youth programs—create potential exposure risks for designated personnel.

This plan establishes the determination of exposure risks, the schedule and method of implementation for methods of compliance, Hepatitis B vaccination protocols, and post-exposure evaluation procedures.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to all employees, contractors, and designated volunteers of Study GRC Inc. who may reasonably be anticipated to have contact with blood or other potentially infectious materials (OPIM) as the result of performing their job duties.

**Inclusions:**
*   **Designated First Aid Providers:** Staff or volunteers explicitly assigned to provide medical assistance at events.
*   **Youth Program Leaders:** Personnel responsible for the supervision of minors where injury response may be required.
*   **Event Safety Officers:** Personnel responsible for managing incidents at physical venues.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Remote Workers:** Staff working exclusively from home offices with no in-person event duties.
*   **"Good Samaritan" Acts:** Employees or volunteers who are not designated as first aid providers but voluntarily assist an injured person. Such acts do not constitute "occupational exposure" under this plan.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Blood** | Human blood, human blood components, and products made from human blood. |
| **Bloodborne Pathogens** | Pathogenic microorganisms that are present in human blood and can cause disease in humans. These pathogens include, but are not limited to, Hepatitis B virus (HBV) and human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). |
| **Occupational Exposure** | Reasonably anticipated skin, eye, mucous membrane, or parenteral contact with blood or OPIM that may result from the performance of an employee's duties. |
| **OPIM** | Other Potentially Infectious Materials (e.g., semen, vaginal secretions, cerebrospinal fluid, synovial fluid, pleural fluid, pericardial fluid, peritoneal fluid, amniotic fluid, saliva in dental procedures, any body fluid that is visibly contaminated with blood). |
| **Universal Precautions** | An approach to infection control. According to the concept of Universal Precautions, all human blood and certain human body fluids are treated as if known to be infectious for HIV, HBV, and other bloodborne pathogens. |
| **Parenteral** | Piercing mucous membranes or the skin barrier through such events as needlesticks, human bites, cuts, and abrasions. |
| **Exposure Incident** | A specific eye, mouth, other mucous membrane, non-intact skin, or parenteral contact with blood or OPIM that results from the performance of an employee's duties. |

### 4. Policy Statements and Procedures

#### 4.1 Exposure Determination
Study GRC Inc. has performed an exposure determination without regard to the use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).

**Category A: Job Classifications in which ALL employees have occupational exposure:**
*   *None currently identified.* (Study GRC does not employ full-time medical staff).

**Category B: Job Classifications in which SOME employees have occupational exposure:**
*   **Event Safety Officers:** Only those assigned to physical events.
*   **Youth Program Facilitators:** Only those designated to oversee physical activities.
*   **Designated First Aiders:** Individuals specifically rostered to provide medical aid at conferences.

*Note: A list of all tasks and procedures in which occupational exposure occurs for Category B employees is maintained in the **EHS Roles and Responsibilities Matrix**.*

#### 4.2 Universal Precautions
All employees and designated volunteers must utilize **Universal Precautions**. All blood or OPIM will be considered infectious regardless of the perceived status of the source individual.

#### 4.3 Engineering and Work Practice Controls
Engineering and work practice controls shall be used to eliminate or minimize employee exposure.

1.  **Hand Washing:** Hand washing facilities must be readily accessible at all event venues. If hand washing facilities are not feasible (e.g., outdoor field events), the Organization shall provide antiseptic hand cleanser in conjunction with clean cloth/paper towels or antiseptic towelettes. Hands must be washed with soap and running water as soon as feasible after using antiseptic towelettes.
2.  **Sharps Disposal:** Contaminated needles and other contaminated sharps shall not be bent, recapped, or removed. They must be placed in appropriate containers that are closable, puncture-resistant, leakproof on sides and bottom, and labeled or color-coded.
3.  **Prohibited Actions:** Eating, drinking, smoking, applying cosmetics or lip balm, and handling contact lenses are prohibited in work areas where there is a reasonable likelihood of occupational exposure (e.g., inside a designated First Aid tent).
4.  **Minimizing Splashing:** All procedures involving blood or OPIM shall be performed in such a manner as to minimize splashing, spraying, spattering, and generation of droplets of these substances.

#### 4.4 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
Where occupational exposure remains after the institution of engineering and work practice controls, the Organization shall provide appropriate PPE at no cost to the employee/volunteer.

1.  **Availability:** PPE (gloves, CPR masks, eye protection) will be included in all **Event Safety Kits**.
2.  **Gloves:** Gloves shall be worn when it can be reasonably anticipated that the employee may have hand contact with blood, OPIM, mucous membranes, and non-intact skin. Hypoallergenic gloves, glove liners, powder-less gloves, or other similar alternatives shall be readily accessible to those who are allergic to the gloves normally provided.
3.  **Disposal:** All PPE must be removed prior to leaving the work area. Contaminated PPE must be placed in a designated biohazard bag for proper disposal.

#### 4.5 Hepatitis B Vaccination
In compliance with OSHA 1910.1030(f), Study GRC Inc. shall make the Hepatitis B vaccination series available to all employees who have occupational exposure.

1.  **Timing:** The vaccination will be made available after the employee has received the training required in Section 4.8 and within 10 working days of initial assignment to all employees who have occupational exposure.
2.  **Cost:** The vaccination shall be made available at no cost to the employee, at a reasonable time and place, and under the supervision of a licensed physician/healthcare professional.
3.  **Declination:** Employees who decline the Hepatitis B vaccination offered shall sign the **Hepatitis B Vaccination Declination Form**.
4.  **Post-Exposure:** If an employee initially declines the vaccination but at a later date while still covered under the standard decides to accept the vaccination, the Organization shall make it available at that time.

#### 4.6 Post-Exposure Evaluation and Follow-Up
Following a report of an exposure incident, the Organization shall make immediately available to the exposed employee a confidential medical evaluation and follow-up.

**Procedure:**
1.  **Immediate Action:** Wash the exposed area with soap and water. Flush splashes to the nose, mouth, or skin with water. Irrigate eyes with water or saline.
2.  **Reporting:** Report the incident immediately to the **Event Safety Officer** or **Executive Director**. Complete the **OSHA 301 Form (Incident Report)**.
3.  **Medical Evaluation:** The Organization will direct the employee to a healthcare professional. The evaluation will include:
    *   Documentation of the route(s) of exposure.
    *   Identification and documentation of the source individual (unless infeasible or prohibited by law).
    *   Collection and testing of blood for HBV and HIV serological status (with consent).
    *   Post-exposure prophylaxis, when medically indicated.
    *   Counseling and evaluation of reported illnesses.
4.  **Written Opinion:** The Organization shall obtain and provide the employee with a copy of the evaluating healthcare professional's written opinion within 15 days of the completion of the evaluation.

#### 4.7 Communication of Hazards
1.  **Labels and Signs:** Warning labels shall be affixed to containers of regulated waste, refrigerators and freezers containing blood or OPIM, and other containers used to store, transport, or ship blood or OPIM.
2.  **Symbol:** Labels shall include the biohazard symbol and the word "BIOHAZARD".
3.  **Color:** Red bags or red containers may be substituted for labels.

#### 4.8 Information and Training
The Organization shall ensure that all employees with occupational exposure participate in a training program at no cost and during working hours.

1.  **Frequency:** Training must be provided at the time of initial assignment to tasks where occupational exposure may take place and at least annually thereafter.
2.  **Content:** Training shall include:
    *   A copy and explanation of the OSHA standard.
    *   Epidemiology and symptoms of bloodborne diseases.
    *   Modes of transmission.
    *   Explanation of this ECP and how to obtain a copy.
    *   Recognition of tasks that may involve exposure.
    *   Use and limitations of methods to reduce exposure (engineering controls, work practices, PPE).
    *   Information on the Hepatitis B vaccine.
    *   Emergency procedures and reporting.
3.  **Interactive:** The training must provide an opportunity for interactive questions and answers with the person conducting the training session.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ultimate responsibility for the ECP. Ensures budget is available for PPE, vaccinations, and post-exposure evaluations. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Acts as the Safety Lead. Maintains the ECP, coordinates training, and manages records. |
| **Event Safety Officer** | Ensures Universal Precautions are followed at events. Maintains Event Safety Kits. Manages immediate incident response. |
| **Designated First Aid Providers** | Adhere to Universal Precautions. Use provided PPE. Report all exposure incidents immediately. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Supports the emotional well-being of staff following an exposure incident (non-medical support). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this plan puts the individual and the community at risk.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees or volunteers who fail to follow Universal Precautions or fail to use provided PPE may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Recordkeeping:** Medical records required by this standard shall be maintained in accordance with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1020. These records shall be kept confidential and maintained for at least the duration of employment plus 30 years. Training records shall be maintained for 3 years from the date on which the training occurred.
