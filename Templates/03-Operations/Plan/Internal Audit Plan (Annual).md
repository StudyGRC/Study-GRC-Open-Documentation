### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Internal Audit Plan (Annual)** is to define the scope, timing, and resource allocation for internal audit activities at Study GRC Inc. for the upcoming Fiscal Year. This plan is designed to provide independent, objective assurance and consulting services to add value and improve the Organization's operations.

This plan ensures that:
1.  Audit resources are focused on high-risk areas identified during the annual risk assessment.
2.  The Organization maintains compliance with **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)** regarding federal awards.
3.  Internal controls regarding financial reporting, youth protection, and information security are operating effectively.
4.  Governance processes defined in the **Bylaws** are adhered to.

### 2. Scope
This plan covers the Fiscal Year beginning January 1 and ending December 31 (per **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3**).

**In Scope:**
*   All operational departments (Education, Operations, IT/Security, Finance).
*   All financial processes, including grant management and procurement.
*   Information technology infrastructure and data privacy practices (Remote-first environment).
*   Youth protection protocols and COPPA compliance.
*   Governance and ethics compliance (Conflict of Interest, Board operations).

**Out of Scope:**
*   The annual *external* financial statement audit (conducted by an independent CPA firm), although internal audit will coordinate to minimize duplication of effort.
*   Legal defense strategy (privileged).

### 4. Plan Content

#### 4.1 Risk Assessment Methodology
This plan was developed based on a quantitative and qualitative risk assessment conducted in Q4 of the preceding year. Factors influencing the selection of audit engagements include:
*   **Financial Materiality:** Volume of transactions and grant funding levels.
*   **Regulatory Requirements:** 2 CFR 200 compliance, Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC), and COPPA.
*   **Operational Change:** Impact of new remote work tools or changes in key personnel.
*   **Prior Audit Findings:** Status of open CAPs from previous internal or external audits.
*   **Stakeholder Input:** Requests from the Audit & Risk Committee and Executive Director.

#### 4.2 Resource Allocation
The Internal Audit function will allocate resources as follows:
*   **Assurance Projects (Audits):** 60%
*   **Advisory/Consulting:** 15%
*   **Follow-Up on Prior Findings:** 10%
*   **External Audit Coordination:** 10%
*   **Administration/Planning:** 5%

#### 4.3 Annual Audit Schedule
The following engagements are scheduled for the Fiscal Year. The Audit & Risk Committee may adjust this schedule based on emerging risks.

| Quarter | Audit Engagement Title | Risk Rating | Objective/Scope | Regulatory Driver |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Q1** | **Governance & Ethics Review** | High | Verify Annual Conflict of Interest disclosures (**Bylaws Art. IX**), Board minutes completeness, and Officer election procedures. | IRS Form 990; Bylaws |
| **Q1** | **Financial Controls & Cash Management** | High | Test bank reconciliations, segregation of duties, and signature authority compliance. | GAAP; Fraud Prevention |
| **Q2** | **Federal Grant Compliance (2 CFR 200)** | Critical | Test allowable costs, time and effort reporting (PARs), and procurement standards for federal awards. | 2 CFR 200 Subpart E & F |
| **Q2** | **IT Security & Remote Access** | High | Review MFA implementation, VPN logs, and device encryption compliance in the remote-first environment. | NIST CSF; CISA CPG |
| **Q3** | **Youth Protection & Data Privacy** | Critical | Audit background check adjudication, COPPA consent mechanisms, and "Two-Deep" leadership compliance. | TX Family Code; COPPA |
| **Q3** | **Human Resources & Payroll** | Medium | Verify employee classification (Exempt/Non-Exempt), remote work agreements, and state tax withholdings. | FLSA; TX Labor Code |
| **Q4** | **Vendor Management & Procurement** | Medium | Review vendor due diligence, contract management, and competitive bidding documentation. | 2 CFR 200.318 |
| **Q4** | **Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery** | Medium | Test the effectiveness of the BCP/DRP and backup restoration procedures. | ISO 22301 |

#### 4.4 Contingency and Emerging Risks
A reserve of 150 audit hours is set aside to address:
*   Whistleblower investigations (per **Whistleblower Protection Policy**).
*   Special requests from the Board of Directors.
*   Rapid response to significant cybersecurity incidents.

#### 4.5 Reporting Standards
1.  **Engagement Reports:** A final report will be issued for each audit within 10 business days of fieldwork completion, graded as *Satisfactory*, *Needs Improvement*, or *Unsatisfactory*.
2.  **Quarterly Summary:** The Internal Audit Lead must present a summary of activities, significant findings, and CAP status to the Audit & Risk Committee quarterly.
3.  **Annual Report:** A year-end report summarizing assurance coverage and systemic trends.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Reviews and approves this Annual Audit Plan; ensures the Internal Audit function has sufficient resources and independence. |
| **Internal Audit Lead** | Develops the plan, executes engagements, tracks CAPs, and reports to the Committee. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures management cooperation with auditors and provides resources for remediation. |
| **Process Owners** | Participate in audit fieldwork, validate findings, and implement Corrective Action Plans within agreed timelines. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to execute this plan may result in a lack of assurance regarding the Organization's control environment, potentially leading to regulatory fines, loss of tax-exempt status, or grant debarment.

**Management Override:** Any attempt by management to restrict the scope of the Internal Audit function or influence findings must be reported immediately to the Chair of the Audit & Risk Committee.
