### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Chemical Hygiene Plan (CHP) is to protect Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, and program participants from health hazards associated with the use of hazardous chemicals in laboratory or educational demonstration settings. This Plan establishes the procedures, equipment, personal protective equipment (PPE), and work practices that are capable of protecting personnel from the health hazards presented by hazardous chemicals used in the workplace.

This document is drafted to strictly satisfy the requirements of **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1450** (Occupational Exposure to Hazardous Chemicals in Laboratories).

### 2. Scope
This Plan applies to all Study GRC Inc. personnel (employees, contractors, and volunteers) engaged in the laboratory use of hazardous chemicals.

**Applicability:**
*   **Educational Workshops:** Temporary laboratory setups used for STEM education, demonstrations, or training events.
*   **Kit Assembly:** Areas where chemical components for educational kits are stored, handled, or assembled.
*   **Rented Facilities:** Any third-party facility where Study GRC Inc. personnel are directing chemical handling activities.

**Exclusions:**
*   This Plan does not apply to chemically impregnated test media (e.g., dip-and-read strips) or commercially prepared kits where the hazardous chemicals are contained in a manner that prevents potential exposure.
*   This Plan does not apply to the use of standard office supplies (e.g., cleaning products, toner) used for their intended purpose.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Action Level** | A concentration designated in 29 CFR 1910 for a specific substance, calculated as an eight (8)-hour time-weighted average, which initiates certain required activities such as exposure monitoring and medical surveillance. |
| **Chemical Hygiene Officer (CHO)** | An employee designated by the employer who is qualified by training or experience to provide technical guidance in the development and implementation of the provisions of the Chemical Hygiene Plan. |
| **Hazardous Chemical** | A chemical for which there is statistically significant evidence based on at least one study conducted in accordance with established scientific principles that acute or chronic health effects may occur in exposed employees. |
| **Laboratory** | A facility where the "laboratory use of hazardous chemicals" occurs. For Study GRC Inc., this includes temporary educational setups and demonstration areas. |
| **Permissible Exposure Limit (PEL)** | The maximum amount or concentration of a chemical that a worker may be exposed to under OSHA regulations. |
| **SDS** | Safety Data Sheet. |

### 4. Plan Elements

#### 4.1 Designation of Responsible Personnel
In accordance with 29 CFR 1910.1450(e)(3)(vii), Study GRC Inc. must designate a **Chemical Hygiene Officer (CHO)**.
*   The **VP of Education & Training** shall serve as the primary CHO unless another qualified individual is formally appointed by the Executive Director.
*   The CHO is responsible for the implementation, maintenance, and annual review of this CHP.

#### 4.2 Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
All personnel must adhere to the following SOPs when handling hazardous chemicals:

**4.2.1 General Rules**
*   **No Eating/Drinking:** Eating, drinking, smoking, applying cosmetics, or handling contact lenses is strictly prohibited in areas where hazardous chemicals are used or stored.
*   **Unattended Operations:** Reactions or experiments involving hazardous chemicals must never be left unattended unless fail-safe provisions are in place.
*   **Horseplay:** Practical jokes or other behavior that might confuse, startle, or distract another worker is prohibited.

**4.2.2 Personal Hygiene**
*   Hands must be washed with soap and water immediately after removing gloves and before leaving the laboratory/demonstration area.
*   Long hair and loose clothing must be confined/tied back when working with chemicals or open flames.

**4.2.3 Housekeeping**
*   Work areas must be kept clean and free of obstructions.
*   Access to emergency equipment (eyewash stations, fire extinguishers, exits) must never be blocked.
*   Chemicals must be returned to proper storage immediately after use.

#### 4.3 Control Measures
Study GRC Inc. utilizes a hierarchy of controls to minimize exposure:

**4.3.1 Engineering Controls**
*   **Ventilation:** Volatile hazardous chemicals must only be used in well-ventilated areas. If a permanent fume hood is not available (e.g., at a temporary event site), the CHO must verify that general room ventilation is sufficient to keep exposure below PELs, or the activity must be cancelled.
*   **Containment:** Secondary containment (trays/bins) must be used for all liquid chemicals during transport and storage.

**4.3.2 Administrative Controls**
*   **Inventory Limits:** The quantity of chemicals on hand must be restricted to the minimum amount necessary for the specific educational event or kit run.
*   **Substitution:** Whenever feasible, hazardous chemicals shall be replaced with non-hazardous or less hazardous substitutes for educational demonstrations.

#### 4.4 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
PPE is the last line of defense and is mandatory as defined below:
*   **Eye Protection:** ANSI Z87.1 compliant chemical splash goggles must be worn at all times when handling liquid chemicals. Safety glasses with side shields are the minimum requirement for solid chemicals.
*   **Skin Protection:** Chemical-resistant gloves (appropriate for the specific chemical per the SDS) must be worn.
*   **Body Protection:** Lab coats or aprons must be worn to protect street clothing.
*   **Footwear:** Closed-toe shoes are mandatory. Sandals or perforated shoes are prohibited in laboratory areas.

#### 4.5 Procurement, Distribution, and Storage
*   **Procurement:** No hazardous chemical may be purchased or accepted without prior approval from the CHO.
*   **Receiving:** Shipments must be inspected immediately for breakage or leakage.
*   **Labeling:** All containers must be labeled in accordance with GHS standards (Name, Signal Word, Hazard Statement, Pictogram).
*   **Storage:**
    *   Chemicals must be stored by compatibility, not merely alphabetically.
    *   Flammables must be stored in approved flammable storage cabinets.
    *   Corrosives must be stored below eye level.

#### 4.6 Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
*   An SDS must be maintained for every hazardous chemical in the inventory.
*   **Access:** SDSs must be readily accessible to all personnel during work hours. For remote/traveling events, a digital repository (accessible offline) or a physical binder must be present at the site.

#### 4.7 Medical Consultation and Examinations
In compliance with 29 CFR 1910.1450(g), Study GRC Inc. provides medical attention to personnel under the following circumstances:
1.  **Symptoms:** Whenever an individual develops signs or symptoms associated with a hazardous chemical to which they may have been exposed.
2.  **Monitoring:** Where exposure monitoring reveals an exposure level routinely above the Action Level (or PEL).
3.  **Events:** Whenever an event takes place (spill, leak, explosion) resulting in the likelihood of a hazardous exposure.

*   **Provider:** Medical examinations will be performed by or under the direct supervision of a licensed physician at no cost to the employee/volunteer.
*   **Information Provided to Physician:** The CHO must provide the physician with the chemical identity, description of conditions of exposure, and description of symptoms.

#### 4.8 Information and Training
All personnel must receive training **prior** to their initial assignment to a work area where hazardous chemicals are present.
*   **Frequency:** Training must be repeated annually or when new hazards are introduced.
*   **Content:**
    *   The contents of OSHA 1910.1450.
    *   The location and availability of this CHP and SDSs.
    *   Permissible Exposure Limits (PELs).
    *   Signs and symptoms associated with exposure.
    *   Methods to detect the presence or release of chemicals.
    *   Physical and health hazards of chemicals in the work area.
    *   Measures to protect themselves (SOPs, PPE, Emergency Procedures).

#### 4.9 Waste Disposal
*   Chemical waste must **never** be disposed of in sanitary drains (sinks) or regular trash.
*   Waste must be collected in compatible, labeled containers ("Hazardous Waste").
*   Disposal must be coordinated through a licensed hazardous waste transporter in compliance with EPA and local regulations.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ultimate responsibility for chemical hygiene within the organization. Ensures resources are available for training and equipment. |
| **Chemical Hygiene Officer (CHO)** | Develops and implements the CHP. Conducts annual audits. Approves chemical procurement. Investigates accidents. Ensures SDS availability. |
| **Instructors / Supervisors** | Ensure strict adherence to the CHP during events/workshops. Ensure students/participants wear required PPE. Report safety issues to the CHO immediately. |
| **Employees / Volunteers** | Plan and conduct each operation in accordance with the CHP. Develop good personal chemical hygiene habits. Wear required PPE. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Plan poses severe health risks and legal liability.
*   **Violations:** Any deviation from this CHP, including failure to wear PPE or unauthorized chemical procurement, will result in disciplinary action.
*   **Consequences:** Disciplinary measures may include retraining, removal from laboratory/instructional duties, termination of employment, or termination of volunteer status.
*   **Legal Action:** Gross negligence resulting in injury or environmental damage may result in civil or criminal liability.
