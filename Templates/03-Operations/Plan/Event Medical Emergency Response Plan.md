### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Event Medical Emergency Response Plan (the "Plan") is to establish a standardized, rapid, and effective protocol for managing medical emergencies during physical events hosted by Study GRC Inc. This Plan is designed to protect the health and safety of all attendees, staff, and volunteers, minimize the impact of injuries or illnesses, and ensure compliance with the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) General Duty Clause and applicable Texas state laws regarding duty of care.

### 2. Scope
This Plan applies to all **physical, in-person events** organized, hosted, or managed by Study GRC Inc., regardless of location or size.

*   **In Scope:** Conferences, workshops, board meetings, community meetups, and training sessions held at physical venues.
*   **Applies to:** All employees, contractors, volunteers, Board Directors, and event participants (including minors).
*   **Exclusions:** Virtual/remote events (covered under separate Remote Work Safety protocols) and third-party events where Study GRC Inc. is solely a sponsor or attendee and does not exercise control over the venue.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Medical Emergency** | Any situation in which a person requires immediate medical intervention to prevent death or serious harm (e.g., cardiac arrest, severe bleeding, anaphylaxis). |
| **Non-Emergency Incident** | Minor injuries or illnesses requiring basic first aid but not immediate professional medical transport (e.g., minor cuts, headaches). |
| **Incident Commander (IC)** | The designated Study GRC Inc. staff member or volunteer lead responsible for managing the event site during a crisis. |
| **EMS** | Emergency Medical Services (9-1-1 responders). |
| **Good Samaritan** | An individual who, in good faith, administers emergency care at the scene of an emergency. |
| **AED** | Automated External Defibrillator. |

### 4. Plan Procedures

#### 4.1 Pre-Event Preparedness
Prior to the commencement of any physical event, the Event Lead must ensure the following:
1.  **Venue Assessment:** Identify the location of the nearest hospital, venue-specific AEDs, and emergency exits.
2.  **Medical Liaison:** Designate a specific staff member or volunteer as the "Medical Liaison" for the event.
3.  **Communication:** Verify cell service availability and establish a primary communication channel (e.g., radio channel or dedicated WhatsApp group) for safety alerts.
4.  **First Aid Kit:** Ensure a fully stocked, OSHA-compliant First Aid Kit is accessible at the registration desk or main command post.

#### 4.2 Emergency Response Protocol (The "Check-Call-Care" System)

**Step 1: Check the Scene and the Victim**
*   Ensure the scene is safe for responders. Do not enter if there are electrical hazards, fire, or violence.
*   Determine responsiveness of the victim.

**Step 2: Call for Help (Activation)**
*   **Life-Threatening:** Immediately designate a specific person to call **9-1-1**.
    *   *Script:* "This is [Name] at [Venue Address]. We have a victim with [Condition]. We are located at [Specific Room/Floor]."
*   **Notification:** Notify the Incident Commander immediately.

**Step 3: Care (Render Aid)**
*   **Scope of Practice:** Staff and volunteers must **only** provide care consistent with their training (e.g., CPR/AED, Basic First Aid). **Do not** perform medical procedures or administer medication (except assisting with an auto-injector if permitted by state law/training) unless licensed to do so.
*   **AED Usage:** If the victim is unconscious and not breathing, deploy an AED immediately if available.
*   **Privacy:** Establish a perimeter. Use screens or staff members to block the view of bystanders to protect the victim's dignity and privacy (HIPAA/Privacy best practices).

#### 4.3 Protocol for Minors (Youth Protection)
Given Study GRC Inc.'s K-12 focus and youth programming:
1.  **Parental Notification:** In the event of *any* injury to a minor, the parent/guardian must be notified immediately using the contact information provided in the *Parental Consent and Liability Waiver*.
2.  **Consent to Treat:** If a parent is not present during a life-threatening emergency, implied consent is assumed for life-saving measures. For non-life-threatening issues, staff must attempt to contact the parent before providing care, unless a signed *Medical Treatment Authorization Form* is on file.
3.  **Two-Deep Leadership:** Never leave a staff member alone with an injured minor in a closed room. Maintain "Two-Deep" supervision (two adults present) at all times during treatment or transport waiting.

#### 4.4 Transport
*   **Ambulance:** Required for chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, head injury with loss of consciousness, or anaphylaxis.
*   **Personal Vehicle:** Staff and volunteers are **prohibited** from transporting injured attendees in personal vehicles due to liability risks. If an ambulance is refused but transport is needed, a taxi or ride-share service should be used, accompanied by a friend or family member of the victim.

#### 4.5 Post-Incident Documentation
1.  **Incident Report:** An *Incident Investigation Report (OSHA 301 equivalent)* must be completed within 24 hours of the incident.
2.  **Witness Statements:** Collect names and contact info of witnesses.
3.  **Board Notification:** For serious injuries requiring hospitalization, the Executive Director must notify the Board of Directors within 4 hours.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Incident Commander (Event Lead)** | Takes overall charge of the scene; directs staff; ensures 9-1-1 is called; manages crowd control; notifies Executive Director. |
| **Medical Liaison / Safety Officer** | Primary point of contact for first aid; retrieves AED; interfaces with arriving EMS; ensures First Aid Kit is stocked. |
| **Staff & Volunteers** | Assist with crowd control; guide EMS to the victim; act as "scribes" to document timeline of events; protect victim privacy. |
| **Executive Director** | Manages external communication (media/legal); initiates insurance claims; notifies the Board; reviews Incident Report. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Provides emotional support to the victim’s family or traumatized witnesses; manages follow-up "care calls" after the event. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**6.1 Good Samaritan Protection**
Pursuant to the **Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code § 74.151**, Study GRC Inc. staff and volunteers who in good faith administer emergency care are generally protected from civil damages for acts performed during the emergency, unless the act is willfully or wantonly negligent.

**6.2 Indemnification**
In accordance with **Article VII (Indemnification)** of the Bylaws, Study GRC Inc. shall indemnify volunteers and staff against claims arising from their provision of emergency assistance, provided they acted in good faith and within the scope of their role and training.

**6.3 Failure to Comply**
Failure to report an injury, failure to call 9-1-1 in a life-threatening situation, or practicing medicine without a license may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential personal legal liability.
