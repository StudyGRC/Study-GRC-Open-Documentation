### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this **Gap Analysis and Action Plan** is to establish a systematic methodology for identifying, documenting, and remediating discrepancies between Study GRC Inc.'s current operational state ("Current Profile") and its desired state of compliance and security ("Target Profile").

This plan ensures the Organization fulfills its fiduciary duties under **Bylaws Article III (Board Governance)** and **Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** by proactively managing risks associated with cybersecurity, federal grant compliance (2 CFR 200), and Texas nonprofit statutes. It serves as the operational driver for the Organization's **Risk Register**.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to all organizational domains, including but not limited to:
*   **Information Security:** Alignment with NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0.
*   **Federal Compliance:** Adherence to Uniform Guidance (2 CFR 200) for federal awards.
*   **Data Privacy:** Compliance with TDPSA, COPPA, and GDPR where applicable.
*   **Governance:** Adherence to Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and Internal Bylaws.

**Exclusions:**
Minor operational inefficiencies that do not pose a legal, financial, or reputational risk are managed at the department level and are excluded from the formal institutional Gap Analysis unless they aggregate to a significant risk.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Current Profile** | A snapshot of the cybersecurity and compliance outcomes the Organization is currently achieving. |
| **Target Profile** | The desired state of cybersecurity and compliance outcomes selected based on risk appetite, mission requirements, and legal obligations. |
| **Gap** | The deficiency or delta between the Current Profile and the Target Profile (e.g., a missing policy, unimplemented technology, or lack of training). |
| **POAM** | **Plan of Action and Milestones**. A document that identifies tasks needing to be accomplished to remediate known vulnerabilities or gaps. |
| **Risk Register** | A central repository of identified risks, their severity, and the actions taken to manage them. |
| **Control Owner** | The individual responsible for implementing and maintaining a specific control or remediation action. |

### 4. Plan Components and Methodology

#### 4.1 Frequency and Triggers
A formal Gap Analysis must be conducted:
1.  **Annually:** To support the preparation of the Annual Financial Report and Transparency disclosures required by **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2**.
2.  **Post-Incident:** Following any significant security incident or data breach.
3.  **Fundamental Actions:** Prior to any Fundamental Action (Merger, Dissolution, Asset Sale) as defined in **Bylaws Article X**.
4.  **New Awards:** Upon receipt of a new Federal Grant with differing compliance requirements.

#### 4.2 The Gap Analysis Process
The Organization utilizes a four-step cycle to manage gaps:

**Step 1: Define the Target Profile**
The Board and Operational Leadership must define the required standards.
*   *Example:* "We must implement MFA on all accounts (NIST PR.AA-01)."
*   *Example:* "We must have a procurement policy compliant with 2 CFR 200.318."

**Step 2: Assess the Current Profile**
The CISO and Compliance Officers assess actual practices against the Target Profile.
*   *Method:* Interviews, technical scans, policy reviews, and observation.
*   *Remote-First Context:* Particular attention must be paid to decentralized endpoints and home office environments.

**Step 3: Identify and Document Gaps**
Any deviation where *Current < Target* is recorded as a Gap. Gaps are categorized by domain:
*   **Governance (GOV):** Missing policies or oversight.
*   **People (PPL):** Lack of training or staffing.
*   **Process (PRC):** Inefficient or non-compliant workflows.
*   **Technology (TECH):** Missing hardware, software, or configurations.

**Step 4: Risk Scoring**
Each Gap is assigned a risk score to determine prioritization:
*   **Critical:** Immediate threat to operations, funding, or legal standing. (Remediate < 30 days).
*   **High:** Significant risk of data loss or non-compliance. (Remediate < 90 days).
*   **Medium:** Moderate risk; compensating controls exist. (Remediate < 6 months).
*   **Low:** Minor deviation; accept risk or remediate as resources allow.

#### 4.3 The Action Plan (Remediation)
For every identified Gap, a **Plan of Action and Milestones (POAM)** entry must be created in the Risk Register. The Action Plan must include:

1.  **Specific Action:** What exactly will be done? (e.g., "Purchase and deploy YubiKeys for all Admins").
2.  **Resource Requirements:** Budget, personnel hours, or external expertise needed.
3.  **Timeline:** Start date and projected completion date.
4.  **Milestones:** Interim check-points for long-term projects.
5.  **Status Tracking:** Open, In Progress, Delayed, Completed, Risk Accepted.

#### 4.4 Risk Acceptance
If a Gap cannot be closed due to technical limitations or prohibitive costs, the Board of Directors may formally **Accept the Risk**.
*   Risk Acceptance must be documented in writing.
*   Must be reviewed annually to verify if the acceptance justification remains valid.
*   **Restriction:** Risks related to mandatory statutory compliance (e.g., IRS requirements, Texas Youth Protection laws) cannot be accepted; they must be mitigated.

#### 4.5 Reporting and Transparency
*   **Quarterly:** The CISO/Executive Director reports the status of High/Critical gaps to the Audit and Risk Committee.
*   **Annually:** A summary of the Gap Analysis and remediation progress is included in the Board's annual review of internal controls.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves the Target Profile. Reviews Critical risks and approves Risk Acceptance for high-impact items. |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Provides oversight of the Gap Analysis process. Verifies that the Action Plan is being executed on schedule. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Allocates budget and resources required to close identified gaps. Ensures operational teams prioritize remediation. |
| **CISO** | Owns the Gap Analysis process for IT/Security. Maintains the master Risk Register. Reports progress to the Board. |
| **Department Heads** | Serve as Control Owners. Responsible for executing specific remediation tasks within their departments (HR, Finance, Ops). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to accurately report gaps or falsification of remediation status is a violation of the **Board Code of Ethics** and the **Whistleblower Protection Policy**.

Failure to execute assigned remediation tasks within the Action Plan timeline without valid justification may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from volunteer service.
