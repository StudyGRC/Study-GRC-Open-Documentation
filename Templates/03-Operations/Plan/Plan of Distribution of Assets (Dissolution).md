### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Plan of Distribution of Assets (the "Plan") is to establish the legally binding procedure for the orderly liquidation, satisfaction of liabilities, and distribution of remaining assets of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") in the event of dissolution. This Plan ensures compliance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.305**, Internal Revenue Code (IRC) Section 501(c)(3), and **Article XI** of the Organization’s Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This Plan applies to all tangible and intangible assets, financial accounts, intellectual property, and liabilities held by the Organization at the time a resolution to dissolve is adopted.

*   **Applies to:** Board of Directors, Officers, Voting Members, and any appointed Liquidating Trustee.
*   **Trigger Event:** This Plan becomes operational only upon the affirmative vote of at least two-thirds (2/3) of all Voting Members to voluntarily dissolve the Organization, pursuant to **Bylaws Article X**.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Eligible Recipient** | An entity organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, or scientific purposes that qualifies as an exempt organization under IRC § 501(c)(3), or a government entity for public purpose. |
| **Fundamental Action** | A significant event altering the core structure of the Organization, including dissolution, requiring a supermajority vote per **Bylaws Article X**. |
| **Liquidating Trustee** | The Officer or Director designated by the Board to oversee the winding-up process. |
| **Work Product** | All content, code, curriculum, and materials created by the Organization, defined as "work made for hire" in **Bylaws Article IX**. |
| **Restricted Assets** | Assets held by the Organization upon condition requiring return, transfer, or conveyance upon dissolution (e.g., unspent grant funds with clawback clauses). |

### 4. Plan Statements

#### 4.1 Authorization and Adoption
In accordance with **TBOC § 22.302** and **Bylaws Article X**, the Board of Directors must adopt a resolution recommending dissolution and directing that the question of dissolution be submitted to a vote of the Voting Members.
*   **4.1.1:** The Board shall adopt this Plan of Distribution regarding the Organization's assets.
*   **4.1.2:** This Plan is contingent upon approval by at least two-thirds (2/3) of all Voting Members.

#### 4.2 Inventory and Valuation
Upon approval of dissolution, the Liquidating Trustee must immediately cease all regular business activities except those required to wind up affairs.
*   **4.2.1:** A full inventory of all assets (cash, hardware, software, intellectual property) and liabilities (accounts payable, contract obligations) must be generated within thirty (30) days.
*   **4.2.2:** All tangible assets not transferred to an Eligible Recipient in-kind shall be liquidated for fair market value to generate cash for the settlement of debts.

#### 4.3 Satisfaction of Liabilities
Before any distribution to charitable entities, the Organization must apply its assets to satisfy debts in the following order of priority, pursuant to **TBOC § 22.304**:
1.  **Administrative Expenses:** Costs associated with the liquidation (legal fees, accounting fees, filing fees).
2.  **Employee Wages:** Any outstanding compensation due to employees (if applicable).
3.  **Taxes:** Any outstanding federal, state, or local taxes.
4.  **General Creditors:** All other substantiated debts and obligations.

#### 4.4 Return of Conditional Assets
Assets held by the Organization subject to limitations permitting their use only upon a condition requiring return, transfer, or conveyance upon dissolution (e.g., specific grant restrictions) must be returned or transferred in accordance with such requirements.

#### 4.5 Distribution of Remaining Assets
After the discharge of all liabilities and the return of conditional assets, all remaining assets shall be distributed exclusively for charitable and educational purposes.
*   **4.5.1 Selection of Recipients:** The Board of Directors shall select one or more Eligible Recipients. Priority shall be given to organizations that:
    *   Are tax-exempt under IRC § 501(c)(3).
    *   Share a similar mission to Study GRC Inc. (e.g., advancing GRC knowledge, open-source education, or removing financial barriers to learning).
*   **4.5.2 Prohibition on Private Inurement:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.4**, no assets shall be distributed to any Director, Officer, Member, or private individual.

#### 4.6 Disposition of Intellectual Property (Open Source Mandate)
To honor the Organization’s commitment to "zero-cost, open-source resources" (**Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**):
*   **4.6.1:** Intellectual Property (IP) owned by the Organization (curriculum, code, frameworks) shall **not** be sold to a for-profit entity for exclusive use.
*   **4.6.2:** IP shall be transferred to an Eligible Recipient capable of maintaining the open-source nature of the work, or released into the public domain if a suitable recipient cannot be found, ensuring continued public access.

#### 4.7 Final Filing
Upon completion of the distribution, the Liquidating Trustee shall execute and file the **Certificate of Termination** (Form 652) with the Texas Secretary of State and file the final IRS Form 990 (marked "Final Return").

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Adopts the resolution to dissolve and selects Eligible Recipients for asset distribution. |
| **Voting Members** | Approves the Dissolution and Plan of Distribution via a 2/3 supermajority vote (**Bylaws Art. X**). |
| **Liquidating Trustee** | Executes the sale of assets, payment of debts, and transfer of remaining funds/IP. |
| **Treasurer** | Ensures all final financial reports are accurate and tax filings are completed. |
| **Secretary** | Records the minutes of the dissolution vote and maintains records during the retention period. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Plan may result in personal liability for Directors under **TBOC § 22.226** for wrongful distribution of assets. Any distribution made to non-exempt entities or private individuals may result in the revocation of tax-exempt status and the imposition of excise taxes under IRC § 4958 (Intermediate Sanctions).
