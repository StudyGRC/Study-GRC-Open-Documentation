### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Heat Illness Prevention Plan (the "Plan") is to eliminate or minimize the risk of heat-related illness and injury to employees, volunteers, contractors, and event participants associated with Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization").

Given the Organization's incorporation and frequent operation within the State of Texas, where extreme heat is a recognized environmental hazard, this Plan establishes mandatory protocols to comply with the General Duty Clause of the Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA) and to uphold the Organization's commitment to community safety.

### 2. Scope
This Plan applies to all in-person activities conducted by the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   Outdoor community events, fundraisers, and educational workshops.
*   Indoor events held in facilities without adequate climate control.
*   Logistical setup and teardown activities (loading/unloading) performed by staff or volunteers.

**Exclusions:** This Plan does not apply to employees or volunteers working remotely from their personal residences or climate-controlled home offices, provided those environments are maintained at safe temperatures.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Heat Index (HI)** | A measure indicating how hot it feels when relative humidity is factored in with the actual air temperature. |
| **Heat Exhaustion** | A condition caused by excessive loss of water and salt, usually through sweating. Symptoms include heavy sweating, weakness, dizziness, nausea, and headache. |
| **Heat Stroke** | A life-threatening condition where the body's temperature regulation fails. Symptoms include confusion, loss of consciousness, seizures, and hot/dry skin or profuse sweating. **This is a medical emergency.** |
| **High Heat** | Environmental conditions where the Heat Index equals or exceeds 90°F (32.2°C). |
| **Acclimatization** | The physiological process of the body adjusting to changes in environmental temperature. |
| **Potable Water** | Water that is safe to drink, free from contamination, and suitably cool. |

### 4. Procedures and Protocols

#### 4.1 Environmental Monitoring
The designated Event Manager or Site Supervisor must monitor weather conditions prior to and during any in-person activity.
1.  **Forecast Review:** Weather forecasts must be reviewed 48 hours and 24 hours prior to an event.
2.  **Real-Time Monitoring:** During events, the Heat Index must be checked hourly using a reliable weather application (e.g., OSHA-NIOSH Heat Safety Tool) or on-site equipment.

#### 4.2 Risk Thresholds and Action Levels
The Organization adopts the following tiered response system based on the Heat Index (HI):

*   **Level 1: Caution (HI < 90°F):** Basic hydration and optional shade availability.
*   **Level 2: High Heat (HI 90°F - 100°F):** Mandatory "High Heat Procedures" (Section 4.5) activated. Pre-shift meetings regarding heat safety are required.
*   **Level 3: Extreme Danger (HI > 100°F):**
    *   Non-essential physical labor must cease.
    *   Consideration must be given to canceling, postponing, or relocating the event to a climate-controlled environment.
    *   Work/Rest cycles must be strictly enforced (e.g., 15 minutes work / 45 minutes rest).

#### 4.3 Provision of Water
Access to water is a fundamental right for all staff and volunteers.
1.  **Availability:** Cool, potable water must be available at all times at no cost to the individual.
2.  **Quantity:** The Organization must supply enough water to provide at least one (1) quart per employee/volunteer per hour for the entire shift.
3.  **Location:** Water stations must be located as close as practicable to the work/activity area.
4.  **Encouragement:** Supervisors must actively encourage frequent consumption of small amounts of water, rather than large amounts infrequently.

#### 4.4 Access to Shade and Cooling
1.  **Shade Requirement:** When the temperature exceeds 80°F, shade must be present to accommodate all staff/volunteers on a break/recovery period.
2.  **Nature of Shade:** Shade may be natural (trees) or artificial (tents, pavilions). Shade is not considered adequate if heat in the area of shade defeats the purpose of shade (e.g., inside a hot car).
3.  **Preventative Recovery:** Employees or volunteers suffering from heat stress must be allowed to take a preventative cool-down rest in the shade for a period of no less than 5 minutes.

#### 4.5 High Heat Procedures (HI ≥ 90°F)
When the Heat Index exceeds 90°F, the following additional procedures are mandatory:
1.  **Pre-Shift Meeting:** Review high heat hazards and emergency response procedures before work begins.
2.  **Mandatory Observation:** Supervisors must observe employees for signs of heat illness.
3.  **Buddy System:** Personnel must be paired up and instructed to monitor each other for signs of heat illness.
4.  **Communication:** Ensure reliable communication (cell phone or radio) is available to summon emergency medical services.

#### 4.6 Emergency Response Protocol
If a person exhibits signs of severe heat illness (Heat Stroke):
1.  **Call 911 Immediately.** Do not delay.
2.  **Move the Victim:** Move the person to a shaded, cool area.
3.  **Cool the Victim:** Remove outer clothing. Fan the victim and mist with water or apply ice packs to armpits and groin.
4.  **Do Not Leave Alone:** Stay with the victim until emergency services arrive.
5.  **Fluids:**
    *   If conscious and alert: Offer sips of cool water.
    *   If unconscious or vomiting: **DO NOT** give fluids.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures adequate budget is allocated for hydration, shade structures, and safety equipment. Authorizes event cancellation due to extreme weather. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Oversees participant welfare during community events; ensures water stations are accessible to attendees. |
| **Event Manager / Site Supervisor** | Acts as the "Competent Person" for safety. Monitors Heat Index, enforces breaks, and initiates emergency response if necessary. |
| **Staff & Volunteers** | Responsible for self-monitoring, utilizing the buddy system, and reporting any symptoms of heat illness immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Health and safety are paramount.
*   **Stop Work Authority:** Any staff member or volunteer has the authority and obligation to halt activities if they believe heat conditions pose an immediate threat to life or health, without fear of retaliation (refer to *Whistleblower Protection Policy*).
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Supervisors who willfully deny access to water, shade, or necessary recovery breaks may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination or removal from the Board/Volunteer corps.
