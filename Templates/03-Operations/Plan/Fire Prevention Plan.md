### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Fire Prevention Plan is to eliminate the causes of fire, prevent loss of life and property by fire, and comply with the Occupational Safety and Health Administration’s (OSHA) standard on fire prevention plans, specifically **29 CFR 1910.39**. This plan outlines the fuel sources (hazardous materials) and ignition sources common to our operations and defines the methods required to control them.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to all operations conducted by Study GRC Inc., specifically addressing three distinct operational environments:
1.  **The Registered Office:** As defined in Bylaws Article I, Section 1.2.
2.  **Remote Work Locations:** Home offices used by employees and contractors (limited to equipment provided by the Organization and general safety guidance).
3.  **Event Venues:** Temporary physical locations rented or utilized for conferences, training sessions, or community gatherings.

**Exclusions:** This plan does not supersede local municipal fire codes or the specific fire safety plans of third-party venues (e.g., hotels, convention centers), which take precedence during on-site events.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Combustible Material** | A solid or liquid that can be easily ignited and burned (e.g., paper, cardboard, wood, certain plastics). |
| **Flammable Liquid** | Any liquid having a flashpoint at or below 199.4°F (93°C). |
| **Ignition Source** | Anything that provides heat, spark, or flame sufficient to cause combustion (e.g., open flames, static electricity, hot surfaces, electrical circuits). |
| **Class A Fire** | Fires involving ordinary combustibles such as wood, paper, cloth, rubber, and many plastics. |
| **Class C Fire** | Fires involving energized electrical equipment. |
| **Egress** | A continuous and unobstructed path of exit travel from any point in a building or structure to a public way. |

### 4. Plan Elements (OSHA 1910.39 Compliance)

#### 4.1 Major Fire Hazards
The following are identified as the primary fire hazards associated with Study GRC Inc. operations:

*   **Office/Remote Environments:**
    *   Accumulation of waste paper and cardboard (Class A).
    *   Overloaded electrical outlets, power strips, and extension cords (Class C).
    *   Malfunctioning IT equipment (servers, laptops, monitors) generating excessive heat.
    *   Unauthorized use of space heaters.
*   **Event Environments:**
    *   High-voltage Audio/Visual (AV) equipment and temporary cabling.
    *   Decorative materials (drapes, banners) that are not flame-retardant.
    *   Accumulation of packaging materials during setup/teardown.
    *   Catering heating elements (chafing dishes, Sterno fuel).

#### 4.2 Handling and Storage Procedures
To control the accumulation of flammable and combustible waste materials:
*   **Waste Disposal:** All combustible waste materials (paper, packing material) must be disposed of in metal or fire-resistant containers and removed from the work area daily.
*   **Storage:** Combustible materials must not be stored in mechanical rooms, electrical closets, or within 18 inches of light fixtures or sprinkler heads.
*   **Event Logistics:** Shipping boxes and pallets must be removed from the immediate event floor to a designated loading dock or storage area immediately after setup.

#### 4.3 Potential Ignition Sources and Control Procedures
*   **Electrical Equipment:**
    *   Only UL-approved power strips with internal circuit breakers may be used. Daisy-chaining (plugging one power strip into another) is strictly prohibited.
    *   Electrical cords must be inspected regularly for fraying or damage.
*   **Space Heaters:**
    *   Portable space heaters are prohibited in the Registered Office and at Event Venues unless explicitly authorized by the Venue Manager.
    *   If authorized, heaters must have a tip-over shut-off switch and be kept at least 3 feet away from combustible materials.
*   **Open Flames:**
    *   Candles, incense, and open flames are prohibited in all work areas.
    *   Sterno fuel for catering must be managed by licensed catering vendors with a fire extinguisher present.
*   **Smoking:**
    *   Smoking (including vaping) is prohibited within all Organization facilities and within 25 feet of any entrance, in accordance with local ordinances.

#### 4.4 Fire Protection Equipment and Systems
*   **Maintenance Responsibility:**
    *   **Registered Office:** The landlord/property manager is responsible for maintaining the building's sprinkler system and wall-mounted extinguishers. The Executive Director must verify current inspection tags annually.
    *   **Remote Offices:** Employees are strongly encouraged to maintain a functional smoke detector and a multi-purpose (ABC) fire extinguisher in their workspace.
    *   **Event Venues:** The Event Lead must verify the location and accessibility of fire extinguishers and pull stations during the initial site walkthrough.

#### 4.5 Housekeeping
*   Exits, stairwells, and corridors must remain free of obstruction at all times.
*   IT equipment (laptops, servers) must be kept free of dust accumulation and have adequate ventilation to prevent overheating.

### 5. Specific Operational Protocols

#### 5.1 Remote Work Fire Safety (Home Offices)
While the Organization cannot inspect private residences, all remote employees must adhere to the following safety standards as part of the **Remote Work Agreement**:
1.  **Electrical Safety:** Do not overload residential circuits. Ensure home wiring is up to code.
2.  **Egress:** Identify two ways out of the home workspace in case of emergency.
3.  **Equipment:** Turn off non-essential work equipment at the end of the workday.

#### 5.2 Event Venue Fire Safety
For every physical event hosted by Study GRC Inc., the designated **Event Lead** must perform the following:
1.  **Pre-Event Check:** Confirm that all emergency exits are unlocked and unobstructed.
2.  **Capacity Control:** Ensure attendance does not exceed the posted occupancy load for the room.
3.  **Announcement:** At the start of the event, announce the location of emergency exits to all attendees.
4.  **Wiring:** Ensure all temporary cables (AV/Power) are taped down or covered with cable ramps to prevent tripping and electrical damage.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

In accordance with OSHA 1910.39(d), the following personnel are responsible for maintaining the fuel source hazards and fire protection equipment:

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ultimate responsibility for the Fire Prevention Plan. Responsible for ensuring the Registered Office complies with lease/fire safety requirements. |
| **Director of Operations** | Responsible for the control of fuel source hazards (waste disposal, storage). Responsible for maintaining this Plan. |
| **Event Lead / Coordinator** | Responsible for on-site fire safety verification at third-party venues (checking exits, extinguishers, and vendor compliance). |
| **All Employees** | Responsible for maintaining a fire-safe workspace, reporting hazards, and knowing the location of exits and extinguishers. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this plan, particularly regarding the unauthorized use of open flames, daisy-chaining power strips, or blocking emergency exits, may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
