### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Strategic Plan is to define the long-term vision, strategic goals, and operational roadmap for Study GRC Inc. for the three-year period commencing [Current Year]. This document serves as the primary governance tool to ensure all organizational activities remain strictly aligned with the charitable and educational purposes defined in **Article I, Section 1.3** of the Bylaws. It provides the framework for resource allocation, grant seeking, and performance measurement to mitigate the risk of mission drift and ensure compliance with IRS 501(c)(3) operational tests.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to the entire Organization, including the Board of Directors, Operational Officers, Advisory Board, Committees, employees, and volunteers. It governs the prioritization of projects, the development of the annual budget, and the establishment of departmental Objectives and Key Results (OKRs).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Mission Drift** | The gradual divergence of a non-profit organization from its stated charitable purpose, potentially jeopardizing tax-exempt status. |
| **OKR** | Objectives and Key Results; a goal-setting framework used to define measurable goals and track their outcomes. |
| **The Ecosystem** | The community-driven platform and repository of open-source resources maintained by the Organization. |
| **Fundamental Action** | Significant events altering the core structure (e.g., mergers, dissolution) as defined in Bylaws Article X. |
| **K-12 Focus** | Educational initiatives specifically targeted at primary and secondary education students to introduce GRC concepts early. |

### 4. Strategic Pillars and Goals

The Organization’s strategy for the [Year]-[Year+3] horizon is built upon four foundational pillars derived from the Bylaws.

#### 4.1 Pillar I: Educational Excellence & Open Source Accessibility
**Alignment:** Bylaws § 1.3 ("Removing financial barriers by providing zero-cost, open-source resources").

*   **Strategic Goal 1.1:** Develop and publish a comprehensive, open-source GRC Common Body of Knowledge (CBK).
    *   **Year 1:** Launch the initial repository with core governance and risk modules.
    *   **Year 2:** Expand content to include specialized compliance frameworks (e.g., NIST, ISO, SOC2) and K-12 specific introductory tracks.
    *   **Year 3:** Establish a peer-review committee to validate content accuracy and currency.
*   **Strategic Goal 1.2:** Democratize access to GRC certification preparation.
    *   **Objective:** Create zero-cost study guides and practice exams for major industry certifications.

#### 4.2 Pillar II: Community Growth & Inclusive Culture
**Alignment:** Bylaws § 1.3 ("Fostering a community-driven ecosystem... Empowering learners").

*   **Strategic Goal 2.1:** Build a vibrant, safe, and inclusive online community.
    *   **Year 1:** Launch the official community platform with the "Director of Kindness and Generosity" (DKG) moderating culture.
    *   **Year 2:** Achieve a membership base of 1,000 active Community Participants.
    *   **Year 3:** Launch a formal Mentorship Program pairing experienced practitioners with entrants.
*   **Strategic Goal 2.2:** Youth Outreach and Safeguarding.
    *   **Objective:** Partner with three (3) K-12 institutions or youth organizations to pilot GRC literacy programs, ensuring strict adherence to the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.

#### 4.3 Pillar III: Financial Sustainability & Grant Readiness
**Alignment:** Bylaws § 1.4 (Nonprofit Status) and Article VIII (Finance).

*   **Strategic Goal 3.1:** Diversify revenue streams to ensure longevity.
    *   **Year 1:** Secure 501(c)(3) determination and establish the "Supporting Member" dues infrastructure.
    *   **Year 2:** Achieve federal grant readiness (compliance with 2 CFR 200) and secure initial seed grants.
    *   **Year 3:** Secure corporate sponsorships to cover 50% of operational technology costs.
*   **Strategic Goal 3.2:** Maintain impeccable financial transparency.
    *   **Objective:** Publish Annual Financial Reports and Form 990s on the public website within 30 days of filing, exceeding regulatory minimums.

#### 4.4 Pillar IV: Governance Maturity & Operational Resilience
**Alignment:** Bylaws Article III (Board Governance) and Article IX (Policies).

*   **Strategic Goal 4.1:** Transition from "Founding Board" to "Staggered Board."
    *   **Objective:** Execute the transition plan defined in Bylaws § 3.11 by January 2028, ensuring a pipeline of qualified Director candidates is established via the Advisory Board.
*   **Strategic Goal 4.2:** Achieve operational security maturity.
    *   **Objective:** The CISO shall implement a security posture equivalent to NIST CSF Implementation Tier 3 (Repeatable) to protect member data and intellectual property.

### 5. Implementation and Monitoring

#### 5.1 Annual Operating Plan
The Executive Director must translate this 3-Year Strategic Plan into an **Annual Operating Plan** and **Budget**, to be presented to the Board for approval every January (Bylaws § 2.5).

#### 5.2 Quarterly Reviews
The Board of Directors shall review progress against Strategic Goals on a quarterly basis. The Executive Director is required to present a "Strategic Dashboard" highlighting:
*   Progress on OKRs.
*   Variance from the Strategic Plan.
*   Emerging risks or opportunities requiring a pivot.

#### 5.3 Adaptive Management
The Board reserves the right to amend this plan via a majority vote if external factors (e.g., regulatory changes, funding shifts) necessitate a change in direction, provided such changes remain within the scope of the Articles of Incorporation.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Strategic Plan; monitors high-level progress; ensures the plan prevents mission drift. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Owns the execution of the plan; translates strategic goals into tactical OKRs; reports progress to the Board. |
| **VP of Education** | Executes Pillar I; manages curriculum development and open-source contributions. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Executes Pillar II; manages community health and member engagement metrics. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Executes Pillar III; aligns the annual budget with strategic priorities; monitors financial sustainability. |
| **CISO** | Executes Pillar IV (Security); ensures technology infrastructure supports strategic goals securely. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to align operational activities and budget allocations with this Strategic Plan may result in:
1.  **For Officers/Staff:** Performance improvement plans or termination of employment/service for mismanagement.
2.  **For the Organization:** Risk of "Mission Drift," potentially endangering tax-exempt status or eligibility for federal grants.

Significant deviations from this plan must be authorized by the Board of Directors in writing.
