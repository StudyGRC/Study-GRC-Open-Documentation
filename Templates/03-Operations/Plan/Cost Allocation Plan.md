### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Cost Allocation Plan (CAP) is to mandate the methodology used by Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") to identify, accumulate, and distribute costs to specific funding sources, programs, and grants. This plan ensures that the Organization claims full recovery of allowable costs in a manner that is consistent, equitable, and compliant with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and Federal Uniform Guidance (2 CFR Part 200, Subpart E).

This plan serves as the mathematical and legal basis for financial reporting to the Board of Directors and external funding agencies.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to all financial transactions, budgeting processes, and grant management activities within the Organization. It governs:
*   **All Employees and Contractors:** Whose time or expenses are charged to more than one functional area.
*   **All Funding Sources:** Including federal grants, private foundation grants, and unrestricted general funds.
*   **Operational Context:** Specifically addresses the Organization's remote-first structure, where traditional allocation bases (e.g., square footage) are less relevant than digital usage or labor hours.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Allocable Cost** | A cost is allocable to a Federal award or other cost objective if the goods or services involved are chargeable or assignable to that cost objective in accordance with relative benefits received. |
| **Direct Costs** | Costs that can be identified specifically with a particular final cost objective, such as a Federal award, or other internally or externally funded activity, or that can be directly assigned to such activities relatively easily with a high degree of accuracy. |
| **Indirect Costs (F&A)** | Costs incurred for a common or joint purpose benefitting more than one cost objective, and not readily assignable to the cost objectives specifically benefitted, without effort disproportionate to the results achieved. |
| **Allocation Basis** | The method used to distribute shared costs (e.g., Full-Time Equivalents (FTE), Total Direct Costs, Labor Hours). |
| **Unallowable Costs** | Costs that cannot be charged to federal awards, either directly or indirectly (e.g., alcoholic beverages, lobbying, bad debts). |
| **Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC)** | All direct salaries and wages, applicable fringe benefits, materials and supplies, services, travel, and up to the first $25,000 of each subaward. MTDC excludes equipment, capital expenditures, charges for patient care, rental costs, tuition remission, scholarships and fellowships, participant support costs, and the portion of each subaward in excess of $25,000. |

### 4. Plan Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles of Allocation
In accordance with 2 CFR § 200.403, for a cost to be allowable and allocated to a specific grant or program, it must satisfy the following criteria:
1.  **Necessary and Reasonable:** The cost is required for the performance of the award and is not excessive.
2.  **Allocable:** The cost benefits the award in proportion to the amount charged.
3.  **Consistent Treatment:** Costs incurred for the same purpose in like circumstances must be treated consistently as either direct or indirect costs.
4.  **Adequately Documented:** Supported by accounting records (invoices, receipts, timesheets).

#### 4.2 Classification of Costs
The Organization classifies costs into three functional categories: **Program Services**, **Management & General (M&G)**, and **Fundraising**.

**4.2.1 Direct Costs**
The following costs are charged directly to the specific program or grant they benefit:
*   Salaries and benefits of staff working directly on program deliverables (e.g., VP of Education developing curriculum).
*   Consultant fees for specific project tasks.
*   Materials, supplies, and software licenses used exclusively for a specific program.
*   Travel expenses incurred for specific program activities.
*   Participant support costs (e.g., scholarships for GRC training).

**4.2.2 Indirect (Shared) Costs**
The following costs are pooled and allocated because they benefit the entire organization:
*   Salaries and benefits of the Executive Director (unless time is tracked to a specific project), CFO, and administrative support.
*   Board of Directors meeting expenses.
*   General liability, D&O, and cyber insurance.
*   General accounting and legal services.
*   Organization-wide software (e.g., Slack, Google Workspace, HRIS) where user-specific billing is not feasible.
*   Audit fees.

**4.2.3 Unallowable Costs**
Per 2 CFR 200 Subpart E, the following costs are identified in the General Ledger and excluded from any billing to federal awards:
*   Fundraising expenses (must be tracked as a separate functional category but cannot be charged to federal grants).
*   Lobbying activities.
*   Fines and penalties.
*   Alcoholic beverages.
*   Bad debts.

#### 4.3 Allocation Methodologies
Due to the Organization's remote-first nature, "Square Footage" is not utilized as a primary allocation basis. The Organization utilizes the following bases for distributing shared costs:

| Cost Category | Allocation Basis | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shared Personnel (Admin)** | Total Direct Labor Hours | Admin support correlates with the volume of staff activity. |
| **Rent/Occupancy (if applicable)** | N/A (Remote First) | If physical space is acquired, allocation will be based on FTE usage. |
| **General Software/IT** | Full-Time Equivalents (FTE) | Software costs scale with the number of staff members. |
| **General Insurance** | Total Direct Costs | Risk exposure correlates with the total volume of financial activity. |
| **Audit/Legal Fees** | Total Direct Costs | Professional oversight covers the aggregate of organizational activity. |

#### 4.4 Indirect Cost Rate Election
Until such time that the Organization negotiates a specific rate with a cognizant federal agency, Study GRC Inc. elects to use the **De Minimis Rate of 10%** of Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC) as allowed under 2 CFR § 200.414(f).

1.  **Application:** This 10% rate is applied to MTDC to cover indirect costs for all federal awards that do not have a statutory cap on indirect costs.
2.  **Consistency:** This election is used indefinitely until the Organization chooses to negotiate for a rate, which it may do at any time.

#### 4.5 Time and Effort Reporting
To support the allocation of salary and benefits—the Organization's largest cost center—all employees funded by more than one cost objective must complete **Personnel Activity Reports (PARs)** or Timesheets in accordance with the *Time and Effort Reporting Policy*.
*   Allocations based on budget estimates alone are strictly prohibited.
*   Adjustments to the General Ledger must be made quarterly to reflect actual time worked vs. budgeted time.

#### 4.6 Monthly Closing and Allocation Process
1.  **Coding:** All expenses are coded to a specific Department and Class upon entry into the accounting system.
2.  **Pool Accumulation:** Indirect costs are accumulated in a specific "Indirect Cost Pool" cost center.
3.  **Distribution:** At month-end, the CFO calculates the allocation based on the methodologies in Section 4.3 and posts a Journal Entry to distribute costs from the pool to the respective programs/grants.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves the Cost Allocation Plan annually; reviews financial reports to ensure reasonable cost ratios. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures operational adherence to the plan; certifies time and effort reports for direct reports. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Maintains the General Ledger; performs monthly allocation calculations; ensures compliance with 2 CFR 200; prepares the Indirect Cost Proposal (if applicable). |
| **Program Managers** | Review monthly budget-to-actual reports to verify that allocated costs assigned to their programs are accurate and reasonable. |
| **All Staff** | Accurately code expenses and track time to the correct project codes. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this plan may result in the disallowance of costs by grantors, requiring the Organization to repay funds from unrestricted reserves.

*   **Internal Audit:** The Finance Committee will review cost allocations semi-annually.
*   **External Audit:** This plan serves as the primary evidence for the Single Audit regarding the treatment of indirect costs.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Intentional miscoding of expenses to hide cost overruns or misuse restricted funds constitutes fraud and will result in immediate termination and potential legal action.
