### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Emergency Succession Plan is to establish a clear, legally binding protocol for the immediate restoration of governance in the catastrophic event that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") is left with zero (0) serving Directors. This plan operationalizes **Article III, Section 3.10** of the Bylaws ("Contingency for 'No Board'"), ensuring the Organization avoids involuntary dissolution, receivership, or operational paralysis.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies strictly to the **"Zero-Director Event"** scenario.
*   **Applies to:** Voting Members (Supporting and Honorary), Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director), and the Registered Agent.
*   **Exclusions:** This plan does not apply to standard Board vacancies where at least one (1) Director remains in office (refer to *Board Vacancy Filling Procedure GOV-PRC-005*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Zero-Director Event** | A specific crisis scenario where the number of active, serving Directors on the Board reaches zero (0) due to simultaneous resignation, removal, death, or incapacitation. |
| **The Convener** | The Voting Member who formally issues the "Call to Meeting" to initiate the emergency election process. |
| **Interim Board** | The Board of Directors elected during the Special Emergency Meeting to serve until the next Annual Meeting or as defined by the transition plan. |
| **Operational Officer** | Non-voting staff or volunteer leadership (e.g., CEO, CISO) who manage day-to-day operations but lack corporate governance authority. |

### 4. Plan Statements

#### 4.1 Detection and Declaration of Emergency
Upon the resignation, removal, or death of the final remaining Director:
1.  **Immediate Notification:** The Executive Director (CEO) or the highest-ranking Operational Officer must immediately verify the vacancy and declare a **"State of Governance Emergency."**
2.  **Registered Agent Notification:** The Executive Director must notify the Organization's Registered Agent in Texas of the status to ensure legal correspondence is routed correctly.
3.  **Asset Freeze:** The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or Treasurer (if an officer distinct from the Board) must restrict all non-essential financial outflows until a new Board is seated, preserving assets for the incoming governance body.

#### 4.2 The "Convener" Protocol
Per **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.10**, *any* Voting Member may call the Special Emergency Meeting. To ensure order and prevent duplicative notices, the following hierarchy of "Conveners" is established, though it does not legally restrict any Member from acting:
1.  **Primary Convener:** The Executive Director (if they hold Voting Membership status).
2.  **Secondary Convener:** The longest-serving Voting Member (by tenure).
3.  **Tertiary Convener:** Any Voting Member in good standing.

The Convener is responsible for issuing the **Notice of Special Emergency Meeting**.

#### 4.3 Notice of Special Emergency Meeting
The Convener must issue a written notice to all Voting Members via the Organization's primary communication channels (email and community platform).
*   **Timeline:** The notice must be sent at least **10 days** prior to the meeting date, in strict compliance with Bylaws Article III, Section 3.10.
*   **Content:** The notice must state:
    *   The specific purpose: "Election of an Interim Board of Directors due to total vacancy."
    *   The date, time, and virtual meeting link.
    *   The call for immediate self-nominations.

#### 4.4 Emergency Nomination Process
Due to the urgency of the situation, the standard 14-day nomination window (Bylaws Art. II, Sec 2.5) is waived in favor of an expedited process:
1.  **Floor Nominations:** Nominations will be accepted electronically up to 24 hours prior to the meeting and from the "virtual floor" during the meeting.
2.  **Eligibility:** Nominees must be Voting Members in good standing.
3.  **Vetting:** The Operational Officers will perform a rapid verification of "Good Standing" status for all nominees prior to the vote.

#### 4.5 Conduct of the Special Emergency Meeting
1.  **Chairperson:** The Convener shall act as the temporary Chairperson solely for the purpose of facilitating the election.
2.  **Quorum:** A majority (more than 50%) of Voting Members must be present (virtually) or represented to constitute a quorum (Bylaws Art. II, Sec 2.4).
3.  **Platform:** The meeting will be held via the Organization's standard video conferencing tool (e.g., Zoom/Teams) to allow for identity verification.

#### 4.6 Election of Interim Board
1.  **Minimum Seats:** The Membership must elect a minimum of three (3) Directors to satisfy Bylaws Article III, Section 3.2.
2.  **Voting Method:** Voting shall be conducted via the Organization's secure electronic voting system.
3.  **Threshold:** Candidates receiving the highest number of votes are elected.
4.  **Immediate Effect:** Elected Directors assume office immediately upon the certification of the vote by the Convener.

#### 4.7 Post-Election Transition
1.  **Officer Appointment:** The new Board must immediately convene (following the Member meeting) to elect a President, Secretary, and Treasurer (Bylaws Art. IV).
2.  **Bank Access:** The new Secretary must generate a resolution authorizing the new Officers to access bank accounts and unfreeze assets.
3.  **State Filing:** The new Board must file a "Periodic Report" or update with the Texas Secretary of State to reflect the new governance structure.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Detects the emergency; secures the membership roll; facilitates the logistics of the emergency meeting; acts as Primary Convener if eligible. |
| **Voting Members** | Receive notice; attend the Special Emergency Meeting to establish quorum; nominate candidates; vote to elect the Interim Board. |
| **The Convener** | Issues the legal Notice of Meeting; chairs the meeting until the election is complete. |
| **Registered Agent** | Receives legal service of process on behalf of the Organization during the interregnum. |
| **Interim Directors** | Assume fiduciary duty immediately upon election; restore operational and financial controls. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to execute this plan during a Zero-Director Event may result in the Organization's involuntary dissolution by the Texas Secretary of State, loss of 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status, and the forfeiture of all organizational assets to a successor charity or the government.

Any Voting Member who attempts to obstruct the calling of this emergency meeting or the election process acts in violation of the Bylaws and may be subject to expulsion and legal liability for damages caused to the Organization.
