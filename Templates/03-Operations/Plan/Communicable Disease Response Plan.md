### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Communicable Disease Response Plan (the "Plan") is to safeguard the health and well-being of Study GRC Inc.’s employees, volunteers, members, and event participants. This Plan establishes a structured framework for preventing, detecting, and responding to communicable disease outbreaks (e.g., COVID-19, Influenza, Norovirus) within the Organization’s operations.

This Plan fulfills the Board of Directors' fiduciary duty of care under **Article III** of the Bylaws and aligns with the Organization’s commitment to fostering a safe, inclusive community ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This Plan applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All employees, officers, Board Directors, and volunteers.
*   **Operations:** All remote work environments and physical gatherings, including conferences, workshops, and board meetings.
*   **Participants:** Attendees of in-person events, with specific provisions for youth participants in K-12 programs.

**Exclusions:** This Plan does not constitute medical advice. Individuals are expected to consult healthcare professionals for personal medical decisions.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Communicable Disease** | An infectious disease transmissible (as from person to person) by direct contact with an affected individual or the individual's discharges or by indirect means (as by a vector). |
| **Close Contact** | Being within 6 feet of an infected person for a cumulative total of 15 minutes or more over a 24-hour period, or having direct physical contact. |
| **Isolation** | The separation of people who have a specific infectious illness from those who are healthy and the restriction of their movement to stop the spread of that illness. |
| **Quarantine** | The separation and restriction of the movement of people who were exposed to a contagious disease to see if they become sick. |
| **Vulnerable Population** | Individuals at higher risk for severe illness, including older adults and people of any age with certain underlying medical conditions. |
| **Event Commander** | The designated staff member or volunteer responsible for safety enforcement at a specific physical event. |

### 4. Plan Elements

#### 4.1 Governance and Activation
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V**, the Executive Director (CEO) serves as the primary Incident Commander for health emergencies. The Plan operates under three threat levels:

*   **Level 1 (Green):** Standard precautions. No active outbreak. Routine hygiene encouraged.
*   **Level 2 (Yellow):** Localized transmission in a region where an event is planned or where staff reside. Enhanced screening and masking may be recommended.
*   **Level 3 (Red):** Widespread transmission or declared Public Health Emergency. Cancellation of in-person events; mandatory remote operations for all staff.

#### 4.2 Prevention Protocols (Standard Operating Procedures)
To mitigate risk under the General Duty Clause of the OSH Act:

1.  **Remote-First Default:** As a remote-first organization, daily operations are naturally distanced. Staff are encouraged to work from home if they or a household member feel ill, without fear of reprisal.
2.  **Hygiene Resources:** For any in-person gathering, the Organization must provide adequate handwashing facilities and/or alcohol-based hand sanitizer (at least 60% alcohol).
3.  **Vaccination:** The Organization strongly encourages all eligible personnel and participants to stay up to date with vaccinations recommended by the CDC and Texas DSHS.

#### 4.3 In-Person Event Protocols
For conferences, workshops, and meetups:

1.  **Pre-Event Screening:** All attendees (staff, volunteers, and participants) must complete a self-attestation of health 24-48 hours prior to the event.
2.  **Symptom Check:** Individuals exhibiting symptoms (fever >100.4°F, cough, shortness of breath) will be denied entry.
3.  **Refund Policy:** To encourage responsible behavior, the Organization guarantees a 100% refund of registration fees for any participant who cancels due to illness or suspected exposure.
4.  **Youth Protection:** For K-12 programs, parents/guardians must sign an acknowledgment of risk. If a minor exhibits symptoms on-site, they will be isolated immediately, and the parent/guardian will be contacted for immediate pickup.

#### 4.4 Response to Symptomatic Individuals
If an individual becomes symptomatic during an Organization-sponsored activity:

1.  **Immediate Isolation:** The individual must be moved to a designated isolation area away from others.
2.  **Safe Transport:** The Event Commander will assist in arranging private transport to the individual's home or a medical facility. Public transportation should be avoided.
3.  **Area Disinfection:** Any area used for prolonged periods by the symptomatic person must be closed off and disinfected per CDC guidelines.

#### 4.5 Notification and Contact Tracing
In the event of a confirmed positive case following an event:

1.  **Reporting:** Staff and volunteers are required to report a positive diagnosis to the Executive Director if they were present at an in-person event within 48 hours of symptom onset.
2.  **Notification:** The Organization will notify all participants who may have been Close Contacts within 24 hours of learning of the exposure.
3.  **Privacy Protection:** In accordance with ADA and HIPAA standards, notifications **must not** disclose the identity of the infected individual.
    *   *Example Notification:* "We have been notified that an individual present at [Event Name] on [Date] has tested positive for [Disease]. You are receiving this notice because you may have been in close contact."

#### 4.6 Business Continuity
To ensure the Organization’s mission continues during a widespread outbreak:

1.  **Cross-Training:** Critical functions (Finance, IT Security) must have at least one backup person identified in the **Delegation of Authority Matrix**.
2.  **Board Succession:** If the Executive Director is incapacitated, the Board President shall assume temporary operational oversight until the ED recovers or an interim is appointed, per **Bylaws Article III**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Plan; authorizes cancellation of major events based on public health data; ensures financial reserves exist for refunds/cancellations. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Acts as Incident Commander; determines Threat Levels; communicates with legal counsel regarding liability. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Manages community communication; ensures notifications are empathetic and clear; coordinates refunds. |
| **Event Commander** | Enforces on-site protocols (screening, isolation); maintains the incident log. |
| **Staff & Volunteers** | Adhere to hygiene protocols; self-report symptoms/exposure immediately; stay home if sick. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Zero Tolerance for Harassment:** The Organization maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment based on health status, mask-wearing choices (where optional), or vaccination status.
*   **Right to Remove:** The Organization reserves the right to remove any individual from an event who refuses to comply with active health and safety protocols (e.g., refusing to leave after exhibiting symptoms).
*   **Liability:** Failure to comply with this Plan by staff may result in disciplinary action. The Organization shall indemnify officers and volunteers acting in good faith to enforce this Plan, pursuant to **Bylaws Article VII**.
