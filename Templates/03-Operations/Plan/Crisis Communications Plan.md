### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Crisis Communications Plan is to establish a structured framework for managing internal and external communications during significant emergency situations or reputational threats affecting Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This plan ensures that the Organization maintains control of the narrative, provides accurate and timely information to stakeholders, protects the safety and privacy of its community (particularly minors), and safeguards the Organization’s reputation and 501(c)(3) status.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers of Study GRC Inc. It covers all communication channels, including but not limited to the Organization’s website, social media platforms (LinkedIn, Discord, etc.), email newsletters, and press releases.

**Inclusions:**
*   Data breaches or cybersecurity incidents.
*   Youth protection incidents or allegations of misconduct.
*   Financial improprieties or investigations.
*   Sudden loss of executive leadership.
*   Natural disasters affecting remote operations or major events.

**Exclusions:**
*   Routine operational issues (e.g., minor website downtime) that do not pose a reputational or safety risk.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Crisis** | A significant event that threatens the Organization's operations, reputation, legal standing, or the safety of its stakeholders. |
| **Crisis Communications Team (CCT)** | The designated group of individuals responsible for decision-making and message crafting during a crisis. |
| **Holding Statement** | A pre-approved, temporary statement issued immediately after a crisis breaks to acknowledge the situation while facts are gathered. |
| **Spokesperson** | The single individual authorized to speak on record to the media or public regarding the crisis. |

### 4. Plan Procedures

#### 4.1 Crisis Activation
Any Director, Officer, or staff member who identifies a potential crisis must immediately notify the Executive Director (CEO) and the President of the Board.
*   **4.1.1** If the crisis involves the CEO, the President of the Board must be notified immediately.
*   **4.1.2** Upon notification, the CEO (or President) will determine if the event meets the definition of a "Crisis" and activate the Crisis Communications Team (CCT).

#### 4.2 Crisis Communications Team (CCT) Composition
The CCT shall consist of the following roles:
1.  **Executive Director (CEO):** Team Lead.
2.  **President of the Board:** Governance oversight.
3.  **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO):** Technical lead (required for data/IT incidents).
4.  **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG):** Community sentiment and member welfare.
5.  **Legal Counsel:** (Internal or External) for liability review.

#### 4.3 Immediate Action Protocol (The First 60 Minutes)
Upon activation of the CCT, the following steps must be taken immediately:
1.  **Secure Information:** Establish a secure, out-of-band communication channel (e.g., Signal or a dedicated locked Slack channel) for the CCT.
2.  **Halt Automated Communications:** The DKG or Marketing Lead must immediately pause all scheduled social media posts, newsletters, and marketing campaigns to prevent "tone-deaf" messaging.
3.  **Fact Gathering:** The CCT must assemble known facts. Speculation is strictly prohibited.
4.  **Determine Severity Level:**
    *   **Level 1 (Low):** Localized issue, low reputational risk (e.g., minor event cancellation).
    *   **Level 2 (Medium):** Operational disruption or moderate negative feedback (e.g., website outage, controversial forum post).
    *   **Level 3 (High):** Legal threat, data breach, youth safety incident, or executive scandal.

#### 4.4 Spokesperson Designation
To ensure consistency, Study GRC Inc. adheres to a "Single Voice" policy.
*   **Primary Spokesperson:** Executive Director (CEO).
*   **Alternate Spokesperson:** President of the Board.
*   **Subject Matter Experts:** The CISO or CFO may speak on technical/financial details *only* if explicitly authorized and prepped by the CCT.
*   **Prohibition:** No other volunteers, staff, or board members may speak to the media or post official statements regarding the crisis.

#### 4.5 Stakeholder Communication Hierarchy
Communications shall be released in the following order of priority, unless legal requirements (e.g., data breach notification laws) dictate otherwise:

1.  **Internal Leadership:** Board of Directors and Operational Officers.
2.  **Internal Staff/Volunteers:** To prevent rumors and ensure they know how to respond to inquiries.
3.  **Directly Affected Parties:** (e.g., Victims of a breach, parents of involved youth).
4.  **Voting Members:** Supporting and Honorary Members.
5.  **General Community/Public:** Via website transparency page and social media.

#### 4.6 Youth Protection Specifics
In the event of a crisis involving a minor (participant under 18):
*   **4.6.1** The identity of the minor **MUST NEVER** be released publicly or confirmed to the media.
*   **4.6.2** All communications must align with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.
*   **4.6.3** Law enforcement instructions regarding information release take precedence over this plan.

#### 4.7 Drafting and Approval Process
1.  **Drafting:** The CEO or DKG drafts the initial statement (Holding Statement) and subsequent updates.
2.  **Legal Review:** For Level 3 crises, Legal Counsel must review all statements to minimize liability and ensure compliance with Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification) and Article IX (Confidentiality).
3.  **Board Approval:** The Board of Directors must approve the strategy for Level 3 crises.
4.  **Release:** Statements are published via official channels only.

#### 4.8 Post-Crisis Review
Within 14 days of the crisis resolution, the CCT must convene to conduct a "Hot Wash" or After-Action Review (AAR).
*   **4.8.1** A report summarizing the incident, the communication effectiveness, and lessons learned must be presented to the Board.
*   **4.8.2** This plan shall be updated based on the AAR findings.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Provides final approval on high-stakes communications; ensures alignment with Bylaws and Mission. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Serves as the primary Spokesperson; leads the CCT; approves Level 1 and Level 2 communications. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Monitors community sentiment on Discord/Socials; pauses marketing automation; drafts community-facing messages. |
| **CISO** | Provides technical accuracy for security-related communications; liaises with forensic investigators. |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Strictly adhere to the "No Comment" policy; refer all media inquiries to the CEO; refrain from speculating on personal social media accounts. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this plan, particularly the unauthorized release of confidential information or speaking to the media without authorization, constitutes a violation of the *Board Code of Ethics* and *Confidentiality Policy*.

**Consequences:**
*   **Volunteers/Staff:** Disciplinary action up to and including termination of role/employment.
*   **Directors:** Removal for cause pursuant to Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6.
*   **Legal Action:** The Organization reserves the right to pursue legal remedies for damages resulting from unauthorized disclosures.
