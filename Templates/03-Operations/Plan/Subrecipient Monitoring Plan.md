### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Subrecipient Monitoring Plan is to establish a risk-based framework for managing and overseeing subawards issued by Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). As a Pass-Through Entity (PTE) of federal funds, the Organization is mandated by **2 CFR § 200.332** to monitor the activities of subrecipients to ensure that the subaward is used for authorized purposes, in compliance with federal statutes, regulations, and the terms and conditions of the subaward, and that subaward performance goals are achieved.

This plan aligns with **Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** of the Bylaws, ensuring fiscal integrity and the protection of the Organization’s tax-exempt status.

### 2. Scope
This plan applies to all subawards issued by Study GRC Inc. to third-party entities ("Subrecipients") using federal or state grant funds.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Contractors/Vendors:** This plan does not apply to procurement contracts for goods and services (e.g., buying laptops, hiring a cleaning service), which are governed by the *Procurement Policy (FIN-POL-003)*.
*   **Beneficiaries:** This plan does not apply to individual beneficiaries of the Organization’s charitable programs (e.g., students receiving scholarships).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pass-Through Entity (PTE)** | A non-federal entity (Study GRC Inc.) that provides a subaward to a subrecipient to carry out part of a federal program. |
| **Subrecipient** | A non-federal entity that receives a subaward from a pass-through entity to carry out part of a federal program. Distinct from a contractor. |
| **Subaward** | An award provided by a PTE to a subrecipient for the subrecipient to carry out part of a federal award received by the PTE. |
| **Single Audit** | An audit required by 2 CFR 200 Subpart F for entities expending $750,000 or more in federal awards during a fiscal year. |
| **Management Decision** | The evaluation by the PTE of the audit findings and corrective action plan and the issuance of a written decision to the auditee. |

### 4. Plan Components

#### 4.1 Pre-Award Risk Assessment
Pursuant to **2 CFR § 200.332(b)**, the Organization must evaluate each subrecipient's risk of non-compliance with federal statutes, regulations, and the terms of the subaward *prior* to issuing funds.

1.  **Assessment Tool:** The CFO and Grants Manager will utilize the *Subrecipient Risk Assessment Tool (FIN-ASM-002)* to evaluate:
    *   Prior experience with the same or similar subawards.
    *   Results of previous audits (including Single Audits).
    *   Whether the subrecipient has new personnel or new/substantially changed systems.
    *   Extent and results of federal awarding agency monitoring.
2.  **Risk Classification:** Based on the assessment, the subrecipient will be classified as **Low**, **Medium**, or **High** risk. This classification determines the intensity of monitoring described in Section 4.2.

#### 4.2 Monitoring Procedures
Monitoring activities will be tailored based on the Risk Classification assigned during the pre-award phase.

**Level 1: Baseline Monitoring (All Subrecipients)**
*   **Financial Review:** Review of all invoices and reimbursement requests to ensure costs are allowable, allocable, and reasonable (2 CFR 200 Subpart E).
*   **Performance Review:** Quarterly review of technical/programmatic reports to ensure progress toward goals.
*   **Single Audit Review:** Verification that the subrecipient has completed a Single Audit if they met the $750,000 threshold (2 CFR 200.332(f)).

**Level 2: Targeted Monitoring (Medium Risk)**
*   *Includes all Level 1 activities, plus:*
*   **Desk Reviews:** Detailed review of supporting documentation (receipts, timesheets) for a sample of invoices at least twice per grant period.
*   **Frequent Communication:** Monthly check-in calls with the subrecipient’s project director.
*   **Technical Assistance:** Providing training or templates to assist with compliance.

**Level 3: Advanced Monitoring (High Risk)**
*   *Includes all Level 1 and Level 2 activities, plus:*
*   **Site Visits:** On-site (or virtual equivalent) reviews of financial and programmatic records and operations.
*   **100% Transaction Testing:** Requirement to submit full supporting documentation for *every* reimbursement request.
*   **Agreed-Upon Procedures:** Requirement for specific external audit procedures if deemed necessary.

#### 4.3 Financial and Performance Reporting
1.  **Invoicing:** Subrecipients must submit invoices [Monthly/Quarterly] using the Organization’s standard *Subrecipient Invoice Template*.
2.  **Certification:** All invoices must include the certification required by 2 CFR 200.415 ("By signing this report, I certify to the best of my knowledge...").
3.  **Program Reports:** The VP of Education & Training (or designated Program Officer) must review performance reports to verify that the work invoiced was actually performed.

#### 4.4 Audit Review and Management Decisions
Pursuant to **2 CFR § 200.332(d)(3)** and **2 CFR § 200.521**:
1.  **Collection:** The Organization will verify within six (6) months of the subrecipient's fiscal year-end whether a Single Audit was required and performed.
2.  **Review:** The CFO will review audit reports from the Federal Audit Clearinghouse (FAC).
3.  **Management Decision:** If the subrecipient's audit contains findings related to the subaward provided by Study GRC Inc., the Organization must issue a **Management Decision** within six (6) months of acceptance of the audit report by the FAC. This decision must state whether the audit finding is sustained, the reasons for the decision, and the expected auditee action.

#### 4.5 Enforcement and Remedies for Non-Compliance
If a subrecipient fails to comply with federal statutes, regulations, or the terms of the subaward, the Organization may take action as outlined in **2 CFR § 200.338**, including:
1.  Temporarily withholding cash payments pending correction of the deficiency.
2.  Disallowing all or part of the cost of the activity or action not in compliance.
3.  Suspending or terminating the subaward.
4.  Recommending suspension or debarment proceedings to the federal awarding agency.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Provides high-level oversight of financial integrity per Bylaws Art. III; approves the Subrecipient Monitoring Plan. |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews summary reports of subrecipient monitoring activities and high-risk issues during Board meetings. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns this plan; conducts financial risk assessments; reviews Single Audits; issues Management Decisions; authorizes payments. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Conducts programmatic monitoring; verifies that subrecipient activities align with the grant's educational goals; approves performance reports. |
| **Grants Manager** | Maintains the *Subrecipient Monitoring Log*; collects risk assessment data; tracks audit deadlines; acts as primary liaison for compliance questions. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this plan puts the Organization at risk of disallowed costs, loss of federal funding, and reputational damage.
*   **Internal:** Employees who fail to follow these monitoring procedures may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **External:** Subrecipients who fail to comply with monitoring requests or corrective actions may have their funding suspended or terminated and may be legally liable for the return of funds.
