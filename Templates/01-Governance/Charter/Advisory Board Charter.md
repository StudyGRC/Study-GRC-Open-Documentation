### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Charter is to define the structure, authority, responsibilities, and operating procedures of the Advisory Board of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This document establishes the Advisory Board as a non-governing body dedicated to providing strategic counsel, technical expertise, and community advocacy, while strictly delineating its role from that of the legal Board of Directors (the "Governing Board") and Operational Leadership in accordance with **Article VI** of the Organization's Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This Charter applies to:
*   All appointed members of the Advisory Board.
*   The Governing Board of Directors.
*   Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director, CISO) regarding communication protocols.

**Exclusions:** This Charter does not apply to ad-hoc project committees or task forces created by Operational Leadership that do not hold the designation of "Advisory Board."

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Advisory Board** | A body of appointed volunteers who provide non-binding strategic advice to the Organization. They do not have voting rights or fiduciary duties equivalent to Directors. |
| **Governing Board** | The legally elected Board of Directors with fiduciary responsibility and voting authority for the Organization. |
| **Operational Officer** | Individuals managing day-to-day execution (e.g., CEO, VP of Education), as defined in Bylaws Article V. |
| **Facilitated Communication** | Communication between Operational Officers and Advisory Board members that is arranged, approved, or attended by a member of the Governing Board. |
| **Non-Binding Advice** | Recommendations made by the Advisory Board that the Organization is under no legal obligation to adopt or implement. |

### 4. Charter Statements

#### 4.1 Authority and Limitations
4.1.1 **Non-Voting Status:** In accordance with **Bylaws Section 6.2**, Advisory Board members have **no voting rights** regarding the governance, finance, or operations of the Organization.
4.1.2 **No Fiduciary Authority:** Advisory Board members do not bear the statutory fiduciary duties of the Governing Board and cannot legally bind the Organization to contracts, debts, or obligations.
4.1.3 **Advisory Nature:** The role of the Advisory Board is strictly consultative. All recommendations are subject to the review and approval of the Governing Board or Operational Leadership, as appropriate.

#### 4.2 Composition and Appointment
4.2.1 **Appointment:** Members of the Advisory Board are appointed by a majority vote of the Governing Board.
4.2.2 **Eligibility:**
*   Candidates must possess specific expertise in GRC, non-profit management, education, or technology.
*   **Bylaws Section 6.2 Restriction:** Individuals currently serving as voting Directors on the Governing Board are **not eligible** for appointment to the Advisory Board.
4.2.3 **Term of Service:** Advisory Board members serve a renewable term of one (1) year. There is no limit to the number of consecutive terms a member may serve.
4.2.4 **Compensation:** Advisory Board members serve as volunteers and shall not receive compensation for their service, though they may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses per the *Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy*.

#### 4.3 Duties and Responsibilities
The Advisory Board shall support the Organization by:
*   **Strategic Guidance:** Reviewing and providing feedback on the Organization's long-term strategic plan and GRC curriculum roadmap.
*   **Resource Development:** Assisting in fundraising efforts, identifying grant opportunities, and facilitating introductions to potential donors or partners.
*   **Advocacy:** Serving as ambassadors for the Organization within the GRC and cybersecurity communities.
*   **Technical Expertise:** Advising on emerging trends in Governance, Risk, and Compliance to ensure educational resources remain relevant.

#### 4.4 Communication Protocols (The "Firewall")
To maintain clear lines of authority and prevent operational confusion, the following protocols mandated by **Bylaws Section 6.2** must be strictly observed:

4.4.1 **Facilitated Communication:** All formal communication (meetings, strategic emails, official requests) between the Advisory Board and Operational Officers must be facilitated by a member of the Governing Board.
4.4.2 **Prohibition on Direct Tasking:** Operational Officers are prohibited from formally soliciting advice or assigning tasks to the Advisory Board without prior approval and facilitation by the Governing Board.
4.4.3 **Mandatory Reporting:** If an Operational Officer engages in substantive communication with an Advisory Board member (e.g., during a casual encounter or emergency), the Officer must provide a briefing regarding the nature and content of such communication to the Governing Board via electronic communication or at the next scheduled Board meeting.

#### 4.5 Meetings
4.5.1 **Frequency:** The Advisory Board shall meet at least quarterly, or more frequently as requested by the Governing Board.
4.5.2 **Chairperson:** The Governing Board shall appoint a Liaison or Chair to preside over Advisory Board meetings.
4.5.3 **Attendance:** Meetings may be held virtually. While quorum is not legally required for a non-voting body, active participation is required for re-appointment.

#### 4.6 Ethics and Confidentiality
4.6.1 **Conflict of Interest:** Advisory Board members must adhere to the Organization's *Conflict of Interest Policy* and disclose any financial or professional interests that may conflict with their advisory role.
4.6.2 **Non-Disclosure:** All Advisory Board members must execute a *Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)* prior to their appointment. They must maintain the confidentiality of all proprietary information, trade secrets, and strategic plans disclosed during their service.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Governing Board** | Appoints Advisory members; sets the agenda for Advisory meetings; facilitates communication with Operations; reviews Advisory recommendations. |
| **Advisory Board Member** | Attends meetings; provides expert counsel; promotes the mission; adheres to confidentiality and communication protocols. |
| **Board Liaison** | A designated Director who acts as the primary point of contact between the Advisory Board and the Governing Board/Operations. |
| **Operational Officers** | May present to the Advisory Board *only* when invited/facilitated by the Governing Board; must report any un-facilitated contact. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Removal:** An Advisory Board member may be removed at any time, with or without cause, by a majority vote of the Governing Board.
*   **Protocol Violation:** Operational Officers found violating the communication protocols defined in Section 4.4 may be subject to disciplinary action under the *Progressive Discipline Policy*.
*   **Automatic Termination:** Failure to sign the required NDA or Conflict of Interest Disclosure will result in immediate termination of the Advisory Board appointment.
