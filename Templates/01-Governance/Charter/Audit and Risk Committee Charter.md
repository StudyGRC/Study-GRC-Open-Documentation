### 1. Purpose
The Audit and Risk Committee (the "Committee") is a standing committee established by the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") pursuant to **Article VI, Section 6.1** of the Bylaws. The primary purpose of the Committee is to assist the Board in fulfilling its oversight responsibilities regarding:
1.  The integrity of the Organization’s financial statements and financial reporting processes.
2.  The independence, qualifications, and performance of the Organization’s external auditors.
3.  The Organization’s compliance with legal and regulatory requirements, including **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)** for federal awards.
4.  The effectiveness of the Organization’s internal controls and enterprise risk management (ERM) framework, with specific focus on cybersecurity, youth protection, and remote operational risks.

### 2. Scope
This Charter applies to the Committee members, the Board of Directors, and the Operational Officers (specifically the CEO, CFO, and CISO) regarding their reporting obligations to the Committee.

**Exclusions:** The Committee does not perform the day-to-day financial management, which remains the responsibility of the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and management team. The Committee provides oversight and strategic guidance, not operational execution.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Independent Director** | A voting member of the Board who is not an employee of the Organization and has no material financial relationship with the Organization other than their service on the Board. |
| **Material Weakness** | A deficiency, or a combination of deficiencies, in internal control over financial reporting, such that there is a reasonable possibility that a material misstatement of the entity's financial statements will not be prevented or detected on a timely basis. |
| **Single Audit** | An audit required by **2 CFR 200 Subpart F** for non-federal entities that expend $750,000 or more in federal awards during a fiscal year. |
| **Enterprise Risk Management (ERM)** | The culture, capabilities, and practices that organizations integrate with strategy-setting and apply when they carry out that strategy, with a purpose of managing risk in creating, preserving, and realizing value. |

### 4. Charter Statements

#### 4.1 Composition and Qualifications
4.1.1 **Membership:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Section 6.1**, the Committee must consist of at least two (2) members.
4.1.2 **Chair:** The Committee must be chaired by an active, voting Director of the Board.
4.1.3 **Independence:** All members of the Committee must be Independent Directors. No member may accept any consulting, advisory, or other compensatory fee from the Organization other than for Board service.
4.1.4 **Financial Literacy:** At least one member of the Committee must have "financial expertise," defined as employment experience in finance or accounting, requisite professional certification in accounting, or other comparable experience.

#### 4.2 Meetings and Quorum
4.2.1 **Frequency:** The Committee shall meet at least quarterly, or more frequently as circumstances dictate.
4.2.2 **Quorum:** A majority of the Committee members constitutes a quorum for the transaction of business.
4.2.3 **Executive Sessions:** The Committee may meet privately in executive session with the external auditor, the CFO, the CISO, or legal counsel to discuss any matters that the Committee or these groups believe should be discussed privately.

#### 4.3 Authority
In accordance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.218**, the Board delegates the following authority to the Committee:
4.3.1 **Investigation:** The authority to investigate any matter brought to its attention with full access to all books, records, facilities, and personnel of the Organization.
4.3.2 **Advisor Retention:** The authority to retain independent counsel, accountants, or other advisors to advise the Committee or assist in the conduct of an investigation, subject to Board-approved budget limits.
4.3.3 **Auditor Oversight:** The sole authority to appoint, compensate, and oversee the work of any registered public accounting firm employed by the Organization.

#### 4.4 Responsibilities: Financial Reporting and Disclosures
4.4.1 **Annual Financial Review:** Review and discuss with management and the external auditor the annual audited financial statements prior to filing or distribution.
4.4.2 **IRS Form 990:** Review the IRS Form 990 prior to its filing, ensuring the narrative accurately reflects the Organization's governance and activities, in compliance with **Bylaws Section 8.2**.
4.4.3 **Accounting Policies:** Review significant accounting and reporting issues, including complex or unusual transactions and highly judgmental areas (e.g., valuation of in-kind donations or crypto-assets).

#### 4.5 Responsibilities: External Audit and Compliance
4.5.1 **Auditor Selection:** Recommend to the Board the selection, retention, or termination of the external auditor.
4.5.2 **Single Audit Compliance:** Ensure compliance with **2 CFR 200 Subpart F** regarding the Single Audit, if the federal expenditure threshold is met.
4.5.3 **Auditor Independence:** Evaluate the external auditor’s independence and review all non-audit services performed by the auditors to ensure they do not impair independence.

#### 4.6 Responsibilities: Risk Management
4.6.1 **Risk Assessment:** Periodically review the Organization's major financial, operational, and reputational risk exposures and the steps management has taken to monitor and control such exposures.
4.6.2 **Cybersecurity Oversight:** Receive quarterly briefings from the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) regarding the Organization's security posture, data privacy compliance (GDPR/TDPSA), and incident response readiness.
4.6.3 **Youth Protection:** Collaborate with the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) to review risks associated with youth programs, ensuring compliance with the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**.
4.6.4 **Insurance:** Annually review the Organization’s insurance coverage (D&O, Cyber, General Liability) to ensure adequacy.

#### 4.7 Responsibilities: Ethics and Whistleblower
4.7.1 **Whistleblower Policy:** Oversee the administration of the **Whistleblower Protection Policy** (GOV-POL-009).
4.7.2 **Complaint Handling:** Establish procedures for the receipt, retention, and treatment of complaints received by the Organization regarding accounting, internal accounting controls, or auditing matters.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Committee Chair** | Sets the agenda for meetings; presides over meetings; reports Committee actions and recommendations to the full Board of Directors. |
| **Committee Members** | Review financial reports and risk assessments; participate in voting on Committee recommendations; maintain independence. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Prepares financial statements; serves as the primary liaison to the external auditor; provides financial data to the Committee (Non-voting). |
| **Chief Info. Security Officer (CISO)** | Reports on cybersecurity risks, data breaches, and IT control effectiveness (Non-voting). |
| **External Auditor** | Performs the annual audit; reports findings and internal control deficiencies directly to the Committee. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
The Committee shall conduct an annual self-evaluation of its performance and compliance with this Charter. Failure of a Committee member to adhere to the confidentiality or independence requirements of this Charter may result in removal from the Committee by the Board of Directors, pursuant to **Bylaws Section 3.6**.
