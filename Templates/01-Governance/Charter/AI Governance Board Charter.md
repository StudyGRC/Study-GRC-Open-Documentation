### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the AI Governance Board (AIGB) is to establish a centralized oversight body responsible for the strategic direction, risk management, and ethical deployment of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies within Study GRC Inc.

As a non-profit dedicated to GRC knowledge and open-source resources, Study GRC Inc. must model best practices in the "Govern" function of the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF). This Charter empowers the AIGB to ensure that all AI systems—particularly those interacting with our K-12 community or generating educational content—are safe, secure, unbiased, and compliant with applicable laws (including COPPA and TDPSA).

### 2. Scope
This Charter applies to the entire Organization, including:
*   **Personnel:** All employees, Board Directors, Operational Officers, volunteers, and contractors.
*   **Technologies:** All AI systems developed, procured, or utilized by the Organization, including but not limited to Generative AI (LLMs), predictive analytics, and automated decision-making systems.
*   **Data:** All organizational data, specifically including member data and youth participant data, processed by AI systems.

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal use of AI tools on non-organizational devices that do not process Study GRC Inc. data or intellectual property.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **AI System** | An engineered or machine-based system that can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, generate outputs such as predictions, recommendations, or decisions influencing real or virtual environments. |
| **Generative AI** | A class of AI models that can create new content, including text, images, audio, and code, in response to user prompts (e.g., ChatGPT, Midjourney, GitHub Copilot). |
| **High-Risk AI** | AI systems used in safety-critical applications, or those affecting the rights, opportunities, or safety of individuals—specifically including any interaction with minors (Youth Protection). |
| **NIST AI RMF** | The National Institute of Standards and Technology Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (Map, Measure, Manage, Govern). |
| **Algorithmic Bias** | Systematic and repeatable errors in a computer system that create unfair outcomes, such as privileging one arbitrary group of users over others. |

### 4. Charter Statements

#### 4.1 Authority and Establishment
Pursuant to **Article VI, Section 6.1** of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc., the Board of Directors hereby establishes the AI Governance Board (AIGB). The AIGB shall operate as a specialized oversight committee. While it may include Board Directors, it is primarily an operational body delegated the authority to enforce AI safety standards.

#### 4.2 Composition
The AIGB shall consist of a minimum of three (3) and a maximum of seven (7) voting members, appointed by the Executive Director (CEO) and ratified by the Board of Directors. Mandatory representation includes:

1.  **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO):** Serves as the AIGB Chair.
2.  **VP of Education & Training:** Represents curriculum integrity and academic honesty.
3.  **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG):** Represents community ethics, inclusion, and youth protection.
4.  **Legal/Compliance Representative:** Oversees regulatory adherence (COPPA, TDPSA, IP).

#### 4.3 Mandate and Responsibilities (The "Govern" Function)
The AIGB is charged with the following duties:

*   **4.3.1 Strategy and Policy Approval:** Review and approve all AI-related policies (e.g., *Artificial Intelligence Acceptable Use Policy*) before submission to the Board of Directors.
*   **4.3.2 Risk Management:** Maintain the *AI Use Case Inventory* and oversee the execution of *Generative AI Risk Assessments*. The AIGB has the authority to halt any AI project deemed to pose unacceptable risk to the Organization or its community.
*   **4.3.3 Procurement Vetting:** Serve as the final approval gate for the procurement of third-party AI tools. The AIGB must verify that vendors meet data privacy standards, particularly regarding the non-retention of Study GRC data for model training.
*   **4.3.4 Youth Protection:** Rigorously evaluate any AI tool intended for use by or with minors to ensure strict compliance with COPPA and the Organization’s *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.
*   **4.3.5 Intellectual Property Stewardship:** Ensure that the use of Generative AI for content creation does not violate the Organization's commitment to open-source licensing or infringe upon third-party copyrights.

#### 4.4 Meeting Frequency and Quorum
*   **Frequency:** The AIGB shall meet at least quarterly. Ad-hoc meetings may be called by the Chair to review urgent procurement requests or incident reports.
*   **Quorum:** A simple majority of seated AIGB members constitutes a quorum.
*   **Voting:** Decisions are made by a majority vote. In the event of a tie, the Chair (CISO) holds the deciding vote.

#### 4.5 Reporting
The AIGB Chair must submit a summary report to the Board of Directors bi-annually, detailing:
*   Approved AI use cases.
*   Denied high-risk applications.
*   Incidents involving AI (e.g., data leakage, hallucinations affecting curriculum).
*   Emerging regulatory changes affecting the Organization.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **AIGB Chair (CISO)** | Schedules meetings, sets agendas, maintains the *AI Use Case Inventory*, and serves as the primary liaison to the Board of Directors. |
| **AIGB Members** | Review risk assessments, vote on AI adoption proposals, and monitor compliance within their respective functional areas. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures the AIGB has sufficient resources and authority to enforce its decisions; resolves disputes between the AIGB and operational teams. |
| **Board of Directors** | Retains ultimate fiduciary responsibility; reviews AIGB reports and ratifies high-level AI governance policies. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
The AIGB has the authority to audit any department's use of AI. Unauthorized deployment of AI systems ("Shadow AI") or bypass of AIGB review processes constitutes a violation of this Charter.

Failure to comply with the directives of the AIGB may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and revocation of access to organizational systems.
