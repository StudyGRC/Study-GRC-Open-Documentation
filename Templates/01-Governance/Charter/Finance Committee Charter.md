### 1. Purpose
The Finance Committee (the "Committee") is a standing committee established by the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") pursuant to **Article VI** of the Bylaws. The primary purpose of the Committee is to assist the Board in fulfilling its fiduciary responsibilities regarding the oversight of the Organization’s financial health, assets, and strategies.

This Charter defines the Committee's authority to oversee financial planning, monitor the integrity of financial statements, ensure compliance with federal grant requirements under **2 CFR Part 200**, and maintain a robust system of internal financial controls.

### 2. Scope
This Charter applies to the financial oversight of all funds, assets, and liabilities held by Study GRC Inc.

*   **In Scope:**
    *   Annual budgeting and long-term financial planning.
    *   Oversight of financial reporting and transparency.
    *   Monitoring of federal and state grant compliance (financial aspects).
    *   Investment and reserve management.
    *   Oversight of the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or equivalent operational staff regarding financial duties.
*   **Out of Scope:**
    *   Day-to-day bookkeeping and transactional execution (responsibility of Operational Leadership).
    *   Independent Audit oversight (responsibility of the Audit & Risk Committee, unless otherwise directed by the Board).

### 4. Charter Statements

#### 4.1 Composition and Authority
4.1.1 **Establishment:** The Committee is established under **Article VI, Section 6.1** of the Bylaws.
4.1.2 **Chairperson:** The Treasurer of the Board shall serve as the Chair of the Finance Committee.
4.1.3 **Membership:** The Committee shall consist of at least three (3) members:
*   The Treasurer (Chair).
*   At least one additional Voting Director.
*   External members with financial expertise may be appointed by the Board, provided the Committee is chaired by a Director.
4.1.4 **Staff Liaison:** The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or Executive Director shall serve as a non-voting staff liaison to provide information and execute Committee directives.

#### 4.2 Meetings and Quorum
4.2.1 **Frequency:** The Committee must meet at least quarterly, or more frequently as required to review monthly financial statements prior to Board meetings.
4.2.2 **Quorum:** A majority of the Committee members constitutes a quorum.
4.2.3 **Minutes:** Minutes of all meetings must be recorded and submitted to the Board of Directors for review at the next scheduled Board meeting.

#### 4.3 Financial Planning and Budgeting
4.3.1 **Annual Budget:** The Committee must review the annual operating budget proposed by Operational Leadership. The Committee shall challenge assumptions, ensure alignment with the Strategic Plan, and recommend the final budget to the Board for approval prior to the start of the fiscal year.
4.3.2 **Long-Range Planning:** The Committee shall work with the Board to develop multi-year financial forecasts to ensure the long-term sustainability of the Organization.

#### 4.4 Financial Monitoring and Reporting
4.4.1 **Periodic Review:** The Committee must review monthly or quarterly financial statements, including the Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet), Statement of Activities (Income Statement), and Cash Flow Statement.
4.4.2 **Variance Analysis:** The Committee shall monitor actual financial performance against the approved budget and require explanations from Operational Leadership for significant variances (e.g., +/- 10%).
4.4.3 **Transparency:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII**, the Committee ensures the Annual Financial Report is prepared and made available to members and the public.

#### 4.5 Federal Grant Compliance (2 CFR 200)
4.5.1 **Cost Principles:** The Committee must ensure that the Organization maintains policies and procedures to determine the allowability of costs in accordance with **2 CFR 200 Subpart E**.
4.5.2 **Financial Management Systems:** The Committee shall verify that the Organization’s financial management systems can identify the source and application of funds for federally-funded activities (2 CFR 200.302).
4.5.3 **Indirect Costs:** The Committee oversees the negotiation and application of the Organization’s Indirect Cost Rate (NICRA) or the election of the de minimis rate.

#### 4.6 Internal Controls and Risk Management
4.6.1 **Policy Oversight:** The Committee shall review and recommend updates to the *Financial Policies and Procedures Manual* and *Internal Controls Framework* annually.
4.6.2 **Segregation of Duties:** The Committee must ensure adequate segregation of duties exists between the authorization, custody, and recording of financial transactions to prevent fraud.
4.6.3 **Insurance:** The Committee shall annually review the Organization’s insurance coverage (D&O, General Liability, Cyber, etc.) to ensure assets are adequately protected.

#### 4.7 Tax and Regulatory Compliance
4.7.1 **IRS Form 990:** The Committee shall review the IRS Form 990 and all related schedules prior to filing, ensuring the return accurately reflects the Organization’s financial position and governance practices.
4.7.2 **UBIT Monitoring:** The Committee shall monitor revenue streams for potential Unrelated Business Income Tax (UBIT) exposure.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Appoints Committee members; approves the Annual Budget; approves the Annual Financial Report; retains final legal authority. |
| **Treasurer (Chair)** | Sets meeting agendas; presides over meetings; reports Committee findings and recommendations to the full Board. |
| **Committee Members** | Review financial materials prior to meetings; ask probing questions regarding financial health; vote on recommendations to the Board. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Prepares financial statements, budgets, and variance reports; serves as the primary information source for the Committee; implements Committee directives. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures operational alignment with the approved budget; authorizes expenditures within delegated limits. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
6.1 **Fiduciary Liability:** Committee members must act in good faith and with the care an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would exercise under similar circumstances. Failure to do so may result in personal liability or removal from the Committee.
6.2 **Conflict of Interest:** All Committee members must strictly adhere to the *Conflict of Interest Policy*. Members must recuse themselves from discussion and voting on any matter in which they have a financial interest.
6.3 **Removal:** The Board of Directors may remove any member of the Committee at any time, with or without cause, by a majority vote of the Board.
