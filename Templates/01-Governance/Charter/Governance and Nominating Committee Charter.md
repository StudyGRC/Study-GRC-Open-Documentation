### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Governance and Nominating Committee (the "Committee") is to assist the Board of Directors (the "Board") of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") in fulfilling its oversight responsibilities regarding the Organization's governance structure and Board composition. The Committee is responsible for identifying and vetting qualified individuals to serve as Directors, managing the election process in accordance with Article II of the Bylaws, overseeing the evaluation of the Board and management, and monitoring compliance with corporate governance policies and the Texas Business Organizations Code ("TBOC").

### 2. Scope
This Charter applies to the operations, authority, and responsibilities of the Governance and Nominating Committee.
*   **In Scope:** Board recruitment, Board orientation, Board self-assessment, Bylaws review, policy governance, and ethical oversight (Conflict of Interest).
*   **Out of Scope:** Financial audits (Audit Committee), executive compensation (unless specifically delegated by the Board), and day-to-day operational hiring.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Independent Director** | A Director who is not an officer or employee of the Organization and has no material relationship that would interfere with the exercise of independent judgment. |
| **Voting Member** | Supporting Members and Honorary Members in good standing who hold voting rights as defined in Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1. |
| **Skill Matrix** | A tool used to map the existing skills, experience, and demographics of the Board against the strategic needs of the Organization. |

### 4. Charter Statements

#### 4.1. Composition and Appointment
4.1.1 **Membership:** Pursuant to Bylaws Article VI, Section 6.1, the Committee must consist of at least two (2) members.
4.1.2 **Chair:** The Committee must be chaired by an active Board Director.
4.1.3 **Independence:** A majority of Committee members must be Independent Directors.
4.1.4 **Appointment:** Members of the Committee are appointed by the Board and serve at the pleasure of the Board.

#### 4.2. Meetings and Quorum
4.2.1 **Frequency:** The Committee shall meet at least quarterly, with increased frequency during the election cycle (October – January).
4.2.2 **Venue:** Meetings may be held virtually in accordance with the Organization's remote-first operating model.
4.2.3 **Quorum:** A majority of the Committee members constitutes a quorum.
4.2.4 **Minutes:** The Committee must maintain minutes of its meetings and report significant actions to the full Board.

#### 4.3. Responsibilities: Board Composition and Nominations
4.3.1 **Gap Analysis:** The Committee must annually review the composition of the Board using a Skill Matrix to identify gaps in expertise (e.g., legal, financial, fundraising, youth protection, cybersecurity) prior to the nomination period.
4.3.2 **Nomination Process:** The Committee shall manage the nomination timeline in strict compliance with Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5, ensuring:
*   The nomination period opens at least 14 days before the Annual Meeting.
*   All nominees submit a "Nomination Statement" disclosing background and potential conflicts.
4.3.3 **Vetting:** Given the Organization's focus on K-12 education and youth, the Committee must ensure all Board nominees undergo and pass a background check consistent with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* prior to being placed on the ballot.
4.3.4 **Candidate Transparency:** The Committee must verify that all nominees are Voting Members in good standing (Bylaws Section 3.3) and facilitate the distribution of candidate information to the membership.

#### 4.4. Responsibilities: Governance and Compliance
4.4.1 **Bylaws Review:** The Committee shall review the Bylaws at least annually and recommend amendments to the Board as necessary to reflect changing laws, best practices, or organizational needs.
4.4.2 **Policy Oversight:** The Committee is responsible for the maintenance of the *Master Policy Index* and ensuring that all Governance (Level 1) policies are reviewed on a bi-annual basis.
4.4.3 **Board Orientation:** The Committee must develop and oversee an orientation program for new Directors, covering fiduciary duties, the Organization’s strategic plan, and the *Code of Ethics*.
4.4.4 **Board Assessment:** The Committee shall facilitate an annual self-assessment of the Board’s performance and effectiveness.

#### 4.5. Responsibilities: Ethics and Conflicts of Interest
4.5.1 **Annual Disclosures:** The Committee shall administer the annual Conflict of Interest disclosure process for all Directors and Officers as required by Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1.
4.5.2 **Conflict Review:** The Committee shall review potential conflicts of interest involving Directors and make recommendations to the Board regarding the management or recusal requirements for such conflicts.
4.5.3 **Code of Conduct:** The Committee shall review and update the *Board of Directors Code of Ethics and Conduct* periodically.

#### 4.6. Authority
4.6.1 **Access:** The Committee has full access to all books, records, facilities, and personnel of the Organization.
4.6.2 **Advisors:** The Committee may retain, at the Organization's expense, independent legal, search firm, or other advisors as it deems necessary, subject to the Board’s budget approval.
4.6.3 **Delegation:** The Committee may form and delegate authority to subcommittees when appropriate.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Appoints Committee members; approves the Charter; reviews and votes on Committee recommendations regarding Bylaws and Policies. |
| **Committee Chair** | Sets meeting agendas; presides over meetings; reports Committee activities to the Board; ensures the election timeline is met. |
| **Committee Members** | Attend meetings; review nominee qualifications; conduct interviews; participate in Bylaws reviews and strategic governance planning. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains minutes of Committee meetings; ensures nomination notices are sent to Members in compliance with Bylaws Article II. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides staff support to the Committee; assists with the identification of skill gaps based on operational needs. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this Charter, particularly regarding the confidentiality of candidate vetting information or the integrity of the election process, may result in removal from the Committee and potential removal from the Board for cause, as outlined in Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6.
