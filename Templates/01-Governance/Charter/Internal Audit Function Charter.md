### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Internal Audit Function (IAF) is to provide independent, objective assurance and consulting services designed to add value and improve Study GRC Inc.'s operations. The IAF helps the Organization accomplish its objectives by bringing a systematic, disciplined approach to evaluate and improve the effectiveness of risk management, control, and governance processes.

This Charter establishes the Internal Audit Function's position within the Organization, including the nature of the Chief Audit Executive’s (CAE) functional reporting relationship with the Board of Directors; authorizes access to records, personnel, and physical properties relevant to the performance of engagements; and defines the scope of internal audit activities.

### 2. Scope
The scope of internal audit activities encompasses, but is not limited to, objective examinations of evidence for the purpose of providing independent assessments to the Audit & Risk Committee and Management on the adequacy and effectiveness of governance, risk management, and control processes for Study GRC Inc.

**Applies to:**
*   All departments, programs, and functions of Study GRC Inc.
*   All employees, volunteers, contractors, and Board members.
*   All financial, operational, and technology systems.
*   Compliance with external regulations, including Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and Federal Uniform Guidance (2 CFR 200).

**Exclusions:**
*   The IAF does not assume operational responsibility for the activities it audits.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Independence** | The freedom from conditions that threaten the ability of the internal audit activity to carry out internal audit responsibilities in an unbiased manner. |
| **Objectivity** | An unbiased mental attitude that allows internal auditors to perform engagements in such a manner that they believe in their work product and that no quality compromises are made. |
| **Assurance Services** | An objective examination of evidence for the purpose of providing an independent assessment on governance, risk management, and control processes for the organization. |
| **Consulting Services** | Advisory and related client service activities, the nature and scope of which are agreed with the client, intended to add value and improve an organization’s governance, risk management, and control processes without the internal auditor assuming management responsibility. |
| **CAE** | Chief Audit Executive; the person responsible for effectively managing the internal audit activity in accordance with the internal audit charter and the Definition of Internal Auditing. |

### 4. Charter Statements

#### 4.1 Independence and Objectivity
To ensure the independence of the Internal Audit Function, the CAE (or designated Internal Audit Lead) reports:
*   **Functionally** to the Audit & Risk Committee of the Board of Directors.
*   **Administratively** (i.e., day-to-day operations) to the Executive Director (CEO).

The IAF must remain free from interference by any element in the organization, including matters of audit selection, scope, procedures, frequency, timing, or report content.

Internal auditors must exhibit the highest level of professional objectivity in gathering, evaluating, and communicating information about the activity or process being examined. Internal auditors must make a balanced assessment of all the relevant circumstances and not be unduly influenced by their own interests or by others in forming judgments.

**Prohibited Conduct:**
*   Internal auditors shall **not** perform any operational duties for Study GRC Inc.
*   Internal auditors shall **not** initiate or approve accounting transactions external to the internal audit department.
*   Internal auditors shall **not** direct the activities of any Study GRC Inc. employee not employed by the internal audit department, except to the extent such employees have been appropriately assigned to auditing teams or to otherwise assist the internal auditors.

#### 4.2 Authority
The Internal Audit Function, with strict accountability for confidentiality and safeguarding records and information, is authorized full, free, and unrestricted access to any and all of Study GRC Inc.’s:
*   Records (paper and electronic);
*   Physical properties;
*   Personnel; and
*   Digital systems and data environments.

All employees and volunteers are requested to assist the internal audit activity in fulfilling its roles and responsibilities. The IAF also has free and unrestricted access to the Board Chair and the Chair of the Audit & Risk Committee.

#### 4.3 Responsibility
The IAF has the responsibility to:
1.  Submit, at least annually, to the Audit & Risk Committee a risk-based internal audit plan for review and approval.
2.  Communicate to the Audit & Risk Committee the impact of resource limitations on the internal audit plan.
3.  Review and adjust the internal audit plan, as necessary, in response to changes in Study GRC Inc.’s business, risks, operations, programs, systems, and controls.
4.  Perform audit engagements related to compliance with 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance) for federal awards.
5.  Evaluate the reliability and integrity of information and the means used to identify, measure, classify, and report such information.
6.  Evaluate the systems established to ensure compliance with those policies, plans, procedures, laws, and regulations which could have a significant impact on the organization.
7.  Report periodically on the internal audit activity’s purpose, authority, responsibility, and performance relative to its plan.
8.  Report significant risk exposures and control issues, including fraud risks, governance issues, and other matters needed or requested by the Audit & Risk Committee.
9.  Evaluate specific operations at the request of the Board or Management, as appropriate.

#### 4.4 Quality Assurance and Improvement Program
The internal audit activity will maintain a quality assurance and improvement program that covers all aspects of the internal audit activity. The program will include an evaluation of the internal audit activity’s conformance with the IIA Standards and an evaluation of whether internal auditors apply the IIA Code of Ethics. The program also assesses the efficiency and effectiveness of the internal audit activity and identifies opportunities for improvement.

#### 4.5 Standards of Practice
The IAF will govern itself by adherence to the mandatory elements of The Institute of Internal Auditors' (IIA) International Professional Practices Framework (IPPF), including the Core Principles for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing, the Code of Ethics, the International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing, and the Definition of Internal Auditing.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Approves the Internal Audit Charter and the risk-based internal audit plan. Receives communications from the CAE on the internal audit activity’s performance relative to its plan and other matters. |
| **Chief Audit Executive (CAE)** | Manages the internal audit activity, ensures independence, develops the audit plan, and communicates results to the Board and Management. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Provides administrative support to the IAF (e.g., budgeting, HR administration) without interfering in the scope or substance of audit work. |
| **Management** | Cooperates with audit inquiries, provides access to records, and responds to audit findings with corrective action plans within designated timelines. |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensures the IAF has sufficient resources and authority to fulfill its duties as outlined in Bylaws Article VI. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Charter, including the obstruction of audit activities, withholding of information, or retaliation against internal audit personnel, constitutes a serious violation of Study GRC Inc. policy.

Such violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (pursuant to Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6), or termination of volunteer assignment.
