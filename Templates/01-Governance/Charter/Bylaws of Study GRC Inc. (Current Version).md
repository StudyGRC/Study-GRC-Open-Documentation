### 1. Purpose
The purpose of these Bylaws is to establish the fundamental rules and regulations governing the internal affairs, management, and operations of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") in accordance with the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.102. These Bylaws define the rights and duties of members, directors, and officers to ensure the Organization operates exclusively for charitable and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

### 2. Scope
These Bylaws apply to:
*   **The Corporation:** Study GRC Inc. as a legal entity.
*   **The Board of Directors:** The governing body responsible for strategic oversight.
*   **Officers:** Both Board Officers and Operational Officers.
*   **Membership:** All classes of membership (Community, Supporting, and Honorary).
*   **Committees:** All standing and advisory committees appointed by the Board.

### 4. Policy Statements (Bylaws Articles)

#### 4.1 Name and Purposes (Article I)
*   **4.1.1 Name:** The name of the organization is Study GRC Inc.
*   **4.1.2 Registered Office:** The Organization must maintain a registered office and agent in the State of Texas as required by the TBOC.
*   **4.1.3 Nonprofit Purpose:** The Organization is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes, specifically:
    *   Fostering a community-driven ecosystem advancing governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) knowledge;
    *   Removing financial barriers by providing zero-cost, open-source resources; and
    *   Empowering learners and practitioners by simplifying the GRC domain.
*   **4.1.4 Non-Inurement:** No part of net earnings shall inure to the benefit of private persons, except for reasonable compensation for services rendered.

#### 4.2 Membership (Article II)
*   **4.2.1 Classes:**
    *   *Community Participants (Non-Voting):* No voting rights.
    *   *Supporting Members (Voting):* Financial contributors or volunteers as defined in the Membership Policy Manual.
    *   *Honorary Members (Voting):* Lifetime achievement recognition; voting rights for designated term.
*   **4.2.2 Voting Rights:** One (1) vote per Voting Member. Cumulative voting is prohibited.
*   **4.2.3 Proxy Prohibition:** Pursuant to TBOC § 22.160(b), voting by proxy is strictly prohibited. Votes must be cast personally or via the designated secure electronic system.
*   **4.2.4 Quorum:** A majority (more than 50%) of Voting Members constitutes a quorum.
*   **4.2.5 Meetings:**
    *   *Annual Meeting:* Held in January.
    *   *Notice:* Written notice delivered 10 to 60 days prior.
    *   *Nominations:* Opens at least 14 days before the Annual Meeting. Nominees must provide a "Nomination Statement" disclosing conflicts of interest.
    *   *Elections:* Electronic voting window remains open for seven (7) days following the Annual Meeting.
*   **4.2.6 Inspection of Records:** In compliance with TBOC § 22.158 and § 22.351, Voting Members in good standing may inspect the voting list and books for a proper meeting-related purpose upon written demand.

#### 4.3 Board of Directors (Article III)
*   **4.3.1 Authority:** The affairs of the Organization are managed by the Board.
*   **4.3.2 Composition:** Minimum of three (3); Maximum of ten (10).
*   **4.3.3 Terms:** Directors serve two (2) year staggered terms.
*   **4.3.4 Meetings:** The Board must meet at least monthly, virtually or as agreed.
*   **4.3.5 Removal:**
    *   *By Board:* Majority vote for cause.
    *   *By Members:* Two-thirds (2/3) vote for cause.
*   **4.3.6 Vacancies:** Filled by election for the unexpired term. If active Directors fall below three (3), vacancies must be filled immediately.
*   **4.3.7 Action Without Meeting:** Permitted via unanimous written consent (including electronic means).
*   **4.3.8 Contingency ("Doomsday Clause"):** If zero (0) Directors remain, any Voting Member may call a Special Emergency Meeting with 10 days' notice to elect an interim Board.
*   **4.3.9 Transitional Term:** The Initial Board serves a fixed term ending January 2028 to ensure foundational stability.

#### 4.4 Board Officers (Article IV)
*   **4.4.1 Required Officers:** President, Secretary, Treasurer. The President and Secretary cannot be the same person (TBOC § 22.231).
*   **4.4.2 Election:** Elected annually by the Board.

#### 4.5 Operational Leadership (Article V)
*   **4.5.1 Role:** Operational Officers manage day-to-day execution. They may be volunteers or compensated (subject to Conflict of Interest Policy).
*   **4.5.2 Executive Director (CEO):** Reports to the Board. All other Operational Officers report to the CEO.
*   **4.5.3 Key Roles:**
    *   Vice President of Education & Training.
    *   Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).
    *   Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).
    *   Chief Financial Officer (CFO).
*   **4.5.4 Removal by Petition:** Voting Members may remove an Operational Officer via a two-thirds (2/3) affirmative vote of *all* Voting Members.

#### 4.6 Committees & Advisory Board (Article VI)
*   **4.6.1 Board Committees:** Must be chaired by a Director.
*   **4.6.2 Advisory Board:** Non-voting volunteers providing strategic advice.
    *   *Restriction:* Operational Officers may not formally communicate with the Advisory Board without Board facilitation.
    *   *Reporting:* Any communication between Operational Officers and the Advisory Board must be reported to the Board of Directors.

#### 4.7 Indemnification (Article VII)
The Organization shall indemnify Directors, Officers, and volunteers to the fullest extent permitted by law for acts performed in good faith.

#### 4.8 Records, Transparency & Finance (Article VIII)
*   **4.8.1 Financial Reports:** The Board must prepare an annual financial report per TBOC § 22.352.
*   **4.8.2 Transparency:** Annual Financial Reports and IRS Form 990 filings must be publicly available on the Organization’s website.
*   **4.8.3 Fiscal Year:** Calendar year (January 1 – December 31).

#### 4.9 Policies & Data Protection (Article IX)
*   **4.9.1 Conflict of Interest:** Annual disclosure required. Interested persons must recuse themselves from voting on related matters.
*   **4.9.2 Intellectual Property (Open Source):**
    *   Work Product created by leaders/volunteers is "work made for hire" owned by the Organization.
    *   **Commitment:** The Organization must keep resources open-source and free. Exclusive rights preventing public use shall never be granted.

#### 4.10 Fundamental Actions (Article X)
Approval of Fundamental Actions (Amendment of Certificate, Dissolution, Merger, Sale of Assets) requires an affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3)** of *all* Voting Members.

#### 4.11 Dissolution (Article XI)
Upon dissolution, assets must be distributed for exempt purposes within the meaning of IRC Section 501(c)(3).

#### 4.12 Amendments (Article XII)
*   **4.12.1 Standard:** Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Directors.
*   **4.12.2 Protected Sections:** Amendments affecting Member voting rights, Director removal, or Organization Purpose require a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Voting Members.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Strategic management, policy approval, financial oversight, and appointment of Officers. |
| **Voting Members** | Electing Directors, removing Directors/Officers for cause, and voting on Fundamental Actions. |
| **President** | Presides over Board meetings and ensures Board resolutions are carried out. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains corporate records, minutes, and membership rolls; ensures notice requirements are met. |
| **Treasurer** | Oversees financial management and reporting; chairs the Finance Committee. |
| **Executive Director** | Manages daily operations, staff, and implementation of the strategic plan. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with these Bylaws may result in:
1.  **For Directors/Officers:** Removal from office for cause.
2.  **For Members:** Termination of membership privileges.
3.  **Legal Action:** Civil liability for breaches of fiduciary duty or unauthorized acts.

Any action taken in violation of the voting thresholds or notice requirements defined herein shall be considered void *ab initio*.
