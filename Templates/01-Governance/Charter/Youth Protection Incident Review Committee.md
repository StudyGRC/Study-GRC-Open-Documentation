### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Youth Protection Incident Review Committee (the "Committee") is to provide objective oversight, accountability, and root cause analysis regarding all reported incidents, allegations, or "near misses" involving minors within the Study GRC Inc. ecosystem.

This Charter establishes the Committee’s authority to investigate violations of the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, recommend disciplinary actions, and mandate operational changes to prevent recurrence. This body serves as a critical risk management function to protect the safety of youth participants and the legal integrity of the Organization.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   All reported incidents involving "Community Participants" (as defined in Bylaws Article II) under the age of 18.
*   Allegations of misconduct against Volunteers, Staff, Directors, or Members regarding interactions with minors.
*   Digital safety incidents occurring on Organization-managed platforms (e.g., Discord, Zoom, Learning Management Systems).
*   Physical safety incidents occurring at in-person events.

**Exclusions:**
*   This Committee does not replace law enforcement. In cases of suspected abuse or neglect, **Mandatory Reporting to Texas DFPS or local law enforcement takes precedence** over internal review.
*   General member grievances unrelated to youth safety or safeguarding.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reviewable Event** | Any report of abuse (physical, sexual, emotional), neglect, grooming behaviors, bullying, or violation of the *Two-Deep Leadership Policy* or *Digital Interaction Guidelines*. |
| **Near Miss** | An unplanned event that did not result in injury, illness, or damage—but had the potential to do so (e.g., an adult accidentally left alone in a breakout room with a minor). |
| **Recusal** | The act of a Committee member removing themselves from the review process due to a conflict of interest or involvement in the incident. |
| **Root Cause Analysis** | A systematic process for identifying the fundamental causes of an event to prevent recurrence. |

### 4. Charter Statements

#### 4.1 Authority and Establishment
Pursuant to **Article VI, Section 6.1** of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc., the Board of Directors hereby establishes the Youth Protection Incident Review Committee.
*   **Delegated Authority:** The Committee is authorized to access all relevant organizational records (including server logs, chat transcripts, and personnel files) necessary to conduct investigations.
*   **Independence:** The Committee operates independently of the operational staff involved in the incident to ensure impartiality.

#### 4.2 Committee Composition
The Committee shall consist of at least three (3) but no more than five (5) voting members, appointed by the Board of Directors.
1.  **Chair:** A member of the Board of Directors (typically the Director overseeing Risk or Governance).
2.  **Operational Lead:** The Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or Executive Director (unless implicated).
3.  **Subject Matter Expert:** A qualified individual with experience in youth protection, HR, or legal compliance (may be an external advisor).

*Note: Any individual named in an allegation or who witnessed the incident must recuse themselves immediately.*

#### 4.3 Meeting Frequency and Triggers
*   **Emergency Sessions:** Must convene within **24 hours** of a "High Severity" report (e.g., allegation of abuse or grooming).
*   **Standard Sessions:** Must convene within **5 business days** of a "Medium/Low Severity" report (e.g., policy deviation without harm).
*   **Quarterly Reviews:** The Committee shall meet quarterly to review anonymized aggregate data, identify trends, and audit compliance with youth protection policies.

#### 4.4 Review Process and Procedures
The Committee must adhere to the following workflow for all Reviewable Events:

1.  **Immediate Safety Verification:** Confirm that the minor is safe and that the accused individual has been temporarily suspended pending investigation.
2.  **Mandatory Reporting Check:** Verify that reports to Texas DFPS or law enforcement have been filed if the threshold for suspicion of abuse is met (Texas Family Code §261.101).
3.  **Fact-Finding:** Review evidence, including:
    *   Incident Reports (Form YP-FRM-002).
    *   Digital logs (chat history, login times).
    *   Witness statements.
4.  **Determination:** Determine by majority vote whether a violation of policy occurred.
5.  **Corrective Action:** Issue binding recommendations, which may include:
    *   Permanent ban/expulsion of the offender (per Bylaws Article II).
    *   Termination of employment or volunteer agreement.
    *   Revision of policies or technical controls.
    *   Retraining of staff/volunteers.

#### 4.5 Confidentiality and Data Protection
*   All Committee proceedings are strictly confidential.
*   Records identifying minors must be redacted in general Board reports.
*   Full unredacted records shall be stored in a secure, access-controlled repository (Legal Hold) compliant with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

#### 4.6 Reporting to the Board
The Committee Chair must provide a summary report to the Board of Directors during Executive Session at the next scheduled Board meeting following a Reviewable Event. This report must include:
*   Nature of the incident (anonymized).
*   Policy gaps identified.
*   Actions taken.
*   Status of external reporting (Law Enforcement/Insurance).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Committee Chair** | Convenes meetings, ensures adherence to this Charter, and serves as the primary liaison to the Board of Directors. |
| **Committee Members** | Review evidence objectively, participate in root cause analysis, and vote on disciplinary recommendations. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Acts as the primary intake officer for incident reports and ensures immediate safety measures (suspensions) are enforced prior to Committee review. |
| **Legal Counsel (External)** | Provides guidance on reporting obligations, liability, and preservation of evidence (consulted as needed). |
| **Secretary** | Maintains confidential minutes of Committee meetings and tracks the implementation of Corrective Actions. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Failure to Convene:** Failure of the Committee to convene within the mandated timeframes following a reported incident constitutes a breach of fiduciary duty and governance standards.
*   **Obstruction:** Any employee or volunteer found obstructing the Committee’s investigation (e.g., deleting logs, coaching witnesses) faces immediate termination.
*   **Retaliation:** Retaliation against any individual participating in a Committee review is strictly prohibited under the *Whistleblower Protection Policy*.
