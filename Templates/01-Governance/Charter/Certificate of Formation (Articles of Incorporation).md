### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the legal existence of **Study GRC Inc.** as a domestic nonprofit corporation under the laws of the State of Texas. This Charter serves as the primary governing instrument of the Organization, defining its legal name, registered agent, management structure, and specific tax-exempt purposes in compliance with the **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC)** and **Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code**.

### 2. Scope
This Charter applies to the entire legal entity of Study GRC Inc. It is the supreme governing document of the Organization; in the event of any inconsistency between this Certificate of Formation and the Bylaws or any other policy, this Certificate shall prevail.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **TBOC** | Texas Business Organizations Code; the statutes governing the formation and operation of entities in Texas. |
| **Registered Agent** | An individual or entity designated to receive service of process (legal mail) on behalf of the Organization. |
| **501(c)(3)** | A section of the Internal Revenue Code allowing for federal tax exemption of nonprofit organizations. |
| **Organizer** | The individual responsible for signing and filing the initial Certificate of Formation with the Secretary of State. |

*(Note: Per Bylaws Article III, Section 3.2, the Board shall consist of not less than three (3) directors.)*

#### 4.4 Article 4 – Membership
The corporation shall have members. The classes of membership, voting rights, and qualifications are set forth in the Bylaws of the corporation (specifically Bylaws Article II).

#### 4.5 Article 5 – Purpose (Nonprofit & Tax-Exempt Status)
The corporation is organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

The specific purposes for which the corporation is organized include:
1.  Fostering a community-driven ecosystem advancing governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) knowledge;
2.  Removing financial barriers by providing zero-cost, open-source resources; and
3.  Empowering learners and practitioners by simplifying the GRC domain.

#### 4.6 Article 6 – Prohibited Activities
No part of the net earnings of the corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its members, trustees, officers, or other private persons, except that the corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth in Article 5 hereof.

No substantial part of the activities of the corporation shall be the carrying on of propaganda, or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, and the corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distribution of statements) any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.

#### 4.7 Article 7 – Limitation of Liability
To the fullest extent permitted by Texas law, a director of the corporation shall not be liable to the corporation or its members for monetary damages for an act or omission in the director's capacity as a director, except that this Article does not eliminate or limit the liability of a director for:
1.  A breach of the director's duty of loyalty to the corporation or its members;
2.  An act or omission not in good faith or that involves intentional misconduct or a knowing violation of the law;
3.  A transaction from which the director received an improper benefit, whether or not the benefit resulted from an action taken within the scope of the director's office; or
4.  An act or omission for which the liability of a director is expressly provided for by an applicable statute.

#### 4.8 Article 8 – Indemnification
The corporation shall indemnify any director or officer or former director or officer of the corporation against all expenses actually and reasonably incurred by them in connection with the defense of any action, suit, or proceeding in which they are made a party by reason of being or having been such director or officer, to the maximum extent permitted by the TBOC and as further defined in Bylaws Article VII.

#### 4.9 Article 9 – Dissolution
Upon the dissolution of the corporation, assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code, or to the federal government, or to a state or local government, for a public purpose. Any such assets not so disposed of shall be disposed of by a Court of Competent Jurisdiction of the county in which the principal office of the corporation is then located, exclusively for such purposes or to such organization or organizations, as said Court shall determine, which are organized and operated exclusively for such purposes.

#### 4.10 Article 10 – Action Without Meeting
Any action required or permitted by the TBOC to be taken at a meeting of the members or directors may be taken without a meeting if a consent in writing, setting forth the action so taken, is signed by a sufficient number of members or directors as would be necessary to take that action at a meeting at which all of the members or directors were present and voted, in accordance with Bylaws Article III, Section 3.9.

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain a valid Certificate of Formation, including the maintenance of a Registered Agent and the filing of periodic reports with the Texas Secretary of State, will result in the **involuntary forfeiture** of the corporation’s right to conduct business and the loss of limited liability protection for Directors.

Amendments to this Certificate are considered a "Fundamental Action" under Bylaws Article X and require an affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3)** of all Voting Members.
