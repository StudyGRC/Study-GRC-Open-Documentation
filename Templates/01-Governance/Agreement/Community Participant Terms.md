### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Agreement is to establish the terms of service, code of conduct, and legal relationship between Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") and individuals classified as **Community Participants**. This document formalizes the non-voting membership status defined in **Article II, Section 2.1** of the Bylaws, ensures the protection of the Organization’s intellectual property, and mandates a safe, inclusive environment for all learners, including youth participants.

### 2. Scope
This Agreement applies to all individuals who access, browse, or participate in the Organization’s ecosystem ("Services"), including but not limited to:
- The Study GRC Inc. public website and member portals.
- Community communication platforms (e.g., Discord, Slack, Forums).
- Learning Management Systems (LMS) and educational repositories.
- Virtual and in-person events, webinars, and workshops.

**Exclusions:** This document does not supersede the specific rights and obligations of **Supporting Members** (Voting) or **Directors**, which are governed by separate agreements and the Bylaws.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Community Participant** | As defined in Bylaws Art. II §2.1, an individual who participates in the community or attends events but holds **no voting rights** in the Organization. |
| **User Contributions** | Any text, code, images, video, or other material posted, uploaded, or transmitted by a Participant to the Services. |
| **The Services** | Collectively, the website, community platforms, educational content, and events provided by Study GRC Inc. |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18. |
| **DKG** | Director of Kindness and Generosity; the Operational Officer responsible for community culture. |

### 4. Terms and Conditions

#### 4.1 Membership Status and Voting Rights
Pursuant to **Article II, Section 2.1** of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc.:
- **Non-Voting Status:** Community Participants are considered non-voting members. Participation in the community does not confer any rights to vote on corporate matters, elect Directors, or approve Fundamental Actions.
- **No Ownership:** Participation does not convey any equity, ownership interest, or proprietary right in the Organization.
- **Voluntary Participation:** Participation is voluntary and at-will. The Organization reserves the right to revoke access to any Participant at any time, with or without cause.

#### 4.2 Code of Conduct and Ethics
To maintain the integrity of the Organization’s mission, all Participants must adhere to the following standards:
- **Professionalism:** Interactions must remain professional and respectful. Harassment, hate speech, discrimination, and bullying are strictly prohibited.
- **No Solicitation:** Participants may not use the Services for unauthorized commercial solicitation, recruitment, or marketing of third-party products without express written permission from the Board.
- **Academic Integrity:** Cheating on assessments, plagiarism of resources, or facilitating academic dishonesty within the GRC certification domain is prohibited.

#### 4.3 Intellectual Property (IP) Rights
- **Organization Content:** All educational materials, code, and resources provided by Study GRC Inc. are protected by copyright. Unless otherwise marked (e.g., Creative Commons), Participants may not reproduce, distribute, or sell Organization Content without permission.
- **User Contributions:** By posting User Contributions, the Participant grants Study GRC Inc. a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use, reproduce, modify, and display said content for educational and charitable purposes.
- **Open Source Commitment:** Code contributions submitted to Organization repositories are subject to the specific Open Source License (e.g., MIT, Apache 2.0) attached to that repository.

#### 4.4 Youth Protection and Safeguarding
Given the Organization’s K-12 focus, strict adherence to youth protection is mandatory:
- **Prohibited Contact:** Adult Participants are strictly prohibited from initiating private, one-on-one direct messages (DMs) with known Minors. All interactions must occur in public channels.
- **Reporting:** Participants must immediately report any suspected grooming, abuse, or inappropriate behavior toward a Minor to the Director of Kindness and Generosity or the Legal Department.
- **COPPA Compliance:** Participants under the age of 13 must obtain verifiable parental consent before accessing community platforms, in accordance with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.

#### 4.5 Data Privacy and Security
- **Data Collection:** The Organization collects limited personal data (e.g., email, username) to facilitate access. This is governed by the *Master Data Privacy Policy*.
- **No Expectation of Privacy:** Participants acknowledge that public community channels are not private. The Organization reserves the right to monitor and log all activity on its platforms to ensure safety and compliance.

#### 4.6 Disclaimers and Limitation of Liability
- **Educational Purpose Only:** Resources provided by Study GRC Inc. are for educational purposes only and do not constitute legal or professional advice.
- **Indemnification:** Participants agree to indemnify and hold harmless Study GRC Inc., its Directors, Officers, and volunteers from any claims arising out of the Participant's violation of this Agreement or misuse of the Services.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| **Community Participant** | Acknowledges and abides by these Terms, the Code of Conduct, and the Privacy Policy. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Oversees community moderation, handles dispute resolution, and enforces culture standards. |
| **Moderators** | Volunteer staff designated to monitor channels and enforce these Terms in real-time. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Ensures platform security and investigates technical violations (e.g., spam bots, unauthorized access). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Agreement may result in immediate disciplinary action, including but not limited to:
1. **Verbal/Written Warning:** For minor, first-time offenses.
2. **Temporary Suspension:** Revocation of access for a specified period (e.g., 7 days).
3. **Permanent Ban:** Indefinite termination of Community Participant status and blocking of access to all Services.
4. **Legal Action:** Referral to law enforcement for illegal acts (e.g., threats of violence, child exploitation, severe IP theft).

The Organization reserves the right to bypass progressive discipline and impose an immediate ban for severe violations.
