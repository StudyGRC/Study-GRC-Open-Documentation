### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and procedures by which Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") recovers indirect costs (Facilities and Administrative costs) associated with federal awards and pass-through funding. This policy ensures compliance with **2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance)**, specifically Subpart E (Cost Principles), and defines the Organization’s election to use the **De Minimis Rate** or pursue a formally **Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement (NICRA)** with a Federal Cognizant Agency.

This policy aligns with **Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** of the Bylaws, ensuring fiscal responsibility and accurate financial reporting.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   All applications for federal funding (direct or pass-through).
*   All financial management of awarded grants.
*   The Finance Department and Program Managers responsible for budget development.
*   Subrecipients receiving pass-through funds from Study GRC Inc.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Indirect Costs (F&A)** | Costs incurred for a common or joint purpose benefitting more than one cost objective, and not readily assignable to the cost objectives specifically benefited, without effort disproportionate to the results achieved (e.g., rent, utilities, general administrative salaries). |
| **Direct Costs** | Costs that can be identified specifically with a particular final cost objective, such as a federal award, or other internally or externally funded activity, or that can be directly assigned to such activities relatively easily with a high degree of accuracy. |
| **NICRA** | **Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement**. A formal document issued by a Federal Cognizant Agency reflecting the final agreed-upon indirect cost rate. |
| **De Minimis Rate** | A standard indirect cost rate of **10%** of Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC) that non-federal entities may elect to use indefinitely if they do not have a current negotiated rate (per 2 CFR 200.414(f)). |
| **MTDC** | **Modified Total Direct Costs**. The base to which the indirect cost rate is applied. It includes all direct salaries and wages, applicable fringe benefits, materials and supplies, services, travel, and up to the first $25,000 of each subaward. |
| **Cognizant Agency** | The federal agency responsible for negotiating and approving the indirect cost rate (usually the agency providing the largest amount of direct federal funding to the Organization). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Election of Indirect Cost Rate
It is the policy of Study GRC Inc. to recover the full costs of managing federal awards, including overhead and administrative expenses.
*   **Default Election:** Until such time that the Organization's indirect costs exceed the standard threshold and a formal proposal is submitted, Study GRC Inc. elects to use the **10% De Minimis Rate** as authorized by **2 CFR 200.414(f)**.
*   **Negotiated Rate:** If the Organization chooses to negotiate a higher rate, it must submit an Indirect Cost Rate Proposal (ICRP) to its Cognizant Agency for Indirect Costs. Once a NICRA is issued, that rate must be used for all applicable grants until it expires or is amended.

#### 4.2 Application of the Rate
The approved rate (De Minimis or NICRA) must be applied consistently to the **Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC)** base for all federal awards, unless a specific federal statute or regulation restricts or caps indirect costs for a specific program (e.g., a training grant capped at 8%).

#### 4.3 Prohibition on Double Charging
Costs classified as **Indirect** must not be charged to a federal award as **Direct** costs.
*   *Example:* The salary of the Chief Financial Officer is typically an indirect cost covered by the NICRA. It cannot be charged directly to a grant unless the CFO is performing distinct, quantifiable technical work on that specific project (e.g., "Key Personnel" role).

#### 4.4 Pass-Through Entity Responsibilities
When Study GRC Inc. acts as a Pass-Through Entity (issuing subawards to other organizations):
*   We must honor the subrecipient’s approved NICRA.
*   If the subrecipient does not have a NICRA, we must allow them to use the 10% De Minimis rate.
*   We may not force a subrecipient to accept a rate lower than their federally negotiated rate unless required by federal statute.

### 5. Procedures

#### 5.1 Calculating Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC)
To determine the amount of indirect costs Study GRC Inc. may claim, the Finance Department must calculate the MTDC base.

**Formula:**
`Total Direct Costs - Exclusions = MTDC`

**Inclusions (Subject to Indirect Rate):**
*   Salaries and Wages.
*   Fringe Benefits.
*   Materials and Supplies.
*   Services (Consultants).
*   Travel.
*   The first $25,000 of each subaward (per subrecipient, per period of performance).

**Exclusions (NOT Subject to Indirect Rate):**
*   Equipment (tangible personal property >$5,000 useful life >1 year).
*   Capital Expenditures.
*   Charges for patient care.
*   Rental costs (if treated as direct).
*   Tuition remission.
*   Scholarships and fellowships.
*   Participant Support Costs (stipends, subsistence, travel for trainees).
*   The portion of each subaward in excess of $25,000.

#### 5.2 Budgeting for Indirect Costs
During the grant proposal phase:
1.  **Identify Direct Costs:** Estimate all allowable direct costs.
2.  **Identify Exclusions:** Separate items listed in Section 5.1.
3.  **Calculate Base:** Subtract exclusions from Total Direct Costs to get MTDC.
4.  **Apply Rate:** Multiply MTDC by 0.10 (or current NICRA rate).
5.  **Documentation:** Include a statement in the budget narrative: *"Study GRC Inc. has elected to use the 10% De Minimis Indirect Cost Rate in accordance with 2 CFR 200.414(f)."* OR attach the current NICRA letter.

#### 5.3 Accounting and Drawdowns
1.  **General Ledger:** Indirect costs must be recorded in the accounting system (e.g., QuickBooks) periodically (monthly or quarterly).
2.  **Journal Entry:**
    *   *Debit:* Grant Expense - Indirect Costs.
    *   *Credit:* Unrestricted Revenue - Indirect Cost Recovery.
3.  **Drawdown:** Indirect costs are eligible for reimbursement immediately as they are accrued, proportional to the direct costs incurred.

#### 5.4 Negotiating a NICRA (Future State)
If the Board determines that the 10% rate is insufficient to cover actual administrative costs:
1.  **Preparation:** The CFO shall prepare an Indirect Cost Rate Proposal (ICRP) based on audited financial statements.
2.  **Submission:** The ICRP must be submitted to the Cognizant Agency within six (6) months after the close of the fiscal year.
3.  **Negotiation:** The CFO and Executive Director will negotiate with the federal agency.
4.  **Execution:** Upon agreement, the NICRA is signed by a representative of the Cognizant Agency and the Study GRC Inc. Executive Director.
5.  **Distribution:** The new NICRA must be distributed to all funding agencies to adjust future billing rates.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Responsible for calculating MTDC, ensuring consistent application of the rate, and preparing the Indirect Cost Rate Proposal if a negotiated rate is sought. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Authorized signatory for the NICRA (if negotiated) and responsible for certifying that indirect costs are not double-billed as direct costs. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Responsible for ensuring grant budgets under their purview accurately reflect the correct indirect cost rate and exclusions (e.g., Participant Support Costs). |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews the financial health of the organization and approves the decision to seek a negotiated rate vs. maintaining the De Minimis rate. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to correctly calculate MTDC or misapplication of the indirect cost rate can result in **disallowed costs**, requiring Study GRC Inc. to repay funds to the federal government.

*   **Audit:** Indirect cost calculations are a primary focus of the Single Audit (2 CFR 200 Subpart F).
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Intentional manipulation of cost pools to inflate indirect cost recovery constitutes fraud and will result in immediate termination and legal action under the False Claims Act.
