### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Agreement is to establish the strict confidentiality obligations required of all participants in Executive Sessions of the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This Agreement ensures that sensitive matters—including personnel issues, legal strategies, and proprietary data—remain protected, thereby upholding the **Fiduciary Duty of Loyalty** and minimizing legal liability.

### 2. Scope
This Agreement applies to:
*   **Directors:** All voting members of the Board of Directors.
*   **Officers:** Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director, CISO) invited to attend specific sessions.
*   **Guests/Advisors:** Any external parties (e.g., legal counsel, auditors, consultants) invited to participate in an Executive Session.

This Agreement applies to all information disclosed during Executive Sessions, regardless of whether the meeting is held in person or virtually.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Session** | A portion of a Board meeting open only to Directors and specifically invited guests, excluding the general public and non-essential staff, to discuss sensitive or privileged matters. |
| **Confidential Information** | Any data, discussion, or materials presented during an Executive Session, including but not limited to personnel records, legal advice (attorney-client privilege), financial irregularities, or strategic trade secrets. |
| **Fiduciary Duty** | The legal obligation of Directors to act in the best interest of the Organization, which includes the duty to maintain the confidentiality of non-public information. |
| **Remote Environment** | Any location outside of a physical Organization office where a participant accesses the meeting (e.g., home office, co-working space). |

### 4. Agreement Statements

#### 4.1 Fiduciary Obligation and Duty of Loyalty
By participating in an Executive Session, the undersigned acknowledges that maintaining confidentiality is a core component of their Fiduciary Duty to Study GRC Inc. Unauthorized disclosure of Executive Session proceedings is considered a breach of this duty and may result in personal liability and removal from office.

#### 4.2 Strict Non-Disclosure
4.2.1 **Prohibition on Sharing:** The undersigned agrees not to disclose, discuss, or disseminate any Confidential Information to any person who was not present in the Executive Session, including:
*   Spouses, partners, or family members.
*   Staff members or volunteers not explicitly authorized by the Board.
*   General members of the Organization.
*   Media or public outlets.

4.2.2 **Social Media and Electronic Communications:** The undersigned is strictly prohibited from posting, tweeting, or referencing the existence of specific Executive Session topics or outcomes on any social media platform or public forum.

#### 4.3 Remote Meeting Security Protocols
Recognizing Study GRC Inc. operates as a remote-first organization, the undersigned agrees to the following security measures during virtual Executive Sessions:
4.3.1 **Audio Privacy:** Participants must use headphones or ensure they are in a private room where the discussion cannot be overheard by others (including smart home devices, which should be muted).
4.3.2 **Visual Privacy:** Screens must be positioned away from windows or public views.
4.3.3 **Prohibition on Recording:** The recording of Executive Sessions (audio or video) by any participant other than the Secretary (for official minute-taking purposes only) is strictly prohibited.
4.3.4 **Data Disposal:** Any digital files or notes shared during the session must be deleted or stored in the Organization’s secure, access-controlled repository immediately following the meeting. Local copies on personal devices are prohibited.

#### 4.4 Handling of Minutes and Records
4.4.1 **Separate Minutes:** Minutes of Executive Sessions shall be kept separate from the regular Board meeting minutes.
4.4.2 **Restricted Access:** Executive Session minutes are accessible *only* to Voting Directors and those specific individuals authorized by the Board. They shall not be included in the transparency disclosures described in Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2.

#### 4.5 Duration of Obligation
The obligation to maintain confidentiality regarding Executive Session matters is **perpetual**. It survives the termination of the undersigned’s term as a Director, Officer, or volunteer. Confidentiality may only be lifted if:
*   The information becomes public through no fault of the undersigned; or
*   The Board of Directors votes affirmatively to release the information.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Participant (Signatory)** | Adheres strictly to non-disclosure rules; secures their remote environment; destroys local copies of sensitive documents. |
| **Presiding Officer (President)** | Announces the start and end of the Executive Session; verifies that unauthorized persons have left the physical or virtual room. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains a separate, secure log of Executive Session minutes; ensures these minutes are not distributed in the general Board packet. |
| **CISO** | Establishes the technical standards for secure document sharing during Executive Sessions. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**6.1 Breach of Agreement**
Any violation of this Agreement constitutes "Cause" for removal under **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6 (Removal of Directors)**.

**6.2 Legal Recourse**
Study GRC Inc. reserves the right to pursue legal action against any individual who breaches this Agreement, seeking injunctive relief and damages for any harm caused to the Organization.

**6.3 Loss of Indemnification**
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification)**, indemnification is contingent upon acting in good faith. A breach of this confidentiality agreement may be deemed an act of bad faith, thereby nullifying the Organization's obligation to indemnify the individual against resulting liabilities.
