### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Work Made for Hire Assignment (the "Agreement") is to clearly establish ownership of intellectual property created for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). In accordance with **Article IX, Section 9.3** of the Organization's Bylaws, this agreement ensures that all resources, code, and educational materials created by contributors are owned by the Organization. This centralization of ownership is necessary to legally protect the Organization’s assets and fulfill its charitable mission of providing zero-cost, open-source resources to the public without encumbrance.

### 2. Scope
This Agreement applies to all individuals and entities ("Contributors") who create content, code, designs, or other intellectual property for the Organization.
*   **Applies to:** Employees, Independent Contractors, Volunteers, Board Directors, Officers, and Interns.
*   **Exclusions:** Intellectual property created by a Contributor entirely on their own time, without the use of the Organization’s equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secret information, and which does not relate to the Organization’s business or actual/demonstrably anticipated research and development.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Work Product** | Any and all ideas, inventions, discoveries, improvements, designs, software code, curriculum, documentation, graphics, audio/visual materials, and other copyrightable works created by the Contributor in the course of their service to the Organization. |
| **Background IP** | Intellectual property owned by the Contributor prior to their engagement with the Organization, or developed independently outside the scope of this Agreement. |
| **Moral Rights** | Rights of an author to claim authorship, object to modifications, or withdraw a work from circulation, distinct from economic copyright. |
| **Open Source** | A licensing model (e.g., MIT, CC-BY-SA) that allows software or content to be freely used, modified, and shared by the public. |
| **17 U.S.C. § 101** | The section of the U.S. Copyright Act defining a "work made for hire." |

### 4. Agreement Terms

#### 4.1 Work Made for Hire and Assignment
The Contributor acknowledges and agrees that all Work Product created during their engagement with the Organization shall be considered a "work made for hire" to the fullest extent permitted by the United States Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. § 101). The Organization shall be the sole and exclusive owner of all copyrights and other intellectual property rights in and to the Work Product.

**Fallback Assignment:**
To the extent that any Work Product may not, by operation of law, be considered a "work made for hire" (e.g., work created by volunteers or independent contractors that falls outside specific statutory categories), the Contributor hereby **irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys** to the Organization all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all copyrights, patents, trade secrets, and other proprietary rights, worldwide and in perpetuity.

#### 4.2 Waiver of Moral Rights
To the extent permitted by applicable law, the Contributor hereby waives and agrees not to enforce any "moral rights" or similar rights (such as rights of integrity or attribution) they may have in the Work Product. This waiver allows the Organization to modify, adapt, translate, or combine the Work Product as necessary to maintain its educational resources.

#### 4.3 Background Intellectual Property
The Contributor retains ownership of their Background IP. However, if the Contributor incorporates any Background IP into the Work Product (e.g., including a snippet of personal code into an Organization project), the Contributor hereby grants the Organization a **non-exclusive, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide license** to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and sublicense such Background IP as part of the Work Product.
*   **Requirement:** Contributors must disclose any Background IP included in deliverables prior to submission.

#### 4.4 The Organization's Open Source Commitment
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, the Organization holds these rights solely to protect its charitable mission. The Organization commits that it will not use this assignment to lock away knowledge for private gain. The Organization agrees to license the Work Product to the public under appropriate Open Source licenses (e.g., Creative Commons for content, MIT/Apache for code) to ensure the community retains free access.

#### 4.5 Further Assurances
The Contributor agrees to execute any additional documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Organization to perfect, record, or enforce the Organization’s ownership of the Work Product, even after the Contributor’s relationship with the Organization has ended.

#### 4.6 No Infringement
The Contributor represents and warrants that the Work Product is their original work and does not infringe upon the intellectual property rights, privacy rights, or other rights of any third party. The Contributor agrees not to use or disclose any confidential information or trade secrets of former employers or third parties in the creation of the Work Product.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Contributor** | Creates original work; discloses Background IP; executes this Agreement prior to commencing work; refrains from using unlicensed third-party material. |
| **Project Manager / Dept Head** | Ensures all team members (volunteers/contractors) have signed this Agreement before granting access to repositories or assigning tasks. |
| **Secretary / Legal** | Maintains executed Agreements in the secure personnel/volunteer records system; manages copyright registration if necessary. |
| **Board of Directors** | Oversees the "Open Source Commitment" to ensure assets held by the Organization are properly licensed back to the public. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Agreement puts the Organization’s intellectual property and tax-exempt status at risk.
*   **Refusal to Sign:** Individuals who refuse to execute this assignment cannot participate in content creation or code development roles.
*   **Violation:** Unauthorized use of the Organization’s IP, or incorporation of infringing third-party material, may result in immediate termination of the volunteer/employment relationship and potential legal action for damages and injunctive relief.
