### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this **Governance Document Template** is to establish a mandatory, standardized format for all policies, standards, procedures, and guidelines created within Study GRC Inc.

By enforcing a consistent structure, this template ensures:
1.  **Regulatory Compliance:** Alignment with Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and 2 CFR 200 requirements for documentation standards.
2.  **Clarity and Usability:** Ensuring all stakeholders, from Board Directors to volunteers, can easily navigate and understand organizational rules.
3.  **Audit Readiness:** Facilitating efficient review by external auditors and the IRS by maintaining uniform document metadata and control histories.

### 2. Scope
This template applies to **all** formal governance documents generated by Study GRC Inc. personnel, including Board Directors, Officers, employees, and volunteers.

**In Scope:**
*   **Policies:** High-level governance rules (Level 1).
*   **Standards:** Quantifiable requirements and specifications (Level 2).
*   **Procedures:** Step-by-step operational instructions (Level 2).
*   **Guidelines:** Recommended best practices (Level 3).

**Out of Scope:**
*   Informal communications (emails, Slack messages).
*   Marketing materials or blog posts not related to governance.
*   Third-party contracts (though contracts must reference relevant policies).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Policy** | A high-level statement of intent and rules that dictates "what" must be done and "why." Policies require Board or Executive Director approval. |
| **Procedure** | A specific, step-by-step description of "how" to perform a task to achieve a policy's intent. |
| **Standard** | A mandatory requirement, code, or specification (e.g., "Passwords must be 12 characters"). |
| **Guideline** | A non-mandatory recommendation or best practice. |
| **Must / Shall** | Indicates a mandatory requirement. Failure to comply is a violation. |
| **Should** | Indicates a recommendation or best practice. Compliance is expected but not strictly mandatory if a valid justification exists. |
| **MDR** | Master Documentation Registry. The central index of all controlled documents. |

### 4. Template Standards and Drafting Rules

All governance documents must adhere to the following drafting standards to ensure consistency and enforceability.

#### 4.1 Document Control Block
Every document must begin with the **Document Control** table exactly as it appears at the top of this template.
*   **Document ID:** Must follow the format `[Dept]-[Type]-[###]` (e.g., `HR-POL-005`).
*   **Classification:** Must be categorized as **Public**, **Internal**, or **Confidential** based on the Data Classification Standard (IS-STD-002).

#### 4.2 Writing Style and Tone
*   **Active Voice:** Authors must use active voice.
    *   *Correct:* "The Manager approves the request."
    *   *Incorrect:* "The request is approved by the Manager."
*   **Plain English:** Avoid legal jargon ("heretofore," "aforementioned") unless citing specific statutes. Language should be accessible to a general audience, including youth program volunteers.
*   **Gender Neutrality:** Use role titles (e.g., "The Chair") or "they/them." The use of "he/she" is prohibited.

#### 4.3 Section Requirements
*   **1. Purpose:** Must be 1-3 sentences explaining the risk mitigated or the operational goal.
*   **2. Scope:** Must explicitly state who is affected and any specific exclusions.
*   **3. Definitions:** Must define all acronyms and technical terms used in the document.
*   **4. Policy Statements / Procedures:**
    *   If a **Policy**: Use clear rule statements (e.g., "4.1 Prohibition on Gifts").
    *   If a **Procedure**: Use numbered, chronological steps (e.g., "4.1.1 Log into the portal").
*   **6. Compliance:** Must include the standard enforcement language regarding disciplinary action.

#### 4.4 Version Control
*   **Drafts:** Must be numbered v0.1, v0.2, etc.
*   **Approved Versions:** Must be numbered v1.0, v2.0, etc.
*   **Minor Edits:** Typos or hyperlink updates that do not change the meaning may be numbered v1.1, v1.2.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Document Author** | Drafts the content using this template and ensures alignment with the Bylaws and existing policies. |
| **Subject Matter Expert (SME)** | Reviews the draft for technical accuracy and operational feasibility. |
| **Approving Authority** | Reviews and formally approves the document (Board of Directors for Policies; Executive Director for Procedures). |
| **Secretary / Document Controller** | Assigns the Document ID, updates the Master Documentation Registry (MDR), and archives the signed version. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to utilize this template for formal governance documentation may result in the rejection of the document by the Approving Authority. Documents that do not adhere to this standard will not be recognized as official Study GRC Inc. policy and will be returned to the author for remediation.

Repeated failure to adhere to documentation standards by leadership may result in performance management actions.
