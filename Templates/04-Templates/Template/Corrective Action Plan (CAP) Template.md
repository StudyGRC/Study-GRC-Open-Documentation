### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Corrective Action Plan (CAP) Template is to provide a standardized framework for Study GRC Inc. to respond to audit findings, internal control deficiencies, and non-compliance events. This document ensures that the Organization meets the requirements of **2 CFR § 200.511(c)** regarding auditee responsibilities, specifically the requirement to prepare a corrective action plan for each audit finding.

This template facilitates the identification of root causes, the assignment of accountability, and the establishment of timelines to remediate issues and prevent recurrence, aligning with the transparency goals outlined in **Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This template applies to all Departments, Operational Officers, and Staff of Study GRC Inc. It must be utilized in response to:
*   External Financial Audits (including Single Audits under Uniform Guidance).
*   Internal Audits conducted by the Audit & Risk Committee.
*   Grantor/Funder Site Visits or Monitoring Reviews.
*   Internal investigations resulting in substantiated findings of non-compliance.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Finding** | A deficiency in internal control, non-compliance with laws/regulations, or questioned costs identified during an audit or review. |
| **Root Cause** | The underlying reason the issue occurred (e.g., process failure, training gap, system error), rather than just the symptom. |
| **Questioned Cost** | A cost that is questioned by the auditor because of an alleged violation of a provision of a law, regulation, contract, grant, or other agreement. |
| **Material Weakness** | A deficiency, or combination of deficiencies, in internal control, such that there is a reasonable possibility that a material misstatement of the entity's financial statements will not be prevented, or detected and corrected, on a timely basis. |
| **Process Owner** | The specific individual responsible for the day-to-day management of the area where the finding occurred. |

### 4. Template Instructions
*   **Mandatory Use:** This form must be completed for **each** individual finding.
*   **Specificity:** Responses must be specific. Avoid vague phrases like "We will try harder."
*   **Timeline:** All CAPs must be drafted within 10 business days of receiving the Draft Audit Report.
*   **Disagreement:** If the Organization disagrees with a finding, Section D must be completed in detail.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Process Owner** | Conducts root cause analysis; drafts specific action steps; implements changes; reports progress to CFO. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews CAP for feasibility and compliance with **2 CFR 200**; allocates necessary resources; approves final CAP submission. |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Provides oversight for Material Weaknesses; monitors CAP progress quarterly pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI**; ensures "Tone at the Top" supports compliance. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ultimate accountability for organizational compliance; signatory on management representation letters. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to implement an approved Corrective Action Plan may result in:
1.  **Disciplinary Action:** Up to and including termination for negligence in fiduciary duties.
2.  **Funding Risk:** Suspension or debarment from federal grant eligibility.
3.  **Governance Action:** Removal of Officers by the Board pursuant to **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6** for failure to protect the Organization's assets and reputation.
