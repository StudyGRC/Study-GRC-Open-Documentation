### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to provide a standardized framework for the creation, management, and governance of all committees within Study GRC Inc. In accordance with **Article VI of the Bylaws**, every committee—whether a Board Committee with delegated authority or an Advisory Committee—must operate under a clear "Charge" or "Charter" approved by the Board of Directors. This template ensures that all committees have defined goals, specific authority limits, and clear reporting requirements to maintain organizational alignment and compliance.

### 2. Scope
This template applies to:
*   **Standing Board Committees** (e.g., Finance, Governance).
*   **Advisory Committees** (e.g., Industry Advisory Board).
*   **Ad-Hoc Committees** or **Task Forces** created for specific, time-bound projects.

This template must be completed by the proposed Committee Chair and approved by the Board of Directors prior to the committee commencing official business.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Committee Charge** | A formal document (Charter) that defines the scope, authority, membership, and deliverables of a committee. |
| **Board Committee** | A committee delegated specific management authority by the Board. Per **Bylaws Section 6.1**, it must have at least two members and be led by an active Board Director. |
| **Advisory Committee** | A committee appointed to provide non-binding advice and support. Per **Bylaws Section 6.2**, it has no voting rights in governance matters and no authority to bind the Organization financially or legally. |
| **Ex Officio** | A member who serves on a committee by virtue of holding another office (e.g., the President serving on all committees). |

### 4. Template Instructions & Structure

To establish a new committee, the **Committee Charge Form** below must be completed in its entirety.

#### 4.1 Usage Instructions
1.  **Drafting:** The proposed Committee Chair or the Board Secretary drafts the content using the structure in **Section 4.2**.
2.  **Authority Check:** Ensure the "Authority & Limitations" section explicitly states whether the committee can authorize spending (Board Committee) or only recommend spending (Advisory Committee).
3.  **Approval:** The completed Charge must be presented to the Board of Directors for a vote.
4.  **Review:** The Charge must be reviewed annually by the committee to ensure it remains relevant.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves all Committee Charges. Has the sole authority to dissolve committees or remove committee members for cause. |
| **Committee Chair** | Drafts the Charge, ensures the committee operates within the defined scope, and reports activities to the Board. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains the official file of all active Committee Charges and ensures minutes are submitted to the corporate records. |
| **Committee Members** | Adhere to the Charge, the Conflict of Interest Policy, and the Code of Ethics. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Failure to Define Scope:** Any committee operating without an approved Charge is acting without authorization. Actions taken, funds spent, or commitments made by such a committee may be deemed null and void by the Board of Directors.

**Scope Creep:** Committees exceeding the authority granted in Section 4 of their Charge (e.g., an Advisory Committee attempting to sign contracts) will be subject to immediate review, and members may be removed pursuant to **Bylaws Section 3.6**.
