### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a standardized format and mandatory approval workflow for all official Press Releases issued by Study GRC Inc. This ensures that all external communications align with the Organization’s mission as defined in **Bylaws Article 1.3**, protect the Organization’s brand reputation, maintain legal compliance regarding 501(c)(3) restrictions, and safeguard the privacy of community participants, particularly minors.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all Directors, Officers, Committee Members, and volunteers drafting or issuing formal statements to media outlets, news wires, or public archives on behalf of Study GRC Inc.

*   **In Scope:** Official press releases, media advisories, and formal organizational statements.
*   **Out of Scope:** Routine social media posts (governed by *Social Media Policy*), internal newsletters, and informal community discord announcements.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Boilerplate** | Standardized text used at the bottom of a press release to describe the Organization. |
| **Embargo** | A request to news organizations to hold a story until a specific date and time. |
| **For Immediate Release** | A status indicating the information can be published immediately upon receipt. |
| **Media Contact** | The designated individual authorized to respond to follow-up inquiries (usually the DKG or Executive Director). |
| **Fundamental Action** | Significant events (mergers, dissolution, asset sales) as defined in **Bylaws Article X**. |

### 4. Approval Procedures and Standards

#### 4.1 Mandatory Approval Workflow
No press release may be distributed until the following approval chain is completed and documented (via email or project management ticket):

1.  **Drafting:** The Author creates the draft using the template in Section 4.4.
2.  **Brand & Fact Check:** The **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** reviews for tone, brand alignment, and accuracy.
3.  **Legal/Privacy Review (Conditional):**
    *   If the release quotes a minor, a **Parental Consent Form** must be verified on file.
    *   If the release involves a partnership or contract, the **Board Secretary** or Legal Counsel must review.
    *   If the release involves a **Fundamental Action** (Bylaws Art. X), the **Board of Directors** must approve.
4.  **Final Authorization:** The **Executive Director (CEO)** provides final sign-off for distribution.

#### 4.2 Content Standards
*   **Political Neutrality:** As a 501(c)(3), press releases must **never** endorse or oppose political candidates.
*   **Youth Protection:** In accordance with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, full names of minors (under 18) shall not be used unless a specific media release waiver is signed by a parent/guardian. Use first name and last initial only (e.g., "Alex S.").
*   **AP Style:** Drafts must generally adhere to Associated Press (AP) Stylebook guidelines for datelines, capitalization, and punctuation.

#### 4.3 Boilerplate Text (Mandatory)
The following text must appear at the bottom of every release:

> **About Study GRC Inc.**
> Study GRC Inc. is a Texas-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to fostering a community-driven ecosystem for Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) knowledge. We remove financial barriers by providing zero-cost, open-source resources and empower learners by simplifying the GRC domain. For more information, visit [Website URL].

#### 4.4 Press Release Template
All drafts must utilize the following structure:

***

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**
**[OR]**
**EMBARGOED UNTIL [DATE] AT [TIME] [TIME ZONE]**

**[HEADLINE: Be Direct, Active, and Engaging (Max 100 Characters)]**

**[Sub-headline: Optional sentence expanding on the news (Italics)]**

**[CITY], Texas** – **[Date]** – Study GRC Inc., a nonprofit dedicated to democratizing GRC knowledge, today announced [The Core News Hook: What happened? Why does it matter?].

"[Quote from Executive Director or Subject Matter Expert. The quote should express opinion, vision, or emotion, not just facts]," said [Name], [Title] of Study GRC Inc. "[Second sentence of quote reinforcing the mission]."

**Key Highlights:**
*   **[Point 1]:** [Detail]
*   **[Point 2]:** [Detail]
*   **[Point 3]:** [Detail]

[Body Paragraph: Provide context, background, or statistics. If referencing a partnership, describe the partner organization briefly.]

"[Quote from Partner or Beneficiary - Optional]," added [Name], [Title] of [Partner Organization].

For more information on this initiative or to access open-source GRC resources, please visit [Specific URL].

**About Study GRC Inc.**
[Insert Mandatory Boilerplate from Section 4.3]

**Media Contact:**
[Name of Contact]
[Title]
[Email Address]
[Phone Number - Optional]

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Author** | Drafts the release using the approved template and gathers necessary facts/quotes. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Reviews for brand voice, inclusivity, and adherence to the *Code of Ethics*. Acts as primary Media Contact unless otherwise designated. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Provides final authorization for distribution. Ensures alignment with the Strategic Plan. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves releases related to crisis management, executive leadership changes, or Fundamental Actions. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this standard, particularly regarding the unauthorized release of sensitive information or the release of a minor's identity without consent, may result in disciplinary action. This may include termination of volunteer status or employment, and in severe cases involving data privacy violations, legal action.
