### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to provide standardized, legally compliant templates for acknowledging charitable contributions made to Study GRC Inc. These templates ensure compliance with **Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 170(f)(8)** regarding "Substantiation of Charitable Contributions."

Proper acknowledgment is required for donors to claim a tax deduction for any single contribution of $250 or more. Furthermore, these templates serve to foster donor relationships by expressing gratitude on behalf of the Organization.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all incoming donations, including:
*   **Cash Contributions:** Checks, credit card payments, wire transfers, and cryptocurrency.
*   **In-Kind Contributions:** Physical goods, software licenses, or equipment.
*   **Quid Pro Quo Contributions:** Payments made partly as a contribution and partly for goods or services provided to the donor.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Volunteer Services:** Per IRS regulations, the value of volunteer time is **not** tax-deductible and should not be acknowledged using these financial templates. (Refer to *HR-POL-065 Volunteer Recognition Program*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **CWA** | Contemporaneous Written Acknowledgment. A written statement from the charity to the donor that substantiates the gift. |
| **Quid Pro Quo** | A payment made to a charity where the donor receives something of value in return (e.g., a ticket to a fundraising dinner). Only the amount *exceeding* the value of the benefit is deductible. |
| **Good Faith Estimate** | An honest estimate of the fair market value of goods or services provided to a donor in exchange for a contribution. |
| **In-Kind** | Non-cash contributions of tangible or intangible property. |

### 4. Template Guidelines & Requirements

To ensure legal validity under IRS Pub 1771, all letters generated using these templates **must** include:
1.  **Organization Name:** Study GRC Inc.
2.  **Amount of Cash Contribution:** Clearly stated.
3.  **Description of Non-Cash Contribution:** A description of the property, but **never** a valuation of the property (for In-Kind gifts).
4.  **No Goods or Services Statement:** A statement that no goods or services were provided in return for the contribution, *OR* a description and good faith estimate of the value of goods or services, if any, that were provided.

**Timing:** Acknowledgments must be provided to the donor before the donor files their tax return for the year of the contribution (typically by January 31 of the following year).

### 5. Templates

#### 5.1 Template A: Standard Cash/Monetary Donation
*Use this for direct donations where the donor received nothing in return.*

**[Organization Letterhead]**

**Date:** [Date of Letter]

**Donor Name:** [Donor Full Name]
**Address:** [Donor Address]

**RE: Acknowledgment of Charitable Contribution**

Dear [Donor Name],

On behalf of the Board of Directors and the community at **Study GRC Inc.**, thank you for your generous donation of **$[Amount]** received on **[Date of Gift]**.

Study GRC Inc. is a Texas non-profit corporation and a tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. Your contribution is tax-deductible to the fullest extent allowed by law.

**Legal Substantiation:**
In compliance with IRS requirements, we certify that **no goods or services were provided to you** by Study GRC Inc. in exchange for this contribution. Therefore, the full amount of your gift is tax-deductible.

Your support directly advances our mission to foster a community-driven ecosystem for GRC knowledge and remove financial barriers through open-source resources.

With sincere gratitude,

*[Signature]*

**[Name of Signatory]**
[Title, e.g., Director of Kindness and Generosity]
Study GRC Inc.
EIN: [Insert EIN]

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Responsible for generating and sending acknowledgment letters within 30 days of donation receipt. Maintains the donor database. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Responsible for verifying that "Quid Pro Quo" valuations (Good Faith Estimates) are accurate and defensible during audits. |
| **Board of Directors** | Responsible for ensuring the Organization maintains its 501(c)(3) status, without which these letters are invalid. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to provide a timely and accurate CWA may result in the disallowance of the donor’s tax deduction and potential penalties for the Organization under IRC § 6714 (regarding Quid Pro Quo disclosure failures).

Any staff member found falsifying acknowledgment letters (e.g., backdating letters or inflating in-kind descriptions) will be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal action, in accordance with the **Code of Ethics and Conduct**.
