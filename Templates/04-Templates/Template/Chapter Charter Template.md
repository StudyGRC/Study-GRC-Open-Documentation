### 1. Purpose
This **Chapter Charter** ("Charter") serves as the formal binding agreement establishing a subordinate unit ("Chapter") of **Study GRC Inc.** ("Organization"). It defines the scope of authority, operational requirements, and legal relationship between the Organization and the Chapter.

The purpose of this Charter is to grant the Chapter the right to use the Organization’s name, brand, and resources to advance the charitable and educational mission defined in **Article I, Section 1.3** of the Bylaws, while ensuring strict adherence to legal, financial, and ethical standards.

### 2. Scope
This Charter applies to the specific Local Chapter identified in Section 4.1. It governs all officers, volunteers, and participants acting on behalf of the Chapter.

**Exclusions:** This Charter does not grant the Chapter separate corporate legal status, independent 501(c)(3) status, or the authority to incur debt or sign contracts binding the Organization without express written authorization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Parent Organization** | Study GRC Inc., the Texas Non-Profit Corporation and 501(c)(3) entity. |
| **Chapter** | An unincorporated division or programmatic arm of the Parent Organization, authorized to operate in a specific geographic or virtual locality. |
| **Chapter Leadership** | Individuals appointed or elected to manage the day-to-day operations of the Chapter (e.g., Chapter Chair). |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a Chapter that is fully compliant with all reporting, financial, and policy requirements of the Parent Organization. |
| **Intellectual Property (IP)** | Trademarks, logos, curriculum, and materials owned by Study GRC Inc. as defined in **Bylaws Article IX**. |

### 4. Charter Provisions

#### 4.1 Establishment and Identity
*   **Name:** The Chapter shall be known as **"Study GRC - [City/Region Name]"**.
*   **Territory:** The Chapter is authorized to operate primarily within [Geographic Region/City].
*   **Effective Date:** This Charter is effective as of [Date] and remains in effect until revoked or surrendered.

#### 4.2 Mission Alignment
The Chapter must operate exclusively for the charitable and educational purposes set forth in **Article I, Section 1.3** of the Study GRC Inc. Bylaws:
1.  Fostering a community-driven GRC ecosystem;
2.  Removing financial barriers via zero-cost resources; and
3.  Empowering learners.

The Chapter is prohibited from engaging in any political campaign activity or substantial lobbying, in accordance with IRS 501(c)(3) regulations.

#### 4.3 Legal Status and Authority
*   **No Separate Entity:** The Chapter is **not** a separate legal entity. It is a component of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Contracting Authority:** Chapter Leadership **does not** have the authority to sign contracts, leases, or legal agreements. All contracts must be submitted to the Parent Organization’s Executive Director for review and execution in accordance with the **Signature Authority Policy**.
*   **Liability:** The Chapter must operate in a manner that minimizes liability. All activities must comply with the **Master EHS Policy** and **Event Safety Planning Checklist**.

#### 4.4 Financial Management
*   **Bank Accounts:** The Chapter is prohibited from opening independent bank accounts. All financial transactions must be processed through the Parent Organization or a sub-account authorized and monitored by the Chief Financial Officer (CFO).
*   **Fundraising:** All fundraising activities must be pre-approved by the Parent Organization to ensure compliance with state charitable solicitation laws and **IRS Form 990** reporting requirements.
*   **Dues:** The Chapter may not levy mandatory membership dues separate from the Parent Organization’s **Membership Dues Structure**.

#### 4.5 Governance and Leadership
*   **Structure:** The Chapter shall be managed by a Leadership Committee consisting of, at minimum:
    *   **Chapter Chair:** Primary liaison to HQ.
    *   **Chapter Vice-Chair:** Supports operations and succession.
*   **Selection:** Chapter Leaders are appointed by the Parent Organization’s VP of Education & Training (or designee) for a term of one (1) year, renewable upon performance review.
*   **Removal:** The Parent Organization reserves the right to remove any Chapter Leader at any time, with or without cause, to protect the interests of the Organization.

#### 4.6 Intellectual Property and Branding
*   **License:** The Parent Organization grants the Chapter a revocable, non-exclusive license to use the "Study GRC" name and logo solely for official Chapter activities.
*   **Compliance:** All usage must strictly adhere to the **Brand Identity and Style Guide**.
*   **Ownership:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all content, curriculum, or resources created by the Chapter or its volunteers are "works made for hire" and remain the sole property of Study GRC Inc.

#### 4.7 Youth Protection and Safeguarding
Given the Organization’s focus on K-12 education:
*   **Mandatory Compliance:** The Chapter must strictly adhere to the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**.
*   **Screening:** All Chapter volunteers with access to minors must undergo background checks per the **Background Check Policy**.
*   **Two-Deep Leadership:** No adult may ever be alone with a minor during Chapter activities.

#### 4.8 Reporting Requirements
The Chapter must submit the following to the Parent Organization:
1.  **Activity Reports:** Quarterly summaries of events and attendance.
2.  **Financial Reports:** Monthly reconciliation of any petty cash or approved expenses.
3.  **Incident Reports:** Immediate reporting of any safety, legal, or ethical violations.

#### 4.9 Revocation and Dissolution
*   **Revocation:** The Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. may revoke this Charter at any time for failure to comply with policies, inactivity, or conduct detrimental to the Organization.
*   **Surrender:** The Chapter may voluntarily surrender this Charter upon written notice to the Parent Organization.
*   **Asset Return:** Upon revocation or dissolution, all physical assets, funds, data, and records held by the Chapter must be immediately returned to Study GRC Inc.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors (HQ)** | Final authority on Charter approval and revocation. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Operational oversight of the Chapter; appoints Chapter Leadership. |
| **Chapter Chair** | Ensures local compliance with this Charter; serves as the primary point of contact. |
| **Chapter Leadership Team** | Executes local programming in alignment with the Organization's mission. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with the provisions of this Charter may result in:
1.  Immediate suspension of Chapter operations.
2.  Revocation of the Charter and the right to use the Study GRC name.
3.  Removal of Chapter Leadership.
4.  Legal action for trademark infringement or recovery of assets.
