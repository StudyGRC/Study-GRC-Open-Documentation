### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a standardized format for reporting the results of internal audits, risk assessments, and compliance reviews within Study GRC Inc. This template ensures that audit findings are communicated clearly, objectively, and consistently to the Audit & Risk Committee and Operational Leadership. It is designed to satisfy the reporting requirements of **2 CFR 200** (Uniform Guidance) and the Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA) International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing.

### 2. Scope
This template must be used for all formal internal audit engagements conducted by Study GRC Inc. staff, volunteers acting in an audit capacity, or external vendors co-sourced for internal audit functions.

**Applies to:**
*   Financial Audits
*   Operational/Process Audits
*   IT and Cybersecurity Audits
*   Compliance Reviews (e.g., Youth Protection, GDPR, Grant Compliance)

**Exclusions:**
*   Informal consulting engagements or ad-hoc advisory memos (unless requested by the Board).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Criteria** | The standards, policies, or regulations used as the benchmark for evaluation (e.g., "What should be"). |
| **Condition** | The factual evidence found during the audit (e.g., "What is"). |
| **Cause** | The underlying reason for the difference between the Criteria and the Condition. |
| **Effect** | The risk or impact resulting from the condition (e.g., financial loss, reputational damage). |
| **Management Action Plan (MAP)** | The specific steps management agrees to take to remediate the finding, including owners and due dates. |

#### 4.5 Appendix A: Risk Rating Definitions

| Rating | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **High** | Critical control weakness that exposes the organization to significant financial loss, regulatory non-compliance (e.g., loss of 501(c)(3) status), or severe reputational damage. Requires immediate Board attention. |
| **Medium** | Control weakness that could lead to financial loss or operational inefficiency. Requires corrective action within a defined timeframe (e.g., 90 days). |
| **Low** | Minor procedural deviation or opportunity for process improvement. Low risk of material impact. |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Internal Auditor** | Responsible for drafting the report, ensuring factual accuracy of the "Condition," and validating that the "Criteria" is current. |
| **Auditee (Management)** | Responsible for reviewing draft findings for factual accuracy and providing the "Management Response" (Action Plan, Owner, Date) within 5 business days of receipt. |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Responsible for reviewing the final report, challenging management on risk acceptance, and monitoring the completion of Action Plans pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI**. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to utilize this template for formal audit reporting may result in the rejection of the report by the Audit & Risk Committee. Falsification of audit findings, omission of material facts, or misrepresentation of management responses constitutes a violation of the **Board of Directors Code of Ethics and Conduct** and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination or removal from the Board.
