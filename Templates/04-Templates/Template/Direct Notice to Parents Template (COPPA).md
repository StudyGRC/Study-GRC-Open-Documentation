### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to provide a standardized, legally compliant template for notifying parents or legal guardians when **Study GRC Inc.** intends to collect personal information from a child under the age of 13. This template ensures compliance with the **Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) § 312.4**, which mandates "Direct Notice" to parents prior to data collection.

### 2. Scope
This template applies to all Study GRC Inc. personnel, automated systems, and third-party vendors involved in the registration or onboarding of users.
*   **In Scope:** Any registration process where the user indicates they are under 13 years of age.
*   **Exclusions:** General website visitors who do not attempt to create an account or provide personal information.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **COPPA** | Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, a US federal law protecting the privacy of children under 13. |
| **Direct Notice** | A specific notification sent directly to a parent (usually via email) containing required disclosures about data practices. |
| **Personal Information (PI)** | Individually identifiable information including first and last name, email address, physical address, telephone number, or persistent identifiers (cookies/IP addresses). |
| **Verifiable Parental Consent** | Any reasonable effort (taking into consideration available technology) to ensure that a parent of a child receives notice of the operator's personal information collection, use, and disclosure practices, and authorizes the collection. |

**Subject:** ACTION REQUIRED: Permission for your child to join Study GRC

**Dear Parent or Guardian,**

Your child, **[Child's First Name or Username]**, has attempted to register for an account with **Study GRC Inc.**, a non-profit organization dedicated to open-source education in governance, risk, and compliance.

Because your child indicated they are under the age of 13, federal law (the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act, or COPPA) requires us to obtain your verifiable permission before we can activate their account or collect any personal information from them.

**We will not collect, use, or disclose any personal information from your child if you do not provide this consent.**

### 1. What Information We Collect
If you grant permission, Study GRC Inc. intends to collect the following limited information to provide educational services:
*   **Account Information:** Username, password, and your email address (for account recovery and parental notification).
*   **Educational Data:** Course progress, quiz scores, and completion certificates.
*   **Technical Data:** Persistent identifiers (cookies) used solely to maintain their login session and track course progress.

### 2. How We Use This Information
*   **Internal Use Only:** We use this information to track your child's learning progress and issue badges or certificates.
*   **No Third-Party Advertising:** Study GRC Inc. is a non-profit. We **do not** sell your child's data, nor do we use it for behavioral advertising.
*   **Public Visibility:** By default, your child's profile is **private**. They cannot share personal information with the public unless you specifically configure their account to allow it.

### 3. Your Rights
*   You may review the personal information we have collected from your child.
*   You may refuse to permit further collection or use of the child's information.
*   You may request the deletion of your child's personal information.

For full details, please review our **Children’s Privacy Policy** here: [Insert Link to Policy YTH-POL-002].

### 4. How to Provide Consent
To grant permission for your child to use Study GRC resources, please complete **one** of the following steps:

*   **Option A (Digital Form):** Click the link below to sign a digital consent form using a credit card or government ID for identity verification (no charge will be made):
    *   [Link to Secure Verification Portal]
*   **Option B (Signed Form):** Print, sign, and scan the attached "Parental Consent Form" and email it to privacy@studygrc.org.

### 5. If You Do Not Consent

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to utilize this approved template when onboarding users under 13 constitutes a violation of **Bylaws Article IX (Data Protection)** and federal law.
*   **Internal:** Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
*   **External:** Failure to provide Direct Notice can result in FTC fines of up to $50,120 per violation.
