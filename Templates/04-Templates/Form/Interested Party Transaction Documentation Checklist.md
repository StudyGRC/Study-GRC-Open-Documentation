### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this checklist is to establish a **Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness** under Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 4958 and to ensure compliance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.230**. This document serves as the primary audit defense tool to substantiate that any transaction involving an Interested Party was fair, reasonable, and in the best interest of Study GRC Inc.

This form must be completed **contemporaneously** with the approval of the transaction and attached to the meeting minutes.

### 2. Scope
This checklist applies to **all** proposed financial transactions, arrangements, or compensation agreements between Study GRC Inc. and an **Interested Party**.

**Exclusions:**
*   De minimis transactions (value under $100).
*   Transactions available to the general public or membership on identical terms.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Interested Party** | Any Director, Officer, or member of a committee with Board-delegated powers, who has a direct or indirect financial interest. |
| **Financial Interest** | An ownership or investment interest in, or a compensation arrangement with, any entity or individual with which the Organization is negotiating a transaction. |
| **Comparability Data** | Appropriate data used to determine fair market value (e.g., independent appraisals, written offers from unrelated parties, salary surveys). |
| **Rebuttable Presumption** | A legal standard where the IRS presumes a transaction is reasonable if the organization follows specific steps (approval by authorized body, reliance on comparability data, and contemporaneous documentation). |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Interested Party** | Must fully disclose the interest and leave the meeting during discussion and voting. |
| **Secretary** | Responsible for completing this checklist, ensuring it is signed, and filing it with the official meeting minutes. |
| **Board Chair** | Ensures the Interested Party leaves the room and facilitates the discussion among disinterested members. |
| **Audit Committee** | Reviews this checklist annually to ensure consistency in applying the Conflict of Interest Policy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

Failure to properly document an Interested Party Transaction may result in:
1.  **Automatic Voiding:** The transaction may be voidable under Texas Law.
2.  **IRS Intermediate Sanctions:** The Interested Party may be subject to an excise tax of **25%** of the excess benefit (rising to **200%** if not corrected).
3.  **Organization Penalties:** Organization managers (Directors) who knowingly approved an excess benefit transaction may be personally liable for a **10%** excise tax.
4.  **Grant Non-Compliance:** Violation of **2 CFR 200.318(c)(1)** may result in the suspension or termination of federal grant funding.
