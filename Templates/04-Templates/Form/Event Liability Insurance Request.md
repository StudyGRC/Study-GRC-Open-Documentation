### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to standardize the process for requesting **Certificates of Insurance (COI)** or **Special Event Liability Riders** for Study GRC Inc. activities. This ensures that all organizational events—whether physical or hybrid—are adequately covered under the Organization’s General Liability policies, protecting the Organization’s assets and satisfying the indemnification requirements outlined in **Bylaws Article VII**.

### 2. Scope
This request form applies to:
*   **All Departments and Local Chapters** planning physical gatherings, workshops, conferences, or meetups.
*   **Virtual Events** exceeding 500 participants (requiring Cyber/Media Liability review).
*   **Third-Party Venues** requiring proof of insurance.

**Exclusions:**
*   Standard internal administrative meetings held virtually.
*   Informal gatherings not sponsored or branded by Study GRC Inc.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Certificate of Insurance (COI)** | A document issued by an insurance company/broker that verifies the existence of an insurance policy and summarizes the coverage conditions. |
| **Additional Insured** | A status requested by a venue (landlord) requiring them to be protected under Study GRC’s policy for claims arising out of Study GRC’s operations at that location. |
| **General Liability (GL)** | Insurance coverage that protects against claims of bodily injury or property damage. |
| **Special Event Rider** | A short-term insurance policy purchased to cover a specific event that falls outside the scope of the standard annual GL policy (e.g., events with alcohol or high-risk physical activities). |

### 4. Request Protocol & Form Data
*Note: This section outlines the procedure for submission and the specific data fields required in the digital intake system (e.g., Jira, Google Forms, or PDF).*

#### 4.1 Submission Timeline
To ensure the insurance broker has sufficient time to process the request and issue a COI, this request must be submitted:
*   **Standard COI Request:** Minimum **14 days** prior to the event.
*   **Special Event Rider (High Risk/Alcohol):** Minimum **30 days** prior to the event.

#### 4.3 Approval Workflow
1.  **Submission:** Requester submits form.
2.  **Risk Review:** CFO or Treasurer reviews for coverage gaps (e.g., liquor liability exclusions).
3.  **Broker Processing:** Approved requests are sent to the Organization's Insurance Broker.
4.  **Issuance:** Broker issues COI to the Venue and Requester.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Organizer (Requester)** | Completes the request accurately; ensures venue contract requirements are understood; uploads necessary safety plans. |
| **CFO / Treasurer** | Reviews request against current policy limits; approves "Additional Insured" costs; authorizes purchase of Special Event Riders if necessary. |
| **Insurance Broker** | Generates the official Certificate of Insurance; advises on coverage gaps. |
| **VP of Education** | Verifies that events involving minors comply with Youth Protection policies before insurance is authorized. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to submit this request within the specified timeline may result in:
1.  **Event Cancellation:** The Board reserves the right to cancel any event that does not have valid proof of insurance.
2.  **Personal Liability:** Organizers proceeding without insurance authorization may be held personally liable for damages, as **Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification)** may not apply to acts taken in violation of organizational policy.
3.  **Venue Breach:** Failure to provide a COI is often a breach of contract with the venue, resulting in financial penalties.
