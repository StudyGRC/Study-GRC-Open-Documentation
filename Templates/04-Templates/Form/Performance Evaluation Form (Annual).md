### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this form is to document the annual performance review process for employees and Operational Officers of Study GRC Inc. This evaluation is designed to:
1.  Assess individual performance against the Job Description and established goals.
2.  Align individual contributions with the Organization's mission to foster a community-driven GRC ecosystem.
3.  Identify professional development needs and career growth opportunities.
4.  Provide a legally defensible record of performance to support compensation decisions (in compliance with 2 CFR 200.430) and employment actions.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:** All full-time and part-time employees, and Operational Officers appointed under **Bylaws Article V** (including the VP of Education, Director of Kindness and Generosity, CISO, and CFO).

**Exclusions:** Independent contractors (evaluated via contract deliverables), Volunteers (evaluated via Volunteer Feedback Form), and the Executive Director (evaluated by the Board of Directors using the Executive Director Evaluation Instrument).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **SMART Goals** | Objectives that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. |
| **Core Values** | The fundamental beliefs of Study GRC Inc., including Open Source Transparency, Inclusivity, and Community-First focus. |
| **Rating Scale** | The quantitative measurement used to score performance (See Section 4.1). |
| **PIP** | Performance Improvement Plan; a formal document used when performance falls below "Meets Expectations." |

### 4. Instructions and Rating Scale

**Instructions:**
1.  **Employee Self-Assessment:** The employee completes Sections I through VI and submits the form to their Manager two weeks prior to the review meeting.
2.  **Manager Assessment:** The Manager reviews the self-assessment, adds their ratings and comments, and drafts the goals for the coming year.
3.  **Review Meeting:** Both parties meet virtually to discuss the evaluation.
4.  **Finalization:** Signatures are obtained via the Organization’s electronic signature platform.

**4.1 Rating Scale**

| Rating | Label | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4** | **Exceeds Expectations** | Consistently delivers results that far surpass the requirements of the role. Demonstrates exceptional initiative and leadership. |
| **3** | **Meets Expectations** | Consistently meets the requirements of the role. Work is high-quality and reliable. A valued contributor. |
| **2** | **Needs Improvement** | Meets some requirements but misses others. Performance is inconsistent. A Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) may be required. |
| **1** | **Unacceptable** | Consistently fails to meet requirements. Immediate improvement is required to maintain employment. |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Complete self-assessment honestly; participate actively in the review meeting; execute the Development Plan. |
| **Manager** | Provide objective, evidence-based feedback; draft the evaluation; ensure goals are aligned with Strategic Plan; follow up on Development Plan. |
| **Human Resources** | Maintain the *Performance Management Policy*; archive completed forms in the Personnel File; audit ratings for consistency and bias. |
| **Executive Director** | Review and approve evaluations for all direct reports (Operational Officers); ensure budget allocation for approved Development Plans. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**Confidentiality:** This document contains sensitive personnel information and must be handled in strict accordance with the *Employee Personnel File Management Policy* and *Data Privacy Policy*. Access is restricted to the Employee, Manager, HR, and Executive Director.

**At-Will Employment:** Nothing in this performance evaluation alters the "At-Will" employment relationship between the Employee and Study GRC Inc. This form is not a contract of employment for a definite period.

**Failure to Participate:** Failure by a Manager to complete evaluations on time may result in disciplinary action. Failure by an employee to participate in the process may impact eligibility for merit increases or promotion.
