### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this form is to facilitate the assessment of workstation setups for employees and volunteers of Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). This process is designed to:
1.  Mitigate the risk of Musculoskeletal Disorders (MSDs) and Repetitive Strain Injuries (RSIs).
2.  Ensure compliance with the **Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA) General Duty Clause**.
3.  Serve as the initial intake mechanism for equipment-related requests associated with **Reasonable Accommodations** under the **Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)**.
4.  Support the Organization's "Remote-First" operational model by ensuring home office environments meet minimum safety standards.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, employees, and long-term volunteers who perform more than 10 hours of computer-based work per week on behalf of the Organization.

**Exclusions:** Short-term contractors or vendors who provide their own equipment and facilities.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Ergonomics** | The applied science of equipment design intended to maximize productivity by reducing operator fatigue and discomfort. |
| **MSD** | Musculoskeletal Disorder; injuries or disorders of the muscles, nerves, tendons, joints, cartilage, and spinal discs (e.g., Carpal Tunnel Syndrome). |
| **Remote Worksite** | The designated workspace within the individual's home or off-site location as defined in the *Remote Work Agreement*. |
| **Reasonable Accommodation** | A modification or adjustment to a job, the work environment, or the way things are usually done that enables a qualified individual with a disability to enjoy an equal employment opportunity. |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requester** | Complete the form truthfully; attempt self-correction using provided guidelines prior to submission; provide photos/video access for assessment. |
| **Supervisor** | Review the request for operational impact; validate that the employee is active and in good standing. **Note:** Supervisors do *not* determine medical necessity. |
| **Safety Officer / HR** | Review the ergonomic assessment; determine if equipment purchase is necessary; coordinate with medical providers if an ADA claim is involved; authorize procurement. |
| **Finance Dept** | Process approved procurement requests in accordance with the *Procurement Policy*. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**Evaluation Process:**
Upon receipt of this form, the Safety Officer or HR representative will review the self-assessment. The Organization reserves the right to require a virtual consultation before authorizing the purchase of equipment.

**Equipment Ownership:**
Any equipment purchased by Study GRC Inc. as a result of this evaluation remains the property of the Organization (refer to *Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3* regarding Organization Ownership). Upon termination of employment or volunteer service, equipment must be returned unless otherwise agreed in writing.

**False Claims:**
Falsifying information on this form to obtain equipment for personal use or to fraudulently claim a workplace injury may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination and legal action.
