### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this form is to document the details of any suspected abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a minor or vulnerable adult participating in Study GRC Inc. programs or events. This document serves as an internal record of the incident and confirms that the mandatory reporting obligations under Texas Family Code Chapter 261 have been fulfilled.

**IMPORTANT:** Completion of this form **does not** replace the legal obligation to report suspected abuse to the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) or law enforcement.

### 2. Scope
This form must be utilized by all Study GRC Inc. employees, Board Directors, volunteers, and contractors ("Personnel") who:
1.  Witness suspected abuse or neglect.
2.  Receive a disclosure of abuse or neglect from a victim or third party.
3.  Have cause to believe that a minor's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect.

This scope includes incidents occurring during in-person events, remote/virtual sessions, or incidents occurring outside of Study GRC Inc. activities that are disclosed during Organization operations.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Abuse** | Mental, emotional, physical, or sexual injury to a child or failure to prevent such injury; includes sexual conduct, controlled substance use, or trafficking. |
| **Neglect** | The failure to provide a child with food, clothing, shelter, or medical care, or leaving a child in a situation where the child is at risk of harm. |
| **Mandated Reporter** | Under Texas law, *any person* who has cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect. |
| **DFPS** | Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (Child Protective Services). |
| **Reporter** | The individual completing this form and making the report to authorities. |

### 4. Instructions and Procedures

**4.1 Immediate Action**
If a child is in **imminent danger**, call **911** immediately.

**4.2 Mandatory Reporting Requirement**
Pursuant to Texas Family Code § 261.101, you must make a report to the Texas Abuse Hotline (**1-800-252-5400** or **txabusehotline.org**) within 48 hours (sooner is recommended).

**4.3 Form Completion**
*   Complete this form **after** ensuring the immediate safety of the child and contacting authorities.
*   Be objective. Record facts, observations, and exact quotes. Do not speculate or investigate.
*   **Do not** attempt to interview the child beyond what is necessary to establish safety.
*   **Do not** notify the alleged perpetrator.

**4.4 Submission**
Submit this completed form immediately via secure, encrypted email to the **Youth Protection Officer** or the **Executive Director**. If the Executive Director is implicated, submit directly to the **Board President**.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reporter (Staff/Volunteer)** | Complete form accurately; make external report to DFPS/Police; maintain strict confidentiality. |
| **Youth Protection Officer** | Receive form; secure record in restricted file; coordinate with Legal Counsel; ensure accused is removed from contact with minors pending investigation. |
| **Executive Director** | Oversee internal response; manage communications (if necessary) under advice of counsel. |
| **Board of Directors** | Review anonymized incident reports for policy gaps; ensure indemnification procedures are followed per Bylaws Article VII. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to report suspected abuse is a **Class A Misdemeanor** under Texas Family Code § 261.109 (or a State Jail Felony if the intent is to conceal).

Internally, failure by any Study GRC Inc. Personnel to report suspected abuse or to complete this documentation will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, revocation of membership, and permanent ban from the Organization.
