### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a standardized mechanism for the collection, review, and management of medical information, specifically allergies and chronic conditions, for participants in Study GRC Inc. programs. This form ensures the Organization can:
1.  Provide **Reasonable Accommodations** in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.
2.  Mitigate health risks during in-person events and regarding physical materials sent to remote participants (e.g., swag boxes, science kits).
3.  Equip emergency responders with critical information in the event of a medical crisis.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All participants, volunteers, staff, and contractors attending in-person events hosted by Study GRC Inc., or recipients of physical goods/kits mailed by the Organization.
*   **Minors:** For participants under the age of 18, this form must be completed by a parent or legal guardian.
*   **Exclusions:** This form does not apply to purely digital interactions where no physical goods are exchanged and no physical attendance is required.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anaphylaxis** | A severe, potentially life-threatening allergic reaction requiring immediate medical attention (e.g., administration of epinephrine). |
| **Reasonable Accommodation** | Modifications or adjustments to the program environment that enable a participant with a disability or medical condition to participate, without imposing an undue hardship on the Organization. |
| **Sensitive PII** | Personally Identifiable Information that, if lost, compromised, or disclosed without authorization, could result in substantial harm, embarrassment, inconvenience, or unfairness to an individual. Medical data is classified as Sensitive PII. |
| **Trigger** | A specific substance, activity, or environment that precipitates a medical episode or allergic reaction. |

### 4. Policy Statements and Form Data Elements

#### 4.1 Data Privacy and Retention
Information collected via this form is strictly confidential. It will be shared only on a **Need-to-Know** basis with:
*   The Event Safety Officer / Medical Lead.
*   Emergency Medical Services (EMS) personnel in the event of a crisis.
*   Catering vendors (anonymized where possible) for dietary safety.
*   **Retention:** This form is retained for the duration of the event/program plus three (3) years for liability purposes, after which it is securely destroyed in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

#### 4.2 Duty to Disclose
While disclosure is voluntary, Study GRC Inc. cannot be held responsible for accommodating medical conditions or allergies that are not disclosed in writing at least fourteen (14) days prior to an event or shipment date.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Participant / Guardian** | Responsible for providing accurate, up-to-date medical information and carrying necessary emergency medications (e.g., EpiPens, Inhalers). |
| **Event Lead** | Responsible for reviewing disclosures 14 days prior to the event and coordinating with vendors (catering/facilities) to implement accommodations. |
| **Safety Officer** | Responsible for maintaining physical or secure digital copies of these forms on-site during the event for immediate access during emergencies. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Responsible for ensuring the intake process is inclusive and that accommodations are delivered with dignity and privacy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to disclose critical medical information may result in the Organization's inability to provide safe accommodations or emergency response.
*   **Staff/Volunteers:** Unauthorized disclosure of information contained in these forms is a violation of the *Data Privacy Policy* and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Falsification:** Deliberate falsification of medical needs to secure preferential treatment (e.g., claiming a medical need for a specific seat without basis) constitutes a violation of the *Code of Conduct*.
