### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the formal application process and qualification criteria for groups seeking to establish an official Local Chapter of Study GRC Inc. This form serves as the primary due diligence instrument to ensure that all prospective chapters align with the Organization’s mission, governance standards, and legal requirements prior to the granting of a Charter.

### 2. Scope
This application applies to any group of individuals, regardless of geographic location, seeking to use the "Study GRC" name, brand, or resources to operate a local community group.

*   **In Scope:** New chapter formation, reactivation of dissolved chapters.
*   **Out of Scope:** Informal study groups not seeking official branding, funding, or organizational insurance coverage.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Charter** | The official legal instrument granting a Local Chapter the right to operate under the Study GRC Inc. umbrella. |
| **Provisional Status** | A six-month probationary period for new chapters to demonstrate viability and compliance before receiving full Charter status. |
| **Chapter Officers** | The designated leadership team (President, Secretary, Treasurer) responsible for the local entity, mirroring Bylaws Article IV. |
| **Catchment Area** | The defined geographic region (e.g., "Greater Austin Metro") the chapter intends to serve. |

### 4. Expansion Criteria (Prerequisites)
*Before submitting this application, the proposed leadership team must verify compliance with the following criteria:*

4.1 **Membership Requirement:** A minimum of three (3) founding officers must be **Supporting Members** in good standing as defined in **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1**.
4.2 **Mission Alignment:** The proposed chapter must agree to strictly adhere to **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, specifically the mandate to provide "zero-cost, open-source resources." Chapters are prohibited from charging mandatory membership fees for access to core educational content.
4.3 **Leadership Structure:** The chapter must identify a President, Secretary, and Treasurer. Per **Bylaws Article IV, Section 4.1**, the President and Secretary cannot be the same person.
4.4 **Youth Protection:** If the Chapter intends to host events for K-12 participants, all officers must pass a background check in accordance with **HR-POL-024 (Background Check Policy)** prior to approval.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Applicant Team** | Completes the application truthfully and ensures all officers meet membership requirements. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Reviews the application for strategic alignment and viability. Recommends approval/denial. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Vets the community reputation of the proposed leaders. |
| **Board of Directors** | Grants final approval for the Charter and authorizes the use of the corporate brand. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Submission of false information on this application will result in immediate denial and potential revocation of the applicants' individual memberships in Study GRC Inc.

Operating a group using the "Study GRC" name without an approved Charter constitutes trademark infringement and will result in legal action.
