### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this form is to document the justification for procuring goods or services without full and open competition. Under **2 CFR 200.320(c)**, noncompetitive procurement (sole source) may only be used under specific, limited circumstances. This form serves as the primary audit evidence to demonstrate compliance with federal Uniform Guidance and Study GRC Inc.'s procurement standards. Failure to adequately justify a noncompetitive purchase may result in **disallowed costs** and the requirement to repay grant funds.

### 2. Scope
This form must be completed and approved **prior** to the commitment of funds for any procurement action exceeding the **Micro-Purchase Threshold ($10,000)** where:
1.  Competition is restricted to a single source; or
2.  Solicitation was attempted but deemed inadequate.

**Applies to:** All employees, Operational Officers, and Board members initiating purchases on behalf of Study GRC Inc.
**Exclusions:** Purchases under the Micro-Purchase Threshold ($10,000 or less), provided the price is reasonable.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Sole Source** | A procurement method where only one vendor possesses the unique ability or capability to meet the specific requirement of the solicitation. |
| **Public Exigency** | An emergency situation where the urgency for the requirement will not permit a delay incident to competitive solicitation (e.g., immediate threat to life, property, or data security). |
| **Micro-Purchase** | A purchase of supplies or services using simplified acquisition procedures, the aggregate amount of which does not exceed $10,000 (or current federal threshold). |
| **Price Reasonableness** | The determination that a price is fair and proper, considering market conditions, historical pricing, and cost analysis. |

#### Section II: Regulatory Justification (2 CFR 200.320(c))
*Select **ONE** category below that applies to this procurement. You must provide supporting evidence in Section III.*

**☐ A. Single Source Availability (2 CFR 200.320(c)(2))**
The item or service is available only from a single source. This usually implies a patent, copyright, proprietary technology, or unique expertise that no other vendor possesses.

**☐ B. Public Exigency or Emergency (2 CFR 200.320(c)(3))**
A public exigency or emergency for the requirement will not permit a delay resulting from competitive solicitation. (Note: Poor planning does not constitute an emergency).

**☐ C. Authorized by Federal Agency (2 CFR 200.320(c)(4))**
The Federal awarding agency or pass-through entity expressly authorized this noncompetitive procurement in response to a written request from Study GRC Inc. *(Must attach authorization letter/email).*

**☐ D. Inadequate Competition (2 CFR 200.320(c)(5))**
After solicitation of a number of sources, competition is determined inadequate (e.g., a competitive bid was issued, but only one responsive bid was received).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requestor** | Completes the form, conducts market research, and provides evidence of price reasonableness. |
| **Department Head** | Validates the technical necessity and confirms that the requirement cannot be met by other means. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews for compliance with 2 CFR 200.320, verifies budget availability, and ensures price analysis is documented. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Provides final approval for all noncompetitive procurements over $10,000. |
| **Board of Directors** | Must approve any noncompetitive procurement involving a "Related Party" or Conflict of Interest (Bylaws Art. IX). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Submission of false or misleading information on this form constitutes a violation of the **Board of Directors Code of Ethics** and the **Anti-Fraud Policy**.

Failure to comply with the justification requirements of **2 CFR 200.320** may result in:
1.  **Disciplinary Action:** Up to and including termination of employment.
2.  **Financial Liability:** The individual may be held personally liable for unauthorized commitments.
3.  **Grant Revocation:** Loss of federal funding or suspension/debarment of the Organization.
