### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to provide a standardized format for documenting formal disciplinary actions within Study GRC Inc. This form serves as an official record of performance deficiencies or behavioral misconduct, the corrective actions required, and the consequences of failure to improve. It ensures consistency, fairness, and legal defensibility in the application of the Organization's Progressive Discipline Policy.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All Operational Officers (as defined in Bylaws Article V), employees, independent contractors (subject to contract terms), and volunteers.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   **Board Directors:** Removal of Directors is governed strictly by **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6** (Removal of Directors) and does not utilize this operational HR form.
    *   **Voting Members:** Expulsion of members is governed by the *Member Expulsion Due Process Policy* and **Bylaws Article II**.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Progressive Discipline** | A disciplinary model where the severity of the penalty increases with each infringement or failure to improve performance. |
| **At-Will Employment** | The legal doctrine in Texas stating that employment may be terminated by the employer or employee at any time, for any reason (non-discriminatory), or for no reason at all. |
| **PIP** | Performance Improvement Plan; a formal document outlining specific goals required to regain good standing. |

### 4. Instructions for Use

This section outlines the requirements for Issuing Officers (Managers) when utilizing the template below.

#### 4.1 Preparation and Fact-Finding
Before issuing a written warning, the Manager must:
1.  Verify the facts of the incident or performance data.
2.  Review the *Employee Handbook* or *Volunteer Code of Conduct* to cite the specific policy violated.
3.  Check the individual's personnel file for prior Verbal Warnings or related disciplinary history.

#### 4.2 Drafting the Warning
*   **Specificity:** Avoid vague language (e.g., "bad attitude"). Use objective facts (e.g., "Employee interrupted the client three times during the call on [Date].").
*   **Consequences:** Clearly state that failure to improve will result in further action, up to termination.

#### 4.3 Delivery (Remote Context)
*   The warning must be delivered during a synchronous meeting (video conference).
*   If a physical signature cannot be obtained due to the Organization's remote-first nature, an electronic signature (e.g., DocuSign) or a reply-via-email stating "I acknowledge receipt of this document" is required.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Issuing Manager** | Drafts the warning with factual accuracy; conducts the disciplinary meeting; ensures the signed copy is filed. |
| **Recipient** | Acknowledges receipt of the warning; adheres to the corrective actions outlined. |
| **Executive Director / HR** | Reviews Written Warnings prior to issuance to ensure consistency and mitigate legal risk; maintains the document in the Personnel File. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
This form is a tool for compliance with the Organization's *Progressive Discipline Policy*.

*   **At-Will Disclaimer:** Nothing in this document alters the "At-Will" nature of employment at Study GRC Inc. The Organization reserves the right to terminate employment at any time, with or without prior notice or progressive discipline, for conduct deemed sufficiently severe.
*   **Record Retention:** This document will be retained in the individual's personnel file for a minimum of 5 years post-separation, in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.
