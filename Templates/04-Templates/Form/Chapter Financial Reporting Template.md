### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to standardize the financial data collection process for all Study GRC Inc. Local Chapters. As a 501(c)(3) organization potentially operating under a Group Exemption Letter, Study GRC Inc. is federally mandated to maintain centralized financial oversight of all subordinate units. This form ensures:
1.  Accurate consolidation of financial data for the Organization’s IRS Form 990.
2.  Compliance with IRS Revenue Procedure 80-27 regarding Group Exemptions.
3.  Monitoring of Unrelated Business Income (UBI) and lobbying expenditures.
4.  Transparency and fraud prevention within distributed chapter operations.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All chartered Local Chapters of Study GRC Inc., regardless of size, revenue, or geographic location.
*   **Users:** Chapter Treasurers (primary completers) and Chapter Presidents (approvers).
*   **Exclusions:** Informal meetups or study groups that do not hold a formal Charter or bank account.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Group Exemption** | An IRS ruling that recognizes a group of organizations as tax-exempt under a single determination letter held by the central organization. |
| **Restricted Funds** | Assets/Cash that are limited by a donor-imposed restriction (e.g., a grant specifically for a youth scholarship). |
| **UBIT** | Unrelated Business Income Tax. Income generated from a trade or business regularly carried on that is not substantially related to the charitable purpose (e.g., selling advertising). |
| **Fiscal Year** | The accounting period for the Organization, defined in **Bylaws Article VIII** as the Calendar Year (Jan 1 – Dec 31). |
| **Supporting Documentation** | Official bank statements, receipts, and invoices required to substantiate the figures reported in this form. |

### 4. Instructions and Requirements

#### 4.1 Reporting Frequency and Deadlines
Chapters must submit this report according to the following schedule:
*   **Quarterly Reports:** Due by the 15th of the month following the quarter end (April 15, July 15, Oct 15).
*   **Annual Consolidated Report:** Due by **January 31st** of the following year.

#### 4.2 Submission Protocol
1.  This form must be completed digitally. Handwritten forms are not accepted.
2.  **Bank Statements:** Copies of all bank account statements for the reporting period **must** be attached.
3.  **Zero Activity:** If a Chapter had no financial activity, this form must still be filed indicating "Zero Activity" to maintain active Charter status.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Conduct
*   Commingling Chapter funds with personal funds of any officer.
*   Reporting "Miscellaneous" expenses exceeding $100 without detailed itemization.
*   Withholding information regarding potential litigation or regulatory inquiries.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chapter Treasurer** | Maintains financial records, performs bank reconciliations, prepares this form, and attaches supporting documentation. |
| **Chapter President** | Reviews the financial report for accuracy and signs the certification. Ensures Board approval of expenditures. |
| **HQ Finance Dept** | Reviews submitted forms, consolidates data for the IRS Form 990, and audits for compliance with 501(c)(3) restrictions. |
| **HQ CFO** | Final authority on financial reporting standards. Issues corrective action plans for non-compliant chapters. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to submit accurate financial reports by the designated deadlines constitutes a material breach of the **Chapter Affiliation Agreement**. Consequences may include:
1.  Suspension of Chapter funding or revenue sharing.
2.  Removal of Chapter Officers.
3.  Revocation of the Chapter Charter.
4.  Legal action to recover misappropriated funds.
