### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the protocol and required format for substantiating business expenses incurred on behalf of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") when original itemized receipts are lost, destroyed, or unattainable.

This affidavit serves as a substitute for original documentation to satisfy Internal Revenue Service (IRS) substantiation requirements (IRS Publication 463) and Federal Uniform Guidance (2 CFR 200) regarding allowable costs. It ensures the Organization maintains financial integrity and compliance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors seeking reimbursement for out-of-pocket expenses or reconciling Corporate Credit Card transactions.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Lodging/Hotel Expenses:** This affidavit may **not** be used for lodging expenses. Duplicate folios must be obtained from the hotel/venue.
*   **Airfare:** This affidavit may **not** be used for airfare, as digital ticket receipts are always retrievable from the carrier.
*   **Expenses exceeding $75.00:** While this affidavit may be accepted for amounts over $75.00 at the discretion of the CFO, habitual loss of receipts for high-value items may result in denial of reimbursement.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Itemized Receipt** | A vendor-issued document showing the date, vendor name, specific items purchased, price per item, total amount, and proof of payment. A credit card signature slip is *not* an itemized receipt. |
| **Substantiation** | Evidence sufficient to establish that an expense was actually incurred and was business-related. |
| **Affidavit** | A written statement confirmed by oath or affirmation, for use as evidence. |
| **2 CFR 200** | Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards (Federal Grant Regulations). |

### 4. Policy Statements & Usage Requirements

#### 4.1 Last Resort Protocol
The Missing Receipt Affidavit is a mechanism of **last resort**. It must only be used after the individual has made a good-faith effort to obtain a duplicate receipt from the vendor.

#### 4.2 Frequency of Use
Frequent use of this affidavit suggests a failure in record-keeping controls.
*   **Limit:** An individual may submit no more than **three (3)** Missing Receipt Affidavits within a single fiscal year.
*   **Excessive Use:** Submissions beyond this limit will trigger a review by the Treasurer or CFO and may result in the revocation of corporate credit card privileges or denial of future reimbursements.

#### 4.3 Required Information
To be valid, the affidavit must reconstruct the details of the transaction with high specificity, including:
1.  **Vendor Name and Location.**
2.  **Date and Time of Transaction.**
3.  **Detailed Description of Expense** (e.g., "Lunch" is insufficient; "Lunch for 3 people: J. Doe, A. Smith, B. Jones to discuss GRC Curriculum" is required).
4.  **Total Amount.**
5.  **Reason the original receipt is missing.**

#### 4.4 Unallowable Items
In accordance with the Organization's **Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy (FIN-POL-037)** and **Grant Allowable Costs Policy (FIN-POL-045)**, the signer must certify that no alcohol was purchased (unless segregated and charged to non-federal funds) and that the expense was not personal in nature.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requestor** | Exhausts all options to find the original receipt. Completes the affidavit truthfully and completely. Accepts financial liability if the expense is deemed fraudulent or unallowable. |
| **Approving Manager** | Verifies the business purpose of the expense. Ensures the affidavit is not being used to hide unallowable costs (e.g., alcohol on federal grants). |
| **Finance Dept / CFO** | Reviews the affidavit for compliance with IRS and Grant rules. Tracks the frequency of use per employee. Has final authority to reject the affidavit. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
**Internal Controls:** This form serves as a critical internal control document. Falsification of this affidavit constitutes fraud and a violation of the **Board of Directors Code of Ethics and Conduct** and **Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)**.

**Consequences:**
*   **Rejection:** Incomplete affidavits will be rejected, and the expense will be the personal liability of the individual.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Submission of a false affidavit will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer status, and potential legal action for theft.
