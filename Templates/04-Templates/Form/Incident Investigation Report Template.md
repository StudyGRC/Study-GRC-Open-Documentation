### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this **Incident Investigation Report** is to provide a standardized framework for documenting, analyzing, and resolving incidents, accidents, and near-misses within Study GRC Inc. This document is designed to move beyond assigning blame and instead focus on identifying the **Root Cause** of an event to prevent recurrence.

This form satisfies recordkeeping requirements under OSHA 1904 (where applicable), Texas Labor Code, and the Organization’s risk management protocols.

### 2. Scope
This template applies to all incidents involving:
*   **Personnel:** Employees, Volunteers, Contractors, and Board Members.
*   **Participants:** Adult learners and Youth program participants.
*   **Assets:** Physical equipment, facilities, and digital infrastructure.
*   **Locations:** Remote workspaces, physical event venues, and official digital community channels.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Incident** | An unplanned event that results in personal injury, property damage, or environmental harm. |
| **Near Miss** | An unplanned event that did not result in injury, illness, or damage but had the potential to do so. |
| **Root Cause** | The fundamental, underlying, system-related reason why an incident occurred that identifies a correctable failure in management systems. |
| **Corrective Action** | Steps taken to remove the causes of an existing nonconformity or undesirable situation. |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Investigator** | Gathers facts, interviews witnesses, performs Root Cause Analysis, and drafts the initial report. |
| **Involved Party** | Provides accurate and truthful accounts of the incident; cooperates with the investigation. |
| **Safety Officer** | Reviews the report for quality and thoroughness; ensures the Root Cause is systemic, not just behavioral. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves closure of high-severity incidents; ensures resources are available for Corrective Actions. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to report an incident, falsification of investigation details, or retaliation against individuals participating in an investigation is a violation of the **Code of Ethics and Conduct** and the **Whistleblower Protection Policy**. Such actions may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
