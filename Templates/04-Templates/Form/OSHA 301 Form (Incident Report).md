### 1. Purpose
This form serves as the official record of a work-related injury or illness for Study GRC Inc. employees. It is designed to satisfy the requirements of the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) under **29 CFR 1904.301**. This document provides the necessary data to populate the OSHA 300 Log and facilitates the internal investigation required to prevent recurrence.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All Study GRC Inc. employees (W-2 status), including remote/telecommuting staff and on-site staff at events.
*   **Exclusions:** This specific form is not for use regarding volunteers, independent contractors, or event participants (use *EHS-FRM-020 General Incident Report* for non-employees), unless the contractor is under the direct day-to-day supervision of Study GRC Inc. as defined by OSHA recordkeeping standards.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Recordable Injury/Illness** | An injury or illness resulting in death, days away from work, restricted work or transfer to another job, medical treatment beyond first aid, or loss of consciousness. |
| **First Aid** | Limited medical care as strictly defined by OSHA (e.g., cleaning wounds, applying bandages, using non-prescription meds at non-prescription strength). First aid incidents do not require this form unless requested by management. |
| **Work-Related (Remote)** | For home offices, an injury is work-related only if it occurs while the employee is performing work for pay or compensation in the home, and the injury is directly related to the performance of work rather than the general home environment. |

### 4. Instructions for Completion
1.  **Timeline:** This form must be completed within **seven (7) calendar days** of receiving information that a recordable work-related injury or illness has occurred.
2.  **Confidentiality:** This form contains Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and Protected Health Information (PHI). It must be stored in a secure, access-controlled environment (e.g., locked personnel file or encrypted HR drive).
3.  **Routing:** Once completed, submit securely to the VP of Operations or designated Safety Officer for review and entry into the OSHA 300 Log.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Affected Employee** | Report the injury to a supervisor immediately, or as soon as medically possible. Provide accurate details regarding the event. |
| **Supervisor** | Ensure the employee receives medical attention. Complete this form (EHS-FRM-018) within 7 days. Investigate the root cause. |
| **VP of Operations / Safety Officer** | Review the form for completeness. Determine recordability. Enter data into the OSHA 300 Log within 7 days. Maintain records for 5 years. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to complete this form accurately and within the mandated timeline constitutes a violation of federal law (29 CFR 1904) and Study GRC Inc. policy.
*   **Regulatory Penalty:** OSHA may impose civil penalties for failure to maintain records.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Failure to report injuries or falsification of this document may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
