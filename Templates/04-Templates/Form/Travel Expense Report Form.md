### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Travel Expense Report Form** is to standardize the collection of data required to reimburse Study GRC Inc. personnel for reasonable and necessary expenses incurred while traveling on official Organization business. This form serves as the primary mechanism for **substantiation** as required by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) "Accountable Plan" rules and 2 CFR § 200.474 (Travel Costs) for federal grant compliance.

### 2. Scope
This form applies to all **Study GRC Inc.** Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and authorized contractors seeking reimbursement for travel-related costs.
*   **Inclusions:** Transportation, lodging, subsistence (meals/incidentals), and related items incurred during approved travel.
*   **Exclusions:** General office supplies not related to travel, or expenses incurred without prior authorization via the *Travel Authorization Request Form* (FIN-FRM-038).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Substantiation** | Documentary evidence (receipts, logs) proving the amount, time, place, and business purpose of an expense. |
| **Accountable Plan** | An IRS arrangement for reimbursing employees for business expenses where reimbursement is not counted as taxable income. |
| **GSA Rates** | Per diem rates established by the U.S. General Services Administration, used as the ceiling for federal grant reimbursements. |
| **Unallowable Cost** | Expenses that cannot be charged to federal grants (e.g., alcohol, entertainment), per 2 CFR 200 Subpart E. |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Traveler** | Submit form within 30 days of trip completion. Attach all receipts >$25 (or as required by grant). Ensure no alcohol is charged to federal projects. |
| **Approving Manager** | Verify the business purpose and that expenses align with the approved *Travel Authorization Request*. |
| **Grant Manager** | Verify that the "Grant/Project Code" is correct and funds are available. |
| **Finance Dept (CFO)** | Review for mathematical accuracy, receipt substantiation, and compliance with GSA rates. Process payment. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to submit this form with proper substantiation within **60 days** of the expense being incurred will result in the reimbursement being treated as **taxable income** (W-2 wages) to the traveler, in accordance with IRS Accountable Plan regulations.

Submission of fraudulent receipts or intentional misclassification of unallowable costs (e.g., hiding alcohol expenses under "Meals") constitutes fraud and will result in immediate termination of employment or volunteer status, and potential legal action under the False Claims Act.
