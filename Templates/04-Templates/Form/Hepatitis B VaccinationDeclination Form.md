### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this form is to document the offer of the Hepatitis B vaccination series to Study GRC Inc. employees and designated volunteers who have been identified as having occupational exposure to blood or other potentially infectious materials (OPIM). This documentation is required to ensure compliance with **OSHA Standard 29 CFR 1910.1030 (Bloodborne Pathogens)**.

### 2. Scope
This form applies to all Study GRC Inc. personnel (employees and specific volunteer roles) identified in the **Bloodborne Pathogens Exposure Control Plan** as having occupational exposure. This primarily includes, but is not limited to:
*   Designated First Aid providers at in-person events.
*   Security personnel at in-person events.
*   Staff responsible for cleaning up biological spills.

**Exclusions:** Remote-only staff with no in-person event responsibilities are not required to complete this form unless their role changes to include occupational exposure risks.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Occupational Exposure** | Reasonably anticipated skin, eye, mucous membrane, or parenteral contact with blood or OPIM that may result from the performance of an employee's duties. |
| **HBV** | Hepatitis B Virus. |
| **OPIM** | Other Potentially Infectious Materials (e.g., bodily fluids). |
| **Antibody Testing** | Laboratory testing to determine if an individual has developed immunity to Hepatitis B. |

### 4. Instructions and Disclosures

**4.1 Availability**
Study GRC Inc. makes the Hepatitis B vaccination series available to all employees with occupational exposure:
*   At no cost to the employee.
*   At a reasonable time and place.
*   Performed by or under the supervision of a licensed physician or healthcare professional.

**4.2 Efficacy and Safety**
The Hepatitis B vaccination is a non-infectious, yeast-based vaccine given in three doses over a six-month period. It is highly effective in preventing Hepatitis B infection. Contraindications (reasons not to take the vaccine) should be discussed with a healthcare provider (e.g., allergy to yeast).

**4.3 Voluntary Participation**
While Study GRC Inc. strongly encourages vaccination for at-risk personnel, acceptance is voluntary. However, refusal requires the execution of the **Mandatory Declination Statement** (Section 6) as required by federal law.

### 9. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to complete this form prior to engaging in duties with occupational exposure is a violation of Study GRC Inc. safety protocols and OSHA regulations. Personnel refusing to sign either the consent or the declination may be restricted from performing specific duties or subject to disciplinary action.

**Confidentiality:** This record contains Protected Health Information (PHI). Access is strictly limited to the individual, the Safety Officer, and HR personnel responsible for compliance, in accordance with **29 CFR 1910.1020**.
