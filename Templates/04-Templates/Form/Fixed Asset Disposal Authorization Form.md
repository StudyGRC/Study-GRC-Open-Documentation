### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to standardize the process for removing capitalized assets from Study GRC Inc.'s balance sheet and physical inventory. This form ensures compliance with **2 CFR 200.313(e)** regarding the disposition of equipment acquired with federal funds, prevents unauthorized theft or loss of assets, and ensures all data-bearing devices are sanitized in accordance with NIST standards prior to release.

### 2. Scope
This form applies to all **Capitalized Assets** (tangible property with a useful life of more than one year and a per-unit acquisition cost exceeding the capitalization threshold, typically $5,000) and **Sensitive Controlled Assets** (e.g., laptops, tablets, servers) regardless of cost.

**Exclusions:** Consumable office supplies and low-value peripherals (mice, keyboards) not tracked in the Fixed Asset Register.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Disposition** | The act of selling, scrapping, donating, trading in, or returning an asset to a grantor. |
| **Fair Market Value (FMV)** | The price that property would sell for on the open market. |
| **Federal Interest** | The right of the federal government to recover the fair market value of the federal share of property upon disposition. |
| **Data Sanitization** | The process of irreversibly removing or destroying data stored on a memory device (per NIST SP 800-88). |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Asset Custodian** | Initiates the form, verifies physical condition, and ensures the asset is secured until disposal is authorized. |
| **CISO / IT Dept** | Performs data sanitization on digital assets and signs Section E to certify no data leakage will occur. |
| **Chief Financial Officer** | Reviews funding source to determine federal interest; updates the Fixed Asset Register; calculates gain/loss on disposal. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides final authorization for high-value assets to ensure fiduciary oversight. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Federal Compliance:** Failure to obtain prior approval for the disposal of federally funded assets with a residual FMV exceeding $5,000 is a violation of **2 CFR 200.313** and may result in a "Questioned Cost" audit finding and repayment liability.
*   **Internal Controls:** Disposing of assets without this form constitutes a failure of internal controls, potentially triggering a material weakness finding in the annual Single Audit.
*   **Sanctions:** Employees who dispose of, sell, or appropriate organizational assets without this approved form are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination and legal prosecution for theft.
