### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a standardized framework for evaluating the Environmental, Health, Safety (EHS), and behavioral performance of third-party contractors engaged by Study GRC Inc. This evaluation ensures that all external partners adhere to the Organization’s safety standards, particularly regarding physical safety at events and the safeguarding of minors. This process mitigates liability risks, ensures compliance with OSHA multi-employer worksite standards, and aligns with the Organization's commitment to a safe community ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all **Physical Contractors** and **On-Site Service Providers** performing work for Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to:
*   Event production and audiovisual vendors.
*   Venue support staff and security personnel.
*   Logistics and transportation providers.
*   Exhibitors and booth setup crews.

**Exclusions:** This standard does not apply to purely digital/remote contractors (e.g., software developers, graphic designers) unless their duties require physical presence at Organization-hosted events.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Contractor** | Any external entity or individual contracted to perform physical work or services for Study GRC Inc. |
| **Incident** | An unplanned event that results in personal injury, property damage, or near-miss. |
| **Youth Protection Zone** | Any physical area where minors (K-12 participants) are present or expected to be present. |
| **Stop Work Authority** | The right and obligation of any Study GRC staff member to halt contractor work immediately if an imminent danger is observed. |
| **PPE** | Personal Protective Equipment (e.g., high-visibility vests, safety toe footwear, gloves). |

### 4. Evaluation Standards and Criteria
The Contractor Safety Performance Evaluation must be completed by the designated Study GRC Project Manager or Safety Lead within 7 days of contract completion or immediately following a specific event.

#### 4.1 Evaluation Scoring Rubric
Contractors are rated on a scale of **1 (Unacceptable)** to **5 (Excellent)**. A score of **1** in any "Critical" category results in immediate "Do Not Rehire" status.

**4.1.1 Pre-Work Compliance (Administrative)**
*   **Insurance Verification:** Did the contractor provide a Certificate of Insurance (COI) listing Study GRC Inc. as additional insured prior to mobilization?
*   **Safety Plan Submission:** Did the contractor submit a Job Hazard Analysis (JHA) or safety plan relevant to their scope of work?
*   **Background Checks:** Did the contractor confirm background checks for all staff accessing Youth Protection Zones, in compliance with *HR-POL-024 Background Check Policy*?

**4.1.2 On-Site Safety Performance (Operational)**
*   **PPE Adherence:** Did contractor personnel wear appropriate PPE for the task (e.g., rigging, electrical work)?
*   **Housekeeping:** Was the work area kept free of trip hazards, debris, and obstruction of emergency exits?
*   **Electrical Safety:** Were cables properly ramped/taped? Was equipment properly grounded?
*   **Venue Respect:** Did the contractor adhere to venue-specific fire and safety codes?

**4.1.3 Youth Protection & Conduct (Critical)**
*   **Interaction with Minors:** Did contractor staff maintain professional boundaries? (Zero tolerance for unauthorized one-on-one interaction).
*   **Language and Behavior:** Was language and conduct appropriate for a K-12 inclusive environment?
*   **Restricted Access:** Did contractor staff respect "Authorized Personnel Only" and "Youth Only" zones?

**4.1.4 Incident Reporting & Response**
*   **Timeliness:** Were any incidents or near-misses reported to Study GRC leadership immediately?
*   **Cooperation:** Did the contractor cooperate fully with any safety investigations?

#### 4.2 Performance Classification
Based on the aggregate score from Section 4.1, the contractor is classified as follows:

*   **Preferred (Score 4.5 - 5.0):** Pre-approved for future contracts; eligible for multi-year agreements.
*   **Qualified (Score 3.0 - 4.4):** Approved for future work; minor corrective actions may be requested.
*   **Probationary (Score 2.0 - 2.9):** May be rehired only with a specific Safety Improvement Plan (SIP) and enhanced oversight.
*   **Disqualified (Score < 2.0 or any Critical Failure):** Placed on the "Do Not Rehire" list. Legal counsel review required.

#### 4.3 Evaluation Form Fields
The following data points must be captured in the evaluation record:
1.  Contractor Company Name.
2.  Project/Event Name & Date.
3.  Scope of Work Description.
4.  Name of Study GRC Evaluator.
5.  Specific description of any safety violations (if applicable).
6.  Corrective Actions taken on-site (if applicable).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Project Manager / Event Lead** | Conducts the evaluation; monitors contractor on-site; exercises Stop Work Authority if necessary. |
| **Contractor** | Adheres to safety standards; reports incidents; ensures their staff are trained. |
| **Director of Operations** | Reviews all evaluations; maintains the "Do Not Rehire" list; approves Probationary contractors. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Consults on evaluations involving contractors working directly within educational workshops involving minors. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to complete this evaluation for major contractors exposes the Organization to liability and violates internal oversight controls.
*   **Internal:** Staff failing to conduct evaluations may face disciplinary action.
*   **External:** Contractors receiving a "Disqualified" rating will be barred from bidding on future Study GRC Inc. projects. Serious safety violations may result in immediate contract termination for cause and potential legal action for breach of contract and indemnification recovery pursuant to **Bylaws Article VII**.
