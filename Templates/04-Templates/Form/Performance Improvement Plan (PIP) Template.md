### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to provide a structured framework for addressing performance deficiencies or behavioral issues with employees or Operational Officers of Study GRC Inc. This Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) serves as a formal step within the Organization's **Progressive Discipline Policy**. It is designed to clearly communicate expectations, define specific goals for improvement, and provide the necessary support to help the individual succeed, while documenting the process for legal and compliance purposes.

### 2. Scope
This template applies to:
*   **Employees:** All full-time and part-time paid staff.
*   **Operational Officers:** Appointed leadership roles (e.g., CISO, DKG) as defined in **Bylaws Article V**, subject to the removal procedures therein.
*   **Contractors:** Where deemed appropriate by the Executive Director and legal counsel.

**Exclusions:** This form is generally not used for casual volunteers, who are subject to the *Volunteer Code of Conduct* and simplified dismissal procedures, unless the volunteer holds a critical operational role.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **PIP** | Performance Improvement Plan; a formal document outlining performance gaps and required remedies. |
| **SMART Goals** | Objectives that are **S**pecific, **M**easurable, **A**chievable, **R**elevant, and **T**ime-bound. |
| **At-Will Employment** | The legal doctrine (Texas Labor Code) stating that employment may be terminated by the employer or employee at any time, for any reason or no reason, with or without notice. |
| **Gap Analysis** | The comparison between the expected performance standard and the actual observed performance. |

### 4. Instructions for Use
1.  **Drafting:** The Manager must draft this document prior to meeting with the employee. Goals must be objective and measurable.
2.  **Review:** The Executive Director or HR delegate must review the draft to ensure consistency and lack of bias.
3.  **Meeting:** The PIP must be presented in a synchronous meeting (video conference or in-person).
4.  **Documentation:** The signed copy must be stored in the Employee’s Personnel File (HR-POL-042).

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Manager** | Drafts the PIP with objective data; conducts the PIP meeting; documents all check-ins; determines final outcome (pass/fail). |
| **Employee** | Participates in the process; executes the action items; proactively requests assistance if obstacles arise. |
| **Executive Director** | Reviews PIPs for consistency and fairness prior to issuance; ensures alignment with organizational culture. |
| **HR Manager** | Retains the signed document in the personnel file; advises on legal risks (e.g., potential discrimination claims). |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure by a Manager to properly document performance issues using this template before termination (except in cases of gross misconduct) may result in disciplinary action against the Manager. Failure by the Employee to meet the terms of the PIP will result in progressive discipline, up to and including termination.
