### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the official format and protocol for acknowledging non-cash (in-kind) contributions made to Study GRC Inc. This form ensures compliance with Internal Revenue Service (IRS) substantiation requirements, specifically **IRS Publication 1771** and **Internal Revenue Code §170(f)(8)**. It provides donors with the necessary Contemporaneous Written Acknowledgment (CWA) required to claim a tax deduction for charitable contributions of property.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All non-cash donations of tangible personal property (e.g., computer hardware, event supplies), intellectual property, or real property accepted by Study GRC Inc.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   **Cash/Monetary Donations:** Acknowledged via a separate Cash Donation Receipt.
    *   **Volunteer Services:** While valued, the IRS does not permit tax deductions for the value of time or services rendered. Services may be acknowledged for recognition purposes but **must not** be issued this specific tax receipt form.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **In-Kind Donation** | A charitable contribution of goods, property, or assets other than cash. |
| **Fair Market Value (FMV)** | The price that property would sell for on the open market. **Note:** The Organization does not determine FMV for the donor. |
| **Contemporaneous Written Acknowledgment (CWA)** | A written statement from the charity to the donor that describes the donation and states whether goods or services were provided in exchange. |
| **Quid Pro Quo** | A situation where the donor receives something of value (goods or services) in return for their contribution. |

### 4. Usage Protocols and Instructions

#### 4.1 Prohibition on Valuation
Pursuant to IRS regulations, Study GRC Inc. personnel **must not** assign a monetary value to the donated property on this receipt.
*   **Donor Responsibility:** It is the sole responsibility of the donor to determine the Fair Market Value (FMV) of the contribution for their tax records.
*   **Organization Responsibility:** The Organization is responsible only for providing a detailed **description** of the property received.

#### 4.2 Timing of Issuance
To be considered "contemporaneous," this acknowledgment must be provided to the donor:
1.  Before the donor files their tax return for the year of the contribution; or
2.  By the due date (including extensions) of the return.
*   **Standard:** Study GRC Inc. aims to issue this receipt within **30 days** of accepting the item.

#### 4.3 IRS Form 8283 (Donations over $500)
If a donor claims a deduction of more than $500 for non-cash assets, they must file IRS Form 8283. If the value exceeds $5,000, Study GRC Inc. must sign Part IV of Section B of Form 8283 to acknowledge receipt. This form (FIN-FRM-008) does not replace Form 8283 but supports it.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Donor** | Completes the description of items; determines Fair Market Value; retains receipt for tax purposes. |
| **CFO / Treasurer** | Reviews accepted items against the *Gift Acceptance Policy*; signs the acknowledgment; ensures no value is assigned by the Org. |
| **Development Committee** | Distributes the form to donors; ensures a copy of the completed form is archived in the Organization's financial records. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to provide a compliant acknowledgment may result in the disallowance of the donor's tax deduction and potential penalties for the Organization under IRS regulations regarding "Quid Pro Quo" disclosure failures. Issuing false receipts or assigning fraudulent values to donations is strictly prohibited and constitutes grounds for immediate termination and legal action.
