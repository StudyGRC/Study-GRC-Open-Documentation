### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this form is to provide clear notification to individuals regarding the collection, use, and retention of biometric data and to obtain written consent prior to such collection. This document ensures Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") complies with the **Texas Capture or Use of Biometric Identifier Act (CUBI)**, Texas Business & Commerce Code § 503.001, and Article IX (Policies & Data Protection) of the Organization's Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This form applies to all individuals from whom the Organization, or its authorized third-party vendors, requires the collection of biometric identifiers.
*   **Applies to:** Employees, Contractors, Volunteers, Board Directors, and Program Participants (including minors via parent/guardian).
*   **Exclusions:** Information not defined as a "biometric identifier" under Texas law (e.g., physical descriptions, photographs not used for facial recognition geometry).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Biometric Identifier** | As defined by Texas CUBI, a retina or iris scan, fingerprint, voiceprint, or record of hand or face geometry. |
| **Biometric Information** | Any information, regardless of how it is captured, converted, stored, or shared, based on an individual's biometric identifier used to identify an individual. |
| **CUBI** | The Texas Capture or Use of Biometric Identifier Act (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code Ann. § 503.001 et seq). |
| **Authorized Vendor** | A third-party service provider (e.g., background check agency, identity verification platform) contracted by the Organization. |

### 4. Disclosures and Consent Terms

By signing this form, the individual acknowledges and agrees to the following terms regarding their Biometric Information:

#### 4.1 Notification of Collection
The Organization, or its Authorized Vendors, may collect, capture, or otherwise obtain your Biometric Identifiers. The specific type of data to be collected includes:
*   [ ] **Fingerprints** (e.g., for background checks pursuant to Bylaws Article IX and Youth Protection policies).
*   [ ] **Face Geometry** (e.g., for identity verification during remote voting or proctored examinations).
*   [ ] **Voiceprint** (e.g., for customer service authentication).
*   [ ] **Other:** ________________________

#### 4.2 Purpose of Collection
The Biometric Information is collected solely for the following purposes:
1.  **Security and Identification:** To verify identity for access to secure systems, physical locations, or the Organization's electronic voting system (Bylaws Section 2.3).
2.  **Safety and Compliance:** To conduct criminal history background checks for volunteers and staff working with youth, as required by the Organization's *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.
3.  **Fraud Prevention:** To prevent timekeeping fraud or identity theft.

#### 4.3 Retention and Destruction Schedule
In compliance with CUBI § 503.001(c)(3), the Organization will not retain Biometric Information longer than is reasonably necessary.
*   **Destruction Timeline:** Biometric Information will be permanently destroyed not later than the **first anniversary (1 year)** of the date the purpose for collecting the identifier expires (e.g., termination of employment, conclusion of volunteer service, or end of membership).
*   **Method:** Destruction shall be performed in a secure manner that renders the data unreadable and irretrievable.

#### 4.4 Prohibition on Sale and Disclosure
The Organization will **not** sell, lease, or otherwise trade your Biometric Information. The Organization will not disclose or disseminate your Biometric Information unless:
1.  You (or your parent/guardian) provide prior written consent (via this form);
2.  The disclosure is required to complete a financial transaction requested by you;
3.  The disclosure is required by state or federal law or municipal ordinance; or
4.  The disclosure is required pursuant to a valid warrant or subpoena issued by a law enforcement agency.

#### 4.5 Data Security Standard
The Organization shall store, transmit, and protect Biometric Information using a reasonable standard of care within the Organization’s industry. This includes encryption at rest and in transit, which shall be at least as protective as the manner in which the Organization stores, transmits, and protects other confidential and sensitive information.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Individual (Signatory)** | Provide accurate information and report any concerns regarding data privacy immediately. |
| **Human Resources / Volunteer Coordinator** | Ensure this form is signed *prior* to the collection of any biometric data and filed securely in the personnel/volunteer record. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Ensure technical controls (encryption, access control) for stored biometric data meet CUBI standards. |
| **Authorized Vendors** | Process data strictly in accordance with the Data Processing Agreement (DPA) and Texas law. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to provide consent may result in the inability to serve in specific roles (e.g., roles requiring background checks) or access specific systems. Unauthorized capture, sale, or misuse of biometric data by any Organization personnel is a violation of the *Master Data Privacy Policy* and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination and legal prosecution.
