### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a rigid chain of custody for all minors participating in in-person Study GRC Inc. events. This form serves as the primary accountability record to ensure that minors are released solely to parents, legal guardians, or designees explicitly authorized in writing. This document mitigates liability risks associated with custody interference and negligence, aligning with the Organization's charitable purpose to foster a safe educational environment (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3).

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All Study GRC Inc. staff, volunteers, and event coordinators managing in-person educational workshops, community meetups, or training sessions where participants under the age of 18 are present.
*   **Exclusions:** Virtual/Online events (governed by *Digital Interaction with Minors Policy*); events restricted exclusively to adults (18+).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authorized Guardian** | A parent, legal guardian, or individual explicitly named in the *Parental Consent and Liability Waiver* as having permission to transport the minor. |
| **Check-Out Officer** | The designated Study GRC volunteer or staff member responsible for manning the exit control point and verifying identities. |
| **Government-Issued ID** | A valid Driver’s License, State ID, Passport, or Military ID containing a photograph and legal name. |
| **Floater** | A minor participant remaining at the venue after the scheduled conclusion of the event. |

### 4. Instructions for Use

#### 4.1 Pre-Event Preparation
1.  **Data Population:** The Event Lead must populate the "Participant Name" and "Authorized Pick-Up Names" columns **prior** to the event, utilizing data from the *Parental Consent and Liability Waiver*.
2.  **Printing:** This roster must be printed on physical paper to ensure availability regardless of power/internet status.
3.  **Privacy:** The roster must be kept on a clipboard facing the Check-Out Officer to prevent other parents from viewing contact details or authorized guardian lists of other children.

#### 4.2 Check-Out Procedure
1.  **Identification:** The Check-Out Officer **must** request Government-Issued ID from the individual attempting to pick up the minor.
2.  **Verification:** The name on the ID must match a name listed in the "Authorized Pick-Up Names" column.
    *   *Note:* If the name does not match, the minor **cannot** be released. The Event Lead must be summoned immediately to contact the primary parent on file.
3.  **Signature:** The Authorized Guardian must sign the roster physically.
4.  **Staff Validation:** The Check-Out Officer must initial the entry to certify they performed the ID check.

#### 4.3 Post-Event Retention
1.  **Scanning:** Immediately following the event, this roster must be scanned and uploaded to the secure *Youth Protection Records* repository.
2.  **Destruction:** The physical paper copy must be shredded within 48 hours of digital archiving, in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 7. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Lead** | Ensures the roster is pre-filled accurately from registration data; handles custody disputes or unauthorized pick-up attempts. |
| **Check-Out Officer** | Physically verifies IDs against the roster; refuses release if ID does not match; ensures all fields are completed. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Audits sign-out sheets post-event to ensure compliance with safety protocols. |

### 8. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to utilize this form, or releasing a minor to an individual not listed on this roster, constitutes a critical violation of the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*. Such violations will result in immediate removal from volunteer duties, potential termination of employment, and may trigger reporting requirements to Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) and local law enforcement.
