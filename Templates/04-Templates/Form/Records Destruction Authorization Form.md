### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Records Destruction Authorization Form** is to create a defensible, permanent audit trail documenting the routine destruction of Organization records that have met their required retention periods. This form serves as evidence that the destruction of records was conducted in the ordinary course of business, in compliance with the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy**, and not for the purpose of concealing evidence (spoliation).

### 2. Scope
This form applies to all **Study GRC Inc.** personnel, including Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors who possess Organization records.

*   **In Scope:** All official records (paper and electronic) that have exceeded the retention periods defined in the **Records Retention Schedule (GOV-STD-013)**.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Drafts and duplicate copies (convenience copies) that are not official records.
    *   **CRITICAL:** Any record currently subject to a **Legal Hold (GOV-PRC-015)**. Destruction of records under Legal Hold is strictly prohibited and constitutes a violation of federal law.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Retention Period** | The specific time period, mandated by law or policy, that a record must be kept before it can be destroyed. |
| **Legal Hold** | A directive to suspend the destruction of records due to pending or anticipated litigation, audits, or government investigations. |
| **Secure Destruction** | The disposal of records in a manner that renders them unreadable and irretrievable (e.g., cross-cut shredding for paper; NIST SP 800-88 sanitization for digital media). |
| **Record Series** | A group of related records filed and used together as a unit and evaluated as a unit for retention purposes (e.g., "Accounts Payable Invoices"). |

### 4. Authorization Procedure & Form Fields

#### 4.1 Pre-Destruction Verification
Before completing this form, the Requester must verify:
1.  The records have met or exceeded the retention period listed in **GOV-STD-013**.
2.  The records are **not** relevant to any open audit, investigation, or litigation.
3.  No active **Legal Hold** covers these specific records.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Requester** | Identifies records eligible for destruction and initiates the authorization form. Ensures accuracy of record dates. |
| **Department Head** | Verifies that the records are no longer needed for operational business purposes. |
| **Compliance Officer** | **Critical Control Point:** Checks the Legal Hold Registry to ensure no records relevant to litigation are approved for destruction. |
| **IT / Operations** | Executes the physical or digital destruction according to **NIST SP 800-88** standards and signs the Verification of Destruction. |
| **Secretary** | Retains this signed form permanently as proof of compliance with the Document Retention Policy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to utilize this form prior to destroying official Organization records may result in disciplinary action.

**Unauthorized destruction of records** during a government investigation or litigation (Spoliation of Evidence) is a federal crime punishable by fines and imprisonment under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act and other statutes.
