### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to mandate the annual aggregation, certification, and posting of work-related injury and illness data in compliance with **29 CFR 1904.32**. This summary provides Study GRC Inc. employees with transparency regarding workplace safety performance during the previous calendar year.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to **Study GRC Inc.** as a singular establishment.
*   **Inclusions:** All full-time, part-time, and temporary employees on the Organization's payroll.
*   **Exclusions:** Volunteers and independent contractors (unless supervised on a day-to-day basis by Study GRC personnel).
*   **Remote Context:** As a remote-first organization, "posting" requirements defined herein include digital accessibility for distributed staff.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **OSHA 300 Log** | The Log of Work-Related Injuries and Illnesses (Form 300) used to classify and record specific details of recordable incidents. |
| **OSHA 300A** | The Summary of Work-Related Injuries and Illnesses. This is the specific form that must be posted publicly. It contains totals, not personal details. |
| **Recordable Incident** | An injury or illness resulting in death, days away from work, restricted work or transfer to another job, medical treatment beyond first aid, or loss of consciousness. |
| **Certifying Executive** | An owner of the company, an officer of the corporation, the highest-ranking company official working at the establishment, or the immediate supervisor of the highest-ranking official. |
| **Posting Period** | The mandatory timeframe (February 1 to April 30) during which the previous year's summary must be visible to employees. |

### 4. Procedures and Form Requirements

#### 4.1 Data Aggregation
The Document Owner must review the **OSHA 300 Log** for the previous calendar year to ensure accuracy. The totals from the Log must be transferred to the OSHA 300A Summary fields defined below.

#### 4.2 Form Content Requirements
The generated OSHA 300A form must contain the following data points. If no injuries or illnesses occurred, **zeros (0)** must be entered in the specific fields. Leaving fields blank is prohibited.

**A. Number of Cases**
*   Total number of deaths.
*   Total number of cases with days away from work.
*   Total number of cases with job transfer or restriction.
*   Total number of other recordable cases.

**B. Number of Days**
*   Total number of days away from work.
*   Total number of days of job transfer or restriction.

**C. Injury and Illness Types (Total Count)**
1.  Injuries
2.  Skin disorders
3.  Respiratory conditions
4.  Poisonings
5.  Hearing loss
6.  All other illnesses

**D. Establishment Information**
*   **Establishment Name:** Study GRC Inc.
*   **Street Address:** [Registered Office Address per Bylaws 1.2]
*   **Industry Description:** Educational Support Services / Non-Profit
*   **NAICS Code:** [Applicable Code, e.g., 611710]
*   **Annual Average Number of Employees:** [Number]
*   **Total Hours Worked by All Employees Last Year:** [Number]

#### 4.3 Certification
Pursuant to **29 CFR 1904.32(b)(3)**, a company executive must certify that they have examined the OSHA 300 Log and that the annual summary is correct and complete.
*   **Authorized Signatory:** Executive Director (CEO) or President of the Board.
*   **Requirement:** Physical or verifiable electronic signature and date.

#### 4.4 Posting Requirements
*   **Timeline:** The certified OSHA 300A must be posted no later than **February 1** of the year following the year covered by the records and must remain in place until **April 30**.
*   **Physical Location:** A copy must be posted at the Registered Office.
*   **Digital Posting (Remote Workforce):** To ensure notice to remote employees who do not report to a physical location, the certified OSHA 300A must be posted on the **Organization’s Intranet (e.g., Notion/SharePoint) "Notices" page** and distributed via the primary internal communication channel (e.g., Slack/Teams announcement).

#### 4.5 Privacy Protection
The OSHA 300A Summary **must not** contain any employee names, personal identifiers, or specific descriptions of incidents. It is strictly a statistical summary.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Operations** | Aggregates data from the OSHA 300 Log; prepares the 300A form; ensures digital and physical posting. |
| **Executive Director** | Reviews the data for accuracy and signs the Certification section as the Company Executive. |
| **Employees** | Responsible for reviewing the summary to understand organizational safety performance. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to prepare, certify, or post the OSHA 300A Summary is a violation of federal law.
*   **Regulatory Penalty:** OSHA may issue citations and civil penalties for paperwork violations, currently adjusted for inflation (approx. $16,131 per violation).
*   **Internal Discipline:** Failure by the Document Owner to prepare this form by the deadline may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, due to the creation of legal liability for the Organization.
