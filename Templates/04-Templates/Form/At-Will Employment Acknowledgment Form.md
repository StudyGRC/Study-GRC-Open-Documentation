### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this form is to explicitly define the employment relationship between Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") and its employees. It serves to formally notify employees of their "at-will" status under Texas law, ensuring there is no ambiguity regarding the tenure of employment or the conditions under which the employment relationship may be terminated. This document mitigates the risk of implied contract claims.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All paid employees of Study GRC Inc., including full-time, part-time, and temporary staff, regardless of their remote work location.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   **Volunteers:** Volunteers serve without compensation and have no employment relationship with the Organization.
    *   **Independent Contractors:** Contractors are governed by specific Service Agreements (HR-CON-005).
    *   **Contractual Executives:** Employees who hold a specific, written employment contract signed by the President of the Board that explicitly alters the at-will relationship for a defined term.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **At-Will Employment** | A legal doctrine in Texas wherein an employer may terminate an employee for any reason (that is not illegal) or for no reason at all, with or without notice. Conversely, the employee may resign at any time, for any reason, with or without notice. |
| **Implied Contract** | An agreement created by actions of the parties involved, but not written or spoken. This form expressly negates the formation of implied contracts regarding job security. |
| **Operational Officer** | As defined in Bylaws Article V, individuals managing day-to-day execution (e.g., Executive Director, CISO). Unless a separate contract exists, these roles remain at-will. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 The At-Will Relationship
Study GRC Inc. is a Texas Nonprofit Corporation. In accordance with Texas common law, employment with the Organization is "at-will." This means that the employment relationship is for an indefinite period and may be terminated by either the employee or the Organization at any time, with or without cause, and with or without advance notice.

#### 4.2 No Contract of Employment
Neither the Employee Handbook, this Acknowledgment Form, nor any other Organization policy constitutes an employment contract, express or implied. No document or statement provided during the hiring process or subsequent employment guarantees employment for any specific duration.

#### 4.3 Prohibition on Verbal Alterations
No supervisor, manager, or representative of the Organization, other than the Board of Directors or the Executive Director (acting with Board approval), has the authority to enter into any agreement for employment for any specified period or to make any promises or commitments contrary to the at-will policy.
*   Any such agreement altering the at-will status must be in writing and signed by the President of the Board of Directors.
*   Oral representations regarding "job security," "permanent status," or "probationary periods" do not alter the at-will nature of employment.

#### 4.4 Effect of Remote Work
The Organization's status as a "Remote-First" entity does not alter the at-will relationship. The location of the employee's home office or the provision of equipment does not create an expectation of continued employment.

#### 4.5 Bylaws Integration
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.1**, Operational Officers may be removed by the Board or the Executive Director at any time. This form confirms that such removal is consistent with the at-will doctrine, subject only to any specific severance provisions that may be contained in a separate, written executive agreement.

| Role | Responsibility |

| **Executive Director** | Maintain the signed original in the employee’s personnel file (HR-POL-042). |
| **Board of Directors** | The only body authorized to approve employment contracts that deviate from the at-will standard. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Signature of this form is a mandatory condition of employment. Failure to sign this acknowledgment may result in the withdrawal of an employment offer or termination of employment.
