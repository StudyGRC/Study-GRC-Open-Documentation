### 1. Purpose
This document establishes the official mechanism for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") to obtain **Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC)** prior to collecting, using, or disclosing personal information from children under the age of 13. This form ensures compliance with the **Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)** and aligns with **Bylaws Article VI (Youth Protection)** and **Article IX (Data Protection)**.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All Operational Officers, volunteers, and systems that interact with users under the age of 13.
*   **Trigger Condition:** This form must be executed and verified **before** a child under 13 is granted access to Study GRC Inc. community platforms, educational resources, or events where Personal Identifiable Information (PII) is collected.
*   **Exclusions:** This form is not required for one-time contact where PII is not stored (e.g., a one-time question answered via email and immediately deleted), per COPPA exemptions.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Child** | An individual under the age of 13. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information (e.g., full name, home address, email address, telephone number, geolocation information, screen name). |
| **Operator** | Study GRC Inc. |
| **VPC** | Verifiable Parental Consent; a method reasonably calculated, in light of available technology, to ensure that the person providing consent is the child's parent. |
| **Direct Notice** | A specific notice provided to parents regarding the operator's practices with regard to the collection, use, or disclosure of personal information from children. |

### 4. Instructions for Use

#### 4.1 Distribution
This form serves as the **Direct Notice** required by 16 CFR § 312.4. It must be provided to the parent/guardian via email or secure download link upon a child's attempt to register.

#### 4.2 Acceptable Return Methods (Verification)
To satisfy the FTC's "Verifiable" standard, this form must be returned using one of the following methods:
1.  **Signed Scan/Photo:** The parent prints, signs, and scans (or photographs) the form, returning it to `privacy@studygrc.org`.
2.  **Video Conference:** The parent displays the signed form and their government-issued ID during a video call with the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or designated staff.
3.  **Digital Signature with KBA:** Use of a Board-approved third-party vendor (e.g., DocuSign) that utilizes Knowledge-Based Authentication (KBA) or identity verification.

#### 4.3 Record Retention
Completed forms are **Confidential Records**. They must be stored securely in the Organization’s encrypted document repository and retained for as long as the child’s account is active, plus three (3) years, in accordance with the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy (GOV-POL-012)**.

### 5. FORM CONTENT (Parent-Facing)

*(The section below constitutes the document to be presented to the Parent/Guardian)*

**STUDY GRC INC. - PARENTAL CONSENT FOR CHILD’S DATA PRIVACY**

**Notice to Parents/Guardians:**
In compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), Study GRC Inc. requires your permission to collect personal information from your child (under age 13) to allow them to participate in our educational community. We will not collect this information without your signed consent.

**1. Information We Collect**
To provide our educational services, we wish to collect the following from your child:
*   **Required:** Username (Screen Name), Email Address (for password recovery/login), and Password.
*   **Optional:** Course progress data, quiz scores, and digital badges earned.
*   **Passive:** IP address and browser type (used solely for security and site functionality).

**2. How We Use This Information**
*   To create and manage your child’s user account.
*   To track educational progress through our GRC curriculum.
*   To issue certificates of completion.
*   **Note:** Study GRC Inc. is a non-profit; we **never** sell your child’s data to third parties for marketing or advertising purposes.

**3. Disclosure to Third Parties**
We do not disclose your child's personal information to the public. Data is only shared with service providers (e.g., our hosting provider or Learning Management System) who have agreed to maintain the confidentiality, security, and integrity of the information.

**4. Your Rights**
As a parent/guardian, you have the right to:
*   Review the personal information we have collected from your child.
*   Request the deletion of your child’s personal information.
*   Refuse further collection or use of the information (this may result in the termination of the account).

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to obtain this form prior to data collection constitutes a violation of federal law (COPPA) and **Bylaws Article VI**.
*   **Staff/Volunteers:** Violation of this procedure will result in immediate suspension of system access and potential termination of role.
*   **Organization:** Failure to comply may result in FTC civil penalties (up to $50,120 per violation).
