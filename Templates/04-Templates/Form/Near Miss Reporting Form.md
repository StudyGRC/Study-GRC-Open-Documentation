### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this form is to capture data regarding "Near Miss" incidents—unplanned events that did not result in injury, illness, or damage, but had the potential to do so. Collecting this data allows Study GRC Inc. to proactively identify hazards, analyze trends, and implement corrective actions to prevent future accidents. This form supports a non-punitive safety culture focused on continuous improvement.

### 2. Scope
This form applies to all operations conducted by Study GRC Inc., including:
*   **Physical Events:** Conferences, meetups, and workshops.
*   **Remote Work:** Home office hazards and digital security close calls.
*   **Youth Programs:** Interactions involving minors where safety protocols were nearly breached.
*   **Personnel:** All employees, Voting Members, volunteers, and contractors.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Near Miss** | An unplanned event that did not result in injury, illness, or damage – but had the potential to do so. Often referred to as a "close call." |
| **Hazard** | A potential source of harm or adverse health effect on a person or persons. |
| **Reporter** | The individual observing and documenting the Near Miss. |
| **Root Cause** | The fundamental, underlying, system-related reason why an incident occurred. |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Personnel** | Responsible for identifying hazards and submitting this form immediately upon observing a Near Miss. |
| **Director of Operations** | Responsible for reviewing all submissions, assigning risk levels, and ensuring corrective actions are implemented. |
| **Board of Directors** | Responsible for reviewing aggregated Near Miss data annually to identify systemic risks and approve safety budget allocations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Study GRC Inc. operates under a **Non-Retaliation Policy**. No individual shall face disciplinary action for reporting a Near Miss in good faith. Failure to report a known high-risk Near Miss, or concealing a hazard, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
