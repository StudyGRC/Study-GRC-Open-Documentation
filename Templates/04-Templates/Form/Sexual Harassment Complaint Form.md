### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to provide a standardized, secure, and confidential mechanism for employees, volunteers, contractors, and members of Study GRC Inc. to report incidents of alleged sexual harassment. This form ensures that the Organization captures all necessary information to conduct a prompt, thorough, and impartial investigation in compliance with the **Anti-Harassment and Non-Discrimination Policy**, **Texas Labor Code Chapter 21**, and **EEOC** guidelines.

### 2. Scope
This form applies to all individuals associated with Study GRC Inc., including:
*   **Internal:** Board Directors, Officers, Employees, and Volunteers.
*   **External:** Contractors, Vendors, Members, and Program Participants.

This form covers incidents occurring:
*   In the physical workplace (if applicable).
*   During remote work operations (e.g., Zoom, Slack, Discord, Email).
*   At Organization-sponsored events, conferences, or social gatherings.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Complainant** | The individual filing the complaint who alleges they have been subjected to or witnessed harassment. |
| **Respondent** | The individual(s) accused of the alleged harassment. |
| **Sexual Harassment** | Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when submission is made a term of employment/service, or creates a hostile environment. |
| **Retaliation** | Adverse action taken against an individual for filing a complaint or participating in an investigation. Retaliation is strictly prohibited. |

### 4. Instructions and Form Data
**Instructions:**
1.  Complete this form to the best of your ability. You do not need to answer every question if the information is unavailable, but specific details aid the investigation.
2.  Submit this form directly to the **Human Resources Director** or, if the complaint involves HR, to the **Executive Director** or **Board President**.
3.  Study GRC Inc. takes all complaints seriously. Information provided here will be treated as **Confidential** to the extent permitted by law and the requirements of a fair investigation.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Complainant** | Provide truthful, accurate information and cooperate with the investigation. Preserve all evidence. |
| **HR / Compliance Officer** | Receive the form, maintain strict confidentiality, initiate the **Sexual Harassment Investigation Procedure**, and ensure non-retaliation protections are in place. |
| **Executive Director** | Oversee the resolution process (unless implicated) and ensure resources are available for a fair investigation. |
| **Board of Directors** | Receive high-level anonymized reports of incidents; handle complaints directly if the Executive Director is the Respondent. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Non-Retaliation:** Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who reports harassment or participates in an investigation. Retaliation is a separate disciplinary offense.

**Confidentiality:** The Organization will protect the confidentiality of the Complainant and the Respondent to the extent possible, consistent with the need to conduct a thorough investigation and take corrective action.

**False Reports:** While the Organization encourages the reporting of all perceived incidents of harassment, false accusations made with malice or intent to harm may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or revocation of membership.
