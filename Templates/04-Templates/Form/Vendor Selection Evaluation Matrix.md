### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this **Vendor Selection Evaluation Matrix** is to establish a standardized, quantifiable, and objective method for evaluating proposals and bids received by Study GRC Inc. This document ensures compliance with **2 CFR § 200.319 (Competition)** and **2 CFR § 200.320 (Methods of Procurement)** by removing subjective bias from the selection process. It serves as the primary audit artifact demonstrating that the Organization selected the vendor providing the "Best Value" through a transparent process.

### 2. Scope
This matrix must be utilized for all **Competitive Proposals (RFP)** and **Sealed Bids** where the estimated value exceeds the Simplified Acquisition Threshold (currently $250,000), or for any procurement between $50,000 and $250,000 where a qualitative comparison is required beyond simple price analysis.

**Applies to:**
*   Evaluation Committee Members
*   Procurement Officers
*   Department Heads initiating large purchases

**Exclusions:**
*   Micro-purchases (under $50,000) where price is reasonable.
*   Sole Source procurements (requires separate Justification Form FIN-FRM-006).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Responsive Bidder** | A vendor who has complied with all material aspects of the solicitation (RFP/RFQ), including submission deadlines and format. |
| **Responsible Bidder** | A vendor who has the capability, integrity, and reliability to perform the contract requirements. |
| **Best Value** | The selection method that weighs price against technical merit, quality, and past performance to determine the most advantageous offer. |
| **Mandatory Criteria** | Pass/Fail requirements. If a vendor fails any mandatory criterion, they are disqualified immediately. |
| **SAM.gov** | System for Award Management; used to verify suspension and debarment status. |

### 4. Instructions for Use

1.  **Conflict of Interest Check:** Before receiving this matrix, all Evaluators must sign a specific *Conflict of Interest Disclosure* for the specific procurement, pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1**.
2.  **Independent Scoring:** Evaluators must score proposals independently before meeting as a group.
3.  **Weighting:** The "Weight" column must be established *prior* to opening bids. Price must always be a weighted factor unless prohibited by specific grant statutes.
4.  **Scoring Scale:** Use the following scale for the "Raw Score" column:
    *   **0 - Non-Compliant:** Does not meet requirement.
    *   **1 - Poor:** Minimally addresses requirement; significant gaps.
    *   **3 - Acceptable:** Meets minimum requirements.
    *   **5 - Excellent:** Exceeds requirements; adds significant value.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Evaluator** | Reviews proposals objectively, assigns raw scores based on evidence, and documents justification for scores. |
| **Procurement Officer** | Establishes weights prior to bid opening, aggregates scores from all evaluators, validates math, and conducts the SAM.gov debarment check. |
| **CFO** | Reviews the final aggregated matrix to ensure the selection aligns with budget and "Best Value" principles before contract award. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
**Objectivity Requirement:** Scores must be based strictly on the content of the written proposal and reference checks. External knowledge not contained in the proposal (rumors, personal relationships) must not influence the score.

**Record Retention:** This completed form is a **Federal Grant Record**. Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII** and **2 CFR § 200.334**, this document must be retained for a minimum of three (3) years from the date of submission of the final expenditure report.

**Consequences:** Manipulation of scores to favor a specific vendor constitutes fraud. Violations will result in immediate termination of employment and referral to the Audit Committee and relevant federal authorities.
