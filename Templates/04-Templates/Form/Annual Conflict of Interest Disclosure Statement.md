### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statement is to protect the tax-exempt interest of **Study GRC Inc.** (the "Organization") when it is contemplating entering into a transaction or arrangement that might benefit the private interest of an officer or director of the Organization or might result in a possible excess benefit transaction.

This form is required to satisfy **IRS Form 990, Part VI, Section B, Line 12c**, which asks if the organization regularly and consistently monitors and enforces compliance with its conflict of interest policy.

### 2. Scope
This disclosure requirement applies to all "Interested Persons," defined as:
*   **Directors:** Members of the Board of Directors.
*   **Officers:** President, Secretary, Treasurer, and any other Board-appointed officers.
*   **Key Employees:** Operational leadership (e.g., Executive Director, CFO) and any employee with substantial influence over the Organization's financial affairs.
*   **Committee Members:** Members of committees with Board-delegated powers (e.g., Finance Committee).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Conflict of Interest** | A situation where a person in a position of authority has a competing professional or personal interest that makes it difficult to act impartially. |
| **Financial Interest** | An interest, direct or indirect, through business, investment, or family, in any entity with which the Organization has a transaction or arrangement. |
| **Family Member** | Includes a spouse, domestic partner, ancestors, brothers and sisters (whether whole or half blood), children (whether natural or adopted), grandchildren, great-grandchildren, and spouses of brothers, sisters, children, grandchildren, and great-grandchildren. |
| **Transaction** | Any financial agreement, contract, or exchange between the Organization and another party, including the sale or lease of property, the lending of money, or the furnishing of goods, services, or facilities. |

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Interested Person** | Complete this form annually and update it immediately upon the emergence of a new potential conflict. |
| **Secretary of the Board** | Distribute this form to all required parties annually in January. Collect and review all forms for completeness. Maintain signed forms in the corporate records for IRS inspection. |
| **Board Chair / President** | Review disclosed conflicts to determine if they require recusal from specific votes or deliberations. |
| **Audit Committee** | Review the aggregate disclosures to ensure no systemic risks or excess benefit transactions are occurring. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to file this disclosure statement, or the filing of a materially false statement, constitutes a violation of the Organization's Conflict of Interest Policy and **Bylaws Article IX**.

Violations may result in disciplinary action, including removal from the Board or termination of employment. If a conflict was not disclosed and resulted in a transaction that was not in the best interest of the Organization, the Organization may seek restitution or void the transaction to the extent permitted by Texas law.
