### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Strategic Plan Tracking Dashboard** is to provide a standardized, quantifiable mechanism for monitoring Study GRC Inc.'s progress toward the goals outlined in its **3-Year Strategic Plan**. This tool ensures alignment between daily operations and the Organization’s charitable and educational purposes as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**.

Furthermore, this standard satisfies federal grant compliance requirements under **2 CFR § 200.301 (Performance Measurement)**, requiring the Organization to relate financial data to performance accomplishments.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to:
*   **The Board of Directors:** For oversight and strategic governance.
*   **Operational Officers (CEO, CFO, etc.):** For execution, data entry, and reporting.
*   **Department Heads:** For tracking specific departmental Key Performance Indicators (KPIs).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **KPI (Key Performance Indicator)** | A quantifiable measure used to evaluate the success of an organization, employee, or project in meeting objectives for performance. |
| **OKR (Objectives and Key Results)** | A goal-setting framework used to define measurable goals and track their outcomes. |
| **RAG Status** | A color-coded system (Red, Amber, Green) used to visualize project health and status. |
| **Strategic Pillar** | A high-level thematic area of focus defined in the Strategic Plan (e.g., "Content Expansion," "Community Growth"). |
| **Variance** | The difference between the planned target and the actual performance. |

### 4. Dashboard Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 Dashboard Structure and Layout
The Strategic Plan Tracking Dashboard must be maintained in a centralized, secure digital format (e.g., Spreadsheet, Project Management Software) accessible to the Board and Operational Officers. The Dashboard must contain, at a minimum, the following data fields:

1.  **Strategic Pillar:** The high-level category from the Strategic Plan.
2.  **Objective:** The specific goal statement.
3.  **Key Result (Metric):** The quantifiable target (e.g., "Increase membership by 20%").
4.  **Owner:** The specific Operational Officer responsible for delivery.
5.  **Timeline:** Start Date and Target Completion Date.
6.  **Current Value:** The actual metric as of the reporting date.
7.  **Target Value:** The goal metric.
8.  **RAG Status:** Visual health indicator (defined in Section 4.3).
9.  **Comments/Blockers:** Narrative explanation of status, particularly for Amber/Red items.

#### 4.2 Reporting Frequency
*   **Operational Review:** The Executive Director must review and update the dashboard **monthly** with the Operational Leadership team.
*   **Board Review:** A summary of the Dashboard must be presented to the Board of Directors **quarterly** at regular meetings (Bylaws Article III, Section 3.4).

#### 4.3 RAG Status Definitions
To ensure consistency in reporting, the following definitions must be applied to the status indicators:

| Status | Color | Definition | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **On Track** | **GREEN** | Performance is within 5% of the target trajectory. No significant risks identified. | Continue monitoring. |
| **At Risk** | **AMBER** | Performance deviates by 6-15% from target, or a risk has emerged that may delay completion. | Mitigation plan must be noted in the "Comments" field. |
| **Off Track** | **RED** | Performance deviates by >15%, or a critical blocker prevents progress. | Immediate escalation to the Executive Director; potential Board notification required if material impact exists. |
| **Completed** | **BLUE** | Objective has been met or exceeded. | Archive and celebrate. |

#### 4.4 Data Integrity and Grant Compliance
*   **Source Data:** All KPIs reported in the dashboard must be traceable to source data (e.g., financial reports, LMS analytics, membership rolls).
*   **Federal Grants:** For objectives funded by federal awards, the dashboard metrics must align with the approved grant work plan. Failure to meet these targets requires documentation in the dashboard to support the **Performance Progress Report (PPR)** required by 2 CFR § 200.329.

#### 4.5 Re-Forecasting and Pivots
If a Strategic Objective becomes obsolete or unachievable due to external factors (e.g., regulatory changes, funding shifts):
1.  The "Owner" must propose a modification.
2.  The Executive Director must approve operational pivots.
3.  **Fundamental Changes:** If the change alters the core mission or requires a budget amendment >10%, Board approval is required pursuant to **Bylaws Article III**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews the dashboard quarterly to ensure the Organization remains true to its mission (Bylaws Art. I). Approves the initial Strategic Plan. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Owns the Dashboard. Ensures data accuracy and timeliness. Reports variances to the Board. |
| **Department Heads** | Responsible for updating the "Current Value" and "Comments" for objectives within their specific domain by the 5th of every month. |
| **Governance Committee** | Audits the dashboard process annually to ensure it effectively measures mission impact. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain the Strategic Plan Tracking Dashboard results in a lack of transparency and accountability.
*   **For Officers:** Failure to update or falsification of dashboard data may result in disciplinary action or removal for cause under **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.3**.
*   **For the Organization:** Failure to track performance against federal grant objectives may result in the suspension or termination of the federal award (2 CFR § 200.339).
