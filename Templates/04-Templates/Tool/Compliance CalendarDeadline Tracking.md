### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish a centralized, mandatory **Compliance Calendar** to track and manage all statutory, regulatory, contractual, and governance deadlines. This tool serves as the primary mechanism for **Accountability**, ensuring Study GRC Inc. maintains its legal standing, tax-exempt status, and grant eligibility by preventing missed filings or lapsed requirements.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to the Board of Directors, Operational Officers (CEO, CFO), and Department Heads. It covers:
*   **Federal Filings:** IRS (Tax-Exempt), Department of Labor.
*   **State Filings:** Texas Secretary of State, Texas Comptroller, and other states where the Organization has "nexus" (employees or physical presence).
*   **Governance Requirements:** Mandated by the Bylaws (meetings, elections).
*   **Grant Compliance:** Federal (2 CFR 200) and private foundation reporting cycles.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Statutory Deadline** | A date fixed by law (e.g., IRS Form 990 due date) which cannot be moved without a formal extension request. |
| **Governance Deadline** | A date fixed by the Organization's Bylaws (e.g., Annual Meeting, Officer Elections). |
| **Nexus** | A connection with a state (such as employing remote staff) that triggers tax or reporting obligations in that specific jurisdiction. |
| **Responsible Owner** | The specific individual (by role) accountable for executing the filing or task. |
| **Cure Period** | The window of time allowed to correct a missed deadline before penalties or revocation of status occurs. |

### 4. Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1. Centralized Tracking System
The Organization must maintain a digital, cloud-based Compliance Calendar (e.g., Asana, Jira, or a dedicated GRC platform). This system must:
1.  Send automated email notifications to the **Responsible Owner** and the **Executive Director** at 60, 30, and 7 days prior to the deadline.
2.  Require a "Proof of Completion" attachment (e.g., filing receipt, confirmation number) to close a task.
3.  Be accessible to the Board of Directors for oversight purposes.

#### 4.3. Foreign Qualification (Remote Work Nexus)
Because Study GRC Inc. is "Remote-First," the HR Department must update the Compliance Calendar whenever an employee is hired in a new state.
*   **Requirement:** Register as a "Foreign Non-Profit Corporation" in the employee's state of residence.
*   **Deadline:** Typically within 30 days of hire.
*   **Tracking:** Annual report deadlines for *each* state must be added to the Master Calendar.

#### 4.4. Deadline Extension Protocol
If a **Statutory Deadline** cannot be met:
1.  The Responsible Owner must notify the Executive Director at least 7 days prior to the deadline.
2.  A formal extension (e.g., IRS Form 8868) must be filed *before* the original deadline expires.
3.  The Compliance Calendar must be updated immediately with the extended due date.

#### 4.5. Verification and Audit
*   **Monthly Review:** The Secretary shall review the Compliance Calendar during the monthly Board Meeting to confirm all past-due items were completed.
*   **Document Retention:** Proof of filing (receipts) must be stored in the central repository (Google Drive/SharePoint) in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Owner of the Compliance Calendar tool. Responsible for all Governance and State Corporate filings (Periodic Reports). |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Responsible for all IRS (990, 941), State Tax, and Financial Audit deadlines. |
| **Executive Director** | Responsible for Grant reporting, SAM.gov maintenance, and operational filings. |
| **HR Manager** | Responsible for multi-state registrations (Foreign Qualification) triggered by remote employees. |
| **Board of Directors** | Oversight. Reviews the "Compliance Report" during monthly meetings to ensure no critical deadlines are missed. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain the Compliance Calendar or missing a **Statutory Deadline** due to negligence may result in:
1.  **Disciplinary Action:** Up to and including termination for the Responsible Owner.
2.  **Financial Liability:** The Organization may seek reimbursement for avoidable penalties or interest from the negligent party, to the extent permitted by law.
3.  **Governance Consequences:** Failure to file the Periodic Report or Form 990 for three consecutive years results in automatic revocation of tax-exempt status and corporate charter; this is considered a breach of Fiduciary Duty.
