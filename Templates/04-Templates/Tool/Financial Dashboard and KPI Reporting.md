### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to define the mandatory specifications, metrics, and reporting cadence for the **Financial Dashboard and KPI Reporting** tool. This tool serves as the primary mechanism for the Board of Directors to exercise its fiduciary duty of oversight, ensuring the financial solvency of Study GRC Inc., monitoring compliance with federal grant requirements (2 CFR 200), and facilitating data-driven strategic decisions. This standard ensures that financial data is presented in a digestible, visual, and accurate format to support the Board's governance role as defined in **Bylaws Article III**.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to:
*   **The Treasurer and CFO:** Responsible for the preparation and validation of the dashboard.
*   **The Board of Directors:** Responsible for reviewing the dashboard to satisfy their Duty of Care.
*   **Grant Managers:** Responsible for providing program-specific financial data.

This document governs the *format and content* of the monthly financial presentation; it does not replace the full detailed financial statements (Statement of Financial Position/Activities) required for the annual audit.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Days Cash on Hand (DCOH)** | The number of days the Organization can continue to pay its operating expenses using the cash currently available, assuming no new revenue comes in. |
| **Current Ratio** | A liquidity ratio that measures the Organization's ability to pay short-term obligations or those due within one year (Current Assets / Current Liabilities). |
| **Program Expense Ratio** | The percentage of total expenses spent directly on the Organization's mission (Program Services) versus administrative or fundraising costs. |
| **Restricted Net Assets** | Funds that are subject to donor-imposed restrictions (e.g., specific grants or time-bound limitations). |
| **Burn Rate** | The rate at which the Organization spends its cash pool over a specific period, usually monthly. |
| **Variance** | The difference between the budgeted amount and the actual amount incurred or received. |

### 4. Dashboard Specifications and Reporting Standards

#### 4.1 Reporting Frequency and Distribution
In accordance with **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.4** (Regular Meetings), the Financial Dashboard must be updated and distributed monthly.
*   **Timing:** The dashboard must be distributed to the Board of Directors via the secure board portal no less than **three (3) business days** prior to the scheduled Board meeting.
*   **Format:** The dashboard must be presented as a single-page (or digital equivalent) high-level summary, accompanied by the detailed financial packet.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Visual Indicators (Traffic Light System)
To facilitate rapid assessment by non-financial Board members, the dashboard must utilize a "Traffic Light" status indicator for key metrics:
*   **Green:** Metric is within target range or better (e.g., Budget variance < 5%).
*   **Yellow:** Metric is outside target range but manageable; requires monitoring (e.g., Budget variance 5% - 10%).
*   **Red:** Metric is at a critical level; requires immediate Board discussion and corrective action (e.g., Budget variance > 10% or DCOH < 60 days).

#### 4.3 Required Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The dashboard must track the following four categories of metrics:

**4.3.1 Liquidity & Solvency (Health)**
*   **Days Cash on Hand:** Target > 90 Days.
*   **Current Ratio:** Target > 1.5.
*   **Cash Balance:** Split visualization showing *Unrestricted Cash* vs. *Restricted Cash*.

**4.3.2 Budget Performance (Execution)**
*   **YTD Revenue vs. Budget:** Visual bar chart.
*   **YTD Expenses vs. Budget:** Visual bar chart.
*   **Significant Variances:** A list of any line item with a variance greater than **10% or $5,000** (whichever is lower), accompanied by a brief explanatory note.

**4.3.3 Grant & Program Compliance (Federal/Mission)**
*   **Grant Spend-Down Rate:** Percentage of grant funds utilized vs. time elapsed in the grant period (to prevent under-spending or over-spending).
*   **Program Expense Ratio:** Target > 75% (to ensure 501(c)(3) mission alignment).
*   **Cost per Beneficiary:** (e.g., Cost per student educated or certification granted).

**4.3.4 Fundraising & Revenue (Sustainability)**
*   **Donor Retention Rate:** Percentage of recurring donors.
*   **Average Gift Size:** Trend line over the last 12 months.
*   **Diversification Index:** Visual pie chart showing revenue mix (Grants vs. Individual Donations vs. Corporate Sponsorships).

#### 4.4 Narrative Summary
The dashboard must include a "CFO Commentary" section (max 200 words) summarizing:
1.  Major cash inflows/outflows expected in the next 30 days.
2.  Status of any pending large grants.
3.  Any emerging financial risks (e.g., inflation impact, vendor price hikes).

#### 4.5 Technology Platform
*   The dashboard shall be generated using the Organization's approved accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks Online, Xero) or a connected visualization tool (e.g., Tableau, PowerBI).
*   Manual spreadsheet manipulation should be minimized to reduce the risk of human error.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns the dashboard tool; ensures data accuracy; writes the narrative commentary; distributes to the Board. |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews the dashboard for irregularities prior to distribution; leads the financial discussion during the Board meeting. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews the dashboard monthly; questions "Red" status indicators; approves corrective actions for significant variances. |
| **Grant Managers** | Provide accurate spending data and projections for restricted funds to the CFO by the 5th of each month. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Fiduciary Duty:** Failure to maintain and review this dashboard may constitute a breach of the Board's Duty of Care under Texas Business Organizations Code.
*   **Grant Compliance:** Failure to track restricted funds via this dashboard may result in non-compliance with **2 CFR 200**, leading to the loss of federal funding.
*   **Internal Controls:** Intentional manipulation of dashboard data to hide financial distress is a serious violation of the **Code of Ethics** and may result in immediate termination and legal action.
