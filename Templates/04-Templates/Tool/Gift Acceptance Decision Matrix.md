### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Gift Acceptance Decision Matrix is to provide a standardized, risk-based framework for evaluating potential contributions to Study GRC Inc. This tool ensures that the acceptance of any gift supports the Organization’s charitable mission, protects its tax-exempt status under IRS 501(c)(3), and mitigates reputational, legal, and financial risks. This document operationalizes the requirements set forth in the *Gift Acceptance Policy* and aligns with the financial transparency mandates in **Article VIII** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This matrix applies to all forms of contributions offered to Study GRC Inc., including cash, tangible personal property, intangible property (IP), real estate, cryptocurrency, and in-kind services.

**Applies to:**
*   Board of Directors
*   Operational Officers (CEO, CFO, DKG)
*   Fundraising Committees and Volunteers

**Exclusions:**
*   Standard government grant applications (governed by *Grant Management Procedures*).
*   Fee-for-service contracts (governed by *Procurement Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Unrestricted Gift** | A contribution where the donor places no limitations on how the funds or assets may be used. |
| **Restricted Gift** | A contribution where the donor specifies a particular use (e.g., "for the scholarship fund only") or time period for expenditure. |
| **UBIT** | Unrelated Business Income Tax. Income generated from a trade or business regularly carried on that is not substantially related to the charitable purpose. |
| **Illiquid Asset** | Assets that cannot be easily converted into cash (e.g., private stock, real estate, complex artwork). |
| **OFAC** | Office of Foreign Assets Control. A federal agency that enforces economic and trade sanctions. |
| **Vendor Neutrality** | The principle that Study GRC Inc. does not endorse specific commercial products. Gifts requiring endorsement violate this principle. |

### 4. Decision Matrix (Traffic Light Protocol)

All potential gifts must be categorized into one of three levels based on the criteria below.

#### 4.1 Level 1: Green Light (Pre-Approved)
*Authority: Operational Officers (CEO/CFO) may accept immediately without Board review.*

| Criteria | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Asset Type** | Cash, Checks, Credit Card donations, ACH transfers. |
| **Asset Type** | Publicly traded securities (stocks/bonds) with immediate liquidity. |
| **Value** | Single contributions under $10,000 USD. |
| **Restrictions** | Unrestricted or restricted to Board-approved existing programs (e.g., "Scholarship Fund"). |
| **Source** | Individuals, Foundations, or Corporations with no known negative reputational history. |
| **Conditions** | No goods or services provided in exchange (other than de minimis token items). |

#### 4.2 Level 2: Yellow Light (Review Required)
*Authority: Requires review by the Treasurer and approval by the Executive Director. May be escalated to the Finance Committee if deemed necessary.*

| Criteria | Description | Risk Factor |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Asset Type** | Cryptocurrency. | Volatility; Wallet Security; Anonymity. |
| **Asset Type** | In-Kind Software/Hardware. | Hidden costs of maintenance; Vendor lock-in. |
| **Value** | Single contributions between $10,000 and $50,000 USD. | AML (Anti-Money Laundering) threshold; Reliance risk. |
| **Restrictions** | Funding for a *new* program not yet approved by the Board. | Mission drift; Administrative burden. |
| **Conditions** | Naming rights (e.g., "The [Donor] Scholarship"). | Reputational association duration. |
| **Source** | Foreign donors (Non-US). | Anti-Terrorism/OFAC compliance. |

#### 4.3 Level 3: Red Light (Board Approval or Rejection)
*Authority: Must be rejected by the CEO OR referred to the full Board of Directors for a vote. High presumption of rejection.*

| Criteria | Description | Risk Factor |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Asset Type** | Real Estate; Private (Closely Held) Stock; Time-Shares; Automobiles. | Environmental liability; Liquidation difficulty; UBIT. |
| **Value** | Contributions exceeding $50,000 USD (Significant reliance). | Disproportionate influence on operations. |
| **Restrictions** | Restrictions that violate the Organization's Open Source commitment (e.g., donor demands IP ownership). | Violation of Bylaws Art. IX §9.3; Loss of 501(c)(3) status. |
| **Conditions** | Quid Pro Quo requiring endorsement of a commercial product (e.g., "Must recommend our GRC tool"). | Violation of Vendor Neutrality; UBIT. |
| **Conditions** | Gifts with "Clawback" provisions based on arbitrary metrics. | Financial instability. |
| **Source** | Entities on the OFAC Sanctions List or known hate groups. | Illegal; Severe reputational damage. |

### 5. Risk Assessment Questionnaire

For any gift falling into **Level 2 (Yellow)** or **Level 3 (Red)**, the following assessment must be documented prior to acceptance.

**5.1 Financial Feasibility**
*   Does the asset require cash to maintain (insurance, taxes, storage)?
*   Is the asset marketable? Can it be sold within 30 days?
*   Will acceptance trigger UBIT?

**5.2 Legal & Compliance**
*   Does the donor require a valuation? (Note: Study GRC **must not** provide appraisals for tax purposes).
*   Does the gift create a Conflict of Interest as defined in **Bylaws Article IX**?
*   Is the donor legally authorized to transfer the asset?

**5.3 Mission Alignment**
*   Does the gift impose restrictions that prevent the content from being Open Source?
*   Does the gift imply an endorsement of a specific political candidate or legislation? (Prohibited by 501(c)(3) regulations).
*   Is the administrative cost of tracking the restricted funds higher than the value of the gift?

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fundraising Lead / DKG** | Initial intake of gift offer. Categorizes gift using the Traffic Light Protocol. Rejects obvious Level 3 prohibited items (e.g., illegal items). |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Conducts financial due diligence on Level 2 and Level 3 gifts. Ensures AML/KYC checks are performed on donors >$5,000. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Approves Level 2 gifts. Escalates Level 3 gifts to the Board. Signs Gift Agreements. |
| **Board of Directors** | Final authority on Level 3 gifts. Reviews and updates this Matrix bi-annually. |
| **Legal Counsel (External)** | Consulted for Real Estate, complex IP, or gifts involving significant contractual obligations. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to utilize this matrix when accepting gifts may result in the acceptance of liabilities that threaten the Organization's solvency or tax-exempt status.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Staff or Officers accepting gifts in violation of this matrix (specifically Level 3 items without Board vote) are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.
*   **Return of Gifts:** Gifts accepted in error that violate legal statutes or IRS regulations must be returned to the donor immediately, documented by a *Donation Refund and Reversal* record.
