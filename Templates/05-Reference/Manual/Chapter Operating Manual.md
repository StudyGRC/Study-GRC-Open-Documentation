### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Manual is to establish a standardized operational framework for all Local Chapters affiliated with Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). It ensures that all Chapters operate in alignment with the Organization’s 501(c)(3) charitable mission, maintain strict financial and legal compliance, adhere to youth protection standards, and uphold the Organization's brand integrity. This Manual serves as the primary reference for Chapter leadership regarding rights, responsibilities, and operational procedures.

### 2. Scope
This Manual applies to all recognized Local Chapters, Chapter Officers, volunteers acting on behalf of a Chapter, and prospective groups seeking charter status.

**Exclusions:** This Manual does not apply to informal study groups or online-only cohorts that have not entered into a formal Charter Agreement with the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Local Chapter** | An officially chartered subdivision of the Organization, authorized to operate in a specific geographic area to advance the Organization's mission. |
| **Charter Agreement** | The binding legal contract between Study GRC Inc. (HQ) and the Local Chapter leadership that grants the right to use the name and resources. |
| **HQ** | The central governing body and operational leadership of Study GRC Inc. |
| **Good Standing** | A status indicating the Chapter is current on all reporting, financial, and training requirements. |
| **Work Product** | Any content, curriculum, code, or resources created by the Chapter, which belongs to the Organization per Bylaws Article IX. |

### 4. Procedures and Standards

#### 4.1 Chapter Formation and Chartering
To establish a Local Chapter, the following steps must be completed:
1.  **Application:** A minimum of three (3) Voting Members in good standing must submit a Chapter Application to HQ.
2.  **Leadership Designation:** The application must identify a Chapter President and a Chapter Treasurer. These individuals must pass background checks in accordance with the *Background Check Policy*.
3.  **Charter Agreement:** Upon approval, Chapter Officers must sign the *Local Chapter Affiliation Agreement*.
4.  **Probationary Period:** New Chapters operate under a provisional status for six (6) months, subject to monthly reviews by the VP of Education & Training.

#### 4.2 Legal Status and Governance
4.2.1 **Legal Structure:** Local Chapters are **not** separate legal entities. They are administrative branches of Study GRC Inc. Chapters must not incorporate separately, file their own Articles of Incorporation, or obtain a separate Employer Identification Number (EIN) without express written consent from the Board of Directors.
4.2.2 **Bylaws Adherence:** Chapters must adhere to the *Bylaws of Study GRC Inc.* Chapters may not adopt local bylaws that conflict with the Organization's Bylaws.
4.2.3 **Dissolution:** In the event a Chapter is dissolved or its Charter revoked, all assets, records, and data must be immediately returned to HQ.

#### 4.3 Financial Management
4.3.1 **Banking:** Chapters are prohibited from opening independent bank accounts. All financial transactions must be processed through HQ or a centralized banking sub-account system managed by the Chief Financial Officer (CFO).
4.3.2 **Revenue and Dues:**
*   If the Chapter collects event fees or donations, these funds are the property of Study GRC Inc. and must be remitted to HQ within five (5) business days.
*   Chapters operate on a budget allocation model approved annually by HQ.
4.3.3 **Contracting Authority:** Chapter Officers **do not** have the authority to sign contracts (e.g., venue rentals, vendor agreements) that exceed $500 or extend beyond a single event duration. Contracts exceeding these limits must be signed by the Executive Director or President of the Board.

#### 4.4 Brand Identity and Intellectual Property
4.4.1 **Logo Usage:** Chapters must use the specific Chapter Logo provided by HQ. Modification of the logo, colors, or fonts is strictly prohibited without approval from the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).
4.4.2 **Intellectual Property Assignment:** Pursuant to **Article IX, Section 9.3** of the Bylaws, all educational materials, presentations, and resources created by the Chapter are "works made for hire." Ownership resides with Study GRC Inc. to ensure they remain open-source and free for the community.
4.4.3 **Social Media:** Chapter social media accounts must follow the naming convention `@StudyGRC_[CityName]` and must grant administrative access to the HQ Marketing team for continuity.

#### 4.5 Events and Operations
4.5.1 **Insurance:** All in-person events must be registered with HQ at least 30 days in advance to ensure coverage under the Organization’s General Liability Insurance policy.
4.5.2 **Code of Conduct:** The *Professional Ethics and Community Guidelines* must be read or displayed at the start of every Chapter event.
4.5.3 **Youth Protection (Mandatory):**
*   Because the Organization serves K-12 populations, strict adherence to the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* is required.
*   **Two-Deep Leadership:** No adult may ever be alone with a minor during any Chapter activity. A minimum of two background-checked adults must be present at all times.
*   **Reporting:** Any suspicion of abuse or misconduct must be reported to HQ and local authorities immediately.

#### 4.6 Reporting Requirements
Chapters must submit the following to the VP of Education & Training:
1.  **Quarterly Activity Report:** detailing events held, attendance numbers, and success stories.
2.  **Financial Reconciliation:** (If applicable) submitting receipts for approved expense reimbursements within 30 days of expenditure.
3.  **Annual Officer Roster:** Updated contact information for local leadership by December 31st.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Primary HQ liaison for Chapters; approves budgets and event plans; monitors compliance. |
| **Chapter President** | Local operational leader; ensures events occur; primary point of contact for HQ; ensures Code of Conduct enforcement. |
| **Chapter Treasurer** | Manages local budget requests; submits receipts to HQ; ensures no unauthorized financial accounts exist. |
| **Executive Director** | Signatory authority for high-value Chapter contracts; final arbiter of Charter disputes. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Manual may result in:
1.  **Probation:** Temporary suspension of funding and event authorization.
2.  **Removal of Officers:** HQ reserves the right to remove Chapter Officers for cause (e.g., ethical violations, financial mismanagement).
3.  **Charter Revocation:** Permanent dissolution of the Chapter relationship.

**Zero Tolerance:** Any violation of the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* or financial fraud will result in immediate Charter revocation and referral to law enforcement.
