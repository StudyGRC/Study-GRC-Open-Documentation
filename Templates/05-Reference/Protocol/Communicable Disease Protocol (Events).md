### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to establish standardized measures to mitigate the transmission of communicable diseases (including but not limited to COVID-19, Influenza, and Norovirus) during in-person events organized by Study GRC Inc. This protocol aligns with CDC Best Practices to fulfill the Organization's duty of care to its members, staff, and the public, while ensuring business continuity and minimizing liability.

### 2. Scope
This protocol applies to all **in-person** gatherings, conferences, workshops, and meetings hosted or legally organized by Study GRC Inc., regardless of location.
*   **Applies to:** All employees, Board Directors, volunteers, contractors, attendees, speakers, and vendors present at the event site.
*   **Exclusions:** Virtual-only events; third-party events where Study GRC Inc. is solely a sponsor or attendee (in such cases, the host organization’s protocols apply).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Communicable Disease** | An infectious disease transmissible (as from person to person) by direct contact with an affected individual or the individual's discharges or by indirect means (as by a vector). |
| **Close Contact** | Defined per CDC guidelines as being within 6 feet of an infected person for a cumulative total of 15 minutes or more over a 24-hour period. |
| **Event Safety Officer** | The designated individual (staff or volunteer) responsible for on-site health and safety enforcement. |
| **Vulnerable Population** | Individuals at higher risk for severe illness, including older adults and people of any age with certain underlying medical conditions. |

### 4. Protocol Statements

#### 4.1 Pre-Event Risk Assessment
Prior to any in-person event, the Event Manager must conduct a risk assessment based on current community transmission levels in the host location.
1.  **Transmission Review:** Consult CDC "COVID-19 by County" or local health department data 14 days prior to the event.
2.  **Venue Vetting:** Ensure the venue has adequate ventilation (HVAC filtration or ability to open windows) and sanitation facilities.
3.  **Communication:** Pre-event emails must include a "Health & Safety" section outlining expectations.

#### 4.2 Participant Health Screening & Attendance
To protect the community, the following rules apply strictly to attendance:
1.  **Symptom Prohibition:** No individual may enter the event space if they have experienced symptoms of a communicable disease (fever >100.4°F, new cough, vomiting, diarrhea) within the last 24 hours.
2.  **Exposure Protocol:** Individuals who have had a known Close Contact with a confirmed case of a highly infectious disease (e.g., COVID-19) within the last 5 days must wear a high-quality mask (N95/KN95) while indoors or test negative prior to entry, subject to current CDC guidance.
3.  **Right to Deny Entry:** Study GRC Inc. reserves the right to deny entry to any individual exhibiting visible symptoms of illness. Refunds for health-related cancellations will be handled liberally to encourage sick participants to stay home.

#### 4.3 On-Site Hygiene and Prevention Measures
1.  **Sanitization:** Hand sanitizer (at least 60% alcohol) must be available at registration desks, food stations, and entryways.
2.  **Masking:**
    *   **Low/Medium Risk Levels:** Masks are optional but welcomed.
    *   **High Risk Levels:** The Organization may mandate masking for indoor activities.
3.  **Food Service:** Buffet-style meals should be avoided during periods of high transmission. If used, sneeze guards and hand sanitizer must be present at the start of the line.
4.  **Physical Distancing:** Where feasible, seating arrangements should allow for spacing between non-household members.

#### 4.4 Symptom Management During Event
If a participant develops symptoms while at the event:
1.  **Immediate Isolation:** The individual must be immediately separated from the group. A designated "Isolation Area" (or the individual's hotel room/vehicle) must be identified beforehand.
2.  **Masking:** The symptomatic individual must be provided with a medical-grade mask immediately.
3.  **Departure:** The individual must leave the event premises as soon as safely possible.
4.  **Youth Participants:** If the symptomatic individual is a minor, the **Authorized Pick-Up/Release Procedure** must be followed immediately to transfer custody to the parent/guardian. Two-Deep Leadership must be maintained while isolating the minor.

#### 4.5 Post-Event Reporting and Notification
1.  **Self-Reporting:** Participants are encouraged to report to the Event Safety Officer if they test positive for a communicable disease within 5 days of the event conclusion.
2.  **Notification:** Upon receipt of a confirmed report, Study GRC Inc. will notify all attendees of a potential exposure.
    *   **Anonymity:** The identity of the infected individual **must** remain confidential and will not be disclosed in the notification, in compliance with privacy standards and the *Director of Kindness and Generosity's* mandate for respectful community treatment.
    *   **Content:** The notice will include the date of exposure and a link to CDC guidance for monitoring symptoms.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final authority on event cancellation or modification due to public health risks. |
| **Event Manager / Safety Officer** | Conducts pre-event risk assessment; enforces on-site protocols; manages isolation of symptomatic attendees. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Manages communication with symptomatic individuals to ensure they are treated with dignity and empathy while being asked to leave; handles post-event exposure notifications. |
| **Participants/Attendees** | Responsible for self-monitoring symptoms and adhering to hygiene requirements. |
| **Parents/Guardians** | Responsible for immediate pickup of symptomatic minors. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Refusal to Comply:** Any individual refusing to comply with hygiene mandates (e.g., masking when required) or refusing to leave upon displaying symptoms will be removed from the venue.
*   **Liability:** Participation in in-person events is voluntary. Per the *Event Participant Liability Waiver*, attendees assume the risk of exposure. However, gross negligence by staff in enforcing this protocol may result in disciplinary action.
*   **Indemnification:** Staff and volunteers acting in good faith to enforce this protocol are indemnified per **Bylaws Article VII**.
