### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this protocol is to operationalize **Article VI, Section 6.2** of the Study GRC Inc. Bylaws. It establishes strict channels of communication between the Organization’s Operational Officers and the Advisory Board. This protocol ensures that:
1.  The Board of Directors maintains its role as the sole governing authority.
2.  The Advisory Board remains a strategic, non-binding advisory body and does not inadvertently drift into operational management.
3.  All strategic advice is transparently recorded and reported to the Board of Directors to prevent information asymmetry or "shadow governance."

### 2. Scope
This protocol applies to:
*   **Voting Directors:** Members of the legal Board of Directors.
*   **Advisory Board Members:** Volunteers appointed to provide non-binding advice.
*   **Operational Officers:** The Executive Director (CEO), CISO, CFO, VP of Education, and other staff/volunteers managing day-to-day operations.

**Exclusions:** This protocol does not apply to purely social interactions (e.g., greetings at public events) provided no Organization business, strategy, or operational matters are discussed.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Facilitated Communication** | Communication where a Voting Director is either physically present (video/in-person) or included in the correspondence (cc'd on email, present in a group chat) to oversee the exchange. |
| **Formal Communication** | Any exchange of information regarding the Organization's strategy, finances, operations, personnel, or intellectual property. |
| **Mandatory Reporting** | The requirement to formally document and disclose specific interactions to the Board of Directors within a set timeframe. |
| **Shadow Governance** | A risk scenario where advisors or non-fiduciaries influence operational decisions without the knowledge or consent of the legal Board of Directors. |

### 4. Protocol Statements

#### 4.1 General Prohibition on Direct Access
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI, Section 6.2**, Operational Officers are **prohibited** from initiating or engaging in Formal Communication with Advisory Board members regarding Organization business without the express prior approval and facilitation of a Voting Director.

#### 4.2 Authorized Communication Channels
All business-related communication between Operations and the Advisory Board must occur through one of the following channels:
1.  **Joint Meetings:** Scheduled meetings where at least one Voting Director is present to moderate.
2.  **Facilitated Email:** Emails where a Voting Director is included in the "To" or "Cc" line.
3.  **Monitored Digital Channels:** Designated Slack/Discord channels where Voting Directors have visibility. Direct Messages (DMs) regarding business matters between Officers and Advisors are strictly prohibited.

#### 4.3 Request for Engagement
If an Operational Officer requires specific expertise from an Advisory Board member:
1.  The Officer must submit a written request to the Board President or Secretary.
2.  The request must detail the **Purpose**, **Topic**, and **Desired Outcome** of the consultation.
3.  Upon approval, a Voting Director will schedule and attend the meeting or facilitate the email introduction.

#### 4.4 Mandatory Reporting Procedure
In the event that communication occurs between an Operational Officer and an Advisory Board member (whether approved, accidental, or unsolicited), the Operational Officer must execute the following reporting steps:

**4.4.1 Reporting Trigger**
A report must be filed if:
*   An approved facilitated meeting concludes.
*   An Advisory Board member reaches out directly to an Officer regarding business.
*   An accidental or unauthorized discussion regarding business occurs.

**4.4.2 Reporting Timeline**
*   **Standard:** Within 48 hours of the communication.
*   **Urgent/Risk:** Immediately, if the communication involves legal risks, PR crises, or financial commitments.

**4.4.3 Reporting Format**
The Officer must submit a **Briefing Note** to the Board of Directors (via the Secretary or designated Board communication channel) containing:
1.  **Date/Time of Contact.**
2.  **Participants.**
3.  **Nature of Communication** (e.g., Strategic Advice, Technical Question, Unsolicited Feedback).
4.  **Summary of Discussion.**
5.  **Action Items or Recommendations** resulting from the exchange.

#### 4.5 Handling Unsolicited Contact
If an Advisory Board member contacts an Operational Officer directly regarding business:
1.  The Officer must politely pause the conversation.
2.  The Officer must state: *"Per our Governance Bylaws, I need to loop in a Board Director before we discuss this further."*
3.  The Officer must immediately copy a Voting Director into the communication loop.
4.  The Officer must file a report per Section 4.4.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board President** | Approves requests for engagement; designates Directors to facilitate specific Advisory interactions. |
| **Voting Directors** | Act as chaperones/facilitators for all communications; ensure advice aligns with strategic goals; intervene if Advisors overstep into operations. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains the *Officer-Advisory Board Communication Log*; ensures Briefing Notes are included in Board Meeting minutes. |
| **Operational Officers** | Adhere to the prohibition on direct contact; submit engagement requests; file Mandatory Reports for all interactions. |
| **Advisory Board** | Respect communication boundaries; direct all strategic advice to the Board of Directors rather than operational staff. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Strict adherence to this protocol is required to maintain the Organization's legal governance structure.

*   **Operational Officers:** Failure to report communications or engaging in unauthorized "back-channel" communications with Advisors may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer service.
*   **Advisory Board Members:** Repeated attempts to bypass the Board of Directors or direct operational staff may result in removal from the Advisory Board pursuant to the *Advisory Board Appointment/Removal Process*.
