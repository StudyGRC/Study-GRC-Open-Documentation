### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to define the authority, responsibilities, and qualification requirements for the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This position is established pursuant to **Article V, Section 5.2** of the Bylaws to ensure the Organization maintains financial integrity, adheres to Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), complies with federal grant regulations (2 CFR 200), and fulfills all tax-exempt reporting obligations.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to the individual appointed by the Board of Directors to serve as the CFO. This role is an **Operational Officer** position and is distinct from the Board Director role of **Treasurer**, though the two roles require close collaboration.

### 4. Position Responsibilities

The CFO is responsible for the strategic and tactical management of the Organization's financial operations.

#### 4.1 Financial Planning and Analysis
*   **Budgeting:** Lead the development of the annual operating budget in collaboration with the Executive Director and Department Heads for Board approval.
*   **Forecasting:** Maintain a rolling 12-month cash flow forecast to ensure solvency and operational continuity.
*   **Strategic Advisory:** Provide financial modeling and risk analysis for new programs, partnerships, or "Fundamental Actions" (as defined in Bylaws Article X).

#### 4.2 Accounting and Controllership
*   **Bookkeeping:** Oversee or perform day-to-day recording of financial transactions, ensuring accurate coding to the Chart of Accounts.
*   **Month-End Close:** Execute the month-end close process within 15 days of the month's end, including bank reconciliations and accruals.
*   **Financial Reporting:** Prepare monthly financial statements (Statement of Financial Position, Statement of Activities, Budget vs. Actuals) for the Executive Director and the Board Treasurer.

#### 4.3 Grant Management and Federal Compliance
*   **Uniform Guidance:** Ensure all financial operations comply strictly with **2 CFR Part 200** (Uniform Guidance), specifically regarding Allowable Costs (Subpart E) and Procurement (Subpart D).
*   **Drawdowns:** Manage the timing and execution of federal fund drawdowns to minimize cash on hand, in compliance with federal cash management standards.
*   **Reporting:** Prepare and submit Federal Financial Reports (FFR/SF-425) and maintain the Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA).
*   **Time & Effort:** Oversee the collection and retention of Personnel Activity Reports (PARs) for all staff paid via federal funds.

#### 4.4 Treasury and Risk Management
*   **Cash Management:** Manage banking relationships and monitor cash balances to meet obligations.
*   **Internal Controls:** Design, implement, and monitor the **Internal Controls Framework** to prevent fraud, waste, and abuse.
*   **Audit Coordination:** Serve as the primary liaison for the annual independent audit and/or Single Audit (if the $750,000 federal expenditure threshold is met).
*   **Tax Compliance:** Ensure timely filing of IRS Form 990, state franchise tax reports, and charitable solicitation registrations.

#### 4.5 Operational Leadership
*   **Policy Enforcement:** Enforce the Travel & Expense Reimbursement Policy and Procurement Policy across the organization.
*   **Team Management:** Supervise any volunteer bookkeepers, grant managers, or contracted accountants.

### 5. Reporting Structure and Authority

#### 5.1 Dual Reporting Relationship
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.2**, the CFO operates under a dual reporting structure to ensure both operational efficiency and fiduciary oversight:
1.  **Operational Reporting:** The CFO reports directly to the **Executive Director (CEO)** for day-to-day priorities, performance management, and strategic alignment.
2.  **Fiduciary Reporting:** The CFO has a dotted-line reporting relationship to the **Board Treasurer**. The CFO must provide the Treasurer with transparent access to all financial records and must immediately report any financial irregularities or potential solvency risks to the Treasurer.

#### 5.2 Authority Limits
*   **Bank Access:** The CFO is an authorized signer on bank accounts but requires a second signature (typically the Treasurer or President) for transactions exceeding the threshold defined in the **Signature Authority Policy**.
*   **Contracts:** The CFO may sign vendor contracts within the approved budget up to the limit defined in the **Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix**.

### 6. Qualifications and Requirements

#### 6.1 Professional Qualifications
*   **Education:** Bachelor’s degree in Accounting, Finance, or Business Administration required. CPA (Certified Public Accountant) or MBA preferred.
*   **Experience:** Minimum of 5 years of financial management experience. Experience with 501(c)(3) non-profit accounting and federal grant management (2 CFR 200) is mandatory.
*   **Technical Skills:** Proficiency in cloud-based accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks Online, Xero), Excel/Google Sheets (advanced modeling), and payroll systems.

#### 6.2 Ethical and Legal Requirements
*   **Background Check:** Must pass a comprehensive criminal background check and credit check (graded adjudication) due to access to financial assets and sensitive data.
*   **Conflict of Interest:** Must execute the Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure and recuse themselves from transactions where a personal interest exists.
*   **Fiduciary Duty:** Must act in good faith and with the care an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would exercise under similar circumstances (Texas BOC § 22.221).

### 7. Roles and Responsibilities Matrix

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **CFO** | Executes day-to-day financial operations, compliance, and reporting. |
| **Executive Director** | Sets strategic priorities; approves operational expenses within budget. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides Board-level oversight; reviews monthly reports; presents financials to the Board. |
| **Audit Committee** | Selects independent auditors; reviews audit results; oversees whistleblower complaints. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Annual Budget; approves the CFO appointment; bears ultimate fiduciary liability. |

### 8. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to perform the duties outlined in this position description, or violation of financial policies (including but not limited to the **Anti-Fraud Policy** or **Conflict of Interest Policy**), may result in immediate removal from office by the Board of Directors or the Executive Director, termination of employment/volunteer status, and potential civil or criminal legal action.
