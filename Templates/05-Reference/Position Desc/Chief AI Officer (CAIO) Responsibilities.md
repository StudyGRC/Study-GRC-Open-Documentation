### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to formally establish the role, authority, and responsibilities of the **Chief Artificial Intelligence Officer (CAIO)** within Study GRC Inc. As an organization dedicated to Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC), it is imperative that our internal adoption of Artificial Intelligence (AI) exemplifies the highest standards of ethics, safety, and transparency. This role is established to ensure compliance with emerging federal guidance (including OMB M-24-10 and Executive Order 14110) and to mitigate risks associated with algorithmic bias, data privacy, and intellectual property in the context of our educational mission.

### 2. Scope
This Position Description applies to the individual appointed by the Board of Directors or Executive Director to serve as the CAIO.
*   **Applies to:** The CAIO’s oversight authority extends to all Departments, employees, volunteers, and contractors utilizing AI systems, Generative AI tools, or automated decision-making processes.
*   **Exclusions:** This role is distinct from the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), though the two roles must collaborate closely. The CAIO focuses on model governance, ethics, and output validity, while the CISO focuses on the security of the underlying infrastructure and data confidentiality.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **AI System** | A machine-based system that can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, make predictions, recommendations, or decisions influencing real or virtual environments. |
| **Generative AI** | The class of AI models that emulate the structure and characteristics of input data in order to generate derived synthetic content (text, images, code). |
| **Safety-Impacting AI** | AI systems whose output produces actions or decisions that could significantly impact the safety, rights, or well-being of individuals (specifically relevant to our K-12 youth participants). |
| **NIST AI RMF** | The National Institute of Standards and Technology Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (Map, Measure, Manage, Govern). |

### 4. Position Responsibilities

Pursuant to **Article V (Operational Leadership)** of the Bylaws, the CAIO is an Operational Officer responsible for the strategic and safe deployment of AI technologies.

#### 4.1 Strategic Governance and Leadership
4.1.1 **AI Governance Board Chair:** The CAIO shall chair the internal AI Governance Board, convening representatives from Legal, IT/Security, and Education to review high-risk use cases.
4.1.2 **Policy Development:** Responsible for authoring and maintaining the *Artificial Intelligence Acceptable Use Policy* and *Generative AI Risk Assessment Standards*.
4.1.3 **Inventory Management:** Maintain a comprehensive, publicly available inventory of all AI use cases within the Organization to ensure transparency, in alignment with Bylaws Article VIII (Transparency).

#### 4.2 Risk Management (NIST AI RMF Alignment)
4.2.1 **Risk Assessments:** Conduct mandatory impact assessments for any proposed AI tool prior to procurement or deployment. This includes evaluating:
*   **Bias and Equity:** Ensuring educational content generated or curated by AI does not propagate stereotypes or historical biases.
*   **Hallucination Risk:** Verifying the accuracy of GRC educational materials generated by AI.
4.2.2 **Youth Protection:** Collaborate with the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) to ensure no AI systems interact with minors (under 18) without strict "Human-in-the-Loop" oversight and COPPA-compliant parental consent mechanisms.
4.2.3 **Intellectual Property:** Ensure that the use of Generative AI respects the Organization's commitment to Open Source principles (Bylaws Article IX) and does not infringe on third-party copyrights or inadvertently place proprietary Organization data into the public domain.

#### 4.3 Operational Compliance
4.3.1 **Vendor Vetting:** Review all third-party software procurement requests to identify embedded AI capabilities and ensure vendors meet Study GRC’s privacy standards.
4.3.2 **Training and Literacy:** Develop and deliver mandatory AI literacy training for staff and volunteers, ensuring they understand the limitations and risks of the tools they use.
4.3.3 **Incident Response:** Lead the response to any "AI Incidents," defined as events where an AI system produces harmful, biased, or legally compromising outputs.

#### 4.4 Authority to Halt Operations
4.4.1 **Stop-Work Authority:** The CAIO possesses the unilateral authority to cease the operation of any AI system that is deemed to pose an imminent risk to the Organization’s reputation, the safety of community members, or legal compliance status.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief AI Officer (CAIO)** | Primary owner of AI strategy, ethics, and model governance. Reports to the Executive Director. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Provides resources and organizational backing for the CAIO’s mandates; resolves conflicts between operational speed and AI safety. |
| **CISO** | Secures the AI infrastructure (APIs, cloud environments) and assists the CAIO with data leakage prevention (DLP). |
| **VP of Education** | Consults with CAIO to ensure AI-generated curriculum is accurate and pedagogically sound. |
| **Board of Directors** | Receives an annual "State of AI" report from the CAIO detailing risks, adoption rates, and compliance status. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

Failure of the CAIO to adequately perform these duties may result in removal from the officer position as outlined in **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.3**.

Failure of employees or volunteers to adhere to the directives issued by the CAIO regarding AI usage constitutes a violation of the *Code of Conduct* and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
