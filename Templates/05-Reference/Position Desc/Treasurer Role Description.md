### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to define the authority, responsibilities, and qualifications of the Treasurer of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). The Treasurer is a statutory officer required by the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and Article IV of the Organization's Bylaws. This role serves as the primary custodian of the Organization's financial integrity, ensuring fiscal solvency, regulatory compliance with federal grant standards (2 CFR 200), and transparency to the membership and public.

### 2. Scope
This position description applies to the individual elected by the Board of Directors to serve as Treasurer.
*   **Inclusions:** Oversight of financial strategy, banking relationships, audit compliance, and the performance of the Finance Committee.
*   **Distinction:** While the Treasurer provides governance oversight, day-to-day bookkeeping and financial execution are managed by the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or operational staff, as defined in Bylaws Section 5.2.

### 4. Position Responsibilities

#### 4.1 General Authority and Fiduciary Duties
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IV**, the Treasurer is an Officer of the Board. The Treasurer must:
*   **4.1.1** Serve as the principal financial officer of the Organization.
*   **4.1.2** Exercise a "Duty of Care" to ensure the Organization’s assets are protected, invested, and used strictly for charitable and educational purposes (Bylaws Section 1.3).
*   **4.1.3** Ensure the maintenance of correct and complete books of account and records (Bylaws Section 8.1).

#### 4.2 Financial Oversight and Reporting
*   **4.2.1 Monthly Review:** Review monthly financial statements (Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Statement of Cash Flows) prepared by the CFO/Operations team prior to Board meetings.
*   **4.2.2 Board Presentation:** Present the financial status of the Organization at regular Board meetings, highlighting variances against the budget, cash flow risks, and grant utilization rates.
*   **4.2.3 Annual Financial Report:** Oversee the preparation of the Annual Financial Report required by **TBOC § 22.352** and ensure its availability to Voting Members.
*   **4.2.4 Transparency:** Ensure the Annual Financial Report and IRS Form 990 are posted to the Organization’s "Transparency" webpage in compliance with **Bylaws Section 8.2**.

#### 4.3 Budgeting and Strategy
*   **4.3.1 Finance Committee Chair:** Serve as the Chair of the Finance Committee (if established under Bylaws Section 6.1).
*   **4.3.2 Annual Budget:** Lead the annual budget development process in collaboration with the Executive Director and CFO. Present the proposed budget to the Board for approval prior to the start of the fiscal year.
*   **4.3.3 Solvency Monitoring:** Monitor the Organization’s reserves and liquidity to ensure the ability to meet short-term and long-term obligations.

#### 4.4 Compliance and Audit (Federal & State)
*   **4.4.1 Internal Controls:** Periodically review the Organization’s *Internal Controls Framework* and *Segregation of Duties Matrix* to prevent fraud, particularly in a remote-first environment.
*   **4.4.2 Grant Compliance:** Ensure financial management systems comply with **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)**, specifically regarding allowable costs, time and effort reporting, and procurement standards.
*   **4.4.3 Audit Liaison:** Serve as the primary Board liaison with external auditors. If the Organization expends $750,000 or more in federal awards, oversee the Single Audit process.
*   **4.4.4 Tax Filings:** Review and recommend Board approval of the IRS Form 990 prior to filing.

#### 4.5 Oversight of Operational Finance
*   **4.5.1 CFO Relationship:** While the CFO reports administratively to the Executive Director (CEO), the CFO reports functionally to the Treasurer regarding financial integrity, audit matters, and internal control violations (Bylaws Section 5.2).
*   **4.5.2 Whistleblower Channel:** Serve as a primary intake point for financial whistleblower complaints if the Executive Director is implicated.

#### 4.6 Banking and Signatory Authority
*   **4.6.1 Signatory:** Serve as a primary authorized signer on Organization bank accounts.
*   **4.6.2 Disbursement Approval:** Review and approve disbursements that exceed the Executive Director’s delegated authority threshold, as defined in the *Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix*.

### 5. Qualifications and Term

#### 5.1 Qualifications
*   Must be a Voting Member in good standing.
*   Must possess financial literacy (ability to read and understand nonprofit financial statements).
*   Experience with nonprofit accounting, federal grants management, or CPA licensure is highly preferred.
*   Must not hold the office of President or Secretary concurrently (TBOC § 22.231).

#### 5.2 Term of Office
*   Elected annually by the Board of Directors (Bylaws Section 4.3).
*   May serve consecutive terms without limit, subject to annual re-election.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities Matrix

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Treasurer** | Provides oversight, reviews reports, chairs Finance Committee, ensures compliance, reports to Board. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Annual Budget, reviews financial reports, elects the Treasurer. |
| **CFO (Operational)** | Executes day-to-day bookkeeping, prepares financial statements, manages payroll/AP, reports functionally to Treasurer. |
| **Executive Director** | Implements the approved budget, authorizes expenditures within DoA limits, manages the CFO. |
| **External Auditor** | Conducts independent audit/review; reports findings to the Treasurer/Board. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to perform the duties described herein may result in removal from office by a majority vote of the Board of Directors (Bylaws Section 3.6) or by the Membership (Bylaws Section 5.3).

Gross negligence or intentional misconduct regarding financial assets may result in personal liability, revocation of indemnification (Bylaws Article VII), and legal action.
