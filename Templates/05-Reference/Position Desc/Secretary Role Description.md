### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to define the statutory authority, duties, and responsibilities of the Corporate Secretary of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This role is established to ensure the integrity of the Organization’s governance framework, maintain accurate corporate records in compliance with the Texas Business Organizations Code ("TBOC"), and serve as the primary custodian of the Organization’s non-financial legal documents.

### 2. Scope
This description applies to the individual elected by the Board of Directors to serve as the Secretary.
*   **Inclusions:** Oversight of Board and Membership meeting minutes, maintenance of the Corporate Record Book, certification of corporate acts, and management of membership rolls.
*   **Exclusions:** Financial record-keeping (assigned to the Treasurer) and day-to-day operational administrative tasks, unless specifically delegated by the Board.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |

| **Corporate Record Book** | The master repository (physical or digital) containing the Certificate of Formation, Bylaws, Minutes, and Resolutions. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Statutory Authority and Election
*   **4.1.1 Requirement:** Pursuant to **TBOC § 22.231** and **Article IV, Section 4.1** of the Bylaws, the Organization must have a Secretary.
*   **4.1.2 Separation of Duties:** In strict compliance with Texas law, the Secretary **must not** be the same individual currently serving as the President.
*   **4.1.3 Election and Term:** The Secretary is elected annually by the Board of Directors at the first meeting following the Annual Election. The term is one (1) year, renewable without limit, subject to removal by the Board.

#### 4.2 Custodianship of Records
*   **4.2.1 Corporate Minute Book:** The Secretary must maintain the official Corporate Minute Book (digital or physical), ensuring it contains:
    *   The Certificate of Formation and all amendments.
    *   The current and historical Bylaws.
    *   Minutes of all Board and Membership meetings.
    *   A register of the names and addresses of all Directors and Officers.
*   **4.2.2 Record Integrity:** As a remote-first organization, the Secretary must ensure that digital records are stored securely, backed up in accordance with the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy (GOV-POL-012)**, and protected against unauthorized alteration.

#### 4.3 Meeting Administration and Minutes
*   **4.3.1 Notices:** The Secretary is responsible for issuing proper notice of all Board and Member meetings in accordance with **Bylaws Article II (Section 2.5)** and **Article III**, ensuring statutory timelines (e.g., 10 to 60 days for Member meetings) are met.
*   **4.3.2 Recording Minutes:** The Secretary must attend all Board and Member meetings to record the minutes. Minutes must include:
    *   Date, time, and location (or virtual platform) of the meeting.
    *   List of attendees and verification of **Quorum** (Bylaws Sections 2.4 and 3.5).
    *   A summary of discussions, motions made, seconds, and vote outcomes.
    *   Disclosure of any Conflicts of Interest and recusals (Bylaws Section 9.1).
*   **4.3.3 Absence:** If the Secretary is unable to attend a meeting, they must designate an Assistant Secretary or a Director to act as Secretary *Pro Tem* for that specific meeting.

#### 4.4 Membership and Voting Oversight
*   **4.4.1 Voting List:** In compliance with **TBOC § 22.158** and **Bylaws Section 2.6**, the Secretary must prepare and make available an alphabetical list of voting members entitled to notice. This list must be available for inspection starting two business days after notice of the meeting is given.
*   **4.4.2 Election Certification:** The Secretary oversees the integrity of the electronic voting system used for Director elections (Bylaws Section 2.5) and certifies the final results to the Board and Membership.

#### 4.5 Certification and Compliance
*   **4.5.1 Certification of Acts:** The Secretary is the only officer authorized to certify copies of resolutions, Bylaws, or minutes as being true and correct copies of the Organization's records (often required by banks or grantors).
*   **4.5.2 Registered Agent Coordination:** The Secretary ensures the Organization maintains a valid Registered Agent and Office in Texas (Bylaws Section 1.2) and files necessary updates with the Texas Secretary of State (e.g., Form 401 or 424).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Executes all duties defined in Section 4; acts as the primary advisor to the Board on procedural bylaws compliance (Parliamentarian duties, unless otherwise assigned). |
| **Board President** | Coordinates with the Secretary to set meeting agendas; relies on the Secretary to verify quorum before calling meetings to order. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides financial reports to the Secretary for inclusion in the official minutes/records. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides operational data and membership rosters to the Secretary to facilitate accurate record-keeping and meeting notices. |
| **Governance Committee** | Reviews this Role Description annually to ensure alignment with current laws and organizational needs. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to perform the duties of the Secretary—specifically regarding the maintenance of records and minutes—may expose the Organization to legal liability, loss of tax-exempt status, or "piercing of the corporate veil."

*   **Removal:** The Secretary may be removed from office by the Board of Directors with or without cause, as outlined in **Bylaws Article IV**.
*   **Liability:** The Organization indemnifies the Secretary for acts performed in good faith (Bylaws Article VII), but not for acts of gross negligence, intentional misconduct, or knowing violation of the law.
