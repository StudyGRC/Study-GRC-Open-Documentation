### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to codify the authority, responsibilities, and qualification requirements for the **Vice President of Education and Training** (VP-ET). This document serves to align the operational leadership role with Article V of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc., ensuring that the Organization’s educational mission—fostering a community-driven ecosystem and removing financial barriers to GRC knowledge—is executed with professional rigor, pedagogical soundness, and legal compliance.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to:
*   The individual appointed by the Board of Directors to serve as the VP of Education and Training.
*   The Executive Director (CEO) regarding supervision of this role.
*   The Board of Directors regarding the appointment and removal process.

**Exclusions:** This document does not apply to the governance duties of a Board Director, even if the individual holding this Operational Officer role is simultaneously serving on the Board (Dual Service).

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Appointment, Term, and Reporting Structure
4.1.1 **Appointment:** Pursuant to Bylaws Section 5.1, the VP-ET is appointed by the Board of Directors.
4.1.2 **Term:** The VP-ET serves a renewable term not to exceed two (2) years. There is no limitation on the number of consecutive terms served.
4.1.3 **Reporting:** The VP-ET reports directly to the Executive Director (CEO) for all operational matters.
4.1.4 **Compensation:** This role may be volunteer-based or compensated. Any compensation must be approved by the Board in compliance with the Conflict of Interest Policy and IRS regulations regarding intermediate sanctions.

#### 4.2 Core Responsibilities and Authority
The VP-ET is the primary architect of the Organization’s educational strategy. Responsibilities include:
4.2.1 **Curriculum Development:** Designing, vetting, and maintaining a zero-cost, open-source GRC curriculum that aligns with industry standards (e.g., NIST, ISO) and the Organization’s mission.
4.2.2 **Instructor Management:** Recruiting, vetting, and supervising volunteer instructors and subject matter experts (SMEs). This includes enforcing the *Instructor Code of Ethics*.
4.2.3 **LMS Administration:** Overseeing the technical and pedagogical administration of the Learning Management System (LMS), ensuring uptime, data integrity, and user usability.
4.2.4 **Certification Integrity:** Establishing and maintaining rigorous standards for any certificates of completion or credentials issued by the Organization to prevent fraud or devaluation.

#### 4.3 Compliance and Standards
4.3.1 **Intellectual Property (IP):** The VP-ET must ensure all curriculum materials are either original works of the Organization or properly licensed open-source materials. Per Bylaws Section 9.3, the VP-ET assigns all rights to materials they create in this capacity to Study GRC Inc.
4.3.2 **Accessibility:** The VP-ET must ensure all educational resources meet **WCAG 2.2 Level AA** standards to ensure inclusivity for learners with disabilities.
4.3.3 **Youth Protection:** Given the Organization’s K-12 focus, the VP-ET must strictly adhere to the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, ensuring no unmonitored one-on-one interactions with minors occur within the educational platform.
4.3.4 **Federal Grant Compliance:** If the VP-ET’s time or resources are funded by federal grants, they must maintain accurate time-and-effort reporting (Personnel Activity Reports) in compliance with **2 CFR 200.430**.

#### 4.4 Financial Authority
4.4.1 **Budget Management:** The VP-ET is responsible for proposing an annual education budget to the CEO and managing expenses within that approved limit.
4.4.2 **Spending Authority:** The VP-ET holds spending authority up to the limit defined in the *Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix* for educational software, licensing, and content creation tools.

#### 4.5 Removal and Vacancy
4.5.1 **Removal by Board:** The Board may remove the VP-ET at any time, with or without cause.
4.5.2 **Removal by Member Petition:** Pursuant to Bylaws Section 5.3, Voting Members may petition for the removal of the VP-ET. Removal requires an affirmative vote of at least two-thirds (2/3) of all Voting Members.
4.5.3 **Resignation:** The VP-ET may resign by providing written notice to the Executive Director and the Board.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Executes the educational strategy, manages the LMS, supervises instructors, and ensures curriculum quality and accessibility. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Provides strategic direction, approves the education budget, and conducts annual performance evaluations of the VP-ET. |
| **Board of Directors** | Appoints the VP-ET, approves compensation (if any), and provides high-level oversight of educational impact. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Collaborates with the VP-ET to ensure the LMS and student data are secured according to the *Master Information Security Policy*. |
| **Volunteer Instructors** | Deliver content under the supervision and guidance of the VP-ET. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to perform the duties outlined in this Standard, or violation of the *Code of Ethics*, *Conflict of Interest Policy*, or *Child Safeguarding Policy*, may result in immediate removal from office by the Board of Directors or by Member Petition (Bylaws §5.3). Legal action may be pursued for theft of Intellectual Property or financial malfeasance.
