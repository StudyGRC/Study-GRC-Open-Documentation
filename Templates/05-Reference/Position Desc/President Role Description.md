### 1. Purpose
This document defines the authority, duties, and qualifications for the office of **President** of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This role is established to satisfy the requirements of the **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.231** and **Article IV** of the Organization’s Bylaws. The President serves as the principal governance officer, ensuring the Board of Directors fulfills its fiduciary duties and strategic oversight responsibilities.

### 2. Scope
This description applies to the individual elected by the Board of Directors to serve as President.

*   **Inclusion:** Governance oversight, presiding over Board meetings, and signatory authority for Fundamental Actions.
*   **Distinction:** This role is distinct from the **Executive Director (CEO)**. While the President leads the *Board* (Governance), the Executive Director leads the *Staff* (Operations).
    *   *Note: Per Bylaws Section 5.1, a Director may simultaneously serve as an Operational Officer, but the roles remain functionally distinct.*

### 4. Policy Statements & Duties

#### 4.1 Legal Authority and Term
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IV**, the President is an Officer of the Organization elected annually by the Board of Directors.
*   **4.1.1 Statutory Requirement:** Per **TBOC § 22.231**, the Organization must have a President.
*   **4.1.2 Separation of Offices:** The President **must not** simultaneously hold the office of **Secretary**.
*   **4.1.3 Term:** The President serves a one (1) year term, renewable upon re-election by the Board.

#### 4.2 Presiding Officer Duties
The President acts as the lead facilitator for all governance meetings.
*   **4.2.1 Meeting Leadership:** The President presides over all meetings of the Board of Directors and the Annual Meeting of Members.
*   **4.2.2 Agenda Setting:** The President collaborates with the Executive Director and Secretary to set the agenda for Board meetings, ensuring critical governance, financial, and strategic matters are prioritized.
*   **4.2.3 Rules of Order:** The President enforces the **Board Meeting Rules of Order (GOV-GUD-016)** to ensure efficient, respectful, and productive discourse.

#### 4.3 Governance Oversight
The President is responsible for the integrity of the Board’s process.
*   **4.3.1 Bylaws Compliance:** The President ensures that Board actions and decisions comply with the **Bylaws** and applicable law.
*   **4.3.2 Committee Oversight:** The President appoints Committee Chairs (unless otherwise specified in a Committee Charter) and serves as an *ex-officio* member of all Board committees, with the exception of the Audit Committee if independence is required.
*   **4.3.3 Board Development:** The President leads the orientation of new Directors and the annual Board Self-Assessment process.

#### 4.4 Operational Liaison
The President serves as the primary conduit between the Board and the Operational Leadership.
*   **4.4.1 CEO Supervision:** The President acts as the direct supervisor to the Executive Director (CEO) regarding performance evaluation, compensation review, and professional development.
*   **4.4.2 Communication:** The President ensures clear communication of Board directives to the Executive Director and reports operational concerns back to the Board.

#### 4.5 Signatory Authority
*   **4.5.1 Contracts:** The President is authorized to sign contracts, instruments, and documents on behalf of the Organization, except where the signing and execution thereof shall be expressly delegated by the Board to some other officer or agent.
*   **4.5.2 Fundamental Actions:** The President must sign documents related to Fundamental Actions (mergers, asset sales, dissolution) following the required Member and Board approvals defined in **Bylaws Article X**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **President** | Presides over meetings; enforces Bylaws; supervises the Executive Director; signs legal instruments; represents the Board to external stakeholders. |
| **Secretary** | Records minutes; maintains the corporate seal (if any); ensures notice is given for meetings. **(Cannot be the President)**. |
| **Treasurer** | Oversees financial management; chairs the Finance Committee; reports financial status to the Board. |
| **Executive Director** | Manages day-to-day operations; implements Board strategy; reports to the President/Board. |
| **Board of Directors** | Elects the President; sets strategic direction; holds the President accountable. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**6.1 Fiduciary Duty**
The President must act in good faith, with ordinary care, and in a manner the President reasonably believes to be in the best interest of the Organization (Duty of Care, Duty of Loyalty, Duty of Obedience).

**6.2 Conflict of Interest**
The President must strictly adhere to the **Conflict of Interest Policy (GOV-POL-005)**. If a conflict arises regarding the President, they must recuse themselves from the discussion and vote, yielding the chair to the Treasurer or another disinterested Officer for that specific agenda item.

**6.3 Removal**
Per **Bylaws Section 4.3**, the President may be removed from the *Officer* role (distinct from their role as a Director) by the Board of Directors whenever, in its judgment, the best interests of the Organization will be served thereby. Removal requires a majority vote of the Board.
