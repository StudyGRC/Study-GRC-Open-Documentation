### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to codify the authority, responsibilities, and qualifications for the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)**. Pursuant to **Article V, Section 5.2** of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc., this role is established to operationalize the Organization’s commitment to a "community-driven ecosystem." The DKG serves as the primary architect of organizational culture, the chief enforcer of community standards, and the ombudsman for member welfare, ensuring that the Organization’s mission to "remove barriers" includes removing social and behavioral barriers to learning.

### 2. Scope
This position description applies to the individual appointed by the Board of Directors to serve as the DKG.
*   **Applies to:** The appointee, their direct reports (if any), and their interactions with all Members, Directors, Officers, volunteers, and community participants.
*   **Exclusions:** This role does not hold authority over financial controls (CFO domain) or technical security infrastructure (CISO domain), except where those domains intersect with community conduct or harassment.

### 4. Position Standards

#### 4.1 Appointment, Tenure, and Reporting
*   **4.1.1 Appointment:** The DKG is appointed by the Board of Directors.
*   **4.1.2 Reporting Line:** In accordance with **Bylaws Section 5.2**, the DKG reports directly to the Executive Director (CEO).
*   **4.1.3 Term:** The DKG serves a renewable term not to exceed two (2) years. There is no limit on consecutive terms.
*   **4.1.4 Compensation:** The DKG may serve as a volunteer or receive reasonable compensation, subject to the Conflict of Interest Policy and Board approval (**Bylaws Section 5.1**).

#### 4.2 Core Responsibilities
The DKG is responsible for the following functional areas:

**4.2.1 Community Culture & Engagement**
*   Develop and execute strategies to foster an inclusive, psychological safe environment for all learners, specifically addressing the needs of K-12 participants and adult career-switchers.
*   Design and implement the "Volunteer Appreciation Program" to prevent burnout and recognize contributions.
*   Act as the primary point of contact for new member onboarding regarding culture and conduct.

**4.2.2 Conduct Enforcement & Moderation**
*   Own and maintain the **Code of Conduct** and **Community Guidelines**.
*   Oversee the moderation of all digital communication channels (e.g., Slack, Discord, Forums) to ensure compliance with anti-harassment policies.
*   Investigate reports of behavioral misconduct, bullying, or exclusion.
*   **Authority to Act:** The DKG holds the delegated authority to issue warnings, temporary suspensions, or recommend permanent expulsion of members to the Board.

**4.2.3 Conflict Resolution (Ombudsman Function)**
*   Serve as a neutral mediator for disputes between members or volunteers.
*   Implement restorative justice practices to resolve interpersonal conflicts within the community.
*   Receive and process grievances that do not rise to the level of Whistleblower complaints (which go to the Board/Compliance).

**4.2.4 Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI)**
*   Audit organizational resources and events to ensure accessibility and representation.
*   Advise the VP of Education & Training on inclusive language within curriculum materials.

#### 4.3 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The DKG’s performance will be evaluated based on:
*   **Community Sentiment:** Measured via Net Promoter Score (NPS) or annual member satisfaction surveys.
*   **Retention Rate:** Volunteer and member retention statistics.
*   **Resolution Time:** Average time to resolve Code of Conduct reports.
*   **Incident Rate:** Frequency of reported harassment or toxic behavior (target: reduction).

#### 4.4 Qualifications
*   **Experience:** Demonstrated experience in community management, human resources, social work, or conflict resolution.
*   **Skills:** High emotional intelligence (EQ), crisis de-escalation, and written communication.
*   **Ethics:** Unblemished ethical record; must pass a Level 2 Background Check (due to proximity to youth programs).
*   **Membership:** Must be a Voting Member in good standing.

#### 4.5 Removal and Vacancy
*   **Removal by Board:** The DKG may be removed by the Board with or without cause.
*   **Removal by Members:** Per **Bylaws Section 5.3**, Voting Members may petition for the removal of the DKG. Removal requires a two-thirds (2/3) affirmative vote of all Voting Members.
*   **Resignation:** Must provide 30 days' written notice to the Executive Director.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities Matrix

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Executes community strategy, enforces Code of Conduct, mediates disputes, reports systemic culture risks to CEO. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Provides strategic oversight, approves budget for community initiatives, conducts annual performance review of DKG. |
| **Board of Directors** | Appoints the DKG, approves changes to the Code of Conduct, serves as the final appeal body for expulsion recommendations. |
| **Voting Members** | Adhere to community standards; retain the right to petition for DKG removal per Bylaws Art. V. |
| **Moderators (Volunteers)** | Report directly to the DKG; assist in day-to-day monitoring of digital channels. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to perform the duties outlined in this Position Description, or violation of the Organization's Code of Ethics, may result in disciplinary action. This may include performance improvement plans, suspension of authority, or removal from office by the Board of Directors.

Any "Fundamental Action" (as defined in Bylaws Art. X) or policy change proposed by the DKG that affects member voting rights or corporate structure must be approved by the Board and/or Membership as required by the Bylaws.
