### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to define the authority, responsibilities, and qualification standards for the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This role is established pursuant to **Article V, Section 5.2** of the Bylaws to ensure the Organization maintains a robust security posture that protects member data, safeguards youth participants, and ensures compliance with federal grant requirements (2 CFR 200) and the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0.

### 2. Scope
This position description applies to the individual appointed by the Board of Directors to serve as the CISO. The CISO’s jurisdiction extends to all information systems, data assets, intellectual property, and technology infrastructure owned or leased by the Organization, as well as the security practices of all employees, volunteers, and third-party vendors.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **NIST CSF 2.0** | The National Institute of Standards and Technology Cybersecurity Framework, serving as the primary control standard for the Organization. Functions include: Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. |
| **Operational Officer** | A leadership role defined in Bylaws Article V, responsible for day-to-day execution, reporting to the Executive Director. |
| **COPPA** | Children's Online Privacy Protection Act; federal law regulating the collection of data from children under 13. |
| **Zero Trust** | A security model based on the principle of maintaining strict access controls and not trusting anyone by default, even those already inside the network perimeter. |
| **Fiduciary Duty** | The legal obligation to act in the best interest of the Organization, exercising duty of care and duty of loyalty. |

### 4. Position Standards and Duties

The CISO is an Operational Officer reporting directly to the Executive Director (CEO). The CISO is responsible for the strategic leadership of the Organization's information security program.

#### 4.1 Governance and Strategy [NIST Function: GOVERN]
4.1.1 **Strategic Alignment:** The CISO must develop and maintain a multi-year cybersecurity strategy that aligns with the Organization’s mission to provide open-source GRC resources and safe community spaces.
4.1.2 **Policy Architecture:** The CISO is the owner of the *Master Information Security Policy* (IS-POL-001) and must ensure all sub-policies (Acceptable Use, Access Control, etc.) are reviewed annually and approved by the appropriate authority.
4.1.3 **Reporting Structure:**
*   **Direct Report:** The CISO reports to the Executive Director (CEO).
*   **Board Interface:** The CISO shall provide quarterly reports to the Board of Directors regarding the Organization's risk posture.
*   **Advisory Board Restriction:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI, Section 6.2**, the CISO is prohibited from formally communicating with the Advisory Board without prior approval and facilitation by a Board Director.

#### 4.2 Risk Management [NIST Function: IDENTIFY]
4.2.1 **Asset Management:** The CISO must maintain an accurate inventory of all hardware, software, and data assets, classifying them based on sensitivity (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted).
4.2.2 **Supply Chain Risk:** The CISO must oversee the *Vendor Security Risk Assessment* process to ensure third-party service providers meet the Organization's security standards, particularly regarding student/youth data privacy.
4.2.3 **Youth Protection Risk:** The CISO must collaborate with the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) to identify digital risks to youth participants (e.g., grooming, data harvesting) and implement technical controls to mitigate them.

#### 4.3 Defense and Operations [NIST Function: PROTECT]
4.3.1 **Zero Trust Implementation:** The CISO must architect and enforce a Zero Trust security model for the Organization’s remote-first workforce, ensuring identity verification for every access request.
4.3.2 **Data Privacy Compliance:** The CISO serves as the primary Data Protection Officer (DPO) equivalent, ensuring compliance with COPPA, GDPR, and the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA).
4.3.3 **Access Control:** The CISO must enforce the Principle of Least Privilege across all systems, requiring Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all administrative and volunteer access.
4.3.4 **Training:** The CISO must oversee the *Security Awareness Training Program*, ensuring all volunteers and staff complete annual training relevant to their roles.

#### 4.4 Threat Management [NIST Function: DETECT & RESPOND]
4.4.1 **Continuous Monitoring:** The CISO must implement capabilities to detect anomalous activity, unauthorized access, and potential data exfiltration attempts.
4.4.2 **Incident Command:** The CISO serves as the Incident Commander for all cybersecurity incidents. They have the authority to sever network connections or disable accounts immediately to contain active threats without prior executive approval.
4.4.3 **Incident Response Plan:** The CISO must maintain and test the *Incident Response Plan* (IS-PLN-001) at least annually via tabletop exercises.

#### 4.5 Resilience and Recovery [NIST Function: RECOVER]
4.5.1 **Business Continuity:** The CISO is responsible for the *Disaster Recovery Plan*, ensuring that critical open-source resources and community platforms can be restored within the defined Recovery Time Objective (RTO).
4.5.2 **Backup Integrity:** The CISO must verify that immutable backups are performed regularly and tested for data integrity to protect against ransomware.

#### 4.6 Qualification Standards
4.6.1 **Professional Certification:** The CISO must hold at least one advanced certification (e.g., CISSP, CISM, CISA, or equivalent) in good standing.
4.6.2 **Experience:** Demonstrated experience in building or managing security programs, with a preference for non-profit or educational sector experience.
4.6.3 **Background Check:** As a role with access to sensitive youth data, the CISO must pass an enhanced background check (Level 3) prior to appointment.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **CISO** | Executes the duties defined in Section 4; serves as the primary advisor on cyber risk; manages IT/Security volunteers. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Provides strategic direction; approves budget for security tools; conducts performance evaluations of the CISO. |
| **Board of Directors** | Appoints the CISO; reviews and approves Level 1 Governance Policies; provides oversight of enterprise risk. |
| **IT/Security Volunteers** | Implement technical controls and monitor systems under the direction of the CISO. |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Adhere to security policies and report suspected incidents to the CISO immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to perform the duties outlined in this Position Description may result in removal from office.
*   **Removal by Board:** The Board may remove the CISO at any time with or without cause.
*   **Removal by Members:** As an Operational Officer, the CISO is subject to removal by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Voting Members per **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.3**.
*   **Legal Action:** Breaches of confidentiality or fiduciary duty may result in legal action and termination of membership.
