### 1. Purpose
This document defines the authority, responsibilities, and reporting structure for the Executive Director (also referred to as the Chief Executive Officer or CEO) of Study GRC Inc. It establishes the boundaries between Board governance and operational management, ensuring alignment with the Organization’s Bylaws, mission, and federal compliance obligations.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to the individual appointed by the Board of Directors to serve as the Executive Director. It also serves as the primary criteria for the Board’s annual performance evaluation of the Executive Director.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (ED)** | The highest-ranking operational officer of the Organization, responsible for day-to-day management. |
| **The Board** | The Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc., acting as the governing body. |
| **Operational Officers** | Leadership roles defined in Bylaws Article V (e.g., CISO, CFO, VP of Education) who report to the ED. |
| **AOR** | Authorized Organization Representative; the individual authorized to sign and submit federal grant applications and accept awards. |
| **2 CFR 200** | Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards (Federal Grant Regulations). |

### 4. Policy Statements (Job Description)

#### 4.1 General Authority and Reporting
**4.1.1** In accordance with **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.2**, the Executive Director is the primary leader of the Organization’s operations.
**4.1.2** The Executive Director reports directly to the Board of Directors and serves at the pleasure of the Board.
**4.1.3** The Executive Director has the authority to hire, terminate, and manage staff and volunteers, with the exception of specific Operational Officers appointed directly by the Board (e.g., CISO, CFO), over whom the ED retains day-to-day supervisory authority but not unilateral termination authority.

#### 4.2 Governance and Board Relations
**4.2.1** **Strategic Planning:** The ED must collaborate with the Board to define the Organization's strategic vision and is responsible for translating that vision into an actionable Operational Plan.
**4.2.2** **Reporting:** The ED must provide a comprehensive operational report at every regular Board meeting, detailing progress toward goals, financial status, and key performance indicators (KPIs).
**4.2.3** **Policy Implementation:** The ED is responsible for ensuring that all policies adopted by the Board (e.g., Conflict of Interest, Whistleblower) are implemented and enforced across the organization.

#### 4.3 Financial Management and Grant Compliance
**4.3.1** **Fiduciary Duty:** The ED, in coordination with the CFO and Treasurer, must ensure the financial integrity of the Organization.
**4.3.2** **Budget Execution:** The ED is authorized to execute the annual budget approved by the Board. Any expenditure exceeding the approved budget variance threshold defined in the *Financial Policies and Procedures Manual* requires prior Board approval.
**4.3.3** **Federal Compliance:** The ED serves as the Authorized Organization Representative (AOR). They must ensure strict adherence to **2 CFR 200** regarding the management of federal funds, including allowable costs, procurement standards, and time/effort reporting.
**4.3.4** **Fundraising:** The ED acts as the chief fundraiser, cultivating relationships with donors, grantors, and corporate sponsors to ensure financial sustainability.

#### 4.4 Operational and Program Leadership
**4.4.1** **Remote-First Culture:** The ED must maintain an effective infrastructure for a remote-first workforce, ensuring staff and volunteers have the tools and security protocols (per CISO standards) required to work effectively.
**4.4.2** **Program Delivery:** The ED oversees the VP of Education & Training to ensure the delivery of high-quality, open-source GRC resources and zero-cost educational materials.
**4.4.3** **Community Management:** The ED oversees the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) to foster a diverse, inclusive, and harassment-free community ecosystem.

#### 4.5 Risk Management and Youth Protection
**4.5.1** **Child Safeguarding:** Given the Organization's K-12 focus, the ED must enforce the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*. The ED is personally responsible for ensuring all staff and volunteers in youth-facing roles undergo required background checks.
**4.5.2** **Legal Compliance:** The ED must ensure the Organization complies with all applicable laws, including Texas Business Organizations Code, IRS regulations, and data privacy laws (GDPR, TDPSA).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Appoints the ED; approves the annual budget and strategic plan; conducts annual performance review of the ED. |
| **Executive Director** | Manages day-to-day operations; executes Board strategy; ensures compliance; manages staff and Operational Officers. |
| **CFO** | Reports to the ED for daily operations but maintains a dotted reporting line to the Treasurer for financial oversight. |
| **CISO** | Reports to the ED for daily operations but retains independent authority to enforce security policies and report risks to the Board. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**6.1** **Performance Review:** The Board shall review the Executive Director’s performance annually against the goals outlined in the Strategic Plan and this position description.
**6.2** **Removal:**
*   **By Board:** The Executive Director may be removed by a majority vote of the Board of Directors at any time, with or without cause.
*   **By Members:** Per **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.3**, Voting Members may petition for the removal of the Executive Director, requiring a two-thirds (2/3) affirmative vote of all Voting Members.
