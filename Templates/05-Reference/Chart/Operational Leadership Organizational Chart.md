### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to clearly define the operational hierarchy, reporting lines, and separation of duties within Study GRC Inc. By establishing a definitive Organizational Chart, the Organization ensures clarity in decision-making authority, accountability for deliverables, and efficient communication flow. This document satisfies the requirement for organizational clarity between the Governance Body (Board of Directors) and Operational Leadership.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all Operational Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Inclusions:** All functional departments (Education, IT/Security, Community, Finance).
*   **Exclusions:** This document does not govern the internal structure of Board Committees, which are defined in the Bylaws and specific Committee Charters.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Governance** | The strategic oversight function performed by the Board of Directors, focused on mission alignment, fiduciary duty, and policy approval. |
| **Operations** | The day-to-day execution of the Organization's mission, managed by the Executive Director and staff. |
| **Solid Line Reporting** | Direct supervisory authority. The supervisor conducts performance reviews and approves leave/expenses. |
| **Dotted Line Reporting** | Functional oversight or advisory relationship without direct disciplinary authority (e.g., CFO to Treasurer). |
| **Chain of Command** | The order of authority and power in an organization that is followed for communication and decision-making. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Organizational Hierarchy Structure
The Organization operates under a functional hierarchy designed to support a remote-first, community-driven ecosystem. The hierarchy is divided into two distinct levels: **Governance** and **Operations**.

**4.1.1 The Governance Level**
*   **Board of Directors:** The supreme governing body.
*   **Advisory Board:** Non-voting advisors who provide strategic counsel. They possess **no operational authority** and must communicate through the Board or with Board approval, per Bylaws Article VI.

**4.1.2 The Operational Level**
*   **Executive Director (CEO):** The sole employee/volunteer reporting directly to the Board of Directors. The CEO acts as the bridge between Governance and Operations.
*   **Operational Officers (C-Suite/VPs):** Department heads reporting directly to the CEO.

#### 4.2 Definitive Reporting Lines (Textual Chart)
The following hierarchy constitutes the official chain of command for Study GRC Inc.:

1.  **Board of Directors**
    *   *Direct Report:* **Executive Director (CEO)**
2.  **Executive Director (CEO)**
    *   *Direct Report:* **Vice President of Education & Training**
        *   *Scope:* Curriculum Development, Learning Management System (LMS), Certification Programs.
    *   *Direct Report:* **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)**
        *   *Scope:* Community Management, Volunteer Coordination, Youth Protection, Event Culture.
    *   *Direct Report:* **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**
        *   *Scope:* IT Infrastructure, Data Privacy (GDPR/COPPA), Cybersecurity, Platform Security.
    *   *Direct Report:* **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)**
        *   *Scope:* Bookkeeping, Grant Financial Reporting, Payroll, Expense Management.
        *   *Note:* The CFO maintains a **Dotted Line** reporting relationship to the **Board Treasurer** for audit and compliance oversight.

#### 4.3 Visual Chart Maintenance
The Executive Director must maintain a visual representation (diagram) of this structure.
*   The visual chart must be updated within **30 days** of any creation, elimination, or restructuring of a role.
*   The current visual chart must be accessible to all personnel via the Organization’s central document repository.

#### 4.4 Separation of Duties
To maintain internal controls and prevent conflicts of interest:
*   **Governance vs. Operations:** While Bylaws Article V permits a Director to serve as an Operational Officer, the roles are distinct. When acting as an Officer, the individual reports to the CEO. When acting as a Director, they are a peer of the Board.
*   **Finance:** The individual authorizing an expenditure (e.g., VP of Education) cannot be the same individual processing the payment (CFO), subject to the thresholds defined in the *Financial Policies and Procedures Manual*.

#### 4.5 Succession of Authority
In the event of the temporary unavailability of the Executive Director (CEO), operational decision-making authority delegates in the following order, unless otherwise specified by a Board resolution:
1.  Vice President of Education & Training
2.  Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
3.  Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the creation of new C-Level/VP operational roles. Hires and evaluates the Executive Director. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Defines the sub-structure within departments. Hires/appoints Operational Officers. Ensures the Org Chart is up to date. |
| **Operational Officers** | Manage the day-to-day activities of their respective departments. Ensure staff/volunteers understand their reporting lines. |
| **Human Resources (HR)** | Maintains the official personnel roster and ensures job descriptions align with the Organizational Chart. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to the reporting lines established in this Standard (e.g., "circumventing the chain of command") may result in disciplinary action. Unauthorized creation of titles or roles that imply authority not granted by this Standard is strictly prohibited.
