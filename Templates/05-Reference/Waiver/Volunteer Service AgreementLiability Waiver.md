### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Volunteer Service Agreement and Liability Waiver (the "Agreement") is to clearly define the relationship between Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") and the individual volunteer (the "Volunteer"). This document establishes the legal status of the Volunteer, assigns intellectual property rights to the Organization in accordance with Bylaws Article IX, outlines confidentiality obligations, and manages liability risks pursuant to the **Texas Charitable Immunity and Liability Act of 1987** (Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code, Chapter 84).

### 2. Scope
This Agreement applies to all individuals providing unpaid services to the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   Members of the Board of Directors and Advisory Board.
*   Operational Officers serving in a volunteer capacity.
*   Committee members.
*   Event staff, content creators, and community moderators.

**Exclusions:** This document does not apply to paid employees or independent contractors, whose relationships are governed by separate employment contracts or Master Service Agreements (MSAs).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer** | An individual rendering services for or on behalf of the Organization who does not receive compensation in excess of reimbursement for expenses incurred. |
| **Work Product** | Any content, code, curriculum, documentation, designs, or other materials created, developed, or contributed by the Volunteer in the course of their service to the Organization. |
| **Confidential Information** | Non-public information including donor lists, member data, security vulnerabilities, strategic plans, and proprietary financial data. |
| **Youth** | Any individual under the age of 18. |

### 4. Agreement Terms and Conditions

#### 4.1 Nature of Relationship (Non-Employment)
The Volunteer understands and agrees that:
1.  They are serving as a volunteer and not as an employee of the Organization.
2.  They have no expectation of compensation, wages, or benefits (including health insurance or workers' compensation) for their services.
3.  This relationship is "at-will" and may be terminated by either party at any time, with or without cause or notice.

#### 4.2 Regulatory Compliance and Ethics
The Volunteer agrees to abide by all Organization policies, including but not limited to:
1.  **Code of Ethics:** Acting with honesty and integrity.
2.  **Conflict of Interest:** Disclosing any conflicts as required by Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1.
3.  **Youth Protection:** Adhering strictly to the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*. The Volunteer acknowledges that Study GRC Inc. has a zero-tolerance policy regarding abuse or misconduct involving minors.

#### 4.3 Intellectual Property Assignment
Pursuant to **Article IX, Section 9.3** of the Organization's Bylaws, the Volunteer agrees to the following:
1.  **Work Made for Hire:** All Work Product created by the Volunteer during their service shall be considered a "work made for hire" to the fullest extent permitted by law.
2.  **Assignment:** To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as a work made for hire, the Volunteer hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Organization all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all copyrights, patents, and trade secrets.
3.  **Moral Rights:** The Volunteer waives any "moral rights" regarding the Work Product.
4.  **Open Source Commitment:** The Volunteer acknowledges the Organization’s commitment to keeping resources open-source and free. The Volunteer grants the Organization the right to license the Work Product under open-source licenses (e.g., Creative Commons, MIT, Apache) at the Organization's sole discretion.

#### 4.4 Confidentiality and Data Privacy
Pursuant to **Article IX, Section 9.2** of the Bylaws:
1.  The Volunteer agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not to disclose, copy, or use such information outside the scope of their volunteer duties.
2.  The Volunteer agrees to comply with the Organization's *Data Privacy Policy* and *Information Security Policy*, specifically regarding the handling of member data (GDPR/CCPA compliance) and the prohibition of unauthorized access to IT systems.

#### 4.5 Waiver and Release of Liability
In consideration of the opportunity to volunteer, the Volunteer agrees to the following:
1.  **Assumption of Risk:** The Volunteer understands that services may involve inherent risks, including but not limited to physical injury during events or ergonomic strain during remote work. The Volunteer expressly assumes all such risks.
2.  **Release:** To the fullest extent permitted by Texas law, the Volunteer releases, waives, and discharges Study GRC Inc., its directors, officers, and agents from any and all liability, claims, demands, or causes of action arising out of or related to any loss, damage, or injury that may be sustained by the Volunteer while performing duties for the Organization.
3.  **Statutory Immunity:** The parties acknowledge that the Organization and its non-compensated directors/officers are entitled to the protections and immunities provided by the **Texas Charitable Immunity and Liability Act (CPRC Chapter 84)**.

#### 4.6 Indemnification
The Organization agrees to indemnify the Volunteer against third-party claims arising from the Volunteer’s good faith performance of duties, strictly in accordance with and subject to the limitations defined in **Article VII (Indemnification)** of the Bylaws and applicable Texas law. Indemnification is void if the Volunteer acted with gross negligence, malice, or intentional misconduct.

#### 4.7 Media Release
The Volunteer grants the Organization permission to use their name, image, voice, and likeness in photographs, video recordings, and electronic media for the purpose of promoting the Organization’s mission, without compensation.

#### 4.8 Background Checks
For roles involving access to financial assets, sensitive data, or direct interaction with Youth, the Volunteer consents to a background check pursuant to the *Background Check Policy*. Continued service is contingent upon satisfactory results.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer** | Review, sign, and adhere to the terms of this Agreement. Update the Organization immediately if personal contact information or conflict status changes. |
| **Volunteer Coordinator** | Ensure this Agreement is executed prior to the Volunteer commencing service. Maintain the signed document in the secure Personnel File. |
| **Executive Director** | Review and approve any requested modifications to this standard agreement (in consultation with Legal). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to sign this Agreement will result in the inability to serve as a volunteer. Violation of the terms within this Agreement, particularly regarding Confidentiality, Youth Protection, or Intellectual Property, may result in immediate termination of the volunteer relationship and potential legal action for damages or injunctive relief.
