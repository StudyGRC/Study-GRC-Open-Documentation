### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the mandatory legal agreement between **Study GRC Inc.** (the "Organization") and individuals participating in Organization-sponsored activities. This Waiver mitigates legal liability, ensures participants provide informed consent regarding potential risks (physical, digital, and health-related), and secures necessary permissions for media usage and medical intervention. This document aligns with the risk management objectives mandated by the Organization's insurance carriers and Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code.

### 2. Scope
This Waiver applies to:
*   **Individuals:** All attendees, speakers, volunteers, exhibitors, and guests.
*   **Activities:** All events organized, hosted, or sponsored by Study GRC Inc., whether held in-person, virtually (remote), or in a hybrid format.
*   **Minors:** Participants under the age of 18 must have this Waiver executed by a parent or legal guardian.

**Exclusions:** This Waiver does not replace the **Employment Agreement** for W-2 employees, though employees attending events outside the scope of their employment duties may be required to sign.

### 4. Agreement Terms and Conditions

By registering for or attending an Event hosted by the Organization, the Participant agrees to the following terms. These terms constitute the official **Waiver and Release of Liability**.

#### 4.1 Mandatory Execution
Participation in any Event is conditional upon the acceptance of this Waiver. Failure to accept these terms prohibits entry to the Event venue or access to the virtual Event platform.

#### 4.2 Assumption of Risk
The Participant acknowledges and understands that:
1.  **Physical Risks:** In-person events involve inherent risks of physical injury, illness, or property damage.
2.  **Communicable Disease:** Despite preventative measures, attending in-person events carries an inherent risk of exposure to communicable diseases (including but not limited to COVID-19). The Participant voluntarily assumes all risks related to exposure.
3.  **Virtual Risks:** Participation in online events involves risks related to technology, including but not limited to data privacy breaches, unauthorized access, or exposure to third-party content, consistent with the **Master Information Security Policy (IS-POL-001)**.

#### 4.3 Release and Waiver of Liability
To the fullest extent permitted by Texas law, the Participant hereby releases, waives, and forever discharges **Study GRC Inc.**, its Directors (as defined in **Bylaws Article III**), Officers, volunteers, and agents from any and all liability, claims, and demands of whatever kind or nature, either in law or in equity, which arise or may hereafter arise from the Participant’s activities with the Organization.
*   This release discharges the Organization from liability or claim that the Participant may have against the Organization with respect to any bodily injury, personal injury, illness, death, or property damage that may result from the Participant’s activities, whether caused by the negligence of the Organization or its officers, directors, employees, or agents or otherwise.

#### 4.4 Indemnification
The Participant agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the Organization from any and all Claims, actions, suits, procedures, costs, expenses, damages, and liabilities, including attorney’s fees, brought as a result of the Participant’s involvement in the Event, and to reimburse the Organization for any such expenses incurred.

#### 4.5 Medical Authorization (In-Person Events)
In the event of an injury or medical emergency during an in-person Event, the Participant:
1.  Authorizes the Organization to secure emergency medical treatment.
2.  Agrees that the Participant is solely responsible for any costs incurred for such medical treatment (e.g., ambulance transport, hospitalization).
3.  Releases the Organization from any liability arising out of the provision or failure to provide such medical treatment.

#### 4.6 Media Release
The Participant grants the Organization permission to use the Participant’s likeness in a photograph, video, or other digital media ("Media") in any and all of its publications, including web-based publications, without payment or other consideration.
*   **Ownership:** The Participant understands and agrees that all Media will become the property of the Organization.
*   **Opt-Out:** Participants wishing to opt-out must notify the **Director of Kindness and Generosity** in writing prior to the Event start date.

#### 4.7 Code of Conduct and Ethics
The Participant agrees to abide by the Organization’s **Professional Ethics and Community Guidelines**. The Organization reserves the right to revoke Event access, without refund, for any Participant who engages in harassment, discrimination, or disruptive behavior.

#### 4.8 Minors (Participants under 18)
If the Participant is a minor:
1.  The undersigned parent or legal guardian warrants that they have the legal authority to sign this Waiver on behalf of the minor.
2.  The parent/guardian agrees to be bound by all terms of this Waiver, including the Indemnification clause, on behalf of the minor.

#### 4.9 Governing Law and Severability
This Waiver shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the **State of Texas**. If any term or provision of this Waiver is held to be illegal, unenforceable, or invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Participant** | Review the Waiver carefully; provide accurate information; sign/accept prior to Event entry. |
| **Event Manager** | Ensure the Waiver is integrated into the registration platform; verify execution for all attendees; maintain records of signed waivers. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Handle Media Release opt-out requests; manage Code of Conduct violations during the Event. |
| **Chief Legal Officer** | Review Waiver language annually to ensure compliance with changing Texas laws and insurance requirements. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

*   **Denial of Entry:** Individuals who refuse to sign or accept this Waiver will be denied access to the Event. No refunds will be issued for failure to comply with this requirement.
*   **Record Retention:** Executed waivers must be retained in accordance with the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy (GOV-POL-012)** for a period of no less than two (2) years following the conclusion of the Event (or until the statute of limitations expires for minors).
*   **Legal Action:** Violation of the Indemnification clause may result in the Organization seeking legal damages from the Participant.
