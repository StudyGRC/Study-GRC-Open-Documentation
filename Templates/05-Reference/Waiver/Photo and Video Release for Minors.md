### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the legal framework under which Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") may capture, use, and publish the likeness, voice, and work of minors participating in its programs. This waiver ensures compliance with privacy laws (including COPPA and Texas privacy statutes), protects the Organization from liability regarding the Right of Publicity, and supports the Organization’s charitable mission by allowing the use of media for educational, fundraising, and grant reporting purposes.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All parents or legal guardians of minors (individuals under the age of 18) participating in Study GRC Inc. events, workshops, online communities, or programs.
*   **Media Covered:** Photographs, digital images, video recordings, audio recordings, and written or video testimonials.
*   **Exclusions:** This release does not apply to security surveillance footage used solely for safety purposes, nor does it override the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy (YTH-POL-001)** regarding prohibited one-on-one private interactions.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18 years. |
| **Likeness** | The visual depiction of an individual, including their physical appearance, voice, and name. |
| **Media Assets** | Any photos, videos, audio clips, or digital files captured by Study GRC Inc. staff, volunteers, or contractors. |
| **Composite Image** | A group photograph or video where individuals are not the primary focus and are not individually identified by name (e.g., a wide shot of a classroom). |
| **Revocation** | The formal process by which a parent/guardian withdraws consent for future use of their child's likeness. |

### 4. Terms of Release (Waiver Content)

*Note: The following text constitutes the legal agreement presented to the Parent/Guardian for signature.*

#### 4.1 Grant of Rights
By signing this release, the Parent/Guardian grants Study GRC Inc., its directors, officers, employees, volunteers, and agents (collectively, the "Organization"), the irrevocable (subject to Section 4.5), worldwide, royalty-free right and license to:
1.  **Capture:** Photograph, video, and record the audio of the Minor named below during their participation in Organization activities.
2.  **Use and Publish:** Use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and publicly display such Media Assets in any format, now known or hereafter created.
3.  **Purpose:** These assets may be used for:
    *   Educational materials and curriculum.
    *   Grant reporting and donor stewardship (demonstrating impact).
    *   Marketing, publicity, and social media promotion of the Organization’s nonprofit mission.
    *   The Organization’s official website and transparency reports.

#### 4.2 Privacy and Safety Protections
To ensure the safety of minors, the Organization agrees to the following restrictions:
1.  **No Full Names:** The Organization will **not** publish the Minor’s full name (First and Last) in conjunction with their photo on any public social media platform or website without specific, separate written consent for a special recognition (e.g., an award announcement).
2.  **First Name Only:** The Organization may use the Minor’s first name only (e.g., "Student Alex") if necessary for context.
3.  **Geotagging:** The Organization will not include specific home addresses or precise real-time geolocation data in public metadata associated with images of the Minor.

#### 4.3 Ownership of Intellectual Property
Pursuant to **Article IX, Section 9.3** of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc., all Media Assets created by the Organization’s staff or volunteers are the sole property of Study GRC Inc. The Parent/Guardian acknowledges that they have no ownership rights, title, or interest in the Media Assets and waives any right to inspect or approve the finished product.

#### 4.4 Release of Liability
The Parent/Guardian hereby releases, discharges, and holds harmless Study GRC Inc. from any and all claims, demands, or causes of action that they may hereafter have by reason of anything contained in the photographs or video, including but not limited to:
*   Claims for libel or defamation.
*   Invasion of privacy.
*   Violation of the Right of Publicity.
*   Claims for compensation or royalties.

#### 4.5 Right to Revoke (Opt-Out)
The Parent/Guardian retains the right to revoke this consent at any time for **future** uses.
*   **Procedure:** Revocation must be submitted in writing to the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).
*   **Effect:** Upon receipt, the Organization will stop using the Minor’s likeness in *new* materials.
*   **Limitations:** The Organization is not required to recall or destroy printed materials or static digital media already in the public domain prior to the revocation notice.

#### 4.6 Selection of Consent Level
*The Parent/Guardian must select one of the following options on the signature form:*

*   **[ ] OPTION A: FULL CONSENT.** I grant permission for my child’s photo/video to be used for all purposes described above.
*   **[ ] OPTION B: INTERNAL ONLY.** I grant permission for my child to be photographed for internal records, grant reporting, and security only. **Do not** publish my child's face on social media or the public website.
*   **[ ] OPTION C: NO MEDIA.** I **do not** grant permission for my child to be photographed or recorded. I understand my child may be asked to step out of group photos.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Parent/Guardian** | Review the waiver carefully, select the appropriate consent level, and sign prior to the Minor's participation. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Maintain the central registry of "No Media" minors; ensure event photographers are aware of restricted individuals (e.g., via color-coded lanyards or wristbands). |
| **Program Managers** | Verify the consent status of a minor *before* uploading any images to social media or the website. |
| **Volunteer Photographers** | Adhere to the "No Full Names" policy and respect the privacy of minors identified as "No Media." |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Violation:** Unauthorized publication of a minor's image against the wishes of the parent (Option B or C) is a violation of the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**.
*   **Remediation:** Upon discovery of an unauthorized posting, the content must be removed immediately (within 24 hours).
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Staff or volunteers who willfully violate this policy or ignore "No Media" designations may face disciplinary action, up to and including termination of their role.
