### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to establish the mandatory steps for **Study GRC Inc.** ("the Organization") to designate an agent with the United States Copyright Office to receive notifications of claimed copyright infringement.

Compliance with this procedure is a strict legal prerequisite for the Organization to claim the "Safe Harbor" limitations on liability regarding user-generated content, as defined under **17 U.S.C. § 512(c)** of the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA). Failure to maintain a valid registration exposes the Organization to direct and contributory liability for copyright infringement committed by community members or users.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to:
*   **The Board of Directors and Officers:** Specifically the Secretary or designated Legal Officer responsible for corporate filings.
*   **The IT/Web Operations Team:** Responsible for posting required notices on the Organization’s public-facing domains.
*   **The Designated Agent:** The individual or role assigned to receive and process takedown notices.

This procedure covers the initial registration, mandatory triennial renewals, and updates required upon changes to contact information.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Designated Agent** | The individual or specific role (e.g., "Copyright Manager") identified by the Organization to receive notifications of claimed infringement. |
| **Service Provider** | Under DMCA § 512(k)(1)(B), an entity that provides online services or network access (e.g., Study GRC Inc.). |
| **Safe Harbor** | Legal protection that limits a Service Provider's financial liability for copyright infringement by its users, provided specific conditions (including this registration) are met. |
| **eCO** | The United States Copyright Office’s electronic registration system. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Selection of the Designated Agent
The Board of Directors or the Executive Director must appoint a Designated Agent.
1.  To ensure continuity, the Organization shall register a **role title** (e.g., "Copyright Agent" or "Secretary") rather than a specific individual's personal name, where permitted by the Copyright Office system.
2.  The email address registered **must** be a dedicated distribution list (e.g., `copyright@studygrc.org` or `legal@studygrc.org`) that forwards to the Secretary and at least one other Officer to prevent missed notices.
3.  The physical address used must be the Organization’s Registered Office or official mailing address, not the private home address of a volunteer, to ensure privacy and stability.

#### 4.2 Initial Filing with the U.S. Copyright Office
The Responsible Officer must perform the following steps via the DMCA Designated Agent Directory:
1.  **Account Creation:** Access the [U.S. Copyright Office DMCA System](https://dmca.copyright.gov/osp/). Create a login.gov account if one does not exist for the Organization.
2.  **Service Provider Information:** Enter the full legal name: **Study GRC Inc.**
    *   Add any "Doing Business As" (DBA) names or alternate names used by the platform (e.g., "Study GRC Community").
    *   Enter the physical street address (Texas Registered Office).
3.  **Designated Agent Information:** Enter the contact details for the agent selected in Section 4.1.
    *   Name/Title.
    *   Mailing Address.
    *   Phone Number.
    *   Email Address.
4.  **Payment:** Pay the required filing fee (currently $6.00 per designation) via the Organization’s corporate credit card.
5.  **Confirmation:** Download the PDF certificate of registration and save it to the **Legal Records Repository** (`LEG-REG-001`).

#### 4.3 Public Posting Requirements
Per **DMCA § 512(c)(2)**, the Organization must make the Designated Agent's contact information publicly available on its website.
1.  **Location:** The IT/Web Team must maintain a link in the footer of all web properties titled "DMCA Notice" or "Copyright Policy."
2.  **Content:** The linked page must display:
    *   Name of Designated Agent.
    *   Full Address.
    *   Telephone Number.
    *   Email Address.
3.  **Consistency:** The information on the website **must match exactly** the information filed with the Copyright Office.

#### 4.4 Maintenance and Updates
1.  **Trigger Events:** The Responsible Officer must update the registration within **30 days** if:
    *   The Organization changes its physical address.
    *   The Designated Agent’s email or phone number changes.
    *   The Organization adopts a new domain name or public-facing brand.
2.  **Process:** Log in to the DMCA Designated Agent Directory, edit the profile, and pay the amendment fee (if applicable).

#### 4.5 Mandatory Renewal (Triennial)
1.  **Frequency:** Unlike corporate formation documents, DMCA registrations **expire every three (3) years**.
2.  **Tracking:** The Secretary must add the expiration date to the **Compliance Calendar** (`LEG-CAL-001`).
3.  **Renewal Action:** The Responsible Officer must log in to the system and "Renew" the designation prior to the expiration date, regardless of whether information has changed. Failure to renew results in immediate loss of Safe Harbor protection.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary / CLO** | Acts as the Document Owner; performs the actual filing, payment, and renewal within the Copyright Office system. Monitors the `copyright@` email alias. |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensures the `copyright@` email alias is monitored by active personnel during leadership transitions. |
| **IT / Web Team** | Updates the website footer and "DMCA Notice" page immediately upon notification of any changes to the Agent's contact details. |
| **Treasurer** | Approves the credit card transaction for filing fees. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Strict Liability Warning:** Failure to maintain a valid, active registration with the U.S. Copyright Office renders the Organization ineligible for DMCA Safe Harbor protection. This means Study GRC Inc. could be held liable for copyright infringement based solely on content posted by community members, potentially resulting in statutory damages of up to $150,000 per work infringed.

Any Officer or volunteer found to have negligently allowed this registration to lapse, or who disabled the public notice on the website without authorization, may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including removal from office and termination of volunteer status.
