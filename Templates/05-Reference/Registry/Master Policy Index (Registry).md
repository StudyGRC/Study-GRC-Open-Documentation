### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Master Policy Index (MDR) is to establish a centralized, authoritative "Single Source of Truth" for all governance, operational, and compliance documentation within Study GRC Inc. This registry ensures compliance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** and **2 CFR § 200.303 (Internal Controls)** by maintaining a comprehensive inventory of approved policies, standards, procedures, and forms. It serves to prevent version control errors, facilitate audit readiness, and ensure transparency for members and stakeholders.

### 2. Scope
This registry encompasses all "Controlled Documents" generated by Study GRC Inc.
*   **In Scope:** All Policies, Standards, Procedures, Guidelines, Charters, Plans, and official Forms/Templates across all functional domains (Governance, HR, IT, Finance, etc.).
*   **Out of Scope:** Ad-hoc emails, meeting notes (excluding official Minutes), draft working papers not yet approved, and external reference materials not adopted as internal standards.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Controlled Document** | A document that undergoes a formal creation, review, approval, and distribution process, and is tracked via version control. |
| **MDR** | Master Documentation Registry; the central database or list of all active controlled documents. |
| **Level 1 (Policy)** | High-level statement of intent and rules approved by the Board. Mandatory compliance. |
| **Level 2 (Standard/Procedure)** | Quantifiable requirements or step-by-step instructions approved by the Executive Director. Mandatory compliance. |
| **Level 3 (Guideline)** | Best practices or advice approved by Department Heads. Discretionary compliance. |
| **Retired** | A document status indicating the item is no longer in force but is retained for archival/audit purposes. |

### 4. Registry Management Standards

#### 4.1 Single Source of Truth
The Master Policy Index maintained within the organization’s secure cloud repository (and mirrored on the public Transparency Page where applicable) is the only valid source for active documentation. Local copies saved to individual desktops are considered "Uncontrolled" and potentially obsolete.

#### 4.2 Categorization Schema
All documents within this registry are organized by the following Functional Domains to ensure logical retrieval:

*   **Domain I:** Governance & Corporate Structure
*   **Domain II:** Membership Operations
*   **Domain III:** Operational Leadership
*   **Domain IV:** Financial Integrity & Grant Compliance
*   **Domain V:** HR & Volunteer Management
*   **Domain VI:** Youth Protection & Child Safeguarding
*   **Domain VII:** Data Privacy, AI, & Biometrics
*   **Domain VIII:** IT & Cybersecurity
*   **Domain IX:** Digital Accessibility
*   **Domain X:** EHS & Operations
*   **Domain XI:** Program Ops & Chapter Mgmt
*   **Domain XII:** Legal, Compliance, & IP
*   **Domain XIII:** Strategic Communications
*   **Domain XIV:** Technology & Systems Admin
*   **Domain XV:** Internal Controls & Audit
*   **Domain XVI:** Advanced Compliance
*   **Domain XVII:** Meta-Governance

#### 4.3 Document Status Codes
The Registry must track the lifecycle status of every document:
1.  **Draft:** Under development; not yet effective.
2.  **Active:** Fully approved and currently in force.
3.  **Under Review:** Active, but currently undergoing scheduled revision.
4.  **Retired:** Obsolete; superseded or revoked.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Corporate Secretary** | Acts as the primary custodian of the Registry. Ensures all documents are properly categorized, numbered, and archived. |
| **Document Owner** | Responsible for the content accuracy, scheduled reviews, and drafting of their assigned documents. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves all Level 1 (Governance) Policies and Fundamental Actions. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves Level 2 (Operational) Standards and Procedures. Ensures staff are aware of the Registry location. |
| **Audit Committee** | Periodically reviews the Registry to ensure coverage of key risks and compliance with 2 CFR 200. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
The Master Policy Index is a mandatory governance tool.
*   **Audit Trail:** Any alteration to the Registry or the documents contained therein must be logged in the Version Control History.
*   **Unauthorized Documents:** Policies or procedures created outside of this Registry framework are considered invalid and unenforceable.
*   **Failure to Maintain:** Failure to maintain this Registry may result in non-compliance findings during the Annual Financial Audit or Federal Single Audit.
