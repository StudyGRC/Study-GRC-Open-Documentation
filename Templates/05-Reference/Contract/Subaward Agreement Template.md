### 1. Purpose
This Standard establishes the mandatory legal framework and template for **Subaward Agreements** issued by Study GRC Inc. ("PTE") to third-party entities ("Subrecipients").

Its purpose is to ensure that all federal funds passed through to Subrecipients are managed in strict compliance with **2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance)**, specifically §200.332 (Requirements for pass-through entities), and to protect Study GRC Inc. from liability regarding disallowed costs, non-performance, or regulatory violations.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All agreements where Study GRC Inc. assigns a portion of a Federal Award to another entity to carry out a public purpose authorized by the Federal Award.
*   **Exclusions:** This template must **not** be used for the procurement of goods or services for Study GRC Inc.'s internal use (e.g., buying software, hiring a janitorial service). For those transactions, use the *Master Vendor Agreement (LEG-STD-024)*.
*   **Determination:** Prior to using this template, the *Subrecipient vs. Contractor Checklist (FIN-CHK-051)* must be completed to confirm the relationship is a Subaward.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pass-Through Entity (PTE)** | Study GRC Inc., the entity that provides a subaward to a subrecipient to carry out part of a federal program. |
| **Subrecipient** | A non-federal entity that receives a subaward from a pass-through entity to carry out part of a federal program. |
| **Uniform Guidance (UG)** | 2 CFR Part 200, "Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards." |
| **UEI** | Unique Entity Identifier (generated via SAM.gov). |
| **FAIN** | Federal Award Identification Number. |
| **Modified Total Direct Cost (MTDC)** | The base cost upon which indirect costs are calculated, excluding equipment, capital expenditures, and the portion of each subaward in excess of $25,000. |

### 4. Standard: Subaward Agreement Template Structure

All Subawards issued by Study GRC Inc. must utilize the following structure and contain the mandatory clauses defined below.

#### 4.1 Preamble and Execution
**SUBAWARD AGREEMENT**
**Between:** Study GRC Inc. ("PTE")
**And:** [Subrecipient Legal Name] ("Subrecipient")

This Agreement is entered into as of [Effective Date]. The PTE has received a Federal Award from [Federal Agency Name] and hereby awards a portion of said funds to the Subrecipient to perform the Scope of Work described herein.

#### 4.2 Article I: Federal Award Information (Mandatory per 2 CFR 200.332(a))
The Schedule of Awards (Attachment A) is incorporated by reference and includes the following mandatory data:
1.  Subrecipient Name and UEI.
2.  Federal Award Identification Number (FAIN).
3.  Federal Award Date.
4.  Subaward Period of Performance.
5.  Amount of Federal Funds Obligated by this Action.
6.  Total Amount of Federal Funds Obligated to the Subrecipient.
7.  Total Amount of the Federal Award committed to the Subrecipient.
8.  Federal Award Project Description.
9.  Name of Federal Awarding Agency.
10. Assistance Listing Number (ALN) and Title.
11. Identification of whether the award is R&D.
12. Indirect Cost Rate (De Minimis 10% or Negotiated Rate).

#### 4.3 Article II: Scope of Work and Budget
1.  **Scope:** The Subrecipient shall perform the work described in **Attachment B (Scope of Work)** with the standard of care and diligence customary in the industry.
2.  **Budget:** Allowable costs are limited to the categories and amounts listed in **Attachment C (Budget)**.
3.  **Prior Approval:** Deviations from the budget exceeding 10% of the total award amount, or changes in Key Personnel, require prior written approval from the PTE’s Executive Director.

#### 4.4 Article III: Compensation and Invoicing
1.  **Reimbursement:** PTE shall reimburse Subrecipient for allowable costs incurred.
2.  **Invoicing:** Subrecipient shall submit invoices [Monthly/Quarterly] to `finance@studygrc.org`. Invoices must include a certification signed by an authorized official: *"By signing this report, I certify to the best of my knowledge and belief that the report is true, complete, and accurate, and the expenditures, disbursements and cash receipts are for the purposes and objectives set forth in the terms and conditions of the Federal award."*
3.  **Final Invoice:** Must be submitted no later than 60 days after the end date of the Period of Performance.

#### 4.5 Article IV: Compliance with Federal Regulations
The Subrecipient agrees to comply with all applicable Federal statutes, regulations, and the terms and conditions of the Federal Award, including but not limited to:
1.  **Uniform Guidance:** 2 CFR Part 200, specifically Subpart D (Post Federal Award Requirements), Subpart E (Cost Principles), and Subpart F (Audit Requirements).
2.  **Conflict of Interest:** Subrecipient must disclose in writing any potential conflict of interest to the PTE in accordance with applicable Federal policy.
3.  **Record Retention:** Financial records, supporting documents, statistical records, and all other records pertinent to the Subaward shall be retained for a period of three (3) years from the date of submission of the final expenditure report.
4.  **Access to Records:** The PTE, the Federal Awarding Agency, the Comptroller General of the United States, or any of their authorized representatives, shall have the right of access to any documents, papers, or other records of the Subrecipient which are pertinent to the Federal award.

#### 4.6 Article V: Intellectual Property and Open Source
1.  **Federal Rights:** The Federal Government reserves a royalty-free, nonexclusive, and irrevocable license to reproduce, publish, or otherwise use, and to authorize others to use, for Federal Government purposes: (a) The copyright in any work developed under this grant; and (b) Any rights of copyright to which the Subrecipient purchases ownership with grant support (2 CFR 200.315).
2.  **Open Source Commitment:** Consistent with Study GRC Inc.'s Bylaws Article 1.3, Subrecipient agrees that any educational resources, software code, or curriculum developed under this Subaward shall be licensed under [Specify License, e.g., Creative Commons BY-SA 4.0 or MIT License] to ensure zero-cost access for the community.

#### 4.7 Article VI: Termination and Suspension
1.  **For Cause:** PTE may terminate this Agreement immediately if Subrecipient materially fails to comply with any term of this Agreement or the Federal Award.
2.  **For Convenience:** Either party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days written notice. In such event, PTE will pay Subrecipient for all allowable costs incurred up to the date of termination.
3.  **Suspension:** PTE reserves the right to suspend payments if Subrecipient fails to submit required reports or if PTE suspects fraud or mismanagement.

#### 4.8 Article VII: Indemnification
To the extent permitted by law, Subrecipient shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Study GRC Inc., its officers, directors (as defined in Bylaws Article III), and employees from any and all claims, liabilities, losses, or expenses (including legal fees) arising out of the negligence, willful misconduct, or breach of this Agreement by the Subrecipient.

#### 4.9 Article VIII: Single Audit (Subpart F)
1.  If Subrecipient expends $750,000 or more in Federal awards during their fiscal year, they must have a single or program-specific audit conducted for that year in accordance with the provisions of 2 CFR 200 Subpart F.
2.  Subrecipient must provide PTE with a copy of the audit report or a link to the Federal Audit Clearinghouse within nine (9) months of the end of the audit period.

#### 4.10 Article IX: General Provisions
1.  **Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas.
2.  **Independent Contractor:** Subrecipient is an independent entity and not an employee, agent, or partner of Study GRC Inc.
3.  **Entire Agreement:** This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final signatory authority on all Subaward Agreements. Responsible for ensuring strategic alignment. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Responsible for risk assessment, budget verification, and financial monitoring of the Subrecipient. |
| **Program Manager** | Responsible for monitoring the technical progress and Scope of Work performance of the Subrecipient. |
| **Subrecipient** | Responsible for performing the work, complying with regulations, and submitting timely invoices/reports. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to utilize this template for Subawards, or unauthorized modification of the mandatory Federal clauses (Articles I, IV, V, and VIII), constitutes a violation of Study GRC Inc.'s internal controls and may result in disciplinary action.

Failure by the Subrecipient to comply with the terms of this Agreement may result in the disallowance of costs, suspension of payments, termination of the award, and reporting to the Federal Awardee Performance and Integrity Information System (FAPIIS).
