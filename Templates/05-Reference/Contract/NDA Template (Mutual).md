### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is to establish the legal framework for the exchange of proprietary, sensitive, and confidential information between **Study GRC Inc.** ("The Organization") and an external party. This Agreement ensures that both parties can discuss potential business relationships, partnerships, or services while protecting their respective Intellectual Property, donor data, and strategic interests in accordance with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.2**.

### 2. Scope
This Agreement applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, vendors, and potential partners who may have access to Confidential Information during the course of their relationship with the Organization.

*   **Inclusions:** Strategic plans, donor lists, security configurations, unreleased educational content, and personal data of members/staff.
*   **Exclusions:** Publicly available open-source repositories, public website content, and information explicitly designated as "Public" under the Data Classification Standard.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Disclosing Party** | The party that shares Confidential Information with the other party. |
| **Receiving Party** | The party that receives Confidential Information from the Disclosing Party. |
| **Confidential Information** | Any non-public information, technical data, or know-how, including but not limited to research, product plans, donor lists, markets, software, developments, inventions, processes, designs, drawings, engineering, hardware configuration information, marketing, or finances. |
| **Trade Secret** | Information that derives independent economic value from not being generally known and is the subject of efforts to maintain its secrecy (as defined by the Texas Uniform Trade Secrets Act). |

### 4. Agreement Terms

#### 4.1 Mutual Exchange
This Agreement is entered into by and between **Study GRC Inc.**, a Texas Non-Profit Corporation, and **[Counterparty Name]** (collectively, the "Parties"). The Parties wish to explore a business opportunity of mutual interest and, in connection with this opportunity, may disclose to each other certain Confidential Information.

#### 4.2 Identification of Confidential Information
Confidential Information may be disclosed in written or other tangible form (including on magnetic media) or by oral, visual, or other means.
*   **4.2.1** Information need not be marked "Confidential" to be protected if the nature of the information or the circumstances of disclosure reasonably indicate its confidential nature.
*   **4.2.2** Specific to Study GRC Inc., **Donor Lists**, **Youth Participant Data**, and **Security Vulnerability Reports** are automatically classified as Confidential.

#### 4.3 Exclusions from Confidential Information
Confidential Information shall not include any information that the Receiving Party can prove:
*   **4.3.1** Was in the public domain at the time it was disclosed or has entered the public domain through no fault of the Receiving Party;
*   **4.3.2** Was known to the Receiving Party, without restriction, at the time of disclosure;
*   **4.3.3** Is disclosed with the prior written approval of the Disclosing Party;
*   **4.3.4** Was independently developed by the Receiving Party without any use of the Confidential Information; or
*   **4.3.5** Is released by Study GRC Inc. under an Open Source License (e.g., MIT, Apache 2.0, CC-BY-SA).

#### 4.4 Obligations of Receiving Party
The Receiving Party agrees:
*   **4.4.1** To maintain the Confidential Information in strict confidence;
*   **4.4.2** Not to disclose such Confidential Information to any third parties without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;
*   **4.4.3** To use such Confidential Information only for the specific purpose of evaluating or pursuing the business relationship; and
*   **4.4.4** To restrict access to the Confidential Information to employees, contractors, or legal counsel who have a "need to know" and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as this Agreement.

#### 4.5 Compelled Disclosure
If the Receiving Party is required by law or court order to disclose Confidential Information, they must:
*   **4.5.1** Provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice (unless prohibited by law) to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order; and
*   **4.5.2** Disclose only the minimum information required to comply with the legal process.

#### 4.6 Term and Termination
*   **4.6.1** This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of **[Number, e.g., 3] years** from the Effective Date.
*   **4.6.2** The obligations of confidentiality regarding **Trade Secrets** and **Personally Identifiable Information (PII)** shall survive strictly in perpetuity or until such information legally enters the public domain.

#### 4.7 Return or Destruction of Materials
Upon termination of discussions or at the request of the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party must immediately return or securely destroy (in accordance with NIST SP 800-88) all copies of Confidential Information and certify such destruction in writing.

#### 4.8 Intellectual Property Rights
*   **4.8.1** Nothing in this Agreement grants any license or right to the Receiving Party regarding the Confidential Information.
*   **4.8.2** Per **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, Study GRC Inc. retains all ownership of its content, code, and materials.

#### 4.9 Whistleblower Immunity (Defend Trade Secrets Act)
Pursuant to 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b), an individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official, or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director / CEO** | Authorized signatory for Study GRC Inc. Ensures NDAs are executed prior to data sharing. |
| **Contract Owner** | The Study GRC staff member managing the relationship. Responsible for ensuring the counterparty receives and signs the NDA. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews any requested redlines or modifications to this template. |
| **Counterparty** | Adheres to the confidentiality obligations defined herein. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

*   **6.1 Injunctive Relief:** The Parties acknowledge that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be insufficient. Therefore, the Disclosing Party is entitled to seek injunctive relief to prevent further breaches.
*   **6.2 Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the **State of Texas**, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.
*   **6.3 Severability:** If any provision of this Agreement is found to be unenforceable, the remainder shall be enforced as fully as possible.
