### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Agreement is to establish the terms and conditions under which Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") issues a Corporate Credit Card to the undersigned individual (the "Cardholder"). This Agreement is designed to mitigate financial risk, prevent fraud, ensure compliance with IRS substantiation requirements and Federal Grant regulations (2 CFR 200), and strictly define personal liability for misuse.

### 2. Scope
This Agreement applies to all employees, officers, and directors of Study GRC Inc. who are issued a physical or virtual corporate credit card. This Agreement must be signed and filed with the Finance Department prior to the activation of any card.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cardholder** | The individual named on the card who is authorized to execute transactions on behalf of the Organization. |
| **Substantiation** | Documentary evidence (receipts, invoices) proving the amount, date, place, and business character of an expense, as required by IRS Publication 463. |
| **Disallowed Cost** | An expense that is not reimbursable or allowable under federal grant regulations (2 CFR 200 Subpart E) or Organization policy. |
| **Merchant Category Code (MCC)** | A four-digit number used to classify the business by the type of goods or services it provides. |

### 4. Agreement Terms and Conditions

#### 4.1 Issuance and Security
4.1.1 **Privilege, Not Right:** The Corporate Credit Card is a privilege granted to specific personnel to facilitate business operations. The Organization reserves the right to revoke this privilege at any time, with or without cause.
4.1.2 **Custody:** The Cardholder is responsible for the physical security of the card and the digital security of the card number.
4.1.3 **Non-Transferability:** The card may only be used by the Cardholder whose name appears on it. Sharing the card, card number, CVV, or PIN with any other person (including other employees or family members) is strictly prohibited.
4.1.4 **Digital Wallets:** Cardholders may add the card to secure digital wallets (e.g., Apple Pay, Google Pay) on devices protected by biometrics or strong passcodes, provided the device is enrolled in the Organization’s Mobile Device Management (MDM) system per `IS-POL-022`.

#### 4.2 Authorized Use
4.2.1 **Business Purpose:** The card must be used strictly for legitimate business expenses directly related to the Organization's mission and operations.
4.2.2 **Tax Exemption:** As a 501(c)(3) organization in Texas, Study GRC Inc. is exempt from State Sales Tax. The Cardholder **must** present the Texas Sales and Use Tax Exemption Certification to vendors prior to purchase. The Cardholder is personally liable for avoidable sales tax charges incurred due to negligence.
4.2.3 **Budgetary Compliance:** All purchases must align with approved budget line items.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Uses
The following uses are strictly prohibited and constitute a breach of this Agreement:
*   **Personal Expenses:** Any purchase for personal, family, or household use.
*   **Cash Advances:** ATM withdrawals, cash back at point of sale, or traveler’s checks.
*   **Alcohol:** Unless explicitly pre-approved by the Board for a specific non-federal funding source event. (Strictly prohibited on Federal Grants).
*   **Fuel for Personal Vehicles:** Fuel must be claimed via mileage reimbursement; corporate cards may only be used for fuel for rental vehicles or Organization-owned vehicles.
*   **Split Transactions:** Splitting a single purchase into multiple transactions to bypass transaction limits.
*   **Restricted Merchants:** Transactions at casinos, liquor stores, escort services, or other merchants deemed inconsistent with the Organization's ethical standards.

#### 4.4 Spending Limits and Controls
4.4.1 **Transaction Limits:** The Cardholder agrees to adhere to the single-transaction limit and monthly credit limit assigned to their card profile.
4.4.2 **Overrides:** Requests for temporary limit increases must be submitted in writing to the CFO and approved prior to the transaction.

#### 4.5 Substantiation and Reconciliation
4.5.1 **Receipt Requirement:** The Cardholder must obtain and upload an itemized receipt for **every** transaction, regardless of amount. Credit card slips showing only the total are insufficient; the receipt must show line-item details.
4.5.2 **Timeliness:** All receipts and expense categorizations must be submitted via the Organization’s expense management software within five (5) business days of the month-end close.
4.5.3 **Missing Receipts:** If a receipt is lost, the Cardholder must complete a *Missing Receipt Affidavit* (`FIN-FRM-040`). Frequent use of affidavits (more than 3 times per fiscal year) may result in card revocation.

#### 4.6 Federal Grant Compliance (2 CFR 200)
4.6.1 **Allowability:** If the card is used for expenses charged to a federal award, the Cardholder certifies that the cost is reasonable, allocable, and allowable under 2 CFR 200 Subpart E.
4.6.2 **Unallowable Costs:** If an audit determines a cost charged to the card is unallowable, the Cardholder may be required to reimburse the Organization if the cost was incurred due to negligence or willful misconduct.

#### 4.7 Separation from Employment
Upon resignation or termination of employment, the Cardholder must immediately surrender the card to the HR or Finance Department. No further transactions are permitted after the notice of separation is given, unless explicitly authorized by the Executive Director.

### 5. Liability and Payroll Deduction Authorization

#### 5.1 Personal Liability
The Cardholder accepts personal liability for any charges that are:
*   Personal in nature;
*   Unauthorized or prohibited by this Agreement;
*   Unsubstantiated (no receipt provided); or
*   Incurred after the termination of employment.

#### 5.2 Reimbursement to Organization
The Cardholder agrees to reimburse the Organization for any improper charges within ten (10) days of notification.

#### 5.3 Deduction Authorization (Texas Payday Law Compliance)
**By signing this Agreement, the Cardholder explicitly authorizes Study GRC Inc. to deduct from the Cardholder’s wages, salary, bonuses, or final paycheck any amounts owed to the Organization resulting from personal, unauthorized, or unsubstantiated charges on the Corporate Credit Card.** This authorization is intended to comply with the Texas Labor Code requirements for written authorization of deductions.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cardholder** | Secure the card, make authorized purchases, obtain tax exemptions, and submit itemized receipts on time. |
| **Approving Manager** | Review transactions for validity, budget alignment, and proper coding before approval. |
| **CFO / Treasurer** | Set spending limits, audit receipts for compliance, and manage card issuance/cancellation. |
| **Auditor** | Periodically review card activity for compliance with 2 CFR 200 and Internal Controls. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Agreement may result in:
1.  Immediate revocation of the Corporate Credit Card.
2.  Disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
3.  Legal action to recover misappropriated funds.
4.  Criminal prosecution in cases of intentional fraud or theft.
