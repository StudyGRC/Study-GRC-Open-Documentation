### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Non-Disclosure Agreement (the "Agreement") is to establish a legal framework for the protection of Confidential Information belonging to **Study GRC Inc.** (the "Discloser") when shared with a Recipient. This Agreement ensures compliance with **Article IX, Section 9.2** of the Organization’s Bylaws, which mandates the execution of confidentiality agreements for personnel accessing sensitive data. It is designed to protect the Organization’s intellectual property, donor privacy, youth participant data, and security infrastructure.

### 2. Scope
This Agreement applies to any individual or entity (the "Recipient") who is granted access to non-public information regarding Study GRC Inc.'s operations.
*   **Applies to:** Employees, Volunteers, Board Directors, Contractors, Vendors, and Advisory Board Members.
*   **Exclusions:** Information explicitly designated as "Public" or "Open Source" by the Discloser, subject to the specific exclusions defined in Section 4.3.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Confidential Information** | All non-public information, technical data, or know-how, including but not limited to: donor lists, youth participant PII, unpatched security vulnerabilities, private cryptographic keys, financial records, strategic plans, and unpublished curriculum. |
| **Recipient** | The individual or entity receiving Confidential Information from Study GRC Inc. |
| **Trade Secret** | Information that derives independent economic value from not being generally known to other persons who can obtain economic value from its disclosure or use, as defined by the Texas Uniform Trade Secrets Act. |
| **Work Product** | As defined in Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3: Content, code, and materials created by the Recipient during their service to the Organization. |

### 4. Agreement Terms (Policy Statements)

#### 4.1 Obligations of Confidentiality
The Recipient agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence. The Recipient must:
1.  Use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing their duties for the Organization (the "Permitted Purpose").
2.  Restrict disclosure of Confidential Information to those employees or agents who have a specific need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than this Agreement.
3.  Take all reasonable precautions to protect the confidentiality of the information, which must be at least as protective as the measures the Recipient uses to protect their own most confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable standard of care.

#### 4.2 Prohibited Actions
The Recipient is strictly prohibited from:
1.  Reverse engineering, decompiling, or disassembling any software or security protocols disclosed by Study GRC Inc., unless explicitly authorized for security testing purposes.
2.  Using Confidential Information for personal gain or for the benefit of any third party.
3.  Disclosing donor names, contact information, or donation history to any external party or unauthorized internal personnel.

#### 4.3 Exclusions from Confidential Information
Confidential Information does not include information that:
1.  Is or becomes generally available to the public other than as a result of a disclosure by the Recipient.
2.  Was available to the Recipient on a non-confidential basis prior to its disclosure by Study GRC Inc.
3.  Is independently developed by the Recipient without use of or reference to the Discloser's Confidential Information.
4.  **Open Source Exception:** Code or documentation that Study GRC Inc. has officially released under an Open Source License (e.g., MIT, Apache 2.0) is not Confidential Information. However, *unreleased* code, private signing keys, and security vulnerabilities prior to public disclosure/patching remain Confidential.

#### 4.4 Intellectual Property and Ownership
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**:
1.  All Confidential Information remains the sole property of Study GRC Inc.
2.  The Recipient acknowledges that nothing in this Agreement grants any license or property right to the Confidential Information.
3.  Any Work Product created by the Recipient utilizing Confidential Information constitutes a "work made for hire," and all rights, title, and interest are assigned to Study GRC Inc.

#### 4.5 Compelled Disclosure
If the Recipient is compelled by law or court order to disclose Confidential Information, the Recipient must provide Study GRC Inc. with prompt prior written notice (to the extent legally permitted) to allow the Organization to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

#### 4.6 Term and Termination
1.  **Term:** This Agreement is effective as of the date of signature and continues until the relationship is terminated.
2.  **Survival:** The obligations of confidentiality regarding general Confidential Information shall survive for a period of **five (5) years** following termination. The obligations regarding **Trade Secrets** and **Personally Identifiable Information (PII)** (including donor and youth data) shall survive indefinitely.

#### 4.7 Return of Materials
Upon termination of the relationship or written request by Study GRC Inc., the Recipient must immediately return or securely destroy (and certify destruction of) all documents, notes, and electronic media containing Confidential Information.

#### 4.8 Whistleblower Immunity (Defend Trade Secrets Act)
Pursuant to 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b), the Recipient shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official, or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Recipient** | Read, understand, sign, and strictly adhere to the confidentiality obligations defined herein. |
| **Hiring Manager / Volunteer Coordinator** | Ensure this Agreement is executed *prior* to granting the Recipient access to any internal systems or data. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Define technical controls (e.g., DLP, Access Control) to enforce the confidentiality of data covered by this Agreement. |
| **General Counsel** | Maintain this template and pursue legal remedies in the event of a breach. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Equitable Relief:** The Recipient acknowledges that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to Study GRC Inc. for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Therefore, Study GRC Inc. shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief to prevent or stop such a breach, in addition to any other remedies available at law.

**Disciplinary Action:** Internal Recipients (employees/volunteers) found in violation of this Agreement are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and legal prosecution.
