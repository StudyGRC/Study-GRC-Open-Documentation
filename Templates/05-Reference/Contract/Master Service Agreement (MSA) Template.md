### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Master Service Agreement (MSA) Template is to establish a standardized, legally binding framework for relationships between **Study GRC Inc.** ("Organization") and third-party service providers ("Provider"). This document mitigates organizational risk by defining baseline terms regarding intellectual property ownership, data privacy, liability, and termination rights prior to the commencement of specific work. It allows for multiple projects to be executed under Statements of Work (SOWs) without renegotiating legal terms for every engagement.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All Operational Officers, Department Heads, and the Executive Director when engaging external vendors, consultants, agencies, or software providers for services exceeding $5,000 in value or involving access to Confidential Information.
*   **Exclusions:** This template does not apply to W-2 employment contracts, simple commodity purchases (e.g., office supplies), or off-the-shelf software with non-negotiable Terms of Service (ToS), though legal review is still recommended for the latter.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Master Service Agreement (MSA)** | The governing contract that outlines the general terms and conditions of the business relationship. |
| **Statement of Work (SOW)** | A specific document attached to the MSA that details the scope, timeline, deliverables, and fees for a specific project. |
| **Work Product** | All deliverables, code, content, curriculum, and materials created by the Provider for the Organization. |
| **Confidential Information** | Non-public information including member data, donor lists, strategic plans, and security configurations. |

### 4. Template Content (Standard Terms & Conditions)

**MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT**

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of `[Date]` ("Effective Date") by and between **Study GRC Inc.**, a Texas Non-Profit Corporation ("Organization"), and `[Provider Name]`, a `[State]` `[Entity Type]` ("Provider").

#### 4.1. Services and Statements of Work
**4.1.1. Scope.** Provider agrees to perform the services ("Services") described in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW") attached hereto or subsequently executed by the parties.
**4.1.2. Precedence.** In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, the terms of this Agreement shall control unless the SOW expressly states that it overrides a specific section of this Agreement.
**4.1.3. Non-Exclusivity.** This Agreement does not create an exclusive relationship. The Organization retains the right to contract with other providers for similar services.

#### 4.2. Compensation and Payment
**4.2.1. Fees.** The Organization shall pay Provider the fees set forth in the applicable SOW.
**4.2.2. Invoicing.** Provider shall submit invoices `[Monthly / Upon Milestone Completion]`.
**4.2.3. Payment Terms.** Undisputed invoices shall be paid within thirty (30) days of receipt (Net 30).
**4.2.4. Expenses.** The Organization shall not reimburse Provider for expenses unless expressly authorized in the SOW and compliant with the Organization's *Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy*.

#### 4.3. Relationship of Parties
**4.3.1. Independent Contractor.** Provider is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a partnership, joint venture, or employer-employee relationship.
**4.3.2. Taxes.** Provider is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes applicable to the payments received. The Organization will not withhold taxes but will issue IRS Form 1099 where required by law.

#### 4.4. Intellectual Property (Bylaws Article IX Compliance)
**4.4.1. Work Made for Hire.** To the fullest extent permitted by law, all Work Product created by Provider under this Agreement shall be deemed a "work made for hire."
**4.4.2. Assignment.** If any Work Product does not qualify as a "work made for hire," Provider hereby irrevocably assigns to the Organization all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all copyrights, patents, and trade secrets.
**4.4.3. Open Source Mission.** Provider acknowledges that the Organization operates under a charitable purpose to provide open-source resources. Provider warrants that Work Product delivered does not infringe on third-party rights that would impede the Organization's ability to release materials publicly.

#### 4.5. Confidentiality and Data Security
**4.5.1. Non-Disclosure.** Provider agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not to disclose it to third parties without prior written consent.
**4.5.2. Data Privacy.** If Provider processes Personal Data of the Organization’s members (including minors), Provider agrees to comply with the Organization's *Data Privacy Policy*, the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), and, where applicable, COPPA.
**4.5.3. Security Standards.** Provider represents that it maintains reasonable security measures to protect the Organization's data and shall notify the Organization within twenty-four (24) hours of any known or suspected security breach.

#### 4.6. Representations and Warranties
**4.6.1. Professional Standards.** Provider warrants that Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.
**4.6.2. Compliance with Laws.** Provider shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, including but not limited to laws prohibiting discrimination and harassment.
**4.6.3. Youth Protection.** If Services involve interaction with minors, Provider agrees to undergo background checks as requested and adhere to the Organization’s *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.

#### 4.7. Indemnification and Liability
**4.7.1. Indemnification.** Provider agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Organization, its Directors, Officers, and volunteers from any claims, damages, or expenses arising out of Provider’s negligence, misconduct, or breach of this Agreement.
**4.7.2. Limitation of Liability.** Except for indemnification obligations and breaches of confidentiality, neither party’s liability shall exceed the total fees paid under the applicable SOW in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim.

#### 4.8. Term and Termination
**4.8.1. Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.
**4.8.2. Termination for Convenience.** The Organization may terminate this Agreement or any SOW at any time upon thirty (30) days' written notice.
**4.8.3. Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within ten (10) days.

#### 4.9. General Provisions
**4.9.1. Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas.
**4.9.2. Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved first through good-faith negotiation, followed by mediation in Texas, before resorting to litigation.
**4.9.3. Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final signatory authority for all MSAs. Ensures strategic alignment. |
| **Chief Legal Officer** | Maintains this template. Reviews any requested deviations or "redlines" from Providers. |
| **Department Head** | Responsible for drafting the specific Statement of Work (SOW) and managing Provider performance. |
| **Chief Financial Officer** | Verifies budget availability prior to execution and manages invoice payments. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Mandatory Use:** Use of this template is mandatory for all service engagements meeting the scope criteria defined in Section 2.
*   **Unauthorized Contracts:** Contracts signed by unauthorized personnel or without using this approved template (unless vetted by Legal) may be considered voidable by the Organization.
*   **Violations:** Employees found executing agreements outside of this protocol may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination, particularly if such action exposes the Organization to significant liability or IP loss.
