### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this **Master Vendor Agreement (MVA)** is to establish a standardized, legally binding framework for the procurement of goods and services by Study GRC Inc. This template ensures that all external vendor relationships comply with the Organization's Bylaws, Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC), and federal grant regulations (2 CFR 200). It is designed to mitigate liability, protect Intellectual Property (IP), ensure data privacy, and safeguard the Organization’s tax-exempt status.

### 2. Scope
This template applies to all **Independent Contractors**, **Consultants**, **Service Providers**, and **Vendors** engaged by Study GRC Inc. where the value of services exceeds the micro-purchase threshold or involves access to sensitive data, intellectual property creation, or interaction with minors.

*   **Inclusions:** Software development, curriculum design, IT support, marketing services, and operational consulting.
*   **Exclusions:** W-2 Employment contracts, simple retail purchases of off-the-shelf goods (unless a contract is required by the vendor), and real estate leases (governed by separate standards).

### 4. Policy Statements (Contract Terms)

*Note: The following sections represent the standard terms to be included in the executed agreement.*

#### 4.1 Engagement and Scope of Services
**4.1.1 Statement of Work.** The Vendor shall provide services as described in attached Statements of Work (SOW). Each SOW shall be incorporated into this Agreement by reference. In the event of a conflict between an SOW and this MVA, the terms of this MVA shall control.
**4.1.2 Standard of Care.** Vendor warrants that services will be performed in a professional, workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards applicable to the Vendor’s trade.

#### 4.2 Compensation and Payment
**4.2.1 Invoicing.** Vendor shall submit invoices monthly or upon milestone completion as defined in the SOW. Invoices must detail the specific services performed and hours worked (if applicable).
**4.2.2 Payment Terms.** The Organization shall pay undisputed invoices within thirty (30) days of receipt (Net 30).
**4.2.3 Tax-Exempt Status.** The Organization is a tax-exempt entity under IRS Section 501(c)(3) and Texas Tax Code. The Organization shall provide a Texas Sales and Use Tax Resale/Exemption Certificate. Vendor shall not charge sales tax on exempt goods/services.

#### 4.3 Independent Contractor Relationship
**4.3.1 Classification.** Vendor is an independent contractor, not an employee. Vendor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes, including self-employment tax.
**4.3.2 No Benefits.** Vendor is not eligible for the Organization’s employee benefits, including health insurance, retirement plans, or paid time off.

#### 4.4 Intellectual Property (Bylaws Article IX Alignment)
**4.4.1 Work Made for Hire.** To the fullest extent permitted by law, all Deliverables created by Vendor for the Organization are "works made for hire."
**4.4.2 Assignment.** If any Deliverables do not qualify as works made for hire, Vendor hereby irrevocably assigns to the Organization all right, title, and interest in and to the Deliverables, including all copyrights, patents, and trade secrets.
**4.4.3 Open Source Mission.** Vendor acknowledges the Organization’s mission to provide open-source resources. Vendor warrants that Deliverables do not infringe on third-party IP and grants the Organization the right to license Deliverables under open-source licenses (e.g., Creative Commons, MIT) at its discretion.

#### 4.5 Confidentiality and Data Security
**4.5.1 Non-Disclosure.** Vendor agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not to disclose it to third parties without written consent.
**4.5.2 Data Privacy.** Vendor agrees to comply with all applicable data protection laws (including GDPR, CCPA, and TDPSA). If Vendor processes Personal Data, they must execute the Organization’s **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)**.
**4.5.3 Security Standards.** Vendor represents that they maintain reasonable cybersecurity controls to prevent unauthorized access to the Organization’s data.

#### 4.6 Youth Protection and Safeguarding
**4.6.1 Background Checks.** If the Scope of Services involves direct contact (physical or digital) with minors, Vendor personnel must undergo a criminal background check approved by the Organization prior to commencing work.
**4.6.2 Conduct.** Vendor agrees to adhere to the Organization’s **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy** and **Code of Conduct** when interacting with youth participants.

#### 4.7 Indemnification and Insurance
**4.7.1 Indemnification.** Vendor agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Organization, its Directors, Officers, and volunteers from any claims, liabilities, or expenses arising from Vendor’s negligence, misconduct, or breach of this Agreement.
**4.7.2 Insurance.** Vendor shall maintain, at its own expense, General Liability Insurance and Professional Liability (E&O) Insurance with limits reasonably sufficient to cover the risks associated with the services provided.

#### 4.8 Termination
**4.8.1 For Convenience.** The Organization may terminate this Agreement or any SOW at any time upon thirty (30) days written notice.
**4.8.2 For Cause.** Either party may terminate immediately upon material breach by the other party if such breach is not cured within ten (10) days of notice.
**4.8.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Vendor shall immediately deliver all Work Product (finished or unfinished) to the Organization.

#### 4.9 Federal Grant Provisions (2 CFR 200 Compliance)
*Required for all contracts funded by federal awards.*
**4.9.1 Debarment and Suspension.** Vendor certifies that neither it nor its principals are presently debarred, suspended, proposed for debarment, declared ineligible, or voluntarily excluded from participation by any federal department or agency (SAM.gov verification).
**4.9.2 Anti-Lobbying.** Vendor certifies that no federal appropriated funds have been paid to influence an officer or employee of any agency or Member of Congress in connection with this contract (Byrd Anti-Lobbying Amendment).
**4.9.3 Equal Employment Opportunity.** Vendor shall comply with E.O. 11246, "Equal Employment Opportunity," as amended.
**4.9.4 Access to Records.** Vendor agrees to provide the Organization, the Federal awarding agency, the Comptroller General of the United States, or any of their authorized representatives access to any books, documents, papers, and records of the Vendor which are directly pertinent to this contract for the purposes of making audits, examinations, excerpts, and transcriptions.

#### 4.10 General Provisions
**4.10.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas. Venue for any disputes shall be [County], Texas.
**4.10.2 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Authorized signatory for contracts up to the limit defined in the *Delegation of Authority Matrix*. |
| **Procurement Officer** | Responsible for customizing this template for specific engagements, verifying SAM.gov status, and managing the SOW process. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews any substantive changes or redlines requested by the Vendor to this template. |
| **Vendor** | Adheres to all terms, delivers services per SOW, and maintains required insurance/compliance. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to utilize this template for vendor engagements exceeding the micro-purchase threshold may result in:
1.  **Internal Audit Findings:** Non-compliance with 2 CFR 200 procurement standards.
2.  **Financial Liability:** Uninsured losses or tax liabilities due to misclassification.
3.  **Disciplinary Action:** Employees circumventing this process may face disciplinary action up to and including termination.
