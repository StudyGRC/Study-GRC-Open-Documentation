### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is to mandate the specific contractual terms and conditions required when Study GRC Inc. (the "Controller") engages a third-party vendor (the "Processor") to process Personal Data. This document ensures compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) Article 28, the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), and **Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)** of the Study GRC Inc. Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all vendors, contractors, service providers, and partners ("Processors") who access, collect, store, transmit, or process Personal Data on behalf of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Inclusions:** Cloud service providers, email marketing platforms, donor management systems, HR/Payroll processors, and IT support vendors.
*   **Exclusions:** Vendors who do not have access to Personal Data (e.g., hardware suppliers who do not access the device software).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Controller** | Study GRC Inc., the entity that determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data. |
| **Processor** | The third-party entity processing Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. |
| **Personal Data** | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including "Personal Data" as defined by GDPR and "Personal Identifying Information" as defined by TDPSA. |
| **Sub-processor** | Any third party engaged by the Processor to assist in fulfilling its obligations with respect to providing the Services. |
| **Data Breach** | A breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed. |
| **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** | Modular contractual clauses adopted by the European Commission to ensure appropriate data protection safeguards for data transfers from the EEA to third countries. |

### 4. Policy Statements (Contractual Clauses)

All agreements executed between Study GRC Inc. and a Processor must contain the following binding clauses:

#### 4.1 Instructions and Purpose
The Processor shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, including with regard to transfers of personal data to a third country or an international organization, unless required to do so by Union or Member State law to which the Processor is subject. The Processor shall immediately inform the Controller if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes the GDPR, TDPSA, or other data protection provisions.

#### 4.2 Confidentiality
The Processor ensures that persons authorized to process the Personal Data (employees, contractors, agents) have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality. This obligation survives the termination of the agreement.

#### 4.3 Security of Processing (GDPR Art. 32)
Taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, and the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing, the Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including inter alia as appropriate:
1.  The pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data.
2.  The ability to ensure the ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems and services.
3.  The ability to restore the availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident.
4.  A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures for ensuring the security of the processing.

#### 4.4 Sub-processing
The Processor shall not engage another processor (Sub-processor) without prior specific or general written authorization of the Controller.
*   **General Authorization:** The Processor must inform the Controller of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of other processors, giving the Controller the opportunity to object to such changes.
*   **Liability:** Where a Sub-processor fails to fulfill its data protection obligations, the initial Processor shall remain fully liable to the Controller for the performance of that Sub-processor's obligations.

#### 4.5 Assistance with Data Subject Rights
Taking into account the nature of the processing, the Processor shall assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as this is possible, for the fulfillment of the Controller's obligation to respond to requests for exercising the Data Subject's rights (e.g., Right to Access, Rectification, Erasure, Portability) laid down in Chapter III of the GDPR and the TDPSA.

#### 4.6 Incident and Breach Notification
The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay, and in no case later than **24 hours** after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach. The notification must include:
1.  The nature of the breach.
2.  The categories and approximate number of data subjects concerned.
3.  The likely consequences of the breach.
4.  Measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the breach.

#### 4.7 Return or Deletion of Data
At the choice of the Controller, the Processor shall delete or return all the Personal Data to the Controller after the end of the provision of services relating to processing, and delete existing copies unless Union, Member State, or Texas law requires storage of the Personal Data.

#### 4.8 Audit Rights
The Processor shall make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in this Article and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or another auditor mandated by the Controller.

#### 4.9 International Transfers
If the Processor transfers Personal Data outside the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom to a country not deemed to have adequate data protection laws, the Processor agrees to abide by the **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)**, which are hereby incorporated by reference into this agreement.

#### 4.10 Youth Data Protection (COPPA & K-12 Focus)
In alignment with Study GRC Inc.'s mission and **Bylaws Article I (Purposes)** regarding educational resources:
1.  The Processor acknowledges that data may belong to children under the age of 13.
2.  The Processor is strictly prohibited from using such data for behavioral advertising, profiling, or any commercial purpose unrelated to the direct provision of services to the Controller.
3.  The Processor must comply with all applicable requirements of the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).

#### 4.11 Intellectual Property
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, the Processor acknowledges that any data provided by the Controller remains the sole property of the Controller. The Processor acquires no rights or licenses to the data except for the limited purpose of performing the services.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Contract Owner** | Ensures this DPA is included in the master contract before sharing any data with a vendor. |
| **CISO** | Reviews the Processor's security measures (Section 4.3) via the *Vendor Security Risk Assessment (VSRA)*. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Approves any redlines or modifications requested by the Processor. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves DPAs associated with "Fundamental Actions" or contracts exceeding significant financial thresholds as defined in the *Delegation of Authority Matrix*. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to execute a valid DPA prior to sharing Personal Data with a third party constitutes a violation of Study GRC Inc. policy and **Bylaws Article IX**.
*   **Internal:** Employees found violating this standard may face disciplinary action up to and including termination.
*   **External:** Processors found in breach of this agreement may face immediate contract termination, legal action for damages, and reporting to relevant regulatory authorities (e.g., ICO, Texas Attorney General).
