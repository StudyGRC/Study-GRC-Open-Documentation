### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish the legal and operational terms governing the relationship between Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") and individuals invited to speak, present, or instruct ("the Speaker"). This agreement ensures the Organization secures necessary Intellectual Property (IP) rights to fulfill its Open Source mission (Bylaws Article IX), protects the Organization from liability, and ensures all content aligns with the Organization's charitable and educational purposes under 501(c)(3) regulations.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all guest speakers, panelists, instructors, and presenters engaged by Study GRC Inc. for webinars, conferences, workshops, or recorded content, whether the engagement is paid, volunteer, remote, or in-person.

**Exclusions:** This does not apply to internal Board of Directors meetings or internal staff administrative meetings.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Work Product** | All materials created or presented by the Speaker, including slide decks, handouts, code repositories, video recordings, and audio recordings. |
| **Likeness** | The Speaker’s name, voice, image, photograph, and biographical information. |
| **Open Source** | Resources made freely available to the public for use, modification, and distribution, consistent with the Organization's mission. |
| **Solicitation** | The act of selling, advertising, or promoting commercial products or services for personal or corporate financial gain during an educational session. |

### 4. Policy Statements (Agreement Terms)

By accepting an engagement with Study GRC Inc., the Speaker agrees to the following terms:

#### 4.1. Nature of Engagement
The Speaker serves as an independent contractor, not an employee. The Speaker has no authority to bind the Organization to any contract or obligation.

#### 4.2. Intellectual Property and Content License
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX (Intellectual Property)** and the Organization's mission to remove financial barriers to knowledge:

*   **4.2.1 Ownership:** The Speaker retains copyright ownership of their pre-existing content and materials presented.
*   **4.2.2 Grant of License:** The Speaker grants Study GRC Inc. a non-exclusive, perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide license to record, reproduce, distribute, display, perform, and create derivative works of the Work Product. This includes the right to publish the recording and materials on the Organization’s website, social media, and learning management systems under a Creative Commons (CC-BY or CC-BY-SA) license.
*   **4.2.3 Warranty of Originality:** The Speaker warrants that they are the sole creator of the content or have obtained all necessary permissions for third-party materials (images, charts, data) included in the presentation. The Speaker warrants that the content does not infringe upon the IP rights of any third party.

#### 4.3. Media Release
The Speaker grants the Organization permission to photograph, audio record, and video record their presentation. The Organization may use the Speaker's Likeness for archival, educational, and promotional purposes related to the Organization's mission.

#### 4.4. Content Standards and Code of Conduct
*   **4.4.1 Educational Purpose:** The presentation must be educational in nature. Overt Solicitation, "sales pitches," or promotion of specific commercial products for financial gain is strictly prohibited to protect the Organization's tax-exempt status.
*   **4.4.2 Youth Protection:** Acknowledging the Organization's K-12 focus, the Speaker agrees to strictly adhere to the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy (HR-POL-060)**. Content must be free of profanity, sexual themes, or violence.
*   **4.4.3 Professionalism:** The Speaker must adhere to the **Volunteer Code of Conduct**, treating all attendees with respect and refraining from discriminatory, harassing, or offensive language.

#### 4.5. Compensation and Expenses
*   **4.5.1 Volunteer Status:** Unless a separate "Speaking Fee Addendum" is executed, the engagement is pro bono (volunteer).
*   **4.5.2 Reimbursement:** Travel or material expenses will only be reimbursed if pre-approved in writing by the Executive Director, in accordance with the **Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy (FIN-POL-037)**.

#### 4.6. Indemnification
The Speaker agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Study GRC Inc., its Directors, Officers, and agents from any claims, damages, or liabilities arising out of the Speaker’s breach of this agreement, specifically including claims of copyright infringement regarding the Speaker's presentation materials.

#### 4.7. Cancellation
*   **4.7.1 By Speaker:** The Speaker must provide at least 14 days' notice of cancellation, barring genuine emergencies.
*   **4.7.2 By Organization:** The Organization reserves the right to cancel the engagement or pull the Speaker's content at any time if the Speaker violates the Code of Conduct or if the content is deemed incompatible with the Organization's mission.

#### 4.8. Governing Law
This agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures this Agreement is executed prior to confirming any speaker slot. Reviews presentation materials for compliance with content standards. |
| **Speaker** | Signs the agreement, delivers original content, and adheres to the Code of Conduct. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves any deviations from standard terms (e.g., speaking fees) or exceptions to the standard IP license. |
| **Marketing Team** | Ensures proper attribution of the Speaker in all published materials. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to execute this agreement will result in the revocation of the invitation to speak.
*   **Violation of IP Warranty:** If a Speaker presents infringing material, the content will be immediately removed, and the Organization may seek legal recourse for damages.
*   **Violation of Conduct:** Speakers violating the Code of Conduct or Youth Protection standards will be immediately removed from the event and permanently barred from future participation.
