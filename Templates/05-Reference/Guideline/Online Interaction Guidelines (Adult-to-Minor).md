### 1. Purpose
The purpose of these guidelines is to establish clear, transparent boundaries for all digital interactions between adult representatives of Study GRC Inc. and minor participants (under the age of 18). These guidelines are designed to:
1.  Protect minors from potential grooming, exploitation, and cyberbullying.
2.  Protect adult volunteers and staff from misunderstandings and false allegations.
3.  Ensure compliance with the Organization’s commitment to transparency and the Texas Family Code regarding the safety of children.

### 2. Scope
This document applies to all **Adult Representatives** (employees, board members, contractors, and volunteers) when interacting with **Minors** through any digital medium associated with Study GRC Inc.

**Inclusions:**
*   Official communication platforms (e.g., Discord, Slack, Microsoft Teams).
*   Video conferencing tools (e.g., Zoom, Google Meet).
*   Email and project management tools.
*   Social media interactions.

**Exclusions:**
*   Interactions between a parent/guardian and their own child.
*   Interactions between minors (peer-to-peer), which are covered under the *Youth Code of Conduct*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Adult Representative** | Any individual age 18 or older formally affiliated with Study GRC Inc. in a volunteer or professional capacity. |
| **Minor** | Any participant under the age of 18. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The mandate that an adult must never be alone with a minor. In a digital context, this means no private, one-on-one communication without a third party present (copy/cc). |
| **Grooming** | The predatory act of maneuvering a minor into a position of vulnerability or isolation to commit abuse. |
| **Authorized Channel** | Communication platforms officially sanctioned, monitored, and administered by Study GRC Inc. IT security. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 The "Digital Two-Deep" Rule
To ensure transparency, **one-on-one private communication between an unrelated adult and a minor is strictly prohibited.**

*   **4.1.1 Public Channels First:** All communications should occur in public channels (e.g., public Discord channels, forum threads) where content is visible to the community and moderators.
*   **4.1.2 Group DMs:** If a direct message is necessary for specific project work, it must include at least **two** Adult Representatives (e.g., the Volunteer and the DKG or a Program Manager).
*   **4.1.3 Email Protocol:** When emailing a minor, the Adult Representative must Copy (CC) a parent/guardian OR another staff member/official organizational email address (e.g., `safety@studygrc.org`).
*   **4.1.4 Unsolicited DMs:** If a minor initiates a private Direct Message (DM) to an adult:
    *   Do not reply with substantive conversation.
    *   Reply immediately adding a third party (e.g., "I am adding [Staff Name] to this chat so we can help you.") OR redirect them to a public channel.
    *   Take a screenshot of the initial contact for records if the content is concerning.

#### 4.2 Authorized Platforms Only
Adult Representatives must strictly adhere to using **Authorized Channels** for all Study GRC Inc. business.

*   **4.2.1 Personal Accounts:** Adults must **never** use personal social media accounts (Facebook Messenger, Snapchat, Instagram DMs, WhatsApp, personal cell phone numbers) to communicate with minors regarding organizational business.
*   **4.2.2 Friend Requests:** Adults should not accept "Friend" or "Follow" requests from minor participants on personal social media platforms. If a request is received, the adult should decline it.
*   **4.2.3 Gaming Platforms:** Interactions on gaming platforms (e.g., Steam, Xbox Live) must only occur via official Organization accounts or events, never personal gamertags in private lobbies.

#### 4.3 Video Conferencing Protocols
When conducting webinars, tutoring, or meetings via video (Zoom/Meet):

*   **4.3.1 No One-on-One Video:** A minimum of two adults must be present on the call if minors are participating. If the second adult drops off, the call must be terminated immediately.
*   **4.3.2 Recording:** Sessions involving minors should be recorded for transparency, with prior notification given to all participants. Recordings are stored securely per the *Data Retention Policy*.
*   **4.3.3 Appearance and Environment:** Adults must appear in professional attire. Backgrounds must be neutral or use an organizational virtual background. Adults must ensure no inappropriate items (alcohol, political signage, offensive material) are visible.

#### 4.4 Content and Tone
*   **4.4.1 Professionalism:** Interactions must remain constructive, encouraging, and focused on the GRC educational mission.
*   **4.4.2 Secrets:** Adults must never ask a minor to keep a secret, nor promise to keep a minor's secret (unless it involves a legal privilege, which generally does not apply to volunteers).
*   **4.4.3 Over-Sharing:** Adults should not share personal burdens, relationship issues, or financial problems with minors. This creates an inappropriate emotional dependency.

#### 4.5 Hours of Operation
Digital interactions should respect reasonable hours. Unless an official event is scheduled, adults should avoid messaging minors late at night (e.g., between 9:00 PM and 7:00 AM local time) to maintain professional boundaries.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Adult Representatives** | Adhere strictly to the Digital Two-Deep rule; report any accidental or attempted breaches of these guidelines immediately. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Monitor official channels for compliance; act as the designated third party in sensitive communications; manage the "Friend Request" rejection protocol. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Configure platforms (Discord/Slack) to technically limit private DMs between adults and minors where feasible; maintain audit logs of communications. |
| **Parents/Guardians** | Monitor their child’s online activity; ensure their child understands they should not DM adults privately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Adherence to these guidelines is mandatory for maintaining the safety of the Study GRC Inc. community.

*   **Zero Tolerance:** Any evidence of grooming, sexual solicitation, or abusive language will result in immediate termination of the relationship with Study GRC Inc. and a report to law enforcement and the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services.
*   **Violations of Process:** Failure to follow the "Two-Deep" rule (even with innocent intent) may result in retraining, suspension, or removal from volunteer duties.
