### 1. Purpose
The purpose of these guidelines is to empower Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") Local Chapters to effectively build community and disseminate knowledge while protecting the Organization’s reputation, tax-exempt status, and intellectual property. This document establishes the standards for voice, visual identity, and risk management across all Chapter-managed communication channels to ensure a unified, professional, and inclusive brand presence.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to all **Chapter Officers**, **Volunteers**, and **Members** who have administrative access to or authority over official Chapter communication channels.

**In Scope:**
*   Social Media Platforms (e.g., LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Discord, Instagram).
*   Chapter Newsletters and Email Marketing.
*   Event promotion materials (digital and print).
*   Public-facing community interactions (comments, forums).

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal social media accounts of volunteers, provided they do not claim to speak on behalf of the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Official Chapter Account** | Any social media profile, page, or group created to represent a specific Study GRC Local Chapter (e.g., "Study GRC - Dallas"). |
| **Brand Assets** | The Organization’s logos, color palettes, typography, and taglines as defined in the *Brand Identity and Style Guide*. |
| **Prohibited Content** | Content that violates the Organization's values or legal obligations, including hate speech, political campaigning, commercial solicitation without approval, or copyright infringement. |
| **DKG** | Director of Kindness and Generosity; the Operational Officer responsible for community culture. |

### 4. Guidelines and Standards

#### 4.1 Account Governance and Security
To ensure business continuity and security, all Chapter digital assets are the property of Study GRC Inc., not the individual volunteer.

*   **4.1.1 Naming Convention:** Accounts must follow the standard naming convention: `Study GRC [City/Region]` (e.g., "Study GRC London").
*   **4.1.2 Credential Management:**
    *   Credentials must not be tied to a volunteer's personal email address.
    *   Chapters must use a shared password manager (e.g., 1Password, Bitwarden) or an official Chapter email alias (e.g., `london@studygrc.org`).
    *   Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) must be enabled on all platforms where available.
*   **4.1.3 Access Revocation:** Upon the departure of a Chapter Officer, passwords must be rotated within 24 hours.

#### 4.2 Brand Visual Identity
Consistency builds trust. Chapters must adhere to the *Brand Identity and Style Guide*.

*   **4.2.1 Logos:** Use only the official Chapter Logo provided by HQ. Do not alter, stretch, recolor, or recreate the logo.
*   **4.2.2 Imagery:** Use high-quality, professional images. Avoid pixelated graphics or unapproved clip art.
*   **4.2.3 Templates:** Whenever possible, use the Canva templates provided by the Marketing team for event flyers and announcements.

#### 4.3 Voice and Tone
Our voice is **Educational, Inclusive, Professional, and Kind**.

*   **4.3.1 The "Kindness" Standard:** In accordance with the mission of the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG), all communications must be respectful. Avoid sarcasm, aggressive debates, or "calling out" individuals publicly.
*   **4.3.2 Educational Focus:** Content should focus on GRC knowledge sharing, mentorship, and career development.
*   **4.3.3 Neutrality:** As a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, Chapters **must not** engage in political campaigning, endorse political candidates, or lobby for legislation not directly related to the Organization’s mission.

#### 4.4 Content Guardrails and Prohibited Conduct
*   **4.4.1 Commercial Solicitation:** Chapters may not post purely commercial advertisements for third-party vendors unless part of an approved Sponsorship Agreement.
*   **4.4.2 Intellectual Property:** Do not post copyrighted material (images, articles, charts) without proper attribution or license. Refer to *Bylaws Article IX* regarding IP ownership.
*   **4.4.3 Youth Protection (Strict Compliance):**
    *   **No Direct Messaging:** Chapter accounts must never initiate private Direct Messages (DMs) with minors (under 18).
    *   **Photography:** Do not post photos of minors without a signed *Parental Consent and Liability Waiver*.
    *   **Tagging:** Do not tag minors in social media posts.

#### 4.5 Crisis Management
If a Chapter account receives hostile comments, threats, or is involved in a controversy:
1.  **Do not engage** or argue in the comment section.
2.  **Screenshot** the interaction immediately.
3.  **Hide** the comment if it violates platform community standards (e.g., hate speech).
4.  **Escalate** immediately to the DKG and the VP of Education & Training via the internal incident reporting channel.

#### 4.6 Accessibility
All digital communications must be accessible to individuals with disabilities, aligning with the *Digital Accessibility Policy*.
*   **Alt Text:** All images must include descriptive Alternative Text.
*   **CamelCase:** Use CamelCase for hashtags (e.g., use `#StudyGRC` instead of `#studygrc`) to ensure screen readers can parse the words.
*   **Video Captions:** All uploaded video content must include closed captions.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chapter President** | Ultimately accountable for all content published on Chapter channels; ensures credentials are secured. |
| **Chapter Social Media Lead** | Creates and schedules content; monitors engagement; ensures adherence to brand guidelines. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Provides oversight on community culture; handles escalations regarding behavioral violations. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Approves the creation of new Chapter accounts; holds authority to shut down non-compliant accounts. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with these guidelines may result in:
1.  **Content Removal:** Immediate removal of the offending post.
2.  **Access Revocation:** Removal of administrative rights for the specific volunteer.
3.  **Chapter Probation:** Suspension of the Chapter’s charter or funding.
4.  **Legal Action:** In cases of severe IP infringement or defamation.

Per *Bylaws Article IX*, all content created for the Chapter is "work made for hire" and is the sole property of Study GRC Inc.
