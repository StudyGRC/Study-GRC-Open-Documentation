### 1. Purpose
The purpose of these guidelines is to ensure that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") maintains a consistent, professional, and accurate voice in all public communications. As a 501(c)(3) non-profit dedicated to open-source GRC education, our reputation is our most valuable asset. These guidelines establish protocols for interacting with media outlets, podcasters, and industry analysts to mitigate reputational risk and maximize mission impact.

### 2. Scope
This document applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers of the Organization.

**In Scope:**
*   Traditional media interviews (TV, Radio, Print).
*   Digital media (Podcasts, Blogs, YouTube channels, Livestreams).
*   Panel discussions and conference keynotes where the individual is representing Study GRC Inc.
*   Official quotes or statements provided to third parties.

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal social media interactions (governed by `HR-POL-060 Social Media Policy`), provided the individual does not claim to speak on behalf of the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authorized Spokesperson** | An individual explicitly designated by the Board or Executive Director to speak on record on behalf of the Organization. |
| **Media Inquiry** | Any request for information, interviews, or comments from a journalist, blogger, podcaster, or industry analyst. |
| **Bridging** | A communication technique used to transition from a difficult or irrelevant question to a key message or positive subject. |
| **Off the Record** | A verbal agreement that information provided will not be published. **Note:** Study GRC personnel should assume *nothing* is truly "off the record." |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 Authorization and Spokesperson Designation
Per **Bylaws Article IV**, the President and the Executive Director (CEO) are the primary Authorized Spokespersons for the Organization.
*   **4.1.1** No other Director, Officer, volunteer, or staff member may accept a media interview on behalf of the Organization without prior approval from the Executive Director or the Board President.
*   **4.1.2** All incoming media inquiries must be immediately routed to the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or the Executive Director for vetting.
*   **4.1.3** Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) may be designated to speak on specific technical topics (e.g., the CISO speaking on data privacy trends), provided the scope of the interview is limited to that domain.

#### 4.2 Core Messaging Pillars
When representing Study GRC Inc., spokespersons must align with the Organization's tax-exempt purpose as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**:
1.  **Accessibility:** We remove financial barriers to GRC knowledge.
2.  **Community-Driven:** We are an ecosystem of learners and practitioners, not a vendor.
3.  **Neutrality:** We provide objective, open-source resources. We do not endorse specific commercial products or political candidates.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Topics
To maintain 501(c)(3) compliance and protect the Organization, spokespersons must **never** discuss:
*   **Political Endorsements:** Support or opposition for candidates for public office (strictly prohibited by IRS regulations).
*   **Confidential Data:** Specific details regarding members, donors, or students (violates `IS-POL-001 Master Information Security Policy`).
*   **Legal Matters:** Pending litigation, disputes, or sensitive internal investigations.
*   **Speculation:** Rumors regarding competitors, partners, or unannounced organizational changes.

#### 4.4 Interview Best Practices
*   **Preparation:** Never enter an interview unprepared. Request the topic or questions in advance. Prepare 3 key "talking points" that tie back to the Mission.
*   **"On the Record":** Assume microphones are always on and email chains are publishable. Never say anything you would not want to see as a headline.
*   **Bridging:** If asked a question outside your scope or one that invites speculation, use a bridge:
    *   *"That’s an interesting question, but what we focus on at Study GRC is..."*
    *   *"I can’t speak to that specific incident, but generally, our policy is..."*
*   **Accuracy:** If you do not know the answer, say *"I don't have that information right now, but I can follow up with you."* Do not guess.

#### 4.5 Youth Protection in Media
Given the Organization's K-12 focus and **Child Safeguarding Policy (`HR-POL-070`)**:
*   **4.5.1** No photos, videos, or names of minors (under 18) participating in Study GRC programs may be released to the media without a signed `Parental Consent and Media Release Form`.
*   **4.5.2** Interviews with minors must always be supervised by a background-checked adult staff member or volunteer.

#### 4.6 Crisis Communication
In the event of a crisis (e.g., data breach, scandal):
*   Refer strictly to the **Crisis Communications Plan (`COM-PLN-003`)**.
*   The Executive Director (CEO) is the *sole* spokesperson during a crisis unless they designate otherwise.
*   "No comment" is discouraged; instead, use: *"We are currently investigating the situation and will provide an update as soon as we have verified facts."*

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Serves as the primary voice of the Organization; approves all other spokespersons; manages crisis communications. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Acts as the primary media contact; vets incoming inquiries; prepares briefing materials for spokespersons. |
| **Board President** | Speaks on matters of Governance, Board strategy, or in the absence of the CEO. |
| **Volunteers/Staff** | Must decline direct media requests and route them to the DKG/CEO; must adhere to messaging pillars if authorized to speak. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with these guidelines may result in disciplinary action.
*   **Unauthorized Representation:** Speaking on behalf of the Organization without authorization is a violation of the **Code of Ethics** and may result in termination of volunteer status or employment.
*   **Misrepresentation:** Intentionally spreading false information or violating the 501(c)(3) political prohibition may result in immediate removal and legal action.
