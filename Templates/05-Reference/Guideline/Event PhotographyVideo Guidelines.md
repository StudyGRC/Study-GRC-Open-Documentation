### 1. Purpose
The purpose of these guidelines is to establish a respectful and legally compliant framework for capturing photography, video, and audio recordings at Study GRC Inc. events (both in-person and virtual). These guidelines aim to balance the Organization’s legitimate interest in marketing and community documentation with the privacy rights, safety, and comfort of our members, particularly regarding the protection of minors and individuals who wish to opt out of media capture.

### 2. Scope
This document applies to:
*   **Events:** All official Study GRC Inc. gatherings, conferences, workshops, and social mixers, whether physical or virtual.
*   **Personnel:** Staff, Board Directors, volunteers, and contracted photographers/videographers.
*   **Attendees:** General members and guests capturing content for personal or social media use.

**Exclusions:** This document does not cover security surveillance footage, which is governed by the *Physical Security Policy*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18. |
| **Media Assets** | Digital or analog photographs, video footage, audio recordings, and screenshots. |
| **Opt-Out Indicator** | A physical identifier (e.g., a red lanyard or sticker) used at in-person events to signal that an attendee does not consent to being photographed. |
| **General Ambience** | Wide-angle shots of a crowd where no single individual is the focal point or readily identifiable. |
| **Verified Consent** | Written permission obtained via a waiver or release form, specifically required for minors or close-up feature shots. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 General Notice and Implied Consent (Adults)
For adult attendees at Study GRC Inc. events, the Organization operates under a "Notice of Filming" standard.
*   **Signage:** Clear signage must be posted at the registration desk and main entry points stating: *"Photography and video recording are in progress. Entry constitutes consent to be photographed for Study GRC Inc. media."*
*   **Virtual Events:** The host must verbally announce or display a slide indicating that the session is being recorded before recording commences.

#### 4.2 Protection of Minors (Strict Adherence)
Given the Organization’s K-12 focus and *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, special care is mandatory for minors.
*   **No Implicit Consent:** Minors cannot provide legal consent.
*   **Mandatory Waiver:** No close-up photography or video of a minor's face is permitted unless a *Parental Consent and Liability Waiver* is on file.
*   **Identification:** At in-person events involving youth, minors with signed waivers should be visually distinguishable (e.g., specific badge color) from those without waivers.
*   **Default Rule:** If a photographer is unsure of a subject's age or consent status, they must refrain from taking the photo.

#### 4.3 Opt-Out Mechanisms (In-Person)
To respect individual privacy, Study GRC Inc. provides a "No-Photo" option.
*   **Indicators:** The registration desk will provide color-coded identifiers (e.g., Red Lanyards or "No Photo" stickers).
*   **Photographer Responsibility:** Official photographers must actively scan for these indicators. If a person with a "No Photo" indicator appears in a shot, the photographer must either:
    1.  Delete the photo; or
    2.  Crop/Blur the individual in post-production so they are unrecognizable.
*   **Speaker Exception:** Individuals voluntarily stepping onto a stage to present or speak are considered public figures for the duration of their talk, and the Opt-Out indicator may not apply to official stage coverage unless pre-negotiated.

#### 4.4 Virtual Event Screenshots
*   **Notification:** Attendees must be notified if the chat log or participant video grid will be captured for external publication.
*   **Name Sanitization:** Before publishing screenshots of virtual meetings publicly, the Communications team should blur or redact participant names unless specific permission has been granted.

#### 4.5 Intellectual Property and Usage
*   **Organization Ownership:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX (Intellectual Property)**, all media assets created by officers, staff, or volunteers while performing their duties are "works made for hire" and are the sole property of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Attendee Usage:** Attendees are encouraged to share photos on social media but must respect the *Code of Conduct*. Harassing photography or recording (e.g., upskirting, persistent recording after being asked to stop) is grounds for immediate expulsion.

#### 4.6 Takedown Requests (Right to Erasure)
Study GRC Inc. respects the "Right to Erasure" aligned with GDPR and best practices.
*   **Process:** If an individual finds a photo of themselves on a Study GRC Inc. platform that they wish removed, they may contact `privacy@studygrc.org`.
*   **Action:** The Organization will make reasonable efforts to remove the image from its digital channels within 7 business days, provided the individual is the primary subject of the image.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Manager** | Ensure "Filming in Progress" signage is visible and Opt-Out indicators (lanyards/stickers) are available at registration. |
| **Official Photographer** | Adhere to these guidelines, specifically avoiding "No Photo" indicators and strictly following rules regarding minors. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Oversee the culture of respect regarding photography and handle on-site disputes regarding media capture. |
| **Communications Team** | Review all media prior to publication to ensure no "No Photo" participants are identifiable and that minor consent is verified. |
| **Attendees** | Respect the privacy requests of other attendees and refrain from photographing those with Opt-Out indicators. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to these guidelines may result in the following actions:
*   **For Staff/Volunteers:** Disciplinary action up to and including termination of role.
*   **For Contractors:** Breach of contract and immediate termination of services.
*   **For Attendees:** Expulsion from the event without refund for violating the *Code of Conduct* (e.g., taking non-consensual photos for harassment purposes).
