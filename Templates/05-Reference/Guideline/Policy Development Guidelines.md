### 1. Purpose
The purpose of these guidelines is to establish a standardized framework for the creation, review, approval, and maintenance of all governance documentation within Study GRC Inc. These guidelines ensure that all organizational policies and procedures are consistent, legally compliant, accessible, and written in "Plain English" to facilitate understanding across our diverse, remote-first community.

### 2. Scope
These guidelines apply to the development of all **Controlled Documents** within the Organization.

*   **Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, Committee Members, and Staff responsible for drafting Policies, Standards, Procedures, Guidelines, and Forms.
*   **Exclusions:** Informal communications (e.g., emails, Slack messages), marketing copy, and ephemeral project documentation not intended for institutional governance.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Controlled Document** | Any official document that mandates behavior, sets standards, or outlines steps for execution, subject to version control. |
| **Policy (Level 1)** | A high-level statement of intent and rules. It answers "What" is required and "Why." Mandatory compliance. Approved by the Board. |
| **Standard (Level 2)** | Quantifiable requirements, specifications, or limits (e.g., password complexity). Mandatory compliance. Approved by the Executive Director or CISO. |
| **Procedure (Level 2)** | Step-by-step instructions to achieve a specific task. It answers "How." Mandatory compliance. Approved by Operational Leadership. |
| **Guideline (Level 3)** | Recommended best practices or advice. Discretionary compliance. Approved by Department Heads. |
| **SME** | Subject Matter Expert; an individual with deep technical or operational knowledge relevant to the document topic. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 Document Hierarchy and Authority
Authors must select the correct document type based on the intent of the content. Misclassification (e.g., labeling a mandatory rule as a "Guideline") creates compliance risks.

*   **Policies** govern the organization's stance on risk and compliance (e.g., *Conflict of Interest Policy*).
*   **Procedures** operationalize policies (e.g., *Interested Party Transaction Review Protocol*).
*   **Standards** set technical or metric boundaries (e.g., *Encryption Standards*).

#### 4.2 The Document Lifecycle
All Controlled Documents must follow this lifecycle to ensure quality and buy-in:

1.  **Drafting:** The Author utilizes the approved template and consults with SMEs.
2.  **Consultation:** Stakeholders affected by the policy are given an opportunity to review (e.g., IT must review HR policies regarding remote work technology).
3.  **Legal/Compliance Review:** High-risk documents (HR, Finance, Data Privacy) must be reviewed for alignment with Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance).
4.  **Approval:** The document is submitted to the Approving Authority defined in the *Delegation of Authority Matrix*.
5.  **Publication:** The document is assigned an ID, logged in the Master Documentation Registry, and published to the central repository.
6.  **Training:** Relevant staff/volunteers are notified or trained.

#### 4.3 Writing Standards (Style Guide)
To ensure clarity and reduce ambiguity, Authors must adhere to the following writing standards:

*   **Active Voice:** Use active voice to clearly identify who is responsible.
    *   *Correct:* "The Treasurer must review the bank reconciliation."
    *   *Incorrect:* "The bank reconciliation is to be reviewed."
*   **Mandatory vs. Discretionary Language:**
    *   Use **"Must"** or **"Shall"** for mandatory requirements.
    *   Use **"Should"** for strong recommendations.
    *   Use **"May"** for discretionary actions.
*   **Gender Neutrality:** Use role titles (e.g., "The Chair") or "they/them/their." Avoid "he/she."
*   **Plain English:** Avoid legalese (e.g., "heretofore," "aforementioned") unless strictly necessary for legal citations.
*   **Granularity:** Procedures should be detailed enough that a new volunteer could complete the task without supervision.

#### 4.4 Formatting and Structure
All documents must utilize the **Standard Governance Document Template**.

*   **Naming Convention:** Documents must be titled clearly and assigned an ID in the format: `[DEPT]-[TYPE]-[###]`.
    *   *Example:* `HR-POL-005` (Human Resources Policy #5).
*   **Section Headers:** The standard sections (Purpose, Scope, Definitions, Statements, Roles, Compliance) must be preserved. If a section is not applicable, mark it "N/A" rather than deleting it.
*   **Citations:** When referencing external laws, cite the specific code (e.g., *Texas BOC § 22.353* or *2 CFR 200.318*).

#### 4.5 Intellectual Property
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all governance documents created for the Organization are "works made for hire." Authors agree that the copyright for these documents resides solely with Study GRC Inc.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Document Author** | Drafts the content, consults with SMEs, and ensures adherence to these guidelines. |
| **Subject Matter Expert (SME)** | Reviews drafts for technical accuracy and operational feasibility. |
| **Governance Committee** | Maintains the *Master Documentation Registry* and assigns Document IDs. |
| **Approving Authority** | Reviews the final draft for strategic alignment and risk, and formally signs/approves the document. |
| **Secretary** | Ensures Board-level policies are recorded in the minutes and stored in accordance with the *Document Retention Policy*. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
While this document is a Guideline, the *Governance Committee* reserves the right to reject any Policy, Standard, or Procedure that does not adhere to the formatting, style, and lifecycle requirements outlined herein. Documents not properly approved and registered in the Master Documentation Registry are considered invalid and unenforceable.
