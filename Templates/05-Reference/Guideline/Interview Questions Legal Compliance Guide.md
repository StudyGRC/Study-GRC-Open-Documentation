### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this guide is to provide Study GRC Inc. hiring managers, interview panelists, and volunteers with a clear framework for conducting interviews that comply with federal and state anti-discrimination laws. This guide ensures that all candidates are evaluated solely on their ability to perform the job, mitigating the risk of legal liability under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), and the Texas Labor Code.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to all individuals involved in the recruitment, screening, and interviewing process for Study GRC Inc., including:
*   Employees and Operational Officers.
*   Members of the Board of Directors conducting interviews.
*   Volunteers serving on interview panels.
*   Third-party recruiters acting on behalf of the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Protected Class** | A group of people with a common characteristic who are legally protected from employment discrimination. Includes race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, or gender identity), national origin, age (40 or older), disability, and genetic information. |
| **BFOQ** | **Bona Fide Occupational Qualification.** A very rare legal exception where a specific characteristic (e.g., religion or gender) is reasonably necessary to the normal operation of that particular business or enterprise. |
| **Essential Functions** | The fundamental job duties that an employee must be able to perform, with or without reasonable accommodation. |
| **Disparate Impact** | Employment practices that are facially neutral in their treatment of different groups but that in fact fall more harshly on one group than another. |

### 4. Guidelines for Interview Inquiries

Interviewers must focus strictly on the candidate's ability to perform the **Essential Functions** of the role. Questions that elicit information regarding a candidate's membership in a Protected Class are prohibited unless a BFOQ exists.

#### 4.1 Name, Age, and National Origin
**Legal Basis:** Title VII; ADEA; IRCA.

| Subject | 🔴 **PROHIBITED (Do Not Ask)** | 🟢 **ACCEPTABLE (You May Ask)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Name** | "Is that a [Nationality] name?"<br>"What was your maiden name?" | "Have you worked under any other name?" (Only for background check purposes). |
| **Age** | "How old are you?"<br>"What year did you graduate high school?"<br>"When do you plan to retire?" | "Are you at least 18 years of age?"<br>"Can you provide proof of age after hire?" (If required for youth protection laws). |
| **National Origin / Citizenship** | "Where were you born?"<br>"Are you a US Citizen?"<br>"Is English your first language?" | "Are you legally authorized to work in the United States?"<br>"What languages do you read, speak, or write fluently?" (Only if relevant to the job). |

#### 4.2 Family, Marital Status, and Gender
**Legal Basis:** Title VII (Sex Discrimination); Pregnancy Discrimination Act.

| Subject | 🔴 **PROHIBITED (Do Not Ask)** | 🟢 **ACCEPTABLE (You May Ask)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Marital Status / Family** | "Are you married?"<br>"Do you have children?"<br>"Do you plan to have a family?"<br>"What does your spouse do?" | "This position requires some travel/overtime. Is that a problem for you?"<br>"Are you willing to relocate if necessary?" |
| **Pregnancy** | "Are you pregnant?"<br>"Do you plan on having more children?" | "This job requires lifting 25lbs. Can you perform this function with or without accommodation?" |
| **Childcare** | "Do you have reliable childcare?" | "This position requires work between 9 AM and 5 PM. Can you meet this schedule?" |

#### 4.3 Disability and Health
**Legal Basis:** Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).

| Subject | 🔴 **PROHIBITED (Do Not Ask)** | 🟢 **ACCEPTABLE (You May Ask)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Health Status** | "Do you have a disability?"<br>"Have you ever filed a Workers' Comp claim?"<br>"How many sick days did you take last year?" | "Can you perform the essential functions of this job with or without reasonable accommodation?"<br>"Please describe/demonstrate how you would perform [Specific Task]." |
| **Physical Traits** | "How tall are you?"<br>"How much do you weigh?" | (Only if BFOQ) "This equipment requires the operator to be within a specific height range for safety. Do you meet this requirement?" |

#### 4.4 Religion and Lifestyle
**Legal Basis:** Title VII (Religious Discrimination).

| Subject | 🔴 **PROHIBITED (Do Not Ask)** | 🟢 **ACCEPTABLE (You May Ask)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Religion** | "What church do you attend?"<br>"Will you need off for [Religious Holiday]?"<br>"Do your religious beliefs prevent you from working weekends?" | "This job requires work on Saturdays. Are you able to work that schedule?" |
| **Organizations** | "What clubs or societies do you belong to?" (Unless professional). | "List any professional or trade groups you belong to that are relevant to this position." |

#### 4.5 Criminal History and Youth Protection
**Context:** As a K-12 focused non-profit, Study GRC Inc. prioritizes youth safety. However, inquiries must be targeted and compliant with Fair Chance hiring practices where applicable.

| Subject | 🔴 **PROHIBITED (Do Not Ask)** | 🟢 **ACCEPTABLE (You May Ask)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Arrests** | "Have you ever been arrested?" (Arrests are not convictions). | "Have you ever been convicted of a crime?" (Note: It is best practice to ask this *after* a conditional offer is made, via the Background Check Consent Form). |
| **Youth Safety** | "Do you have kids of your own?" (Implies family status). | "This role involves working with minors. Can you describe your experience maintaining appropriate boundaries with youth?"<br>"Are you willing to submit to a comprehensive background check as required by our Youth Protection Policy?" |

#### 4.6 Remote Work and Logistics
**Context:** Study GRC Inc. is a remote-first organization.

| Subject | 🔴 **PROHIBITED (Do Not Ask)** | 🟢 **ACCEPTABLE (You May Ask)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Living Situation** | "Do you own or rent your home?"<br>"Who lives with you?" | "Do you have a quiet, secure workspace where you can maintain data confidentiality?"<br>"Do you have a reliable internet connection sufficient for video conferencing?" |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Ensures all interview questions are prepared in advance and vetted against this guide. Stops any interview panelist who asks a prohibited question and redirects the conversation. |
| **Interview Panelist** | Reviews this guide prior to participating in interviews. Limits questions to the candidate's skills, experience, and ability to perform the job. |
| **HR / Compliance** | Maintains this guide in accordance with changing laws. Provides training to hiring managers. Reviews job descriptions to ensure "Essential Functions" are clearly defined. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this guideline may result in:
1.  **Legal Liability:** Exposure of the Organization to lawsuits and fines from the EEOC or Texas Workforce Commission.
2.  **Disciplinary Action:** Violations of this guide, particularly those resulting in discrimination claims, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or removal from volunteer service.

If a candidate volunteers information regarding a Protected Class (e.g., "I can't work Tuesdays because I have dialysis"), the interviewer must **not** pursue the topic further, must **not** record this information in interview notes, and must evaluate the candidate solely on their stated ability to perform job functions.
