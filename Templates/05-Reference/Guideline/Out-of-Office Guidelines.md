### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this guideline is to ensure operational continuity and maintain professional communication standards within Study GRC Inc.’s remote-first environment. As an organization that relies heavily on asynchronous communication, the absence of an immediate response can create confusion or delay critical workflows. These guidelines establish the protocols for configuring Out-of-Office (OOO) notifications to manage stakeholder expectations and ensure clear escalation paths during staff or volunteer absence.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to all **Authorized Users** who possess a corporate email address (`@studygrc.org`) or access internal collaboration platforms (e.g., Slack, Discord, Microsoft Teams). This includes:
*   Employees (Full-time and Part-time)
*   Members of the Board of Directors
*   Operational Officers
*   Volunteers with external-facing responsibilities
*   Contractors with Study GRC email identities

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Out of Office (OOO)** | A status indicating that an individual is unavailable for work-related communication for a specific duration due to leave, illness, travel, or other reasons. |
| **Delegate** | A designated team member authorized to handle urgent matters or make decisions on behalf of the absent individual. |
| **Auto-Responder** | An automated email reply sent to incoming messages notifying the sender of the recipient's absence. |
| **Status Indicator** | A visual cue or text line in collaboration tools (e.g., Slack, Discord) indicating availability. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 Activation Thresholds
Personnel should activate OOO notifications under the following circumstances:
*   **Full Day Absence:** Any planned absence lasting one (1) full business day or longer.
*   **Partial Availability:** When working significantly reduced hours where response times will exceed 24 hours.
*   **Holidays:** During personal holidays or vacations, even if the organization is technically open.
*   **Medical/Emergency:** As soon as feasible during unexpected medical or family emergencies.

#### 4.2 Communication Channels
To ensure comprehensive coverage, OOO status must be updated across two primary channels:
1.  **Email Client:** An automated reply must be configured on the `@studygrc.org` account.
2.  **Collaboration Platforms:** A status update (e.g., emoji + text) must be set on Slack/Discord to alert internal team members before they type a message.

#### 4.3 Content Requirements
An effective OOO message must be concise and professional. It should include:
1.  **Dates:** Clearly state the **Start Date** and **Return Date**.
2.  **Access Level:** Specify if you have **No Access** to email or **Limited Access** (checking intermittently).
3.  **Escalation Path:** Identify a **Delegate** for urgent matters (Name and Email).
    *   *Note:* You must obtain the Delegate's consent before listing them in your auto-responder.
4.  **General Inbox:** If applicable, direct inquiries to a shared inbox (e.g., `support@studygrc.org` or `finance@studygrc.org`) rather than a specific person to ensure the request is ticketed.

#### 4.4 Security and Privacy (OpSec)
To maintain personnel safety and cybersecurity hygiene:
*   **Do Not Reveal Specific Location:** Avoid phrases like "I am vacationing in Paris." Use generic phrasing like "I am away from the office."
*   **Do Not Reveal Personal Contact Info:** Do not include personal cell phone numbers or personal email addresses in the auto-reply unless explicitly authorized by the CISO for executive leadership continuity.
*   **Phishing Risk:** Detailed OOO messages provide data for social engineering. Keep details minimal.

#### 4.5 Sample Templates

**Option A: Standard Professional (External & Internal)**
> "Thank you for your email. I am currently out of the office starting [Start Date] and returning on [Return Date]. I will not have access to email during this time.
>
> For urgent matters regarding [Topic], please contact [Delegate Name] at [Delegate Email]. Otherwise, I will respond to your message upon my return.
>
> Best regards,
> [Your Name]"

**Option B: Short Term/Limited Access**
> "I am away from my desk until [Time/Date] with limited access to email. Expect a delay in my response. For immediate assistance with [Project], please reach out to the [Department] team via Slack."

#### 4.6 Handover Protocols
For absences exceeding three (3) business days:
1.  **Handover Note:** A brief document or email should be sent to the immediate manager and the designated Delegate outlining pending tasks, deadlines occurring during the absence, and file locations.
2.  **Calendar:** Ensure the absence is blocked on the shared organizational calendar.

#### 4.7 Deactivation
Personnel are responsible for deactivating the auto-responder and clearing status indicators immediately upon returning to work to signal availability.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authorized User** | Configures OOO messages accurately and timely; secures Delegate consent; deactivates upon return. |
| **Manager / Team Lead** | Ensures team members have appropriate coverage plans; approves Delegates. |
| **IT Systems Administrator** | Authorized to access user accounts to enable OOO messages in emergency situations (e.g., incapacitation) upon request from HR or Executive Leadership. |
| **Delegate** | Monitors escalated issues and responds within agreed service levels during the primary user's absence. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
While this is a guideline, consistent failure to communicate availability in a remote-first environment negatively impacts organizational performance and team cohesion.
*   **Performance Management:** Repeated failure to set OOO notifications resulting in missed deadlines or stakeholder frustration may be addressed during performance reviews.
*   **Security Violations:** Including sensitive internal data or excessive personal information in auto-responders constitutes a violation of the *Data Privacy Policy* and may result in disciplinary action.
