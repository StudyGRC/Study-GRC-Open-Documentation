### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a standardized, equitable, and transparent framework for Board of Directors candidate presentations during the Annual Meeting of Members. These guidelines operationalize **Article II, Section 2.5** of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc., ensuring that all Voting Members have sufficient information to make informed decisions while maintaining order and efficiency during the proceedings.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to:
*   **Nominees:** Individuals standing for election to the Board of Directors.
*   **Presiding Officer:** The individual chairing the Annual Meeting (typically the President).
*   **Election Officials:** The Secretary or designated Timekeeper.
*   **Voting Members:** Individuals participating in the Question and Answer (Q&A) segment.

This document specifically governs the "Nominee Presentations" segment of the Annual Meeting agenda.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Nominee** | A Voting Member in good standing who has met all nomination requirements to run for a seat on the Board of Directors. |
| **The Floor** | The virtual stage or speaking privileges granted to a specific individual during the meeting. |
| **Hard Stop** | An immediate cessation of speaking privileges enforced by the Presiding Officer or Timekeeper when a time limit expires. |
| **Candidate Statement** | The written profile, vision, and conflict disclosure submitted by the Nominee prior to the meeting. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 General Principles
To ensure fairness, the Organization adheres to a strict policy of neutrality. All Nominees shall be afforded the same amount of time, the same audio/visual capabilities, and the same moderation standards during the Q&A session.

#### 4.2 Presentation Format and Logistics
*   **Virtual Environment:** As a remote-first organization, presentations will occur via the designated video conferencing platform. Nominees are expected to have their camera enabled during their presentation to foster trust and community connection.
*   **Visual Aids:** Unless otherwise specified in the Meeting Notice, the use of slide decks or screen sharing by Nominees is **discouraged** to prevent technical delays. Nominees should focus on verbal delivery.
*   **Order of Speaking:** Nominees will present in alphabetical order by last name, unless a random draw is conducted by the Secretary at the start of the segment.

#### 4.3 Time Limits (The "Five-Minute Rule")
Pursuant to **Bylaws Section 2.5**, every Nominee is granted a strict five (5) minute window.
*   **Timekeeping:** The Secretary (or designee) will display a visible timer or provide verbal cues at the 1-minute and 30-second marks.
*   **Enforcement:** At the 5-minute mark, the Presiding Officer will thank the speaker and mute their microphone if they do not immediately conclude. This ensures the meeting respects the time of the membership.

#### 4.4 Content Recommendations
Nominees are encouraged to address the following core areas during their address:
1.  **Professional Background:** Relevant experience in GRC, non-profit management, or technology.
2.  **Vision for Study GRC:** Specific goals they wish to achieve during their term.
3.  **Commitment:** Availability to meet the monthly meeting requirements and committee work.
4.  **Conflict of Interest Disclosure:** A mandatory verbal summary of any potential conflicts (e.g., employment with a competitor or vendor), reinforcing the written disclosure provided during nomination.

#### 4.5 Question and Answer (Q&A) Segment
Following the presentation, a "reasonable and fair period" will be opened for Member Q&A.
*   **Moderation:** The Presiding Officer will moderate questions to ensure they are relevant to the Organization’s mission and governance.
*   **Question Format:** Members may submit questions via the chat function or by raising their virtual hand.
*   **Prohibited Content:** Personal attacks, questions regarding non-public personnel matters, or commercial solicitations are strictly prohibited.
*   **Time Limit per Answer:** Nominees should limit responses to two (2) minutes per question.

#### 4.6 Absentee Nominees
In accordance with **Bylaws Section 2.5**, participation in the live presentation is encouraged but **not mandatory** for election eligibility.
*   **Pre-Recorded Video:** A Nominee unable to attend may submit a video file (MP4) not exceeding 5 minutes in length to the Secretary at least 48 hours prior to the meeting.
*   **Written Statement:** Alternatively, a Nominee may designate a proxy (or the Secretary) to read their written Candidate Statement to the membership.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Presiding Officer** | Moderates the session, enforces decorum, manages the Q&A queue, and has final authority on "Hard Stop" enforcement. |
| **Secretary** | Acts as the official Timekeeper, manages the queue of speakers, and verifies that all Nominees have submitted required disclosures. |
| **Nominee** | Prepares remarks, adheres to time limits, maintains professional conduct, and ensures technical readiness (internet/audio/video). |
| **Voting Members** | Listen respectfully and pose relevant, constructive questions during the Q&A period. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to these guidelines may result in the following actions by the Presiding Officer:
*   **Muting:** Immediate muting of the microphone for exceeding time limits or using profanity.
*   **Removal:** Removal from the virtual meeting room for severe violations of the **Code of Ethics and Conduct** or **Community Guidelines**.
*   **Disqualification:** While the presentation itself is optional, failure to submit the required "Nomination Statement" and Conflict of Interest disclosure prior to the meeting renders a candidate ineligible for the ballot.
