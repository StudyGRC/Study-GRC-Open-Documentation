### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this guideline is to establish clear protocols for the conduct of Board of Directors meetings held via electronic communications systems. This document ensures that Study GRC Inc. maintains compliance with the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.002 regarding remote meetings, adheres to *Robert’s Rules of Order Newly Revised* (RONR), and facilitates efficient, equitable decision-making within a remote-first organizational structure.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to all Regular, Special, and Emergency meetings of the Board of Directors and Board Committees of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Inclusions:** Video conferences (e.g., Zoom, Teams), teleconferences, and hybrid meetings.
*   **Exclusions:** Asynchronous actions taken via written consent (email/electronic voting) as defined in Bylaws Section 3.9, and informal working sessions where no binding votes are taken.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **The Chair** | The Presiding Officer of the meeting (usually the President), responsible for enforcing rules and maintaining order. |
| **Quorum** | The minimum number of Directors (majority) required to be present to conduct business, per Bylaws Section 3.5. |
| **The Floor** | The exclusive right to speak during the meeting. In a virtual context, this is granted by the Chair via verbal recognition. |
| **Unanimous Consent** | A voting method where the Chair asks if there are objections; if none are raised, the motion passes without a roll call. |
| **Simultaneous Communication** | The legal requirement (TBOC) that all participants must be able to hear and communicate with each other in real-time. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 Technical Requirements and Setup
To ensure legal validity under Texas law, the meeting platform must allow for simultaneous aural communication.
*   **4.1.1 Video Preference:** Directors should maintain active video feeds whenever possible to facilitate non-verbal communication and identity verification.
*   **4.1.2 Audio Quality:** Directors must use a headset or a quiet environment to prevent feedback loops.
*   **4.1.3 Mute Protocol:** All participants must keep microphones muted when not holding the Floor to minimize background noise.
*   **4.1.4 Chat Function:** The chat window may be used for sharing links or technical troubleshooting but **must not** be used for formal motions, seconds, or binding votes unless explicitly authorized by the Chair for a specific item.

#### 4.2 Establishing Quorum
*   **4.2.1 Verification:** At the start of the meeting, the Secretary shall verify the presence of a Quorum (majority of Directors) via visual or audible roll call.
*   **4.2.2 Continuous Quorum:** If technical difficulties cause a Director to drop off, resulting in the loss of a Quorum, business must be suspended immediately until the Director reconnects or the meeting must be adjourned.

#### 4.3 Obtaining the Floor
To prevent "talking over" one another, strict adherence to recognition protocols is required.
*   **4.3.1 Seeking Recognition:** A Director wishing to speak must use the "Raise Hand" digital feature of the meeting platform. If the feature is unavailable, the Director may physically raise their hand on video.
*   **4.3.2 Recognition:** A Director may not speak until recognized by name by the Chair.
*   **4.3.3 Interruption:** A Director may only interrupt a speaker for a "Point of Order" (e.g., a breach of rules) or a "Point of Personal Privilege" (e.g., audio failure preventing participation).

#### 4.4 Processing Motions
*   **4.4.1 Making a Motion:** A Director recognized by the Chair states, "I move that [text of proposal]."
*   **4.4.2 Seconding:** Another Director must say, "Second." In virtual meetings, a Director may unmute to say "Second" without waiting for formal recognition, or type "Second" in the chat if the Chair allows.
*   **4.4.3 Debate:** The Chair will alternate between those speaking in favor and those speaking against the motion.
*   **4.4.4 Amendments:** Amendments must be precise. The Secretary may require the mover to type the exact wording of the amendment into the Chat for clarity before the vote.

#### 4.5 Voting Procedures
Per Bylaws Section 2.3, proxy voting is prohibited. Votes must be cast personally.
*   **4.5.1 Unanimous Consent:** For routine matters (e.g., approving minutes), the Chair may ask, "Is there any objection to [the motion]?" If there is silence for 5 seconds, the motion passes.
*   **4.5.2 Roll Call Vote:** For controversial items, financial expenditures, or Fundamental Actions, a Roll Call vote is required. The Secretary calls each name, and the Director answers "Aye," "No," or "Abstain."
*   **4.5.3 Show of Hands:** The Chair may request a "Show of Hands" (physical or digital) if the outcome is clearly not in doubt.
*   **4.5.4 Results:** The Chair must announce the final count and whether the motion carries or fails before moving to the next agenda item.

#### 4.6 Executive Session
*   **4.6.1 Security:** Upon entering Executive Session, the Host must remove all non-Board members (staff, guests) to a Waiting Room or disconnect them.
*   **4.6.2 Recording:** Cloud or local recording of the meeting must be stopped immediately upon entering Executive Session.
*   **4.6.3 Confidentiality:** Directors must ensure they are in a private location where the conversation cannot be overheard by non-members.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chair (President)** | Facilitates the meeting, recognizes speakers, rules on points of order, and ensures the agenda is followed. |
| **Secretary** | Records minutes, tracks motions/seconds, performs roll call votes, and captures the text of amendments. |
| **Directors** | Adhere to mute protocols, seek recognition before speaking, and ensure their technology supports participation. |
| **Meeting Host (IT)** | Manages technical aspects (Waiting Room, Muting participants with background noise, Screen Sharing). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Rulings:** The Chair’s ruling on procedural matters is final unless appealed by a Director and overturned by a majority vote.
*   **Disruption:** The Chair reserves the right to mute or remove any participant (including Board members) who persistently disrupts the meeting or refuses to adhere to these Rules of Order, following a verbal warning.
*   **Legal Validity:** Failure to maintain Quorum or adhere to voting protocols may render decisions made during the meeting legally voidable under TBOC.
