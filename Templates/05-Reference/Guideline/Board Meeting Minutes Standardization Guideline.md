### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Guideline is to establish a consistent standard for recording, approving, and maintaining minutes for meetings of the Board of Directors, Board Committees, and Membership of Study GRC Inc.

Accurate minutes are critical for:
1.  **Legal Compliance:** Satisfying the requirements of **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.353**, which mandates that a corporation keep minutes of proceedings.
2.  **Corporate Memory:** Providing an official historical record of decisions, policy adoptions, and strategic direction.
3.  **Liability Protection:** Evidencing that Directors fulfilled their fiduciary duties (Duty of Care and Duty of Loyalty) by documenting due diligence and the rationale behind key decisions.
4.  **Audit Readiness:** Ensuring readiness for IRS examinations, financial audits, and grant compliance reviews (2 CFR 200).

### 2. Scope
This Guideline applies to:
*   **Regular and Special Meetings** of the Board of Directors.
*   **Annual and Special Meetings** of the Voting Membership.
*   **Committee Meetings** where the committee has Board-delegated authority (e.g., Finance Committee, Governance Committee).

**Exclusions:** This guideline does not apply to the Advisory Board (which has no voting authority per Bylaws Article VI) or informal working groups, unless specific recommendations are being formally presented to the Board.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minutes** | The official, permanent legal record of the actions taken and decisions made during a meeting. Minutes are a record of *what was done*, not necessarily *what was said*. |
| **Quorum** | The minimum number of voting members/directors who must be present to conduct business. Per **Bylaws Section 3.5**, a majority of Directors constitutes a Board quorum. |
| **Resolution** | A formal expression of opinion, will, or intent voted on by an official body (e.g., "Resolved, that the Board approves the FY2024 Budget"). |
| **Executive Session** | A portion of the meeting closed to non-members (and sometimes staff) to discuss confidential matters such as personnel issues, litigation, or sensitive negotiations. |
| **Action Without Meeting** | A decision made via written consent (including electronic means) by all Directors entitled to vote, as permitted by **Bylaws Section 3.9**. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 General Principles of Minute Taking
Minutes should be objective, factual, and concise. They should focus on **decisions made** and **actions assigned**, rather than providing a verbatim transcript of discussions.
*   **Voice:** Use the past tense (e.g., "The Board approved..." rather than "The Board approves...").
*   **Tone:** Maintain a neutral, professional tone. Avoid adjectives or emotional language.

#### 4.2 Essential Content Elements
Every set of minutes must include the following header information to ensure validity:
1.  **Entity Name:** Study GRC Inc.
2.  **Type of Meeting:** Regular, Special, Emergency, or Annual.
3.  **Date and Time:** Start time (Call to Order) and End time (Adjournment).
4.  **Location:** Due to the Organization's remote-first nature, specify the virtual platform used (e.g., "Held via Secure Zoom Conference").
5.  **Attendees:**
    *   **Present:** List Directors by name.
    *   **Absent:** List Directors not present.
    *   **Guests/Staff:** List Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director) or guests present.
6.  **Quorum Declaration:** A specific statement confirming that a quorum was present, satisfying **Bylaws Section 3.5**.
7.  **Presiding Officer:** Name of the Chair/President and the Recording Secretary.

#### 4.3 Recording Actions and Votes
For every motion or formal decision, the minutes must record:
1.  **The Motion:** The exact wording of the proposal.
2.  **Mover/Seconder:** The names of the Directors who moved and seconded the motion (optional but recommended for historical clarity).
3.  **The Vote:** The outcome of the vote (Passed/Failed).
    *   *Best Practice:* Record the vote count (e.g., "Motion carried 5-0").
    *   *Dissent/Abstention:* If a Director specifically requests their dissent or abstention be recorded, or if the vote is split, names should be recorded (e.g., "Directors A and B voted Nay").

#### 4.4 Handling Conflicts of Interest
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX (Conflict of Interest)**, if a Director has a conflict regarding a specific agenda item:
1.  The minutes must explicitly state that the Director disclosed the conflict.
2.  The minutes must record that the Director recused themselves from the discussion and the vote.
3.  The minutes should note if the Director left the virtual meeting room during the discussion.

#### 4.5 Executive Session
When the Board enters Executive Session:
1.  The minutes must record the **time** the Board entered Executive Session and the **general topic** (e.g., "Personnel Matter," "Legal Consultation").
2.  **Do not** record the specific details of the discussion in the general minutes.
3.  Any formal action (vote) taken resulting from the Executive Session should generally be ratified once the Board exits Executive Session and returns to the open meeting, ensuring the decision is part of the official record.

#### 4.6 Electronic Voting / Action Without Meeting
Per **Bylaws Section 3.9**, actions taken via email or electronic voting outside of a meeting must be documented. These should be:
1.  Compiled into a "Unanimous Written Consent" document; OR
2.  Entered into the minutes of the *next* scheduled Board meeting as a ratification of prior electronic action.

#### 4.7 Attachments and Exhibits
Documents reviewed during the meeting (e.g., Financial Reports, Committee Reports) should be referenced in the minutes and stored in the Organization’s secure document repository (e.g., Google Drive/SharePoint) with a link or reference code (e.g., "See Attachment A: Q3 Financial Report").

#### 4.8 Review and Approval Process
1.  **Drafting:** The Secretary (or designee) should draft minutes within 7 days of the meeting.
2.  **Review:** The President/Chair reviews the draft for accuracy.
3.  **Distribution:** Draft minutes are sent to the Board prior to the next meeting.
4.  **Approval:** At the next meeting, the Board votes to approve the minutes (with any necessary corrections).
5.  **Signature:** Once approved, the Secretary signs the official copy. Digital signatures are acceptable.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Primary responsibility for recording, drafting, and maintaining the minute book. Ensures minutes are signed and stored in accordance with the **Document Retention Policy**. |
| **Board President** | Reviews draft minutes for accuracy regarding the flow of the meeting and decisions made. Leads the approval process at the subsequent meeting. |
| **Directors** | Responsible for reviewing draft minutes to ensure their votes and recusals are accurately reflected before voting to approve. |
| **Executive Director** | May assist in taking notes if requested by the Secretary but does not sign the official minutes unless acting as Secretary pro tem. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain accurate minutes is a violation of **TBOC § 22.353** and the Organization’s **Bylaws Article VIII**. Inaccurate or missing minutes can pierce the corporate veil, exposing Directors to personal liability, and may jeopardize the Organization’s 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status during an IRS audit.
