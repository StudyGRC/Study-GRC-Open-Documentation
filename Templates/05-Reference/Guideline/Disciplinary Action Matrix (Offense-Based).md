### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Disciplinary Action Matrix is to provide a standardized framework for addressing employee and volunteer misconduct and performance issues. This guideline ensures that Study GRC Inc. applies disciplinary measures fairly, consistently, and without discrimination, thereby mitigating legal risks associated with wrongful termination or disparate treatment claims. It serves as a reference for management to determine appropriate consequences based on the severity and frequency of offenses.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to all employees (full-time, part-time, temporary) and volunteers of Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Board of Directors:** Disciplinary action and removal of Directors are governed strictly by **Article III, Section 3.6** of the Bylaws.
*   **Operational Officers:** Removal of Officers appointed by the Board is governed by **Article V, Section 5.3** of the Bylaws.
*   **Independent Contractors:** Addressed via contract termination clauses, not progressive discipline.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Progressive Discipline** | A process of increasing the severity of consequences for repeated violations or performance issues, typically moving from verbal warnings to termination. |
| **At-Will Employment** | The legal doctrine (Texas Labor Code) stating that employment may be terminated by the employer or employee at any time, for any lawful reason, with or without cause or notice. This Matrix does not alter the At-Will status. |
| **Gross Misconduct** | Actions so severe that they fundamentally break the trust required for the employment relationship, justifying immediate termination without prior warning. |
| **Lookback Period** | The timeframe (rolling 12 months) during which prior disciplinary actions remain active for the purpose of escalation. |

### 4. Guidelines

This Matrix is a tool for guidance. Management retains the discretion to deviate from these steps based on the specific context, intent, and impact of the behavior, provided such deviation is documented and approved by Human Resources or Legal Counsel.

#### 4.1 Progressive Discipline Levels

1.  **Level 1: Documented Verbal Warning:** A formal conversation documenting the issue.
2.  **Level 2: Written Warning:** A formal notice placed in the personnel file outlining consequences of further failure.
3.  **Level 3: Final Written Warning / Suspension:** The last step before termination; may include unpaid suspension pending investigation.
4.  **Level 4: Termination:** Separation from the organization.

#### 4.2 Offense Matrix

**Category A: Attendance & Reliability (Remote Context)**

| Offense | 1st Occurrence | 2nd Occurrence | 3rd Occurrence | 4th Occurrence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Unexcused Absence / No-Show** | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |
| **"Ghosting"** (Unresponsive during work hours > 4 hours without notice) | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |
| **Tardiness** (Meetings or start times) | Coaching | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 |
| **Failure to clock in/out** (Non-Exempt staff) | Coaching | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 |

**Category B: Performance & Professionalism**

| Offense | 1st Occurrence | 2nd Occurrence | 3rd Occurrence | 4th Occurrence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Substandard Work Quality** (Errors, missed deadlines) | Coaching/PIP | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3/4 |
| **Insubordination** (Refusal to follow direct, lawful orders) | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 | - |
| **Unprofessional Conduct** (Disrespectful tone, minor conflicts) | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |
| **Violation of Social Media Policy** (HR-POL-060) | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |

**Category C: Security & Data Privacy**

| Offense | 1st Occurrence | 2nd Occurrence | 3rd Occurrence | 4th Occurrence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Unintentional Security Violation** (e.g., unlocked screen, minor policy breach) | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |
| **Password Sharing** (Violation of IS-POL-001) | Level 3 | Level 4 | - | - |
| **Unauthorized Data Access** (Curiosity/Browsing) | Level 3 | Level 4 | - | - |
| **Failure to Report Lost Device** (Within 24 hours) | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 | - |

**Category D: Gross Misconduct (Zero Tolerance)**
*Note: These offenses typically result in immediate termination (Level 4) upon substantiation.*

| Offense | Consequence | Regulatory/Legal Context |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Youth Protection Violation** | **Level 4** | Violation of TX Family Code; Immediate report to authorities. |
| **Harassment / Discrimination** | **Level 4** | Violation of Title VII / EEOC; Zero tolerance. |
| **Theft / Fraud / Embezzlement** | **Level 4** | Criminal act; Violation of 2 CFR 200 (Federal Funds). |
| **Workplace Violence / Threats** | **Level 4** | OSHA General Duty Clause; Immediate removal. |
| **Intentional Data Breach / Sabotage** | **Level 4** | Violation of CFAA / TX Penal Code. |
| **Working Under Influence (Drugs/Alcohol)** | **Level 4** | Violation of Drug-Free Workplace Policy. |
| **Undisclosed Conflict of Interest** | **Level 3 or 4** | Violation of Bylaws Art. IX; IRS Form 990 implications. |

#### 4.3 Determining Factors
When determining the appropriate level of discipline within the matrix, the following factors must be documented:
1.  **Severity:** Did the action cause financial loss, reputational damage, or safety risks?
2.  **Intent:** Was the action malicious, negligent, or accidental?
3.  **History:** Does the individual have a clean record, or is there a pattern of behavior?
4.  **Tenure:** While not an excuse, tenure may influence the decision between coaching vs. formal warning for minor offenses.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Manager** | Identifies infractions, documents facts objectively, and consults the Matrix. Delivers Level 1 and Level 2 actions. |
| **Human Resources** | Reviews all proposed disciplinary actions for consistency with this Matrix and past precedents. Approves Level 3 actions. |
| **Executive Director** | Final approving authority for all Level 4 (Termination) actions to ensure alignment with organizational risk tolerance. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews Level 4 actions involving protected classes, whistleblowers, or complex contractual issues to ensure legal defensibility. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
This document is a guideline and does not constitute a contract of employment. Study GRC Inc. reserves the right to bypass steps in the progressive discipline process based on the severity of the infraction.

Failure to apply this matrix consistently (e.g., favoring certain employees over others for the same offense) constitutes a violation of the **Equal Employment Opportunity Policy** and may result in disciplinary action against the manager responsible.
