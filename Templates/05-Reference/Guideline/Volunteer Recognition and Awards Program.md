### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Guideline is to establish a structured framework for acknowledging, appreciating, and retaining the volunteers who sustain Study GRC Inc. As a nonprofit dedicated to fostering a community-driven ecosystem, our volunteers are our most valuable asset. This program aims to standardize recognition methods to ensure equity and consistency while strictly adhering to IRS regulations regarding **de minimis** benefits to maintain the distinction between "volunteer" and "employee" status.

### 2. Scope
This Guideline applies to all individuals classified as **Volunteers**, including:
*   Board Directors (non-compensated);
*   Advisory Board members;
*   Committee members;
*   Content creators, instructors, and community moderators;
*   Event staff.

**Exclusions:** This document does not apply to paid employees or independent contractors receiving market-rate compensation for services.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **De Minimis Benefit** | A property or service provided to a volunteer that has so little value (taking into account how frequently it is provided) that accounting for it would be unreasonable or administratively impracticable (e.g., t-shirts, coffee mugs, plaques). |
| **DKG** | Director of Kindness and Generosity; the Operational Officer defined in Bylaws Article V responsible for community culture. |
| **Nominal Value** | For the purposes of this Organization, a tangible item with a fair market value of less than $75.00 USD. |
| **Reputational Capital** | Non-monetary recognition that enhances a volunteer's professional standing (e.g., LinkedIn recommendations, digital badges, public citations). |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 General Principles of Recognition
To effectively foster the "community-driven ecosystem" mandated by Bylaws Article I, recognition should adhere to the **FAST** principle:
*   **Frequent:** Recognition should not be limited to annual events.
*   **Authentic:** Appreciation must be genuine and specific to the contribution.
*   **Specific:** Cite exactly what the volunteer did (e.g., "Thank you for moderating the incident response workshop," rather than "Thanks for helping").
*   **Timely:** Recognition should occur as close to the achievement as possible.

#### 4.2 Tiers of Recognition

**4.2.1 Tier 1: Informal & Immediate (Day-to-Day)**
*   **Mechanism:** Public shout-outs in community channels (e.g., Slack/Discord `#kudos` channel), verbal praise during meetings, or handwritten notes.
*   **Authority:** All Staff, Board Members, and Committee Chairs.
*   **Frequency:** Ongoing/Daily.

**4.2.2 Tier 2: Formal & Reputational (Quarterly/Project-Based)**
*   **Mechanism:**
    *   **Digital Open Badges:** Issuance of verifiable digital credentials (e.g., "Curriculum Contributor," "Community Guardian") for display on LinkedIn.
    *   **Professional References:** The Executive Director or Board President providing written letters of recommendation for volunteers seeking employment.
    *   **"Contributor of the Quarter":** A featured profile on the Organization’s transparency blog or newsletter.
*   **Authority:** Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).

**4.2.3 Tier 3: Tangible Awards (Annual)**
*   **Mechanism:** Physical awards presented during the Annual Meeting (Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5).
*   **Allowable Items:** Plaques, trophies, branded apparel (swag), or challenge coins.
*   **Tax Compliance:** To avoid triggering taxable income reporting (Form 1099) or creating an implied employment contract, **cash awards and cash-equivalents (general use gift cards like Visa/Amex) are strictly prohibited** for volunteers. Store-specific gift cards of nominal value may be permitted only upon specific Finance Committee approval.

#### 4.3 Annual Award Categories
The Organization establishes the following standing awards to be presented at the Annual Meeting:

1.  **The GRC Vanguard Award:** Presented to a volunteer who has significantly advanced the Organization's open-source curriculum.
2.  **The Community Shield:** Presented to a moderator or safety lead who has exemplified the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.
3.  **The Distinguished Service Award:** Reserved for long-term service (3+ years) by Board or Committee members.

#### 4.4 Budgeting and Fiscal Responsibility
*   The DKG must submit a "Volunteer Appreciation Budget" annually for Board approval.
*   Expenditures must be tracked under the "Volunteer Relations" line item.
*   Individual gifts must remain below the IRS *de minimis* threshold to ensure they remain non-taxable.

#### 4.5 Eligibility for Supporting Membership
Per Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1, volunteers who demonstrate significant commitment through service hours may be granted **Supporting Member (Voting)** status without a financial contribution.
*   **Threshold:** 50 hours of documented service within a calendar year.
*   **Process:** The DKG verifies hours via the *Volunteer Hours Tracking Log* and submits the roster to the Secretary prior to the Record Date of the Annual Meeting.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Primary owner of this program; manages the budget, selects award recipients, and organizes recognition events. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the annual budget for recognition; presents Tier 3 awards at the Annual Meeting. |
| **Finance Committee** | Reviews all tangible gift proposals to ensure compliance with IRS *de minimis* rules. |
| **Committee Chairs** | Responsible for nominating team members for Tier 2 and Tier 3 recognition. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to the financial limitations of this guideline (specifically regarding cash gifts) may result in tax liabilities for the Organization and the volunteer. Unauthorized issuance of awards or misrepresentation of the Organization's endorsement may result in disciplinary action or removal from volunteer service.
