### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this guideline is to establish clear protocols for moderating online spaces (including Discord, forums, and virtual classrooms) where minors are present. This document supports the Organization’s mission to "foster a community-driven ecosystem" (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3) by ensuring that ecosystem remains safe, inclusive, and compliant with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). It provides moderators with specific indicators of risk and actionable steps to prevent bullying, harassment, and predatory behavior (grooming).

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to all **Moderators**, **Community Managers**, **Volunteers**, and **Staff** who oversee digital communication channels owned or operated by Study GRC Inc.

**In Scope:**
*   Public chat channels (e.g., Discord, Slack).
*   Video conferencing sessions (e.g., Zoom, Teams).
*   Learning Management System (LMS) discussion boards.

**Exclusions:**
*   Technical configuration of privacy settings (covered in *IS-STD-012 Online Privacy Settings*).
*   Physical events (covered in *OPS-POL-040 Event Safety*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18. |
| **COPPA** | Children's Online Privacy Protection Act; U.S. federal law protecting the privacy of children under 13. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information (e.g., full name, home address, school name, phone number). |
| **Grooming** | The predatory act of building an emotional connection with a minor to gain their trust for the purpose of sexual abuse, sexual exploitation, or trafficking. |
| **Doxing** | The act of publicly revealing previously private personal information about an individual without their consent. |
| **Two-Deep Rule** | A safety protocol requiring at least two background-checked adults to be present during any interaction with a minor. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 General Moderation Stance
Moderators are the "first responders" of community culture. When interacting with minors, moderators must maintain a tone that is professional, encouraging, and firm regarding safety boundaries.
*   **Neutrality:** Moderators must remain neutral in disputes unless a violation of the Code of Conduct occurs.
*   **Visibility:** Moderation actions should generally be visible to the community to demonstrate that rules are enforced, except when removing sensitive PII.

#### 4.2 COPPA Compliance and PII Protection
To comply with COPPA and protect youth privacy, moderators must aggressively filter PII.
*   **Immediate Removal:** If a user known or suspected to be a minor posts PII (e.g., "I go to Lincoln High in Austin" or "My number is 555-0199"), the moderator must immediately delete the message.
*   **Education:** Send a standardized private warning to the user explaining *why* the post was removed (e.g., "For your safety, please do not post your school name or phone number in public channels.").
*   **Screen Names:** Users under 18 should be advised not to use their real full name as their display name. Moderators may enforce a nickname change if a full name is used.

#### 4.3 Adult-Minor Interaction Protocols
To prevent grooming and ensure safety, strict boundaries are enforced regarding how adults and minors interact.
*   **Prohibition on Direct Messages (DMs):** Adults (including staff and volunteers) are **prohibited** from initiating private Direct Messages with minors. All support must happen in public channels or via the official ticketing system.
*   **Redirecting to Public:** If a minor DMs a moderator, the moderator must reply once: *"I cannot chat privately. Please post your question in #general-help,"* and then cease the private interaction.
*   **"Reply-All" Culture:** In forum or email settings, adults must always Copy (CC) another adult or a generic organizational address (e.g., safety@studygrc.org) when replying to a minor.

#### 4.4 Identifying and Responding to Grooming Indicators
Moderators must be vigilant for subtle signs of predatory behavior by adult users toward minors. Indicators include:
*   **Gift Giving:** Offering skins, games, nitro, or currency to a specific minor.
*   **Special Attention:** An adult consistently singling out a specific minor for praise, defense, or conversation.
*   **Secrecy:** An adult encouraging a minor to switch to a different, unmonitored platform (e.g., "Add me on Snapchat," "Let's talk on WhatsApp").
*   **Boundary Testing:** An adult sharing overly personal problems or asking the minor about their romantic life/sexual history.

**Action:** If these indicators are observed, the moderator must capture screenshots immediately and escalate to the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** via the *Suspected Abuse Reporting Form*. Do not confront the suspect publicly.

#### 4.5 Bullying and Cyberbullying
Study GRC Inc. maintains a zero-tolerance policy for bullying.
*   **Definition:** Repeated aggressive behavior intended to hurt another individual, physically, mentally, or emotionally.
*   **Intervention:** Moderators must intervene immediately to stop the behavior.
*   **Sanctions:**
    1.  **First Offense:** Warning and temporary mute (24 hours).
    2.  **Second Offense:** Ban from the platform.
    *   *Note:* If the bullying includes hate speech or threats of violence, skip to immediate Ban.

#### 4.6 Crisis Intervention (Self-Harm)
If a minor posts content indicating intent to harm themselves or others:
1.  **Do not delete the message** (unless it contains PII), as it may be needed for emergency response.
2.  **Do not offer clinical advice.**
3.  **Provide Resources:** Post the standard crisis resource block (e.g., "If you are in crisis, please text HOME to 741741 or call 988").
4.  **Escalate:** Immediately notify the **Executive Director** and **DKG**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Moderator** | Monitors chat logs, enforces guidelines, removes PII, and reports suspicious behavior. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Oversees the moderation team, handles escalations regarding grooming or severe bullying, and manages bans. |
| **CISO** | Configures automated content filters (e.g., bad word lists, link blocking) to assist moderators. |
| **Executive Director** | Responsible for legal reporting to law enforcement if a credible threat or abuse is identified. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to these guidelines may result in the revocation of moderation privileges.
*   **Volunteers:** Violation of the "No DM" rule with minors is grounds for immediate dismissal.
*   **Legal:** Failure to remove known PII of a child under 13 may result in organizational liability under COPPA.
