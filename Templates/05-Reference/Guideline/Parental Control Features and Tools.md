### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this guideline is to empower parents and legal guardians with the knowledge and technical resources necessary to supervise their child’s participation in Study GRC Inc. programs. While Study GRC Inc. implements robust internal moderation and safety protocols, this document outlines how parents can utilize platform-specific features and third-party tools to align their child’s digital experience with their family’s values and safety expectations.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to:
*   **External:** Parents and Legal Guardians of participants under the age of 18 (Minors).
*   **Internal:** Staff and Volunteers responsible for configuring educational platforms (LMS, Community Servers, Video Conferencing) and communicating with families.
*   **Systems:** Official Study GRC Inc. communication channels, including but not limited to the Learning Management System (LMS), Discord/Slack community servers, and Zoom/Teams environments.

**Exclusions:** This guideline does not cover parental controls for third-party websites or resources linked externally that are not owned or operated by Study GRC Inc.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Observer Access** | A specific permission level within a Learning Management System (LMS) that allows a parent to view course content, grades, and due dates associated with their child without the ability to submit work or participate in discussions. |
| **Digital Wellbeing Tools** | Operating system-level features (e.g., Apple Screen Time, Google Family Link) that manage screen time, app access, and content filtering. |
| **Direct Messaging (DM)** | Private, one-on-one communication between two users on a platform. |
| **Moderation Bot** | Automated software used by Study GRC Inc. to scan public chats for profanity, slurs, or prohibited content and automatically block or flag it. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 Platform-Specific Parental Features
Study GRC Inc. selects platforms that prioritize user safety. Parents are encouraged to utilize the following native features:

**4.1.1 Learning Management System (LMS)**
*   **Observer Accounts:** Upon request, Study GRC Inc. will provide parents with "Observer" credentials linked to their child’s student account. This allows parents to monitor academic progress and course materials.
*   **Activity Logs:** Parents may request an export of their child’s login history and submission timestamps to verify attendance and participation.

**4.1.2 Community Platforms (e.g., Discord/Slack)**
*   **Privacy Settings:** Parents should review their child's "Privacy & Safety" settings. Study GRC Inc. recommends setting "Allow direct messages from server members" to **OFF** for minors, ensuring all communication occurs in public, moderated channels.
*   **Friend Requests:** Parents are advised to disable the ability for strangers to send friend requests to their child’s account.
*   **Data Requests:** In compliance with COPPA and our *Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) Procedure*, parents may request a copy of all data (chat logs) associated with their child’s account.

**4.1.3 Video Conferencing (e.g., Zoom)**
*   **No 1:1 Private Chat:** Study GRC Inc. disables private 1:1 chat between participants during live sessions. Parents should be aware that their child can only chat publicly or directly to the host/instructor.
*   **Session Recording:** Live sessions are often recorded for educational review. Parents are notified of this practice upon registration.

#### 4.2 Recommended Third-Party Controls
Study GRC Inc. acknowledges that organization-side controls have limits. We recommend parents utilize OS-level controls to manage the broader digital environment:

*   **Microsoft Family Safety (Windows):** Use to set screen time limits for Study GRC applications, filter inappropriate web content, and receive weekly activity reports.
*   **Apple Screen Time (macOS/iOS):** Use "Downtime" to schedule study hours and "Content & Privacy Restrictions" to prevent the installation of unapproved apps alongside Study GRC tools.
*   **Google Family Link (Android/Chromebooks):** Use to approve or block apps the child wants to download and to remotely lock the device when it is time to take a break.

#### 4.3 Organization-Side Safety Configurations
To reduce the burden on parents, Study GRC Inc. implements the following default safety configurations ("Safety by Design"):

*   **Profanity Filters:** Automated filters are active on all community text channels to block offensive language immediately.
*   **Link Scanning:** Automated bots scan posted links to prevent navigation to known malicious or adult-content websites.
*   **Two-Deep Leadership:** As per the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, no adult staff member is permitted to have a private, unobserved 1:1 video call or DM with a minor without a third party present or explicit parental inclusion.

#### 4.4 Transparency and Communication
*   **Open Door Policy:** Parents are entitled to join public community channels (as non-voting Community Participants) to observe the culture and interactions within the Study GRC ecosystem.
*   **Incident Notification:** If a child is involved in a safety incident (e.g., bullying, exposure to inappropriate content), the *Director of Kindness and Generosity* will notify the parent/guardian immediately, regardless of the child's role in the incident.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Develops and distributes "Parental Safety Guides" during onboarding. Serves as the primary point of contact for parents regarding safety tools. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Configures platform back-ends (LMS/Discord) to ensure maximum privacy defaults for minor accounts. Audits moderation bot efficacy. |
| **Instructors/Volunteers** | Enforce the use of public channels for communication. Direct parents to this guideline if they have concerns about monitoring. |
| **Parents/Guardians** | Responsible for configuring local device settings (OS level) and reviewing their child's privacy settings within the applications. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
While the use of parental control tools by families is discretionary, Study GRC Inc.'s adherence to the *Organization-Side Safety Configurations* (Section 4.3) is mandatory.

Failure by staff to maintain these safety configurations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. Failure by parents to supervise their children effectively, resulting in repeated violations of the *Youth Code of Conduct*, may result in the suspension of the child's participation privileges to preserve the safety of the community.
