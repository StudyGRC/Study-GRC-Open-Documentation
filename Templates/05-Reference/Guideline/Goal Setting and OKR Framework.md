### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Guideline is to establish a standardized framework for setting, tracking, and evaluating performance goals within Study GRC Inc. By utilizing the **Objectives and Key Results (OKR)** methodology, this framework ensures that individual and departmental efforts are directly aligned with the Organization’s charitable and educational purposes as defined in **Article I, Section 1.3** of the Bylaws. This framework promotes transparency, accountability, and a results-oriented culture suitable for a remote-first, volunteer-integrated workforce.

### 2. Scope
This framework applies to:
*   **Operational Officers** (as defined in Bylaws Article V).
*   **Paid Employees** (Full-time and Part-time).
*   **Key Volunteer Leaders** (e.g., Committee Chairs, Chapter Leads) who have assumed significant operational responsibilities.

**Exclusions:** This framework does not apply to casual volunteers, Community Participants, or Advisory Board members, unless they voluntarily opt-in for professional development purposes.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **OKR (Objectives and Key Results)** | A goal-setting framework used to define measurable goals and track their outcomes. |
| **Objective (The "What")** | A qualitative, inspirational, and time-bound goal to be achieved (e.g., "Become the leading source of open-source GRC training"). |
| **Key Result (The "How")** | A quantitative benchmark that measures progress toward the Objective (e.g., "Publish 5 new course modules by Q3"). |
| **KPI (Key Performance Indicator)** | A metric used to evaluate the success of an organization or of a particular activity in which it engages (often "business as usual" metrics). |
| **Stretch Goal** | An ambitious goal that is likely unachievable in full but drives high performance. Achieving 70% of a stretch goal is considered success. |
| **Cadence** | The frequency with which goals are set and reviewed (typically Annual for Strategy, Quarterly for Execution). |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 The OKR Philosophy
Study GRC Inc. adopts the OKR methodology to bridge the gap between our high-level mission ("Fostering a community-driven ecosystem") and daily tasks.
*   **Alignment:** Every individual OKR must trace back to a Department OKR, which must trace back to an Organizational Strategic Goal.
*   **Transparency:** In accordance with the spirit of **Bylaws Article VIII (Transparency)**, OKRs should generally be visible to all team members to foster collaboration and reduce redundancy, except where sensitive HR data is involved.
*   **Outcome over Output:** Goals should focus on the *impact* of work (e.g., "Increase student pass rate to 90%") rather than just the *activity* (e.g., "Hold 5 study sessions").

#### 4.2 The Goal Setting Cycle
The Organization operates on a dual cadence:

1.  **Annual Strategic Goals (The "North Star"):**
    *   Set by the **Board of Directors** and **Executive Director** in Q4 of the preceding year.
    *   Derived from the 3-Year Strategic Plan.
2.  **Quarterly Tactical OKRs:**
    *   Set by Teams and Individuals.
    *   **Drafting:** Weeks 1-2 of the Quarter.
    *   **Execution:** Weeks 3-11.
    *   **Grading/Review:** Week 12.

#### 4.3 Drafting Effective OKRs
Staff and volunteers should use the **SMART** criteria when drafting Key Results:
*   **S**pecific: Clear and unambiguous.
*   **M**easurable: Must have a number or binary (done/not done) status.
*   **A**chievable: Realistic yet ambitious (Stretch).
*   **R**elevant: Aligns with the mission.
*   **T**ime-bound: Has a deadline (usually end of Quarter).

**Example Structure:**
*   **Objective:** Democratize access to GRC education for underrepresented groups.
*   **Key Result 1:** Launch the "Intro to GRC" scholarship program with 50 participants by March 31.
*   **Key Result 2:** Achieve a course completion rate of 80% for scholarship recipients.
*   **Key Result 3:** Secure 3 corporate sponsors to fund zero-cost resources.

#### 4.4 Scoring and Evaluation
At the end of the cycle, Key Results are graded on a scale of **0.0 to 1.0**:
*   **0.0 - 0.3:** Failed to make significant progress.
*   **0.4 - 0.6:** Made progress, but fell short of completion.
*   **0.7 - 1.0:** Achieved or exceeded the goal.

*Note: Consistently scoring 1.0 may indicate that goals are not ambitious enough (Sandbagging). The target average is 0.7.*

#### 4.5 Check-Ins and Feedback
*   **Weekly/Bi-Weekly:** Managers and direct reports should discuss OKR progress during 1:1 meetings.
*   **Mid-Quarter Review:** A formal check to see if Key Results need to be adjusted due to shifting priorities or unforeseen blockers.
*   **Documentation:** All goals and progress updates must be recorded in the Organization’s designated performance management tool (e.g., Notion, Lattice, or HRIS) to ensure remote visibility.

#### 4.6 Performance Reviews vs. OKRs
While OKRs inform performance reviews, they are not the *sole* determinant of performance.
*   **OKRs** measure *what* was achieved.
*   **Core Values/Competencies** measure *how* it was achieved (e.g., collaboration, integrity, adherence to the Code of Ethics).
*   Compensation adjustments (if applicable) consider both OKR completion and behavioral competencies.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Defines the long-term Strategic Plan and approves the Annual Organizational Objectives. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Translates Board strategy into operational priorities; approves Department-level OKRs. |
| **Department Heads** | Draft Department OKRs; ensure team alignment; facilitate scoring and review for their direct reports. |
| **Employees / Volunteers** | Draft individual OKRs in collaboration with managers; update progress weekly; participate in self-reflection/grading. |
| **HR / Operations** | Maintains the goal-tracking system; provides training on writing effective OKRs; audits alignment. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
This document serves as a guideline for best practices in performance management. However, participation in the goal-setting process is **mandatory** for all paid employees and Operational Officers.

*   **Failure to Participate:** Failure to draft goals or update progress may result in a "Does Not Meet Expectations" rating during annual performance reviews, which can impact compensation, promotion eligibility, or renewal of volunteer officer terms.
*   **At-Will Disclaimer:** Nothing in this guideline alters the At-Will employment status of paid staff. The setting of goals does not constitute an employment contract or a guarantee of bonus/compensation.
