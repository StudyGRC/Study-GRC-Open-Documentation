### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Brand Identity and Style Guide is to establish a cohesive visual and verbal identity for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). As a community-driven ecosystem dedicated to advancing Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) knowledge, maintaining a consistent brand builds trust, ensures accessibility, and protects the Organization’s intellectual property. This guide provides the framework for creating materials that align with our mission of removing financial barriers and empowering learners.

### 2. Scope
This guide applies to all visual and written communications representing Study GRC Inc.
*   **Applies to:** All employees, Board Directors, volunteers, contractors, Local Chapters, and authorized partners creating content, presentations, social media posts, or marketing materials.
*   **Exclusions:** Personal communications by volunteers or staff that do not reference their affiliation with the Organization or purport to represent the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Brand Assets** | The collective visual elements including the logo, color palette, typography, and imagery owned by the Organization. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 Visual Identity: The Logo
The Study GRC Inc. logo is the primary identifier of the Organization. It represents our commitment to open-source knowledge and community structure.

*   **4.1.1 Primary Lockup:** The standard logo (Symbol + Wordmark) must be used on all official documents, headers, and primary digital assets.
*   **4.1.2 Minimum Size:** To ensure legibility, the logo must never be rendered smaller than:
    *   **Digital:** 32px height.
    *   **Print:** 0.5 inches height.
*   **4.1.3 Clear Space:** A minimum clear space equivalent to the height of the "S" in "Study" must be maintained around the logo on all sides. No other elements (text, graphics, page edges) may intrude into this space.
*   **4.1.4 Prohibited Usage:**
    *   Do **not** stretch, skew, or distort the aspect ratio.
    *   Do **not** change the colors of the logo elements.
    *   Do **not** place the logo on a background that compromises contrast (e.g., a dark logo on a dark background).
    *   Do **not** add drop shadows, gradients, or outlines to the logo.

#### 4.2 Color Palette
Our color palette reflects professionalism, trust, and accessibility.

*   **4.2.1 Primary Colors:**
    *   **Study GRC Blue:** `#0056D2` (Trust, Intelligence)
    *   **Open Source White:** `#FFFFFF` (Clarity, Space)
    *   **Governance Slate:** `#2C3E50` (Stability, Text)
*   **4.2.2 Secondary (Accent) Colors:**
    *   **Action Green:** `#27AE60` (Growth, Approval)
    *   **Alert Amber:** `#F39C12` (Caution, Highlight)
    *   **Error Red:** `#C0392B` (Risk, Stop)
*   **4.2.3 Accessibility Compliance:** All text and essential graphical elements must maintain a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 against the background color to satisfy WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards per the *Digital Accessibility Policy*.

#### 4.3 Typography
To align with our open-source mission, we utilize open-license typefaces that are legible and universally available.

*   **4.3.1 Primary Typeface (Headings):** **Montserrat** (Bold/Semi-Bold). Used for headlines, titles, and major calls to action.
*   **4.3.2 Secondary Typeface (Body):** **Open Sans** (Regular/Italic). Used for body text, documents, and long-form content.
*   **4.3.3 Fallback Fonts:** If the primary fonts are unavailable, use **Arial** or **Helvetica**.

#### 4.4 Tone of Voice
Our voice is the verbal expression of our culture, overseen by the Director of Kindness and Generosity.

*   **4.4.1 Core Attributes:**
    *   **Empowering:** We simplify complex GRC topics. Use active voice and plain English. Avoid gatekeeping jargon.
    *   **Inclusive:** We serve a diverse, global community including K-12 students. Language must be gender-neutral, non-violent, and welcoming.
    *   **Professional:** We are experts. Content should be well-researched, accurate, and cited.
    *   **Kind:** We assume positive intent. Corrections or debates should be constructive, never demeaning.
*   **4.4.2 Writing Style:**
    *   Use "We" when speaking as the Organization.
    *   Capitalize "Board" and "Organization" when referring specifically to Study GRC Inc.
    *   Avoid "legalese" unless drafting a formal legal policy.

#### 4.5 Local Chapter Branding
Local Chapters are vital to our ecosystem but must maintain brand consistency to protect the Organization's 501(c)(3) status and liability shield.

*   **4.5.1 Chapter Naming:** Chapters must use the format: `Study GRC [City/Region Name]`.
*   **4.5.2 Chapter Logos:** Chapters will be provided with specific "Chapter Lockups" by the Operations team. Chapters may **not** create their own unique logos or modify the Study GRC logo.
*   **4.5.3 Independence Disclaimer:** All Chapter websites or social media profiles must state: *"A Local Chapter of Study GRC Inc., a Texas 501(c)(3) Nonprofit."*

#### 4.6 Imagery and Photography
*   **4.6.1 Diversity:** Imagery should reflect the diversity of our global community in terms of race, gender, age, and ability.
*   **4.6.2 Youth Protection:** When using images of minors (K-12 programs), the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* applies.
    *   Faces of minors must not be published without a signed *Photo and Video Release*.
    *   Metadata (location data) must be stripped from images of minors before publishing.
*   **4.6.3 Authenticity:** Prefer candid photos of community events over generic stock photography.

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Adherence to this guide is mandatory to protect the Organization’s intellectual property and reputation.
*   **Brand Misuse:** Unauthorized modification of the logo or use of the brand for personal commercial gain is a violation of the *Conflict of Interest Policy* and *Intellectual Property Policy*.
*   **Chapter Compliance:** Failure by a Local Chapter to adhere to these guidelines may result in the revocation of the Chapter Charter.
*   **Enforcement:** The Executive Director retains the authority to demand the removal of any asset that does not comply with these guidelines.
