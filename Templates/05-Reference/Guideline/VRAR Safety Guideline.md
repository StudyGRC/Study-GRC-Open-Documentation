### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this guideline is to establish safety protocols for the use of Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) technologies within Study GRC Inc. educational programs and events. As an organization dedicated to advancing GRC knowledge through innovative means (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3), we utilize immersive technology to enhance learning. This document aims to mitigate physical risks, physiological side effects (such as cybersickness), and content-related risks associated with these emerging technologies.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, and participants involved in activities where VR or AR hardware and software are utilized.
*   **Inclusions:** In-person workshops, remote training sessions requiring VR headsets, and AR mobile application usage.
*   **Exclusions:** Personal use of VR/AR equipment by staff or members outside of official Organization business or curriculum.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Virtual Reality (VR)** | A simulated experience that employs 3D near-eye displays and pose tracking to give the user an immersive feel of a virtual world. |
| **Augmented Reality (AR)** | An interactive experience where real-world environments are enhanced by computer-generated perceptual information (usually via smartphones or tablets). |
| **Chaperone/Guardian** | The digital safety boundary system built into VR headsets that alerts users when they are nearing physical obstacles. |
| **Cybersickness** | A form of motion sickness caused by a mismatch between visual input and the vestibular system (inner ear) during VR use. |
| **Spotter** | A designated individual responsible for monitoring the physical safety of a VR user during in-person events. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 Physical Environment Safety
To prevent injury from collisions, tripping, or falls, the following environmental standards must be met before engaging in VR activities:

*   **4.1.1 Minimum Play Space:** For "Room Scale" VR, a minimum clear area of 6ft x 6ft (2m x 2m) is required. For "Stationary" VR, a clear radius of arm-span length plus 1 foot is required.
*   **4.1.2 Obstacle Clearance:** The play area must be cleared of tripping hazards, including cables, rugs, pets, and furniture. Ceiling fans within reach must be turned off.
*   **4.1.3 Boundary Setup:** Users must configure the hardware's digital safety boundary (e.g., Guardian or Chaperone system) prior to launching any application.
*   **4.1.4 Spotter Requirement:** During in-person Study GRC events, a "Spotter" should be present to verbally warn the user if they drift near boundaries or bystanders.

#### 4.2 Health and Physiology
Extended use of immersive technology can affect user health. The following protocols apply:

*   **4.2.1 Age Restrictions:** In alignment with most hardware manufacturer Terms of Service (e.g., Meta, HTC), VR headsets should not be used by children under the age of 13. For participants under 13, AR (tablet/phone-based) alternatives should be prioritized.
*   **4.2.2 Seizure Warning:** Individuals with a history of photosensitive epilepsy should consult a medical professional before using VR.
*   **4.2.3 The 20-20-20 Rule:** To prevent eye strain, users should take a break every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away, for at least 20 seconds.
*   **4.2.4 Cybersickness Management:** If a user experiences dizziness, nausea, or disorientation, they must immediately remove the headset and sit down. They should not attempt to "push through" the sensation.

#### 4.3 Hygiene and Sanitation
Sharing equipment carries a risk of transmitting communicable diseases.

*   **4.3.1 Sanitization:** VR headsets (specifically the facial interface and controllers) must be wiped down with non-abrasive, anti-bacterial wipes (alcohol-free for lenses) between every user.
*   **4.3.2 Barriers:** Disposable VR hygiene masks (sweat guards) should be provided for in-person public demonstrations.
*   **4.3.3 Hand Washing:** Users should sanitize hands prior to and after handling shared equipment.

#### 4.4 Content and Digital Safety
Pursuant to the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**, the digital environment must be as safe as the physical one.

*   **4.4.1 Approved Content:** Only applications and experiences vetted and approved by the VP of Education & Training may be used.
*   **4.4.2 Social VR Restrictions:** "Social" VR platforms (e.g., VRChat, Rec Room) where users interact with the general public are prohibited for youth programs unless in a private, password-protected instance moderated by Study GRC staff.
*   **4.4.3 Data Privacy:** Staff must ensure that VR devices are configured to minimize data collection. Biometric data (eye tracking, movement patterns) should not be recorded or stored unless explicitly authorized by the user (and parent/guardian if a minor) via a specific consent form.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Approves VR/AR curriculum and software selection to ensure alignment with GRC educational goals. |
| **Event Safety Coordinator** | Ensures physical environments for in-person events meet safety standards (clearance, hygiene). |
| **Instructor / Facilitator** | Acts as the "Spotter" during sessions; enforces hygiene protocols; monitors users for signs of distress. |
| **Participant (User)** | Adheres to safety boundaries; immediately reports discomfort or equipment issues. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow these guidelines may result in the immediate suspension of VR/AR privileges during the event.
*   **Negligence:** Staff or volunteers who willfully ignore safety protocols (e.g., allowing a minor under 13 to use a headset without parental waiver and strict supervision, or failing to sanitize equipment) may face disciplinary action up to and including termination of volunteer status.
*   **Liability:** Adherence to these guidelines is essential for maintaining the Organization's indemnification protections as outlined in **Bylaws Article VII**.
