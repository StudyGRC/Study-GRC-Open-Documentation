### 1. Purpose
This guideline establishes the operational framework for managing "Dual Service" scenarios where an individual serves simultaneously as a voting member of the Board of Directors and as an Operational Officer (volunteer or compensated). While Bylaws Article V, Section 5.1 explicitly permits Dual Service, this overlap creates inherent risks regarding segregation of duties, self-dealing, and circular reporting lines. This document defines the "Rules of Engagement" to ensure fiduciary duties are met and internal controls remain robust.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to:
*   **Dual Service Members:** Any individual holding a seat on the Board of Directors while simultaneously holding an Operational Officer title (e.g., Executive Director, CISO, CFO).
*   **The Board of Directors:** Regarding the management of meetings and votes involving Dual Service Members.

**Exclusions:** This guideline does not apply to Board Officers (President, Secretary, Treasurer) who do not hold separate Operational titles, as those are purely governance roles defined in Article IV.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dual Service Member** | An individual who holds both a governance role (Director) and an execution role (Operational Officer) simultaneously. |
| **Governance Hat** | The mindset and authority exercised when acting as a fiduciary Director, focusing on oversight, strategy, and compliance. |
| **Operational Hat** | The mindset and authority exercised when acting as a staff member/officer, focusing on execution, day-to-day management, and reporting to the CEO. |
| **Circular Reporting** | A situation where an Operational Officer (e.g., CISO) reports to the CEO for daily work, but the CEO reports to the Board (which includes that CISO) for performance evaluation. |
| **Recusal** | The act of removing oneself from participation in a decision or vote due to a conflict of interest. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 The "Two Hats" Principle
Dual Service Members must clearly distinguish which "hat" they are wearing during interactions.
*   **Board Meetings:** The individual wears the **Governance Hat**. They are peers to other Directors and superiors to the Executive Director (unless they *are* the Executive Director).
*   **Daily Operations:** The individual wears the **Operational Hat**. They are subject to the authority of the Executive Director and the policies of the Organization. Being a Board Director does not grant them immunity from operational management or disciplinary procedures applicable to staff.

#### 4.2 Compensation and Financial Benefit
Pursuant to IRS regulations regarding "Intermediate Sanctions" and Bylaws Article III:
*   **Strict Recusal:** A Dual Service Member **must not** be present during discussions or votes regarding their own compensation, benefits, or employment contract.
*   **Budget Approval:** While a Dual Service Member may advocate for their department's budget (Operational Hat), they should generally abstain from the final vote approving the budget (Governance Hat) if it includes a material change to their specific compensation or departmental resources that could be perceived as self-dealing.

#### 4.3 Performance Evaluation and Circular Reporting
To mitigate Circular Reporting risks:
*   **CEO Evaluation:** If a Dual Service Member reports to the CEO operationally (e.g., the CISO is also a Director), that Member **must recuse** themselves from the Board's formal performance evaluation and compensation review of the CEO.
    *   *Rationale:* A subordinate cannot objectively evaluate their supervisor without fear of retaliation or expectation of favor.
*   **Self-Evaluation:** A Dual Service Member cannot participate in the Board's review of their own operational performance.

#### 4.4 Executive Sessions
*   **Exclusion:** The Board may hold Executive Sessions to discuss the performance, conduct, or compensation of Operational Officers. Dual Service Members holding the specific operational role under discussion must be excluded from these sessions.
*   **Minutes:** The Secretary must document that the Dual Service Member left the meeting prior to the discussion.

#### 4.5 Operational Authority vs. Board Authority
*   **Chain of Command:** A Dual Service Member must not use their Board title to bypass operational chain of command.
    *   *Example:* A Director who serves as the "Director of Kindness" (Operational) cannot override a decision made by the CEO by claiming "I am a Board Member." In operational matters, the CEO's authority is paramount.
*   **Information Access:** While Directors generally have broad access to records, a Dual Service Member should access operational data through standard staff channels (Operational Hat) rather than demanding it via Board authority, unless investigating a governance issue.

#### 4.6 Conflict of Interest (COI) Disclosure
In addition to the Annual Disclosure required by the *Conflict of Interest Policy*, Dual Service Members must verbally disclose their dual status at the start of any Board discussion where the topic directly impacts their operational department.
*   *Phrasing:* "Acting as the [Operational Title], I recommend X. However, as a Director, I will abstain from the vote to preserve independence."

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dual Service Member** | Proactively identify conflicts; recuse from votes regarding own pay or supervisor's performance; respect operational chain of command. |
| **Board President** | Enforce recusal rules; ensure Dual Service Members do not dominate meetings with operational minutiae; manage Executive Sessions. |
| **Secretary** | Accurately record recusals and departures from the room in the meeting minutes to prove separation of duties during audits. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Manage Dual Service Members as standard staff/volunteers during daily operations without deference to their Board status. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this guideline undermines the Organization's internal controls and tax-exempt status.
*   **Violations:** A Dual Service Member who refuses to recuse themselves from conflicting votes or who leverages their Board seat to undermine operational leadership may be subject to removal from the Board (per Bylaws Section 3.6) or termination of their Operational Officer role.
*   **Audit Trail:** All recusals must be documented. Failure to document may result in audit findings regarding lack of internal controls.
