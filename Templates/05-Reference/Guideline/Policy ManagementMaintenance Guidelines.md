### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Guideline is to establish a standardized lifecycle for all governance documents within Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). To ensure compliance with Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and federal grant requirements (2 CFR 200), policies must not only be drafted but actively maintained, reviewed, and retired when obsolete. This document defines the "Lifecycle Management" process to prevent policy stagnation and ensure the Master Documentation Registry (MDR) remains accurate and legally defensible.

### 2. Scope
This Guideline applies to all **Governance Documents** created, approved, or utilized by Study GRC Inc., including:
*   Policies (Level 1)
*   Standards and Procedures (Level 2)
*   Guidelines, Forms, and Templates (Level 3)

It applies to all Board Directors, Officers, employees, and volunteers who act as **Document Owners** or **Approving Authorities**.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Document Owner** | The specific role (e.g., CISO, Treasurer) responsible for drafting, maintaining, and reviewing a document. |
| **Lifecycle** | The stages of a document's existence: Creation, Approval, Implementation, Maintenance/Review, and Retirement. |
| **Master Documentation Registry (MDR)** | The central, authoritative inventory of all active governance documents. |
| **Sunset/Retirement** | The formal process of deactivating a document that is no longer relevant or has been superseded. |
| **SME** | Subject Matter Expert; an individual consulted during the drafting phase for technical accuracy. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 The Document Lifecycle
All governance documents at Study GRC Inc. must follow a five-stage lifecycle to ensure integrity and compliance.

**Stage 1: Drafting & Consultation**
*   **Needs Assessment:** The Document Owner identifies a regulatory requirement (e.g., new 2 CFR 200 update) or operational risk.
*   **Template Usage:** All drafts must utilize the approved `GOV-TMP-001 Governance Document Template`.
*   **Plain English:** Drafts must adhere to the Organization's Style Guide (Active voice, gender-neutral, mandatory language for requirements).
*   **SME Review:** Relevant stakeholders must be consulted (e.g., CISO for IT policies, Treasurer for Finance policies).

**Stage 2: Approval**
*   Approval authority is determined by the **Document Governance Framework**:
    *   **Level 1 (Policies):** Must be approved by the Board of Directors (recorded in Minutes per Bylaws Article VIII).
    *   **Level 2 (Standards/Procedures):** Approved by the Executive Director (CEO).
    *   **Level 3 (Guidelines):** Approved by the relevant Department Head.

**Stage 3: Publication & Communication**
*   Upon approval, the document is assigned a unique ID (e.g., `HR-POL-005`) and logged in the MDR.
*   The document is published to the central repository (e.g., Intranet/SharePoint).
*   **Notification:** Relevant staff/volunteers must be notified. For critical policies (e.g., Code of Ethics), acknowledgment of receipt is required.

**Stage 4: Maintenance & Periodic Review**
*   Documents are not "set and forget." They must be reviewed according to the schedule defined in Section 4.2 below.
*   Reviews confirm:
    *   Regulatory compliance (Are laws still the same?).
    *   Operational accuracy (Do we still do it this way?).
    *   Link integrity (Do references to other docs still work?).

**Stage 5: Retirement (Sunsetting)**
*   Obsolete documents must be formally retired, not just deleted.
*   Retired documents are moved to an "Archive" folder for audit trails (retained per the *Document Retention Policy*).
*   The MDR status is updated to "Retired."

#### 4.2 Standard Review Cycles
Unless otherwise mandated by law, documents should adhere to the following review cadence:

| Document Type | Review Frequency | Trigger Events |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bylaws & Charter** | Every 2 Years | Legislative changes to TBOC; Strategic shift. |
| **Level 1 Policies** | Annual | Change in Board composition; Audit findings. |
| **Level 2 Procedures** | Bi-Annual (Every 2 yrs) | Technology changes; Process optimization. |
| **Level 3 Guidelines** | As Needed (Max 3 yrs) | User feedback; Software updates. |

#### 4.3 Version Control Standards
To maintain a clear audit trail, Version Control must be used:
*   **Drafts:** v0.1, v0.2, v0.9 (Pre-approval).
*   **Initial Release:** v1.0 (Approved).
*   **Minor Updates:** v1.1, v1.2 (Typos, broken links, formatting - does not require full re-approval by Board, only Owner).
*   **Major Updates:** v2.0, v3.0 (Substantive changes to rules, scope, or intent - requires full re-approval by the Approving Authority).

#### 4.4 Policy Obsolescence
If a policy is found to be in conflict with the Bylaws or federal law (2 CFR 200), the **Bylaws and federal law supersede the policy** immediately. The Document Owner must initiate an emergency revision (vX.1) to resolve the conflict.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Governance Committee** | Oversees the Policy Management Framework; conducts annual audit of the MDR to ensure reviews are occurring. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Approves Level 2 documents; ensures staff have access to current policies. |
| **Document Owner** | Drafts content; tracks "Next Review Date"; initiates the review process 30 days prior to expiration. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains the Master Documentation Registry (MDR) and archives retired Board-level policies. |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Responsible for reading and adhering to the *current* version of policies applicable to their role. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this guideline results in "Policy Rot," creating legal liability and audit findings.
*   **Audit Risk:** Outdated policies are a primary cause of findings during Single Audits (2 CFR 200 Subpart F).
*   **Enforcement:** Document Owners who consistently fail to review their assigned documents by the "Next Review Date" may be subject to performance management action.
