### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to provide guidance for preventing, recognizing, and treating cold stress-related illnesses and injuries. While Study GRC Inc. operates primarily as a remote-first organization, in-person events, conferences, and community gatherings may occur during winter months or in cold climates. This guideline aligns with the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) General Duty Clause to provide a workplace free from recognized hazards.

### 2. Scope
This guideline applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, contractors, and event participants engaged in Organization-sanctioned activities.

**Specific Applications:**
*   Outdoor setup/teardown for events or conferences.
*   Travel to and from Organization events during severe winter weather.
*   Outdoor community gatherings or social events.

**Exclusions:**
*   This document does not cover HVAC maintenance or facility management, as Study GRC Inc. does not own real estate facilities.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cold Stress** | A condition that occurs when the body can no longer maintain its normal temperature due to heat loss exceeding heat production. Common forms include hypothermia, frostbite, and trench foot. |
| **Hypothermia** | A medical emergency that occurs when the body loses heat faster than it can produce it, causing a dangerously low body temperature (below 95°F). |
| **Frostbite** | An injury caused by freezing of the skin and underlying tissues, most common on fingers, toes, nose, ears, cheeks, and chin. |
| **Wind Chill** | A measure of how cold it feels when wind speed is factored in with air temperature. Wind removes the insulating layer of heat from the body surface. |
| **The Buddy System** | A safety procedure where individuals are paired up to monitor each other's well-being and signs of distress. |

### 4. Guidelines

#### 4.1 Risk Assessment and Environmental Monitoring
Before any outdoor activity or travel during winter months, the Event Manager or designated lead should assess the risk.
*   **Weather Check:** Monitor the National Weather Service (NWS) for temperature, wind speed, and precipitation.
*   **Wind Chill Factor:** Use the NWS Wind Chill Chart. If the wind chill is below 20°F (-6°C), outdoor exposure times should be strictly limited.
*   **Texas Context:** As a Texas-based entity, personnel should be aware that local infrastructure and individual tolerance for cold may be lower than in northern climates. Extra caution is advised during "Blue Norther" events or ice storms.

#### 4.2 Protective Clothing (The Layering System)
Proper clothing is the primary defense against cold stress. Personnel working or attending events outdoors should adopt a three-layer system:
1.  **Inner Layer (Wicking):** Synthetic fibers (polyester, polypropylene) or wool to wick moisture away from the skin. **Avoid cotton**, as it holds moisture and loses insulation value when wet.
2.  **Middle Layer (Insulation):** Wool or fleece to trap body heat.
3.  **Outer Layer (Protection):** A wind-breaking and water-repellent layer (nylon, Gore-Tex) that allows some ventilation to prevent overheating.

**Additional Gear:**
*   **Headgear:** A knit mask or hat that covers the ears (up to 40% of body heat can be lost through the head).
*   **Footwear:** Insulated, waterproof boots with good traction.
*   **Gloves:** Insulated gloves or mittens (mittens provide better warmth).

#### 4.3 Work/Rest Cycles and Shelter
For staff or volunteers performing physical labor (e.g., loading/unloading event equipment) in cold environments:
*   **Warm-Up Breaks:** Schedule frequent short breaks in warm, dry areas (heated tents, vehicles, or lobbies).
*   **Scheduling:** Schedule outdoor tasks for the warmest part of the day (typically 11:00 AM – 2:00 PM).
*   **Pacing:** Avoid heavy exertion that causes excessive sweating, which leads to damp clothing and rapid cooling.

#### 4.4 Hydration and Nutrition
Cold weather suppresses thirst, but dehydration increases the risk of cold stress.
*   **Fluids:** Drink warm, sweetened fluids (sugar water, sports drinks).
*   **Avoid:** Caffeine and alcohol, as they accelerate heat loss and dehydration.
*   **Food:** Eat warm, high-calorie foods (carbohydrates) to maintain energy reserves for heat production.

#### 4.5 Symptom Recognition and Response
All personnel should utilize the **Buddy System** to monitor for signs of cold stress, as victims often do not recognize their own symptoms due to confusion.

**Hypothermia Signs:**
*   *Mild:* Shivering, fatigue, loss of coordination, confusion.
*   *Severe:* No shivering, blue skin, dilated pupils, slowed pulse/breathing, loss of consciousness.
*   *Response:* Move to a warm area immediately. Remove wet clothing. Wrap in dry blankets. **Call 911 immediately.**

**Frostbite Signs:**
*   *Symptoms:* Reduced blood flow, numbness, tingling or stinging, waxy/white or grayish-yellow skin.
*   *Response:* Get into a warm room. Do **not** rub the area. Do **not** use heating pads directly on the skin. Use warm (not hot) water. Seek medical attention.

#### 4.6 Vehicle Safety (Travel)
Staff traveling for Organization business during winter conditions should:
*   Ensure the vehicle has a full tank of gas and proper tire tread.
*   Carry an emergency kit containing: blankets, flashlight, water, non-perishable food, and a portable phone charger.
*   Stay with the vehicle if stranded.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Manager / Lead** | Monitors weather conditions; determines if outdoor activities must be cancelled or modified; enforces break schedules. |
| **Volunteers & Staff** | Adhere to clothing guidelines; monitor self and "buddies" for symptoms; report unsafe conditions immediately. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Ensures a culture where safety is prioritized over task completion; facilitates access to warm rest areas during events. |
| **Executive Director** | Authorizes the cancellation of events due to severe weather risks. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
While these are guidelines, disregard for safety protocols that endangers oneself or others is a violation of the Organization’s safety culture.
*   **Event Removal:** The Event Manager has the authority to remove any volunteer or staff member from outdoor duties if they lack appropriate gear or exhibit signs of cold stress.
*   **Liability:** Failure to adhere to these guidelines may impact eligibility for workers' compensation or insurance coverage in the event of an incident, subject to state law.
