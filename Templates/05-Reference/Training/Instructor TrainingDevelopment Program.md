### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Instructor Training and Development Program is to ensure that all individuals delivering educational content on behalf of Study GRC Inc. possess the pedagogical skills, technical proficiency, and ethical grounding necessary to advance the Organization’s mission of "simplifying the GRC domain." This program establishes a standardized quality assurance framework to mitigate reputational risk, ensure compliance with youth protection standards, and guarantee that all instruction aligns with the Organization’s open-source, community-driven values.

### 2. Scope
This program applies to all individuals authorized to design, facilitate, or instruct educational sessions, workshops, or courses for Study GRC Inc.

*   **In Scope:** Volunteer Instructors, Contracted Educators, Staff Instructors, and Chapter Workshop Facilitators.
*   **Out of Scope:** Guest Speakers (one-time appearances under supervision), Panelists, and Administrative Support Staff not involved in content delivery.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Instructor** | Any individual approved by the Organization to lead educational sessions, whether virtual or in-person. |
| **Pedagogy** | The method and practice of teaching, specifically adapted here for both adult learners (Andragogy) and youth participants. |
| **Work Product** | As defined in Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3, content created for the Organization which is considered "work made for hire." |
| **LMS** | Learning Management System; the software application used to document, track, and deliver educational courses. |
| **Shadowing** | The process where a Candidate Instructor observes a Certified Instructor to learn delivery techniques and platform management. |

### 4. Program Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase I: Onboarding and Compliance (Mandatory)
Before delivering any content, all Instructor candidates must complete the following compliance modules. Failure to complete Phase I results in immediate disqualification.

1.  **Organizational Orientation:** Review of Study GRC Inc. Bylaws, specifically Article I (Purposes) and Article IX (Intellectual Property). Instructors must acknowledge that all course materials created are open-source and owned by the Organization.
2.  **Code of Ethics & Conduct:** Review and signature of the *Instructor Code of Ethics*, emphasizing neutrality, non-solicitation, and professional behavior.
3.  **Youth Protection Training:** Mandatory completion of the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* training. This includes:
    *   Recognizing signs of abuse.
    *   Mandatory reporting procedures (Texas Family Code §261.101).
    *   "Two-Deep Leadership" protocols for virtual and physical classrooms.
4.  **Background Check Adjudication:** Verification that the candidate has passed the required background check per the *Background Check Policy*.

#### 4.2 Phase II: Pedagogical Training
To ensure the mission of "simplifying the GRC domain" is met, instructors must complete training on instructional design and delivery.

1.  **Simplification Framework:** Training on how to translate complex regulatory concepts (e.g., NIST, ISO, GDPR) into accessible language for diverse audiences.
2.  **Audience Adaptation:**
    *   **Track A (Professional):** Andragogy principles, focusing on practical application, case studies, and career advancement.
    *   **Track B (K-12/Youth):** Engagement strategies, gamification, and age-appropriate digital safety analogies.
3.  **Inclusive Instruction:** Training on *Digital Accessibility* (WCAG 2.2 principles) to ensure presentations are accessible to learners with visual, auditory, or cognitive disabilities.

#### 4.3 Phase III: Technical Proficiency
Instructors must demonstrate competence with the Organization’s technology stack to prevent disruptions during delivery.

1.  **Virtual Classroom Management:** Training on the approved video conferencing platform (e.g., Zoom/Teams), including:
    *   Managing breakout rooms.
    *   Handling "Zoom-bombing" or disruptive participants (Security protocols).
    *   Using live captioning tools.
2.  **LMS Administration:** Procedures for uploading materials, grading assessments (if applicable), and tracking attendance in the Learning Management System.
3.  **GitHub/Open Source Tools:** For technical courses, training on how to manage repositories, pull requests, and lab environments.

#### 4.4 Phase IV: Certification and Shadowing
Prior to solo instruction, candidates must undergo a practical evaluation.

1.  **The "Teach-Back" Session:** The candidate must present a 20-minute micro-lesson to the VP of Education & Training or a designate. Grading criteria include:
    *   Clarity of communication.
    *   Technical smoothness.
    *   Adherence to the "Simplification" mission.
2.  **Shadowing Requirement:** The candidate must co-facilitate at least one (1) session with a Senior Instructor.
3.  **Final Authorization:** Upon successful completion, the VP of Education & Training issues a formal "Authorized Instructor" designation, valid for one (1) year.

#### 4.5 Phase V: Continuous Improvement and Recertification
Quality assurance is an ongoing process.

1.  **Participant Feedback Loop:** After every course, participant survey data (NPS and qualitative feedback) is reviewed. Instructors maintaining an average rating below 3.5/5.0 will be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).
2.  **Annual Refresher:** Instructors must complete an annual 2-hour refresher course covering:
    *   Updates to GRC frameworks.
    *   Changes in Youth Protection laws.
    *   New educational tools or policies.
3.  **Peer Review:** Instructors are encouraged to audit one peer session annually to share best practices.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Overall program owner. Approves curriculum, certifies instructors, and reviews feedback metrics. |
| **Instructor Candidate** | Completes all training modules, submits to background checks, and adheres to IP and ethics policies. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Facilitates "Culture Fit" training components to ensure inclusive and welcoming classroom environments. |
| **HR / Compliance Officer** | Administers background checks and maintains records of signed IP assignments and Code of Conduct acknowledgments. |
| **Senior Instructor** | Acts as a mentor for shadowing sessions and provides objective feedback during "Teach-Back" evaluations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Participation in this program is mandatory for all active instructors.
*   **Non-Compliance:** Failure to complete Phase I (Compliance) training prohibits the individual from instructing any session.
*   **Performance Issues:** Failure to maintain quality standards (Phase V) may result in the revocation of Instructor status and removal from the schedule.
*   **Safety Violations:** Any breach of Youth Protection protocols taught in this program will result in immediate suspension pending investigation, per the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.
