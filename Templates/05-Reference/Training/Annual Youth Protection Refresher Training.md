### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to define the mandatory curriculum, frequency, and certification requirements for the **Annual Youth Protection Refresher Training**. This training ensures that all Study GRC Inc. personnel remain vigilant regarding the safety of minors, fully understand their legal obligations as Mandated Reporters under the Texas Family Code, and adhere to the Organization’s specific protocols for remote and digital interactions. This standard mitigates liability risks and upholds the Organization's charitable purpose as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**.

### 2. Scope
This training requirement applies to:
*   **Covered Individuals:** All Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers who:
    *   Interact directly with minors (in-person or virtually);
    *   Have administrative access to platforms where minors congregate (e.g., Discord, LMS); or
    *   Have access to Personally Identifiable Information (PII) of minors.
*   **Exclusions:** Third-party vendors with no access to program participants or data.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18 years. |
| **Mandated Reporter** | Under Texas Family Code § 261.101, any person having cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect. |
| **Grooming** | The predatory act of maneuvering a minor into a position of vulnerability for sexual abuse, often beginning with non-sexual behavior to build trust (e.g., special favors, secrecy). |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The policy requirement that at least two background-checked adults must be present during any interaction with a minor, whether physical or virtual. |
| **COPPA** | Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, a federal law regulating the collection of personal information from children under 13. |

### 4. Training Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 Frequency and Timing
4.1.1 **Initial Training:** All Covered Individuals must complete the full Youth Protection Training module within **14 days** of their start date and *prior* to any unsupervised access to systems containing minor data or interaction with minors.
4.1.2 **Annual Refresher:** All Covered Individuals must complete the Refresher Training annually. The compliance window opens on September 1st and closes on September 30th of each fiscal year.
4.1.3 **Lapsed Certification:** Any individual who fails to complete the training by the deadline will have their system access suspended immediately until compliance is achieved.

#### 4.2 Curriculum Requirements
The Annual Refresher Training must include, at a minimum, the following four modules:

**Module A: Legal Obligations (Texas Specific)**
*   **Mandatory Reporting:** Review of Texas Family Code § 261.101.
*   **No Delegation:** Emphasis that the duty to report cannot be delegated to a supervisor.
*   **Timeframe:** Requirement to report "immediately" (within 48 hours).
*   **Immunity:** Explanation of immunity from civil/criminal liability for reports made in good faith.

**Module B: Digital Safety & Remote Protocols**
*   **Virtual Two-Deep Rule:** Prohibition of 1-on-1 Direct Messages (DMs), private video calls, or breakout rooms with minors without a second adult observer.
*   **Digital Grooming Indicators:** Recognizing signs of grooming in a text-based environment (e.g., excessive use of emojis, moving conversations to unmonitored platforms, sharing "secrets").
*   **Camera/Background Etiquette:** Standards for professional backgrounds and attire during video calls.

**Module C: Data Privacy (COPPA & Bylaws)**
*   **Data Minimization:** Review of **Bylaws Article IX** regarding data protection.
*   **COPPA Compliance:** Strict prohibition on collecting data from users under 13 without Verifiable Parental Consent.
*   **Right to Erasure:** Procedures for handling requests to delete a minor's data.

**Module D: Incident Reporting Procedures**
*   **Internal Reporting:** How to file an internal incident report via the *Suspected Abuse Reporting Form*.
*   **External Reporting:** Contact information for the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) and local law enforcement.

#### 4.3 Assessment and Certification
4.3.1 **Knowledge Check:** The training must conclude with a graded assessment.
4.3.2 **Passing Score:** A minimum score of **100%** is required. Individuals may retake the assessment until a passing score is achieved.
4.3.3 **Acknowledgment:** Upon passing, the individual must electronically sign a statement reaffirming their commitment to the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* and the *Code of Conduct*.

#### 4.4 Record Keeping
4.4.1 **Documentation:** The HR/Compliance department shall maintain a *Training Registry* documenting the name, date of completion, and assessment score for all personnel.
4.4.2 **Retention:** Training records must be retained for a minimum of **seven (7) years** post-employment or service conclusion, in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this standard is a violation of the Organization's risk management protocols.
*   **Immediate Action:** Automatic suspension of access to all Study GRC Inc. systems and revocation of volunteer/employment status regarding youth programs.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Persistent failure to complete training or evidence of disregarding training protocols may result in termination of employment or removal from the Board (pursuant to **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6** for Directors).
