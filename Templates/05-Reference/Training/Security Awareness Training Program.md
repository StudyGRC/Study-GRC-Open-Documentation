### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Security Awareness Training Program** is to reduce the risk of organizational data loss and system compromise by ensuring that all personnel possess the knowledge required to identify, report, and mitigate information security threats.

As Study GRC Inc. operates as a remote-first, community-driven non-profit handling sensitive data (including youth data and federal grant information), human error remains the primary attack vector. This program satisfies the "Protect" function of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (PR.AT) and demonstrates the internal controls required by **2 CFR 200.303** for federal award compliance.

### 2. Scope
This program applies to all individuals with access to Study GRC Inc. information systems, data, or physical facilities.

*   **Applies to:**
    *   Board of Directors (per Bylaws Article III).
    *   Officers and Employees.
    *   Volunteers and Interns with system access (e.g., email, Slack, CRM).
    *   Contractors and Third-Party Vendors with access to internal data.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Community Participants (Non-Voting Members) who do not hold volunteer roles or access internal systems.
    *   Vendors who do not access Study GRC Inc. data or networks (e.g., purely physical maintenance).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Phishing** | A cybercrime in which a target is contacted by email, telephone, or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data. |
| **Social Engineering** | The psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. |
| **Privileged User** | An individual who has administrative access to critical systems (e.g., System Administrators, CISO, DevOps). |
| **COPPA** | Children's Online Privacy Protection Act; federal law regulating the collection of data from minors under 13. |
| **Insider Threat** | A security risk that originates from within the targeted organization (e.g., employees, former employees, contractors). |

### 4. Program Requirements

#### 4.1 Training Frequency and Timing
To ensure continuous vigilance, training is mandatory at the following intervals:

1.  **New Hire/Volunteer Onboarding:** All personnel must complete the "Foundations of Security" module within **14 days** of their start date. System access may be restricted until completion.
2.  **Annual Refresher:** All personnel must complete the comprehensive Annual Security Awareness certification by **October 31st** of each fiscal year.
3.  **Ad-Hoc/remedial:** Personnel who fail simulated phishing tests or are involved in a security incident may be required to complete immediate remedial training.

#### 4.2 Core Curriculum Content
The training curriculum must be reviewed annually by the CISO to ensure relevance. Mandatory topics include:

*   **Phishing & Social Engineering:** Identifying red flags in emails, SMS (smishing), and voice calls (vishing).
*   **Password Hygiene:** Compliance with `IS-STD-004` (Password Policy), including the use of password managers and MFA.
*   **Remote Work Security:** Securing home Wi-Fi, use of VPNs, and the "Clean Desk/Clear Screen" requirements for remote environments.
*   **Data Classification & Handling:** Proper handling of Public, Internal, and Confidential data.
*   **Youth Protection & Privacy:** Specific training on **COPPA** compliance and safeguarding the data of minors, aligning with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.
*   **Incident Reporting:** How and when to report suspicious activity to the Security Team.
*   **Mobile Device Security:** BYOD protocols and lost device procedures.

#### 4.3 Phishing Simulations
To test the effectiveness of the training, the CISO shall conduct:
*   **Monthly Phishing Simulations:** Simulated attacks sent to a random sample or the entire organization.
*   **Reporting:** Users are expected to report suspicious emails using the "Report Phishing" button or via email to `security@studygrc.org`.
*   **Consequences:** Users who click a simulated malicious link will be immediately redirected to a "Teachable Moment" micro-training page. Repeated failures (3+ in a rolling 12-month period) will result in a formal review with the user's manager.

#### 4.4 Role-Based Training
Certain roles require specialized training beyond the general curriculum:
*   **Developers/IT Staff:** Secure coding practices (OWASP Top 10), cloud security configuration, and API security.
*   **Finance/HR:** Business Email Compromise (BEC) fraud detection, wire transfer verification protocols, and sensitive PII handling.
*   **Board of Directors:** High-level training on fiduciary duties related to cybersecurity governance and strategic risk.

#### 4.5 Tracking and Records
*   The Organization must maintain records of training completion for a minimum of **three (3) years** to satisfy audit requirements.
*   Completion metrics (percentage of staff trained) will be reported to the Board of Directors quarterly.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Develops curriculum, manages the training platform, executes phishing simulations, and reports compliance metrics to the Board. |
| **HR / Volunteer Coordinator** | Ensures new hires/volunteers are enrolled in training immediately upon onboarding; enforces completion as a condition of continued employment/service. |
| **Managers / Dept Heads** | Ensure their direct reports complete training on time; address repeated phishing failures with staff. |
| **Employees / Volunteers** | Complete all assigned training modules within the designated timeframe; report suspicious activity. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Participation in the Security Awareness Training Program is a condition of employment and volunteer service.

*   **Non-Compliance:** Failure to complete mandatory training within the allotted timeframe will result in the **suspension of system access** (e.g., email, Slack, Google Workspace) until the training is completed.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Continued refusal to participate, or gross negligence in applying security training principles, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, in accordance with the *Employee Handbook* and *Bylaws Article III*.
