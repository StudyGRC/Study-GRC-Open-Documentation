### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Board Member Orientation Program is to ensure that all newly elected or appointed Directors of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") are fully equipped to discharge their legal, financial, and strategic responsibilities immediately upon assuming office. This program mitigates organizational risk by ensuring Directors understand their **Fiduciary Duties** under Texas law, the Organization’s specific regulatory environment (including Federal Grant compliance and Youth Protection), and the operational mechanics of a remote-first governance structure.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Incoming Directors:** All individuals elected by the Membership or appointed by the Board to fill a vacancy.
*   **Returning Directors:** Directors re-elected after a hiatus of more than one (1) year.

**Exclusions:**
*   Advisory Board members (covered under *GOV-CHR-005 Advisory Board Charter*).
*   Operational Officers (unless they simultaneously hold a voting Board seat).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fiduciary Duty** | The legal obligation to act in the best interest of the Organization. Includes the Duty of Care, Duty of Loyalty, and Duty of Obedience. |
| **Duty of Care** | The requirement to act with the level of care, prudence, and diligence that a person in a like position would exercise under similar circumstances. |
| **Duty of Loyalty** | The requirement to prioritize the Organization's interests over personal or professional interests (managing Conflicts of Interest). |
| **Duty of Obedience** | The requirement to ensure the Organization complies with applicable laws, regulations, and its own Bylaws and mission. |
| **2 CFR 200** | "Uniform Guidance" – Federal regulations governing the management of federal grant awards, requiring specific oversight from the Board. |
| **Onboarding Window** | The period between the certification of election results and the Director's first voting meeting (not to exceed 30 days). |

### 4. Program Requirements

The Orientation Program is mandatory. It consists of three distinct phases: Administrative Compliance, Core Knowledge Transfer, and Operational Integration.

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Administrative Compliance (Pre-Service)
Before accessing sensitive Board materials or casting a vote, the incoming Director must execute the following documents, managed by the Secretary:

1.  **Conflict of Interest Disclosure:** Completion of the *Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statement* (per Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1).
2.  **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA):** Execution of the standard NDA regarding proprietary data, donor lists, and executive session discussions (per Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.2).
3.  **Code of Ethics Acknowledgment:** Signature confirming adherence to the *Board of Directors Code of Ethics and Conduct*.
4.  **Intellectual Property Assignment:** Acknowledgment that work created for the Organization is "work made for hire" and assigned to the Organization (per Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3).

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Core Knowledge Transfer (The Orientation Session)
The Governance Committee or Board President must conduct a synchronous orientation session (virtual or in-person) covering the following curriculum:

**A. Governance & Legal Framework**
*   **Bylaws Deep Dive:** Review of Article III (Directors), Article X (Fundamental Actions), and the "Doomsday Clause" (Section 3.10).
*   **Fiduciary Duties:** Specific training on Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) Chapter 22 requirements.
*   **D&O Insurance:** Overview of Directors & Officers Liability Insurance coverage and Indemnification (Bylaws Article VII).

**B. Financial Oversight & Federal Compliance**
*   **Financial Health:** Review of the current Balance Sheet, P&L, and the most recent IRS Form 990.
*   **Grant Compliance:** Overview of Board responsibilities regarding *2 CFR 200* (Uniform Guidance), specifically regarding procurement oversight and internal controls.
*   **Budgeting:** Explanation of the fiscal year cycle (Bylaws Article VIII) and budget approval process.

**C. Mission & Risk Management**
*   **Strategic Plan:** Review of the current 3-Year Strategic Plan.
*   **Youth Protection:** Mandatory review of the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* due to the Organization's K-12 focus.
*   **Open Source Philosophy:** Understanding the Organization's commitment to zero-cost resources and non-exclusive licensing.

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Operational Integration (Remote-First Tools)
To ensure effective participation in a remote-first environment, the Secretary or CISO must provide training on:

1.  **Secure Communication:** Setup and usage of official Board communication channels (e.g., encrypted email, dedicated Slack channels).
2.  **Electronic Voting:** Training on the designated secure electronic voting system used for "Action Without Meeting" (Bylaws Section 3.9).
3.  **Document Repository:** Access protocols for the Board Portal/Secure Drive where minutes and financials are stored.

#### 4.4 Mentorship
For the first three (3) months of service, a new Director shall be paired with an incumbent Director ("Board Mentor") to answer questions regarding parliamentary procedure (Robert's Rules) and institutional history.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Incoming Director** | Must complete all Phase 1 documentation within 5 business days of election. Must attend Phase 2 orientation before the first regular Board meeting. |
| **Secretary** | Distributes the "Board Welcome Packet," collects signed forms, and maintains the orientation completion records. |
| **President / Chair** | Leads the Phase 2 Knowledge Transfer session. Assigns Board Mentors. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides operational briefings, financial reports, and strategic plan status updates during Phase 2. |
| **Governance Committee** | Reviews and updates the Orientation Program materials annually to reflect changes in laws or Bylaws. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**6.1 Mandatory Completion**
Completion of the Board Member Orientation Program is a qualification for continued service.

**6.2 Failure to Complete**
Failure to complete Phase 1 (Administrative Compliance) or Phase 2 (Knowledge Transfer) within forty-five (45) days of election constitutes "Cause" for removal.
*   **Mechanism:** The Board may initiate removal proceedings pursuant to **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6 (Removal of Directors)**, citing failure to perform duties and potential negligence in fiduciary capacity.

**6.3 Record Keeping**
Signed acknowledgments of orientation completion shall be retained in the Organization’s corporate records for the duration of the Director's tenure plus seven (7) years.
