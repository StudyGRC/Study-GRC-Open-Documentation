### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to mandate and define the requirements for the **Workplace Harassment Prevention Training Program** at Study GRC Inc. This program is designed to educate all personnel on their rights and responsibilities regarding the maintenance of a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free environment. This document ensures the Organization’s compliance with **Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964**, the **Texas Labor Code Chapter 21**, and **EEOC** best practices for prevention and remediation.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all individuals affiliated with Study GRC Inc., regardless of location or tenure.
*   **In Scope:**
    *   Board of Directors (pursuant to Bylaws Article III, Section 3.3 regarding Professional Ethics).
    *   Operational Officers and Employees.
    *   Volunteers and Interns.
    *   Independent Contractors and Consultants (where contractually stipulated).
*   **Environment:** Applies to all work environments, including remote workspaces (Slack, Zoom, Discord), physical events, and conferences.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Protected Characteristic** | Attributes protected by federal or state law, including race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, or gender identity), national origin, age (40 or older), disability, and genetic information. |
| **Sexual Harassment** | Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when submission is made a term of employment, used as a basis for decisions, or creates a hostile environment. |
| **Hostile Work Environment** | A workplace situation where an employee is subject to unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that is severe or pervasive enough to create a work environment that a reasonable person would consider intimidating, hostile, or abusive. |
| **Quid Pro Quo** | "This for that" harassment; occurs when a person in authority makes employment decisions (hiring, promotion, retention) contingent upon an employee's acceptance or rejection of unwelcome sexual conduct. |
| **Retaliation** | Adverse action taken against an individual because they engaged in protected activity, such as filing a harassment charge, complaining of discrimination, or participating in an investigation. |

### 4. Program Standards

#### 4.1 Mandatory Participation
All individuals defined in the Scope must complete the designated Harassment Prevention Training.
*   **New Hires/Volunteers:** Must complete the initial training module within **30 days** of their start date.
*   **Board Members:** Must complete training within **60 days** of election or appointment.
*   **Existing Personnel:** Must complete recertification training annually.

#### 4.2 Curriculum Requirements
The training curriculum must be reviewed annually by Legal Counsel or the Compliance Officer to ensure alignment with current EEOC guidance and Texas law. The curriculum must include, at a minimum:
1.  **Definitions and Examples:** Clear explanations of prohibited conduct, including sexual harassment, discrimination, and microaggressions.
2.  **Remote-First Context:** Specific scenarios addressing digital harassment (e.g., inappropriate Slack messages, offensive Zoom backgrounds, cyberbullying, exclusion from digital channels).
3.  **Bystander Intervention:** Strategies for individuals who witness harassment to intervene safely and effectively.
4.  **Reporting Mechanisms:** Detailed instruction on how to file a complaint internally (via the Whistleblower Complaint Intake Form) or externally (EEOC/TWC).
5.  **Non-Retaliation:** Explicit instruction that retaliation against reporters is strictly prohibited and illegal.
6.  **Leadership Responsibilities:** (For Managers/Directors) Specific training on how to receive complaints, duty to act, and maintaining confidentiality.

#### 4.3 Training Delivery Methods
*   **Asynchronous Learning:** The primary method shall be the Organization’s Learning Management System (LMS) or approved third-party compliance platform (e.g., KnowBe4, Traliant).
*   **Synchronous Sessions:** Live workshops may be required for leadership or following a substantiated incident within a specific department.
*   **Accessibility:** All training materials must meet WCAG 2.2 AA standards (captioning, screen reader compatibility) per the Organization's *Digital Accessibility Policy*.

#### 4.4 Manager and Supervisor Supplement
Individuals in supervisory roles (including Board Directors and Operational Officers) must complete a **Supplemental Leadership Module** (minimum 30 minutes additional) focusing on:
*   Identifying subtle signs of a hostile environment.
*   The legal liability of "knew or should have known."
*   Proper documentation of incidents.
*   Avoiding actions that could be construed as retaliation.

#### 4.5 Tracking and Record Retention
*   **Completion Certificates:** Must be generated upon passing a comprehension assessment (minimum score 80%).
*   **Retention:** Records of training completion, including date, attendee name, and curriculum version, must be retained for a minimum of **four (4) years** post-employment/service, in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Human Resources** | Selects the training vendor/content; assigns training to personnel; monitors compliance rates. |
| **Managers / Dept Heads** | Ensure their direct reports complete training on time; model appropriate behavior. |
| **Employees / Volunteers** | Complete assigned training within the deadline; apply training concepts to daily interactions. |
| **Board of Directors** | Completes training; ensures the organization budgets for compliant training resources. |
| **IT / Systems Admin** | Ensures the LMS is accessible and secure; manages user provisioning for training platforms. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to complete the mandatory Workplace Harassment Prevention Training within the prescribed timeframe will result in:
1.  **Suspension of Access:** Immediate revocation of access to Organization systems (Slack, Email, Shared Drives).
2.  **Disciplinary Action:** For employees, this may include written warnings up to termination. For volunteers and Board members, failure to comply constitutes a violation of the *Code of Ethics* and may be grounds for removal under **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6 (Removal of Directors)** or the *Volunteer Handbook*.
