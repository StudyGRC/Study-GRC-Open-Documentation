### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Mandated Reporter Training Program is to ensure that all Study GRC Inc. personnel are fully educated on their legal and ethical obligations to recognize and report suspected child abuse or neglect. As a Texas-based non-profit operating under **Texas Family Code Chapter 261**, and given our mission to foster an educational community including K-12 participants, this program establishes the mandatory training curriculum required to mitigate risk and, most importantly, protect the safety and well-being of youth in our ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This program applies to all individuals associated with Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"), regardless of their physical location, including:
*   **Board of Directors:** As defined in **Bylaws Article III**.
*   **Officers:** Including Operational Officers defined in **Bylaws Article V**.
*   **Employees:** Full-time and part-time.
*   **Volunteers:** Including Advisory Board members, mentors, and community moderators.
*   **Contractors:** Specifically those with access to user data or direct interaction with community members.

**Exclusions:** Third-party vendors with no access to community platforms or user data (e.g., external auditors, landscaping services for physical offices).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Mandated Reporter** | Under Texas law, *any person* who has cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect. |
| **Abuse** | Mental, emotional, physical, or sexual injury to a child, or failure to prevent such injury, as defined in **Texas Family Code § 261.001**. |
| **Neglect** | The leaving of a child in a situation where the child would be exposed to a substantial risk of physical or mental harm, including failure to provide necessary food, shelter, or medical care. |
| **DFPS** | Texas Department of Family and Protective Services. |
| **Universal Mandate** | The legal standard in Texas requiring *all* individuals to report suspicions of abuse, not just professionals (teachers, doctors, etc.). |
| **Grooming** | Predatory behavior used to build trust with a child and/or their caregivers to gain access for abuse, often occurring via digital platforms. |

### 4. Program Requirements

#### 4.1. Legal Basis and Policy
Pursuant to **Texas Family Code § 261.101**, a person having cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect by any person shall immediately make a report.
*   **Non-Delegation:** The duty to report cannot be delegated. An individual cannot fulfill this duty solely by reporting to a supervisor or the Board of Directors. The report must be made to the appropriate state agency or law enforcement.
*   **Immunity:** Individuals who report in good faith are immune from civil or criminal liability (Texas Family Code § 261.106).

#### 4.2. Training Frequency and Timing
To ensure compliance and awareness, training must occur at the following intervals:
1.  **Initial Training:** Must be completed **prior** to the individual's first unsupervised interaction with community members, or within 14 days of onboarding, whichever comes first.
2.  **Annual Refresher:** All personnel must retake the training annually (every 12 months) to remain in Good Standing.
3.  **Ad-Hoc Training:** Required immediately following any major update to Texas Family Code regarding reporting requirements.

#### 4.3. Curriculum Standards
The training curriculum utilized by Study GRC Inc. must meet or exceed the standards set by the Texas DFPS. The curriculum must cover:

**4.3.1. Recognition of Abuse and Neglect**
*   Physical indicators (unexplained injuries).
*   Behavioral indicators (withdrawal, aggression, regression).
*   Sexual abuse indicators (age-inappropriate knowledge).
*   **Digital Indicators:** Specific focus on online grooming, cyberbullying, and sextortion, relevant to our remote-first environment.

**4.3.2. Reporting Procedures**
*   How to file a report via the Texas Abuse Hotline (1-800-252-5400) or TxAbuseHotline.org.
*   Reporting timelines (Immediate requirement).
*   Information required for a report (names, addresses, nature of abuse).

**4.3.3. Confidentiality and Rights**
*   Confidentiality of the reporter's identity.
*   Whistleblower protections for reporters.
*   **Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification):** Clarification that the Organization indemnifies volunteers acting in good faith compliance with this law.

#### 4.4. Approved Training Resources
The Organization utilizes the following approved resources for training delivery:
1.  **Texas DFPS Training:** The official "Reporting Suspected Abuse or Neglect of a Child: A Guide for Education Professionals" (or the general public equivalent).
2.  **Internal LMS Module:** A custom module developed by the VP of Education & Training that specifically addresses *online* safety and digital interaction risks.

#### 4.5. Assessment and Certification
*   **Testing:** Participants must achieve a score of **80% or higher** on the post-training assessment.
*   **Certification:** Upon completion, a Certificate of Completion must be generated.
*   **Acknowledgment:** Participants must sign (electronically) a statement confirming they understand their legal duty to report and that failure to do so is a crime (Class A Misdemeanor or State Jail Felony).

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**6.1. Failure to Train**
Failure to complete the Mandated Reporter Training within the prescribed timeline will result in:
*   Immediate suspension of access to Organization systems and community platforms.
*   Ineligibility to vote (for Voting Members) or hold office until compliance is met.

**6.2. Failure to Report**
Failure to report suspected abuse is a crime under Texas law. Internally, any evidence that an individual suspected abuse but failed to report it will result in:
*   **Immediate Termination:** Of employment, volunteer status, or Board service.
*   **Legal Action:** The Organization will cooperate fully with law enforcement regarding the failure to report.

**6.3. False Reporting**
Maliciously making a false report is a criminal offense and a violation of the **Board Code of Ethics**. Such actions will result in immediate termination and referral to law enforcement.
