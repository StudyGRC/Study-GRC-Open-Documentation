### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this standard is to define the mandatory training requirements for all personnel responsible for creating, editing, or publishing digital content on behalf of Study GRC Inc. This ensures that all educational resources, marketing materials, and communications are accessible to individuals with disabilities, aligning with our mission to "remove financial barriers" and "empower learners" as stated in **Article I, Section 1.3** of the Bylaws. This standard mitigates legal risk under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and ensures compliance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 Level AA.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all **Content Creators** operating within Study GRC Inc.

*   **In Scope:**
    *   Employees, Contractors, and Volunteers (including Board Members and Advisory Board members) who produce digital assets.
    *   Creation of: Blog posts, social media content, educational slide decks, PDFs, video scripts/production, email newsletters, and markdown documentation for open-source repositories.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Backend software developers (covered under *ACC-TRN-001 Accessibility Training for Developers*).
    *   Personnel with no role in external or internal content generation.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Content Creator** | Any individual who authors, designs, or publishes digital information, including text, images, audio, or video. |
| **WCAG 2.2 AA** | Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.2, Level AA. The international technical standard for web accessibility adopted by Study GRC Inc. |
| **Assistive Technology (AT)** | Software or hardware used by people with disabilities to interact with digital content (e.g., screen readers, magnifiers, switch devices). |
| **Alt Text** | Alternative text descriptions for images that allow screen reader users to understand the visual content. |
| **Semantic Structure** | The use of proper HTML or document tags (e.g., H1, H2, Lists) to convey meaning and hierarchy, rather than just visual styling. |
| **Remediation** | The process of fixing accessibility errors in existing content. |

### 4. Training Standards and Curriculum

All Content Creators must complete the **"Authoring for Accessibility"** training module prior to being granted publishing rights on Study GRC platforms.

#### 4.1 Training Delivery and Frequency
*   **Initial Training:** Must be completed within 14 days of onboarding or prior to the first publication of content, whichever comes first.
*   **Annual Refresher:** A condensed refresher course covering updates to WCAG standards and common internal errors must be completed annually.
*   **Remedial Training:** Required if a Content Creator repeatedly produces non-compliant content (flagged 3+ times in Quality Assurance reviews).

#### 4.2 Core Curriculum Requirements
The training program must cover, at a minimum, the following five competency areas:

**4.2.1 Module 1: The "Why" and The User Experience**
*   Understanding the Study GRC commitment to inclusivity.
*   Overview of disability types (Visual, Auditory, Motor, Cognitive).
*   Demonstration of how Assistive Technology (Screen Readers) interacts with documents.
*   Legal implications (ADA, Section 508).

**4.2.2 Module 2: Text and Structure (The Skeleton)**
*   **Headings:** Correct use of H1-H6 hierarchies; avoiding skipping levels.
*   **Lists:** Using true bullet/number formatting vs. manual typing.
*   **Links:** Writing descriptive link text (avoiding "Click Here" or raw URLs).
*   **Plain Language:** Writing for cognitive accessibility (aiming for Grade 8 reading level where possible).

**4.2.3 Module 3: Visual Accessibility**
*   **Alt Text:** How to write effective descriptions for informational images vs. marking decorative images as "null."
*   **Color Contrast:** Ensuring a minimum 4.5:1 ratio for normal text and 3:1 for large text/graphics (WCAG 1.4.3).
*   **Color Reliance:** Ensuring color is not the *only* means of conveying information (e.g., "required fields are red").

**4.2.4 Module 4: Multimedia and Interaction**
*   **Video:** Requirement for synchronized Closed Captions (CC) and Audio Descriptions where necessary.
*   **Audio:** Requirement for full text transcripts for podcasts or audio-only files.
*   **Flashing Content:** Prohibition of content that flashes more than 3 times per second (seizure prevention).

**4.2.5 Module 5: Document Specifics (PDF & Slides)**
*   **Slide Decks:** Reading order verification, unique slide titles.
*   **PDFs:** Tagging structures, setting document language, and ensuring export from source files (Word/PPT) preserves accessibility tags.
*   **Social Media:** Usage of "CamelCase" hashtags (e.g., #StudyGRC vs #studygrc) and image descriptions in platform settings.

#### 4.3 Assessment and Certification
*   Participants must pass a final assessment with a score of **80% or higher**.
*   The assessment must include a practical component (e.g., "Fix this inaccessible slide").
*   Upon passing, the user is issued a "Certified Content Creator" badge within the LMS, unlocking publishing permissions.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Owns the training curriculum; ensures content remains current with WCAG updates; reviews training effectiveness annually. |
| **Content Creators** | Complete mandatory training within required timeframes; apply principles to all work product. |
| **HR / Volunteer Coordinator** | Assigns training during onboarding; tracks completion rates; restricts system access for non-compliant users. |
| **Digital Accessibility Lead** | Serves as the Subject Matter Expert (SME) for training questions; conducts spot-checks on creator output. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this standard may result in the revocation of publishing privileges.
*   **First Violation:** Warning and requirement to re-take specific training modules.
*   **Repeated Violations:** Removal of direct publishing access (content must be proxied through a QA reviewer) and potential disciplinary action up to and including termination of volunteer assignment or employment.
