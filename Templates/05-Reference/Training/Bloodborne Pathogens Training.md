### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the mandatory training requirements regarding Bloodborne Pathogens (BBP) for Study GRC Inc. personnel. This program is designed to eliminate or minimize employee and volunteer occupational exposure to blood and other potentially infectious materials (OPIM) in accordance with **OSHA Standard 29 CFR 1910.1030**.

This training ensures that all designated personnel understand the health hazards associated with bloodborne pathogens, the protective measures available, and the procedures to follow in the event of an exposure incident.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, contractors, and volunteers who have been identified as having **Occupational Exposure**.

*   **In-Scope Personnel:**
    *   Designated First Aid Responders at physical events (conferences, workshops).
    *   Event Safety Coordinators and Security personnel.
    *   Youth Program Supervisors responsible for minor safety.
    *   Any staff member assigned duties where contact with blood or OPIM is "reasonably anticipated."
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Remote-only employees with no physical interaction duties.
    *   General administrative volunteers at events (e.g., registration desk) unless designated as emergency responders.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bloodborne Pathogens (BBP)** | Pathogenic microorganisms that are present in human blood and can cause disease in humans. These pathogens include, but are not limited to, Hepatitis B virus (HBV), Hepatitis C virus (HCV), and Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). |
| **Occupational Exposure** | Reasonably anticipated skin, eye, mucous membrane, or parenteral contact with blood or OPIM that may result from the performance of an employee's duties. |
| **OPIM** | Other Potentially Infectious Materials (e.g., bodily fluids containing visible blood, saliva in dental procedures, etc.). |
| **Universal Precautions** | An approach to infection control wherein all human blood and certain human body fluids are treated as if known to be infectious for HIV, HBV, and other bloodborne pathogens. |
| **Exposure Incident** | A specific eye, mouth, other mucous membrane, non-intact skin, or parenteral contact with blood or OPIM that results from the performance of an employee's duties. |

### 4. Training Program Requirements

In compliance with **29 CFR 1910.1030(g)(2)**, Study GRC Inc. mandates the following training protocols.

#### 4.1 Training Frequency and Timing
Training must be provided to all personnel with occupational exposure:
1.  **At the time of initial assignment** to tasks where occupational exposure may take place (prior to the start of the event or duty).
2.  **At least annually** thereafter (within one year of their previous training).
3.  **Immediately** when changes such as modification of tasks or procedures affect the employee's occupational exposure (additional training limited to the new exposures created).

#### 4.2 Mandatory Training Content
The training program must contain, at a minimum, the following elements:

1.  **Regulatory Access:** An explanation of the OSHA Bloodborne Pathogen Standard (29 CFR 1910.1030) and instructions on how to access the text of the standard.
2.  **Epidemiology:** A general explanation of the epidemiology and symptoms of bloodborne diseases (specifically HIV, HBV, and HCV).
3.  **Transmission:** An explanation of the modes of transmission of bloodborne pathogens.
4.  **Exposure Control Plan (ECP):** An explanation of Study GRC Inc.’s ECP and the method by which the employee can obtain a copy of the written plan.
5.  **Risk Recognition:** An explanation of the appropriate methods for recognizing tasks and other activities that may involve exposure to blood and OPIM.
6.  **Control Measures:** An explanation of the use and limitations of methods that will prevent or reduce exposure, including appropriate engineering controls, work practices, and personal protective equipment (PPE).
7.  **PPE Usage:** Information on the types, proper use, location, removal, handling, decontamination, and disposal of PPE (e.g., gloves, eye protection, resuscitation masks).
8.  **Hepatitis B Vaccine:** Information on the Hepatitis B vaccine, including its efficacy, safety, method of administration, the benefits of being vaccinated, and that the vaccine will be offered free of charge to designated employees.
9.  **Emergency Procedures:** An explanation of the procedure to follow if an emergency involving blood or OPIM occurs (e.g., spill cleanup protocols).
10. **Exposure Incident Protocol:** An explanation of the procedure to follow if an exposure incident occurs, including the method of reporting the incident and the medical follow-up that will be made available.
11. **Signage:** An explanation of the signs and labels and/or color coding required by the standard (Biohazard labels).
12. **Interactive Q&A:** An opportunity for interactive questions and answers with the person conducting the training session.

#### 4.3 Training Format and Delivery
*   **Format:** Training may be conducted via online Learning Management Systems (LMS) or in-person sessions.
*   **Interactivity:** If training is computer-based, provisions must be made for trainees to ask questions and receive answers from a qualified trainer (e.g., via live chat, scheduled call, or in-person briefing) to satisfy the interactive requirement of the OSHA standard.
*   **Language:** Training materials must be appropriate in content and vocabulary to the educational level, literacy, and language of employees.

#### 4.4 Recordkeeping
In accordance with **29 CFR 1910.1030(h)(2)**, training records must be maintained for **three (3) years** from the date on which the training occurred. Records must include:
*   The dates of the training sessions.
*   The contents or a summary of the training sessions.
*   The names and qualifications of persons conducting the training.
*   The names and job titles of all persons attending the training.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensure adequate resources are allocated for training materials, PPE, and Hepatitis B vaccinations. |
| **Event Safety Coordinator** | Develop or source compliant training content; schedule training sessions; maintain training records; ensure the Exposure Control Plan is up to date. |
| **Designated Responders** | Complete all mandatory training prior to assignment; adhere to Universal Precautions; report any exposure incidents immediately. |
| **HR / Operations** | Verify training completion before deploying staff to high-risk roles at events. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to complete this mandatory training will result in the immediate disqualification of the individual from serving in any role with potential occupational exposure (e.g., First Aid Responder).

Violations of safety protocols learned in training (e.g., failure to use PPE, failure to report exposure) may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, in accordance with the **Employee Handbook** and **Volunteer Code of Conduct**.
