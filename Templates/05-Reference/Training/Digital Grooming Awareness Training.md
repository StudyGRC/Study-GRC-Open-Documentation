### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the mandatory curriculum, learning objectives, and certification requirements for **Digital Grooming Awareness Training** at Study GRC Inc. As a remote-first organization with a K-12 focus, the risk of digital grooming within our open-source community and educational platforms is a critical threat vector. This standard ensures all personnel are equipped to identify, prevent, and report the behavioral patterns associated with the grooming of minors, satisfying requirements under the Texas Family Code and federal youth protection standards.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all individuals acting on behalf of Study GRC Inc. who have, or may have, access to minors (individuals under 18) or data regarding minors.
*   **In Scope:** Board of Directors, Operational Officers, Employees, Contractors, Volunteers, Community Moderators, and Mentors.
*   **Exclusions:** Third-party vendors with no access to community platforms (e.g., Discord, LMS) or constituent data.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Digital Grooming** | The predatory act of building an emotional connection with a minor via online platforms to gain their trust for the purpose of sexual abuse, exploitation, or trafficking. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The mandate that an adult must never be alone with a minor. In a digital context, this means no private, one-on-one Direct Messages (DMs) or unmonitored voice/video channels. |
| **Boundary Violation** | Any behavior that infringes on the physical, emotional, or psychological space of a minor, or that blurs the professional line between a Study GRC representative and a beneficiary. |
| **Targeting** | The initial phase of grooming where a predator identifies a vulnerable child based on observed emotional needs, isolation, or lack of supervision. |
| **Desensitization** | The gradual process of normalizing inappropriate topics, language, or requests to lower a minor's inhibitions. |

### 4. Training Program Standards

#### 4.1 Mandatory Completion
All personnel defined in the Scope must complete this training:
1.  **Initial Training:** Must be completed and passed (80% score or higher) **prior** to gaining access to any Study GRC Inc. communication platforms (e.g., Discord, Slack, LMS) or commencing work.
2.  **Annual Refresher:** Must be retaken annually to remain in Good Standing.

#### 4.2 Module 1: Understanding the Grooming Lifecycle
Training materials must explicitly cover the six stages of the grooming cycle in a digital context:
1.  **Target Selection:** Identifying minors who appear lonely, seek validation, or express frustration with parents/guardians in public chats.
2.  **Trust Formation:** The predator positions themselves as a "mentor," "ally," or "special friend," often using shared interests (e.g., GRC topics, gaming, coding) to build rapport.
3.  **Fulfilling Needs:** Providing excessive praise, in-game currency, skins, or "exclusive" educational resources to create indebtedness.
4.  **Isolation:** Creating an "us vs. them" dynamic; encouraging the minor to hide communications from parents or other moderators (e.g., "They wouldn't understand us").
5.  **Sexualization:** Gradually introducing sexual content, innuendo, or requests for images/video.
6.  **Maintenance/Control:** Using blackmail (sextortion) or threats of exposure to maintain silence and compliance.

#### 4.3 Module 2: Digital Red Flags and Indicators
Personnel must be trained to recognize specific behavioral indicators of grooming within Study GRC Inc. platforms:
*   **Platform Migration:** Requests to move conversation from monitored channels (Study GRC Discord) to unmonitored apps (Snapchat, WhatsApp, private DMs).
*   **Special Status:** A volunteer showing favoritism to a specific minor (e.g., giving them unearned moderator privileges or "secret" access).
*   **Excessive Gifting:** Unexplained gifts, subscriptions, or financial assistance offered to a minor.
*   **Temporal Anomalies:** Communication occurring late at night or during school hours.
*   **Secrecy:** Use of phrases like "Don't tell anyone," "This is our secret," or "Delete this message after reading."
*   **Emotional Volatility:** A minor exhibiting sudden withdrawal, aggression, or anxiety when away from their device.

#### 4.4 Module 3: Prevention Protocols
Training must reinforce Study GRC Inc. operational rules designed to prevent grooming:
*   **The "No DM" Rule:** Strict prohibition of 1-on-1 Direct Messages between adults and minors. All communication must occur in public channels or group threads with at least two adults present (Two-Deep Leadership).
*   **Professional Distance:** Prohibition of discussing personal sexual history, drug use, or adult problems with minors.
*   **Camera Policy:** In video calls, backgrounds must be neutral. Adults must be fully clothed. Recording requires explicit parental consent.

#### 4.5 Module 4: Reporting and Intervention
Training must provide clear instructions on the legal and organizational duty to report:
*   **Immediate Action:** If a grooming attempt is suspected, the observer must immediately screenshot the evidence and report it to the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)**.
*   **Mandatory Reporting (Texas):** Pursuant to Texas Family Code Chapter 261, any person having cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect **must** report immediately to the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (1-800-252-5400) or local law enforcement.
*   **Non-Retaliation:** Reiteration of the Whistleblower Protection Policy (GOV-POL-009) protecting reporters acting in good faith.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Develops curriculum content; maintains training records; serves as the primary internal point of contact for grooming reports. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Configures platform monitoring tools (e.g., keyword flagging in Discord) to detect grooming language; audits access logs. |
| **HR / Volunteer Coordinator** | Ensures no personnel are onboarded or granted system access without verified training completion. |
| **All Personnel** | Completes training annually; adheres to the "No DM" rule; reports suspected grooming behavior immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to complete this training within the designated timeframe will result in immediate revocation of system access and suspension of duties.

Any violation of the prevention protocols outlined in this training (e.g., engaging in private DMs with a minor, failing to report suspected grooming) will result in:
1.  **Immediate Termination:** Of employment or volunteer status.
2.  **Permanent Ban:** From the Study GRC community ecosystem.
3.  **Legal Action:** Referral to law enforcement agencies and the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC).
