### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a mandatory training framework regarding **2 CFR Part 200**, *Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards* (commonly known as "Uniform Guidance").

As a 501(c)(3) organization eligible for and receiving federal grant funding, Study GRC Inc. must maintain effective internal controls as required by **2 CFR § 200.303**. This program ensures that all Directors, Officers, and employees involved in the administration of federal awards possess the necessary knowledge to manage funds compliantly, thereby mitigating the risk of disallowed costs, audit findings, and reputational damage.

### 2. Scope
This program applies to all personnel who interact with federal grant funds or data.

*   **Applies to:**
    *   **Board of Directors:** Regarding fiduciary oversight and Single Audit review.
    *   **Operational Officers:** Specifically the Executive Director, CFO, and VP of Education & Training.
    *   **Program Staff:** Any employee or contractor with authority to initiate spending or approve expenses charged to a federal award.
    *   **Grant Administrators:** Personnel responsible for reporting, time and effort tracking, and procurement.

*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Volunteers and Community Participants (as defined in **Bylaws Article II**) who do not have financial authority or access to grant management systems.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Uniform Guidance (UG)** | The set of regulations (2 CFR Part 200) consolidating federal grant rules, cost principles, and audit requirements. |
| **Internal Controls** | Processes designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives in effectiveness/efficiency of operations, reliability of reporting, and compliance with laws (2 CFR § 200.61). |
| **Allowable Cost** | A cost that is necessary, reasonable, allocable, and consistently treated, per 2 CFR § 200.403. |
| **Single Audit** | An audit required for non-federal entities that expend $750,000 or more in federal awards during a fiscal year (2 CFR § 200.501). |
| **Time and Effort Reporting** | The process of certifying that salaries charged to federal awards reflect the actual work performed (2 CFR § 200.430). |

### 4. Program Requirements

#### 4.1 Training Frequency and Tiers
To ensure compliance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** and federal statutes, training is categorized by role:

*   **Tier 1: General Awareness (Board of Directors)**
    *   **Frequency:** Upon onboarding and annually thereafter.
    *   **Focus:** High-level overview of fiduciary liabilities, Single Audit requirements, and the "Tone at the Top" regarding compliance.
*   **Tier 2: Advanced Practitioner (Officers, Finance, Program Managers)**
    *   **Frequency:** Upon hire (within 30 days) and annually thereafter.
    *   **Focus:** Deep dive into Cost Principles (Subpart E), Procurement (Subpart D), and Reporting.

#### 4.2 Curriculum Standards
The training content must be updated annually to reflect changes in the Compliance Supplement. The curriculum must cover, at a minimum:

**4.2.1 Cost Principles (Subpart E)**
*   Determining Allowability: Necessary, Reasonable, and Allocable.
*   Selected Items of Cost: Specifically addressing Travel, Equipment, and Compensation.
*   **Prohibited Costs:** Alcohol, lobbying, bad debts, and entertainment.

**4.2.2 Procurement Standards (2 CFR § 200.317 - 200.327)**
*   Micro-purchases vs. Simplified Acquisition vs. Competitive Proposals.
*   Conflict of Interest in procurement (referencing **Bylaws Article IX**).
*   Domestic preferences and MWBE inclusion.

**4.2.3 Compensation and Personnel Services (2 CFR § 200.430)**
*   Time and Effort reporting requirements.
*   Handling "Remote-First" workforce documentation and incidental personal use of equipment.

**4.2.4 Administrative Requirements**
*   Financial Management Systems standards.
*   Record Retention (3 years from submission of final expenditure report).
*   Subrecipient Monitoring (if applicable).

#### 4.3 Delivery Methods
Given the Organization's remote-first structure, training shall be delivered via:
1.  **Asynchronous Learning Management System (LMS):** Modules covering the core curriculum.
2.  **Live Webinars:** Annual updates provided by the CFO or external legal counsel.
3.  **External Certification:** Key financial staff (e.g., CFO) are encouraged to obtain external grants management certification (e.g., NGMA), funded via the Professional Development Policy.

#### 4.4 Competency Assessment
Mere attendance is insufficient.
*   **Testing:** All Tier 2 participants must complete a competency assessment (quiz) at the conclusion of the training.
*   **Passing Score:** A minimum score of **80%** is required.
*   **Remediation:** Staff failing to achieve a passing score must retake the training and assessment within 7 days.

#### 4.5 Documentation and Recordkeeping
In accordance with **Bylaws Section 8.1 (Maintenance of Records)**, the Organization must maintain:
*   Dates of training sessions.
*   Curriculum/materials used.
*   Attendance logs (digital signatures/LMS logs).
*   Assessment scores.
*   These records must be retained for a minimum of three (3) years and made available to auditors and pass-through entities upon request.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Allocates budget for training resources; reviews annual compliance reports; ensures the "Tone at the Top" supports strict adherence to UG. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Mandates staff attendance; ensures operational workflows align with training concepts; enforces disciplinary action for non-compliance. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | **Program Owner.** Develops/selects curriculum; monitors regulatory changes; tracks completion rates; maintains training records for audit. |
| **Program Managers** | Complete Tier 2 training; apply principles to daily spending and grant activities; ensure their direct reports (if any) are trained. |
| **All Staff (Grant Funded)** | Participate in required training; pass assessments; report suspected non-compliance via the Whistleblower Policy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this training program constitutes a significant risk to the Organization's tax-exempt status and financial viability.

*   **Access Revocation:** Staff members who do not complete the required training within the designated timeframe will have their financial signature authority and access to procurement systems suspended immediately.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Continued refusal to participate or repeated failure to apply Uniform Guidance principles in daily work may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Cost Liability:** Any employee found to have willfully ignored training protocols, resulting in disallowed costs that the Organization must repay, may be subject to legal action for recovery of funds.
