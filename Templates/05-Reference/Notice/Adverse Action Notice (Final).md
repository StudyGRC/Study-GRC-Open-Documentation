### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the mandatory legal standard and procedure for issuing a **Final Adverse Action Notice** at Study GRC Inc. This process ensures compliance with the **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) § 615(a)** when the Organization denies employment, volunteer status, or contracting privileges based, in whole or in part, on information contained in a consumer report (background check).

Strict adherence to this standard mitigates the risk of class-action litigation and ensures fair, transparent treatment of all candidates.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all recruitment and vetting processes for:
*   **Employees:** Full-time, part-time, and temporary staff.
*   **Volunteers:** Including Board Directors, Advisory Board members, and Community Moderators.
*   **Contractors:** Independent contractors subject to background screening.

**Exclusions:** This standard does not apply to decisions made solely on information obtained outside of a Consumer Reporting Agency (e.g., a direct reference check or interview performance), provided no credit or criminal history report was utilized.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Adverse Action** | A denial of employment or any other decision for employment purposes that adversely affects any current or prospective employee or volunteer. |
| **CRA** | **Consumer Reporting Agency**. The third-party vendor (e.g., Checkr, Sterling, GoodHire) used by Study GRC Inc. to conduct background checks. |
| **FCRA** | The **Fair Credit Reporting Act** (15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.), a federal law regulating the collection of consumers' credit information and access to their credit reports. |
| **Final Notice** | The formal notification sent to a candidate *after* the Pre-Adverse Action waiting period, confirming the decision to deny the position. |
| **Review Window** | The period (minimum 5 business days) between the Pre-Adverse Action Notice and the Final Adverse Action Notice. |

### 4. Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 Prerequisites for Issuance
A Final Adverse Action Notice may **only** be issued if:
1.  A **Pre-Adverse Action Notice** (HR-STD-006) was previously sent to the candidate.
2.  A minimum of **five (5) business days** has elapsed since the Pre-Adverse Action Notice was delivered.
3.  The candidate has either not responded, or their dispute/explanation was reviewed by the Adjudication Committee and deemed insufficient to clear the concern according to the *Background Check Adjudication Matrix*.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Content (FCRA § 615(a))
To satisfy federal law, the Final Adverse Action Notice **must** contain the following five elements. Use of the template in **Appendix A** is mandatory to ensure these elements are present:
1.  **Notice of Action:** A statement that the Organization has taken adverse action (denied the application) based on the report.
2.  **CRA Contact Information:** The name, address, and toll-free telephone number of the CRA that provided the report.
3.  **No Decision Role:** A statement that the CRA **did not** make the decision to take the adverse action and is unable to provide the candidate the specific reasons why the adverse action was taken.
4.  **Right to Copy:** A notice of the consumer's right to obtain a free copy of their consumer report from the CRA within 60 days.
5.  **Right to Dispute:** A notice of the consumer's right to dispute the accuracy or completeness of any information in the consumer report with the CRA.

#### 4.3 Delivery Method
*   **Electronic:** If the candidate consented to electronic communications (via the *Background Check Consent Form*), the notice may be sent via secure email or the CRA’s secure portal.
*   **Physical:** If no electronic consent exists, the notice must be sent via USPS First Class Mail.
*   **Documentation:** The date and method of delivery must be logged in the *Applicant Tracking Record*.

#### 4.4 Record Retention
In accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy* and *EEOC* regulations, a copy of the Final Adverse Action Notice and the associated background report must be retained in a secure, confidential file for a minimum of **five (5) years** following the decision.

#### 4.5 Revocation of Access
Upon issuance of the Final Adverse Action Notice:
*   Any provisional access to Study GRC Inc. systems (Slack, Google Workspace, GitHub) must be immediately revoked by the CISO or IT Administrator.
*   If the individual was a candidate for the Board of Directors, the Nominating Committee must be notified immediately to remove the candidate from the ballot, pursuant to **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.3** (Qualifications).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **HR / Ops Manager** | Verifies the 5-day waiting period has passed and drafts the Final Notice using the approved template. |
| **Executive Director** | Reviews and approves Final Notices for senior leadership or Board candidate positions. |
| **Adjudication Committee** | Provides the final "No Hire" decision prior to the issuance of this notice. |
| **IT Administrator** | Revokes system access immediately upon notification of Final Adverse Action. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly adhere to the Adverse Action process constitutes a violation of federal law.
*   **Legal Risk:** Violations of FCRA § 615(a) can result in statutory damages of $100 to $1,000 *per violation*, plus punitive damages and attorney's fees.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Employees or volunteers found to have bypassed this procedure or finalized a rejection without sending the required notice will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

### Appendix A: Final Adverse Action Notice Template

**[Date]**

**[Candidate Name]**
**[Candidate Address]**
**[Candidate Email]**

**RE: Notice of Adverse Action (Employment/Volunteer Decision)**

Dear **[Candidate Name]**,

Study GRC Inc. thanks you for your application for the position of **[Position Title]**.

We are writing to inform you that we have decided not to offer you employment/volunteer status at this time. This decision was influenced, in whole or in part, by information contained in a consumer report (background check) previously provided to you.

After the required waiting period following our previous "Pre-Adverse Action Notice," and after reviewing any information you may have provided during that time, we have finalized our decision.

In accordance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), we are providing you with the following information:

1.  **Source of Report:** The consumer report was obtained from the following Consumer Reporting Agency (CRA):
    *   **Agency Name:** [Name of Background Check Vendor, e.g., Checkr, Inc.]
    *   **Address:** [Vendor Address]
    *   **Phone:** [Vendor Toll-Free Phone Number]
    *   **Website:** [Vendor Website]

2.  **Decision Maker:** Please note that the agency listed above provided the report but **did not** make the decision to take this adverse action and is unable to provide you with the specific reasons why the adverse action was taken.

3.  **Your Rights:** Under the FCRA, you have the right to:
    *   Obtain a free copy of your consumer report from the agency listed above, provided you request it within 60 days of receiving this notice.
    *   Dispute the accuracy or completeness of any information contained in the report directly with the consumer reporting agency.

We appreciate your interest in Study GRC Inc. and wish you the best in your future endeavors.

Sincerely,

**[Name of Authorized Signer]**
**[Title, e.g., VP of Operations]**
Study GRC Inc.
