### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Member Data Privacy Notice ("Notice") is to provide transparency regarding how Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") collects, uses, stores, and protects the Personal Data of its members. This Notice is designed to satisfy the transparency requirements of the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), ensuring that our community understands their rights and our obligations regarding their privacy.

### 2. Scope
This Notice applies to all classes of membership as defined in **Bylaws Article II** (Community Participants, Supporting Members, and Honorary Members). It covers Personal Data collected through our website, event registration platforms, learning management systems, and direct communication channels.

**Exclusions:**
*   Employee and contractor human resources records (covered under *HR-POL-005 Employee Privacy Policy*).
*   Anonymized or aggregated data that cannot reasonably identify a specific individual.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Personal Data** | Any information that is linked or reasonably linkable to an identified or identifiable individual. |
| **Sensitive Data** | Personal Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, mental or physical health diagnosis, sexuality, or citizenship/immigration status; or genetic/biometric data. |
| **Processing** | Any operation performed on Personal Data, such as collection, storage, disclosure, analysis, or deletion. |
| **Member** | Any individual falling into the classes defined in **Bylaws Section 2.1** (Community, Supporting, or Honorary). |
| **Sale of Data** | The exchange of Personal Data for monetary or other valuable consideration. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Data We Collect
To fulfill our non-profit mission and manage membership governance, we collect the following categories of data:
*   **Identity Data:** Name, username, and government-issued ID (only if required for specific verification per **Bylaws Section 2.6**).
*   **Contact Data:** Email address, mailing address (for Supporting Members), and phone number.
*   **Transaction Data:** Details of membership dues payments or donations (processed via secure third-party payment processors; we do not store raw credit card numbers).
*   **Profile Data:** Interests, preferences, feedback, and survey responses used to tailor educational content.
*   **Technical Data:** IP addresses, login data, browser type, and usage data collected via cookies to secure our digital infrastructure.

#### 4.2. How We Use Your Data
We process Personal Data solely for the following purposes:
*   **Membership Administration:** Managing voting rights, quorum verification, and maintaining the registry of members as required by **Bylaws Section 2.6**.
*   **Service Delivery:** Providing access to educational resources, the online community, and events.
*   **Communication:** Sending notices regarding Annual Meetings (**Bylaws Section 2.5**), elections, and organizational updates.
*   **Legal Compliance:** Fulfilling obligations under the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and IRS regulations.
*   **Security:** Detecting and preventing fraud, unauthorized access, or violations of our Code of Conduct.

#### 4.3. Data Sharing and Disclosure
**We do not sell your Personal Data.**
We may share data only in the following circumstances:
*   **Service Providers:** With trusted third-party vendors (e.g., cloud hosting, email delivery, payment processors) who are contractually bound to protect your data.
*   **Legal Requirements:** To comply with a subpoena, court order, or applicable law (e.g., IRS reporting).
*   **Member Inspection:** As mandated by **Bylaws Section 2.6** and TBOC § 22.158, a list of voting members may be inspected by other members for proper purposes. This list is limited to names, email addresses, and voting entitlement.

#### 4.4. Your Privacy Rights
In alignment with the TDPSA and CCPA, members have the following rights:
*   **Right to Know/Access:** You may request a copy of the specific Personal Data we have collected about you.
*   **Right to Correction:** You may request that we correct inaccurate Personal Data.
*   **Right to Deletion:** You may request that we delete your Personal Data, subject to our legal retention obligations (e.g., financial records required by the IRS).
*   **Right to Portability:** You may request a copy of your data in a portable, machine-readable format.
*   **Right to Non-Discrimination:** We will not deny services or charge different rates for exercising your privacy rights.

To exercise these rights, submit a verifiable request to `privacy@studygrc.org`.

#### 4.5. Data Security
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX**, we implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical security measures to protect Personal Data. This includes encryption in transit and at rest, access controls based on the principle of least privilege, and regular security assessments managed by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).

#### 4.6. Children's Privacy
Study GRC Inc. provides K-12 educational resources. However, we do not knowingly collect Personal Data from children under the age of 13 without verifiable parental consent, in compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). If we learn we have collected data from a child under 13 without consent, we will delete it immediately.

#### 4.7. International Data Transfers
As a remote-first organization, Personal Data may be stored on servers located outside your state or country of residence. By using our services, you acknowledge that your data may be transferred to, and processed in, the United States.

#### 4.8. Changes to This Notice
We reserve the right to amend this Notice at any time. Material changes will be communicated to members via email or a prominent notice on our website at least 30 days prior to the effective date of the change.

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Notice by any employee, volunteer, or contractor may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action.

If a Member believes their privacy rights have been violated, they may file a complaint with the CISO at `privacy@studygrc.org` or follow the *Whistleblower Protection Policy* for anonymous reporting.
