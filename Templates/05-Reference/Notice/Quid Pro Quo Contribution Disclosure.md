### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Notice is to establish the mandatory disclosure requirements for "Quid Pro Quo" contributions in compliance with **Internal Revenue Code (IRC) §6115**. This document ensures that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") provides accurate, written disclosure statements to donors who make payments partly as a contribution and partly in consideration for goods or services provided by the Organization. This standard supports the transparency mandates outlined in **Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all fundraising activities, special events, membership drives, and solicitation campaigns conducted by Study GRC Inc.

*   **Applies to:** All Officers, Directors, fundraising staff, event coordinators, and the Finance Department.
*   **Specific Threshold:** Applies specifically to any solicitation or receipt of a contribution exceeding **$75.00** where the donor receives goods or services in exchange.
*   **Exclusions:** Entirely tax-deductible contributions where no goods or services are provided, or contributions meeting the IRS "Token Exception" criteria.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Quid Pro Quo Contribution** | A payment made to a charity by a donor partly as a contribution and partly for goods or services provided to the donor by the charity. |
| **Good Faith Estimate** | An estimate of the Fair Market Value (FMV) of goods or services, based on the cost to purchase similar items or services in the open market. |
| **Fair Market Value (FMV)** | The price at which property would change hands between a willing buyer and a willing seller, neither being under any compulsion to buy or sell and both having reasonable knowledge of relevant facts. |
| **Token Exception** | Low-cost articles (e.g., bookmarks, stickers, mugs) provided to donors where the cost is insubstantial, as defined annually by IRS inflation adjustments (Revenue Procedures). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Mandatory Disclosure Threshold
Pursuant to IRC §6115, Study GRC Inc. **must** provide a written disclosure statement to any donor who makes a payment exceeding **$75.00** if the payment is partly a contribution and partly for goods and services provided by the Organization.

#### 4.2 Required Content of Disclosure
The written disclosure must contain the following two elements:
1.  **Deductibility Statement:** A statement informing the donor that the amount of the contribution that is deductible for federal income tax purposes is limited to the excess of the amount of any money (and the value of any property other than money) contributed by the donor over the value of the goods or services provided by the Organization.
2.  **Valuation:** A **Good Faith Estimate** of the value of such goods or services.

#### 4.3 Timing and Format of Disclosure
*   **Timing:** The disclosure must be provided either at the time of solicitation (e.g., on the ticket order form) or upon receipt of the contribution (e.g., in the acknowledgment letter).
*   **Visibility:** The disclosure must be reasonably likely to come to the attention of the donor. It cannot be buried in fine print. It must be conspicuous.

#### 4.4 Valuation Methodology
The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or Treasurer is responsible for determining the Good Faith Estimate of goods/services provided.
*   **Commercial Equivalents:** If the Organization provides a gala dinner, the FMV is the cost of a comparable dinner at a commercial restaurant, not the actual catering cost to the non-profit.
*   **Unique Items:** If the item is unique (e.g., a private training session with a GRC expert), the FMV is estimated by reference to the commercial rate of similar consulting services.

#### 4.5 Exceptions to Disclosure
Disclosure is **not** required in the following specific circumstances:
1.  **Token Exception:** If the goods/services provided have insubstantial value (e.g., branded swag, stickers) as defined by current IRS Revenue Procedures (e.g., the "low cost article" limit).
2.  **Membership Benefits:** If the payment is for a membership (e.g., "Supporting Member" per Bylaws Article II) and the benefits are limited to:
    *   Annual recurring rights (voting rights).
    *   Admission to members-only events where the cost per person is within the "low cost" limits.
    *   Discounts on goods/services (e.g., training) that are available to the public.
3.  **Intangible Religious Benefits:** (Not applicable to Study GRC Inc. as a secular educational organization).

#### 4.6 Sample Disclosure Language
> *"Thank you for your contribution of $150.00 to Study GRC Inc. In exchange for your contribution, you received a dinner with an estimated fair market value of $60.00. Therefore, for federal income tax purposes, your charitable contribution deduction is limited to $90.00."*

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fundraising / Event Lead** | Identifies any goods/services provided in exchange for donations and notifies Finance prior to solicitation. Ensures disclosure language is on tickets/invitations. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Calculates and documents the Good Faith Estimate (FMV) for all goods/services. Approves the final text of disclosure statements. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides oversight to ensure FMV calculations are reasonable and defensible during the annual audit. |
| **Donor Relations Manager** | Ensures all acknowledgment letters for Quid Pro Quo contributions >$75 contain the required legal text before mailing/emailing. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with IRC §6115 requirements results in a penalty of **$10 per contribution**, up to a maximum of **$5,000 per fundraising event or mailing**.

Furthermore, failure to provide accurate disclosures undermines donor trust and violates the Organization's commitment to transparency under **Bylaws Article VIII**. Intentional omission of these disclosures may result in disciplinary action against responsible staff, up to and including termination.
