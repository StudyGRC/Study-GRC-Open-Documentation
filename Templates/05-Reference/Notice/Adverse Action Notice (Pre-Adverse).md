### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the mandatory protocol for issuing a **Pre-Adverse Action Notice** in compliance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA). This procedure ensures that Study GRC Inc. provides candidates (employees and volunteers) with due process and a reasonable opportunity to dispute inaccurate information in their background check report *before* a final decision is made to deny employment or volunteer service.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all background checks conducted by Study GRC Inc. for:
*   Prospective and current employees.
*   Prospective and current volunteers (specifically those in Youth-Serving roles).
*   Independent contractors (where a consumer report is obtained).

**Exclusions:** This procedure does not apply to decisions based solely on internal policy violations unrelated to a Consumer Report (e.g., resume fraud discovered via reference checks not conducted by a CRA).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Adverse Action** | A denial of employment, volunteer role, or other benefit based in whole or in part on information contained in a Consumer Report. |
| **Pre-Adverse Action** | A preliminary notice sent to a candidate indicating that the Organization is *considering* taking adverse action based on a background check, but a final decision has not yet been made. |
| **Consumer Reporting Agency (CRA)** | The third-party vendor (e.g., Checkr, Sterling, GoodHire) that compiles and provides the background check report. |
| **Individualized Assessment** | A review process required by the EEOC to determine if a criminal record is job-related and consistent with business necessity, rather than applying a blanket ban. |
| **Summary of Rights** | A document published by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) titled *"A Summary of Your Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act."* |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Trigger for Pre-Adverse Action
If a background check returns information that may disqualify a candidate based on the **Background Check Adjudication Matrix (HR-STD-028)** or **Youth Protection Policy (YP-POL-001)**, the Reviewing Officer must first conduct an **Individualized Assessment**.

If the assessment suggests that the candidate should be disqualified, the Organization **MUST** issue a Pre-Adverse Action Notice *before* making a final decision.

#### 4.2 Required Contents of the Notice
The Pre-Adverse Action package sent to the candidate must include three specific items:
1.  **The Pre-Adverse Action Letter:** Stating that a negative decision is being *considered* (see Template in Section 9).
2.  **A Copy of the Consumer Report:** The actual background check report used to make the preliminary assessment.
3.  **A Summary of Your Rights Under the FCRA:** The official CFPB document.

#### 4.3 Delivery Method
*   **Electronic:** If the candidate consented to electronic delivery (standard in our application process), the notice may be sent via the secure background check platform or encrypted email.
*   **Physical Mail:** If electronic consent was not obtained or delivery fails, the notice must be sent via USPS First Class Mail.

#### 4.4 The Waiting Period (The "Cure" Period)
Study GRC Inc. mandates a waiting period of **five (5) business days** starting from the date the notice is sent.
*   **Purpose:** This allows the candidate time to review the report and contact the CRA to dispute inaccuracies.
*   **Prohibition:** No final hiring decision or rejection notification may be communicated during this window.

#### 4.5 Handling Disputes
*   **If the Candidate Disputes:** If the candidate notifies the Organization or the CRA of a dispute within the 5-day window, the Organization will pause the hiring decision until the CRA completes its reinvestigation (usually within 30 days).
*   **If No Dispute:** If 5 business days pass without a dispute or new information, the Organization may proceed to **Final Adverse Action**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reviewing Officer (HR/Ops)** | Conducts individualized assessment; triggers the Pre-Adverse Action notice; ensures the 5-day waiting period is observed. |
| **Consumer Reporting Agency (CRA)** | Provides the background report; handles the actual investigation of disputes filed by the candidate. |
| **Executive Director** | Final approver for adverse actions involving senior leadership or high-risk overrides. |
| **Candidate** | Responsible for reviewing the report and initiating a dispute with the CRA if the information is inaccurate. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to strictly adhere to the Pre-Adverse Action process is a violation of federal law (FCRA).
*   **Legal Risk:** Class-action lawsuits for FCRA violations are common and costly. Statutory damages range from $100 to $1,000 *per violation*, plus punitive damages and attorney fees.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Staff members who bypass this step or reject a candidate immediately upon receiving a negative report without the waiting period will face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

### 8. Appendix: Pre-Adverse Action Notice Template

**[Date]**

**[Candidate Name]**
**[Candidate Address/Email]**

**RE: IMPORTANT - PENDING EMPLOYMENT/VOLUNTEER DECISION**

Dear **[Candidate Name]**,

You recently authorized **Study GRC Inc.** to obtain a consumer report (background check) for employment or volunteer purposes. We are writing to inform you that we have received the report and are currently reviewing it.

**We are considering denying your application based, in whole or in part, on the information contained in the enclosed report.**

**PLEASE NOTE: No final decision has been made at this time.**

We are providing this notice to give you an opportunity to review the report and dispute any information you believe is inaccurate or incomplete.

**Enclosed with this letter are:**
1.  A copy of the consumer report obtained from **[Name of CRA, e.g., Checkr/Sterling]**.
2.  A copy of the document *"A Summary of Your Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act."*

**Your Right to Dispute**
If you believe the information in the report is inaccurate or incomplete, you should contact the Consumer Reporting Agency immediately to file a dispute. Their contact information is listed below:

**[Name of CRA]**
**[Address of CRA]**
**[Phone Number of CRA]**
**[Website of CRA]**

If you file a dispute or wish to provide Study GRC Inc. with additional context regarding the records (such as evidence of rehabilitation or explanation of circumstances), please contact us at **[HR Email Address]** within **five (5) business days** of this notice.

If we do not hear from you or receive notice of a dispute within five business days, we will proceed with making a final decision based on the information currently available.

Sincerely,

**[Name]**
**[Title]**
Study GRC Inc.

*Enclosures: Consumer Report, A Summary of Your Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act*
