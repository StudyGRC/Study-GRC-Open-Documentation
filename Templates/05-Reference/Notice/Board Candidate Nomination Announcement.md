### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the mandatory standard and content requirements for the **Board Candidate Nomination Announcement**. This notice formally initiates the election cycle for Study GRC Inc. Its objective is to ensure compliance with **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5**, guarantee transparency in the selection of leadership, and solicit qualified candidates from the Voting Membership to serve on the Board of Directors.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to:
*   **The Board Secretary:** Responsible for issuing the notice.
*   **The Governance Committee:** Responsible for overseeing the nomination process.
*   **Voting Members:** The recipients of the notice and potential candidates.

**Exclusions:** This process does not apply to the appointment of Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director, CISO), which are Board-appointed roles per Bylaws Article V.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Voting Member** | A Supporting Member or Honorary Member in good standing as defined in Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1. |
| **Nomination Statement** | A mandatory submission by a candidate including their background, position statement, and conflict of interest disclosure (Bylaws Art. II, §2.5). |
| **Good Standing** | A member who is current on dues (if applicable) and not subject to any disciplinary suspension or expulsion. |
| **Record Date** | The date established by the Board for determining which members are eligible to receive notice and vote. |

### 4. Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 Timing and Delivery
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5**, the Nomination Announcement must be delivered to all Voting Members no later than **14 days** prior to the scheduled Annual Meeting.
*   **Delivery Method:** The notice must be sent via the primary email address on record for each Voting Member.
*   **Verification:** The Secretary must retain a record of the transmission (e.g., email log or campaign report) to prove compliance with notice requirements.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Content Requirements
The announcement must explicitly include the following elements to be considered valid:
1.  **Open Positions:** The number of Board seats available for election.
2.  **Term Duration:** Specification of the two (2) year staggered term (Bylaws Art. III, §3.2).
3.  **Eligibility Criteria:** Requirement that candidates must be Voting Members in good standing (Bylaws Art. III, §3.3).
4.  **Nomination Statement Requirements:** Instructions to submit a background summary, vision/position statement, and Conflict of Interest disclosure.
5.  **Presentation Requirement:** Notice that nominees must present (5 minutes) at the Annual Meeting (Bylaws Art. II, §2.5).
6.  **Deadlines:** The specific date and time by which nominations must be received.

#### 4.3 Official Announcement Template
The following text must be used as the basis for the announcement. Bracketed text `[...]` indicates fields that must be updated annually.

***

**SUBJECT:** OFFICIAL NOTICE: Call for Board of Directors Nominations – Study GRC Inc.

**TO:** Voting Members of Study GRC Inc.
**FROM:** The Board of Directors
**DATE:** [Date of Transmission]

**1. Call for Leadership**
In accordance with **Article II, Section 2.5** of the Study GRC Inc. Bylaws, the Board of Directors hereby opens the nomination period for the [Year] Board Election. We are seeking dedicated leaders to help steer our mission of fostering a community-driven GRC ecosystem and removing financial barriers to knowledge.

**2. Open Positions**
There are **[Number]** Director positions open for election during this cycle.
*   **Term:** Directors shall serve a two (2) year term commencing immediately upon certification of election results.
*   **Role:** Directors are responsible for the strategic governance, financial oversight, and legal compliance of the Organization.

**3. Eligibility**
To be eligible for nomination, a candidate must:
*   Be a **Voting Member** (Supporting or Honorary) in good standing.
*   Adhere to the Organization’s Professional Ethics and Community Guidelines.
*   Be willing to fulfill the duties of a Director, including attending monthly virtual meetings.

**4. How to Nominate (The "Nomination Statement")**
Self-nominations are encouraged. To submit a nomination, you must complete the **Candidate Application Form [Link]** by **[Date/Time - at least 14 days before Annual Meeting]**.

Your submission **MUST** include the following three (3) components per Bylaws Section 2.5:
1.  **Background:** A brief professional biography.
2.  **Position Statement:** A statement outlining your vision for Study GRC Inc.
3.  **Conflict of Interest Disclosure:** A full disclosure of any potential conflicts (financial, professional, or personal) that may intersect with the Organization’s interests.

**5. The Election Process**
*   **Presentations:** All nominees will be granted a five (5) minute window to present their qualifications and vision during the **Annual Meeting on [Date of Meeting]**.
*   **Q&A:** A Question and Answer session will follow the presentations.
*   **Voting:** Voting will **not** take place during the meeting. An electronic voting window will open immediately following the Annual Meeting and remain open for seven (7) days.

**6. Deadline**
Nominations close strictly at **[Time, Time Zone] on [Date]**. Late submissions cannot be accepted due to the statutory notice requirements for ballot preparation.

For questions regarding the duties of a Director or the election process, please contact the Governance Committee at [Email Address].

Sincerely,

[Name]
Secretary, Study GRC Inc.

***

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Prepares the notice using the template in Section 4.3; ensures distribution to the valid Voting Member list; archives proof of notice. |
| **Governance Committee** | Reviews submitted Nomination Statements for completeness and eligibility (Good Standing check); prepares the final ballot. |
| **Voting Member** | Reviews the notice; submits nominations if interested; participates in the election process. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to issue this notice within the timeframe mandated by **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5** (at least 14 days prior to the Annual Meeting) may render the subsequent election **voidable**.

If the notice is not sent on time, the Annual Meeting must be rescheduled to accommodate the required statutory notice period.
