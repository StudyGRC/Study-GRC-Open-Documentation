### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this disclosure is to provide transparency regarding how Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") shares, discloses, or grants access to Personal Information collected from children. This document is drafted in strict compliance with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and the "COPPA 2.0" proposed rule enhancements, specifically addressing the requirement to distinguish between consent for data *collection* and consent for data *disclosure*.

As a 501(c)(3) non-profit dedicated to open-source education (Bylaws Article I), the Organization does not monetize children's data. This notice clarifies the limited circumstances under which third-party service providers access data solely to support internal operations.

### 2. Scope
This notice applies to:
*   **Data Subjects:** Children under the age of 13 (and under 16 where applicable by local regulation) participating in Study GRC Inc. educational programs, community events, or online platforms.
*   **Data Types:** All Personal Information (PI) as defined by COPPA, including but not limited to names, usernames, persistent identifiers (cookies/IP addresses), and audio/video files.
*   **Recipients:** All external vendors, contractors, and service providers.

**Exclusions:** This notice does not apply to general voting members or adult participants, whose data is governed by the *Master Data Privacy Policy (Global)*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Child** | An individual under the age of 13, or under the age of 16 depending on specific program requirements and local laws. |
| **Service Provider** | An entity that processes data *on behalf* of the Organization for a specific internal operational purpose (e.g., web hosting) and is contractually prohibited from using the data for its own purposes. |
| **Third Party** | An entity that receives data and may use it for its own purposes or for purposes unrelated to the direct educational service. |
| **Support for Internal Operations** | Activities necessary to maintain the technical functioning of the Organization’s site or service, including authentication, security, and content delivery. |
| **Targeted Advertising** | Selecting and serving advertisements based on a user's behavior over time and across non-affiliated websites. |

### 4. Notice Statements

#### 4.1 The "No Sale" Commitment
Pursuant to Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection) and Article I (Nonprofit Purpose), Study GRC Inc. **does not sell** children's personal information to third parties. We do not share children's information with third parties for the purpose of cross-context behavioral advertising, marketing, or profiling.

#### 4.2 Distinction of Consent (COPPA 2.0 Compliance)
The Organization recognizes that a parent's consent to *collect* and *use* their child's information for educational programs does not automatically constitute consent to *disclose* that information to third parties.
*   **Collection Consent:** Required for the child to participate in the program.
*   **Disclosure Consent:** Required only if we intend to share data with entities that are not "Service Providers" supporting internal operations. **The Organization currently does not engage in such sharing.** If this changes, a separate, optional consent mechanism will be provided.

#### 4.3 Disclosure to Service Providers (Internal Operations)
We disclose children's information only to third-party Service Providers who provide support for the internal operations of our website and educational platforms. These providers are contractually bound to:
1.  Maintain the confidentiality, security, and integrity of the information.
2.  Use the information **only** for the specific service they provide to Study GRC Inc.
3.  Delete the information when it is no longer necessary for that service.

**Categories of Service Providers:**

| Category | Purpose of Disclosure | Data Elements Shared |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cloud Hosting & Infrastructure** | To host the website, databases, and learning management systems. | Encrypted storage of all user data. |
| **Identity & Authentication** | To manage secure logins and prevent unauthorized access. | Usernames, hashed passwords, IP addresses. |
| **Video Conferencing** | To facilitate virtual classrooms and mentorship sessions. | Video, audio, display names during sessions. |
| **Security & Analytics** | To detect fraud, prevent cyberattacks, and analyze aggregate site performance (non-marketing). | IP addresses, device identifiers, usage logs. |

*A current list of specific vendors is available upon request by contacting the Director of Kindness and Generosity.*

#### 4.4 Public Disclosure Risks
Certain features of our community (e.g., forums, group chats) may allow a child to make personal information publicly available.
*   **Moderation:** All youth-accessible areas are moderated per the *Online Community Moderation (Youth) Guideline*.
*   **Default Settings:** Privacy settings for children are set to "High/Private" by default.
*   **Warning:** Parents are advised to instruct children never to post phone numbers or addresses in chat functions.

#### 4.5 Legal and Safety Disclosures
Regardless of parental consent status, the Organization may disclose personal information if permitted or required by law to:
*   Comply with a court order, subpoena, or legal process.
*   Comply with the *Mandatory Reporting Procedure (Texas)* regarding suspected child abuse or neglect (Texas Family Code §261.101).
*   Protect the safety of a child who may be in immediate danger.
*   Protect the security or integrity of our services (e.g., liability in the event of a breach).

#### 4.6 Parental Rights and Control
Parents/Guardians retain full control over the disclosure of their child's data. Parents may:
1.  **Review:** Request to see the specific data shared with Service Providers.
2.  **Revoke:** Revoke consent for future collection or use.
3.  **Stop Sharing:** Request that we stop sharing data with Service Providers (Note: This may result in the inability to continue providing the educational service to the child).

To exercise these rights, parents must submit a *Data Subject Access Request (DSAR)* to the Director of Kindness and Generosity.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Vets all Service Providers to ensure they meet security standards and contractually agree to data limitations (No secondary use). |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Serves as the primary point of contact for parents regarding privacy questions and DSARs. |
| **Legal/Compliance Committee** | Reviews this disclosure annually to ensure alignment with FTC updates and COPPA 2.0 rule changes. |
| **Service Providers** | Must adhere to the *Third-Party Data Processing Agreement (DPA)* limiting data use to internal operations support. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Internal:** Failure by employees or volunteers to adhere to this disclosure (e.g., unauthorized sharing of youth data) will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination and legal action.

**External:** Service Providers found to be using children's data for unauthorized purposes (e.g., marketing) will face immediate contract termination and will be reported to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) for COPPA violations.
