### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Schedule is to establish a systematic, mandatory timeline for the review, update, and re-authorization of all Study GRC Inc. governance documents. This framework ensures that the Organization’s policies remain compliant with evolving legal requirements (including Texas Business Organizations Code and IRS regulations), align with current operational practices, and satisfy the internal control requirements of federal grants (2 CFR 200).

### 2. Scope
This Schedule applies to all controlled documents within the Master Documentation Registry (MDR), including:
*   **Level 1:** Governance Policies and Charters.
*   **Level 2:** Standards, Procedures, and Plans.
*   **Level 3:** Guidelines, Forms, and Templates.

**Exclusions:** This schedule does not apply to transactional records (e.g., meeting minutes, invoices, completed forms), which are governed by the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy (GOV-POL-012)*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Document Owner** | The specific role (e.g., CFO, CISO) assigned responsibility for the maintenance and accuracy of a specific document. |
| **Review Cycle** | The maximum allowable time elapsed between the Effective Date of a document and its mandatory review. |
| **Material Change** | A revision that alters the scope, intent, responsibilities, or rules of a document (e.g., changing a spending limit). Requires re-approval by the original Authority. |
| **Administrative Change** | Minor corrections such as typo fixes, formatting updates, or updating hyperlinks that do not alter the policy's intent. Does not require full Board re-vote. |
| **Event-Driven Review** | An unscheduled review triggered by a specific incident, such as a security breach, change in law, or audit finding. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Standard Review Cadence
To ensure continuous compliance, documents are assigned a review frequency based on risk exposure and regulatory requirements.

**4.1.1 Annual Review (High Risk)**
The following categories **must** be reviewed annually (every 12 months):
*   **Financial Integrity:** All policies related to 2 CFR 200 compliance, procurement, and internal controls.
*   **Cybersecurity & IT:** All policies mapped to NIST CSF, Incident Response Plans, and Access Controls.
*   **Youth Protection:** All safeguarding policies, codes of conduct, and reporting procedures.
*   **Human Resources:** Employee Handbook, Compensation, and Anti-Harassment policies.
*   **Bylaws:** Article XII (Amendments) implies regular review; the Governance Committee shall review Bylaws annually for necessary updates.

**4.1.2 Bi-Annual Review (Standard Risk)**
The following categories **must** be reviewed at least bi-annually (every 24 months):
*   **General Governance:** Conflict of Interest (policy text), Whistleblower, Record Retention.
*   **Operational Procedures:** Day-to-day workflows not tied to financial controls or safety.
*   **Communications:** Social media, branding, and PR guidelines.

**4.1.3 Tri-Annual Review (Low Risk)**
The following categories **must** be reviewed at least tri-annually (every 36 months):
*   **Job Descriptions:** Unless the role changes significantly.
*   **Templates & Forms:** Reviewed for usability and data collection relevance.

#### 4.2 Event-Driven Triggers
Regardless of the scheduled review date, a document **must** undergo immediate review and potential revision upon:
1.  **Legislative Changes:** Enactment of new state or federal laws (e.g., changes to Texas BOC, COPPA, or GDPR).
2.  **Audit Findings:** Identification of a deficiency during a Single Audit or internal risk assessment.
3.  **Incident Occurrence:** Following a security breach, safety incident, or whistleblower complaint where policy gaps were identified as a root cause.
4.  **Major Structural Change:** Mergers, dissolutions, or significant reorganizations as defined in Bylaws Article X (Fundamental Actions).

#### 4.3 The Review Process
The Document Owner is responsible for executing the review according to the following workflow:

1.  **Notification:** The Secretary or Governance Committee shall notify Document Owners 30 days prior to the expiration of the current version.
2.  **Assessment:** The Owner evaluates the document against:
    *   Current laws and regulations.
    *   Current organizational practices (does the policy match what we actually do?).
    *   Effectiveness (is the policy mitigating the risk?).
3.  **Drafting:**
    *   If **No Changes** are needed: The Owner updates the "Next Review Date" and signs off.
    *   If **Changes** are needed: The Owner drafts revisions in "Track Changes" or "Suggestion Mode."
4.  **Approval:**
    *   **Level 1 (Policies):** Submitted to the Board of Directors for vote.
    *   **Level 2 (Procedures):** Submitted to the Executive Director for approval.
5.  **Publication:** The new version (e.g., v2.0) is published to the central repository, and the old version is archived.

#### 4.4 Version Control Standards
*   **Major Versions (v1.0, v2.0):** Used for initial release and Material Changes. Requires full re-approval by the Approving Authority.
*   **Minor Versions (v1.1, v1.2):** Used for Administrative Changes. May be approved by the Document Owner or Executive Director without full Board vote.

#### 4.5 Archival
Pursuant to the *Document Retention Policy*, previous versions of policies must be archived and retained for a minimum of seven (7) years to support historical audits (e.g., proving what policy was in effect during a specific transaction in the past).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Governance Committee** | Maintains the Master Policy Index; monitors compliance with this Schedule; reports overdue reviews to the Board. |
| **Document Owner** | Conducts the actual review of assigned documents; drafts amendments; ensures content accuracy. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves Material Changes to Level 1 Governance Policies; adopts the Annual Review Schedule. |
| **Executive Director** | Reviews and approves Material Changes to Level 2 Standards and Procedures; ensures staff are trained on updated policies. |
| **Secretary** | Ensures updated policies are properly recorded in the corporate minutes and uploaded to the secure repository. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this schedule may result in:
*   **Audit Findings:** Non-compliance with 2 CFR 200.303 (Internal Controls).
*   **Legal Liability:** Reliance on outdated or unlawful policy language during litigation.
*   **Operational Risk:** Inconsistent application of rules.

Documents that have passed their "Next Review Date" by more than 90 days may be flagged as "Non-Compliant" by the Audit Committee and may be temporarily suspended until reviewed.
