### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish a rigorous, data-driven methodology for determining the compensation of "Disqualified Persons" (Operational Officers) at Study GRC Inc. This document is designed to satisfy the requirements of **Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 4958** regarding "Intermediate Sanctions."

By strictly adhering to this benchmarking standard, the Organization intends to establish a **Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness** for all compensation arrangements, thereby protecting the Organization’s tax-exempt status and shielding its Directors and Officers from excise tax liabilities associated with excess benefit transactions.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to the evaluation and approval of compensation packages for all individuals defined as **Operational Officers** in **Article V** of the Bylaws, and any other individual deemed a "Disqualified Person" by the IRS.

**Specific Roles Included:**
*   Executive Director (CEO)
*   Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
*   Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
*   Vice President of Education & Training
*   Any other key employee with substantial influence over the Organization’s affairs.

**Exclusions:**
*   General staff, contractors, or volunteers who do not hold "substantial influence" as defined by the IRS.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Disqualified Person (DQP)** | Any person who was in a position to exercise substantial influence over the affairs of the Organization at any time during a five-year lookback period. |
| **Excess Benefit Transaction** | A transaction in which an economic benefit is provided by a tax-exempt organization to a DQP that exceeds the value of the consideration (services) received. |
| **Rebuttable Presumption** | A legal standard where the IRS presumes compensation is reasonable if the organization follows three specific steps: (1) Independent Body Approval, (2) Reliance on Comparability Data, and (3) Adequate Documentation. |
| **Total Compensation** | The sum of all economic benefits, including base salary, bonuses, deferred compensation, insurance premiums, housing allowances, and taxable fringe benefits. |
| **Comparability Data** | Data regarding compensation paid by similarly situated organizations for functionally comparable positions. |

### 4. Standards and Methodology

To establish the Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness, the Board of Directors (or an authorized Independent Committee) must utilize the following methodology to generate the Benchmarking Study.

#### 4.1 Independence Requirement
The benchmarking study must be reviewed and the compensation approved by an authorized body of the Board composed **entirely** of individuals who do not have a conflict of interest with respect to the transaction, per **Bylaws Article IX**.
*   The DQP being evaluated cannot be present during the debate or vote on their compensation.

#### 4.2 Comparability Data Criteria
The Benchmarking Study must utilize data from at least **three (3)** independent sources. Data must be matched based on the following "Similarly Situated" criteria:

1.  **Geographic Scope:**
    *   Due to the Organization's "Remote-First" nature, data shall not be limited to the Organization's registered office (Texas).
    *   For technical roles (e.g., CISO), **National** nonprofit data shall be used to reflect the talent market.
    *   For administrative roles, **Regional** (Texas/Southwest) or National data may be used depending on recruitment scope.
2.  **Organizational Size:**
    *   Annual Budget/Revenue (comparable within ±50% of Study GRC’s current or projected budget).
    *   Total Assets.
    *   Number of Employees/Volunteers.
3.  **Functional Sector:**
    *   Primary: Educational Services / Professional Associations.
    *   Secondary: Technology / Cybersecurity Nonprofits.

#### 4.3 Authorized Data Sources
The Benchmarking Study must rely on current, objective data sources, including but not limited to:
*   **IRS Form 990 Filings:** Direct review of Schedule J for at least 5 comparable 501(c)(3) organizations.
*   **Published Compensation Surveys:** (e.g., GuideStar/Candid, ASAE, NonProfit Times, or industry-specific GRC salary reports).
*   **Written Employment Offers:** Documented competing offers from other organizations for the same individual (if applicable).
*   **Independent Compensation Consultant:** Opinions or reports from qualified third-party firms (optional but recommended for CEO/ED compensation).

#### 4.4 Calculation of Reasonable Range
The study must synthesize the data to create a "Reasonable Compensation Range":
1.  **25th Percentile:** Minimum competitive baseline.
2.  **50th Percentile (Median):** Target standard for fully qualified officers.
3.  **75th Percentile:** Maximum cap, reserved for exceptional performance, unique technical expertise (e.g., specialized GRC knowledge), or retention criticality.

*Note: Compensation exceeding the 75th percentile of the comparability data requires a specific, written justification in the Board minutes detailing the unique value proposition of the officer.*

#### 4.5 Total Compensation View
The Benchmarking Study must analyze **Total Compensation**, not just base salary. The study must account for:
*   Base Salary.
*   Incentive/Performance Bonuses.
*   Health/Retirement Benefits contributions.
*   Housing or Technology stipends (Remote work allowances).

#### 4.6 Documentation Requirements (The "Contemporaneous" Rule)
The Board must document the approval **before** the compensation begins. The Benchmarking Study report must be attached to the Board Minutes and include:
*   The terms of the transaction and the date it was approved.
*   The members of the authorized body who were present and those who voted.
*   The comparability data obtained and relied upon (including the specific surveys or 990s used).
*   Any actions taken with respect to consideration of the transaction by anyone who is otherwise a member of the authorized body but who had a conflict of interest.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Acts as the "Authorized Body." Reviews the Benchmarking Study, votes on compensation, and ensures no conflicts of interest exist during the vote. |
| **Treasurer** | Oversees the compilation of the Benchmarking Study. Ensures financial feasibility of the proposed compensation ranges within the budget. |
| **Governance Committee** | Verifies that the data sources meet the "Comparability" criteria defined in Section 4.2. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews the final Benchmarking Study and Board Minutes to ensure they satisfy the legal requirements for the Rebuttable Presumption under IRC § 4958. |
| **Executive Director** | May provide job descriptions and performance data but **must not** participate in the collection of salary data for their own position or influence the selection of comparable organizations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this standard puts the Organization and its leadership at significant financial risk.

*   **Excise Taxes:** Under IRC § 4958, the IRS may impose an excise tax of **25%** of the excess benefit on the Disqualified Person. If not corrected, this rises to **200%**.
*   **Manager Liability:** Organization Managers (Directors/Officers) who knowingly participate in an excess benefit transaction may be personally liable for a tax of **10%** of the excess benefit (capped at $20,000 per transaction).
*   **Revocation:** Severe or repeated violations may result in the revocation of the Organization's 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status.
