### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Playbook is to establish a structured, rapid-response protocol for managing significant reputational threats arising on or amplified by social media platforms. As a 501(c)(3) organization dedicated to GRC knowledge and youth education, Study GRC Inc. relies heavily on public trust. A mishandled social media crisis can jeopardize our tax-exempt status, federal grant eligibility (under 2 CFR 200), and community standing. This document ensures the organization speaks with one voice, mitigates damage, and adheres to the ethical standards mandated in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**.

### 2. Scope
This Playbook applies to:
*   **Official Channels:** All social media accounts owned by Study GRC Inc. (LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Discord, YouTube, etc.).
*   **Personnel:** All Directors, Officers, employees, and volunteers.
*   **Incidents:** Events originating online or offline that generate significant negative sentiment, viral misinformation, or legal risk on social platforms.

**Exclusions:** Routine customer service inquiries, minor negative feedback (e.g., a broken link report), or constructive criticism, which are handled under the *Community Moderation Guidelines*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Social Media Crisis** | An event that triggers a significant volume of negative mentions, threatens the organization's reputation, violates legal statutes, or poses a safety risk to community members. |
| **Crisis Response Team (CRT)** | The designated group responsible for executing this playbook. |
| **Holding Statement** | A pre-approved, temporary statement issued to acknowledge the situation while a full response is formulated. |
| **Trolling** | Deliberate, bad-faith provocation intended to elicit an emotional response or disrupt community operations. |
| **Severity Level** | A classification system (1-4) used to determine the scale of the response required. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Identification and Assessment
Any team member who identifies a potential crisis must immediately notify the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)**. The DKG will assess the situation using the Severity Matrix below.

**Severity Matrix:**

| Level | Description | Response Authority | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 (Low)** | Isolated negative comment; broken link; minor factual error. | Community Manager | Standard customer service response. Monitor. |
| **2 (Medium)** | Sustained negative thread; criticism from influential account; minor policy violation. | DKG | DKG drafts response. Notify Executive Director. |
| **3 (High)** | Viral negative sentiment; accusation of ethical breach; offensive content posted by rogue actor. | Executive Director | **Activate CRT.** Pause all scheduled posts. Legal review required. |
| **4 (Critical)** | Youth safety incident; data breach; criminal allegation; lawsuit threat. | Board of Directors | **Activate CRT.** Immediate Legal/Board involvement. Law Enforcement notification if applicable. |

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Activation and Containment
If the incident is **Level 3 or 4**, the DKG must activate the Crisis Response Team (CRT).

1.  **Notify CRT:** Contact Executive Director, CISO, and President of the Board via the emergency Signal group or phone (do not use Slack/Discord if the platform itself is compromised).
2.  **Secure Accounts:** If the crisis involves a security breach (e.g., Account Takeover), the CISO must immediately revoke tokens, change passwords, and enable lockdown mode per the *Master Information Security Policy*.
3.  **Pause Automation:** The DKG must suspend all pre-scheduled social media posts and marketing emails to prevent "tone-deaf" communication during the crisis.
4.  **Centralize Communication:** All personnel are prohibited from posting about the incident on personal accounts. Refer to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.2** regarding confidentiality.

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Response Strategy
The CRT will determine the appropriate response strategy based on the incident type.

**4.3.1 The "3 As" Protocol**
For non-legal/non-criminal errors (e.g., an offensive post made in error), the response must follow the "3 As":
1.  **Acknowledge:** Admit the mistake quickly. Do not hide.
2.  **Apologize:** Offer a sincere, unqualified apology (avoid "we are sorry *if* you were offended").
3.  **Act:** State the specific steps being taken to fix it and prevent recurrence.

**4.3.2 Scenario: Youth Protection Incident**
*   **Trigger:** Allegation of misconduct involving a minor in the community.
*   **Action:**
    1.  Do **not** engage publicly with the accuser beyond a generic "We are investigating."
    2.  Immediately report to the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (as per *Mandatory Reporting Procedure*).
    3.  Preserve all digital evidence (screenshots, logs) immediately.
    4.  Legal Counsel must draft all public statements.

**4.3.3 Scenario: Cybersecurity/Data Breach**
*   **Trigger:** Leaked user data or compromised admin account.
*   **Action:**
    1.  CISO leads the technical response.
    2.  Public statement must focus on transparency and user protection steps.
    3.  Refer to *Data Breach Notification Procedure* for statutory notification timelines (GDPR/TX Business Code).

**4.3.4 Scenario: Disinformation/Trolling**
*   **Trigger:** Coordinated attack spreading false information.
*   **Action:**
    1.  Do not engage in arguments.
    2.  Post a single "Fact Sheet" or correction on the official website.
    3.  Link to the Fact Sheet from social channels.
    4.  Use platform reporting tools to flag Terms of Service violations.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Drafting and Approval
1.  **Drafting:** The DKG or Communications Lead drafts the response (Holding Statement or Full Statement).
2.  **Legal/Exec Review:**
    *   **Level 3:** Executive Director must approve.
    *   **Level 4:** Board President and Legal Counsel must approve.
3.  **Publishing:** Post the statement to the primary platform where the crisis originated first, then cross-post to the website "Transparency" page.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Post-Crisis Review
Within 72 hours of the crisis resolution:
1.  **Post-Mortem Meeting:** The CRT meets to analyze the root cause.
2.  **Report:** The DKG submits a "Crisis Incident Report" to the Board, detailing:
    *   Timeline of events.
    *   Sentiment analysis (pre- and post-response).
    *   Operational gaps identified.
3.  **Policy Update:** Update this Playbook or the *Employee Handbook* based on lessons learned.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final decision-maker on strategy and messaging for Level 3 incidents. Primary spokesperson. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | **CRT Lead.** Monitors sentiment, drafts initial statements, pauses automation, manages community moderation. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Secures compromised accounts, preserves digital evidence, advises on data privacy implications. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves strategy for Level 4 (Critical) incidents. Oversees reputational risk per **Bylaws Article III**. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews all statements for liability, defamation risk, and regulatory compliance (2 CFR 200, COPPA). |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Acts as "sensors" to report issues; maintains strict confidentiality during active crises. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this Playbook during a crisis—specifically unauthorized posting, leaking confidential information, or failing to report a Level 4 incident—constitutes a violation of the **Board Code of Ethics** and **Employee Handbook**.

Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (per **Bylaws Section 3.6**), or expulsion from membership (per **Bylaws Section 2.1**).
