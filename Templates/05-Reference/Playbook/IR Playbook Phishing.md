### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Playbook is to define a standardized, repeatable response procedure for phishing attacks targeting Study GRC Inc. Phishing is the primary vector for credential theft, ransomware, and Business Email Compromise (BEC). This document ensures the Organization satisfies the **Respond** function of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) by minimizing the "dwell time" of an attacker and reducing the impact of compromised accounts on our remote-first infrastructure.

### 2. Scope
This procedure applies to all Information Systems owned or operated by Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to:
*   **Corporate Email:** Google Workspace (Gmail).
*   **Communication Platforms:** Slack, Discord.
*   **Endpoints:** Laptops and mobile devices used by Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors.

**Exclusions:** Generic spam (unsolicited marketing) that does not contain malicious payloads or social engineering elements is out of scope for *incident response* but should be marked as spam by the user.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Phishing** | A cybercrime in which a target is contacted by email, telephone, or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data. |
| **Spear Phishing** | A targeted phishing attempt disguised as a trusted sender, often using specific information about the target. |
| **BEC (Business Email Compromise)** | A specific type of phishing where an attacker compromises legitimate business email accounts to conduct unauthorized transfers of funds. |
| **IOC (Indicator of Compromise)** | Evidence that indicates a network intrusion or data breach (e.g., malicious IP address, URL, file hash). |
| **Forwarding Rule** | An email setting that automatically sends incoming mail to another address; often used by attackers to maintain persistence. |

### 4. Procedures

#### 4.1 Phase 1: Detection and Analysis
**Trigger:** A user reports a suspicious email via the "Report Phishing" button/Slack channel, or an alert is generated by the Google Workspace Security Center.

1.  **Triage:** The Incident Response Team (IRT) must validate the report within **60 minutes** of receipt.
2.  **Analyze Header and Content:**
    *   Check `Return-Path` vs. `From` address.
    *   Analyze links (URLs) using a sandbox tool (e.g., VirusTotal, URLScan.io). **DO NOT** click links on a production machine.
    *   Check for urgent language, financial requests, or unexpected attachments.
3.  **Determine Scope:**
    *   Has the user clicked the link?
    *   Did the user enter credentials?
    *   Did the user download/open an attachment?
    *   Have other users received the same email? (Perform a log search in Google Workspace Admin).

#### 4.2 Phase 2: Containment
If the email is malicious, immediate containment is required to prevent spread.

**Scenario A: Email Received, No Interaction**
1.  **Delete:** IT/Security deletes the email from the user's inbox.
2.  **Purge:** IT/Security performs a bulk search-and-delete for the same message ID across the entire organization.
3.  **Block:** Add the sender domain/IP and malicious URL to the Blocklist in the Email Gateway/Google Admin.

**Scenario B: User Clicked Link / Entered Credentials (Account Compromise)**
1.  **Reset Password:** Immediately force a password reset for the affected user.
2.  **Revoke Sessions:** In Google Admin/IdP, revoke all active cookies and refresh tokens to disconnect the attacker.
3.  **MFA Reset:** If the attacker may have enrolled a new MFA device, reset MFA settings and force re-enrollment.
4.  **Isolate Device:** If an attachment was opened, the user must disconnect their device from the network (Wi-Fi/VPN) immediately to prevent lateral movement.

#### 4.3 Phase 3: Eradication
Once the account is locked, the IRT must remove the attacker's persistence mechanisms.

1.  **Audit Forwarding Rules:** Check the user's email settings for unauthorized forwarding rules (e.g., "Forward all mail to attacker@gmail.com"). **Delete immediately.**
2.  **Audit Delegates:** Ensure no unauthorized account delegates were added.
3.  **Check Outbound Mail:** Review sent items to ensure the account was not used to blast phishing emails to internal staff or external partners.
4.  **App Permissions:** Review "Third-party apps with account access" (OAuth tokens) and revoke any unrecognized apps added during the compromise window.

#### 4.4 Phase 4: Recovery
Restoring business operations while monitoring for recurrence.

1.  **Re-enable Account:** Once the user has been verified via video call or secondary channel, provide temporary credentials.
2.  **Device Re-imaging:** If malware was confirmed (Scenario B, Step 4), the device must be wiped and re-imaged before reconnecting to organizational resources.
3.  **Enhanced Monitoring:** Place the user account on "High Alert" monitoring for 48 hours.
4.  **Communication:** If the account sent phishing emails to external contacts, the CISO and Legal Counsel must draft a notification to affected parties to warn them, in compliance with the *Public Disclosure and Transparency Policy*.

#### 4.5 Phase 5: Post-Incident Activity
1.  **Documentation:** Complete the Incident Report Form, logging all IOCs, timelines, and actions taken.
2.  **Retrospective:** If the incident resulted in data access or financial loss, conduct a "Lessons Learned" meeting within 5 business days.
3.  **Training:** The affected user must complete a remedial "Spotting Phishing" training module within 7 days.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reporting User** | Immediately report suspicious emails; disconnect device if instructed. |
| **Incident Commander (CISO)** | Orchestrates the response; authorizes bulk deletion; declares "Breach" status if data is exfiltrated. |
| **IT Operations** | Executes technical containment (password resets, log searches, device isolation). |
| **Legal Counsel** | Determines notification obligations under Texas Business & Commerce Code § 521.053 if PII was accessed. |
| **Human Resources** | Manages disciplinary process if the incident involved gross negligence or policy violations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Mandatory Reporting:** Failure to report a suspected compromise immediately is a violation of the *Master Information Security Policy*.
*   **Testing:** The Organization conducts periodic simulated phishing tests. Repeated failures (3+ in 12 months) will result in mandatory intensive training and a formal performance review.
*   **Authority:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V**, the CISO has the authority to suspend any user account—including those of Board Directors—to protect the integrity of the Organization's systems during an active incident.
