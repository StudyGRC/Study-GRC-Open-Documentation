### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the specific, measurable performance objectives for the Executive Director (CEO) of Study GRC Inc. for the current fiscal year. This framework aligns the Executive Director’s focus with the Organization’s strategic mission to foster a community-driven GRC ecosystem, ensures compliance with **Bylaws Article V (Operational Leadership)**, and provides the objective basis for the annual performance evaluation and compensation review in accordance with IRS regulations regarding reasonable compensation.

### 2. Scope
This document applies exclusively to the position of **Executive Director (CEO)**. It serves as the primary agreement between the Board of Directors and the Executive Director regarding priority initiatives, expected outcomes, and success metrics for the designated fiscal year.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **KRA** | Key Result Area. A broad category of performance critical to the success of the organization. |
| **KPI** | Key Performance Indicator. A quantifiable measure used to evaluate the success of an organization or employee in meeting objectives for performance. |
| **Single Audit** | An organization-wide financial audit and compliance audit required for non-profits expending $750,000 or more in federal awards (2 CFR 200 Subpart F). |
| **Unrestricted Net Assets** | The portion of the organization's net assets that is neither permanently restricted nor temporarily restricted by donor-imposed stipulations. |

### 4. Performance Objectives & Goals

The Executive Director’s performance shall be evaluated based on the following four Key Result Areas (KRAs).

#### 4.1 KRA 1: Financial Stewardship & Grant Compliance (Weight: 30%)
*Objective:* Ensure the financial health, sustainability, and regulatory compliance of the Organization, specifically regarding federal funding and donor transparency.

*   **Goal 1.1: Audit & Compliance**
    *   **Target:** Achieve a "Clean" (Unqualified) opinion on the annual financial audit and, if applicable, the Federal Single Audit (2 CFR 200 Subpart F).
    *   **Metric:** Zero material weaknesses or significant deficiencies reported in the Auditor’s Management Letter.
*   **Goal 1.2: Revenue Diversification**
    *   **Target:** Secure `[$ Amount]` in new funding from sources other than the primary federal grant to reduce dependency.
    *   **Metric:** Total non-federal revenue recognized by December 31.
*   **Goal 1.3: Operating Reserves**
    *   **Target:** Maintain or increase Unrestricted Net Assets to cover a minimum of `[Number]` months of operating expenses.
    *   **Metric:** Month-end financial reports verified by the Treasurer.

#### 4.2 KRA 2: Mission Impact & Program Growth (Weight: 30%)
*Objective:* Advance the Organization’s purpose of providing zero-cost, open-source GRC resources and empowering learners.

*   **Goal 2.1: Educational Resource Expansion**
    *   **Target:** Launch `[Number]` new open-source GRC training modules or curriculum units.
    *   **Metric:** Publication of resources to the community repository under the approved open-source license.
*   **Goal 2.2: Community Engagement**
    *   **Target:** Increase the number of "Supporting Members" (Voting Members) by `[Percentage]%`.
    *   **Metric:** Membership roll data as verified by the Secretary per **Bylaws Section 2.6**.
*   **Goal 2.3: K-12 Outreach & Youth Program**
    *   **Target:** Successfully execute `[Number]` youth-focused cyber safety or GRC awareness events.
    *   **Metric:** Event attendance logs and post-event impact surveys.

#### 4.3 KRA 3: Operational Integrity, Security, & Risk (Weight: 20%)
*Objective:* Mature the organization’s infrastructure, ensuring data privacy and security in a remote-first environment.

*   **Goal 3.1: Cybersecurity Maturity**
    *   **Target:** Achieve a Tier `[Number]` rating on the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) Organizational Profile.
    *   **Metric:** CISO validation of the annual internal security assessment.
*   **Goal 3.2: Youth Protection Compliance**
    *   **Target:** Maintain 100% compliance with background checks for all staff and volunteers interacting with minors.
    *   **Metric:** Zero incidents of non-compliance reported; 100% pass rate on "Two-Deep Leadership" spot checks.
*   **Goal 3.3: Data Privacy (COPPA/GDPR)**
    *   **Target:** Implement and validate the new "Age Verification Mechanism" for online community participants.
    *   **Metric:** Successful deployment and legal sign-off on the privacy workflow.

#### 4.4 KRA 4: Governance, Culture, & Leadership (Weight: 20%)
*Objective:* Foster a healthy, inclusive culture and maintain transparent, effective relations with the Board of Directors.

*   **Goal 4.1: Board Transparency**
    *   **Target:** Provide monthly operational dashboards and financial reports to the Board at least 3 days prior to scheduled meetings.
    *   **Metric:** 100% on-time delivery rate of Board packets.
*   **Goal 4.2: Staff & Volunteer Culture**
    *   **Target:** Achieve a Net Promoter Score (NPS) of `[Score]` or higher on the annual internal staff/volunteer satisfaction survey.
    *   **Metric:** Annual survey results administered by the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).
*   **Goal 4.3: Strategic Planning**
    *   **Target:** Lead the review and update of the 3-Year Strategic Plan for Board approval by Q4.
    *   **Metric:** Board adoption of the updated Strategic Plan.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the annual goals; conducts the mid-year check-in and year-end evaluation; determines compensation adjustments based on performance. |
| **Governance Committee** | Facilitates the goal-setting process; gathers data for the evaluation; ensures the process complies with the Bylaws. |
| **Executive Director** | Executes strategies to achieve these goals; provides regular progress reports to the Board; alerts the Board immediately if goals become unattainable due to external factors. |
| **Treasurer** | Validates financial metrics (KRA 1) for the evaluation process. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Evaluation Cycle:** The Board shall conduct a formal review of these goals annually in January of the following year. A mid-year informal review shall occur in July to assess progress and adjust targets if necessary due to significant changes in the operating environment.
*   **Compensation Linkage:** Achievement of these goals is a primary factor in determining merit-based salary increases or performance bonuses, subject to the **Operational Officer Compensation Policy** and IRS regulations regarding excess benefit transactions.
*   **Performance Issues:** Failure to meet the minimum threshold of performance in critical KRAs (specifically Financial Stewardship and Risk Management) may result in a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) or termination of employment, in accordance with the **Executive Director Employment Agreement** and **Bylaws Article V**.
