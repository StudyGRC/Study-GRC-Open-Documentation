### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Statement is to formalize Study GRC Inc.’s (the "Organization") commitment to digital inclusion. In accordance with **Bylaws Article 1.3**, which mandates the Organization to "remove financial barriers" and "empower learners," we recognize that true empowerment requires removing technical and functional barriers for individuals with disabilities.

This document serves to inform the public, members, and regulatory bodies of our compliance standards regarding the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), ensuring transparency in our operations as required by our governance framework.

### 2. Scope
This Statement applies to all digital assets owned, operated, or controlled by Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to:
*   **Public Website:** The primary domain and all subdomains.
*   **Learning Management Systems (LMS):** Course interfaces, quizzes, and educational modules.
*   **Digital Resources:** Downloadable documents (PDFs, templates), video content, and multimedia assets.
*   **Third-Party Integrations:** Where feasible, the Organization mandates accessibility compliance from third-party vendors (e.g., donation portals, community forums), though total control over external code may be limited.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **WCAG** | Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. A series of guidelines for improving web accessibility, developed by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). |
| **Level AA** | The intermediate level of conformance in WCAG, which is the standard target for most organizations to ensure broad accessibility. |
| **Assistive Technology** | Software or hardware used by people with disabilities to interact with digital content (e.g., screen readers like NVDA or JAWS, screen magnifiers, voice recognition software). |
| **ARIA** | Accessible Rich Internet Applications. A set of attributes that define ways to make web content and web applications more accessible to people with disabilities. |
| **Remediation** | The process of correcting digital content or code to meet accessibility standards. |

### 4. Statement of Commitment & Standards

#### 4.1 Conformance Standard
Study GRC Inc. is committed to ensuring its digital content is accessible to the widest possible audience, regardless of technology or ability. We are actively working to ensure our website and digital materials substantially conform to the **Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2, Level AA**.

#### 4.2 Core Accessibility Measures
To ensure compliance, the Organization implements the following measures:
*   **Semantic HTML:** We utilize proper heading structures (H1-H6) to ensure logical navigation for screen readers.
*   **Text Alternatives:** All non-decorative images must contain descriptive Alternative Text (Alt Text).
*   **Keyboard Navigability:** All interactive elements (menus, forms, buttons) are accessible via keyboard without requiring a mouse.
*   **Color Contrast:** We maintain a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text to accommodate users with visual impairments.
*   **Video Captions:** All pre-recorded video content produced by the Organization includes closed captioning or transcripts.

#### 4.3 Feedback and Grievance Mechanism
We welcome feedback on the accessibility of our site. If a user encounters a barrier, they are encouraged to contact us. In compliance with our **Transparency Policy**, we commit to acknowledging such reports within two (2) business days.

*   **Email:** accessibility@studygrc.org
*   **Postal Address:** [Insert Registered Office Address]

When reporting an issue, users are requested to specify the webpage URL and the specific nature of the problem (e.g., "The donation form button is not focusable with a keyboard").

#### 4.4 Assessment Approach
Study GRC Inc. assesses the accessibility of its website through the following methods:
*   **Automated Testing:** Regular scans using automated accessibility evaluation tools (e.g., WAVE, Axe).
*   **Manual Auditing:** Periodic manual reviews of critical user paths (e.g., registration, checkout) using keyboard-only navigation.
*   **User Testing:** Where feasible, engaging with community members who utilize assistive technology to validate user experience.

#### 4.5 Known Limitations (Transparency)
Despite our best efforts, some content may not yet be fully adapted to the strictest accessibility standards. We are currently working to resolve the following known limitations:
*   **Legacy Documents:** Some older PDF resources created prior to [Year] may not be fully tagged for screen readers. We are updating these upon request.
*   **Third-Party Content:** User-generated content in community forums or external content linked from our site may not meet our internal standards.
*   **Complex Charts:** Some complex GRC diagrams may rely on visual context; we are working to provide text-based summaries for these assets.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Owner of this Statement; ensures all educational curriculum and LMS platforms meet WCAG 2.2 AA standards. |
| **Web Developer / IT Admin** | Responsible for the technical implementation of ARIA labels, semantic code, and resolving reported bugs. |
| **Content Creators** | Responsible for ensuring all new blog posts, documents, and images include proper hierarchy and alt text before publishing. |
| **Executive Director** | Responsible for allocating budget and resources for accessibility tools, audits, and remediation. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Accessibility is not a one-time project but an ongoing requirement of our operational excellence.

*   **Non-Compliance:** Failure to adhere to accessibility standards in new content creation may result in the removal of that content until remediation is complete.
*   **Vendor Requirements:** Software vendors failing to meet our **Digital Accessibility Policy** requirements may face contract termination or non-renewal.
*   **Legal Mitigation:** This Statement serves as a good-faith demonstration of our intent to comply with Title III of the ADA. In the event of a formal complaint, the Organization will prioritize the remediation of the specific barrier identified.
