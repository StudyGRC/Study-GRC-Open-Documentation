### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish the mandatory recording standard for all interactions between Operational Officers and the Advisory Board of Study GRC Inc. This log ensures strict compliance with **Bylaws Article VI, Section 6.2**, which mandates that the Board of Directors retains sole governing authority and that no "shadow governance" structures emerge. This record serves as the official audit trail to verify that all strategic advice flows through the Board of Directors and that Operational Officers do not bypass the Board's oversight.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all **Operational Officers** (including the Executive Director/CEO, CISO, CFO, etc.) and members of the **Advisory Board**.

*   **In Scope:** All formal communications, including scheduled meetings, email exchanges regarding organizational strategy, requests for introductions, and solicitation of professional advice.
*   **Out of Scope:** Strictly social or incidental interactions (e.g., exchanging pleasantries at a public conference) where no organizational business, strategy, or operations are discussed.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Facilitating Director** | A voting member of the Board of Directors designated to oversee and participate in a specific communication between an Operational Officer and an Advisory Board member. |
| **Formal Communication** | Any interaction (verbal, written, or digital) where organizational business, strategy, partnerships, or operational challenges are discussed, or where advice is sought. |
| **Operational Officer** | Leadership roles defined in **Bylaws Article V**, including the Executive Director, VP of Education, CISO, and CFO. |
| **Shadow Governance** | A risk scenario where non-voting advisors unduly influence operational leadership without the knowledge or consent of the voting Board of Directors. |

### 4. Recording Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 Pre-Communication Authorization
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI, Section 6.2**, Operational Officers are prohibited from initiating formal communication with the Advisory Board without prior approval.
1.  The Operational Officer must submit a request to the Board (via the President or Secretary) detailing the purpose of the proposed contact.
2.  The Board must assign a **Facilitating Director** to oversee the interaction.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Data Fields
Every authorized interaction must be recorded in the Master Communication Log (maintained by the Secretary) within 48 hours of the interaction. The log entry must contain the following data points:

1.  **Date & Time:** Timestamp of the interaction.
2.  **Medium:** (e.g., Zoom, Email, In-Person, Slack).
3.  **Participants:** Names of the Operational Officer(s) and Advisory Board Member(s).
4.  **Facilitating Director:** Name of the Board Director present (or copied on email).
5.  **Topic/Subject:** Brief summary of the advice sought or business discussed.
6.  **Key Outcomes/Advice:** Summary of the guidance provided by the Advisor.
7.  **Action Items:** Any follow-up tasks generated from the discussion.

#### 4.3 Reporting Protocol
1.  **Immediate Briefing:** If a Facilitating Director cannot be present (under emergency exception approved by the President), the Operational Officer must provide a written briefing to the full Board within 24 hours.
2.  **Monthly Review:** The Secretary shall present the *Officer-Advisory Board Communication Log* as a consent agenda item at every regular Board meeting to ensure transparency.

#### 4.4 Log Format Template
The official log shall be maintained in the Organization’s secure record-keeping system using the following schema:

| Date | Operational Officer | Advisory Member | Facilitating Director | Topic Category | Summary of Advice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| [YYYY-MM-DD] | [Name] | [Name] | [Name] | [e.g., Fundraising] | [Brief Description] |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Operational Officer** | Responsible for obtaining prior approval for communications and drafting the initial log entry for submission to the Secretary. |
| **Facilitating Director** | Responsible for attending the meeting/being copied on emails to ensure the discussion remains within scope; verifies the accuracy of the log entry. |
| **Corporate Secretary** | Responsible for maintaining the Master Log, ensuring it is tamper-proof, and presenting it during Board meetings for review. |
| **Board President** | Authority to approve emergency communications if a Facilitating Director is unavailable. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Strict adherence to this logging standard is required to maintain the legal distinction between the governing Board and the non-voting Advisory Board.

*   **Operational Officers:** Failure to obtain approval or log communications constitutes a violation of **Bylaws Article VI** and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including removal from office as outlined in **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.3**.
*   **Advisory Board Members:** Repeated engagement in unauthorized operational directives may result in removal from the Advisory Board.
