### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to mandate the inspection, maintenance, and testing of portable fire extinguishers within facilities controlled by Study GRC Inc. This standard ensures compliance with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.157** and **NFPA 10**, guaranteeing that fire suppression equipment is fully functional and accessible to protect life and property in the event of an emergency.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all physical locations owned, leased, or directly managed by Study GRC Inc., including:
*   The Registered Office (as defined in Bylaws Article I, Section 1.2).
*   Storage facilities housing organizational assets.
*   Temporary event venues where Study GRC Inc. assumes facility control.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Remote Home Offices:** This standard does not apply to the private residences of remote employees, consistent with OSHA’s directive on home offices, unless the employee is storing hazardous materials on behalf of the Organization.
*   **Co-Working/Shared Spaces:** Where the Organization occupies a shared facility (e.g., WeWork), the landlord is primarily responsible for infrastructure; however, Study GRC Inc. staff must verify the presence and location of extinguishers.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Class A/B/C** | Classification of fires: **A** (Combustibles like paper/wood), **B** (Liquids like gasoline/oil), **C** (Electrical equipment). |
| **Hydrostatic Testing** | Pressure testing of the extinguisher cylinder to verify its strength against unwanted rupture. |
| **Visual Inspection** | A "quick check" that an extinguisher is available and will operate. It is intended to give reasonable assurance that the extinguisher is fully charged and operable. |
| **Tag** | The physical card attached to the extinguisher recording the inspection history. |

### 4. Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 General Requirements (OSHA 1910.157(c))
1.  **Accessibility:** Fire extinguishers must be mounted, located, and identified so that they are readily accessible to employees without subjecting the employees to possible injury.
2.  **Mounting:** Extinguishers having a gross weight not exceeding 40 pounds must be installed so that the top of the fire extinguisher is not more than 5 feet above the floor.
3.  **Travel Distance:** Extinguishers must be located so that the maximum travel distance for employees to any extinguisher is 75 feet (for Class A hazards).

#### 4.2 Monthly Visual Inspection Procedure (OSHA 1910.157(e)(2))
The designated Safety Officer or Office Manager must perform a visual inspection of all fire extinguishers every 30 days. The inspection must verify:
1.  **Location:** The extinguisher is in its designated place.
2.  **Visibility:** No obstructions block access or visibility.
3.  **Pressure:** The pressure gauge reading is in the operable range (green zone) or position.
4.  **Fullness:** The extinguisher feels full (hefting/weighing).
5.  **Condition:** Tires, wheels, carriage, hose, and nozzle are in good condition (no cracks, dry rot, or blockage).
6.  **Safety Seal:** The pull pin and tamper seal are intact.

**Recording:** The inspector must initial and date the physical tag on the extinguisher AND update the **Master Inspection Log** (Section 4.5).

#### 4.3 Annual Maintenance (OSHA 1910.157(e)(3))
Study GRC Inc. must engage a certified third-party fire protection vendor to perform a thorough maintenance check once every 12 months.
*   The vendor must replace the tamper seal and update the service tag.
*   The Organization must retain the vendor's service report for at least one year.

#### 4.4 Hydrostatic Testing (OSHA 1910.157(f))
*   **Stored Pressure Extinguishers:** Must be hydrostatically tested every 12 years.
*   **Carbon Dioxide Extinguishers:** Must be hydrostatically tested every 5 years.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain fire safety equipment is a violation of federal law and poses a severe risk to life safety.
*   **Regulatory Penalty:** Violations of OSHA 1910.157 can result in civil penalties of up to $16,131 per violation (adjusted for inflation).
*   **Internal Discipline:** Failure to perform or falsification of inspection logs will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
