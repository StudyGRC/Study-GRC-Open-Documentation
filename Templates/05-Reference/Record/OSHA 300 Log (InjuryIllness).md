### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this standard is to establish the mandatory requirements for recording and reporting work-related fatalities, injuries, and illnesses in compliance with the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) Regulation **29 CFR Part 1904**. While Study GRC Inc. operates primarily as a remote-first organization, accurate recordkeeping is essential for monitoring the safety of our workforce, complying with federal law, and satisfying potential federal grant requirements regarding workplace safety.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all **Employees** (W-2 status) of Study GRC Inc., regardless of location.

*   **Inclusions:**
    *   Remote employees working from home offices.
    *   Employees traveling on organization business (e.g., conferences, training events).
    *   Temporary workers supervised on a day-to-day basis by Study GRC Inc.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   **Volunteers:** Injuries sustained by volunteers are tracked separately via the *General Liability Incident Log* and are not recorded on the OSHA 300 Log unless reclassified by legal counsel.
    *   **Independent Contractors:** Contractors are responsible for their own OSHA recording unless they are directly supervised by Study GRC Inc. personnel on a day-to-day basis.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **OSHA Form 300** | The Log of Work-Related Injuries and Illnesses. Used to classify work-related injuries and illnesses and to note the extent and severity of each case. |
| **OSHA Form 300A** | The Summary of Work-Related Injuries and Illnesses. A summary of the incidents from Form 300, which must be certified and posted annually. |
| **OSHA Form 301** | The Injury and Illness Incident Report. A detailed form for each individual recordable injury (or an equivalent form like a Workers' Comp First Report of Injury). |
| **Recordable Injury** | An injury or illness is recordable if it results in: death, days away from work, restricted work or transfer to another job, medical treatment beyond first aid, loss of consciousness, or a significant injury/illness diagnosed by a PLHCP. |
| **First Aid** | Specific treatments listed in 29 CFR 1904.7(b)(5)(ii) that are **not** recordable (e.g., using non-prescription meds at non-prescription strength, cleaning wounds, using bandages/band-aids). |
| **Work-Relatedness** | An event or exposure in the work environment either caused or contributed to the resulting condition or significantly aggravated a pre-existing injury or illness. |
| **PLHCP** | Physician or Other Licensed Health Care Professional. |

### 4. Procedures and Standards

#### 4.1 General Maintenance Requirement
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, the Organization must maintain correct and complete records. The VP of Operations or their designee must maintain the OSHA 300 Log on a calendar-year basis (January 1 to December 31).

#### 4.2 Determination of Recordability
Upon receipt of an *Accident/Injury Report*, the Document Owner must determine recordability within **seven (7) calendar days** using the following decision matrix:

1.  **Did the employee experience an injury or illness?**
2.  **Is the injury or illness work-related?** (See Section 4.3 and 4.4).
3.  **Is it a new case?** (Not a recurrence of an old injury).
4.  **Does it meet general recording criteria?**
    *   Death.
    *   Days away from work.
    *   Restricted work or transfer to another job.
    *   Medical treatment beyond first aid.
    *   Loss of consciousness.
    *   Significant injury/illness diagnosed by a PLHCP.

#### 4.3 Remote Work (Home Office) Criteria
Given the Organization's remote-first nature, strict adherence to OSHA Directive CPL 02-00-125 is required. An injury/illness occurring in a home office is **recordable only if**:
1.  The injury or illness occurs while the employee is performing work for pay or compensation in the home; **AND**
2.  The injury or illness is directly related to the performance of work rather than the general home environment.

*   *Example (Recordable):* An employee drops a heavy box of work-related training manuals on their foot, requiring a cast.
*   *Example (Not Recordable):* An employee trips over the family dog while rushing to answer a work phone call.

#### 4.4 Travel and Event Criteria
Injuries sustained while traveling for Study GRC Inc. (e.g., attending a GRC conference) are work-related if the employee was engaged in work activities "in the interest of the employer."
*   **Detours:** Injuries sustained during a personal detour from a reasonably direct route of travel (e.g., a side trip for vacation) are **not** recordable.
*   **Hotel/Lodging:** Injuries occurring in a hotel room are generally **not** recordable unless the employee was performing work duties (e.g., writing a report) at the time.

#### 4.5 Recording Protocol
If a case is determined to be recordable:
1.  **Form 301 (Incident Report):** Complete within 7 calendar days of receiving information that a recordable injury occurred.
2.  **Form 300 (The Log):** Enter the details on the master log.
    *   **Privacy Cases:** If the injury involves sensitive areas (sexual assault, mental illness, HIV, needle sticks), do **not** enter the employee's name on the Form 300. Enter "Privacy Case" in the name field and maintain a separate, confidential list tracking the case number to the employee name.

#### 4.6 Annual Summary (Form 300A)
1.  **Review:** At the end of the calendar year, the VP of Operations must review the Form 300 for accuracy.
2.  **Certification:** The Executive Director (CEO) must certify that they have examined the OSHA 300 Log and that the annual summary (Form 300A) is correct and complete.
3.  **Posting:** The Form 300A must be posted electronically (via the company intranet/HR portal) and in any physical office locations from **February 1 to April 30** of the year following the covered year.
4.  **Retention:** The OSHA 300, 300A, and 301 forms must be retained for **five (5) years** following the end of the calendar year that these records cover.

#### 4.7 Electronic Submission to OSHA
If Study GRC Inc. reaches 20 or more employees, or meets specific high-hazard industry criteria defined by the Department of Labor, the Organization must electronically submit Form 300A data to OSHA via the Injury Tracking Application (ITA) by March 2nd of the following year.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Employees** | Immediately report any work-related injury or illness to their supervisor, regardless of severity. |
| **Managers** | Ensure the injured employee receives medical attention (if needed) and submit the initial *Accident/Injury Report* to HR/Ops within 24 hours. |
| **VP of Operations** | Serve as the Recordkeeper. Determine recordability, maintain the Log, and ensure privacy of sensitive medical data. |
| **Executive Director** | Certify the Annual Summary (Form 300A) as accurate. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Provide guidance on complex determinations of "work-relatedness" in remote or travel scenarios. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain an accurate OSHA 300 Log is a violation of federal law and may result in significant civil penalties from the Department of Labor.
*   **Falsification:** Knowingly falsifying this record is a criminal offense.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Employees who fail to report work-related injuries, or managers who discourage reporting, are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination, in accordance with the *Employee Handbook*.
