### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish the mandatory requirements for recording, verifying, and valuing volunteer time contributed to Study GRC Inc. Accurate tracking is essential for:
1.  **Grant Compliance:** Substantiating "Cost Sharing" or "Matching" funds as required by federal regulations (2 CFR § 200.306) and private grant agreements.
2.  **Financial Reporting:** Recognizing contributed services in financial statements in accordance with FASB ASC 958-605.
3.  **Membership Governance:** Verifying eligibility for "Supporting Member" (Voting) status based on service hours, pursuant to **Article II, Section 2.1** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all individuals performing unpaid work for Study GRC Inc., including:
*   **Board of Directors:** Time spent on governance, meetings, and committee work.
*   **Advisory Board:** Time spent consulting or mentoring.
*   **Operational Officers & Staff:** Unpaid executive and administrative work.
*   **General Volunteers:** Content creators, moderators, event staff, and technical contributors.

**Exclusions:**
*   Passive attendance at webinars or training sessions (unless serving as a moderator/facilitator).
*   Travel time to/from volunteer sites, unless explicitly authorized by a specific grant agreement.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **In-Kind Contribution** | Non-monetary contributions of goods or services. For this standard, specifically refers to volunteer labor. |
| **Cost Sharing (Match)** | The portion of project costs not paid by Federal funds. Volunteer hours are often used to meet this requirement. |
| **Independent Sector Rate** | The estimated dollar value of volunteer time (national or state average) published annually by Independent Sector, used for general volunteer valuation. |
| **Specialized Skills** | Services requiring technical expertise (e.g., legal, IT security, accounting) that would otherwise need to be purchased. |
| **Substantiation** | Evidence sufficient to satisfy an auditor that the work was performed (e.g., timesheets, logs, work products). |

### 4. Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 Mandatory Recording
All volunteers seeking recognition for their time (for membership status or grant match) must record their hours. Hours not recorded in the official system within 30 days of service performance may be deemed ineligible for verification.

#### 4.2 Required Data Fields
To satisfy audit requirements under 2 CFR § 200.306(b), the Tracking Log (whether digital or spreadsheet) must contain the following specific data points:
1.  **Volunteer Name:** Full legal name.
2.  **Date of Service:** Specific date (ranges are not permitted for grant reporting).
3.  **Duration:** Hours and minutes (e.g., 1.5 hours).
4.  **Description of Activity:** A clear, non-generic description of the work performed (e.g., "Drafted Cybersecurity Policy," "Moderated Community Forum," not just "Volunteering").
5.  **Grant/Project Code:** (If applicable) The specific project the work supported.
6.  **Certification:** A signature (digital or physical) by the volunteer attesting to the accuracy of the time.
7.  **Supervisory Approval:** A signature or digital approval by a Director or Officer verifying the work was performed.

#### 4.3 Valuation of Hours
The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) is responsible for assigning monetary value to volunteer hours based on the following hierarchy:

*   **Tier 1: General Volunteer Time:** Valued at the current "Independent Sector" estimated value of volunteer time for the State of Texas.
*   **Tier 2: Specialized Professional Services:** Valued at the volunteer’s standard professional billing rate *only if*:
    1.  The service requires specialized skills (e.g., legal, coding, accounting);
    2.  The volunteer possesses those skills; and
    3.  The volunteer provides a standard rate sheet or employment verification letter to HR.

#### 4.4 Verification Workflow
1.  **Submission:** Volunteer submits hours via the Organization’s designated portal (or Form HR-FRM-005) by the 5th of the following month.
2.  **Review:** The Department Head or Committee Chair reviews the entry for accuracy.
3.  **Approval:** Approved entries are locked in the system.
4.  **Rejection:** Entries lacking sufficient description or evidence of work will be returned for correction.

#### 4.5 Supporting Member Status
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article II**, the Secretary shall review the Volunteer Hours Tracking Log annually in January. Volunteers who meet the minimum hour threshold defined in the *Membership Policy Manual* shall be designated as "Supporting Members" with voting rights for the upcoming year.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer** | Accurately records time, provides descriptions, and submits logs by deadlines. |
| **Supervisor / Dept Head** | Verifies that work was performed and approves logs. Ensures descriptions are audit-ready. |
| **CFO** | Determines valuation rates (Tier 1 vs. Tier 2). Books in-kind revenue/expense to the General Ledger. |
| **Grant Manager** | Allocates verified hours to specific grant matching requirements. |
| **Secretary** | Uses the log to certify Voting Membership eligibility. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Falsification of Records:** Intentionally inflating volunteer hours constitutes fraud.
*   **Consequence:** Immediate termination of volunteer status, revocation of membership, and potential legal action if the falsified hours were used for federal grant reporting (False Claims Act liability).

**Failure to Log:**
*   **Consequence:** Hours will not be counted toward "Supporting Member" voting rights or grant matching. Retroactive credit is generally not permitted after the fiscal quarter closes.
