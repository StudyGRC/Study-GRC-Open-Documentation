### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **UBIT Activity Tracking Log** is to systematically identify, record, and analyze all revenue-generating activities of Study GRC Inc. to determine potential Unrelated Business Income Tax (UBIT) liability. This record ensures compliance with Internal Revenue Code (IRC) §§ 511-514 and facilitates the accurate preparation of IRS Form 990-T.

This log serves as the primary evidentiary record to distinguish between tax-exempt function income and unrelated business income (UBI), protecting the Organization’s 501(c)(3) status by monitoring the extent of non-exempt commercial activities.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all departments, committees, and operational units of Study GRC Inc. that generate revenue through means other than direct charitable contributions or government grants.

**In Scope:**
*   Sale of merchandise (e-commerce).
*   Advertising income (website, newsletters, events).
*   Corporate sponsorship payments.
*   Service fees (consulting, specific training workshops).
*   Rental income (if applicable).
*   Debt-financed income.

**Exclusions:**
*   Pure charitable donations (Section 170(c)).
*   Government grants.
*   Membership dues (unless providing specific commercial benefits).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **UBI (Unrelated Business Income)** | Income from a trade or business, regularly carried on, that is not substantially related to the charitable, educational, or scientific purpose of the organization. |
| **Trade or Business** | Any activity conducted for the production of income from selling goods or performing services. |
| **Regularly Carried On** | Activities that show a frequency and continuity, and are pursued in a manner similar to comparable commercial activities of non-exempt organizations. |
| **Substantially Related** | An activity that contributes importantly to the accomplishment of the Organization's exempt purpose (other than through the production of funds). |
| **Qualified Sponsorship Payment** | A payment where there is no arrangement or expectation that the payor will receive any substantial return benefit other than the use or acknowledgment of the name or logo (i.e., no advertising). |
| **Fragmented Activity** | The IRS rule allowing the separation of taxable activities (e.g., advertising sales) from exempt activities (e.g., educational content) within the same overall project. |

### 4. Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 The Three-Part Test Analysis
For every revenue stream entered into the Log, the Finance Department must apply the IRS Three-Part Test. If all three apply, the income is likely taxable unless an exception exists:
1.  **Trade or Business:** Is the Organization selling goods or services?
2.  **Regularly Carried On:** Is the activity frequent (e.g., a permanent web store vs. an annual bake sale)?
3.  **Not Substantially Related:** Does the activity *itself* advance the mission (education/GRC knowledge), or does it merely raise money?

#### 4.2 Required Data Fields
The UBIT Activity Tracking Log (maintained in the Organization's secure financial system) must contain the following data fields for each revenue stream:

*   **Activity ID:** Unique identifier for the revenue stream.
*   **Description:** Detailed nature of the activity (e.g., "Banner Ads on Study GRC Homepage").
*   **Gross Revenue:** Total income received before expenses.
*   **Direct Expenses:** Expenses directly connected to the production of that specific income.
*   **Allocated Indirect Expenses:** Portion of overhead (salaries, rent, software) reasonably allocated to the activity.
*   **UBI Determination:** (Yes/No/Partial).
*   **Exception Code:** (See Section 4.4).
*   **Net UBI:** (Gross Revenue minus Allowable Deductions).

#### 4.3 Expense Allocation Methodology
To minimize tax liability, Study GRC Inc. shall track expenses using the "Proximate and Primary" standard:
*   **Direct Costs:** Costs that exist *solely* because of the taxable activity (e.g., cost of goods sold for non-educational merch, commissions paid to ad salespeople).
*   **Dual-Use Allocation:** For resources used for both exempt and taxable purposes (e.g., the CEO's time or server costs), expenses must be allocated on a reasonable basis (e.g., time spent, square footage, or bandwidth usage).

#### 4.4 Exception Codes (Safe Harbors)
Revenue streams that meet the Three-Part Test may still be exempt. The Log must cite one of the following codes if excluding income from UBIT:
*   **[VOL] Volunteer Labor:** Substantially all work (85%+) is performed by uncompensated volunteers.
*   **[CON] Convenience:** Activity is carried on primarily for the convenience of members/students (e.g., a cafeteria).
*   **[DON] Donated Merchandise:** Selling merchandise that was substantially all donated (e.g., thrift shop model).
*   **[QSP] Qualified Sponsorship:** Acknowledgment of a donor without qualitative or comparative language, price information, or calls to action.
*   **[ROY] Royalties:** Passive income for the use of IP.

#### 4.5 Specific Activity Guidelines

**4.5.1 Advertising vs. Sponsorship**
*   **Taxable Advertising:** Messages containing qualitative language ("Best GRC Tool"), price information, or calls to action ("Buy Now"). This must be logged as UBI.
*   **Non-Taxable Sponsorship:** Messages containing only the logo, slogan, and contact info. This is logged as Exempt QSP.

**4.5.2 Merchandise Sales**
*   **Related (Exempt):** Items with educational value (e.g., GRC textbooks, study guides).
*   **Unrelated (Taxable):** Items with no causal relationship to the mission (e.g., pet accessories with the logo), unless the Volunteer Labor exception applies.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Final authority on UBI determination; approves the Log prior to Form 990-T filing; ensures expense allocation methodologies are reasonable and consistent. |
| **Controller / Finance Manager** | Maintains the Log; performs monthly reviews of new revenue streams; calculates expense allocations. |
| **Operational Officers (e.g., VP of Education)** | Must notify Finance *prior* to launching new revenue-generating initiatives (e.g., new merch lines, new ad slots) to allow for UBIT assessment. |
| **Audit Committee** | Reviews the UBIT Activity Tracking Log annually as part of the Form 990 review process defined in **Bylaws Article VIII**. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to accurately track and report UBI can result in:
1.  **IRS Penalties:** Fines for underpayment of estimated taxes and late filing.
2.  **Interest:** Charges on unpaid taxes.
3.  **Revocation of Status:** Excessive UBI can jeopardize the Organization’s 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status if the IRS determines the organization is primarily operating for commercial purposes.

Any employee or officer found to be intentionally concealing revenue streams or falsifying expense allocations will be subject to disciplinary action under the **Code of Ethics and Conduct**.
