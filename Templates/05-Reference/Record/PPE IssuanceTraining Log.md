### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a mandatory recording standard for the issuance of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and the verification of associated training for Study GRC Inc. personnel. This record serves as the primary evidence of compliance with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.132, ensuring that the Organization fulfills its duty to provide protective gear at no cost and ensures users understand its proper application, limitations, and maintenance. This log supports the Organization's commitment to accountability and risk management as outlined in the Master EHS Policy.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, and contractors ("Personnel") who are required to wear PPE based on a Hazard Assessment.
*   **Inclusions:** Physical events (e.g., setup/teardown), IT equipment disposal/handling, and health-related protocols (e.g., communicable disease prevention).
*   **Exclusions:** Standard remote office work where no specific physical hazards exist beyond ergonomic considerations.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **PPE** | Personal Protective Equipment. Gear designed to protect the wearer's body from injury or infection (e.g., gloves, masks, safety glasses, high-visibility vests). |
| **Issuance** | The formal act of providing PPE to a specific individual for their exclusive or temporary use. |
| **Donning/Doffing** | The procedures for properly putting on (donning) and taking off (doffing) PPE to ensure effectiveness and prevent cross-contamination. |
| **Limitations** | The specific boundaries of protection provided by a piece of equipment (e.g., a dust mask does not protect against chemical vapors). |

### 4. Procedures and Recording Standards

#### 4.1 Issuance Requirements
In accordance with OSHA regulations, Study GRC Inc. must provide required PPE at no cost to the worker.
4.1.1 **Condition Check:** Prior to issuance, the issuer must inspect the PPE to ensure it is sanitary and in reliable condition.
4.1.2 **Fit Test:** Where applicable (e.g., respirators or specific eye protection), the issuer must ensure the PPE fits the individual user properly.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Training Components
Issuance of equipment alone is insufficient. Before performing work requiring PPE, the recipient must be trained on the following. This training is verified by their signature on the Log (Section 9).
1.  **When** PPE is necessary.
2.  **What** PPE is necessary.
3.  **How** to properly don, doff, adjust, and wear PPE.
4.  **Limitations** of the PPE.
5.  **Care**, maintenance, useful life, and disposal of the PPE.

#### 4.3 Retraining Triggers
Retraining and re-logging are required if:
*   Changes in the workplace render previous training obsolete.
*   Changes in the types of PPE to be used render previous training obsolete.
*   Inadequacies in an affected user's knowledge or use of the assigned PPE indicate that the user has not retained the requisite understanding or skill.

#### 4.4 Record Retention
Completed logs must be retained by the Operations Department for a minimum of five (5) years following the separation of the employee or volunteer, or as required by the Records Retention Schedule (GOV-STD-013).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Supervisor / Event Lead** | Identifies required PPE, conducts training, issues equipment, and ensures the Log is signed and archived. |
| **Recipient (Staff/Volunteer)** | Attends training, inspects PPE before each use, uses PPE as trained, and signs the Log acknowledging receipt and understanding. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Audits PPE Logs for events to ensure compliance with safety standards and volunteer protection protocols. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation. Personnel refusing to wear required PPE or sign the Issuance/Training Log will be prohibited from performing the associated tasks or entering the designated hazard area.
