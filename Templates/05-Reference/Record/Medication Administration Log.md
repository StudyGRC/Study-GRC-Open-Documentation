### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Medication Administration Log (MAL)** is to create an immutable, accurate, and time-stamped record of all pharmaceutical agents administered to participants under the supervision of Study GRC Inc. This record serves to:
1.  Prevent medication errors (e.g., missed doses, double dosing, or incorrect dosage).
2.  Ensure continuity of care for participants, specifically minors in youth programs.
3.  Provide a legal audit trail demonstrating "duty of care" and compliance with parental/guardian instructions and medical authorizations.
4.  Satisfy documentation requirements for liability insurance and Texas youth protection standards.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all Study GRC Inc. events, workshops, and programs—whether in-person or hybrid—where the Organization assumes temporary custody or supervision of participants (specifically minors).

*   **In Scope:**
    *   Prescription medications provided by parents/guardians.
    *   Over-the-Counter (OTC) medications (e.g., acetaminophen, ibuprofen, antihistamines) authorized for administration.
    *   Emergency medications (e.g., EpiPens, Inhalers).
*   **Out of Scope:**
    *   Self-administered medications by adult participants (18+) who retain full capacity.
    *   General first aid supplies not classified as medication (e.g., bandages, ice packs), which are recorded in the *Incident/Injury Report*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Designated Health Officer (DHO)** | The specific staff member or volunteer authorized by the Event Director to manage and administer medications. Must be background-checked and trained. |
| **MAR** | Medication Administration Record. The industry-standard term for the log used to track drug administration. |
| **Five Rights** | The standard safety protocol for medication: Right Patient, Right Drug, Right Dose, Right Route, Right Time. |
| **PRN** | *Pro re nata* (Latin). Medication administered only "as needed" (e.g., for a headache) rather than on a fixed schedule. |
| **Adverse Reaction** | Any unexpected or dangerous reaction to a drug. |

### 4. Procedures and Recording Standards

#### 4.1 Pre-Requisites for Logging
No entry shall be made in this log, and no medication administered, without a corresponding **Medical Treatment Authorization Form** signed by a parent or legal guardian on file. The DHO must verify the physical medication matches the authorization form before the event begins.

#### 4.2 The "Five Rights" Verification
Prior to administering medication and creating a log entry, the DHO must verbally or mentally verify:
1.  **Right Participant:** Verify identity (name badge/photo).
2.  **Right Medication:** Check the label against the Authorization Form.
3.  **Right Dose:** Verify the amount/strength.
4.  **Right Route:** Verify method (oral, topical, etc.).
5.  **Right Time:** Verify the scheduled time or PRN interval.

#### 4.3 Required Data Fields
The Medication Administration Log must contain the following specific data points for every single dose administered:

1.  **Participant Name:** Full legal name.
2.  **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD format.
3.  **Time:** 24-hour format (e.g., 14:30) indicating exactly when the medication was taken.
4.  **Medication Name:** Brand or generic name (e.g., "Tylenol/Acetaminophen").
5.  **Dosage Administered:** Exact amount (e.g., "500mg" or "1 tablet").
6.  **Reason for Administration:** Required for PRN medications (e.g., "Complaint of headache").
7.  **Route:** (e.g., Oral, Injection, Inhalation).
8.  **Administered By:** Signature or Initials of the DHO.
9.  **Comments/Observations:** Any notes regarding the participant's status (e.g., "Participant vomited 10 mins later" or "Pain relief reported").

#### 4.4 Refusal or Missed Doses
If a participant refuses medication or a scheduled dose is missed:
1.  Record the time the dose *should* have been given.
2.  Circle the time or mark "MISSED/REFUSED" clearly in the log.
3.  Document the reason in the comments (e.g., "Participant refused due to nausea").
4.  Notify the Event Director and Parent/Guardian immediately.

#### 4.5 Medication Error Protocol
If an error occurs (wrong dose, wrong time, wrong participant):
1.  **Do not** erase or white-out the log entry.
2.  Draw a single line through the entry, write "ERROR," and initial it.
3.  Record the actual events on a new line.
4.  Initiate the *Incident/Injury Reporting Procedure* immediately.
5.  Contact emergency services if the error poses a health risk.

#### 4.6 Record Security and Retention
*   **Active Use:** During events, the Log must be kept in a secure, non-public location (e.g., locked box or supervised health station) to protect participant privacy (HIPAA/Privacy considerations).
*   **Archival:** Upon event conclusion, physical logs must be digitized and stored in the secure Organization record repository.
*   **Retention Period:** Logs involving minors must be retained until the minor reaches the age of majority (18) plus two (2) years, or for seven (7) years, whichever is longer, in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Designated Health Officer (DHO)** | Physically administers medication, verifies the "Five Rights," and legibly completes the Log entry immediately upon administration. |
| **Event Director** | Ensures blank Logs are available, designates the DHO, and audits the Log at the end of each day for completeness. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Maintains the master template and ensures training on proper documentation practices. |
| **Parent/Guardian** | Provides accurate medication information and authorizes administration via the *Medical Treatment Authorization Form*. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to accurately maintain the Medication Administration Log constitutes a violation of safety protocols and the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.
*   **Falsification of Records:** Intentionally falsifying a medical log is grounds for immediate termination of employment or volunteer service and may result in legal action.
*   **Negligence:** Failure to log medications may result in the loss of indemnification protection under **Bylaws Article VII**, as such failure may be deemed acting outside of "good faith."
