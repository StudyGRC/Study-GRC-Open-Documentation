### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Member Engagement Activity Tracking Log** is to establish a standardized method for recording, verifying, and valuing the non-financial contributions of members and volunteers. This record serves two critical governance and compliance functions:
1.  **Governance Eligibility:** It provides the evidentiary basis for determining "Supporting Member" status and voting rights eligibility pursuant to **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1**, for members qualifying through volunteer service rather than financial dues.
2.  **Federal Grant Compliance:** It substantiates "Third-Party In-Kind Contributions" (Cost Sharing or Matching) in accordance with **2 CFR § 200.306**, allowing the Organization to leverage volunteer time against federal grant matching requirements.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all **Supporting Members**, **Board Directors**, **Operational Officers**, and **Volunteers** performing services for Study GRC Inc.
*   **Inclusions:** Committee work, content creation, mentorship, event staffing, technical development, and administrative support.
*   **Exclusions:** Passive participation (e.g., attending a webinar without an active role), general networking, or activities not directly advancing the Organization's exempt purpose.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Supporting Member** | A voting member who has demonstrated commitment through financial contribution OR volunteer service as defined in the Membership Policy Manual and Bylaws Article II. |
| **In-Kind Contribution** | The value of non-cash contributions (goods or services) provided by third parties to the Organization. |
| **Grant Match** | The portion of project costs not paid by Federal funds (unless otherwise authorized by Federal statute). |
| **Substantiation** | The requirement to provide proof (time logs, work products) that the volunteer service was actually performed. |
| **Independent Sector Rate** | The estimated dollar value of volunteer time used for financial reporting, unless the volunteer is performing their professional trade (e.g., a CPA doing an audit), in which case their professional rate applies. |

### 4. Standards and Log Structure

#### 4.1 Mandatory Data Fields
To satisfy audit requirements under 2 CFR § 200.306(b)(4) and Bylaws Article II, the Activity Tracking Log must contain the following specific data points for every entry:

1.  **Volunteer Name:** Full legal name (matching membership records).
2.  **Date of Service:** Specific date(s) the activity occurred.
3.  **Activity Category:** (See Section 4.2).
4.  **Description of Duties:** A brief narrative of work performed (e.g., "Drafted Section 4 of IS Policy," "Moderated Discord Q&A for 2 hours").
5.  **Hours Contributed:** Time spent, recorded in 0.25-hour increments.
6.  **Supervisor/Verifier:** The Board Member or Committee Chair responsible for oversight.
7.  **Grant Association:** (Optional) If the work is tied to a specific grant project (e.g., "NSF-2024-GRC"), it must be tagged.

#### 4.2 Authorized Activity Categories
Only activities that are "integral and necessary" to the project or organization may be logged for Grant Match or Voting Eligibility.

*   **Governance:** Board meetings, committee meetings, strategic planning.
*   **Content Development:** Writing articles, creating courseware, coding open-source tools.
*   **Community Support:** Moderating forums, answering technical GRC questions, mentorship sessions.
*   **Operations:** IT maintenance, bookkeeping assistance, event coordination.

#### 4.3 Valuation Standard
For the purpose of Grant Matching financial reports:
*   **General Volunteer Time:** Shall be valued at the current **Independent Sector Value of Volunteer Time** for the State of Texas.
*   **Professional Services:** Pursuant to 2 CFR § 200.306(e), services requiring specialized skills (e.g., legal, accounting, cybersecurity engineering) provided by a professional in that field may be valued at that individual's standard professional rate, provided the rate is reasonable and customary.

#### 4.4 Verification Protocol
*   **Self-Reporting:** Members are responsible for logging their hours via the Organization’s designated portal (e.g., Volunteer Management System or Secure Form) by the 5th business day of the following month.
*   **Audit Trail:** The Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or Secretary must perform a quarterly review of the log. Entries lacking sufficient description or verification may be rejected for both voting eligibility and grant matching.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Supporting Member / Volunteer** | Accurately record hours and activity descriptions within 30 days of service performance. |
| **Committee Chair / Manager** | Validate the accuracy of logs submitted by team members; certify that work was performed satisfactorily. |
| **Secretary** | Review the log annually (January) to certify the list of **Voting Members** for the Annual Meeting pursuant to Bylaws Section 2.6. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Calculate the financial value of logged hours for inclusion in the Annual Financial Report and Federal Grant Reports (SF-425). |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Maintain the integrity of the logging system and train members on proper recording procedures. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Voting Rights:** Failure to log sufficient activity hours (as defined in the Membership Policy Manual) may result in the reclassification of a member from "Supporting Member" (Voting) to "Community Participant" (Non-Voting) for the upcoming fiscal year.
*   **Grant Disallowance:** Inaccurate or fraudulent logging of hours that leads to a federal audit finding or disallowance of costs will result in immediate termination of membership and potential legal action for fraud.
*   **Record Retention:** These logs are financial records and must be retained for a minimum of three (3) years from the date of submission of the final expenditure report to the funding agency, in accordance with **2 CFR § 200.334**.
