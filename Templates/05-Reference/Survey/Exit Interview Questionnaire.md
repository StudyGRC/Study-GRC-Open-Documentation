### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Exit Interview Questionnaire** is to gather honest, constructive feedback from departing employees and key volunteers regarding their experience with Study GRC Inc. This data is utilized to:
1.  Identify trends in employee retention and turnover.
2.  Uncover potential compliance, ethical, or cultural risks (including harassment or discrimination).
3.  Assess the effectiveness of leadership, remote work infrastructure, and the "Kindness and Generosity" culture.
4.  Drive continuous improvement in organizational operations and the employee lifecycle.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All employees (full-time, part-time) and key volunteers (e.g., Committee Chairs, Advisory Board members) voluntarily separating from the Organization.
*   **Exclusions:** Involuntary terminations for cause (gross misconduct), though a modified version may be administered at the discretion of Legal Counsel.
*   **Administration:** This questionnaire may be administered electronically or verbally by a designated neutral party (e.g., HR representative or a Board Director if the departing member reports to the CEO).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Constructive Discharge** | A situation where an employee resigns because the employer created a hostile work environment or imposed intolerable working conditions. |
| **Protected Activity** | Reporting violations of law, ethics, or policy (whistleblowing) which is protected from retaliation. |
| **Aggregated Data** | Data combined from multiple sources where individual identities are removed to show general trends. |

### 4. Standard Questionnaire Structure

The Exit Interview shall consist of the following sections. The Interviewer or the Electronic Form must present these questions neutrally.

#### 4.1 Part I: Reason for Departure
*Please indicate the primary and secondary factors influencing your decision to leave.*

1.  **Primary Reason:** (Select one: Career Opportunity, Compensation/Benefits, Management, Work-Life Balance, Organizational Culture, Personal Reasons, Other).
2.  **Trigger Event:** Was there a specific event that caused you to start looking for a new opportunity?
3.  **Opportunity Comparison:** What does your new position offer that Study GRC Inc. does not?

#### 4.2 Part II: Role, Enablement, and Remote Work
*Reflecting on your daily duties and the remote-first environment.*

1.  **Job Description Accuracy:** Did your actual duties align with the job description provided during hiring? If not, how did they differ?
2.  **Resources and Tools:** Did you have the necessary equipment, software, and access to perform your job effectively?
3.  **Remote Culture:** Did you feel connected to the team despite working remotely? How effective were the communication channels (Slack, Zoom, etc.)?
4.  **Training:** Were you provided with adequate training and professional development opportunities?

#### 4.3 Part III: Management and Leadership
*Feedback regarding your direct supervisor and executive leadership.*

1.  **Support:** Did your manager provide clear goals and consistent support?
2.  **Feedback:** Did you receive regular, constructive feedback on your performance?
3.  **Recognition:** Did you feel your contributions were recognized and valued?
4.  **Openness:** Did you feel comfortable raising concerns or ideas to leadership without fear of negative consequences?

#### 4.4 Part IV: Culture, Ethics, and Compliance
*Study GRC Inc. prioritizes a culture of "Kindness and Generosity" and strict adherence to GRC principles.*

1.  **Core Values:** Did you experience the "Director of Kindness and Generosity" initiatives in a meaningful way?
2.  **Inclusion:** Did you feel the environment was inclusive and free from discrimination or harassment?
3.  **Ethics/Compliance (Crucial):** During your employment, did you witness or suspect any unethical conduct, fraud, conflict of interest, or violation of law that has not been addressed?
    *   *Note: If "Yes," the interviewer must immediately pause and refer to the **Whistleblower Investigation Procedure**.*
4.  **Safety (Youth Protection):** (If applicable) Did you observe any gaps in our Youth Protection protocols regarding interactions with minors?

#### 4.5 Part V: Final Comments
1.  **Recommendation:** Would you recommend Study GRC Inc. to a friend as a place to work or volunteer? Why or why not?
2.  **Change Agent:** If you could change one thing about the Organization, what would it be?
3.  **Open Floor:** Is there anything else you would like to share?

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Departing Member** | Encouraged to provide candid, honest feedback. Participation is voluntary but highly recommended. |
| **HR / Operations** | Administer the questionnaire, ensure confidentiality, and store records securely. |
| **Executive Director** | Review aggregated feedback reports to identify strategic improvements. |
| **Board of Directors** | Review anonymized exit data annually to assess executive performance and organizational health. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

*   **Confidentiality:** Individual responses to this questionnaire will be kept confidential to the fullest extent possible. Specific comments will only be shared with management in an aggregated or anonymized format, unless a specific legal or safety risk (e.g., fraud, harassment, child safety) is disclosed that requires investigation.
*   **Non-Retaliation:** Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual for feedback provided during an exit interview.
*   **Data Retention:** Exit Interview records shall be retained in accordance with the **Records Retention Schedule (HR-STD-013)** for a period of 3 years post-separation, after which they will be destroyed or permanently anonymized.
*   **Waiver:** This questionnaire is for informational purposes only and does not constitute a waiver of any legal rights by the Organization or the Employee.
