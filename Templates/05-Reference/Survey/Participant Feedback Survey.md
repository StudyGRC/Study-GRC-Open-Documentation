### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to standardize the collection, analysis, and utilization of participant feedback across all Study GRC Inc. programs, events, and educational offerings. This standard ensures that the Organization:
1.  Quantifiably measures mission effectiveness in "fostering a community-driven ecosystem" per **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**.
2.  Complies with federal grant reporting requirements regarding program performance monitoring (2 CFR § 200.329).
3.  Protects the privacy of participants, particularly minors, in accordance with the Organization's *Data Privacy Policy* and COPPA regulations.
4.  Provides a consistent feedback loop for the continuous improvement of curriculum and community culture.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all external-facing programs executed by Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to:
*   Live webinars and workshops.
*   Asynchronous training courses.
*   Community events and conferences.
*   Local Chapter meetings.

**Exclusions:**
*   Internal Board of Directors self-assessments.
*   Employee or volunteer satisfaction surveys (covered under HR policies).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **NPS (Net Promoter Score)** | A market research metric that is based on a single survey question asking respondents to rate the likelihood that they would recommend a company, product, or service to a friend or colleague. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information. Data that can be used to identify a specific individual. |
| **Learning Outcome** | A specific statement describing what a learner is expected to know, understand, or be able to do at the end of a learning activity. |
| **COPPA** | Children's Online Privacy Protection Act. Federal law regulating the collection of personal information from children under 13. |
| **Likert Scale** | A psychometric scale commonly involved in research that employs questionnaires (e.g., 1 to 5 rating). |

### 4. Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1 Survey Design Principles
All feedback instruments deployed by the Organization must adhere to the following design principles:
1.  **Neutrality:** Questions must be phrased neutrally to avoid leading the respondent.
2.  **Brevity:** Surveys should take no longer than 5 minutes to complete, unless part of a longitudinal study approved by the Board.
3.  **Accessibility:** All digital surveys must be WCAG 2.2 AA compliant (screen reader accessible, high contrast).
4.  **Age Appropriateness:** Surveys intended for K-12 audiences must use simplified language and must **not** collect PII unless verifiable parental consent is on file.

#### 4.2 Data Privacy and Anonymity
*   **Default Anonymity:** By default, feedback surveys shall be anonymous.
*   **Optional Identification:** If PII (name/email) is requested (e.g., for a prize drawing or follow-up), it must be an optional field with a clear disclaimer linking to the *Privacy Policy*.
*   **Youth Protection:** For programs involving participants under age 13, no free-text fields allowing PII entry shall be used without direct moderation or parental consent, in compliance with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.

#### 4.3 Standard Survey Instrument (Mandatory Questions)
To ensure data consistency across programs, the following "Core Questions" must be included in every post-event/post-course survey. Program Managers may add specific questions *after* this core set.

**Section A: Satisfaction & NPS**
1.  *On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend this [Course/Event] to a friend or colleague?* (NPS)
2.  *Overall, how would you rate the quality of this [Course/Event]?* (1-5 Stars)

**Section B: Learning & Effectiveness (Mission Alignment)**
3.  *To what extent do you agree with the statement: "I gained new knowledge or skills that I can apply to my studies or career."* (Likert: Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)
4.  *Did the content help simplify a complex GRC topic for you?* (Yes / Somewhat / No)

**Section C: Instructor/Delivery**
5.  *The instructor/speaker was knowledgeable about the subject matter.* (Likert: Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)
6.  *The learning environment (virtual or physical) was inclusive and welcoming.* (Likert: Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)

**Section D: Open Feedback**
7.  *What was the most valuable part of this experience?* (Free Text)
8.  *How can we improve this experience for future learners?* (Free Text)

#### 4.4 Administration and Timing
*   **Timing:** Surveys must be distributed within 24 hours of the conclusion of a live event or immediately upon completion of an asynchronous module.
*   **Platform:** Surveys must be hosted on Organization-approved platforms (e.g., Microsoft Forms, SurveyMonkey) that meet security standards defined by the CISO.

#### 4.5 Analysis and Reporting
1.  **Aggregation:** Data must be aggregated monthly by the *VP of Education & Training*.
2.  **Thresholds:**
    *   Any session receiving an average rating below 3.5/5.0 triggers an automatic review by the *Director of Kindness and Generosity* and the *VP of Education*.
    *   Any report of harassment or safety violations in free-text fields must be immediately escalated to the *Legal/Compliance* team per the *Whistleblower Protection Policy*.
3.  **Grant Reporting:** Aggregated "Knowledge Gain" metrics must be prepared for the *Treasurer* and *Grant Manager* for inclusion in the Annual Report and federal grant performance reports (2 CFR 200.328).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Owner of the survey standard; responsible for analyzing learning outcomes and curriculum adjustments. |
| **Program Manager** | Responsible for distributing the survey link and ensuring high response rates. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Reviews feedback regarding community culture, inclusivity, and "welcoming environment" metrics. |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Audits survey tools and data collection practices for compliance with GDPR, CCPA, and COPPA. |
| **Executive Director** | Reviews aggregated feedback quarterly to assess strategic alignment. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this standard—specifically the fabrication of survey data or the unauthorized collection of PII from minors—constitutes a violation of the *Code of Ethics* and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
