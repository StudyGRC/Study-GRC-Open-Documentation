### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to define the mandatory components, delivery mechanisms, and quality standards for the **Member Onboarding Welcome Packet**. This toolkit ensures that all new members—regardless of classification—receive consistent, accurate, and timely information regarding their rights, responsibilities, and resources within Study GRC Inc.

This document mitigates the risk of member disenfranchisement, ensures compliance with **Bylaws Article II (Membership)** regarding voting rights notification, and establishes the cultural tone of the organization from the first point of contact.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to the onboarding process for all classes of membership defined in **Bylaws Section 2.1**:
1.  **Community Participants** (Non-Voting)
2.  **Supporting Members** (Voting)
3.  **Honorary Members** (Voting)

**Exclusions:** This standard does not apply to one-time donors who do not seek membership status, or casual website visitors who have not registered for community access.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Welcome Packet** | A digital collection of documents, links, and resources delivered to a new member upon acceptance of their registration. |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a member who has fulfilled all financial obligations (if applicable) and is not currently subject to disciplinary action or suspension. |
| **Voting Member** | A Supporting or Honorary Member entitled to vote on Organization matters, specifically the election of Directors and Fundamental Actions. |
| **DKG** | Director of Kindness and Generosity; the Operational Officer responsible for community culture and member experience. |

### 4. Standards and Procedures

#### 4.1. Mandatory Packet Components
The Welcome Packet must be generated and delivered digitally. It must contain the following distinct modules, tailored to the specific Membership Class.

**4.1.1. The "Mission & Culture" Module (All Members)**
*   **Welcome Letter:** A personalized message from the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or the President, reinforcing the mission to "remove financial barriers" and "simplify the GRC domain."
*   **Code of Conduct & Ethics:** A direct link to the *Community Code of Conduct* and *Anti-Harassment Policy*. The member must acknowledge that participation is contingent upon adherence to these standards.
*   **Resource Access Guide:** Instructions for accessing:
    *   The Open-Source Resource Library.
    *   Community communication channels (e.g., Slack, Discord, Forums).
    *   Learning Management System (LMS).

**4.1.2. The "Governance & Rights" Module (Voting Members Only)**
*   **Bylaws Access:** A direct link to the current *Bylaws of Study GRC Inc.*, specifically highlighting **Article II (Membership)** and **Article III (Board of Directors)**.
*   **Voting Rights Certification:** A clear statement confirming the member's status as a **Voting Member** (Supporting or Honorary).
*   **Election Cycle Information:** A summary of the Annual Meeting timeline (January), nomination procedures, and the "No Proxy Voting" rule per **Bylaws Section 2.3**.
*   **Transparency Portal:** A link to the public transparency page containing Annual Financial Reports and IRS Form 990s (per **Bylaws Section 8.2**).

**4.1.3. The "Administrative" Module (Supporting Members Only)**
*   **Tax Receipt:** A compliant receipt for membership dues, noting the tax-deductible nature of the contribution under IRS 501(c)(3) regulations (minus the fair market value of goods received, if any).
*   **Subscription Management:** Instructions on how to manage, upgrade, or cancel recurring financial contributions.

#### 4.2. Delivery Mechanism and Timing
*   **Automation:** The Welcome Packet must be triggered automatically via the Organization’s CRM or Membership Management System immediately upon successful registration or payment processing.
*   **Format:** The packet shall be delivered via email with secure links to a dedicated "New Member Portal" on the Study GRC website.
*   **Accessibility:** All documents within the packet must adhere to the **Digital Accessibility Policy (WCAG 2.2 AA)** to ensure inclusivity for members with disabilities.

#### 4.3. Verification of Receipt
To ensure compliance with notice requirements:
*   The system must log the timestamp of the Welcome Packet delivery.
*   For Voting Members, the system must request a digital acknowledgment (e.g., a "click-to-accept") confirming receipt of the Bylaws and Code of Conduct.

#### 4.4. Content Maintenance
*   **Review Cycle:** The DKG must review the Welcome Packet content bi-annually to ensure all links are active and policy references (e.g., Bylaws amendments) are current.
*   **Bylaws Updates:** If the Bylaws are amended per **Article XII**, the "Governance & Rights" module must be updated within 7 business days.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | **Owner.** Responsible for the tone, content creation, and overall user experience of the Welcome Packet. |
| **Secretary** | Responsible for verifying that the "Governance & Rights" module accurately reflects the current Bylaws and voting procedures. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Responsible for ensuring the secure delivery of the packet and the integrity of the digital acknowledgment logs. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Responsible for ensuring tax receipts in the "Administrative" module comply with IRS requirements. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to provide the Welcome Packet—specifically the Governance & Rights module to Voting Members—may result in challenges to the validity of Organization elections or disciplinary actions against the Organization for failure to provide proper notice.

Staff or volunteers found to be manually suppressing Welcome Packets or altering their contents without authorization may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of role.
