### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to define the scope, access protocols, and confidentiality protections of the Study GRC Inc. Employee Assistance Program (EAP). This resource is designed to support the mental health and well-being of our workforce by providing confidential, professional assistance for personal and work-related problems. This program aligns with the Organization's commitment to "fostering a community-driven ecosystem" by ensuring the health of the individuals driving that mission.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Eligible Users:** All full-time and part-time paid employees of Study GRC Inc., as well as their spouses/domestic partners and dependent children living in the same household.
*   **Geographic Scope:** Services are available to remote employees regardless of their location within the United States.
*   **Exclusions:** Independent contractors and volunteers are not eligible for paid EAP clinical services unless explicitly specified in their engagement agreement, though they may access public resource referrals provided by the Director of Kindness and Generosity.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **EAP (Employee Assistance Program)** | A voluntary, work-based program that offers free and confidential assessments, short-term counseling, referrals, and follow-up services to employees who have personal and/or work-related problems. |
| **Confidentiality** | The legal and ethical duty to protect the privacy of EAP users. Study GRC Inc. receives only aggregate statistical data and never receives names or details of individuals accessing the service without explicit written consent. |
| **Formal Referral** | A recommendation by a supervisor or HR for an employee to utilize EAP services, often based on performance issues. Note: Use of the EAP remains voluntary unless mandated as a condition of continued employment in a specific "Last Chance Agreement." |
| **HIPAA** | Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act; federal law protecting sensitive patient health information. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Program Overview and Cost
Study GRC Inc. provides the EAP at **zero cost** to the employee. The Organization pays the full premium for this service.
*   **Session Limits:** Eligible users are entitled to up to **[Insert Number, e.g., 3 or 5]** free counseling sessions per issue, per year.
*   **Beyond the Limit:** If long-term treatment is required beyond the free sessions, the EAP provider will facilitate a referral to a provider within the employee’s health insurance network. Costs incurred after the free sessions are the responsibility of the employee/insurance plan.

#### 4.2 Scope of Services
The EAP provides support for a wide range of issues, including but not limited to:
*   **Mental Health:** Anxiety, depression, stress management, and grief counseling.
*   **Substance Abuse:** Assessment and referral for alcohol or drug dependency.
*   **Family/Personal:** Marital conflict, parenting issues, and domestic violence support.
*   **Work-Life Services:** Financial planning, legal consultation (e.g., wills, landlord disputes), and elder care referrals.
*   **Crisis Intervention:** Immediate support following traumatic events.

#### 4.3 Confidentiality and Privacy
Confidentiality is the cornerstone of the EAP.
*   **Third-Party Administration:** The EAP is administered by an external third-party vendor. Study GRC Inc. does not own or store clinical records.
*   **Employer Knowledge:** Study GRC Inc. management and the Board of Directors **cannot** access information regarding who uses the EAP or why, except in the following rare circumstances:
    1.  The employee provides explicit written consent.
    2.  There is an imminent threat to the safety of the employee or others (Duty to Warn).
    3.  Suspected child or elder abuse (Mandatory Reporting requirements).
    4.  A court order requires disclosure.

#### 4.4 Accessing Services
*   **Availability:** Services are available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.
*   **Modality:** Due to the remote nature of Study GRC Inc., services are available via telephone, video telehealth, and secure text messaging.

#### 4.5 Management Referrals
*   **Informal Referral:** A manager may suggest the EAP to an employee who appears to be struggling. This is a supportive suggestion, not a disciplinary action.
*   **Performance Context:** While the EAP can assist with personal issues affecting work performance, utilizing the EAP does not exempt an employee from standard performance requirements or disciplinary procedures defined in the *Employee Handbook*.

#### 4.6 Non-Retaliation
Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee for utilizing EAP services or for seeking help for mental health or substance abuse issues. Any manager found retaliating against an employee for EAP usage will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Oversees the EAP vendor relationship; ensures staff are aware of the program; reviews aggregate utilization reports to assess organizational health trends without identifying individuals. |
| **Managers/Supervisors** | Promote EAP awareness; refer employees to EAP when appropriate; maintain strict confidentiality regarding any employee disclosures about EAP usage. |
| **Employees** | Responsible for self-referral and utilizing services when needed; responsible for any costs incurred beyond the free sessions provided by the EAP. |
| **EAP Vendor** | Provides clinical services; maintains HIPAA compliance; provides aggregate reporting to Study GRC Inc. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with the confidentiality provisions of this policy—specifically, any attempt by management to uncover the identity of EAP users without legal cause—will result in immediate disciplinary action.

This program is a benefit, not a contract of employment. Study GRC Inc. reserves the right to modify or terminate the EAP benefit at the discretion of the Board of Directors, provided proper notice is given to employees.
