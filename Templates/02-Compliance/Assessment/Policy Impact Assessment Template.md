### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Policy Impact Assessment (PIA)** is to evaluate the necessity, feasibility, and operational burden of proposed policies, standards, or significant procedural changes before they are adopted. This assessment ensures that Study GRC Inc. engages in responsible **Resource Planning** by quantifying the costs (financial, human capital, and technical) associated with compliance, thereby preventing "policy bloat" and ensuring alignment with the Organization’s mission and Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This assessment must be completed by the **Policy Author** or **Sponsor** for:
1.  All new Level 1 (Governance Policies) and Level 2 (Standards/Procedures) documents.
2.  Major revisions to existing documents that significantly alter operational requirements or resource allocations.

**Exclusions:**
*   Minor administrative updates (e.g., fixing typos, updating hyperlinks).
*   Level 3 Guidelines (unless they have significant IT infrastructure implications).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Compliance Burden** | The aggregate time, effort, and cost required for staff and volunteers to adhere to the proposed rule. |
| **Implementation Cost** | One-time costs required to launch the policy (e.g., purchasing software, conducting initial training). |
| **Maintenance Cost** | Recurring costs required to sustain compliance (e.g., annual license fees, ongoing audit hours). |
| **Stakeholder** | Any individual, group, or department (e.g., Volunteers, Board, IT, Finance) affected by the proposed policy. |

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Policy Author** | Completes this PIA with accurate data and realistic resource estimates. |
| **Subject Matter Expert (SME)** | Reviews the PIA to validate technical or legal assumptions (e.g., CISO reviews IT impacts). |
| **Department Head** | Certifies that the required resources (budget/time) are available or planned. |
| **Governance Committee** | Reviews the PIA alongside the policy draft to determine if the benefit outweighs the compliance burden. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Submission of a Policy Impact Assessment is a mandatory prerequisite for the ratification of any Level 1 or Level 2 document.
*   **Incomplete Assessments:** Drafts submitted without a completed PIA, or with vague resource planning (e.g., "TBD"), will be rejected by the Governance Committee.
*   **Post-Implementation Review:** If actual resource usage exceeds the PIA estimate by more than 25%, the policy must be flagged for immediate review and potential amendment.
