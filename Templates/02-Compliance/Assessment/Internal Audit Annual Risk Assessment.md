### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the **Internal Audit Annual Risk Assessment** is to establish a systematic, quantitative, and qualitative methodology for identifying, measuring, and prioritizing risks facing Study GRC Inc.

This assessment serves as the foundational document for developing the **Annual Internal Audit Plan**. By aligning audit resources with the highest risk areas—specifically focusing on Federal Grant compliance (2 CFR 200), Youth Protection, and Cybersecurity in a remote-first environment—this framework ensures the Organization fulfills its fiduciary duties and maintains compliance with Article VI of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This assessment methodology applies to the entire **Audit Universe** of Study GRC Inc., encompassing all operational, financial, technological, and governance functions.

**Inclusions:**
*   All grant-funded programs and associated compliance requirements.
*   Information Security and Data Privacy (NIST/GDPR/COPPA alignment).
*   Youth Protection protocols and K-12 engagement.
*   Financial reporting and donor management.
*   Remote workforce operations and Human Resources.

**Exclusions:**
*   This document defines the *methodology* for the assessment. The actual *results* (The Risk Register) are generated annually as a separate output.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit Universe** | The aggregate of all auditable units, processes, systems, and functions within the Organization. |
| **Inherent Risk** | The level of risk in a process or activity before considering the effectiveness of internal controls. |
| **Residual Risk** | The remaining risk after management has implemented internal controls. |
| **Risk Appetite** | The amount of risk the Board of Directors is willing to accept in pursuit of the Organization's mission. |
| **Impact** | The severity of the consequence if a risk event occurs (Financial, Reputational, Legal). |
| **Likelihood** | The probability that a risk event will occur within the audit cycle. |

### 4. Assessment Methodology

The Internal Audit function (or designated independent assessor) must perform this assessment annually, prior to the start of the new fiscal year, to finalize the Audit Plan.

#### 4.1 The Audit Universe Identification
The assessor must update the Audit Universe to reflect changes in the Organization’s structure. The Universe is categorized into four domains:

1.  **Strategic & Governance:** Board oversight, Bylaws compliance, strategic planning, and donor relations.
2.  **Operational:** Youth programs, volunteer management, remote work infrastructure, and event management.
3.  **Financial & Compliance:** Federal grant management (2 CFR 200), tax filings (Form 990), payroll, and procurement.
4.  **Technology & Security:** Cybersecurity (CISO domain), data privacy (COPPA/GDPR), and IT asset management.

#### 4.2 Risk Scoring Criteria
Each auditable unit within the Universe must be scored on a scale of 1 (Low) to 5 (High) across two dimensions: **Impact** and **Likelihood**.

**A. Impact Criteria (1-5)**
*   **Financial:** Potential loss >$50k (High) vs. <$1k (Low).
*   **Compliance:** Risk of losing 501(c)(3) status or Federal funding (High) vs. minor administrative error (Low).
*   **Reputational:** National negative media coverage/loss of trust (High) vs. internal dissatisfaction (Low).
*   **Safety:** Physical harm to a minor or staff member (Critical/High).

**B. Likelihood Criteria (1-5)**
*   **History:** Has this failed before?
*   **Complexity:** Is the process manual, complex, or subject to frequent change?
*   **Turnover:** Has there been significant leadership change in this area?
*   **Volume:** Does this process handle a high volume of transactions or data?

#### 4.3 Risk Calculation Formula
The Total Risk Score is calculated to determine the **Inherent Risk**:
> **Risk Score = (Impact Rating × 0.6) + (Likelihood Rating × 0.4)**

*Note: Impact is weighted higher due to the Organization's zero-tolerance policy regarding Youth Protection and Federal Compliance violations.*

#### 4.4 Management Input & Control Review
The assessor must interview Operational Officers (CEO, CFO, CISO, VP of Education) to validate scoring and assess the strength of existing controls.
*   **Strong Controls:** Deduct 1-2 points from the Inherent Risk score.
*   **Weak/Non-Existent Controls:** Add 1-2 points to the Inherent Risk score.
*   **Result:** This produces the **Residual Risk Score**.

#### 4.5 Prioritization (The Heat Map)
Auditable units are plotted on a Heat Map based on Residual Risk.
*   **Tier 1 (Critical):** Must be audited annually. (e.g., Youth Protection, Cyber Security, Single Audit Prep).
*   **Tier 2 (High):** Must be audited at least every 2 years.
*   **Tier 3 (Medium):** Audited every 3 years or via "spot checks."
*   **Tier 4 (Low):** Audited only if specific triggers occur or resources allow.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Reviews and approves the Risk Assessment methodology and the final Annual Audit Plan. Ensures alignment with Bylaws Article VI. |
| **Internal Audit Lead** | Facilitates the assessment, conducts interviews, scores risks, and drafts the Audit Plan. |
| **Operational Officers (C-Suite)** | Provide transparent data regarding process changes, control failures, and emerging risks during interviews. |
| **Board of Directors** | Sets the organizational Risk Appetite which guides the scoring thresholds. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to conduct this Annual Risk Assessment constitutes a violation of the Audit & Risk Committee Charter and compromises the Organization's ability to demonstrate "due care" in the management of Federal funds and charitable assets.

If high-risk areas (Tier 1) are identified but excluded from the Annual Audit Plan due to resource constraints, this decision must be explicitly documented and formally accepted by the Board of Directors in the meeting minutes.
