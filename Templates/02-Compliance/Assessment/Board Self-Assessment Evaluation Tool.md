### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish a mandatory, systematic process for the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") to evaluate its own performance, effectiveness, and compliance with its fiduciary duties.

This assessment is designed to:
1.  Ensure the Board is effectively overseeing the Organization’s mission as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**.
2.  Identify gaps in Board composition, skills, or governance processes.
3.  Demonstrate a commitment to good governance practices as recommended by the IRS (Form 990, Part VI) and the Texas Business Organizations Code ("TBOC").
4.  Foster a culture of continuous improvement and accountability among Directors.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to the **Board of Directors** as a collective body and to individual **Voting Directors**.

*   **Inclusions:** All elected Directors serving a term as defined in **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.2**.
*   **Exclusions:** This tool does not apply to the Advisory Board (governed by Bylaws Article VI) or Operational Officers, except where their interaction with the Board is being evaluated.

### 4. Assessment Standards & Methodology

#### 4.1 Frequency and Timing
The Board Self-Assessment must be conducted **annually**. The assessment period shall align with the end of the fiscal year to inform the nomination process for the Annual Meeting held in January (per **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5**).

#### 4.2 Administration and Anonymity
4.2.1 The Governance Committee (or Secretary, if no committee exists) shall administer the assessment via a secure, electronic survey platform.
4.2.2 Responses must remain **anonymous** to encourage candid feedback. Aggregated data will be presented to the Board for discussion.
4.2.3 Individual Director participation is mandatory as part of the Duty of Care.

#### 4.3 Evaluation Domains (The Tool)
The assessment shall evaluate performance across five (5) core domains. Directors must rate the following statements on a scale of 1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree), with an option for "N/A" or comments.

**Domain I: Mission and Strategy**
1.  The Board has a clear understanding of the Organization’s mission to advance GRC knowledge and remove financial barriers (**Bylaws Art. 1.3**).
2.  The Board effectively monitors the progress of the Strategic Plan.
3.  Board decisions are consistently aligned with the charitable and educational purposes of the Organization.

**Domain II: Fiduciary Oversight & Finance**
1.  The Board receives accurate, timely financial reports as required by **Bylaws Article VIII**.
2.  The Board understands the budget and asks appropriate questions regarding revenue and expenses.
3.  The Board ensures compliance with the Conflict of Interest Policy (**Bylaws Art. IX**) and reviews annual disclosures.
4.  The Board ensures adequate resources are available to support the Organization's programs.

**Domain III: Board Operations & Culture (Remote Context)**
1.  Board meetings are well-organized, with agendas and materials provided at least 10 days in advance (**Bylaws Art. II, Section 2.5**).
2.  The remote meeting technology facilitates effective communication and decision-making.
3.  Directors attend meetings prepared and actively participate in discussions.
4.  The Board culture encourages open, respectful debate and diverse viewpoints.
5.  Minutes accurately reflect the actions and decisions of the Board.

**Domain IV: Governance & Composition**
1.  The Board composition reflects the necessary skills, diversity, and expertise (e.g., GRC, Legal, Finance, Education) to govern effectively.
2.  The Board effectively manages the recruitment and orientation of new Directors.
3.  The distinction between Board governance and Operational management (**Bylaws Art. V**) is clear and respected.

**Domain V: Executive Oversight**
1.  The Board provides clear direction and support to the Executive Director (CEO).
2.  The Board conducts a formal performance evaluation of the Executive Director annually.
3.  Compensation for the Executive Director and key officers is reviewed for reasonableness and approved by independent Directors.

#### 4.4 Review and Action Plan
4.4.1 The results of the assessment must be compiled into a summary report.
4.4.2 The Board must dedicate one meeting (or a specific agenda item) to discuss the findings.
4.4.3 The Board must develop an **Action Plan** to address any areas scoring an average of 3.0 or lower, or where significant divergence in opinion exists.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Governance Committee** | Designs the survey, distributes the tool, compiles results, and drafts the Action Plan. |
| **Board President** | Leads the Board discussion regarding the assessment results and ensures the Action Plan is executed. |
| **Board Secretary** | Ensures the completion of the assessment is recorded in the minutes (without recording specific confidential responses). |
| **Individual Directors** | Complete the assessment honestly and within the designated timeframe. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to conduct this assessment constitutes a deviation from the Organization's governance standards and may be cited in the Annual Governance Review.

Individual Directors who consistently fail to participate in the self-assessment process may be subject to review by the Governance Committee, which could lead to a recommendation for removal for cause (failure to perform duties) under **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**.
