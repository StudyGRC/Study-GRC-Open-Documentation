### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to establish a mandatory, standardized framework for evaluating the risk of non-compliance posed by subrecipients prior to the issuance of a subaward.

As a Pass-Through Entity (PTE) handling federal funds, Study GRC Inc. is mandated by **2 CFR 200.332(b)** to evaluate each subrecipient's risk of non-compliance with Federal statutes, regulations, and the terms and conditions of the subaward. This assessment determines the appropriate level of subrecipient monitoring required to mitigate financial and reputational risk to the Organization.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to **all** proposed subawards where Study GRC Inc. acts as a Pass-Through Entity (PTE) using federal grant funds.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Contractors/Vendors:** Entities providing goods and services for the Organization's own use (procurement) are excluded from this specific assessment, though they are subject to vendor due diligence (see *FIN-POL-022 Procurement Policy*).
*   **Beneficiaries:** Individuals receiving services as part of the program mission (e.g., students, scholarship recipients).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pass-Through Entity (PTE)** | Study GRC Inc., when it provides a subaward to a subrecipient to carry out part of a Federal program. |
| **Subrecipient** | A non-Federal entity that receives a subaward from a PTE to carry out part of a Federal program. |
| **Subaward** | An award provided by a PTE to a subrecipient for the subrecipient to carry out part of a Federal award received by the PTE. |
| **Single Audit** | An audit required by 2 CFR 200 Subpart F for non-Federal entities that expend $750,000 or more in Federal awards during a fiscal year. |
| **SAM.gov** | System for Award Management; the official U.S. government system for checking suspension and debarment status. |

### 4. Risk Assessment Standards & Scoring Matrix

The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or their designee must utilize the following scoring matrix to quantify risk. A total score is calculated to categorize the subrecipient as **Low**, **Medium**, or **High** risk.

#### 4.1 Threshold Requirement (Pass/Fail)
Before scoring, the subrecipient must pass the following checks. If any are "Fail," the subaward **cannot** be issued.
1.  **Suspension/Debarment:** Entity is not listed as excluded on SAM.gov.
2.  **Conflict of Interest:** No undisclosed conflicts exist per *Bylaws Article IX*.
3.  **Organizational Eligibility:** Entity has a valid UEI (Unique Entity ID) and is legally formed.

#### 4.2 Risk Scoring Matrix (2 CFR 200.332(b) Factors)

**A. Prior Experience with Similar Awards**
| Criteria | Points |
| :--- | :--- |
| Extensive experience with Study GRC Inc. or similar federal awards (5+ years). | 0 |
| Moderate experience (2-5 years); some federal funding history. | 3 |
| New to Study GRC Inc. or new to federal funding (<2 years). | 5 |

**B. Audit History (Single Audit / Financial Review)**
| Criteria | Points |
| :--- | :--- |
| Clean Single Audit (or audited financials) for past 2 years; no material findings. | 0 |
| Audit conducted but contained minor findings (not material weaknesses). | 3 |
| Material weaknesses, significant deficiencies, or no audit history available. | 5 |

**C. Personnel and Systems**
| Criteria | Points |
| :--- | :--- |
| Stable key personnel; mature accounting system (e.g., ERP) compliant with GAAP. | 0 |
| Recent turnover in key finance/admin roles; basic accounting system (e.g., QuickBooks). | 3 |
| Brand new finance staff; manual/spreadsheet-based accounting; no timekeeping system. | 5 |

**D. Federal Agency Monitoring Results**
| Criteria | Points |
| :--- | :--- |
| Recent federal monitoring resulted in no findings. | 0 |
| No recent federal monitoring occurred. | 3 |
| Recent federal monitoring resulted in findings or questioned costs. | 5 |

**E. Award Complexity & Dollar Value**
| Criteria | Points |
| :--- | :--- |
| Award is <$25,000 AND <10% of Subrecipient's total budget. | 0 |
| Award is $25,000 - $100,000 OR 10-25% of Subrecipient's total budget. | 3 |
| Award is >$100,000 OR >25% of Subrecipient's total budget. | 5 |

#### 4.3 Risk Classification and Required Monitoring

Based on the Total Score (Sum of A through E), the Subrecipient is classified as follows:

| Total Score | Risk Level | Mandatory Monitoring Actions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0 - 8** | **LOW** | • Standard annual performance review.<br>• Routine financial report review.<br>• "Advance" payments permitted (if allowed by grant). |
| **9 - 15** | **MEDIUM** | • **Quarterly** programmatic and financial check-ins.<br>• Detailed transaction testing (sampling) twice per year.<br>• Technical assistance/training required.<br>• "Reimbursement Only" payment method preferred. |
| **16 - 25** | **HIGH** | • **Monthly** financial review and transaction testing.<br>• **Mandatory** "Reimbursement Only" payment method (No advances).<br>• On-site or virtual site visit required within first 6 months.<br>• Specific Conditions added to Subaward Agreement (2 CFR 200.208). |

### 5. Assessment Procedure

#### 5.1 Data Collection
The Program Manager responsible for the partnership must issue the *Subrecipient Questionnaire* to the potential partner. This questionnaire collects:
*   Most recent Single Audit or Audited Financial Statements.
*   List of key personnel and financial systems used.
*   History of federal funding.

#### 5.2 Evaluation and Scoring
The CFO (or designated Grants Manager) reviews the questionnaire and independent data sources (SAM.gov, Federal Audit Clearinghouse).
1.  Verify the entity is not suspended or debarred.
2.  Apply the Scoring Matrix (Section 4.2).
3.  Document the score and determination on the *Subrecipient Risk Assessment Form (FIN-FRM-055)*.

#### 5.3 Specific Conditions for High Risk
If a subrecipient is deemed **High Risk**, the CFO must draft "Specific Conditions" to be included in the Subaward Agreement, pursuant to **2 CFR 200.208**. These may include:
*   Requiring payments as reimbursements rather than advances.
*   Withholding authority to proceed to the next phase until receipt of evidence of acceptable performance.
*   Requiring additional, more detailed financial reports.
*   Requiring additional project monitoring.
*   Requiring the subrecipient to obtain technical or management assistance.

#### 5.4 Annual Re-Assessment
Risk assessments are not static. The CFO must re-evaluate all active subrecipients:
*   Annually (aligned with the Fiscal Year defined in *Bylaws Article VIII*).
*   Upon the issuance of a new Single Audit Report.
*   If significant non-compliance is detected during the award period.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Program Manager** | Identifies potential subrecipients; distributes questionnaires; assesses programmatic capacity (Criteria A). |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Performs financial due diligence; executes the Risk Scoring Matrix; determines final Risk Level; approves Monitoring Plan. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides Board-level oversight for any subawards classified as "High Risk" or exceeding $100,000, ensuring alignment with *Bylaws Article VIII*. |
| **Executive Director** | Signs the final Subaward Agreement, acknowledging the assigned risk level and monitoring plan. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement

Failure to properly assess subrecipient risk constitutes a violation of federal regulations (2 CFR 200.332).
*   **Internal Consequence:** Disciplinary action for responsible officers.
*   **External Consequence:** Potential disallowance of costs by the federal awarding agency (Study GRC Inc. becomes liable for funds misspent by the subrecipient).
*   **Record Retention:** All Risk Assessment Forms and supporting documentation must be retained for a period of three (3) years from the date of submission of the final expenditure report, in accordance with *FIN-POL-012 Document Retention Policy*.
