### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to define the minimum quantitative and qualitative operational requirements for all Local Chapters affiliated with Study GRC Inc. These standards ensure consistency in member experience, adherence to the Organization’s charitable mission, and compliance with federal and state regulations regarding non-profit governance. This document serves as the primary benchmark for the Annual Chapter Performance Review.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all chartered Local Chapters, Student Chapters, and Special Interest Groups (SIGs) operating under the legal umbrella or brand affiliation of Study GRC Inc. It applies to all Chapter Officers and volunteer leaders responsible for chapter administration.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chartered Chapter** | A local group that has signed the *Local Chapter Affiliation Agreement* and received formal approval from the Board of Directors. |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a Chapter that has met all reporting requirements, financial obligations, and performance metrics defined in this Standard. |
| **NPS (Net Promoter Score)** | A metric used to gauge member satisfaction based on post-event surveys. |
| **Active Participant** | An individual who has attended at least one Chapter event or contributed to a Chapter project within the rolling 12-month period. |
| **HQ** | The central governing body and operational leadership of Study GRC Inc. |

### 4. Performance Standards

All Chartered Chapters must adhere to the following minimum standards to maintain Good Standing. Failure to meet these standards may result in probation or revocation of the Chapter Charter.

#### 4.1 Governance and Leadership
4.1.1 **Officer Composition:** Each Chapter must maintain a minimum of two (2) distinct leadership roles: a Chapter President (or Chair) and a Chapter Secretary/Treasurer.
4.1.2 **Succession Planning:** Chapter leadership terms must not exceed two (2) consecutive years without a confirmed succession plan filed with HQ.
4.1.3 **Communication with HQ:** Chapter leadership must participate in at least 75% of the scheduled "All-Chapter Leader" coordination calls hosted by the VP of Education & Training.

#### 4.2 Programming and Events
4.2.1 **Event Frequency:** Chapters must host a minimum of **four (4) educational events** per fiscal year (one per quarter).
4.2.2 **Content Alignment:** 100% of event content must align with the Organization’s purpose as defined in **Bylaws Article 1.3** (advancing GRC knowledge).
4.2.3 **Vendor Neutrality:** Events must be strictly educational. Sales pitches, marketing demos, or "pay-to-play" speaker slots are strictly prohibited.
4.2.4 **Delivery Mode:** To support the Organization's remote-first and inclusive mission, at least **50%** of chapter events must be accessible virtually (either fully virtual or hybrid).

#### 4.3 Membership and Engagement
4.3.1 **Minimum Size:** A Chapter must maintain a minimum roster of **ten (10) Active Participants**.
4.3.2 **Growth Target:** Chapters are expected to demonstrate a net growth in Active Participants of at least **5%** year-over-year.
4.3.3 **Satisfaction:** Chapters must achieve an average Net Promoter Score (NPS) of **+30** or higher on post-event surveys administered via the HQ centralized platform.

#### 4.4 Financial Integrity and Reporting
4.4.1 **Budget Adherence:** Chapters must operate within the budget approved by HQ. Any expenditure exceeding **$250** outside of the approved budget requires prior written authorization from the Chief Financial Officer (CFO).
4.4.2 **Reporting Cadence:** Chapters must submit the *Chapter Activity Report* (covering attendance, finances, and upcoming plans) no later than **15 days** after the close of each quarter.
4.4.3 **Prohibited Accounts:** Chapters are strictly prohibited from opening independent bank accounts, credit lines, or payment processing accounts (e.g., PayPal, Stripe) using the Study GRC Inc. name or EIN. All financial transactions must flow through HQ.

#### 4.5 Compliance and Ethics
4.5.1 **Code of Conduct:** 100% of Chapter Officers must sign the *Volunteer Code of Conduct* and *Conflict of Interest Disclosure* annually.
4.5.2 **Youth Protection:** Any Chapter organizing events involving K-12 participants must strictly adhere to the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy (HR-POL-060)*. This includes mandatory background checks for all event volunteers and adherence to the "Two-Deep Leadership" rule.
4.5.3 **Brand Usage:** All Chapter communications, slide decks, and social media assets must utilize the current *Brand Identity and Style Guide*. Unauthorized modification of the Study GRC Inc. logo is prohibited.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chapter President** | Accountable for the Chapter's overall adherence to these standards and submission of quarterly reports. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Responsible for monitoring Chapter performance, conducting annual reviews, and providing resources to help Chapters meet standards. |
| **CFO** | Responsible for auditing Chapter financial requests and ensuring compliance with 501(c)(3) financial controls. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Responsible for mediating conflicts within Chapter leadership and ensuring community culture aligns with the mission. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

#### 6.1 Performance Review Cycle
Chapter performance is evaluated annually in January. Chapters will receive a rating of:
*   **Exemplary:** Exceeds all standards.
*   **Compliant:** Meets all minimum standards.
*   **At-Risk:** Fails to meet 1-2 standards (e.g., missed one event, late reporting).
*   **Non-Compliant:** Fails to meet 3+ standards or violates ethical policies.

#### 6.2 Probation and Revocation
*   **Probation:** Chapters rated "At-Risk" or "Non-Compliant" will be placed on a 90-day Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).
*   **Revocation:** Failure to cure deficiencies within the PIP period, or any single violation of the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* or *Financial Integrity* standards, will result in the immediate revocation of the Chapter Charter and dissolution of the local leadership team.
