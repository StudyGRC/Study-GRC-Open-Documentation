### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish mandatory retention periods for the physical and electronic records of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This Standard ensures compliance with **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.353**, Internal Revenue Service (IRS) regulations, and Federal Grant requirements (2 CFR 200). It is designed to preserve the Organization’s institutional memory, ensure financial transparency as required by **Bylaws Article VIII**, and facilitate the legal destruction of documents that are no longer necessary for operations or compliance.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all "Records" (as defined below) generated, received, or maintained by Study GRC Inc., regardless of physical format or storage location.
*   **Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors.
*   **Includes:** Paper files, electronic documents, emails, database records, slack/discord messages, and audio/video recordings.
*   **Exclusions:** Drafts, duplicate copies, and casual correspondence that do not document a business decision or transaction.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Record** | Any recorded information, regardless of form, made or received by the Organization in connection with the transaction of public business or pursuant to law. |
| **Retention Period** | The minimum required length of time a Record must be maintained and accessible. |
| **Permanent** | Records that must be kept indefinitely for the life of the Organization. |
| **Disposition** | The final action taken on a record when the retention period expires (e.g., secure destruction or transfer to historical archives). |
| **Legal Hold** | A directive to suspend the destruction of records due to pending or anticipated litigation, audit, or investigation. |
| **2 CFR 200** | Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards (Uniform Guidance). |

#### 4.2 Electronic Records & Email
*   **Email:** Emails are not a permanent storage medium. Emails constituting a "Record" (e.g., Board votes via email, contract negotiations) must be exported to the appropriate permanent repository (e.g., Shared Drive, Document Management System) and retained per the schedule above.
*   **Transient Email:** Routine correspondence (meeting scheduling, casual conversation) should be deleted after **2 Years** to reduce storage costs and discovery liability.
*   **Slack/Discord:** Message history shall be retained for **1 Year** unless flagged for a specific incident or investigation.

#### 4.3 Suspension of Record Disposal (Legal Hold)
In the event of a "Legal Hold" issued by the Board, Legal Counsel, or the Executive Director due to litigation, audit, or investigation:
1.  **Immediate Cessation:** All destruction of records (including automated deletion schedules) relevant to the hold must stop immediately.
2.  **Scope:** This applies to all physical and electronic records, including backups.
3.  **Violation:** Destruction of records under a Legal Hold is a serious offense (Spoliation of Evidence) and may result in immediate termination and criminal liability.

#### 4.4 Disposition and Destruction
*   **Confidential Records:** Records containing PII, PHI, financial data, or sensitive governance info must be destroyed via **secure shredding** or **cryptographic erasure** (NIST SP 800-88).
*   **Public Records:** Non-sensitive records may be recycled or deleted normally.
*   **Documentation:** A "Records Destruction Log" must be maintained for all destruction of Governance, Financial, and Legal records.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board Secretary** | Custodian of all Governance, Membership, and Board-level records. Ensures Minutes are signed and archived. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Custodian of all Financial, Tax, and Grant records. Ensures compliance with IRS and 2 CFR 200 retention. |
| **Executive Director** | Responsible for Operational, HR, and Safety records. Issues Legal Holds when necessary. |
| **CISO** | Responsible for the technical implementation of retention (backups, archives) and secure destruction (digital wiping). |
| **Staff & Volunteers** | Responsible for filing records in the correct repository and adhering to the "Legal Hold" policy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Unauthorized Destruction:** Destroying records before their retention period expires, or during a Legal Hold, is grounds for immediate dismissal and potential legal action.
*   **Failure to Archive:** Failing to transfer critical business records from personal drives/email to the central repository is a performance violation.
