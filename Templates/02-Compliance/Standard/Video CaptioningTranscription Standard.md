### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to define the technical and quality requirements for captioning and transcribing time-based media (video and audio) produced or distributed by Study GRC Inc. This ensures compliance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 Level AA and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). Furthermore, this Standard supports the Organization’s mission as defined in **Bylaws Article 1.3** to "remove financial barriers" and "empower learners" by ensuring educational resources are accessible to the deaf, hard-of-hearing, and neurodiverse communities, as well as non-native speakers.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all time-based media content created, published, or hosted by Study GRC Inc.

**Applies to:**
*   Pre-recorded educational videos (e.g., YouTube tutorials, LMS course modules).
*   Live-streamed events (e.g., webinars, virtual conferences).
*   Social media video content (e.g., LinkedIn clips, promotional videos).
*   Audio-only content (e.g., podcasts).
*   Third-party content embedded on Study GRC Inc. platforms.

**Exclusions:**
*   Live, unrecorded internal meetings (unless an accommodation is requested).
*   Audio-only internal voice notes not intended for permanent record or distribution.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Closed Captions (CC)** | Text display of spoken words and sounds that can be toggled on/off by the user. |
| **Open Captions** | Captions that are "burned in" to the video file and cannot be turned off. |
| **Transcript** | A text document containing a verbatim or edited record of all spoken words and significant sound effects from a media file. |
| **Time-Based Media** | Media that has a duration, such as audio or video, where the user must consume the content over time. |
| **CART** | Communication Access Real-time Translation; instant translation of the spoken word into English text using a stenotype machine, notebook computer, and real-time software. |
| **Speaker Identification** | Text indicating who is speaking, usually denoted by name or role (e.g., "Instructor:"). |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Accuracy and Quality
Automated (AI-generated) captions are **insufficient** for final publication without human review. All captions must meet the following quality metrics:
*   **Accuracy Rate:** Captions must achieve a minimum of **99% accuracy**. This includes correct spelling of GRC-specific terminology (e.g., "NIST," "ISO 27001," "GDPR").
*   **Punctuation:** Proper punctuation must be used to convey the cadence and meaning of the speech.
*   **Grammar:** Captions must reflect the spoken language, including contractions, unless an "Edited Transcript" is specified for clarity.

#### 4.2 Synchronization
Captions must be synchronized with the audio to ensure the text aligns with the visual context.
*   **Lag Time:** Captions must appear within **±1 second** of the audio start time.
*   **Duration:** Captions must remain on screen long enough to be read (minimum 1 second) but must disappear when the audio ceases.

#### 4.3 Formatting and Style
To ensure readability, captions must adhere to the following formatting rules:
*   **Line Limit:** Maximum of **two (2) lines** of text per caption frame.
*   **Character Limit:** Maximum of **32 characters** per line.
*   **Case:** Mixed case must be used. All-caps is reserved for shouting or loud noises.
*   **Speaker Identification:** When multiple speakers are present, the speaker must be identified at the start of their turn (e.g., `[Instructor]:` or `>> Instructor:`).
*   **Non-Speech Sounds:** Significant sound effects must be captioned in brackets (e.g., `[upbeat intro music]`, `[silence]`, `[alarm ringing]`).

#### 4.4 Visual Presentation (Open Captions)
If Open Captions are used (e.g., for social media where CC is not supported), they must meet contrast requirements:
*   **Contrast Ratio:** Text must have a contrast ratio of at least **4.5:1** against the background.
*   **Background:** Use of a semi-transparent or solid text box behind the text is mandatory if the video background is dynamic.
*   **Placement:** Captions must be placed in the lower third of the screen but **must not** obscure essential visual information (e.g., lower-third name titles, on-screen text, or logos).

#### 4.5 Transcripts
A separate text transcript must be provided for all pre-recorded audio and video content.
*   **Format:** Transcripts must be available as HTML text (on the same page) or a downloadable accessible PDF/Word document.
*   **Structure:** Transcripts must use headings and paragraph breaks to improve readability.
*   **Searchability:** Transcripts must be indexable by search engines to improve resource discoverability.

#### 4.6 Live Captioning
*   **Major Events:** For Annual Meetings or major conferences, professional CART services must be utilized to ensure real-time accuracy.
*   **Routine Webinars:** Automated live captions (e.g., Zoom/Teams auto-captions) are permissible, provided a disclaimer regarding potential inaccuracies is displayed.
*   **Post-Event:** If a recording of a live event is published, the automated captions must be corrected to meet the 99% accuracy standard (Section 4.1) before permanent publication.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all curriculum and training videos meet this standard prior to release. Allocates budget for professional captioning where necessary. |
| **Content Creators / Volunteers** | Responsible for editing automated captions, adding speaker IDs, and verifying synchronization. |
| **IT / Web Team** | Ensures the video player technology on the Organization's website supports Closed Captioning and transcript downloads. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Monitors community feedback regarding accessibility barriers and reports issues to the Board. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard may result in the removal of non-compliant content from public platforms until remediation is complete. Repeated failure by staff or volunteers to adhere to these accessibility standards may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
