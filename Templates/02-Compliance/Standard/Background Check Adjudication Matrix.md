### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish objective, risk-based criteria for evaluating criminal history records obtained during the background check process for employees, volunteers, and contractors of Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization").

This Standard ensures compliance with the **Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) Enforcement Guidance** on the Consideration of Arrest and Conviction Records, the **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)**, and **Texas Family Code** requirements for youth-serving organizations. It aims to balance the Organization’s commitment to safety, asset protection, and youth safeguarding with fair hiring practices and the elimination of systemic bias.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all background check adjudications performed by the Organization.
*   **In Scope:** All prospective and current employees, Board Directors, volunteers with unsupervised access to minors or sensitive data, and independent contractors with systems access.
*   **Exclusions:** This matrix does not apply to credit checks, which are governed separately under the *Financial Integrity Policy*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Adjudication** | The process of reviewing a background check report to determine if a candidate meets the Organization's standards for employment or service. |
| **Green Factors** | The three components defined by the EEOC for individualized assessment: (1) The nature and gravity of the offense; (2) The time that has passed since the offense; and (3) The nature of the job held or sought. |
| **Lookback Period** | The specific timeframe for which criminal history is considered relevant. Generally 7 years, unless otherwise required by law or for specific high-risk offenses. |
| **Youth-Facing Role** | Any position involving direct interaction (virtual or physical) with minors, or access to data belonging to minors. |
| **Adverse Action** | A decision not to hire, promote, or retain an individual based in whole or in part on information in a consumer report. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Role Risk Classifications
To apply this matrix, the Adjudicator must first assign the position to a Risk Tier.

| Tier | Description | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1: General** | Low-risk roles with no access to sensitive data, finances, or minors. | General administrative volunteers, content contributors (non-admin). |
| **Tier 2: Sensitive** | Roles with access to proprietary IP, member PII, or moderate financial authority. | Operational Officers, IT Staff, HR, Marketing leads. |
| **Tier 3: Fiduciary** | Roles with significant financial authority, check signing power, or strategic control. | Board Directors, Treasurer, CFO, CEO. |
| **Tier 4: Youth-Facing** | **Critical Safety.** Any role with unsupervised access to minors or student data. | Mentors, Instructors, Moderators of youth channels, DKG. |

#### 4.2 Adjudication Matrix
The following table dictates the initial adjudication decision based on the type of offense and the Role Tier.

*   **PASS:** The offense does not impact eligibility.
*   **REVIEW:** Requires Individualized Assessment (See Section 4.3).
*   **FAIL:** Disqualifying offense (subject to pre-adverse action notice).

| Offense Category | Tier 1 (General) | Tier 2 (Sensitive) | Tier 3 (Fiduciary) | Tier 4 (Youth-Facing) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sexual Offenses** (Rape, assault, exploitation, possession of CSAM) | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | **FAIL** |
| **Violent Felonies** (Homicide, kidnapping, aggravated assault) | **REVIEW** | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | **FAIL** |
| **Crimes Against Children** (Abuse, neglect, endangerment) | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | **FAIL** |
| **Financial Felonies** (Embezzlement, fraud, money laundering, forgery) | **REVIEW** | **REVIEW** | **FAIL** | **REVIEW** |
| **Theft/Property Crimes** (Burglary, robbery, grand larceny) | **PASS** (>5 yrs)<br>**REVIEW** (<5 yrs) | **REVIEW** | **REVIEW** | **REVIEW** |
| **Drug Distribution/Manufacturing** | **REVIEW** | **REVIEW** | **REVIEW** | **FAIL** |
| **Drug Possession** (Simple possession) | **PASS** | **PASS** | **REVIEW** (<3 yrs) | **REVIEW** (<5 yrs) |
| **Cybercrimes** (Hacking, identity theft, unauthorized access) | **REVIEW** | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | **REVIEW** |
| **Driving Offenses** (DUI/DWI) | **PASS** | **PASS*** | **PASS*** | **PASS*** |

*\*Note: If the role involves driving on behalf of the Organization or transporting minors, a DUI within the last 5 years is a FAIL.*

#### 4.3 Individualized Assessment (The "Green Factors")
For any result marked **REVIEW**, the Adjudicator **must** document an assessment based on the following factors before making a final decision:

1.  **Nature and Gravity of Offense:** Was it a violent act? Was there a victim? Was it a "victimless" statutory crime?
2.  **Time Elapsed:** Has the candidate demonstrated rehabilitation? Have they maintained a clean record since the offense? (e.g., A theft conviction 6 years ago with no subsequent issues may be acceptable for a Tier 2 role).
3.  **Nature of the Job:** Does the crime relate directly to the duties?
    *   *Example:* A conviction for check fraud is highly relevant to a Treasurer (Tier 3) but may not be relevant to a Content Creator (Tier 1).
    *   *Example:* A conviction for cyber-stalking is disqualifying for a Community Moderator (Tier 4).

#### 4.4 Lookback Periods
Unless otherwise mandated by state law:
*   **Sexual Offenses / Crimes Against Children:** Lifetime lookback.
*   **Violent Felonies:** 10 Years.
*   **General Felonies:** 7 Years.
*   **Misdemeanors:** 5 Years.
*   **Pending Charges:** Must be treated as "Review." The Organization may suspend the hiring decision until the charge is resolved.

#### 4.5 Automatic Disqualifiers (Zero Tolerance)
Regardless of the "Green Factors," the following result in automatic disqualification due to the Organization's status as a youth-serving 501(c)(3) and the Bylaws Article III requirements for ethical conduct:
1.  Registered Sex Offenders (any tier, any jurisdiction).
2.  Convictions for the manufacture or distribution of Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM).
3.  Convictions involving the death of a child.
4.  Current inclusion on the GSA Excluded Parties List System (SAM.gov) for federal contractors (relevant to Grant Compliance).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Initiates the background check request. Does **not** receive specific details of criminal history, only the final adjudication status (Pass/Fail). |
| **Candidate** | Provides accurate information and consent. Has the right to dispute inaccurate information with the background check vendor. |
| **HR Director / Adjudicator** | Reviews the report against this Matrix. Conducts Individualized Assessments. Issues Pre-Adverse and Adverse Action notices in compliance with FCRA. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Consulted on complex "Review" cases or when a dispute regarding accuracy arises. |
| **Executive Director** | Final approver for any exception to a "Fail" designation (rare and must be documented). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Confidentiality:** Background check reports are highly confidential. They must be stored separately from personnel files in a secure, access-controlled repository (see *HR-POL-042 Employee File Access Policy*).
*   **FCRA Compliance:** If a decision is made to deny employment/service based on the report, the Organization must follow the two-step Adverse Action process (Pre-Adverse Notice -> Waiting Period -> Final Adverse Notice).
*   **Violation:** Failure to adhere to this standard (e.g., hiring a Tier 4 individual with a disqualifying record) constitutes a severe violation of the *Child Safeguarding Policy* and may result in immediate termination of the hiring authority and legal liability for the Organization.
