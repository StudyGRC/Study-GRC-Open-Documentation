### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish objective, quantifiable criteria for evaluating the Motor Vehicle Records (MVRs) of volunteers and employees who operate vehicles on behalf of Study GRC Inc. This standard ensures compliance with the Organization’s commercial general liability and non-owned auto insurance policies, mitigates liability risk, and ensures the safety of the community—specifically regarding the transportation of equipment, resources, or protected individuals (youth).

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all **Authorized Drivers**.

*   **In Scope:**
    *   Any volunteer, employee, or contractor requesting authorization to drive a vehicle owned, leased, or rented by Study GRC Inc.
    *   Any volunteer, employee, or contractor authorized to use a Personally Owned Vehicle (POV) for Organization business where reimbursement is requested or where the driving is an essential function of the role (e.g., transporting event supplies, transporting other members).
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Commuting to and from a stationary work location or event venue (considered personal time).
    *   Volunteers who do not operate vehicles as part of their assigned duties.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authorized Driver** | An individual who has successfully passed the MVR check and provided proof of valid licensure and insurance, formally cleared to drive for Organization business. |
| **MVR (Motor Vehicle Record)** | A report from the Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) or equivalent state agency detailing an individual's driving history, including violations, suspensions, and accident reports. |
| **Major Violation** | A serious traffic offense indicating high risk (e.g., DUI/DWI, reckless driving, driving with a suspended license). |
| **Minor Violation** | A less severe traffic infraction (e.g., speeding <15 mph over limit, failure to yield, improper turn). |
| **POV** | Personally Owned Vehicle. |
| **Youth Transportation** | The act of transporting any individual under the age of 18 who is not a direct family member of the driver. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Screening Frequency
To maintain "Authorized Driver" status, MVR checks must be conducted:
1.  **Pre-Engagement:** Prior to the individual operating any vehicle on behalf of the Organization.
2.  **Annually:** A recurring check conducted every 12 months for all active Authorized Drivers.
3.  **For Cause:** Immediately upon reasonable suspicion of a new violation or following a workplace accident involving a vehicle.

#### 4.2 Evaluation Criteria (Grading Grid)
The Organization utilizes a tiered grading system to determine eligibility.

| Grade | Criteria Definition | Action / Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clear (Pass)** | • Valid Driver’s License.<br>• 0 Major Violations in past 5 years.<br>• Max 1 Minor Violation in past 3 years.<br>• 0 At-Fault Accidents in past 3 years. | **Approved.** Driver is authorized to operate vehicles. |
| **Borderline (Conditional)** | • 2 Minor Violations in past 3 years.<br>• OR 1 At-Fault Accident in past 3 years.<br>• 0 Major Violations. | **Conditional Approval.** Requires Executive Director review. May require completion of a defensive driving course within 30 days. |
| **Unacceptable (Fail)** | • Any **Major Violation** in past 5 years.<br>• 3+ Minor Violations in past 3 years.<br>• 2+ At-Fault Accidents in past 3 years.<br>• Suspended or Revoked License.<br>• Any violation involving drugs/alcohol. | **Prohibited.** Individual is disqualified from driving for the Organization. |

#### 4.3 Classification of Violations
For the purpose of the Grading Grid in Section 4.2, violations are classified as follows:

**Type A: Major Violations (Automatic Disqualification)**
*   Driving Under the Influence (DUI/DWI) or refusal to submit to a chemical test.
*   Reckless or Careless Driving.
*   Hit and Run / Leaving the scene of an accident.
*   Vehicular Manslaughter or Homicide.
*   Driving with a Suspended or Revoked License.
*   Fleeing or eluding a police officer.
*   Speeding in excess of 20 mph over the posted limit.
*   Passing a stopped school bus.

**Type B: Minor Violations**
*   Speeding (1-19 mph over limit).
*   Failure to yield right of way.
*   Failure to obey a traffic signal or sign.
*   Improper turn or lane change.
*   Distracted driving (cell phone use where prohibited).

#### 4.4 Youth Transportation Addendum
Due to the Organization’s K-12 focus and *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, stricter standards apply to drivers transporting minors.
*   **Zero Tolerance:** Any driver with a history of DUI/DWI, regardless of how long ago it occurred, is permanently disqualified from transporting youth.
*   **Experience:** Drivers transporting youth must have held a valid driver’s license for a minimum of three (3) consecutive years.

#### 4.5 Insurance Requirements for POV Use
Volunteers or employees using a Personally Owned Vehicle (POV) must provide a Certificate of Insurance (COI) or Declarations Page proving the following minimum coverage limits:
*   **Bodily Injury Liability:** $100,000 per person / $300,000 per accident.
*   **Property Damage Liability:** $50,000 per accident.

*Note: The Organization’s Non-Owned Auto Liability policy is excess coverage and applies only after the driver’s primary insurance limits are exhausted.*

#### 4.6 Duty to Report
Authorized Drivers must notify the Organization within **24 hours** (or the next business day) of:
1.  Any new moving violation or accident (whether personal or business-related).
2.  Any suspension, revocation, or expiration of their driver’s license.
3.  Any cancellation or lapse of their personal auto insurance.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer / Employee** | • Provide written consent for MVR checks per FCRA requirements.<br>• Maintain valid licensure and insurance.<br>• Self-report violations immediately. |
| **HR / Volunteer Coordinator** | • Initiate MVR orders via the designated background check vendor.<br>• Review results against the Grading Grid (Section 4.2).<br>• Maintain confidentiality of records. |
| **Executive Director** | • Review and adjudicate "Borderline" cases.<br>• Authorize exceptions only where insurance carrier approval is obtained in writing. |
| **Insurance Carrier** | • May provide final determination on driver eligibility based on underwriting guidelines. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to meet the criteria defined in this Standard will result in the revocation of driving privileges.
*   **Indemnification Impact:** Per **Bylaws Article VII**, indemnification is contingent upon acting in "good faith." Operating a vehicle without authorization, with a suspended license, or while under the influence constitutes a lack of good faith and may void legal protection and indemnification by the Organization.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** If driving is an essential function of an employee's role and they become "Unacceptable" per Section 4.2, this may result in termination of employment. Volunteers will be reassigned to non-driving roles or dismissed.
