### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish the mandatory criteria and methodology for classifying positions at Study GRC Inc. as either "Exempt" or "Non-Exempt" in compliance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and the Texas Payday Law. Accurate classification is critical to ensure fair compensation, prevent wage theft, and mitigate legal and financial risks associated with misclassification, including back-tax liabilities and penalties.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all current and future employment positions within Study GRC Inc.
*   **Inclusions:** All full-time, part-time, and temporary employees.
*   **Exclusions:** Independent contractors (1099), volunteers, and unpaid interns (governed by separate policies).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Exempt** | Employees who are **not** entitled to overtime pay under the FLSA. These employees are paid a salary to perform a job, regardless of the number of hours worked. |
| **Non-Exempt** | Employees who **are** entitled to overtime pay (1.5 times the regular rate) for all hours worked over 40 in a workweek. |
| **FLSA** | The Fair Labor Standards Act, the federal law establishing minimum wage, overtime pay, recordkeeping, and youth employment standards. |
| **Salary Basis Test** | A requirement that an employee regularly receives a predetermined amount of compensation each pay period on a weekly, or less frequent, basis. |
| **Duties Test** | An analysis of the employee's primary job duties to determine if they qualify for a specific exemption (Executive, Administrative, Professional, Computer, or Outside Sales). |

### 4. Standard Statements

#### 4.1 Default Classification
**4.1.1 Presumption of Non-Exempt Status**
All positions at Study GRC Inc. are presumed to be **Non-Exempt** unless the position specifically satisfies **all three** of the following tests:
1.  **Salary Basis Test:** The employee is paid a predetermined and fixed salary that is not subject to reduction because of variations in the quality or quantity of work performed.
2.  **Salary Level Test:** The employee is paid at least the minimum salary threshold established by the Department of Labor (DOL).
3.  **Duties Test:** The employee’s primary job duties involve the kind of work associated with exempt status (Executive, Administrative, Professional, Computer, or Outside Sales).

#### 4.2 Salary Level Thresholds
To qualify as Exempt, an employee must be compensated at a rate not less than the current standard salary level set by the DOL.
*   **Effective July 1, 2024:** \$844 per week (\$43,888 annually).
*   **Effective January 1, 2025:** \$1,128 per week (\$58,656 annually).
*   **Future Adjustments:** This Standard automatically adopts any future adjustments to the salary threshold as mandated by federal regulation.

*Note: Employees paid below these thresholds are automatically Non-Exempt, regardless of their job duties.*

#### 4.3 The Duties Test Criteria
To be classified as Exempt, the position must meet the primary duty requirements of one of the following categories:

**4.3.1 Executive Exemption**
*   Primary duty is managing the enterprise or a customarily recognized department/subdivision;
*   Customarily and regularly directs the work of at least two or more other full-time employees; **and**
*   Has the authority to hire or fire other employees (or their suggestions regarding hiring/firing are given particular weight).

**4.3.2 Administrative Exemption**
*   Primary duty is the performance of office or non-manual work directly related to the management or general business operations of the employer or the employer’s customers; **and**
*   Primary duty includes the exercise of **discretion and independent judgment** with respect to matters of significance.

**4.3.3 Professional Exemption (Learned or Creative)**
*   **Learned:** Primary duty requires knowledge of an advanced type in a field of science or learning customarily acquired by a prolonged course of specialized intellectual instruction (e.g., Lawyers, Accountants, Doctors).
*   **Creative:** Primary duty requires invention, imagination, originality, or talent in a recognized field of artistic or creative endeavor.

**4.3.4 Computer Employee Exemption**
*   Must be employed as a computer systems analyst, computer programmer, software engineer, or other similarly skilled worker in the computer field.
*   Primary duty must consist of:
    1.  The application of systems analysis techniques and procedures, including consulting with users, to determine hardware, software, or system functional specifications;
    2.  The design, development, documentation, analysis, creation, testing, or modification of computer systems or programs, including prototypes, based on and related to user or system design specifications; or
    3.  The design, documentation, testing, creation, or modification of computer programs related to machine operating systems.
*   *Exclusion:* Help desk technicians and IT support staff who strictly follow scripts or manuals generally do **not** qualify for this exemption and must be classified as Non-Exempt.

#### 4.4 Safe Harbor for Salary Deductions
**4.4.1 Improper Deductions Prohibited**
Study GRC Inc. prohibits improper deductions from the salaries of Exempt employees. Deductions from pay are permissible only in specific circumstances defined by the FLSA (e.g., full-day absences for personal reasons other than sickness or disability).

**4.4.2 Complaint Mechanism**
If an Exempt employee believes an improper deduction has been made from their salary, they must report it immediately to the CFO. The Organization will promptly reimburse any improper deduction and make a good faith commitment to comply in the future.

#### 4.5 Reclassification Triggers
A classification review must be conducted by HR/Finance:
1.  Upon the creation of any new job description.
2.  When a job description is significantly altered.
3.  When the federal salary threshold changes.
4.  If an employee’s salary is reduced below the federal threshold (e.g., moving from full-time to part-time).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Hiring Manager** | Drafts accurate job descriptions detailing primary duties and ensuring they reflect actual day-to-day responsibilities. |
| **CFO / HR Lead** | Conducts the FLSA analysis using the Salary and Duties tests. Has final authority on all classification decisions. Maintains records of the analysis. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves new positions and ensures budgetary compliance with Overtime or Salary requirements. |
| **Employee** | Acknowledges their classification status upon hiring and reports any discrepancies between their job description and actual duties performed. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard—specifically the intentional misclassification of employees to avoid paying overtime—is a violation of federal law and Organization policy.

*   **Internal Discipline:** Managers found to be coercing employees to work "off the clock" or misrepresenting job duties to achieve Exempt status will face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Remediation:** If a position is found to be misclassified, Study GRC Inc. will reclassify the position immediately and calculate/remit any retroactive overtime pay owed in accordance with the FLSA and Texas Payday Law statutes of limitation (generally 2 years, or 3 years for willful violations).
