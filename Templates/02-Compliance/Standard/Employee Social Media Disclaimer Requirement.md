### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to protect the legal standing, reputation, and 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). As a remote-first organization, our workforce frequently interacts online. This document establishes mandatory requirements for distinguishing personal opinions expressed by workforce members on social media from the official positions of the Organization. This distinction is critical to mitigate liability regarding defamation, copyright infringement, and unauthorized political campaigning.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all **Covered Persons**, defined as:
*   Employees (Full-time and Part-time);
*   Members of the Board of Directors;
*   Operational Officers;
*   Volunteers; and
*   Contractors.

**Applicability Trigger:** This Standard applies specifically to Covered Persons who identify themselves as affiliated with Study GRC Inc. (e.g., by listing their job title, the Organization's name, or using the Organization's branding) on personal social media profiles or in personal content.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Social Media** | Any online platform used for social networking, microblogging, or content sharing, including but not limited to LinkedIn, X (formerly Twitter), Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Discord, and personal blogs. |
| **Affiliation** | The public association of an individual with the Organization, established by listing a job title, employer name, or description of role in a user profile, bio, or post. |
| **Disclaimer** | A specific statement asserting that the content shared is personal in nature and does not represent the Organization. |
| **Political Campaign Intervention** | Any activity that supports or opposes a candidate for public office. As a 501(c)(3), the Organization is strictly prohibited from engaging in this activity. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Mandatory Disclaimer Usage
If a Covered Person identifies their affiliation with Study GRC Inc. in the biographical section ("Bio," "About," or "Headline") of a personal social media account, they **must** include a disclaimer indicating that their views are their own.

#### 4.2 Approved Disclaimer Text
Covered Persons must use one of the following approved formats, or a substantially similar variation approved by the Director of Human Resources:

**4.2.1 Short Form (Character-Limited Platforms, e.g., X/Twitter, TikTok)**
> "Views are my own."
> *OR*
> "Tweets/Posts are my own."

**4.2.2 Long Form (Professional Platforms, e.g., LinkedIn, Personal Blogs)**
> "The views and opinions expressed here are my own and do not necessarily reflect the official policy or position of Study GRC Inc."

**4.2.3 Speaking Engagements/Slide Decks**
When presenting at external conferences where the individual is introduced by their Study GRC Inc. title but speaking on a topic not explicitly sanctioned as an organizational stance:
> "The content of this presentation represents the speaker's personal views and experience and is not an official statement from Study GRC Inc."

#### 4.3 Placement and Visibility
*   **Location:** The disclaimer must be placed in the permanent "Bio" or "About" section of the profile. Placing the disclaimer solely in a transient post (e.g., a "Story") is insufficient.
*   **Legibility:** The disclaimer must be readable and not obscured by other text or links.

#### 4.4 Prohibited Visual Association
To prevent confusion between personal and official accounts:
*   **Profile Pictures:** Covered Persons **must not** use the Study GRC Inc. logo, seal, or official iconography as their personal profile picture.
*   **Header Images:** Covered Persons may use Organization-provided branded header images (e.g., "I work at Study GRC") only if the mandatory disclaimer (Section 4.1) is present.

#### 4.5 Specific Restriction: Political Activity
Pursuant to Article I, Section 1.3 of the Bylaws (Nonprofit & Tax-Exempt Purpose), the Organization is prohibited from intervening in political campaigns.
*   If a Covered Person engages in online political advocacy or campaigning on a profile where they also list their employment with Study GRC Inc., the disclaimer requirement is **absolute and zero-tolerance**.
*   Failure to include a disclaimer while engaging in political speech while identified as a Study GRC Inc. representative may result in immediate disciplinary action to protect the Organization's tax-exempt status.

#### 4.6 Endorsements and Commercial Activity
Covered Persons **must not** use their affiliation with the Organization to endorse products, services, or vendors on personal social media in a manner that implies the Organization itself has endorsed said product, unless explicitly authorized under the *Procurement Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Covered Persons** | Ensure all personal social media profiles listing Study GRC Inc. affiliation contain the required disclaimer. Update profiles within 7 days of onboarding. |
| **Director of HR** | Verify disclaimer compliance during the new hire onboarding process and conduct periodic spot-checks. |
| **Marketing/Comms Dept** | Provide guidance on acceptable language and answer questions regarding brand usage. |
| **Managers** | Monitor team compliance and address missing disclaimers during performance reviews if necessary. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

Specifically, failure to use a disclaimer that results in the Organization being implicated in:
1.  Defamation lawsuits;
2.  Copyright infringement claims; or
3.  IRS investigations regarding prohibited political campaign intervention;

will be treated as "Cause" for removal or termination under Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6.
