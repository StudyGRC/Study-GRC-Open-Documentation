### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this standard is to ensure a consistent, professional, and legally compliant brand identity across all electronic communications sent on behalf of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). As a remote-first organization, email signatures function as our primary digital business card. Uniform signatures reinforce our credibility as a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt entity, ensure recipients have accurate contact information, and provide necessary legal disclaimers regarding confidentiality and non-profit status.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all employees, Board Directors, officers, volunteers, contractors, and interns who are issued a Study GRC Inc. email address (e.g., `name@studygrc.org`) or who send email on behalf of the Organization.

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal email accounts not used for Organization business.
*   Automated transactional emails (e.g., system notifications), though these must follow separate branding guidelines defined in the *Brand Identity and Style Guide*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Signature Block** | The text and/or images automatically appended to the bottom of an email message. |
| **Legal Disclaimer** | Mandatory text regarding confidentiality, privilege, and tax-exempt status. |
| **Call to Action (CTA)** | A hyperlink or brief text encouraging a specific action (e.g., "Donate Here" or "Register for Webinar"). |
| **Pronouns** | Words used to refer to a person (e.g., he/him, she/her, they/them), included to foster an inclusive environment. |

### 4. Standard Requirements

All personnel must configure their email client (Outlook, Gmail, Mobile Mail, etc.) to use the standardized signature format defined below.

#### 4.1 General Formatting
To ensure accessibility and visual consistency:
*   **Font:** Sans-serif (Arial, Calibri, or Verdana).
*   **Size:** 10pt or 11pt for body text; 8pt or 9pt for the legal disclaimer.
*   **Color:** Black (#000000) or Dark Grey (#333333). The Organization's primary brand color may be used for the Name or Organization Name only.
*   **Background:** White (no colored backgrounds or patterns).

#### 4.2 Mandatory Elements
The following elements **must** be included in the signature block:
1.  **Full Name:** As listed in the Organization’s directory.
2.  **Job Title:** Official title as defined in the *Position Description* or *Bylaws*.
3.  **Organization Name:** Study GRC Inc.
4.  **Website:** [www.studygrc.org](http://www.studygrc.org)
5.  **Legal Disclaimer:** See Section 4.6.

#### 4.3 Optional Elements
The following elements are permitted but not required:
1.  **Pronouns:** (e.g., she/her/hers) – Encouraged to support the Organization's mission of inclusivity.
2.  **Phone Number:** Direct line or mobile, if the individual wishes to be contacted via phone.
3.  **Physical Address:** The Organization's registered office address (if relevant for shipping/mail).
4.  **Social Media Links:** Links to the **Organization’s** official LinkedIn or Twitter/X profiles. Links to personal profiles are prohibited.
5.  **Approved Logo:** The official Study GRC Inc. logo (small resolution, max height 50px).

#### 4.4 Prohibited Elements
To maintain professionalism, the following are strictly prohibited:
1.  **Inspirational Quotes:** Religious verses, political slogans, or personal philosophical quotes.
2.  **Personal Social Media:** Links to personal Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, etc.
3.  **Large Images:** Headshots, large banners, or images that distract from the message content or trigger spam filters.
4.  **Animation:** GIFs or moving text.
5.  **Non-Standard Fonts:** Comic Sans, Papyrus, or cursive scripts.

#### 4.5 Approved Template Structure
Users must adhere to the following layout:

> **[First Name] [Last Name]** (Optional: Pronouns)
> [Job Title]
> **Study GRC Inc.**
>
> [Phone Number] | [Email Address]
> [Website URL]
>
> *Empowering the GRC Community through Open-Source Education.*
>
> [Insert Legal Disclaimer - See 4.6]

#### 4.6 Mandatory Legal Disclaimer
The following text must appear at the very bottom of the signature in a smaller font (8pt-9pt):

> *Study GRC Inc. is a Texas Nonprofit Corporation and a tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.*
>
> *CONFIDENTIALITY NOTICE: The contents of this email message and any attachments are intended solely for the addressee(s) and may contain confidential and/or privileged information and may be legally protected from disclosure. If you are not the intended recipient of this message or their agent, or if this message has been addressed to you in error, please immediately alert the sender by reply email and then delete this message and any attachments. If you are not the intended recipient, you are hereby notified that any use, dissemination, copying, or storage of this message or its attachments is strictly prohibited.*

#### 4.7 Mobile Device Signatures
"Sent from my iPhone" or "Sent from Android" signatures are unprofessional and prohibited. Mobile signatures must be updated to a simplified version of the standard:

> **[Name]**
> [Title], Study GRC Inc.
> www.studygrc.org

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Users** | Configure email clients to match this standard within 5 business days of onboarding or policy update. |
| **IT / Systems Admin** | Provide the official logo file and technical support for updating signatures. |
| **Marketing / Comms** | Update the "Call to Action" or tagline text periodically and notify staff of changes. |
| **Managers** | Verify that direct reports have compliant signatures during the onboarding process. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this standard may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment. Non-compliant signatures that misrepresent the Organization or violate the *Code of Ethics* will be treated as a violation of the *Acceptable Use Policy*.
