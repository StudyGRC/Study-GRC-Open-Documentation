### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish mandatory minimum requirements for Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR), Automated External Defibrillator (AED), and First Aid certification for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") staff and volunteers during in-person events. This Standard mitigates organizational liability, ensures compliance with "duty of care" obligations, and aligns with Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code regarding emergency care.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all in-person gatherings, conferences, workshops, and community meetups organized or sponsored by the Organization.
*   **Applies to:** All employees, contractors, and volunteers designated as "Event Staff," "Safety Marshals," or "Youth Supervisors."
*   **Exclusions:** This Standard does not apply to purely virtual/remote events or third-party events where Study GRC Inc. is solely an attendee or sponsor without operational control.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Certified Responder** | An individual holding a current, valid certification in CPR/AED and First Aid from an accredited certifying body (e.g., American Red Cross, American Heart Association). |
| **High-Risk Event** | Any event involving physical activity (e.g., sports, hiking), remote locations (>30 mins from EMS), or the presence of minors (K-12). |
| **AED** | Automated External Defibrillator; a medical device used to help those experiencing sudden cardiac arrest. |
| **Duty to Act** | A legal obligation to provide care, established when an individual is designated by the Organization to be responsible for the safety of others. |
| **Blended Learning** | Training that combines online conceptual learning with an in-person skills assessment. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Certification Requirements
All individuals designated as Certified Responders for an event must hold valid certification meeting the following criteria:
1.  **Accreditation:** Certifications must be issued by the American Red Cross (ARC), American Heart Association (AHA), American Safety & Health Institute (ASHI), or an equivalent nationally recognized body.
2.  **Methodology:** "Online-only" certifications are **prohibited** for designated safety staff. Certification must include a hands-on skills assessment (classroom or blended learning).
3.  **Currency:** Certifications must be current (not expired) for the entire duration of the event.
4.  **Scope:**
    *   **General Events:** Adult CPR/AED and Basic First Aid.
    *   **Youth Programs:** Adult and Pediatric CPR/AED and Basic First Aid.

#### 4.2 Staffing Ratios
Event planners must ensure the following minimum ratios of Certified Responders to attendees are met and documented on the Event Roster:

| Event Type | Minimum Ratio |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Indoor Event** (Conference/Meeting) | 1 Certified Responder per 75 Attendees |
| **Youth Event** (Minors present) | 1 Certified Responder per 20 Attendees |
| **High-Risk / Outdoor Event** | 1 Certified Responder per 15 Attendees |

*Note: If an event has fewer than the minimum number of attendees, at least one (1) Certified Responder must be present at all times.*

#### 4.3 Equipment Standards
1.  **First Aid Kits:** A fully stocked First Aid Kit compliant with ANSI/ISEA Z308.1-2021 (Class A) must be accessible within three (3) minutes of any location within the event venue.
2.  **AED Availability:**
    *   For events exceeding 100 attendees, an AED must be available on-site.
    *   If the venue does not provide an AED, the Organization must rent or provide a portable unit.
    *   Certified Responders must be briefed on the location of the AED prior to the event start.

#### 4.4 Duty of Care and Good Samaritan Acts
1.  **Designated Staff:** Staff specifically assigned as "Safety Personnel" have a legal **Duty to Act** within the scope of their training during an emergency. They must not exceed their level of training (e.g., performing invasive procedures).
2.  **Undesignated Staff:** Staff not designated as safety personnel who assist in an emergency are considered to be acting under the Good Samaritan Law (Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code § 74.151), provided they act in good faith and without compensation for the specific act of aid.

#### 4.5 Documentation and Verification
1.  **Pre-Event:** Copies of certification cards for all designated Certified Responders must be uploaded to the secure Event Management Drive 72 hours prior to the event.
2.  **Post-Incident:** Any administration of First Aid or CPR must be documented using the **Incident Investigation Report Form (OSHA 301 equivalent)** within 24 hours of the occurrence.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Manager** | Ensures staffing ratios are met during the planning phase; verifies validity of certifications; ensures First Aid Kits/AEDs are on-site. |
| **Certified Responder** | Maintains active certification; carries certification card (digital or physical) during events; provides care within scope of training; completes incident reports. |
| **Director of Operations** | Allocates budget for staff training and equipment procurement; audits compliance with this Standard annually. |
| **Human Resources** | Tracks employee certification expiration dates and coordinates recertification training. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard places the Organization at significant legal and financial risk.
*   **Event Cancellation:** Events that cannot meet the minimum staffing ratios defined in Section 4.2 may be cancelled or postponed by the Executive Director or Risk Management Committee.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Staff designated as Certified Responders who fail to act or who act with gross negligence may face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Legal Liability:** Failure to adhere to this standard may result in the forfeiture of indemnification protection under Bylaws Article VII.
