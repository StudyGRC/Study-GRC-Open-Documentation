### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to document the assessment of workplace hazards and the selection of appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) for Study GRC Inc. activities. This document ensures compliance with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.132(d)**, which requires employers to assess the workplace to determine if hazards are present, or are likely to be present, which necessitate the use of PPE.

This Standard establishes the minimum requirements for protecting employees, volunteers, and participants from specific physical and health hazards associated with in-person events, hardware workshops, and logistics operations.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all **Study GRC Inc.** employees, volunteers, contractors, and event participants engaged in:
*   **In-Person Events:** Conferences, meetups, and training sessions.
*   **Logistics Operations:** Shipping, receiving, and setup/teardown of event equipment.
*   **Technical Workshops:** Hands-on labs involving hardware, soldering, or physical server management.

**Exclusions:** This Standard does not apply to standard remote home-office administrative work, which is governed by the *Remote Worksite Safety Self-Assessment (HR-FRM-015)*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)** | Equipment worn to minimize exposure to hazards that cause serious workplace injuries and illnesses. |
| **Hazard Assessment** | The process of identifying hazards, analyzing the risk, and determining appropriate controls. |
| **ANSI** | American National Standards Institute; the organization that sets the design and performance standards for PPE. |
| **Engineering Controls** | Physical changes to the work area or equipment to remove the hazard (e.g., ventilation, machine guards). PPE is used only when engineering controls are insufficient. |
| **Decibel (dBA)** | A unit used to measure the intensity of a sound. |

### 4. Standards and Requirements

#### 4.1 Hierarchy of Controls
Study GRC Inc. adheres to the hierarchy of controls. PPE is the line of defense of last resort. Before relying on PPE, the Event Manager or Safety Lead must attempt to mitigate hazards via:
1.  **Elimination/Substitution:** Removing the hazard entirely.
2.  **Engineering Controls:** Isolating people from the hazard.
3.  **Administrative Controls:** Changing the way people work.

#### 4.2 Hazard Assessment and PPE Selection Matrix
The following matrix identifies specific tasks performed during Study GRC operations, the associated hazards, and the **mandatory** PPE required.

| Task / Activity | Potential Hazard(s) | Required PPE | ANSI/OSHA Standard |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Event Setup & Teardown** (Moving crates, staging furniture, cabling) | Impact to feet (dropping objects), cuts/abrasions to hands, pinch points. | **Hand Protection:** Leather or cut-resistant mechanics gloves.<br>**Foot Protection:** Closed-toe shoes required; safety-toe boots recommended for loads >50lbs. | ANSI/ISEA 105 (Hand)<br>ASTM F2413 (Foot) |
| **Server/Hardware Racking** (Installing IT equipment in racks) | Sharp metal edges, pinch points, falling equipment. | **Hand Protection:** Cut-resistant gloves (Level A2 or higher).<br>**Foot Protection:** Closed-toe shoes. | ANSI/ISEA 105 |
| **Soldering / Electronics Repair Workshops** | Molten metal burns, flux fumes, lead exposure, eye injury from clipped wires. | **Eye Protection:** Safety glasses with side shields.<br>**Respiratory:** Fume extractor (Engineering Control) or N95 mask if ventilation is poor.<br>**Hand Protection:** Heat-resistant gloves (optional based on dexterity). | ANSI/ISEA Z87.1 (Eye)<br>NIOSH 42 CFR 84 (Resp) |
| **Cable Management / Wiring** (Cutting, stripping, crimping) | Flying debris (wire clippings), cuts. | **Eye Protection:** Safety glasses with side shields. | ANSI/ISEA Z87.1 |
| **Sanitation / Cleanup** (Post-event cleaning, biological spills) | Chemical splashes, biological pathogens. | **Hand Protection:** Nitrile or latex disposable gloves.<br>**Eye Protection:** Splash goggles (if mixing concentrated chemicals). | OSHA 1910.138 |
| **High Noise Environments** (Server rooms >85 dBA, concert venues) | Hearing damage, tinnitus. | **Hearing Protection:** Earplugs or earmuffs with NRR rating of 25dB or higher. | OSHA 1910.95 |
| **VR/AR Demonstrations** (Public use of headsets) | Biological transfer (eye infections, skin conditions). | **Hygiene:** Disposable sanitary face masks/gaskets for headsets.<br>**Sanitization:** Alcohol wipes between users. | General Hygiene |

#### 4.3 Equipment Standards
All PPE purchased or provided by Study GRC Inc. must meet the following American National Standards Institute (ANSI) specifications:
*   **Eye and Face Protection:** Must comply with **ANSI/ISEA Z87.1-2020**.
*   **Head Protection:** Must comply with **ANSI/ISEA Z89.1-2014**.
*   **Foot Protection:** Must comply with **ASTM F2412-18a** and **ASTM F2413-18**.
*   **Hand Protection:** Selection must be based on the performance characteristics of the glove in relation to the tasks to be performed (ANSI/ISEA 105-2016).

#### 4.4 Inspection and Maintenance
*   **Pre-Use Inspection:** Users must inspect PPE prior to each use.
*   **Defective Equipment:** Any PPE found to be cracked, torn, soiled beyond cleaning, or structurally compromised must be immediately discarded and replaced. **Do not use defective PPE.**
*   **Storage:** PPE must be stored in a clean, dry location away from direct sunlight and contaminants when not in use.

#### 4.5 Training Requirements
Per OSHA 1910.132(f), any personnel required to wear PPE must be trained on:
1.  When PPE is necessary.
2.  What PPE is necessary.
3.  How to properly don, doff, adjust, and wear PPE.
4.  The limitations of the PPE.
5.  The proper care, maintenance, useful life, and disposal of the PPE.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures budget is allocated for the purchase of compliant PPE. Responsible for high-level safety oversight pursuant to **Bylaws Article V**. |
| **Event Safety Lead / Manager** | Conducts on-site hazard assessments using this matrix. Distributes PPE to staff/volunteers. Enforces PPE usage. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Inspect PPE before use. Wear PPE as directed. Report damaged equipment immediately. |
| **Instructors** | Ensure workshop participants are wearing required PPE (e.g., safety glasses) before allowing them to handle tools. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard puts the individual and the Organization at risk.
*   **Refusal to Wear PPE:** Personnel refusing to wear required PPE will be immediately removed from the hazardous area or task.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Repeated violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, in accordance with the *Progressive Discipline Policy (HR-POL-048)*.
