### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish mandatory requirements for the retention, storage, and protection of procurement records associated with Study GRC Inc. operations and federal awards. This Standard ensures the Organization maintains a verifiable audit trail to substantiate the allowability, allocability, and reasonableness of costs in compliance with **2 CFR 200.334** (Uniform Guidance) and IRS regulations, thereby mitigating the risk of disallowed costs and financial liability.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all employees, contractors, and Board members involved in the purchasing of goods or services on behalf of Study GRC Inc.
*   **In Scope:** All procurement documentation related to operational funds, private donations, and Federal grant awards (direct or pass-through).
*   **Exclusions:** General non-financial correspondence not related to a specific transaction or vendor selection decision.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **2 CFR 200.334** | The section of the Uniform Guidance (Federal law) mandating retention requirements for records for non-Federal entities receiving Federal awards. |
| **Procurement Transaction** | The complete lifecycle of acquiring goods or services, from the initial identification of need through invoice payment and contract closeout. |
| **Source Documentation** | Original documents that serve as evidence of the transaction (e.g., invoices, receipts, contracts, purchase orders). |
| **Final Expenditure Report** | The final financial report submitted to the Federal awarding agency, which triggers the start of the federal retention clock. |
| **Legal Hold** | A directive to preserve all forms of relevant information when litigation is reasonably anticipated. |

### 4. Standard Statements

#### 4.1. Retention Period Requirements
To satisfy both Federal audit requirements and IRS statutes of limitations, Study GRC Inc. enforces the following retention timelines:

*   **4.1.1 General Standard:** All procurement records must be retained for a minimum of **seven (7) years** from the date of the transaction or the submission of the final expenditure report, whichever is later.
*   **4.1.2 Federal Award Specifics:** Pursuant to 2 CFR 200.334, records pertinent to a Federal award must be retained for three years from the date of submission of the **final expenditure report**. However, Study GRC Inc. defaults to the stricter 7-year standard defined in 4.1.1 to ensure overlap with IRS audit windows.
*   **4.1.3 Real Property and Equipment:** Records for equipment acquired with Federal funds must be retained for **three (3) years after final disposition** (sale, scrapping, or transfer) of the property.
*   **4.1.4 Litigation or Audit:** If any litigation, claim, or audit is started before the expiration of the retention period, the records must be retained until all litigation, claims, or audit findings involving the records have been resolved and final action taken.

#### 4.2. Required Documentation Package
Every procurement transaction must maintain a "Procurement File" containing the following elements, scaled by the purchase threshold defined in the *Procurement Policy*:

| Procurement Method | Required Retained Documentation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Micro-Purchase** (<$10k) | 1. Vendor Invoice/Receipt.<br>2. Evidence of receipt of goods/services.<br>3. Brief notation of price reasonableness (if price is suspect). |
| **Small Purchase** ($10k - $250k) | 1. All items in Micro-Purchase.<br>2. **Rate/Price Quotations:** Minimum of three (3) written quotes.<br>3. **Selection Memo:** Documentation of why the vendor was chosen. |
| **Sealed Bids / Competitive Proposals** (>$250k) | 1. All items in Small Purchase.<br>2. **Solicitation:** Copy of the RFP/IFB.<br>3. **Evaluation:** Scorecards/Matrix signed by evaluators.<br>4. **Cost Analysis:** Independent estimate of costs.<br>5. **Contract:** Fully executed agreement.<br>6. **Suspension/Debarment:** Proof of SAM.gov verification. |
| **Non-Competitive** (Sole Source) | 1. Invoice/Contract.<br>2. **Justification Form:** Approved "Single Source Justification" citing the specific regulatory exception (e.g., 2 CFR 200.320(c)). |

#### 4.3. Digital Storage and Format (Remote-First)
As a remote-first organization, Study GRC Inc. relies on digital record-keeping.

*   **4.3.1 Digital Originals:** Electronic versions of records (PDF scans of physical receipts, digital invoices) are considered the official "original" records, provided they are legible and unalterable.
*   **4.3.2 Central Repository:** All procurement records must be stored in the designated secure cloud storage directory (e.g., `Finance Drive > FY[Year] > Expenses > [Vendor Name]`). Storing records solely on local hard drives or personal email accounts is **prohibited**.
*   **4.3.3 Naming Convention:** Files must be named consistently to facilitate retrieval: `YYYY-MM-DD_VendorName_Description_Amount.pdf`.

#### 4.4. Security and Access Control
*   **4.4.1 Access Rights:** Access to the Procurement Record repository is restricted to the Finance Department, the Executive Director, and external Auditors.
*   **4.4.2 Data Protection:** The repository must be protected by Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) in accordance with the *Master Information Security Policy*.
*   **4.4.3 PII Redaction:** Before archiving, any unnecessary Personally Identifiable Information (PII) (e.g., full credit card numbers on receipts) must be redacted.

#### 4.5. Disposal of Records
*   **4.5.1 Authorization:** No procurement records may be destroyed without written authorization from the CFO and verification that the retention period (Section 4.1) has expired.
*   **4.5.2 Method:** Digital records must be permanently deleted (purged from "Trash/Recycle Bin"). Physical records (if any) must be cross-cut shredded.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns this Standard. Authorizes the destruction of records. Ensures the central repository is organized and compliant with 2 CFR 200. |
| **Program Managers / Purchasers** | Responsible for uploading complete documentation (quotes, receipts, justifications) to the central repository within 5 business days of the transaction. |
| **Executive Director** | Reviews and approves any exceptions to this standard. Signs off on legal holds in the event of litigation. |
| **External Auditor** | Reviews retained documentation annually to verify compliance with Uniform Guidance. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard may result in **disallowed costs**, requiring Study GRC Inc. to repay Federal funds from non-Federal sources.

Internal consequences for violation of this Standard include disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Deliberate destruction of records during an active audit or investigation is a criminal offense and will result in immediate termination and legal referral.
