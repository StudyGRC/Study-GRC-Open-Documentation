### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish mandatory verification protocols for all content, code, and data generated by Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools used by Study GRC Inc. This Standard mitigates the risks of "hallucinations" (factually incorrect outputs), bias, intellectual property infringement, and security vulnerabilities.

As a 501(c)(3) organization dedicated to "advancing governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) knowledge" (Bylaws Article 1.3), the accuracy and integrity of our educational resources are paramount. This Standard ensures that all AI-assisted outputs meet the rigorous professional standards required of a GRC authority.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all **Authorized Users** (employees, volunteers, Board members, and contractors) who utilize AI tools (including but not limited to Large Language Models, image generators, and code assistants) to produce work product for the Organization.

**In Scope:**
*   Educational content (articles, wikis, course materials).
*   Technical artifacts (software code, scripts, configurations).
*   Administrative documents (policies, emails, grant reports).
*   External communications.

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal use of AI on personal devices not related to Organization business.
*   Low-risk internal brainstorming or summarization tasks that do not result in a final decision or public output without further review.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **AI Hallucination** | A phenomenon where an AI model generates false, misleading, or nonsensical information but presents it as fact. |
| **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)** | The mandatory process of human review and intervention before AI-generated content is finalized or published. |
| **Critical Output** | Any content that provides legal, regulatory, or technical advice; software code deployed to production; or financial reporting data. |
| **Source Fabrication** | The creation of non-existent citations, case law, or regulatory references by an AI model. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 The "Zero Trust" Output Mandate
All AI-generated content is presumed to be **draft quality** and potentially inaccurate until verified. No AI output may be published, deployed, or sent to external stakeholders without explicit Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) verification.

#### 4.2 Verification Levels by Content Type
Authorized Users must apply the following verification rigor based on the classification of the output:

| Classification | Examples | Verification Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1: Low Risk** | Internal meeting summaries, email drafts (non-legal), idea generation. | **Review for Tone & Clarity:** User must read the output to ensure it makes sense and aligns with the *Director of Kindness and Generosity* guidelines. |
| **Level 2: Educational** | Wiki articles, training modules, newsletters, social media posts. | **Fact-Check & Bias Review:** 100% verification of facts, dates, and definitions against authoritative sources (e.g., eCFR, NIST pubs). |
| **Level 3: Critical/Technical** | Code, security configs, legal/compliance advice, financial reports. | **SME Validation & Testing:** Must be reviewed by a qualified Subject Matter Expert (SME). Code must be compiled/run in a sandbox. |

#### 4.3 Citation and Fact Verification
Given the Organization's focus on GRC, specific attention must be paid to regulatory citations.
*   **4.3.1 Regulatory References:** If AI cites a specific law (e.g., "2 CFR 200.318"), the User **must** verify the text of the regulation via an official government source (e.g., ecfr.gov) to ensure the AI has not fabricated the clause or context.
*   **4.3.2 Case Law:** If AI cites legal precedents, the User must verify the case existence and the accuracy of the holding.
*   **4.3.3 URL Verification:** Users must click every link generated by AI to ensure it is active and leads to the claimed destination, preventing "link rot" or malicious redirects.

#### 4.4 Technical Code Verification
To protect the Organization's infrastructure and open-source users:
*   **4.4.1 Static Analysis:** All AI-generated code must be scanned for vulnerabilities (e.g., hardcoded secrets, injection flaws) prior to merging.
*   **4.4.2 Functional Testing:** Code must be executed in a non-production environment to verify it performs the intended function.
*   **4.4.3 Licensing Check:** Users must review code for distinct patterns that may indicate plagiarism of GPL/restrictive licensed code, ensuring compliance with Bylaws Article 9.3 (Open Source Commitment).

#### 4.5 Bias and Ethical Review
In accordance with the Organization's *Code of Ethics*, AI outputs must be screened for:
*   **Stereotyping:** Unfair generalizations regarding gender, race, or ability.
*   **Tone:** Aggressive or exclusionary language.
*   **Accessibility:** Ensuring AI-generated formatting (e.g., markdown tables, alt text) meets WCAG 2.2 AA standards per the *Digital Accessibility Policy*.

#### 4.6 Transparency and Labeling
*   **4.6.1 Internal Labeling:** When submitting work for peer review that was significantly generated (>50%) by AI, the User must disclose the tools used.
*   **4.6.2 External Labeling:** Public-facing resources significantly generated by AI must bear a disclaimer: *"Portions of this content were drafted with the assistance of AI and reviewed by human subject matter experts."*

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authorized User (Creator)** | Primary responsibility for the accuracy of the output. Acts as the first Human-in-the-Loop. Liable for errors in their submission. |
| **Subject Matter Expert (SME)** | Performs Level 3 validation for technical or regulatory accuracy. Has the authority to reject AI outputs that do not meet GRC standards. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all curriculum materials generated with AI assistance undergo Level 2 verification before publication. |
| **CISO** | Maintains tools for code scanning and establishes the sandbox environments for Level 3 technical verification. |
| **Executive Director** | Final authority on acceptable risk levels for AI adoption. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this Standard—specifically the publication of unverified, hallucinatory, or biased AI content—will be treated as negligence.

*   **First Offense:** Mandatory retraining on AI ethics and verification protocols.
*   **Severe/Repeated Offense:** Disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, particularly if the unverified output causes reputational damage or legal liability to the Organization.
