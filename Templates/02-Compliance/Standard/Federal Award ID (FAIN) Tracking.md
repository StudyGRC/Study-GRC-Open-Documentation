### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish the mandatory requirements for identifying, recording, and tracking the **Federal Award Identification Number (FAIN)** within Study GRC Inc.'s financial management systems.

Accurate FAIN tracking is the cornerstone of federal grant compliance. It ensures that the Organization meets the financial management standards of **2 CFR § 200.302(b)(1)**, which requires the identification of all federal awards received and expended. This Standard ensures data integrity for the Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA), facilitates accurate subrecipient monitoring, and supports transparency in public reporting.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to:
*   **Departments:** Finance, Grant Management, and Program Operations.
*   **Funds:** All federal financial assistance received by Study GRC Inc., whether received directly from a federal awarding agency or as a subrecipient through a pass-through entity (PTE).
*   **Systems:** The Organization’s General Ledger (GL), Grant Management Software, and document repositories.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **FAIN** | **Federal Award Identification Number**. A unique identification number assigned to a specific federal award by the federal awarding agency. It is the primary key for tracking federal funds. |
| **ALN** | **Assistance Listing Number** (formerly CFDA). A five-digit number assigned to identify a federal assistance program. |
| **Notice of Award (NoA)** | The official legal document issued by a federal agency or pass-through entity that notifies the recipient of an award and contains the terms and conditions, including the FAIN. |
| **SEFA** | **Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards**. A financial schedule required by 2 CFR § 200.510 that lists total expenditures for each federal program. |
| **Pass-Through Entity (PTE)** | A non-federal entity that provides a subaward to a subrecipient (e.g., Study GRC Inc. receiving federal funds via a State Agency). |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 FAIN Identification and Verification
Upon receipt of any Notice of Award (NoA) or subaward agreement:
1.  The **Grant Manager** must locate the FAIN on the official award document.
2.  If the FAIN is missing, ambiguous, or generic (e.g., labeled only as "Grant #1"), the Grant Manager must immediately contact the Federal Agency Grants Officer or the PTE to obtain the specific FAIN in writing before any funds are drawn down or expended.
3.  The FAIN must be verified against the **USASpending.gov** database to ensure it corresponds to the correct Fiscal Year and Agency.

#### 4.2 Financial System Configuration
To comply with **2 CFR § 200.302**, the accounting system must be configured to tag every federal transaction with the FAIN.

*   **4.2.1 Chart of Accounts/Dimensions:** A unique Project Code or Class must be created for each grant. The description field of this Project Code **must** include the FAIN.
*   **4.2.2 One-to-One Mapping:** Multiple FAINs cannot be commingled into a single General Ledger project code. If a program is funded by multiple awards (e.g., consecutive years), a distinct code must be established for each FAIN to prevent the commingling of funds.
*   **4.2.3 Revenue Recognition:** All journal entries recording revenue (drawdowns or advances) must explicitly reference the FAIN in the memo or reference field.

#### 4.3 Subaward Flow-Down Requirements
If Study GRC Inc. acts as a Pass-Through Entity (issuing subawards to other organizations):
1.  Pursuant to **2 CFR § 200.332(a)(1)**, the Subaward Agreement generated by Study GRC Inc. **must** clearly display the FAIN of the originating federal award.
2.  The Finance Department must track subrecipient expenditures linked specifically to the originating FAIN to ensure aggregate reporting accuracy.

#### 4.4 SEFA Preparation and Reporting
The FAIN serves as the primary unique identifier for the Annual Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA).
1.  **Data Integrity:** The Annual Financial Report (referenced in **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2**) must utilize the FAIN to aggregate expenditures for the SEFA.
2.  **Reconciliation:** The CFO must perform a quarterly reconciliation between the General Ledger expenses and the authorized amount associated with each FAIN to ensure no expenditures exceed the specific award ceiling.

#### 4.5 Record Retention
In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records)** and **2 CFR § 200.334**:
1.  The original NoA containing the FAIN must be stored digitally in the central grant repository.
2.  File naming conventions for grant documents must include the FAIN (e.g., `[FAIN]_Notice_of_Award.pdf`) to facilitate rapid retrieval during Single Audits.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Grant Manager** | Identifies the FAIN upon award receipt; validates the FAIN with the awarding agency; ensures the FAIN is included in all subaward agreements. |
| **Finance Manager** | Creates the unique Project Code in the accounting system mapped to the FAIN; ensures all expenses are coded to the correct FAIN-linked project. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews the SEFA for accuracy; ensures the financial system structure supports FAIN-level segregation of duties and reporting. |
| **Internal Auditor** | Periodically tests a sample of transactions to verify they are traced to the correct FAIN in the General Ledger. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to accurately track federal funds by FAIN constitutes a significant internal control deficiency and a violation of federal law.
*   **Internal:** Employees found neglecting this standard may face disciplinary action up to and including termination.
*   **External:** Inaccurate FAIN tracking may result in audit findings, questioned costs, the return of funds to the federal government, and suspension or debarment from future federal funding.
