### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to clearly define the specific duties, authority limits, and reporting lines for all Officers of Study GRC Inc., ensuring strict compliance with **Article IV** (Board Officers) and **Article V** (Operational Leadership) of the Bylaws. This document serves to distinguish the **Governance** function (oversight) from the **Operational** function (execution), minimizing role ambiguity and ensuring accountability within the Organization’s remote-first, community-driven structure.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to:
*   **Board Officers:** President, Secretary, Treasurer, and any Vice-Presidents elected by the Board.
*   **Operational Officers:** Executive Director (CEO), Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), VP of Education, and Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board Officer** | An individual elected by the Board of Directors to perform specific governance and fiduciary duties as mandated by the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and Bylaws Article IV. |
| **Operational Officer** | An individual appointed by the Board to manage the day-to-day execution of the Organization's mission, as defined in Bylaws Article V. |
| **Fiduciary Duty** | The legal obligation to act in the best interest of the Organization, encompassing the Duty of Care, Duty of Loyalty, and Duty of Obedience. |
| **Ex-Officio** | A member of a body (e.g., a committee) who is part of it by virtue of holding another office. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Distinction Between Governance and Operations
To maintain non-profit integrity and prevent conflicts of interest:
*   **Governance (Board Officers):** Focuses on strategic direction, policy creation, financial oversight, and legal compliance. They ensure the Organization stays true to its 501(c)(3) mission.
*   **Operations (Operational Officers):** Focuses on implementation, program delivery, volunteer management, and day-to-day decision-making.

#### 4.2 Board Officer Matrix (Governance)
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IV**, the following officers are elected annually by the Board.

| Role Title | Primary Function | Specific Responsibilities | Authority Limits |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **President** | **Board Leadership** | 1. Preside over all Board and Member meetings.<br>2. Set meeting agendas in coordination with the Secretary.<br>3. Serve as the primary liaison between the Board and the Executive Director.<br>4. Appoint Committee Chairs (subject to Board approval). | Cannot unilaterally bind the Organization to contracts >$5,000 without Board vote. Cannot hold the office of Secretary simultaneously (TBOC § 22.231). |
| **Secretary** | **Corporate Records & Compliance** | 1. Record and maintain minutes of all Board and Member meetings.<br>2. Maintain the official Membership Roll and verify Quorum.<br>3. Ensure proper notice is given for all meetings per Bylaws Art. II & III.<br>4. Custodian of the Certificate of Formation and Bylaws. | Authority to certify Board Resolutions and corporate documents for banks/agencies. |
| **Treasurer** | **Financial Oversight** | 1. Chair the Finance Committee.<br>2. Review financial reports prepared by the CFO.<br>3. Present financial status to the Board quarterly.<br>4. Ensure annual tax filings (IRS Form 990) are completed and reviewed. | Does not perform day-to-day bookkeeping (segregation of duties). Has signatory authority on bank accounts as a secondary approver. |

#### 4.3 Operational Officer Matrix (Management)
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V**, the following officers are appointed by the Board to execute the mission.

| Role Title | Primary Function | Specific Responsibilities | Reporting Line |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | **Strategic Execution** | 1. Manage day-to-day operations and staff/volunteers.<br>2. Execute the Strategic Plan approved by the Board.<br>3. Serve as the primary spokesperson for the Organization.<br>4. Negotiate contracts and partnerships. | Reports to: **Board of Directors** |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | **Financial Management** | 1. Manage daily bookkeeping, accounts payable/receivable.<br>2. Prepare monthly financial statements.<br>3. Manage payroll and expense reimbursements.<br>4. Coordinate with external auditors. | Reports to: **CEO**<br>*(Dotted line to Treasurer for oversight)* |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | **Security & Privacy** | 1. Manage the Organization’s technology stack and data privacy.<br>2. Enforce the Master Information Security Policy.<br>3. Oversee incident response and digital asset protection.<br>4. Manage IT volunteers. | Reports to: **CEO** |
| **VP of Education & Training** | **Curriculum** | 1. Develop and maintain GRC educational resources.<br>2. Oversee the quality of open-source materials.<br>3. Manage certification or training programs. | Reports to: **CEO** |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | **Culture & Community** | 1. Moderate the online community to ensure inclusivity.<br>2. Manage scholarship or financial aid programs.<br>3. Lead conflict resolution within the community.<br>4. Foster a "pay-it-forward" culture. | Reports to: **CEO** |

#### 4.4 Dual Service and Conflicts
*   **Dual Roles:** As permitted by **Bylaws Section 5.1**, a Director may simultaneously serve as an Operational Officer (e.g., a Director may also be the CISO).
*   **Recusal:** If a Board vote concerns the compensation, performance evaluation, or removal of an Operational Officer who is also a Director, that individual **must recuse** themselves from the vote in accordance with the **Conflict of Interest Policy**.
*   **Prohibited Combination:** The President and Secretary may **never** be the same person.

#### 4.5 Vacancy and Succession
*   **Board Officer Vacancy:** Must be filled by a majority vote of the remaining Directors at the next meeting.
*   **Operational Officer Vacancy:** The Board may appoint an Interim Officer or delegate duties to the CEO until a permanent replacement is found.
*   **Emergency Succession:** In the event the CEO is incapacitated, the President shall assume temporary operational authority until an Interim CEO is appointed.

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to perform the duties outlined in this Matrix constitutes a breach of fiduciary duty or employment/volunteer agreement.
*   **Board Officers:** May be removed for cause by a majority vote of the Board (Bylaws § 3.6).
*   **Operational Officers:** May be removed by the Board or by a petition of two-thirds (2/3) of Voting Members (Bylaws § 5.3).
