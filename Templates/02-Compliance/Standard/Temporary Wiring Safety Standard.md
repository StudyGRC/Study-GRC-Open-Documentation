### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish mandatory requirements for the use of temporary electrical wiring, extension cords, and power strips within Study GRC Inc. operations. This Standard is designed to prevent electrical fires, shock hazards, and tripping incidents in compliance with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.305 (Wiring Methods) and NFPA 70 (National Electrical Code).

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, contractors, and exhibitors utilizing electrical equipment during:
*   **Physical Events:** Conferences, workshops, training sessions, and pop-up labs.
*   **Temporary Operations:** Remodeling, maintenance, or repair activities.
*   **Remote Work:** Use of Organization-owned equipment in home offices.

**Exclusions:** This Standard does not apply to permanent building wiring systems, which fall under the jurisdiction of the facility owner or landlord.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Temporary Wiring** | Electrical wiring and extension cords used for a period not exceeding 90 days for Christmas/decorative lighting, or during construction, remodeling, maintenance, repair, or demolition of buildings. |
| **Daisy-Chaining** | The practice of plugging one power strip (RPT) or extension cord into another to increase reach or outlet count. This is strictly prohibited. |
| **RPT (Relocatable Power Tap)** | A UL-listed power strip, usually with a surge protector and circuit breaker. |
| **GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter)** | A device that shuts off an electric power circuit when it detects that current is flowing along an unintended path, such as through water or a person. |
| **Ampacity** | The maximum current, in amperes, that a conductor can carry continuously under the conditions of use without exceeding its temperature rating. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Permissible Use and Duration
4.1.1 **Time Limit:** Temporary electrical power and lighting installations may be used for a period not to exceed 90 days for holiday decorative lighting and similar purposes.
4.1.2 **Immediate Removal:** Temporary wiring must be removed immediately upon completion of the project or event for which it was installed.
4.1.3 **Substitute Prohibition:** Temporary wiring (extension cords) must **never** be used as a substitute for the permanent wiring of a structure.

#### 4.2 Equipment Specifications
4.2.1 **Certification:** All extension cords, RPTs, and electrical devices must be listed by a nationally recognized testing laboratory (e.g., UL, ETL).
4.2.2 **Grounding:** Only three-wire (grounded) extension cords designated for hard or extra-hard usage (e.g., types S, ST, SO, STO) are permitted. Two-wire (ungrounded) cords are prohibited for Organization equipment.
4.2.3 **Condition:** Equipment with frayed insulation, exposed wires, missing ground prongs, or cracked casings must be immediately removed from service and destroyed or tagged "DO NOT USE."

#### 4.3 Prohibited Configurations (Daisy-Chaining)
4.3.1 **Series Connection:** Connecting an RPT into another RPT (power strip into power strip) is strictly prohibited.
4.3.2 **Extension Cord to RPT:** Connecting an RPT into an extension cord is prohibited unless the extension cord is specifically rated for the total load and the temporary setup is for a duration of less than one work shift.
4.3.3 **High-Load Devices:** Appliances generating significant heat or load (e.g., space heaters, laser printers, coffee makers, microwave ovens) must be plugged directly into a wall receptacle. They must not be plugged into extension cords or RPTs.

#### 4.4 Physical Protection and Placement
4.4.1 **Trip Hazards:** Cords running across walkways or high-traffic areas must be secured using gaffer tape, cable ramps, or rubber cord protectors.
4.4.2 **Pinch Points:** Cords must not be run through doorways, windows, or holes in walls, ceilings, or floors where they may be pinched or abraded.
4.4.3 **Concealment:** Cords must not be run above drop ceilings or under carpets/rugs where heat cannot dissipate.
4.4.4 **Strain Relief:** Cords must not be attached to building surfaces using staples or nails. Cords must not support the weight of lighting fixtures.

#### 4.5 Wet Locations and Outdoor Use
4.5.1 **GFCI Requirement:** All temporary wiring used outdoors or in wet locations (e.g., near catering setups, restrooms) must be protected by a Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI).
4.5.2 **Outdoor Rating:** Only cords specifically marked for outdoor use (e.g., "W" rating in the code, such as SJTW) may be used outdoors.

#### 4.6 Load Management
4.6.1 **Ampacity Calculation:** The sum of the ampacity of all devices plugged into a cord or RPT must not exceed the rating of that cord/RPT.
4.6.2 **Touch Test:** If a cord feels hot to the touch, it is overloaded. It must be disconnected immediately.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Coordinator** | Ensures all temporary wiring setups at events comply with this Standard; has authority to disconnect unsafe setups. |
| **Staff / Volunteers** | Visually inspect cords before use; report damaged equipment; adhere to "no daisy-chaining" rules. |
| **Safety Officer** | Conducts spot checks during event setup; maintains inventory of compliant cable ramps and extension cords. |
| **Executive Director** | Allocates budget for compliant electrical equipment and cable management safety tools. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard creates a significant fire and life safety risk.
*   **Immediate Action:** Any wiring found in violation of this Standard (e.g., daisy-chained strips, frayed cords) will be immediately disconnected and confiscated by the Safety Officer or Event Coordinator.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Repeated violations by staff or volunteers may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Liability:** Contractors or exhibitors found in violation may be expelled from the event without refund if they refuse to rectify the safety hazard.
