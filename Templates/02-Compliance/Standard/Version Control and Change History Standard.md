### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish a mandatory, uniform methodology for tracking the evolution of Study GRC Inc.'s governance documentation. Strict version control ensures that:
1.  **Integrity:** Only current, authorized documents are used for decision-making and operations.
2.  **Auditability:** A clear, chronological audit trail exists for every modification, satisfying the record-keeping requirements of **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**.
3.  **Accountability:** Authorship and approval of changes are explicitly documented to prevent unauthorized modifications.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all **Controlled Documents** generated by Study GRC Inc.

**Applies to:**
*   Policies, Standards, Procedures, and Guidelines.
*   Forms, Templates, and Charters.
*   Formal Plans (e.g., Strategic Plan, Incident Response Plan).

**Exclusions:**
*   Ephemeral communications (emails, Slack messages).
*   Meeting minutes (which follow a separate approval process).
*   Marketing materials or blog posts not related to governance.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Controlled Document** | A document that provides evidence of governance, direction, or execution of the Organization's mission and requires formal approval. |
| **Major Revision (X.0)** | A change that alters the scope, intent, rules, or requirements of the document. Requires re-approval by the Approving Authority. |
| **Minor Revision (0.Y)** | A change that clarifies language, corrects typos, updates hyperlinks, or fixes formatting without changing the underlying rule or intent. |
| **Draft Version** | A document currently under development or review that has not yet received formal approval for implementation. |
| **Retired/Superseded** | A document version that is no longer in force but is retained for historical and legal audit purposes. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Version Numbering Syntax
All Controlled Documents must utilize the following decimal numbering convention (`vX.Y`):

1.  **Draft Phase (v0.Y):**
    *   The initial draft begins at **v0.1**.
    *   Subsequent drafts increment by 0.1 (e.g., v0.2, v0.3) until approval.
2.  **Initial Release (v1.0):**
    *   Upon formal approval by the Approving Authority, the document is promoted to **v1.0**.
3.  **Post-Approval Revisions:**
    *   **Minor Changes:** Increment the decimal (e.g., v1.0 $\rightarrow$ v1.1). These do not require full Board re-vote unless specified by the Bylaws, but must be approved by the Document Owner.
    *   **Major Changes:** Increment the integer (e.g., v1.4 $\rightarrow$ v2.0). These require full re-approval by the original Approving Authority (e.g., Board of Directors).

#### 4.2 Change History Log Requirements
Every Controlled Document must contain a **Revision History** table as the final section of the document. This table must contain, at minimum, the following columns:

*   **Version:** The number assigned to the specific iteration.
*   **Date:** The date the change was finalized (YYYY-MM-DD).
*   **Author:** The name and title of the individual who performed the editing.
*   **Change Description:** A summary of what was changed.
    *   *Acceptable:* "Updated Section 4.2 to align with new NIST 2.0 standards."
    *   *Unacceptable:* "Edits."

#### 4.3 Document Header Data
The first page of every Controlled Document must include a metadata table containing the **Version Number** and the **Effective Date**. The Effective Date must match the date of the final approval, not necessarily the drafting date.

#### 4.4 File Naming Convention
To ensure file system integrity within the Organization’s cloud storage (Google Drive/SharePoint), files must be named using the following convention:

`[Document ID]_[Short Title]_v[Version Number].[Extension]`

*   **Example:** `IS-POL-001_InfoSec_Policy_v2.0.pdf`
*   **Prohibited:** `InfoSec Policy FINAL FINAL v2.docx`

#### 4.5 Archival of Superseded Versions
When a new version (e.g., v2.0) is approved and published:
1.  The previous version (e.g., v1.0) **must not be deleted**.
2.  The previous version must be moved to a designated "Archive" or "Superseded" folder.
3.  The filename of the old version must be appended with `_SUPERSEDED`.
    *   *Rationale:* In the event of a legal claim or audit, the Organization must be able to produce the specific policy that was in force at the time of the incident.

#### 4.6 Track Changes and Redlining
During the review process for a **Major Revision**:
1.  The author must utilize "Suggesting Mode" or "Track Changes" to visualize modifications against the previous version.
2.  A "Clean" copy (changes accepted) and a "Redline" copy (changes visible) must be presented to the Approving Authority to facilitate efficient review.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Document Owner** | Responsible for drafting updates, incrementing version numbers correctly, and updating the Revision History log. |
| **Approving Authority** | Reviews the nature of changes to determine if they constitute a Major or Minor revision and provides formal authorization. |
| **Secretary / Compliance Officer** | Maintains the **Master Documentation Registry (MDR)**; ensures superseded versions are archived correctly; audits documents for adherence to this Standard. |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Responsible for verifying they are utilizing the most current version of a document (vX.Y) before taking action. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this standard—specifically the alteration of documents without version incrementing or the deletion of historical versions—compromises the Organization's legal standing and audit readiness.

Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment. Deliberate falsification of version history or dates is considered a breach of the **Code of Ethics** and may result in immediate removal.
