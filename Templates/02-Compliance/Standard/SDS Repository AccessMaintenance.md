### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to define the mandatory requirements for the maintenance, organization, and accessibility of Safety Data Sheets (SDS) within Study GRC Inc. This document ensures compliance with the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200), guaranteeing that all employees, volunteers, and contractors have immediate, barrier-free access to critical safety information regarding hazardous chemicals used during Organization operations.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all Study GRC Inc. operations where hazardous chemicals are present.
*   **Inclusions:**
    *   Physical storage locations (if applicable).
    *   Temporary event venues (conferences, workshops, training sessions).
    *   Remote work scenarios where the Organization provides specific chemical supplies (e.g., equipment cleaning kits, demonstration materials).
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Consumer products used in home offices for personal use (e.g., household glass cleaner) are exempt under OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1200(b)(6)(ix), provided they are used in the same duration and frequency as a normal consumer.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)** | A standardized document (formerly MSDS) containing information on the potential health effects of exposure to chemicals and safe working procedures. Must adhere to the GHS (Globally Harmonized System) 16-section format. |
| **Hazardous Chemical** | Any chemical which is classified as a physical hazard or a health hazard, a simple asphyxiant, combustible dust, pyrophoric gas, or hazard not otherwise classified. |
| **Immediate Access** | The ability to retrieve an SDS without barriers (such as locked doors, unknown passwords, or supervisor permission) within a reasonable timeframe (typically < 5 minutes) during a work shift. |
| **Master Repository** | The central, cloud-based storage location containing the current version of all SDSs. |
| **Event Binder** | A physical or offline-accessible digital backup of SDSs specific to materials present at a temporary venue. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Master Repository Configuration
To ensure data integrity and accessibility, the Master Repository must adhere to the following specifications:
*   **4.1.1 Centralization:** All SDSs must be stored in the designated Organization Cloud Drive (e.g., Google Workspace/SharePoint) under the folder path: `Operations > EHS > Safety Data Sheets (Master)`.
*   **4.1.2 Naming Convention:** Files must be named using the format: `[Product Name]_[Manufacturer]_[Revision Date YYYY-MM-DD].pdf`.
*   **4.1.3 Indexing:** A "Master Chemical Inventory List" (spreadsheet) must be maintained in the root of the repository, hyperlinking to the specific PDF for each chemical.

#### 4.2 Accessibility Requirements
Pursuant to OSHA "Right-to-Know" regulations, access must be uninhibited:
*   **4.2.1 Digital Access:** All personnel with an Organization email address must have "Viewer" permissions to the Master Repository.
*   **4.2.2 Barrier-Free Access:** Access to the repository must **not** require a specific request to a manager. If a device is password-protected, the password must be shared with all personnel in the work area.
*   **4.2.3 Backup System:** In the event of a power or internet outage, a secondary method of access must be available. For remote-first operations, this is defined as the "Event Binder" (see Section 4.4) during physical gatherings.

#### 4.3 Maintenance and Updates
*   **4.3.1 Acquisition:** Whenever a new hazardous chemical is purchased, the purchaser must obtain the SDS from the manufacturer and upload it to the Master Repository **before** the chemical is placed into use.
*   **4.3.2 Review Cycle:** The Master Chemical Inventory List must be audited annually to ensure all SDSs are current (within the last 3-5 years, or confirming no newer version exists).
*   **4.3.3 Archival:** Superseded SDSs must not be deleted. They must be moved to an `Archive` subfolder and retained for **30 years** post-use, in compliance with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1020 (Access to employee exposure and medical records).

#### 4.4 Event-Specific Standards
Given the Organization's nature as a remote entity that hosts physical events, the following standards apply to temporary venues:
*   **4.4.1 Venue Inventory:** 14 days prior to an event, the Event Manager must compile a list of all Organization-supplied chemicals (e.g., whiteboard cleaners, demo labs, sanitizers) to be transported to the venue.
*   **4.4.2 The "Travel Binder":** A physical binder or a dedicated, offline-capable tablet containing the specific SDSs for that event's inventory must be present at the event registration desk or operations hub.
*   **4.4.3 QR Code Access:** A QR code linking directly to the Master Repository must be posted in the event staff area.

#### 4.5 Contractor and Vendor Coordination
*   **4.5.1 Incoming Chemicals:** Third-party vendors (e.g., cleaning crews, caterers) bringing hazardous chemicals onto premises controlled by Study GRC Inc. must provide access to their SDSs upon request.
*   **4.5.2 Outgoing Information:** Study GRC Inc. must provide contractors access to the Master Repository if they will be exposed to Organization-owned chemicals during their work.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ultimate accountability for organizational compliance with OSHA Hazard Communication standards. |
| **Director of Operations** | Owner of the Master Repository. Conducts annual audits of the Chemical Inventory List. |
| **Event Manager** | Ensures the "Travel Binder" or offline backup is created and present for all physical events. Verifies SDS availability for any materials shipped to the venue. |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Responsible for reviewing the SDS prior to using a new chemical. Must report any missing SDSs to the Director of Operations immediately. |
| **Purchasing Agent** | Responsible for requesting the SDS from the vendor at the time of purchase. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain an accessible SDS repository constitutes a violation of federal law and poses a significant liability risk to the Organization.
*   **Internal:** Failure to upload an SDS for a new chemical or failure to provide access during an event may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **External:** Non-compliance may result in OSHA citations and civil penalties ranging from thousands to hundreds of thousands of dollars per violation.
