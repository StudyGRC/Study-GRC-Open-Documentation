### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish a mandatory framework for the separation of conflicting functional responsibilities within Study GRC Inc.'s financial and operational processes. This Standard is designed to prevent error, fraud, and conflicts of interest by ensuring that no single individual has exclusive control over a transaction from initiation to final reconciliation.

This document aligns with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** and satisfies the internal control requirements set forth in **2 CFR § 200.303** for federal grant compliance.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all financial and operational transactions conducted by Study GRC Inc.
*   **Applies to:** Board Directors, Operational Officers (CEO, CFO), employees, contractors, and volunteers involved in financial processing, procurement, payroll, or asset management.
*   **Exclusions:** None. Where staffing limitations prevent full segregation, the Compensating Controls defined in Section 4.3 must be applied.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Custody** | Having physical possession of assets (e.g., checks, cash, inventory) or control over electronic access to assets (e.g., bank signatories, credit card holders). |
| **Authorization** | The authority to approve transactions, bind the organization to agreements, or override system controls (e.g., signing contracts, approving invoices). |
| **Record Keeping** | The process of creating and maintaining records of transactions in the accounting system (e.g., posting journal entries, updating vendor master files). |
| **Reconciliation** | The process of verifying the accuracy of records by comparing them to external sources (e.g., bank statements, inventory counts). |
| **Compensating Control** | An alternative mechanism (usually independent review) implemented to mitigate risk when optimal segregation of duties is not feasible due to staffing constraints. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 General Principles of Segregation
To ensure financial integrity, the following four functions must be separated among different individuals for any distinct transaction cycle:
1.  **Authorization** (Approving the transaction)
2.  **Custody** (Holding the asset/funds)
3.  **Record Keeping** (Recording the transaction)
4.  **Reconciliation** (Verifying the transaction)

#### 4.2 Incompatible Duties Matrix
The following matrix defines specific duties that **must not** be performed by the same individual. An "X" indicates a conflict that requires segregation.

| Duty A | Duty B | Conflict Risk | Required Segregation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vendor Master File Mgmt** | **Invoice Approval** | Creation of fictitious vendors. | **Mandatory** |
| **Vendor Master File Mgmt** | **Check Signing / Payment** | Directing payments to self. | **Mandatory** |
| **Purchase Request** | **Purchase Approval** | Unauthorized spending. | **Mandatory** |
| **Invoice Approval** | **Check Signing / Payment** | Payment of invalid invoices. | **Mandatory** |
| **Check Preparation** | **Check Signing** | Alteration of checks. | **Mandatory** |
| **Check Signing** | **Bank Reconciliation** | Concealment of theft. | **Mandatory** |
| **Cash/Check Receipt** | **Bank Deposit Prep** | Skimming funds before deposit. | **Mandatory** |
| **Bank Deposit Prep** | **Accounts Receivable Entry** | Lapping / Skimming. | **Mandatory** |
| **Payroll Rate Entry** | **Payroll Processing** | Unauthorized salary increases. | **Mandatory** |
| **Payroll Processing** | **Payroll Distribution** | Ghost employees. | **Mandatory** |
| **General Ledger Entry** | **Bank Reconciliation** | Concealment of accounting errors/fraud. | **Mandatory** |

#### 4.4 Compensating Controls (Small Team Exception)
Recognizing that Study GRC Inc. is a non-profit with a lean operational structure, strict segregation may occasionally be impossible. In such instances, the following **Compensating Controls** are mandatory:

1.  **Bank Statement Review:** If the CFO/Bookkeeper both records transactions and prepares payments, the **Treasurer** (or a designated Director without check-signing authority) must receive the bank statement directly (or view it online) and document their review monthly.
2.  **Audit Trail Review:** The **Secretary** or **Treasurer** must review the "Audit Trail" or "Activity Log" from the accounting software quarterly to identify any changes to Vendor Master Files or deleted transactions.
3.  **Dual Signatures:** All disbursements exceeding **$5,000** require two authorized signatures (electronic or physical), typically the CEO and the Treasurer.
4.  **Payroll Verification:** The **Treasurer** must validate the payroll register against the approved salary schedule semi-annually.

#### 4.5 Information System Access Controls
Segregation of duties must be enforced via IT permissions in the accounting system (e.g., QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite):
*   Users with "Admin" privileges must not perform daily accounting entries.
*   The individual responsible for Bank Reconciliations must have "Read Only" access to the Check Register, or the system must log all edits they make.
*   Approval workflows must be system-enforced where possible, preventing a user from approving their own requisition.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ultimate oversight of internal controls. Approves this Standard. Reviews financial reports for anomalies. |
| **Treasurer** | Serves as the primary independent reviewer for reconciliations. Ensures the CFO/Bookkeeper does not have unchecked control over funds. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures operational staff adhere to this matrix. Authorizes expenditures within the budget but does not record them in the GL. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Implements the matrix. Configures software permissions. Reports any potential conflicts or breaches to the Treasurer immediately. |
| **Staff / Volunteers** | Must not share passwords or bypass approval workflows. Must report suspected fraud via the Whistleblower Policy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Strict adherence to this Segregation of Duties Matrix is a condition of employment and volunteer service.
*   **Violations:** Circumventing these controls (e.g., sharing passwords to bypass approval steps) will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Fraud:** Any evidence of intentional fraud facilitated by a lack of segregation will be referred to legal counsel and law enforcement.
