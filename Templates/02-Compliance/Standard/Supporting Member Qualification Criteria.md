### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish objective, quantifiable, and transparent criteria for the "Supporting Member" classification within Study GRC Inc. This document operationalizes **Article II, Section 2.1** of the Bylaws by defining the specific financial contributions or volunteer service thresholds required to obtain and maintain voting rights. This Standard ensures that governance participation is accessible to all community members regardless of financial status, aligning with the Organization’s 501(c)(3) mission to remove barriers to GRC knowledge.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to:
*   All individuals seeking to become or remain Voting Members of the Organization.
*   The Board Secretary and Treasurer responsible for validating membership rolls.
*   The Volunteer Coordinator responsible for tracking service hours.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Community Participants:** Individuals who utilize resources but do not meet the thresholds defined herein remain non-voting participants.
*   **Honorary Members:** Individuals appointed directly by the Board for lifetime achievement are exempt from the annual requalification criteria defined in Section 4.2.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Supporting Member** | A voting class of membership defined in Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1, entitled to one (1) vote on governance matters. |
| **Qualifying Period** | The twelve (12) month period immediately preceding the Record Date of a meeting or election. |
| **Record Date** | The specific date established by the Board for determining which members are eligible to receive notice and vote (per Texas BOC § 22.158). |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a member who has met all qualification criteria, has no outstanding disciplinary actions, and has adhered to the Code of Ethics. |
| **In-Kind Contribution** | Non-cash donations of goods or professional services (e.g., legal, accounting) valued at fair market rates. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 General Eligibility Requirements
To qualify as a Supporting Member, an individual must:
1.  Be a natural person (corporations and other entities are not eligible for voting membership).
2.  Be at least eighteen (18) years of age.
3.  Provide a valid name and email address for the official Membership Roll.
4.  Affirmatively agree to the **Board of Directors Code of Ethics and Conduct** and the **Community Guidelines**.

#### 4.2 Qualification Pathways
Pursuant to Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1, an individual must demonstrate commitment through **ONE** of the following two pathways during the Qualifying Period.

**4.2.1 Pathway A: Financial Contribution**
*   **Threshold:** A minimum cumulative contribution of **$100.00 USD** within the Qualifying Period.
*   **Method:** Contributions may be made via single donation, monthly recurring subscription, or employer matching gifts credited to the individual.
*   **Exclusions:** Purchase of merchandise (t-shirts, mugs) where goods are received does not count toward the contribution threshold unless the amount exceeds the fair market value of the goods received (per IRS regulations).

**4.2.2 Pathway B: Volunteer Service (Time Contribution)**
*   **Threshold:** A minimum of **twenty (20) hours** of documented volunteer service within the Qualifying Period.
*   **Eligible Activities:**
    *   Content creation (writing articles, developing curriculum).
    *   Community moderation (Discord/Forum management).
    *   Event support (virtual or physical).
    *   Committee service.
    *   Technical development (coding, IT support).
*   **Verification:** Hours must be logged in the Organization’s official **Volunteer Hours Tracking Log** and verified by a Committee Chair or Operational Officer.

#### 4.3 In-Kind Contributions
Professional services provided pro bono (e.g., legal counsel, CPA services, graphic design) may be counted toward Pathway A (Financial) based on the fair market value of the service, provided the service was formally requested by the Board or an Operational Officer.

#### 4.4 Maintenance of Good Standing
Status as a Supporting Member is not permanent and must be maintained annually.
1.  **Annual Review:** Membership qualifications are reviewed annually prior to the Annual Meeting.
2.  **Lapse:** If a member fails to meet the Financial OR Service threshold during a Qualifying Period, their status automatically reverts to "Community Participant" (Non-Voting) effective immediately after the expiration of their current term.
3.  **Reinstatement:** A Community Participant may regain Supporting Member status immediately upon meeting the threshold requirements for the current period.

#### 4.5 Disqualification
A member is considered **NOT** in Good Standing and ineligible to vote if:
1.  They have formally resigned in writing.
2.  They have been suspended or expelled for cause (e.g., harassment, ethical violations) pursuant to the **Member Expulsion Due Process Policy**.
3.  They have failed to satisfy the criteria in Section 4.2.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Supporting Member** | Responsible for ensuring their own contributions (financial or time) meet the threshold and are properly recorded prior to the Record Date. |
| **Secretary** | Responsible for certifying the alphabetical list of Voting Members entitled to vote prior to any meeting (Bylaws § 2.6). |
| **Treasurer** | Responsible for verifying financial contribution records and providing data to the Secretary. |
| **Volunteer Coordinator** | Responsible for auditing the Volunteer Hours Tracking Log and certifying that service thresholds have been met. |
| **Board of Directors** | Responsible for setting the Record Date and approving any changes to the contribution thresholds defined in this Standard. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to meet the criteria defined in this Standard will result in the classification of the individual as a "Community Participant" with no voting rights.

Any attempt to falsify financial records or volunteer hours to obtain voting rights constitutes a violation of the **Code of Ethics** and may result in a permanent ban from the Organization.

Disputes regarding qualification status must be submitted in writing to the Secretary within five (5) business days of the publication of the Voting List.
