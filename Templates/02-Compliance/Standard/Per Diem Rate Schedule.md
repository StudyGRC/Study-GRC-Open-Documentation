### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish the allowable per diem rates for lodging, meals, and incidental expenses (M&IE) incurred by Study GRC Inc. personnel during official business travel. This document ensures compliance with **2 CFR § 200.474 (Travel Costs)** for federal grant expenditures and aligns with IRS "Accountable Plan" rules to ensure reimbursements are non-taxable.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all **Study GRC Inc.** employees, Board Directors, volunteers, and contractors (where specified by contract) traveling on authorized Organization business.

*   **In Scope:** Overnight travel away from the individual's tax home (defined as a radius greater than 50 miles).
*   **Exclusions:** Day trips (no overnight stay), personal travel combined with business travel, and expenses deemed "Unallowable" under the *Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy* (FIN-POL-037).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **CONUS** | Continental United States (the 48 contiguous states and the District of Columbia). |
| **OCONUS** | Outside Continental United States (includes Alaska, Hawaii, and U.S. Territories). |
| **GSA** | U.S. General Services Administration, the federal agency that sets domestic per diem rates. |
| **M&IE** | Meals and Incidental Expenses. A flat daily rate to cover breakfast, lunch, dinner, and small fees (e.g., tips for porters/baggage carriers). |
| **Standard CONUS Rate** | The default rate applied to locations that do not have a specific "Non-Standard Area" (NSA) designation. |
| **Lodging Cap** | The maximum allowable reimbursement for hotel/accommodation costs (excluding taxes) based on the location and month of travel. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Authority and Source of Rates
Study GRC Inc. strictly adheres to the federal per diem rates established by the GSA for CONUS travel. Rates are updated annually by the federal government, typically effective October 1st (start of the Federal Fiscal Year).

*   **Official Source:** [www.gsa.gov/perdiem](https://www.gsa.gov/perdiem)
*   **International/OCONUS:** For travel to Alaska, Hawaii, or international locations, the Organization utilizes the rates established by the Department of Defense (DTMO) or the Department of State, respectively.

#### 4.2 Lodging Standards (Maximum Allowable Cost)
Unlike M&IE, lodging is **not** paid as a flat allowance. It is reimbursed based on **actual receipts** up to the GSA maximum rate for the specific location and month.

1.  **Rate Determination:** Travelers must verify the GSA lodging rate for their destination city/county for the specific month of travel.
2.  **Taxes:** Lodging taxes are reimbursable *in addition* to the GSA lodging cap.
3.  **Overage:** Any lodging cost exceeding the GSA rate is considered **Unallowable** for federal grant purposes. Such overages must be:
    *   Pre-approved by the Executive Director; AND
    *   Paid using unrestricted (non-federal) funds; OR
    *   Covered personally by the traveler.

#### 4.3 Meals and Incidental Expenses (M&IE) Standards
M&IE is reimbursed as a flat daily allowance. Receipts for individual meals are **not** required when claiming the per diem rate, provided the travel is substantiated (dates, location, business purpose).

##### 4.3.1 Rate Tiers
The M&IE total is determined by the GSA tier assigned to the destination. As of the Effective Date of this Standard, the tiers range from the Standard CONUS rate to high-cost locality rates.

*   **Incidental Expenses:** The GSA rate includes \$5.00 per day for incidentals (tips to porters, baggage carriers, hotel staff). This amount is included in the full M&IE payout.

##### 4.3.2 First and Last Day of Travel
Pursuant to Federal Travel Regulations, travelers are eligible for only **75%** of the full M&IE rate on the day of departure and the day of return, regardless of the time of day travel begins or ends.

##### 4.3.3 Meal Deductions (Provided Meals)
If a meal is provided to the traveler at no cost (e.g., included in a conference registration fee, provided by a host, or paid for by another entity), the M&IE claim **must be reduced** by the specific value of that meal component.

**Deduction Breakdown Formula:**
| Meal Component | Percentage of Total M&IE (Approx.) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Breakfast** | ~17% |
| **Lunch** | ~18% |
| **Dinner** | ~43% |
| **Incidentals** | Fixed \$5.00 (Retained by traveler) |

*Note: Travelers must consult the specific GSA M&IE Breakdown table for the exact dollar amount to deduct based on the destination's tier.*

#### 4.5 Grant Compliance (2 CFR 200)
For travel charged to federal awards:
1.  Reimbursement cannot exceed the rates specified in this Standard.
2.  Alcohol is strictly **Unallowable** and is never covered by the per diem (it is excluded from the M&IE calculation).
3.  Participation in "Community Participant" events or "Honorary Member" functions does not automatically authorize per diem unless specific business duties are performed.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Traveler** | Identify the correct GSA rate for the destination prior to booking. Accurately deduct provided meals from reimbursement requests. |
| **Approving Manager** | Verify that the claimed rates match the GSA table for the travel dates and location. Ensure "First/Last Day" calculations are applied correctly. |
| **Finance Department** | Audit expense reports against GSA historical data. Flag and segregate any overages as "Unallowable Costs" to prevent charging to federal grants. |
| **Executive Director** | Authorize exceptions for lodging overages in extenuating circumstances (e.g., conference hotel is the only viable option), ensuring non-federal funds are used for the excess. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard may result in the rejection of reimbursement claims, disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action for fraudulent claims.

**False Claims Act Notice:** Intentionally claiming per diem for days not traveled or meals already provided while charging federal grants constitutes a violation of the False Claims Act.
