### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this standard is to establish the minimum qualification, vetting, and competency requirements for all individuals authorized to deliver educational content on behalf of Study GRC Inc. This standard ensures the integrity of our curriculum, the safety of our participants (specifically regarding youth programs), and the alignment of all instruction with our charitable mission to foster a community-driven GRC ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all individuals seeking to serve as Instructors, Facilitators, Workshop Leaders, or Curriculum Creators for Study GRC Inc., regardless of whether the role is compensated or volunteer-based.

**Inclusions:**
*   Lead Instructors for certification courses.
*   Workshop facilitators.
*   Content creators developing self-paced modules.
*   Guest Lecturers delivering more than 15% of a course's content.

**Exclusions:**
*   Guest speakers or panelists appearing for a single session (under 60 minutes) under the direct supervision of a qualified Lead Instructor.
*   Administrative support staff or moderators not delivering educational content.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Instructor** | Any individual authorized by the Organization to teach, train, or facilitate educational sessions. |
| **SME** | Subject Matter Expert; an individual with demonstrated authority and experience in a specific GRC domain. |
| **Youth-Facing Role** | Any instructional role that involves direct interaction (virtual or physical) with participants under the age of 18, or access to data belonging to minors. |
| **Good Standing** | An active status within the professional community, free of ethical sanctions, criminal convictions relevant to the role, or violations of the Organization’s Code of Conduct. |
| **Demonstrated Competence** | Proof of ability through certification, work history, or practical demonstration (e.g., a teaching audition). |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Professional Subject Matter Expertise (SME)
To ensure the high quality of education mandated by our mission, all Instructors must meet the following SME criteria relevant to the specific course they intend to teach:

*   **4.1.1 Experience:** A minimum of three (3) years of verifiable professional experience in the specific domain (e.g., Cybersecurity, Audit, Privacy, Risk Management) being taught.
*   **4.1.2 Certification:** Possession of relevant, active industry certifications (e.g., CISSP, CISA, CIPP, CRISC) equivalent to or higher than the level of the course being taught.
    *   *Exception:* Instructors teaching introductory or "fundamentals" courses may hold associate-level certifications if approved by the VP of Education.
*   **4.1.3 Practical Application:** Evidence of practical application of knowledge (e.g., published white papers, GitHub repositories, conference presentations, or verified project history).

#### 4.2 Instructional Competence
Subject matter expertise alone is insufficient. Instructors must demonstrate the ability to transfer knowledge effectively.

*   **4.2.1 Teaching Audition:** All new Instructor candidates must perform a 20-minute "teaching audition" (live or recorded) reviewed by the VP of Education or their designee.
*   **4.2.2 Communication Skills:** Candidates must demonstrate proficiency in the language of instruction, including the ability to simplify complex GRC concepts as per Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3.
*   **4.2.3 Remote Delivery Proficiency:** Given the Organization's remote-first nature, Instructors must demonstrate technical proficiency with the Organization’s Learning Management System (LMS), video conferencing tools (e.g., Zoom/Teams), and collaboration platforms (e.g., Slack/Discord).

#### 4.3 Safety and Background Screening
Pursuant to the Organization's duty of care and *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, all Instructors must undergo screening.

*   **4.3.1 General Screening:** All Instructors must pass a standard identity verification and professional reference check.
*   **4.3.2 Youth-Facing Screening:** Any Instructor assigned to K-12 programs or courses open to minors **must** pass a Level 2 Background Check (including criminal history and sex offender registry search) prior to any interaction with participants.
    *   **Disqualification:** Any history of violence, sexual misconduct, or crimes against children results in immediate and permanent disqualification.
*   **4.3.3 Re-Screening:** Background checks for Youth-Facing Instructors must be renewed every two (2) years.

#### 4.4 Ethical and Mission Alignment
Instructors represent Study GRC Inc. and must adhere to our governance and ethical standards.

*   **4.4.1 Code of Conduct:** Instructors must sign and adhere to the *Board of Directors Code of Ethics and Conduct* (applied to Instructors) and the *Volunteer Code of Conduct*.
*   **4.4.2 Intellectual Property Assignment:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, Instructors must execute a "Work Made for Hire" agreement or IP Assignment deed, acknowledging that materials created for the course are the property of Study GRC Inc. to ensure they remain open-source and accessible.
*   **4.4.3 Non-Solicitation:** Instructors are prohibited from using the classroom environment to solicit consulting business, sell third-party products, or recruit for private employment without explicit written Board approval.

#### 4.5 Continuing Qualification
Instructor qualification is not permanent and must be maintained.

*   **4.5.1 Feedback Score:** Instructors must maintain an average participant feedback score of 4.0/5.0. Scores below this threshold for two consecutive cohorts will trigger a performance review.
*   **4.5.2 Content Updates:** Instructors are required to review and update their course materials at least annually to reflect changes in the GRC landscape (e.g., new laws, framework updates).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Final authority on approving Instructor technical qualifications and SME status. Conducts teaching auditions. |
| **HR / Operations** | Administers background checks and verifies identity. Maintains the *Instructor Registry*. |
| **Instructor Candidate** | Submits truthful documentation of experience, certifications, and consents to background screening. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Reviews Instructor candidates for cultural fit and alignment with community values. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to meet the standards defined herein will result in the denial of the application to teach.

For active Instructors, failure to maintain these standards (including lapsed certifications, failed background re-checks, or violation of the IP policy) will result in immediate revocation of Instructor status and removal from the schedule.

**Waiver Process:** Exceptions to the "Years of Experience" or "Certification" requirements (Section 4.1) may be granted only for individuals with exceptional, demonstrable industry contribution, upon written approval by both the VP of Education and the Executive Director.
