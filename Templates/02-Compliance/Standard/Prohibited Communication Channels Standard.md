### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish strict technical and behavioral boundaries regarding digital and physical communication between Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") representatives and Youth Participants. This Standard mitigates the risks of grooming, sexual abuse, and false allegations by ensuring all interactions occur on **Authorized Channels** that are transparent, auditable, and subject to the "Two-Deep Leadership" rule. This document supports the ethical qualifications for Directors and Members as outlined in **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.3**.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all "Organization Representatives," including:
*   Board of Directors and Officers.
*   Employees and Contractors.
*   Volunteers (including Mentors, Instructors, and Moderators).
*   Adult Community Participants interacting with minors in Organization-hosted spaces.

**Exclusions:** This Standard does not apply to communication between a parent/legal guardian and their own child.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Youth Participant** | Any individual under the age of 18 participating in Organization programs, events, or online communities. |
| **Authorized Channel** | A communication platform owned, managed, and monitored by the Organization, where logs are retained for audit purposes (e.g., Organization Slack/Discord public channels, Organization email). |
| **Prohibited Channel** | Any communication platform that is personal, unmonitored, ephemeral (disappearing), or lacks an audit trail accessible by the Organization. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The mandate that a minimum of two Organization-vetted adults must be present (physically or digitally) during any interaction with a Youth Participant. |
| **Ephemeral Messaging** | Communication platforms where messages automatically disappear after being viewed or after a set period (e.g., Snapchat, Signal, Instagram Vanish Mode). |
| **Grooming** | The predatory act of maneuvering a minor into a position of vulnerability for sexual abuse, often initiated through secretive or private communication. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 General Communication Principles
4.1.1 **Transparency:** All communications between Organization Representatives and Youth Participants must be viewable by a third party (parent, guardian, or another Organization Representative).
4.1.2 **No One-on-One Private Messaging:** Private Direct Messages (DMs) between an adult Organization Representative and a Youth Participant are strictly prohibited unless a third vetted adult is included in the group message.
4.1.3 **Auditability:** All communications must occur on platforms where the Organization retains administrative access to history and logs.

#### 4.2 Authorized Channels (Green List)
The following channels are approved for use, subject to the restrictions in Section 4.1:

*   **Organization-Hosted Community Platforms (e.g., Discord, Slack):**
    *   **Public Channels:** Allowed.
    *   **Group DMs:** Allowed **ONLY** if at least two vetted adults are present in the chat.
*   **Organization Email:**
    *   Emails to Youth Participants must copy (cc) the youth's parent/guardian OR another Organization staff member.
*   **Video Conferencing (Zoom/Teams):**
    *   Allowed for scheduled sessions.
    *   Sessions must be recorded or attended by a second adult.
    *   Breakout rooms must be monitored (roaming moderators).
*   **Project Management Tools (e.g., GitHub, Trello):**
    *   Comments on code/tickets are allowed as they are public record.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Channels (Red List)
Organization Representatives must **NEVER** use the following channels to communicate with Youth Participants regarding Organization business or personal matters:

*   **Personal Social Media:** Facebook Messenger, Instagram DMs, Twitter/X DMs, TikTok, LinkedIn DMs.
*   **Personal Contact Methods:** Personal email addresses (Gmail, Yahoo, etc.), personal cell phone numbers (text/SMS/calls), or WhatsApp.
*   **Gaming Platforms:** Steam Chat, PlayStation Network (PSN), Xbox Live, Roblox Chat, or in-game voice chats outside of official Organization-hosted servers.
*   **Ephemeral/Anonymous Apps:** Snapchat, Telegram (Secret Chats), Signal, Kik, Whisper, or any platform designed to hide identity or delete history.
*   **Dating Apps:** Any interaction on platforms such as Tinder, Bumble, or Grindr.

#### 4.4 Technical Controls and Configuration
4.4.1 **Platform Configuration:** Where technically feasible, the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) must configure Organization platforms (e.g., Discord) to technically prevent adults from initiating private DMs with users tagged as "Youth."
4.4.2 **Friend Requests:** Organization Representatives must not send or accept "Friend Requests" from Youth Participants on personal social media platforms.

#### 4.5 Emergency Exceptions
4.5.1 In the event of a life-threatening emergency or immediate safety risk where Authorized Channels are unavailable, an Organization Representative may use a personal device (e.g., cell phone) to contact a Youth Participant or their parents.
4.5.2 **Mandatory Reporting:** Any such emergency communication must be documented and reported to the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** within 24 hours, including the time, duration, and content of the communication.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Representatives** | Strictly adhere to the Authorized Channels list; refuse and report attempts by youth to communicate via Prohibited Channels. |
| **CISO** | Implement technical controls to restrict DMs on Organization platforms; maintain audit logs of Authorized Channels. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Monitor community interactions; receive reports of violations; conduct investigations into prohibited communications. |
| **Parents/Guardians** | Monitor their child's device usage; report any suspicious or private communication attempts by Organization staff to the Board. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Zero Tolerance:** Violation of this Standard is considered a critical breach of the Organization’s **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**.

*   **Staff/Volunteers:** Violations will result in immediate suspension pending investigation, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer service, and permanent ban from the Organization.
*   **Legal Action:** If a violation involves suspected grooming or abuse, the Organization will immediately report the individual to law enforcement and the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services in accordance with **Texas Family Code Chapter 261**.
