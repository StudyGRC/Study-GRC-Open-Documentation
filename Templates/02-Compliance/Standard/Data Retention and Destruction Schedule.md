### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to define the specific retention periods for Organization records and data to ensure compliance with legal obligations, including the **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC)**, **IRS regulations** for 501(c)(3) entities, and the **Storage Limitation** principle under GDPR Article 5(1)(e) and the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA).

This Standard mandates that data is not retained longer than necessary for the purposes for which it is processed, while ensuring critical business records are preserved for historical, financial, and legal defense purposes.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all **Records** (physical and electronic) generated, received, or maintained by Study GRC Inc., its employees, Directors, Officers, and volunteers.

**Inclusions:**
*   Corporate governance documents.
*   Financial and tax records.
*   Member and donor data.
*   Employment and volunteer personnel files.
*   IT system logs and security audit trails.
*   Intellectual property assignments.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Transitory Records:** Casual communications (e.g., "Lunch is at noon" Slack messages), rough drafts, and duplicate copies used solely for reference, provided they do not contain substantive business decisions.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Record** | Any information, regardless of physical form or characteristic, that documents a transaction or activity of the Organization and that is preserved for its administrative, legal, fiscal, or historical value. |
| **Retention Period** | The minimum required length of time a Record must be maintained by the Organization. |
| **Legal Hold** | A temporary suspension of the destruction of Records due to pending or anticipated litigation, audits, or government investigations. |
| **Storage Limitation** | The privacy principle dictating that personal data should not be kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data are processed. |
| **Secure Destruction** | The process of rendering data unreadable and irretrievable (e.g., cross-cut shredding, cryptographic erasure). |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 General Retention Rule
Unless a specific retention period is listed in the schedule below, or a **Legal Hold** is in effect, Records should generally be retained for **two (2) years** after the completion of the project or activity to which they relate, and then securely destroyed.

#### 4.2 Legal Hold Override
**CRITICAL:** In the event of a **Legal Hold** notification issued by the Chief Legal Officer or Board of Directors, **ALL** destruction of relevant records must cease immediately. Failure to preserve records under a Legal Hold may result in severe legal penalties (spoliation of evidence).

#### 4.4 Destruction Standards
When the retention period expires, data must be destroyed using methods that prevent reconstruction:

1.  **Paper Records:** Must be cross-cut shredded or incinerated. Binning intact documents is **prohibited**.
2.  **Digital Records:** Must be permanently deleted (purged from "Recycle Bin").
3.  **Storage Media:** Hard drives, USBs, or tapes must be cryptographically erased (wiped) or physically destroyed (crushed/degaussed) prior to disposal, in accordance with NIST SP 800-88.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this Standard and authorizes any permanent destruction of Corporate Governance records. |
| **Chief Legal Officer** | Maintains the Retention Schedule, issues **Legal Hold** notices, and interprets retention requirements for complex cases. |
| **CISO / IT Dept** | Configures automated retention policies (e.g., log rotation, email archiving) and executes secure digital destruction. |
| **Department Heads** | Ensures physical and digital records within their department (HR, Finance, Ops) are reviewed annually for destruction eligibility. |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Adheres to the schedule; does not save business records on personal devices; complies immediately with Legal Holds. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Audit:** The Audit & Risk Committee will periodically review compliance with this schedule.

**Enforcement:** Failure to comply with this Standard—specifically the unauthorized destruction of records under Legal Hold or the retention of sensitive data beyond its useful life—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential civil or criminal liability.
