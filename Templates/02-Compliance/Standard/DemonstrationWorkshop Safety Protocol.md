### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish mandatory safety requirements for all hands-on demonstrations, workshops, and educational events conducted by Study GRC Inc. This document ensures the physical safety of participants, instructors, and observers, mitigates liability risks associated with practical training (e.g., hardware hacking, lock picking, soldering), and ensures compliance with the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) General Duty Clause and organizational insurance requirements.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All employees, contractors, volunteers, and guest instructors representing Study GRC Inc.
*   **Participants:** All attendees engaging in hands-on activities.
*   **Activities:** All physical workshops, "villages" (e.g., Lock Picking Village), hardware tear-downs, and distributed/remote workshops where physical kits are provided by the Organization.

**Exclusions:**
*   Purely lecture-based presentations or webinars with no physical interaction or hardware components.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **High-Risk Activity** | Any educational activity involving heat sources (soldering), sharp objects (lock picks, knives), electricity (circuit testing), or chemical agents (solvents). |
| **PPE** | Personal Protective Equipment (e.g., safety glasses, gloves, hearing protection). |
| **Person in Charge (PIC)** | The designated instructor or staff member with ultimate authority over the safety of a specific workshop area. |
| **Stop Work Authority** | The right and obligation of any individual (staff or participant) to immediately halt an activity if they perceive an imminent danger. |
| **JSA** | Job Safety Analysis; a brief assessment of risks associated with a specific task. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Pre-Event Risk Assessment
Prior to any hands-on workshop, the Event Coordinator or Lead Instructor must classify the activity level:
*   **Level 1 (Low Risk):** Software-only labs, paper-based exercises. (Standard classroom safety applies).
*   **Level 2 (Moderate Risk):** Lock picking, server rack mounting, cabling. (Requires eye protection for specific tasks, pinch-point warnings).
*   **Level 3 (High Risk):** Soldering, dremel use, chemical etching, hardware destruction/tear-down. (Requires full PPE, ventilation, and strict supervision ratios).

#### 4.2 Physical Environment Setup
4.2.1 **Egress:** A minimum of 36 inches of clearance must be maintained in all aisles. Emergency exits must never be blocked by equipment, boxes, or cables.
4.2.2 **Cable Management:** All cables running across walkways must be taped down (gaffer tape) or covered with cable ramps to prevent tripping hazards.
4.2.3 **Ventilation:** For activities involving soldering or solvents, the workspace must have active ventilation (fume extractors or open airflow) sufficient to prevent the accumulation of hazardous fumes.
4.2.4 **Fire Safety:** A Class ABC fire extinguisher must be visible and accessible within 50 feet of any Level 3 activity.

#### 4.3 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
4.3.1 **Mandatory Eye Protection:** ANSI Z87.1 compliant safety glasses must be worn by all participants and observers within 5 feet of:
*   Soldering activities.
*   Wire cutting or stripping.
*   Use of rotary tools (e.g., Dremels).
*   Tensioning of lock picks where pick breakage is a risk.
4.3.2 **Hand Protection:** Cut-resistant gloves must be available for hardware tear-downs involving sharp chassis edges.
4.3.3 **Enforcement:** Instructors have the authority and duty to remove any participant who refuses to wear required PPE.

#### 4.4 Tool and Equipment Safety
4.4.1 **Inspection:** All tools must be inspected by the Instructor prior to the start of the workshop.
*   Power cords must be free of frays or exposed wiring.
*   Soldering iron tips must be secure.
*   Hand tools must be free of cracks or defects.
4.4.2 **Sharps Control:** Lock picks, exacto knives, and screwdrivers must be accounted for at the beginning and end of the session.
4.4.3 **Unauthorized Tools:** Participants are prohibited from bringing their own power tools unless explicitly authorized and inspected by the PIC.

#### 4.5 Youth Protection Integration
Pursuant to the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**:
4.5.1 **Supervision Ratios:**
*   **Level 1 Activities:** 1 Adult : 15 Youth.
*   **Level 2 Activities:** 1 Adult : 10 Youth.
*   **Level 3 Activities:** 1 Adult : 5 Youth.
4.5.2 **Prohibited Tools:** Youth under the age of 12 are prohibited from using soldering irons or rotary tools without 1-on-1 direct supervision by an authorized adult.
4.5.3 **Two-Deep Leadership:** No adult may ever be alone with a single minor in a workshop setting.

#### 4.6 Distributed (Remote) Workshop Safety
For virtual workshops where Study GRC Inc. mails physical kits to participants:
4.6.1 **Safety Insert:** Every kit must include a printed "Safety Warning Sheet" detailing specific hazards (e.g., "Warning: Contains small parts," "Warning: Hot surfaces").
4.6.2 **Waiver Requirement:** Kits will not be shipped until a digital Liability Waiver is signed.
4.6.3 **Instructor Briefing:** The virtual instructor must dedicate the first 5 minutes of the session to a safety briefing, requiring participants to visually confirm (on camera) they have a safe workspace.

#### 4.7 Emergency Response
4.7.1 **First Aid:** A stocked First Aid kit must be present at the Instructor's station.
4.7.2 **Incident Reporting:** Any injury, no matter how minor (e.g., small cut, minor burn), must be documented using the **Accident/Injury Reporting Form (OSHA 301 equivalent)** immediately following the session.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Lead Instructor (PIC)** | Conducts pre-event tool inspection; enforces PPE usage; delivers safety briefing; exercises Stop Work Authority. |
| **Event Coordinator** | Ensures venue meets physical setup standards (egress, ventilation); verifies insurance coverage; collects waivers. |
| **Director of Operations** | Reviews and updates this Standard; investigates reported incidents. |
| **Participant** | Adheres to all safety instructions; wears assigned PPE; reports unsafe conditions immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard may result in:
*   **Participants:** Immediate removal from the workshop without refund.
*   **Staff/Volunteers:** Disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Legal:** Potential civil liability or criminal negligence charges in the event of preventable injury.
