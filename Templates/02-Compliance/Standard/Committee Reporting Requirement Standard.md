### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this standard is to establish mandatory reporting protocols for all Standing Committees, Special Committees, and Advisory Boards of Study GRC Inc. This standard ensures transparency, maintains the Board of Directors' fiduciary oversight, and guarantees that committee activities align with the Organization’s mission and Bylaws. It provides a consistent framework for documenting committee decisions, recommendations, and activities to facilitate informed decision-making by the Board.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to:
*   **Standing Committees** (e.g., Finance, Governance).
*   **Special/Ad-Hoc Committees** created for specific projects.
*   **The Advisory Board** and any Advisory Committees.
*   **Committee Chairs** and Board Liaisons responsible for submitting reports.

**Exclusions:** Informal working groups or task forces that have not been chartered by the Board and do not possess delegated authority or advisory standing.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Committee Charge** | The official charter or mandate approved by the Board outlining the committee's goals, authority, and membership (Bylaws § 6.1). |
| **Delegated Authority** | The power granted by the Board to a committee to make binding decisions on behalf of the Organization without further Board ratification. |
| **Read-Ahead Material** | Documents distributed to the Board prior to a meeting to allow for review before discussion. |
| **Action Item** | A specific recommendation or motion arising from a committee that requires a vote by the full Board of Directors. |
| **Information Item** | Updates provided to the Board that do not require a vote (e.g., status updates, research findings). |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 Reporting Frequency
4.1.1 **Standing Committees:** Must submit a written report to the Board of Directors at least **quarterly**, or more frequently if the committee has met and generated Action Items requiring Board approval.
4.1.2 **Special/Ad-Hoc Committees:** Must submit a written report upon the completion of significant milestones defined in their Committee Charge, or immediately prior to a Board meeting where a decision is requested.
4.1.3 **Advisory Board:** Must submit a report via the Board Liaison semi-annually, or within 10 days of any formal meeting of the Advisory Board.

#### 4.2 Report Submission Timeline
4.2.1 All written committee reports must be submitted to the Secretary of the Board no later than **five (5) business days** prior to the scheduled Board of Directors meeting.
4.2.2 Reports submitted after this deadline may be excluded from the agenda at the discretion of the President or Chair, potentially delaying necessary approvals.

#### 4.3 Mandatory Report Content
All committee reports must be submitted in the Organization’s approved format and include the following data points:
1.  **Meeting Details:** Date(s) of committee meeting(s) covered by the report.
2.  **Attendance:** List of members present and absent (to verify quorum per committee rules).
3.  **Executive Summary:** A brief (1-2 paragraph) overview of activities.
4.  **Action Items (Motions):** Explicit text of any motions the committee recommends the Board adopt. These must be clearly separated from informational text.
5.  **Financial Impact:** If a recommendation involves expenditure, a budget impact statement must be included.
6.  **Risk Assessment:** Identification of any new risks (legal, reputational, operational) identified by the committee.
7.  **Next Steps:** Planned activities for the upcoming period.

#### 4.4 Advisory Board Communication Reporting
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI, Section 6.2**, specific reporting is required regarding Operational Officer interactions with the Advisory Board:
4.4.1 If an Operational Officer (e.g., CEO, CISO) communicates with the Advisory Board or its members regarding organizational business, a **Briefing Report** must be filed.
4.4.2 The Briefing Report must be submitted to the Board of Directors by the facilitating Board Director or the Operational Officer within **48 hours** of the communication or at the next scheduled Board meeting, whichever is sooner.
4.4.3 The Briefing Report must detail the nature of the inquiry, the advice given, and any potential conflicts of interest.

#### 4.5 Verbal Presentation Standards
4.5.1 To maximize Board meeting efficiency, Committee Chairs must assume that Directors have read the written report (Read-Ahead Material).
4.5.2 Verbal presentations during Board meetings are strictly limited to:
*   Answering questions regarding the written report.
*   Presenting **Action Items** for a vote.
*   Highlighting critical risks or urgent matters.
4.5.3 Reading the written report verbatim during the Board meeting is prohibited.

#### 4.6 Archival
4.6.1 Once accepted by the Board, committee reports must be appended to the official Board Meeting Minutes.
4.6.2 The Secretary is responsible for storing these reports in the Organization’s central governance repository in accordance with the *Records Retention Schedule*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Committee Chair** | Drafts the report, ensures accuracy of attendance/voting records within the committee, and submits the report to the Secretary by the deadline. |
| **Secretary of the Board** | Compiles committee reports into the Board Meeting Agenda/Packet; enforces submission deadlines; archives accepted reports. |
| **Board Liaison** | Facilitates reports from the Advisory Board; ensures Operational Officer communication briefings are filed (Bylaws § 6.2). |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews Read-Ahead Material prior to meetings; votes on Action Items presented by committees. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this standard may result in the following actions:
*   **Agenda Exclusion:** Committee recommendations may be removed from the Board agenda, delaying critical projects or approvals.
*   **Loss of Chairship:** Repeated failure to submit required reports may result in the removal of the Committee Chair by the Board President.
*   **Revocation of Authority:** The Board may revoke delegated authority from a committee that fails to maintain transparent reporting standards.
