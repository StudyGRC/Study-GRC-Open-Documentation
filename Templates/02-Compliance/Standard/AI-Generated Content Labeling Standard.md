### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to mandate specific disclosure and labeling requirements for all content created, modified, or enhanced using Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies within Study GRC Inc.

As an educational non-profit dedicated to Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC), maintaining the trust of our community is paramount. This Standard mitigates the risks of misinformation ("hallucinations"), protects the Organization’s reputation for authenticity, and ensures compliance with emerging transparency regulations and the Organization's ethical commitment to "fostering a community-driven ecosystem" as defined in **Bylaws Article 1.3**.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all **Public-Facing Content** and **Educational Resources** produced by Study GRC Inc. employees, Board members, volunteers, and contractors.

**In Scope:**
*   Educational articles, guides, and curriculum materials.
*   Source code, scripts, and technical configurations.
*   Graphics, images, and video content.
*   Social media posts and official press releases.
*   Chatbot or automated support responses.

**Out of Scope:**
*   Internal administrative communications (e.g., internal emails, meeting summaries) that are not published externally.
*   Content where AI usage is limited strictly to spelling, grammar correction, or basic translation (e.g., Grammarly, Microsoft Editor), provided the core ideas and syntax remain human-authored.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **AI-Generated Content** | Content where the primary draft, structure, or code was produced by a Generative AI tool (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, GitHub Copilot, Midjourney) based on a human prompt. |
| **AI-Assisted Content** | Content originally drafted by a human, where AI was used to refine tone, summarize, or expand on specific sections, but the human author retains significant creative control. |
| **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)** | The mandatory process where a qualified human subject matter expert reviews, fact-checks, and approves AI output before publication. |
| **Hallucination** | A phenomenon where an AI model generates false, misleading, or nonsensical information presented as fact. |
| **Watermarking** | The embedding of a visible or invisible digital marker in content to indicate its provenance. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 General Labeling Requirements
All content published by the Organization must be categorized and labeled according to the degree of AI involvement. Silence regarding provenance is prohibited; if AI was used significantly, it must be disclosed.

#### 4.2 Text-Based Content (Articles, Guides, Policy Templates)
For written documentation, the following disclosure statements must be placed clearly within the document (e.g., header, footer, or introduction):

*   **Category A: Fully Human-Authored**
    *   *Requirement:* No label required, or optional: "Authored by [Name]."
*   **Category B: AI-Assisted (Human Drafted, AI Refined)**
    *   *Requirement:* "This content was drafted by [Name] and refined using AI tools for clarity."
*   **Category C: AI-Generated (AI Drafted, Human Reviewed)**
    *   *Requirement:* "This resource was generated by Artificial Intelligence under the supervision of Study GRC Inc. subject matter experts. Reviewed by: [Name/Role]."

**Prohibition:** No text-based GRC advice may be published without a named human reviewer (HITL) to verify accuracy against current regulations (e.g., NIST, ISO, GDPR).

#### 4.3 Source Code and Technical Scripts
To ensure security and maintainability, code generated by tools like GitHub Copilot must include a standard comment block at the top of the file or function.

**Required Syntax:**
```python
# ------------------------------------------------------------------
# PROVENANCE: AI-GENERATED / AI-ASSISTED
# REVIEWER: [Maintainer Name/ID]
# DATE: [YYYY-MM-DD]
# NOTE: Code logic verified for security and functionality.
# ------------------------------------------------------------------
```

#### 4.4 Visual Media (Images and Video)
*   **Visible Labeling:** Images generated entirely by AI (e.g., DALL-E, Midjourney) must feature a visible caption or watermark stating: *"Image generated by AI."*
*   **Metadata:** Where technically feasible, the file metadata (IPTC/EXIF) must be tagged with `Source: AI-Generated`.
*   **Deepfakes:** The creation or use of realistic AI-generated depictions of real human beings (deepfakes) without their explicit, written consent is **strictly prohibited** per the **Professional Ethics and Community Guidelines**.

#### 4.5 Chatbots and Automated Interactions
Any automated system (e.g., a Discord bot or website support agent) interacting with community members must self-identify immediately.

*   **Standard Greeting:** "I am an automated AI assistant for Study GRC. I can help find resources, but I am not a human legal expert."

#### 4.6 Verification of Accuracy (Anti-Hallucination)
Pursuant to the Organization's mission to "empower learners," ensuring accuracy is critical.
1.  **Fact-Checking:** The human reviewer must manually verify all citations, regulatory references (e.g., "NIST SP 800-53 r5"), and legal claims made by the AI.
2.  **Liability Disclaimer:** All AI-generated educational resources must include the Organization’s standard legal disclaimer: *"This content is for educational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice."*

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Content Creator / Author** | Responsible for accurately categorizing content and applying the correct labels during the drafting phase. |
| **Subject Matter Expert (Reviewer)** | Acts as the "Human-in-the-Loop" (HITL); responsible for verifying the accuracy of AI-generated content and signing off on the label. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all curriculum and training materials comply with this labeling standard before release. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Monitors community channels to ensure AI bots are not impersonating humans or misleading members. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer** | Defines technical standards for watermarking and metadata tagging; audits code repositories for proper attribution. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Standard—specifically the intentional concealment of AI usage in a manner that misleads the community—is a violation of the **Board of Directors Code of Ethics** and the **Professional Ethics Guidelines**.

Violations may result in:
1.  Immediate removal of the non-compliant content.
2.  Revocation of publishing privileges.
3.  Disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
