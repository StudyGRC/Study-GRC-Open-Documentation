### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to define the mandatory content, formatting, and accessibility requirements for the "Transparency" section of the Study GRC Inc. website. This Standard ensures compliance with Internal Revenue Service (IRS) regulations regarding public inspection of tax-exempt organization documents (IRC § 6104), satisfies the transparency mandates established in **Article VIII, Section 8.2** of the Bylaws, and fosters public trust through proactive disclosure of governance and financial data.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to:
*   **Web Content:** The specific webpage(s) designated for public disclosure (e.g., `studygrc.org/transparency`).
*   **Personnel:** The Board Secretary, Treasurer, Communications Team, and Web Development/IT staff responsible for publishing content.
*   **Documents:** All governance, financial, and operational documents designated for public release.

**Exclusions:** This Standard does not authorize the publication of confidential personnel records, proprietary trade secrets, or donor lists (Schedule B of Form 990), which must be protected in accordance with the *Data Privacy Policy*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Form 990** | The annual information return filed by 501(c)(3) organizations with the IRS. |
| **Public Inspection** | The legal requirement for tax-exempt organizations to make specific documents available to the public upon request or via the web. |
| **Redaction** | The process of obscuring or removing sensitive information (e.g., signatures, home addresses, donor names) from a document prior to publication. |
| **Machine-Readable** | A data format that can be easily processed by a computer (e.g., a text-based PDF rather than a scanned image). |

### 4. Standard Requirements

#### 4.1 Accessibility and Location
To ensure "widely available" status under IRS regulations:
1.  **Navigation:** A link labeled "Transparency," "Public Filings," or "Financials" must be present in the global footer of the Organization’s primary website.
2.  **No Barriers:** Access to these documents must **not** require:
    *   User account registration or login.
    *   Payment of a fee.
    *   Submission of personal data (e.g., email capture forms).
3.  **File Format:** All documents must be provided in **PDF format**. Whenever possible, files should be text-searchable (OCR) rather than flat images to support accessibility tools.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Regulatory Documents (IRS & State)
The following documents must be available for download at all times:
1.  **IRS Determination Letter:** The official letter from the IRS recognizing the Organization’s 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status.
2.  **IRS Form 1023:** The original application for tax exemption, including all supporting schedules and attachments (redacted for PII).
3.  **IRS Form 990:** The three (3) most recent annual information returns.
    *   **Redaction Requirement:** **Schedule B (Schedule of Contributors)** must be omitted or redacted to protect donor privacy, as permitted by law.
    *   **Signatures:** Officers' physical signatures on the web version should be redacted to prevent forgery/identity theft.

#### 4.3 Governance Documents
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII**, the following governance documents must be published to demonstrate organizational integrity:
1.  **Certificate of Formation:** The charter filed with the Texas Secretary of State.
2.  **Bylaws:** The current, effective version of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc.
3.  **Conflict of Interest Policy:** To demonstrate compliance with IRS Form 990, Part VI.
4.  **Whistleblower Protection Policy:** To demonstrate compliance with Sarbanes-Oxley and IRS recommendations.
5.  **Board of Directors List:** A current list of serving Directors.

#### 4.4 Financial and Impact Reporting
1.  **Annual Financial Reports:** As required by **Bylaws Section 8.2**, a report summarizing support, revenue, expenses, and changes in fund balances.
2.  **Audited Financial Statements:** If the Organization undergoes an independent financial audit or review, the final report must be posted.

#### 4.5 Content Maintenance and Currency
1.  **Update Frequency:**
    *   **Form 990:** Must be uploaded within thirty (30) days of filing with the IRS.
    *   **Board List:** Must be updated within fourteen (14) days of any election or resignation.
    *   **Bylaws:** Must be updated within fourteen (14) days of any amendment.
2.  **Archive:** While only the last three years of Form 990 are legally required, the Organization shall maintain a minimum of five (5) years of financial reports on the website to demonstrate long-term stability.

#### 4.6 Security and Redaction Standards
Before any document is uploaded to the Transparency page, it must undergo a **Redaction Review** by the Secretary or CISO to ensure the removal of:
1.  **Personal Identifiable Information (PII):** Home addresses, personal phone numbers, and social security numbers of Directors or Staff.
2.  **Bank Details:** Any visible routing or account numbers.
3.  **Signatures:** Physical wet signatures (names should remain typed/printed).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | **Document Owner.** Responsible for gathering governance documents, ensuring the Bylaws are current, and approving the final redacted versions for publication. |
| **Treasurer** | Responsible for providing the finalized, filed versions of IRS Form 990 and Annual Financial Reports to the Secretary. |
| **CISO** | Responsible for establishing redaction tools/standards and auditing the page annually to ensure no PII leakage. |
| **Webmaster / IT** | Responsible for the technical implementation, ensuring uptime of the page, and verifying broken links are fixed immediately. |
| **Executive Director** | Responsible for final sign-off on the "Annual Financial Report" narrative before publication. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain public access to IRS Form 990 and the Exemption Application may result in daily penalties assessed by the IRS against the Organization and the responsible individuals (IRC § 6652(c)).

Internal failure to comply with this Standard may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board, particularly if such failure results in the exposure of confidential donor data or PII.
