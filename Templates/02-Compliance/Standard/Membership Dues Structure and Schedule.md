### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish the financial and service-based contribution requirements for membership in Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This document operationalizes **Article II** of the Bylaws by defining the specific thresholds for "demonstrated commitment" required to obtain Voting Membership rights.

This Standard ensures the Organization maintains a sustainable revenue model for governance administration while strictly adhering to the Organization's mission of removing financial barriers to GRC knowledge.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all individuals seeking to join the Organization or renew their membership status.
*   **Inclusions:** All classes of membership defined in the Bylaws.
*   **Exclusions:** Corporate sponsorships, grants, and donations made outside of the membership structure.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Community Participant** | A non-voting class of membership with free access to open-source resources and community events. |
| **Supporting Member** | A voting class of membership for individuals who have demonstrated commitment through financial contribution or volunteer service. |
| **Honorary Member** | A voting class of membership awarded by the Board for lifetime achievement; exempt from dues. |
| **Service-in-Lieu** | A mechanism allowing members to substitute volunteer hours for financial dues to maintain Voting Member status. |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a member who is current on dues or service requirements and is not under disciplinary suspension. |
| **Membership Year** | The 12-month period commencing on the date of enrollment or renewal. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1. General Principles
In accordance with **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, the Organization is dedicated to removing financial barriers. Therefore:
1.  Access to the Organization’s educational content, open-source repositories, and general community forums **must** remain free of charge.
2.  Membership Dues apply solely to the privilege of **governance participation** (Voting Rights) and administrative support, not educational access.
3.  Dues are considered charitable contributions to the extent allowed by law (IRS 501(c)(3)).

#### 4.2. Class A: Community Participants (Non-Voting)
*   **Annual Dues:** $0.00 (Free).
*   **Service Requirement:** None.
*   **Rights:** Access to public content, community forums, and events. No voting rights.

#### 4.3. Class B: Supporting Members (Voting)
To qualify as a Supporting Member and exercise voting rights under **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.2**, an individual must satisfy **one** of the following two tracks annually:

##### 4.3.1. Financial Contribution Track
*   **Standard Rate:** $50.00 USD per Membership Year.
*   **Student / Hardship Rate:** $25.00 USD per Membership Year.
    *   *Verification:* Applicants requesting this rate must self-certify current enrollment in an academic institution or financial hardship. No invasive documentation is required, relying on the Honor Code.

##### 4.3.2. Service-in-Lieu Track (Volunteer Service)
Individuals may waive the financial fee by contributing time to the Organization.
*   **Requirement:** Minimum of ten (10) documented volunteer hours within the preceding 12 months.
*   **Eligible Activities:** Content creation, moderation, mentorship, code contribution, or administrative support.
*   **Verification:** Hours must be logged in the Organization’s Volunteer Tracking System and approved by a Department Head.

#### 4.4. Class C: Honorary Members (Voting)
*   **Annual Dues:** $0.00 (Waived for Life/Term).
*   **Service Requirement:** None.
*   **Designation:** Status is conferred solely by a majority vote of the Board of Directors.

#### 4.5. Payment and Processing
*   **Timing:** Dues are payable upon application for Voting Membership and annually thereafter on the anniversary of enrollment.
*   **Methods:** The Organization accepts Credit Card, ACH, and approved Cryptocurrencies (subject to *FIN-POL-060 Cryptocurrency Donation Policy*).
*   **Proration:** Dues are not prorated.
*   **Refunds:** Membership dues are non-refundable, even in the event of resignation or termination of membership.

#### 4.6. Delinquency and Loss of Good Standing
*   **Grace Period:** Members have a thirty (30) day grace period following their renewal date to pay dues or log required service hours.
*   **Lapse:** If requirements are not met by the end of the grace period, the member is automatically reclassified as a **Community Participant (Non-Voting)**.
*   **Reinstatement:** A member may reinstate Voting status at any time by paying the full annual dues or verifying accrued service hours. Voting rights are restored immediately upon processing.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews dues rates annually and ensures proper accounting of revenue. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains the official roll of Voting Members and verifies "Good Standing" prior to any vote or election. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Approves Service-in-Lieu hours and manages the volunteer tracking log. |
| **Voting Member** | Responsible for timely payment of dues or submission of volunteer hours to maintain voting rights. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to pay dues or complete service requirements results in the automatic loss of **Good Standing**. Members not in Good Standing are ineligible to:
1.  Vote in Board elections or on Fundamental Actions.
2.  Inspect corporate books and records under **Bylaws Section 2.6**.
3.  Serve on the Board of Directors.
