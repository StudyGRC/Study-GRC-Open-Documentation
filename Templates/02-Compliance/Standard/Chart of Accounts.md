### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to establish a uniform **Chart of Accounts (CoA)** for Study GRC Inc. This framework ensures financial data is recorded consistently to satisfy Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB) ASC 958 for not-for-profit entities, and federal grant compliance requirements under 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance).

This standard facilitates the preparation of the Annual Financial Report required by **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2**, supports the filing of IRS Form 990, and enables accurate tracking of restricted funds and functional expenses.

### 2. Scope
This standard applies to all financial transactions, budgeting processes, and reporting activities within the Organization.
*   **Applies to:** Finance Department, Budget Owners, Grant Managers, and external bookkeeping services.
*   **Exclusions:** Non-financial operational metrics not tied to the general ledger.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Natural Classification** | The nature of the expense (e.g., Salaries, Rent, Supplies) regardless of the program it supports. |
| **Functional Classification** | The purpose of the expense (e.g., Program Services, Management & General, Fundraising). |
| **Restricted Net Assets** | Funds subject to donor-imposed restrictions (time or purpose) that must be tracked separately from general operating funds. |
| **Unallowable Costs** | Costs that cannot be charged to federal awards per 2 CFR 200 Subpart E (e.g., alcohol, lobbying, bad debts). |
| **Contra Account** | An account that reduces the balance of another account (e.g., Accumulated Depreciation reduces Fixed Assets). |

### 4. Standard Statements

#### 4.1 Account Coding Structure
To ensure granular reporting, Study GRC Inc. utilizes a multi-dimensional coding structure. All transactions must be coded using the following format:
`[Account]-[Fund]-[Department]-[Project]`

*   **Account (4 Digits):** The Natural Classification (defined in Sections 4.2 - 4.5).
*   **Fund (2 Digits):** Source restrictions (e.g., 01 General, 02 Federal Grant, 03 Restricted Scholarship).
*   **Department (2 Digits):** Functional area (e.g., 10 Admin, 20 Education, 30 IT/Security).
*   **Project (3 Digits):** Specific grant or initiative (e.g., 101 Annual Conf, 205 Open Source Repo).

#### 4.5 Functional Allocation Codes (Department Segment)
Expenses must be tagged with one of the following to satisfy IRS Form 990 Part IX:
*   **10 Management & General:** Accounting, Board meetings, Legal, General HR.
*   **20 Program Services:** Education, Content Creation, Community Management.
*   **30 Fundraising:** Grant writing, Donor solicitation, Fundraising events.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owner of the Chart of Accounts. Authorizes the creation of new accounts. Ensures alignment with GAAP and Grant requirements. |
| **Bookkeeper / Accountant** | Records daily transactions using the correct codes. Flags ambiguous transactions to the CFO. |
| **Program Managers** | Responsible for coding invoices and expense reports to the correct Project and Department codes before submission. |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews the Chart of Accounts annually for relevance and compliance with Board reporting needs. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Audit Trail:** Modifications to the Chart of Accounts (renaming, merging, or deleting accounts) must be documented to preserve historical data integrity.
*   **Grant Compliance:** Misclassification of "Unallowable Costs" (Series 9500) as allowable expenses constitutes a violation of federal regulations and may result in the repayment of funds and loss of grant eligibility.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Failure to adhere to this standard, particularly regarding the intentional miscoding of expenses to conceal their nature, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal action.
