### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to ensure that all synchronous (live) events, webinars, and meetings hosted by Study GRC Inc. are accessible to individuals who are Deaf, hard of hearing, or have cognitive processing disabilities. This document establishes mandatory technical and operational requirements for live captioning to ensure compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Title III, Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2, and the Organization’s Bylaws Article 1.3 commitment to "removing barriers."

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to:
*   **All Public-Facing Events:** Webinars, workshops, conferences, and town halls hosted by Study GRC Inc.
*   **Internal Governance Meetings:** Board of Directors meetings and Committee meetings where a participant has requested accommodation.
*   **Training Sessions:** Educational programming delivered via video conferencing platforms (e.g., Zoom, Teams, Discord).

**Exclusions:**
*   Spontaneous, unrecorded internal 1:1 voice calls between staff members where no accommodation request is on file.
*   Physical in-person events without a digital component (governed separately by *Event Venue Accessibility Standards*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **CART (Communication Access Realtime Translation)** | The instant translation of the spoken word into English text using a stenotype machine, notebook computer, and realtime software. Produced by a human operator. |
| **ASR (Automatic Speech Recognition)** | Technology that converts spoken language into text using Artificial Intelligence algorithms without human intervention. |
| **Synchronous Media** | Audio or video content that is streamed live with no significant delay. |
| **WER (Word Error Rate)** | A common metric for the performance of a speech recognition or machine translation system. |
| **Accommodation Request** | A formal notification from a participant indicating a specific need for an auxiliary aid or service. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 General Requirement
All virtual events hosted by Study GRC Inc. must provide a mechanism for participants to view live captions (subtitles) in real-time. The "Closed Captioning" feature must be enabled by the host prior to the start of the broadcast.

#### 4.2 Accuracy Tiers and Selection
The method of captioning (Human CART vs. AI ASR) is determined by the nature of the event and specific accommodation requests.

*   **Tier 1: Mandatory Human CART (98%+ Accuracy)**
    *   **Trigger:** Any event where a specific accommodation request for CART has been received from a registrant.
    *   **Trigger:** High-stakes paid training courses or certification reviews.
    *   **Trigger:** Annual Member Meetings (Bylaws Article II, Section 2.5).
    *   **Requirement:** A qualified CART provider must be contracted. The stream must achieve a minimum accuracy rate of 98%.

*   **Tier 2: High-Quality ASR (AI Captioning)**
    *   **Trigger:** General free community webinars, weekly meetups, or internal team meetings where no specific CART request exists.
    *   **Requirement:** The platform used must utilize an enterprise-grade ASR engine (e.g., Zoom Live Transcription, Microsoft Teams Live Captions).
    *   **Threshold:** If ASR accuracy drops below acceptable levels (e.g., due to technical accents or jargon), the host must attempt to remediate or provide a transcript immediately post-event.

#### 4.3 Technical Specifications
To satisfy this Standard, the captioning delivery must meet the following technical criteria:
1.  **Latency:** The delay between the spoken word and the appearance of text must not exceed six (6) seconds.
2.  **Synchronization:** Captions must remain synchronized with the audio source.
3.  **User Control:** The platform must allow the end-user to:
    *   Toggle captions On/Off.
    *   Adjust font size.
    *   Move the caption window to avoid obscuring visual content (e.g., slides).
4.  **Recording:** If the live event is recorded, the caption track (VTT or SRT file) must be saved and embedded in the Video on Demand (VOD) release.

#### 4.4 Speaker and Audio Protocols
To ensure the captioning system (Human or AI) functions correctly, all presenters must adhere to the following:
1.  **Microphone Quality:** Use a dedicated headset or external microphone. Built-in laptop microphones are prohibited for primary speakers due to background noise interference.
2.  **Pacing:** Speak at a moderate pace.
3.  **Turn-Taking:** Avoid talking over other participants.
4.  **Jargon:** When using complex GRC acronyms, speakers should articulate clearly or provide a glossary in the chat.

#### 4.5 Third-Party Platform Vetting
Any third-party platform (e.g., webinar software, virtual conference venue) procured by Study GRC Inc. must be evaluated for accessibility compliance. Vendors that do not support live captioning integration are prohibited from use for public events.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures budget allocation for Human CART services for Tier 1 events. Approves vendor contracts for captioning providers. |
| **Event Host / Moderator** | Enables captioning features at the start of every session. Monitors caption quality during the event. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Manages the intake of Accommodation Requests from the community and notifies the Education team at least 72 hours prior to an event. |
| **IT / CISO** | Configures global settings on corporate platforms (Zoom/Teams) to ensure captioning is available by default. |
| **Presenters/Speakers** | Adhere to audio protocols defined in Section 4.4. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this standard puts the Organization at risk of legal action under the ADA and reputational damage within the community.
*   **Event Cancellation:** Events that cannot meet the Tier 1 requirement when a valid accommodation request is pending may be postponed or cancelled by the Executive Director.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Staff or volunteers who repeatedly fail to enable accessibility features may face disciplinary action, up to and including termination of their role.
