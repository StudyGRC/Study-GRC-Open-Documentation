### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to clearly define the hierarchy of accountability and specific responsibilities regarding Environmental, Health, and Safety (EHS) within Study GRC Inc. This document ensures compliance with the Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA) General Duty Clause and Texas Labor Code by eliminating ambiguity regarding who is responsible for safety outcomes in a remote-first, event-driven non-profit organization.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all levels of the Organization, including the Board of Directors, Operational Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers.
*   **Inclusions:** Remote worksites (home offices), physical event venues, and third-party locations where Organization business is conducted.
*   **Exclusions:** The private, non-work-related activities of staff within their own homes, outside of designated working hours.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **EHS** | Environmental, Health, and Safety; the discipline and framework for protecting the safety and health of workers and the public. |
| **General Duty Clause** | Section 5(a)(1) of the OSH Act, requiring employers to provide a workplace free from recognized hazards. |
| **Remote Worksite** | The designated area within an employee or volunteer's home where official duties are performed. |
| **Event Venue** | Any temporary physical location (hotel, conference center, school) where Study GRC Inc. hosts programming. |
| **Incident** | An unplanned event that results in personal injury, property damage, or a "near miss." |

### 4. Standard Statements

#### 4.1 Accountability Hierarchy
While safety is a shared responsibility, accountability flows downward from the Board of Directors to individual contributors.
*   **Strategic Oversight:** The Board of Directors retains ultimate fiduciary responsibility for ensuring the Organization has adequate policies and insurance to manage safety risks (per Bylaws Article III and VII).
*   **Operational Execution:** The Executive Director (CEO) is the "Accountable Executive" for EHS compliance.
*   **Implementation:** Department Heads and Event Managers are responsible for implementing safety standards within their specific domains.

#### 4.2 Remote-First Context
As a remote-first entity, the Organization delegates day-to-day control of the physical environment to the employee, subject to the *Remote Worksite Safety Self-Assessment*.
*   **Organization Responsibility:** Providing safety training, ergonomic guidelines, and reporting mechanisms.
*   **Employee Responsibility:** Maintaining a hazard-free home office environment (e.g., electrical safety, trip hazards).

#### 4.3 Event Safety
During physical events (conferences, workshops), EHS responsibilities shift to on-site management.
*   The designated **Event Safety Officer** (ESO) has the authority to halt any activity deemed immediately dangerous to life or health (IDLH) without prior Board approval.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities Matrix (RACI)

### 6. Specific Role Descriptions

#### 6.1 Board of Directors
*   Ensures EHS risks are included in the Organization’s Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) strategy.
*   Reviews annual safety reports and major incident investigations.
*   Authorizes indemnification for officers acting in good faith regarding safety decisions (Bylaws Article VII).

#### 6.2 Executive Director (CEO)
*   Serves as the primary "Employer" representative for OSHA compliance.
*   Ensures adequate resources (financial and personnel) are available to meet safety standards.
*   Reports "Fundamental Actions" or catastrophic risks to the Board immediately.

#### 6.3 Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)
*   *Context:* Per Bylaws Article V, this role manages community culture.
*   **EHS Responsibility:** Oversees the "Psychological Safety" aspect of EHS, including mental health resources, anti-harassment culture, and conflict de-escalation during community interactions.

#### 6.4 Event Manager / Safety Officer
*   Conducts pre-event hazard assessments.
*   Maintains the on-site First Aid Kit and Emergency Contact List.
*   Delivers the "Safety Briefing" at the start of all physical workshops.

#### 6.5 All Employees and Volunteers
*   **Duty to Comply:** Must adhere to all safety policies and wear required PPE (if applicable during events).
*   **Duty to Report:** Must immediately report any unsafe condition, injury, or near-miss to their supervisor or via the Whistleblower form.
*   **Right to Refuse:** May refuse to perform work they reasonably believe poses an imminent danger of death or serious physical harm.

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to the responsibilities defined in this matrix may result in:
1.  **Corrective Action:** Retraining or revision of the safety plan.
2.  **Disciplinary Action:** Up to and including termination of employment or volunteer service for gross negligence or willful violation of safety standards.
3.  **Legal Action:** Referral to civil or criminal authorities if violations involve criminal negligence or intentional harm.
