### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to ensure that all video content produced, published, or distributed by Study GRC Inc. is accessible to individuals who are blind or have low vision. By mandating Audio Description (AD), the Organization aligns with its mission to remove barriers to Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) knowledge and ensures compliance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2, specifically Success Criterion 1.2.5 (Level AA).

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all **prerecorded** synchronized media (video with audio) produced by Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, and contractors.

*   **In Scope:**
    *   Educational tutorials and course modules uploaded to the Learning Management System (LMS).
    *   Marketing and promotional videos hosted on the Organization’s website or social media channels (e.g., YouTube, LinkedIn).
    *   Recordings of live webinars or events once they are archived and published for on-demand viewing.
*   **Out of Scope:**
    *   Live video streams (real-time).
    *   "Talking Head" videos where the visual content provides no additional information beyond what is spoken (see Section 4.2).
    *   Third-party content linked by the Organization but not hosted or controlled by the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audio Description (AD)** | A narration track added to the soundtrack to describe important visual details that cannot be understood from the main audio track alone (e.g., "The presenter points to the red slice of the pie chart"). |
| **Synchronized Media** | Audio or video synchronized with another format for presenting information and/or with time-based interactive components. |
| **Integrated Description** | A production technique where the presenter or actors verbally describe visual elements as part of the main script, negating the need for a separate audio description track. |
| **Talking Head** | A video consisting primarily of a person speaking to the camera, where background or visual context is decorative and not essential to understanding the content. |
| **WCAG 2.2** | Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.2, the international standard for web accessibility. |

### 4. Standards

#### 4.1 General Requirement
All prerecorded video content containing visual information necessary for the comprehension of the content **must** include Audio Description. This ensures that users who cannot see the video can understand the visual information via the auditory channel.

#### 4.2 The "Talking Head" Exemption
Audio Description is **not required** if the video is exclusively a "talking head" format where:
1.  The visual content serves only as a placeholder for the audio; AND
2.  No text, charts, graphics, or meaningful actions appear on screen that are not explicitly spoken by the presenter.

#### 4.3 Approved Methods of Implementation
Content creators must utilize one of the following methods to satisfy this standard, listed in order of preference:

**4.3.1 Method A: Integrated Description (Preferred)**
*   Scripting must be developed so that visual information is described by the speaker naturally during the recording.
*   *Example:* Instead of saying "Click here," the presenter must say, "Click the 'Submit' button located in the top right corner."
*   If Integrated Description is executed successfully, a separate AD track is not required.

**4.3.2 Method B: Separate Audio Track**
*   If the original audio does not describe the visuals, a secondary audio track containing the descriptions must be provided.
*   This track must be user-selectable (toggled on/off) within the video player.

**4.3.3 Method C: Fixed Version**
*   If the video player does not support multiple audio tracks, a second version of the video file with the Audio Description permanently mixed into the main audio track must be provided via a clearly labeled link (e.g., "View Audio Described Version").

#### 4.4 Content Requirements for Description
When creating Audio Description (Method B) or scripting Integrated Description (Method A), the following elements **must** be described if they are essential to understanding the content:
*   **Text on Screen:** Titles, bullet points, or lower-thirds that are not spoken verbatim.
*   **Data Visualizations:** Trends in charts, graphs, or diagrams (e.g., "The graph shows a sharp increase in cyber incidents in Q4").
*   **Actions:** Physical demonstrations, software navigation paths, or significant changes in setting.
*   **Context:** Facial expressions or body language if they contradict or significantly add to the spoken dialogue.

#### 4.5 Technical Specifications
*   **Timing:** Descriptions must be inserted during natural pauses in the dialogue to avoid talking over the primary speaker.
*   **Volume:** The description volume must be consistent with the main program audio.
*   **Platform Support:** Video players utilized on the Study GRC website must support the delivery of AD (either via toggle or alternative link).

#### 4.6 Vendor and Contractor Compliance
Any third-party vendor or contractor hired to produce video content for Study GRC Inc. **must** be contractually obligated to deliver content that meets WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards, including the provision of Audio Description or Integrated Description.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Content Creators / Instructors** | Responsible for writing scripts that utilize **Integrated Description** whenever possible to minimize post-production costs. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Responsible for reviewing video content prior to publication to verify that visual information is adequately described. |
| **IT / Web Team** | Responsible for ensuring the Organization's video player technology supports optional audio tracks or alternative video links. |
| **Marketing Team** | Responsible for ensuring promotional videos on social media include AD or are scripted with Integrated Description. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this standard may result in the removal of non-compliant content from public-facing platforms until remediation is complete. Repeated failure by staff or volunteers to adhere to accessibility standards may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
