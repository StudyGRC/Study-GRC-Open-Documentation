### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Standard is to define the specific limits of authority delegated to Officers, Directors, and employees to execute contracts, approve expenditures, and legally bind Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This document establishes a robust Internal Control framework to prevent fraud, ensure fiscal responsibility, and maintain compliance with Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance) requirements for federal grant recipients.

### 2. Scope
This Standard applies to all:
*   **Financial Instruments:** Checks, wire transfers, ACH payments, and credit card transactions.
*   **Legal Agreements:** Contracts, Memoranda of Understanding (MOUs), grant agreements, and settlements.
*   **Employment Matters:** Offer letters, severance agreements, and contractor engagements.

**Exclusions:** This Standard does not supersede the "Fundamental Actions" clause defined in Article X of the Bylaws (e.g., mergers, dissolution), which require Voting Member approval.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Signature Authority** | The power to legally bind the Organization to a contract or financial obligation. |
| **Budgeted Expenditure** | An expense that has been explicitly included in the Annual Budget approved by the Board of Directors. |
| **Unbudgeted Expenditure** | An expense not included in the Annual Budget, or an expense that exceeds the specific line-item allocation by more than 10%. |
| **Dual Signature** | A requirement for two authorized individuals to sign a single instrument (check or contract) to validate the transaction. |
| **Fundamental Action** | As defined in Bylaws Article X, actions requiring Member vote (e.g., amending the Certificate of Formation, voluntary dissolution). |

### 4. Standard Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles of Authority
4.1.1 **Delegation Source:** Per Bylaws Article III, the Board of Directors manages the affairs of the Organization. This Matrix represents the Board's formal delegation of day-to-day management authority to Operational Leadership (Bylaws Article V).
4.1.2 **Prohibition on Structuring:** It is strictly prohibited to split a single transaction or contract into multiple smaller amounts ("structuring") to bypass the authorization limits defined in this Matrix.
4.1.3 **Conflict of Interest:** No individual may sign or approve a transaction in which they have a financial interest, as defined in the *Conflict of Interest Policy*.
4.1.4 **Digital Signatures:** Given the Organization's remote-first nature, secure digital signatures (e.g., DocuSign, Adobe Sign) are legally binding and preferred, provided the audit trail is preserved.

#### 4.2 Execution Matrix: Financial & Contractual Limits

The following limits apply to **Budgeted Expenditures**. All **Unbudgeted Expenditures** exceeding $1,000 require prior written approval from the Treasurer or President.

| Tier | Transaction Value (USD) | Required Approver(s) / Signatory | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1** | **$0.01 – $2,999.99** | Any **Operational Officer** (e.g., VP Education, CISO, DKG) | Must be within the Officer's specific department budget. |
| **Tier 2** | **$3,000.00 – $24,999.99** | **Executive Director (CEO)** | May be delegated to CFO in writing for specific recurring costs (e.g., payroll). |
| **Tier 3** | **$25,000.00 – $99,999.99** | **Executive Director (CEO)** AND **Treasurer** (Dual Authorization) | Requires two distinct signatures/approvals. |
| **Tier 4** | **$100,000.00+** | **Board of Directors** (Majority Vote) | Requires a Board Resolution recorded in the minutes prior to execution. |

#### 4.3 Specific Transaction Types

**4.3.1 Bank Checks and Electronic Transfers**
*   **Up to $5,000:** Single signature/approval by Executive Director or Treasurer.
*   **Over $5,000:** Dual signature/approval required (Executive Director + Treasurer OR President).
*   **Prohibition:** Signing blank checks is strictly prohibited.

**4.3.2 Federal Grant Agreements**
*   **Application Submission:** The Executive Director is authorized to submit grant applications.
*   **Award Acceptance:**
    *   Awards < $100,000: Executive Director may sign.
    *   Awards > $100,000: Board of Directors must vote to accept the award and its terms.

**4.3.3 Employment and Contractors**
*   **Operational Staff/Contractors:** Executive Director has authority to hire/terminate within the approved staffing budget.
*   **Operational Officers:** Appointment requires Board approval (Bylaws Article V).
*   **Executive Director:** Hiring, termination, and compensation changes require a Board vote.

**4.3.4 Legal Settlements**
*   Any agreement to settle a lawsuit or legal dispute involving a payment or admission of liability requires **Board of Directors** approval, regardless of the dollar amount.

**4.3.5 Intellectual Property (IP)**
*   **Inbound IP (Licensing):** Follows the standard financial tiers in Section 4.2.
*   **Outbound IP (Assignment/Exclusive Licensing):** Any agreement granting *exclusive* rights to Organization IP to a third party requires **Board of Directors** approval to ensure compliance with Bylaws Article IX (Open Source Commitment).

#### 4.4 Fundamental Actions (Bylaws Article X)
Notwithstanding any other provision in this Matrix, the following actions require an affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3) of all Voting Members**:
1.  Amending the Certificate of Formation.
2.  Voluntarily dissolving the Organization.
3.  Selling all or substantially all assets.
4.  Mergers or consolidations.

**No Officer or Director has the authority to sign documents executing these actions without the certified results of a Member Vote.**

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves this Matrix annually. Authorizes Tier 4 transactions and Fundamental Actions. |
| **Treasurer** | Serves as the secondary approver for Tier 3 transactions. Monitors compliance with this Matrix during monthly financial reviews. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Primary signatory for Tier 1 and Tier 2 transactions. Ensures all staff are aware of their specific limits. |
| **CFO / Bookkeeper** | Verifies that the correct number of signatures/approvals are present before releasing funds. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains the repository of Board Resolutions authorizing Tier 4 transactions. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**6.1 Unauthorized Acts:** Any contract signed or expenditure approved by an individual exceeding their authority as defined in this Matrix may be deemed voidable by the Organization.
**6.2 Personal Liability:** Individuals who incur obligations on behalf of the Organization without proper authority may be held personally liable for those obligations.
**6.3 Disciplinary Action:** Failure to comply with this Standard may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (for cause), and potential legal action for breach of fiduciary duty.
