### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Employee Handbook (the "Handbook") is to establish the terms of employment, operational standards, and behavioral expectations for employees of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). It serves to ensure compliance with the Texas Labor Code, federal employment laws, and the Organization’s Bylaws, while fostering a safe, inclusive, and compliant remote-first work environment.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:** All full-time, part-time, and temporary employees of Study GRC Inc.
**Exclusions:** This Handbook does not apply to Independent Contractors (1099), Volunteers, or members of the Board of Directors (unless they are also employed as Operational Officers).

**Disclaimer:** This Handbook is not a contract of employment. It does not guarantee employment for any specific duration.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **At-Will Employment** | An employment relationship where either the employer or the employee may terminate the relationship at any time, for any reason (that is not illegal), or for no reason at all, with or without notice. |
| **Exempt Employee** | Employees paid on a salary basis who meet specific duty tests under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and are not eligible for overtime pay. |
| **Non-Exempt Employee** | Employees paid on an hourly or salary basis who are entitled to overtime pay for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. |
| **Remote-First** | An operational model where the primary place of performance is the employee's home office or a non-centralized location. |
| **Work Product** | As defined in Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3, all content, code, and materials created by employees during their service. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Employment at Will
Pursuant to Texas law, employment with the Organization is "at-will." This means that the employment relationship may be terminated by either the employee or the Organization at any time, with or without cause, and with or without advance notice. No representative of the Organization, other than the Board of Directors (via written agreement signed by the President), has the authority to enter into any agreement for employment for any specified period or to make any agreement contrary to the at-will standard.

#### 4.2 Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO)
The Organization provides equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants without regard to race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, or gender identity), national origin, age, disability, genetic information, or veteran status, in accordance with applicable federal laws and the Texas Labor Code Chapter 21.

#### 4.3 Anti-Harassment and Non-Discrimination
The Organization maintains a zero-tolerance policy regarding harassment and discrimination.
*   **Sexual Harassment:** Includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature.
*   **Reporting:** Any employee who feels they have been harassed or discriminated against must report the incident immediately to the Executive Director or, if the complaint involves the Executive Director, to the Board President.
*   **Non-Retaliation:** The Organization strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports harassment or participates in an investigation.

#### 4.4 Employment Classifications
*   **Full-Time:** Employees regularly scheduled to work at least 30 hours per week.
*   **Part-Time:** Employees regularly scheduled to work fewer than 30 hours per week.
*   **Temporary:** Employees hired for a specific project or limited duration.

#### 4.5 Compensation and Timekeeping
*   **Pay Periods:** Employees are paid on a [Bi-Weekly/Monthly] basis.
*   **Timekeeping:** All Non-Exempt employees must accurately record all time worked using the Organization’s designated timekeeping system.
*   **Grant Compliance:** In accordance with 2 CFR 200.430, all employees (Exempt and Non-Exempt) working on federal grants must complete Personnel Activity Reports (PARs) or time sheets that accurately reflect the distribution of activity across specific cost objectives.
*   **Overtime:** Non-Exempt employees will receive overtime pay at a rate of 1.5 times their regular rate for hours worked in excess of 40 in a workweek. All overtime must be pre-approved by the Executive Director.

#### 4.6 Remote Work and Telecommuting
*   **Workspace:** Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe, secure, and ergonomic workspace at their remote location.
*   **Availability:** Employees are expected to be available via the Organization’s communication channels (e.g., Slack, Email, Video) during agreed-upon core hours.
*   **Jurisdiction:** Regardless of the employee's physical location, the laws of the State of Texas shall govern the employment relationship to the fullest extent permitted by law.

#### 4.7 Leave and Time Off
*   **Paid Time Off (PTO):** Eligibility and accrual rates are defined in the employee’s offer letter.
*   **Jury Duty:** In accordance with Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code § 122.001, the Organization will grant leave for jury service. Retaliation for jury service is prohibited.
*   **Voting Leave:** In accordance with the Texas Election Code, employees are granted reasonable time off to vote on election days if they do not have two consecutive hours outside of working hours while polls are open.
*   **Military Leave:** Granted in accordance with USERRA and Texas Government Code Chapter 437.

#### 4.8 Code of Conduct and Ethics
*   **Conflicts of Interest:** Employees must adhere to the Conflict of Interest Policy (Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1). Any outside employment or financial interest that competes or conflicts with the Organization must be disclosed annually.
*   **Youth Protection:** As an organization serving K-12 populations, all employees must adhere to the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*. Background checks are mandatory and continuous.
*   **Intellectual Property:** Per Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3, all work created by employees within the scope of their employment is "work made for hire" and is the exclusive property of the Organization.

#### 4.9 Data Privacy and Security
*   **Confidentiality:** Employees must execute a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) upon hire.
*   **Acceptable Use:** Organization-provided technology and accounts must be used for business purposes. Employees have no expectation of privacy regarding data transmitted or stored on Organization systems.

#### 4.10 Separation of Employment
*   **Resignation:** Employees are requested to provide at least two weeks' written notice of resignation.
*   **Return of Property:** Upon separation, employees must return all Organization property (laptops, keys, data) immediately.
*   **Final Pay:** Final wages will be paid in accordance with the Texas Payday Law (Texas Labor Code Chapter 61).

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Violations of this Handbook may result in disciplinary action, ranging from verbal or written warnings to suspension or termination of employment. The Organization reserves the right to determine the appropriate level of discipline based on the severity and frequency of the infraction. Legal action may be pursued for theft, fraud, or breach of confidentiality.
