### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the guidelines for the capitalization, depreciation, and management of tangible assets held by Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy ensures compliance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), Internal Revenue Service (IRS) regulations, and federal Uniform Guidance (2 CFR 200.313) regarding equipment acquired with federal funds.

This policy distinguishes between assets that must be capitalized on the Statement of Financial Position and those that should be expensed in the period purchased.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all tangible personal property, information technology systems, furniture, and equipment acquired by the Organization through purchase, donation, or construction.

**Exclusions:**
*   Intangible assets (governed by the Intellectual Property Policy, Bylaws Article IX).
*   Supplies and materials with a per-unit cost below the capitalization threshold.
*   Routine repairs and maintenance that do not extend the useful life of an asset.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Capital Asset** | Tangible property with a useful life exceeding one year and a per-unit acquisition cost meeting or exceeding the Capitalization Threshold. |
| **Capitalization Threshold** | The dollar amount ($5,000) above which an asset is recorded on the balance sheet rather than expensed. |
| **Acquisition Cost** | The net invoice price of the equipment, including the cost of any modifications, attachments, accessories, or auxiliary apparatus necessary to make it usable for the purpose for which it is acquired. Ancillary charges, such as taxes, duty, protective in-transit insurance, freight, and installation may be included. |
| **Stewardship Asset** | Items with a cost between $500 and $4,999 (e.g., laptops, tablets) that do not meet the capitalization threshold but require tracking for security and insurance purposes. |
| **Supplies** | All tangible personal property other than those described in the definition of Equipment. A computing device is a supply if the acquisition cost is less than the lesser of the capitalization level established by the non-Federal entity for financial statement purposes or $5,000. |
| **Book Value** | The cost of the asset less accumulated depreciation. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Capitalization Threshold
In accordance with 2 CFR 200.313 and GAAP, the Organization shall capitalize all tangible assets that meet **both** of the following criteria:
1.  The asset has a useful life of more than one (1) year; and
2.  The asset has an acquisition cost of **$5,000 or more** per unit.

Assets not meeting these criteria shall be expensed as "Supplies" or "Equipment Expense" in the period acquired.

#### 4.2 Group Assets
Assets that individually fall below the capitalization threshold but are purchased in bulk (e.g., 50 student tablets) may be capitalized as a group asset if the total cost exceeds $5,000 and the assets function as a singular system. This determination is at the discretion of the CFO.

#### 4.3 Contributed Assets
Donated fixed assets shall be recorded at their Fair Market Value (FMV) at the date of the gift. If the FMV exceeds the capitalization threshold, the asset shall be capitalized and depreciated.

#### 4.4 Depreciation Method and Useful Life
Capitalized assets shall be depreciated using the **Straight-Line Method** over the estimated useful life of the asset. No depreciation is taken in the month of acquisition; a full month's depreciation is taken in the month of disposition.

**Standard Useful Lives:**

| Asset Class | Useful Life |
| :--- | :--- |
| Computer Equipment (Servers, Networking) | 3 Years |
| Office Equipment (Copiers, Phone Systems) | 5 Years |
| Furniture and Fixtures | 7 Years |
| Software (Purchased/Major Development) | 3 Years |
| Leasehold Improvements | Life of Lease or 10 Years (shorter of) |

#### 4.5 Federal Interest and Title (2 CFR 200.313)
For equipment acquired with federal grant funds:
1.  **Title:** Title vests in the Organization upon acquisition, subject to the condition that the equipment is used for the authorized purposes of the project until no longer needed.
2.  **Encumbrance:** The Organization must not encumber the property (e.g., use it as collateral for a loan) without approval of the Federal awarding agency.
3.  **Priority Use:** The equipment must be used in the program or project for which it was acquired as long as needed, whether or not the project or program continues to be supported by the Federal award.

#### 4.6 Inventory Management and Property Records
In compliance with 2 CFR 200.313(d)(1), the CFO must maintain a Fixed Asset Register containing the following data elements for all Capital Assets:
1.  Description of the property.
2.  Serial number or other identification number.
3.  Source of funding for the property (including the Federal Award Identification Number (FAIN), if applicable).
4.  Who holds title.
5.  Acquisition date.
6.  Cost of the property.
7.  Percentage of Federal participation in the project costs for the Federal award under which the property was acquired.
8.  Location, use, and condition of the property.
9.  Disposition data including the date of disposal and sale price of the property.

#### 4.7 Physical Inventory
A physical inventory of all Capital Assets and Stewardship Assets must be taken and the results reconciled with the property records at least once every **two (2) years**.
*   Due to the Organization's remote-first nature, this may be conducted via video verification or certified attestation by the remote employee holding the asset.

#### 4.8 Safeguarding and Maintenance
*   **Control System:** The Organization must maintain adequate internal controls to prevent loss, damage, or theft of the property. Any loss, damage, or theft must be investigated.
*   **Maintenance:** The Organization must maintain the property in good condition.
*   **Remote Work:** Employees working remotely are responsible for the physical security of Organization-owned assets in their possession, per the *Remote Work and Telecommuting Policy*.

#### 4.9 Disposition of Assets
When original or replacement equipment acquired under a Federal award is no longer needed for the original project or program:
1.  **Items < $5,000 (Current FMV):** May be retained, sold, or disposed of with no further obligation to the Federal awarding agency.
2.  **Items > $5,000 (Current FMV):** The Organization must request disposition instructions from the Federal awarding agency.
3.  **Non-Federal Assets:** May be sold or disposed of upon approval by the CFO (or Board, if book value exceeds $10,000).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves the Fixed Asset Capitalization Policy; reviews financial reports including depreciation. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Maintains the Fixed Asset Register; calculates depreciation; authorizes asset disposals; coordinates the biennial physical inventory. |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures staff compliance with asset safeguarding; approves purchases within budget authority. |
| **Employees/Volunteers** | Safeguard assets in their custody; report lost/stolen equipment immediately; participate in inventory verification. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in inaccurate financial reporting, audit findings, and the disallowance of costs by federal grantors.

Employees found to have been negligent in the safeguarding of Organization assets, or who fail to return assets upon termination of employment, may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal action for the recovery of the asset's value.
