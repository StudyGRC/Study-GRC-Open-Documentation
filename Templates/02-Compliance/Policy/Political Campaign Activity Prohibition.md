### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") maintains strict compliance with the Internal Revenue Code (IRC) Section 501(c)(3), which imposes an absolute prohibition on political campaign intervention. As a tax-exempt charitable and educational organization, Study GRC Inc. is legally forbidden from participating or intervening in any political campaign on behalf of, or in opposition to, any candidate for public office. Violation of this prohibition threatens the Organization’s tax-exempt status and may result in the imposition of excise taxes.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Individuals:** All Directors, Officers, Operational Leaders, employees, contractors, and volunteers when acting in their official capacity or as representatives of the Organization.
*   **Assets:** The use of any Organization resources, including but not limited to financial funds, facilities, email lists, social media accounts (e.g., LinkedIn, Discord, Slack), software licenses (e.g., Zoom), and intellectual property.
*   **Activities:** All programs, publications, events, and communications conducted by the Organization.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not restrict the rights of individuals to participate in the political process in their *private, personal capacity*, provided they do not use the Organization’s resources or name to do so.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Candidate** | An individual who offers themselves, or is proposed by others, as a contestant for an elective public office (national, state, or local). |
| **Public Office** | Any position in the government (federal, state, or local) that is filled by a vote of the electorate. |
| **Political Campaign Intervention** | Any action that favors or opposes one or more candidates for public office. This includes endorsements, financial contributions, and distributing statements. |
| **Non-Partisan** | Conduct that is neutral, unbiased, and does not favor any specific political party or candidate. |
| **Lobbying vs. Campaigning** | *Lobbying* is attempting to influence legislation (laws), which is permitted within specific limits. *Campaigning* is attempting to influence the election of a person, which is strictly prohibited. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 The Absolute Prohibition
In accordance with Article I, Section 1.3 of the Bylaws and IRC Section 501(c)(3), Study GRC Inc. must not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distributing of statements), any political campaign on behalf of (or in opposition to) any candidate for public office. This is a zero-tolerance policy.

#### 4.2 Prohibited Activities
The following activities are strictly prohibited for the Organization and its representatives acting in an official capacity:
1.  **Endorsements:** Making public statements, oral or written, that support or oppose any candidate, political party, or Political Action Committee (PAC).
2.  **Financial Contributions:** Donating Organization funds or resources to a candidate, party, or PAC.
3.  **In-Kind Contributions:** Providing free or discounted use of Organization facilities, office space, staff time, or technology (e.g., using the Organization’s Zoom account for a fundraiser).
4.  **Rating Candidates:** Publishing or distributing rating sheets that evaluate candidates, even if the rating is based on "educational" criteria (e.g., "GRC-friendliness").
5.  **Biased Voter Guides:** Distributing voter guides or questionnaires that evidence a bias or preference for a candidate or party.
6.  **Fundraising:** Soliciting votes or financial contributions for a candidate.

#### 4.3 Use of Digital Platforms and Assets
As a remote-first organization, digital assets are our primary infrastructure.
1.  **Social Media:** Official Organization accounts (LinkedIn, X/Twitter, etc.) must not follow, "like," "share," or "retweet" campaign materials in a manner that implies endorsement.
2.  **Community Channels:** The Organization’s community platforms (e.g., Discord, Slack, Forums) must remain non-partisan. Moderators are authorized to remove content posted by members that constitutes electioneering or solicitation for political candidates.
3.  **Email Lists:** The Organization’s membership and donor lists are proprietary assets and shall never be sold, rented, or loaned to a candidate, political party, or PAC.

#### 4.4 Personal Political Activity
Directors, Officers, and staff are entitled to participate in the political process as private citizens. However, to protect the Organization:
1.  **Disclaimer:** When expressing personal political views in a public forum where the individual is known to be associated with Study GRC Inc., they must clearly state that their views are personal and do not represent the Organization.
2.  **Separation of Resources:** Individuals must not use the Organization’s letterhead, email address (e.g., name@studygrc.org), social media handles, or working hours for personal political activity.
3.  **Uniformity:** Directors and Officers should refrain from wearing Organization-branded apparel (e.g., logo t-shirts) while attending political rallies or campaign events.

#### 4.5 Permissible Non-Partisan Activities
The Organization may engage in the following, provided they are strictly non-partisan and educational in nature:
1.  **Issue Advocacy:** The Organization may take positions on public policy issues (e.g., data privacy legislation, cybersecurity funding) relevant to its mission, even if candidates also hold views on those issues. This must focus on the *issue*, not the *candidate*.
2.  **Voter Education:** Conducting unbiased training on the electoral process, provided no preference is shown for any candidate or party.
3.  **Candidate Forums:** If the Organization hosts a forum, *all* viable candidates for the specific office must be invited, questions must be prepared by an independent non-partisan panel, and topics must cover a broad range of issues.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ultimate oversight of this policy; ensures no Board actions or resolutions violate the prohibition. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures operational compliance; reviews public communications for potential risks; enforces the policy regarding staff and volunteers. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Monitors community channels (Discord/Slack) to ensure member discussions do not devolve into prohibited campaigning or electioneering. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Responsible for separating their personal political activities from their professional role and Organization resources. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the Organization at existential risk.
1.  **Disciplinary Action:** Violations of this policy by employees or volunteers will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer service.
2.  **Board Removal:** A Director who utilizes the Organization’s resources for political campaigning is subject to removal for cause under Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6.
3.  **Legal Penalties:** Individuals responsible for authorizing prohibited political expenditures may be personally liable for IRS excise taxes under IRC Section 4955.
4.  **Correction:** In the event of an inadvertent violation, the Organization will take immediate steps to correct the action (e.g., retracting a statement, requesting a return of a contribution) and document the correction in the corporate records.
