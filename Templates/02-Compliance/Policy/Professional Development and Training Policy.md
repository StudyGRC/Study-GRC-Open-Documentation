### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the framework for the continuous professional growth of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") staff and volunteers. As an entity dedicated to advancing Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) knowledge, the Organization is committed to ensuring its workforce maintains the highest level of competency. This policy defines the criteria for accessing professional development opportunities, ensures compliance with federal grant regulations regarding training costs (2 CFR § 200.472), and protects the Organization’s financial resources through structured approval and repayment mechanisms.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   All full-time and part-time employees.
*   Volunteers and Board Members (specifically for Mandatory Compliance Training).

**Exclusions:**
*   **Tuition Reimbursement:** Degree-granting programs (Associates, Bachelors, Masters, PhD) are governed separately under the *Tuition Reimbursement Policy (HR-POL-076)*.
*   **Contractors:** Independent contractors are expected to maintain their own professional qualifications at their own expense, unless specific training is required by the Organization to access proprietary systems.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Professional Development (PD)** | Activities designed to enhance professional growth, including seminars, conferences, workshops, certification bootcamps, and self-study courses. |
| **Mandatory Training** | Training required by law, regulation, or organizational policy (e.g., Cybersecurity Awareness, Youth Protection, Harassment Prevention) to ensure compliance and safety. |
| **Allowable Cost** | Costs that meet the criteria of 2 CFR § 200.403, being necessary, reasonable, and allocable to a specific federal award or organizational function. |
| **Certification** | A credential issued by a third-party professional body (e.g., ISACA, ISC2, IIA) verifying specific skills or knowledge (e.g., CISSP, CISA, CIPP). |
| **Clawback Period** | A defined timeframe during which an employee must repay training costs if they voluntarily resign from the Organization. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles
The Organization encourages employees to pursue professional development that aligns with the Organization's strategic mission and the employee's specific job duties. Investment in training is subject to budget availability and operational requirements.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Compliance Training
To maintain the integrity of the Organization and the safety of its community:
*   **Onboarding:** All new hires and volunteers must complete assigned mandatory training (e.g., Data Privacy, Youth Protection) within their first 30 days.
*   **Recertification:** Annual refresher training (e.g., Security Awareness) is mandatory for all active staff and volunteers.
*   **Failure to Complete:** Failure to complete mandatory training by the assigned deadline may result in suspension of system access or disciplinary action.

#### 4.3 Discretionary External Training
Employees may request funding for external training events, conferences, or certifications. Approval is not automatic and is based on the following criteria:
1.  **Relevance:** The training must be directly related to the employee’s current role or a documented succession plan.
2.  **Budget:** Funds must be available in the department’s approved fiscal year budget.
3.  **Performance:** The employee must be in good standing (not on a Performance Improvement Plan).

#### 4.4 Certification Expenses
Given the Organization's focus on GRC, obtaining relevant certifications (e.g., CISA, CRISC, CISSP) is highly encouraged.
*   **Exam Fees:** The Organization may pay for or reimburse the cost of one (1) attempt at a certification exam per fiscal year.
*   **Maintenance Fees:** Annual maintenance fees (AMFs) or dues for professional associations are reimbursable only if the certification is a requirement listed in the employee's Job Description.
*   **Study Materials:** Reasonable costs for study guides or practice tests may be covered, subject to manager approval.

#### 4.5 Federal Grant Funding Compliance
When training costs are charged directly to a Federal award, the Organization strictly adheres to **2 CFR § 200.472**:
*   The training must be for the benefit of the employee's performance on the specific Federal award.
*   The cost must be reasonable and consistent with market rates.
*   Training that is general in nature (e.g., general leadership skills not specific to the grant's objectives) must be charged to indirect costs or general operating funds, not the grant directly.

#### 4.6 Repayment Agreement (Clawback)
To protect the Organization's charitable assets, any single professional development expense (or cumulative event cost including travel) exceeding **$1,000.00 USD** requires the employee to sign a *Training Reimbursement Agreement* prior to the expenditure.
*   **0-12 Months:** If the employee voluntarily resigns within 12 months of the training completion date, they must repay **100%** of the costs.
*   **12-24 Months:** If the employee voluntarily resigns between 12 and 24 months, they must repay **50%** of the costs.
*   **Exemptions:** Repayment is waived if the separation is due to layoff, reduction in force, or termination without cause.

#### 4.7 Travel for Training
Travel expenses associated with professional development (flights, lodging, per diem) must adhere strictly to the *Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy (FIN-POL-037)*.

#### 4.8 Knowledge Sharing
Employees who attend Organization-funded conferences or major training events are required to present a summary of key learnings to their team or the wider organization within 30 days of return. This ensures the investment benefits the collective knowledge base.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Identifies training needs; submits *Training Request Forms* with sufficient lead time; signs *Reimbursement Agreements* if applicable; provides proof of completion/attendance. |
| **Manager** | Reviews requests for relevance and budget availability; ensures adequate coverage during employee absence; verifies knowledge sharing occurs post-training. |
| **Human Resources** | Assigns mandatory training; maintains training records in the personnel file; drafts and retains signed *Reimbursement Agreements*. |
| **Finance Department** | Verifies budget availability; reviews grant allowability (2 CFR 200 compliance); processes payments/reimbursements; enforces clawback provisions during offboarding. |
| **Executive Director** | Provides final approval for training expenses exceeding $2,500 or international travel for training purposes. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Documentation:** All training completed using Organization funds must be documented. Failure to provide proof of attendance or completion may result in the expense being reclassified as personal, requiring the employee to reimburse the Organization.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Failure to comply with this policy, including falsifying training records or failing to repay owed training costs upon separation, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment and legal action to recover funds.
