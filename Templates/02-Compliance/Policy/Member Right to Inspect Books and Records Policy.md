### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to codify the rights of Voting Members to inspect the books and records of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") in accordance with the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and the Organization’s Bylaws. This policy establishes a transparent, secure, and orderly process for inspection while protecting the privacy of members, the confidentiality of sensitive data, and the intellectual property of the Organization.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Voting Members:** Supporting Members and Honorary Members in good standing.
*   **Records:** Books of account, minutes of proceedings, and the membership list.

**Exclusions:**
*   Community Participants (Non-Voting) do not have statutory inspection rights under this policy.
*   This policy does not grant access to legally privileged communications, sensitive personnel records (HIPAA/PII), or proprietary trade secrets protected under the Organization's Intellectual Property Policy.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Proper Purpose** | A purpose reasonably related to the member’s interest as a member of the Organization. Examples include ascertaining the financial condition of the Organization or determining the propriety of corporate management. |
| **Improper Purpose** | Any purpose not related to the member's interest in the Organization, including but not limited to: commercial solicitation, harassment of staff or members, or selling member data to third parties. |
| **Books and Records** | Financial statements, general ledgers, minutes of Board and Member meetings, and the alphabetical list of Voting Members. |
| **Remote Inspection** | The review of documents via secure electronic means (e.g., video conference screenshare, secure data room) in lieu of physical inspection, given the Organization's remote-first structure. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Right to Inspect
In accordance with **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.6** and **TBOC § 22.351**, a Voting Member, on written demand stating a **Proper Purpose**, is entitled to examine and copy, at the member's expense, the Organization's books, records of account, minutes, and membership list.

#### 4.2. Request Procedure
To exercise this right, a member must submit a **Member Record Inspection Request Form** (GOV-FRM-032) to the Secretary of the Board. The request must:
1.  Be submitted at least five (5) business days before the requested inspection date.
2.  State the specific records requested (broad "fishing expeditions" are not permitted).
3.  Clearly articulate the **Proper Purpose** for the inspection.

#### 4.3. Verification of Proper Purpose
The Organization reserves the right to deny a request if:
1.  The member refuses to furnish an affidavit that the inspection is not desired for a purpose that is in the interest of a business or object other than the interest of the Organization.
2.  The member has within the previous two (2) years sold or offered for sale a list of members of any corporation, or aided any person in procuring a list for such purpose.
3.  The request is deemed to be for an **Improper Purpose** (e.g., marketing, solicitation).

#### 4.4. Remote-First Inspection Protocol
As Study GRC Inc. maintains a remote-first operational structure without a central physical archive open to the public:
1.  **Virtual Inspection:** Inspections will primarily be conducted via a secure video conferencing session (e.g., Zoom/Teams) where the Secretary or a designee shares their screen. The member may view the documents but may not record the session.
2.  **Secure Data Room:** For voluminous records, the Organization may, at its discretion, grant temporary, view-only access to a secure digital data room.
3.  **Physical Inspection:** If a physical inspection is legally mandated or mutually agreed upon, it shall occur at the Organization’s Registered Office or another location designated by the Board during regular business hours.

#### 4.5. Protection of Member Data (The Membership List)
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.6**, access to the Membership List is strictly regulated to prevent abuse.
1.  **Redaction:** The Organization may redact non-statutory personal information (e.g., phone numbers, physical addresses) unless strictly required by law, retaining only the name and voting entitlement.
2.  **Prohibition on Commercial Use:** The member must sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) specifically agreeing that the membership list will not be used for advertising, fundraising for other entities, or commercial solicitation.

#### 4.6. Costs and Expenses
In accordance with **TBOC § 22.351**, the requesting member must bear the cost of the inspection.
1.  **Labor:** The Organization may charge a reasonable hourly rate for the time spent by staff or legal counsel to retrieve, redact, and supervise the inspection of records.
2.  **Copies:** If copies are requested, the member will be charged a reasonable fee per page or per digital file to cover administrative costs.
3.  **Pre-Payment:** An estimate of costs will be provided, and payment must be received prior to the release of any copies.

#### 4.7. Limitations on Access
The Board of Directors may withhold access to:
1.  **Personnel Records:** Files containing medical info, performance reviews, or individual salaries (aggregate compensation data is available in the Form 990).
2.  **Attorney-Client Privilege:** Documents reflecting communications with legal counsel.
3.  **Whistleblower Identities:** Records that would reveal the identity of a whistleblower protected under the **Whistleblower Protection Policy**.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Acts as the primary custodian of records; reviews inspection requests for completeness and Proper Purpose; facilitates remote inspections. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves or denies requests based on the validity of the "Proper Purpose"; sets fee schedules for reproduction/labor. |
| **Voting Member** | Submits written demand; signs NDA/Affidavit of Proper Purpose; pays associated costs; adheres to use restrictions. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Advises on redaction requirements and statutory compliance regarding specific requests. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Violation of Use Restrictions:**
Any member who uses information obtained through an inspection for an Improper Purpose (specifically commercial solicitation or harassment) shall be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including **termination of membership** as outlined in **Bylaws Article II** and **Article III**, and may face legal action for damages.

**Denial of Rights:**
If the Organization improperly denies a valid request, the member may seek judicial enforcement as provided by TBOC § 22.351.
