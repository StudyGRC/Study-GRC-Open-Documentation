### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a **Zero Tolerance** standard regarding the participation of known sex offenders within Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). As an organization dedicated to education that serves K-12 youth and vulnerable populations through remote and in-person programs, the safety of our community is paramount. This policy mandates the exclusion of any individual registered as a sex offender to prevent harm, maintain a secure environment, and mitigate organizational liability.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies globally to all individuals associated with the Organization, without exception.
**Applies to:**
*   **Governance:** Board of Directors, Officers, and Advisory Board members.
*   **Workforce:** Employees, Independent Contractors, and Interns.
*   **Volunteers:** All volunteers, including mentors, moderators, and event staff.
*   **Membership:** Voting Members (Supporting/Honorary) and Community Participants.
*   **External:** Vendors requiring access to Organization systems or events, and guest speakers.

**Exclusions:**
*   None. There are no exceptions to this policy.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Registered Sex Offender** | Any individual who is currently required to register as a sex offender under the laws of any jurisdiction, including but not limited to the United States (state, territorial, tribal, or federal), or any international jurisdiction. |
| **Sexual Offense** | Any criminal offense involving sexual misconduct, regardless of whether the offense resulted in a requirement to register as a sex offender, including but not limited to sexual assault, sexual abuse of a minor, possession of child pornography, or sexual harassment. |
| **Covered Position** | Any role within the Organization (paid or unpaid) that involves interaction with youth, access to sensitive data, or decision-making authority. |
| **NSOPW** | The Dru Sjodin National Sex Offender Public Website (www.nsopw.gov), coordinated by the U.S. Department of Justice. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Zero Tolerance Standard
Study GRC Inc. maintains a strict **Zero Tolerance** stance. No individual who is a Registered Sex Offender, or who has been convicted of a Sexual Offense involving a minor (regardless of registration status), is eligible to:
1.  Hold a seat on the Board of Directors or Advisory Board.
2.  Be employed by the Organization in any capacity.
3.  Serve as a volunteer, mentor, or instructor.
4.  Hold Voting Membership status.
5.  Attend Organization-sponsored events (virtual or physical) where minors may be present.
6.  Participate in the Organization’s online communities (e.g., Slack, Discord, Forums).

#### 4.2 Mandatory Screening
To enforce this policy, the Organization shall conduct screenings as defined in the *Background Check Policy (HR-POL-015)*:
*   **Pre-Engagement:** All Directors, Officers, Employees, and Volunteers must undergo a check of the National Sex Offender Registry (via NSOPW or third-party vendor) prior to the commencement of service.
*   **Recurring Screening:** The Organization reserves the right to conduct periodic re-screenings of all active personnel and volunteers annually.
*   **Voting Members:** Supporting Members and Honorary Members are subject to screening upon application or nomination, pursuant to Bylaws Article II.

#### 4.3 Duty to Disclose
All individuals within the Scope of this policy have an affirmative duty to disclose their status:
*   **Applicants:** Must disclose any past convictions of Sexual Offenses or registration requirements during the application process.
*   **Current Personnel/Members:** Must immediately (within 24 hours) notify the Executive Director or Board President if they are charged with or convicted of a Sexual Offense, or if they are required to register as a sex offender during their tenure.

#### 4.4 Immediate Removal and Disqualification
Upon confirmation that an individual is a Registered Sex Offender:
*   **Applicants:** Will be immediately disqualified from consideration.
*   **Directors:** Will be subject to immediate removal for cause pursuant to Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6.
*   **Employees/Volunteers:** Will be immediately terminated.
*   **Members:** Membership will be revoked immediately pursuant to the *Member Expulsion Due Process Policy*.
*   **Community Participants:** Will be permanently banned from all digital platforms and physical events.

#### 4.5 False Positives and Appeals
The Organization recognizes the risk of mistaken identity (e.g., name matches).
*   If an individual is identified as a potential match, they will be suspended temporarily pending verification.
*   The individual will be provided an opportunity to demonstrate that they are **not** the person listed in the registry (e.g., via fingerprints or government ID verification).
*   **Limitation:** Appeals are strictly limited to cases of mistaken identity. The Organization does not entertain appeals based on "rehabilitation," "time served," or "circumstances of the offense."

#### 4.6 Online Community Anonymity
While the Organization supports privacy, safety takes precedence. If the Organization receives a credible report or discovers through other means that an anonymous or pseudonymous user in our online community is a Registered Sex Offender, that user will be banned immediately. The Organization will cooperate with law enforcement if such presence violates the offender's parole or probation terms.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Enforces this policy regarding Board composition; reviews appeals regarding mistaken identity for high-level officers. |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures all operational staff and volunteers are screened; makes final determination on bans for community participants. |
| **HR / Security Team** | Conducts NSOPW searches and background checks; maintains confidential records of screenings. |
| **Volunteers/Staff** | Required to self-report status changes; required to report known violations by others. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy, including failure to disclose status or falsification of application materials, will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board, revocation of membership, and a permanent ban from all Organization activities.

The Organization reserves the right to report individuals to law enforcement if their participation in Organization activities violates the terms of their sex offender registration or probation/parole conditions.
