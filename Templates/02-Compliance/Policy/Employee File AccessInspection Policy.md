### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the guidelines and procedures governing the access, inspection, and review of personnel files by current and former employees of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy balances the Organization’s commitment to transparency and the "Director of Kindness" ethos with the legal requirement to protect the confidentiality of sensitive data and the Organization's proprietary information.

This policy is drafted in compliance with **Texas Labor Code § 52.053**, ensuring that truthful statements regarding discharge are managed appropriately, while defining the Organization's property rights over business records.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Current Employees:** Full-time and part-time staff currently on the payroll.
*   **Former Employees:** Individuals previously employed by the Organization, subject to specific limitations defined herein.
*   **The Organization’s Leadership:** Officers and Directors accessing files for legitimate business purposes.

**Exclusions:**
*   This policy does not apply to Independent Contractors or Volunteers, whose records are governed separately under the *Vendor Management Policy* and *Volunteer Handbook*, respectively.
*   This policy does not grant access to records protected by attorney-client privilege or active investigation files regarding Whistleblower complaints or harassment allegations.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Personnel File** | The official, confidential record of an employee's history with the Organization, maintained by the Human Resources function (or designated Operational Officer). |
| **Shadow File** | Unofficial notes, diaries, or records kept by a manager outside of the official Personnel File. These are discouraged and generally not part of the official inspection process unless legally discoverable. |
| **Designated HR Representative** | The Operational Officer (e.g., CFO or Executive Director) responsible for maintaining employee records. |
| **Supervised Inspection** | The review of records in the presence (physical or virtual via screenshare) of a Designated HR Representative to ensure record integrity. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Ownership of Records
All personnel files and records are the exclusive property of Study GRC Inc. While the Organization grants access privileges as described below, employees do not own the physical or digital records contained within their file.

#### 4.2 Content of the Official Personnel File
The Organization maintains one official personnel file for each employee. This file typically contains:
*   Employment application and resume.
*   Offer letters and employment contracts.
*   Performance evaluations and goal-setting documents.
*   Disciplinary notices, warnings, and Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs).
*   Compensation history and payroll authorization forms.
*   Training certifications and acknowledgments of policies.
*   Resignation letters or termination documentation.

#### 4.3 Excluded Materials (Restricted Access)
To protect the privacy of others and the integrity of business operations, the following items are **excluded** from employee inspection unless required by a valid court order:
*   **References:** Letters of reference or background check data obtained from third parties (subject to FCRA requirements).
*   **Investigation Records:** Notes, witness statements, or reports related to active or closed internal investigations (e.g., harassment, theft, whistleblower complaints) to protect witness confidentiality.
*   **Succession Planning:** Documents relating to future management planning, potential promotability, or organizational restructuring that have not yet been communicated to the employee.
*   **Medical Records:** In compliance with the ADA, medical records are kept in a separate, secure file and are not part of the general personnel file inspection.

#### 4.4 Access by Current Employees
Current employees may request to inspect their official personnel file under the following conditions:
1.  **Frequency:** Inspection is permitted once every six (6) months, or upon a change in job status (e.g., promotion, transfer, disciplinary action).
2.  **Request Protocol:** Requests must be submitted in writing (email is acceptable) to the Designated HR Representative.
3.  **Scheduling:** Inspections will be scheduled at a mutually convenient time, typically within five (5) business days of the request.
4.  **Remote Inspection:** As a remote-first organization, inspections will primarily be conducted via a secure video conferencing session (e.g., Zoom/Teams) where the Designated HR Representative shares their screen. The employee may view the documents but may not screenshot, record, or download files without express permission.
5.  **Corrections:** If an employee disagrees with information in their file, they may submit a written rebuttal or explanation to be attached to the disputed document. The Organization is not obligated to remove or alter the original document unless it is factually erroneous (e.g., wrong name, incorrect date).

#### 4.5 Access by Former Employees & Discharge Statements
1.  **General Access:** Former employees do not have an automatic right to inspect their full personnel file under Texas law, and such access is granted solely at the discretion of the Executive Director.
2.  **Statement of Discharge (Texas Labor Code § 52.053):**
    *   Upon written request by a former employee who was discharged (terminated), the Organization shall provide a written statement of the reason for the discharge.
    *   This statement will be truthful and based on the documentation within the Personnel File.
    *   Per **Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification)** and Texas law, the Organization and its officers are immune from civil liability for truthful disclosures made to the employee or a prospective employer, unless the disclosure is known to be false or made with malice.

#### 4.6 Access by Government Agencies
The Organization will provide access to personnel files to federal, state, or local government agencies (e.g., EEOC, DOL, IRS) when required by law, regulation, or valid subpoena.

#### 4.7 Reproduction and Copying
*   Employees are generally entitled to copies of documents they have previously signed (e.g., offer letters, acknowledgments, evaluations).
*   The Organization reserves the right to charge a reasonable administrative fee for the reproduction of the entire file, where permitted by law.
*   Digital copies of sensitive internal documents (e.g., internal memos regarding the employee) may be withheld to prevent data leakage, provided the employee is allowed to view them.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Submit written requests for access; maintain confidentiality of proprietary information seen during inspection. |
| **Designated HR Representative** | Schedule inspections; redact third-party privacy information prior to inspection; supervise the review process; ensure no records are altered or destroyed during inspection. |
| **Executive Director** | Approve or deny access requests from former employees (excepting statutory discharge statements); authorize the removal of any documents from the file. |
| **Managers** | Forward any requests for file access immediately to the Designated HR Representative; refrain from maintaining secret "shadow files" that contradict official records. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action.
*   **Unauthorized Access:** Any employee found accessing another employee's personnel file without authorization will be subject to immediate termination.
*   **Alteration of Records:** Any attempt to alter, remove, or destroy records during an inspection is grounds for immediate termination and potential legal action.
*   **Management Compliance:** Managers found withholding performance documentation from the official file may face disciplinary action.
