### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish standards for the reimbursement of reasonable and necessary expenses incurred by authorized individuals while conducting business on behalf of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy ensures compliance with **IRS "Accountable Plan" rules** (to prevent reimbursements from being treated as taxable income) and **2 CFR § 200.474** (Travel Costs) regarding the use of federal grant funds. It is designed to ensure fiscal responsibility, transparency, and the prudent use of charitable assets.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and authorized contractors of the Organization. It covers all travel, entertainment, and business-related expenses incurred while performing official duties.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not cover expenses related to independent contractors unless specifically stipulated in their Master Service Agreement (MSA) or Statement of Work (SOW).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Accountable Plan** | An IRS-defined arrangement for reimbursing employees for business expenses. Under this plan, reimbursements are not reported as taxable income if they meet specific requirements (business connection, substantiation, and return of excess). |
| **GSA Rates** | Per diem rates established by the U.S. General Services Administration for lodging, meals, and incidental expenses, used as the ceiling for allowable costs. |
| **Reasonable Cost** | A cost that, in its nature and amount, does not exceed that which would be incurred by a prudent person under the circumstances prevailing at the time the decision was made to incur the cost (2 CFR § 200.404). |
| **Substantiation** | Documentary evidence (receipts, logs) sufficient to support the amount, time, place, and business purpose of an expense. |
| **Fly America Act** | A federal regulation (49 U.S.C. 40118) requiring the use of U.S. flag air carriers for federally funded international travel. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles
The Organization assumes no obligation to reimburse expenses that are not in compliance with this policy.
*   **Prudence:** Travelers must exercise the same care in incurring expenses that a prudent person would exercise if traveling on personal business.
*   **Budgetary Alignment:** All travel must be approved in advance and align with the approved annual budget or specific grant budget.
*   **Prohibition on Alcohol:** The Organization **strictly prohibits** the use of federal grant funds for the purchase of alcoholic beverages (2 CFR § 200.423). Alcohol purchased with non-federal funds must be segregated and requires explicit prior Board approval.

#### 4.2 IRS Accountable Plan Requirements
To ensure reimbursements remain non-taxable, all individuals must strictly adhere to the following three rules:
1.  **Business Connection:** Expenses must have a direct business connection and be incurred while performing services for the Organization.
2.  **Substantiation:** Expenses must be substantiated to the Finance Department within **60 days** of the expense being incurred. Substantiation includes original receipts and a documented business purpose.
3.  **Return of Excess:** If an advance was provided, any amount exceeding the actual substantiated expenses must be returned to the Organization within **120 days**.

#### 4.3 Travel Authorization
*   **Pre-Approval:** All overnight travel or single expenses anticipated to exceed $500 must be pre-approved via the *Travel Authorization Request Form*.
*   **Grant-Funded Travel:** Travel charged to a federal award must be necessary to the federal award and consistent with the Organization’s policies.

#### 4.4 Transportation
*   **Air Travel:**
    *   Travelers must book the lowest available economy/coach class fare.
    *   Business or First Class is **prohibited** unless necessary for a documented medical disability (requires ADA accommodation on file).
    *   For federally funded travel, compliance with the **Fly America Act** is mandatory.
*   **Ground Transportation:**
    *   **Personal Vehicle:** Reimbursed at the current IRS Standard Mileage Rate. This rate covers gas, wear and tear, and insurance. Gas receipts are not reimbursed for personal vehicles.
    *   **Rental Cars:** Allowed when more economical than ride-sharing/taxis. Travelers must rent Compact or Mid-size vehicles.
    *   **Ride-Share/Taxi:** Reasonable fares for Uber, Lyft, or taxis are reimbursable. Luxury options (e.g., Uber Black) are prohibited.

#### 4.5 Lodging
*   **Cost Limits:** Lodging costs must not exceed the GSA Per Diem rate for the destination city, excluding taxes.
*   **Exceptions:**
    *   Staying at a "conference hotel" where the event is held is permissible even if it exceeds the GSA rate, provided it is the most logical option for participation.
    *   Safety concerns warranting a higher rate must be documented.
*   **Room Charges:** Personal charges (movies, mini-bar, spa services) are non-reimbursable.

#### 4.6 Meals and Incidental Expenses (M&IE)
*   **Per Diem Method:** The Organization utilizes the GSA M&IE Per Diem rates for the destination city. Travelers claiming Per Diem do not need to submit receipts for individual meals but must submit proof of travel dates/location.
*   **Proration:** Per Diem is prorated at 75% on the first and last day of travel.
*   **Provided Meals:** If a meal is provided by a conference or host, the Traveler must deduct the corresponding meal amount from their daily Per Diem claim.

#### 4.7 Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The following are strictly non-reimbursable:
*   Spouse, partner, or family member travel costs.
*   Traffic citations, parking tickets, or toll violations.
*   Travel insurance (unless for international travel where required).
*   Personal entertainment (gym fees, souvenirs, golf).
*   Lost or stolen personal property.
*   Late fees on personal credit cards.

#### 4.8 Documentation and Receipts
*   **Receipt Threshold:** Receipts are required for all lodging, airfare, and rental cars regardless of cost, and for any other single expense over **$25.00**.
*   **Missing Receipts:** If a receipt is lost, the Traveler must complete a *Missing Receipt Affidavit*. Frequent use of affidavits may result in revocation of travel privileges.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Traveler** | Exercises prudence; submits *Travel Authorization Request* prior to travel; submits *Expense Report* with receipts within 60 days of return. |
| **Approving Manager** | Reviews expenses for business relevance, budget availability, and compliance with this policy before approving. Cannot approve their own expenses. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Final reviewer of all expense reports; ensures correct General Ledger (GL) coding and grant allocation; denies non-compliant claims. |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews and approves expense reports submitted by the Executive Director (CEO) or CFO to ensure segregation of duties. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Tax Consequences:** Failure to submit substantiation within 60 days will result in the reimbursement being treated as taxable income, subject to withholding and reported on the employee’s Form W-2.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Submission of fraudulent receipts, inflating expenses, or using grant funds for prohibited items (e.g., alcohol) constitutes gross misconduct. Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer status, and potential legal action for fraud.
*   **Grant Disallowance:** Any cost disallowed by a federal auditor due to policy violation must be reimbursed to the Organization by the individual who incurred the cost, if deemed negligent.
