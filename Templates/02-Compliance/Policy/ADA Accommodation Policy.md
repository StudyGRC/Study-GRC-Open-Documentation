### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish Study GRC Inc.’s commitment to full compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990, the ADA Amendments Act of 2008 (ADAAA), and Chapter 21 of the Texas Labor Code. This policy defines the framework for providing **Reasonable Accommodations** to qualified individuals with disabilities, ensuring equal access to employment opportunities and participation in the Organization’s educational programs and community ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Employment:** All current employees, job applicants, and independent contractors (where applicable by law).
*   **Volunteers:** All Board Directors, Committee Members, and operational volunteers.
*   **Program Participants:** Learners, community members, and attendees of Study GRC Inc. events and digital platforms.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not require the Organization to lower quality or production standards, nor to provide personal use items (e.g., eyeglasses, hearing aids) that are needed both on and off the job.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Disability** | A physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities; a record of such an impairment; or being regarded as having such an impairment. |
| **Qualified Individual** | An individual who, with or without reasonable accommodation, can perform the **Essential Functions** of the employment position or meet the essential eligibility requirements for program participation. |
| **Reasonable Accommodation** | A modification or adjustment to a job, the work environment, or the way things are usually done that enables a qualified individual with a disability to enjoy an equal employment opportunity or participate in a program. |
| **Essential Functions** | The fundamental job duties of the employment position. A function is essential if the job exists to perform that function, or if the function is highly specialized. |
| **Undue Hardship** | An action requiring significant difficulty or expense when considered in light of factors such as the Organization's size, financial resources, and the nature and structure of its operation. |
| **Interactive Process** | The collaborative dialogue between the Organization and the individual requesting accommodation to identify the precise limitations resulting from the disability and potential reasonable accommodations that could overcome those limitations. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Commitment
Study GRC Inc. prohibits discrimination against qualified individuals on the basis of disability in all aspects of employment and program delivery. The Organization must provide Reasonable Accommodations to known physical or mental limitations of an otherwise qualified individual with a disability, unless doing so would impose an **Undue Hardship** on the operation of the business.

#### 4.2 The Interactive Process
The Organization utilizes a mandatory "Interactive Process" to determine appropriate accommodations. This process must be flexible, interactive, and individualized.

1.  **Initiation:** The process begins when an individual requests an accommodation or when a manager/leader becomes aware of a potential need. A request does not need to use the term "ADA" or be in writing initially, though the *Reasonable Accommodation Request Form* (HR-FRM-017) must be completed for documentation.
2.  **Dialogue:** The Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or designated HR representative will discuss the specific limitations and potential solutions with the individual.
3.  **Determination:** The Organization has the ultimate discretion to choose between effective accommodations. If the requested accommodation is not feasible, the Organization will attempt to identify an alternative effective accommodation.

#### 4.3 Employment Accommodations (Remote-First Context)
As a remote-first organization, Study GRC Inc. recognizes that accommodations often involve digital accessibility and flexible workflows rather than physical facility modifications. Examples of potential accommodations include, but are not limited to:
*   Providing screen reader software or speech-to-text tools.
*   Modifying work schedules to accommodate medical appointments or therapy.
*   Providing materials in alternative formats (e.g., large print, accessible PDF).
*   Allowing unpaid leave for medical treatment (beyond FMLA requirements).
*   Reassignment to a vacant position for which the employee is qualified.

#### 4.4 Program and Event Accommodations
To fulfill the Organization's mission of removing barriers to GRC knowledge, all public-facing programs and events must be accessible.
*   **Digital Events:** Live webinars must offer captioning or transcripts.
*   **Web Content:** All educational resources must adhere to the *Digital Accessibility Policy* (IS-POL-090) and WCAG 2.2 AA standards.
*   **Physical Events:** If physical gatherings occur, venues must be vetted for wheelchair accessibility and other physical access requirements.

#### 4.5 Medical Documentation and Confidentiality
*   **Right to Request:** If a disability or the need for accommodation is not obvious, the Organization may request limited medical documentation from a health care provider to substantiate the disability and functional limitations.
*   **Confidentiality:** All medical information obtained in connection with the reasonable accommodation process must be kept **confidential** and maintained in files separate from the individual’s personnel file, in accordance with the *Employee Personnel File Management Policy* (HR-POL-042).

#### 4.6 Undue Hardship Determination
An accommodation may be denied if it poses an Undue Hardship. This determination is made by the Executive Director (CEO) and must consider:
*   The nature and net cost of the accommodation.
*   The overall financial resources of the Organization.
*   The impact of the accommodation on the operation of the facility or program, including the impact on the ability of other employees to perform their duties.

#### 4.7 Retaliation Prohibited
Retaliation against an individual for requesting an accommodation, or for participating in an investigation regarding a disability discrimination complaint, is strictly prohibited and constitutes a violation of this policy and the *Whistleblower Protection Policy* (GOV-POL-009).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Serves as the primary ADA Coordinator; manages the Interactive Process; maintains confidential medical files; approves or denies requests in consultation with Legal/Executive leadership. |
| **Managers / Supervisors** | Identify potential accommodation needs; refer requests immediately to the DKG; participate in the Interactive Process regarding operational feasibility; implement approved accommodations. **Managers are prohibited from denying an accommodation request without DKG review.** |
| **Employees / Volunteers** | Initiate the request for accommodation; provide necessary medical documentation upon request; participate in the Interactive Process in good faith. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final authority on determinations of "Undue Hardship" or significant financial expenditures related to accommodations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy, including failure to engage in the Interactive Process or retaliation against a requester, may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment. Legal violations may also result in civil liability for the Organization and individual supervisors under the ADA and Texas state law.
