### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to mandate the acquisition and maintenance of Cyber Liability Insurance to transfer the financial risks associated with data breaches, cyberattacks, and information security incidents. As a remote-first organization handling sensitive data (including potential youth data), Study GRC Inc. faces significant exposure to ransomware, social engineering, and regulatory fines. This policy ensures the Organization has the financial resources to fund legal defense, forensic investigations, and regulatory notifications in compliance with Texas Business & Commerce Code § 521.053 and other applicable laws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the Board of Directors, Operational Officers (specifically the CEO, CFO, and CISO), and any personnel involved in the procurement of insurance or the management of legal and financial risks.

**Inclusions:**
*   Selection of coverage limits and deductibles.
*   Accuracy of insurance applications and attestations.
*   Incident reporting procedures related to insurance claims.

**Exclusions:**
*   General Liability or Directors & Officers (D&O) insurance (covered under separate policies), except where coverage overlaps.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **First-Party Coverage** | Insurance covering the Organization's own direct costs resulting from a cyber incident (e.g., forensic investigation, data recovery, ransom payments, public relations, notification costs). |
| **Third-Party Coverage** | Insurance covering liability for damages claimed by third parties (e.g., members, donors, employees) resulting from the Organization's failure to protect data. |
| **Retroactive Date** | The date from which the policy covers incidents. Incidents occurring before this date are not covered. |
| **Attestation** | The formal declaration made to the insurance carrier regarding the Organization's security controls (e.g., "We use MFA on all accounts"). |
| **Notice of Circumstance** | A formal notification to the insurer that an event has occurred which *may* give rise to a claim, preserving the right to coverage. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Mandatory Coverage
Pursuant to the fiduciary duties outlined in **Bylaws Article III**, the Organization must maintain active Cyber Liability Insurance at all times. The policy must cover both First-Party and Third-Party liabilities.

#### 4.2 Minimum Coverage Requirements
The insurance policy procured must, at a minimum, provide coverage for:
1.  **Incident Response Costs:** Legal counsel (Breach Coach), digital forensics, and crisis communications.
2.  **Data Breach Notification:** Costs associated with notifying impacted individuals and regulators as required by Texas law and GDPR.
3.  **Cyber Extortion:** Ransomware negotiation and payment (subject to legality verification).
4.  **Social Engineering/Funds Transfer Fraud:** Theft of funds via phishing or business email compromise.
5.  **Regulatory Defense & Fines:** Legal defense against regulatory investigations (e.g., OCR, FTC) and coverage for fines where insurable by law.

#### 4.3 Coverage Limits and Deductibles
4.3.1 **Risk-Based Limits:** Coverage limits must be determined annually based on a quantitative risk assessment conducted by the CISO and CFO. This assessment must consider the volume of PII/sensitive data held and potential downtime costs.
4.3.2 **Deductible/Retention:** The Organization must maintain a cash reserve in the operating budget equal to at least 100% of the policy's self-insured retention (deductible) amount.

#### 4.4 Application Accuracy and Attestation
4.4.1 **Verification of Controls:** Misrepresentation of security controls on an insurance application can result in claim denial (Rescission). Therefore, the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) must review and validate all technical answers on the insurance application prior to submission.
4.4.2 **Material Changes:** If the Organization makes a material change to its IT environment that reduces security posture (e.g., disabling MFA), the CISO must notify the CFO immediately to determine if the insurer must be notified.

#### 4.5 Claims Reporting and Notification
4.5.1 **Immediate Notification:** In the event of a confirmed or suspected cyber incident, the Incident Response Team must notify the CFO and Legal Counsel immediately.
4.5.2 **Timely Filing:** The CFO is responsible for filing a "Notice of Circumstance" or formal claim with the carrier within the time window specified in the insurance contract (typically 72 hours or "as soon as practicable").
4.5.3 **Vendor Panel Usage:** Unless a specific endorsement allows "Choice of Counsel," the Organization must utilize the insurance carrier’s pre-approved panel of legal and forensic vendors to ensure coverage is not voided.

#### 4.6 Retroactive Date Continuity
The Organization must maintain the original Retroactive Date in all policy renewals. If the carrier is changed, "Prior Acts" coverage must be purchased to ensure no gap in coverage for latent breaches that occurred but were not yet discovered.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the annual insurance budget and reviews the adequacy of coverage limits relative to organizational risk. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns the procurement process; manages the broker relationship; ensures premiums are paid; files claims. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Validates technical accuracy of insurance applications; participates in risk assessments to set limits; identifies reportable incidents. |
| **General Counsel / Legal** | Reviews policy terms and exclusions; coordinates with the insurance-assigned Breach Coach during an incident. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain continuous coverage or failure to report a known incident to the carrier in a timely manner puts the Organization’s assets at catastrophic risk.
*   **Operational Officers** found to have knowingly provided false information on an insurance application may be subject to termination and legal action for breach of fiduciary duty.
*   **Employees** who fail to report a security incident, thereby jeopardizing insurance coverage, are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.
