### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the ownership, protection, and management of Intellectual Property (IP) created within Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). In accordance with **Article IX, Section 9.3** of the Bylaws, this policy ensures that the Organization holds clear legal title to all resources created by its community to fulfill its charitable mission: providing zero-cost, open-source GRC knowledge to the public. This policy balances the Organization's need for legal ownership with its irrevocable commitment to open-source licensing.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all "Contributors," defined as:
*   Members of the Board of Directors and Advisory Board;
*   Officers and Employees;
*   Volunteers and Committee Members;
*   Independent Contractors and Consultants (unless a separate written agreement states otherwise).

This policy covers all "Work Product" created, developed, or contributed by Contributors in the course of their service to the Organization, or through the use of the Organization’s resources, funds, or confidential information.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to IP created by Contributors entirely on their own time, without the use of the Organization’s equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secret information, and which does not relate to the Organization’s business or actual/demonstrably anticipated research and development.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Work Product** | Any work of authorship, invention, discovery, improvement, design, software code, curriculum, graphic, process, or method created by a Contributor for the Organization. |
| **Background IP** | Intellectual property owned by a Contributor prior to their engagement with the Organization, or created independently of their service to the Organization. |
| **Work Made for Hire** | A legal doctrine (17 U.S.C. § 101) where the employer/organization is considered the legal author of a work created by an employee within the scope of their employment. |
| **Open Source** | A licensing model that allows software or content to be freely used, modified, and shared. (e.g., Creative Commons, MIT License). |
| **Moral Rights** | Personal rights of creators to claim authorship and object to modifications of their work, distinct from economic rights. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Organization Ownership and Assignment
To ensure the Organization can legally license materials to the public, the Organization must hold the copyright.
*   **Employees:** All Work Product created by employees within the scope of their employment is considered a "Work Made for Hire," and ownership vests immediately in the Organization.
*   **Volunteers and Contractors:** By serving the Organization and accepting this policy (via the Volunteer Agreement or Contract), Contributors hereby irrevocably assign, transfer, and convey to the Organization all right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including all copyrights, patents, and trade secrets.
*   **Further Assurances:** Contributors agree to execute any necessary documents (e.g., specific assignment deeds) requested by the Organization to perfect these rights.

#### 4.2 The Open Source Commitment (The "Public Trust" Clause)
Pursuant to **Bylaws Section 9.3**, the Organization holds these rights solely to protect the mission of democratizing GRC knowledge.
*   **Licensing Mandate:** The Organization shall license its educational content (curriculum, guides, documentation) under a permissive license, such as **Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike (CC BY-SA)**.
*   **Code Licensing:** The Organization shall license its software and code under a permissive Open Source Initiative (OSI) approved license, such as the **MIT License** or **Apache 2.0**.
*   **Prohibition on Exclusivity:** The Organization is strictly prohibited from granting exclusive commercial rights to any third party that would restrict the public’s free access to the Work Product. The Organization shall never "lock down" community-created resources behind a paywall for private profit.

#### 4.3 Background Intellectual Property
Contributors retain ownership of their Background IP. However, if a Contributor incorporates their Background IP into Organization Work Product (e.g., a volunteer uses a diagram they drew years ago in a new presentation):
*   The Contributor grants the Organization a non-exclusive, perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide license to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and sublicense that Background IP as part of the Work Product.
*   Contributors must disclose any Background IP included in deliverables to the Board or their direct supervisor prior to inclusion.

#### 4.4 Third-Party Intellectual Property
Contributors must respect the IP rights of others.
*   **Prohibition on Infringement:** Contributors must not incorporate third-party copyrighted materials (images, text, code) into the Organization’s resources without explicit written permission or a valid open-source license compatible with the Organization’s licensing strategy.
*   **Verification:** Contributors are responsible for verifying the licensing status of any assets (e.g., stock photos, code libraries) before use.

#### 4.5 Trademarks and Branding
While educational content is open source, the Organization’s identity is protected to prevent fraud and impersonation.
*   **Ownership:** The names "Study GRC," the Organization’s logos, and associated service marks are the sole property of the Organization.
*   **Usage:** These trademarks may not be used by third parties (including Members) for commercial purposes or to imply endorsement without express written permission from the Board.
*   **Distinction:** The Open Source licenses applied to content (Section 4.2) do *not* grant rights to use the Organization’s trademarks.

#### 4.6 Waiver of Moral Rights
To facilitate the collaborative, evolving nature of open-source projects, Contributors waive any "moral rights" or rights of "droit moral" (such as the right to be named as author or the right to object to modifications) in the Work Product to the fullest extent permitted by law. *Note: The Organization will make reasonable efforts to attribute contributors in documentation, but this is a courtesy, not a legal obligation preventing the modification of the work.*

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Guardians of the Open Source Commitment; approve licensing strategies; authorize trademark usage. |
| **Chief Legal Officer** | Maintains IP assignment records; manages trademark filings; handles DMCA notices and infringement claims. |
| **VP of Education** | Ensures all curriculum contributors have signed Volunteer Agreements/IP Assignments; verifies third-party content licensing. |
| **Contributors** | Disclose Background IP; avoid infringement; execute assignment agreements; create content aligned with the mission. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Internal:** Failure to comply with this policy (e.g., incorporating stolen IP, refusing to assign rights) may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board, or revocation of Membership.
*   **External:** The Organization reserves the right to take legal action to protect its Trademarks or to enforce the terms of its Open Source licenses against third parties who violate them (e.g., a company taking the content and attempting to copyright it exclusively).
