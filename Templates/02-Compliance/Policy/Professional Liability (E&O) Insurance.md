### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to mandate and govern the maintenance of Professional Liability Insurance, also known as Errors and Omissions (E&O) insurance, for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization").

As an educational non-profit dedicated to advancing Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) knowledge through open-source resources and training, the Organization faces specific "Services Risks." These risks include allegations of negligence, misrepresentation, copyright infringement, or failure to deliver services as promised. This policy ensures the Organization transfers these financial risks to an insurance carrier, thereby protecting the Organization’s assets and fulfilling the indemnification obligations outlined in **Article VII** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the Organization’s risk management strategy regarding all "Professional Services" provided by the entity.

*   **Applies to:** The Organization as a legal entity, its Directors, Officers, Employees, Contractors (where specified), and Volunteers acting within the scope of their duties.
*   **Exclusions:** This policy does not cover General Liability (bodily injury/property damage), Directors & Officers (D&O) management liability (covered under a separate policy), or criminal/fraudulent acts established by final adjudication.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Professional Liability (E&O)** | Insurance coverage that protects against claims of inadequate work or negligent actions made by a client or third party. |
| **Professional Services** | Services performed by the Organization for a fee or gratis, including but not limited to: educational instruction, curriculum development, publication of technical standards, certification administration, and dissemination of GRC advice. |
| **Wrongful Act** | Any actual or alleged breach of duty, neglect, error, omission, misstatement, or misleading statement in the performance of Professional Services. |
| **Defense Costs** | Reasonable and necessary legal fees and expenses incurred in the investigation and defense of a Claim. |
| **Retroactive Date** | The date from which the Organization has held uninterrupted professional liability coverage. Claims arising from acts prior to this date are not covered. |
| **Tail Coverage** | An Extended Reporting Period (ERP) endorsement that allows the Organization to report claims made after a policy has expired for wrongful acts that occurred while the policy was active. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Mandatory Coverage Requirement
The Organization must maintain a Professional Liability (E&O) insurance policy in good standing at all times. This coverage is essential to mitigate the financial impact of lawsuits alleging that the Organization’s educational materials, advice, or services caused financial harm to a third party.

#### 4.2 Minimum Coverage Limits
Unless otherwise authorized by a resolution of the Board of Directors based on a specific risk assessment, the Organization shall maintain Professional Liability coverage with the following minimum limits:
*   **Per Claim Limit:** Minimum of $1,000,000.
*   **Aggregate Limit:** Minimum of $2,000,000.
*   **Deductible/Retention:** Must not exceed $25,000 per claim without explicit Finance Committee approval.

#### 4.3 Scope of Insured Activities
The policy procured must explicitly cover the specific nature of Study GRC Inc.'s operations, including:
1.  **Educational Activities:** Instruction, workshops, and webinars (remote and in-person).
2.  **Publishing:** Creation and distribution of open-source content, white papers, and technical frameworks.
3.  **Intellectual Property Liability:** Coverage for unintentional copyright infringement, plagiarism, or piracy (crucial for open-source resource providers).
4.  **Media Liability:** Defamation, libel, and slander arising from community moderation or publications.
5.  **Technology Services:** Failure of technology platforms used to deliver educational content (Tech E&O).

#### 4.4 Integration with Indemnification
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification)**, the Organization has pledged to indemnify its agents. This insurance policy shall serve as the primary funding mechanism for such indemnification regarding Professional Services claims. Corporate assets shall only be used for indemnification and defense costs after insurance limits have been exhausted or if coverage is denied.

#### 4.5 Claims Reporting and Notification
Insurance policies are "Claims-Made" policies, meaning coverage is triggered only if the claim is reported during the policy period.
1.  **Immediate Notification:** Any Director, Officer, Employee, or Volunteer who receives a demand letter, threat of litigation, or notice of a legal claim must notify the Chief Legal Officer (CLO) and CFO immediately (within 24 hours).
2.  **Potential Claims:** Staff must report "circumstances that may reasonably give rise to a claim" (e.g., a student threatening to sue over a failed certification exam) to the CLO for evaluation on whether to file a "Notice of Circumstance" with the carrier.
3.  **No Admission of Liability:** No individual is authorized to admit liability, settle a claim, or incur defense costs without the written consent of the insurance carrier and the Board of Directors.

#### 4.6 Continuity and Tail Coverage
To prevent gaps in coverage:
1.  **Renewal:** The CFO must initiate the renewal process at least 60 days prior to the policy expiration date.
2.  **Retroactive Date:** The Organization must maintain the original Retroactive Date in all subsequent policy renewals to ensure coverage for past acts.
3.  **Tail Coverage:** In the event of a merger, acquisition, or dissolution (Fundamental Action), the Organization must purchase "Tail Coverage" (Extended Reporting Period) for a minimum of three (3) years, or the maximum statutory period for limitations on tort actions in Texas.

#### 4.7 Review of Exclusions
The CFO and CLO must annually review policy exclusions to ensure that core mission activities (e.g., providing advice on cybersecurity standards) are not excluded from coverage.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the annual insurance budget and authorizes coverage limits. Reviews significant claims. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Acts as the primary liaison with insurance brokers. Ensures premiums are paid on time. Maintains custody of policy documents. |
| **Chief Legal Officer (CLO)** | Reviews policy terms for adequacy. Manages the legal defense of claims in coordination with the insurance carrier's appointed counsel. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all curriculum and educational activities fall within the scope of "Professional Services" defined in the policy. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Responsible for adhering to professional standards and immediately reporting any potential errors, omissions, or threats of litigation. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain this insurance exposes the Organization and its individual leaders to unlimited personal and corporate liability.
*   **Lapse in Coverage:** Any lapse in coverage due to non-payment or administrative error will result in an immediate internal audit and potential disciplinary action against the responsible officer.
*   **Failure to Report:** Failure by a staff member or volunteer to report a known claim or threat, resulting in a denial of coverage by the carrier, may result in termination of employment or volunteer service and forfeiture of indemnification rights.
