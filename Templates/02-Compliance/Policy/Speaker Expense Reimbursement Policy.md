### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish clear guidelines for the reimbursement of reasonable and necessary expenses incurred by guest speakers, panelists, and instructors ("Speakers") while supporting the mission of Study GRC Inc. This policy ensures fiscal responsibility, maintains strict budget controls, and ensures compliance with IRS regulations regarding 501(c)(3) status and the prohibition of private inurement.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all non-employee Speakers invited to present at Study GRC Inc. events, workshops, webinars, or conferences.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Employees:** Reimbursements for Study GRC Inc. employees are governed by the *Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy (HR-POL-040)*.
*   **Contractors:** Independent contractors whose signed agreements explicitly include a flat fee covering all travel and incidental expenses.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reimbursable Expense** | A cost that is reasonable, necessary, and directly related to the Speaker's participation in a Study GRC Inc. event, substantiated by documentation. |
| **Substantiation** | Documentary evidence (receipts, logs) proving the amount, date, place, and business character of an expense. |
| **GSA Rate** | The per diem rates established by the U.S. General Services Administration, used as a ceiling for lodging and meal reimbursements. |
| **Private Inurement** | The transfer of a non-profit's income or assets to a private individual (insider) for less than fair market value. |
| **Prior Approval** | Written authorization (email or contract) from an authorized Budget Holder granted before an expense is incurred. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles
In accordance with **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.4 (Nonprofit Status)**, no part of the net earnings of the Organization shall inure to the benefit of private persons. Therefore, reimbursements must strictly reflect actual costs incurred and must not serve as disguised compensation.
*   Reimbursement is a privilege, not a right, and is contingent upon prior written agreement.
*   Study GRC Inc. assumes no liability for expenses that were not pre-approved or that violate this policy.

#### 4.2 Budget Control and Prior Authorization
*   **4.2.1 Budget Check:** Before extending an offer to reimburse a Speaker, the Event Manager must verify that sufficient funds exist within the approved event budget.
*   **4.2.2 Written Agreement:** The specific categories and maximum limits (caps) of reimbursable expenses must be detailed in the *Speaker Agreement* signed by both parties prior to the event.
*   **4.2.3 Cap Enforcement:** Expenses exceeding the agreed-upon cap will be the sole financial responsibility of the Speaker.

#### 4.3 Allowable Expenses
Reimbursements are limited to the following categories, provided they are reasonable and necessary:

*   **4.3.1 Air Travel:**
    *   Reimbursement is limited to **Economy/Coach class** fares.
    *   Bookings should be made at least 14 days in advance to secure favorable rates.
    *   Upgrades, early boarding fees, or change fees (unless caused by Study GRC Inc.) are not reimbursable.
*   **4.3.2 Lodging:**
    *   Reimbursement is limited to the cost of a standard room plus tax.
    *   Costs must not exceed the federal **GSA Per Diem Rate** for the event location unless a specific exception is granted by the CFO due to hotel block requirements.
    *   Incidental room charges (movies, mini-bar, spa services) are not reimbursable.
*   **4.3.3 Ground Transportation:**
    *   Reasonable costs for taxi, rideshare (Uber/Lyft standard), or shuttle service between the airport, hotel, and venue are reimbursable.
    *   Personal vehicle mileage is reimbursed at the current **IRS Standard Mileage Rate**.
*   **4.3.4 Meals:**
    *   Meals are reimbursed based on actual receipts up to a daily maximum defined by the GSA M&IE rate for the location.
    *   **Alcohol Prohibition:** As a federal grant recipient, Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits the reimbursement of alcoholic beverages. Receipts containing alcohol must have the alcohol costs clearly deducted.

#### 4.4 Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The following are strictly non-reimbursable:
*   Expenses for spouses, partners, or guests.
*   First-class or business-class travel upgrades.
*   Alcoholic beverages.
*   Personal entertainment (tours, golf, movies).
*   Fines (parking tickets, traffic violations).
*   Lost or stolen personal property.
*   Travel insurance.

#### 4.5 Substantiation and Submission
*   **4.5.1 Deadline:** Expense reports must be submitted within **30 days** of the event conclusion. Claims submitted after 60 days will be denied and treated as taxable income if paid, per IRS regulations.
*   **4.5.2 Receipts:** Itemized receipts are required for all individual expenses exceeding **$25.00**. Credit card statements alone are not sufficient substantiation.
*   **4.5.3 Form:** Speakers must use the official *Speaker Expense Claim Form (FIN-FRM-015)*.

#### 4.6 Honoraria vs. Reimbursement
An honorarium is a token payment for services, distinct from expense reimbursement.
*   Honoraria are taxable income and will be reported on IRS Form 1099-NEC if the cumulative annual total paid to the Speaker exceeds $600.
*   Expense reimbursements substantiated by receipts are generally not taxable.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Speaker** | Incurs expenses in accordance with this policy; submits accurate *Speaker Expense Claim Form* with valid receipts within 30 days. |
| **Event Manager** | Negotiates reimbursement caps prior to engagement; verifies that claims match the signed *Speaker Agreement* and approved budget. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews claims for compliance with IRS rules and GSA rates; authorizes final payment; maintains audit trail. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves exceptions to policy limits (e.g., lodging above GSA rate) in rare, justified circumstances. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in the denial of reimbursement claims.
*   **Fraud:** Submission of fraudulent receipts or duplicate claims (double-dipping) will result in immediate termination of the relationship with the Speaker and potential legal action to recover funds.
*   **IRS Compliance:** Failure to substantiate expenses within 60 days may require Study GRC Inc. to report payments as taxable income to the IRS.
