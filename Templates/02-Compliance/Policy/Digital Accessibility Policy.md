### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") provides digital resources, websites, and educational materials that are accessible to all individuals, regardless of disability or impairment. This policy aligns with the Organization’s Bylaws Article 1.3, which mandates the "removal of financial barriers" and the "empowerment of learners," by extending that mandate to include the removal of technological and sensory barriers.

This policy establishes the standards required to comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, and global best practices for digital inclusivity.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all digital assets owned, developed, or maintained by the Organization.

**Applies to:**
*   **Public-Facing Assets:** The Organization’s website, blog, and public repositories.
*   **Educational Platforms:** Learning Management Systems (LMS), courseware, quizzes, and certification exams.
*   **Digital Content:** Documents (PDFs, Word), multimedia (video, audio), and social media posts.
*   **Internal Tools:** Intranets and internal software used by employees, volunteers, and contractors (to ensure inclusive employment practices).
*   **Personnel:** All employees, Board members, volunteers, and contractors who create digital content or code.

**Exclusions:**
*   Legacy content published prior to [Effective Date] that is not currently being used for active instruction, unless a specific request for accommodation is received.
*   Third-party content or platforms over which the Organization has no control, provided that reasonable efforts have been made to select accessible alternatives.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **WCAG 2.2 AA** | Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.2, Level AA. This is the technical standard adopted by the Organization for compliance. |
| **Assistive Technology (AT)** | Software or hardware used by people with disabilities to interact with digital content (e.g., screen readers, magnifiers, switch controls). |
| **Alternative Text (Alt Text)** | A text description of an image used by screen readers to describe the image's content and function. |
| **VPAT** | Voluntary Product Accessibility Template. A document explaining how an information and communication technology (ICT) product, such as software, conforms to accessibility standards. |
| **Remediation** | The process of correcting digital content or code to meet accessibility standards. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Technical Standard for Compliance
The Organization adopts the **Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 Level AA** as its technical standard. All new web pages, web applications, and digital documents produced by the Organization must conform to WCAG 2.2 Level AA success criteria.

#### 4.2 Website and Software Development
4.2.1 **Development Lifecycle:** Accessibility testing must be integrated into the software development lifecycle (SDLC). Code reviews must include checks for semantic HTML, keyboard navigability, and ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) compliance.
4.2.2 **Keyboard Access:** All interactive elements (menus, forms, buttons) must be fully functional using a keyboard alone, without requiring a mouse.
4.2.3 **Color Contrast:** Text and interactive elements must meet the minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 against the background, as defined by WCAG.

#### 4.3 Educational Content and Multimedia
4.3.1 **Video Content:** All pre-recorded video content produced by the Organization must include synchronized closed captions. Audio descriptions must be provided for visual information not conveyed through audio.
4.3.2 **Live Events:** Virtual webinars and live training sessions must offer live captioning (CART or automated) upon request or as a standard practice for large audiences.
4.3.3 **Documents:** All downloadable documents (PDF, DOCX, PPT) must be tagged for accessibility, including proper heading structures, reading order, and alt text for images.
4.3.4 **Images:** All informative images across web and social media platforms must include descriptive Alternative Text. Decorative images must be marked as null/artifact to be ignored by screen readers.

#### 4.4 Procurement of Third-Party Technology
4.4.1 **Vendor Selection:** When selecting third-party platforms (e.g., donation portals, event ticketing, LMS), the Organization must prioritize vendors that demonstrate a commitment to accessibility.
4.4.2 **VPAT Review:** For any software purchase or subscription exceeding $5,000 or affecting the core educational mission, the vendor must provide a current VPAT. The CISO or designee must review the VPAT to identify critical accessibility gaps.

#### 4.5 Feedback and Accommodation
4.5.1 **Accessibility Statement:** The Organization must maintain a publicly available "Accessibility Statement" on its website, outlining its commitment, current compliance status, and contact information for reporting barriers.
4.5.2 **Feedback Mechanism:** A dedicated communication channel (e.g., `accessibility@studygrc.org`) must be monitored to receive reports of accessibility barriers.
4.5.3 **Reasonable Accommodation:** Upon receiving a request for accommodation (e.g., a user cannot access a specific PDF), the Organization must provide an accessible alternative format within a reasonable timeframe, typically not exceeding five (5) business days.

#### 4.6 Remediation Prioritization
Accessibility defects identified through audits or user feedback shall be classified and prioritized in the Organization's issue tracking system as follows:
*   **Critical:** Blocks a user from completing a primary task (e.g., donating, registering for a course). Must be remediated immediately.
*   **High:** Significant barrier but a workaround exists. Remediate within 30 days.
*   **Medium/Low:** Minor inconvenience or cosmetic issue. Remediate during the next scheduled maintenance cycle.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Digital Accessibility Policy and ensures budget is available for necessary tools and remediation. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures organizational culture prioritizes inclusivity and holds department heads accountable for compliance. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all curriculum, courseware, and educational resources meet accessibility standards before publication. |
| **CISO / IT Department** | Maintains the technical infrastructure, conducts annual accessibility audits, and integrates testing tools into the deployment pipeline. |
| **Content Creators / Volunteers** | Responsible for applying accessibility best practices (headings, alt text, plain language) to the content they create. |
| **Procurement Officer** | Verifies VPATs and accessibility compliance during the vendor selection process. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

**Legal Risk:** Non-compliance with ADA and Section 504 can result in litigation and reputational damage. Deliberate bypass of accessibility controls (e.g., disabling automated captioning to save costs without approval) is considered a violation of the Organization's Code of Ethics.
