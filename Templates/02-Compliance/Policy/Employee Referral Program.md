### 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Employee Referral Program is to encourage current Study GRC Inc. employees to identify and refer qualified candidates for open positions within the Organization. This policy aims to attract top talent who align with the Organization’s mission of fostering a community-driven GRC ecosystem, while reducing recruitment costs and time-to-hire. This policy aligns with **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.4**, which authorizes the payment of reasonable compensation for services rendered.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all active, paid employees of Study GRC Inc., with the specific exclusions listed below.

**Exclusions:**
The following individuals are **not** eligible to receive referral bonuses, though they are encouraged to refer candidates:
*   **Board of Directors:** To maintain strict adherence to **Bylaws Article III** and prevent private inurement.
*   **Operational Officers:** Specifically the Executive Director, CFO, and VP of HR (or equivalent roles with hiring authority).
*   **Hiring Managers:** Individuals directly responsible for the hiring decision for the specific open position.
*   **HR Personnel:** Staff members involved in the recruiting or onboarding process.
*   **Volunteers:** Volunteers may refer candidates but are not eligible for monetary compensation to maintain their volunteer status and avoid FLSA misclassification risks.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Referral Bonus** | A monetary incentive paid to an eligible employee for the successful hiring and retention of a referred candidate. |
| **Qualified Candidate** | An applicant who meets the essential requirements of the job description, is not currently employed by Study GRC Inc., and has not applied within the last 12 months. |
| **Good Standing** | An employee who is not currently on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) and has no active disciplinary actions on file. |
| **Hire Date** | The first day the new employee performs work for the Organization. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Eligibility
To be eligible for a referral bonus, the referrer must be an active employee in **Good Standing** at the time of the referral submission **and** at the time of the bonus payout.

#### 4.2 Candidate Eligibility
Referral bonuses will not be paid for:
*   Candidates who are former employees of Study GRC Inc. (re-hires) separated for less than 12 months.
*   Candidates who are immediate family members of the referrer (spouse, child, parent, sibling), to prevent conflicts of interest as defined in **Bylaws Article IX**.
*   Candidates who are already in the Organization’s applicant tracking system (ATS) or who have applied directly within the last 12 months.
*   Candidates referred for temporary, intern, or volunteer positions (unless specifically designated as "Bonus Eligible" by the Executive Director).

#### 4.3 Referral Process
4.3.1 **Submission:** Referrals must be submitted via the official **Employee Referral Form** or the Organization’s Applicant Tracking System (ATS) *before* the candidate interviews. Verbal referrals or emails sent directly to hiring managers without formal submission will not be honored.
4.3.2 **Application:** The referred candidate must acknowledge the referral in their application process.

#### 4.4 Bonus Structure and Tiers
Referral bonuses are categorized based on the difficulty of filling the role. Amounts are subject to budget approval and review by the Finance Committee.

*   **Tier 1 (General Operations/Admin):** $250.00
*   **Tier 2 (Specialized/Technical):** $500.00 (e.g., Curriculum Developers, IT Security roles).
*   **Tier 3 (Executive/Leadership):** $1,000.00 (Requires Board approval per **Bylaws Article V**).

#### 4.5 Payment Terms
To ensure retention and quality of hire, bonuses are paid in two installments:
1.  **50%** paid after the new employee completes **90 days** of continuous employment.
2.  **50%** paid after the new employee completes **180 days** of continuous employment.

Both the referrer and the new hire must be employed by Study GRC Inc. at the time of payout.

#### 4.6 Tax Implications
All referral bonuses are considered taxable income. They will be processed through payroll and subject to all applicable federal and state tax withholdings.

#### 4.7 Conflict of Interest
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX (Conflict of Interest)**, no employee shall participate in the interview, selection, or salary negotiation process for a candidate they have referred. If a conflict exists, the referrer must recuse themselves from the hiring decision.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Referring Employee** | Identify qualified talent, submit referrals via the official process, and refrain from influencing the hiring decision. |
| **Hiring Manager** | Review referred candidates based solely on merit and qualifications, without bias regarding the source. |
| **Human Resources** | Verify eligibility of the referral, track retention periods, and authorize bonus payments. |
| **Payroll/Finance** | Process bonus payments in accordance with tax laws and the approved budget. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Fraudulent Activity:** Any attempt to manipulate the referral program (e.g., splitting bonuses with candidates, referring fictitious candidates, or colluding to hire unqualified staff) constitutes fraud.

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, forfeiture of pending bonuses, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation.
