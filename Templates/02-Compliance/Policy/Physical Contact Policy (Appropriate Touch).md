### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish clear, mandatory boundaries regarding physical contact between Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") representatives and program participants, specifically minors and vulnerable adults. This policy is designed to prevent abuse, misunderstandings, and false allegations, ensuring a safe environment consistent with the Organization’s mission to foster a community-driven ecosystem. This policy aligns with the ethical standards mandated in **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.3**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all "Organization Personnel," defined as employees, Directors, Officers, volunteers, contractors, and guest speakers acting on behalf of Study GRC Inc.

**Applicability:**
*   **In-Person Events:** Conferences, workshops, meetups, and hackathons.
*   **Hybrid Interactions:** Any physical component of a primarily remote program.

**Exclusions:**
*   Physical contact between adults who are not in a position of authority over one another (e.g., two adult attendees at a conference), provided such contact is consensual and adheres to the general Code of Conduct.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18. |
| **Vulnerable Adult** | An individual age 18 or older who, due to mental or physical disability, age, or illness, may be unable to take care of themselves or protect themselves against harm or exploitation. |
| **Appropriate Touch** | Physical contact that is non-sexual, unambiguous, public, and generally accepted as a professional or friendly gesture (e.g., handshake, fist bump). |
| **Prohibited Touch** | Physical contact that is sexual in nature, aggressive, physically disciplining, or culturally/socially inappropriate for a professional mentorship relationship. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The requirement that at least two Organization-approved adults must be present during any interaction with a minor. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles of Contact
Organization Personnel must limit physical contact with participants to that which is necessary for the program's purpose or is a socially acceptable greeting. All physical contact must be:
1.  **Consensual:** The participant must welcome or initiate the contact.
2.  **Public:** Occurring in an open environment with other adults present.
3.  **Non-Sexual:** Devoid of any sexual connotation or gratification.

#### 4.2 Permissible Physical Contact
The following forms of contact are generally considered appropriate in a mentorship or educational setting, provided they are welcomed by the recipient:
*   Handshakes.
*   "High-fives" or fist bumps.
*   Brief pats on the shoulder or upper back (e.g., to congratulate).
*   "Side hugs" (arm around the shoulder), specifically when initiated by the participant or required for a group photo.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Physical Contact
To protect both participants and Organization Personnel, the following are strictly prohibited:
*   **Frontal Hugs:** Full-body frontal hugs (chest-to-chest), unless initiated by a participant in severe distress and immediately de-escalated to a side hug.
*   **Lap Sitting:** Allowing a minor to sit on an adult’s lap.
*   **Kissing:** Any form of kissing.
*   **Massage:** Giving massages or back rubs.
*   **Roughhousing:** Wrestling, tickling, piggyback rides, or horseplay.
*   **Corporal Punishment:** Any physical discipline (hitting, spanking, shaking).
*   **Restraint:** Physically restraining a participant, except when necessary to prevent immediate physical harm to themselves or others (see Section 4.5).

#### 4.4 The "Ask First" Standard
Organization Personnel must respect personal boundaries. Even for permissible contact (e.g., a high-five or side hug), Personnel must be attuned to the participant's body language. If a participant recoils or hesitates, contact must be withheld. Personnel are encouraged to verbally ask, "Is a high-five okay?" before initiating contact.

#### 4.5 Emergency Exceptions
In emergency situations involving medical distress or immediate safety threats, the preservation of life and safety takes precedence over standard contact restrictions.
*   **Medical Aid:** Personnel certified in CPR/First Aid may provide necessary physical care (e.g., chest compressions, Heimlich maneuver) consistent with their training.
*   **Safety Intervention:** Personnel may use reasonable physical force to separate participants fighting or to prevent a participant from running into traffic/danger.
*   **Reporting:** Any use of emergency physical contact must be documented in an Incident Report and submitted to the Board within 24 hours.

#### 4.6 Reporting Violations
Pursuant to the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy (YTH-POL-001)** and Texas Family Code Chapter 261:
*   Any observation of prohibited physical contact must be interrupted immediately.
*   The incident must be reported to the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or a Board Officer immediately.
*   If the contact is suspected to be sexual abuse or physical abuse, it must be reported to the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) or law enforcement within 48 hours.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Organization Personnel** | Adhere strictly to the boundaries defined in this policy; report observed violations immediately. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Serve as the primary point of contact for questions regarding appropriate touch; maintain Incident Report logs. |
| **Event Leads** | Ensure this policy is covered during volunteer orientation prior to any in-person event. |
| **Board of Directors** | Review and adjudicate serious violations; ensure compliance with state reporting laws. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy is a serious violation of the Organization's ethical standards.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations may result in immediate removal from the event, suspension of membership, termination of employment/volunteer status, and permanent ban from the community.
*   **Legal Action:** Instances of sexual misconduct or physical abuse will be referred to law enforcement and relevant state agencies.
