### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish standards for the collection, use, safeguarding, storage, retention, and destruction of Biometric Identifiers and Biometric Information by Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy ensures compliance with the **Texas Capture or Use of Biometric Identifier Act (CUBI)** (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 503.001 et seq.) and protects the privacy and security of employees, volunteers, members, and program participants.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, contractors, and Voting Members. It covers any system, process, or vendor engagement that involves the capture or processing of biometric data.

**Inclusions:**
*   Facial recognition systems used for identity verification (e.g., exam proctoring).
*   Fingerprint scanning for background checks (if retained by the Organization).
*   Voiceprint authentication systems.

**Exclusions:**
*   Photographs or video recordings used solely for media, marketing, or security surveillance *without* the use of facial recognition software to identify individuals based on face geometry.
*   Biometric data stored locally on a user’s personal device (e.g., Apple FaceID, Windows Hello) that is not transmitted to or stored by the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Biometric Identifier** | As defined by CUBI, a retina or iris scan, fingerprint, voiceprint, or record of hand or face geometry. |
| **Biometric Information** | Any information, regardless of how it is captured, converted, stored, or shared, based on an individual's biometric identifier used to identify an individual. |
| **Capture** | The process of collecting or recording a biometric identifier. |
| **CUBI** | The Texas Capture or Use of Biometric Identifier Act (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 503.001). |
| **Commercial Purpose** | The sale, lease, or disclosure of biometric data for profit. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. General Prohibition and Minimization
Study GRC Inc. shall not capture a biometric identifier of an individual for a commercial purpose. The Organization adheres to a strict data minimization principle; biometric data will only be collected when strictly necessary for:
1.  Security authentication (e.g., access to high-security data environments).
2.  Identity verification for certification exams or high-stakes assessments.
3.  Legal or regulatory compliance (e.g., specific background check requirements).

#### 4.2. Notice and Consent (Pre-Collection)
Pursuant to CUBI § 503.001(b), before capturing a biometric identifier, the Organization must:
1.  **Inform** the individual (or the individual's parent/legal guardian if a minor) that a biometric identifier is being collected;
2.  **Explain** the specific purpose for the collection and the length of time the data will be retained; and
3.  **Obtain** the individual's explicit consent to the capture and storage of the biometric identifier.

*Note: Consent must be captured via the "Biometric Information Consent Form" (PRIV-FRM-051) prior to any data collection.*

#### 4.3. Prohibition on Sale and Disclosure
The Organization shall not sell, lease, or otherwise disclose biometric data to any third party unless:
1.  The individual consents to the disclosure for identification purposes;
2.  The disclosure completes a financial transaction requested or authorized by the individual;
3.  The disclosure is required or permitted by a federal or state statute; or
4.  The disclosure is made by or to a law enforcement agency for a law enforcement purpose in response to a warrant.

#### 4.4. Standard of Care and Security
In accordance with Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection) and CUBI § 503.001(c)(2), the Organization shall store, transmit, and protect from disclosure all biometric identifiers using reasonable care. This care shall be at least as protective as the manner in which the Organization stores, transmits, and protects other confidential information (e.g., Social Security numbers, financial account numbers).
*   **Encryption:** All biometric data must be encrypted at rest and in transit.
*   **Access Control:** Access is restricted to the specific Operational Officers or IT administrators whose job function requires it.

#### 4.5. Retention and Destruction Schedule
Pursuant to CUBI § 503.001(c)(1), Study GRC Inc. shall destroy a biometric identifier within a reasonable time, but **no later than the first anniversary (1 year)** of the date the purpose for collecting the identifier expires.

*   **Employment/Volunteerism:** Data is destroyed within 30 days of the termination of the relationship.
*   **Exam Proctoring:** Data is destroyed within 30 days of the final certification of exam results, unless a longer period (up to 1 year) is required for fraud investigation.

#### 4.6. Vendor Management
If the Organization utilizes third-party vendors (e.g., remote proctoring services) to collect biometric data:
1.  The vendor must sign a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) confirming compliance with CUBI.
2.  The vendor must not use the data for training their own AI models or for any purpose other than the contracted service.

#### 4.7. Protection of Minors
Given the Organization's K-12 focus, the collection of biometric data from minors is strictly prohibited without:
1.  Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC) in alignment with COPPA standards.
2.  A specific, documented educational or safety necessity approved by the Board of Directors.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Ensures technical controls (encryption, access) meet CUBI standards; approves vendors handling biometric data. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Identifies proctoring needs; ensures exam vendors comply with this policy; manages data lifecycle for learners. |
| **Human Resources (HR)** | Administers consent forms for employees/volunteers; triggers data destruction upon termination. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews consent forms and vendor contracts for CUBI and privacy law compliance. |
| **Employees/Volunteers** | Must report any suspected unauthorized capture or disclosure of biometric data immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy constitutes a violation of Texas law and Organizational governance.

*   **Internal Discipline:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Civil Liability:** Under CUBI, the Organization may be liable for civil penalties of up to **$25,000 per violation**.
*   **Legal Action:** Individuals who violate this policy may be subject to personal legal liability.
