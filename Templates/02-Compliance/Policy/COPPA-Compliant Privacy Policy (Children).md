### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define Study GRC Inc.’s (the "Organization") commitment to protecting the privacy of children who use our educational resources and online community. This policy ensures strict compliance with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), and best practices for youth safeguarding. It establishes the legal framework for how the Organization collects, uses, and discloses personal information from children under the age of 13.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Digital Properties:** All websites, applications, learning management systems (LMS), and online services operated by Study GRC Inc. that are directed to children or where the Organization has actual knowledge that it is collecting data from a child.
*   **Personnel:** All Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors.
*   **Data:** Any Personally Identifiable Information (PII) collected from a child under the age of 13.

**Exclusions:**
*   General audience areas of the Organization’s website where age-gating prevents access by children under 13.
*   Data collected from parents or guardians regarding themselves.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Child** | An individual under the age of 13. (Note: For GDPR compliance, this may extend to under 16 depending on member location, but COPPA specifically governs under 13). |
| **COPPA** | The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, a U.S. federal law designed to protect the privacy of children under 13 on the internet. |
| **Personal Information (PII)** | Information that identifies a child, including first and last name, physical address, online contact information (email, IM handle), telephone number, Social Security number, persistent identifiers (cookies, IP addresses) used for tracking, or a photograph/video/audio file containing the child’s image or voice. |
| **Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC)** | Any reasonable effort (taking into consideration available technology) to ensure that a parent of a child receives notice of the operator's personal information collection, use, and disclosure practices, and authorizes the collection, use, and disclosure, as applicable, of personal information and the subsequent use of that information before that information is collected from that child. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Commitment to Privacy
Study GRC Inc. is dedicated to fostering a safe, open-source educational environment. We do not monetize child data. We do not sell, rent, or trade Personal Information of children to third parties for marketing or advertising purposes.

#### 4.2 Information Collection Practices
The Organization must minimize data collection to only what is reasonably necessary for a child to participate in an educational activity.

*   **4.2.1 Active Collection:** We may collect a child's name, email address (or parent's email), and username only after obtaining Verifiable Parental Consent.
*   **4.2.2 Passive Collection:** We may collect persistent identifiers (cookies, IP addresses) solely for the support of internal operations (e.g., maintaining user sessions, site security, and load balancing). These identifiers must not be used for behavioral tracking or targeted advertising.

#### 4.3 Direct Notice to Parents
Prior to collecting any Personal Information from a child, the Organization must provide a **Direct Notice** to the parent or legal guardian. This notice must:
1.  State that the Organization wishes to collect Personal Information from the child.
2.  Specify the types of Personal Information the Organization collects.
3.  Describe how the information will be used and disclosed.
4.  Link to this Privacy Policy.
5.  Explain how the parent can provide Verifiable Parental Consent.

#### 4.4 Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC)
The Organization must obtain VPC before collecting, using, or disclosing Personal Information from a child. Acceptable methods for obtaining VPC include:
*   Providing a consent form to be signed by the parent and returned via mail, fax, or electronic scan.
*   Requiring the parent to use a credit card, debit card, or other online payment system in connection with a monetary transaction (e.g., a nominal donation or membership verification fee).
*   Verifying a parent's identity through a video conference call with trained staff.
*   "Email Plus" method (for internal use only): Sending an email to the parent and receiving a confirmation, followed by a secondary confirmation (e.g., a delayed email) to ensure the parent is aware.

#### 4.5 Use of Information
Personal Information collected from children is used solely for:
*   Providing access to educational courses and materials.
*   Tracking educational progress within the Learning Management System (LMS).
*   Facilitating participation in moderated study groups.
*   System administration and security.

**Prohibited Conduct:** The Organization shall not use Personal Information collected from children for behavioral advertising, building user profiles for commercial purposes, or third-party marketing.

#### 4.6 Disclosure to Third Parties
The Organization generally does not share children's Personal Information with third parties. Exceptions include:
*   **Service Providers:** Third parties who provide support for the internal operations of the website (e.g., LMS hosting, technical support) and who have agreed in writing (via Data Processing Addendum) to maintain the confidentiality, security, and integrity of the information.
*   **Legal Requirements:** When required by law, such as in response to a court order or subpoena, or to protect the safety of a child (see *Mandatory Reporting Procedure*).

#### 4.7 Parental Rights and Control
In accordance with COPPA, parents/guardians have the right to:
1.  Review the Personal Information collected about their child.
2.  Request the deletion of their child’s Personal Information.
3.  Refuse to allow further collection or use of the child’s information.
4.  Agree to the collection and use of their child’s information without agreeing to the disclosure of that information to third parties (except for Service Providers).

To exercise these rights, parents must contact the Privacy Officer (see Section 4.9).

#### 4.8 Data Retention and Security
*   **Retention:** Children's data is retained only as long as is reasonably necessary to fulfill the educational purpose for which it was collected. Once the purpose is fulfilled or the account is closed, data must be deleted using secure disposal methods (NIST SP 800-88).
*   **Security:** The Organization implements reasonable procedures to protect the confidentiality, security, and integrity of personal information collected from children, including encryption at rest and in transit, as defined in the *Master Information Security Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this policy and ensures adequate resources for compliance mechanisms (e.g., consent management tools). |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Acts as the designated Privacy Officer. Oversees technical implementation of data protection, encryption, and data deletion requests. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

**Legal Penalties:** Violations of COPPA can result in significant civil penalties imposed by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) (up to $50,120 per violation). The Organization treats COPPA compliance as a critical risk management priority.
