### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Online Privacy Policy is to provide transparency regarding how Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") collects, uses, protects, and shares Personal Data gathered through our external-facing digital properties. This policy ensures compliance with the California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA), aligns with the principles of the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), and fulfills the transparency requirements mandated by Article VIII, Section 8.2 and Article IX of the Organization’s Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all visitors, users, donors, and members accessing the Organization’s website (e.g., studygrc.org), community forums, and related digital services.

*   **Inclusions:** All external data collection points, including contact forms, donation portals, newsletter sign-ups, and automated tracking technologies (cookies).
*   **Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to internal Human Resources data regarding employees or contractors, which is governed by the *Employee Handbook* and *Internal Data Privacy Policy*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Personal Data** | Any information that identifies, relates to, describes, or is reasonably capable of being associated with a particular individual (e.g., name, email, IP address). |
| **Sensitive Data** | A subset of Personal Data requiring heightened protection, including biometric data, precise geolocation, and data revealing racial origin, religious beliefs, or health status (as defined by TDPSA). |
| **Processing** | Any operation performed on Personal Data, such as collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion. |
| **Cookies** | Small text files placed on a user's device to track usage patterns, preferences, and session information. |
| **Do Not Track (DNT)** | A web browser setting that requests that a web application disable its tracking of an individual user. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Information Collection
The Organization collects information in two primary categories:

1.  **Voluntarily Provided Information:** We collect Personal Data that you voluntarily provide when you:
    *   Register for membership (Supporting or Community Participants per Bylaws Article II).
    *   Donate funds (processed via third-party secure payment processors).
    *   Subscribe to newsletters or educational resources.
    *   Submit inquiries via contact forms.
    *   *Data types may include:* Name, email address, mailing address, phone number, and payment information (tokenized).

2.  **Automated Information:** When you visit our website, we automatically collect specific data to ensure site functionality and security, including:
    *   IP address and browser type.
    *   Device information.
    *   Operating system.
    *   Interaction data (pages visited, time spent).

#### 4.2 Use of Information
Pursuant to our non-profit mission defined in Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3, we process Personal Data for the following purposes:
*   **Service Delivery:** To provide the educational resources, community access, and GRC tools requested by the user.
*   **Communication:** To send administrative notices, newsletters, and responses to inquiries.
*   **Transaction Processing:** To process donations and membership dues.
*   **Security:** To detect and prevent fraud, unauthorized access, or malicious activity.
*   **Legal Compliance:** To satisfy tax reporting (IRS) and regulatory obligations.

#### 4.3 Data Sharing and Disclosure
The Organization maintains a strict commitment to donor and member privacy.
*   **No Sale of Data:** We do not sell, rent, or trade Personal Data to third parties for monetary or other valuable consideration.
*   **Service Providers:** We share data with trusted third-party vendors (Processors) who assist in operating our website, conducting business, or servicing users (e.g., Stripe for payments, Mailchimp for emails). These vendors are bound by Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) to keep information confidential.
*   **Legal Requirements:** We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or to protect the rights and safety of the Organization and its members.

#### 4.4 Children’s Privacy (COPPA Compliance)
Given the Organization’s focus on K-12 education, we strictly adhere to the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).
*   We do not knowingly collect Personal Data from children under the age of 13 without verifiable parental consent.
*   If we discover that we have collected information from a child under 13 without consent, we will delete that information immediately.
*   Please refer to our separate *Children’s Privacy Policy* for detailed procedures regarding youth data.

#### 4.5 Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA) Disclosures
Although Study GRC Inc. is a non-profit entity, we voluntarily align with TDPSA transparency standards to ensure the highest level of trust.
*   **Targeted Advertising:** We do not engage in targeted advertising based on your activity across non-affiliated websites.
*   **Sensitive Data:** We do not collect Sensitive Data (biometrics, genetic data, etc.) through our public website without your explicit, opt-in consent.

#### 4.6 CalOPPA Disclosures (California)
*   **Do Not Track Signals:** Our systems do not currently recognize or respond to "Do Not Track" headers from web browsers.
*   **Third-Party Tracking:** Third-party analytics providers (e.g., Google Analytics) may collect data about your online activities over time and across different websites when you use our site.

#### 4.7 User Rights and Control
Users may exercise the following rights regarding their Personal Data:
*   **Right to Access:** Request a copy of the Personal Data we hold about you.
*   **Right to Correction:** Request updates to incorrect or incomplete data.
*   **Right to Deletion:** Request the deletion of your Personal Data, subject to our *Document Retention Policy* and legal tax record requirements.
*   **Right to Opt-Out:** Unsubscribe from marketing emails via the "Unsubscribe" link provided in the footer of all communications.

To exercise these rights, users must submit a request to: `privacy@[studygrc.org]`.

#### 4.8 Data Security
The Organization implements reasonable administrative, technical, and physical security measures to protect Personal Data, supervised by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) as outlined in Bylaws Article V, Section 5.2. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure.

#### 4.9 External Links
Our website contains links to external sites (e.g., open-source repositories, partner organizations). We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those third-party sites.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Owns this policy; ensures technical controls (encryption, access) are effective; manages data breach responses. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Ensures community communications comply with opt-out requests; manages donor privacy expectations. |
| **Webmaster / IT Team** | Implements the privacy notice on the website footer; ensures cookie banners function correctly. |
| **Users / Members** | Responsible for keeping their own account credentials confidential and updating their personal information. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy, such as unauthorized sharing of member data or failure to honor opt-out requests, may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action.
