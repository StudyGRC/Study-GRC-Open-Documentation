### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and constraints for the use of personally owned devices (Bring Your Own Device or "BYOD") to access Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") resources, data, and networks. As a remote-first organization, Study GRC Inc. permits the use of personal devices to facilitate flexibility; however, this privilege must be balanced with the obligation to protect the Organization’s intellectual property, member data, and youth protection records in accordance with **Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, members of the Board of Directors, contractors, volunteers, and interns (collectively "Users") who utilize personally owned electronic devices to access, store, or process Organizational data.

**Applies to:**
*   Smartphones (iOS, Android)
*   Tablets
*   Laptops and Desktop Computers (Windows, macOS, Linux)
*   Wearable technology (Smartwatches) accessing Organizational communications (e.g., Slack, Email)

**Exclusions:**
*   Devices used solely for multi-factor authentication (MFA) generation without accessing other Organizational data.
*   Publicly accessible computers (e.g., library kiosks), which are strictly prohibited from accessing non-public Organizational resources.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)** | The practice of allowing personnel to use their own computers, smartphones, or other devices for work purposes. |
| **Organizational Data** | Any data, file, record, or communication created, received, or maintained by Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to donor lists, youth participant records, financial data, and intellectual property. |
| **MDM (Mobile Device Management)** | Security software used by the Organization to monitor, manage, and secure mobile devices that access Organizational data. |
| **Jailbreaking/Rooting** | The process of removing software restrictions imposed by the device manufacturer, which compromises the device's security model. |
| **Remote Wipe** | The ability to remotely delete data from a device. This may be a "Full Wipe" (factory reset) or an "Enterprise Wipe" (removal of specific Organizational applications and data). |
| **Containerization** | A technology method that separates personal data from work data on a single device. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Authorization and Eligibility
Use of personal devices for Organizational business is a privilege, not a right. Study GRC Inc. reserves the right to revoke this privilege at any time if a User fails to comply with security standards.
*   **4.1.1** Users must agree to the terms of this policy prior to accessing Organizational resources on a personal device.
*   **4.1.2** The Organization reserves the right to deny access to devices that do not meet minimum security configurations (see Section 4.3).

#### 4.2 Expectation of Privacy
While Study GRC Inc. respects the privacy of its Users, the security of Organizational Data is paramount.
*   **4.2.1** The Organization will **not** access, view, or copy personal content (photos, personal emails, personal contacts) stored on the device.
*   **4.2.2** The Organization reserves the right to monitor, log, and analyze traffic and access patterns related to Organizational applications (e.g., Google Workspace, Slack, Financial Systems) accessed via the device.
*   **4.2.3** In the event of litigation or a regulatory investigation, the device may be subject to legal hold or forensic analysis. Users agree to cooperate with legal requests regarding Organizational Data stored on their device.

#### 4.3 Minimum Security Requirements
To access Organizational resources, personal devices must adhere to the following security standards:
*   **4.3.1 Passcode/Biometrics:** Devices must be secured with a strong passcode (minimum 6 digits or alphanumeric) or biometric authentication (FaceID/TouchID). Pattern locks are prohibited.
*   **4.3.2 Encryption:** Full-disk encryption must be enabled (e.g., BitLocker for Windows, FileVault for macOS, native encryption for iOS/Android).
*   **4.3.3 Operating System Updates:** Devices must run a vendor-supported operating system version. Patches and security updates must be installed within 14 days of release.
*   **4.3.4 Antivirus/Anti-malware:** Windows and macOS devices must have active, up-to-date antivirus software.
*   **4.3.5 Screen Lock:** Devices must be configured to automatically lock after a maximum of 15 minutes of inactivity.

#### 4.4 Prohibited Actions
Users are strictly prohibited from:
*   **4.4.1** Using "Jailbroken" or "Rooted" devices to access Organizational Data.
*   **4.4.2** Storing "Sensitive" or "Restricted" data (as defined in the Data Classification Standard), specifically **Youth Protection Data**, directly on the device’s local storage. Such data must be accessed only via secure cloud applications (e.g., Google Drive) without local caching where possible.
*   **4.4.3** Sharing the device PIN or password with family members or third parties if the device is logged into Organizational sessions.
*   **4.4.4** Disabling security controls (e.g., turning off encryption) while Organizational Data remains on the device.

#### 4.5 Lost or Stolen Devices
*   **4.5.1** Users must report lost or stolen devices that contain Organizational Data or active login sessions to the CISO or IT Department **immediately** (within 4 hours of discovery).
*   **4.5.2** Upon notification, the Organization is authorized to perform a Remote Wipe. The Organization will attempt an "Enterprise Wipe" (removing only work data) first. However, if the device does not support containerization or the risk is deemed critical, a "Full Wipe" may be executed. The User acknowledges this risk to personal data.

#### 4.6 Termination of Relationship
Upon resignation, termination of employment, or end of volunteer service:
*   **4.6.1** Users must remove all Organizational Data and uninstall Organizational applications from their personal devices within 24 hours.
*   **4.6.2** The IT Department will revoke access tokens and may perform an Enterprise Wipe to ensure all proprietary data is removed.
*   **4.6.3** Per **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all Work Product remains the property of Study GRC Inc. Users have no right to retain copies of Organizational Data on personal devices after their term of service ends.

#### 4.7 Support and Liability
*   **4.7.1** Users are responsible for the physical maintenance, carrier costs, and repair of their personal devices.
*   **4.7.2** Study GRC Inc. is not liable for loss of personal data, damage to the device, or corruption of personal files resulting from the implementation of security controls (e.g., MDM) or Remote Wipes, provided the Organization acted in accordance with this policy.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **User (Employee/Volunteer)** | Ensures device meets security standards; reports lost/stolen devices immediately; prevents unauthorized access by family/friends. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Defines security configurations; manages MDM strategy; executes remote wipes when necessary; investigates security incidents involving BYOD. |
| **IT Department** | Provides guidance on configuring devices; manages access controls and de-provisioning. |
| **Human Resources** | Ensures BYOD agreement is signed during onboarding; coordinates with IT during offboarding to ensure data removal. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in the revocation of BYOD privileges, disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action.

If a User’s negligence regarding their personal device leads to a data breach involving Protected Health Information (PHI), Youth Data, or Personally Identifiable Information (PII), the User may be held personally liable to the extent permitted by law, notwithstanding the indemnification provisions in **Bylaws Article VII**, if the act is determined to be gross negligence or willful misconduct.
