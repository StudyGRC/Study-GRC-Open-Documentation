### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and procedures for documenting the time and effort of Study GRC Inc. employees to ensure that personnel compensation costs charged to Federal awards are accurate, allowable, and properly allocated. This policy ensures compliance with **2 CFR § 200.430 (Compensation—personal services)** of the Uniform Guidance, mitigating the risk of audit disallowances, financial penalties, and violations of the False Claims Act.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees (Exempt and Non-Exempt) whose compensation is:
1.  Paid, in whole or in part, with Federal funds (direct or indirect); or
2.  Used to meet a cost-sharing or matching requirement of a Federal award.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to independent contractors or consultants. Contractor time is substantiated via invoices and the *Procurement Policy (FIN-POL-022)*, not through Personnel Activity Reports (PARs).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cost Objective** | A specific grant, contract, project, service, or other activity to which costs are assigned (e.g., "Grant A," "General Admin," "Fundraising"). |
| **Total Professional Activity (TPA)** | The total effort expended by an employee in their position at Study GRC Inc., constituting 100% of their compensated time, regardless of the number of hours worked. |
| **Personnel Activity Report (PAR)** | The official document (timesheet or effort certification) used to certify the distribution of an employee's effort across various cost objectives. |
| **Split-Funded Employee** | An employee whose work is divided among multiple cost objectives (e.g., 50% Grant A, 50% General Admin). |
| **Single-Funded Employee** | An employee working 100% of their time on a single Federal award or cost objective. |
| **Internal Control** | A process designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives in the effectiveness and efficiency of operations, reliability of financial reporting, and compliance with laws. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Standard of Documentation
In accordance with **2 CFR § 200.430(i)** and **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, charges to Federal awards for salaries and wages must be based on records that accurately reflect the work performed. These records must:
1.  Be supported by a system of internal control which provides reasonable assurance that the charges are accurate, allowable, and properly allocated.
2.  Be incorporated into the official records of Study GRC Inc.
3.  Reasonably reflect the **total activity** for which the employee is compensated by the Organization, not exceeding 100% of compensated activities.
4.  Encompass both federally assisted and all other activities (including non-federal grants, fundraising, and general administration) on an integrated basis.
5.  Comply with the established accounting policies and practices of the Organization.

#### 4.2 After-the-Fact Determination
Budget estimates (i.e., estimates determined before the services are performed) alone do not qualify as support for charges to Federal awards. All time and effort reporting must reflect an **after-the-fact determination** of the actual activity of each employee.

#### 4.3 Reporting Frequency and Format
To ensure accuracy in a remote-first environment, Study GRC Inc. mandates the following reporting cadence:

*   **Non-Exempt Employees:** Must complete timesheets reflecting hours worked per cost objective on a **bi-weekly** basis, coinciding with the payroll cycle.
*   **Exempt Employees:** Must complete a Personnel Activity Report (PAR) certifying the percentage of effort allocated to each cost objective on a **monthly** basis.

#### 4.4 The Personnel Activity Report (PAR)
The PAR must meet the following criteria to be valid:
1.  **Distribution of Effort:** It must account for 100% of the employee's Total Professional Activity.
2.  **Granularity:** It must identify the specific Federal award (by Grant ID/FAIN) or cost objective (e.g., "Fundraising," "Admin").
3.  **Certification:** It must be signed (electronically or physically) by the employee and countersigned by a supervisor who has **suitable means of verification** that the work was performed.
    *   *Note:* In a remote environment, "suitable means of verification" includes review of work output, digital logs, and regular check-ins, not necessarily physical observation.

#### 4.5 Reconciliation of Budget to Actuals
The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) will compare the actual effort reported on PARs against the salary distribution in the payroll system quarterly.
*   **Variance Threshold:** If the difference between the budgeted estimate and the actual effort reported exceeds **10%**, the CFO must adjust the General Ledger (GL) to reflect the actual effort.
*   **Short-Term Fluctuations:** Significant changes in an employee's work activity must be identified and entered into the payroll system prospectively.

#### 4.6 Cost Sharing and Matching
Salaries and wages of employees used to meet cost-sharing or matching requirements of Federal awards must be supported in the same manner as salaries and wages claimed for reimbursement from Federal awards. These hours must be clearly designated as "Cost Share" on the PAR to prevent double-counting or unallowable reimbursement.

#### 4.7 Prohibited Conduct
*   **Rubber Stamping:** Supervisors must not approve PARs without verifying that the work was actually performed on the specific cost objective.
*   **100% Rule Violation:** An employee cannot be certified for more than 100% effort.
*   **Retroactive Manipulation:** Adjusting effort reports solely to "use up" remaining grant funds is strictly prohibited and constitutes fraud.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Accurately track time/effort across specific cost objectives. Submit PARs/Timesheets by the designated deadline. Self-correct if actual work deviates from the job description. |
| **Supervisor** | Review PARs for accuracy based on knowledge of the employee's work. Reject and return PARs that do not reflect actual activities. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Maintain the Time and Effort reporting system. Perform quarterly reconciliations between PARs and Payroll. Process journal entries for reallocations. Ensure record retention per *FIN-STD-013*. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensure organizational culture supports accurate reporting. Approve PARs for direct reports (e.g., VP of Education, DKG). |
| **Board Treasurer** | Review aggregated payroll allocation reports during quarterly Finance Committee meetings to ensure alignment with grant budgets. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts Study GRC Inc. at risk of disallowed costs, which must be repaid to the Federal government using unrestricted (non-federal) funds.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees or supervisors who falsify time and effort records are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Legal Action:** Intentional falsification of time records submitted to the Federal government is a violation of the **False Claims Act (31 U.S.C. §§ 3729–3733)** and may result in civil or criminal penalties for the individual and the Organization.
