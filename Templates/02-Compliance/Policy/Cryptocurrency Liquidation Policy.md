### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a mandatory framework for the immediate management and disposition of cryptocurrency and digital asset donations received by Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization").

As a 501(c)(3) non-profit, the Organization acts as a fiduciary for public funds. Cryptocurrencies are inherently volatile assets subject to rapid and unpredictable market fluctuations ("Market Risk"). To adhere to the **Prudent Investor Rule** and protect the Organization’s financial stability, this policy mandates the immediate liquidation of such assets into fiat currency (USD) to preserve capital for charitable programming.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Assets:** All forms of cryptocurrency, virtual currency, digital tokens, stablecoins, and Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) received by the Organization.
*   **Personnel:** The Treasurer, Chief Financial Officer (CFO), and any staff or volunteers authorized to access the Organization’s digital wallets or payment processors.
*   **Systems:** All third-party cryptocurrency payment processors (e.g., Coinbase Commerce, BitPay, The Giving Block) and self-custody wallets managed by the Organization.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to non-cash stock donations, which are governed separately under the *Investment Policy* and *Gift Acceptance Policy*, though the principle of rapid liquidation generally applies there as well.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cryptocurrency** | A digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security and operates independently of a central bank (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum). |
| **Fiat Currency** | Government-issued currency that is not backed by a physical commodity, such as gold or silver, but by the government that issued it (specifically, the US Dollar). |
| **Liquidation** | The process of selling a non-cash asset (cryptocurrency) on the open market to convert it into cash (Fiat Currency). |
| **Volatility** | The rate at which the price of an asset increases or decreases for a set of returns. High volatility means the price can change dramatically over a short time period. |
| **Slippage** | The difference between the expected price of a trade and the price at which the trade is executed. |
| **Gas/Network Fee** | A fee paid to the network miners/validators to process a transaction on a blockchain. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Mandate: Immediate Liquidation
It is the strict policy of Study GRC Inc. to sell all contributed cryptocurrency and digital assets immediately upon receipt. The Organization **does not** hold cryptocurrency as an investment or for speculative purposes ("HODLing").
*   The objective is to convert the donation into US Dollars (USD) to fund the Organization's mission as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**.
*   The Organization accepts the risk that the value may increase after the sale; the priority is mitigating the risk that the value will decrease.

#### 4.2 Automated Liquidation (Preferred Method)
Wherever technically feasible, the Organization must utilize third-party payment processors (e.g., The Giving Block, Coinbase Commerce) configured to **auto-liquidate** donations.
*   The account settings for these processors must be configured to "Instant Exchange" or "Auto-Sell" to convert incoming crypto to USD immediately at the point of transaction.
*   Funds must be settled into the Organization's insured bank account via ACH or wire transfer at the earliest available settlement window.

#### 4.3 Manual Liquidation Protocols
If a donation is received into a wallet that does not support auto-liquidation (e.g., a direct transfer to a self-custody wallet), the authorized officer must:
1.  Initiate the sale of the asset on a regulated exchange within **one (1) business day** of becoming aware of the receipt.
2.  If the asset lacks liquidity or cannot be sold on a major exchange, the Treasurer must be notified immediately to determine a disposition strategy.

#### 4.4 Valuation and Receipts
In accordance with IRS regulations and the *Gift Acceptance Policy*:
*   **Donor Acknowledgment:** The donor shall receive an acknowledgment letter describing the asset type and amount (e.g., "1.5 Bitcoin"). The Organization **shall not** assign a dollar value to the contribution in the acknowledgment letter. It is the donor's responsibility to obtain a qualified appraisal for tax deduction purposes.
*   **Internal Accounting:** For internal accounting purposes, the donation shall be recorded at the **Fair Market Value (FMV)** at the time of receipt.
*   **Realized Gain/Loss:** Any difference between the FMV at receipt and the net cash proceeds realized after liquidation (due to volatility or slippage) shall be recorded as a "Realized Gain/Loss on Sale of Assets."

#### 4.5 Transaction Fees and Expenses
The Organization accepts that "Gas Fees," "Network Fees," and "Exchange Fees" are necessary costs of doing business.
*   These fees shall be deducted from the gross proceeds of the sale.
*   Staff are authorized to pay reasonable network fees to ensure the timely processing of the liquidation transaction.

#### 4.6 Prohibited Conduct
*   **Speculation:** Deliberately delaying the sale of an asset in anticipation of a price increase is strictly prohibited.
*   **Direct Spending:** Cryptocurrency must not be used directly to pay vendors, employees, or contractors. All assets must be converted to USD before being disbursed to ensure proper tax reporting (Form 1099/W-2) and audit trails.
*   **Personal Wallets:** Organization funds or assets must never pass through a personal wallet or account belonging to a Director, Officer, or employee.

#### 4.7 Exceptions
Exceptions to the immediate liquidation requirement may only be granted by a **unanimous written vote** of the Board of Directors. Such exceptions are generally reserved for:
*   NFTs or specific tokens that are intended to be held as part of a digital archive or museum collection related to the Organization's educational mission.
*   Tokens that are "locked" or "vested" by smart contract and cannot be physically sold until a future date.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Treasurer** | Oversees the crypto-asset merchant accounts; reviews monthly reconciliation of crypto receipts to bank deposits; authorizes manual liquidations if automation fails. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Ensures payment processors are configured for auto-liquidation; records transactions in the general ledger; ensures IRS Form 8282 is filed if required (for assets sold within 3 years). |
| **Executive Director** | May accept crypto donations on behalf of the organization but must immediately notify Finance for liquidation. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves this policy; votes on any exceptions to the immediate liquidation rule. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Audit Trail:** All crypto transactions must be traceable. The Finance team must retain export logs from payment processors showing the time of receipt, the exchange rate used, and the final USD settlement amount.
*   **Liability:** Failure to liquidate assets in a timely manner resulting in a significant loss of value may be considered a breach of fiduciary duty.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation.
