### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a mandatory framework for human accountability in the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) systems at Study GRC Inc. As a 501(c)(3) organization dedicated to education and community, we must ensure that algorithmic decision-making does not displace human judgment, particularly in matters affecting individual rights, youth safety, and educational integrity. This policy operationalizes the "Accountability" function of the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) and aligns with the ethical obligations outlined in Article IX of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors of Study GRC Inc.

**In Scope:**
*   All AI systems used for generating content (Generative AI).
*   All AI systems used for automated decision-making or profiling (e.g., resume screening, community moderation bots, financial fraud detection).
*   Third-party AI tools integrated into Organization workflows.

**Exclusions:**
*   Low-impact, non-decision-making tools (e.g., spam filters, spell-checkers, basic routing algorithms) unless they result in the denial of service or access to a member.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)** | A capability that allows a human to intervene in an automated process *before* the final decision or action is executed. |
| **Human-over-the-Loop (HOTL)** | A supervisory capability where a human monitors the AI system's operation and can intervene or override decisions retroactively, but does not approve every single action in real-time. |
| **High-Impact Decision** | Any decision that significantly affects an individual’s legal rights, access to the community, employment/volunteer status, or safety (physical or psychological). |
| **Algorithm Aversion** | The tendency to reject AI advice; conversely, **Automation Bias** is the tendency to over-rely on AI advice without scrutiny. This policy seeks to balance both. |
| **Hallucination** | A phenomenon where a Generative AI model perceives patterns that are nonexistent or imperceptible to human observers, creating outputs that are nonsensical or factually incorrect. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 The Principle of Ultimate Human Responsibility
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article III (Governance)**, the Board of Directors and appointed Operational Officers retain ultimate responsibility for the actions of the Organization. AI systems are tools to assist, not replace, human judgment. Study GRC Inc. prohibits the delegation of final accountability to any software or algorithm.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Requirements
The following categories of AI usage require strict Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) review **prior** to final execution or publication:

*   **4.2.1 Educational Content Generation:** Any curriculum, exam questions, or educational resources generated by AI must be reviewed for accuracy, bias, and pedagogical value by the **VP of Education & Training** or a designated Subject Matter Expert (SME) before release. This mitigates the risk of "hallucinations" entering the knowledge base.
*   **4.2.2 Youth Safety & Moderation:** While automated tools may flag potential violations in community channels (e.g., Discord), no minor or student may be permanently banned or reported to law enforcement based solely on an algorithmic determination. A human moderator must verify the context.
*   **4.2.3 Employment and Volunteer Screening:** AI tools may be used to sort or rank applications, but no candidate shall be rejected solely by an automated system. A human must review all rejections for roles defined in **Bylaws Article V (Operational Leadership)**.
*   **4.2.4 Financial Transactions:** Any AI-driven financial anomaly detection that triggers a freeze of assets or funds exceeding $500 must be verified by the **Treasurer** or **CFO**.

#### 4.3 Human-over-the-Loop (HOTL) and Monitoring
For lower-risk operational tasks (e.g., chatbot inquiries, scheduling), a Human-over-the-Loop approach is permitted.
*   **4.3.1 Periodic Audits:** The **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** must conduct quarterly audits of automated decision logs to identify potential bias or drift.
*   **4.3.2 Override Capability:** All AI systems deployed must have a "Kill Switch" or manual override function accessible to authorized Operational Officers.

#### 4.4 Disclosure of AI Interaction
Transparency is a core value. When members or the public interact with an AI agent (e.g., a support bot), the system must clearly disclose that it is an AI.
*   If an AI generates a draft for a human volunteer who then sends it, the volunteer accepts full ownership of the sentiment and accuracy of that message.

#### 4.5 Documentation of Oversight
To satisfy regulatory accountability:
*   Reviewers of AI-generated content must log their approval.
*   In the event of an adverse decision challenged by a member (per the **Member Grievance Dispute Procedure**), the Organization must be able to demonstrate the specific human human who reviewed and ratified the AI's recommendation.

#### 4.6 Prohibition on "Rubber Stamping"
Mere passive approval of AI outputs does not constitute oversight. Reviewers must demonstrate **meaningful control**, defined as:
1.  Understanding how the system reached a conclusion.
2.  Having the authority to reject the conclusion.
3.  Having the time and resources to review the underlying data.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Sets the risk appetite regarding AI adoption; approves High-Impact Decision categories. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Maintains the inventory of AI tools; audits compliance with HITL/HOTL requirements; ensures "Kill Switch" functionality. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Serves as the primary HITL for all AI-generated educational materials; ensures curriculum accuracy. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Serves as the primary HITL for community moderation actions flagged by AI; ensures culture is preserved over algorithmic efficiency. |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Required to critically evaluate AI outputs (avoiding Automation Bias) and report suspected hallucinations or bias. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—specifically the deployment of autonomous decision-making tools without authorized human oversight—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

If an unreviewed AI output results in legal liability or significant reputational damage, the responsible individual may be subject to the **Indemnification** limitations outlined in **Bylaws Article VII**, specifically regarding actions not taken in "good faith."
