### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the governance framework for the Central Document Repository (CDR), ensuring that Study GRC Inc. maintains a "Single Source of Truth" for all organizational records. This policy balances the Organization's commitment to transparency and open-source knowledge sharing with the legal and ethical obligation to protect sensitive data, intellectual property, and personal privacy. This policy operationalizes the record-keeping mandates defined in **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all digital records, documents, and data assets owned or generated by Study GRC Inc.
*   **Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, volunteers, and Voting Members.
*   **Systems:** Includes all sanctioned cloud storage platforms (e.g., Google Workspace, SharePoint), code repositories (e.g., GitHub), and financial systems.
*   **Exclusions:** Transient communications (e.g., Slack messages) that have not been formalized into a record, and personal notes not intended for the official organizational record.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Central Document Repository (CDR)** | The authorized, cloud-based storage system(s) designated by the CISO as the official location for organizational files. |
| **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)** | A method of restricting network access based on the roles of individual users within the Organization. |
| **Principle of Least Privilege** | The security concept that a user should only have access to the specific data and resources needed to complete their assigned task. |
| **Data Owner** | The Department Head or Officer responsible for the classification, integrity, and maintenance of a specific subset of data (e.g., the Treasurer is the Data Owner for financial records). |
| **Transparency Page** | The public-facing section of the Organization's website where public records are proactively disclosed per Bylaws Article VIII. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Single Source of Truth
To ensure data integrity and version control, all final versions of policies, procedures, financial reports, and governance documents must be stored in the designated CDR. Storing official records solely on local hard drives, personal devices, or unauthorized third-party cloud services ("Shadow IT") is strictly prohibited.

#### 4.2 Data Classification and Access Tiers
Access to the CDR is governed by the sensitivity of the information, defined as follows:

*   **Level 1: Public (Transparency)**
    *   **Definition:** Information intended for public consumption or required by law to be disclosed.
    *   **Examples:** IRS Form 990, Bylaws, Certificate of Formation, Educational Curricula (Open Source), Annual Financial Reports.
    *   **Access:** Open to the world via the Transparency Page or public GitHub repositories.
    *   **Bylaws Reference:** Article VIII, Section 8.2.

*   **Level 2: Internal (Community/Volunteer)**
    *   **Definition:** Operational documents required for volunteers and staff to perform their duties.
    *   **Examples:** Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), Meeting Agendas, Project Plans, Training Materials.
    *   **Access:** Read-only for active Volunteers; Read/Write for assigned contributors.

*   **Level 3: Confidential (Restricted)**
    *   **Definition:** Sensitive information protected by law, contract, or privacy ethics.
    *   **Examples:** Donor lists, Volunteer PII, background check results, unpublished financial details, passwords/keys.
    *   **Access:** Strictly limited to specific roles (e.g., HR, Finance, Board) on a "need-to-know" basis.

#### 4.3 Voting Member Inspection Rights
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.6** and **Texas Business Organizations Code § 22.351**, Voting Members in good standing have the right to inspect books and records.
*   **Procedure:** Access is not automatic. Members must submit a *Member Record Inspection Request Form*.
*   **Limitation:** Access is granted for a "proper purpose" related to the member's interest in the Organization. Access to Level 3 (Confidential) data involving personnel records or other members' private data may be redacted or withheld to protect privacy rights.

#### 4.4 Access Control Administration
*   **Provisioning:** Access rights are granted at the time of onboarding based on the individual's specific role (e.g., "VP of Education"). Access is not attached to the individual's personal identity but to their organizational account.
*   **Deprovisioning:** Access to the CDR must be revoked immediately (within 24 hours) upon the termination of a volunteer, employee, or contractor, or upon their removal from a specific role.
*   **MFA Requirement:** All accounts with administrative or "Write" access to the CDR must be secured with Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

#### 4.5 Audit and Logging
The CDR must be configured to maintain an immutable audit log recording:
*   The identity of the user accessing the file.
*   The type of action taken (View, Edit, Delete, Download).
*   The timestamp of the action.
*   These logs shall be retained in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

#### 4.6 Intellectual Property Protection
While the Organization champions Open Source resources (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3), the *master* editable files in the CDR are the property of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Work Made for Hire:** As defined in **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all content created within the CDR by officers and volunteers is the property of the Organization.
*   **Export Restrictions:** Bulk exporting or downloading of the CDR for personal use or unauthorized distribution is prohibited.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this policy and defines the Organization's transparency strategy. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Configures technical controls (permissions, MFA, logging) for the CDR. Conducts periodic access audits. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Acts as the primary Data Owner; authorizes access requests for Level 3 (Confidential) data. |
| **Department Heads** | Ensure their team's documents are correctly classified and stored in the CDR, not on personal drives. |
| **All Users** | Adhere to data classification rules; protect their credentials; report unauthorized access. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—including unauthorized sharing of Confidential data, bypassing access controls, or refusing to store official records in the CDR—may result in disciplinary action. Consequences may include immediate revocation of system access, removal from volunteer/staff roles, termination of membership (per Bylaws Article II), and legal action for breach of the Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA).
