### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to maintain a safe, secure, and productive environment for Study GRC Inc.’s employees, volunteers, members, and the K-12 communities we serve. This policy establishes a standardized, risk-based framework for conducting background checks to mitigate liability, prevent fraud, and strictly adhere to youth protection standards.

This policy ensures compliance with the **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)**, **Texas Family Code**, and **EEOC guidance** regarding the consideration of criminal history, utilizing a "Graded Adjudication" method to ensure fairness and relevance.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals seeking to affiliate with Study GRC Inc. in the following capacities:
*   **Employees:** Full-time, part-time, and temporary staff.
*   **Board of Directors:** As required by Bylaws Article III, Section 3.3 (Qualifications).
*   **Operational Officers:** As defined in Bylaws Article V.
*   **Volunteers:** Specifically those with unsupervised access to minors, access to sensitive financial data, or administrative privileges on IT systems.
*   **Independent Contractors:** Those with access to proprietary data or youth participants.

**Exclusions:** One-time event volunteers who do not have unsupervised access to minors or access to sensitive data are exempt from comprehensive background checks, provided they are supervised by a cleared individual at all times.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Adjudication** | The process of reviewing background check results against the Organization's standards to determine eligibility. |
| **Adverse Action** | A denial of employment, volunteer status, or promotion based in whole or in part on information obtained in a background report. |
| **Covered Individual** | Any person subject to this policy (applicant, employee, volunteer). |
| **Graded Matrix** | A tiered system that assigns different levels of screening depth based on the risk profile of the position (e.g., General vs. Youth-Facing). |
| **Green Factors** | EEOC criteria for individualized assessment: (1) Nature/gravity of offense, (2) Time passed since offense/completion of sentence, (3) Nature of the job held or sought. |
| **Consumer Reporting Agency (CRA)** | A third-party vendor that compiles and provides background information (e.g., Checkr, Sterling). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Authority and Conditional Offers
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article III (Directors)** and **Article V (Operational Leadership)**, moral and ethical standing is a qualification for leadership.
*   All offers of employment, Board nomination, or volunteer assignment for covered positions are **contingent** upon the satisfactory completion of a background check.
*   Refusal to consent to a background check will result in the immediate withdrawal of the offer or termination of candidacy.

#### 4.2 Frequency of Screening
*   **Initial Screening:** Must be completed *before* the individual commences work or assumes their role.
*   **Re-Screening:**
    *   **Youth-Facing Roles:** Every twenty-four (24) months.
    *   **Financial/Fiduciary Roles:** Every thirty-six (36) months.
    *   **For Cause:** Checks may be run immediately if the Organization has a reasonable suspicion of new criminal activity, subject to applicable law.

#### 4.3 The Graded Adjudication Matrix
Study GRC Inc. utilizes a tiered approach. The depth of the check is determined by the role's risk profile.

| Tier | Role Category | Screening Components |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1** | **General Staff / Low-Risk Volunteers** | • SSN Trace & Identity Verification<br>• National Criminal Database Search<br>• Sex Offender Registry (National) |
| **Tier 2** | **Fiduciary & Executive**<br>*(Board, Officers, Finance)* | • **All Tier 1 checks**<br>• Federal Criminal Search<br>• Global Watchlist (Terrorist/Sanctions)<br>• Credit History (where relevant/legal for CFO/Treasurer) |
| **Tier 3** | **Youth-Facing / High Trust**<br>*(Educators, Mentors, Admins)* | • **All Tier 1 checks**<br>• County Criminal Search (7-year residence history)<br>• DOJ/NSOPW (National Sex Offender Public Website)<br>• Motor Vehicle Record (if driving required) |

#### 4.4 Adjudication Standards
Results are not an automatic "Pass/Fail" except for specific disqualifying offenses.

**4.4.1 Automatic Disqualification (Zero Tolerance)**
To protect the Organization’s 501(c)(3) status and youth beneficiaries, the following records will result in automatic ineligibility:
*   Conviction of any sex offense (regardless of time elapsed).
*   Conviction of any crime involving violence against a child.
*   Inclusion on the National Sex Offender Public Website (NSOPW).

**4.4.2 Individualized Assessment (The "Green Factors")**
For other offenses (e.g., theft, drug offenses, non-violent felonies), the Adjudication Committee must apply the EEOC "Green Factors" before making a decision:
1.  **The Nature and Gravity of the Offense:** Was it a minor misdemeanor or a serious felony?
2.  **The Time That Has Passed:** Did the offense occur 10 years ago with a clean record since?
3.  **The Nature of the Job:** Is the crime relevant to the role? (e.g., a DUI is relevant for a driver, but perhaps not for a remote web developer).

#### 4.5 FCRA Compliance Procedure
Study GRC Inc. strictly follows the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) workflow:

1.  **Disclosure:** The Organization must provide a clear, standalone document stating that a background check will be run.
2.  **Authorization:** The individual must sign a written acknowledgment and authorization.
3.  **Pre-Adverse Action:** If the Organization intends to deny based on the report, it must send:
    *   A "Pre-Adverse Action Notice."
    *   A copy of the background report.
    *   A copy of "A Summary of Your Rights Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act."
    *   The individual has **five (5) business days** to dispute the accuracy of the report with the CRA.
4.  **Final Adverse Action:** If the individual does not dispute the report or the dispute is resolved against them, the Organization sends a "Final Adverse Action Notice."

#### 4.6 Confidentiality and Data Protection
*   Background reports are **Confidential** information.
*   Reports are stored separately from personnel files in a secure, access-controlled repository.
*   Access is restricted to the HR Manager and the Adjudication Committee.
*   Reports are retained and destroyed in accordance with the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy** (HR-POL-012).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director / HR** | Initiates checks, manages the CRA vendor, and ensures FCRA paperwork compliance. |
| **Adjudication Committee** | Composed of the ED and one Board Director. Reviews "Yellow Flag" results to apply Individualized Assessment. |
| **Candidate / Volunteer** | Provides accurate information (DOB, SSN, Address History) and timely consent. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves this policy; serves as the final appeals body for Executive-level disqualifications. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action.
*   **For Candidates:** Falsification of application materials regarding criminal history is grounds for immediate rejection.
*   **For Staff:** Failure to report a new arrest or conviction within 72 hours (as required by the Employee Handbook) may result in termination.
*   **Legal:** Non-compliance with FCRA procedures can subject the Organization to federal lawsuits and statutory damages.
