### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Investment Policy is to establish clear guidelines for the management of the financial assets of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy is designed to safeguard the Organization’s funds while ensuring sufficient liquidity to meet operating requirements and providing a reasonable rate of return.

This policy is drafted in compliance with the **Texas Uniform Prudent Management of Institutional Funds Act (TUPMIFA)** (Texas Property Code Chapter 163) and the fiduciary duties outlined in **Article III** of the Organization’s Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all financial assets held by the Organization, including:
*   Operating Funds.
*   Reserve Funds.
*   Endowment Funds (if established).
*   Restricted and Unrestricted Net Assets.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to the day-to-day management of petty cash or immediate transactional checking accounts used for payroll and accounts payable, which are governed by the *Cash Handling and Petty Cash Policy (FIN-POL-008)*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Prudent Investor Rule** | A legal standard requiring fiduciaries to invest trust assets as a "prudent person" would, exercising reasonable care, skill, and caution, considering the probable safety of capital as well as the probable income. |
| **TUPMIFA** | Texas Uniform Prudent Management of Institutional Funds Act; the state law governing the management and investment of funds held by charitable institutions. |
| **Liquidity** | The ease with which an asset can be converted into cash without affecting its market price. |
| **Fiduciary** | A person (or body) who holds a legal or ethical relationship of trust with one or more other parties (e.g., the Board of Directors). |
| **Short-Term Reserves** | Funds intended to be used within 12 months. |
| **Long-Term Reserves** | Funds not anticipated to be needed for operations for at least 12 months. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Standard of Care
In accordance with TUPMIFA and **Bylaws Article III**, the Board of Directors and any delegates (e.g., Finance Committee, Investment Managers) must act in good faith and with the care an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would exercise under similar circumstances.

Investment decisions must be made not in isolation but in the context of the institutional fund’s portfolio of investments as a whole and as a part of an overall investment strategy having risk and return objectives reasonably suited to the fund and to the institution.

#### 4.2 Investment Objectives
The primary objectives of the Organization’s investment activities, in priority order, are:
1.  **Preservation of Capital:** Minimizing the probability of loss of principal.
2.  **Liquidity:** Ensuring funds are available to meet reasonably anticipated operating requirements (payroll, grant deliverables, vendor payments).
3.  **Return on Investment:** Maximizing yield within the constraints of the first two objectives.

#### 4.3 Delegation of Authority
*   **Board of Directors:** Retains ultimate responsibility for the investment portfolio. The Board must approve this policy and any changes to the asset allocation strategy.
*   **Finance Committee:** As authorized by **Bylaws Article VI**, the Finance Committee is responsible for recommending investment strategies, selecting financial institutions, and monitoring performance.
*   **Treasurer:** Oversees the implementation of the policy and ensures proper reporting to the Board.

#### 4.4 Asset Allocation and Tiers
To manage risk and liquidity, funds shall be divided into tiers:

**Tier 1: Operating Funds (0-6 Months Liquidity)**
*   **Objective:** Immediate liquidity and capital preservation.
*   **Authorized Vehicles:** Business Checking Accounts, Savings Accounts, Money Market Accounts (FDIC Insured).
*   **Target Balance:** Sufficient to cover 3-6 months of average operating expenses.

**Tier 2: Short-Term Reserves (6-24 Months Liquidity)**
*   **Objective:** Yield enhancement with high safety.
*   **Authorized Vehicles:** Certificates of Deposit (CDs) (FDIC Insured), US Treasury Bills/Notes, High-yield Savings.
*   **Maturity:** Maximum maturity of 24 months.

**Tier 3: Long-Term Reserves / Quasi-Endowment (24+ Months)**
*   **Objective:** Long-term growth and inflation hedging.
*   **Authorized Vehicles:** Investment Grade Corporate Bonds, Broad Market Index Funds (Equities/Fixed Income).
*   **Constraint:** Tier 3 investments may only be established if Tier 1 and Tier 2 targets are fully funded.

#### 4.5 Prohibited Investments
To minimize undue risk, the Organization strictly prohibits investment in:
*   Cryptocurrency or Digital Assets (Note: Donated crypto must be liquidated immediately per the *Cryptocurrency Liquidation Policy*).
*   Derivatives, Options, or Futures.
*   Short Sales or Margin Trading.
*   Private Placements or Hedge Funds.
*   Individual Stocks (to avoid concentration risk; broad index funds are permitted in Tier 3).
*   Real Estate (speculative investment).
*   Any investment that would jeopardize the Organization’s 501(c)(3) status.

#### 4.6 Diversification
Investments shall be diversified to minimize the risk of large losses.
*   **Concentration Limit:** With the exception of US Treasury securities and fully FDIC-insured deposits, no more than 10% of the total investment portfolio may be invested in a single issuer.

#### 4.7 Conflict of Interest
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX (Conflict of Interest)**, Directors, Officers, and members of the Finance Committee must disclose any material financial interest in financial institutions or investment vehicles used by the Organization. The Organization shall not invest in any entity in which a Director or Officer has a controlling interest.

#### 4.8 Socially Responsible Investing (SRI)
Where financial returns are comparable, the Organization prefers investments that align with its mission of education, open-source access, and ethical governance. The Organization will attempt to avoid investments in entities that conflict with its *Code of Ethics*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Investment Policy; reviews annual investment performance; declares "Financial Emergencies" allowing access to restricted reserves. |
| **Finance Committee** | Reviews the portfolio quarterly; recommends changes to asset allocation; evaluates investment manager performance. |
| **Treasurer** | Executes transfers between tiers; ensures compliance with TUPMIFA; reports investment balances at every Board meeting. |
| **CFO / Executive Director** | Manages day-to-day cash flow to determine amounts available for investment; executes trades/transfers as directed by the Treasurer/Finance Committee. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including removal from the Board or termination of employment.

Fiduciaries who act in good faith and in accordance with the Prudent Investor Rule and this policy shall be indemnified by the Organization to the fullest extent permitted by **Bylaws Article VII**.
