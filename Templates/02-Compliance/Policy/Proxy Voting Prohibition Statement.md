### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to strictly enforce the prohibition of proxy voting within Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") as mandated by the Bylaws. This policy ensures that all governance decisions, elections, and fundamental actions reflect the direct, personal will of the active Voting Members. By requiring personal participation, the Organization safeguards the integrity of its community-driven ecosystem and prevents the consolidation of voting power through vote harvesting or delegation.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **All Classes of Voting Membership:** Specifically Supporting Members and Honorary Members.
*   **All Governance Events:** Annual Meetings, Special Meetings, and any action requiring a membership vote.
*   **All Voting Methods:** In-person ballots, virtual meeting polls, and asynchronous electronic voting systems.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to Board of Directors meetings, where Directors must also vote in person or via electronic consent, but where specific statutory rules regarding quorum and presence apply under a separate policy.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Proxy Voting** | The practice of a member delegating their voting power to another individual (a "proxy") to vote on their behalf in their absence. |
| **Personal Vote** | A vote cast directly by the individual member to whom the membership rights belong, either physically or through authenticated electronic means. |
| **Voting Member** | A Supporting Member or Honorary Member in good standing as defined in Article II of the Bylaws. |
| **Secure Electronic Voting System** | The Organization’s authorized digital platform used to cast ballots, which authenticates the user's identity (e.g., via unique email link or multi-factor authentication). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Absolute Prohibition of Proxies
Pursuant to **Article II, Section 2.3** of the Bylaws and in accordance with the **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.160(b)**, voting by proxy is strictly prohibited for all matters submitted to a vote of the members.
*   No member may authorize another person to act for them by proxy.
*   No power of attorney or similar legal instrument shall be accepted for the purpose of casting a vote in Organization elections or business.

#### 4.2 Requirement for Personal Action
Voting rights are personal to the member and are non-transferable. To have a vote counted, the Voting Member must cast the vote personally.
*   **In-Person/Virtual Meetings:** The member must be present (physically or virtually) at the time the vote is called.
*   **Electronic Ballots:** The member must personally access the Secure Electronic Voting System using credentials or unique access links issued specifically to them.

#### 4.3 Distinction Regarding Electronic Voting
Participation via the Organization’s designated Secure Electronic Voting System does **not** constitute proxy voting.
*   When a member casts a vote via the authorized digital platform, it is legally considered a **Personal Vote** cast by the member.
*   Sharing login credentials or unique voting links with another individual to allow them to vote on one's behalf is a violation of this policy and the Organization's *Computer Use and Security Policy*.

#### 4.4 Invalidity of Proxy Instruments
Any written or verbal attempt to designate a proxy will be deemed void *ab initio* (from the beginning).
*   The Secretary and/or the Election Committee shall automatically reject any ballot cast by an individual claiming to represent a Voting Member.
*   The Organization is under no obligation to notify the absent member that their attempted proxy vote was rejected before the close of the voting window.

#### 4.5 Non-Transferability of Membership
Membership in the Organization, and all rights arising therefrom (including voting rights), are non-transferable and non-assignable. Membership rights terminate immediately upon the death, resignation, or expulsion of the member. No voting rights may be exercised by an executor, administrator, or heir.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board Secretary** | Ultimate authority for validating voting rosters and ensuring no proxy votes are accepted or counted. |
| **Election Committee** | Responsible for configuring the Secure Electronic Voting System to ensure one-person-one-vote integrity and rejecting invalid ballots. |
| **Voting Members** | Responsible for maintaining the security of their own voting credentials and casting their votes personally. |
| **Presiding Officer** | Responsible for announcing the prohibition of proxies at the commencement of any meeting where a vote will occur. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Any vote attempted via proxy will be discarded and will not count toward the final tally or toward the establishment of a quorum.

**Disciplinary Action:**
*   **Credential Sharing:** Any Voting Member found to have knowingly shared their electronic voting credentials with another party to circumvent this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of membership for cause (violation of Bylaws).
*   **Coercion:** Any attempt to coerce a member into surrendering their voting rights or credentials to another party will be treated as a violation of the *Code of Ethics and Conduct*.
