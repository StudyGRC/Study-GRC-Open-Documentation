### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish clear, non-negotiable boundaries regarding the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) systems within Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization").

As a 501(c)(3) non-profit dedicated to Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) and serving a community that includes K-12 educational environments, the Organization must adhere to the highest ethical standards. This policy mitigates risks associated with bias, discrimination, privacy violations, copyright infringement, and harm to vulnerable populations (specifically minors). It aligns with the **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF)** and the Organization’s commitment to ethical conduct as outlined in **Bylaws Article III**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, contractors, and temporary staff.
*   **Systems:** All AI/ML technologies, including but not limited to Generative AI (e.g., ChatGPT, Midjourney, GitHub Copilot), Predictive Analytics, and Automated Decision-Making Systems (ADMS).
*   **Assets:** All Organization-owned data, devices, and networks.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to standard, non-generative algorithms used in basic computing functions (e.g., spam filters, spell checkers) unless those tools are integrated with Generative AI features.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **High-Risk AI System** | An AI system used in critical infrastructure, education, employment, or essential private/public services where the output could significantly impact a person's life, safety, or rights. |
| **Generative AI (GenAI)** | AI systems capable of generating new content (text, images, code, audio) in response to prompts (e.g., GPT-4, Claude, DALL-E). |
| **Deepfake** | Synthetic media in which a person in an existing image or video is replaced with someone else's likeness, or a person is artificially made to say or do something they did not say or do. |
| **Hallucination** | A phenomenon where an AI model generates false, misleading, or nonsensical information presented as fact. |
| **Automated Decision-Making (ADM)** | The process of making a decision by automated means without any human involvement. |
| **Confidential Information** | As defined in the Organization's Data Classification Standard, including PII, donor records, and unpublished strategic plans. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Unacceptable Risk (Absolute Prohibitions)
The following uses of AI are strictly prohibited under any circumstances due to their potential for severe harm or violation of fundamental rights:

1.  **Social Scoring:** The use of AI to evaluate or classify the trustworthiness of members, volunteers, or employees based on social behavior or predicted personality traits.
2.  **Subliminal Manipulation:** AI systems designed to deploy subliminal techniques to distort a person’s behavior in a manner that causes or is likely to cause physical or psychological harm.
3.  **Real-Time Biometric Identification:** The use of AI for "real-time" remote biometric identification (e.g., facial recognition) in public spaces or during virtual events, except where explicitly required by law enforcement with a valid warrant.
4.  **Generation of CSAM:** The creation, manipulation, or distribution of Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) or any synthetic imagery depicting minors in sexually suggestive scenarios. **Zero Tolerance:** Violations will result in immediate termination and referral to federal law enforcement.
5.  **Deepfakes of Real Individuals:** Creating synthetic audio, video, or images of real individuals (staff, members, or public figures) without their explicit, written, and informed consent.

#### 4.2. Youth Protection and Education
Given the Organization's focus on K-12 education and youth engagement:

1.  **Direct Interaction with Minors:** AI chatbots or agents must not interact directly with users under the age of 13 without verifiable parental consent (COPPA compliance) and real-time human supervision.
2.  **Profiling Minors:** AI shall not be used to profile, track, or predict the behavior of minors for marketing, advertising, or behavioral modification purposes.
3.  **Synthetic Imagery of Minors:** The generation of synthetic images representing minors, even if not sexually explicit, is prohibited to prevent potential misuse or grooming risks.

#### 4.3. Employment and Human Resources
To ensure compliance with Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) laws:

1.  **Automated Hiring/Firing:** AI systems shall not be the sole determinant in hiring, termination, promotion, or disciplinary decisions. A "Human-in-the-Loop" is mandatory for all HR-related decisions.
2.  **Emotion Recognition:** The use of AI to infer emotions or state of mind of employees or volunteers during interviews or performance reviews is prohibited due to lack of scientific validity and potential for bias.

#### 4.4. Data Privacy and Intellectual Property
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX (Intellectual Property)** and **Article IX (Confidentiality)**:

1.  **Public Input of Confidential Data:** Personnel must not input Non-Public, Confidential, or Restricted data (e.g., member emails, financial passwords, donor lists) into public, open-access Generative AI tools (e.g., the free version of ChatGPT) where the data may be used to train the model.
2.  **Code Generation Licensing:** Personnel must not use AI coding assistants to generate code for the Organization's open-source repositories unless the tool guarantees that the output does not infringe on third-party intellectual property or introduce restrictive licensing (e.g., GPL contamination into an MIT-licensed project).
3.  **Undisclosed AI Authorship:** Personnel must not publish AI-generated content (articles, policy drafts, educational materials) as their own work. Significant AI contributions must be disclosed.

#### 4.5. Accuracy and Academic Integrity
1.  **Unverified Dissemination:** AI-generated educational content regarding GRC regulations, laws, or standards must be reviewed by a qualified Subject Matter Expert (SME) for accuracy prior to publication. Publishing unverified AI "hallucinations" as fact is a violation of the **Code of Ethics**.
2.  **Legal Advice:** AI tools shall not be used to generate specific legal advice for members. General informational summaries may be generated but must carry a clear disclaimer.

#### 4.6. Impersonation and Transparency
1.  **Bot Disclosure:** Any AI system interacting with the community (e.g., a Discord support bot) must clearly identify itself as an AI/Bot at the beginning of the interaction. Impersonating a human is prohibited.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves this policy; ensures alignment with the Organization's 501(c)(3) mission. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Maintains the "Blocked AI Tools List"; monitors network traffic for prohibited usage; conducts investigations into violations. |
| **AI Governance Board** | Reviews requests for exceptions (where applicable); assesses new AI tools for ethical risks; updates policy based on evolving technology. |
| **Department Heads** | Ensures staff and volunteers are trained on this policy; verifies human review of AI-generated content. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Adhere to this policy; report suspected unethical AI use or "shadow AI" deployments. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy constitutes a violation of the Organization's **Code of Ethics**.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (per **Bylaws Section 3.6**), or revocation of membership.
*   **Legal Action:** Unauthorized use of AI to generate illegal content (e.g., CSAM, non-consensual deepfakes) or to steal Intellectual Property will result in immediate legal action and referral to appropriate law enforcement agencies.
