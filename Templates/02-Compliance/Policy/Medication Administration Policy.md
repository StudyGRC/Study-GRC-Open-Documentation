### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict guidelines for the possession, storage, and administration of medication during Study GRC Inc. in-person events, workshops, and programs. This policy is designed to ensure the health and safety of all participants, particularly minors, while mitigating liability risks associated with medication errors, unauthorized access, and adverse reactions.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, contractors, and program participants attending in-person events.
*   **Inclusions:** Prescription medications, over-the-counter (OTC) medications, vitamins, and herbal supplements.
*   **Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to remote work environments where employees or participants are located in their own private residences.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Medication** | Any substance used for medical treatment, including prescription drugs, over-the-counter (OTC) drugs, vitamins, and supplements. |
| **Self-Administration** | The act of a participant taking their own medication without physical assistance from staff, though potentially under staff supervision. |
| **Assisted Administration** | The act of a designated staff member storing medication and providing it to the participant at the prescribed time. |
| **Designated Health Officer (DHO)** | An adult staff member or volunteer specifically authorized by the Event Director to manage medication storage and logs. |
| **Controlled Substance** | A drug or chemical whose manufacture, possession, and use are regulated by the government (e.g., ADHD stimulants, opioid pain relievers). |
| **Emergency Medication** | Medication required for immediate life-saving intervention (e.g., Epinephrine auto-injectors, rescue inhalers, glucagon). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles
Study GRC Inc. is not a medical facility. Staff and volunteers are generally prohibited from administering medication unless strictly necessary for ADA accommodation or youth program safety.
*   **4.1.1** Whenever possible, medication dosing schedules should be arranged by the parent/guardian to eliminate the need for administration during program hours.
*   **4.1.2** Study GRC Inc. does **not** maintain a stock supply of OTC medications (e.g., Tylenol, Advil) for general distribution. All medication must be provided by the participant or their guardian.

#### 4.2 Authorization Requirements
No medication will be accepted, stored, or administered without proper documentation.
*   **4.2.1** A **Medication Authorization Form** must be completed and signed by a parent/guardian for any minor requiring medication during an event.
*   **4.2.2** For prescription medications, the medication must be in its original pharmacy container with the prescription label intact and legible. The label serves as the physician's order.
*   **4.2.3** For OTC medications, a parent/guardian must sign a specific consent detailing the dosage and frequency. Staff will not exceed the dosage recommended on the manufacturer’s label without a physician’s written note.

#### 4.3 Storage and Security
To prevent theft, misuse, or accidental ingestion, strict storage protocols must be followed.
*   **4.3.1** All medications (except Emergency Medications) must be turned over to the Designated Health Officer (DHO) upon arrival at the event.
*   **4.3.2** Medications must be stored in a **locked** container or cabinet restricted to authorized staff only.
*   **4.3.3** Medications requiring refrigeration must be stored in a secure refrigerator or a locked box within a refrigerator, separate from food items if possible.
*   **4.3.4** **Exception for Emergency Meds:** Participants with asthma, severe allergies, or diabetes may carry emergency medications (inhalers, EpiPens) on their person, provided a **Self-Carry Consent Form** is on file.

#### 4.4 Administration Procedures
The DHO must adhere to the "Five Rights" of medication administration to prevent errors.
*   **4.4.1 Right Patient:** Verify the participant's identity (name and date of birth).
*   **4.4.2 Right Drug:** Verify the medication label matches the Authorization Form.
*   **4.4.3 Right Dose:** Verify the dosage matches the label/form.
*   **4.4.4 Right Route:** Verify the method of administration (oral, topical, etc.).
*   **4.4.5 Right Time:** Administer within 30 minutes of the scheduled time.

#### 4.5 Documentation (The Medication Log)
Every instance of medication administration must be documented immediately.
*   **4.5.1** The **Medication Administration Log** must record:
    *   Participant Name
    *   Medication Name and Dosage
    *   Date and Time Administered
    *   Signature/Initials of the DHO
    *   Any comments (e.g., "Participant refused dose," "Spilled dose").
*   **4.5.2** Medication errors (wrong dose, wrong time, wrong patient) must be reported immediately to the Event Director and the parent/guardian. An **Incident Report** must be filed.

#### 4.6 Disposal and Return
*   **4.6.1** All unused medication must be returned to the parent/guardian at the conclusion of the event.
*   **4.6.2** If medication is left behind and the owner cannot be contacted within 7 days, it must be disposed of via a local pharmacy take-back program or in accordance with FDA guidelines. Medication must never be flushed down the toilet or thrown in general trash without proper neutralization.

#### 4.7 Prohibited Conduct
*   **4.7.1** Participants are strictly prohibited from sharing any medication (prescription or OTC) with other participants.
*   **4.7.2** Staff members are prohibited from dispensing their own personal medication to participants.
*   **4.7.3** Possession of controlled substances without a valid prescription and proper authorization form is grounds for immediate expulsion and potential law enforcement notification.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Director** | Appoints the Designated Health Officer (DHO); ensures secure storage facilities are available; reviews Incident Reports related to medication. |
| **Designated Health Officer (DHO)** | Collects, secures, and administers medication; maintains the Medication Administration Log; communicates with parents regarding medical issues. |
| **Parent/Guardian** | Completes Authorization Forms; provides medication in original containers; retrieves unused medication at event conclusion. |
| **Participant** | Reports to the DHO at scheduled times for medication; self-administers under supervision (if applicable); does not share medication. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy poses a severe health and safety risk.
*   **Staff/Volunteers:** Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Participants:** Sharing prescription medication is a zero-tolerance offense resulting in immediate expulsion from the program.
*   **Legal:** Negligence in medication administration resulting in harm may lead to civil or criminal liability for the individual and the Organization.
