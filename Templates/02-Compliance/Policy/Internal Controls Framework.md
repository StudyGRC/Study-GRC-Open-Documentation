### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Internal Controls Framework is to establish a system of checks and balances that safeguards the assets of Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"), ensures the accuracy and reliability of accounting data, promotes operational efficiency, and ensures adherence to prescribed managerial policies.

This policy is explicitly designed to satisfy the requirements of **2 CFR § 200.303 (Internal Controls)** for Federal award recipients and aligns with the **COSO (Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission)** Integrated Framework. It provides reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives in the following categories:
1.  Effectiveness and efficiency of operations;
2.  Reliability of reporting for internal and external use; and
3.  Compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all financial and operational activities of the Organization. It is binding upon the Board of Directors, Operational Officers (including the CEO and CFO), employees, volunteers, and contractors who have custody of assets or are involved in financial transaction processing.

**Exclusions:** This framework does not supersede specific donor restrictions that may impose *stricter* controls than those defined herein; in such cases, the stricter standard applies.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Internal Control** | A process, effected by the Board of Directors, management, and other personnel, designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives relating to operations, reporting, and compliance. |
| **COSO Framework** | The standard framework for internal controls, consisting of five components: Control Environment, Risk Assessment, Control Activities, Information & Communication, and Monitoring Activities. |
| **Segregation of Duties (SoD)** | The principle that no single individual should have control over two or more phases of a transaction or operation (Authorization, Custody, and Recordkeeping) to prevent fraud and error. |
| **2 CFR 200.303** | The section of the Uniform Guidance requiring non-Federal entities to establish and maintain effective internal control over the Federal award. |
| **Material Weakness** | A deficiency, or combination of deficiencies, in internal control, such that there is a reasonable possibility that a material misstatement of the entity's financial statements will not be prevented, or detected and corrected, on a timely basis. |
| **Compensating Control** | An alternative control mechanism used to mitigate risk when standard controls (like strict SoD) are not feasible due to staffing limitations. |

### 4. Policy Statements

The Organization adopts the five components of the COSO Framework as the standard for its internal control structure.

#### 4.1 Control Environment (Tone at the Top)
The Board of Directors and Senior Management must demonstrate a commitment to integrity and ethical values.
*   **4.1.1 Ethical Standards:** All personnel must adhere to the *Code of Ethics* and *Conflict of Interest Policy* (Bylaws Article IX). Violations must be reported immediately via the *Whistleblower Protection Policy*.
*   **4.1.2 Organizational Structure:** The Board must maintain a clear organizational structure (Bylaws Article V) where reporting lines for financial authority are distinct. The CFO reports operationally to the CEO but maintains a functional reporting line to the Treasurer/Board for financial integrity matters.
*   **4.1.3 Competence:** The Organization must ensure that staff responsible for financial management and grant compliance possess the requisite knowledge of GAAP and 2 CFR 200 regulations.

#### 4.2 Risk Assessment
The Organization must perform a risk assessment at least annually to identify and analyze risks to the achievement of its objectives.
*   **4.2.1 Identification:** The CFO and Audit Committee (if established) must identify risks related to:
    *   Fraud and theft (misappropriation of assets).
    *   Financial reporting inaccuracies.
    *   IT security breaches (referencing the *Master Information Security Policy*).
    *   Non-compliance with Federal statutes (2 CFR 200).
*   **4.2.2 Change Management:** The risk assessment must specifically address risks associated with significant changes, such as rapid growth, new grant awards, or changes in the regulatory environment.

#### 4.3 Control Activities
Control activities are the actions established through policies and procedures that help ensure management directives to mitigate risks are carried out.

**4.3.1 Segregation of Duties (SoD)**
To the extent possible within a small organization, duties must be segregated so that no one person handles more than one of the following functions for a single transaction:
1.  **Authorization:** Approving the transaction (e.g., signing a check, approving a purchase order).
2.  **Custody:** Holding the asset (e.g., receiving checks, access to bank accounts).
3.  **Recordkeeping:** Recording the transaction in the general ledger.

*   *Exception/Compensating Control:* Where staffing size prevents full segregation, the Treasurer or a designated Board member must perform detailed monthly reviews of bank statements, reconciliations, and payroll reports to provide independent oversight.

**4.3.2 Authorization and Approval**
*   Financial authority limits are defined in the *Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix*.
*   All expenditures exceeding the threshold defined in the DoA must require dual authorization.
*   Pursuant to Bylaws Article VIII, the Board must approve the annual budget. Expenditures significantly outside the approved budget require Board amendment.

**4.3.3 Safeguarding of Assets**
*   **Physical Assets:** Physical access to checks, cash (if any), and equipment must be restricted to authorized personnel.
*   **Digital Assets:** Access to online banking, accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks), and donor databases must be protected by Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) as per the *Access Control Policy*.

**4.3.4 IT General Controls**
*   The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) is responsible for ensuring that financial systems are backed up, patched, and secured against unauthorized access.

#### 4.4 Information and Communication
*   **4.4.1 Financial Reporting:** The Organization must prepare accurate and timely financial reports. Pursuant to Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2, an annual financial report must be presented to the Board and made available for public transparency.
*   **4.4.2 Grant Reporting:** Financial data used for Federal Financial Reports (FFR) must be traced directly to the general ledger to ensure accuracy.
*   **4.4.3 Internal Communication:** Staff must have a confidential channel (Whistleblower mechanism) to communicate concerns regarding internal control weaknesses or potential fraud without fear of retaliation.

#### 4.5 Monitoring Activities
*   **4.5.1 Ongoing Monitoring:** The CFO must perform monthly variance analysis (Budget vs. Actuals) to identify anomalies.
*   **4.5.2 Separate Evaluations:** The Board (or Audit Committee) must conduct periodic reviews of the internal control system. This may include an external Single Audit (if the Organization expends $750,000 or more in Federal funds) or a financial review by an independent CPA.
*   **4.5.3 Remediation:** Any deficiencies identified during monitoring (internal or external audit findings) must be documented in a Corrective Action Plan (CAP) and remediated promptly.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ultimate responsibility for the internal control system. Approves this policy. Reviews financial reports and audit findings. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides Board-level oversight of financial operations. Reviews monthly bank reconciliations if SoD cannot be achieved by staff. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Responsible for the operational implementation of the control environment. Ensures staff adhere to policies. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Designs and maintains the internal control framework. Conducts risk assessments. Prepares financial reports. |
| **Audit Committee** | (If appointed) Oversees the external audit process and monitors the effectiveness of internal controls. |
| **All Employees** | Responsible for adhering to control procedures (e.g., obtaining approvals, submitting receipts) and reporting control deficiencies. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this Internal Controls Framework puts the Organization at significant risk of fraud, financial loss, and loss of tax-exempt status or Federal funding.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board/Officer position.
*   **Legal Action:** Instances of suspected fraud or embezzlement will be referred to legal counsel and appropriate law enforcement authorities.
*   **Federal Reporting:** Material weaknesses in internal control over Federal awards must be disclosed to the Federal awarding agency as required by 2 CFR 200.
