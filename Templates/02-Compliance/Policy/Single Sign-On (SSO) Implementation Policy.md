### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the mandatory requirement for Single Sign-On (SSO) across Study GRC Inc.'s technology ecosystem. This policy aims to centralize identity management, reduce the attack surface associated with password fatigue, streamline user provisioning and deprovisioning, and ensure compliance with **NIST SP 800-53 Control IA-2 (Identification and Authentication)**.

By enforcing SSO, the Organization ensures that access to organizational assets is mediated through a central, auditable, and secure Identity Provider (IdP), aligning with the data protection mandates outlined in **Article IX** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers (collectively "Users") accessing Organization resources.
*   **Assets:** All cloud-based applications (SaaS), internal tools, infrastructure, and third-party services utilized by Study GRC Inc.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   "Break-glass" or emergency root accounts which must remain local for disaster recovery.
    *   Legacy applications explicitly exempted by the CISO due to technical incompatibility, subject to compensating controls.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Single Sign-On (SSO)** | An authentication scheme that allows a user to log in with a single ID and password to any of several related, yet independent, software systems. |
| **Identity Provider (IdP)** | A centralized system (e.g., Google Workspace, Okta, Azure AD) that creates, maintains, and manages identity information for principals and provides authentication services to relying applications. |
| **Service Provider (SP)** | The application or service (e.g., Slack, GitHub, Zoom) that the user is trying to access, which relies on the IdP for authentication. |
| **SAML 2.0 / OIDC** | Standardized data formats (Security Assertion Markup Language / OpenID Connect) for exchanging authentication and authorization data between an IdP and an SP. |
| **Shadow IT** | Information technology systems, devices, software, applications, and services managed without explicit organizational approval or centralized IT oversight. |
| **SCIM** | System for Cross-domain Identity Management; a standard for automating the exchange of user identity information between identity domains (auto-provisioning). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Centralized Identity Management (NIST IA-2)
To satisfy NIST IA-2, Study GRC Inc. must uniquely identify and authenticate organizational users (or processes acting on behalf of users).
*   **4.1.1 Designated IdP:** The Organization shall maintain a single, authoritative Identity Provider (IdP) to serve as the source of truth for all user identities.
*   **4.1.2 Mandatory Integration:** All business applications that support SAML 2.0, OIDC, or equivalent federation protocols must be integrated with the Designated IdP. Local (direct) login to these applications is prohibited for standard users unless technically unavoidable.
*   **4.1.3 Credential Ownership:** Per **Bylaws Article IX (Intellectual Property & Data)**, all identities created within the IdP are the property of the Organization. Users have no expectation of privacy regarding the metadata or access logs generated by their authentication activities.

#### 4.2 Procurement and Vendor Selection
*   **4.2.1 SSO as a Gatekeeper:** The availability of SSO functionality is a mandatory requirement for the procurement of new software or services.
*   **4.2.2 The "SSO Tax":** While the Organization seeks cost-effective solutions, the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and CISO shall prioritize enterprise-tier plans that include SSO over cheaper tiers that exclude it, recognizing that security is a non-negotiable operational cost.
*   **4.2.3 Vendor Disqualification:** Vendors that do not support SSO or demand exorbitant fees for security features (SSO Tax) may be disqualified during the **Vendor Security Risk Assessment (VSRA)** process unless no viable alternative exists.

#### 4.3 Authentication Standards
*   **4.3.1 MFA Enforcement:** The IdP must be configured to enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all users, in accordance with the **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Mandate (IS-POL-015)**. SSO integration must not bypass MFA requirements.
*   **4.3.2 Session Management:** The IdP must be configured with appropriate session timeout values based on the sensitivity of the data, forcing re-authentication periodically.

#### 4.4 Provisioning and Deprovisioning
*   **4.4.1 Automated Provisioning:** Where supported, applications must utilize SCIM to automate the creation and removal of user accounts based on IdP group membership.
*   **4.4.2 Immediate Revocation:** Upon the termination of a user's employment or volunteer service, their account must be disabled at the IdP level immediately. This action must propagate to all connected Service Providers to revoke access instantly.

#### 4.5 Exceptions and Compensating Controls
*   **4.5.1 Technical Incompatibility:** If a critical business application does not support SSO, a formal exception request must be submitted to the CISO.
*   **4.5.2 Compensating Controls:** For approved exceptions, the following controls are mandatory:
    *   The password must be unique, complex (20+ characters), and stored in the Organization-approved Password Manager.
    *   MFA must be enabled natively on the application if available.
    *   The account must be manually added to the **Offboarding Checklist** to ensure manual revocation upon departure.

#### 4.6 Prohibition of Shadow IT
Users are strictly prohibited from creating accounts on third-party services for Organization business using personal email addresses or credentials not managed by the Designated IdP.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Owns this policy; selects the Designated IdP; approves/denies exception requests; monitors IdP logs for anomalous behavior. |
| **IT Systems Administrator** | Configures SAML/OIDC connections; manages SCIM provisioning; maintains the registry of integrated applications. |
| **Hiring Managers** | Ensures new hires are assigned to the correct IdP groups (e.g., "Finance Team", "Volunteers") to trigger appropriate access. |
| **Users (Employees/Volunteers)** | Must use the IdP for all access; must not bypass SSO using local credentials; must report any application that allows login without SSO. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews and approves budget allocations for Enterprise/SSO-enabled software tiers. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation.

Attempts to bypass SSO mechanisms, disable federation, or create unauthorized local accounts on integrated systems will be treated as a security incident and investigated under the **Incident Response Plan (IS-PLN-001)**.
