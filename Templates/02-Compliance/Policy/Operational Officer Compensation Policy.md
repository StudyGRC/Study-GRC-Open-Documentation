### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a transparent, compliant, and objective process for determining the compensation of Operational Officers and Key Employees of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy is designed to ensure that compensation arrangements are reasonable, fair, and necessary to attract and retain qualified leadership, while strictly prohibiting "excess benefit transactions" and private inurement in compliance with **Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 4958** and **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC)**.

This policy establishes the procedure to obtain the "Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness" under IRS regulations, protecting the Organization and its Directors from intermediate sanctions and excise taxes.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   **Operational Officers:** Including but not limited to the Executive Director (CEO), Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), and VP of Education & Training, as defined in **Bylaws Article V**.
*   **Key Employees:** Any employee meeting the IRS definition of a "Disqualified Person" or having substantial influence over the affairs of the Organization.
*   **Family Members:** Compensation arrangements for family members of Directors or Officers.

**Exclusions:**
*   General staff and volunteers whose compensation falls within pre-approved salary bands and does not trigger "Disqualified Person" status.
*   Reimbursement of reasonable business expenses (covered under *FIN-POL-040 Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Compensation** | All forms of remuneration, including salary, bonuses, deferred compensation, fringe benefits, liability insurance premiums, and any other financial benefit provided in exchange for services. |
| **Disqualified Person** | Any person who was in a position to exercise substantial influence over the affairs of the Organization at any time during a five-year lookback period (e.g., Voting Directors, CEO, CFO, and their family members). |
| **Excess Benefit Transaction** | A transaction in which an economic benefit is provided by a tax-exempt organization to a Disqualified Person that exceeds the value of the consideration (services) received by the organization. |
| **Rebuttable Presumption** | A legal standard where the IRS presumes a compensation arrangement is reasonable if the organization follows three specific steps: (1) Independent body approval, (2) Reliance on comparable data, and (3) Contemporaneous documentation. |
| **Independent Director** | A Director who is not the person being compensated, is not a family member of that person, and has no financial interest in the transaction. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Compensation Philosophy
In accordance with **Bylaws Article 1.4**, the Organization is authorized to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered. Compensation packages for Operational Officers must be:
1.  **Reasonable:** Not excessive when compared to similar roles in similar organizations.
2.  **Competitive:** Sufficient to attract and retain competency in the GRC (Governance, Risk, Compliance) and EdTech sectors.
3.  **Mission-Aligned:** Reflective of the Organization's status as a donor-funded 501(c)(3) non-profit.

#### 4.2 Authority and Independence
The Board of Directors (or a designated Compensation Committee composed exclusively of Independent Directors) has the sole authority to determine the compensation of the Executive Director (CEO) and other Operational Officers.
*   **Recusal:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX (Conflict of Interest)**, the individual whose compensation is being determined (and any family members) **must not** be present during the debate or vote on their compensation, except to answer questions regarding their value or requirements.
*   **No Vote:** The interested person is strictly prohibited from voting on their own compensation.

#### 4.3 Establishing the Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness
To comply with IRS Intermediate Sanctions regulations, the Board **must** follow these three steps before approving any compensation package for a Disqualified Person:

**4.3.1 Step 1: Review by Authorized Body**
The arrangement must be reviewed and approved by the Board or Committee, composed entirely of individuals who do not have a conflict of interest with respect to the arrangement.

**4.3.2 Step 2: Reliance on Comparable Data**
The Board must obtain and rely upon appropriate data as to comparability prior to making its decision. Acceptable data includes:
*   Compensation surveys for non-profits of similar size, budget, and geographic scope (including remote-first organizations).
*   IRS Form 990 filings of similarly situated organizations.
*   Written offers from similar institutions competing for the same person's services.
*   Independent compensation studies commissioned by the Board.

**4.3.3 Step 3: Contemporaneous Documentation**
The Board must adequately document the basis for its determination concurrently with making that determination. (See Section 4.5).

#### 4.4 Frequency of Review
*   **Initial Appointment:** Compensation is reviewed upon hiring or appointment.
*   **Annual Review:** The Board shall review Operational Officer compensation annually, typically in conjunction with the performance evaluation process defined in *HR-PRC-015 Executive Director Evaluation Process*.
*   **Contract Renewal:** Upon the renewal or modification of any employment contract.

#### 4.5 Documentation Requirements (Minutes)
The minutes of the meeting where compensation is approved must be prepared before the next Board meeting and include:
1.  The terms of the compensation and the date it was approved.
2.  The members of the Board who were present during the debate and those who voted on it.
3.  The comparability data obtained and relied upon and how the data was obtained.
4.  Any actions taken with respect to consideration of the transaction by anyone who is otherwise a member of the Board but who had a conflict of interest.
5.  If the Board determines that reasonable compensation is higher or lower than the range of comparable data, the minutes must record the basis for that specific determination.

#### 4.6 Prohibited Compensation Structures
*   **Revenue Sharing:** Compensation based on a percentage of net earnings is strictly prohibited, as this constitutes private inurement.
*   **Loans:** In accordance with **TBOC § 22.225**, the Organization shall not make a loan to a Director or Officer.
*   **Unapproved Bonuses:** Discretionary bonuses must be approved by the Board in advance based on specific performance metrics; retroactive "thank you" payments that exceed reasonable compensation are prohibited.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Acts as the authorized body to review and approve compensation. Ensures the "Rebuttable Presumption" steps are followed. |
| **Compensation Committee** | (If appointed) Conducts research, gathers comparability data, and makes recommendations to the full Board. |
| **Secretary** | Ensures minutes accurately reflect the discussion, the data relied upon, and the recusal of interested parties. |
| **Interested Person** | (e.g., CEO) Provides necessary information but recuses themselves from the debate and vote regarding their own pay. |
| **Treasurer** | Verifies that the approved compensation is financially sustainable within the Organization's budget. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in:
*   **For the Individual:** Immediate repayment of the "excess benefit" plus interest, and an IRS excise tax of 25% of the excess benefit (increasing to 200% if not corrected).
*   **For the Board:** Directors who knowingly approve an excess benefit transaction may be personally liable for an IRS excise tax of 10% of the excess benefit (up to $20,000 per transaction).
*   **For the Organization:** Revocation of 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status and potential legal action by the Texas Attorney General.

Violations of the recusal requirements will be handled under the *GOV-POL-005 Conflict of Interest Policy*.
