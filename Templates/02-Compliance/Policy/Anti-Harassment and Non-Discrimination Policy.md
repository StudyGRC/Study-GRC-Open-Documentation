### 1. Purpose
Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") is committed to providing a work and learning environment that is free from discrimination and harassment. The purpose of this policy is to define the Organization’s zero-tolerance stance on such behaviors, establish clear reporting mechanisms, and ensure compliance with federal laws (including Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964) and the **Texas Labor Code Chapter 21**. This policy ensures that all individuals are treated with respect and dignity, fostering the "community-driven ecosystem" described in our mission.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals associated with the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   **Internal:** Employees, Officers, Board Directors, and Interns.
*   **External:** Volunteers, Independent Contractors, Vendors, and Consultants.
*   **Community:** Voting Members (Supporting/Honorary) and Community Participants.

**Location:** This policy applies to all Organization-related settings, including:
*   Remote work environments (home offices).
*   Digital communication channels (Slack, Discord, Email, Zoom/Teams).
*   In-person events, conferences, and social gatherings sponsored by the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Discrimination** | Treating an individual or group less favorably based on a Protected Class characteristic regarding hiring, firing, pay, job assignments, promotions, layoff, training, fringe benefits, or any other term or condition of employment or membership. |
| **Harassment** | Unwelcome conduct that is based on a Protected Class that is severe or pervasive enough to create a work environment that a reasonable person would consider intimidating, hostile, or abusive. |
| **Sexual Harassment** | As defined by **Texas Labor Code § 21.141**, an unwelcome sexual advance, a request for a sexual favor, or any other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature. |
| **Protected Class** | Race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, or gender identity), national origin, age (40 or older), disability, genetic information, or veteran status. |
| **Retaliation** | Any adverse action taken against an individual because they filed a complaint, supported another's complaint, or participated in an investigation. |
| **Digital Harassment** | Harassment occurring via electronic means, including "doxing," "stream bombing," unwanted Direct Messages (DMs), or the posting of offensive content in community forums. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO)
Study GRC Inc. provides equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants for employment without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or genetics. This policy applies to all terms and conditions of employment and volunteer service.

#### 4.2 Prohibited Conduct: Discrimination
No Officer, Director, employee, or volunteer shall discriminate against any individual based on a Protected Class. Decisions regarding Membership (per Bylaws Article II) and Board Service (per Bylaws Article III) must be made solely on the basis of merit, qualifications, and the Organization's needs.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Conduct: Harassment
Harassment is strictly prohibited. Prohibited conduct includes, but is not limited to:
*   **Verbal:** Epithets, slurs, negative stereotyping, threatening, intimidating, or hostile acts that relate to a Protected Class.
*   **Non-Verbal:** Displaying or circulating (including via Slack/Discord) written or graphic material that denigrates or shows hostility toward an individual or group.
*   **Digital:** Cyberstalking, non-consensual recording of meetings, or "trolling" with discriminatory intent.

#### 4.4 Prohibited Conduct: Sexual Harassment
In accordance with Texas law, sexual harassment is illegal and violates this policy when:
1.  Submission to the conduct is made either explicitly or implicitly a term or condition of employment or membership (**Quid Pro Quo**);
2.  Submission to or rejection of the conduct is used as the basis for decisions affecting the individual; or
3.  The conduct has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with an individual’s performance or creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive environment (**Hostile Environment**).

**Examples include:** Unwanted sexual advances; demands for sexual favors; sexual jokes and innuendo; offensive comments about a person's body; or the display of sexually explicit content in the Organization's digital workspaces.

#### 4.5 Reporting Procedure
The Organization encourages the reporting of all perceived incidents of discrimination, harassment, or retaliation, regardless of the offender's identity or position.

1.  **Immediate Objection (Optional):** If comfortable, the individual should politely but firmly advise the offender that the behavior is unwelcome and request that it stop.
2.  **Formal Reporting:** If the individual is uncomfortable addressing the offender, or if the behavior continues, they must report the conduct immediately to:
    *   Their immediate supervisor;
    *   The **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** (for Community/Volunteer issues);
    *   The **Executive Director (CEO)**; or
    *   The **Board President** (specifically if the complaint involves the Executive Director).
3.  **Form of Report:** Reports may be oral or written. Written reports are encouraged to ensure accuracy.

#### 4.6 Investigation Protocol
Upon receipt of a complaint, the Organization will undertake a prompt, thorough, and impartial investigation.
*   **Confidentiality:** Confidentiality will be maintained to the extent possible, consistent with the need to conduct an adequate investigation and take corrective action.
*   **Interim Measures:** The Organization may take interim measures (e.g., temporary suspension of access to Slack/Systems) to protect the complainant during the investigation.
*   **Determination:** The investigation will conclude with a determination of whether a violation of this policy occurred.

#### 4.7 Retaliation
Retaliation against an individual for reporting harassment or discrimination or for participating in an investigation is a serious violation of this policy and, like harassment or discrimination itself, will be subject to disciplinary action.

#### 4.8 False Accusations
The Organization recognizes that false accusations of harassment and discrimination can have serious effects on innocent individuals. If an investigation results in a finding that a person who has accused another of harassment or discrimination has acted maliciously or has knowingly made false accusations, the accuser will be subject to disciplinary action.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Personnel & Members** | Conduct themselves in a professional manner; refrain from harassment/discrimination; report observed violations immediately. |
| **Managers & Officers** | Maintain a discrimination-free environment; **Mandatory Duty to Act**: Must report any complaint or observation of harassment to the Board or CEO immediately. Failure to report is a disciplinary offense. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Serve as a primary intake point for community-level disputes; escalate policy violations to the Executive Director/Board. |
| **Board of Directors** | Oversee investigations involving Executive Officers; ensure "immediate and appropriate corrective action" is taken per Texas Labor Code § 21.141. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including:
*   **Employees:** Termination of employment.
*   **Volunteers/Contractors:** Immediate termination of service agreement.
*   **Members:** Expulsion from the Organization pursuant to the due process outlined in the Membership Policy Manual.
*   **Directors:** Removal for Cause pursuant to **Bylaws Section 3.6**.

**Legal Liability:** Under Texas law, individuals (including supervisors and co-workers) may be held *personally liable* for damages if they engage in sexual harassment or fail to take immediate and appropriate corrective action when they know or should have known of the harassment.
