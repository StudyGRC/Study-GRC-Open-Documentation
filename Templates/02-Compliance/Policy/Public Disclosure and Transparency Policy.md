### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") complies with Internal Revenue Service (IRS) regulations regarding the public inspection of tax-exempt organization returns and applications. Furthermore, this policy operationalizes the commitment to transparency mandated in **Article VIII, Section 8.2** of the Bylaws, fostering trust with donors, members, and the general public by making governance and financial information readily accessible.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Records:** The Organization’s Application for Tax Exemption (Form 1023), Annual Information Returns (Form 990 series), and Annual Financial Reports.
*   **Personnel:** All Directors, Officers, employees, and volunteers responsible for financial reporting, record retention, and public communications.
*   **Exclusions:** This policy explicitly excludes the disclosure of donor names and addresses (Schedule B) to the general public, except where required by law, and protects the Personally Identifiable Information (PII) of beneficiaries, particularly minors participating in K-12 programs.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Form 1023** | The Application for Recognition of Exemption filed with the IRS, including all supporting documents and the IRS Determination Letter. |
| **Form 990** | The annual information return filed with the IRS (e.g., Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N). |
| **Public Inspection Copy** | A version of the Form 990 that has been redacted to remove information not required to be disclosed to the public (specifically, the names and addresses of contributors). |
| **Widely Available** | Posting documents on the Organization’s website in a format that can be viewed, downloaded, and printed (e.g., PDF) without a fee or login requirement. |
| **Schedule B** | The schedule attached to Form 990 listing contributors. While filed with the IRS, the names and addresses on this schedule are generally confidential for 501(c)(3) public charities. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Commitment to Transparency
In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII**, the Organization recognizes that transparency is essential to maintaining public trust. The Organization shall make its governing documents, conflict of interest policy, and financial statements available to the public upon request and via its website.

#### 4.2 Federally Mandated Disclosures (IRC § 6104)
The Organization must make the following documents available for public inspection:
1.  **Application for Exemption:** A copy of the approved Form 1023, all supporting papers filed with the IRS, and the IRS Determination Letter. This obligation exists for the life of the Organization.
2.  **Annual Information Returns:** Copies of the Form 990 (or 990-EZ) for the three (3) most recent tax years. This includes all schedules and attachments, with the exception of the names and addresses of contributors on Schedule B.

#### 4.3 Redaction of Sensitive Information
To protect donor privacy and comply with data protection standards:
*   **Schedule B Redaction:** The Treasurer or Chief Financial Officer (CFO) must prepare a "Public Inspection Copy" of the Form 990. This copy must redact the names and addresses of contributors listed on Schedule B. The amounts of contributions and the nature of non-cash contributions shall remain visible.
*   **Youth Protection:** In alignment with the Organization's *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, any incidental references to specific minor beneficiaries in narrative sections of the Form 990 or Annual Report must be anonymized prior to public release.

#### 4.4 Methods of Disclosure

**4.4.1 "Widely Available" (Website Posting)**
To satisfy the IRS requirement without the burden of fulfilling individual copy requests, the Organization shall post the Public Inspection Copies of the documents listed in Section 4.2 on a dedicated "Transparency" page on its official website.
*   Documents must be in a standard format (e.g., PDF).
*   Documents must be accessible without a password or fee.
*   The website must advise that these documents are available for download.

**4.4.2 Written Requests**
If the Organization receives a written request (email or mail) for a copy of the documents:
*   If the documents are posted online (Section 4.4.1), the Organization may respond by providing the specific URL where the documents can be found.
*   If the documents are *not* posted online, the Organization must provide a copy within 30 days. The Organization may charge a reasonable fee for copying and postage, not exceeding the IRS per-page rate.

**4.4.3 In-Person Requests**
As a remote-first organization, Study GRC Inc. does not maintain a permanent physical office open to the public. However, if a registered office is maintained where operations are conducted, requests made in person must be fulfilled immediately (same day) unless the documents are posted online.

#### 4.5 Harassment Campaign Exception
The Organization is not required to comply with requests for copies if the Board determines, based on IRS regulations, that the request is part of a harassment campaign. Such determinations must be documented by legal counsel and the Board Secretary.

#### 4.6 Annual Financial Report to Members
Pursuant to **Bylaws Section 8.2** and **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.352**, the Board shall annually prepare a financial report for the preceding year. This report shall be made available to Voting Members and the public alongside the Form 990.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensures the Organization remains compliant with federal and state disclosure laws. Reviews and approves the Form 990 prior to filing. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Prepares the Form 990 and the "Public Inspection Copy" (redacted Schedule B). Ensures financial data is accurate. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains the permanent file of the Form 1023 and Determination Letter. Oversees the response to written public information requests. |
| **Webmaster / IT** | Ensures the "Transparency" page of the website is up-to-date, accessible, and contains the correct versions of documents. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Advises on redaction requirements and determines if specific requests constitute a harassment campaign. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the Organization at significant legal and financial risk, including IRS penalties for each day a failure to disclose continues (IRC § 6652(c)).

*   **Internal Discipline:** Employees or Officers who willfully withhold public documents in violation of this policy or who improperly disclose confidential donor information (unredacted Schedule B) to the public are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination and removal from office.
*   **Legal Penalties:** The IRS may impose personal penalties on responsible persons who refuse to allow public inspection.
