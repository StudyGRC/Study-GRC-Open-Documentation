### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to formally establish the position, authority, and responsibilities of the Data Protection Officer (DPO) within Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). This policy ensures the Organization complies with Article 37 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), and the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) by designating an independent expert to oversee data privacy strategy and implementation.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the Organization’s Board of Directors, Operational Officers, employees, volunteers, and third-party contractors. It governs the selection, appointment, and operational independence of the DPO.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not cover the specific technical implementation of security controls (managed by the Chief Information Security Officer), except where those controls impact regulatory privacy compliance.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | An enterprise security leadership role required by the GDPR responsible for overseeing a company's data protection strategy and its implementation to ensure compliance with GDPR requirements. |
| **Supervisory Authority** | An independent public authority established by a member state pursuant to Article 51 of the GDPR (e.g., CNIL, ICO) or domestic regulators (e.g., Texas Attorney General, FTC). |
| **Conflict of Interest** | In the context of a DPO, a situation where the individual holds a role that determines the *purposes* and *means* of processing personal data (e.g., CEO, Head of Marketing, Head of HR), rendering them unable to monitor compliance independently. |
| **Data Subject** | An identified or identifiable natural person (e.g., a member, student, or donor). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Mandatory Designation
In accordance with GDPR Article 37(1) and best practices for non-profits processing data of minors (K-12 students), the Organization must designate a Data Protection Officer. This designation is mandatory regardless of whether the Organization meets the strict "large scale" processing thresholds, to demonstrate a commitment to the highest standards of privacy governance.

#### 4.2 Professional Qualifications
The DPO shall be designated on the basis of professional qualities and, in particular, expert knowledge of data protection law and practices. The DPO must possess:
*   In-depth understanding of GDPR, TDPSA, COPPA, and relevant non-profit regulations.
*   Understanding of the Organization’s technical infrastructure and data processing operations.
*   The ability to fulfill the tasks referred to in Section 4.4.

#### 4.3 Position and Independence
To ensure the integrity of the privacy function, the Organization adheres to the requirements of GDPR Article 38:
*   **Reporting Line:** The DPO shall report directly to the highest management level of the Organization (the Board of Directors or the Executive Director).
*   **No Instructions:** The Organization shall not provide instructions to the DPO regarding the exercise of their tasks. The DPO shall not be dismissed or penalized for performing their tasks.
*   **Access:** The DPO must be involved, properly and in a timely manner, in all issues which relate to the protection of personal data.

#### 4.4 Tasks and Responsibilities
Pursuant to GDPR Article 39, the DPO is entrusted with the following tasks:
1.  **Inform and Advise:** Educate the Board, Operational Officers (specifically the CISO and VP of Education), and employees about their obligations under data protection laws.
2.  **Monitor Compliance:** Audit compliance with privacy laws and the Organization's policies, including the assignment of responsibilities, awareness-raising, and training of staff involved in processing operations.
3.  **Advisory on DPIAs:** Provide advice where requested as regards the Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) and monitor its performance pursuant to Article 35.
4.  **Cooperation:** Act as the contact point for the supervisory authority on issues relating to processing.
5.  **Point of Contact:** Serve as the primary contact point for Data Subjects (members/users) regarding privacy concerns and the exercise of their rights (e.g., DSARs).

#### 4.5 Conflict of Interest and Dual Roles
The DPO may fulfill other tasks and duties provided that any such tasks do not result in a conflict of interest.
*   **Prohibited Roles:** The DPO cannot hold a position that defines the *purposes* and *means* of processing personal data. Therefore, the DPO **cannot** simultaneously serve as the Executive Director (CEO), Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Head of Marketing, or Head of Human Resources.
*   **Permissible Roles:** The DPO may be the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) *only if* the CISO role does not include final decision-making authority over business goals for data usage.
*   **External DPO:** The Organization may appoint an external contractor or agency to fulfill the DPO role ("DPO-as-a-Service"), provided a specific individual is named as the lead contact.

#### 4.6 Term and Removal
*   **Appointment:** The DPO is appointed by the Board of Directors upon recommendation by the Executive Director.
*   **Removal:** The DPO may be removed by the Board of Directors only if they no longer fulfill the conditions required for the performance of their duties, or for gross misconduct. They may not be removed solely for providing advice that the Organization disagrees with.

#### 4.7 Publication of Contact Details
The Organization must publish the contact details of the DPO (e.g., `privacy@studygrc.org`) on the Organization’s website and communicate them to the relevant supervisory authorities.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Appoints the DPO; ensures the DPO reports to the highest management level; reviews DPO annual reports. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures the DPO has the necessary resources (budget, staff, access) to maintain expert knowledge and perform duties. |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Executes the tasks defined in Section 4.4; maintains independence; reports compliance risks to the Board. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Collaborates with the DPO to implement technical controls that satisfy privacy requirements; consults DPO on security incidents. |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Must consult the DPO when planning new projects involving personal data (Privacy by Design). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to appoint a qualified DPO or failure to involve the DPO in data protection matters may result in regulatory fines (up to 4% of global turnover under GDPR) and reputational damage.

Internal failure to cooperate with the DPO or obstruction of the DPO's duties by any employee or officer may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
