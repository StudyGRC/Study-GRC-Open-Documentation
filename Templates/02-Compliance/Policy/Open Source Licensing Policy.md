### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to operationalize the charitable mission of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") as defined in **Article I, Section 1.3** of the Bylaws: to remove financial barriers to knowledge by providing zero-cost resources. This policy establishes the standards for licensing the Organization’s intellectual property (IP) to the public, ensuring that educational materials and software produced by the Organization remain free, accessible, and legally shareable, while protecting the Organization's trademarks and liability.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers.
*   **Assets:** All "Work Product" created for the Organization, including but not limited to educational curriculum, slide decks, white papers, software code, scripts, website content, and multimedia.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Confidential Information:** Internal operational documents (e.g., financial reports, personnel files, security configurations) are **not** open source and remain classified as Internal or Confidential.
*   **Trademarks:** The Organization’s name, logos, and brand identity assets are **not** open source and may not be used without express permission, except to attribute the source of the material.
*   **Third-Party Assets:** Content owned by others (e.g., stock photos, guest lectures) licensed to the Organization under restrictive terms.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Open Source** | A decentralized software development model that encourages open collaboration, meaning the source code or content is available to the public for use and/or modification. |
| **Creative Commons (CC)** | A global nonprofit organization that provides free legal tools (licenses) to enable the sharing and reuse of creativity and knowledge. |
| **CC BY 4.0** | Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License. Allows users to distribute, remix, adapt, and build upon the material in any medium or format, so long as attribution is given to the creator. |
| **MIT License** | A permissive free software license that grants broad permissions to use, copy, modify, merge, publish, distribute, sublicense, and/or sell copies of the software. |
| **Work Product** | As defined in **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, any content, code, or materials created by leaders, volunteers, or contractors during their service to the Organization. |
| **Contributor** | Any individual or entity that creates or modifies Work Product for the Organization. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Ownership and Assignment
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, the Organization retains initial copyright ownership of all Work Product created by volunteers and employees in the course of their duties. This centralization of ownership is necessary to legally grant open licenses to the public and defend the open nature of the ecosystem.
*   **4.1.1** All contributors must execute a Volunteer Service Agreement or Employment Contract containing a "Work Made for Hire" or IP Assignment clause prior to contributing.

#### 4.2 Default Outbound Licensing Standards
To maximize impact and minimize barriers, the Organization applies the following default licenses to its public outputs:

*   **4.2.1 Educational Content & Documentation:** All text, curriculum, images, audio, and video produced by the Organization must be licensed under the **Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0)** license.
    *   *Requirement:* Users must give appropriate credit, provide a link to the license, and indicate if changes were made.
*   **4.2.2 Software & Code:** All software, scripts, tools, and configuration files (e.g., Terraform, Ansible) produced by the Organization must be licensed under the **MIT License**.
    *   *Requirement:* The license notice must be preserved in the source code.
*   **4.2.3 Data Sets:** Any public data sets released for research purposes must be licensed under **CC0 1.0 Universal (Public Domain Dedication)** to facilitate unrestricted analysis, unless privacy concerns dictate otherwise.

#### 4.3 Licensing Exceptions
Deviations from the default licenses (e.g., using CC BY-SA or GPL) require prior written approval from the **VP of Education & Training** or the **Board of Directors**.
*   **4.3.1** Exceptions may be granted if a specific project requires compatibility with an existing upstream open-source project that uses a "copyleft" license (e.g., GPLv3).

#### 4.4 Inbound Contributions and Third-Party Material
The Organization must respect the intellectual property rights of others.
*   **4.4.1 Compatibility Check:** Before incorporating third-party open-source material, the contributor must verify that the third-party license is compatible with the Organization’s default license (e.g., one cannot include GPL code in an MIT project without changing the project license).
*   **4.4.2 Prohibited Content:** Personnel must **not** copy, paste, or commit proprietary, pirated, or unlicensed material into the Organization’s repositories or curriculum.
*   **4.4.3 Attribution:** All third-party assets used under fair use or open licenses must be clearly attributed in a "Credits" section or `NOTICE` file.

#### 4.5 Repository Management
*   **4.5.1 License File:** Every public code repository (e.g., GitHub) must contain a root-level file named `LICENSE` containing the full text of the applicable license (MIT).
*   **4.5.2 Content Footer:** All PDF, slide, or web-based educational resources must include a footer or end-slide stating:
    > *"© [Year] Study GRC Inc. Licensed under CC BY 4.0. You are free to share and adapt this work with attribution."*

#### 4.6 Trademark Protection
While the content is open, the brand is not.
*   **4.6.1** The Organization’s logos and the name "Study GRC" may not be used in a way that suggests endorsement of a derivative work.
*   **4.6.2** Derivative works created by the community must remove the Organization’s branding unless explicit permission is granted.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the overall licensing strategy and any fundamental changes to IP policy. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all curriculum and educational materials bear the correct CC BY 4.0 notices before publication. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Ensures all public code repositories contain the correct LICENSE files and do not contain secrets/credentials. |
| **Contributors (Volunteers/Staff)** | Ensure they have the legal right to contribute content and sign the IP Assignment/Volunteer Agreement. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Advises on license compatibility and handles any DMCA notices or infringement claims. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Unlicensed Contributions:** Contributions found to violate third-party IP rights will be immediately removed from the Organization’s platforms.
*   **DMCA Compliance:** The Organization complies with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA). Upon receipt of a valid takedown notice, the Organization will remove infringing content in accordance with the **DMCA Infringement Takedown Procedure**.
