### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the guidelines and procedures for employee leaves of absence at Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). This policy ensures the Organization complies with the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA), the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA), and applicable Texas state laws, while balancing the operational needs of the Organization with the health and well-being of its workforce.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees of the Organization.
*   **Inclusions:** Operational Officers (as defined in Bylaws Article V) and regular staff.
*   **Exclusions:** Independent contractors (1099), temporary agency workers, and volunteers are not eligible for leaves of absence under this policy, though they may request schedule adjustments via their primary point of contact.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **FMLA** | The Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993, a federal law providing eligible employees with job-protected, unpaid leave for qualified medical and family reasons. |
| **Serious Health Condition** | An illness, injury, impairment, or physical or mental condition that involves inpatient care or continuing treatment by a health care provider. |
| **Intermittent Leave** | Leave taken in separate blocks of time due to a single qualifying reason, rather than one continuous period. |
| **Key Employee** | A salaried, FMLA-eligible employee who is among the highest-paid 10% of all the employees employed by the employer within 75 miles of the employee’s worksite. |
| **Rolling 12-Month Period** | The method used to calculate FMLA eligibility, measured backward from the date an employee uses any FMLA leave. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

**4.1.1 Eligibility**
To be eligible for FMLA leave, an employee must:
1.  Have worked for the Organization for at least 12 months (need not be consecutive);
2.  Have worked at least 1,250 hours during the 12 months prior to the start of the leave; and
3.  Work at a location where at least 50 employees are employed by the Organization within a 75-mile radius.
    *   *Note on Remote Work:* For remote employees, the "worksite" is the office to which they report or from which their work is assigned.

**4.1.2 Leave Entitlement**
Eligible employees may take up to **12 workweeks** of unpaid, job-protected leave in a 12-month period for:
*   The birth of a child and to bond with the newborn child within one year of birth.
*   The placement with the employee of a child for adoption or foster care and to bond with the newly placed child within one year of placement.
*   A serious health condition that makes the employee unable to perform the functions of their job.
*   To care for the employee’s spouse, son, daughter, or parent who has a serious health condition.
*   Any qualifying exigency arising out of the fact that the employee’s spouse, son, daughter, or parent is a military member on covered active duty.

**4.1.3 Military Caregiver Leave**
Eligible employees may take up to **26 workweeks** of leave in a single 12-month period to care for a covered service member with a serious injury or illness if the eligible employee is the service member’s spouse, son, daughter, parent, or next of kin.

**4.1.4 Intermittent Leave**
When medically necessary, employees may take FMLA leave intermittently or on a reduced schedule. Employees must make reasonable efforts to schedule leave for planned medical treatment so as not to unduly disrupt the Organization’s operations.

**4.1.5 Benefits and Job Restoration**
*   **Health Insurance:** During FMLA leave, the Organization will maintain the employee’s health coverage under any "group health plan" on the same terms as if the employee had continued to work. The employee must continue to pay their share of premiums.
*   **Restoration:** Upon return from FMLA leave, most employees must be restored to their original or equivalent positions with equivalent pay, benefits, and other employment terms.
*   **Key Employee Exception:** The Organization may deny restoration to a "Key Employee" if necessary to prevent substantial and grievous economic injury to operations.

#### 4.2 ADA and Medical Leave (Non-FMLA)
Employees who are not eligible for FMLA, or who have exhausted their FMLA entitlement, may be eligible for leave as a reasonable accommodation under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).
*   Requests for leave as an accommodation will be evaluated via the **ADA Interactive Process Procedure (HR-PRC-018)**.
*   Leave under the ADA is not guaranteed and depends on whether granting the leave would cause an undue hardship to the Organization.

#### 4.3 Military Leave (USERRA)
The Organization complies with USERRA. Employees who serve in the uniformed services are entitled to unpaid military leave of absence.
*   Advance notice is required unless military necessity prevents it.
*   Employees may elect to use accrued paid time off (PTO) during this leave, but are not required to do so.
*   Reemployment rights are guaranteed for cumulative service of up to five (5) years, provided the employee was not dishonorably discharged and reports back to work within statutory timeframes.

#### 4.4 Jury Duty and Witness Duty
In accordance with Texas law:
*   Employees summoned for jury duty or subpoenaed as a witness will be granted unpaid leave.
*   Employees may use accrued PTO to cover this time.
*   Employees must provide a copy of the summons to their manager as soon as possible.
*   The Organization prohibits any adverse employment action against an employee for serving on a jury.

#### 4.5 Voting Leave
In accordance with Texas Election Code, employees are allowed paid time off to vote on election days unless the polls are open for two (2) consecutive hours outside of the employee's scheduled work shift.

#### 4.6 Bereavement Leave
Full-time employees are eligible for up to three (3) days of paid bereavement leave in the event of the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling, grandparent, or in-law). Additional unpaid time may be requested and granted at the discretion of the Executive Director.

#### 4.7 Personal Leave of Absence
Requests for personal leave not covered by the above categories (e.g., sabbatical, extended education) are discretionary.
*   Must be approved by the Executive Director.
*   Generally unpaid.
*   Job protection is not guaranteed for personal leave unless explicitly stated in a written agreement.

#### 4.8 Pay Status and Concurrent Leave
*   **Unpaid Status:** Unless otherwise specified, leaves of absence are unpaid.
*   **Substitution of Paid Leave:** Employees may choose, or the Organization may require employees, to use accrued PTO concurrently with unpaid FMLA or other leaves.
*   **Short-Term Disability (STD):** If the employee is eligible for STD benefits, FMLA leave runs concurrently with the disability period.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | 1. Provide at least 30 days' advance notice for foreseeable leave.<br>2. Provide medical certification within 15 calendar days of the request.<br>3. Maintain communication with HR regarding return-to-work status.<br>4. Arrange for payment of benefit premiums during unpaid leave. |
| **Manager** | 1. Notify HR/Operations immediately upon receiving a leave request or knowledge of a qualifying event.<br>2. Do not ask for specific medical diagnoses; focus on duration and restrictions.<br>3. Ensure no retaliation occurs against employees requesting leave.<br>4. Reallocate workload during the employee's absence. |
| **HR / Operations** | 1. Determine eligibility and provide required notices (Rights & Responsibilities, Designation Notice) within 5 business days.<br>2. Administer medical certifications and fitness-for-duty reports.<br>3. Track leave usage against entitlements.<br>4. Coordinate with the CISO regarding access suspension/equipment return for extended leaves (>30 days). |
| **Executive Director** | 1. Review and approve discretionary personal leave requests.<br>2. Review "Key Employee" determinations and "Undue Hardship" analyses. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Anti-Retaliation:** The Organization strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee for requesting leave, taking leave, or exercising their rights under FMLA, ADA, or USERRA.
*   **Fraud:** An employee who fraudulently obtains leave (e.g., working another job while on medical leave without prior approval) is subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Failure to Return:** An employee who fails to return to work upon the expiration of approved leave will be considered to have voluntarily resigned, unless an extension has been granted prior to the expiration date.
