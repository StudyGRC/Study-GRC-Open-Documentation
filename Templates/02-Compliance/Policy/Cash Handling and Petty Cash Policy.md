### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict internal controls regarding the handling, processing, and depositing of physical funds (currency, coins, and checks) and the management of Petty Cash funds. This policy is designed to prevent fraud, theft, and error, ensuring the financial integrity of Study GRC Inc. in compliance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and federal internal control standards (2 CFR § 200.303).

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, Board Directors, officers, and volunteers of Study GRC Inc. who handle, transport, or oversee physical funds on behalf of the Organization.

**Inclusions:**
*   Cash receipts from fundraising events, merchandise sales, or donations.
*   Physical checks received via mail or in person.
*   Temporary cash banks (floats) used for events.

**Exclusions:**
*   Electronic funds transfers (ACH, Wire).
*   Credit card processing (covered under *FIN-POL-042 Corporate Credit Card User Agreement*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cash** | Currency (bills), coins, checks, money orders, and cashier's checks. |
| **Custodian** | The specific individual authorized to hold physical possession of and responsibility for a cash fund. |
| **Commingling** | The prohibited practice of mixing personal funds with Organization funds. |
| **Segregation of Duties** | The internal control principle that separates the functions of authorization, custody, and record-keeping to prevent fraud. |
| **Two-Person Rule** | A mandatory procedure requiring two authorized individuals to be present during the counting and transfer of cash. |
| **Imprest System** | A method where a fixed amount of cash is maintained; the fund is replenished only for the exact amount of receipts submitted. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles
4.1.1 **Fiduciary Duty:** All funds received are the property of Study GRC Inc. and must be handled with the highest degree of care and integrity.
4.1.2 **No Commingling:** Organization funds must never be mixed with the personal funds of any employee, volunteer, or officer.
4.1.3 **Transparency:** All cash transactions must be fully documented. "Off-the-books" transactions are strictly prohibited and constitute grounds for immediate termination and legal action.

#### 4.2 Segregation of Duties
To minimize the risk of fraud, the following duties must be separated:
4.2.1 The individual who receives/collects cash must not be the same individual who records the transaction in the accounting ledger.
4.2.2 The individual who reconciles the bank statement must not have physical access to cash deposits.

#### 4.3 Cash Receipts (Inflow)
4.3.1 **Immediate Endorsement:** All checks received must be immediately restrictively endorsed with a stamp or handwriting stating: *"For Deposit Only - Study GRC Inc."*
4.3.2 **Receipt Issuance:** A pre-numbered receipt or electronic equivalent must be issued for all cash donations or payments exceeding $5.00.
4.3.3 **Mail Processing:** Checks received via mail at the Registered Office must be logged in a "Check Register" by the opener before being handed to the Treasurer or CFO for deposit.

#### 4.4 Event Cash Handling
Given the remote-first nature of the Organization, cash handling primarily occurs during physical events.
4.4.1 **Two-Person Rule:** Cash counting at events must always be performed by two unrelated individuals simultaneously. Both must sign the *Cash Count Sheet*.
4.4.2 **Secure Storage:** During events, cash must be kept in a locked cash box or safe. The key/combination must be held by the designated Event Treasurer.
4.4.3 **Transport:** Cash exceeding $1,000 must be transported to the bank or a secure safe by two individuals.

#### 4.5 Petty Cash and Change Funds
Due to the Organization's remote operational model, permanent Petty Cash funds are generally prohibited.
4.5.1 **Temporary Floats:** The CFO may authorize temporary "Change Funds" (floats) for specific events.
4.5.2 **Reconciliation:** Temporary funds must be reconciled and re-deposited into the Organization’s bank account within 48 hours of the event's conclusion.
4.5.3 **Prohibited Uses:** Petty cash/Change funds may **never** be used for:
*   Cashing personal checks (IOUs).
*   Personal loans or salary advances.
*   Payment of wages or services (to avoid tax evasion/1099 non-compliance).
*   Reimbursement of travel expenses (must go through *FIN-POL-037 Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy*).

#### 4.6 Deposits
4.6.1 **Timeliness:** All funds must be deposited into the Organization’s corporate bank account within three (3) business days of receipt. If the total cash on hand exceeds $500, the deposit must be made within 24 hours (or the next business day).
4.6.2 **Verification:** Deposit slips must be matched against the *Cash Count Sheet* and the General Ledger by the CFO or Treasurer.

#### 4.7 Overages and Shortages
4.7.1 **Reporting:** Any discrepancy (overage or shortage) between the cash on hand and the documentation must be reported immediately to the CFO.
4.7.2 **Documentation:** Discrepancies must be recorded in the General Ledger (e.g., "Cash Over/Short" expense account).
4.7.3 **Prohibition on "Making Good":** Employees/volunteers are prohibited from using personal funds to make up a shortage to hide the error, as this obscures potential process failures or theft patterns.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board Treasurer** | Provides oversight of financial policies; reviews significant cash discrepancies; ensures alignment with Bylaws Article IV. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Owns this policy; authorizes temporary cash funds; reviews monthly bank reconciliations; investigates shortages. |
| **Cash Custodian** | The designated individual (usually per event) responsible for the physical security of the cash box; strictly liable for the fund during their shift. |
| **Bookkeeper** | Records deposits in the accounting system; ensures they match bank records; alerts CFO of discrepancies. |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Adhere to the Two-Person Rule; report suspicious activity regarding funds immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the Organization at significant risk of financial loss and regulatory non-compliance.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Legal Action:** Theft, fraud, or intentional misappropriation of funds will result in immediate termination, referral to law enforcement, and civil action to recover lost funds.
*   **Whistleblower Protection:** Individuals reporting suspected cash handling fraud are protected under *GOV-POL-009 Whistleblower Protection Policy*.
