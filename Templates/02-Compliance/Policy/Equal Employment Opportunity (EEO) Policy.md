### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish Study GRC Inc.'s (the "Organization") absolute commitment to providing equal employment opportunities to all employees, volunteers, and applicants. This policy ensures compliance with Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, the Texas Labor Code Chapter 21, and other applicable federal and state laws. It serves to foster a diverse, inclusive, and merit-based culture where all individuals are treated with dignity and respect, free from unlawful discrimination.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all aspects of the employment relationship and volunteer engagement within the Organization.

*   **Applies to:**
    *   All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary).
    *   **Volunteers** and interns (unpaid staff).
    *   Members of the **Board of Directors** and Advisory Board.
    *   Independent contractors (to the extent required by law and contract).
    *   Job applicants and candidates for volunteer positions.
*   **Locations:** This policy applies to all Organization activities, including remote work environments (home offices), virtual meetings (Zoom/Teams), Slack/Discord channels, and in-person events.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Protected Characteristic** | Personal traits protected by law, including race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), national origin, age (40 or older), disability, genetic information, or veteran status. |
| **Discrimination** | Treating an individual or group less favorably regarding employment terms or conditions based on a Protected Characteristic. |
| **Adverse Employment Action** | A significant change in employment status, such as hiring, firing, failing to promote, reassignment with significantly different responsibilities, or a decision causing a significant change in benefits. |
| **Retaliation** | Punishing or taking Adverse Employment Action against an individual for engaging in "protected activity," such as filing a complaint of discrimination or participating in an investigation. |
| **Reasonable Accommodation** | A change to the work environment or the way things are customarily done that enables an individual with a disability or religious requirement to enjoy equal employment opportunities. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Commitment
Study GRC Inc. recruits, hires, trains, promotes, and compensates individuals based on merit, qualifications, and abilities. The Organization does not discriminate against any person because of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other characteristic protected by law.

#### 4.2 Protected Classes and Inclusivity
In accordance with federal and Texas state law, the Organization strictly prohibits discrimination based on:
*   **Race or Color:** Including personal characteristics associated with race (e.g., hair texture, skin color, or facial features).
*   **Religion:** Including religious beliefs, observances, and practices.
*   **Sex:** Including pregnancy, childbirth, breastfeeding, sexual orientation, and gender identity (per *Bostock v. Clayton County*).
*   **National Origin:** Including ethnicity, accent, or language.
*   **Age:** Specifically protecting individuals aged 40 and older.
*   **Disability:** Physical or mental impairments that substantially limit major life activities.
*   **Genetic Information:** Including family medical history.
*   **Military Status:** Past, current, or future military service.

#### 4.3 Scope of Employment Practices
This policy governs all aspects of employment and volunteerism, including but not limited to:
*   Recruitment, advertising, and job application procedures.
*   Hiring, placement, transfer, and promotion.
*   Compensation, payroll, and benefits administration.
*   Professional development, training, and conference attendance.
*   Disciplinary actions, performance evaluations, and termination.
*   Social and recreational programs sponsored by the Organization.

#### 4.4 Reasonable Accommodation
The Organization is committed to complying with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and the Texas Commission on Human Rights Act.
*   **Disability:** We will provide reasonable accommodations to qualified individuals with known disabilities to enable them to perform the essential functions of their job, unless doing so would impose an undue hardship on the operation of the business.
*   **Religion:** We will provide reasonable accommodations for religious practices and beliefs, such as flexible scheduling for religious observances, unless doing so creates an undue hardship.
*   **Process:** Requests for accommodation should be submitted in writing to the Executive Director or the designated HR representative using the *Reasonable Accommodation Request Form*.

#### 4.5 Prohibition of Retaliation
The Organization strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who:
*   Files a complaint of discrimination.
*   Participates in an investigation or proceeding concerning discrimination.
*   Opposes any practice made unlawful by employment discrimination laws.

Retaliation is a serious violation of this policy and will result in disciplinary action, independent of the outcome of the underlying discrimination complaint.

#### 4.6 Governance and Leadership Accountability
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.3**, Directors must adhere to Professional Ethics. A violation of this EEO Policy by a Director constitutes "cause" for removal under **Bylaws Section 3.6**. Similarly, Operational Officers (as defined in **Bylaws Article V**) are held to the highest standard of conduct regarding EEO compliance.

#### 4.7 Third-Party Conduct
The Organization strives to protect its employees and volunteers from discrimination by third parties, including vendors, donors, sponsors, and community participants. If an employee or volunteer experiences discrimination by a non-employee during Organization-related activities, they must report it immediately.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ultimate oversight of EEO compliance; reviewing and approving this policy; handling complaints involving the Executive Director. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Operational implementation of this policy; ensuring all hiring managers are trained; acting as the primary point of contact for accommodation requests. |
| **Managers / Team Leads** | Ensuring their teams operate free from discrimination; immediately reporting any observed or reported violations to the Executive Director. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Treating colleagues with respect; refraining from discrimination; reporting violations immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Any employee, volunteer, or officer found to have engaged in conduct violating this policy will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (per Bylaws Art. III), or severance of the volunteer relationship.

**Reporting Procedure:**
1.  Individuals who believe they have been subjected to discrimination should report the incident to the Executive Director.
2.  If the complaint involves the Executive Director, the report should be made directly to the President of the Board of Directors.
3.  All complaints will be investigated promptly, impartially, and as confidentially as possible.
