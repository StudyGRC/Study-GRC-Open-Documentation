### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to mandate transparency regarding the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within Study GRC Inc. As an organization dedicated to "advancing governance, risk, and compliance knowledge" (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3), we must model ethical leadership. This policy ensures that our members, learners, and the public are never deceived regarding the origin of the content, code, or communications they encounter. It establishes the "Right to Know" whether an interaction is human-driven or machine-generated, thereby maintaining consumer trust and mitigating risks associated with bias, hallucination, and manipulation.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all "Work Product" and communications created, published, or managed by Study GRC Inc.

*   **Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors (collectively "Contributors").
*   **Applies to:** All external-facing content, including educational materials, software code, marketing copy, social media posts, images, video/audio, and customer support interactions.
*   **Exclusions:** Internal-only use of AI for spelling/grammar checking (e.g., Grammarly), basic data sorting, or spam filtering, provided these tools do not substantively alter the meaning or generate net-new content.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Generative AI (GenAI)** | Technology that can create new content (text, images, audio, code) in response to prompts (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney, GitHub Copilot). |
| **AI-Generated Content** | Content where the primary creative effort or output was produced by an AI system with minimal human editing. |
| **AI-Assisted Content** | Content created primarily by a human who used AI as a tool for brainstorming, outlining, or refining, but where the human exercised full editorial control and authorship. |
| **Synthetic Media** | Artificial production or manipulation of audio/video (e.g., "Deepfakes") that mimics reality. |
| **Anthropomorphism** | The attribution of human characteristics, emotions, or intentions to non-human entities (e.g., an AI chatbot claiming "I feel happy to help you"). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 The Principle of Non-Deception
Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits the use of AI to deceive, mislead, or confuse stakeholders. Contributors must not represent AI-generated content as solely human-created. Transparency is the default requirement for all interactions.

#### 4.2 Content Labeling Standards
To ensure clarity, the following labeling standards apply to all public-facing materials:

*   **4.2.1 Textual Content:** Articles, guides, or educational resources significantly generated by AI (>30% of content) must carry a clear disclosure at the beginning of the document.
    *   *Standard Disclosure:* "This content was generated with the assistance of Artificial Intelligence and reviewed by [Role/Name] for accuracy."
*   **4.2.2 Source Code:** In alignment with our Open Source commitment (Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3), any code repositories or snippets generated by AI (e.g., Copilot) must include comments indicating AI origin. This serves to warn users of potential security vulnerabilities common in AI code.
    *   *Standard Comment:* `// Note: Portions of this code were AI-generated. Review for security logic before deployment.`
*   **4.2.3 Visual and Audio Media:** All AI-generated images or synthetic voices must be visibly or audibly captioned.
    *   *Requirement:* Images must have a visible watermark or caption (e.g., "AI-Generated Image"). Metadata labeling (C2PA) alone is insufficient.

#### 4.3 Conversational AI and Chatbots
Any automated system deployed by the Organization to interact with humans (e.g., support bots, discord auto-moderators) must explicitly identify itself as a bot upon the first interaction.

*   **4.3.1 Prohibition on Anthropomorphism:** Bots are prohibited from using language that implies sentience or human identity.
    *   *Prohibited:* "I am reading your message and I understand how you feel."
    *   *Required:* "I am an automated assistant processing your request."
*   **4.3.2 Human Handoff:** Users must always be provided a clear mechanism to request escalation to a human volunteer or staff member, where operationally feasible.

#### 4.4 Youth Protection and AI
Given the Organization's K-12 focus and "Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy," strict scrutiny applies to AI interactions with minors.

*   **4.4.1 No Behavioral Manipulation:** AI systems must not be designed to exploit the credulity or inexperience of children.
*   **4.4.2 Disclosure to Parents:** If a program or tool intended for youth involves AI interaction, this must be explicitly disclosed in the *Parental Consent and Liability Waiver* prior to participation.

#### 4.5 Accuracy and Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)
Transparency does not absolve the Organization of accuracy.
*   **4.5.1 Mandatory Review:** No AI-generated content regarding GRC regulations, laws, or security standards may be published without documented review by a qualified human Subject Matter Expert (SME).
*   **4.5.2 Liability:** The Organization accepts full responsibility for published content, regardless of whether it was drafted by a human or a machine.

#### 4.6 Intellectual Property and Ownership
Pursuant to Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3, Contributors agree that the use of AI tools to generate content for the Organization constitutes "Work Product." Contributors assign all rights to the Organization, regardless of the copyrightability of the raw AI output under current law. Contributors must not use AI tools that claim exclusive ownership over the output, as this violates our Open Source commitment.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures all operational departments adhere to labeling standards; approves high-level AI disclosures. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Validates that AI tools used do not compromise data privacy; oversees technical implementation of watermarking/metadata. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all curriculum materials generated with AI assistance are reviewed by SMEs and properly labeled. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Monitors community sentiment regarding AI use; ensures chatbots interact respectfully and transparently. |
| **Contributors (Staff/Volunteers)** | Responsible for self-reporting AI use and applying appropriate labels/disclosures to their work. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—specifically the intentional concealment of AI use to deceive the community—is a violation of the *Code of Ethics*.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from volunteer roles, or expulsion from membership.
*   **External Consequences:** Deceptive AI practices may trigger reports to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) or relevant state attorneys general under consumer protection laws.
