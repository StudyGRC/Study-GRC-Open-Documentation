### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Code of Ethics and Conduct (the "Code") is to define the high standards of ethical conduct and fiduciary responsibility required of the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization").

This policy is designed to:
1.  Ensure the Organization complies with IRS Form 990 governance standards regarding written policies.
2.  Establish a clear framework for professional behavior, decision-making, and accountability.
3.  Protect the Organization’s tax-exempt status, reputation, and eligibility for federal funding.
4.  Operationalize the governance mandates established in **Article III** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   All elected and appointed members of the Board of Directors ("Directors").
*   Non-voting Advisory Board members (as defined in **Bylaws Article VI**).
*   Board Committee members, regardless of voting status.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fiduciary Duty** | The legal obligation to act in the best interest of the Organization, comprising the Duty of Care, Duty of Loyalty, and Duty of Obedience. |
| **Conflict of Interest** | A situation where a Director’s personal, professional, or financial interests compete—or appear to compete—with the interests of the Organization. |
| **Confidential Information** | Non-public information including, but not limited to, executive session minutes, personnel records, donor lists, intellectual property, and sensitive security data. |
| **Moral Turpitude** | Conduct that is considered contrary to community standards of justice, honesty, or good morals, including but not limited to fraud, theft, or violence. |
| **One Voice** | The governance principle that the Board speaks with a single, unified voice once a decision has been made, regardless of individual dissenting votes. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Fiduciary Duties
Directors must adhere to the three primary legal duties of nonprofit governance:

*   **4.1.1 Duty of Care:** Directors must exercise reasonable care when making decisions as a steward of the Organization. This includes:
    *   Attending meetings regularly and preparing in advance (reviewing agendas and materials).
    *   Participating actively in discussions and exercising independent judgment.
    *   Ensuring financial resources are managed responsibly and in accordance with **2 CFR 200** (Uniform Guidance) for federal awards.
*   **4.1.2 Duty of Loyalty:** Directors must act solely in the best interest of the Organization, never for personal gain or the benefit of a third party. This requires strict adherence to the **Conflict of Interest Policy (GOV-POL-005)**.
*   **4.1.3 Duty of Obedience:** Directors must ensure the Organization remains true to its central mission (as defined in **Bylaws Article I**) and complies with all applicable laws, including the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and IRS regulations.

#### 4.2 Professional Conduct and Culture
*   **4.2.1 Civility and Respect:** In alignment with the Organization’s mission to foster a community ecosystem, Directors must treat fellow Board members, staff, volunteers, and members with respect. Harassment, bullying, or discrimination of any kind is strictly prohibited.
*   **4.2.2 Digital Citizenship:** As a remote-first organization, Directors must maintain professional decorum in all digital communications (Slack, email, Zoom). Directors must not use the Organization’s platforms for personal attacks, commercial solicitation, or illegal activities.
*   **4.2.3 Youth Safeguarding:** Given the Organization’s K-12 focus, Directors are held to the highest standard regarding youth protection. Any Director charged with a crime involving a minor or sexual misconduct will be immediately suspended pending investigation.

#### 4.3 Confidentiality
*   **4.3.1 Non-Disclosure:** Directors will frequently access sensitive information. Directors must not disclose Confidential Information to unauthorized persons, including family members or business associates.
*   **4.3.2 Executive Sessions:** Proceedings of Executive Sessions are strictly confidential. The minutes of these sessions are sealed and accessible only to voting Directors, unless released by a Board vote.
*   **4.3.3 Data Privacy:** Directors must comply with the Organization’s **Master Data Privacy Policy** and protect the personal data of members and donors.

#### 4.4 Public Representation
*   **4.4.1 Spokesperson Authority:** The President and the Executive Director are the only authorized spokespersons for the Organization. Individual Directors must not speak on behalf of the Organization to the media, government officials, or the public without prior authorization.
*   **4.4.2 Dissent:** While robust debate is encouraged during meetings, once a vote is finalized, Directors must support the legitimacy of the Board's decision outside the boardroom ("One Voice" policy).
*   **4.4.3 Social Media:** When posting on personal social media, Directors must clarify that their views are their own and do not represent the official position of Study GRC Inc.

#### 4.5 Interaction with Operational Staff
*   **4.5.1 Governance vs. Management:** The Board governs; the Executive Director manages. Directors must not direct, reprimand, or assign work to operational staff (including the VP of Education or CISO) without the express consent of the Executive Director or a Board resolution.
*   **4.5.2 Advisory Board Communication:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI**, Directors must facilitate all formal communication between Operational Officers and the Advisory Board.

#### 4.6 Legal and Ethical Compliance
*   **4.6.1 Whistleblower Protection:** Directors must not retaliate against any individual who reports a violation of law or policy in good faith, in accordance with the **Whistleblower Protection Policy**.
*   **4.6.2 Anti-Fraud:** Directors must proactively prevent and report waste, fraud, and abuse, particularly regarding the management of grant funds and donor contributions.
*   **4.6.3 Intellectual Property:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX**, Directors acknowledge that any work product created for the Organization during their tenure is "work made for hire" and belongs to the Organization.

| Role | Responsibility |

| **Secretary** | Maintains signed Code acknowledgments in the corporate records. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**6.1 Annual Acknowledgment:**
Every Director must sign a statement annually acknowledging that they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Code.

**6.2 Violations:**
Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action, including:
1.  **Censure:** A formal statement of disapproval recorded in the Board minutes.
2.  **Suspension:** Temporary removal of voting rights or committee assignments.
3.  **Removal:** Removal from the Board for cause, pursuant to **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**.

**6.3 Investigation Process:**
Allegations of ethical violations will be investigated by the Governance Committee (or an ad-hoc committee appointed by the President if the Governance Committee is conflicted). The accused Director will be given notice and an opportunity to be heard prior to any final vote on removal.
