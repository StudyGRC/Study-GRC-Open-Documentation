### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to eliminate the risk of child abuse, sexual misconduct, and grooming, as well as to protect Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") staff and volunteers from false allegations of misconduct. This policy establishes the mandatory "Two-Deep Leadership" standard, ensuring that no adult is ever alone with a minor during any Organization-sanctioned activity, whether physical or virtual.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers ("Authorized Adults") acting on behalf of the Organization. It covers all interactions with Youth Participants, including but not limited to:
*   Physical events, workshops, and conferences.
*   Virtual classrooms, webinars, and training sessions.
*   Online community interactions (e.g., Discord, Slack, Forums).
*   Electronic communications (email, text, social media).

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to interactions between a Youth Participant and their own legal parent or guardian.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The requirement that a minimum of two (2) unrelated Authorized Adults must be present and visibly engaged during any activity involving Youth Participants. |
| **Youth Participant** | Any individual under the age of eighteen (18) participating in Organization programs. |
| **Authorized Adult** | An individual who is 18 years or older, has successfully cleared a background check per policy `HR-POL-024`, and has completed required Youth Protection training. |
| **One-on-One Interaction** | A situation where one adult and one minor are alone, out of sight, or out of hearing range of others, or in a private digital channel without a third party. |
| **Unrelated** | Adults who are not siblings, spouses, or in a parent-child relationship with each other. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 The General Mandate (Rule of Two)
It is the policy of the Organization that **no One-on-One Interaction** shall occur between an Authorized Adult and a Youth Participant.
4.1.1 A minimum of two (2) Authorized Adults must be present for every activity, meeting, or event.
4.1.2 If two Authorized Adults are not available, the activity must be cancelled or rescheduled.
4.1.3 In situations where two adults cannot be present, a ratio of one (1) Authorized Adult to a minimum of three (3) Youth Participants is acceptable only in open, public, and observable environments, provided this exception is approved by the VP of Education & Training.

#### 4.2 Virtual and Remote Environments
Given the Organization's remote-first nature, strict adherence to Two-Deep Leadership in digital spaces is mandatory.
4.2.1 **Video Conferencing:** Two Authorized Adults must be logged into any video session (e.g., Zoom, Teams) before Youth Participants are admitted from the waiting room. If one adult drops off due to technical issues, the session must be terminated immediately.
4.2.2 **Breakout Rooms:** Virtual breakout rooms are prohibited unless each room can be staffed by at least one Authorized Adult, or the platform allows the Host to record and visually monitor all rooms simultaneously.
4.2.3 **Direct Messages (DMs):** Private Direct Messaging between an Authorized Adult and a Youth Participant is strictly prohibited on all platforms. All communication must occur in public channels or group chats where a second Authorized Adult is included.

#### 4.3 Physical Environments
4.3.1 **Line of Sight:** Interactions must occur in open areas where line-of-sight is maintained by other adults.
4.3.2 **Closed Doors:** If a private conversation is necessary (e.g., disciplinary or emotional distress), it must take place in a room with the door wide open, or in a room with a window allowing full visibility from the hallway. A second Authorized Adult must remain just outside the room.

#### 4.4 Electronic Communication
4.4.1 **Transparency:** Authorized Adults must not communicate privately with minors via personal email, text message, or social media.
4.4.2 **Copy Requirement:** Any necessary email correspondence to a Youth Participant must copy (cc) the youth's parent/guardian AND a second Authorized Adult (or a designated organizational alias, e.g., `safety@studygrc.org`).

#### 4.5 Emergency Exception
4.5.1 In the event of a life-threatening emergency where adhering to this policy would result in immediate physical harm to the Youth Participant (e.g., medical emergency requiring transport), the Authorized Adult must prioritize the safety of the child.
4.5.2 The Authorized Adult must document the incident immediately following the event and notify the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) and the Executive Director.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all scheduled programs are staffed with sufficient Authorized Adults to maintain the Two-Deep ratio. |
| **Authorized Adults** | Strictly adhere to the Two-Deep rule; refuse to be placed in One-on-One situations; immediately report violations. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Monitors community channels for compliance; receives reports of policy violations. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Configures technical controls (e.g., disabling DMs, enforcing recording) to support this policy. |
| **Executive Director** | Reviews and approves any emergency exception reports; enforces disciplinary actions. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy is a serious violation of the Organization's Code of Ethics and Bylaws Article III.
*   **Zero Tolerance:** Any intentional violation of the Two-Deep Leadership policy will result in immediate removal from the activity and may result in termination of employment or volunteer service.
*   **Reporting:** Any observer of a One-on-One interaction must interrupt the interaction immediately and report the incident to the Executive Director or Board President.
*   **Legal Action:** If a violation involves suspected abuse, it will be reported to law enforcement and the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services in accordance with `YTH-PRC-002` (Mandatory Reporting Procedure).
