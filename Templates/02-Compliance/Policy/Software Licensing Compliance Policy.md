### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") strictly adheres to all software copyright laws and license agreements. As an organization dedicated to Open Source principles and ethical governance (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3), we must demonstrate absolute integrity in our use of third-party intellectual property. This policy mitigates the legal, financial, and security risks associated with the use of unlicensed, pirated, or unauthorized software, and ensures compliance with federal grant requirements regarding allowable costs and procurement.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All employees, Board Directors, Operational Officers, volunteers, and independent contractors.
*   **Assets:** All Organization-owned computers, servers, mobile devices, and cloud environments.
*   **BYOD:** Personal devices used for Organization business (Bring Your Own Device), specifically regarding software used to access, process, or create Organization data.
*   **Software Types:** Proprietary commercial software, Software-as-a-Service (SaaS), Open Source Software (OSS), freeware, shareware, and fonts.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Commercial Software** | Software that is purchased or licensed from a vendor (e.g., Microsoft Office, Adobe Creative Cloud). |
| **SaaS (Software as a Service)** | Cloud-based software accessed via the internet, typically licensed on a subscription basis per user. |
| **Open Source Software (OSS)** | Software with source code that anyone can inspect, modify, and enhance, governed by specific licenses (e.g., MIT, GPL, Apache). |
| **Freeware/Shareware** | Software available at no cost (freeware) or for a trial period (shareware). "Free" does not imply "license-free." |
| **BSA** | The Software Alliance (formerly Business Software Alliance), a trade group that enforces software copyright compliance. |
| **Shadow IT** | Information technology systems, software, or subscriptions deployed by departments or individuals without central IT or Finance approval. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Mandate
Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits the installation, use, or distribution of pirated, unlicensed, or unauthorized software on any Organization asset or network. All software used for Organization business must be legally licensed. Violation of copyright law is a federal crime and a breach of the Organization's Code of Ethics.

#### 4.2 Software Acquisition and Approval
To prevent "Shadow IT" and ensure license tracking:
1.  **Centralized Procurement:** All software purchases and SaaS subscriptions must be approved by the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) prior to purchase.
2.  **Expensing Prohibition:** Employees may not purchase software on personal credit cards and seek reimbursement without prior written approval.
3.  **Grant Compliance:** Software purchased with federal grant funds must be necessary, reasonable, and allocable to the grant award, in compliance with 2 CFR 200.

#### 4.3 Installation and Administrative Rights
1.  **Restricted Access:** End-users are prohibited from having local administrative rights that allow the installation of software on Organization-owned devices, unless explicitly authorized by the CISO.
2.  **Authorized Installers:** Only the IT Department or designated system administrators may install software.
3.  **Freeware/Shareware:** The use of freeware or shareware requires the same approval process as commercial software to ensure the license permits commercial/non-profit use (many "free" tools are free only for *personal* use).

#### 4.4 Open Source Software (OSS) Usage
As an Open Source advocate, the Organization encourages the use of OSS. However:
1.  **License Compliance:** Users must adhere to the specific terms of the OSS license (e.g., attribution requirements).
2.  **Copyleft Viral Risk:** Developers must not integrate "Copyleft" code (e.g., GPL) into the Organization's proprietary internal tools or distinct projects in a manner that would legally force the Organization to release its own private keys or sensitive configurations, unless approved by the Board.
3.  **Security Vetting:** OSS libraries must be vetted for known vulnerabilities (CVEs) prior to deployment in production environments.

#### 4.5 License Management and Auditing
1.  **Master Software Register:** The IT Department must maintain a Master Software Register (MSR) detailing:
    *   Software Name and Version.
    *   Number of Licenses Purchased vs. Deployed.
    *   License Keys/Serial Numbers.
    *   Expiration/Renewal Dates.
2.  **Annual Audit:** The CISO shall conduct an internal software audit at least annually to reconcile installed software against held licenses.
3.  **Automated Scanning:** The Organization reserves the right to use automated discovery tools (RMM agents) on Organization-owned devices to detect installed software.

#### 4.6 Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)
Personnel using personal devices for Organization business must ensure that any software used to perform work duties (e.g., word processors, PDF editors) is legally licensed. The Organization accepts no liability for personal software piracy committed by volunteers or employees on personal devices.

#### 4.7 Uninstallation and License Harvesting
1.  **Termination:** Upon the departure of an employee or volunteer, all SaaS accounts must be deprovisioned immediately (per the *User Account Deprovisioning Procedure*).
2.  **Reallocation:** Licenses from departed users must be harvested and returned to the license pool for reallocation or cancellation to reduce costs.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Owns this policy; conducts annual audits; approves software for security risks; manages the Master Software Register. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Approves software procurement; ensures budget alignment; maintains proof of purchase (invoices/receipts) for audit defense. |
| **IT Department** | Performs installation; manages administrative privileges; configures RMM tools for inventory. |
| **Employees / Volunteers** | Request software through proper channels; do not install unauthorized software; strictly adhere to license agreements. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves this policy; ensures adequate budget is allocated for necessary software licenses. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation.

*   **Personal Liability:** Individuals who knowingly install pirated software may be held personally liable for civil and criminal penalties under the U.S. Copyright Act.
*   **Legal Defense:** The Organization will not provide legal defense or indemnification for individuals who willfully violate software copyright laws (Bylaws Article VII, Section 7.1 exception for bad faith).
