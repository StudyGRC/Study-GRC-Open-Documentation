### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a zero-tolerance framework against bullying and cyberbullying within Study GRC Inc. As a remote-first organization with a K-12 educational focus, we are committed to providing a psychological and physical environment free from harassment, intimidation, and exploitation. This policy defines prohibited behaviors, mandates intervention protocols, and ensures compliance with Texas state laws regarding harassment and youth protection.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals associated with Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to:
*   **Personnel:** Board of Directors, Officers, Employees, and Contractors.
*   **Volunteers:** Committee members, mentors, and moderators.
*   **Membership:** Supporting Members, Honorary Members, and Community Participants (as defined in Bylaws Article II).
*   **Participants:** Students, learners, and event attendees.

**Jurisdiction:** This policy covers conduct that occurs:
1.  On Study GRC Inc. premises or at official events (virtual or physical).
2.  Through Study GRC Inc. technology resources (e.g., Discord, Slack, LMS, Email).
3.  Off-premises or on personal accounts if the conduct creates a hostile environment for a participant or substantially disrupts the Organization’s operations.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bullying** | A single significant act or a pattern of acts by one or more individuals directed at another that exploits an imbalance of power and involves written or verbal expression, expression through electronic means, or physical conduct that satisfies the criteria of harm or intimidation. |
| **Cyberbullying** | Bullying that is done through the use of any electronic communication device, including through the use of a cellular or other type of telephone, a computer, a camera, electronic mail, instant messaging, text messaging, a social media application, an Internet website, or any other Internet-based communication tool. |
| **Imbalance of Power** | When a person or group uses their power—such as physical strength, access to embarrassing information, popularity, or position of authority (e.g., Moderator vs. Student)—to control or harm others. |
| **Doxing** | The act of publicly revealing previously private personal information about an individual (e.g., home address, real name of a pseudonymous user) without their consent, typically with malicious intent. |
| **Retaliation** | Any adverse action taken against an individual because they reported a violation of this policy or participated in an investigation. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Zero Tolerance Standard
Study GRC Inc. maintains a strict zero-tolerance stance on bullying. "Zero tolerance" means that every reported incident will be investigated, and substantiated violations will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, revocation of membership, or banning from community platforms.

#### 4.2 Prohibited Conduct
The following behaviors are explicitly prohibited:
1.  **Verbal/Written Aggression:** Name-calling, slurs, taunting, or threats of harm.
2.  **Social Exclusion:** Intentionally inciting others to exclude or ostracize a person from the community or study groups.
3.  **Doxing and Outing:** Publishing private information or threatening to disclose sensitive information (including sexual orientation or gender identity) without consent.
4.  **Cyberstalking:** Repeated, unwanted monitoring or communication that causes fear or distress.
5.  **Flaming:** Posting hostile or insulting messages in public forums intended to ignite conflict.
6.  **Impersonation:** Creating fake accounts ("sock puppets") to harass others or evade bans.

#### 4.3 Youth Protection Specifics
Given the Organization's K-12 focus, special protections apply to minors:
*   **Adult-to-Minor:** Any bullying behavior by an adult directed toward a minor is grounds for immediate suspension pending investigation and potential referral to law enforcement.
*   **Mandatory Reporting:** In accordance with Texas Family Code Chapter 261, any staff or volunteer who suspects a minor is being abused or neglected (including severe bullying resulting in mental injury) must report it to the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) or law enforcement within 48 hours.

#### 4.4 Intervention and Reporting
*   **Duty to Intervene:** Any staff member or volunteer who witnesses bullying must intervene immediately to stop the behavior, provided it is safe to do so.
*   **Reporting Mechanism:** Incidents should be reported to the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** via the designated confidential reporting form or email.
*   **Anonymous Reporting:** The Organization shall maintain a channel for anonymous reporting; however, formal disciplinary action based solely on an anonymous report may be limited by due process requirements.

#### 4.5 Investigation and Due Process
Upon receipt of a report, the Organization will:
1.  **Preserve Evidence:** The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) or designate will secure relevant logs, screenshots, and communications.
2.  **Interim Measures:** The accused may be temporarily suspended or muted to ensure the safety of the complainant during the investigation.
3.  **Fact-Finding:** An impartial investigator will interview relevant parties.
4.  **Determination:** A determination of responsibility will be made based on the "preponderance of the evidence" standard (i.e., it is more likely than not that the policy was violated).

#### 4.6 Retaliation
Retaliation against any person who reports bullying or assists in an investigation is strictly prohibited. Retaliation is considered a separate and serious violation of this policy and will result in immediate disciplinary action.

#### 4.7 False Reports
Intentionally making a false report of bullying to harm another person is a violation of this policy and subject to disciplinary action. This does not apply to reports made in good faith that are later unsubstantiated.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Acts as the primary point of contact for reports; oversees the cultural climate; ensures victims are supported. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Assists in digital forensics; preserves server logs and chat history for investigations; enforces technical bans. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews appeals regarding membership revocation or Director removal (per Bylaws Art. III §3.6); approves final policy updates. |
| **Volunteers/Moderators** | Responsible for immediate intervention in public channels (e.g., deleting posts, issuing warnings) and escalating incidents to the DKG. |
| **All Members** | Responsible for adhering to the Code of Conduct and reporting observed violations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Violations of this policy will be adjudicated based on severity, frequency, and impact.

*   **Community Participants:** May be subject to immediate ban from Discord/LMS without refund.
*   **Voting Members:** May face expulsion from the Organization pursuant to Bylaws Article II.
*   **Directors/Officers:** May be removed for cause pursuant to Bylaws Article III §3.6 and Article V §5.3.
*   **Employees:** Subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.

**Legal Referral:** If conduct constitutes a criminal offense (e.g., harassment under Texas Penal Code § 42.07, terroristic threats, or distribution of harmful material to minors), the Organization will refer the matter to appropriate law enforcement agencies.
