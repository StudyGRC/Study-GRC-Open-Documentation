### 1. Purpose
Study GRC Inc. is committed to supporting the health and well-being of its employees and their families. In compliance with Section 7 of the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), specifically §207(r) (as amended by the Providing Urgent Maternal Protections for Nursing Mothers Act or "PUMP Act"), this policy establishes the guidelines for providing reasonable break time and private locations for employees to express breast milk for their nursing child.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees (full-time, part-time, exempt, and non-exempt), contractors, and volunteers of Study GRC Inc.

**Applicability by Work Environment:**
*   **Remote/Home Office:** Applies to the scheduling and "camera-off" allowances for remote staff.
*   **Physical Events/Offices:** Applies to the provision of physical space during on-site meetings, conferences, or temporary co-working arrangements managed by the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Nursing Employee** | An employee who is lactating and needs to express breast milk for their nursing child. |
| **Reasonable Break Time** | The amount of time needed to express milk, which varies by individual. This includes time to set up, express milk, clean up, and store the milk. |
| **Private Space** | A place, **other than a bathroom**, that is shielded from view and free from intrusion from coworkers and the public. |
| **Completely Relieved from Duty** | A status where the employee is not performing any work duties (active or passive) during the break. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Right to Accommodation
For a period of one (1) year following the birth of a child, Study GRC Inc. must provide a Nursing Employee with reasonable break time to express breast milk as frequently as needed.

#### 4.2 Break Time and Frequency
4.2.1 **Frequency:** The frequency and duration of breaks will vary depending on the Nursing Employee’s needs. There is no fixed limit on the number of breaks.
4.2.2 **Scheduling:** Nursing Employees are encouraged to communicate with their direct supervisor regarding their anticipated schedule to minimize disruption to operations, though the biological needs of the employee take precedence.

#### 4.3 Compensation
4.3.1 **Non-Exempt Employees:**
*   If the employee is **completely relieved from duty** during the break, the time is unpaid, unless the employee chooses to use available paid break time (e.g., standard 15-minute rest breaks).
*   If the employee is interrupted or required to perform work (e.g., answering emails, attending a conference call) while pumping, they must be compensated for the entire break period.
4.3.2 **Exempt Employees:**
*   Salaried exempt employees will not have their salary reduced for time taken to express milk, provided they are ready, willing, and able to work during the remainder of the workweek.

#### 4.4 Location Requirements (Remote & On-Site)
4.4.1 **Remote Work Environment:**
*   For employees working from a home office, Study GRC Inc. respects the employee's privacy.
*   Nursing Employees are authorized to turn off their cameras and mute microphones during virtual meetings if they choose to express milk during a call, without penalty or requirement to explain the reason for the "camera-off" status.
*   Supervisors must respect "Do Not Disturb" statuses set for this purpose.

4.4.2 **Organization-Sponsored Events and Physical Locations:**
*   When Study GRC Inc. hosts in-person events (e.g., board meetings, conferences, training sessions), the Organization must designate a **Private Space**.
*   **Minimum Standards:** The space must be functional for expressing milk. It must contain:
    *   A place to sit (other than the floor).
    *   A flat surface (other than the floor) to place the pump.
    *   Access to electricity for the pump.
    *   A door that locks or appropriate signage/barriers to ensure privacy.
*   **Prohibition:** A bathroom (toilet stall or restroom common area) is **never** a permissible location for this accommodation.

#### 4.5 Storage of Milk
For on-site events or office work, Study GRC Inc. will provide access to a refrigerator or a safe place for the Nursing Employee to store breast milk. If a refrigerator is not available, the Organization will facilitate the use of personal coolers/ice packs.

#### 4.6 Anti-Retaliation and Harassment
4.6.1 **Zero Tolerance:** Retaliation against an employee for exercising their rights under this policy or the FLSA is strictly prohibited.
4.6.2 **Prohibited Conduct:** This includes, but is not limited to:
*   Denying break requests.
*   Harassing the employee about the frequency or duration of breaks.
*   Penalizing the employee in performance reviews for time taken to express milk.
*   Making disparaging comments regarding lactation.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Nursing Employee** | Notify supervisor of the need for accommodation (optional but recommended for planning). Adhere to the policy regarding work status (working vs. relieved of duty) for accurate timekeeping. |
| **Supervisor/Manager** | Approve schedule adjustments to accommodate breaks. Ensure the employee is not retaliated against. Respect privacy and "camera-off" requirements. |
| **Event Planners / Ops** | Ensure any rented venue for Organization events includes a designated Private Space compliant with Section 4.4.2. |
| **Human Resources** | Address any grievances or disputes regarding this policy. Ensure compliance with FLSA posting requirements. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy, including the failure to provide space or time, or engaging in retaliation against a Nursing Employee, may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

Violations of the PUMP Act may also subject the Organization to legal liability, including damages and penalties under the Fair Labor Standards Act.
