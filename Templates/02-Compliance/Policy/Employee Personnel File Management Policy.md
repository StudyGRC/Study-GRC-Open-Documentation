### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a standardized framework for the creation, maintenance, retention, and security of employee personnel files at Study GRC Inc. This policy ensures compliance with federal and state laws—including the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), and Texas Labor Code—while satisfying the record-keeping requirements of 2 CFR 200 for federal grant compliance. It aims to protect the privacy of employees by strictly regulating access to Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and Protected Health Information (PHI).

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all records associated with current and former employees of Study GRC Inc.
*   **In Scope:** All full-time, part-time, temporary, and seasonal employees.
*   **Exclusions:** Independent Contractor records (managed under Procurement/Accounts Payable) and Volunteer records (managed under the Volunteer Management Policy), except where background checks overlap.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Personnel File (General)** | The primary file containing documents related to an employee's hiring, performance, and employment history. |
| **Confidential Medical File** | A strictly segregated file containing any medical information, disability accommodations, or health insurance records, as required by the ADA. |
| **I-9 File** | A separate file containing USCIS Form I-9 and supporting identity documentation, kept distinct to facilitate government audits without exposing other personnel data. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information (e.g., Social Security Numbers, home addresses, bank details). |
| **Need-to-Know** | The principle that access to information is granted only to those who require it to perform their specific job duties. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 The Segregated File System
To ensure compliance with the ADA and data privacy standards, Study GRC Inc. utilizes a **Three-File System**. Documents must never be commingled between these categories.

**4.1.1 The General Personnel File**
This file contains standard employment information accessible to HR and the employee’s direct manager (on a limited basis). Contents include:
*   Employment application and resume.
*   Offer letter and employment contract.
*   Job description and acknowledgment.
*   Performance evaluations and goal sheets.
*   Disciplinary notices and warnings (excluding medical reasons).
*   Training certifications and attendance records.
*   Resignation or termination documentation.

**4.1.2 The Confidential Medical File (Restricted)**
Pursuant to the ADA, medical records must be kept **separate** from the General Personnel File. Access is strictly limited to the HR Director and Legal Counsel. Managers may not access this file. Contents include:
*   Medical leave requests (FMLA) and doctor’s notes.
*   ADA accommodation requests and medical certifications.
*   Health insurance enrollment forms and beneficiary designations.
*   Workers' compensation claims.
*   Drug testing results.

**4.1.3 The Payroll and Compliance File (Restricted)**
To protect sensitive financial data and facilitate audits, the following are kept separate:
*   USCIS Form I-9 (stored in a master I-9 binder or distinct digital folder).
*   Background check results (FCRA compliance).
*   W-4 forms and direct deposit authorization.
*   Garnishment orders.
*   Time and Effort reports (required for 2 CFR 200 grant compliance).

#### 4.2 Digital Record Keeping (Remote-First Context)
As a remote-first organization, Study GRC Inc. maintains personnel files primarily in digital format.
*   **Encryption:** All digital personnel files must be stored in the Organization’s designated secure cloud environment (e.g., encrypted HRIS or secure cloud drive) with encryption at rest and in transit.
*   **Access Control:** Access rights must be configured using Role-Based Access Control (RBAC).
*   **Audit Logs:** The storage system must maintain an audit log of all views, edits, and downloads of personnel files.

#### 4.3 Access and Confidentiality
*   **HR & Legal:** Have full access to all files for administrative and legal purposes.
*   **Management:** Managers may request access *only* to the General Personnel File of their direct reports for performance management purposes. They may **not** access Medical or Payroll files.
*   **Third Parties:** Personnel files are not released to third parties (e.g., landlords, banks) without the written consent of the employee, except where required by law (e.g., subpoena, government audit).

#### 4.4 Employee Right to Inspect
Although Texas law does not mandate employee access to personnel files, Study GRC Inc. grants employees the privilege to review their own General Personnel File to foster transparency.
*   **Procedure:** Employees must submit a written request to HR.
*   **Supervision:** Review will be conducted via a secure screen-share session or supervised viewing to prevent the alteration or removal of documents.
*   **Exclusions:** Employees do not have the right to review references provided confidentially by third parties or records related to ongoing internal investigations.

#### 4.5 Prohibited Content
The following items must **never** be placed in a personnel file:
*   Whistleblower complaints (must be kept in a separate investigation file).
*   Legal case notes or attorney-client privileged communications.
*   Notes regarding an employee’s political or religious beliefs.
*   Unsubstantiated rumors or personal notes not shared with the employee.

#### 4.6 Retention and Destruction
*   **Retention Period:** Personnel files are retained for the duration of employment plus **seven (7) years** following separation, or longer if required by specific grant conditions (2 CFR 200.334).
*   **Destruction:** Upon expiration of the retention period, files must be destroyed in accordance with the *Data Retention and Destruction Policy*. Digital files must be permanently purged/sanitized; physical files must be cross-cut shredded.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **HR Director** | Acts as the primary custodian of records; ensures files are segregated, secure, and up-to-date; approves access requests. |
| **Managers** | Responsible for forwarding all relevant performance documentation to HR for inclusion in the file; maintains confidentiality of any information accessed. |
| **Employees** | Responsible for notifying HR of changes to personal information (address, emergency contact) to keep records current. |
| **CISO / IT Dept** | Ensures the technical security (encryption, access controls, backups) of the digital repository housing personnel files. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—particularly regarding the unauthorized access, disclosure, or mishandling of Confidential Medical Files or PII—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment and potential legal action.

Violations involving federal grant documentation (Time & Effort) may result in the disallowance of costs and personal liability.
