### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") conducts all international activities, financial transactions, and grantmaking in strict compliance with United States laws regarding anti-terrorism, export controls, and foreign asset control.

As a 501(c)(3) public charity dedicated to fostering a global GRC community (Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3), the Organization must prevent its funds, assets, or resources from being diverted to support terrorism, money laundering, or other illegal activities. This policy establishes the framework for compliance with the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) regulations, the USA PATRIOT Act, and Executive Order 13224.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors of the Organization. It covers all international activities, including but not limited to:
*   **Financial Transactions:** Payments to vendors, contractors, or speakers located outside the United States.
*   **Grantmaking:** Grants, scholarships, or donations made to non-U.S. organizations or individuals.
*   **Partnerships:** Formal agreements with non-U.S. entities.
*   **Material Support:** Provision of technical assistance, training, or proprietary hardware/software to non-U.S. persons.

**Exclusion:** In accordance with the "Berman Amendment" to the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA), the mere dissemination of **publicly available** open-source information or educational materials (as defined in Bylaws Section 1.3) to the general public does not generally require specific screening, provided no substantive "service" or financial transaction accompanies the information.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **OFAC** | The Office of Foreign Assets Control of the U.S. Department of the Treasury, which administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions. |
| **SDN List** | The list of "Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons" maintained by OFAC. U.S. persons are generally prohibited from dealing with individuals or entities on this list. |
| **Expenditure Responsibility (ER)** | A requirement under IRC §4945(h) where the grantor exerts all reasonable efforts and establishes adequate procedures to see that the grant is spent solely for the purpose for which made. |
| **Equivalency Determination (ED)** | A good-faith determination that a foreign grantee is equivalent to a U.S. public charity (described in IRC §509(a)(1), (2), or (3)). |
| **Material Support** | Any property, tangible or intangible, or service, including currency, financial services, lodging, training, expert advice or assistance, safehouses, false documentation or identification, communications equipment, facilities, weapons, lethal substances, explosives, personnel, and transportation. |
| **FCPA** | The Foreign Corrupt Practices Act, prohibiting the payment of bribes to foreign officials to assist in obtaining or retaining business. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Sanctions Compliance
The Organization shall not engage in any financial transaction, contract, or provision of services with:
1.  Any individual or entity designated on the OFAC SDN List.
2.  Any individual or entity located in a country or territory subject to comprehensive U.S. sanctions (e.g., Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, and the Crimea, Donetsk, and Luhansk regions of Ukraine), unless authorized by a General or Specific License issued by OFAC.

#### 4.2 Anti-Terrorism Screening
Prior to the release of any funds (including honorariums, reimbursements, grants, or vendor payments) to a non-U.S. person or entity, the Organization must perform a risk-based screening.

*   **Mandatory Screening:** The CFO or designee must screen the payee’s name and key principals against the OFAC SDN List and other relevant watchlists (e.g., BIS Entity List).
*   **Documentation:** A record of the screening result (e.g., a timestamped screenshot or PDF from the screening tool) must be attached to the payment request or contract in the Organization’s financial records.
*   **Positive Matches:** If a potential match is found, the transaction must be frozen immediately. The matter must be escalated to the Executive Director and Legal Counsel for verification.

#### 4.3 Foreign Grantmaking Procedures
When making grants to non-U.S. organizations to further the charitable purposes outlined in Bylaws Article I, the Organization must utilize one of two methods to ensure tax compliance:

1.  **Equivalency Determination (ED):** The Organization collects an affidavit and supporting documents (financial data, governing documents) from the foreign grantee to determine that the foreign organization is the equivalent of a U.S. public charity. This determination must be written and approved by Legal Counsel or a qualified tax practitioner.
2.  **Expenditure Responsibility (ER):** If ED is not feasible, the Organization must exercise Expenditure Responsibility. This requires:
    *   A pre-grant inquiry.
    *   A written grant agreement limiting the use of funds to charitable purposes.
    *   Annual reports from the grantee on how funds were spent.
    *   Reporting the grant on the Organization’s IRS Form 990-F.

#### 4.4 Anti-Bribery and Corruption (FCPA)
The Organization strictly prohibits offering, promising, paying, or authorizing the payment of anything of value to a **Foreign Official** to influence an official act or decision, or to secure an improper advantage. This prohibition applies to all employees and third-party agents acting on behalf of the Organization.

*   **Facilitating Payments:** "Grease payments" (small payments to expedite routine governmental actions) are prohibited under this policy, even if technically permissible under the FCPA, to ensure the highest ethical standards.

#### 4.5 Export Controls and Technical Data
While the Organization is dedicated to open-source resources (Bylaws Section 1.3), the transfer of certain encryption software, proprietary technology, or "defense services" may be subject to the Export Administration Regulations (EAR) or International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR).

*   **Review:** Before engaging in a project involving the transfer of non-public technical data or hardware to foreign nationals, the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) must review the transfer for export control compliance.

#### 4.6 Diversion of Assets
The Organization reserves the right to suspend or terminate any grant, contract, or relationship if it discovers that the other party has:
1.  Diverted funds for non-charitable purposes.
2.  Provided support to terrorism or violent extremism.
3.  Become subject to U.S. or international sanctions.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ultimate oversight of international activities; approves high-risk foreign grants or partnerships. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures operational procedures are in place for screening; signs off on Equivalency Determinations. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Executes OFAC screenings; maintains documentation of screenings; manages Expenditure Responsibility reporting; ensures Form 990 Schedule F compliance. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Reviews technology transfers for Export Control (EAR/ITAR) compliance. |
| **Employees/Volunteers** | Responsible for identifying international components of projects and submitting payee information for screening prior to making commitments. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

Violations of U.S. sanctions and anti-terrorism laws can result in severe civil and criminal penalties for both the individual and the Organization, including fines exceeding $1,000,000 and imprisonment. The Organization will cooperate fully with federal authorities in the investigation of any suspected violation.
