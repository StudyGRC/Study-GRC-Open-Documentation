### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the minimum requirements for maintaining a "Clean Desk" and "Clear Screen" to ensure that sensitive and confidential information—whether in physical or electronic format—is secured against unauthorized access, compromise, or theft. This policy directly supports **Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)** by safeguarding the Organization’s intellectual property and the privacy of our members and youth participants.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Study GRC Inc. personnel, including:
*   **Personnel:** Employees, Board Directors, Officers, Volunteers, and Contractors.
*   **Locations:** Any location where Organization business is conducted, including home offices (remote work), co-working spaces, public venues (e.g., coffee shops, libraries), and during travel.
*   **Assets:** All workstations, laptops, mobile devices, printers, and physical documents containing Organization data.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Sensitive Information** | Any data classified as "Confidential" or "Restricted" per the *Data Classification Standard*, including but not limited to youth participant PII, donor financial data, and unpublished intellectual property. |
| **Workspace** | The immediate physical area where a user performs work, including desks, tables, and surrounding shelves/cabinets, regardless of location (home or public). |
| **Unattended** | A state in which the user is not present at the workstation or is out of direct line-of-sight of their workspace for any duration. |
| **Lock Screen** | A technical control that blanks the computer screen and requires a password, PIN, or biometric authentication to resume activity. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Clear Screen Requirements (Digital Security)
To prevent unauthorized viewing of electronic information ("shoulder surfing") or access to unattended sessions:

*   **4.1.1 Mandatory Locking:** Users must lock their computer screen (e.g., `Win + L` on Windows, `Ctrl + Cmd + Q` on macOS) immediately whenever they leave their workstation unattended, even if only for a brief moment or within a home office.
*   **4.1.2 Automated Timeout:** All Organization-managed devices must be configured to automatically activate a password-protected screen saver or lock screen after a maximum of **10 minutes** of inactivity. Users are prohibited from using "mouse jigglers" or software to bypass this control.
*   **4.1.3 Public Usage:** When working in public spaces (e.g., airports, coffee shops):
    *   Users must position their screens to prevent observation by passersby.
    *   The use of a privacy screen filter is **mandatory** when accessing Sensitive Information in public.
*   **4.1.4 Projectors and Screenshares:** Before sharing a screen during virtual meetings (e.g., Zoom, Teams), users must close or minimize all non-essential windows, specifically email clients and messaging apps (e.g., Slack/Discord), to prevent accidental data leakage.

#### 4.2 Clean Desk Requirements (Physical Security)
To prevent the theft or unauthorized inspection of physical documents and media:

*   **4.2.1 Secure Storage:** Sensitive Information (hard copies, USB drives, external hard drives) must be removed from the desk and locked in a drawer or filing cabinet when the workspace is unoccupied for an extended period (e.g., lunch breaks, end of the workday).
*   **4.2.2 End of Day:** At the end of each working day, the workspace must be cleared of all Sensitive Information. Documents must not be left on desks, printers, or in accessible wastebins.
*   **4.2.3 Printing Restrictions:**
    *   Printing of Sensitive Information is strongly discouraged and should only occur when absolutely necessary.
    *   Documents must be retrieved from the printer immediately.
    *   Users working from home must ensure family members or guests do not have access to printed Organization documents.
*   **4.2.4 Disposal:** Physical documents containing Sensitive Information must not be disposed of in standard trash or recycle bins. They must be cross-cut shredded. If a shredder is not available in a remote work environment, the documents must be stored securely until they can be destroyed properly.

#### 4.3 Remote and Home Office Specifics
Recognizing Study GRC Inc. is a remote-first organization:

*   **4.3.1 Shared Living Spaces:** If a workspace is located in a shared living area (e.g., living room, kitchen), Sensitive Information must never be left unattended. It must be physically secured or removed from the area when the user steps away.
*   **4.3.2 Video Backgrounds:** Users must ensure that no Sensitive Information (e.g., whiteboards with diagrams, sticky notes with passwords) is visible in the background during video conference calls. The use of virtual backgrounds is encouraged to mitigate this risk.
*   **4.3.3 Password Management:** Passwords and access codes must strictly **not** be written down on sticky notes or pads located on or near the computer.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Personnel** | Adhere to the Clean Desk and Clear Screen requirements daily. Report any suspected unauthorized access to the CISO. |
| **CISO / IT Security** | Configure and enforce automated screen-lock policies via Mobile Device Management (MDM) or Group Policy. Conduct periodic checks (virtual or physical) for compliance. |
| **Management** | Model appropriate behavior and ensure their teams have the necessary resources (e.g., shredders, locking cabinets) if physical document handling is required. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Compliance with this policy is mandatory.
*   **Verification:** The CISO or designated IT staff may perform random spot checks (e.g., verifying screen lock timeout settings) or walkthroughs at physical events.
*   **Violations:** Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Legal:** Significant breaches resulting from negligence regarding this policy may trigger legal action, particularly if youth data or donor financial information is compromised.
