### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to minimize organizational risk associated with the installation and use of unauthorized software and cloud-based services ("Shadow IT"). Unvetted software introduces significant security vulnerabilities, legal liability regarding licensing compliance, and data privacy risks—particularly concerning the Organization's obligations under COPPA (for youth programs) and GDPR/TDPSA. This policy establishes the framework for software governance, approval, and license management.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All employees, Board members, Operational Officers, contractors, and volunteers of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Assets:** All Organization-owned devices (laptops, servers, mobile devices) and networks.
*   **BYOD:** Personally owned devices used to access Organization data or systems, specifically regarding applications used to process Organization data.
*   **Services:** All software applications, browser extensions, and cloud-based (SaaS) platforms.

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal software installed on personally owned devices (BYOD) that does not interact with, store, or process Study GRC Inc. data or networks.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Unauthorized Software** | Any software, application, script, or browser extension that has not gone through the formal IT security review process and has not been added to the Approved Software List. |
| **Shadow IT** | The use of information technology systems, devices, software, applications, and services without explicit IT department approval. This includes SaaS tools (e.g., Trello, Dropbox, ChatGPT) used for business purposes without vetting. |
| **Freeware/Shareware** | Software that is available free of charge or for a nominal fee. "Free" cost does not imply "Free" of risk or licensing obligations for non-profit/commercial use. |
| **SaaS (Software as a Service)** | Cloud-based applications accessed via the internet (e.g., Google Workspace, Slack, Canva). |
| **Approved Software List (ASL)** | The official register of software and SaaS platforms vetted for security, privacy, and compatibility, maintained by the CISO. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Prohibition
Users must not download, install, or execute any software on Organization-owned devices unless that software is explicitly listed on the **Approved Software List (ASL)**.
*   This prohibition applies to executable files (.exe, .dmg), installer packages (.msi, .pkg), scripts, and browser extensions.
*   Users generally will not be granted local administrative privileges on Organization-owned endpoints to enforce this control.

#### 4.2 Shadow IT and SaaS Restrictions
To protect data integrity and privacy, Users are strictly prohibited from creating accounts on or using third-party cloud services (SaaS) for Organization business without prior approval.
*   **Data Residency:** Organization data (including member lists, donor data, and youth participant information) must never be uploaded to unapproved cloud storage or processing tools.
*   **Click-Through Agreements:** Users are prohibited from accepting "Terms of Service" or "End User License Agreements" (EULA) on behalf of the Organization for unapproved software.

#### 4.3 High-Risk Categories
The following categories of software are strictly prohibited on any device connected to the Organization's VPN or internal network, unless authorized in writing by the CISO for specific security testing purposes:
1.  **Peer-to-Peer (P2P) File Sharing:** BitTorrent, uTorrent, etc.
2.  **Cryptocurrency Miners:** Any software used to mine digital currency.
3.  **Hacking/Cracking Tools:** Password crackers, network sniffers, or vulnerability scanners (except for authorized Security Team personnel).
4.  **Remote Access Tools (RATs):** TeamViewer, AnyDesk, or LogMeIn (unless deployed by IT for support).
5.  **Anonymizers:** TOR browsers or unauthorized VPN clients.

#### 4.4 Software Approval Process
Users requiring software not currently on the ASL must submit a **Software Request Ticket** to the IT/Security team. The CISO (or designee) will evaluate the request based on:
1.  **Security:** Vulnerability history, encryption standards, and supply chain risk.
2.  **Privacy:** Compliance with GDPR, TDPSA, and COPPA (specifically if the tool interacts with data of minors).
3.  **Licensing:** Cost and validity of non-profit licensing.
4.  **Compatibility:** Interoperability with existing systems.

#### 4.5 Licensing Compliance
Study GRC Inc. adheres strictly to copyright laws and software license agreements.
*   **Piracy:** The installation or use of pirated, cracked, or unlicensed software is zero-tolerance misconduct.
*   **Personal Licenses:** Users may not use "Home/Student" or "Personal" editions of software for Organization business unless the license explicitly permits non-profit/commercial use.

#### 4.6 Monitoring and Removal
Pursuant to the **Acceptable Use Policy**, the Organization reserves the right to scan Organization-owned devices and networks to detect unauthorized software.
*   **Automated Removal:** The IT Department is authorized to remotely disable or uninstall unauthorized software found on Organization assets without prior notice to the user.
*   **Quarantine:** Devices found running high-risk unauthorized software may be immediately quarantined (disconnected) from the network until remediated.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Maintains the Approved Software List (ASL). Conducts security and privacy reviews of requested software. Authorizes exceptions for security testing tools. |
| **IT Department** | Enforces technical controls (e.g., removing admin rights, blocking installation execution). Conducts regular scans for unauthorized software. Manages license keys. |
| **Operational Officers / Managers** | Ensure their teams are aware of this policy. Prevent the purchase of software/SaaS on corporate credit cards without prior CISO approval. |
| **Users (Employees/Volunteers)** | Verify software is on the ASL before use. Submit requests for new tools. Refrain from using personal cloud accounts for Organization data. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation.
*   **Legal Liability:** Individuals involved in the willful installation of pirated software may be held personally liable for civil and criminal penalties under copyright law.
*   **Revocation of Access:** Users violating this policy may have their network access or device privileges immediately revoked.
