### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish clear guidelines for the reimbursement of reasonable and necessary business expenses incurred by employees working remotely. This policy ensures Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") complies with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), Internal Revenue Service (IRS) "Accountable Plan" rules, and 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance) regarding allowable costs for federal grantees. It aims to prevent the shifting of the Organization's operating costs to employees while maintaining fiscal responsibility.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:** All full-time and part-time W-2 employees of Study GRC Inc. designated as "Remote" or "Hybrid" workers.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Independent Contractors:** Contractors are expected to provide their own equipment and supplies unless explicitly stated otherwise in their Master Service Agreement (MSA).
*   **Volunteers:** Volunteer reimbursement is governed separately by the *Volunteer Handbook* and is generally limited to pre-approved travel or specific program supplies.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Accountable Plan** | An IRS arrangement for reimbursing employees for business expenses where the reimbursement is not counted as taxable income. Requires business connection, substantiation, and return of excess funds. |
| **Necessary Expenditure** | An expense that is appropriate and helpful to the development and conduct of the Organization’s business and is directly related to the employee’s job duties. |
| **Substantiation** | Documentary evidence (receipts, invoices, logs) proving the amount, date, place, and business character of an expense. |
| **2 CFR 200** | Federal regulations (Uniform Guidance) governing the administration of federal grants, specifically regarding "Allowable Costs." |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Reimbursement Principle
Study GRC Inc. will reimburse employees for all necessary and reasonable business expenses incurred in the direct performance of their job duties. Reimbursements are intended to make the employee whole, not to serve as a salary supplement or bonus.

#### 4.2 IRS Accountable Plan Compliance
To ensure reimbursements remain non-taxable to the employee, the Organization operates under an IRS Accountable Plan. Employees must strictly adhere to the following:
1.  **Business Connection:** The expense must have been paid or incurred while performing services as an employee of the Organization.
2.  **Substantiation:** Employees must submit an expense report with documentary evidence (receipts) within **30 days** of incurring the expense.
3.  **Return of Excess:** If an advance was provided, any amount exceeding the actual substantiated expense must be returned to the Organization within **15 days**.

#### 4.3 Reimbursable Remote Work Expenses

**4.3.1 Organization-Provided Equipment**
The Organization generally provides the primary computer (laptop) and necessary software licenses required for the role. Employees should not purchase primary computing hardware with the expectation of reimbursement without prior written approval from the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) and CFO.

**4.3.2 Internet and Connectivity**
Recognizing the remote-first nature of the Organization:
*   The Organization may provide a reasonable monthly stipend (e.g., $50.00) or partial reimbursement for high-speed internet access if the employee is required to maintain a connection quality exceeding typical household needs for business continuity.
*   **Federal Grant Restriction:** For employees funded by federal grants, internet costs must be allocated strictly based on usage or an approved cost allocation methodology in compliance with 2 CFR 200.452. Flat stipends charged to federal awards without usage logs are prohibited.

**4.3.3 Office Supplies and Consumables**
Reasonable costs for office supplies (paper, printer ink, notebooks) necessary for daily operations are reimbursable.
*   **Pre-Approval Threshold:** Any single supply purchase exceeding **$100.00** requires prior written approval from the employee’s direct supervisor.

**4.3.4 Ergonomic and Safety Equipment**
Pursuant to the *Remote Worksite Safety Self-Assessment*, if an ergonomic assessment identifies a specific need (e.g., monitor riser, ergonomic keyboard) to prevent injury, these items may be reimbursed with prior approval from Human Resources.

#### 4.4 Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The following are generally **not** reimbursable, as they represent personal overhead or hybrid use:
*   Mortgage or rent payments for the employee’s home.
*   Utility bills (electricity, water, gas, heating/cooling).
*   Home maintenance, insurance, or cleaning services.
*   Furniture (desks, chairs, lamps) unless required as a Reasonable Accommodation under the ADA.
*   Coffee, snacks, or meals consumed at the home office.
*   Commuting costs (if the employee chooses to work from a co-working space not mandated by the Organization).

#### 4.5 Federal Grant Compliance (2 CFR 200)
When expenses are charged to federal awards:
*   Costs must be **Allowable** under 2 CFR 200 Subpart E.
*   Costs must be **Allocable** (chargeable to the grant in proportion to the benefit received).
*   Alcohol is strictly prohibited.
*   The Organization reserves the right to deny reimbursement for any expense that would result in a disallowed cost audit finding.

#### 4.6 FLSA and Minimum Wage Protection
In accordance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), the Organization ensures that no employee is required to pay for business expenses if doing so would reduce their net earnings below the applicable federal or state minimum wage.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Incurs expenses responsibly; submits expense reports with valid receipts within 30 days; ensures expenses comply with this policy. |
| **Approving Manager** | Reviews expense reports for business necessity and budget alignment; denies non-compliant expenses. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Oversees the reimbursement process; ensures compliance with IRS Accountable Plan and 2 CFR 200; authorizes final payouts. |
| **Human Resources** | Reviews requests for ergonomic furniture or ADA accommodations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—including submitting falsified receipts, claiming personal expenses as business expenses, or failing to return excess advances—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment and legal action for fraud.

Expenses submitted more than **60 days** after being incurred may be rejected or, if reimbursed, processed as taxable income subject to withholding, in accordance with IRS regulations.
