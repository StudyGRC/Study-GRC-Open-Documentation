### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict guidelines regarding the refund or reversal of charitable contributions made to Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). As a tax-exempt 501(c)(3) organization, the Organization holds funds in trust for the public good. This policy balances the Organization’s fiduciary duty to preserve charitable assets with the need to treat donors fairly in the event of genuine administrative errors or fraudulent activity.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all charitable contributions received by the Organization, regardless of the payment method (credit card, ACH, check, cryptocurrency, or stock). It applies to all employees, Board Directors, and volunteers involved in fundraising, financial processing, or donor relations.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to payments made for goods or services (e.g., event tickets or merchandise), which are governed by the *Terms of Sale* and *Consumer Refund Policy*, unless the payment includes a distinct charitable contribution component.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Irrevocable Gift** | A transfer of assets where the donor relinquishes all control and rights to the asset. By law, charitable donations are generally considered irrevocable once accepted. |
| **Administrative Error** | A clerical or technical mistake made by the donor or the Organization (e.g., a typo resulting in a $500 donation instead of $50, or a duplicate transaction). |
| **Restricted Gift** | A donation designated by the donor for a specific purpose or project, accepted by the Organization under the terms of the *Gift Acceptance Policy*. |
| **Chargeback** | A demand by a credit card provider for a retailer to make good the loss on a fraudulent or disputed transaction. |
| **Net Proceeds** | The actual amount of cash received by the Organization after the deduction of third-party processing fees, gas fees (crypto), or brokerage commissions. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principle of Irrevocability
Pursuant to the Organization's status as a 501(c)(3) public charity and the *Nonprofit Purpose* defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, all charitable donations are deemed **irrevocable** upon receipt. The Organization is legally prohibited from returning charitable funds to a donor for "change of mind" or "financial hardship," as this may constitute private inurement or the misuse of public funds.

#### 4.2 Permissible Grounds for Refund
The Organization will only consider refund requests under the following specific circumstances:
1.  **Administrative/Clerical Error:** The donor made a verifiable data entry error (e.g., entered the wrong amount or selected the wrong frequency for a recurring gift).
2.  **Duplicate Processing:** A technical glitch caused a single transaction to be processed multiple times.
3.  **Unauthorized Transaction:** The donor claims the transaction was fraudulent or made without their consent (e.g., stolen credit card).
4.  **Failure of Purpose (Restricted Gifts):** In rare instances where a Restricted Gift cannot be used for its designated purpose, and the donor refuses to re-designate the funds (see Section 4.5).

#### 4.3 Time Limit for Requests
Refund requests based on Administrative Error or Duplicate Processing must be submitted in writing within **thirty (30) days** of the transaction date. Requests made after this window will be reviewed solely at the discretion of the Board of Directors and are generally denied to ensure accurate financial reporting and tax compliance.

#### 4.4 Refund Processing and Verification
*   **Written Request:** All requests must be made in writing (email is acceptable) and include the donor’s name, transaction date, amount, and the reason for the request.
*   **Original Method of Payment:** Refunds will strictly be issued to the original method of payment (e.g., back to the originating credit card). Refunds will never be issued via cash or check for a credit card donation to prevent money laundering.
*   **Deduction of Fees:** For errors caused by the donor, the Organization reserves the right to refund the **Net Proceeds** (donation amount minus processing fees charged to the Organization), ensuring the Organization does not incur a financial loss due to the error.

#### 4.5 Restricted Gifts and Cy Pres
If the Organization accepts a Restricted Gift but subsequently determines that the project is no longer viable or the intent cannot be fulfilled:
1.  The Organization will contact the donor to request a re-designation of the funds to a similar purpose.
2.  If re-designation is refused, the Organization may offer a refund.
3.  If the donor is unreachable, the Board will exercise its powers under the *cy pres* doctrine to utilize the funds for a purpose as close as possible to the original intent, consistent with **Bylaws Article I**.

#### 4.6 Cryptocurrency and Securities Reversals
Due to the volatility of assets such as cryptocurrency and stocks:
*   Refunds for non-cash assets will be calculated based on the **lesser of**:
    1.  The fair market value of the asset at the time of the donation; or
    2.  The **Net Proceeds** realized by the Organization upon liquidation of the asset.
*   The Organization will not cover losses due to market fluctuation. If a donor donates 1 BTC (valued at $50k) and the value drops to $40k before liquidation, the refund is limited to the $40k realized.

#### 4.7 Tax Implications
*   **Donor Responsibility:** It is the donor's sole responsibility to correct their tax filings with the IRS or relevant tax authority if a deduction was claimed for a donation that was subsequently refunded.
*   **Organization Responsibility:** Upon issuing a refund, the Organization will declare the original tax receipt/acknowledgment void. If the refund occurs in a different tax year than the donation, the Organization will issue a Form 1099 or relevant tax document if required by law to treat the refund as income to the recipient.

#### 4.8 Approval Thresholds
Refunds are subject to the following approval hierarchy:
*   **Up to $1,000:** Approved by the Chief Financial Officer (CFO).
*   **$1,001 to $5,000:** Approved by the Executive Director (CEO).
*   **Above $5,000:** Requires approval by the Treasurer or the Board of Directors.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Donor** | Submits refund requests in writing within the designated timeframe; responsible for adjusting their own tax filings. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews refund requests for validity; calculates Net Proceeds; ensures original receipts are voided in the donor database. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Approves mid-level refunds; manages donor relationships during the dispute process. |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews and approves high-value refund requests; reports material refunds to the Board during quarterly meetings. |
| **Board of Directors** | Final authority on refunds falling outside standard policy parameters or exceeding $5,000. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this policy may result in financial loss to the Organization, violation of IRS regulations regarding private inurement, and loss of public trust.

*   **Staff/Volunteers:** Violation of this policy (e.g., issuing unauthorized refunds) may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination and legal action for restitution.
*   **Fraud:** Any suspected fraudulent activity regarding refunds (e.g., collusion to refund a donation while retaining the tax deduction) will be reported to the IRS and relevant law enforcement agencies.
