### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards for physical security controls required to protect Study GRC Inc.’s (the "Organization") people, physical assets, and information systems. As a remote-first organization, this policy addresses the unique challenges of securing distributed assets, the registered office, and temporary physical environments (e.g., events) to prevent unauthorized physical access, damage, and interference with the Organization’s information and information processing facilities.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All employees, Board Directors, Operational Officers, volunteers, and contractors.
*   **Locations:**
    *   The Organization’s Registered Office (as defined in Bylaws Article I, Section 1.2).
    *   Remote Worksites (home offices and co-working spaces) where Organization data is accessed.
    *   Temporary physical sites, including conference venues and meeting rooms used for Organization business.
*   **Assets:** All physical computing hardware, storage media, and hard-copy records owned or leased by the Organization.

**Exclusions:** Public areas of third-party venues not under the exclusive control of the Organization are excluded, though personnel must exercise due care in such environments.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Controlled Area** | Any physical space where sensitive information or critical IT assets are stored or processed, requiring authorized access (e.g., server room, finance office, event registration desk). |
| **Remote Worksite** | A location other than the Registered Office where personnel perform work duties, typically a home office. |
| **Physical Asset** | Tangible property owned by the Organization, including laptops, mobile devices, hard drives, printed files, and networking equipment. |
| **Visitor** | Any individual who is not a current employee, Director, or authorized volunteer of the Organization. |
| **Tailgating** | The act of an unauthorized person following an authorized person into a secured area without proper validation. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles
Physical security is the first line of defense for digital assets. All personnel must adhere to the principle of "Defense in Depth," ensuring that physical barriers complement technical controls.
*   **4.1.1** Physical access to Controlled Areas must be restricted to authorized personnel only.
*   **4.1.2** Physical security controls must be commensurate with the identified risks to the assets protected.

#### 4.2 Registered Office Security
Pursuant to Bylaws Article I, Section 1.2, the Organization maintains a Registered Office.
*   **4.2.1 Access Control:** Keys, access cards, or entry codes to the Registered Office must be issued only to the Executive Director, Board Officers, and specific designees.
*   **4.2.2 Records Storage:** In accordance with Bylaws Article VIII (Records), physical books, records, and minutes stored at the Registered Office must be kept in locked fire-resistant cabinets.
*   **4.2.3 Audit:** A log of issued keys/access cards must be maintained by the Secretary or their designee and audited annually.

#### 4.3 Remote Worksite Security
As a remote-first entity, the Organization relies on the physical security of individual Remote Worksites.
*   **4.3.1 Perimeter Security:** Personnel must ensure their Remote Worksite has basic physical security, including locking doors and windows when the workspace is unoccupied.
*   **4.3.2 Equipment Protection:** Organization-issued devices (laptops, tablets) must not be left unattended in vehicles or public places. If transport is necessary, devices must be concealed in a locked trunk.
*   **4.3.3 Screen Privacy:** Personnel must position screens to prevent observation by unauthorized persons (e.g., family members, guests, or passersby through windows).
*   **4.3.4 Clean Desk:** Personnel must adhere to the *Clean Desk and Clear Screen Policy (IS-POL-030)*, ensuring sensitive hard-copy documents are secured when not in use and shredded prior to disposal.

#### 4.4 Event and Temporary Site Security
During conferences, workshops, or board meetings held at temporary locations:
*   **4.4.1 Equipment Setup:** IT equipment (projectors, laptops, registration terminals) must never be left unattended. Use of Kensington locks or secure storage rooms is mandatory during breaks or overnight.
*   **4.4.2 Access Control:** For private meetings or "Staff Only" areas, physical signage must be posted, and entry monitored.
*   **4.4.3 Youth Protection:** In alignment with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, physical access to areas designated for youth programs must be strictly controlled. Adults not vetted via background checks are prohibited from entering designated youth-only zones without an escort.

#### 4.5 Visitor Management
*   **4.5.1 Identification:** In Controlled Areas (e.g., a rented meeting room or the Registered Office), all visitors must be identified and verified.
*   **4.5.2 Escort Requirement:** Visitors must be escorted by authorized personnel at all times while within a Controlled Area.
*   **4.5.3 Logs:** A visitor log (digital or physical) must be maintained for any access to the Registered Office containing sensitive data archives.

#### 4.6 Asset Delivery and Disposal
*   **4.6.1 Secure Delivery:** Delivery of IT assets must be made to verified addresses. Personnel must confirm receipt immediately.
*   **4.6.2 Secure Disposal:** Physical media and equipment containing storage must be sanitized or destroyed in accordance with NIST SP 800-88 prior to leaving the Organization's possession, as detailed in the *Data Retention and Destruction Policy (GOV-POL-012)*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Owns this policy; defines physical security standards for hardware and remote sites. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures the Registered Office complies with physical security requirements; authorizes access lists. |
| **Event Coordinators** | Responsible for implementing physical security plans (equipment and access) during Organization events. |
| **All Personnel** | Responsible for securing their Remote Worksite, protecting issued assets, and reporting lost/stolen devices immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation.
*   **Lost Assets:** Negligence leading to the theft or loss of Organization physical assets may result in a requirement for the individual to reimburse the Organization for the replacement cost, subject to applicable labor laws.
