### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a standardized framework for reviewing, negotiating, and accepting grant agreements, cooperative agreements, and government contracts. This policy ensures that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") only enters into agreements that are financially viable, legally compliant, and aligned with the Organization’s tax-exempt mission. Specifically, this policy mandates compliance with **2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance)** regarding risk management and cost principles to protect the Organization from unallowable costs, unfunded mandates, and incompatible legal obligations.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all incoming grant awards, subawards, and cooperative agreements from:
*   Federal, state, and local government agencies.
*   Private foundations and corporate giving programs.
*   Pass-through entities issuing subawards to the Organization.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to unrestricted individual donations or standard fundraising gifts, which are governed by the *Gift Acceptance Policy (FIN-POL-058)*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Grant Agreement** | A legal instrument of financial assistance between a Federal awarding agency or pass-through entity and a non-Federal entity. |
| **2 CFR 200** | The "Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards" (Uniform Guidance). |
| **Indirect Costs (F&A)** | Facilities and Administrative costs that are incurred for common or joint objectives and cannot be readily identified with a particular final cost objective. |
| **Cost Sharing (Match)** | The portion of project costs not paid by Federal funds (unless otherwise authorized by Federal statute). |
| **Open Source Mandate** | The Organization's Bylaw-driven requirement (Art. IX, Sec 9.3) that resources remain open-source and free. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Negotiation Authority
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V**, the Executive Director (CEO) is the primary authorized signatory for the Organization. However, the authority to negotiate and sign Grant Agreements is subject to the limits defined in the *Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix*.
*   **Board Approval:** Grant agreements exceeding the financial threshold defined in the DoA Matrix, or those requiring a Fundamental Action (Bylaws Article X), must be ratified by the Board of Directors prior to execution.
*   **Legal Review:** Any Grant Agreement containing non-standard indemnification clauses, intellectual property transfers, or deviation from the Organization's standard indirect cost rate must undergo legal review.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Risk Assessment (Pre-Award)
Before accepting an award, the Operational Leadership must conduct a risk assessment to ensure the Organization can comply with the terms. The assessment must verify:
1.  **Programmatic Capacity:** The Organization has the staffing and technical expertise to deliver the Scope of Work (SOW) within the proposed timeline.
2.  **Financial Feasibility:** The award amount is sufficient to cover Direct Costs and allowable Indirect Costs.
3.  **Remote Work Compatibility:** The SOW permits the Organization's remote-first operational model and does not impose on-site requirements that cannot be met.
4.  **Youth Protection:** The agreement does not conflict with the Organization's *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, particularly regarding data privacy (COPPA) and one-on-one interactions.

#### 4.3 Negotiation of Financial Terms (2 CFR 200 Compliance)
To ensure financial integrity and compliance with **2 CFR 200 Subpart E (Cost Principles)**:
*   **Indirect Cost Recovery:** The Organization must negotiate for the recovery of indirect costs. If the Organization does not have a negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement (NICRA), it must elect to charge the **de minimis rate of 10%** of modified total direct costs (MTDC) as permitted by **2 CFR 200.414(f)**, unless the funder has a statutory cap.
*   **Cost Sharing:** Voluntary committed cost sharing is prohibited unless specifically required by the Federal funding notice (**2 CFR 200.306**). The Organization shall not offer voluntary matching funds during negotiation without express Board Treasurer approval.
*   **Payment Terms:** The Organization must negotiate for advance payments or working capital advances where possible, or net-30 reimbursement terms at a minimum, to maintain cash flow.

#### 4.4 Intellectual Property (IP) Rights
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, the Organization is dedicated to providing open-source resources.
*   **Rights Retention:** The Organization must retain ownership of pre-existing IP (Background IP).
*   **Open Licensing:** Negotiators must reject terms that grant the funder *exclusive* rights to the Work Product that would prevent the Organization from releasing the materials to the public under an open-source license (e.g., CC-BY, MIT), unless the project is classified as "Work for Hire" for a specific commercial contract rather than a grant.
*   **Federal Rights:** The Organization acknowledges the Federal government's standard royalty-free, non-exclusive, and irrevocable right to reproduce, publish, or otherwise use the work for Federal purposes (**2 CFR 200.315**).

#### 4.5 Prohibited Terms (Red Lines)
The Organization shall not sign agreements containing the following terms without a specific resolution from the Board of Directors:
1.  **Unlimited Indemnification:** Clauses requiring the Organization to indemnify the funder for the funder's own negligence.
2.  **Unfunded Mandates:** Requirements to perform work or reporting that are not covered by the budget or allowable indirect costs.
3.  **Political Activity:** Requirements that would force the Organization to engage in prohibited political campaign intervention (violating IRS 501(c)(3) status).
4.  **Discriminatory Practices:** Terms requiring hiring or service delivery practices that violate the Organization's *Equal Employment Opportunity Policy*.

#### 4.6 Subaward vs. Contractor Determination
During negotiation, the Organization must correctly classify its role. If the Organization is receiving funds as a **Subrecipient**, it must ensure the agreement includes all data elements required by **2 CFR 200.332** (e.g., FAIN, CFDA number, indirect cost rate). If the agreement is misclassified as a Contractor (Vendor) agreement, the Organization must negotiate to correct the classification to ensure proper audit treatment.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Lead negotiator; responsible for ensuring the SOW is achievable and signing agreements within DoA limits. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews all budgets for cost allowability, verifies indirect cost rates, and assesses financial risk. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves agreements exceeding DoA thresholds; resolves impasses regarding "Red Line" terms. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews complex agreements for liability, IP, and regulatory compliance issues. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Validates that educational deliverables defined in the grant are pedagogically sound and aligned with the mission. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this policy may result in the acceptance of unfavorable terms that jeopardize the Organization's financial stability or tax-exempt status.
*   **Unauthorized Signatures:** Agreements signed by individuals without proper authority as defined in this policy and the DoA Matrix may be considered voidable by the Organization and may result in disciplinary action against the individual, up to and including termination.
*   **Audit Findings:** Failure to negotiate terms compliant with 2 CFR 200 may result in audit findings and the repayment of disallowed costs.
