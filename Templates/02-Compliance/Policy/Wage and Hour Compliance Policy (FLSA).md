### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") complies fully with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), the Texas Payday Law (Texas Labor Code Chapter 61), and applicable federal grant regulations (2 CFR 200). This policy establishes the standards for employee classification, timekeeping, overtime compensation, and payroll practices to ensure fair and timely compensation for all staff while mitigating legal and financial risks associated with wage and hour violations.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:** All current employees (full-time, part-time, and temporary) of the Organization.
**Exclusions:**
*   **Independent Contractors:** Individuals classified as 1099 contractors are not covered by wage and hour laws but are governed by their specific Service Agreements.
*   **Volunteers:** Individuals serving without expectation of compensation, including Board Directors (unless compensated per Bylaws Article V) and Advisory Board members.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Exempt Employee** | An employee who is paid on a salary basis and meets specific duty tests (Executive, Administrative, Professional, or Computer) under the FLSA, making them ineligible for overtime pay. |
| **Non-Exempt Employee** | An employee entitled to minimum wage and overtime pay for all hours worked over 40 in a workweek. |
| **Workweek** | The Organization’s standard workweek begins at 12:00 a.m. Sunday and ends at 11:59 p.m. Saturday. |
| **Hours Worked** | All time during which an employee is required to be on the Organization’s premises, on duty, or at a prescribed workplace (including authorized remote work locations). |
| **Off-the-Clock Work** | Work performed by a non-exempt employee that is not reported in the timekeeping system. This practice is strictly prohibited. |
| **Texas Payday Law** | Texas Labor Code Chapter 61, which regulates the payment of wages, including frequency, delivery, and deductions. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Employment Classification
The Organization classifies all positions as either "Exempt" or "Non-Exempt" based on the actual duties performed and the salary level, not merely the job title.
*   **Determination:** The Executive Director, in consultation with HR/Legal counsel, determines the classification of each role prior to hiring.
*   **Notification:** Employees will be informed of their FLSA status in their Offer Letter and Job Description.
*   **Safe Harbor:** The Organization prohibits improper deductions from the salaries of Exempt employees. If an Exempt employee believes an improper deduction has occurred, they must report it immediately to the CFO for reimbursement.

#### 4.2 Minimum Wage and Overtime
*   **Minimum Wage:** The Organization compensates all employees at a rate equal to or greater than the federal or state minimum wage, whichever is higher.
*   **Overtime Calculation:** Non-Exempt employees shall receive overtime pay at a rate of 1.5 times their regular rate of pay for all hours worked in excess of 40 hours in a single Workweek.
*   **No Pyramiding:** Paid time off (PTO), holidays, and sick leave do not count as "hours worked" for the purpose of calculating overtime eligibility.

#### 4.3 Remote Work Timekeeping (Non-Exempt)
Given the Organization’s remote-first nature, accurate digital timekeeping is mandatory for Non-Exempt employees.
*   **Recording:** Non-Exempt employees must record their actual start time, end time, and meal breaks daily via the Organization’s designated timekeeping software.
*   **Meal Breaks:** Non-Exempt employees working more than 6 consecutive hours are required to take a minimum 30-minute unpaid, duty-free meal break. Employees must log out during this time.
*   **Prohibition on Off-the-Clock Work:** Non-Exempt employees are strictly prohibited from performing work (including checking emails or Slack messages) outside of logged work hours. Any work performed must be recorded and compensated.
*   **Unauthorized Overtime:** Non-Exempt employees must obtain prior written approval from their supervisor before working overtime. However, if overtime is worked (even without permission), it **must** be recorded and will be paid. Working unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action, but payment will never be withheld.

#### 4.4 Pay Frequency and Delivery
In compliance with Texas Labor Code § 61.011:
*   **Exempt Employees:** Paid at least once per month.
*   **Non-Exempt Employees:** Paid at least twice per month (semi-monthly).
*   **Paydays:** Paydays are established and communicated annually. If a payday falls on a weekend or holiday, payment is issued on the preceding business day.
*   **Method:** Wages are paid via direct deposit. Employees who do not consent to direct deposit will be issued a physical check mailed to their address of record.

#### 4.5 Wage Deductions
In strict compliance with Texas Labor Code § 61.018, the Organization will **not** withhold or divert any part of an employee’s wages unless the deduction is:
1.  Ordered by a court (e.g., child support, garnishment);
2.  Authorized by state or federal law (e.g., IRS tax withholding, FICA); or
3.  **Authorized in writing by the employee** for a lawful purpose (e.g., benefit premiums, 401k contributions).

**Note:** The Organization will not deduct costs for breakage, equipment damage, or unreturned property from a final paycheck unless the employee has signed a specific written authorization allowing such a deduction.

#### 4.6 Final Pay (Separation of Employment)
Pursuant to Texas Labor Code § 61.014:
*   **Involuntary Separation (Termination/Layoff):** Final wages must be paid in full no later than the **6th calendar day** after discharge.
*   **Voluntary Separation (Resignation):** Final wages must be paid no later than the **next regularly scheduled payday**.

#### 4.7 Federal Grant Compliance (Time & Effort)
Because the Organization receives federal funding:
*   Salaries and wages charged to federal awards must be based on records that accurately reflect the work performed.
*   **Personnel Activity Reports (PARs):** Employees funded by federal grants must certify their time distribution (Time & Effort reporting) in accordance with the *Time and Effort Reporting Policy* and 2 CFR 200.430.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the annual budget for compensation and ensures Executive compensation aligns with Bylaws Article V and IRS Intermediate Sanctions rules. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures all job descriptions accurately reflect duties for classification purposes; authorizes overtime when necessary for operations. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Manages payroll processing; ensures compliance with Texas Payday Law deadlines; maintains timekeeping records for audit (minimum 3 years). |
| **Supervisors** | Review and approve timecards for Non-Exempt staff weekly; ensure staff take required breaks; enforce the prohibition on off-the-clock work. |
| **Employees** | Accurately record all hours worked; report any payroll discrepancies immediately; sign necessary deduction authorizations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Supervisors** who encourage or allow off-the-clock work, or who alter employee time records to avoid overtime, are subject to immediate termination.
*   **Employees** who falsify time records (recording more or fewer hours than actually worked) are subject to immediate termination.
*   **Remediation:** Any employee found to have been underpaid due to classification or calculation errors will be issued back pay immediately upon verification of the error.
