### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish clear guidelines for the authorization, performance, and compensation of overtime work for non-exempt employees at Study GRC Inc. This policy ensures compliance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), Texas Labor Code, and 2 CFR § 200.430 (Compensation—Personal Services) regarding federal grant allowability. It is designed to maintain strict budget control while ensuring employees are fairly compensated for all hours worked.

### 2. Scope
*   **Applies to:** All "Non-Exempt" employees of Study GRC Inc., regardless of work location (remote or on-site).
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   "Exempt" employees (Salaried personnel not eligible for overtime under FLSA).
    *   Independent Contractors (compensated based on the terms of their Independent Contractor Agreement).
    *   Volunteers (who perform services without expectation of compensation).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Non-Exempt Employee** | An employee who is subject to the minimum wage and overtime provisions of the FLSA. They are paid an hourly wage or salary and are eligible for overtime pay. |
| **Exempt Employee** | An employee who is not subject to the overtime provisions of the FLSA due to the nature of their job duties (e.g., Executive, Administrative, Professional) and salary level. |
| **Overtime** | Actual hours worked in excess of forty (40) hours in a single standard Workweek. |
| **Workweek** | The Organization's standard seven-day period used for calculating overtime, beginning at 12:00 AM Sunday and ending at 11:59 PM the following Saturday. |
| **Unauthorized Overtime** | Overtime hours worked by a Non-Exempt employee without prior written approval from their direct supervisor. |
| **Off-the-Clock Work** | Work performed by an employee that is not reported on their timesheet. This is strictly prohibited. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Overtime Compensation
In accordance with the FLSA and Texas Labor Code, Non-Exempt employees must be paid one and one-half (1.5) times their regular rate of pay for all hours worked in excess of forty (40) hours in a designated Workweek.
*   Paid Time Off (PTO), holidays, jury duty, or sick leave do not count as "hours worked" for the purpose of calculating the 40-hour overtime threshold.
*   Overtime is calculated based on the Workweek, not the pay period. Hours cannot be averaged over two weeks.

#### 4.2. Mandatory Prior Authorization
To ensure fiscal responsibility and budget adherence, **all overtime work must be authorized in advance.**
*   **Written Request:** Employees must submit a written request (via email or the Organization's timekeeping system) to their direct supervisor detailing the reason for the overtime and the estimated duration.
*   **Supervisor Approval:** The supervisor must provide written approval **before** the work is performed. Supervisors may only approve overtime if sufficient funds exist in the relevant department budget or grant allocation.
*   **Standing Authorization:** In rare operational circumstances, the Executive Director may issue a "Standing Authorization" for specific roles during peak periods (e.g., Annual Conference week), which must be documented in writing with a specific expiration date.

#### 4.3. Emergency Overtime
In the event of a critical emergency (e.g., a cybersecurity breach, server outage, or immediate safety threat to a minor) where prior approval is impossible:
1.  The employee is authorized to perform the necessary work to mitigate the immediate threat.
2.  The employee must notify their supervisor as soon as reasonably possible.
3.  The supervisor must retroactively document the approval and the justification for the emergency variance.

#### 4.4. Remote Work and Digital Communications
Due to the Organization's remote-first nature, boundaries regarding working hours must be strictly observed to prevent accidental overtime accumulation.
*   **Digital Availability:** Non-Exempt employees are not required to check or respond to emails, Slack messages, or other work communications outside of their scheduled work hours.
*   **Compensable Time:** If a Non-Exempt employee reads or responds to work communications outside scheduled hours, this constitutes "work" and must be recorded.
*   **Prohibition:** Managers are prohibited from expecting or requesting Non-Exempt staff to perform work tasks off-hours without authorizing the associated overtime.

#### 4.5. Prohibition of "Off-the-Clock" Work
Study GRC Inc. strictly prohibits "off-the-clock" work. Non-Exempt employees must accurately record **all** time worked.
*   Employees may not "volunteer" their time to perform their normal job duties to avoid overtime limits.
*   Falsifying time records to under-report hours is a violation of this policy and federal law.

#### 4.6. Grant-Funded Positions
For employees whose salaries are funded by federal or state grants:
*   Overtime costs are allowable only if they are authorized by the specific grant agreement and the Organization's internal policies.
*   Supervisors must verify that the grant budget has a specific line item or sufficient flexibility to cover the premium portion (the "half-time") of the overtime payment before approval.

#### 4.7. Compensatory Time ("Comp Time")
As a private non-profit corporation, Study GRC Inc. **does not** utilize "Compensatory Time" (banking hours in lieu of overtime pay) for Non-Exempt employees. All overtime hours worked must be paid out in the applicable pay period.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Non-Exempt Employee** | Monitor total hours worked during the Workweek. Request overtime authorization in writing *before* exceeding 40 hours. Accurately record all hours worked in the timekeeping system. |
| **Supervisor/Manager** | Review workload to minimize the need for overtime. Approve or deny overtime requests based on budget availability and operational necessity. Ensure grant-funded overtime is allowable. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Monitor overall overtime trends against the organizational budget. Audit time records for compliance with 2 CFR 200.430. |
| **Payroll Administrator** | Calculate and process overtime payments accurately based on approved time records. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**The "Pay but Discipline" Rule:**
Under the FLSA, Study GRC Inc. must pay Non-Exempt employees for all overtime hours worked, **even if the overtime was not authorized.** However, working unauthorized overtime is a violation of this policy.

*   **First Violation:** The employee will be paid for the overtime worked but will receive a verbal warning and counseling regarding budget management.
*   **Second Violation:** The employee will be paid for the overtime worked but will receive a written warning.
*   **Continued Violations:** Repeated failure to obtain prior authorization for overtime may result in termination of employment for insubordination and failure to follow financial control policies.

Managers who implicitly or explicitly encourage off-the-clock work, or who fail to manage their team's overtime within budget, are subject to disciplinary action.
