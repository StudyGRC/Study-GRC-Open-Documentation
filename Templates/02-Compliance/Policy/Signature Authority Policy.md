### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a system of internal controls regarding the authority to execute contracts, sign checks, and financially bind Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy is designed to prevent fraud, ensure fiscal responsibility, and maintain compliance with the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC), IRS regulations, and Federal Uniform Guidance (2 CFR 200) regarding internal controls over financial reporting and procurement.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, and contractors of the Organization. It covers all binding legal agreements, including but not limited to:
*   Checks, drafts, and electronic fund transfers (EFT/ACH).
*   Contracts for services, supplies, and software.
*   Grant agreements and reporting certifications.
*   Memoranda of Understanding (MOUs) and partnership agreements.
*   Employment offers and severance agreements.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to non-binding correspondence or routine administrative acknowledgments that do not incur financial or legal liability.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Signature Authority** | The formal power granted to an individual to sign contracts or authorize payments that legally bind the Organization. |
| **Delegation of Authority (DoA)** | The formal transfer of specific decision-making power from the Board to Operational Officers, subject to defined limits. |
| **Dual Signature** | A requirement for two authorized individuals to approve a transaction or sign a document to validate it. |
| **Fundamental Action** | As defined in Bylaws Article X, significant events (mergers, dissolution, asset sales) requiring Member approval. |
| **Structuring** | The prohibited practice of splitting a large transaction into smaller amounts to bypass authorization limits. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Authority
Pursuant to **Article III, Section 3.1** of the Bylaws, the Board of Directors holds the ultimate authority to manage the affairs of the Organization. No individual—regardless of title—has the inherent right to bind the Organization unless authorized by the Bylaws, this Policy, or a specific Board Resolution.

#### 4.2 Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix
The Organization adopts a tiered approach to signature authority. Individuals may only sign documents or authorize expenditures within the limits defined below:

**Tier 1: Board of Directors (Requires Board Vote)**
*   **Threshold:** Transactions > $25,000.
*   **Scope:** Annual Budget approval; Real estate transactions; Litigation settlements; Debt financing; Fundamental Actions (subject to Member vote per Bylaws Article X).
*   **Signature Requirement:** President AND Treasurer (or Secretary).

**Tier 2: Executive Director / CEO**
*   **Threshold:** Transactions up to $25,000.
*   **Scope:** Operational contracts; Grant agreements (within approved strategy); Hiring of staff (within budget); Software licensing.
*   **Signature Requirement:** Executive Director (Single signature allowed up to $5,000; Dual approval required > $5,000).

**Tier 3: Operational Officers (C-Level/VPs)**
*   **Threshold:** Transactions up to $5,000.
*   **Scope:** Department-specific expenses (e.g., VP of Education signing for curriculum tools) strictly within the approved department budget.
*   **Signature Requirement:** Relevant Officer.

#### 4.3 Banking and Disbursements
To prevent embezzlement and fraud, the following controls apply to all bank accounts and payment platforms:

1.  **Dual Authorization:** All checks, wire transfers, and ACH payments exceeding **$5,000** require the signature or digital approval of two (2) authorized signers.
2.  **Self-Dealing Prohibition:** No individual may sign a check or authorize a payment payable to themselves, their immediate family, or a business they control. Reimbursements to the Executive Director must be approved by the Treasurer or President.
3.  **Check Signing:** Blank checks must never be signed.
4.  **Digital Wallets:** Access to corporate credit cards or crypto-wallets is restricted to individuals explicitly authorized by the Treasurer.

#### 4.4 Contract Execution Requirements
Prior to signing any contract, the authorized signer must ensure:
1.  **Budget Availability:** Funds are available in the current approved budget.
2.  **Legal Review:** Contracts with a term longer than **one (1) year**, or involving intellectual property assignment (outside standard employment), must undergo legal review or review by the Board.
3.  **Conflict of Interest:** The signer has verified compliance with the **Conflict of Interest Policy (Bylaws Article IX)**.

#### 4.5 Grant Agreements
Only the **President** or the **Executive Director** may sign Federal or State grant agreements. Acceptance of a grant award constitutes a commitment to comply with 2 CFR 200; therefore, the CFO or Treasurer must review the financial reporting requirements of the grant *before* signature.

#### 4.6 Electronic Signatures
The Organization recognizes electronic signatures as legally binding under the U.S. ESIGN Act and UETA.
*   Signatures must be executed via a secure platform (e.g., DocuSign, Adobe Sign) that provides an audit trail.
*   Pasting an image of a signature into a Word document without an audit trail is **prohibited** for external contracts.

#### 4.7 Prohibited Conduct
*   **Structuring:** Splitting a purchase (e.g., a $30,000 invoice) into multiple smaller invoices (e.g., three $10,000 invoices) to avoid Board approval is strictly prohibited and is grounds for immediate termination.
*   **Unauthorized Commitments:** Any employee who signs a contract without proper authority may be held personally liable for the obligation and subject to disciplinary action.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Signature Authority Policy; Reviews and votes on Tier 1 transactions. |
| **Treasurer** | Conducts monthly reviews of bank statements to verify signature compliance; Authorizes new bank signatories. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Executes Tier 2 transactions; Ensures staff are aware of their limits; Maintains the contract repository. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Maintains the current list of authorized signers; Enforces dual-authorization workflows in accounting software. |
| **Employees** | Must verify authority limits before making verbal or written commitments to vendors. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action. Contracts signed without proper authority may be deemed void by the Organization, and the unauthorized signer may be personally liable for damages.
