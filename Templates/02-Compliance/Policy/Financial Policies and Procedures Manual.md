### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Financial Policies and Procedures Manual is to establish a comprehensive framework for the financial management of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy ensures the Organization maintains the highest standards of financial stewardship, transparency, and accountability in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC), and federal regulations governing non-profit entities.

Specifically, this document is designed to satisfy the financial management system requirements of **2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance)** for federal award recipients, ensuring that all funds are utilized efficiently to advance the Organization's charitable and educational mission as defined in **Article I, Section 1.3** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all financial transactions, assets, liabilities, and funds (restricted and unrestricted) of the Organization. It governs the conduct of the Board of Directors, Officers (including the Treasurer and CFO), employees, volunteers, and contractors who have authority to request, approve, or process financial transactions.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not supersede specific donor restrictions that are more restrictive than these policies, provided such restrictions do not violate legal or ethical standards.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **2 CFR Part 200** | The "Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards" (Uniform Guidance), which governs the management of federal grants. |
| **Internal Controls** | Processes designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives in effectiveness/efficiency of operations, reliability of financial reporting, and compliance with laws. |
| **Segregation of Duties (SoD)** | The principle that no single individual should have control over two or more phases of a transaction or operation (e.g., authorization, custody, record keeping). |
| **Allowable Cost** | A cost that is reasonable, allocable, and consistently treated, as defined by 2 CFR 200 Subpart E. |
| **Capital Asset** | Tangible or intangible assets used in operations having a useful life of more than one year and an acquisition cost exceeding the capitalization threshold ($5,000). |
| **Restricted Funds** | Funds that are limited by a donor-imposed restriction (temporary or permanent) regarding their use or time of availability. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Financial Governance and Ethics
4.1.1 **Fiduciary Duty:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article III**, the Board of Directors holds the ultimate fiduciary responsibility for the Organization.
4.1.2 **Tone at the Top:** Management must demonstrate and communicate a commitment to integrity and ethical values.
4.1.3 **Conflict of Interest:** All financial decisions must adhere strictly to the **Conflict of Interest Policy** (Bylaws Article IX). No Director, Officer, or employee may participate in the selection, award, or administration of a contract if they have a real or apparent conflict of interest.

#### 4.2. Internal Controls (2 CFR § 200.303)
4.2.1 **Segregation of Duties:** To the extent practicable given the Organization's size, duties shall be segregated so that no one individual can initiate, approve, record, and reconcile a transaction.
*   *Mitigating Control:* Where staff size precludes full segregation, the Treasurer or a designated Board member must perform detailed monthly reviews of bank statements and disbursements.
4.2.2 **Access Controls:** Access to financial systems (accounting software, bank accounts) must be restricted based on the Principle of Least Privilege.
4.2.3 **Digital Approvals:** As a remote-first organization, secure digital signatures and auditable workflow approvals (e.g., via email or accounting software) are legally binding and required for all expenditures over **$500**.

#### 4.3. Budgeting and Planning
4.3.1 **Annual Budget:** The CFO, in coordination with the Executive Director, must prepare an annual operating budget.
4.3.2 **Board Approval:** The budget must be reviewed by the Finance Committee (if established) and approved by the Board of Directors prior to the start of the fiscal year (January 1), per **Bylaws Article VIII**.
4.3.3 **Budget Monitoring:** Financial reports comparing actual revenues and expenses to budgeted amounts must be presented to the Board at least quarterly.

#### 4.4. Cash Management and Banking
4.4.1 **Bank Accounts:** The Board of Directors has the sole authority to open or close bank accounts.
4.4.2 **Signatories:**
*   Checks or electronic transfers under **$5,000** require one authorized signer (typically the Executive Director or CFO).
*   Checks or electronic transfers exceeding **$5,000** require two authorized signers (one must be a Board Officer).
4.4.3 **Reconciliations:** Bank accounts must be reconciled monthly by an individual who does not have signature authority over the account. Reconciliations must be reviewed and signed off by the Treasurer or Executive Director.

#### 4.5. Federal Grant Management (2 CFR 200 Subpart E)
4.5.1 **Allowable Costs:** Funds may only be expended if the cost is:
*   **Reasonable:** A "prudent person" would consider the cost appropriate.
*   **Allocable:** The cost benefits the specific award or activity.
*   **Consistent:** Treated consistently across all funding sources.
*   **Documented:** Adequately supported by invoices, receipts, and contracts.
4.5.2 **Time and Effort Reporting:** All employees paid with federal funds must maintain personnel activity reports (PARs) or timesheets that accurately reflect the work performed, in compliance with **2 CFR § 200.430**.
4.5.3 **Indirect Costs:** The Organization will use its negotiated indirect cost rate or the *de minimis* rate of 10% of modified total direct costs (MTDC) as allowed by regulations.

#### 4.6. Procurement
4.6.1 **Standards:** Procurement must be conducted in a manner that provides full and open competition.
4.6.2 **Thresholds (2 CFR § 200.320):**
*   **Micro-purchases (<$10,000):** No quotes required if price is reasonable.
*   **Small Purchases ($10,000 - $250,000):** Rate quotations must be obtained from an adequate number of qualified sources (minimum of two).
*   **Sealed Bids/Competitive Proposals (>$250,000):** Formal RFP process required.

#### 4.7. Financial Reporting and Transparency
4.7.1 **Basis of Accounting:** The Organization shall maintain its financial records on the accrual basis of accounting in accordance with GAAP.
4.7.2 **Annual Financial Report:** Per **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2**, the Board shall annually prepare a report of support, revenue, expenses, and changes in fund balances.
4.7.3 **Public Transparency:** The Annual Financial Report and IRS Form 990 must be made publicly available on the Organization's website.

#### 4.8. Audits
4.8.1 **Single Audit:** If the Organization expends **$750,000** or more in federal awards during a fiscal year, it must undergo a Single Audit in accordance with **2 CFR 200 Subpart F**.
4.8.2 **Independent Audit:** Even if the federal threshold is not met, the Board may elect to commission an independent financial audit or review based on donor requirements or best practices.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the annual budget, financial policies, and reviews financial reports. Designates banking signatories. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides high-level oversight of financial management. Reviews monthly reconciliations. Ensures compliance with **Bylaws Article IV**. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Responsible for the operational execution of the budget. Authorizes expenditures within approval limits. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Manages day-to-day financial operations, bookkeeping, financial reporting, and grant compliance. Reports to the Treasurer for oversight. |
| **Program Managers** | Responsible for managing program-specific budgets and ensuring expenses are allowable and documented. |
| **All Employees** | Responsible for reporting suspected fraud, waste, or abuse in accordance with the Whistleblower Policy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board, and legal action.

**Federal Compliance:** Violation of 2 CFR 200 requirements may result in the disallowance of costs, suspension or debarment from federal funding, and civil or criminal penalties under the False Claims Act.
