### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") operates exclusively for charitable and educational purposes in compliance with Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 501(c)(3). This policy establishes strict prohibitions against **Private Inurement** and **Impermissible Private Benefit** to protect the Organization’s tax-exempt status and ensure that its resources are dedicated solely to the public interest.

This policy operationalizes **Article I, Section 1.4** of the Bylaws, which mandates that no part of the net earnings shall inure to the benefit of private persons.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Individuals:** All Directors, Officers, Operational Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors.
*   **Transactions:** All financial transactions, contracts, compensation arrangements, asset sales/purchases, and intellectual property assignments.
*   **Programs:** The design and execution of all educational and community programs.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Private Inurement** | The transfer of any of the Organization’s income or assets to an "Insider" for less than fair market value. Inurement is absolutely prohibited, regardless of the amount. |
| **Private Benefit** | A benefit conferred upon *any* private individual or entity (insider or outsider) that is more than incidental (qualitatively and quantitatively) to the accomplishment of the Organization's exempt purpose. |
| **Insider (Disqualified Person)** | Any person who is in a position to exercise substantial influence over the affairs of the Organization (e.g., Directors, Officers, key employees, and their family members). |
| **Fair Market Value (FMV)** | The price at which property or services would change hands between a willing buyer and a willing seller, neither being under any compulsion to buy or sell and both having reasonable knowledge of relevant facts. |
| **Incidental Benefit** | A benefit to a private person that is necessary to achieve the charitable purpose (Qualitative) and is insubstantial in amount compared to the public benefit (Quantitative). |
| **Excess Benefit Transaction** | A transaction in which an economic benefit is provided by the Organization to a Disqualified Person that exceeds the value of the consideration (including the performance of services) received for providing such benefit (IRC § 4958). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Absolute Prohibition on Private Inurement
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.4**, the Organization is strictly prohibited from allowing its net earnings to inure to the benefit of any Insider.
*   **Zero Tolerance:** There is no *de minimis* exception for inurement. Even a small amount of inurement can result in the revocation of tax-exempt status.
*   **Prohibited Acts:** Examples of prohibited inurement include, but are not limited to:
    *   Paying dividends or profit shares to Directors or founders.
    *   Paying excessive compensation to an Officer.
    *   Purchasing assets from a Director at a price above FMV.
    *   Selling Organization assets to an Officer at a price below FMV.

#### 4.2 Restriction on Private Benefit
The Organization must serve a public interest, not a private interest. While the Organization may interact with private parties (e.g., vendors, learners), any benefit to private individuals must be **incidental**.
*   **Qualitative Test:** The private benefit must be a necessary byproduct of the charitable activity. (e.g., Paying a vendor FMV to host the website is necessary to educate the public).
*   **Quantitative Test:** The private benefit must be insubstantial compared to the overall public benefit.
*   **Targeted Services:** The Organization shall not tailor its programs or resources specifically to benefit a single private business or a select group of businesses (e.g., creating a certification solely to validate the software of a specific vendor).

#### 4.3 Reasonable Compensation
As authorized by **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.4** and **Article V, Section 5.1**, the Organization may pay reasonable compensation for services rendered.
*   **Determination:** Compensation for employees and contractors must be determined based on appropriate comparability data (e.g., salary surveys for nonprofits of similar size and geography).
*   **Documentation:** The Board or authorized committee must document the basis for the compensation decision *concurrently* with the approval.
*   **Volunteer Prohibition:** Directors serving strictly in a governance capacity shall not receive compensation for their Board service, other than reimbursement for reasonable expenses.

#### 4.4 Intellectual Property and Assets
To prevent private benefit via intellectual property (IP), the Organization strictly enforces **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**.
*   **Work Made for Hire:** All content, code, frameworks, and resources created by Directors, Officers, or volunteers in the course of their service are the sole property of the Organization.
*   **No Reversion:** IP rights shall not revert to the creator upon their departure from the Organization.
*   **Open Source Mandate:** The Organization holds IP rights to ensure resources remain open-source and free. Granting exclusive commercial licenses to a private entity for Organization-owned IP is prohibited if it restricts public access or primarily benefits the licensee.

#### 4.5 Resource Utilization
Organization assets (including funds, equipment, software licenses, donor lists, and the reputation/brand of Study GRC Inc.) shall not be used for personal or private business purposes.
*   **Mailing Lists:** The Organization’s membership and email lists shall not be sold, rented, or given to private entities for commercial solicitation.
*   **Endorsements:** The Organization shall not endorse private products or services in a manner that functions as commercial advertising rather than neutral, educational analysis.

#### 4.6 Excess Benefit Transactions (Intermediate Sanctions)
The Organization shall not engage in any Excess Benefit Transaction as defined by IRC § 4958.
*   **Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness:** To protect against Intermediate Sanctions, the Board must follow the "Rebuttable Presumption" steps for all transactions with Disqualified Persons:
    1.  Approval by an authorized body composed entirely of individuals without a conflict of interest;
    2.  Reliance upon appropriate data as to comparability (FMV); and
    3.  Adequate documentation of the basis for the determination.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ultimate oversight of all financial policies. Approves compensation for Officers and reviews any transaction involving a Disqualified Person. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Ensures all financial transactions are recorded accurately and monitors for potential inurement risks. Maintains documentation of FMV for major contracts. |
| **Governance Committee** | Reviews this policy bi-annually and investigates allegations of private benefit or inurement. |
| **All Personnel** | Responsible for reporting any observed misuse of Organization assets or potential inurement to the Board or via the Whistleblower Policy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Violations of this policy threaten the legal existence of the Organization.
*   **Internal Discipline:** Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (for cause), or termination of volunteer assignment.
*   **Legal Consequences:** Individuals who receive an excess benefit may be personally liable to the IRS for excise taxes (Intermediate Sanctions) of up to 200% of the benefit amount. The Organization may also be subject to excise taxes and revocation of its 501(c)(3) status.
*   **Restitution:** Any individual receiving an unauthorized private benefit or inurement must return the excess benefit (plus interest) to the Organization to correct the transaction.
