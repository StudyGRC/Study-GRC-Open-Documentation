### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and guidelines for determining the allowability of costs charged to federal and state grants, as well as restricted private funding. This policy ensures Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") maintains strict compliance with **2 CFR Part 200 Subpart E (Cost Principles)** of the Uniform Guidance. Adherence to this policy mitigates the risk of audit findings, disallowed costs, and the potential return of funds. This policy supports the financial transparency requirements outlined in **Article VIII** of the Organization’s Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, Board Directors, officers, and contractors who authorize, incur, or record expenses against restricted grant funds. It covers all direct and indirect costs charged to sponsored agreements.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to the expenditure of unrestricted general operating funds, provided such expenditures do not violate the Organization’s 501(c)(3) status or other internal financial policies.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Allowable Cost** | A cost that meets the criteria of being necessary, reasonable, allocable, and consistently treated as defined by 2 CFR 200.403. |
| **Allocable Cost** | A cost that is chargeable to a particular federal award or other cost objective in accordance with relative benefits received (2 CFR 200.405). |
| **Reasonable Cost** | A cost that, in its nature and amount, does not exceed that which would be incurred by a prudent person under the circumstances prevailing at the time the decision was made (2 CFR 200.404). |
| **Direct Cost** | Costs that can be identified specifically with a particular final cost objective, such as a federal award, or other internally or externally funded activity, or that can be directly assigned to such activities relatively easily with a high degree of accuracy. |
| **Indirect (F&A) Cost** | Costs incurred for a common or joint purpose benefitting more than one cost objective, and not readily assignable to the cost objectives specifically benefitted, without effort disproportionate to the results achieved. |
| **Unallowable Cost** | Costs that cannot be charged to a grant, either due to specific regulatory prohibition (e.g., alcohol, lobbying) or failure to meet the criteria of reasonableness or allocability. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Criteria for Allowability
In accordance with **2 CFR 200.403**, for a cost to be allowable under a Federal award, it must meet **all** of the following criteria:
1.  **Necessary and Reasonable:** The cost must be necessary for the performance of the Federal award and be considered reasonable.
2.  **Allocable:** The cost must be allocable to the Federal award.
3.  **Consistent Treatment:** The cost must be treated consistently. A cost may not be assigned to a Federal award as a direct cost if any other cost incurred for the same purpose in like circumstances has been allocated to the Federal award as an indirect cost.
4.  **In Conformity with Law:** The cost must conform to any limitations or exclusions set forth in these principles or in the Federal award as to types or amount of cost items.
5.  **GAAP Compliant:** The cost must be determined in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP).
6.  **Documented:** The cost must be adequately documented (See **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.1**).

#### 4.2 Reasonableness (The "Prudent Person" Test)
A cost is reasonable if, in its nature and amount, it does not exceed that which would be incurred by a prudent person under the circumstances prevailing at the time the decision was made. In determining reasonableness, the Organization shall consider:
*   Whether the cost is of a type generally recognized as ordinary and necessary for the operation of the Organization or the proper performance of the Federal award.
*   The restraints or requirements imposed by such factors as: sound business practices; arm's-length bargaining; Federal, state, local, tribal, and other laws and regulations; and terms and conditions of the Federal award.
*   Market prices for comparable goods or services for the geographic area.

#### 4.3 Allocability
A cost is allocable to a particular Federal award or other cost objective if the goods or services involved are chargeable or assignable to that Federal award or cost objective in accordance with relative benefits received. This standard is met if the cost:
*   Is incurred specifically for the Federal award;
*   Benefits both the Federal award and other work of the Organization and can be distributed in proportions that may be approximated using reasonable methods; and
*   Is necessary to the overall operation of the Organization and is assignable in part to the Federal award in accordance with the Organization's approved Cost Allocation Plan or Indirect Cost Rate.

#### 4.4 Selected Items of Cost (Allowable vs. Unallowable)
The Organization strictly adheres to **2 CFR 200.420 through 200.476** regarding specific items of cost.

**4.4.1 Common Allowable Costs**
*   **Compensation:** Salaries and wages for work performed on the award (must be supported by Time & Effort reporting).
*   **Fringe Benefits:** Employer contributions to social security, employee life, health, unemployment, and worker's compensation, provided they are granted under established written policies.
*   **Travel:** Travel expenses that are consistent with the Organization’s Travel Policy and do not exceed Federal GSA per diem rates.
*   **Materials and Supplies:** Costs for items necessary to carry out the project (e.g., educational kits for K-12 programs).
*   **Equipment:** Special purpose equipment (subject to prior approval thresholds).

**4.4.2 Strictly Prohibited (Unallowable) Costs**
The following costs **must never** be charged to federal grants, either directly or indirectly:
*   **Alcoholic Beverages:** Strictly prohibited (2 CFR 200.423).
*   **Bad Debts:** Losses arising from uncollectible accounts (2 CFR 200.426).
*   **Contributions and Donations:** Cash or property given to other organizations (2 CFR 200.434).
*   **Entertainment:** Costs of entertainment, including amusement, diversion, and social activities (2 CFR 200.438).
*   **Fines and Penalties:** Costs resulting from violations of, or failure of the Organization to comply with, Federal, state, or local laws and regulations (2 CFR 200.441).
*   **Fundraising:** Costs of organized fund raising, including financial campaigns and solicitation of gifts (2 CFR 200.442).
*   **Lobbying:** Costs associated with lobbying activities (2 CFR 200.450).

#### 4.5 Prior Written Approval
Certain costs require prior written approval from the Federal awarding agency to be allowable. The CFO is responsible for identifying these costs and securing approval before expenditure. Examples include:
*   Capital expenditures for general purpose equipment, buildings, and land (2 CFR 200.439).
*   Participant support costs (2 CFR 200.456).
*   Pre-award costs (2 CFR 200.458).

#### 4.6 Documentation Standards
In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII**, all costs charged to grants must be supported by original source documentation (invoices, receipts, payroll records, contracts).
*   Documentation must clearly demonstrate the nexus between the cost and the grant objectives.
*   For remote employees, digital receipts and electronic approvals are acceptable provided they are unalterable and stored in the Organization's central financial system.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves this policy; ensures financial oversight via the Treasurer as per Bylaws Article IV. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Primary owner of this policy. Reviews all grant expenditures for allowability prior to drawdown. Maintains the Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA). |
| **Program Managers** | Responsible for initiating spending requests that are necessary and reasonable for their specific grant programs. Must provide justification for allocability. |
| **Grant Accountant** | Performs the first line of defense review. Verifies that expenses are coded to the correct grant ID and meet 2 CFR 200 criteria before processing payment. |
| **All Employees** | Responsible for submitting accurate expense reports with required documentation and justification. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in the disallowance of costs by the funding agency.
*   **Disallowed Costs:** If a cost is deemed unallowable due to negligence or willful misconduct by an employee, the Organization reserves the right to seek reimbursement from that individual.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations of this policy, particularly those involving fraud or falsification of records, will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment and potential legal action.
