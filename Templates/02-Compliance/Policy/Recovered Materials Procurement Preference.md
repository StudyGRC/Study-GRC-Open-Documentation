### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure Study GRC Inc. complies with federal environmental regulations regarding the procurement of recovered materials. This policy mandates that the Organization gives preference to the purchase of items containing the highest percentage of recovered materials practicable, consistent with maintaining a satisfactory level of competition, as required by **2 CFR § 200.323** and Section 6002 of the Solid Waste Disposal Act.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all procurement actions undertaken by Study GRC Inc. employees, officers, and contractors when utilizing **Federal award funds**.

**Applicability Threshold:**
This policy specifically applies where the purchase price of the item exceeds **$10,000**, or the value of the quantity acquired by the preceding fiscal year exceeded $10,000.

**Exclusions:**
*   Procurements funded entirely by non-federal, unrestricted private donations, unless otherwise mandated by the Board.
*   Procurements where the item is not listed in the EPA Comprehensive Procurement Guidelines (CPG).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Recovered Materials** | Waste materials and by-products that have been recovered or diverted from solid waste, excluding those materials and by-products generated from, and commonly reused within, an original manufacturing process. |
| **EPA CPG** | The Environmental Protection Agency’s Comprehensive Procurement Guideline (40 CFR Part 247), which designates items that are or can be made with recovered materials. |
| **Practicable** | Capable of being performed or done within available means and resources. |
| **Post-Consumer Content** | Material or finished product that has served its intended use and has been diverted or recovered from waste destined for disposal, having completed its life as a consumer item. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Procurement Mandate
Pursuant to the financial stewardship principles outlined in **Article VIII** of the Bylaws, Study GRC Inc. must procure items designated in the EPA Comprehensive Procurement Guidelines (CPG) that contain the highest percentage of recovered materials practicable.

#### 4.2 EPA Designated Categories
Staff making purchases using Federal funds must consult the EPA CPG list prior to procurement. While the CPG covers many categories, as a remote-first technology organization, Study GRC Inc. primarily focuses on:
*   **Paper and Paper Products:** Printing paper, office paper, envelopes, and cardboard packaging.
*   **Non-Paper Office Products:** Binders, clipboards, file folders, plastic desktop accessories, and toner cartridges.
*   **Miscellaneous:** Awards, plaques, and signage.

#### 4.3 Exceptions to the Preference (The "Three Outs")
The preference for recovered materials may be waived **only** if one or more of the following conditions apply. The purchasing officer must document the justification for the waiver in the procurement file:
1.  **Availability:** The item cannot be acquired competitively within a reasonable timeframe.
2.  **Performance:** The item fails to meet reasonable performance standards or specifications necessary for the Organization’s operations.
3.  **Cost:** The item is only available at an unreasonable price (exceeding the cost of a non-recycled alternative by a significant margin, as determined by the CFO).

#### 4.4 Affirmative Procurement Program
To ensure compliance, the Organization maintains an affirmative procurement program that includes:
*   **Preference:** A preference program for purchasing the designated items.
*   **Promotion:** Promotion of the preference program to vendors and contractors.
*   **Estimation:** Procedures for estimating, certifying, and verifying the recovered material content.
*   **Review:** Annual review and monitoring of the effectiveness of the program.

#### 4.5 Solid Waste Management Services
In the unlikely event Study GRC Inc. procures solid waste management services (e.g., for a physical event or temporary office), the Organization must procure services in a manner that maximizes energy and resource recovery.

#### 4.6 Vendor Certification
For purchases exceeding the simplified acquisition threshold, vendors must certify the minimum percentage of recovered materials in the products they supply, in accordance with the specifications of the contract.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Oversees the Affirmative Procurement Program; determines "unreasonable cost" thresholds; conducts annual review of procurement records for compliance. |
| **Purchasing Staff / Requestors** | Consults EPA CPG lists before purchasing; documents justifications if non-compliant items are purchased (e.g., due to cost or delay). |
| **Vendors/Contractors** | Provides certification of recovered material content when required by contract terms. |
| **Internal Auditor** | Verifies compliance with 2 CFR 200.323 during the annual internal audit risk assessment. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in the disallowance of costs by Federal grantors, requiring Study GRC Inc. to reimburse funds from unrestricted reserves.

Internal disciplinary action for violation of this policy may include:
*   Retraining on Federal Grant Compliance.
*   Revocation of purchasing authority or corporate credit card privileges.
*   Termination of employment for repeated or willful negligence resulting in financial loss.
