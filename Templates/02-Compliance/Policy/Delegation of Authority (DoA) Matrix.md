### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix is to establish a clear framework of decision-making authority within Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). It defines the financial and operational limits within which Officers, Directors, and employees may act. This policy ensures:
1.  **Internal Controls:** Compliance with 2 CFR § 200.303 by establishing a control environment that provides reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives.
2.  **Fiscal Responsibility:** Prevention of unauthorized commitments, fraud, or misuse of funds.
3.  **Operational Efficiency:** Empowering operational leadership to act quickly within defined boundaries without requiring Board intervention for routine matters.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the Board of Directors, Advisory Board, Operational Officers (including the Executive Director/CEO), employees, and volunteers. It covers all accounts, assets, and legal obligations of the Organization.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not supersede the specific voting rights of Members regarding "Fundamental Actions" as defined in Article X of the Bylaws.

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles of Authority
4.1.1 **Source of Authority:** All authority resides originally with the Board of Directors (Bylaws Article III). Authority is delegated to the Executive Director (CEO) for operational management (Bylaws Article V), who may further sub-delegate to other Operational Officers.
4.1.2 **No Signature Splitting:** It is strictly prohibited to split a single transaction or contract into multiple smaller parts to bypass authority thresholds (structuring).
4.1.3 **Conflict of Interest:** No individual may approve a transaction, contract, or expense reimbursement that benefits themselves, a family member, or a business in which they have a material interest, per the Conflict of Interest Policy (GOV-POL-005).
4.1.4 **Temporary Delegation:** Authority may be temporarily delegated during absences (e.g., vacation) only via written notice (email is acceptable) to the Treasurer and Secretary.

#### 4.3 Federal Grant Compliance (2 CFR 200)
4.3.1 **Procurement Thresholds:** Regardless of the limits above, all purchases made with Federal funds must adhere to the procurement methods outlined in 2 CFR § 200.320 (Micro-purchase, Small purchase, Sealed bids, Competitive proposals).
4.3.2 **Unallowable Costs:** No individual has the authority to approve expenditures deemed "Unallowable" under 2 CFR § 200 Subpart E (e.g., alcohol, lobbying, bad debts).
4.3.3 **Budget Revisions:** Per 2 CFR § 200.308, the Organization must obtain prior written approval from the Federal awarding agency for specified budget revisions (e.g., change in scope, change in key personnel). The CEO must secure Board approval before submitting such requests.

#### 4.4 Check Signing and Electronic Payments
4.4.1 **Dual Authorization:** All checks, wire transfers, or ACH payments exceeding **$5,000** require two authorized signatures/approvals.
4.4.2 **Authorized Signers:** Authorized signers are typically the President, Treasurer, and Executive Director. The Secretary shall maintain the bank signature cards.
4.4.3 **Prohibition:** An individual cannot authorize a payment to themselves. Reimbursements to the CEO must be approved by the Treasurer or President.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the DoA Matrix, Annual Budget, and transactions exceeding CEO limits. Reviews financial reports to ensure compliance. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Primary operational decision-maker. Ensures all staff are aware of their authority limits. Approves expenditures within the $25,000 threshold. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Oversees the financial health of the organization. Verifies that funds are available before approvals are granted. Monitors compliance with this policy. |
| **Department Heads** | Exercise authority within defined limits (Level 4). Ensure all expenses are substantiated with proper documentation (receipts/invoices). |
| **Secretary** | Maintains the official record of Board resolutions authorizing specific individuals to act on behalf of the Organization. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
6.1 **Unauthorized Actions:** Any contract entered into or expense incurred by an individual in excess of their authority as defined in this matrix may be deemed void by the Organization. The individual may be held personally liable for the financial obligation.
6.2 **Disciplinary Action:** Failure to comply with this policy, including "splitting" transactions to avoid thresholds, will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board/Officer role.
6.3 **Audit:** This policy is subject to annual review by the Audit Committee or Finance Committee to ensure alignment with organizational growth and 2 CFR 200 updates.
