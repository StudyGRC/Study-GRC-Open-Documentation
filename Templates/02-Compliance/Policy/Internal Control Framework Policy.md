### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a comprehensive system of internal controls for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") to provide reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives in the following categories: effectiveness and efficiency of operations, reliability of reporting for internal and external use, and compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

This policy explicitly satisfies the requirements of **2 CFR § 200.303**, mandating that non-federal entities receiving federal awards must establish and maintain effective internal control over the federal award that provides reasonable assurance that the non-federal entity is managing the federal award in compliance with federal statutes, regulations, and the terms and conditions of the federal award.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all aspects of the Organization’s financial and operational activities.
*   **Applies to:** The Board of Directors, Operational Officers (CEO, CFO, CISO, etc.), employees, volunteers, and contractors.
*   **Exclusions:** None. This framework encompasses all funds, including federal grants, private donations, and fee-for-service revenue.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Internal Control** | A process, effected by the Board of Directors, management, and other personnel, designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the achievement of objectives relating to operations, reporting, and compliance. |
| **COSO Framework** | The *Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission* Internal Control-Integrated Framework, widely accepted as the standard for internal control design. |
| **Segregation of Duties (SoD)** | The principle that no single individual should have control over two or more phases of a transaction or operation (Authorization, Custody, Record Keeping, and Reconciliation). |
| **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)** | Federal regulations (Office of Management and Budget) establishing uniform administrative requirements, cost principles, and audit requirements for federal awards. |
| **Material Weakness** | A deficiency, or combination of deficiencies, in internal control, such that there is a reasonable possibility that a material misstatement of the entity's financial statements will not be prevented, or detected and corrected, on a timely basis. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Adoption of the COSO Framework
In accordance with best practices cited in 2 CFR § 200.303, the Organization adopts the **COSO Internal Control—Integrated Framework**. The internal control system must address the five interrelated components:
1.  **Control Environment:** The "Tone at the Top."
2.  **Risk Assessment:** Identification and analysis of relevant risks.
3.  **Control Activities:** Policies and procedures that ensure management directives are carried out.
4.  **Information and Communication:** Capture and exchange of information.
5.  **Monitoring Activities:** Assessment of control system performance over time.

#### 4.2 Control Environment ("Tone at the Top")
*   **4.2.1 Integrity and Ethics:** All Directors, Officers, and staff must adhere to the *Code of Ethics and Conduct* and *Conflict of Interest Policy* (Bylaws Article IX).
*   **4.2.2 Board Oversight:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article III**, the Board of Directors retains ultimate responsibility for the internal control system. The Board must exercise oversight of the development and performance of internal control.
*   **4.2.3 Organizational Structure:** The Executive Director (CEO) must establish reporting lines, authorities, and responsibilities in pursuit of objectives, as outlined in **Bylaws Article V**.

#### 4.3 Risk Assessment
*   **4.3.1 Annual Assessment:** The Organization must perform a risk assessment at least annually to identify and analyze risks to the achievement of its objectives.
*   **4.3.2 Scope of Risk:** This assessment must consider internal and external factors, including fraud risk, cybersecurity threats (managed by the CISO per **Bylaws Section 5.2**), and compliance with federal statutes.
*   **4.3.3 Change Management:** The Organization must identify and assess changes that could significantly impact the system of internal control (e.g., rapid growth, new grant awards, remote work technology changes).

#### 4.4 Control Activities
*   **4.4.1 Segregation of Duties (SoD):** Duties must be divided among different people to reduce the risk of error or inappropriate actions. No single individual shall have control over the entire lifecycle of a transaction.
    *   *Example:* The individual who approves a purchase (Authorization) cannot be the same individual who signs the check (Custody) or reconciles the bank statement (Record Keeping).
*   **4.4.2 Authorization and Approval:** Transactions must be authorized only by persons acting within the scope of their authority as defined in the *Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix*.
*   **4.4.3 Digital Controls:** Given the Organization's remote-first nature, all electronic approvals must utilize secure, auditable systems (e.g., DocuSign, accounting software workflows) that verify identity and prevent retroactive alteration.
*   **4.4.4 Safeguarding Assets:** Access to equipment, inventories, cash, and other assets is restricted to authorized personnel. The CISO is responsible for safeguarding digital assets and data privacy.

#### 4.5 Information and Communication
*   **4.5.1 Financial Reporting:** The Organization must generate and provide relevant, quality financial information to support the functioning of internal control. Per **Bylaws Article VIII**, the Board must receive regular financial reports.
*   **4.5.2 Whistleblower Protection:** The Organization must maintain a mechanism for personnel to communicate information downstream, across, and upstream (including to the Board) regarding potential improprieties without fear of retaliation.

#### 4.6 Monitoring Activities
*   **4.6.1 Ongoing Evaluations:** Management must perform ongoing evaluations (e.g., monthly bank reconciliations, budget-to-actual variance analysis) to ascertain whether the components of internal control are present and functioning.
*   **4.6.2 Separate Evaluations:** The Board (or Audit Committee) may request periodic internal or external audits to evaluate the effectiveness of controls.
*   **4.6.3 Remediation:** Deficiencies must be communicated in a timely manner to parties responsible for taking corrective action, including senior management and the Board of Directors.

#### 4.7 Federal Award Compliance (2 CFR 200.303)
Specific to federal grants, the Organization must:
1.  Take prompt action when instances of noncompliance are identified including noncompliance identified in audit findings.
2.  Take reasonable measures to safeguard protected personally identifiable information (PII) and other information the Federal awarding agency or pass-through entity designates as sensitive.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Provides governance, oversight, and final approval of the Internal Control Framework. Reviews financial reports and audit findings. |
| **Audit Committee** | (If established per Bylaws Art. VI) Acts on behalf of the Board to oversee the internal control structure and external audit process. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Holds primary operational responsibility for the internal control system. Sets the "Tone at the Top" alongside the Board. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Designs, implements, and monitors specific control activities (SoD, reconciliations). Reports on control effectiveness to the Treasurer/Board. |
| **Chief Info. Security Officer (CISO)** | Designs and monitors IT General Controls (ITGC), access controls, and data protection measures. |
| **Employees/Volunteers** | Responsible for adhering to established control procedures and reporting suspected control deficiencies or fraud. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action.

**Material Weaknesses:** Failure to maintain effective internal controls that results in a Material Weakness or Significant Deficiency in a Single Audit may jeopardize the Organization's tax-exempt status and eligibility for future federal funding.
