### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the ownership, licensing, and distribution standards for all educational materials, software code, and creative works produced by or for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization").

Consistent with the Organization’s mission to remove financial barriers to Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) knowledge, this policy operationalizes the commitment to Open Source principles while protecting the Organization’s legal standing and intellectual property rights as defined in **Article IX, Section 9.3** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Individuals:** All Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers (collectively "Contributors").
*   **Assets:** All curriculum, slide decks, study guides, software code, diagrams, audio/video recordings, and white papers created for the Organization.

**Exclusions:**
*   Intellectual property created by a Contributor entirely on their own time, without the use of the Organization’s equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secret information, and which does not relate directly to the Organization’s business or actual/demonstrably anticipated research.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Organizational Ownership
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, the Organization retains exclusive legal ownership of all Work Product created by Contributors during their engagement.
*   **Assignment:** All Contributors must execute a *Work Made for Hire Assignment* or *Volunteer Service Agreement* explicitly assigning all copyright, patent, and trademark rights to Study GRC Inc. prior to commencing work.
*   **Rationale:** Centralized ownership ensures the Organization retains the legal authority to enforce open-source licensing and prevent third parties from locking the community out of these resources.

#### 4.2 Open Source Licensing Strategy
To fulfill the charitable purpose of democratizing GRC knowledge, the Organization mandates the following licensing standards for public release:

*   **Educational Content (Text, Media, Curriculum):** Unless otherwise authorized by the Board, all educational materials shall be released to the public under the **CC BY-NC-SA 4.0** license.
*   **Software and Code:** All software, scripts, and technical tools developed by the Organization shall be released under the **MIT License** to maximize interoperability and adoption.
*   **Dual Licensing:** The Board reserves the right to dual-license materials (e.g., offering a commercial license to for-profit entities) to generate revenue to support the nonprofit mission, provided the open-source version remains available.

#### 4.3 Third-Party Intellectual Property
Contributors must strictly adhere to copyright laws when developing content.
*   **Prohibition on Infringement:** Contributors must not incorporate third-party copyrighted material (images, text, code) into Work Product without explicit written permission or a valid license (e.g., Unsplash, Pexels, or compatible CC licenses).
*   **Fair Use:** Reliance on "Fair Use" doctrine must be reviewed and approved by the VP of Education & Training or Legal Counsel prior to publication.
*   **Attribution:** All third-party assets used must be properly cited and attributed in the "References" or "Credits" section of the Work Product.

#### 4.4 Moral Rights and Attribution
While the Organization retains legal ownership, Study GRC Inc. recognizes the moral rights of its Contributors.
*   **Credit:** The Organization shall make reasonable efforts to list primary authors and contributors on major publications, courseware, and code repositories.
*   **Portfolio Use:** Contributors retain a non-exclusive, perpetual right to display Work Product they created in their personal professional portfolios, provided such display does not violate confidentiality or security policies.

#### 4.5 Youth Protection in Content
Given the Organization’s K-12 focus, all Work Product must be reviewed for age-appropriateness.
*   **PII Prohibition:** Educational content must never contain the Personally Identifiable Information (PII) of any minor.
*   **Imagery:** Photos or videos of minors may only be used if a valid *Parental Consent and Media Release Form* is on file, in accordance with the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**.

#### 4.6 Brand Protection
While the content is open source, the Organization’s trademarks (logos, name, and brand identity) are **not**.
*   Third parties using our open-source content must not use the Study GRC Inc. logo in a way that suggests endorsement or official affiliation without a separate trademark license or Chapter Agreement.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the overall IP strategy and authorizes any exceptions to the standard licensing models (e.g., commercial partnerships). |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all curriculum developers have signed IP assignments; reviews content for copyright compliance; selects appropriate license for release. |
| **Contributors** | Create original work; disclose any third-party content used; sign IP assignment agreements. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Provides guidance on Fair Use; manages trademark registrations; defends against IP infringement claims. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Internal:** Failure to sign IP assignment agreements may result in disqualification from volunteer service or termination of employment. Unauthorized use of third-party copyrighted material may result in disciplinary action.
*   **External:** The Organization reserves the right to pursue legal action against entities that violate the terms of the Creative Commons or MIT licenses (e.g., failing to attribute, or selling NC-licensed content without permission).
