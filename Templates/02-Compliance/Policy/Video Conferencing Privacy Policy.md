### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish mandatory security and privacy standards for the use of video conferencing technologies within Study GRC Inc. As a remote-first organization, video conferencing is our primary venue for collaboration. This policy aims to prevent unauthorized access ("Zoombombing"), protect the privacy of members and minors, ensure compliance with data protection laws (including GDPR and COPPA), and safeguard sensitive organizational information discussed during virtual sessions.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Employees, Directors, Officers, Volunteers, Contractors, and Members (collectively "Users") when hosting or participating in video conferences on behalf of Study GRC Inc.

**Inclusions:**
*   Board Meetings (pursuant to Bylaws Article III).
*   Committee and Working Group meetings.
*   Educational webinars and training sessions.
*   Internal staff and volunteer coordination meetings.

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal video calls made by Users on personal devices that do not involve Study GRC Inc. business, data, or personnel.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Approved Platform** | Video conferencing software formally vetted and authorized by the CISO (currently Zoom and Google Meet). |
| **Host** | The User who schedules the meeting and possesses administrative control over the session (e.g., muting, removing participants). |
| **Zoombombing** | The unwanted, disruptive intrusion into a video conference call by an individual, often resulting in the display of offensive or malicious content. |
| **Waiting Room** | A virtual staging area that prevents participants from joining a meeting until admitted individually by the Host. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information (e.g., names, addresses, ID numbers). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Approved Platforms
To ensure consistent security configurations, Users must only use **Zoom** or **Google Meet** for official Study GRC Inc. business.
*   **4.1.1** The use of consumer-grade tools (e.g., personal Skype, Facebook Messenger Video, WhatsApp) for discussing sensitive organizational data or conducting formal Board business is prohibited.
*   **4.1.2** All Hosts must use Study GRC Inc. provisioned accounts (e.g., `@studygrc.org`) rather than personal email accounts when hosting official meetings to ensure retention and security controls are active.

#### 4.2 Meeting Security Configuration
All meetings must adhere to the "Secure by Default" principle. Hosts are responsible for ensuring the following settings are active:
*   **4.2.1 Access Control:** All meetings must utilize either a unique **Meeting Password/Passcode** or a **Waiting Room**. Publicly posting direct meeting links with embedded passwords on social media is prohibited unless the meeting is a public webinar configured with registration requirements.
*   **4.2.2 Screen Sharing:** Screen sharing must be restricted to "Host Only" by default. The Host may grant sharing privileges to specific participants during the session as needed.
*   **4.2.3 Participant Verification:** For Board Meetings and Voting Member sessions, the Host must verify the identity of attendees against the membership roll in accordance with **Bylaws Section 2.6 (Inspection of Voting List)** and **Section 3.5 (Quorum of Directors)** before admitting them from the Waiting Room.

#### 4.3 Recording and Consent
While Texas is a "one-party consent" state, Study GRC Inc. adopts a higher standard of transparency to build community trust.
*   **4.3.1 Notification:** The Host must verbally notify all attendees *before* recording begins. The video platform's native "Recording in Progress" visual and audio disclaimer must be enabled and never disabled.
*   **4.3.2 Right to Opt-Out:** Participants who do not wish to be recorded must be permitted to turn off their camera and microphone or leave the meeting.
*   **4.3.3 Youth Protection (Strict Prohibition):** Recording meetings where minors (individuals under 18) are present is **strictly prohibited** unless:
    *   Explicit written consent has been obtained from the parent/guardian via the *Parental Consent and Liability Waiver*; AND
    *   The recording is for a specific educational purpose approved by the VP of Education & Training.
*   **4.3.4 Board Executive Sessions:** Recording of Board Executive Sessions is strictly prohibited to maintain attorney-client privilege and confidentiality regarding personnel or legal matters.

#### 4.4 Privacy of Environment
*   **4.4.1 Virtual Backgrounds:** Users are encouraged to use blurred backgrounds or official Study GRC Inc. virtual backgrounds to protect the privacy of their home environments and prevent the accidental display of sensitive information visible in the physical background.
*   **4.4.2 Camera Optional:** Unless necessary for identity verification (e.g., during a Board vote) or specific instructional requirements, requiring participants to have their cameras on is prohibited to respect neurodiversity and privacy.

#### 4.5 Data Retention and Sharing
*   **4.5.1 Storage:** Cloud recordings must be stored only within the Organization’s managed Google Workspace or Zoom Enterprise storage. Downloading recordings to personal devices is prohibited without CISO approval.
*   **4.5.2 Retention Period:** Routine meeting recordings shall be retained for **365 days** and then automatically deleted, unless subject to a Legal Hold (see *Legal Hold Notification Procedure*).
*   **4.5.3 External Sharing:** Links to recordings containing internal business discussions must not be shared publicly. Recordings of public webinars may be published to the Transparency Page or LMS only after review for accidental PII leakage.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Meeting Host** | Enforce security settings (Waiting Rooms, Passwords), manage participant conduct, and obtain consent prior to recording. |
| **CISO** | Configure global admin settings for Zoom/Google Workspace (e.g., enforcing password complexity, default encryption) and audit configuration logs. |
| **VP of Education** | Approve specific instances where recording of educational sessions involving minors is educationally necessary and legally cleared. |
| **Participants** | Maintain the confidentiality of meeting links and passwords; conduct themselves according to the *Code of Ethics*. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (pursuant to **Bylaws Section 3.6**), or revocation of membership.

**Specific Violations:**
*   Recording a minor without parental consent is a critical violation and will result in immediate suspension pending investigation.
*   Sharing internal meeting links on public forums ("leaking") is considered a breach of the *Non-Disclosure Agreement*.
