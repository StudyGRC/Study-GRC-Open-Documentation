### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that the Board of Directors of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") maintains the highest standards of governance, financial oversight, and strategic leadership. As a Texas non-profit corporation operating in the complex domains of Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC), cybersecurity, and youth education, it is imperative that Directors continuously update their skills to exercise their Duty of Care effectively. This policy establishes the requirements for ongoing professional development to mitigate organizational risk and ensure regulatory compliance.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   All Voting Members of the Board of Directors (Supporting and Honorary Members elected to the Board).
*   The Board Secretary (regarding record-keeping).

**Exclusions:**
*   Members of the Advisory Board (unless specific training is mandated by the Board).
*   Operational Officers (covered under HR Professional Development policies), unless they simultaneously serve as Voting Directors.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Continuing Education Unit (CEU)** | A measure used in continuing education programs to assist the professional to maintain their license in their profession. For the purpose of this policy, one (1) CEU equals one (1) hour of participation in an organized learning activity. |
| **Duty of Care** | The legal obligation of a Director to act with the care that an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would exercise under similar circumstances (Texas Business Organizations Code § 22.221). |
| **Mission-Critical Domain** | Subject matter directly related to the Organization's specific operations, including Cybersecurity, Data Privacy (GDPR/COPPA), and Youth Protection. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Mandatory Education Requirement
To remain in good standing, every Voting Director must complete a minimum of **four (4) hours (CEUs)** of governance-related continuing education per fiscal year.

#### 4.2 Core Competency Areas
Directors must select educational activities that enhance their ability to govern the Organization. Acceptable topics include, but are not limited to:

*   **4.2.1 Non-Profit Governance:** Board roles and responsibilities, Robert's Rules of Order, strategic planning, and Texas non-profit law.
*   **4.2.2 Financial Oversight:** Understanding IRS Form 990, non-profit accounting standards, federal grant compliance (2 CFR 200), and audit committee functions.
*   **4.2.3 Mission-Critical Domains:** Trends in GRC, cybersecurity risk management, data privacy laws (TDPSA, CCPA), and youth protection/safeguarding.
*   **4.2.4 Ethics and Compliance:** Conflict of interest management, whistleblower protections, and diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) in leadership.

#### 4.3 Delivery Methods
Education credits may be obtained through:
*   External workshops, seminars, or conferences (in-person or virtual).
*   Live or recorded webinars from reputable providers (e.g., BoardSource, National Council of Nonprofits, legal firms).
*   Internal training sessions organized by the Governance Committee or invited subject matter experts.
*   Self-study (e.g., reading governance books or whitepapers), capped at one (1) hour of the annual requirement.

#### 4.4 New Director Orientation
In addition to the annual requirement, newly elected Directors must complete the **Board Member Orientation Program** within the first sixty (60) days of their term. This orientation does not count toward the four (4) hour annual continuing education requirement for the first year.

#### 4.5 Budget and Reimbursement
The Organization recognizes that education requires resources.
*   The Board shall allocate a specific line item in the annual budget for Board Development.
*   Directors may request reimbursement for reasonable registration fees and materials related to approved training, subject to the **Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy**.
*   Expenditures exceeding $250 per Director per event require prior approval from the Treasurer or President.

#### 4.6 Reporting and Accountability
*   Directors are responsible for tracking their own CEUs and submitting a **Continuing Education Attestation Form** to the Secretary by December 31st of each year.
*   The Governance Committee will review compliance annually prior to the nomination process for term renewals.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Individual Director** | Proactively identifies relevant training, completes the required hours, and submits proof of completion to the Secretary. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains a log of completed CEUs for all Directors; reports non-compliance to the Governance Committee. |
| **Governance Committee** | Curates a list of recommended training resources; reviews policy compliance; considers education history during the re-nomination process. |
| **Treasurer** | Ensures adequate funds are budgeted for Board development and reviews reimbursement requests for compliance. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to meet the continuing education requirements defined in this policy may be considered a failure to perform the duties of the office.

*   **Notification:** The Secretary will notify any Director who has not met the requirement by November 1st of the fiscal year.
*   **Consequence:** Persistent failure to comply may be cited as "Cause" for removal under **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6 (Removal of Directors)** or may result in the Governance Committee declining to nominate the Director for a subsequent term.
