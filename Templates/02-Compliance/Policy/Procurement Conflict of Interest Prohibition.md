### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict standards of conduct governing the performance of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") employees, officers, and agents engaged in the selection, award, and administration of contracts. This policy is specifically designed to satisfy the requirements of **2 CFR 200.318(c)** (Uniform Guidance) regarding federal grant compliance, ensure the integrity of the Organization’s procurement process, and prevent personal gain at the expense of the Organization’s charitable mission.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all **Directors, Officers, Employees, Volunteers, and Agents** of the Organization who participate in the selection, award, or administration of contracts or purchases.

**Scope Inclusions:**
*   Procurement of goods, services, and supplies.
*   Selection of contractors, subrecipients, and vendors.
*   Administration of contracts supported by both unrestricted funds and Federal awards.

**Scope Exclusions:**
*   General governance conflicts not related to the expenditure of funds (covered under *Bylaws Article IX*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Conflict of Interest (Real)** | A situation where an individual has a financial or other interest in, or a tangible personal benefit from, a firm considered for a contract. |
| **Conflict of Interest (Apparent)** | A situation where a reasonable person, with knowledge of the relevant facts, would question the impartiality of the individual involved in the procurement process. |
| **Immediate Family** | Includes a spouse, domestic partner, children, parents, siblings, in-laws, and any other individual residing in the same household as the covered individual. |
| **Financial Interest** | Any monetary interest, including but not limited to salary, consulting fees, equity interests (stock, stock options), or intellectual property rights. |
| **Nominal Value** | Unsolicited items of insignificant monetary value, defined by this Organization as having a fair market value of **less than $25.00 USD**. |
| **Agent** | Any person acting on behalf of the Organization, including third-party contractors or consultants authorized to procure goods or services. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Prohibition on Participation
In accordance with **2 CFR 200.318(c)(1)**, no employee, officer, or agent may participate in the selection, award, or administration of a contract supported by a Federal award (or general Organization funds) if he or she has a real or apparent conflict of interest.

#### 4.2 Existence of a Conflict
A conflict of interest exists when the employee, officer, or agent, or any member of their **Immediate Family**, their partner, or an organization which employs or is about to employ any of the parties indicated herein, has a **Financial Interest** in or a tangible personal benefit from a firm considered for a contract.

#### 4.3 Prohibition on Gratuities and Kickbacks
Officers, employees, and agents of the Organization must neither solicit nor accept gratuities, favors, or anything of monetary value from contractors, potential contractors, or parties to subcontracts.

*   **4.3.1 Exception for Nominal Value:** The Organization permits the acceptance of unsolicited items of **Nominal Value** (under $25.00), such as conference pens, notepads, or modest refreshments during a business meeting, provided they are not presented with the intent to influence a decision.
*   **4.3.2 Cash Prohibition:** The acceptance of cash, gift cards, or cash equivalents of *any* amount is strictly prohibited.

#### 4.4 Duty to Disclose
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX (9.1)**, any individual with a potential real or apparent conflict regarding a procurement action must disclose this interest in writing immediately upon becoming aware of the conflict.
*   Disclosures must be submitted to the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or, in the case of the CFO, to the Board Treasurer.
*   Disclosures must be logged in the *Interested Party Transaction Review Protocol*.

#### 4.5 Mandatory Recusal
Upon disclosure of a conflict:
1.  The conflicted individual must **recuse** themselves entirely from the procurement process.
2.  They may not participate in the drafting of specifications, the evaluation of bids, the negotiation of terms, or the final authorization of the contract.
3.  They must leave the meeting (virtual or physical) during any discussion or vote regarding the transaction.

#### 4.6 Organizational Conflicts of Interest
In compliance with **2 CFR 200.318(c)(2)**, if the Organization has a parent, affiliate, or subsidiary relationship that is not a state, local government, or Indian tribe, the Organization must also maintain written standards of conduct covering organizational conflicts of interest. Relationships with affiliates must be managed such that the Organization is not unable or appears to be unable to be impartial in conducting a procurement action involving a related organization.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employees & Agents** | Must identify and disclose potential conflicts prior to engaging in any procurement activity. Must refuse prohibited gratuities. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews disclosures, determines if a conflict requires recusal, and maintains the record of recusals. Reports significant conflicts to the Board. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and adjudicates conflicts involving Operational Officers or other Directors in accordance with *Bylaws Article IX*. |
| **Grant Manager** | Ensures all procurement documentation (checklists, scorecards) includes signed non-conflict certifications from evaluators. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy is a violation of both Organizational governance and Federal law.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board, or termination of the volunteer/agent relationship.
*   **Legal Consequences:** Violations involving Federal funds may result in the suspension or debarment of the individual or the Organization from receiving future Federal awards.
*   **Reporting:** The Organization is required to report any violations of Federal criminal law involving fraud, bribery, or gratuity violations potentially affecting the Federal award to the relevant Federal awarding agency or pass-through entity (2 CFR 200.113).
