### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict guidelines regarding the supervision of minors in restrooms, locker rooms, and changing areas during Study GRC Inc. sponsored events. This policy is designed to minimize the risk of sexual, physical, or emotional abuse, prevent peer-to-peer misconduct, and protect adult volunteers and staff from false allegations of misconduct. This policy aligns with the Organization's commitment to "Two-Deep Leadership" and compliance with Texas Family Code regarding child welfare.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Study GRC Inc. Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, contractors, and event staff (collectively "Personnel") interacting with minors (individuals under the age of 18) during in-person events, workshops, conferences, or gatherings hosted or sponsored by the Organization.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to restrooms located within private hotel rooms where a minor is staying with their legal guardian, provided no other unrelated Personnel are present.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18 years. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | The mandatory presence of at least two Organization-approved, background-checked adults during any activity involving minors. |
| **Restroom/Changing Area** | Any facility designated for toileting, showering, or changing clothes, including single-stall restrooms, multi-stall restrooms, and locker rooms. |
| **Auditory Supervision** | Supervision where an adult is positioned outside a facility within hearing distance to monitor for distress or misconduct without visually intruding on privacy. |
| **One-on-One Contact** | A situation where a single adult is alone with a single minor, which is strictly prohibited by the Organization. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles of Supervision
4.1.1 **Prohibition of One-on-One Contact:** Under no circumstances shall an adult Personnel member be alone with a minor in a restroom or changing area.
4.1.2 **Privacy Respect:** Personnel must respect the privacy of minors while maintaining a safe environment. Visual supervision should only be utilized when absolutely necessary for health and safety; otherwise, Auditory Supervision is the standard.
4.1.3 **Device Usage:** The use of cameras, cell phones, tablets, or any recording devices by Personnel within restrooms or changing areas is strictly prohibited.

#### 4.2 Multi-Stall Facilities (Restrooms/Locker Rooms)
4.2.1 **Occupancy:** Whenever possible, separate facilities should be designated for adults and minors. If separate facilities are not feasible:
*   Personnel should not occupy a multi-stall restroom at the same time as minors.
*   If Personnel must use the facility simultaneously, they must announce their presence upon entry.
4.2.2 **Supervision Protocol:**
*   If a group of minors is using a multi-stall facility, two background-checked Personnel must conduct Auditory Supervision by standing immediately outside the main entrance.
*   Personnel may only enter the facility to intervene in cases of physical altercation, medical emergency, or suspected abuse. In such cases, two Personnel must enter together.

#### 4.3 Single-Stall Facilities
4.3.1 **Usage:** Only one person may occupy a single-stall facility at a time.
4.3.2 **Assistance:** If a minor requires assistance in a single-stall facility (e.g., a young child or a participant with a disability), the door must remain propped open, and two unrelated Personnel must be present at the doorway to provide assistance while maintaining maximum possible privacy for the minor.

#### 4.4 Changing Areas and Locker Rooms
4.4.1 **Two-Deep Requirement:** If minors are changing clothes (e.g., changing into event t-shirts or costumes), two Personnel must be present.
4.4.2 **Positioning:** Personnel must position themselves to provide supervision of the general area to prevent peer-to-peer abuse (bullying, hazing, sexual misconduct) while avoiding direct visual observation of minors in a state of undress.
4.4.3 **Private Changing:** Minors seeking additional privacy must be permitted to use a single-stall restroom or a private stall within the locker room.

#### 4.5 Emergency Health Situations
4.5.1 In the event of a medical emergency occurring within a restroom or changing area, the health and safety of the minor is paramount.
4.5.2 Personnel must immediately summon a second adult before entering a stall or private area to assist a minor, unless the threat to life is imminent.
4.5.3 Any incident requiring an adult to enter a stall or private area to assist a minor must be documented immediately using the *Incident Report Form*.

#### 4.6 Reporting Violations
4.6.1 Any observation of suspicious behavior, policy violations, or peer-to-peer misconduct in these areas must be reported immediately to the Event Director.
4.6.2 Pursuant to Texas Family Code Chapter 261, any Personnel who has cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect must report the suspicion to the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) or law enforcement.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Event Director** | Ensures appropriate facilities are identified during venue selection; ensures signage is clear; designates specific restrooms for minors vs. adults where feasible. |
| **Volunteers/Staff** | Adhere strictly to the Two-Deep Leadership and Auditory Supervision rules; refrain from using devices in these areas; report suspicious activity. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Oversees the implementation of this policy; manages incident reports related to youth protection; coordinates with legal counsel if reporting is required. |
| **Parents/Guardians** | Retain primary responsibility for assisting their own children with toileting or changing needs during public events where parents are present. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy is a critical violation of Study GRC Inc.’s governance standards.
*   **Volunteers/Staff:** Violations will result in immediate removal from the event, suspension of duties, and potential termination of the volunteer/employment relationship.
*   **Legal Action:** If a violation involves suspected abuse or illegal conduct, Study GRC Inc. will cooperate fully with law enforcement and file all mandatory reports as required by Texas law.
