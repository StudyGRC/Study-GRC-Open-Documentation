### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to formally establish the fiscal year for Study GRC Inc. in accordance with Article VIII, Section 8.3 of the Bylaws. This policy standardizes the Organization’s financial reporting cycle, budgeting timeline, audit schedule, and tax compliance deadlines to ensure consistent financial governance and alignment with federal grant reporting requirements.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Financial Reporting:** All internal and external financial statements.
*   **Budgeting:** The annual operating budget and capital expenditure planning.
*   **Tax Compliance:** IRS Form 990 filings and state-level reporting.
*   **Grant Management:** Alignment of federal award periods with organizational accounting.
*   **Personnel:** The Board of Directors, Officers, Finance Department, and Budget Managers.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fiscal Year (FY)** | A one-year period that companies and governments use for financial reporting and budgeting. |
| **Calendar Year** | The period beginning on January 1 and ending on December 31. |
| **Accrual Basis** | An accounting method where revenue or expenses are recorded when a transaction occurs rather than when payment is received or made. |
| **Hard Close** | The finalization of financial records where no further entries can be made for a specific period. |
| **Grant Year** | The 12-month period defined by a specific grant agreement, which may or may not align with the Organization's Fiscal Year. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Fiscal Year Designation
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3**, the Fiscal Year of Study GRC Inc. shall run concurrently with the calendar year.
*   **Start Date:** January 1 (00:00:00 CST)
*   **End Date:** December 31 (23:59:59 CST)

#### 4.2 Accounting Period Structure
For the purposes of interim reporting and grant compliance, the Fiscal Year is divided into four standard quarters:
*   **Q1:** January 1 – March 31
*   **Q2:** April 1 – June 30
*   **Q3:** July 1 – September 30
*   **Q4:** October 1 – December 31

#### 4.3 Accounting Method
Study GRC Inc. shall utilize the **Accrual Basis** of accounting for all financial reporting to ensure compliance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance) requirements for federal grantees.

#### 4.4 The Financial Calendar and Critical Deadlines
The Organization must adhere to the following annual financial rhythm to ensure governance obligations are met:

| Activity | Deadline / Timing | Regulatory/Bylaw Reference |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Budget Approval** | Approved by Board prior to **Jan 1** | Bylaws Art. III (Governance) |
| **Annual Meeting Financials** | Preliminary Report presented in **January** | Bylaws Art. II, Sec 2.5 |
| **Year-End Hard Close** | **January 31** (of the following year) | Internal Control |
| **IRS Form 1099 Filing** | **January 31** | IRS Regulation |
| **Audit Fieldwork** | **February – April** | 2 CFR 200.501 |
| **IRS Form 990 Filing** | **May 15** (unless extension filed) | IRC § 6033 |
| **Annual Financial Report** | Published to Transparency Page by **June 30** | Bylaws Art. VIII, Sec 8.2 |

#### 4.5 Grant Year vs. Fiscal Year Alignment
Study GRC Inc. acknowledges that federal grant awards (e.g., from Department of Education or NSF) may operate on a different cycle (e.g., Federal Fiscal Year: Oct 1 – Sept 30).
*   **Tracking:** The accounting system must maintain segment/class codes to track grant-specific expenditures independently of the Organization’s Fiscal Year.
*   **Reporting:** Financial reports for grantors must be generated based on the *Grant Year*, while the Organization’s audited financial statements remain on the *Fiscal Year*.

#### 4.6 Changing the Fiscal Year
Per **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3**, the Fiscal Year may only be changed by a formal resolution of the Board of Directors.
*   If the Board votes to change the Fiscal Year, the Organization must file **IRS Form 1128** (Application to Adopt, Change, or Retain a Tax Year) and receive approval before implementing the change.
*   A "Short Year" tax return must be filed for the transition period.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Annual Budget prior to the start of the Fiscal Year. Authorizes any changes to the Fiscal Year definition. |
| **Treasurer** | Ensures the Board receives timely financial reports based on the Fiscal Year schedule. Reviews the Annual Financial Report before publication. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Manages the closing of books (monthly/annually). Ensures tax filings (Form 990) align with the Fiscal Year. Manages the audit process. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Submits the proposed budget for the upcoming Fiscal Year to the Board for review in Q4. |
| **Grant Managers** | Monitors grant spending to ensure alignment with both the Fiscal Year (for internal budget) and Grant Year (for donor reporting). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to the Fiscal Year calendar may result in:
*   **Regulatory Penalties:** IRS fines for late filing of Form 990 ($20 to $100 per day).
*   **Loss of Status:** Automatic revocation of 501(c)(3) status for failure to file for three consecutive years.
*   **Grant Non-Compliance:** Potential suspension or debarment from federal funding for failure to submit timely audits (Single Audit).

Internal violations of this policy (e.g., failure to close books on time) may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
