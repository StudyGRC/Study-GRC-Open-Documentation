### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a standardized, transparent, and merit-based framework for the identification, vetting, appointment, and oversight of Operational Officers within Study GRC Inc. This policy ensures compliance with Article V of the Bylaws and aligns operational leadership with the Organization’s mission to foster a community-driven GRC ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **The Board of Directors:** Regarding their authority to appoint and remove Operational Officers.
*   **Operational Officers:** Individuals serving in executive leadership roles managing day-to-day operations.
*   **Candidates:** Individuals under consideration for Operational Officer positions.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to the election of Board Officers (President, Secretary, Treasurer) which is governed by Article IV of the Bylaws, nor does it apply to general staff, volunteers, or contractors below the Officer level.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Operational Officer** | An individual appointed by the Board to manage specific day-to-day functions of the Organization. Includes the Executive Director (CEO), CFO, CISO, and others defined in Section 4.2. |
| **Dual Service** | The condition wherein an individual serves simultaneously as a Voting Director on the Board and as an Operational Officer. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | The primary Operational Officer responsible for the general management of the Organization's affairs, reporting directly to the Board. |
| **Reasonable Compensation** | Payment for services that does not exceed the fair market value of those services, determined in accordance with IRS regulations and the Organization's Conflict of Interest Policy. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Appointment Authority
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.1**, the Board of Directors holds the exclusive authority to appoint Operational Officers. Appointments require an affirmative vote of a majority of Directors present at a meeting where a quorum is established.

#### 4.2 Designated Operational Roles
The Board may appoint individuals to the following roles. The Board reserves the right to create or dissolve operational titles as business needs dictate, provided they do not conflict with Board Officer roles defined in Bylaws Article IV.

*   **Executive Director / Chief Executive Officer (CEO):** Primary operational leader.
*   **Vice President of Education & Training:** Oversees curriculum, certification, and learning resources.
*   **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG):** Oversees community culture, inclusion, and outreach.
*   **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO):** Oversees technology infrastructure, data privacy, and security compliance.
*   **Chief Financial Officer (CFO):** Oversees bookkeeping and financial management (dotted-line reporting to the Treasurer).

#### 4.3 Terms of Service
*   **Duration:** Operational Officers are appointed for a term not to exceed **two (2) years**.
*   **Renewability:** Terms are renewable indefinitely upon Board approval. There are no term limits for Operational Officers.
*   **At-Will Status:** Appointment as an Officer does not create a contract of employment for a specified duration. Officers may be removed at any time, with or without cause, subject to the procedures in Section 4.8.

#### 4.4 Qualification and Vetting
To ensure the integrity of the Organization, particularly given its K-12 and youth-focused programs:
*   **Background Checks:** All Operational Officer candidates must pass a comprehensive background check (Level 2) prior to appointment, in accordance with the *Background Check Policy*.
*   **Conflict of Interest:** Candidates must execute a Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statement prior to appointment.
*   **Skills Assessment:** Candidates must demonstrate professional competency relevant to the specific domain (e.g., CISO candidates must possess relevant security certifications or equivalent experience).

#### 4.5 Reporting Structure
*   **CEO Reporting:** The Executive Director (CEO) reports directly to the Board of Directors.
*   **Officer Reporting:** All other Operational Officers report directly to the Executive Director (CEO) for day-to-day management, while maintaining accountability to the Board for fiduciary and regulatory compliance.
*   **Advisory Board Restriction:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VI, Section 6.2**, Operational Officers are strictly prohibited from formally communicating with the Advisory Board without prior approval and facilitation by a Board Director.

#### 4.6 Dual Service (Board and Operations)
A member of the Board of Directors may simultaneously serve as an Operational Officer. In such cases:
*   **Recusal:** The individual must recuse themselves from any Board vote regarding their own compensation, performance evaluation, or removal as an Operational Officer.
*   **Distinct Duties:** The individual must clearly distinguish between their governance role (fiduciary oversight) and their operational role (execution).

#### 4.7 Compensation
Operational Officers may serve as volunteers or receive compensation.
*   **Volunteer Service:** Officers serving without pay are entitled to reimbursement for reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of their duties, subject to the *Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy*.
*   **Paid Service:** Any compensation must be approved by the Board in advance. The Board must rely on comparable market data to ensure compensation is "reasonable" under IRS 501(c)(3) regulations.

#### 4.8 Removal and Vacancy
*   **Removal by Board:** The Board may remove any Operational Officer at any time by a majority vote if, in their judgment, the best interests of the Organization will be served thereby.
*   **Removal by Member Petition:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.3**, Voting Members may petition for the removal of an Operational Officer. Removal via this method requires an affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3)** of all Voting Members.
*   **Resignation:** An Officer may resign at any time by delivering written notice to the Board or the CEO.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves all Operational Officer appointments; sets compensation; conducts annual performance review of the CEO. |
| **Governance Committee** | Vets candidates for Officer roles; ensures background checks are completed; recommends compensation packages to the Board. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Nominates candidates for other Operational Officer roles (e.g., CISO, CFO) for Board consideration; manages day-to-day performance of other Officers. |
| **Secretary** | Records all appointments, resignations, and removals in the corporate minutes; maintains the current roster of Officers. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—specifically regarding the unauthorized appointment of officers, failure to conduct background checks, or violation of Advisory Board communication protocols—may result in disciplinary action against the responsible parties, up to and including removal from the Board or termination of employment/volunteer status.
