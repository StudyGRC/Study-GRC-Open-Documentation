### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to encourage the professional development of Study GRC Inc. employees by providing financial assistance for eligible educational coursework. This policy aligns with the Organization’s mission to foster a community-driven ecosystem advancing governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) knowledge. Furthermore, this policy is structured to comply with **Internal Revenue Code (IRC) Section 127** regarding Qualified Educational Assistance Programs, allowing for tax-free benefits up to the statutory limit, and **2 CFR § 200.472** regarding training and education costs for federal grantees.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all **Full-Time Employees** of Study GRC Inc. who have completed their initial probationary period.

**Exclusions:**
*   Part-time employees working fewer than 30 hours per week.
*   Independent Contractors, Consultants, and Temporary Staff.
*   Volunteers and Interns.
*   Members of the Board of Directors (unless simultaneously employed as an Operational Officer per Bylaws Article V).

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Eligibility Requirements
To be eligible for tuition reimbursement, an employee must:
1.  Be a full-time employee in **Good Standing** (not currently on a Performance Improvement Plan or subject to disciplinary action).
2.  Have completed at least **six (6) months** of continuous service prior to the start date of the course.
3.  Secure written pre-approval from their direct supervisor and the CFO prior to enrolling in the course.

#### 4.2 Financial Limits and Tax Implications
4.2.1 **Annual Cap:** Study GRC Inc. will reimburse up to **$5,250 per calendar year** per eligible employee for tuition, mandatory fees, and required textbooks. This aligns with the excludable income limit defined in IRC Section 127.
4.2.2 **Budget Availability:** Reimbursement is subject to the Organization’s annual budget availability. Approval is not guaranteed and is granted on a first-come, first-served basis.
4.2.3 **Taxability:** Amounts reimbursed up to $5,250 are generally tax-free. Any reimbursement exceeding this amount (if authorized by the Board in exceptional cases) will be treated as taxable income and subject to withholding.

#### 4.3 Course Qualification Criteria
Courses must meet one of the following criteria:
1.  Directly relate to the employee’s current job description.
2.  Relate to a future position within the Organization for which the employee is being groomed (succession planning).
3.  Be part of a degree program (Associate’s, Bachelor’s, Master’s, or Doctorate) in a field relevant to the Organization’s mission (e.g., Cybersecurity, Law, Business Administration, Education).

**Prohibited Costs:** Reimbursement will **not** be provided for:
*   Parking fees, transportation, or meals.
*   Optional materials (software, non-required books).
*   Late fees or installment plan interest.
*   Courses taken for sports, hobbies, or recreation.

#### 4.4 Performance and Completion Standards
Reimbursement is contingent upon successful completion of the course.
*   **Undergraduate Courses:** Grade of "C" or better.
*   **Graduate Courses:** Grade of "B" or better.
*   **Pass/Fail Courses:** A "Pass" grade.
*   **Certification Exams:** Proof of passing the exam and obtaining the certification.

#### 4.5 Federal Grant Funding Restrictions
Pursuant to **2 CFR § 200.472**, training and education costs are allowable only if they are related to the field of service. However, tuition reimbursement for a degree program is generally **not** a direct cost chargeable to a specific federal grant unless explicitly approved by the awarding agency. Therefore, tuition reimbursement is typically paid from the Organization’s unrestricted operating funds (indirect cost pool).

#### 4.6 Repayment Obligation (Clawback)
To ensure the Organization receives a return on its investment, employees agree to a service commitment. If an employee voluntarily resigns or is terminated for cause, they must repay reimbursed amounts based on the following schedule:
*   **0 - 12 Months** from reimbursement date: **100% Repayment**.
*   **12 - 24 Months** from reimbursement date: **50% Repayment**.
*   **24+ Months** from reimbursement date: **0% Repayment**.

By accepting reimbursement, the employee authorizes Study GRC Inc. to deduct outstanding repayment amounts from their final paycheck, to the extent permitted by Texas Labor Code.

#### 4.7 Intellectual Property
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, any projects, code, or research produced during the course of study that are subsequently used by Study GRC Inc. or integrated into the Organization's open-source resources shall be considered "Work Product" and owned by the Organization.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Submits pre-approval requests; pays for tuition upfront; submits final grades and receipts for reimbursement; adheres to repayment obligations. |
| **Direct Supervisor** | Reviews applications to ensure coursework is relevant to the employee’s role and professional development goals. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Verifies budget availability; confirms eligibility; processes reimbursement; ensures tax compliance (W-2 reporting if applicable). |
| **Human Resources** | Maintains records of reimbursement agreements; tracks retention periods for clawback enforcement. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Fraud:** Falsification of grades, receipts, or enrollment status constitutes fraud and will result in immediate termination of employment and legal action to recover funds.
*   **Failure to Repay:** Failure to repay owed tuition upon separation may result in the debt being sent to collections or pursued through civil litigation.
*   **Audit:** All reimbursement records are subject to internal and external audit to ensure compliance with IRS regulations and federal grant cost principles.
