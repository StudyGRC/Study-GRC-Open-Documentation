### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Master Environmental, Health, and Safety (EHS) Policy is to establish the overarching framework for protecting the physical and psychological well-being of all individuals associated with Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization").

As a Texas Non-Profit Corporation committed to ethical governance, the Organization acknowledges its legal and moral obligation to provide a workplace and community environment free from recognized hazards. This policy is drafted to explicitly satisfy the **General Duty Clause of the Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970 (OSHA)** and to align with the Organization’s Bylaws regarding the prudent management of affairs.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All employees, officers, Board Directors, and volunteers.
*   **Contractors:** Independent contractors and vendors while engaged in Organization business.
*   **Locations:**
    *   **Remote Worksites:** Home offices or co-working spaces where Organization business is conducted (limited to the designated work area).
    *   **Physical Events:** Conferences, workshops, and meetups hosted or sponsored by the Organization.
    *   **Travel:** Business travel authorized by the Organization.
*   **Beneficiaries:** K-12 students and learners participating in Organization programs (specifically regarding physical safety).

**Exclusions:** This policy does not cover domestic activities within a remote worker's home that are unrelated to assigned work duties.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **General Duty Clause** | Section 5(a)(1) of the OSH Act, requiring employers to furnish a place of employment free from recognized hazards that are causing or are likely to cause death or serious physical harm. |
| **Remote Worksite** | The specific area within an employee or volunteer's residence or chosen location designated for the performance of official duties. |
| **Recognized Hazard** | A danger that is known to the employer or the industry, is likely to cause harm, and can be feasiblely eliminated or reduced. |
| **Incident** | An unplanned event that results in personal injury, property damage, or environmental harm. |
| **Near Miss** | An unplanned event that did not result in injury, illness, or damage but had the potential to do so. |
| **Event Venue** | Any physical location (hotel, school, convention center) temporarily occupied by the Organization for programming. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Commitment to General Duty
In accordance with the OSHA General Duty Clause, Study GRC Inc. shall furnish to each employee and volunteer a place of employment which is free from recognized hazards that are causing or are likely to cause death or serious physical harm. Management must integrate EHS considerations into all business decisions, including budget planning and event scheduling.

#### 4.2 Remote Work Environment Safety
Recognizing the Organization’s "Remote-First" operating model, the Organization extends safety standards to home offices to the extent feasible.
*   **4.2.1 Self-Assessment:** All remote employees must complete an annual *Remote Worksite Safety Self-Assessment* (HR-FRM-005) to identify electrical, ergonomic, or environmental hazards.
*   **4.2.2 Equipment Provision:** Per the *Remote Work Equipment Provision Policy*, the Organization may provide or reimburse for ergonomic equipment to prevent repetitive strain injuries (RSI) or musculoskeletal disorders.
*   **4.2.3 Boundaries:** Liability for workers' compensation is strictly limited to injuries occurring in the designated workspace during agreed-upon working hours.

#### 4.3 Physical Event Safety
For all in-person gatherings, workshops, or conferences:
*   **4.3.1 Venue Vetting:** No venue may be booked without a documented *Event Venue Safety Inspection* (OPS-CHK-010) verifying fire exits, ADA accessibility, and structural safety.
*   **4.3.2 Emergency Action Plans (EAP):** Every physical event must have a specific EAP communicated to all staff and volunteers prior to the start of the event.
*   **4.3.3 Capacity Limits:** Event leads must strictly adhere to venue occupancy limits set by the local Fire Marshal.

#### 4.4 Youth Protection and Physical Safety
Given the Organization’s mission involving K-12 education:
*   **4.4.1 Supervision:** Physical activities involving minors must adhere to the *Two-Deep Leadership Policy* (OPS-POL-020) to ensure adequate supervision and prevent physical accidents or abuse.
*   **4.4.2 Hazardous Materials:** The use of chemicals, sharp tools, or hazardous equipment in educational workshops is prohibited unless a specific *Risk Assessment* is approved by the Executive Director.

#### 4.5 Incident Reporting and Investigation
*   **4.5.1 Mandatory Reporting:** Any work-related injury, illness, or significant near miss must be reported to the Executive Director or Human Resources within 24 hours using the *Accident/Injury Reporting Form* (OPS-FRM-001).
*   **4.5.2 Investigation:** The Organization will investigate all reported incidents to determine root causes and implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence.
*   **4.5.3 OSHA Recordkeeping:** The Organization shall maintain OSHA 300 Logs if required based on employee count and industry classification, in compliance with 29 CFR 1904.

#### 4.6 Prohibition of Retaliation
Pursuant to the *Whistleblower Protection Policy* (GOV-POL-005), no employee or volunteer shall be discharged, discriminated against, or retaliated against for reporting a safety concern, requesting an inspection, or exercising their rights under the OSH Act.

#### 4.7 Alcohol and Substance Use
To maintain a safe environment, the possession, use, or being under the influence of illegal drugs or unauthorized alcohol during work hours or while supervising minors is strictly prohibited. Limited alcohol consumption at social events for adults must comply with the *Event Alcohol Policy* and local laws.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ultimate oversight of EHS compliance; ensures adequate resources are budgeted for safety equipment and insurance (Bylaws Art. III). |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Acts as the primary Safety Officer; approves EHS policies; ensures compliance with OSHA reporting requirements. |
| **Managers / Dept Heads** | Ensure staff have completed safety training; review remote work self-assessments; enforce safety rules at events. |
| **Event Leads** | Conduct venue inspections; brief attendees on emergency exits; manage on-site incidents. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Follow all safety rules; report hazards immediately; maintain a safe remote workspace. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the Organization and its community at risk.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from volunteer service.
*   **Legal Liability:** Failure to adhere to safety standards may result in the denial of indemnification under **Article VII** of the Bylaws if the individual is found to have acted with gross negligence or willful misconduct.
*   **Regulatory Fines:** The Organization reserves the right to seek restitution from individuals whose willful negligence results in regulatory fines (e.g., OSHA citations).
