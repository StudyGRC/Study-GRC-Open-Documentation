### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the requirements for the implementation, management, and operation of the Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system at Study GRC Inc. As a remote-first, cloud-native organization handling sensitive data (including youth data and federal grant information), centralized visibility into network and system activity is critical. This policy ensures that Study GRC Inc. maintains continuous monitoring capabilities to detect, analyze, and respond to security threats in compliance with NIST CSF (Detect Function) and 2 CFR 200.303 (Internal Controls).

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Information Technology (IT) assets, cloud environments, networks, and applications owned, leased, or operated by Study GRC Inc.

**In Scope:**
*   Cloud Infrastructure (e.g., AWS, Azure, GCP).
*   SaaS Applications (e.g., Google Workspace, Slack, GitHub, Financial Systems).
*   Identity and Access Management (IAM) systems.
*   Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) logs from organization-issued devices.
*   Network security appliances (virtual firewalls, VPN concentrators).

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal devices (BYOD) are excluded from direct log ingestion unless specific "Work Profile" data is containerized and managed via Mobile Device Management (MDM) in accordance with the *BYOD Policy*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **SIEM** | Security Information and Event Management. A solution that aggregates and analyzes activity from many different resources across the entire IT infrastructure. |
| **Log** | A record of the events occurring within an organization’s systems and networks. |
| **Event** | Any observable occurrence in a system or network (e.g., a user logging in, a file being accessed). |
| **Alert** | A notification generated by the SIEM when an event or series of events triggers a specific rule indicating a potential security incident. |
| **Correlation** | The process of relating different events to each other to identify patterns that may indicate a security threat. |
| **Tuning** | The process of adjusting SIEM rules to reduce false positives and ensure high-fidelity alerting. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Mandatory Implementation
Study GRC Inc. must maintain a centralized SIEM solution. This system serves as the "single pane of glass" for security monitoring. All Critical and High-risk systems, as defined in the *Data Classification Standard*, must be configured to forward logs to the SIEM.

#### 4.2. Log Ingestion Requirements
To ensure adequate visibility, the following log sources must be ingested into the SIEM:
*   **Authentication & Authorization:** Successful and failed login attempts, MFA challenges, and privilege escalation events (e.g., Okta, Google Workspace logs).
*   **Cloud Infrastructure:** API trails (e.g., AWS CloudTrail), security group changes, and instance creation/deletion.
*   **Endpoint Security:** Malware detections, quarantine events, and unauthorized software installation alerts from the EDR solution.
*   **Network Activity:** VPN connection logs (metadata), firewall deny logs, and remote access sessions.
*   **Data Access:** Access logs related to repositories containing Personally Identifiable Information (PII) of minors or sensitive federal grant financial data.

#### 4.3. Monitoring and Alerting
*   **Continuous Monitoring:** The SIEM must operate 24/7/365.
*   **Automated Alerting:** The CISO must configure the SIEM to generate immediate alerts for high-severity indicators of compromise (IoC), including but not limited to:
    *   Impossible travel (geographic anomalies).
    *   Brute force attacks.
    *   Ransomware behavior patterns.
    *   Unauthorized creation of administrator accounts.
*   **Review Cadence:** Security Operations personnel must review high-priority alerts within four (4) hours of generation during business hours, and strictly adhere to the *Incident Response Plan* for after-hours critical alerts.

#### 4.4. Rule Tuning and Maintenance
*   **False Positive Reduction:** The CISO is responsible for the continuous tuning of correlation rules to minimize alert fatigue.
*   **Rule Review:** SIEM detection rules must be reviewed and tested at least quarterly to ensure they remain effective against evolving threat landscapes (e.g., new attack vectors against non-profits).

#### 4.5. Data Retention and Protection
*   **Retention Period:** In alignment with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy* and *2 CFR 200.334*, SIEM logs must be retained for a minimum of one (1) year.
    *   **Hot Storage:** 90 days (immediately searchable).
    *   **Cold Storage:** 9 months (archived for audit/forensics).
*   **Log Integrity:** The SIEM must be configured to prevent the modification or deletion of ingested logs (Write Once, Read Many - WORM compliance) to preserve the chain of custody for legal evidence.
*   **Access Control:** Access to the SIEM console and data is restricted to the IT Security Team and authorized Legal/Compliance officers. Access is granted on a Principle of Least Privilege basis.

#### 4.6. Incident Response Integration
The SIEM must be integrated into the organization's Incident Response workflow. Confirmed security incidents detected via the SIEM must automatically trigger the creation of a ticket in the IT Service Management (ITSM) system and notify the Incident Response Team.

#### 4.7. System Health Monitoring
"Watching the Watcher": The SIEM infrastructure itself must be monitored for health issues, including agent failure, cessation of log flow, or storage capacity limits. Failure of a critical log source to report for more than 24 hours must be treated as a high-priority operational incident.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Accountable for the overall SIEM strategy, budget approval, and ensuring alignment with NIST CSF. Defines the "Critical" log sources. |
| **Security Operations (SecOps) Team** | Responsible for daily monitoring, alert triage, rule tuning, and incident escalation. |
| **System Administrators / DevOps** | Responsible for configuring servers, cloud instances, and applications to forward logs to the SIEM agent/collector. |
| **Legal / Compliance Officer** | Reviews log retention settings to ensure compliance with federal grant requirements and Bylaws Article VIII. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation.

Intentional disabling of log forwarding agents or tampering with audit trails is considered a severe violation of the *Code of Ethics* and will result in immediate termination and referral to law enforcement.
