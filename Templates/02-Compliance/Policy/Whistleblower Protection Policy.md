### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to encourage and enable employees, volunteers, and other stakeholders to raise serious concerns internally so that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") can address and correct inappropriate conduct and actions. This policy establishes a confidential channel for reporting and strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who reports a violation in good faith, ensuring compliance with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002, the Texas Whistleblower Act, and IRS Form 990 governance standards.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all "Covered Individuals," defined as:
*   **Board of Directors and Officers** (as defined in Bylaws Article III and IV).
*   **Operational Leadership** (as defined in Bylaws Article V).
*   **Employees** (full-time, part-time, and temporary).
*   **Volunteers** (including Committee members and Advisory Board members).
*   **Contractors and Vendors** providing services to the Organization.

**Scope of Reportable Conduct:** This policy covers the reporting of:
*   Financial improprieties, accounting fraud, or internal control failures.
*   Legal violations (federal, state, or local laws).
*   Ethical violations regarding the Organization’s Bylaws or Code of Ethics.
*   Safety issues, including but not limited to Youth Protection concerns.
*   Harassment, discrimination, or abuse of authority.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Whistleblower** | Any Covered Individual who discloses information regarding a violation or suspected violation of laws, regulations, or Organization policies. |
| **Retaliation** | Any adverse action taken against a Whistleblower because they made a report. This includes discharge, demotion, suspension, threats, harassment, or discrimination. |
| **Good Faith** | A sincere belief that the information reported is true, even if the investigation later reveals that the reporter was mistaken. |
| **Adverse Employment Action** | Any action that materially affects the terms and conditions of employment or volunteer service, including termination, reduction in pay, or exclusion from team activities. |
| **Compliance Officer** | The individual designated by the Board to administer this policy. Unless otherwise appointed, this role defaults to the **Chair of the Audit & Risk Committee**. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Code of Ethics
Study GRC Inc. requires its directors, officers, and employees to observe high standards of business and personal ethics in the conduct of their duties and responsibilities. As representatives of the Organization, they must practice honesty and integrity in fulfilling their responsibilities and comply with all applicable laws and regulations.

#### 4.2 No Retaliation
**Zero Tolerance:** No director, officer, employee, or volunteer who in good faith reports a violation shall suffer harassment, retaliation, or adverse employment consequences.
*   **Legal Protection:** This protection is mandated by the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 (18 U.S.C. § 1513(e)) and aligns with the spirit of the Texas Whistleblower Act.
*   **Consequence:** An employee or volunteer who retaliates against someone who has reported a violation in good faith is subject to discipline up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board/Volunteer position.

#### 4.3 Reporting Procedure
Study GRC Inc. maintains an "Open Door Policy" and suggests that employees share their questions, concerns, suggestions, or complaints with someone who can address them properly.

**4.3.1 Reporting Hierarchy**
1.  **Direct Supervisor:** In most cases, an employee or volunteer’s supervisor is in the best position to address an area of concern.
2.  **Executive Director (CEO):** If the reporter is not comfortable speaking with their supervisor or is not satisfied with the supervisor’s response, they are encouraged to speak with the Executive Director.
3.  **Compliance Officer / Board Treasurer:** If the concern involves the Executive Director or financial improprieties, the report should be made directly to the Board Treasurer or the Chair of the Audit & Risk Committee.
4.  **Board President:** If the concern involves the Board Treasurer, the report should be made to the Board President.

**4.3.2 Method of Reporting**
Reports may be submitted:
*   **In Writing:** Via email to the designated officer.
*   **Anonymous Hotline/Form:** Via the Organization’s designated secure electronic reporting form (as defined in `GOV-FRM-010 Whistleblower Complaint Intake Form`).

#### 4.4 Confidentiality
Violations or suspected violations may be submitted on a confidential basis by the complainant or may be submitted anonymously. Reports of violations or suspected violations will be kept confidential to the extent possible, consistent with the need to conduct an adequate investigation.

#### 4.5 Acting in Good Faith
Anyone filing a complaint concerning a violation or suspected violation must be acting in good faith and have reasonable grounds for believing the information disclosed indicates a violation.
*   **Malicious Allegations:** Any allegations that prove not to be substantiated and which prove to have been made maliciously or knowingly to be false will be viewed as a serious disciplinary offense.

#### 4.6 Handling of Reported Violations
The Organization’s Compliance Officer (or designee) will notify the sender and acknowledge receipt of the reported violation or suspected violation within five (5) business days. All reports will be promptly investigated, and appropriate corrective action will be taken if warranted by the investigation.
*   **Board Notification:** The Audit & Risk Committee shall be notified of all such complaints and the resolution of said complaints.

#### 4.7 Accounting and Auditing Matters
The Audit & Risk Committee of the Board of Directors shall address all reported concerns or complaints regarding corporate accounting practices, internal controls, or auditing. The Compliance Officer shall immediately notify the Audit Committee of any such complaint and work with the Committee until the matter is resolved.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Whistleblower** | Reports concerns in good faith; maintains confidentiality regarding the investigation. |
| **Compliance Officer** | Receives reports, conducts initial screening, leads or coordinates investigations, and reports to the Audit Committee. |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Oversees the administration of this policy; reviews investigation outcomes; ensures no retaliation occurs. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this policy; handles complaints regarding the Executive Director or Audit Committee members. |
| **Supervisors/Managers** | Must immediately escalate any whistleblower reports they receive to the Compliance Officer; strictly prohibited from retaliating. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—specifically engaging in retaliation against a whistleblower or failing to report known serious violations—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board of Directors (pursuant to Bylaws Section 3.6), or termination of volunteer assignment. Civil or criminal penalties may also apply under federal and state laws.
