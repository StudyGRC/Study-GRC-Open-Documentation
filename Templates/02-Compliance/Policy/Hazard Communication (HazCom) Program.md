### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Hazard Communication Program (the "Program") is to ensure that all employees, volunteers, and contractors of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") are informed of the contents of the OSHA Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200), the hazardous properties of chemicals with which they work, and safe handling procedures and measures to take to protect themselves from these hazards.

This Program fulfills the Organization's commitment to the "Right to Know" and "Right to Understand" regarding chemical hazards in the workplace.

### 2. Scope
This Program applies to all work operations where employees or volunteers may be exposed to hazardous chemicals under normal working conditions or during a foreseeable emergency.

**Applicability:**
*   **Physical Locations:** Central headquarters, storage facilities, and temporary event venues (e.g., workshops, labs, conferences) managed by the Organization.
*   **Activities:** Science/STEM workshops, 3D printing labs, facility maintenance, and event setup/teardown.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Remote Home Offices:** In accordance with OSHA directives, this policy does not apply to consumer products used by remote employees in their home offices for personal use (e.g., household cleaners, printer toner).
*   **Articles:** Manufactured items other than a fluid or particle (e.g., furniture, pens) which do not release more than very small quantities of a hazardous chemical.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chemical** | Any substance, or mixture of substances. |
| **GHS** | Globally Harmonized System of Classification and Labeling of Chemicals. |
| **Hazardous Chemical** | Any chemical which is classified as a physical hazard or a health hazard, a simple asphyxiant, combustible dust, pyrophoric gas, or hazard not otherwise classified. |
| **SDS** | Safety Data Sheet. A document produced by the chemical manufacturer that details the hazards, physical properties, and safety precautions for a specific chemical. (Formerly MSDS). |
| **Secondary Container** | A container (e.g., spray bottle, bucket) used to transfer a chemical from its original shipping container for immediate or short-term use. |
| **Pictogram** | A composition that may include a symbol plus other graphic elements, such as a border, background pattern, or color, that is intended to convey specific information about the hazards of a chemical. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Responsibility
In accordance with **Bylaws Article V**, the Executive Director retains ultimate responsibility for the safety of operations. The Organization must maintain a list of the hazardous chemicals known to be present at all physical sites and must maintain a Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for each chemical.

#### 4.2 Container Labeling
The Organization strictly adheres to GHS labeling requirements.

*   **4.2.1 Primary Containers:** No container of hazardous chemicals will be released for use until the label is verified. Labels on original shipping containers must contain:
    *   Product Identifier.
    *   Signal Word (Danger or Warning).
    *   Hazard Statement(s).
    *   Pictogram(s).
    *   Precautionary Statement(s).
    *   Name, address, and telephone number of the chemical manufacturer, importer, or other responsible party.
*   **4.2.2 Secondary Containers:** If chemicals are transferred to a secondary container (e.g., a spray bottle for cleaning solution during a workshop), that container must be labeled with the Product Identifier and words, pictures, symbols, or combination thereof, which provide at least general information regarding the hazards of the chemicals.
*   **4.2.3 Exception:** Portable containers intended for immediate use by the employee who performs the transfer do not require labels *only if* the container is used entirely within that shift and is never left unattended.

#### 4.3 Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
*   **4.3.1 Acquisition:** The Director of Operations or designated Event Manager must obtain an SDS for every hazardous chemical present at a physical site.
*   **4.3.2 Access:** SDSs must be readily accessible to employees and volunteers during their work shift.
    *   **Digital Repository:** The Organization maintains a central digital repository (e.g., Google Drive/SharePoint) accessible via mobile device or laptop at all events.
    *   **Backup:** For events with unreliable internet connectivity, a physical binder containing hard copies of SDSs for all chemicals present on-site must be available.
*   **4.3.3 Review:** New SDSs must be reviewed for safety and health implications before the chemical is introduced into the work environment.

#### 4.4 Hazardous Chemical Inventory
*   **4.4.1 Maintenance:** A current list of all hazardous chemicals (The "Chemical Inventory List") must be maintained for each physical storage location or event kit.
*   **4.4.2 Correlation:** The Chemical Inventory List must be cross-referenced with the SDS repository to ensure no SDS is missing.

#### 4.5 Employee and Volunteer Information and Training
Training must be provided to employees and volunteers *before* their initial assignment to a work area where hazardous chemicals are present and whenever a new chemical hazard is introduced.

*   **4.5.1 Training Content:**
    *   The requirements of the OSHA HazCom Standard (1910.1200).
    *   Operations in their work area where hazardous chemicals are present.
    *   Location and availability of the written HazCom Program and SDSs.
    *   Methods and observations used to detect the presence or release of a chemical (e.g., monitoring, visual appearance, odors).
    *   Physical and health hazards of the chemicals.
    *   Measures to protect themselves (e.g., work practices, emergency procedures, and Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)).
    *   Explanation of the labeling system and how to read/understand the SDS.

#### 4.6 Non-Routine Tasks
Periodically, employees or volunteers may be required to perform non-routine tasks (e.g., deep cleaning a storage facility, disposing of old workshop materials). Prior to starting work on such projects, each affected individual must be given information by the Event Manager or Safety Officer about the hazardous chemicals they may encounter during such activity.

#### 4.7 Multi-Employer Worksites (Events and Venues)
When the Organization operates at a third-party venue (e.g., school, conference center) or hires contractors:
*   **4.7.1 Incoming Hazards:** The Organization must request SDSs for any chemicals used by the venue or contractors that Study GRC staff may be exposed to.
*   **4.7.2 Outgoing Information:** The Organization must provide SDS access to venue staff or contractors regarding any chemicals Study GRC brings onto the premises.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures resources are available for compliance; holds ultimate accountability for the Program. |
| **Director of Operations / Safety Officer** | Maintains the Master Chemical Inventory and SDS repository. Conducts annual program review. Coordinates training. |
| **Event Managers / Workshop Leads** | Ensures all chemicals brought to an event are properly labeled and have corresponding SDSs. Verifies secondary container labeling. Enforces PPE use. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Participates in training. Reads labels and SDSs prior to use. Uses required PPE. Reports missing labels or SDSs immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

**Specific Violations:**
*   Removing or defacing labels on chemical containers.
*   Bringing hazardous chemicals onto Organization premises or event sites without prior approval and SDS submission.
*   Refusal to wear prescribed PPE when handling chemicals.
