### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a standardized framework for the creation, approval, maintenance, and retirement of all official governance and operational documents within Study GRC Inc. This policy ensures that the Organization maintains a consistent, legally compliant, and transparent documentation system in accordance with the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) and federal grant requirements (2 CFR 200). It defines the hierarchy of authority to prevent conflicting directives and ensures that all stakeholders have access to clear, authorized guidance.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all official written rules, regulations, and instructions governing the operations of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors.
*   **Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to informal communications (emails, memos), temporary project plans, or external marketing materials, provided they do not contradict established policy.

### 3. Definitions

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Hierarchy of Authority
To ensure legal consistency, the Organization establishes the following hierarchy of authority. Lower-level documents must not contradict higher-level documents.
1.  **Statutes & Regulations:** Federal Law (e.g., IRS Code, 2 CFR 200) and State Law (TBOC).
2.  **Certificate of Formation:** The Organization's charter.
3.  **Bylaws:** The primary governance rules adopted by the Board.
4.  **Governance Policies (Level 1):** Board-approved mandates.
5.  **Operational Standards & Procedures (Level 2):** Executive-approved execution rules.

#### 4.2 Document Development and Drafting
4.2.1 **Need Identification:** Any Voting Member, Director, or Operational Officer may identify the need for a new policy due to regulatory changes, risk identification, or operational necessity.
4.2.2 **Standard Template:** All policies and procedures must be drafted using the Organization’s approved **Master Document Template**. Documents not utilizing this template will be rejected during the approval phase.
4.2.3 **Plain English Requirement:** To ensure accessibility for our diverse community and youth participants, documents must be written in "Plain English."
*   Use active voice (e.g., "The Manager must sign..." not "The form is to be signed...").
*   Avoid legal jargon unless strictly necessary for statutory compliance.
*   Use gender-neutral terminology.

#### 4.3 Approval Authority
Authority to approve documents is delegated based on the document's classification:

*   **Level 1 (Governance Policies):** Must be approved by a majority vote of the **Board of Directors** in accordance with Bylaws Article III. These include policies affecting risk, finance, ethics, and legal compliance.
*   **Level 2 (Standards & Procedures):** Must be approved by the **Executive Director (CEO)**. The Board retains the right to veto or amend any operational procedure that conflicts with the Organization's mission or Bylaws.
*   **Level 3 (Guidelines & Forms):** May be approved by the relevant **Department Head** (e.g., CISO, CFO).

#### 4.4 Review and Maintenance
4.4.1 **Review Cycle:** Every active document must be assigned a "Next Review Date."
*   **Governance Policies:** Must be reviewed at least bi-annually (every 2 years).
*   **HR & Finance Policies:** Must be reviewed annually to ensure compliance with changing laws.
4.4.2 **Responsibility:** The Document Owner is responsible for initiating the review process 30 days prior to the expiration date.

#### 4.5 Version Control and Naming Convention
4.5.1 **Naming Convention:** All documents must be assigned a unique ID following the format: `[DEPT]-[TYPE]-[###]` (e.g., `GOV-POL-001`).
4.5.2 **Versioning:**
*   **Drafts:** Denoted by `v0.X` (e.g., v0.1, v0.2).
*   **Approved:** Denoted by `v1.0`.
*   **Revisions:** Minor updates (typos, hyperlinks) increment the decimal (v1.1); substantive changes requiring re-approval increment the integer (v2.0).

#### 4.6 Transparency and Access
In accordance with Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance) and TBOC § 22.353:
*   **Public Access:** Policies classified as "Public" (e.g., Privacy Policy, Code of Ethics) must be published on the Organization’s transparency webpage.
*   **Internal Access:** All active policies must be stored in the central **Master Documentation Registry (MDR)** accessible to all authorized staff and volunteers.
*   **Shadow Policies Prohibited:** Rules or mandates communicated solely via email, chat, or verbal instruction are unenforceable if they contradict an approved Policy or if they are not formalized into the MDR within 30 days of issuance.

#### 4.7 Archival and Retention
Obsolete or superseded versions of policies must not be deleted. They must be moved to a secure "Archive" folder and retained in accordance with the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy** to maintain an audit trail of rules in effect at any specific point in time.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves Level 1 Governance Policies; ensures policies align with Bylaws and Mission. |
| **Governance Committee** | Reviews drafted policies for regulatory compliance prior to Board submission; maintains the Master Documentation Registry. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Approves Level 2 Operational Procedures; ensures staff/volunteers are trained on applicable policies. |
| **Document Owner** | Drafts content; monitors regulatory changes; initiates scheduled reviews; ensures the document remains accurate. |
| **All Personnel** | Responsible for reading, acknowledging, and adhering to policies relevant to their role. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in the rejection of unauthorized documents, the invalidation of "shadow" rules, and disciplinary action for individuals who attempt to enforce unapproved mandates. Documents that are not properly approved and stored in the Master Documentation Registry are not considered valid defenses in the event of legal or audit scrutiny.
