### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a consistent, fair, and transparent framework for addressing employee and volunteer performance deficiencies or behavioral issues. This policy aims to:
1.  Provide individuals with clear feedback and an opportunity to correct behavior (remediation).
2.  Ensure consistent treatment of all personnel to mitigate risks of discrimination or wrongful termination claims.
3.  Protect the Organization’s mission, assets, and community by providing a mechanism for removing individuals who fail to meet established standards.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:** All paid employees and volunteers of Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Board Directors:** Removal is governed strictly by **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**.
*   **Operational Officers:** Removal of Board-appointed Officers (e.g., CEO, CFO) is governed by **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.3**.
*   **Independent Contractors:** Performance issues are addressed via the terms of the Master Service Agreement (MSA) or contract termination clauses.
*   **Probationary Period:** Employees within their initial 90-day introductory period may be terminated without progressive steps.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **At-Will Employment** | The legal doctrine in Texas stating that employment may be terminated by either the employer or employee at any time, for any reason (unless illegal), or for no reason at all. |
| **Progressive Discipline** | A process of increasingly severe consequences for repeated violations of policy or failure to improve performance. |
| **Gross Misconduct** | Serious acts that justify immediate termination without prior warnings (e.g., theft, violence, youth protection violations). |
| **PIP** | Performance Improvement Plan; a formal document outlining specific goals and timelines for remediation. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 At-Will Employment Disclaimer
Study GRC Inc. is an **At-Will Employer** under Texas law. This policy is a guideline and **does not constitute a contract** of employment. The Organization reserves the right to skip steps in the progressive discipline process or proceed directly to termination depending on the severity of the infraction or the specific circumstances.

#### 4.2 The Progressive Discipline Steps
Unless the infraction constitutes Gross Misconduct (see Section 4.3), the Organization generally follows these four steps:

**4.2.1 Step 1: Verbal Warning (Documented)**
*   The Manager discusses the issue with the employee via video conference or in person.
*   The Manager clearly states that this is a "Verbal Warning."
*   **Documentation:** The Manager must send a follow-up email or log a note in the HR system summarizing the conversation, date, and required change.

**4.2.2 Step 2: Written Warning**
*   If the issue persists or a new issue arises, a formal Written Warning is issued.
*   This document details the violation, refers to previous warnings, and explicitly states that failure to improve will result in further discipline up to termination.
*   The employee must acknowledge receipt (electronic signature or email reply).

**4.2.3 Step 3: Final Written Warning or PIP**
*   **Performance Issues:** A **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** is implemented, typically lasting 30, 60, or 90 days, with clear, measurable objectives (OKRs).
*   **Behavioral Issues:** A **Final Written Warning** is issued, serving as a last notice.
*   This step requires review by the VP of Operations or Executive Director prior to issuance.

**4.2.4 Step 4: Termination of Employment**
*   If the employee fails to meet the requirements of the PIP or violates the Final Warning, employment is terminated.
*   Termination requires approval from the Executive Director and/or Legal Counsel.

#### 4.3 Gross Misconduct (Zero Tolerance)
Certain offenses are so severe that they bypass the progressive steps and result in **immediate termination**. Examples include, but are not limited to:
*   **Youth Protection Violations:** Any violation of the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, including unauthorized one-on-one contact with minors.
*   **Harassment:** Sexual harassment, discrimination, or bullying (violation of *Anti-Harassment Policy*).
*   **Theft/Fraud:** Misappropriation of funds, falsifying time records, or theft of Intellectual Property (violation of *Bylaws Article IX*).
*   **Substance Abuse:** Violation of the *Drug-Free Workplace Policy*.
*   **Breach of Confidentiality:** Unauthorized disclosure of sensitive member data or donor information.

#### 4.4 Documentation and Record Retention
*   All disciplinary actions must be documented contemporaneously.
*   Records are stored in the employee’s secure Personnel File.
*   In accordance with the *Records Retention Schedule*, disciplinary records are retained for **7 years** post-termination to defend against potential litigation.

#### 4.5 Remote Work Considerations
*   Disciplinary meetings must be conducted via synchronous video (e.g., Zoom/Teams) with cameras on, never via chat or email alone.
*   If an employee refuses to attend a disciplinary video call, it is considered insubordination and grounds for immediate termination.

#### 4.6 Appeals
*   Employees may appeal a disciplinary decision (Step 2 or 3) by submitting a written statement to the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** or the **Executive Director** within 5 business days.
*   The appeal review focuses on whether the policy was applied consistently and fairly. The reviewer’s decision is final.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Manager** | Identifies performance gaps; conducts coaching; drafts warnings; ensures all steps are documented in the HR system. |
| **VP of Operations / HR** | Reviews Written Warnings and PIPs for consistency and legal compliance; advises Managers on proper procedure. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves all terminations; serves as the final escalation point for appeals. |
| **Employee/Volunteer** | Adheres to standards of conduct; actively participates in remediation plans; acknowledges receipt of warnings. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to follow this policy by Management (e.g., terminating without cause or documentation in a non-Gross Misconduct scenario) may result in disciplinary action against the Manager.

Employees found in violation of Organization standards will be subject to the discipline described herein, up to and including termination of employment and potential legal action for damages (e.g., in cases of theft or IP theft).
