### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define Study GRC Inc.’s (the "Organization") stance on preventing, recognizing, and responding to violence, threats, and intimidation. In compliance with the **OSHA General Duty Clause** and **Texas Labor Code**, this policy establishes a **Zero Tolerance** standard to ensure a safe environment for all personnel, members, and volunteers, whether operating remotely, digitally, or at physical events.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals associated with the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   **Employees and Contractors:** Full-time, part-time, and temporary staff.
*   **Governance Leadership:** The Board of Directors and Advisory Board members.
*   **Membership Classes:** As defined in **Bylaws Article II** (Community Participants, Supporting Members, and Honorary Members).
*   **Volunteers:** Including event staff and committee members.
*   **Visitors/Vendors:** Any third party engaging with the Organization.

**Applicability:** This policy applies to all Organization-controlled environments, including:
*   Physical premises (leased offices, event venues).
*   Digital workspaces (Slack, Discord, Zoom, Email, GitHub).
*   Organization-sponsored events, conferences, and social gatherings.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Workplace Violence** | Any act or threat of physical violence, harassment, intimidation, or other threatening disruptive behavior that occurs at the work site or in digital channels. |
| **Zero Tolerance** | A strict enforcement policy where no violation is considered minor; all reported incidents are investigated, and substantiated violations result in immediate disciplinary action. |
| **Intimidation** | Actions or words (written or spoken) intended to frighten, coerce, or induce duress. This includes "doxxing" or threatening to release private information. |
| **Prohibited Weapon** | Any firearm, knife with a blade over 5.5 inches, explosive, or instrument used to inflict harm. |
| **Digital Violence** | Aggressive online behavior including cyberstalking, swatting threats, hate speech, or persistent harassment via digital communication platforms. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Zero Tolerance Standard
Study GRC Inc. maintains a **Zero Tolerance** policy regarding workplace violence. Any individual found to have committed an act of violence, made a credible threat, or engaged in significant intimidation will be subject to immediate removal from the premises or digital platform, followed by disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment, revocation of membership (per **Bylaws Article II**), or removal from the Board (per **Bylaws Article III**).

#### 4.2 Prohibited Conduct
The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:
1.  **Physical Acts:** Hitting, shoving, kicking, or physical aggression of any kind.
2.  **Threats:** Verbal, written, or digital statements expressing an intent to inflict harm, damage property, or destroy reputation (e.g., "I know where you live").
3.  **Stalking:** Persistent, unwanted physical or digital surveillance or contact.
4.  **Property Damage:** Intentional destruction of Organization property or the property of others.
5.  **Aggressive Posturing:** Physically blocking paths, shouting in a manner reasonably perceived as threatening, or brandishing objects as weapons.

#### 4.3 Weapons Policy (Texas Context)
While the Organization respects the constitutional rights of individuals:
1.  **Physical Premises:** Possession of firearms or Prohibited Weapons is strictly banned within any building or venue leased or controlled by the Organization for business or event purposes, to the fullest extent permitted by **Texas Penal Code § 30.06 and § 30.07**.
2.  **Parking Lot Exception:** In compliance with **Texas Labor Code § 52.061**, employees may store lawfully possessed firearms in their locked, private vehicles in parking areas, provided the firearm is not visible.
3.  **Event Venues:** All attendees at Organization-hosted conferences or meetups must adhere to the specific weapons policy of the host venue. If the venue allows weapons, the Organization reserves the right to impose stricter limitations for entry into Organization-controlled rooms/areas.

#### 4.4 Remote and Digital Safety
As a remote-first organization, "workplace violence" often manifests digitally. The Organization treats the following as acts of violence:
1.  **Doxxing:** Publishing an individual's private personally identifiable information (PII) with malicious intent.
2.  **Swatting:** Making false reports to law enforcement to trigger an armed response against an individual.
3.  **Platform Abuse:** Using Organization-provided platforms (e.g., Slack, Discord) to send abusive, threatening, or graphically violent content.

#### 4.5 Domestic Violence Affecting the Workplace
The Organization recognizes that domestic violence can spill over into the workplace (remote or physical).
1.  **Support:** Employees who are victims of domestic violence are encouraged to communicate with the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** or HR. The Organization will prioritize the safety of the victim without fear of retaliation.
2.  **Restraining Orders:** Any employee or volunteer who has obtained a protective or restraining order that lists the Organization’s premises or digital workspaces as protected areas must provide a copy to the Executive Director and/or General Counsel to ensure enforcement.

#### 4.6 Reporting Procedures
1.  **Imminent Danger:** If there is an immediate threat to life or safety, individuals must first contact local law enforcement (911) and then notify Organization leadership.
2.  **Non-Emergency Reporting:** All threats or incidents must be reported immediately to the Executive Director or the DKG.
3.  **Whistleblower Protection:** Per the **Whistleblower Protection Policy**, no individual will face retaliation for making a good-faith report of workplace violence.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensures resources are available for security at physical events; reviews high-level disciplinary actions regarding Directors or Officers. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ultimate accountability for policy implementation; acts as the primary liaison with law enforcement for organizational threats. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Serves as the primary intake point for safety concerns; fosters a culture of psychological safety to prevent escalation. |
| **Chief Info. Security Officer (CISO)** | Investigates digital threats, secures compromised accounts, and preserves digital evidence of cyber-violence/harassment. |
| **Managers/Supervisors** | Responsible for recognizing early warning signs of violence and escalating concerns immediately. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Responsible for refraining from violence and reporting any suspicious or threatening behavior immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Violations of this policy are taken with extreme seriousness.

1.  **Investigation:** The Organization will conduct a prompt, objective investigation into all reports.
2.  **Interim Measures:** The accused may be placed on administrative leave or suspended from digital platforms pending the outcome of the investigation.
3.  **Disciplinary Action:**
    *   **Employees:** Termination of employment.
    *   **Members:** Expulsion from membership pursuant to the due process outlined in the **Membership Policy Manual**.
    *   **Directors:** Removal for cause as defined in **Bylaws Section 3.6**.
4.  **Legal Action:** The Organization will cooperate fully with law enforcement and may pursue civil or criminal prosecution against perpetrators of workplace violence.
