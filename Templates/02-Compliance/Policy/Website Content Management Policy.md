### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict standards for the creation, approval, publication, and maintenance of content on Study GRC Inc. digital properties. As an organization dedicated to Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC), maintaining the highest level of **accuracy**, integrity, and legal compliance in our public communications is critical to our reputation and mission. This policy mitigates risks associated with misinformation, intellectual property infringement, data privacy violations, and brand inconsistency.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all **Public-Facing Digital Properties** owned or operated by Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to the main website, learning management systems (LMS), community forums, and code repositories (e.g., GitHub).

**Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, contractors, and committee members who draft, review, or publish digital content.

**Exclusions:**
*   Internal-only intranets (governed by Information Security Policies).
*   Personal social media accounts of staff (governed by the *Social Media and External Communications Policy*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Accuracy and Verification
To maintain the Organization's credibility as an educational authority:
*   **Fact-Checking:** All educational and technical content must be reviewed by a qualified SME prior to publication.
*   **Citations:** Claims regarding laws, regulations (e.g., GDPR, SOX), or frameworks (e.g., NIST, ISO) must include citations or links to authoritative sources.
*   **Currency:** Content must be reviewed periodically to ensure it remains current with evolving GRC standards. Outdated content must be updated, archived, or clearly marked as "Legacy/Historical."

#### 4.2 Authorization and Workflow (Segregation of Duties)
*   **Maker/Checker Rule:** No individual may publish content to the production environment without a documented approval from a second authorized individual, except for pre-approved minor administrative updates (e.g., typo corrections).
*   **Approval Authority:**
    *   **Core Governance Content:** (Bylaws, Financials) requires Board Secretary or Treasurer approval.
    *   **Educational Curriculum:** Requires VP of Education & Training approval.
    *   **Press/News:** Requires Executive Director approval.

#### 4.3 Intellectual Property and Licensing
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX (Intellectual Property)**:
*   **Ownership:** All content created for the website is "work made for hire" and owned by Study GRC Inc., unless explicitly licensed otherwise.
*   **Third-Party Assets:** No copyrighted images, text, or code owned by third parties may be used without express written permission or a valid license (e.g., Creative Commons, Stock Photo License).
*   **Open Source Mandate:** Educational resources provided to the community shall be clearly labeled with the appropriate Open Source license (e.g., MIT, CC-BY-SA) to fulfill the Organization's mission of removing financial barriers.

#### 4.4 Data Privacy and Youth Protection
*   **PII Restriction:** No PII of members, donors, or staff shall be published without explicit written consent.
*   **Youth Safeguarding:** In alignment with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, photos, names, or identifiable details of minors (under 18) are **strictly prohibited** on any public-facing site without a signed *Parental Consent and Media Release Form*.
*   **COPPA Compliance:** Content targeted at children under 13 must comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) regarding data collection.

#### 4.5 Transparency and Financial Disclosures
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, the website must maintain a dedicated "Transparency" page containing:
*   The most recent IRS Form 990 filings.
*   The Annual Financial Report.
*   The current Board of Directors list.
*   The Organization’s Bylaws and Certificate of Formation.

#### 4.6 Accessibility
All web content must adhere to the **Digital Accessibility Policy**, striving for WCAG 2.2 Level AA compliance. This includes mandatory alt-text for images, proper heading structures, and captioning for video content.

#### 4.7 Emergency Control
The Executive Director and the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) retain the authority to immediately remove content or take the website offline in the event of a security breach, legal threat, or significant reputational crisis.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Final accountability for all web content; approves strategic messaging and crisis communications. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Owner of this policy. Approves all curriculum, technical guides, and educational resources for accuracy. |
| **CISO** | Secures the CMS infrastructure; conducts security reviews of code/plugins; manages access controls. |
| **Content Contributors** | Draft content; ensure initial accuracy; obtain necessary IP licenses/permissions. |
| **Webmaster / IT Admin** | Implements technical changes; ensures uptime; manages backups; executes the "Checker" function for deployment. |
| **Board Secretary** | Verifies accuracy of governance documents (Bylaws, Minutes) posted to the Transparency page. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Legal Action:** Posting content that violates copyright law or defames third parties may result in personal legal liability and immediate termination.
*   **Remediation:** Inaccurate content identified by the community or staff must be corrected or retracted within 24 hours of verification.
