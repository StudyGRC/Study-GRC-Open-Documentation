### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Volunteer Handbook is to establish the standards, expectations, and legal framework governing the relationship between Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") and its volunteers. This policy mitigates organizational risk regarding liability, intellectual property ownership, and youth protection while ensuring a safe, professional, and inclusive environment for all contributors. It serves to operationalize the values outlined in the Organization's Bylaws and Code of Ethics.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals providing services to the Organization without expectation of compensation ("Volunteers").

*   **Inclusions:** Committee members, content creators, community moderators, event staff, mentors, and Advisory Board members.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   **Board of Directors:** Governance duties are covered specifically by the Bylaws and Board Manual.
    *   **Paid Employees/Contractors:** Covered by the Employee Handbook or Independent Contractor Agreements.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer** | An individual who performs hours of service for the Organization for civic, charitable, or humanitarian reasons, without promise, expectation, or receipt of compensation for services rendered. |
| **Work Product** | Any content, code, curriculum, graphics, documentation, or other intellectual property created by the Volunteer in the course of their service to the Organization. |
| **Vulnerable Population** | Any individual under the age of 18 (Minors) or adults who are unable to protect their own interests due to physical or mental disability. |
| **Confidential Information** | Non-public data including member lists, donor information, unpublished financial data, and security vulnerabilities. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | A safety policy requiring a minimum of two authorized adults to be present during any interaction with a Minor. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Nature of Volunteer Relationship
Volunteers serve at the discretion of the Organization. The relationship is "at-will" and may be terminated by either party at any time, with or without cause or notice.
*   **4.1.1 No Compensation:** Volunteers agree they are not employees for federal or state tax purposes and are not entitled to wages, benefits, or unemployment compensation.
*   **4.1.2 Supporting Membership:** As defined in **Bylaws Article II**, a Volunteer may qualify as a "Supporting Member" (Voting) based on service hours contributed. However, Volunteer status does not automatically confer Voting Membership unless confirmed by the Membership Policy Manual.

#### 4.2 Screening and Background Checks
To ensure the safety of the community and compliance with Texas youth protection laws:
*   **4.2.1 Mandatory Screening:** All Volunteers must complete an application and screening process appropriate to their role.
*   **4.2.2 Background Checks:** Any Volunteer with direct access to Minors, financial assets, or sensitive member data (e.g., database administrators) must undergo and pass a criminal background check prior to service, in accordance with the **Background Check Policy (HR-POL-024)**.

#### 4.3 Code of Conduct and Ethics
Volunteers represent Study GRC Inc. and must adhere to the highest standards of professionalism.
*   **4.3.1 Anti-Harassment:** The Organization maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, discrimination, or bullying based on race, gender, religion, disability, or any other protected class.
*   **4.3.2 Remote Etiquette:** As a remote-first organization, Volunteers must maintain professionalism in all digital channels (Slack, Discord, Zoom). Trolling, flaming, or the use of offensive language is prohibited.
*   **4.3.3 Conflicts of Interest:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX**, Volunteers must disclose any personal or professional interests that may conflict with their duties.

#### 4.4 Youth Protection and Safeguarding
Given the Organization's K-12 educational focus, strict adherence to the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy (HR-POL-060)** is mandatory.
*   **4.4.1 Two-Deep Leadership:** No Volunteer shall ever be alone in a private one-on-one setting (physical or virtual/DM) with a Minor. A second adult must always be present or copied on communications.
*   **4.4.2 Mandatory Reporting:** All Volunteers are designated as mandatory reporters under Texas Family Code Chapter 261. Any suspicion of child abuse or neglect must be reported immediately to the Director of Kindness and Generosity and appropriate state authorities.

#### 4.5 Intellectual Property and Open Source
To protect the Organization’s mission of providing free resources:
*   **4.5.1 Assignment of Rights:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all Work Product created by a Volunteer is considered a "work made for hire." The Volunteer assigns all right, title, and interest in such Work Product to Study GRC Inc.
*   **4.5.2 Open Source Commitment:** The Organization commits to licensing applicable Work Product under Open Source licenses (e.g., CC-BY, MIT) to ensure public accessibility. Volunteers waive "moral rights" to prevent the Organization from modifying content for educational updates.

#### 4.6 Confidentiality and Data Privacy
Volunteers may have access to sensitive data.
*   **4.6.1 Non-Disclosure:** Volunteers must not disclose Confidential Information to unauthorized parties during or after their service.
*   **4.6.2 Data Handling:** Volunteers must comply with the **Master Data Privacy Policy (IS-POL-001)**. Member data (names, emails) must not be downloaded to personal devices without explicit authorization and security controls (e.g., encryption).

#### 4.7 Use of Organizational Resources
*   **4.7.1 Technology:** Access to Organization systems (Google Workspace, AWS, GitHub) is a privilege. Accounts must be secured with Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) per the **Access Control Policy (IS-POL-009)**.
*   **4.7.2 No Personal Gain:** Volunteers may not use the Organization’s assets, brand, or member lists for personal commercial gain or outside business solicitation.

#### 4.8 Indemnification
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VII**, the Organization shall indemnify Volunteers against claims arising from the good faith performance of their assigned duties, provided the Volunteer was acting within the scope of their authority and not with gross negligence or willful misconduct.

#### 4.9 Resignation and Termination
*   **4.9.1 Resignation:** Volunteers are requested to provide advance notice of resignation to allow for transition of duties.
*   **4.9.2 Termination:** The Organization reserves the right to dismiss a Volunteer for violation of these policies, failure to perform duties, or conduct detrimental to the Organization’s reputation.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer** | Read, sign, and adhere to the Volunteer Handbook and associated policies. Complete required training (e.g., Youth Protection). |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Oversee the volunteer program, manage dispute resolution, and ensure cultural alignment. |
| **Volunteer Supervisor** | Provide specific task instruction, approve Work Product, and monitor compliance with safety protocols. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Manage volunteer access rights and ensure data security compliance. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including:
1.  Revocation of system access.
2.  Termination of Volunteer status.
3.  Expulsion from Membership (if applicable).
4.  Legal action for breach of confidentiality or intellectual property theft.
5.  Referral to law enforcement for violations involving Minors or financial fraud.
