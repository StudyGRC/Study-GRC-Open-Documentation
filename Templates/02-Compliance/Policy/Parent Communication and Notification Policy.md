### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a transparent, consistent, and reliable framework for communication between Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") and the parents or legal guardians of minors participating in our programs. This policy ensures that parents are fully informed regarding program activities, their child's participation, and any incidents that may occur, thereby fostering trust and mitigating liability. It aligns with the Organization's commitment to transparency and compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and Texas Family Code requirements.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, board members, volunteers, and contractors ("Personnel") who interact with minors (individuals under the age of 18) through Study GRC Inc.’s educational programs, community events, or mentorship initiatives.

**Inclusions:**
*   Virtual and in-person educational workshops.
*   Online community moderation and discord/forum interactions.
*   Mentorship programs.

**Exclusions:**
*   General public website traffic where the user’s age is unknown and no account has been created.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor / Youth Participant** | Any individual under the age of 18 participating in Organization activities. |
| **Parent / Guardian** | The biological parent, adoptive parent, or court-appointed legal guardian with authority to make decisions for the Minor. |
| **Authorized Communication Channel** | Official Organization platforms (e.g., official email, Learning Management System, approved Discord servers) that are monitored and archived. |
| **Critical Incident** | An event involving serious injury, medical emergency, allegations of abuse, lost child, or significant behavioral infraction requiring immediate removal. |
| **Direct Notice** | A specific notification sent directly to a parent (via email or hard copy) as required by COPPA regarding data collection. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Principle of Transparency
Study GRC Inc. operates under a "No Secrets" policy regarding parent communication. Parents have the right to know:
*   Who is interacting with their child.
*   The content and curriculum of the program.
*   The platforms and technologies being used.
*   Any behavioral or medical incidents involving their child.

#### 4.2. Authorized Communication Channels
To ensure safety and accountability, all communication between Personnel and Parents must occur through Authorized Communication Channels.
*   **4.2.1 Prohibited Channels:** Personnel are strictly prohibited from using personal social media accounts, personal email addresses, or personal phone numbers to communicate with Parents regarding Organization business, unless in a documented emergency where official channels are inaccessible.
*   **4.2.2 Two-Deep Communication:** Copies of significant correspondence (e.g., disciplinary notices, injury reports) must be copied to a supervisor or a designated central email address (e.g., `youth-safety@studygrc.org`) to maintain an audit trail.

#### 4.3. Routine Program Notifications
The Organization must provide Parents with the following information prior to or at the start of any program:
*   **Program Details:** Dates, times, virtual meeting links, and curriculum overview.
*   **Code of Conduct:** The behavioral expectations for participants (See *Youth Code of Conduct*).
*   **Staff Credentials:** Names and roles of the lead instructors or volunteers.
*   **Privacy Practices:** A link to the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* and *Data Privacy Policy*.

#### 4.4. Incident Notification (Non-Critical)
For minor incidents (e.g., minor behavioral disruptions, technical difficulties, minor scrapes requiring basic first aid), Personnel must notify the Parent within **24 hours** of the event.
*   Notification may be made via email or the official program messaging system.
*   A record of the notification must be logged in the *Incident Report Log*.

#### 4.5. Emergency and Critical Incident Notification
In the event of a Critical Incident, the safety and well-being of the child are paramount.
*   **4.5.1 Immediate Notification:** Personnel must attempt to contact the Parent/Guardian **immediately** via the primary phone number on file once the immediate danger is mitigated and emergency services (if needed) have been contacted.
*   **4.5.2 Medical Emergencies:** If a child requires transport to a medical facility, Personnel will continue attempting to contact the Parent until successful.
*   **4.5.3 Suspected Abuse Exception:** In accordance with the *Mandatory Reporting Procedure* and Texas Family Code, if a parent or guardian is the suspected perpetrator of abuse or neglect, Personnel **must not** notify the parent. Instead, Personnel must follow the direction of Child Protective Services (CPS) or Law Enforcement.

#### 4.6. Data Privacy and COPPA Compliance
*   **4.6.1 Direct Notice:** Before collecting personal information from a child under 13, the Organization must provide Direct Notice to the parent detailing what information is collected and how it is used.
*   **4.6.2 Parental Consent:** Verifiable Parental Consent must be obtained before the child can access the digital community or program.
*   **4.6.3 Right to Review:** Parents have the right to review their child’s personal information, request its deletion, and refuse further collection or use of the information.

#### 4.7. Changes to Policy or Operations
The Organization must notify Parents at least **30 days** in advance of any material changes to:
*   The *Privacy Policy* regarding data sharing practices.
*   Program schedules or locations (virtual or physical).
*   Fees or costs associated with participation.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all program materials include required parental notifications and that staff are trained on this policy. |
| **Program Staff / Volunteers** | Execute daily communications and immediate incident notifications; log all communications in the official record. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Serves as the primary point of contact for non-disciplinary parental inquiries and community culture questions. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Responsible for communication regarding Critical Incidents, legal matters, or media-related inquiries. |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Manages COPPA Direct Notices, consent verification, and Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) from parents. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—specifically the failure to notify parents of incidents or the use of unauthorized communication channels—may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.

**Zero Tolerance:** Deliberately concealing a Critical Incident from a parent (except as noted in Section 4.5.3 regarding abuse suspects) is grounds for immediate dismissal and potential legal action.
