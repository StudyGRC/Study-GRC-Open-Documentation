### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define Study GRC Inc.’s (the "Organization") guidelines regarding employee absence due to jury duty, court-ordered appearances, and participation in legal proceedings. This policy ensures compliance with the **Texas Labor Code Chapter 122**, protects employees from retaliation for fulfilling civic obligations, and establishes clear protocols for compensation and timekeeping during such absences, particularly for staff funded by federal grants.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees of the Organization.
*   **Inclusions:** All W-2 employees, regardless of remote work location.
*   **Exclusions:** Independent contractors (1099), temporary agency workers, and volunteers.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Jury Duty** | The civic obligation to serve as a juror or prospective juror when summoned by a federal, state, or local court. |
| **Subpoena** | A writ ordering a person to attend a court or legislative proceeding. |
| **Work-Related Proceeding** | A legal proceeding in which the employee is requested to appear on behalf of the Organization or due to their professional capacity within the Organization. |
| **Personal Legal Matter** | A legal proceeding in which the employee is a plaintiff, defendant, or voluntary witness in a matter unrelated to the Organization’s business. |
| **Exempt Employee** | An employee paid on a salary basis who is exempt from the overtime provisions of the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Protection Against Retaliation
In strict compliance with **Texas Labor Code § 122.001**, the Organization prohibits the discharge, discipline, intimidation, or coercion of any permanent employee because the employee complies with a valid summons for jury duty. The Organization recognizes jury service as a vital civic duty and supports employees in fulfilling this obligation.

#### 4.2 Notification Requirements
4.2.1 **Advance Notice:** Upon receiving a jury summons or subpoena, the employee must notify their immediate supervisor and the HR department as soon as possible.
4.2.2 **Documentation:** The employee must provide a copy of the summons or subpoena to HR for inclusion in their personnel file.
4.2.3 **Verification:** Upon return to work, the employee must submit a court-issued attendance verification form indicating the dates and times served.

#### 4.3 Compensation for Jury Duty
While Texas law does not mandate *paid* leave for jury duty, the Organization elects to provide income protection to its employees as follows:

4.3.1 **Full-Time Employees:** The Organization will pay the employee’s regular base salary for up to **ten (10) business days** of jury service per rolling 12-month period.
4.3.2 **Extended Service:** If jury duty extends beyond ten (10) days, the additional time will be unpaid, or the employee may elect to use accrued Paid Time Off (PTO).
4.3.3 **Exempt Employees (FLSA):** In accordance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), exempt employees will not incur a deduction in salary for any week in which they perform *any* work for the Organization. If an exempt employee performs no work during a week due to jury duty, and that week falls outside the 10-day paid policy, the Organization reserves the right to make deductions permitted by law.
4.3.4 **Juror Fees:** Employees are permitted to retain any stipend, mileage, or parking reimbursement provided by the court. The Organization does not require these fees to be surrendered.

#### 4.4 Court Appearances (Non-Jury Duty)
4.4.1 **Organization-Related:** If an employee is required to appear in court on behalf of Study GRC Inc., or is subpoenaed to testify regarding matters arising from their employment duties, this time is considered **hours worked**. All associated travel and expenses will be reimbursed per the *Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy (FIN-POL-037)*.
4.4.2 **Personal Matters:** Absences for court appearances where the employee is a plaintiff, defendant, or witness in a personal matter (e.g., divorce, traffic court, civil lawsuit unrelated to work) are not covered by the Jury Duty pay provision. Employees must use accrued PTO or request unpaid leave for these absences.
4.4.3 **Crime Victims:** In accordance with applicable state laws, the Organization will grant reasonable unpaid leave (or allow the use of PTO) for employees to attend court proceedings if they or a close family member are victims of a crime.

#### 4.5 Remote Work and Partial Days
4.5.1 **Return to Work:** Given the Organization's remote-first nature, if an employee is released from court duty with four (4) or more hours remaining in their scheduled workday, they are expected to log in and resume work duties.
4.5.2 **Connectivity:** Employees are not expected to work *while* serving on a jury. However, during breaks or waiting periods, exempt employees may check communications at their discretion, provided it does not violate court rules.

#### 4.6 Grant Compliance (Timekeeping)
For employees whose salaries are funded by federal grants (subject to 2 CFR 200):
4.6.1 **Time Coding:** Jury duty leave must be explicitly recorded on timesheets using the code `LVE-JURY`.
4.6.2 **Allocability:** Paid jury duty leave is considered a fringe benefit and is an allowable cost, provided it is distributed equitably across all activities and funding sources in accordance with the Organization's *Cost Allocation Plan*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Notify supervisor immediately upon receipt of summons; submit court documentation; return to work if released early. |
| **Supervisor** | Adjust work schedules and deliverables to accommodate the absence; ensure the employee is not penalized for the absence. |
| **HR / Payroll** | Verify court documentation; ensure time is coded correctly in the payroll system; monitor the 10-day paid limit. |
| **Executive Director** | adjudicate requests for extended paid leave in exceptional circumstances (e.g., sequestered juries). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Failure to comply** with this policy may result in disciplinary action.
*   **Falsification:** Submitting false documentation regarding jury service or court appearances is considered fraud and will result in immediate termination of employment.
*   **Retaliation:** Any supervisor found retaliating against an employee for serving jury duty will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination, and may be personally liable for damages under Texas Labor Code § 122.002.
