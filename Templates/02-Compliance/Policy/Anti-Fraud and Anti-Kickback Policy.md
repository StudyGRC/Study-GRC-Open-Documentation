### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish controls and standards to prevent, detect, and report fraud, waste, abuse, and corrupt practices within Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy is designed to ensure compliance with the **Federal False Claims Act (31 U.S.C. §§ 3729-3733)**, the **Anti-Kickback Act of 1986**, and **2 CFR § 200.113** regarding mandatory disclosures for federal award recipients. It reinforces the Organization’s commitment to the highest standards of ethical conduct and fiduciary responsibility as mandated by **Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals and entities associated with the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   **Internal:** Board of Directors, Officers, Employees, and Volunteers.
*   **External:** Contractors, Subrecipients, Vendors, Consultants, and Agents acting on behalf of the Organization.

This policy covers all financial and operational activities, with specific emphasis on the management of federal grant funds, procurement processes, and financial reporting.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fraud** | Any intentional act or omission designed to deceive others, resulting in the victim suffering a loss and/or the perpetrator achieving a gain. This includes falsification of documents, misappropriation of assets, and financial statement irregularities. |
| **Kickback** | Any money, fee, commission, credit, gift, gratuity, thing of value, or compensation of any kind which is provided to any prime contractor, prime contractor employee, subcontractor, or subcontractor employee for the purpose of improperly obtaining or rewarding favorable treatment in connection with a contract or subcontract. |
| **False Claim** | Knowingly presenting, or causing to be presented, a false or fraudulent claim for payment or approval to the Federal Government (e.g., billing for services not rendered, double-billing, or falsifying grant reports). |
| **Waste** | The thoughtless or careless expenditure, mismanagement, or abuse of resources to the detriment (or potential detriment) of the Organization or the U.S. Government. |
| **Abuse** | Excessive or improper use of a thing, or to use something in a manner contrary to the natural or legal rules for its use. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Zero Tolerance Policy
The Organization maintains a **zero-tolerance** stance regarding fraud, kickbacks, and corruption. Any Director, Officer, employee, or volunteer found to have engaged in fraudulent activity or the solicitation/acceptance of kickbacks will be subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination and referral to law enforcement.

#### 4.2 Anti-Kickback and Bribery Standards
In accordance with the **Anti-Kickback Act of 1986**:
1.  **Prohibition:** No person may solicit, accept, offer, or provide any kickback, bribe, or rebate (in cash or in-kind) to influence the award of a contract, grant, or business transaction.
2.  **Vendor Relations:** Acceptance of gifts, entertainment, or favors from current or potential vendors that could be perceived as influencing business decisions is strictly prohibited, except as defined in the *Conflict of Interest Policy* (de minimis value).
3.  **Procurement Integrity:** All procurement activities must be conducted in a manner that provides full and open competition, free from organizational conflicts of interest.

#### 4.3 False Claims Act (FCA) Compliance
As a recipient of federal funds, the Organization strictly prohibits the submission of false claims. Prohibited acts under the FCA include, but are not limited to:
1.  **Knowingly** presenting a false or fraudulent claim for payment (e.g., invoicing a grant for work not performed).
2.  **Knowingly** making or using a false record or statement material to a false or fraudulent claim (e.g., falsifying time and effort reports or participant data).
3.  **Conspiring** to commit a violation of the FCA.
4.  **Reverse False Claims:** Knowingly concealing or improperly avoiding/decreasing an obligation to pay or transmit money or property to the Government (e.g., failing to return overpayments).

*Note: Under the FCA, "knowingly" includes actual knowledge, deliberate ignorance of the truth, or reckless disregard of the truth.*

#### 4.4 Mandatory Disclosures (Federal Grants)
Pursuant to **2 CFR § 200.113**, the Organization must disclose, in a timely manner, in writing to the Federal awarding agency or pass-through entity all violations of Federal criminal law involving fraud, bribery, or gratuity violations potentially affecting the Federal award. Failure to make required disclosures can result in any of the remedies described in **2 CFR § 200.339** (Remedies for Noncompliance), including suspension and debarment.

#### 4.5 Fraud Prevention in Remote Operations
Given the Organization's remote-first structure, specific vigilance is required regarding digital fraud:
1.  **Timekeeping:** Falsification of remote work hours or "ghost employees" constitutes fraud.
2.  **Expense Reimbursement:** Submission of altered digital receipts or duplicate reimbursement requests is prohibited.
3.  **Cybersecurity:** Intentional circumvention of security controls to facilitate theft or data manipulation is a violation of this policy.

#### 4.6 Reporting Procedures
1.  **Duty to Report:** Any individual who suspects fraud, waste, abuse, or kickbacks has a duty to report these concerns immediately.
2.  **Reporting Channels:** Reports should be made via the channels defined in the *Whistleblower Protection Policy* (e.g., direct report to the Audit Committee Chair or via the anonymous intake form).
3.  **Qui Tam Provisions:** The Organization recognizes the rights of individuals (relators) to file actions under the False Claims Act (Qui Tam suits) if they have knowledge of fraud against the government.

#### 4.7 Investigation and Confidentiality
1.  **Authority:** The Audit Committee (or a designated Special Committee) has the primary authority to investigate all suspected fraudulent acts as defined in **Bylaws Article VI**.
2.  **Process:** Investigations will be conducted objectively and confidentially to the extent permitted by law.
3.  **Cooperation:** All employees and volunteers must cooperate fully with internal and external investigations.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Sets the "tone at the top" for ethical conduct; reviews reports from the Audit Committee regarding fraud investigations. |
| **Audit Committee** | Oversees the Organization's anti-fraud controls; manages investigations into allegations of fraud or kickbacks; ensures reporting to federal agencies as required by 2 CFR 200.113. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Implements internal controls to prevent fraud; ensures staff training on FCA and anti-kickback rules. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Maintains segregation of duties; conducts regular internal audits of procurement and expense reports; monitors financial data for anomalies. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Act with integrity; safeguard Organization assets; report suspected irregularities immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Violations of this policy are severe. Consequences include:
1.  **Internal Discipline:** Termination of employment, removal from the Board, or termination of volunteer status.
2.  **Civil Liability:** Under the False Claims Act, individuals and the Organization may be liable for treble damages (3x the amount of damages) plus civil penalties per false claim.
3.  **Criminal Prosecution:** Referral to the U.S. Department of Justice or local law enforcement for criminal prosecution under applicable fraud and bribery statutes.
4.  **Debarment:** The Organization or individuals may be debarred from receiving future federal funding.
