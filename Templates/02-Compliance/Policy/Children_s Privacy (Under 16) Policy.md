### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict governance regarding the collection, processing, storage, and protection of data belonging to individuals under the age of 16. This policy ensures Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") complies with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and other relevant global privacy laws. It aims to create a safe, educational environment by prioritizing data minimization and parental control.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All employees, Board Directors, Operational Officers, volunteers, and independent contractors.
*   **Assets:** All websites, applications, learning management systems (LMS), and digital platforms owned or operated by the Organization.
*   **Data:** Any Personally Identifiable Information (PII) collected from users known to be, or reasonably suspected to be, under the age of 16.

**Exclusions:**
*   Data collected from individuals verified to be age 18 or older.
*   Anonymized or de-identified data that cannot reasonably be linked back to a specific child.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Child (Under 13)** | Any individual under the age of 13. Data collection for this group is strictly regulated by COPPA and TDPSA. |
| **Young User (13-15)** | Individuals aged 13 through 15. While not subject to COPPA, this group is afforded enhanced privacy protections under this policy (e.g., GDPR "digital age of consent" variations and California CPRA opt-in requirements). |
| **Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC)** | A method reasonably calculated, in light of available technology, to ensure that the person providing consent is the child's parent or legal guardian (e.g., credit card verification, signed form, video call). |
| **Age Gating** | A technical measure used to prevent access to certain content or data collection features based on the user's self-declared age. |
| **Educational Exemption** | Limited data collection permitted solely for the use and benefit of the school/educational context, where the school consents on behalf of the parent (strictly defined under COPPA). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Age Verification and Neutral Age Gating
To ensure compliance with Bylaws Article IX (Data Protection) and federal law:
4.1.1 **Mandatory Screening:** All public-facing digital entry points (registration pages, newsletter sign-ups, Discord community entry) must employ a neutral age-screening mechanism.
4.1.2 **Neutrality:** The age gate must ask for the user's date of birth or age in a neutral manner that does not encourage falsification (e.g., avoiding checkboxes that say "I am over 13" and instead using a date picker).
4.1.3 **Technical Blocking:** Systems must be configured to automatically block registration or restrict data collection if the input age is under 13, unless a VPC workflow is triggered.

#### 4.2 Treatment of Children Under 13 (COPPA & TDPSA Compliance)
4.2.1 **Zero-Data Default:** The Organization shall not collect PII (including names, emails, IP addresses, or persistent identifiers) from children under 13 without prior Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC).
4.2.2 **Direct Notice:** Before collecting data, the Organization must provide a "Direct Notice" to the parent/guardian detailing what information is collected, how it is used, and disclosure practices.
4.2.3 **VPC Mechanisms:** Acceptable methods for VPC include:
*   Providing a consent form to be signed by the parent and returned via scan/email.
*   Requiring a monetary transaction (e.g., $1 donation) via credit card to verify identity.
*   Video conference verification with trained staff.
4.2.4 **School Consent:** If the Organization provides services directly to schools, the Organization may rely on the school to provide consent, provided the data is used *only* for educational purposes and not for commercial gain.

#### 4.3 Treatment of Young Users (Ages 13-15)
4.3.1 **Prohibition on Data Sale:** The Organization strictly prohibits the sale of data belonging to users known to be under 16.
4.3.2 **Targeted Advertising Ban:** No behavioral advertising or tracking for the purpose of profiling shall be enabled for users under 16.
4.3.3 **Privacy by Default:** Accounts identified as belonging to users aged 13-15 must have privacy settings set to "High" or "Private" by default.

#### 4.4 Parental Rights and Control
Parents/Guardians of users under 16 retain the right to:
4.4.1 **Review:** Inspect the personal information collected from their child.
4.4.2 **Revoke:** Revoke consent and refuse further use or collection of data.
4.4.3 **Delete:** Request the permanent deletion of their child’s personal information.
4.4.4 **Process:** Requests must be submitted to the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) or the Privacy Team and must be fulfilled within 45 days.

#### 4.5 Data Minimization and Retention
4.5.1 **Necessity Principle:** The Organization shall collect only the PII that is reasonably necessary for the child to participate in the educational activity. Conditioning participation on the disclosure of unnecessary PII is prohibited.
4.5.2 **Retention:** Data collected from minors shall be retained only as long as necessary to fulfill the educational purpose or as required by law. Once the purpose is met, data must be securely deleted in accordance with the *Data Retention and Destruction Policy*.

#### 4.6 Third-Party Vendors and Tools
4.6.1 **Vetting:** No third-party tool (e.g., analytics, plugins, chat bots) may be integrated into areas of the platform directed at children unless that vendor has been vetted for COPPA/GDPR-K compliance.
4.6.2 **Data Sharing:** Sharing PII of users under 16 with third parties is prohibited unless the third party is a "Service Provider" bound by contract to use the data solely for Organization operations and to maintain confidentiality.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Responsible for the technical implementation of age gates, data segregation, and security controls for minor data. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Acts as the primary point of contact for parental inquiries, consent verification, and privacy complaints. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures curriculum and educational platforms do not request unnecessary PII from students. |
| **Legal/Compliance Committee** | Reviews this policy bi-annually to ensure alignment with evolving state (TDPSA) and federal (COPPA 2.0) regulations. |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Responsible for immediately reporting any accidental collection of under-13 data to the CISO. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the Organization at significant legal and reputational risk.
*   **Internal:** Violations by staff or volunteers may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Legal:** Non-compliance may result in investigations by the FTC or State Attorneys General, leading to fines (up to $50,120 per violation under COPPA).
*   **Remediation:** Any data found to be collected from a child under 13 without VPC must be deleted immediately from all live and backup systems.
