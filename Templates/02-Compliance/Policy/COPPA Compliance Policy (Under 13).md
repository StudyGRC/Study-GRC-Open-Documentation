### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the legal and ethical obligations of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of Personal Information from children under the age of 13. This policy ensures strict compliance with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and the Federal Trade Commission’s (FTC) 2025 Rule updates ("COPPA 2.0"), safeguarding the privacy and safety of minors within our educational ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **All Personnel:** Employees, Board Directors, Operational Officers, and Volunteers.
*   **All Platforms:** The Organization’s website, Learning Management Systems (LMS), community forums (e.g., Discord, Slack), and any digital service where user data is collected.
*   **Third-Party Vendors:** Service providers who process data on behalf of the Organization.

**Exclusions:** This policy specifically addresses users *under the age of 13*. Users aged 13-17 are covered under the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy (HR-POL-060)* and general privacy standards.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Child** | An individual under the age of 13. |
| **Personal Information (PI)** | Individually identifiable information about an individual collected online, including: first and last name; home or other physical address; online contact information (email, IM identifier, VoIP identifier, video chat identifier); screen name or username; telephone number; SSN; persistent identifier (cookie, IP address, device serial number); photograph, video, or audio file containing a child’s image or voice; and geolocation information. |
| **Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC)** | Any reasonable effort (taking into consideration available technology) to ensure that a parent of a child receives notice of the operator's personal information collection, use, and disclosure practices, and authorizes the collection, use, and disclosure, as applicable, of personal information and the subsequent use of that information before that information is collected from the child. |
| **Direct Notice** | A specific notice sent directly to a parent regarding the collection of their child's PI, distinct from the general online privacy policy. |
| **Mixed Audience** | A website or online service that is directed to children but does not target them as its primary audience. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Prohibition and Neutral Screening
Study GRC Inc. is a "Mixed Audience" platform. While our GRC content is generally professional in nature, our commitment to open-source education may attract learners of all ages.
*   **Age Gating:** All users registering for an account must pass a neutral age screen (e.g., "Please enter your date of birth") that does not encourage falsification.
*   **Under 13 Restriction:** Users identified as under 13 are prohibited from creating accounts or accessing community features unless and until **Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC)** is obtained.
*   **Data Minimization:** In accordance with COPPA 2.0 principles, the Organization shall not condition a child’s participation in an activity on the disclosure of more Personal Information than is reasonably necessary to participate in that activity.

#### 4.2 Direct Notice to Parents
Prior to collecting any PI from a child under 13, the Organization must provide **Direct Notice** to the parent or legal guardian. This notice must be clear, easy to read, and contain:
1.  That the Organization has collected the parent's online contact information from the child, and, if that is the case, the name of the child or the parent, in order to obtain the parent's consent;
2.  That the parent's consent is required for the collection, use, or disclosure of such information, and that the Organization will not collect, use, or disclose any personal information from the child if the parent does not provide such consent;
3.  That the parent may review the website's privacy policy (link provided); and
4.  How the parent can provide consent.

#### 4.3 Verifiable Parental Consent (VPC) Mechanisms
The Organization utilizes the "Sliding Scale" approach for VPC based on the intended use of the data:
*   **Internal Use Only:** If the data is used solely for internal educational tracking and *not* disclosed to third parties or made public (e.g., public profile), the Organization may use the "Email Plus" method (emailing the parent, receiving a reply, and sending a confirmation).
*   **Public Disclosure/Community Interaction:** If the child will be allowed to post in forums, chat, or have a public profile, a higher standard of VPC is required. This includes:
    *   A signed consent form returned via secure upload or mail;
    *   A monetary transaction (e.g., nominal donation) using a credit card; or
    *   Video conference verification with trained staff.

#### 4.4 Exceptions to Prior Consent
The Organization may collect limited PI without prior VPC *only* under the following statutory exceptions:
1.  **One-Time Contact:** To respond to a specific one-time request from the child (email is deleted immediately after responding).
2.  **Safety:** To protect the safety of a child who is participating on the site (parent must be notified immediately).
3.  **Site Security:** To protect the security or integrity of the website (e.g., IP addresses for firewall logs), provided this information is not used for any other purpose.

#### 4.5 Parental Rights and Control
In accordance with Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection), parents of children under 13 have the right to:
*   Review the PI collected from their child.
*   Revoke consent and refuse the further use or collection of PI.
*   Request the deletion of their child’s PI.
*   **Process:** Parents may exercise these rights by submitting a Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) to the Privacy Officer. The Organization must verify the identity of the parent before releasing data.

#### 4.6 Data Retention and Security
*   **Retention:** Personal Information collected from children will be retained only as long as is reasonably necessary to fulfill the purpose for which it was collected. Once the purpose is fulfilled (or upon parental request), data must be securely deleted using measures that render it unrecoverable.
*   **Security:** The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) must establish and maintain reasonable procedures to protect the confidentiality, security, and integrity of PI collected from children. This includes encryption at rest and in transit.

#### 4.7 Third-Party Service Providers
The Organization shall not disclose children's PI to third parties unless:
1.  The third party is a "Service Provider" performing a function on behalf of the Organization (e.g., hosting, LMS provider);
2.  The Service Provider has agreed in writing (via Data Processing Agreement) to maintain the confidentiality and security of the PI; and
3.  The Service Provider is prohibited from using the PI for any other purpose.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensures the Organization maintains adequate resources and insurance for COPPA compliance. Approves this policy. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Accountable for the operational implementation of this policy and the selection of VPC vendors/methods. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Implements technical controls (age gates, encryption, access controls) and oversees data deletion protocols. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Monitors community channels for users who may have misrepresented their age and facilitates the removal of underage users lacking VPC. |
| **Privacy Officer** | Manages the Direct Notice process, verifies parental consent, and handles Parental DSARs. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the Organization at significant legal risk, including civil penalties of up to **$51,744 per violation** (indexed for inflation) by the FTC.

*   **Internal:** Staff or volunteers found violating this policy are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination and removal from the Board (per Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6).
*   **External:** Vendors failing to adhere to these requirements will have their contracts terminated immediately.
