### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure Study GRC Inc. complies with federal regulations regarding the preference for U.S.-produced goods, specifically **2 CFR § 200.322** ("Domestic preferences for procurements"). This policy establishes the requirement that, to the greatest extent practicable, the Organization shall purchase goods, products, and materials produced in the United States when utilizing federal financial assistance. This aligns with the Organization's commitment to fiscal responsibility and compliance as outlined in **Article VIII** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all procurement actions, contracts, and subawards funded **in whole or in part** by federal grants, cooperative agreements, or other federal financial assistance.

*   **Applies to:** All employees, Operational Officers, and contractors authorized to initiate purchases or negotiate contracts on behalf of Study GRC Inc.
*   **Exclusions:** Procurements funded entirely by unrestricted private donations or non-federal revenue sources, provided no federal funds are commingled in the specific transaction.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Produced in the United States** | For iron and steel products, this means that all manufacturing processes, from the initial melting stage through the application of coatings, occurred in the United States. For manufactured products, this means the product was manufactured in the U.S., and the cost of the components of the product that are mined, produced, or manufactured in the U.S. is greater than 55 percent of the total cost of all components. |
| **Greatest Extent Practicable** | A standard requiring the Organization to prioritize domestic products unless they are unavailable in sufficient quantity/quality, or if doing so would result in a cost increase that is unreasonable (typically defined as exceeding 25% of the cost of the foreign equivalent, subject to specific grant terms). |
| **Manufactured Products** | Items composed of non-ferrous metals (e.g., aluminum), plastics and polymer-based products (e.g., polyvinyl chloride, composite building materials), glass (including optical fiber), lumber, or drywall. |
| **Subaward** | An award provided by a pass-through entity (Study GRC Inc.) to a subrecipient for the subrecipient to carry out part of a Federal award. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Mandate
Pursuant to 2 CFR § 200.322, Study GRC Inc. must, to the greatest extent practicable, provide a preference for the purchase, acquisition, or use of goods, products, or materials produced in the United States. This preference applies regardless of the dollar threshold of the purchase, provided federal funds are utilized.

#### 4.2 Specific Material Categories
While this policy applies broadly, the Organization must strictly apply this preference to the following categories as explicitly defined in federal regulation:
*   **Metals:** Iron, steel, and aluminum.
*   **Construction Materials:** Cement and other aggregates.
*   **Manufactured Products:** Including but not limited to polymers, glass, lumber, and drywall.

#### 4.3 Mandatory Contract Provisions
In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)** and federal flow-down requirements, Study GRC Inc. must include a domestic preference clause in all subawards, contracts, and purchase orders exceeding the micro-purchase threshold ($10,000, or as adjusted) that are funded by federal awards.

#### 4.4 Documentation of Non-Availability (Exceptions)
If a domestic product cannot be procured because it is not available in sufficient quantity, is of unsatisfactory quality, or is unreasonably expensive, the purchasing agent must document the exception.
*   **Requirement:** A "Non-Availability Determination" memo must be attached to the purchase requisition.
*   **Content:** The memo must include market research demonstrating that domestic alternatives were sought but found inviable.
*   **Approval:** The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) must approve all Non-Availability Determinations prior to the execution of the purchase.

#### 4.5 Reasonableness of Cost
Study GRC Inc. is not required to purchase domestic goods if the cost is unreasonable. Unless a specific federal statute (such as the Buy American Act applicable to construction) specifies a different differential, Study GRC Inc. defines "unreasonable" as a cost differential where the domestic product exceeds the cost of a non-domestic equivalent by **25% or more**, inclusive of shipping and duties.

#### 4.6 Integration with Procurement Procedures
This policy operates in conjunction with the **Federal Standards Procurement Policy**. When evaluating bids or proposals (RFP/RFQ), the "Domestic Preference" must be a weighted factor in the Vendor Selection Evaluation Matrix.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Final authority on determining "reasonableness of cost" and approving Non-Availability Determinations. Ensures internal controls prevent payment on non-compliant federal procurements. |
| **Grants Manager** | Ensures all subawards and vendor contracts contain the required 2 CFR 200.322 clauses. Monitors subrecipients for compliance. |
| **Purchasing Staff / Requestors** | Responsible for conducting market research to identify U.S.-produced options and documenting justification if a foreign product is selected. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves this policy as part of the Organization's financial governance framework under Bylaws Article VIII. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in **disallowed costs**, requiring Study GRC Inc. to reimburse the federal government using non-federal funds. This poses a significant financial risk to the Organization.

Employees found to have knowingly circumvented domestic preference requirements or falsified non-availability documentation are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
