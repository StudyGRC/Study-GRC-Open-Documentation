### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a standardized, transparent, and equitable framework for evaluating the performance of employees and Key Volunteers at Study GRC Inc. This policy ensures organizational accountability, aligns individual contributions with the Organization’s strategic mission as defined in **Article I** of the Bylaws, and provides the necessary documentation to support compensation decisions, promotions, and, where necessary, corrective action or termination.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **All Paid Employees:** Full-time and part-time staff.
*   **Operational Officers:** As defined in **Bylaws Article V** (e.g., Executive Director, CISO, CFO).
*   **Key Volunteers:** Individuals serving in long-term, high-responsibility volunteer roles (e.g., Committee Chairs, Department Heads) where performance directly impacts operational risk or grant compliance.

**Exclusions:**
*   Ad-hoc or event-based volunteers (governed by the *Volunteer Handbook*).
*   Members of the Board of Directors (governed by *Board Self-Assessment Evaluation Tool*).
*   Advisory Board members.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **OKR (Objectives and Key Results)** | A goal-setting framework used to define measurable goals and track their outcomes. |
| **PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)** | A formal document outlining specific performance deficiencies, required corrective actions, timelines, and consequences for failure to improve. |
| **Key Volunteer** | A volunteer with significant operational authority, access to sensitive data, or management responsibilities over other volunteers. |
| **360-Degree Review** | A feedback process where an individual receives input from supervisors, peers, and direct reports. |
| **At-Will Employment** | The legal doctrine in Texas whereby either the employer or the employee may terminate the employment relationship at any time, for any reason (not prohibited by law), or for no reason at all. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Performance Philosophy
Study GRC Inc. views performance management as a continuous cycle of communication rather than a single annual event. As a remote-first organization, we prioritize **outcomes and impact** over hours worked or physical presence. Performance evaluations must be objective, evidence-based, and directly tied to the Organization’s charitable and educational purposes.

#### 4.2 The Performance Cycle
The performance management cycle follows the Organization’s Fiscal Year (Calendar Year per **Bylaws Article VIII**).
1.  **Goal Setting (January):** Establishment of annual OKRs.
2.  **Mid-Year Check-in (July):** Formal progress review and adjustment of goals.
3.  **Year-End Review (December/January):** Final evaluation of performance against goals and core values.

#### 4.3 Goal Setting and Alignment
*   **Strategic Alignment:** All individual goals must cascade from the Organization’s Strategic Plan.
*   **SMART Criteria:** Goals must be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.
*   **Grant Compliance:** For staff funded by federal grants, performance goals must align with the objectives outlined in the relevant grant award to satisfy **2 CFR 200.430** (Compensation - Personal Services).

#### 4.4 Continuous Feedback and Remote Management
*   **1:1 Meetings:** Managers must conduct regular (minimum bi-weekly) 1:1 video meetings with direct reports to discuss progress, roadblocks, and well-being.
*   **Documentation:** Managers are required to maintain a "Manager’s Log" of significant performance events (positive and negative) to ensure the annual review is based on data, not recency bias.

#### 4.5 Annual Performance Reviews
*   **Self-Evaluation:** All employees and Key Volunteers must complete a self-evaluation form prior to the manager's review.
*   **Manager Evaluation:** Managers must provide a written evaluation assessing:
    1.  Achievement of OKRs.
    2.  Adherence to the *Code of Ethics* and *Core Values*.
    3.  Skill development and training needs.
*   **Executive Director Evaluation:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V**, the Board of Directors shall evaluate the Executive Director annually. This review will be led by the Board President or a designated committee.

#### 4.6 Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)
If an employee or Key Volunteer fails to meet performance standards, the manager, in consultation with HR/Legal, may initiate a PIP.
*   **Issuance:** A PIP is not mandatory prior to termination but may be used when the manager believes performance is correctable.
*   **Duration:** Typically 30 to 90 days.
*   **Content:** The PIP must clearly state the deficiency, the expected standard, the support provided by the organization, and the consequences of failure to improve.

#### 4.7 Compensation and Rewards
*   **Merit Increases:** Salary adjustments are not guaranteed and are subject to budget availability and Board approval.
*   **Federal Grants:** Compensation increases for grant-funded positions must be supported by a documented performance evaluation in accordance with **2 CFR 200.430**.

#### 4.8 Record Retention
Performance reviews, PIPs, and disciplinary records must be retained in the individual’s personnel file in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy* and **Texas Labor Code**. These records are confidential and accessible only to the employee, their direct chain of command, and HR/Legal administrators.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Conducts the annual performance evaluation of the Executive Director; approves the annual compensation budget. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures the performance management system is implemented organization-wide; evaluates direct reports (Operational Officers). |
| **Managers** | Conducts 1:1s, drafts evaluations, initiates PIPs, and ensures staff goals align with the Strategic Plan. |
| **Employees / Key Volunteers** | Participates actively in goal setting, completes self-evaluations, and seeks feedback proactively. |
| **HR / Compliance** | Maintains personnel files, ensures PIPs are legally compliant, and monitors the fairness/equity of the review process. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**At-Will Disclaimer:** Nothing in this policy alters the At-Will employment relationship defined under Texas law. This policy is not a contract of employment.

**Enforcement:** Failure to participate in the performance management process (e.g., a manager failing to deliver a review) may result in disciplinary action. Falsification of performance metrics or evaluation data is considered a violation of the *Code of Ethics* and is grounds for immediate termination.
