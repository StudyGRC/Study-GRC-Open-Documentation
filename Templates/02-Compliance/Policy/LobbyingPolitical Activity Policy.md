### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") maintains its tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code (IRC) by strictly adhering to federal prohibitions regarding political campaign activity and limitations regarding lobbying. This policy distinguishes between prohibited political intervention, limited legislative lobbying, and permitted non-partisan educational advocacy.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, Operational Officers, employees, volunteers, and agents of the Organization. It governs the use of the Organization’s name, resources, financial assets, facilities, and communication channels (including social media, email, and community forums).

**Exclusions:** This policy does not restrict individuals from engaging in political activity in their **private, personal capacity**, provided they do not use the Organization’s resources or imply the Organization’s endorsement.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Political Campaign Activity** | Directly or indirectly participating in, or intervening in, any political campaign on behalf of (or in opposition to) any candidate for elective public office. This includes contributions to campaign funds and making public statements of position. |
| **Lobbying** | Attempting to influence legislation. This includes **Direct Lobbying** (contacting legislators/staff to influence specific legislation) and **Grassroots Lobbying** (encouraging the public to contact legislators regarding specific legislation). |
| **Legislation** | Action by Congress, state legislatures, local councils, or similar governing bodies with respect to acts, bills, resolutions, or similar items (such as the confirmation of appointees). |
| **Non-Partisan Education** | The analysis, study, or research of issues (including GRC topics) and the dissemination of results to the public, provided the materials present a sufficiently full and fair exposition of facts to permit an individual to form an independent opinion. |
| **Public Office** | Any position in government (federal, state, or local) that is filled by an election process. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Absolute Prohibition on Political Campaign Activity
Pursuant to the Organization's tax-exempt purpose defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, Study GRC Inc. is strictly prohibited from engaging in Political Campaign Activity.
4.1.1 **No Endorsements:** The Organization shall not endorse or oppose any candidate for public office.
4.1.2 **No Contributions:** The Organization shall not make any financial contribution or in-kind contribution (staff time, use of facilities, mailing lists) to a candidate, political party, or Political Action Committee (PAC).
4.1.3 **No Rating of Candidates:** The Organization shall not publish or distribute statements that rate candidates, even on a non-partisan basis.

#### 4.2. Limitations on Lobbying (Legislative Activity)
While 501(c)(3) organizations may engage in limited lobbying, it must not constitute a "substantial part" of the Organization's activities unless the 501(h) election has been made.
4.2.1 **Permissible Scope:** Lobbying is permitted only if it relates directly to the Organization’s mission (e.g., advocating for cybersecurity legislation or open-source governance standards) and remains within IRS allowable limits.
4.2.2 **Federal Grant Prohibition:** In accordance with **2 CFR 200.450**, no federal grant funds may be used for lobbying activities. All lobbying expenditures must be segregated from federal award funds.
4.2.3 **Tracking:** Any staff time or expenses related to lobbying must be tracked separately in the financial system to ensure accurate reporting on IRS Form 990, Schedule C.

#### 4.3. Use of Organizational Resources
Organizational resources may not be used for prohibited political activities or unauthorized lobbying.
4.3.1 **Assets:** Computers, printers, office supplies, and telephones owned by the Organization may not be used for political campaign work.
4.3.2 **Digital Channels:** The Organization’s email systems, social media accounts, and website may not be used to support or oppose candidates.
4.3.3 **Community Platforms:** Users of the Organization’s community platforms (e.g., Slack, Discord, Forums) are prohibited from using these spaces for soliciting votes or fundraising for political candidates. The **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** is authorized to moderate and remove such content.

#### 4.4. Personal Political Activity
Directors, Officers, and employees are free to engage in political activity in their individual capacities.
4.4.1 **Disclaimer Requirement:** When expressing personal political views in a public forum where the individual is known to be associated with Study GRC Inc., the individual must explicitly state: *"The views expressed here are my own and do not represent the views of Study GRC Inc."*
4.4.2 **No Use of Title:** Individuals may not use their official Organization title or affiliation in a manner that suggests the Organization supports a candidate (e.g., signing a campaign endorsement letter as "Executive Director of Study GRC Inc.").
4.4.3 **No Uniforms/Logos:** Organization-branded clothing or logos shall not be worn or displayed while participating in political rallies or campaign events.

#### 4.5. Invitations to Candidates
If the Organization invites a candidate to speak at an event:
4.5.1 **Equal Opportunity:** All viable candidates for the same office must be invited and given an equal opportunity to speak.
4.5.2 **Non-Political Capacity:** Candidates may appear in a non-candidate capacity (e.g., as a GRC expert), provided no mention is made of their candidacy or the election.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Responsible for the overall compliance of the Organization with IRS regulations; determines if the Organization will make the 501(h) election. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Approves all public policy statements; ensures no organizational resources are used for political campaigns; monitors lobbying thresholds. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Ensures proper segregation of funds (no federal funds for lobbying); prepares IRS Form 990 Schedule C reporting. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Moderates community forums to remove prohibited political solicitation or campaigning. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Must distinguish between personal and professional acts; must track time spent on any authorized legislative advocacy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Violations of this policy threaten the Organization’s legal existence and tax-exempt status.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (for cause per **Bylaws Section 3.6**), or banning from the community.
*   **Legal Consequences:** The Organization may be subject to IRS excise taxes under IRC Section 4955 for political expenditures. Individuals responsible for the violation may be personally liable for these taxes.
