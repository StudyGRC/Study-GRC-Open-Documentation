### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Volunteer Code of Conduct is to establish clear behavioral expectations and ethical standards for all individuals contributing time and expertise to Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy mitigates reputational, legal, and operational risks by defining acceptable conduct regarding professional ethics, youth protection, intellectual property, and community interaction. It ensures that all volunteer activities align with the Organization’s charitable mission as defined in **Article I** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals serving the Organization in an unpaid capacity, including but not limited to:
*   Members of the Board of Directors (subject additionally to the *Board Code of Ethics*).
*   Advisory Board members.
*   Committee members and Working Group participants.
*   Community Moderators (Discord, Slack, Forum).
*   Content Creators, Instructors, and Curriculum Developers.
*   Event Staff and on-site volunteers.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not replace the *Employee Handbook* for paid staff, though paid staff are expected to uphold these standards when interacting with volunteers.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer** | Any individual who performs services for the Organization without promise, expectation, or receipt of compensation for services rendered. |
| **Vulnerable Population** | Individuals under the age of 18 (minors) or adults who may be vulnerable due to disability, age, or other factors. |
| **Work Product** | Any content, code, curriculum, documentation, or media created by a volunteer specifically for the Organization. |
| **Harassment** | Unwelcome conduct based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or genetic information. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | A safety policy requiring a minimum of two background-checked adults to be present during any interaction with a minor. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Professionalism and Integrity
Volunteers act as representatives of Study GRC Inc. and must conduct themselves with honesty, integrity, and respect.
*   **4.1.1 Good Faith:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article VII**, volunteers must act in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in the best interest of the Organization.
*   **4.1.2 Civil Discourse:** Volunteers must communicate respectfully in all Organization channels. Disagreement is permitted; disrespect, personal attacks, and trolling are prohibited.
*   **4.1.3 Reliability:** Volunteers accepting a role or task must fulfill their commitment or provide timely notice to their coordinator if they are unable to do so.

#### 4.2 Harassment and Non-Discrimination
The Organization maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, discrimination, and bullying.
*   **4.2.1 Prohibited Conduct:** Sexual harassment, hate speech, slurs, and discriminatory jokes are strictly prohibited in all physical and digital spaces (including Slack, Discord, and GitHub).
*   **4.2.2 Retaliation:** Retaliation against any individual who reports a violation of this code in good faith is strictly prohibited and constitutes grounds for immediate dismissal.

#### 4.3 Youth Protection and Safeguarding
Given the Organization's K-12 focus and educational mission, strict adherence to youth protection standards is mandatory.
*   **4.3.1 No One-on-One Private Contact:** Volunteers are strictly prohibited from having private, one-on-one interactions (DM, text, video call) with a known minor. All interactions must occur in public channels or with a second background-checked adult present ("Two-Deep Leadership").
*   **4.3.2 Mandatory Reporting:** Any volunteer who suspects child abuse or neglect must immediately report it to the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (as required by Texas Family Code Chapter 261) and the Organization’s designated Safety Officer.
*   **4.3.3 Grooming Behaviors:** Behaviors such as giving special gifts, sharing excessive personal information, or seeking emotional dependency from minors are prohibited.

#### 4.4 Intellectual Property and Open Source
To protect the Organization's mission of providing zero-cost resources (**Bylaws Article I**), strict IP rules apply.
*   **4.4.1 Assignment of Rights:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all Work Product created by a volunteer for the Organization is considered a "work made for hire." The volunteer assigns all rights, title, and interest in such work to Study GRC Inc.
*   **4.4.2 Open Source Commitment:** The Organization commits to releasing applicable educational resources under Open Source licenses (e.g., CC-BY, MIT). Volunteers agree that their contributions will be distributed under these licenses.
*   **4.4.3 Third-Party Material:** Volunteers must not submit content that violates the copyright, trademark, or proprietary rights of any third party. Plagiarism is grounds for dismissal.

#### 4.5 Confidentiality and Data Privacy
Volunteers may have access to sensitive data, including member lists and donor information.
*   **4.5.1 Non-Disclosure:** Volunteers must not disclose confidential information to unauthorized parties. This includes, but is not limited to, donor identities, unpublished financial data, and security vulnerabilities.
*   **4.5.2 Data Privacy Compliance:** Volunteers handling personal data must comply with the Organization’s *Privacy Policy*, GDPR, and the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA). Exporting member lists for personal or commercial use is strictly prohibited.

#### 4.6 Conflict of Interest
*   **4.6.1 Disclosure:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1**, volunteers holding decision-making authority must disclose any financial or personal interest that may conflict with the interests of the Organization.
*   **4.6.2 Self-Dealing:** Volunteers may not use their position to secure personal financial gain, business advantages, or special privileges for themselves or their family members.

#### 4.7 Technology and Security
*   **4.7.1 Access Control:** Volunteers must use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all Organization accounts (Google Workspace, GitHub, AWS) where available.
*   **4.7.2 Account Sharing:** Sharing login credentials is strictly prohibited.
*   **4.7.3 Social Media:** Volunteers must not claim to speak officially on behalf of the Organization on social media unless explicitly authorized by the Board or the Executive Director.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer** | Read, sign, and adhere to this Code of Conduct. Report known violations. |
| **Volunteer Coordinator** | Ensure all volunteers have signed this Code prior to onboarding. Conduct background checks for youth-facing roles. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Mediate interpersonal disputes and foster a positive community culture. |
| **Executive Director** | Investigate serious violations and determine disciplinary actions. |
| **Board of Directors** | Review appeals regarding volunteer dismissal and oversee high-level ethics violations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including:
1.  Verbal or written warning.
2.  Suspension of volunteer privileges and access to systems.
3.  Permanent termination of volunteer status and expulsion from the Organization.
4.  Legal action (civil or criminal) in cases of theft, fraud, or abuse.

**Reporting Violations:** Violations should be reported via the *Whistleblower Complaint Intake Form* or directly to the Director of Kindness and Generosity. Reports involving youth safety must be escalated immediately.
