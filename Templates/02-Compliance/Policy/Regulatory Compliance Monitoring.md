### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a **Proactive Compliance Framework** for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). Rather than reacting to legal violations after they occur, this policy mandates the continuous identification, assessment, and monitoring of the regulatory environment. This ensures the Organization maintains its 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status, eligibility for federal grants, and public trust by adhering to all applicable federal, state, and international laws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the entire "Compliance Universe" of the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   **Corporate Governance:** Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC).
*   **Tax Exemption:** Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 501(c)(3).
*   **Federal Grants:** 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance).
*   **Data Privacy:** GDPR, TDPSA, COPPA.
*   **Youth Protection:** Texas Family Code (Chapter 261).
*   **Employment:** FLSA, EEOC, Texas Labor Code.

This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, and volunteers.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |

| **Compliance Universe** | The aggregate of all legislative, regulatory, and contractual obligations applicable to the Organization based on its activities and jurisdiction. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 The Proactive Framework
The Organization shall maintain a dynamic **Regulatory Compliance Matrix** that maps external legal requirements to internal controls. This matrix must be reviewed annually to ensure alignment with the Organization's evolving mission and operational footprint.

#### 4.2 Horizon Scanning and Regulatory Updates
The General Counsel or designated Compliance Officer must subscribe to relevant legal updates (e.g., IRS Exempt Organizations Update, Federal Register, Texas Secretary of State alerts).
*   **Quarterly Review:** A review of upcoming regulatory changes must be conducted quarterly.
*   **Impact Assessment:** When a relevant regulation changes, an Impact Assessment must be conducted within 30 days to determine necessary updates to internal policies (e.g., updating the *Privacy Policy* if state data privacy laws change).

#### 4.3 Federal Grant Compliance (2 CFR 200)
As a federal grantee, the Organization must strictly monitor compliance with **2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance)**.
*   **SAM.gov Monitoring:** The Organization must verify its standing in the System for Award Management (SAM) monthly to ensure no lapse in registration.
*   **Subrecipient Monitoring:** If the Organization passes funds to other entities, it must monitor those entities' compliance with federal statutes in accordance with 2 CFR 200.332.

#### 4.4 Corporate and Tax Status Monitoring
To protect the Organization's existence and tax status:
*   **Periodic Filings:** The Organization must track deadlines for the IRS Form 990, Texas Franchise Tax (No Tax Due Report), and Registered Agent filings.
*   **Bylaws Alignment:** In accordance with **Bylaws Article XII**, any change in state law that contradicts current Bylaws must trigger an immediate amendment process to ensure the Bylaws remain legally valid.

#### 4.5 Youth Protection and Safety
Given the Organization's K-12 focus, compliance with **Texas Family Code Chapter 261** (Mandatory Reporting) and **COPPA** (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) is non-negotiable.
*   **Audit:** A specific audit of youth protection protocols (background checks, reporting mechanisms) must be conducted annually.

#### 4.6 Reporting and Escalation
*   **Duty to Report:** Any employee or volunteer who identifies a potential regulatory breach must report it immediately via the channels defined in the *Whistleblower Protection Policy*.
*   **Board Notification:** The Board of Directors must be notified immediately of any *Material Non-Compliance* event, including inquiries from the IRS, Department of Labor, or law enforcement.

#### 4.7 Record Keeping
In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, evidence of compliance monitoring (e.g., audit logs, filing receipts, training rosters) must be retained according to the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ultimate fiduciary responsibility for ensuring the Organization remains compliant with laws. Reviews annual compliance reports. |
| **General Counsel / Compliance Officer** | Owns the Regulatory Compliance Matrix. Conducts Horizon Scanning. Interprets new laws and advises the Board. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures operational teams implement the controls required by regulations. Allocates budget for compliance tools/counsel. |
| **Department Heads** | Responsible for "Subject Specific" compliance (e.g., HR Director monitors Labor Law; CISO monitors Data Privacy laws). |
| **Internal Audit Committee** | Independently verifies that the monitoring activities described in this policy are actually occurring. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this policy can result in catastrophic legal consequences for the Organization, including revocation of 501(c)(3) status and personal liability for Directors and Officers.

Internal violations of this policy (e.g., ignoring a known regulatory change, falsifying compliance records) will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board/Volunteer position.
