### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this **Youth Code of Conduct** is to establish clear behavioral expectations for all minors participating in Study GRC Inc. programs. As a community-driven ecosystem dedicated to advancing Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) knowledge, we are committed to providing a safe, inclusive, and educational environment. This policy ensures that all youth participants understand their responsibilities in fostering a culture of kindness, integrity, and mutual respect, consistent with the Organization’s mission and Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals under the age of eighteen (18) ("Youth Participants") who engage with Study GRC Inc.
**Applies to:**
*   **Virtual Environments:** Official Discord servers, Zoom webinars, learning management systems (LMS), GitHub repositories, and social media channels managed by the Organization.
*   **Physical Environments:** In-person workshops, conferences, and meetups organized or sponsored by Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:**
*   Adult volunteers, staff, and Board members are governed by the *Board of Directors Code of Ethics and Conduct* and the *Volunteer Code of Conduct*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Youth Participant** | Any individual under the age of 18 participating in Study GRC Inc. programs or communities. |
| **Bullying** | Unwanted, aggressive behavior that involves a real or perceived power imbalance. This includes physical, verbal, or social harm. |
| **Cyberbullying** | Bullying that takes place over digital devices like cell phones, computers, and tablets, including sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. |
| **Doxxing** | The act of publicly revealing previously private personal information about an individual without their consent, often for malicious purposes. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | A safety policy requiring that at least two authorized adult background-checked volunteers be present during any interaction with a minor to prevent one-on-one isolation. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information (e.g., full name, home address, phone number, school name). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Core Principles
All Youth Participants are expected to uphold the Organization’s core values:
1.  **Kindness:** Treat all members, instructors, and peers with courtesy and respect.
2.  **Integrity:** Produce honest work and respect intellectual property.
3.  **Inclusion:** Welcome peers of all backgrounds, abilities, and identities.

#### 4.2 Digital Citizenship and Online Safety
Given the remote-first nature of Study GRC Inc., strict adherence to digital safety is mandatory:
*   **4.2.1 Identity:** Participants must not impersonate other users, staff, or volunteers.
*   **4.2.2 Account Security:** Participants may not share their login credentials with anyone other than their parent/guardian.
*   **4.2.3 Personal Information:** Participants are prohibited from posting their own PII or the PII of others in public channels.
*   **4.2.4 Recording:** Unauthorized recording (audio or video) of sessions, instructors, or other participants is strictly prohibited to protect privacy.

#### 4.3 Interactions with Adults
To ensure safety and compliance with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*:
*   **4.3.1 No Private DMs:** Youth Participants are prohibited from initiating or engaging in private Direct Messages (DMs) with adult volunteers or staff. All communication must occur in public channels or ticketed support systems visible to moderation teams.
*   **4.3.2 Reporting:** If an adult attempts to contact a Youth Participant privately or makes them feel uncomfortable, the participant must immediately report this to their parent/guardian and the Director of Kindness and Generosity.

#### 4.4 Prohibited Conduct (Zero Tolerance)
The following behaviors result in immediate removal from the program and potential reporting to law enforcement:
*   **4.4.1 Hate Speech:** Any language or imagery that attacks, threatens, or insults a group based on race, religion, gender, sexual orientation, or disability.
*   **4.4.2 Sexual Content:** Sharing, posting, or soliciting sexually explicit material or language.
*   **4.4.3 Violence:** Threats of physical harm to self or others, or promotion of illegal acts.
*   **4.4.4 Doxxing:** Publishing private information of another participant or volunteer.

#### 4.5 Prohibited Conduct (General)
The following behaviors will result in disciplinary action per Section 5:
*   **4.5.1 Disruption:** Deliberately disrupting educational sessions (e.g., "Zoom bombing," spamming chat).
*   **4.5.2 Academic Dishonesty:** Cheating on assessments or plagiarism of code/content.
*   **4.5.3 Profanity:** Use of vulgar or obscene language.
*   **4.5.4 Trolling:** Deliberately provoking others to elicit an emotional response.

### 5. Disciplinary Procedures

Study GRC Inc. employs a graduated disciplinary framework, though the Organization reserves the right to jump to immediate expulsion for severe violations.

#### 5.1 Level 1: Verbal/Written Warning
*   **Trigger:** Minor infractions (e.g., accidental spamming, mild profanity).
*   **Action:** The participant is contacted by a Moderator or the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG). The specific rule violation is explained, and the behavior must stop immediately.

#### 5.2 Level 2: Suspension and Parental Conference
*   **Trigger:** Repeated Level 1 violations, bullying, or academic dishonesty.
*   **Action:** Temporary suspension from all platforms (Discord, LMS). A mandatory conference (virtual) is held with the DKG, the participant, and the parent/guardian to review the Code of Conduct before reinstatement is considered.

#### 5.3 Level 3: Expulsion
*   **Trigger:** Violation of "Zero Tolerance" rules (Section 4.4), or failure to correct behavior after Level 2 intervention.
*   **Action:** Permanent removal from all Study GRC Inc. programs. No refunds (if applicable) will be issued.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Youth Participant** | Read, understand, and abide by this Code of Conduct. Report unsafe or unkind behavior to leadership. |
| **Parent/Guardian** | Review this Code with their child. Support Study GRC Inc. in enforcing behavioral standards. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Primary enforcer of this policy. Manages disciplinary reviews and parental communications. |
| **Moderators/Instructors** | Monitor channels and sessions for compliance. Issue Level 1 warnings and escalate severe issues to the DKG. |
| **Executive Director** | Final authority on appeals regarding expulsion decisions. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Participation in Study GRC Inc. programs is a privilege, not a right. Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of volunteer assignment or program participation, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation.

By accepting membership or participating in events, the Youth Participant and their Parent/Guardian acknowledge that Study GRC Inc. has the sole discretion to determine if a violation has occurred.
