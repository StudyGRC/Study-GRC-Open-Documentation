### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict boundaries and protocols for all electronic communications between Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") representatives and minors. This policy is designed to prevent abuse, sexual exploitation, and "grooming" behaviors, while simultaneously protecting staff and volunteers from false allegations of misconduct. This policy aligns with the Organization’s charitable and educational purposes defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, and the ethical standards required by **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.3**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers ("Personnel") of Study GRC Inc. It governs all forms of digital interaction including, but not limited to, email, text messaging, social media, video conferencing (e.g., Zoom), gaming platforms (e.g., Discord), and learning management systems.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to interactions between Personnel and their own children or legal dependents, or interactions that occur within the context of a pre-existing personal relationship established prior to the Personnel’s affiliation with the Organization (subject to disclosure to the Board).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor** | Any individual under the age of 18 years. |
| **Digital Two-Deep Leadership** | The requirement that a minimum of two background-checked adults be included in every digital communication thread, channel, or video session with a minor. |
| **Grooming** | The predatory act of maneuvering a minor into a position of vulnerability for sexual abuse, often by establishing an emotional connection, isolating the child, or desensitizing them to boundaries. |
| **Authorized Platform** | A communication channel officially sanctioned, monitored, and logged by the Organization (e.g., Organization email, official Discord server). |
| **Private Message (DM)** | Any one-to-one communication that is not visible to others, including "Direct Messages," "Whispers," or "Private Chats." |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Authorized Communication Channels
Personnel must strictly use **Authorized Platforms** for all communications with minors.
*   **4.1.1** Use of personal email addresses, personal social media accounts, personal gaming handles, or personal mobile numbers to communicate with minors regarding Organization business is **strictly prohibited**.
*   **4.1.2** All Authorized Platforms must have logging and archiving capabilities enabled to ensure an audit trail of all interactions.

#### 4.2 The "Digital Two-Deep" Rule
To prevent isolation—a key tactic in grooming—one-on-one private communication between an adult Personnel member and a minor is **strictly prohibited**.
*   **4.2.1** **Email:** Personnel must copy (CC) a parent/guardian or a second background-checked Personnel member on every email sent to a minor.
*   **4.2.2** **Direct Messages (DMs):** DMs with minors on platforms like Slack or Discord are prohibited unless a group DM is created that includes a second background-checked adult.
*   **4.2.3** **Video Conferencing:** Personnel must never be in a video conference breakout room alone with a minor. If a minor remains alone in a session, the Personnel member must immediately terminate the session or move the minor to the main waiting room.

#### 4.3 Social Media and Gaming Boundaries
*   **4.3.1** Personnel are prohibited from sending or accepting "Friend Requests," "Follow Requests," or connection requests from minors who are program participants on personal social media accounts (e.g., Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok).
*   **4.3.2** Personnel must not "tag" minors in photos or posts without explicit, written parental consent (refer to *Photo and Video Release for Minors*).
*   **4.3.3** Use of ephemeral messaging apps (where messages disappear automatically, e.g., Snapchat) for Organization business is **strictly prohibited**.

#### 4.4 Content and Tone
*   **4.4.1** All digital interactions must remain professional, constructive, and focused on the Organization’s educational mission.
*   **4.4.2** Personnel must not engage in sexualized conversation, share sexually explicit material, or discuss their own personal/sexual life with minors.
*   **4.4.3** Personnel must not encourage minors to keep secrets from their parents or guardians. Any request by a minor to keep a secret must be met with the statement that the Personnel member cannot keep secrets that involve the safety or well-being of the child.

#### 4.5 Hours of Operation
*   **4.5.1** Digital communications with minors should occur during standard waking hours (e.g., 8:00 AM to 9:00 PM local time).
*   **4.5.2** Late-night communication is a red flag for grooming behavior. Unless there is a documented emergency regarding an active event, Personnel must not message minors outside of these hours.

#### 4.6 Video Conferencing Protocols
*   **4.6.1** Cameras must be positioned to show a neutral background. Personnel must be fully and appropriately clothed.
*   **4.6.2** Recording of video sessions involving minors is permitted only for educational or safety purposes and requires prior notification to all participants.
*   **4.6.3** Personnel must utilize "waiting room" features to vet participants before allowing entry to prevent unauthorized access ("Zoom-bombing").

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Personnel** | Adhere strictly to the Digital Two-Deep rule and report any violations or suspicious behavior immediately. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Oversee the implementation of this policy and serve as the primary point of contact for reporting concerns. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Configure Organization platforms (e.g., Discord, Google Workspace) to enforce restrictions where technically feasible (e.g., disabling DMs for certain roles). |
| **Board of Directors** | Review and approve this policy annually; ensure adequate resources for background checks and monitoring tools. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Zero Tolerance:** Study GRC Inc. maintains a zero-tolerance policy regarding the abuse or exploitation of minors.

*   **Internal Discipline:** Failure to comply with this policy, even without malicious intent, may result in disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and permanent expulsion from the Organization.
*   **Legal Reporting:** In accordance with **Texas Family Code Chapter 261**, any Personnel who has cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect must immediately report the case to the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) or local law enforcement.
*   **Cooperation:** The Organization will fully cooperate with law enforcement agencies in the investigation of any alleged illegal conduct.
