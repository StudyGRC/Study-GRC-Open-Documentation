### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to protect the tax-exempt status of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") and to protect the Organization, its Directors, Officers, and Managers from excise tax penalties imposed under Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 4958.

This policy establishes the procedures necessary to establish a **Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness** for compensation arrangements and property transactions involving "Disqualified Persons," ensuring that the Organization does not provide an economic benefit that exceeds the value of the consideration received.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all financial transactions, compensation arrangements, and property transfers between the Organization and any **Disqualified Person** (as defined below).

**Applies to:**
*   Board of Directors (Voting Members).
*   Operational Officers (e.g., Executive Director/CEO, CISO, CFO).
*   Key Employees and substantial contributors.
*   Family members and entities controlled by the individuals listed above.

**Exclusions:**
*   Transactions with vendors or employees who are not Disqualified Persons.
*   De minimis fringe benefits excluded under IRC § 132.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Excess Benefit Transaction (EBT)** | A transaction in which an economic benefit is provided by the Organization to or for the use of a Disqualified Person, and the value of the economic benefit provided exceeds the value of the consideration (including the performance of services) received for providing the benefit. |
| **Disqualified Person (DQP)** | Any person who was in a position to exercise substantial influence over the affairs of the Organization at any time during the five-year period ending on the date of the transaction. This includes Voting Directors, Officers (Bylaws Art. IV & V), and their family members. |
| **Intermediate Sanctions** | Excise taxes imposed by the IRS under IRC § 4958 on Disqualified Persons who receive an excess benefit and on Organization Managers who knowingly approve such transactions. |
| **Rebuttable Presumption** | A legal standard where the burden of proof shifts to the IRS to prove a transaction was excessive, provided the Organization followed specific procedural steps (Independent Body, Comparable Data, Documentation). |
| **Fair Market Value (FMV)** | The price at which property or the right to use property would change hands between a willing buyer and a willing seller, neither being under any compulsion to buy, sell, or transfer property or the right to use property, and both having reasonable knowledge of relevant facts. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Prohibition
Study GRC Inc. is prohibited from engaging in any Excess Benefit Transaction. No part of the net earnings of the Organization shall inure to the benefit of any private shareholder or individual.

#### 4.2 Identification of Disqualified Persons
The Board of Directors must maintain a current list of Disqualified Persons. Pursuant to Bylaws Article III and Article V, the following are automatically deemed DQPs:
*   All Voting Directors.
*   The President, Secretary, and Treasurer.
*   The Executive Director (CEO), Chief Financial Officer (CFO), and Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).
*   Any individual who has contributed more than 2% of the Organization's total donations over the past five years.

#### 4.3 Establishing the Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness
To prevent EBTs, the Organization must follow the "Three Steps" outlined in Treasury Regulation § 53.4958-6 for all compensation arrangements and property transactions involving a DQP.

**4.3.1 Step 1: Approval by an Authorized Body**
The transaction must be reviewed and approved in advance by the Board of Directors or a designated Committee (e.g., Finance Committee) composed entirely of individuals who do not have a Conflict of Interest with respect to the transaction.
*   The interested DQP must recuse themselves from the meeting during the debate and vote, pursuant to Bylaws Article IX (Conflict of Interest).

**4.3.2 Step 2: Reliance on Appropriate Comparable Data**
The Authorized Body must obtain and rely upon appropriate data as to comparability prior to making its decision.
*   **For Compensation:** Data may include compensation levels paid by similarly situated organizations (both taxable and tax-exempt) for functionally comparable positions, current independent compensation surveys, or actual written offers from similar institutions.
*   **For Property:** Data must include independent appraisals or independent offers to purchase/sell similar property.

**4.3.3 Step 3: Adequate Documentation**
The Authorized Body must adequately document the basis for its determination concurrently with making that determination. The documentation (minutes) must include:
*   The terms of the transaction and the date it was approved.
*   The members of the Authorized Body who were present and those who voted on it.
*    The comparability data obtained and relied upon and how the data was obtained.
*   Any actions taken with respect to consideration of the transaction by anyone who is otherwise a member of the Authorized Body but who had a conflict of interest.

#### 4.4 Compensation Review
Compensation for the Executive Director (CEO) and other Operational Officers defined in Bylaws Article V must be reviewed annually.
*   The Board must explicitly determine that the total compensation package (salary, bonuses, benefits, deferred compensation) is reasonable based on the comparable data.
*   If the Organization cannot afford to pay market rates, the Board may approve compensation *below* market rate, but never *above* without risking an EBT.

#### 4.5 Loans to Directors and Officers
Pursuant to Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.055, the Organization is strictly prohibited from making loans to Directors. Loans to Officers who are not Directors must undergo the Rebuttable Presumption process to ensure the interest rate and terms are at Fair Market Value and do not constitute an Excess Benefit.

#### 4.6 Correction of Excess Benefit
If it is determined that an Excess Benefit Transaction has occurred:
1.  The transaction must be "corrected" immediately. Correction means undoing the excess benefit to the extent possible and taking any additional measures necessary to place the Organization in a financial position not worse than that in which it would be if the disqualified person were dealing under the highest fiduciary standards.
2.  The DQP must repay the Organization the excess amount plus interest.
3.  The Organization must file IRS Form 4720 to report the transaction and the correction.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Serves as the "Authorized Body" to approve transactions; identifies Disqualified Persons; ensures the Rebuttable Presumption is established. |
| **Finance Committee** | Gathers comparable data (salary surveys, appraisals) and presents recommendations to the Board. |
| **Secretary** | Ensures minutes reflect the specific elements required for "Adequate Documentation" (Step 3). |
| **Disqualified Person** | Discloses all material facts regarding the transaction; recuses themselves from the final vote; repays any excess benefit if a violation occurs. |
| **Treasurer** | Monitors compliance with this policy during the annual audit and Form 990 preparation. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in:
*   **IRS Excise Taxes:**
    *   **25% tax** on the Disqualified Person for the excess benefit amount.
    *   **200% tax** on the Disqualified Person if not corrected within the taxable period.
    *   **10% tax** (up to $20,000) on any Organization Manager (Director/Officer) who knowingly participated in the transaction.
*   **Organizational Discipline:** Termination of employment, removal from the Board (for cause), or removal from Office.
*   **Revocation:** Potential loss of 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status.
