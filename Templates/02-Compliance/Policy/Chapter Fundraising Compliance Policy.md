### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all fundraising activities conducted by Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") and its affiliated Local Chapters comply with federal tax laws, state charitable solicitation statutes, and the Organization’s ethical standards.

As a 501(c)(3) entity, the Organization must strictly adhere to regulations regarding Unrelated Business Income Tax (UBIT), substantiation of charitable contributions, and multi-state registration requirements. This policy mitigates the legal and reputational risks associated with unauthorized or non-compliant solicitation of funds.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Entities:** The National Organization and all chartered Local Chapters (whether unincorporated associations or subordinate organizations under a Group Exemption).
*   **Individuals:** Board Directors, Officers, Chapter Leaders, volunteers, and employees.
*   **Activities:** All forms of solicitation, including grant applications, corporate sponsorships, special events, online crowdfunding, and direct appeals.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to membership dues, which are governed separately by the *Membership Policy Manual*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Solicitation** | Any direct or indirect request for money, credit, property, financial assistance, or other things of value on the plea or representation that such money or property will be used for a charitable purpose. |
| **State Registration** | The legal requirement to register with a state agency (usually the Attorney General or Secretary of State) *before* soliciting residents of that state. |
| **Qualified Sponsorship** | A payment by a business where there is no arrangement or expectation that the business will receive any substantial return benefit (advertising) other than use or acknowledgment of the name/logo. |
| **UBIT** | **Unrelated Business Income Tax.** Tax on income from a trade or business, regularly carried on, that is not substantially related to the charitable purpose. |
| **Commercial Co-Venture** | An arrangement where a for-profit business advertises that the purchase of a product or service will benefit a charitable organization (e.g., "10% of burger sales go to Study GRC"). |
| **Charleston Principles** | Guidelines used by states to determine when online fundraising triggers a requirement to register in a state based on "interactive" or "targeted" solicitation. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Centralized Authority and State Registration
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, the Organization is operated exclusively for charitable purposes. To protect this status:
1.  **Registration Requirement:** No Local Chapter may initiate a fundraising campaign targeting residents of a specific state (outside of Texas) without verifying that the Organization is currently registered to solicit in that jurisdiction.
2.  **Master Registration List:** The CFO must maintain and publish a "Master State Solicitation Registration List" accessible to all Chapter Leaders.
3.  **Prohibition on Independent Filings:** Local Chapters are strictly prohibited from filing charitable solicitation registrations, incorporation documents, or tax exemptions in their own name without the express written consent of the Board of Directors.

#### 4.2 Allowable Fundraising Activities
Local Chapters are authorized to engage in the following activities, provided they adhere to the *Chapter Operating Manual*:
1.  **Events:** Ticketed educational workshops, networking mixers, or conferences where the fee covers costs and generates a modest surplus.
2.  **Qualified Sponsorships:** Seeking corporate support for events where the sponsor receives acknowledgment (logo placement) but **not** advertising (qualitative language, price comparisons, or calls to action).
3.  **Grants:** Applying for local community grants, provided the grant agreement is reviewed by the CFO prior to signature to ensure compliance with **2 CFR 200** (if federal funds are involved) or donor restrictions.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Fundraising Activities
To mitigate high-risk liabilities, the following are strictly prohibited at the Chapter level:
1.  **Raffles and Gaming:** Conducting raffles, lotteries, bingo, or poker nights is prohibited unless specific written authorization is granted by the Board. (Note: Texas Occupations Code Ch. 2002 strictly regulates "Charitable Raffles" and classifies unauthorized raffles as illegal gambling).
2.  **Professional Fundraisers:** Chapters may not hire outside consultants, telemarketers, or agencies to solicit funds on a commission or percentage basis.
3.  **Commercial Co-Ventures:** Chapters may not enter into agreements where a local business promises a percentage of sales to the Chapter without prior legal review by HQ, as this triggers complex contract and reporting requirements in many states.
4.  **Crowdfunding:** Chapters must use the Organization’s central, approved fundraising platform. Setting up independent GoFundMe or similar accounts linked to personal bank accounts is strictly forbidden and constitutes grounds for immediate removal.

#### 4.4 Gift Processing and Substantiation
To ensure compliance with IRS Publication 1771:
1.  **Receipting:** Local Chapters **may not** issue official tax-deductible receipts to donors. All donations must be processed through HQ or the approved central platform so that the Organization can issue a compliant receipt containing the required "No goods or services were provided" statement.
2.  **Check Handling:** Checks collected by Chapters must be made payable to "Study GRC Inc." (not the individual chapter leader) and deposited/remitted to HQ within five (5) business days.
3.  **In-Kind Donations:** Chapters accepting non-cash gifts (venue space, equipment) must use the *In-Kind Donation Acknowledgment Form* and forward it to the CFO for valuation and recording.

#### 4.5 Unrelated Business Income (UBIT) Avoidance
Chapters must avoid activities that generate UBIT, which can jeopardize the Organization's tax-exempt status.
1.  **Merchandise Sales:** Sales of merchandise (T-shirts, mugs) must be irregular or substantially related to the educational mission.
2.  **Advertising:** Selling advertising space (e.g., in a newsletter or on a website) is prohibited. Chapters must restrict corporate relationships to "Qualified Sponsorships" (acknowledgments only).

#### 4.6 Donor Intent and Restricted Funds
If a donor restricts a contribution specifically for the use of a Local Chapter:
1.  The Organization must honor that restriction pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII (Finance)**.
2.  Funds will be designated in the accounting system as "Temporarily Restricted" for that Chapter's use.
3.  The Organization reserves the right to levy a reasonable administrative fee (not to exceed [Percentage]%) on restricted gifts to cover overhead and compliance costs.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chapter Leader** | Ensures all local fundraising activities are pre-approved and utilize authorized platforms. Remits all physical checks to HQ immediately. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Maintains state charitable solicitation registrations. Issues all official tax receipts. Monitors UBIT exposure. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Reviews Chapter fundraising proposals for alignment with community culture and mission. |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the opening of new state registrations based on cost-benefit analysis. Authorizes any exceptions to the "No Gaming" rule. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the Organization at risk of state fines, loss of tax-exempt status, and criminal liability.

*   **Violations:** Any Chapter found soliciting in an unregistered state or conducting prohibited gaming activities will be subject to immediate suspension of their Charter.
*   **Personal Liability:** Individuals who collect funds outside of approved channels may be held personally liable for fraud or misappropriation and will be referred to law enforcement.
