### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to protect the brand identity, reputation, and intellectual property rights of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). While the Organization champions open-source knowledge sharing, our **identity** (represented by our name, logos, and trademarks) signifies a specific standard of quality, ethics, and community trust. This policy establishes the rules for the use of the Organization’s Marks to prevent confusion, dilution, or unauthorized endorsement, ensuring compliance with Article IX (Intellectual Property) of the Bylaws and applicable trademark laws (Lanham Act).

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Internal Stakeholders:** Board Directors, Officers, Employees, and Volunteers.
*   **Members:** Supporting Members, Honorary Members, and Community Participants.
*   **External Entities:** Local Chapters, Sponsors, Partners, Vendors, and the Media.
*   **Assets:** All names, logos, slogans, domain names, and visual design elements owned by Study GRC Inc.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not restrict "Fair Use" of the Marks for purposes of news reporting, commentary, or criticism, provided such use does not imply endorsement or affiliation where none exists.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **The Marks** | The name "Study GRC," "Study GRC Inc.," the Organization’s logo (symbol and logotype), and any other slogans or designs registered or claimed by the Organization. |
| **Official Assets** | The high-resolution image files (PNG, SVG, EPS) provided directly by the Organization’s Marketing or Operations department. |
| **Commercial Use** | The use of the Marks on merchandise (t-shirts, mugs, etc.) for sale, or in connection with a for-profit product or service. |
| **Dilution** | The weakening of the brand's ability to identify a single source, often caused by unauthorized modification or overuse of the Marks. |
| **Open Source Distinction** | The legal separation between the Organization's *content* (often licensed under Creative Commons) and its *identity* (Trademarks, which are reserved). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Ownership and Rights
Pursuant to **Article IX, Section 9.3** of the Bylaws, Study GRC Inc. retains sole and exclusive ownership of all Marks. No individual, member, chapter, or partner acquires any ownership rights to the Marks through their use. All goodwill generated by the use of the Marks inures to the benefit of the Organization.

#### 4.2 Permitted Use
The following entities are authorized to use the Marks without prior written consent, provided they adhere to the **Brand Identity and Style Guide**:
1.  **Official Local Chapters:** Chapters with a signed and active *Local Chapter Affiliation Agreement* may use the specific "Chapter Logo" provided to them (e.g., "Study GRC Austin").
2.  **Sponsors:** Active sponsors may use the Marks to indicate their support during the term of their sponsorship agreement, subject to the terms defined in said agreement.
3.  **Media/Press:** Accredited media outlets may use the Marks for editorial purposes in news coverage regarding the Organization.

#### 4.3 Visual Standards and Integrity
To maintain brand consistency, all authorized users must:
*   **Use Official Assets:** Only use the files provided in the Official Media Kit. Do not recreate, trace, or screenshot the logo.
*   **No Modification:** Do not stretch, distort, recolor, rotate, or apply effects (shadows, gradients) to the Marks.
*   **Clear Space:** Maintain the minimum clear space around the logo as defined in the *Brand Identity and Style Guide* to ensure legibility.
*   **Backgrounds:** Ensure sufficient contrast between the logo and the background.

#### 4.4 Prohibited Conduct
The following uses of the Marks are strictly prohibited:
1.  **Implied Endorsement:** Using the Marks in a way that suggests Study GRC Inc. endorses a third-party product, service, political candidate, or opinion without express written permission.
2.  **Confusing Similarity:** Using a variation of the Marks or a similar name/logo that is likely to cause confusion among the public (e.g., "Study GRC Pro" or "The GRC Study Group").
3.  **Commercial Merchandise:** Selling clothing, accessories, or other goods bearing the Marks without a specific licensing agreement.
4.  **Disparagement:** Using the Marks in connection with illegal activities, hate speech, pornography, or content that violates the Organization’s *Code of Ethics and Conduct*.
5.  **Combination Marks:** Combining the Study GRC logo with another company's logo to create a new, unified mark.

#### 4.5 Open Source vs. Trademark (The "Forking" Rule)
Study GRC Inc. provides educational resources under open-source licenses (e.g., CC-BY-SA, MIT). However, **Trademarks are not Open Source.**
*   **Content:** You are free to redistribute, modify, and fork the Organization's educational text and code repositories in accordance with the applicable license.
*   **Identity:** If you redistribute or fork a project to create a derivative work, **you must remove the Study GRC Marks** (Name and Logo) from the derivative work, unless the derivative work is an official project of the Organization. You may not use the Marks to label a modified version of the materials in a way that suggests it is the "official" version.

#### 4.6 Chapter Naming and Branding
Local Chapters must strictly adhere to the naming convention: **"Study GRC [City/Region]."**
*   Chapters may not create their own unique logos that do not utilize the official Chapter Logo template provided by the Organization.
*   Upon revocation or termination of a Chapter Charter, the group must immediately cease all use of the Marks and rename their group to remove any reference to "Study GRC."

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Trademark Policy and authorizes high-level licensing agreements or legal action regarding infringement. |
| **Director of Operations / Marketing Lead** | Maintains the *Brand Identity and Style Guide*, manages the distribution of Official Assets, and monitors for unauthorized use. |
| **Local Chapter Leads** | Ensure their chapter's usage of the Marks complies with this policy and the *Affiliation Agreement*. |
| **All Members & Volunteers** | Report potential trademark infringement or misuse to the Director of Operations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Study GRC Inc. actively monitors the use of its Marks.
*   **Non-Compliance:** Unauthorized or improper use will result in a request for correction. Failure to correct the usage may result in a formal Cease and Desist letter.
*   **Internal Consequences:** Violation of this policy by members or volunteers may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of membership or volunteer status (per Bylaws Article II and III).
*   **Legal Action:** The Organization reserves the right to pursue legal action under the Lanham Act and other applicable laws to protect its intellectual property rights against infringement, dilution, or cybersquatting.
