### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and protocols for all internal and external communications issued by or on behalf of Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). This policy is designed to protect the Organization’s reputation, ensure consistency in messaging, mitigate legal risks associated with public statements, and ensure compliance with IRS 501(c)(3) restrictions regarding political activity and lobbying.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all "Organization Representatives," defined as:
*   Members of the Board of Directors.
*   Operational Officers (including the Executive Director, DKG, CISO, CFO).
*   Employees, Contractors, and Interns.
*   Volunteers and Committee Members acting in an official capacity.

This policy governs all forms of communication, including but not limited to press releases, media interviews, social media posts on official channels, public speaking engagements, newsletters, and website content.

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Authorized Spokespersons
To ensure message consistency and accuracy, only designated individuals may speak on behalf of the Organization.
*   **4.1.1 Primary Spokespersons:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IV and V**, the **President of the Board** and the **Executive Director (CEO)** are the primary authorized spokespersons.
*   **4.1.2 Delegated Authority:** The Executive Director may delegate spokesperson authority to Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) or specific Operational Officers (e.g., the CISO regarding a technical security matter) for specific instances. This delegation must be documented in writing or email.
*   **4.1.3 Unauthorized Representation:** No other Board Director, employee, or volunteer may respond to media inquiries or make official statements on behalf of the Organization without prior approval.

#### 4.2 Media Relations and Inquiries
*   **4.2.1 Intake:** All inquiries from news media, journalists, bloggers, or industry analysts must be referred immediately to the Executive Director.
*   **4.2.2 "Off the Record":** Organization Representatives are prohibited from speaking "off the record" or "on background" with media unless explicitly authorized by the Executive Director and accompanied by legal counsel or a designated communications officer.
*   **4.2.3 Press Releases:** All press releases must be approved by the Executive Director prior to distribution. Press releases involving Fundamental Actions (as defined in **Bylaws Article X**), such as mergers or dissolution, require Board approval.

#### 4.3 Crisis Communication
*   **4.3.1 Notification:** In the event of a crisis (e.g., data breach, executive misconduct, sudden financial loss), any Organization Representative who becomes aware of the issue must immediately notify the Executive Director and the Board President.
*   **4.3.2 Chain of Command:** The Board of Directors retains ultimate authority over crisis messaging. The Executive Director will execute the *Crisis Communications Plan* under Board oversight.
*   **4.3.3 Embargo:** During a crisis, a strict communication embargo is automatically in effect. No personnel may discuss the matter internally or externally until authorized.

#### 4.4 Prohibited Communications
*   **4.4.1 Political Campaigning:** As a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt entity, the Organization is strictly prohibited from endorsing or opposing candidates for public office. Organization Representatives may not use official channels or resources to engage in political campaign activity.
*   **4.4.2 Confidentiality:** Disclosure of Confidential Information or Intellectual Property owned by the Organization (as defined in **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**) via public channels is strictly prohibited and constitutes a breach of the Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA).
*   **4.4.3 Defamation and Harassment:** Communications must never contain defamatory, libelous, discriminatory, or harassing content. This aligns with the *Anti-Harassment and Non-Discrimination Policy*.

#### 4.5 Brand Identity and Intellectual Property
*   **4.5.1 Consistency:** All public-facing materials must adhere to the *Brand Identity and Style Guide*.
*   **4.5.2 Logo Usage:** Third parties (partners, sponsors) may not use the Organization’s logo without a signed *Logo and Brand Asset Usage Agreement*.
*   **4.5.3 Open Source Attribution:** When communicating about the Organization's open-source resources, Representatives must accurately represent the licensing terms (e.g., Creative Commons) and attribute contributors appropriately.

#### 4.6 Youth Protection in Media
Given the Organization's K-12 focus and *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*:
*   **4.6.1 Identifiable Information:** No photographs, videos, or full names of minors (under 18) may be published on any channel without a signed *Parental Consent and Media Release Form*.
*   **4.6.2 Anonymity:** When discussing case studies or impact stories involving minors, pseudonyms must be used unless explicit written parental consent is obtained.

#### 4.7 Endorsements
The Organization does not endorse third-party products or services unless part of a Board-approved partnership or sponsorship agreement. Representatives must avoid statements that could be construed as an official endorsement of a vendor, software platform, or commercial entity.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this policy; oversees crisis communication strategy; authorizes statements regarding Fundamental Actions. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Serves as Chief Spokesperson; approves press releases; manages media relationships; enforces brand standards. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Monitors community sentiment; manages day-to-day social media tone; flags potential reputational risks to the ED. |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Adhere to brand guidelines; refer media inquiries to leadership; maintain confidentiality. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (pursuant to **Bylaws Section 3.6**), or termination of volunteer assignment.

Unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information or violation of IRS political activity restrictions may result in legal action and personal liability.
