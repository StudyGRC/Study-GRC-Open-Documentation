### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to minimize the risk of work-related musculoskeletal disorders (MSDs) and repetitive strain injuries (RSIs) among Study GRC Inc. personnel. As a remote-first organization, Study GRC Inc. acknowledges its obligation under the General Duty Clause of the Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA) to provide a workplace free from recognized hazards, including ergonomic hazards, regardless of whether the work is performed in a corporate office or a remote home office.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all **Employees** of Study GRC Inc. working remotely or in any physical facility operated by the Organization.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Independent Contractors:** Contractors are responsible for their own workspace setup and equipment, though they are encouraged to follow these guidelines for their own safety.
*   **Non-Work Activities:** Injuries arising from activities not directly related to assigned job duties (e.g., domestic tasks performed during work hours) are outside the scope of this policy.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Ergonomics** | The applied science of equipment design and workspace configuration intended to maximize productivity by reducing operator fatigue and discomfort. |
| **MSD (Musculoskeletal Disorder)** | Injuries or disorders of the muscles, nerves, tendons, joints, cartilage, and spinal discs (e.g., Carpal Tunnel Syndrome, Tendonitis). |
| **Neutral Body Posture** | A comfortable working posture in which joints are naturally aligned, reducing stress and strain on the muscles, tendons, and skeletal system. |
| **Remote Worksite** | The designated area within an employee's home or other approved location where official Study GRC Inc. business is conducted. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Remote Worksite Design Standards
Since Study GRC Inc. operates primarily as a remote-first entity, employees are responsible for designating a specific workspace that allows for compliance with ergonomic standards.
*   **4.1.1 Designated Space:** Employees must maintain a dedicated workspace that is distinct from high-traffic household areas to ensure safety and focus.
*   **4.1.2 Lighting:** Workspaces must have sufficient lighting (natural or artificial) to perform tasks without eye strain. Glare on monitors must be minimized.
*   **4.1.3 Environment:** The workspace must be free of tripping hazards (cords, loose rugs) and maintained at a temperature comfortable for typing and computer work.

#### 4.2 Neutral Body Posture Requirements
Employees must configure their workstations to support a Neutral Body Posture. The following standards apply:
*   **4.2.1 Monitor Placement:** The top of the monitor screen should be at or slightly below eye level. The monitor should be placed approximately an arm’s length away (20–40 inches).
*   **4.2.2 Keyboard and Mouse:** Input devices must be placed at elbow height. Wrists should remain straight and not bent up, down, or to the side while typing.
*   **4.2.3 Seating:** Chairs must provide lumbar (lower back) support. Feet must rest flat on the floor or on a footrest. Thighs should be parallel to the floor.
*   **4.2.4 Laptop Usage:** Prolonged use of a laptop directly on a desk or lap is prohibited. Laptops must be used with an external monitor, keyboard, and mouse, or placed on a riser to achieve proper eye level.

#### 4.3 Equipment Provision and Stipends
To facilitate compliance with Section 4.2:
*   **4.3.1 Organization-Provided Equipment:** Study GRC Inc. provides computing hardware (laptops) and essential software.
*   **4.3.2 Ergonomic Stipend:** Employees may utilize the remote work stipend (refer to *HR-POL-055 Remote Work Equipment Provision Policy*) to purchase ergonomic chairs, desks, monitor risers, or external peripherals.
*   **4.3.3 Ownership:** Equipment purchased with the stipend remains the property of Study GRC Inc. unless otherwise specified in the *Remote Work Agreement*.

#### 4.4 Work Habits and Micro-Breaks
Static posture is a primary cause of injury. Employees are required to integrate movement into their workday.
*   **4.4.1 The 20-20-20 Rule:** Every 20 minutes, employees must look at something 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds to reduce eye strain.
*   **4.4.2 Micro-Breaks:** Employees must take short breaks (1–2 minutes) every hour to stand, stretch, or change position.
*   **4.4.3 Task Rotation:** Where possible, employees should alternate between typing-intensive tasks and non-computer tasks (e.g., reading, phone calls) to rest specific muscle groups.

#### 4.5 Assessment and Reporting
*   **4.5.1 Self-Assessment:** All new hires must complete the *EHS-FRM-025 Remote Worksite Safety Self-Assessment* within 10 days of employment. Current employees must re-certify this assessment annually.
*   **4.5.2 Early Reporting:** Employees experiencing persistent discomfort (numbness, tingling, stiffness, or pain) must report it to their Manager or HR immediately. Early intervention is critical to preventing MSDs.
*   **4.5.3 Professional Assessment:** If an employee reports persistent pain, Study GRC Inc. reserves the right to require a virtual ergonomic assessment by a qualified third-party specialist before authorizing additional equipment purchases or medical claims.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Approves the budget for ergonomic stipends and ensures organizational commitment to safety. |
| **Director of Operations / HR** | Administers the program, reviews self-assessments, and coordinates third-party evaluations when necessary. |
| **Managers** | Monitor team work habits (e.g., discouraging excessive overtime without breaks) and encourage immediate reporting of discomfort. |
| **Employees** | Set up their remote workspace according to this policy, utilize provided stipends for appropriate equipment, and report hazards or injuries immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Furthermore, failure to maintain a safe remote work environment or to report injuries in a timely manner may jeopardize eligibility for Workers' Compensation benefits under Texas law.
