### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the rights and obligations of Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") and its employees regarding military leave. This policy ensures compliance with the **Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act of 1994 (USERRA)** and applicable Texas state laws. It establishes the Organization’s commitment to prohibiting discrimination against persons because of their service in the uniformed services and guarantees reemployment rights and benefits for those who serve.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all **Employees** (full-time, part-time, and temporary) of the Organization who perform service in the Uniformed Services.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Independent Contractors:** Individuals classified as 1099 Independent Contractors are generally not covered by USERRA reemployment rights, subject to the "economic reality" test defined by federal courts.
*   **Brief, Non-Recurrent Positions:** Employees in positions for which there is no reasonable expectation that employment will continue indefinitely or for a significant period.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Uniformed Services** | Includes the Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Air Force, Space Force, Coast Guard, Army National Guard, Air National Guard, Commissioned Corps of the Public Health Service, and any other category of persons designated by the President in time of war or national emergency. |
| **Service** | The performance of duty on a voluntary or involuntary basis in a uniformed service, including active duty, active duty for training, initial active duty for training, inactive duty training, full-time National Guard duty, and a period for which a person is absent from a position of employment for the purpose of an examination to determine the fitness of the person to perform any such duty. |
| **Escalator Principle** | The legal requirement that a returning service member must be reemployed in the position they would have attained with reasonable certainty if they had remained continuously employed during the period of service. |
| **Undue Hardship** | An action requiring significant difficulty or expense, considering the nature and cost of the action in light of the overall financial resources of the Organization. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Non-Discrimination and Anti-Retaliation
In accordance with federal law and the Organization's *Equal Employment Opportunity Policy*, Study GRC Inc. prohibits discrimination against any person on the basis of past, present, or future membership in the uniformed services. No individual shall be denied initial employment, reemployment, retention in employment, promotion, or any benefit of employment because of this status.

#### 4.2 Advance Notice of Leave
Unless military necessity prevents it, or it is otherwise impossible or unreasonable, employees must provide the Organization with advance notice of their military service.
*   Notice may be either written or verbal.
*   Employees are encouraged to provide a copy of their orders or training schedule to the Executive Director or HR representative as soon as available to assist with workforce planning.

#### 4.3 Compensation During Leave
USERRA does not require employers to pay employees while they are absent for military service. Consequently, military leave at Study GRC Inc. is **unpaid**, with the following exceptions:
*   **Use of Accrued Leave:** Employees may choose, but are not required, to use any accrued Paid Time Off (PTO) during their military service to receive compensation.
*   **Exempt Employees:** In accordance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), exempt employees who work any portion of a workweek and are absent for the remainder of the week due to military service will be paid their full salary for that week, minus any military pay received for that specific week.

#### 4.4 Health Insurance Continuation
*   **Service Less Than 31 Days:** Health insurance coverage will continue as if the employee had remained employed. The employee will be responsible only for their regular share of the premium.
*   **Service 31 Days or More:** The employee may elect to continue health coverage for themselves and their dependents for up to 24 months. The employee may be required to pay up to 102% of the full premium (employer share + employee share + 2% administrative fee).

#### 4.5 Reemployment Rights
Employees returning from military service are entitled to reemployment if:
1.  They gave advance notice of service (unless excused).
2.  The cumulative length of absence does not exceed five (5) years (subject to specific USERRA exceptions).
3.  They were not separated from the service with a disqualifying discharge (e.g., dishonorable).
4.  They report back to work or submit an application for reemployment within the statutory timeframes defined below.

**Reporting Timeframes:**
*   **Service < 31 days:** Must report to work by the beginning of the first regularly scheduled work period after the end of the calendar day of duty, plus 8 hours for safe transportation.
*   **Service 31 – 180 days:** Must submit an application for reemployment no later than 14 days after completing service.
*   **Service > 180 days:** Must submit an application for reemployment no later than 90 days after completing service.

#### 4.6 The "Escalator" Position and Seniority
Upon reinstatement, the employee will be placed in the position they would have held had they remained continuously employed (the "Escalator Position"). This includes:
*   Restoration of seniority.
*   Any pay raises or promotions that would have occurred with reasonable certainty.
*   If the employee is not qualified for the Escalator Position, the Organization will make reasonable efforts (training/education) to qualify the employee.

#### 4.7 Protection from Discharge
To protect the investment of reemployment, a reemployed service member may not be discharged without **cause**:
*   For one (1) year after the date of reemployment if the period of service was more than 180 days.
*   For 180 days after the date of reemployment if the period of service was between 31 and 180 days.

#### 4.8 Remote Work Assets and Security
Due to the Organization's remote-first nature and strict Information Security policies:
*   **Asset Return:** Employees deploying for periods exceeding 30 days must return all Organization-issued hardware (laptops, YubiKeys, etc.) to the IT Department prior to deployment, unless explicit written authorization is granted by the CISO.
*   **Access Suspension:** System access credentials will be temporarily suspended (not deleted) during the leave period to prevent unauthorized access or security risks in non-secure environments.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Provide advance notice of service (verbal or written). Decide whether to use accrued PTO. Return Organization assets prior to deployment. Submit timely application for reemployment upon return. |
| **Manager** | Ensure no retaliation or discouragement occurs regarding the employee's service. Coordinate workload redistribution during the absence. |
| **Executive Director / HR** | Determine eligibility for leave. Manage benefits continuation (health insurance). Calculate the "Escalator Position" regarding pay and seniority. Coordinate equipment return with IT. |
| **CISO** | Securely suspend user accounts during leave. Manage the intake and re-issuance of IT hardware. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy, including denying reemployment rights or retaliating against a service member, constitutes a violation of federal law and Organization policy.

*   **Internal Discipline:** Violations by management or staff may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Legal Liability:** The Organization acknowledges that violations of USERRA may result in court-ordered compliance, compensation for lost wages/benefits, and liquidated damages for willful violations.
