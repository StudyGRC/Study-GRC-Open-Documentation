### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the standards and expectations regarding meal periods and rest breaks for employees of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). While the State of Texas does not mandate specific breaks for adult employees in the private or non-profit sectors, the Organization recognizes that rest and nutrition are essential for employee health, safety, and productivity.

This policy ensures compliance with the **Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)** regarding compensable time and aligns with **2 CFR § 200.430** regarding accurate time reporting for federal grant compliance.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all **Non-Exempt** employees (hourly) of the Organization.

*   **Exempt Employees:** Salaried employees exempt from overtime are expected to manage their time effectively to allow for meals and rest, but are not required to track specific break times in the payroll system.
*   **Minors:** Specific provisions apply to employees under the age of 18 (see Section 4.5).
*   **Exclusions:** Independent Contractors (1099) are free to set their own schedules and break times and are not subject to this policy.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Rest Break** | A short period of rest (typically 15 minutes) during which the employee is paid and considered to be on "work time." |
| **Meal Period** | A longer period (typically 30 to 60 minutes) for the purpose of eating a meal, during which the employee is fully relieved of all duties. This time is unpaid. |
| **Non-Exempt** | Employees who are entitled to minimum wage and overtime pay under the FLSA. |
| **Bona Fide Meal Period** | A period where the employee is completely relieved from duty for the purpose of eating regular meals. The employee is not required to perform any duties, whether active or inactive, while eating. |
| **Work Time** | All time during which an employee is required to be on the Organization's premises (physical or virtual), on duty, or at a prescribed workplace. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Texas Law and Organizational Stance
The Organization acknowledges that under the **Texas Labor Code**, employers are not legally required to provide rest breaks or meal periods to adult employees. However, to promote a healthy remote-work culture and sustain high performance, the Organization elects to provide the breaks outlined below.

#### 4.2 Rest Breaks (Paid)
Non-Exempt employees are permitted and encouraged to take one **15-minute rest break** for every four (4) hours of work.
*   **Compensation:** Rest breaks are considered "hours worked" and are **paid**.
*   **Timekeeping:** Employees **must not** clock out for rest breaks.
*   **Scheduling:** Breaks should be scheduled to minimize disruption to operations (e.g., not during a scheduled team meeting or urgent incident response).
*   **Accumulation:** Rest breaks cannot be accumulated to leave work early or arrive late, nor can they be combined to extend a Meal Period.

#### 4.3 Meal Periods (Unpaid)
Non-Exempt employees working a shift of more than five (5) consecutive hours are provided a **Meal Period** of at least **30 minutes** (up to 60 minutes).
*   **Compensation:** Bona fide meal periods are **unpaid**.
*   **Timekeeping:** Employees **must clock out** at the start of the meal period and **clock in** upon returning.
*   **Relief of Duties:** To be unpaid, the employee must be **fully relieved of all duties**. Employees are prohibited from performing work (e.g., checking Slack, answering emails, attending calls) while clocked out for a meal.
*   **Working Through Lunch:** If an employee is required to work during their meal period, or is not fully relieved of duties, the time constitutes "hours worked" and must be paid. Employees must obtain manager approval before working through a scheduled meal period.

#### 4.4 Nursing Mothers (PUMP Act Compliance)
In accordance with the **FLSA** and the **Providing Urgent Maternal Protections for Nursing Mothers Act (PUMP Act)**, the Organization will provide:
1.  **Reasonable Break Time:** As frequently as needed by the nursing employee to express breast milk for up to one year after the child's birth.
2.  **Private Space:** A place, other than a bathroom, that is shielded from view and free from intrusion from coworkers and the public. For remote employees, this implies the employee manages their home environment, but the Organization will respect "Do Not Disturb" status for these breaks.
3.  **Compensation:** If the employee is completely relieved from duty during these breaks, they are unpaid (unless they run concurrent with a paid Rest Break). If the employee works while pumping (e.g., attending a virtual meeting), they must be paid.

#### 4.5 Minors (Youth Employment)
While Texas law does not mandate breaks for minors in non-hazardous occupations, the Organization enforces stricter standards for employees under age 18 to align with our **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**.
*   **Mandatory Break:** Employees under age 18 **must** take a 30-minute unpaid meal break if they work a shift of 5 consecutive hours or more.
*   **Enforcement:** Managers of minor employees are strictly responsible for enforcing this requirement.

#### 4.6 Remote Work Protocol
Given the Organization's remote-first nature, visual supervision of breaks is not possible. Therefore:
*   **Status Indicators:** Employees must update their status on communication platforms (e.g., Slack, Teams) to "Away" or "Lunch" during breaks to manage availability expectations.
*   **Integrity:** Employees are expected to adhere to the timekeeping policies honestly. Falsification of time records (e.g., clocking out for lunch but continuing to work to hide overtime) is a violation of the **Code of Ethics**.

#### 4.7 Unauthorized Work ("Off-the-Clock")
The Organization strictly prohibits "off-the-clock" work. Employees may not voluntarily work through an unpaid meal period without recording the time. If a manager directs an employee to work during a meal period, the employee must report this time as paid work.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Non-Exempt Employee** | Accurately record start/stop times for unpaid meal periods. Notify the manager immediately if a meal period is missed or interrupted by work duties. Set status to "Away" during breaks. |
| **Manager** | Ensure employees have the opportunity to take breaks. Monitor timecards for missed meal punches. Refrain from contacting employees with work requests during their unpaid meal periods. |
| **Payroll / HR** | Audit time records for compliance. Ensure nursing mothers are provided appropriate accommodations. Address patterns of missed breaks or unauthorized overtime. |
| **Executive Director** | Foster a culture that respects boundaries and encourages rest to prevent burnout. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Managers** who force employees to work off-the-clock or deny reasonable breaks may face disciplinary action.
*   **Employees** who falsify time records (e.g., claiming a break was taken when it was not, or working while clocked out) are subject to termination.
