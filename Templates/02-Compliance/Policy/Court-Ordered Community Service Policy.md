### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish clear guidelines and eligibility criteria for individuals seeking to fulfill court-mandated community service hours with Study GRC Inc. While the Organization is dedicated to fostering a community-driven ecosystem, we must balance this charitable intent with our fiduciary duty to protect our assets, data, and—most importantly—our community members, including minors. This policy mitigates liability risks associated with volunteer engagement and ensures compliance with the Organization's youth protection and information security standards.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals applying for or currently performing volunteer service mandated by a court of law, probation department, or diversion program ("Court-Ordered Volunteers").

**Exclusions:**
*   Standard volunteers serving without legal compulsion.
*   Board Directors and Officers (Court-ordered service is incompatible with the fiduciary requirements of Bylaws Article III).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Court-Ordered Volunteer (CSV)** | An individual seeking to volunteer with the Organization specifically to satisfy a legal requirement imposed by a court or diversion program. |
| **Disqualifying Offense** | A criminal offense that automatically renders an applicant ineligible for service due to the nature of the Organization’s work (e.g., crimes against children, fraud, cybercrimes). |
| **Direct Contact** | Any interaction (physical, verbal, or virtual) with community members, specifically including minors or vulnerable populations. |
| **Sensitive Position** | A role involving access to financial data, personally identifiable information (PII), IT administrative privileges, or unsupervised access to minors. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Eligibility
Study GRC Inc. accepts Court-Ordered Volunteers (CSVs) on a case-by-case basis. Acceptance is a privilege, not a right. The Organization reserves the right to deny any application or terminate any CSV arrangement at any time, with or without cause, if it is determined that the individual's presence poses a risk to the Organization’s mission, reputation, or community.

#### 4.2 Disqualifying Offenses
To ensure the safety of our K-12 focus areas and the integrity of our financial and technical resources, individuals convicted of, or currently charged with, the following offenses are **strictly ineligible** to volunteer:
*   **Violent Crimes:** Assault, battery, domestic violence, or weapons charges.
*   **Sexual Offenses:** Any crime requiring registration as a sex offender or involving sexual misconduct.
*   **Crimes Against Children:** Any offense involving minors, including neglect or endangerment.
*   **Financial Crimes:** Theft, fraud, embezzlement, forgery, or money laundering.
*   **Cybercrimes:** Hacking, identity theft, unauthorized access to computer systems, or online harassment.

#### 4.3 Application and Disclosure
*   **Mandatory Disclosure:** Applicants must disclose that their service is court-ordered *prior* to the start of any volunteer work. Failure to disclose this status immediately disqualifies the applicant.
*   **Documentation:** Applicants must provide official documentation from the court or probation officer detailing the nature of the offense, the number of hours required, and the deadline for completion.

#### 4.4 Screening and Background Checks
In accordance with **HR-POL-024 (Background Check Policy)**, all CSVs must undergo a comprehensive criminal background check at their own expense prior to onboarding.
*   The Organization will not accept a background check provided by the applicant; it must be sourced directly through the Organization’s approved vendor.
*   Adjudication of background checks for CSVs is conducted under a stricter standard than standard volunteers due to the known risk factor.

#### 4.5 Restrictions on Duties
Due to the remote-first nature of the Organization and its regulatory obligations (e.g., COPPA, 2 CFR 200), CSVs are subject to the following operational restrictions:
*   **No Access to Minors:** CSVs are strictly prohibited from roles involving direct contact with youth participants or access to youth data (referencing **Youth Protection Policy**).
*   **No Financial Access:** CSVs shall not handle cash, credit card data, or have access to financial systems (referencing **Internal Controls Framework**).
*   **No IT Admin Privileges:** CSVs shall not hold administrative privileges on any production system, code repository, or cloud environment.
*   **Remote Supervision:** All tasks must be verifiable. Work performed must be output-based (e.g., creating documentation, graphic design) where hours can be reasonably estimated and verified by the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).

#### 4.6 Verification of Hours
*   **Authority to Sign:** Only the Executive Director or the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) are authorized to sign court documents verifying hours.
*   **Accuracy:** The Organization will strictly report only hours actually worked. "Banking" hours or signing for future hours is prohibited and constitutes fraud.
*   **Family Members:** Family members of CSVs who serve as Officers or Directors may not supervise or verify hours for their relatives.

#### 4.7 Conduct and Termination
CSVs must adhere to the **Volunteer Code of Conduct** and **Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)**.
*   **Zero Tolerance:** Any act of aggression, harassment, or theft will result in immediate termination of the volunteer agreement and a report filed with the supervising probation officer or court.
*   **Confidentiality:** CSVs must sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) regarding internal organizational data.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Primary point of contact for CSVs; reviews applications; assigns non-sensitive tasks; verifies hours worked. |
| **Court-Ordered Volunteer (CSV)** | Provides truthful documentation; pays for background check; adheres to strict scope of work; reports hours accurately. |
| **Executive Director** | Final authority on acceptance of CSVs involving complex legal situations; authorized signatory for court documents. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Ensures CSVs are provisioned with "least privilege" access and are restricted from sensitive data sets. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in immediate termination of the volunteer relationship.
*   **For the Volunteer:** Falsification of hours or violation of restrictions will result in a report to the court/probation officer, potentially resulting in legal revocation of the service agreement.
*   **For Staff/Officers:** Falsifying court documents or verifying unworked hours is a violation of the **Code of Ethics** and may result in termination of employment or removal from the Board/Officer role for cause.
