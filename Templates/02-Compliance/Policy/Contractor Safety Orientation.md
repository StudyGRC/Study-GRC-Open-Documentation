### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish mandatory safety orientation requirements for all independent contractors, vendors, and service providers performing work for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). This policy ensures that third-party workers understand the Organization’s safety standards, emergency procedures, and youth protection protocols to mitigate liability, prevent injuries, and maintain a safe environment for members and the public.

This policy aligns with the Organization’s commitment to risk management as outlined in the **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3** (Advancing GRC Knowledge) and protects the Organization’s interests regarding indemnification under **Bylaws Article VII**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all **Contractors** and **Sub-contractors** engaged by Study GRC Inc.

*   **Physical Scope:** Applies to any physical location where the Organization is hosting an event, workshop, meeting, or maintaining an office (e.g., rented conference halls, off-site retreats, temporary workspaces).
*   **Digital Scope:** Applies to contractors with access to the Organization's digital infrastructure, requiring adherence to digital safety and security protocols.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to common carriers (e.g., FedEx, UPS) or delivery personnel who do not enter the premises beyond a reception area or loading dock.

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Mandatory Orientation Requirement
No Contractor may commence work on a physical Worksite or access sensitive digital systems until they have completed the **Contractor Safety Orientation**.
*   For **Physical Events** (e.g., AV setup, booth construction), the orientation must be conducted on-site prior to the start of the shift.
*   For **Remote/Digital Work** (e.g., IT consultants), the orientation must be completed virtually prior to account provisioning.

#### 4.2 Physical Safety Standards (Events & Venues)
Contractors operating at Study GRC Inc. events must adhere to the following:
1.  **Emergency Egress:** Contractors must not block fire exits, aisles, or emergency equipment with gear, cables, or boxes at any time.
2.  **Cable Management:** All cabling (power, data, AV) running across walkways must be taped down or covered with cable ramps to prevent tripping hazards.
3.  **Electrical Safety:** All electrical equipment brought by the Contractor must be in good repair and free of frayed wires. Daisy-chaining of power strips is strictly prohibited.
4.  **PPE:** Contractors are responsible for providing and wearing their own PPE appropriate for the hazards of their work (e.g., steel-toed boots for heavy lifting).

#### 4.3 Youth Protection and Conduct
Given the Organization’s K-12 educational focus, strict adherence to youth protection is mandatory:
1.  **Zero Tolerance:** Contractors are strictly prohibited from being alone with a minor (under 18) in a private, one-on-one setting (physical or digital).
2.  **Restricted Areas:** Contractors may not enter areas designated as "Youth Only" (e.g., changing rooms, specific workshop breakout rooms) unless accompanied by a background-checked Study GRC staff member.
3.  **Background Checks:** Contractors performing duties with high proximity to youth participants must undergo a background check in accordance with **HR-POL-024 (Background Check Policy)**.

#### 4.4 Digital Safety and Data Protection
Contractors accessing the Organization's digital environment must:
1.  **MFA Mandate:** Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all accounts used to access Organization data.
2.  **Device Security:** Confirm that devices used to access Organization data are encrypted and running up-to-date anti-malware software.
3.  **Data Handling:** Agree to the **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)** and **Intellectual Property Policy** as required by **Bylaws Article IX**.

#### 4.5 Insurance and Indemnification
Prior to commencing work, Contractors must provide a Certificate of Insurance (COI) naming **Study GRC Inc.** as an additional insured, meeting the minimum limits defined in the **Vendor Contract Security Requirements (IS-STD-060)**.
*   Contractors agree to hold the Organization harmless for any injuries sustained by the Contractor’s employees while on the Worksite, except in cases of gross negligence by the Organization.

#### 4.6 Incident Reporting
Contractors must immediately report any of the following to their Study GRC Host:
*   Any injury or illness occurring on the Worksite, regardless of severity.
*   Any "Near Miss" incident that could have resulted in injury or property damage.
*   Any potential safety hazard or security vulnerability observed.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures resources are available for safety oversight and enforces the policy for high-value contracts. |
| **Event Safety Coordinator** | Administers the physical safety orientation checklist at events; verifies COIs are on file. |
| **Study GRC Host** | Serves as the primary point of contact; ensures the Contractor receives the orientation; monitors Contractor compliance during work. |
| **Contractor** | Adheres to all safety rules; ensures their employees/sub-contractors are trained; reports incidents immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
**Stop Work Authority:** Any Study GRC Inc. Director, Officer, or designated Safety Coordinator has the authority to immediately stop any Contractor work that poses an imminent threat to life, safety, or the integrity of the Organization’s data.

Failure to comply with this policy constitutes a breach of contract and may result in:
1.  Immediate removal from the Worksite.
2.  Termination of the service agreement without penalty to the Organization.
3.  Permanent debarment from future bidding or contracting with Study GRC Inc.
