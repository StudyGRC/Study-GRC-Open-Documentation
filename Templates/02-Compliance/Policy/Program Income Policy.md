### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards for identifying, accounting for, and reporting **Program Income** generated by activities supported by federal awards. This policy ensures Study GRC Inc. complies with **2 CFR 200.307** (Uniform Guidance) and maintains accurate financial records as mandated by **Article VIII, Section 8.1** of the Organization’s Bylaws. Proper management of Program Income is essential to prevent audit findings, disallowance of costs, and to maximize the impact of grant funding.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all departments, employees, and contractors of Study GRC Inc. involved in the administration, management, or execution of federally funded projects.

*   **Inclusions:** Income generated from fees for services performed, the use or rental of real or personal property acquired under federal awards, the sale of commodities or items fabricated under a federal award, and license fees and royalties on patents and copyrights.
*   **Exclusions:**
    *   Rebates, credits, discounts, and interest earned on any of them (these are treated as applicable credits).
    *   Interest earned on advances of federal funds (governed by 2 CFR 200.305).
    *   Proceeds from the sale of real property, equipment, or supplies (governed by 2 CFR 200.311 and 200.313).
    *   Unrestricted donations or fundraising revenue not directly linked to a federal award.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Program Income** | Gross income earned by the non-Federal entity that is directly generated by a supported activity or earned as a result of the Federal award during the period of performance. |
| **Period of Performance** | The time during which the non-Federal entity may incur new obligations to carry out the work authorized under the Federal award. |
| **Deduction Method** | Program income is deducted from total allowable costs to determine the net allowable costs. Program income must be used for current costs unless the Federal awarding agency authorizes otherwise. |
| **Addition Method** | Program income is added to the Federal award by the Federal agency and the non-Federal entity. The program income must be used for the purposes and under the conditions of the Federal award. |
| **Cost Sharing/Matching Method** | Program income is used to meet the cost sharing or matching requirement of the Federal award. |
| **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)** | Title 2 of the Code of Federal Regulations, Part 200: Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Identification of Program Income
Study GRC Inc. is dedicated to providing zero-cost resources (Bylaws Section 1.3). However, in instances where fees are charged for grant-supported activities (e.g., live instructor-led workshops, conference registration fees), such revenue must be identified immediately as Program Income.

4.1.1 **Source Tracking:** All grant proposals must clearly indicate if Program Income is anticipated.
4.1.2 **Gross Income:** Program Income is recorded as gross income. Costs incidental to the generation of Program Income may be deducted from gross income to determine net Program Income, provided these costs have not been charged to the Federal award.

#### 4.2 Accounting and Segregation
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.1**, the Organization shall keep correct and complete books of account.

4.2.1 **General Ledger Codes:** The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) must establish unique General Ledger (GL) codes for Program Income associated with each specific federal award.
4.2.2 **No Commingling:** Program Income must not be commingled with general operating funds or other grant funds in a manner that prevents accurate tracking.
4.2.3 **Timing:** Program Income must be recorded when earned, consistent with the accrual basis of accounting.

#### 4.3 Use of Program Income
Unless the Federal awarding agency regulations or the terms and conditions of the specific Federal award provide otherwise, Study GRC Inc. applies the **Deduction Method** as the default standard.

4.3.1 **Deduction Method (Default):** Program income will be used to reduce the Federal agency's financial contribution and the non-Federal entity's contribution (if any). It does not increase the total budget of the project.
4.3.2 **Addition Method:** May only be used if explicitly authorized in the Notice of Award (NoA) or via prior written approval from the Federal agency. If approved, income is added to the funds committed to the project to further eligible project objectives.
4.3.3 **Cost Sharing/Matching Method:** May only be used if explicitly authorized in the NoA. If approved, income is used to finance the non-Federal share of the project.

#### 4.4 Order of Expenditure
To minimize the accumulation of federal cash balances, Program Income must be expended **before** requesting additional cash payments from the Federal government, in accordance with **2 CFR 200.305(b)(5)**.

#### 4.5 Income After the Period of Performance
Unless the Federal awarding agency regulations or the terms and conditions of the Federal award provide otherwise, Study GRC Inc. has no obligation to the Federal government regarding Program Income earned **after** the end of the period of performance.

#### 4.6 Intellectual Property and Royalties
In alignment with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, Study GRC Inc. generally releases work as open source. However, should royalties or license fees be generated from a copyright or patent developed under a Federal award:
4.6.1 **Exemption:** Royalties received from copyrights and patents are **not** considered Program Income unless the terms and conditions of the Federal award explicitly state otherwise (2 CFR 200.307(g)).
4.6.2 **Reporting:** Despite the exemption, all such revenue must be reported to the CFO for review against the specific grant agreement.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Program Manager / Principal Investigator** | Identifies potential sources of Program Income during the proposal stage. Ensures fees charged (if any) are consistent with the grant agreement. Notifies Finance immediately when Program Income is generated. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Establishes GL codes. Monitors Program Income accounts. Ensures Program Income is disbursed prior to drawing down federal funds. Prepares financial reports (SF-425) reflecting Program Income. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Reviews and signs grant agreements, acknowledging specific Program Income requirements (Deduction vs. Addition). |
| **Board of Directors** | Provides oversight of financial policies and reviews the Annual Financial Report (Bylaws 8.2) to ensure compliance with federal standards. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in the repayment of disallowed costs to the Federal government, loss of future funding eligibility, and disciplinary action against responsible employees, up to and including termination.

Violations of this policy that constitute fraud or gross mismanagement of federal funds must be reported immediately in accordance with the **Whistleblower Protection Policy**.
