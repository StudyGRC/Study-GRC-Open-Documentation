### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define Study GRC Inc.’s (the "Organization") absolute commitment to providing a safe, secure, and respectful environment for all employees, volunteers, members, and program participants. This policy establishes a **Zero Tolerance** standard for violence, threats of violence, harassment, and intimidation, whether such acts occur in physical spaces, at Organization-sponsored events, or within the Organization’s digital ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals associated with the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   **Personnel:** Employees, Independent Contractors, and Interns.
*   **Governance:** Members of the Board of Directors and Advisory Board.
*   **Volunteers:** Including Committee members and event staff.
*   **Community:** Voting Members and Community Participants.

**Applicability:**
This policy applies to conduct occurring:
1.  On any premises owned, leased, or temporarily controlled by the Organization (e.g., conference venues, off-site meeting locations).
2.  During the course of Organization business, regardless of location.
3.  Through the Organization’s digital communication channels (e.g., Slack, Discord, Email, Zoom).
4.  On personal social media or external channels if the conduct constitutes a credible threat to the safety of Study GRC personnel or operations (e.g., cyberstalking or doxing).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Workplace Violence** | Any act or threat of physical violence, harassment, intimidation, or other threatening disruptive behavior that occurs at the work site or via work channels. It ranges from verbal abuse to physical assaults and homicide. |
| **Digital Violence** | Acts of aggression utilizing technology, including but not limited to "doxing" (publishing private information), "swatting" (filing false police reports to induce a tactical response), cyberstalking, or transmitting threats of physical harm via electronic means. |
| **Credible Threat** | A statement or course of conduct that causes a reasonable person to fear for their safety or the safety of their immediate family. |
| **Weapon** | Any object used to threaten or inflict bodily harm, including but not limited to firearms, knives with blades exceeding 3 inches (unless used as a tool for a specific work task), explosives, or chemical agents. |
| **Zero Tolerance** | A strict enforcement policy where any violation results in immediate corrective action, up to and including termination and legal prosecution, without a requirement for prior warnings. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Zero Tolerance Standard
Study GRC Inc. maintains a Zero Tolerance standard for workplace violence. Any individual found to have committed an act of violence, made a credible threat, or engaged in egregious intimidation will be subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (pursuant to **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**), revocation of Membership (pursuant to the **Membership Policy Manual**), and referral to law enforcement.

#### 4.2 Prohibited Conduct
The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:
1.  **Physical Acts:** Hitting, shoving, kicking, throwing objects, or any physical contact intended to harm or intimidate.
2.  **Threats:** Verbal, written, or electronic statements expressing an intent to inflict harm, damage property, or destroy reputation through illegal means.
3.  **Stalking:** Persistent, unwanted surveillance or contact, whether physical or electronic (cyberstalking), that creates fear or distress.
4.  **Property Damage:** Intentional damage to the Organization's property or the personal property of employees/volunteers.
5.  **Digital Aggression:** Engaging in doxing, swatting, or encouraging third parties to harass or harm Study GRC personnel.
6.  **Intimidation:** Actions or gestures that a reasonable person would perceive as threatening, including blocking exits or aggressive posturing.

#### 4.3 Weapons Policy
*   **Physical Premises:** Possession of weapons is prohibited at all Study GRC Inc. offices and at specific events controlled by the Organization where notice is provided in accordance with state law (e.g., Texas Penal Code §30.06/§30.07 signage for private venues).
*   **Exceptions:** This prohibition does not apply to:
    *   Certified law enforcement officers acting within the scope of their employment.
    *   Security personnel specifically authorized by the Organization’s Executive Director or Board.

#### 4.4 Reporting Procedures
*   **Imminent Danger:** If a situation presents an immediate threat to life or safety, the individual must immediately contact local law enforcement (**911**) and then notify the Organization.
*   **Non-Imminent Threats:** All threats, suspicious activities, or instances of violence must be reported immediately to:
    *   The immediate supervisor;
    *   The **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)**; or
    *   The **Executive Director**.
*   **Whistleblower Channel:** Reports may also be made anonymously via the Organization’s designated Whistleblower intake form, pursuant to the **Whistleblower Protection Policy**.

#### 4.5 Protective and Restraining Orders
Any employee or volunteer who has obtained a protective or restraining order that lists the Organization’s premises (physical or digital) as a protected area must provide a copy of the order to the Human Resources department or the Executive Director. This ensures the Organization can assist in maintaining compliance with the order. All such information will be kept confidential to the extent permitted by law and safety requirements.

#### 4.6 Investigation and Enforcement
*   **Investigation:** The Organization will promptly and thoroughly investigate all reports of violence or threats. The **Director of Kindness and Generosity**, in consultation with Legal Counsel, will oversee investigations.
*   **Interim Measures:** During an active investigation, the accused individual may be placed on administrative leave, suspended from digital platforms, or barred from events to ensure the safety of the community.
*   **Removal of Officers/Directors:** If the accused is an Officer or Director, the Board shall follow the removal procedures outlined in **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6** (Removal of Directors) or **Article V, Section 5.3** (Member Petition for Removal of Officers), citing "Cause" due to violation of this policy.

#### 4.7 Non-Retaliation
Retaliation against any individual who reports a violation of this policy in good faith, or who participates in an investigation, is strictly prohibited and constitutes a separate violation subject to disciplinary action.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Personnel & Volunteers** | Refrain from violence; report any unsafe conditions or threats immediately; cooperate with investigations. |
| **Managers / Team Leads** | Take all reports of violence seriously; act immediately to secure safety; report incidents to Executive Leadership. |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Oversee the implementation of this policy; lead investigations; coordinate with the CISO regarding digital threats. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Assist in investigating digital threats (e.g., log analysis, preserving evidence of cyberstalking); secure digital perimeters. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Authorize emergency suspensions; make final determinations regarding termination of staff; report serious incidents to the Board. |
| **Board of Directors** | Review and approve this policy; handle allegations involving Executive Leadership or fellow Directors. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy constitutes a serious breach of the Organization's Code of Conduct.
*   **Employees:** Violations will result in disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination of employment.
*   **Volunteers/Members:** Violations will result in immediate expulsion from the community and revocation of membership rights.
*   **Legal Action:** The Organization reserves the right to pursue civil and criminal prosecution against any individual who threatens the safety of its people or property.
