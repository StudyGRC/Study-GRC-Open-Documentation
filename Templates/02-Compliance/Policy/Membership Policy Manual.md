### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Membership Policy Manual is to operationalize Article II of the Bylaws of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). It establishes the specific criteria for admission, the definition of "Good Standing," the rights and obligations of members, and the due process procedures regarding discipline and termination. This policy ensures transparency, equity, and compliance with the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) regarding nonprofit membership governance.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals seeking membership, current members of all classes, and the Board of Directors in their capacity as the governing body overseeing membership affairs.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not cover employment contracts or contractor agreements, which are governed by HR policies.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Good Standing** | The status of a member who has paid all required dues, is current on required volunteer hours (if applicable), and is not currently under disciplinary suspension. |
| **Voting Member** | Collectively refers to Supporting Members and Honorary Members who hold the right to vote on Organization matters. |
| **Community Participant** | A non-voting class of membership focused on access to educational resources and community forums. |
| **Dues Schedule** | A Board-approved standard listing current financial contribution requirements for membership. |
| **Record Date** | The specific date established by the Board to determine which members are eligible to receive notice of a meeting and to vote. |
| **Volunteer Service Pathway** | A method of achieving Supporting Membership status through contribution of time rather than financial dues. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Membership Classes and Qualifications
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.1**, the Organization recognizes three classes of membership.

**4.1.1 Community Participants (Non-Voting)**
*   **Qualification:** Open to any individual interested in GRC.
*   **Requirement:** Must register via the Organization’s official portal and agree to the *Privacy Policy* and *Community Code of Conduct*.
*   **Rights:** Access to public forums, newsletters, and open-source resources. No voting rights.

**4.1.2 Supporting Members (Voting)**
*   **Qualification:** Individuals demonstrating active commitment to the mission.
*   **Requirement:** Must maintain "Good Standing" through one of two pathways:
    1.  **Financial Contribution:** Payment of annual dues as defined in the current *Membership Dues Structure*.
    2.  **Volunteer Service:** Completion of a minimum of twenty (20) documented volunteer hours per calendar year, verified by the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).
*   **Rights:** Full voting rights (one vote per member), eligibility to serve on the Board, and access to member-exclusive events.

**4.1.3 Honorary Members (Voting)**
*   **Qualification:** Individuals recognized for lifetime achievement or extraordinary contribution to the GRC field or the Organization.
*   **Requirement:** Nomination by a Director and approval by a majority vote of the Board.
*   **Rights:** Full voting rights for the duration of the term designated by the Board (lifetime or fixed). Exempt from dues and volunteer hour requirements.

#### 4.2 Admission Procedures
**4.2.1 Application**
Prospective Supporting Members must submit an application via the Organization’s secure membership portal. The application must include:
*   Full legal name.
*   Primary email address (for official notice).
*   Affirmation of adherence to the *Code of Ethics*.

**4.2.2 Approval**
*   **Financial Pathway:** Membership is granted automatically upon successful processing of dues and acceptance of the Code of Ethics.
*   **Volunteer Pathway:** Membership is granted upon verification of completed service hours by the Volunteer Coordinator or DKG.
*   **Denial:** The Board reserves the right to deny membership to any applicant who has previously been expelled for cause or whose conduct is deemed contrary to the Organization’s charitable purpose.

#### 4.3 Dues and Financial Obligations
**4.3.1 Establishment of Dues**
Membership dues are set annually by the Board of Directors and published in the *Membership Dues Structure* standard.

**4.3.2 Payment Schedule**
Dues are payable annually on the anniversary of the member's admission.

**4.3.3 Non-Payment and Grace Period**
A member is considered "delinquent" if dues are not paid within thirty (30) days of the due date.
*   **Grace Period:** 30 days.
*   **Lapse:** If dues remain unpaid sixty (60) days past the due date, membership is automatically converted to "Community Participant" status, and voting rights are revoked.

#### 4.4 Good Standing
Only Voting Members in Good Standing may cast votes or stand for election.
*   **Verification:** The Secretary shall verify Good Standing as of the Record Date prior to any Annual or Special Meeting.
*   **Restoration:** A member may restore Good Standing by paying all arrears or completing required service hours, provided they are not under disciplinary suspension.

#### 4.5 Disciplinary Action and Termination
**4.5.1 Voluntary Resignation**
A member may resign at any time by submitting written notice to the Secretary. Resignation does not relieve the member of any outstanding financial obligations (e.g., pledged donations).

**4.5.2 Administrative Termination**
Membership terminates automatically for failure to pay dues (as per Section 4.3.3) or failure to renew the annual volunteer commitment.

**4.5.3 Termination for Cause**
The Board may suspend or expel a member for cause, including but not limited to:
*   Violation of the *Bylaws* or *Code of Ethics*.
*   Harassment, discrimination, or violation of the *Anti-Harassment Policy*.
*   Misappropriation of Organization assets or intellectual property.
*   Conduct detrimental to the reputation of the Organization.

**4.5.4 Due Process (Expulsion Procedures)**
Except for administrative termination (non-payment), a member facing expulsion is entitled to:
1.  **Notice:** Written notice of the proposed expulsion and the reasons for it, sent at least fifteen (15) days before the effective date.
2.  **Opportunity to be Heard:** The member may submit a written statement or request a hearing (virtual) with the Board (or a designated committee) not less than five (5) days before the effective date.
3.  **Final Decision:** The decision of the Board following the review is final and binding.

#### 4.6 Meetings and Voting Mechanics
**4.6.1 Virtual Participation**
Pursuant to the Organization's remote-first nature and **Bylaws Section 2.5**, all member meetings may be held via electronic communications systems.
*   **Requirement:** The system must allow members to hear the proceedings, participate in real-time, and cast votes securely.

**4.6.2 Identity Verification**
To ensure the integrity of the vote, members must authenticate into the meeting or voting platform using their registered credentials.

**4.6.3 Prohibition of Proxy Voting**
In strict compliance with **Bylaws Section 2.3** and **TBOC § 22.160(b)**, proxy voting is prohibited. Members must cast their own votes.

**4.6.4 Voting List Inspection**
In accordance with **Bylaws Section 2.6**, the voting list is available for inspection.
*   **Procedure:** A member must submit a *Member Record Inspection Request Form*.
*   **Restriction:** The list may not be used for commercial solicitation or advertising.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Sets annual dues; approves Honorary Members; serves as the final authority on disciplinary expulsions. |
| **Corporate Secretary** | Maintains the official Membership Roll; verifies Good Standing; issues meeting notices; manages the voting list inspection process. |
| **Treasurer** | Reconciles dues payments; notifies the Secretary of delinquent accounts. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Verifies volunteer hours for members utilizing the Volunteer Service Pathway. |
| **Voting Member** | Maintains contact information; pays dues or fulfills service hours; adheres to the Code of Ethics. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with the financial or behavioral standards defined in this policy will result in the loss of "Good Standing" status. Members not in Good Standing are ineligible to vote or hold office. Serious violations of the Code of Ethics may result in permanent expulsion from the Organization.
