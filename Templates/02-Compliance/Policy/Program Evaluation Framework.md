### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Program Evaluation Framework is to establish a systematic, data-driven approach to measuring the effectiveness, impact, and efficiency of Study GRC Inc.'s programs. As a 501(c)(3) organization, Study GRC Inc. is legally and ethically obligated to demonstrate that its assets are used exclusively to advance its charitable and educational mission. This policy ensures compliance with IRS "Mission Effectiveness" standards and federal grant reporting requirements (2 CFR 200.329), while fostering a culture of continuous improvement and transparency.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all external-facing programs, projects, and initiatives operated by Study GRC Inc., including but not limited to:
*   **Educational Initiatives:** Curriculum development, training sessions, and certification study groups.
*   **Community Ecosystem:** Online forums, mentorship programs, and events.
*   **Open Source Resources:** Development and distribution of GRC tools and documentation.

**Exclusions:** Internal administrative functions (e.g., HR, Finance, IT infrastructure) are monitored via separate operational KPIs and are not the primary subject of this programmatic impact framework.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Logic Model** | A visual representation of the relationship between program resources, activities, outputs, and outcomes. |
| **Inputs** | Resources dedicated to a program (e.g., funding, volunteer hours, technology). |
| **Outputs** | The direct, tangible products of program activities (e.g., number of students taught, lines of code released). |
| **Outcomes** | The changes or benefits resulting from the program (e.g., increased knowledge, job placement, behavior change). |
| **Impact** | The long-term, systemic change attributable to the organization's activities (e.g., reduced barrier to entry in the GRC industry). |
| **KPI** | Key Performance Indicator; a quantifiable measure used to evaluate success. |
| **Mission Drift** | The tendency for an organization to move away from its core mission as defined in the Bylaws, often to chase funding. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Mandate for Evaluation
Pursuant to **Article I, Section 1.3** of the Bylaws, the Organization is dedicated to specific charitable purposes. To verify adherence to these purposes, every major program must undergo regular evaluation. No program shall continue indefinitely without demonstrated evidence of alignment with the mission and tangible impact.

#### 4.2. The Logic Model Requirement
All programs proposed for funding or implementation must be accompanied by a Logic Model that defines:
1.  **Inputs:** What resources are required?
2.  **Activities:** What will we do?
3.  **Outputs:** What will we produce?
4.  **Short-term Outcomes:** What will participants learn/gain immediately?
5.  **Long-term Impact:** How does this advance the GRC domain?

#### 4.3. Data Collection and Privacy
Data collection methods (surveys, LMS analytics, web traffic analysis) must be rigorous and consistent.
*   **Youth Protection:** When evaluating programs involving minors (K-12), data collection must strictly adhere to the **COPPA Compliance Policy**. No personally identifiable information (PII) may be collected from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent.
*   **Anonymity:** Where possible, evaluation data should be anonymized to encourage honest feedback.
*   **GDPR/Privacy:** All data collection must comply with the Organization's **Master Data Privacy Policy**.

#### 4.4. Performance Measurement Standards (2 CFR 200.329)
To satisfy federal grant requirements and best practices, programs must track both **Outputs** and **Outcomes**:
*   **Output Metrics (Volume):** e.g., "500 downloads of the Open Source GRC Tool," "50 attendees at the workshop."
*   **Outcome Metrics (Quality/Change):** e.g., "80% of students passed the certification exam," "60% of mentees reported career advancement within 6 months."

#### 4.5. Evaluation Cycle and Reporting
1.  **Quarterly Review:** Operational Officers must review KPIs for their respective departments and report deviations to the Executive Director.
2.  **Annual Impact Report:** The Board of Directors shall receive a comprehensive "Mission Effectiveness Report" annually. This data will be synthesized into the public Annual Report to ensure transparency with donors and members.
3.  **Grant Reporting:** Specific reporting intervals required by grant agreements (e.g., quarterly or semi-annual) supersede internal schedules for those specific funded programs.

#### 4.6. Program Lifecycle Management
Evaluation findings must drive decision-making:
*   **Scale:** Programs exceeding outcome targets may be prioritized for increased resource allocation.
*   **Remediate:** Programs failing to meet targets will be placed on a "Performance Improvement Plan" (PIP) for a defined period (e.g., 6 months).
*   **Sunset:** Programs that fail to demonstrate mission alignment or impact after remediation must be discontinued to preserve resources.

#### 4.7. Stakeholder Feedback
Evaluation must include qualitative feedback from key stakeholders, including:
*   **Beneficiaries:** Students, learners, and community members.
*   **Volunteers:** Instructors and contributors.
*   **Partners:** Hiring managers and partner organizations.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews the Annual Impact Report; ensures programs remain aligned with Bylaws Article I; approves the addition or sunsetting of major programs. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Accountable for the overall implementation of the Framework; allocates resources based on evaluation findings; reports to the Board. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Defines learning outcomes for educational programs; oversees student assessment data and curriculum efficacy. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Collects qualitative community feedback; monitors community sentiment and engagement metrics. |
| **Program Managers** | Responsible for day-to-day data collection, maintaining Logic Models, and submitting quarterly KPI reports. |
| **Grants Manager** | Ensures all evaluation metrics align with specific donor/grantor reporting requirements (2 CFR 200). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Data Integrity:** Falsification of program data (e.g., inflating attendance numbers, fabricating survey results) is considered a severe ethical violation and grounds for immediate termination of employment or volunteer service.
*   **Funding Contingency:** Internal budget allocation for the subsequent fiscal year is contingent upon the submission of complete evaluation data for the current year.
*   **Grant Compliance:** Failure to track and report required metrics for federally funded programs may result in the loss of funding and legal liability for the Organization.
