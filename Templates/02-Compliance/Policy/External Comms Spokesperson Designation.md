### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish clear authority regarding who is authorized to speak on behalf of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") to external parties, including the media, government regulators, and the general public. This ensures that all information released is accurate, consistent, and aligned with the Organization’s mission, Bylaws, and 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status. This policy mitigates reputational risk and legal liability arising from unauthorized or inaccurate statements.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all **Organizational Personnel**, defined as members of the Board of Directors, Operational Officers, employees, independent contractors, and volunteers.

**Inclusions:**
*   Interviews with print, broadcast, and digital media outlets (including podcasts and blogs).
*   Official press releases and public statements.
*   Testimony or statements to government bodies (e.g., Texas Legislature, IRS).
*   Speaking engagements where the individual is explicitly representing the Organization.

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal social media activity (governed separately by the *Social Media and External Communications Policy*).
*   Casual professional networking where the individual is not making binding statements on behalf of the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Authorized Spokesperson** | An individual explicitly designated by this policy or by the Board of Directors to speak on record for the Organization. |
| **External Communication** | Any exchange of information, verbal or written, between the Organization and the public, media, or government entities. |
| **Crisis Communication** | Communication required during an emergency or event that poses a significant threat to the Organization’s reputation, operations, or stakeholders. |
| **Subject Matter Expert (SME)** | A staff member or volunteer with specialized knowledge (e.g., CISO, VP of Education) who may be authorized to speak on specific technical topics. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Primary Authority
Pursuant to **Article IV (Board Officers)** and **Article V (Operational Leadership)** of the Bylaws, the authority to speak on behalf of the Organization is vested in specific roles based on the nature of the inquiry.

#### 4.2 Designation of Spokespersons
Authority is tiered based on the subject matter:

*   **4.2.1 Governance, Strategy, and Crisis Matters:** The **Board President** is the primary spokesperson for matters regarding Board governance, executive leadership changes, fundamental actions (mergers/dissolution), and high-level crisis management.
*   **4.2.2 Operations, Mission, and Programs:** The **Executive Director (CEO)** is the primary spokesperson for day-to-day operations, educational programs, partnerships, and general organizational stances.
*   **4.2.3 Financial Matters:** The **Treasurer** or **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** (with Executive Director approval) are the only individuals authorized to speak regarding the Organization's financial health, audit results, or tax filings.

#### 4.3 Delegation of Authority
The Executive Director or Board President may designate other Operational Officers or Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) to speak on specific topics (e.g., authorizing the CISO to speak on a cybersecurity trend).
*   **4.3.1** This delegation must be specific to the event or topic.
*   **4.3.2** Delegated spokespersons must receive a briefing on key messaging prior to the engagement.

#### 4.4 Prohibited Conduct
*   **4.4.1 Unauthorized Representation:** No volunteer, general member, or unauthorized employee may identify themselves as a spokesperson for Study GRC Inc. or make statements that could be construed as the official position of the Organization.
*   **4.4.2 "Off the Record" Conversations:** Unauthorized personnel are prohibited from engaging in "off the record" or "background" conversations with journalists regarding Organizational business.
*   **4.4.3 Speculation:** Spokespersons must never speculate on unverified facts, legal matters, or future Board decisions.

#### 4.5 Crisis Communications
In the event of a crisis (e.g., data breach, legal investigation, sudden leadership change), the Organization adheres to a strict **"One Voice"** protocol.
*   **4.5.1** All external inquiries must be directed immediately to the Executive Director and Board President.
*   **4.5.2** Only the designated Crisis Spokesperson (defined in the *Crisis Communications Plan*) is permitted to release information.

#### 4.6 Government and Regulatory Inquiries
Any inquiry from a government agency (e.g., IRS, Texas Attorney General, Department of Education) must be referred immediately to the Executive Director and the Board President. No staff member or volunteer is authorized to respond to regulatory inquiries without legal counsel review, except for routine administrative filings handled by the CFO/Treasurer.

#### 4.7 Advisory Board Restrictions
Per **Article VI, Section 6.2** of the Bylaws, members of the Advisory Board provide non-binding advice and have no voting rights. Consequently, Advisory Board members are **strictly prohibited** from speaking on behalf of the Organization or representing its official position unless explicitly authorized in writing by the Board of Directors for a specific purpose.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board President** | Serves as the primary voice for governance and crisis issues; authorizes high-level statements. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Serves as the primary voice for operations and mission; manages media relationships; approves SME interviews. |
| **All Personnel** | Must politely decline to comment to media/regulators and refer the inquiry to the Executive Director. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Monitors community sentiment and flags potential PR issues to the Executive Director; does *not* speak on behalf of the org regarding policy/crisis. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—specifically, speaking to external media or regulators without authorization in a manner that misrepresents the Organization or causes harm—may result in disciplinary action.
*   **Employees/Officers:** Disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or removal from office.
*   **Volunteers/Members:** Revocation of membership and volunteer status.
*   **Legal Action:** The Organization reserves the right to pursue legal remedies for damages resulting from unauthorized disclosure of confidential information or defamation.
