### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") retains necessary records for an appropriate period to comply with legal, tax, and financial requirements, including IRS Revenue Procedure 98-25 and the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC). Furthermore, this policy establishes protocols for the secure and systematic destruction of records that are no longer required, minimizing data privacy risks and storage costs. This policy also mandates the immediate suspension of document destruction in the event of a lawsuit or government investigation (Legal Hold) to comply with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all physical and electronic documents, records, and data generated or received by the Organization.
*   **Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, contractors, and committee members.
*   **Applies to:** All locations, including remote home offices and third-party cloud service providers (e.g., Google Workspace, AWS, Slack).
*   **Exclusions:** Transitory documents (e.g., post-it notes, rough drafts not kept for record, duplicate copies) that do not serve as official evidence of business transactions.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Record** | Any recorded information, regardless of physical form or characteristics, made or received by the Organization that serves as evidence of its organization, functions, policies, decisions, procedures, operations, or other activities. |
| **Machine-Sensible Record** | Data in an electronic format that is intended to be processed by a computer (e.g., database files, spreadsheets), as defined by IRS Rev. Proc. 98-25. |
| **Legal Hold** | A directive to suspend the destruction of records due to pending or anticipated litigation, audits, or government investigations. |
| **Transitory Document** | Casual communications or drafts that have temporary value and are not required to be retained for legal or operational reasons. |
| **Metadata** | Data that describes other data (e.g., date created, author, file size), which must be retained to ensure the authenticity of electronic records. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Retention Principle
The Organization must maintain complete, accurate, and high-quality records. Records must be retained for the period specified in the **Records Retention Schedule (GOV-STD-013)**. Upon the expiration of the retention period, records must be destroyed unless a Legal Hold is in effect.

#### 4.2 Electronic Records and IRS Compliance
In accordance with **IRS Revenue Procedure 98-25**, the Organization acknowledges that machine-sensible records are considered "books and records" for tax purposes.
*   **4.2.1 Integrity and Accessibility:** Electronic records must be retained in a retrievable format that provides the ability to trace transactions back to the original source documents.
*   **4.2.2 System Documentation:** The Organization must retain documentation describing the electronic data processing system (e.g., schema, data dictionary, user manuals) for as long as the related data is retained, to ensure the data can be interpreted during an audit.
*   **4.2.3 Migration and Obsolescence:** If the Organization changes software systems or formats, legacy records must be migrated to the new system or converted to a standard, non-proprietary format (e.g., PDF/A, CSV) to ensure continued accessibility.

#### 4.3 Email and Communication Platforms
Email and instant messages (e.g., Slack, Discord) are delivery systems, not record categories. Retention is determined by the **content** of the message.
*   **4.3.1 Official Business:** Emails constituting official business records (e.g., contract approvals, policy decisions, financial authorizations) must be saved to the central document repository (e.g., Shared Drive) and retained according to the schedule.
*   **4.3.2 Transitory Communication:** Casual emails and chat messages not related to official business decisions should be deleted after their immediate utility has ended, or subject to automated deletion policies defined by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).

#### 4.4 Suspension of Destruction (Legal Hold)
In the event of a government investigation, audit, or served/threatened litigation, the Organization must immediately halt the destruction of relevant records.
*   **4.4.1 Initiation:** The President or Secretary constitutes the authority to issue a Legal Hold.
*   **4.4.2 Notification:** All affected personnel will be notified in writing. Failure to adhere to a Legal Hold constitutes obstruction of justice and is a criminal offense under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act.
*   **4.4.3 Duration:** The hold remains in effect until officially lifted by the issuing officer.

#### 4.5 Security and Privacy
Records containing Personally Identifiable Information (PII), financial data, or confidential intellectual property must be stored securely with access restricted to authorized personnel, pursuant to the **Master Information Security Policy (IS-POL-001)**.

#### 4.6 Destruction of Records
When the retention period expires, records must be destroyed in a manner that preserves confidentiality.
*   **4.6.1 Paper Records:** Must be shredded using cross-cut shredders or a bonded document destruction service.
*   **4.6.2 Electronic Records:** Must be permanently deleted. Storage media (hard drives, USBs) must be sanitized or physically destroyed in accordance with NIST SP 800-88 Guidelines before disposal or reuse.

#### 4.7 Bylaws Alignment
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**, the Organization shall keep correct and complete books and records of account and minutes of proceedings. This policy serves as the operational standard for satisfying Article VIII requirements.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this policy and ensures the Organization maintains adequate insurance regarding D&O liability related to record keeping. |
| **Secretary** | Acts as the primary Record Custodian for corporate governance documents (Minutes, Bylaws, Articles) and oversees the Legal Hold process. |
| **Treasurer** | Acts as the Record Custodian for all financial, tax, and audit records. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures operational staff and volunteers are trained on this policy and that records are centrally stored, not siloed on personal devices. |
| **CISO** | Manages the technical implementation of backups, archives, and secure data destruction (sanitization). |
| **All Personnel** | Responsible for classifying, filing, and destroying records in their possession according to the Retention Schedule. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Criminal Liability:** Intentional destruction of records to impede a federal investigation is a felony under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002, punishable by fines and imprisonment.
*   **Civil Liability:** Failure to produce records during discovery can result in legal sanctions against the Organization.
