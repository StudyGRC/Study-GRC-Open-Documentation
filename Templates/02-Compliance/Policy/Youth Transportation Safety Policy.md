### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict safety protocols regarding the transportation of minors during events, programs, or activities sponsored by Study GRC Inc. This policy is designed to mitigate the risks of motor vehicle accidents, minimize liability, and eliminate opportunities for abuse or misconduct by ensuring that all transportation occurs under supervision and in compliance with Texas state law.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Study GRC Inc. employees, Board Directors, volunteers, and contractors ("Personnel") who may be involved in the transportation of youth participants (individuals under the age of 18).

**Inclusions:**
*   Transportation in vehicles owned, leased, or rented by the Organization.
*   Transportation in personal vehicles driven by Personnel for Organization business.
*   Emergency transportation scenarios.

**Exclusions:**
*   Transportation arrangements made privately between parents/guardians (e.g., private carpools not organized by Study GRC Inc.).
*   Public transportation used by participants to arrive at or depart from an event independently.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Transportation Principle
It is the primary policy of Study GRC Inc. that parents or legal guardians are responsible for transporting their children to and from Organization events. Organization-provided transportation is an exception, not the rule, and must be explicitly authorized by the Executive Director or the Board of Directors.

#### 4.2 Driver Qualifications and Screening
No individual may transport a minor on behalf of the Organization unless they are designated as an **Authorized Driver**. To qualify, an individual must:
1.  Be at least 21 years of age.
2.  Possess a valid, non-expired driver’s license appropriate for the vehicle type.
3.  Pass a criminal background check in accordance with the *Background Check Policy*.
4.  Submit to a Motor Vehicle Record (MVR) check showing a clean driving history (no DUIs/DWIs, no reckless driving citations, and no more than two moving violations in the past 3 years).
5.  Provide proof of valid auto insurance meeting or exceeding Texas state minimums.

#### 4.3 The "Rule of Three" (Two-Deep Leadership in Vehicles)
To prevent abuse and protect Personnel from false allegations, **one-on-one transportation of a minor is strictly prohibited.**
*   **Minimum Occupancy:** A vehicle must contain at least two adults and one minor, OR one adult and two or more minors.
*   **Preferred Configuration:** Two unrelated adult Authorized Personnel should be present in the vehicle whenever possible.
*   **Exception:** One-on-one transportation is only permitted if the driver is the parent/legal guardian of the minor being transported.

#### 4.4 Vehicle Safety Standards
All vehicles used to transport minors must be in safe operating condition and comply with the Texas Transportation Code.
*   **Seat Belts:** The number of passengers must not exceed the number of functional seat belts. Every passenger must wear a seat belt at all times. "Double buckling" is prohibited.
*   **Inspections:** For personal vehicles, the driver certifies that the vehicle has a current Texas state inspection sticker and is mechanically sound.
*   **Prohibited Vehicles:** The use of motorcycles, open-bed trucks, or 15-passenger vans (unless professionally operated by a third-party charter) is prohibited for transporting minors.

#### 4.5 Driver Conduct
Authorized Drivers must adhere to the following safety rules while the vehicle is in motion:
*   **Obey Laws:** Drivers must obey all speed limits and traffic laws.
*   **Distraction-Free:** The use of mobile phones (including hands-free devices) for texting, calling, or navigation adjustment while driving is prohibited.
*   **Impairment:** Operating a vehicle under the influence of alcohol, illegal drugs, or prescription medication that impairs judgment is grounds for immediate termination and legal reporting.
*   **Fatigue:** Drivers must not operate a vehicle if they are fatigued. On trips exceeding 4 hours, a relief driver or scheduled rest breaks are required.

#### 4.6 Emergency Transportation
In the event of a medical emergency:
1.  **Priority:** Call 911. Professional emergency transport (ambulance) is the primary requirement.
2.  **Contingency:** If an ambulance is unavailable and transport is medically necessary, two adults must accompany the minor in the vehicle.
3.  **Notification:** The minor's parent/guardian must be notified immediately.

#### 4.7 Insurance and Liability
*   **Personal Vehicles:** If a volunteer or employee uses their personal vehicle, their personal auto insurance is the **primary** coverage for liability and physical damage. Study GRC Inc. does not provide comprehensive/collision coverage for personal vehicles.
*   **Indemnification:** Authorized Drivers acting in good faith and within the scope of this policy are eligible for indemnification under Article VII of the Bylaws. Drivers found to be acting with gross negligence (e.g., drunk driving) forfeit indemnification rights.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Approves the list of Authorized Drivers; authorizes specific events where transportation is provided. |
| **Event Manager** | Verifies driver credentials on-site; ensures vehicles are not overcrowded; enforces the Two-Deep rule prior to departure. |
| **Authorized Driver** | Operates vehicle safely; ensures all passengers are buckled; maintains valid license and insurance; reports any new citations immediately. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Maintains records of driver MVR checks and insurance proofs; coordinates with parents regarding pick-up/drop-off logistics. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy is a critical violation of the Organization's safety standards.
*   **Personnel:** Violations may result in immediate removal from driving duties, disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or volunteer service, and potential legal liability.
*   **Legal Reporting:** Any instance of driving under the influence with minors present will be immediately reported to law enforcement.
