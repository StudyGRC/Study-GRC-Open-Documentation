### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish strict guidelines for the solicitation, acceptance, and management of corporate sponsorships for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy ensures that all sponsorship arrangements advance the Organization's charitable mission, maintain the Organization’s independence and integrity, and comply with Internal Revenue Service (IRS) regulations regarding Unrelated Business Income Tax (UBIT), specifically the "Qualified Sponsorship Payment" safe harbor provisions under Internal Revenue Code (IRC) §513(i).

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and committees involved in soliciting or managing relationships with corporate entities, foundations, or other organizations providing financial or in-kind support in exchange for public acknowledgment.

**Inclusions:**
*   Event sponsorships (virtual or in-person).
*   Digital resource sponsorships (e.g., open-source tool funding).
*   Scholarship fund underwriting.

**Exclusions:**
*   Individual charitable donations with no expectation of public acknowledgment beyond standard donor listings (governed by the *Gift Acceptance Policy*).
*   Government grants and contracts (governed by the *Grant Management Procedure*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Qualified Sponsorship Payment (QSP)** | A payment made by a person engaged in a trade or business with no arrangement or expectation that the person will receive any substantial return benefit other than the use or acknowledgment of the name or logo (or product lines) of the person's trade or business in connection with the activities of the Organization. |
| **Advertising** | Any message or other programming material which is broadcast or otherwise transmitted, published, displayed, or distributed, and which promotes or markets any trade or business, or any service, facility, or product. Advertising includes messages containing qualitative or comparative language, price information, or other indications of savings or value, an endorsement, or an inducement to purchase, sell, or use any company, service, facility, or product. |
| **Substantial Return Benefit** | Any benefit other than (1) use or acknowledgment of the payor's name or logo, or (2) goods or services that have an insubstantial value (generally less than 2% of the payment) under IRS regulations. |
| **UBIT** | Unrelated Business Income Tax. Tax required to be paid by exempt organizations on income generated from trade or business activities that are not substantially related to the organization's exempt purpose. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Independence and Mission Alignment
In accordance with **Article I, Section 1.3** of the Bylaws, all sponsorships must align with the Organization's charitable purpose of fostering a GRC community and removing financial barriers to knowledge.
*   **No Influence:** Sponsors shall have no influence over the Organization’s governance, strategic planning, educational curriculum, open-source code repositories, or policy positions.
*   **Non-Endorsement:** Acceptance of sponsorship does not constitute an endorsement by the Organization of the sponsor’s products, services, or opinions.

#### 4.2 Compliance with IRS "Qualified Sponsorship" Rules (UBIT Avoidance)
To maintain the tax-exempt status of sponsorship revenue, the Organization must strictly adhere to IRC §513(i).

**4.2.1 Permissible Acknowledgments**
The Organization may provide the following to sponsors:
*   Display of the sponsor’s name, logo, and slogan (provided the slogan contains no qualitative or comparative descriptions).
*   A list of the sponsor’s locations, telephone numbers, or internet address.
*   Value-neutral descriptions of the sponsor’s product line or services.
*   Visual displays of the sponsor’s products (e.g., at a booth) without active sales.

**4.2.2 Prohibited "Advertising" Activities**
The Organization **must not** provide the following in exchange for sponsorship payments, as these constitute taxable advertising:
*   **Qualitative or Comparative Language:** Using terms such as "the best," "award-winning," "most reliable," or "cheaper than competitors."
*   **Price Information:** Mentioning specific prices, savings, or discounts (e.g., "10% off for members").
*   **Calls to Action:** Language inducing the audience to buy (e.g., "Call now," "Visit our store," "Try it today").
*   **Endorsements:** Statements by the Organization recommending the sponsor’s product.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Sponsors
To protect the Organization’s reputation and the safety of its K-12 and youth beneficiaries, the Organization will not accept sponsorship from entities whose primary business involves:
*   Adult entertainment or pornography.
*   Tobacco, vaping, or nicotine products.
*   Firearms or weapons manufacturing.
*   Gambling or betting services.
*   Entities currently under federal indictment or significant regulatory sanction for fraud, bribery, or human rights violations.

#### 4.4 Youth Protection and Data Privacy
In alignment with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* and COPPA regulations:
*   Sponsors are strictly prohibited from receiving Personal Identifiable Information (PII) of any beneficiary under the age of 18.
*   Sponsors may not directly market to youth participants within the Organization’s digital platforms or events.

#### 4.5 Right of Refusal
The Board of Directors and the Executive Director reserve the right to refuse or return any sponsorship payment that:
*   Is deemed incompatible with the Organization’s mission.
*   Would pose a conflict of interest as defined in **Article IX, Section 9.1** of the Bylaws.
*   Would impose an administrative burden disproportionate to the value of the sponsorship.
*   Would risk the Organization’s 501(c)(3) status.

#### 4.6 Written Agreements
All sponsorships valued at over $1,000 must be memorialized in a written *Sponsorship Agreement*. This agreement must:
*   Clearly state the amount of the contribution.
*   Detail the specific acknowledgments the Organization will provide.
*   Include a clause stating that the Organization retains final approval over all artwork and copy.
*   Include a clause confirming that the payment does not entitle the sponsor to any substantial return benefit.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this policy. Reviews and approves any sponsorship agreements valued over $25,000 or those involving potential reputational risk. |
| **Executive Director** | Implementation of this policy. Approves sponsorship agreements up to $25,000. Ensures all acknowledgments remain within the "Qualified Sponsorship" safe harbor. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Reviews sponsorship income annually to determine if any portion constitutes Unrelated Business Taxable Income (UBTI) and ensures proper filing of IRS Form 990-T if necessary. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | Manages sponsor relationships and ensures deliverables (logos, links) are executed according to the agreement and this policy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in:
*   **Financial Liability:** The Organization may be liable for federal and state corporate income taxes on revenue reclassified as "Advertising."
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees or volunteers who authorize non-compliant acknowledgments or accept prohibited sponsorships may face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Contract Termination:** The Organization reserves the right to terminate sponsorship agreements if a sponsor engages in conduct that violates the *Prohibited Sponsors* criteria (Section 4.3) or the *Code of Ethics*.
