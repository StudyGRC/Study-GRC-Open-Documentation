### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the governance, risk management, and compliance framework for the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Generative AI (GenAI) systems within Study GRC Inc. This policy aims to harness the benefits of AI for educational content creation and operational efficiency while mitigating risks related to data privacy, intellectual property loss, bias, and "hallucinations," in strict alignment with the **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers ("Users") of Study GRC Inc.

**In Scope:**
*   **Generative AI Tools:** Large Language Models (LLMs) (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini), image generators (e.g., Midjourney, DALL-E), and code assistants (e.g., GitHub Copilot).
*   **Embedded AI:** AI features integrated into approved software (e.g., Zoom AI Companion, Microsoft 365 Copilot, Canva Magic Write).
*   **Development:** Custom AI models or wrappers developed by the Organization.

**Exclusions:**
*   Deterministic algorithms (e.g., standard spreadsheet formulas) that do not utilize machine learning or neural networks.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Generative AI (GenAI)** | AI systems capable of generating new content (text, images, code) in response to prompts, rather than simply analyzing existing data. |
| **Public Model** | An AI service where user inputs (prompts) may be used by the vendor to train future models (e.g., the free version of ChatGPT). Data entered here is considered public. |
| **Private/Enterprise Model** | An AI service governed by a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) where inputs are contractually isolated and *not* used for model training (e.g., ChatGPT Enterprise, Azure OpenAI). |
| **Hallucination** | A phenomenon where an AI model generates incorrect, nonsensical, or unverifiable information but presents it as fact. |
| **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)** | The mandatory process of human review and verification of AI-generated outputs before publication or decision-making. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information (e.g., names, emails, addresses). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Governance and Tool Approval (NIST AI RMF: GOVERN)
To prevent "Shadow AI" and ensure supply chain security:
*   **4.1.1 Approved Tools Only:** Users must only use AI tools that have been vetted and approved by the CISO. A list of *Approved AI Tools* is maintained in the **Managed AI Tool Authorization List (IS-STD-041)**.
*   **4.1.2 Account Provisioning:** Users must register for approved tools using their official Study GRC Inc. email credentials. Use of personal accounts for organizational work is prohibited.
*   **4.1.3 Terms of Service Review:** The CISO must review the Terms of Service for all AI tools to determine if user data is used for model training.

#### 4.2 Data Privacy and Confidentiality (NIST AI RMF: MAP)
The classification of data determines which AI tools may be used.
*   **4.2.1 Public Models (Zero Trust):** Users are **strictly prohibited** from entering the following into Public Models (e.g., free ChatGPT, consumer Google Gemini):
    *   Personally Identifiable Information (PII) of members, donors, or staff.
    *   **Any data related to minors/youth participants.**
    *   Confidential financial data or unpublished strategic plans.
    *   Proprietary code or security configurations.
*   **4.2.2 Private Models:** Confidential data may only be processed by Enterprise/Private models where a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is in place confirming that **input data is not used for model training**.
*   **4.2.3 Anonymization:** Before using data in any AI tool for analysis, Users must sanitize the dataset to remove all direct identifiers.

#### 4.3 Intellectual Property and Ownership
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX (Intellectual Property)**:
*   **4.3.1 Work Made for Hire:** All prompts engineered and outputs generated by Users using AI tools during the course of their service are the sole property of Study GRC Inc.
*   **4.3.2 Open Source Commitment:** While the Organization owns the output, consistent with our mission, final educational resources generated via AI will be released under Open Source licenses as defined in the **Intellectual Property Policy**.
*   **4.3.3 Copyright Caution:** Users must not use AI to intentionally replicate copyrighted works of third parties. Users must acknowledge that raw AI output may not be copyrightable under current US law; therefore, significant human modification is required for proprietary claims.

#### 4.4 Accuracy and Human Oversight (NIST AI RMF: MEASURE)
*   **4.4.1 Human-in-the-Loop (HITL):** AI tools are support mechanisms, not replacements for professional judgment. Users must review, verify, and edit all AI-generated content for accuracy, bias, and tone before publication.
*   **4.4.2 Fact-Checking:** Users must independently verify all facts, citations, and legal references generated by AI. "Hallucinations" are common; publishing AI-invented facts is a violation of the **Code of Ethics**.
*   **4.4.3 Automated Decision Making:** AI systems shall **not** be used to make automated final decisions regarding:
    *   Hiring or employment termination.
    *   Scholarship or grant awards.
    *   Disciplinary actions against members or youth participants.

#### 4.5 Youth Protection and Safety
Given the Organization's focus on K-12 education:
*   **4.5.1 No Direct Interaction:** AI chatbots or agents shall not interact directly with youth participants (under 18) unless the tool is specifically COPPA-compliant and approved by the Board.
*   **4.5.2 Profiling Ban:** AI shall not be used to profile, categorize, or predict the behavior of individual youth participants based on their activity data.

#### 4.6 Transparency and Disclosure
*   **4.6.1 Content Labeling:** Educational resources that are substantially generated by AI (more than 50% of content) must include a disclosure statement (e.g., "This resource was drafted with the assistance of AI and reviewed by subject matter experts").
*   **4.6.2 External Communication:** Users must not represent AI-generated text as their own original thought in formal communications with the Board, donors, or regulatory bodies.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the AI Acceptable Use Policy and sets the risk appetite regarding AI adoption. |
| **CISO** | Vets AI vendors, maintains the *Approved AI Tool List*, and configures security settings (e.g., opting out of model training). |
| **Executive Director** | Ensures staff and volunteers are trained on this policy and enforces consequences for misuse. |
| **Users (Staff/Volunteers)** | Adhere to data classification rules, perform HITL verification, and report suspected AI "hallucinations" or bias. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy—specifically the entry of PII or Confidential Data into Public Models—constitutes a severe security violation.

*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Legal Action:** Unauthorized disclosure of sensitive data via AI prompting may result in civil liability.
