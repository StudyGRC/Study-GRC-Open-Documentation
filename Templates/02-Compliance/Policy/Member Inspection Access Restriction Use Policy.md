### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to balance the statutory right of Voting Members to inspect the books and records of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") with the Organization’s legal and ethical obligation to protect the privacy of its members and the security of its data. This policy explicitly defines "Proper Purpose" for inspection and strictly prohibits the use of member contact information for commercial solicitation, marketing, harassment, or any non-governance related activity.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all **Voting Members** (Supporting Members and Honorary Members) requesting access to the Organization’s membership lists, financial records, or meeting minutes.

**Exclusions:**
*   **Community Participants:** Non-voting participants do not have statutory inspection rights under this policy.
*   **Youth Data:** Under no circumstances will the personal information of minors (individuals under 18) participating in K-12 programs be made available for member inspection, regardless of the stated purpose.
*   **Personnel Records:** Employee and contractor personnel files are strictly confidential and excluded from member inspection.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Proper Purpose** | A purpose reasonably related to the requesting individual’s interest as a member of the Organization (e.g., communicating regarding an upcoming vote, verifying election results). |
| **Improper Purpose** | Any purpose not directly related to internal governance, including but not limited to commercial solicitation, fundraising for other entities, or data mining. |
| **Membership List** | An alphabetical list of Voting Members entitled to notice of a meeting, containing names, addresses (email or physical), and the number of votes each member is entitled to cast. |
| **Solicitation** | The act of asking for or trying to obtain something from someone, specifically including requests for money, business, or support for external causes. |
| **TBOC** | Texas Business Organizations Code. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Right to Inspect vs. Restriction on Use
In accordance with **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.6** and **TBOC § 22.351**, a Voting Member constitutes a qualified person to inspect books and records. However, this right is not absolute. The Organization reserves the right to deny inspection requests if the Board of Directors determines, in good faith, that the member lacks a Proper Purpose or intends to use the information for an Improper Purpose.

#### 4.2. Definition of Improper Use (Anti-Solicitation)
Data obtained through an inspection request **must not** be used for any of the following activities:
1.  **Commercial Solicitation:** Marketing goods, services, or business opportunities to members.
2.  **External Fundraising:** Soliciting donations for any organization, charity, or cause other than Study GRC Inc.
3.  **Political Campaigning:** Promoting candidates for public office (outside of internal Board elections).
4.  **List Sale or Rental:** Selling, renting, or transferring the membership list to any third party.
5.  **Harassment:** Using contact information to harass, threaten, or repeatedly contact members who have opted out of communications.

#### 4.3. Requirement of Written Affidavit
Prior to accessing any membership list or contact data, the requesting member must execute a **Member Inspection Use Affidavit**. This legal document must:
1.  State the specific **Proper Purpose** for the inspection.
2.  Certify that the information will be used *solely* for that stated purpose.
3.  Acknowledge that the member agrees not to use the list for commercial or charitable solicitation.
4.  Agree to destroy or return any copies of the data immediately after the stated purpose is fulfilled.

#### 4.4. Protection of Youth and Vulnerable Populations
Given the Organization's mission involving K-12 education:
1.  Requests for membership lists will be sanitized to remove any data related to non-voting Community Participants, specifically minors.
2.  If a Voting Member is a minor, their contact information will be redacted or replaced with the contact information of their parent/guardian, unless otherwise required by a court order.

#### 4.5. Method of Access and Data Loss Prevention
To minimize the risk of data leakage:
1.  **Preferred Method:** Inspection shall primarily be conducted via a secure, read-only screen-sharing session supervised by the Secretary or a designated agent.
2.  **Physical Copies:** If physical copies are legally required and provided, they will be watermarked with the requesting member's name and the date to trace any potential leaks.
3.  **Digital Downloads:** Direct download of membership databases (e.g., CSV/Excel files) is generally prohibited unless essential for the Proper Purpose and approved by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).

#### 4.6. Board Candidate Communications
During Board elections, candidates may wish to communicate with the electorate. To prevent the mass distribution of member emails while ensuring fair campaigning:
1.  The Organization may offer to send a communication on behalf of the candidate to the membership (a "pass-through" email).
2.  This ensures the candidate can convey their message without taking possession of the raw membership list.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Secretary** | Receives inspection requests, evaluates the "Proper Purpose," and maintains the repository of signed Affidavits. |
| **Board of Directors** | Final authority on denying requests deemed to have an Improper Purpose. |
| **CISO** | Approves the technical method of data transfer/inspection to ensure data security. |
| **Requesting Member** | Must sign the Use Affidavit, pay reasonable costs associated with the production of records (if applicable), and strictly adhere to usage limits. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews complex requests or denials to ensure compliance with TBOC § 22.351. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement

**6.1. Violation Consequences**
Any member found to have used inspected records for commercial purposes, solicitation, or harassment in violation of this policy and the signed Affidavit shall be subject to:
1.  **Immediate Termination of Access:** Revocation of any ongoing inspection rights.
2.  **Expulsion:** Removal from membership for "Cause" as defined in **Bylaws Article II**.
3.  **Legal Action:** The Organization reserves the right to seek injunctive relief and damages for breach of the Affidavit and violation of member privacy.

**6.2. Reporting**
Members who receive unauthorized solicitations following a records inspection are encouraged to report the incident immediately to the Secretary or the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG).
