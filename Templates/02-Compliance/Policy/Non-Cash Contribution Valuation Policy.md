### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards by which Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") accepts, acknowledges, and records non-cash (in-kind) contributions. This policy ensures compliance with **IRS Publication 561** (Determining the Value of Donated Property) and **IRS Form 8283** requirements, while maintaining accurate financial records in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP).

Crucially, this policy delineates the separation of duties between the Organization (which records gifts) and the Donor (who is solely responsible for valuing gifts for tax deduction purposes).

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all non-cash contributions received by the Organization, including but not limited to:
*   Tangible personal property (e.g., hardware, equipment, vehicles).
*   Intangible personal property (e.g., patents, copyrights, software licenses).
*   Securities (stocks, bonds).
*   Real estate.
*   Cryptocurrency (subject to additional controls in **FIN-POL-058**).

**Exclusions:**
*   Cash or cash-equivalent contributions (covered under **FIN-POL-045**).
*   Volunteer services (covered under **HR-POL-075**), as the IRS generally prohibits tax deductions for the value of time or services.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fair Market Value (FMV)** | The price that property would sell for on the open market between a willing buyer and a willing seller, with neither being required to act, and both having reasonable knowledge of the relevant facts (IRS Pub 561). |
| **In-Kind Contribution** | A donation of goods, services, or property rather than cash. |
| **Contemporaneous Written Acknowledgment (CWA)** | A written receipt provided by the Organization to the donor, describing the gift and stating whether any goods or services were provided in exchange. |
| **Form 8283** | IRS form filed by donors to report information about non-cash charitable contributions. |
| **Qualified Appraisal** | An appraisal document created by a qualified appraiser in accordance with generally accepted appraisal standards, required by the IRS for deductions over $5,000. |
| **Put into Service** | The date the asset is available for use by the Organization for its exempt purpose. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Acceptance Principle
In accordance with **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, the Organization accepts non-cash contributions that advance its charitable and educational mission to foster GRC knowledge. The Organization reserves the right to decline any gift that:
1.  Does not align with the mission.
2.  Carries excessive maintenance costs or liability risks.
3.  Is difficult to liquidate or utilize.

#### 4.2 Prohibition on Appraising for Donors
To avoid conflicts of interest and potential liability for aiding in tax fraud:
*   **No Valuation for Tax Purposes:** The Organization, its Board Directors, Officers, and employees **must not** provide a dollar valuation to the donor for the purpose of the donor's tax deduction.
*   **Receipt Description:** Donor acknowledgments for non-cash gifts must describe the property in detail (e.g., "One (1) MacBook Pro, Model A2338, Serial #XYZ") but **must not** assign a monetary value to the item.
*   **Exception:** The Organization may state the value of publicly traded securities based on the mean market price on the date of transfer, as this data is publicly verifiable.

#### 4.3 Internal Valuation for Financial Reporting (GAAP)
While the Organization does not value items for the *donor*, it must assign a value for *internal financial reporting* purposes.
*   **GAAP Recording:** The CFO must record in-kind contributions as revenue and assets (or expenses) at their estimated Fair Market Value (FMV) on the date of the gift.
*   **Methodology:** Internal valuation shall be based on:
    1.  Comparable sales of similar items.
    2.  Cost of replacement.
    3.  Independent appraisal (if available).
*   **Separation:** This internal GAAP value is strictly for the Organization's books (e.g., Form 990 reporting) and is **not** to be communicated to the donor as an official tax valuation.

#### 4.4 Substantiation Thresholds and Requirements
The Organization shall adhere to the following IRS substantiation requirements based on the claimed value of the gift:

| Donor's Claimed Value | Organization's Obligation | Donor's Obligation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **< $250** | Provide simple receipt/email describing the item. | Keep receipt. |
| **$250 – $500** | Provide CWA stating "No goods or services were provided in exchange." | Keep CWA. |
| **$501 – $5,000** | Provide CWA. | File Form 8283 (Section A). |
| **> $5,000** | Sign Form 8283 (Section B, Part IV). | Obtain Qualified Appraisal; File Form 8283. |

#### 4.5 Signing IRS Form 8283
When a donor presents IRS Form 8283 for a gift exceeding $5,000:
1.  **Authorized Signer:** Only the **CFO** or **Treasurer** is authorized to sign Form 8283 on behalf of the Organization.
2.  **Acknowledgment Only:** By signing Form 8283, the Organization acknowledges **receipt** of the property on the date specified. The signature **does not** represent agreement with the donor's claimed value.
3.  **Physical Inspection:** The Authorized Signer must verify the physical existence and condition of the item before signing.

#### 4.6 Disposition of Donated Property (Form 8282)
If the Organization sells, exchanges, or otherwise disposes of "charitable deduction property" (property for which the Organization signed Form 8283) within **three (3) years** of the date of receipt:
1.  The Organization must file **IRS Form 8282** (Donee Information Return) within 125 days of the disposition.
2.  The Organization must provide a copy of Form 8282 to the original donor.
3.  This requirement applies to any item valued at more than $500 at the time of donation.

#### 4.7 Contributed Services
Per IRS regulations, the Organization cannot provide a tax-deductible receipt for the value of a volunteer's time or services.
*   **Reimbursement:** The Organization may reimburse out-of-pocket expenses incurred by volunteers (e.g., supplies) if receipts are provided, per **FIN-POL-040**.
*   **Professional Services (GAAP):** For financial reporting only, the Organization will record the value of specialized services (e.g., legal, accounting) if they would otherwise have been purchased, but no tax receipt for this value will be issued to the provider.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Donor** | Solely responsible for determining the Fair Market Value (FMV) of the contribution for tax purposes and obtaining a Qualified Appraisal if required. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Responsible for internal GAAP valuation, signing IRS Form 8283 (Donee Acknowledgment), and filing IRS Form 8282 if assets are sold within 3 years. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Responsible for issuing the Contemporaneous Written Acknowledgment (CWA) describing the gift without assigning monetary value. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides Board-level oversight of non-cash asset acceptance and reviews Form 8283 signings during quarterly audits. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in inaccurate financial reporting, loss of tax-exempt status, or penalties for aiding and abetting tax understatement.

*   **Internal Discipline:** Employees who assign tax values to non-cash gifts in violation of Section 4.2 are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Legal Penalties:** Under IRC § 6701, penalties may apply to any person who aids in the preparation of a tax document (such as a receipt) knowing it will result in an understatement of tax liability (e.g., knowingly validating an inflated appraisal).
