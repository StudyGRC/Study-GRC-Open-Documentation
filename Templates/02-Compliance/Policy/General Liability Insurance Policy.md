### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to mandate the maintenance of Commercial General Liability (CGL) insurance to protect Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") against financial losses resulting from claims of bodily injury, property damage, and personal and advertising injury.

This policy serves as a critical component of the Organization’s **Risk Transfer** strategy, ensuring that the financial burden of operational liabilities is shifted to an insurance carrier rather than depleting the Organization’s charitable assets. It further supports the indemnification provisions outlined in **Article VII** of the Bylaws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all operations, events, and activities conducted by the Organization, whether virtual or physical.

*   **In Scope:**
    *   Bodily Injury and Property Damage liability.
    *   Personal and Advertising Injury (e.g., libel, slander, copyright infringement in advertisements).
    *   Medical Payments for injured parties.
    *   Liability arising from the actions of Directors, Officers, Employees, and Volunteers while acting within the scope of their duties.
    *   Tenants' Legal Liability (for rented premises, including temporary event venues).
*   **Exclusions:** This policy does not govern Directors & Officers (D&O) Liability, Workers' Compensation, or Cyber Liability, which are addressed in separate policies (see Section 7).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Commercial General Liability (CGL)** | A standard insurance policy issued to business organizations to protect them against liability claims for bodily injury and property damage arising out of premises, operations, products, and completed operations; and advertising and personal injury liability. |
| **Occurrence Basis** | A policy form that covers claims resulting from an injury or damage that occurs during the policy period, regardless of when the claim is actually filed. (Preferred over "Claims-Made"). |
| **Aggregate Limit** | The maximum amount the insurer will pay for all covered losses during the policy period. |
| **Per Occurrence Limit** | The maximum amount the insurer will pay for a single incident or claim. |
| **Additional Insured** | A person or entity other than the named insured who is protected under the terms of the policy (often required by venues or partners). |
| **Subrogation** | The right for an insurer to pursue a third party that caused an insurance loss to the insured. |
| **Abuse & Molestation Coverage** | Specific coverage (or buy-back of an exclusion) protecting the Organization against claims alleging sexual misconduct or abuse. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Minimum Coverage Standards
To adequately protect the Organization’s assets, the Board of Directors mandates the following minimum limits for the CGL policy. These limits must be reviewed annually by the CFO and the Insurance Broker.

*   **General Aggregate:** $2,000,000 minimum.
*   **Products/Completed Operations Aggregate:** $2,000,000 minimum.
*   **Personal & Advertising Injury:** $1,000,000 minimum.
*   **Each Occurrence:** $1,000,000 minimum.
*   **Damage to Rented Premises:** $300,000 minimum.
*   **Medical Expense (Any one person):** $5,000 minimum.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Endorsements and Provisions
Given the Organization's focus on K-12 education and remote operations, the following provisions are mandatory:

1.  **Abuse and Molestation Coverage:** Due to the Organization's work with youth, the CGL policy **must not** contain a total exclusion for abuse and molestation. If the standard CGL excludes this, a separate endorsement or standalone policy with limits of no less than $1,000,000 per occurrence must be purchased.
2.  **Hired and Non-Owned Auto Liability:** Coverage must be included to protect the Organization when employees or volunteers use personal vehicles or rented vehicles for Organization business.
3.  **Waiver of Subrogation:** The policy shall include a Waiver of Subrogation in favor of the Organization where required by written contract.
4.  **Defense Costs:** Defense costs must be *outside* the limits of liability (i.e., legal fees do not erode the coverage amount available for settlements/judgments).

#### 4.3 Carrier Requirements
Insurance shall be procured only from carriers with an **A.M. Best rating of "A-" (Excellent)** or better and a Financial Size Category of VII or larger, licensed to do business in the State of Texas.

#### 4.4 Risk Transfer (Vendors and Contractors)
To minimize the Organization's exposure, all third-party vendors, contractors, and service providers performing physical work or high-risk activities (as defined in the *Procurement Policy*) must:
1.  Carry their own CGL insurance with limits of at least $1,000,000 per occurrence.
2.  Name "Study GRC Inc." as an **Additional Insured** on their policy.
3.  Provide a Certificate of Insurance (COI) evidencing such coverage prior to the commencement of services.

#### 4.5 Claims Reporting
Timely reporting is a condition of coverage.
1.  Any Director, Officer, Employee, or Volunteer who becomes aware of an incident involving bodily injury, property damage, or a potential legal claim must report it to the **Executive Director** and **CFO** immediately (within 24 hours).
2.  The CFO is responsible for formally notifying the insurance carrier or broker of the potential claim.
3.  No individual is authorized to admit liability, make voluntary payments, or assume any obligation on behalf of the Organization without the written consent of the insurance carrier.

#### 4.6 Annual Review and Renewal
The CFO shall conduct an annual review of the CGL policy 60 days prior to expiration. This review must consider:
*   Changes in the Organization's size, budget, or scope of activities.
*   New geographic locations or expansion of physical events.
*   Inflationary impacts on coverage limits.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the annual insurance budget and policy limits; ensures alignment with Bylaws Article VII. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ultimate accountability for risk management; signatory on insurance applications. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Acts as the primary liaison with the insurance broker; manages renewals, premium payments, and claims reporting; maintains the COI repository. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Reviews carrier policy forms for exclusions; reviews vendor contracts to ensure adequate downstream risk transfer. |
| **Event Managers** | Ensures all venues receive required proof of insurance and that all vendors provide COIs naming the Organization as Additional Insured. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain the insurance coverage mandated by this policy constitutes a breach of fiduciary duty by the responsible officers.

*   **Lapses in Coverage:** Any lapse in CGL coverage must be reported to the Board of Directors immediately. Operations involving physical interaction or third-party property usage must cease until coverage is reinstated.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Employees or Officers who fail to report incidents timely, or who enter into contracts without verifying vendor insurance (thereby exposing the Organization to uninsured liability), may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
