### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the standards and procedures for the provision, management, and return of hardware, software, and peripheral equipment necessary for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") employees to perform their duties remotely. This policy ensures that the Organization meets its obligations to provide necessary tools for employment, maintains strict inventory control over assets (including those purchased with Federal Grant funds per 2 CFR 200.313), and secures organizational data through standardized, managed devices.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   All full-time and part-time W-2 employees of Study GRC Inc.
*   Specific Independent Contractors only where explicitly stated in their Service Agreement and approved by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).

**Exclusions:**
*   Volunteers (unless specific hardware is authorized by the Board for a designated role).
*   General Independent Contractors (who are expected to provide their own tools as a condition of their independent status).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Organization-Provided Equipment (OPE)** | Any hardware, software, or peripheral purchased by Study GRC Inc. and assigned to a worker. Title remains with the Organization. |
| **Standard Issue Kit** | The baseline set of equipment provided to a specific role (e.g., Laptop, Charger, Security Key). |
| **BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)** | Personal devices owned by the employee used for work purposes, subject to the *Bring Your Own Device Policy*. |
| **MDM (Mobile Device Management)** | Software used by the IT/Security team to secure, monitor, and manage OPE remotely. |
| **2 CFR 200.313** | Federal regulation governing the management, use, and disposal of equipment purchased with federal award funds. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Standard Issue Equipment
To ensure security and compatibility, the Organization provides a Standard Issue Kit to all eligible employees upon hiring.
*   **Primary Computing Device:** One (1) laptop computer configured to the Organization’s security standards.
*   **Security Hardware:** One (1) FIDO2-compliant security key (e.g., YubiKey) for Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
*   **Peripherals:** The Organization may provide or reimburse for one (1) monitor, one (1) keyboard, and one (1) mouse, subject to the *Remote Work Expense Reimbursement Policy*.

#### 4.2 Ownership and Asset Tagging
All OPE remains the sole property of Study GRC Inc.
*   **No Personal Ownership:** Employees have no ownership rights or expectation of privacy regarding data stored on OPE.
*   **Asset Tags:** All OPE valued over $500 or containing data storage capabilities must be tagged with a unique asset ID. Removing or defacing this tag is prohibited.
*   **Federal Interest:** Equipment purchased with federal grant funds is subject to specific tracking requirements, including recording the Federal Award Identification Number (FAIN) associated with the purchase.

#### 4.3 Procurement and Shipping
*   **Centralized Procurement:** All OPE must be purchased directly by the Organization’s IT or Operations department. Employees may not purchase computers personally and seek reimbursement without prior written approval from the Executive Director.
*   **Shipping:** The Organization covers all costs associated with shipping equipment to the employee's remote work location and return shipping upon separation.

#### 4.4 Acceptable Use and Security
*   **Exclusive Use:** OPE is for the exclusive use of the assigned employee. Family members, friends, or other non-employees are strictly prohibited from using OPE.
*   **Configuration:** Employees may not disable security software, MDM profiles, or encryption (BitLocker/FileVault) installed on OPE.
*   **Physical Security:** Employees must take reasonable care to protect OPE from theft, damage, or loss (e.g., not leaving laptops visible in vehicles).

#### 4.5 Maintenance and Repair
*   **Support:** The Organization is responsible for the maintenance and repair of OPE resulting from normal wear and tear.
*   **Negligence:** If damage results from gross negligence or misuse (e.g., liquid spills, dropping device), the Organization reserves the right to take disciplinary action.
*   **Unauthorized Repair:** Employees must not attempt to repair OPE themselves or take it to third-party repair shops without authorization from the CISO.

#### 4.6 Return of Equipment
Upon termination of employment (voluntary or involuntary), or upon request by the Organization:
*   **Timeline:** Employees must return all OPE within five (5) business days of the separation date.
*   **Process:** The Organization will provide prepaid shipping labels and packing instructions.
*   **Data Wiping:** Employees must not wipe or format devices prior to return unless explicitly instructed by the CISO, to preserve data for legal or business continuity purposes.

#### 4.7 Failure to Return
In accordance with Texas labor laws and the *At-Will Employment Acknowledgment*:
*   Failure to return equipment constitutes theft of Organization property.
*   The Organization reserves the right to pursue legal action to recover the equipment or its replacement value.
*   If the employee has signed a written authorization allowing deductions for unreturned equipment, the replacement cost may be deducted from the final paycheck, provided the deduction does not reduce pay below the federal minimum wage.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Approves the budget for Standard Issue Kits and authorizes exceptions to this policy. |
| **CISO / IT Dept** | Selects hardware standards, configures MDM, manages asset inventory, and coordinates shipping/returns. |
| **Hiring Manager** | Notifies IT of new hires at least 10 business days prior to start date to ensure timely equipment delivery. |
| **Employee** | Maintains equipment in good condition, reports loss/theft immediately, and returns equipment upon separation. |
| **Finance Dept** | Maintains depreciation schedules and ensures compliance with 2 CFR 200.313 for grant-funded assets. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Lost/Stolen Devices:** Failure to report a lost or stolen device within 24 hours is a serious security violation.
*   **Unreturned Assets:** The Organization will utilize all legal avenues, including civil litigation and police reports, to recover unreturned property.
