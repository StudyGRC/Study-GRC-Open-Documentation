### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a standardized, non-discriminatory, and risk-aware framework for the recruitment, screening, and selection of volunteers for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). Given the Organization's focus on K-12 education and youth programs, this policy prioritizes the safety of minors and the integrity of the Organization’s intellectual property and assets.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the recruitment and selection of all unpaid contributors, including:
*   **Community Participants** seeking formal volunteer roles.
*   **Supporting Members** fulfilling service requirements.
*   **Advisory Board** appointees.
*   **Interns** (unpaid).

**Exclusions:**
*   Election of the Board of Directors (governed by Bylaws Article III).
*   Hiring of paid employees (governed by HR-POL-020 Recruitment and Hiring Procedure).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteer** | An individual who performs services for the Organization without promise, expectation, or receipt of compensation for services rendered. |
| **Youth-Serving Position** | A volunteer role that involves direct interaction (virtual or physical) with minors, access to data regarding minors, or moderation of youth-focused digital spaces. |
| **General Volunteer** | A volunteer role with no access to minors or sensitive data (e.g., curriculum development, graphic design, administrative tasks). |
| **Screening** | The process of vetting a candidate's background, skills, and suitability, including interviews, reference checks, and criminal history reviews. |
| **Work Product** | As defined in Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3, content, code, and materials created by volunteers during their service. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Non-Discrimination
Study GRC Inc. is an equal opportunity organization. Recruitment of volunteers shall be conducted without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, disability, or veteran status, in compliance with applicable federal and Texas state laws.

#### 4.2 Risk-Based Position Categorization
To ensure appropriate vetting, all volunteer roles must be categorized prior to recruitment:
*   **Level 1 (Low Risk):** No contact with minors; limited access to non-sensitive data. (e.g., Content Writer).
*   **Level 2 (High Risk / Youth-Serving):** Direct contact with minors; access to Personally Identifiable Information (PII); administrative privileges on IT systems. (e.g., Mentor, Community Moderator, System Admin).

#### 4.3 Screening Requirements
All prospective volunteers must undergo a screening process commensurate with their Risk Level.

**4.3.1 Application:** All candidates must submit a written application (HR-FRM-076) detailing their experience, contact information, and reasons for volunteering.

**4.3.2 Interviews:**
*   **Level 1:** At least one (1) interview with the supervising Operational Officer.
*   **Level 2:** At least two (2) interviews, one of which must assess the candidate's suitability for working with youth and adherence to the Code of Conduct.

**4.3.3 Background Checks (Mandatory for Youth Protection):**
Pursuant to the Organization's commitment to youth safety and Texas Family Code Chapter 261:
*   **Level 2 Volunteers** **MUST** undergo and pass a criminal background check prior to beginning service.
*   Any candidate with a history of sexual offenses, crimes against children, or violent felonies is **permanently disqualified** from service.
*   Refusal to consent to a background check results in immediate disqualification.

**4.3.4 Reference Checks:**
*   **Level 2 Volunteers** must provide at least two (2) professional or personal references, which must be verified by the Organization.

#### 4.4 Intellectual Property Assignment
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, the Organization owns all content, code, and materials created by volunteers during their service.
*   **Mandatory Agreement:** All volunteers must sign a *Volunteer Service Agreement* which includes an explicit assignment of Intellectual Property rights to Study GRC Inc. and a waiver of moral rights.
*   **Open Source Commitment:** The Organization recruits volunteers with the understanding that their contributions will be released as open-source resources to remove financial barriers for learners.

#### 4.5 Conflict of Interest
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1**, volunteers must disclose any potential conflicts of interest during the recruitment process.
*   Volunteers employed by direct competitors or vendors must have their application reviewed by the Board or Executive Director to ensure loyalty to the Organization is not compromised.

#### 4.6 Minors as Volunteers
As a K-12 focused organization, Study GRC Inc. encourages student participation.
*   Volunteers under the age of 18 must provide a *Parental Consent and Liability Waiver* signed by a legal guardian.
*   Minor volunteers are prohibited from serving in Level 2 (Youth-Serving) positions where they would have supervisory authority over other minors.

#### 4.7 Remote Work Suitability
Given the Organization's "Remote-First" structure, recruitment must assess the candidate's ability to operate effectively in a virtual environment, including:
*   Access to reliable internet and secure hardware.
*   Proficiency with the Organization’s collaboration tools (e.g., Slack, Zoom).
*   Ability to maintain data confidentiality in a home environment.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Oversees the volunteer recruitment strategy; ensures cultural fit; manages the onboarding experience. |
| **Hiring Manager / Dept Head** | Defines role requirements; conducts interviews; selects candidates based on merit and safety. |
| **HR / Compliance Officer** | Administers background checks; verifies references; maintains volunteer personnel files. |
| **Executive Director** | Final approval authority for Level 2 (High Risk) volunteer appointments. |
| **Volunteer** | Provides truthful information during the application process; executes required agreements (NDA, IP Assignment). |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **Falsification:** Any applicant found to have provided false or misleading information during the recruitment process will be immediately disqualified or dismissed.
*   **Unauthorized Access:** Operational Officers who grant system access to volunteers prior to the completion of mandatory screening (specifically background checks for Level 2) are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Record Retention:** Recruitment records, including applications and background check results, shall be retained in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy* and applicable privacy laws (FCRA/GDPR).
