### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the mandatory framework for Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") to ensure fair and equitable opportunities for Minority Business Enterprises (MBE), Women’s Business Enterprises (WBE), and Labor Surplus Area (LSA) firms in the Organization's procurement processes.

This policy explicitly satisfies the requirements of **2 CFR § 200.321** ("Contracting with small and minority businesses, women's business enterprises, and labor surplus area firms") for all federally funded expenditures. Furthermore, it aligns with the Organization's charitable mission defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3** to foster a community-driven ecosystem by removing barriers to participation.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   All procurement activities, purchasing decisions, and contract negotiations conducted by Study GRC Inc. employees, officers, and volunteers.
*   All contracts and subcontracts funded in whole or in part by federal grants or cooperative agreements.

**Exclusions:**
*   Employment contracts (W-2 personnel), which are governed by the Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) Policy.
*   Procurements where no federal funding is utilized, though adherence to the spirit of this policy is encouraged for non-federal spend.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **MWBE** | A collective term for Minority-Owned and Women-Owned Business Enterprises. |
| **MBE (Minority Business Enterprise)** | A business that is at least 51% owned and controlled by one or more minority group members (e.g., Black, Hispanic, Asian-Pacific, Native American). |
| **WBE (Women's Business Enterprise)** | A business that is at least 51% owned and controlled by one or more women. |
| **LSA (Labor Surplus Area) Firm** | A business located in a civil jurisdiction classified by the U.S. Department of Labor as having a higher-than-average unemployment rate. |
| **Affirmative Steps** | The six specific actions mandated by 2 CFR § 200.321 that the Organization must take to assure MWBEs and LSA firms are used when possible. |
| **Unbundling** | The practice of breaking down large procurement requirements into smaller components to facilitate participation by smaller firms. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Commitment
Study GRC Inc. is committed to supplier diversity. It is the policy of the Organization to provide maximum practicable opportunity to small, minority-owned, and women-owned businesses to compete for and participate in the performance of contracts.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Affirmative Steps (Federal Funding)
Pursuant to **2 CFR § 200.321(b)**, for all procurements utilizing federal funds, the Organization **must** take the following six affirmative steps:

1.  **Solicitation Lists:** Qualified small and minority businesses and women's business enterprises must be placed on solicitation lists. The CFO shall maintain a "Preferred Vendor Register" that tags these entities.
2.  **Direct Solicitation:** The Organization must assure that small and minority businesses and women's business enterprises are solicited whenever they are potential sources.
3.  **Unbundling (Dividing Requirements):** When economically feasible, total requirements must be divided into smaller tasks or quantities to permit maximum participation by small and minority businesses and women's business enterprises.
    *   *Note:* This does not authorize artificial splitting of purchases solely to avoid capitalization thresholds (as prohibited in the Procurement Policy), but rather strategic division to enable smaller vendor participation.
4.  **Delivery Schedules:** The Organization must establish delivery schedules, where the requirement permits, which encourage participation by small and minority businesses and women's business enterprises (e.g., avoiding unreasonable rush orders that disqualify smaller firms).
5.  **Utilization of Resources:** The Organization must use the services and assistance, as appropriate, of such organizations as the Small Business Administration (SBA) and the Minority Business Development Agency (MBDA) of the Department of Commerce to identify qualified vendors.
6.  **Subcontracting Flow-Down:** If the Organization awards a prime contract that involves subcontracts, the prime contractor must be required to take the affirmative steps listed in items (1) through (5) of this section.

#### 4.3 Documentation and Record Keeping
In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.1** (Maintenance of Records), the procurement file for every federally funded purchase exceeding the Micro-Purchase Threshold must contain evidence of compliance with Section 4.2.

*   **Required Evidence:** Screenshots of search results from SBA/MBDA databases, copies of solicitation emails sent to MWBE firms, or a written justification explaining why "Unbundling" (Step 3) was not economically feasible for a specific procurement.
*   **Failure to Document:** Failure to document these steps may result in the cost being disallowed by the federal grantor.

#### 4.4 No Quotas or Set-Asides
This policy mandates **opportunity**, not **outcomes**.
*   The Organization shall not utilize quotas or set-asides that mandate the selection of a vendor solely based on their MWBE status, as this may violate competitive bidding requirements under 2 CFR 200.319 (Competition).
*   MWBE status is a solicitation requirement, not a weighted scoring criteria, unless explicitly authorized by the specific terms of a federal award or state statute.

#### 4.5 Geographic Preference Prohibition
In identifying LSA firms or MWBEs, the Organization must not apply geographic preferences (e.g., "Texas-only vendors") in the evaluation of bids or proposals, except in those cases where applicable federal statutes expressly mandate or encourage geographic preference.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves this policy to ensure alignment with Bylaws Article I (Purposes) and federal compliance. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Primary owner of this policy. Maintains the "Preferred Vendor Register" and ensures the "Six Affirmative Steps" are integrated into the procurement workflow. |
| **Grant Manager** | Verifies that procurement files contain proof of Affirmative Steps prior to authorizing draw-down of federal funds. |
| **Purchasing Staff / Requestors** | Responsible for actively searching for and soliciting MWBE/LSA firms during the market research phase of procurement. |
| **Prime Contractors** | Contractually obligated to adhere to Affirmative Steps 1-5 when awarding subcontracts under an Organization prime contract. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the Organization at risk of audit findings, disallowed costs, and loss of tax-exempt status or federal funding eligibility.

*   **Internal Discipline:** Employees found to have willfully bypassed the Affirmative Steps for federally funded procurements are subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Vendor Compliance:** Prime contractors failing to demonstrate Good Faith Efforts in subcontracting to MWBEs may be subject to contract termination for breach.
