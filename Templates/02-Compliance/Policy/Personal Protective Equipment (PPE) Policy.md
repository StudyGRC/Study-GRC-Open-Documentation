### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to protect the health and safety of Study GRC Inc. employees, volunteers, and contractors by establishing requirements for the assessment, selection, provision, and use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE). This policy ensures compliance with the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) standard **29 CFR 1910.132** and aligns with the Organization’s commitment to maintaining a safe community ecosystem as outlined in the charitable purposes of the **Bylaws, Article I, Section 1.3**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Study GRC Inc. personnel, including:
*   **Employees** (Full-time, part-time, and temporary).
*   **Volunteers** (including Board Members and Committee Members when performing physical tasks).
*   **Contractors** working under the direct supervision of Study GRC Inc.

**Applicability:** This policy applies specifically to physical operations, including but not limited to:
*   In-person events, conferences, and meetups (setup, teardown, and operations).
*   Youth STEM workshops or educational demonstrations involving physical materials.
*   Storage facility management and physical asset inventory.

**Exclusions:** This policy generally does not apply to standard remote administrative work performed in a home office, unless specific hazards are identified that require PPE.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)** | Specialized clothing or equipment worn by an individual for protection against health and safety hazards. Examples include safety glasses, gloves, high-visibility vests, and hearing protection. |
| **Hazard Assessment** | A formal, documented process to identify hazards in the workplace that necessitate the use of PPE. |
| **ANSI** | American National Standards Institute; the organization that sets the design and performance standards for PPE. |
| **Certifying Officer** | The individual designated by the Organization to verify that a hazard assessment has been performed (usually the Event Manager or Safety Officer). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Hazard Assessment and Equipment Selection
In strict compliance with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.132(d)**, Study GRC Inc. shall assess the workplace to determine if hazards are present, or are likely to be present, which necessitate the use of PPE.

1.  **Written Certification:** A written hazard assessment must be completed prior to any physical event or workshop involving potential risks (e.g., lifting heavy AV equipment, chemical handling in science workshops). This document must identify the workplace evaluated, the person certifying that the evaluation has been performed, and the date(s) of the hazard assessment.
2.  **Selection Criteria:** If hazards are identified, the Organization shall select, and have each affected employee/volunteer use, the types of PPE that will protect the affected individual from the hazards identified.
3.  **Fit and Comfort:** The Organization shall select PPE that properly fits each affected individual.

#### 4.2 Provision of PPE
Study GRC Inc. shall provide required PPE at no cost to employees and volunteers, with limited exceptions as defined by OSHA (e.g., everyday clothing, weather-related gear like winter coats).
*   **ANSI Standards:** All PPE provided must meet American National Standards Institute (ANSI) standards as incorporated by reference in OSHA regulations (e.g., ANSI Z87.1 for Eye Protection).

#### 4.3 Training Requirements
Prior to performing work requiring PPE, employees and volunteers must be trained in accordance with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910.132(f)**. Training must cover:
1.  When PPE is necessary.
2.  What PPE is necessary.
3.  How to properly don, doff, adjust, and wear PPE.
4.  The limitations of the PPE.
5.  The proper care, maintenance, useful life, and disposal of the PPE.

**Retraining:** Retraining is required when the workplace changes, the type of PPE changes, or there is evidence that the individual has not retained the requisite understanding.

#### 4.4 Use and Maintenance
1.  **Mandatory Use:** Personnel must wear required PPE at all times when exposed to the identified hazards. Refusal to wear required PPE is grounds for immediate removal from the hazard area and disciplinary action.
2.  **Inspection:** Users must inspect PPE before each use.
3.  **Defective Equipment:** Defective or damaged PPE shall not be used. It must be immediately reported to the supervisor and removed from service/destroyed.

#### 4.5 Specific Hazard Categories (Event & Workshop Context)
Given the nature of Study GRC Inc. operations, the following categories are most relevant:

*   **Hand Protection (29 CFR 1910.138):** Required when hands are exposed to hazards such as skin absorption of harmful substances (e.g., cleaning chemicals), severe cuts or lacerations (e.g., box cutters during setup), or thermal burns.
*   **Eye and Face Protection (29 CFR 1910.133):** Required during STEM workshops involving projectiles, chemical splashes, or soldering. Side shields are mandatory when there is a hazard from flying objects.
*   **Foot Protection (29 CFR 1910.136):** Closed-toe shoes are mandatory for all personnel during event setup and teardown. Steel-toed boots may be required for specific heavy-lift operations.
*   **High-Visibility Apparel:** Required for volunteers/staff managing parking, loading docks, or outdoor crowd control near vehicular traffic.

#### 4.6 Employee-Owned PPE
Employees or volunteers may provide their own PPE only if authorized by the Event Manager. Study GRC Inc. retains the responsibility to ensure the adequacy, maintenance, and sanitation of such equipment.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director** | Ensure adequate budget is allocated for the purchase of compliant PPE. Approve the general Hazard Assessment framework. |
| **Event Manager / Safety Officer** | Conduct and sign the **Hazard Assessment Certification** for specific events. Select appropriate PPE. Conduct or arrange for required training. |
| **Supervisors / Team Leads** | Enforce PPE usage on-site. Ensure defective equipment is discarded. |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Wear PPE as trained. Inspect PPE prior to use. Report changes in exposure or health conditions that might affect PPE use. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the individual and the Organization at significant risk.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Violations will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Indemnification Impact:** Per **Bylaws Article VII (Indemnification)**, indemnification is contingent upon acting in good faith. Willful violation of safety policies, including refusal to wear required PPE, may constitute a lack of good faith, potentially voiding indemnification protections for the individual in the event of an accident.
