### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and procedures by which Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") manages subawards when acting as a Pass-Through Entity (PTE) for federal, state, or other restricted grant funds. This policy ensures compliance with **2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance)**, specifically §§ 200.331 through 200.333, regarding subrecipient monitoring and management. It is designed to mitigate risk, ensure funds are utilized for authorized purposes, and maintain the integrity of the Organization’s tax-exempt status under **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, officers, and directors involved in the administration of grant funds where the Organization serves as a PTE.
*   **In Scope:** All agreements where federal or restricted funds are passed to a third-party entity to carry out a portion of a federal award or program.
*   **Exclusions:** Procurement contracts for goods and services (vendors/contractors) used for the Organization’s own internal use, which are governed by the *Procurement Policy (FIN-POL-022)*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pass-Through Entity (PTE)** | A non-federal entity (Study GRC Inc.) that provides a subaward to a subrecipient to carry out part of a federal program. |
| **Subaward** | An award provided by a PTE to a subrecipient for the subrecipient to carry out part of a federal award received by the PTE. It does not include payments to a contractor or payments to an individual that is a beneficiary of a federal program. |
| **Subrecipient** | A non-federal entity that receives a subaward from a PTE to carry out part of a federal program. A subrecipient has programmatic decision-making responsibility. |
| **Contractor (Vendor)** | An entity that receives a contract for the purpose of obtaining goods and services for the Organization's own use and creates a procurement relationship. |
| **Uniform Guidance** | 2 CFR Part 200, "Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards." |
| **FAIN** | Federal Award Identification Number. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Subrecipient vs. Contractor Determination
Prior to the execution of any agreement involving federal funds, the Organization must make a case-by-case determination whether the party receiving the funds is a **Subrecipient** or a **Contractor**. This determination must be based on the substance of the relationship rather than the form of the agreement, in accordance with **2 CFR § 200.331**.
*   **Subrecipient Characteristics:** Determines who is eligible to receive assistance; has its own performance measured in relation to whether objectives of the federal program were met; has responsibility for programmatic decision-making; is responsible for adherence to applicable federal program requirements.
*   **Contractor Characteristics:** Provides the goods and services within normal business operations; provides similar goods or services to many different purchasers; operates in a competitive environment; provides goods or services that are ancillary to the operation of the federal program.

#### 4.2 Pre-Award Risk Assessment
The Organization must evaluate each subrecipient's risk of noncompliance with federal statutes, regulations, and the terms and conditions of the subaward for purposes of determining the appropriate subrecipient monitoring described in **2 CFR § 200.332(b)**.

#### 4.3 Subaward Agreement Requirements
All subawards must be clearly identified to the subrecipient as a subaward and include all information required by **2 CFR § 200.332(a)**, including but not limited to the FAIN, federal award date, subrecipient's Unique Entity Identifier (UEI), and indirect cost rate.

#### 4.4 Subrecipient Monitoring
The Organization is responsible for monitoring the activities of the subrecipient as necessary to ensure that the subaward is used for authorized purposes, in compliance with federal statutes, regulations, and the terms and conditions of the subaward; and that subaward performance goals are achieved.

#### 4.5 Conflicts of Interest
All subaward selections and management activities must adhere strictly to the **Conflict of Interest Policy** and **Bylaws Article IX**. No subaward may be issued to an entity controlled by a Director or Officer of Study GRC Inc. without strict adherence to the *Interested Party Transaction Review Protocol*.

### 5. Procedures

#### 5.1 Determination and Selection Phase
1.  **Proposal Review:** The Program Manager reviews the potential partner's scope of work.
2.  **Classification:** The CFO or Grant Manager completes the *Subrecipient vs. Contractor Checklist*.
    *   If classified as a **Contractor**, the process moves to the *Procurement Policy*.
    *   If classified as a **Subrecipient**, the process continues below.
3.  **SAM.gov Check:** The Grant Manager verifies the entity is not suspended or debarred via SAM.gov.

#### 5.2 Risk Assessment Phase
The CFO must conduct a risk assessment using the *Subrecipient Risk Assessment Tool*, evaluating:
1.  **Prior Experience:** The subrecipient's prior experience with the same or similar subawards.
2.  **Audit Results:** Results of previous audits (including Single Audits).
3.  **Personnel:** Whether the subrecipient has new personnel or new or substantially changed systems.
4.  **Federal Monitoring:** The extent and results of recent federal monitoring.

*Risk Classification:*
*   **Low Risk:** Standard monitoring.
*   **Medium Risk:** Additional reporting requirements; more frequent contact.
*   **High Risk:** Reimbursement only (no advances); additional documentation required; mandatory technical assistance/training; on-site (or virtual) reviews.

#### 5.3 Agreement Execution
1.  **Drafting:** The Legal/Compliance team drafts the Subaward Agreement containing all required data elements per **2 CFR § 200.332(a)**.
2.  **Indirect Costs:** The agreement must accept the subrecipient's federally negotiated indirect cost rate. If no such rate exists, a de minimis rate of 10% of modified total direct costs (MTDC) may be used.
3.  **Approval:** The Executive Director (CEO) reviews and signs the agreement, pursuant to the operational authority granted in **Bylaws Article V, Section 5.2**.

#### 5.4 Post-Award Monitoring
The Program Manager and Finance Department will perform the following:
1.  **Performance Reports:** Review programmatic reports to ensure progress aligns with the scope of work.
2.  **Financial Review:** Review financial and performance reports required by the PTE.
3.  **Invoice Review:** Verify that costs are allowable, allocable, and reasonable before payment.
4.  **Audit Review:** Verify that every subrecipient is audited as required by Subpart F of 2 CFR Part 200 (if they expend $750,000+ in federal funds).
5.  **Deficiency Management:** If a deficiency is detected, the Organization must issue a *Management Decision* on audit findings within six months and ensure the subrecipient takes timely and appropriate action.

#### 5.5 Closeout
Upon completion of the subaward period:
1.  Final financial and programmatic reports must be received within 90 days (or as specified in the agreement).
2.  The CFO ensures all financial obligations are settled.
3.  The Organization retains all subaward records for a period of three (3) years from the date of submission of the final expenditure report, in accordance with the *Document Retention and Destruction Policy*.

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Final signatory authority on all Subaward Agreements; ensures alignment with strategic mission. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Conducts financial risk assessments; approves risk classifications; oversees financial monitoring and invoice approval; ensures Single Audit compliance. |
| **Program Manager** | Defines Scope of Work; monitors programmatic performance; serves as primary point of contact for the subrecipient. |
| **Grant Manager** | Maintains the *Subrecipient vs. Contractor Checklist*; performs SAM.gov debarment checks; maintains the official subaward file. |
| **Board of Directors** | Reviews and approves this policy; reviews aggregate reports on subaward performance and risk during regular meetings. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy puts the Organization at risk of disallowed costs, loss of federal funding, and legal liability.
*   **Internal:** Employees violating this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **External:** Subrecipients failing to comply with the terms of the Subaward Agreement may face withholding of cash payments, disallowance of costs, suspension, or termination of the subaward, as outlined in **2 CFR § 200.339**.
