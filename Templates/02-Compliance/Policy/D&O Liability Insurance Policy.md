### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to mandate and govern the maintenance of Directors and Officers (D&O) Liability Insurance for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy ensures the financial protection of the Organization’s Directors, Officers, volunteers, and the entity itself against claims alleging wrongful acts in the management of the Organization.

This policy serves to:
1.  Operationalize the indemnification provisions outlined in **Article VII** of the Organization’s Bylaws.
2.  Protect the personal assets of volunteer leaders, thereby enabling the Organization to recruit and retain qualified Board members.
3.  Provide a financial backstop for legal defense costs and settlements resulting from governance disputes, regulatory investigations, or employment practices liability.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the procurement, maintenance, and utilization of insurance coverage for:
*   **Insured Persons:** Past, present, and future Directors, Officers, Committee Members, and Volunteers of the Organization.
*   **The Entity:** Study GRC Inc. as a corporate entity.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not cover acts established by final adjudication to be criminal, fraudulent, or involving illegal personal profit, nor does it cover bodily injury or property damage (which are covered under General Liability).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Wrongful Act** | Any actual or alleged breach of duty, neglect, error, misstatement, misleading statement, omission, or act by the Directors or Officers in their respective capacities. |
| **Side A Coverage** | Insurance coverage that directly indemnifies individual Directors and Officers for losses when the Organization is legally unable or unwilling to indemnify them (e.g., bankruptcy). |
| **Side B Coverage** | Insurance coverage that reimburses the Organization for the amounts it pays to indemnify Directors and Officers. |
| **Side C Coverage** | Insurance coverage for the corporate entity itself (Entity Securities Coverage or Entity Coverage) for claims made against the Organization. |
| **EPLI** | Employment Practices Liability Insurance; often bundled with D&O to cover claims of wrongful termination, discrimination, or harassment. |
| **Tail Coverage** | An Extended Reporting Period (ERP) endorsement that extends the time to report claims for wrongful acts that occurred during the policy period, after the policy has been cancelled or non-renewed. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Mandatory Maintenance of Coverage
The Organization must maintain a Directors and Officers Liability Insurance policy in full force and effect at all times. The lapse of such coverage is strictly prohibited.

#### 4.2 Minimum Coverage Standards
The Board of Directors shall review the coverage limits annually. At a minimum, the policy must include:
*   **Limit of Liability:** A minimum aggregate limit of **$1,000,000** (or a higher amount as deemed necessary by the Board based on asset size and risk exposure).
*   **Coverage Sides:** The policy must provide Side A, Side B, and Side C coverage.
*   **Defense Costs:** The policy must cover defense costs (legal fees), preferably outside the limit of liability where commercially feasible, or within the limit if standard for the nonprofit market.
*   **Employment Practices:** The policy shall include or be accompanied by an Employment Practices Liability (EPLI) endorsement to cover claims related to wrongful termination, harassment, and discrimination.

#### 4.3 Advancement of Defense Costs
Consistent with **Bylaws Article VII**, the Organization prioritizes insurance policies that provide for the **advancement of defense costs** prior to the final disposition of a claim, rather than reimbursement after the fact.

#### 4.4 Exclusions and Conduct
While the Organization provides this insurance, coverage is subject to the terms and conditions of the carrier. The Organization acknowledges that D&O insurance typically excludes:
*   Criminal or fraudulent acts (once proven).
*   Illegal remuneration or personal profit.
*   Claims between Insureds (Insured vs. Insured), except as modified by the policy (e.g., whistleblower exceptions).

#### 4.5 Notification of Claims
Any Director, Officer, or employee who becomes aware of a potential claim, a written demand for monetary or non-monetary relief, or a formal administrative or regulatory proceeding must notify the **Treasurer** and the **President** immediately.
*   Failure to report a claim within the specific window defined by the insurance carrier ("Claims-Made" policy requirements) may result in denial of coverage.
*   No individual may admit liability or settle a claim without the written consent of the insurance carrier.

#### 4.6 Tail Coverage (Extended Reporting Period)
In the event of a merger, acquisition, or dissolution (Fundamental Actions per **Bylaws Article X**), the Organization shall purchase "Tail Coverage" (Extended Reporting Period) for a minimum of **three (3) years** (or the applicable statute of limitations) to cover claims arising from acts committed prior to the transaction.

#### 4.7 Severability of Application
The Organization shall seek policies containing a "Severability of Application" clause. This ensures that the misrepresentations or fraud of one Director or Officer in the insurance application process do not void coverage for other innocent Directors or Officers.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the selection of the insurance carrier and the limits of liability annually. Reviews the adequacy of coverage relative to the Organization's growth. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Acts as the primary liaison with the insurance broker. Ensures premiums are paid on time. Manages the renewal application process. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains current copies of the insurance policy and declarations page in the corporate records. |
| **Directors & Officers** | Required to cooperate fully with the insurer in the defense of any claim and to provide accurate information during the application/renewal process. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to maintain this insurance constitutes a significant lapse in governance and risk management.
*   **Operational Officers** who fail to pay premiums or renew coverage on time may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
*   **Directors** who knowingly allow coverage to lapse may be held personally liable for breaches of fiduciary duty.
