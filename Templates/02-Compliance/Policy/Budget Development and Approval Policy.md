### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the framework for the development, review, approval, and modification of the annual operating budget and grant-specific budgets for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy ensures financial stability, mission alignment, and strict compliance with federal regulations regarding budget revisions, specifically **2 CFR 200.308** (Revision of budget and program plans) for federal awards.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **All Funds:** Unrestricted operating funds, restricted grant funds, and capital expenditures.
*   **Personnel:** The Board of Directors, Finance Committee, Officers (Executive Director, CFO, Treasurer), and Program Managers.
*   **External Obligations:** All federal, state, and private grant awards managed by the Organization.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Operating Budget** | The annual financial plan projecting revenue and expenses for the Organization’s general operations for the Fiscal Year. |
| **Grant Budget** | A specific financial plan attached to a restricted award (e.g., Federal Grant) outlining how funds will be spent over the award's performance period. |
| **Capital Budget** | A plan for the acquisition of fixed assets (equipment, technology, property) exceeding the capitalization threshold defined in the *Fixed Asset Capitalization Policy*. |
| **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)** | Federal regulations (Office of Management and Budget) governing the administration of federal grants. |
| **Participant Support Costs** | Direct costs for items such as stipends or subsistence allowances, travel allowances, and registration fees paid to or on behalf of participants or trainees (but not employees) in connection with conferences or training projects. |
| **Re-budgeting** | The transfer of funds from one budget category (line item) to another. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Fiscal Year and Budget Cycle
In accordance with **Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3**, the Fiscal Year of the Organization is the Calendar Year (January 1 to December 31).
*   **Timeline:** The budget development process must commence no later than October 1st of the preceding year.
*   **Approval Deadline:** The Board of Directors must approve the final Operating Budget prior to the start of the Fiscal Year (December meeting).

#### 4.2 Budget Development Principles
The Executive Director and CFO are responsible for drafting the budget based on the following principles:
1.  **Mission Alignment:** Allocations must prioritize the Organization's charitable purposes as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3** (e.g., open-source resources, GRC education).
2.  **Conservative Revenue Estimation:** Revenue projections must be based on committed funding and historically verifiable trends, not speculative income.
3.  **Full Cost Recovery:** Grant budgets must include applicable Indirect Costs (Facilities and Administrative costs) at the Organization’s Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate (NICRA) or the de minimis rate (10%) as allowed by **2 CFR 200.414**.

#### 4.3 Federal Award Budgeting (2 CFR 200 Compliance)
For all federal awards, the Organization must adhere to **2 CFR 200.308**.
*   **Allowability:** All budgeted costs must be reasonable, allocable, and allowable under **2 CFR 200 Subpart E**.
*   **Consistency:** Costs incurred for the same purpose in like circumstances must be treated consistently as either direct or indirect costs.

#### 4.4 Budget Approval Authority
*   **Finance Committee Review:** The draft budget must be reviewed in detail by the Finance Committee (established under **Bylaws Article VI**) prior to submission to the full Board.
*   **Board Approval:** Final approval requires a majority vote of the Board of Directors.
*   **Deficit Budgets:** The Board generally prohibits the approval of a deficit budget (expenses exceeding revenue) unless a strategic plan is in place to utilize Board-designated reserves to cover the shortfall.

#### 4.5 Budget Revisions and Variances
Significant deviations from the approved budget require authorization.

**4.5.1 Internal Operating Budget Revisions**
*   **Operational Authority:** The Executive Director may reallocate funds between line items within a department budget up to **$5,000 or 10%** of the line item (whichever is lower), provided the total department budget does not increase.
*   **Board Authority:** Any reallocation exceeding the Operational Authority threshold, or any increase in the total expense budget, requires Finance Committee review and Board approval.

**4.5.2 Federal Grant Budget Revisions (2 CFR 200.308)**
Strict adherence to federal prior approval requirements is mandatory. The Organization **must request prior written approval** from the Federal Awarding Agency for:
1.  **Change in Scope:** Any change in the scope or the objective of the project or program (even if there is no associated budget change).
2.  **Key Personnel:** Change in a key person specified in the application or the federal award.
3.  **Disengagement:** The disengagement from the project for more than three months, or a 25% reduction in time devoted to the project, by the approved project director or principal investigator.
4.  **Participant Support Costs:** The transfer of funds budgeted for participant support costs to other categories of expense (**2 CFR 200.308(c)(5)**).
5.  **Subawarding:** The subawarding, transferring, or contracting out of any work under a federal award (unless described in the application and funded in the approved federal award).
6.  **Cost Sharing:** Changes in the amount of approved cost-sharing or matching provided.

**4.5.3 Notification**
The CFO must immediately notify the Executive Director and the Treasurer if projections indicate a potential budget overrun or if a federal prior approval trigger event occurs.

#### 4.6 Monitoring and Reporting
*   **Monthly Review:** The CFO must prepare budget-to-actual variance reports monthly for the Executive Director.
*   **Quarterly Board Reporting:** The Treasurer must present a financial report to the Board at least quarterly, highlighting any variances exceeding **10%** of the budgeted amount.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Grants final approval of the annual Operating Budget and any amendments increasing total expenditures. |
| **Finance Committee** | Reviews draft budgets in detail; recommends approval to the Board; monitors quarterly financial performance. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides oversight of the budgeting process; ensures alignment with Board strategic goals; reports financial health to the Board. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Directs the creation of the budget; authorizes operational reallocations within defined limits; ensures program goals are met within budget. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Prepares technical budget documents; monitors compliance with **2 CFR 200.308**; submits prior approval requests to federal agencies; tracks variances. |
| **Program Managers** | Provide input on resource needs; manage spending within their approved program budgets; alert leadership to potential overruns. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
*   **Federal Non-Compliance:** Failure to obtain prior approval for budget revisions as required by **2 CFR 200.308** may result in cost disallowance, requiring the Organization to repay funds to the federal government from unrestricted reserves.
*   **Unauthorized Spending:** Personnel authorizing expenditures outside of the approved budget or without proper authority may be held personally liable for restitution.
