### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the "Control Environment" for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") in accordance with the COSO Internal Control Framework and Federal Regulation 2 CFR § 200.303. This policy defines the "Tone at the Top"—the ethical atmosphere created by the Organization's leadership—which serves as the foundation for all other components of internal control. It ensures that integrity, ethical values, and competence are prioritized in every decision, protecting the Organization’s mission to foster a community-driven GRC ecosystem.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the entire governance and operational structure of the Organization, specifically:
*   **Governance:** The Board of Directors and Board Committees.
*   **Operational Leadership:** The Executive Director (CEO), C-Suite Officers (CISO, CFO), and Department Heads.
*   **Workforce:** All employees, volunteers, and contractors.

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Commitment to Integrity and Ethical Values
The Board of Directors and Operational Officers must demonstrate a commitment to integrity and ethical values.
*   **4.1.1 Lead by Example:** Leadership must model the behaviors expected of the community. Actions must align with the *Code of Ethics and Conduct* and the mission defined in Bylaws Article I.
*   **4.1.2 Zero Tolerance:** The Organization maintains a zero-tolerance policy regarding fraud, corruption, harassment, and the misuse of assets.
*   **4.1.3 Digital Conduct:** As a remote-first organization, "Tone" is often conveyed via text. Leadership must maintain professional, inclusive, and respectful communication in all digital channels (e.g., Slack, Discord, Email), reinforcing the role of the *Director of Kindness and Generosity* (Bylaws § 5.2).

#### 4.2 Board of Directors Independence and Oversight
Pursuant to Bylaws Article III, the Board of Directors must demonstrate independence from management and exercise oversight of the development and performance of internal control.
*   **4.2.1 Separation of Duties:** While Bylaws § 5.1 allows dual service, the Board must ensure that a majority of voting Directors are independent and free from conflicts that would impair their objectivity in overseeing the Executive Director and CFO.
*   **4.2.2 Challenge Function:** The Board is expected to ask difficult questions and challenge management assumptions constructively. "Rubber-stamping" operational decisions is a violation of the Duty of Care.
*   **4.2.3 Executive Sessions:** The Board shall regularly hold executive sessions without Operational Officers present to discuss sensitive matters regarding management performance and integrity.

#### 4.3 Organizational Structure and Authority
Management, with Board oversight, must establish structures, reporting lines, and appropriate authorities and responsibilities in the pursuit of objectives.
*   **4.3.1 Clear Reporting Lines:** The Organization must maintain a current Organizational Chart. As per Bylaws § 5.2, all Operational Officers report to the Executive Director, who reports to the Board.
*   **4.3.2 Delegation of Authority:** Authority is delegated only to the extent required to achieve objectives. Financial authority limits must be documented in the *Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix*.
*   **4.3.3 No "Shadow" Management:** Decisions must follow the formal hierarchy. Informal influence by founders or former directors that bypasses established governance channels is prohibited.

#### 4.4 Commitment to Competence
The Organization must demonstrate a commitment to attract, develop, and retain competent individuals in alignment with objectives.
*   **4.4.1 Qualified Leadership:** Appointments to roles such as the CISO or CFO (Bylaws § 5.2) must be based on demonstrated professional competence, not solely on tenure or personal relationships.
*   **4.4.2 Youth Protection Competence:** Given the Organization's K-12 focus, all staff and volunteers must demonstrate competence in youth safeguarding protocols.
*   **4.4.3 Background Checks:** Competence includes character. Background checks are mandatory for all positions of trust in accordance with the *Background Check Policy*.

#### 4.5 Accountability
The Organization holds individuals accountable for their internal control responsibilities in the pursuit of objectives.
*   **4.5.1 Performance Management:** The Board must conduct an annual performance review of the Executive Director. The Executive Director must conduct reviews for all Operational Officers.
*   **4.5.2 Consequence Management:** Violations of policy or ethical standards must result in consistent disciplinary action, regardless of the individual's title or status as a "Founding Member."
*   **4.5.3 Transparency:** In accordance with Bylaws Article VIII, the Organization commits to financial transparency, including the public availability of IRS Form 990s and Annual Financial Reports.

#### 4.6 Open Communication and "Speak Up" Culture
*   **4.6.1 Whistleblower Protection:** Management must foster an environment where employees and volunteers feel safe reporting concerns without fear of retaliation, in accordance with the *Whistleblower Protection Policy*.
*   **4.6.2 Bad News Travels Fast:** Operational Officers are obligated to report material risks, compliance failures, or significant errors to the Board immediately. Suppressing negative information is a terminable offense.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Sets the "Tone at the Top." Approves the Code of Ethics. Evaluates the Executive Director's adherence to ethical standards. |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Monitors the effectiveness of the Control Environment. Investigates whistleblower complaints regarding Senior Management. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Cascades the "Tone" to Operational Officers and staff. Ensures job descriptions and hiring practices align with competence standards. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Monitors community culture and internal morale to ensure alignment with the Organization's ethical values. |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Adhere to the Code of Ethics. Report control deficiencies or ethical concerns immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy undermines the Organization's internal control structure.
*   **Directors:** Violations may result in removal for cause pursuant to Bylaws § 3.6.
*   **Operational Officers/Staff:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action.
