### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is to outline the acceptable use of computer equipment, systems, networks, and electronic resources at Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"). This policy is designed to protect the Organization, its employees, volunteers, and the community it serves from risks associated with the misuse of systems, including data breaches, legal liability, and reputational damage. This policy satisfies the "Awareness" control requirement by establishing clear behavioral expectations for all system users.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all "Users" of Study GRC Inc. information systems.
*   **Applies to:** Employees, Board of Directors, volunteers, contractors, consultants, temporaries, and other workers at the Organization, including all personnel affiliated with local chapters.
*   **Systems Covered:** All equipment, software, networks, data, and systems owned, leased, or utilized by the Organization, as well as personal devices (BYOD) when used to access Organizational data.
*   **Exclusions:** Personal use of personal devices that does not involve the Organization’s data, networks, or reputation.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **User** | Any individual who has been granted access to the Organization's Information Systems. |
| **Information Systems** | All electronic means used to create, store, access, transmit, and safeguard data, including email, Internet, software, computers, mobile devices, and cloud platforms (e.g., Google Workspace, Slack, GitHub). |
| **Sensitive Data** | Information classified as Confidential or Restricted, including donor PII, youth participant data, and intellectual property not yet released as open source. |
| **Incidental Use** | Brief, occasional personal use of IT resources that does not interfere with productivity or violate policy. |
| **BYOD** | "Bring Your Own Device" – the practice of using personal devices for Organizational work. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Responsibility and No Expectation of Privacy
4.1.1 Users are responsible for exercising good judgment regarding the reasonableness of personal use.
4.1.2 Users should have no expectation of privacy regarding anything created, sent, or received on the Organization’s Information Systems. The Organization reserves the right to monitor, review, and record all usage and data on its systems without prior notice, to the extent permitted by law.

#### 4.2 Prohibited Conduct
The following activities are strictly prohibited. Violation may result in immediate termination of access and disciplinary action.
4.2.1 **Illegal Activity:** Engaging in any activity that is illegal under local, state, federal, or international law while using Organizational resources.
4.2.2 **Harassment and Abuse:** Using systems to harass, bully, defame, or discriminate against others. This includes the display or transmission of sexually explicit images, messages, or cartoons.
4.2.3 **Youth Protection Violations:** Using Organizational systems to engage in "grooming" behaviors, unmonitored one-on-one private messaging with minors, or accessing/distributing child sexual abuse material (CSAM).
4.2.4 **Political Campaigning:** Using Organizational resources for political campaigning or lobbying activities that jeopardize the Organization’s 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status.
4.2.5 **Commercial Use:** Using resources for outside commercial ventures, personal profit, or solicitation not related to the Organization’s mission.
4.2.6 **Security Circumvention:** Attempting to bypass, break, or probe security controls (e.g., cracking passwords, disabling antivirus) without explicit authorization from the CISO.

#### 4.3 System and Network Security
4.3.1 **Access Credentials:** Users must not share their passwords or account credentials with anyone, including IT staff or supervisors.
4.3.2 **Unattended Devices:** Users must lock their screens (e.g., Windows Key + L or Command + Control + Q) whenever leaving their device unattended, even in a home office environment.
4.3.3 **Unauthorized Software:** Users may not install software that compromises security or violates licensing agreements. "Shadow IT" (using unapproved cloud services for sensitive data) is prohibited.
4.3.4 **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Users must not disable or attempt to bypass MFA mechanisms on any Organizational account.

#### 4.4 Data Protection and Intellectual Property
4.4.1 **Confidentiality:** Users must prevent unauthorized disclosure of Sensitive Data. Sensitive Data must not be stored on public computers or unencrypted portable media.
4.4.2 **Intellectual Property (IP):** In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all content, code, and materials created by Users during their service are "works made for hire" and are the property of the Organization. Users may not claim personal ownership of such work or use it to compete with the Organization.
4.4.3 **Data Exfiltration:** Users are prohibited from automatically forwarding Organizational email to personal email accounts or mass-downloading data upon departure from the Organization.

#### 4.5 Electronic Communication and Social Media
4.5.1 **Professionalism:** Users must maintain a professional tone in all communications (Email, Slack, Discord) representing the Organization.
4.5.2 **Phishing Awareness:** Users must exercise caution when opening email attachments or clicking links from unknown senders and must report suspicious activity to the Security Team immediately.
4.5.3 **Public Representation:** Users must not speak on behalf of the Organization on social media or to the press unless expressly authorized, per the *Communications and Public Relations Policy*.

#### 4.6 Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)
4.6.1 **Security Requirements:** Personal devices used for Organizational business must maintain active antivirus software (where applicable) and receive regular operating system security updates.
4.6.2 **Lost or Stolen Devices:** Users must report the loss or theft of any device containing Organizational data (including email access) to the CISO within 24 hours.
4.6.3 **Remote Wipe:** The Organization reserves the right to remotely wipe Organizational data from personal devices if the device is lost, stolen, or upon the termination of the User's relationship with the Organization.

#### 4.7 Incidental Personal Use
4.7.1 Incidental personal use of systems is permitted provided it:
*   Does not interfere with the User’s duties or productivity.
*   Does not produce a negative impact on system performance.
*   Does not violate any other provision of this policy (e.g., accessing illegal or explicit content).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Users** | Read, understand, and sign the AUP acknowledgment. Adhere to all policy statements defined herein. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Maintain this policy, monitor systems for compliance, and investigate potential violations. |
| **Management / Board** | Ensure their teams/committees are aware of this policy and model appropriate behavior. |
| **HR / Volunteer Coordinator** | Ensure the AUP is signed during the onboarding process for all new employees and volunteers. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (per **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**), or termination of volunteer assignment.

Legal action may be taken for violations involving illegal activities, theft of intellectual property, or breach of confidentiality. The Organization will cooperate fully with law enforcement regarding illegal activities conducted through its systems.
