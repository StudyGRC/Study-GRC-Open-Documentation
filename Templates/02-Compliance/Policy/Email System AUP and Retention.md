### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards for the acceptable use of Study GRC Inc.'s electronic mail (email) systems and to define the mandatory retention periods for email records. This policy ensures that the Organization protects its reputation, secures sensitive data, and complies with record-keeping requirements mandated by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC), and 2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance).

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, Board of Directors, volunteers, contractors, and third parties ("Users") who are issued a Study GRC Inc. email address (e.g., `name@studygrc.org`) or have access to the Organization’s email infrastructure.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to personal email accounts (e.g., Gmail, Yahoo) owned by Users, except to the extent that such accounts are prohibited from being used for official Organization business.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Business Record** | An email that documents a business transaction, decision, policy, or official activity. These records have legal, fiscal, or administrative value and must be retained according to the Records Retention Schedule. |
| **Transitory Record** | An email of temporary value that is not required to document the Organization's business (e.g., meeting confirmations, casual chatter, "thanks" messages). |
| **Phishing** | A cybercrime in which a target is contacted by email by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data. |
| **Legal Hold** | A directive to preserve all forms of relevant information, including email, when litigation is reasonably anticipated. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Acceptable Use
Users are provided with email accounts to facilitate the effective performance of their duties.
4.1.1 **Professionalism:** All emails sent from an Organization account must adhere to the **Code of Ethics and Conduct**. Users must communicate professionally and respectfully.
4.1.2 **Identity:** Users must not misrepresent their identity or affiliation in email communications.
4.1.3 **Incidental Personal Use:** Limited, incidental personal use of email is permitted provided it does not interfere with work duties, consume excessive resources, or violate the Prohibited Conduct section.

#### 4.2 Prohibited Conduct
The following activities are strictly prohibited on Study GRC Inc. email systems:
4.2.1 **Harassment:** Sending content that is offensive, harassing, discriminatory, or creates a hostile work environment based on race, gender, age, disability, or any other protected status.
4.2.2 **Solicitation:** Using email for commercial ventures, religious or political causes, or outside organizations not affiliated with Study GRC Inc.
4.2.3 **Auto-Forwarding:** Configuring automatic forwarding of Organization emails to external personal accounts (e.g., auto-forwarding to a personal Gmail address) is prohibited due to data leakage risks.
4.2.4 **Sensitive Data Transmission:** Sending unencrypted PII, financial data, or sensitive youth information (per **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy**) to external parties without using approved encryption methods.
4.2.5 **Chain Letters:** Distributing chain letters, pyramid schemes, or unauthorized mass mailings (spam).

#### 4.3 Security and Phishing
4.3.1 **Vigilance:** Users must exercise caution regarding attachments and links from unknown senders.
4.3.2 **Reporting:** Suspected phishing attempts or compromised accounts must be reported immediately to the IT/Security team via the designated reporting channel (e.g., `security@studygrc.org` or Slack `#security-alerts`).
4.3.3 **Account Access:** Users may not share their email passwords with anyone. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) must be enabled on all email accounts in accordance with **IS-POL-005 Access Control Policy**.

#### 4.4 Email Retention and Archiving
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article VIII (Records)** and **IRS Rev. Proc. 98-25**, the Organization must retain records that substantiate its tax-exempt status and financial operations.

4.4.1 **System of Record:** The email system is a delivery mechanism, not a permanent storage repository. Emails that constitute **Business Records** (e.g., grant approvals, contract negotiations, Board votes) must be saved to the appropriate central repository (e.g., SharePoint, Google Drive, Financial System) within 30 days of receipt.

4.4.2 **Retention Schedule:**
*   **Transitory Records:** Should be deleted by the User once they are no longer needed for reference (e.g., < 90 days).
*   **General Correspondence:** Retained in the archive system for **3 years**.
*   **Legal, Tax, and Governance Records:** Emails containing final Board minutes, tax filings, or legal agreements must be moved to permanent storage and retained **permanently**.
*   **Grant-Related Correspondence:** Must be retained for **3 years after the submission of the final expenditure report** (per 2 CFR 200.334).

4.4.3 **Automatic Deletion:** The IT Department reserves the right to implement automated retention policies that archive emails older than 3 years and permanently delete items from the "Deleted Items" and "Junk" folders after 30 days.

#### 4.5 Litigation Hold (Legal Hold)
4.5.1 **Suspension of Destruction:** In the event of a Legal Hold issued by the Board or Legal Counsel, all automated deletion and manual destruction of emails must cease immediately.
4.5.2 **Compliance:** Users subject to a Legal Hold must preserve all existing and new emails until notified officially that the hold is lifted. Failure to comply may result in "spoliation of evidence" charges against the Organization.

#### 4.6 No Expectation of Privacy
4.6.1 **Organization Property:** All email messages created, sent, or received via the Organization’s email system are the property of Study GRC Inc.
4.6.2 **Monitoring:** The Organization reserves the right to access, review, copy, and delete any email messages for legal, compliance, or operational reasons, without prior notice to the User, subject to approval by the Executive Director or Board President.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Users** | Use email professionally; identify and save Business Records to central storage; report security threats. |
| **CISO** | Configure spam filters, anti-phishing protocols, and MFA; monitor for security anomalies. |
| **IT Department** | Manage email infrastructure; implement automated retention/archiving rules; execute data retrieval requests. |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Issue Legal Hold notices; update retention schedules based on changing laws. |
| **Executive Director** | Authorize access to employee email accounts during investigations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment, and potential legal action depending on the severity of the violation.

**Specific to Retention:** The willful destruction of Business Records required for litigation or government audits is a criminal offense and will result in immediate termination and referral to law enforcement.
