### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to protect the reputation, brand integrity, and legal standing of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). As a remote-first, community-driven 501(c)(3) nonprofit, our digital presence is our primary operational environment. This policy establishes the rules for official organizational communications and sets standards for the professional use of social media by directors, officers, employees, and volunteers to ensure compliance with IRS regulations, youth protection standards, and intellectual property laws.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Personnel:** All members of the Board of Directors, Operational Officers, employees, contractors, and volunteers.
*   **Channels:**
    *   **Official Channels:** All social media accounts, websites, blogs, and press releases owned or operated by Study GRC Inc.
    *   **Personal Channels:** Personal social media accounts of Personnel when the individual identifies themselves as associated with the Organization or discusses Organization-related business.

**Exclusions:** Strictly personal communications that do not reference the Organization, its stakeholders, or the GRC industry, provided they do not violate the Code of Ethics or Harassment Policy.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Official Spokesperson** | An individual explicitly authorized by the Board or Executive Director to speak on behalf of the Organization to the media or public. |
| **Social Media** | Any online platform for public communication, including but not limited to LinkedIn, X (Twitter), Facebook, Instagram, Discord, Slack, TikTok, and personal blogs. |
| **Confidential Information** | Non-public data including donor lists, student/minor data, strategic plans, and proprietary code/content as defined in Bylaws Article IX. |
| **Political Campaign Intervention** | Any statement favoring or opposing a candidate for public office, which is strictly prohibited for 501(c)(3) organizations. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Official Organization Channels
4.1.1 **Ownership:** All social media accounts created for the Organization are the property of Study GRC Inc. Login credentials must be stored in the Organization’s secure password manager (e.g., 1Password/Bitwarden) and accessible to the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).
4.1.2 **Brand Consistency:** All content posted on Official Channels must adhere to the *Brand Identity and Style Guide*, utilizing correct logos, color palettes, and tone of voice.
4.1.3 **Accessibility:** To support our mission of inclusivity, all visual media posted on Official Channels must include descriptive Alt Text, and videos must include captions, in compliance with the *Digital Accessibility Policy*.

#### 4.2 Authorized Spokespersons
4.2.1 **Designation:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article V**, the Executive Director (CEO) and the President of the Board are the primary Official Spokespersons.
4.2.2 **Delegation:** The Executive Director may delegate authority to the **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** or specific subject matter experts for defined topics. No other volunteer or employee may respond to media inquiries on behalf of the Organization without prior approval.
4.2.3 **Media Inquiries:** All inquiries from news media, industry analysts, or legal entities must be immediately referred to the Executive Director.

#### 4.3 Personal Social Media Use
4.3.1 **Association:** Personnel who identify themselves as associated with Study GRC Inc. on their personal profiles (e.g., "Volunteer at Study GRC" in a LinkedIn bio) must act professionally. Their online behavior reflects on the Organization.
4.3.2 **Disclaimer Requirement:** When posting about industry-related topics or the Organization, Personnel must make it clear that their views are their own.
*   *Required Disclaimer:* "The views expressed here are my own and do not necessarily reflect the views of Study GRC Inc."
4.3.3 **Prohibition on "Official" Appearance:** Personnel may not use the Organization’s logo, letterhead, or trademarks on personal accounts in a manner that suggests the account is an official channel of the Organization.

#### 4.4 Prohibited Content
The following content is strictly prohibited on both Official Channels and Personal Channels (when associated with the Organization):
4.4.1 **Confidential Information:** Posting internal financial data, strategic plans, or sensitive donor information is a violation of the Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) and **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.2**.
4.4.2 **Youth Protection (Zero Tolerance):** Posting photographs, names, or identifiable information of minors (participants under 18) without explicit, written parental consent is strictly prohibited. This aligns with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*.
4.4.3 **Political Campaigning:** As a 501(c)(3) entity, the Organization is absolutely prohibited from endorsing or opposing candidates for public office. Personnel must not use Official Channels for political campaigning.
4.4.4 **Harassment and Hate Speech:** Content that bullies, intimidates, or discriminates against individuals based on race, gender, religion, or other protected classes is prohibited and constitutes grounds for immediate termination.

#### 4.5 Crisis Communications
4.5.1 **Silence Protocol:** In the event of a significant legal, operational, or reputational crisis, all non-authorized Personnel must refrain from posting about the situation.
4.5.2 **Central Command:** All communication during a crisis must be directed by the Crisis Response Team as defined in the *Crisis Communications Plan*.

#### 4.6 Intellectual Property
4.6.1 **Work Made for Hire:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, content created by Personnel for Official Channels is "work made for hire" and is the sole property of Study GRC Inc.
4.6.2 **Copyright Compliance:** Personnel must respect third-party intellectual property and may not post copyrighted material without permission or a valid "Fair Use" justification.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this policy; serves as the final authority on crisis communications strategies. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Serves as Chief Spokesperson; authorizes delegation of media relations; enforces policy compliance. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Manages day-to-day social media operations; monitors brand sentiment; ensures community guidelines are upheld. |
| **CISO** | Secures access to social media accounts; manages access controls and deprovisioning of terminated personnel. |
| **All Personnel** | Adhere to this policy; report potential violations or reputational risks to the DKG or Executive Director. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (pursuant to **Bylaws Article III, Section 3.6**), or termination of volunteer assignment.

Violations involving the unauthorized release of Confidential Information or Youth Data may result in legal action. Violations involving political campaigning on Official Channels may result in immediate dismissal to protect the Organization's tax-exempt status.
