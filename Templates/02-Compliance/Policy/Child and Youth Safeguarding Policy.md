### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish an absolute commitment to the safety, protection, and well-being of all minors participating in Study GRC Inc. programs. This policy mandates a zero-tolerance approach to abuse and defines the legal and ethical obligations of all personnel to prevent, recognize, and report child abuse and neglect in accordance with **Texas Family Code Chapter 261**. This policy serves to mitigate legal, reputational, and operational risks associated with youth-serving activities in both physical and digital environments.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals associated with Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization"), including but not limited to:
*   Members of the Board of Directors.
*   Officers (Operational and Board).
*   Employees (full-time, part-time, and temporary).
*   Volunteers (including Committee members and Mentors).
*   Independent Contractors and Consultants.

**Scope of Application:** This policy governs all interactions with minors, whether occurring in person (events, workshops) or via electronic means (Discord, Zoom, email, social media), consistent with the Organization's remote-first operating model.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minor / Child** | Any individual under the age of 18 years, as defined by Texas law. |
| **Abuse** | Mental, emotional, physical, or sexual injury to a child, or failure to prevent such injury, as defined in **Texas Family Code § 261.001**. |
| **Neglect** | The leaving of a child in a situation where the child would be exposed to a substantial risk of physical or mental harm, including the failure to provide necessary food, shelter, or medical care. |
| **Mandated Reporter** | Under Texas law, **any person** who has cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | A safety protocol requiring that a minimum of two authorized adults be present during any interaction or activity involving minors to ensure transparency and accountability. |
| **Authorized Adult** | An individual who has successfully completed a background check and mandatory youth protection training within the last 12 months. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Zero Tolerance
Study GRC Inc. maintains a **zero-tolerance policy** regarding child abuse, neglect, harassment, or exploitation. Any allegation of abuse will be treated with the utmost seriousness. Any individual found to have engaged in abusive behavior will face immediate termination of employment, volunteer status, or membership, and will be referred to law enforcement.

#### 4.2 Mandatory Reporting (Texas Family Code Chapter 261)
In accordance with **Texas Family Code § 261.101**, every individual associated with the Organization is a Mandated Reporter.

*   **4.2.1 Immediate Reporting Requirement:** Any person having cause to believe that a child's physical or mental health or welfare has been adversely affected by abuse or neglect **must immediately report** the suspicion to the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) via the Texas Abuse Hotline (1-800-252-5400) or [TxAbuseHotline.org](https://www.txabusehotline.org).
*   **4.2.2 Non-Delegation:** The duty to report is personal and **cannot be delegated**. Reporting the suspicion to a supervisor or the Board does **not** satisfy the legal obligation to report to DFPS or law enforcement.
*   **4.2.3 Internal Notification:** After reporting to the appropriate state authorities, the individual must notify the Executive Director or the President of the Board within 24 hours, unless the Executive Director or President is the subject of the report.
*   **4.2.4 Immunity and Protection:** The Organization strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who makes a good-faith report of suspected abuse, pursuant to the Whistleblower Protection Policy (GOV-POL-009).

#### 4.3 Two-Deep Leadership and Interaction Protocols
To protect both youth and adults, the Organization enforces strict interaction boundaries.

*   **4.3.1 Prohibition of One-on-One Contact:** Private, one-on-one interactions between an adult and a minor are strictly prohibited. This applies to physical spaces (closed doors) and digital spaces (Direct Messages/DMs).
*   **4.3.2 The Rule of Two:** A minimum of two Authorized Adults must be present during any Organization-sponsored activity involving minors.
*   **4.3.3 Physical Contact:** Physical contact with minors should be limited to that which is appropriate for a professional educational setting (e.g., a handshake or high-five). Inappropriate or lingering touch is prohibited.

#### 4.4 Digital Safety (Remote-First Context)
Given the Organization's focus on online education and community (Bylaws Art. 1.3), specific digital safeguards are mandatory.

*   **4.4.1 Private Messaging (DMs):** Adults must not initiate or respond to private Direct Messages (DMs) with minors on platforms such as Discord, Slack, or social media. All communication must occur in public channels where other Authorized Adults are present.
*   **4.4.2 Video Conferencing:**
    *   One-on-one video calls with minors are prohibited.
    *   Video sessions must be recorded or monitored by a second Authorized Adult.
    *   Backgrounds must be professional and neutral.
*   **4.4.3 Social Media:** Personnel are prohibited from "friending" or "following" program participants who are minors on personal social media accounts.

#### 4.5 Screening and Background Checks
Pursuant to the **Background Check Policy (HR-POL-024)**, the Organization must conduct due diligence on all individuals with access to minors.

*   **4.5.1 Mandatory Screening:** All staff and volunteers designated as having "Regular Access" to minors must undergo a criminal background check, including a check of the Sex Offender Registry, prior to beginning service.
*   **4.5.2 Disqualification:** Any individual with a conviction for a crime of violence, sexual offense, or crime against a child is permanently disqualified from service.

#### 4.6 Training and Education
*   **4.6.1 Initial Training:** All Authorized Adults must complete a state-approved Youth Protection Training course (e.g., Texas DSHS approved) regarding the identification and reporting of abuse prior to working with minors.
*   **4.6.2 Annual Refresher:** Training must be renewed annually.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Ensures the Organization maintains adequate insurance (General Liability/Abuse & Molestation) and reviews this policy annually. |
| **Executive Director** | Operationalizes this policy; ensures all staff/volunteers are screened and trained; serves as the primary internal point of contact for safety concerns. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Fosters a safe community culture; assists in monitoring online community channels for policy violations. |
| **All Personnel & Volunteers** | Adhere to the Code of Conduct; maintain Two-Deep Leadership; fulfill legal Mandatory Reporting obligations. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy is a violation of the Organization's Code of Ethics and Texas Law.

*   **Internal Discipline:** Violations will result in immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (per Bylaws Art. III, Sec 3.6), or revocation of membership.
*   **Legal Action:** Failure to report suspected abuse is a Class A Misdemeanor or a State Jail Felony in Texas. The Organization will cooperate fully with law enforcement in the prosecution of any individual who harms a child or fails to report abuse.
