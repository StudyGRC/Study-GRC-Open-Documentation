### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a mandatory framework of professional conduct, ethical behavior, and instructional integrity for all individuals delivering educational content on behalf of Study GRC Inc. As a 501(c)(3) non-profit dedicated to open-source knowledge and K-12 education, this Code ensures that our learning environment remains safe, inclusive, commercially neutral, and aligned with our mission to remove barriers to GRC knowledge.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals who design, deliver, facilitate, or assess educational content for the Organization.
*   **Applies to:** Volunteer Instructors, Contracted Educators, Guest Lecturers, Content Creators, and Mentors.
*   **Exclusions:** Administrative volunteers who do not interact with learners in an instructional capacity.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Instructor** | Any individual authorized by the Organization to deliver educational material, facilitate workshops, or mentor students, regardless of compensation status. |
| **Learner** | Any individual participating in Study GRC Inc. programs, including adult professionals and K-12 students. |
| **Vulnerable Population** | Individuals under the age of 18 (minors) or adults with cognitive disabilities requiring special safeguarding. |
| **Commercial Solicitation** | The act of promoting personal business services, consulting firms, or paid third-party products for personal financial gain during instructional time. |
| **Two-Deep Leadership** | A safety protocol requiring a minimum of two authorized adults to be present during any interaction with a minor to prevent abuse and false allegations. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Professional Competence and Integrity
Instructors must uphold the highest standards of the GRC profession.
*   **4.1.1 Accuracy:** Instructors must ensure that all information delivered is accurate, current, and reflective of accepted industry standards (e.g., NIST, ISO).
*   **4.1.2 Scope of Expertise:** Instructors must only teach topics within their demonstrated area of competence. If a question arises outside their expertise, they must acknowledge the limitation rather than providing speculative or incorrect information.
*   **4.1.3 Preparation:** Instructors are required to be fully prepared for all sessions, including testing technology and reviewing materials prior to the scheduled start time.

#### 4.2 Youth Protection and Safeguarding
Given the Organization’s focus on K-12 education, strict adherence to youth protection is mandatory.
*   **4.2.1 Two-Deep Leadership:** In accordance with the *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy*, Instructors are strictly prohibited from having one-on-one private interactions (physical or virtual) with a minor. A second authorized adult must always be present.
*   **4.2.2 Private Messaging:** Instructors must not use Direct Messages (DMs) or personal communication channels (social media, personal email, text) to communicate with minors. All communication must occur in public Organization channels or via official Organization email addresses with a parent/guardian copied.
*   **4.2.3 Mandatory Reporting:** Instructors are designated as mandatory reporters. Any suspicion of abuse or neglect must be reported immediately to the Executive Director and relevant state authorities as per Texas Family Code §261.101.

#### 4.3 Intellectual Property and Open Source Commitment
*   **4.3.1 Work Made for Hire:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, all educational materials (slides, handouts, code, recordings) created by the Instructor for the Organization are considered "work made for hire." Ownership of these materials vests exclusively in Study GRC Inc.
*   **4.3.2 Open Source Alignment:** Instructors acknowledge that materials will be released under open-source licenses to further the Organization's charitable mission.
*   **4.3.3 Third-Party IP:** Instructors must not use copyrighted materials (images, text, charts) from third parties without explicit written permission or a valid license. Plagiarism is grounds for immediate dismissal.

#### 4.4 Commercial Neutrality and Non-Solicitation
As a tax-exempt entity, the Organization must not serve as a marketing funnel for private interests.
*   **4.4.1 No Sales Pitches:** Instructors are prohibited from using instructional time to sell software, consulting services, or books.
*   **4.4.2 Vendor Neutrality:** Unless a course is explicitly designated as training for a specific tool, Instructors must remain vendor-neutral, discussing concepts rather than promoting specific commercial products.
*   **4.4.3 Recruitment:** Instructors may not use the classroom roster or learner contact information to recruit for employment or solicit clients.

#### 4.5 Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI)
*   **4.5.1 Zero Tolerance for Harassment:** Instructors must create a psychological safe space. Harassment, discrimination, or bullying based on race, gender, religion, age, disability, or sexual orientation is strictly prohibited.
*   **4.5.2 Inclusive Language:** Instructors must use inclusive language and respect the pronouns and identities of all Learners.
*   **4.5.3 Accessibility:** Instructors must cooperate with the Organization to ensure materials are accessible to Learners with disabilities (e.g., describing visual elements on slides, using high-contrast text).

#### 4.6 Conflict of Interest
*   **4.6.1 Disclosure:** Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.1**, Instructors must disclose any financial interest in products or companies discussed during their instruction.
*   **4.6.2 Transparency:** If an Instructor is employed by a vendor relevant to the subject matter, this relationship must be disclosed to Learners at the beginning of the course (e.g., "I am an employee of [Company], but I am teaching this concept neutrally").

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Instructor** | Adheres strictly to this Code; completes annual ethics and youth protection training; discloses conflicts of interest. |
| **VP of Education & Training** | Ensures all Instructors have signed this Code; reviews course materials for IP and commercial neutrality compliance. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Investigates complaints regarding Instructor behavior, harassment, or culture violations. |
| **Executive Director** | Acts as the final authority on disciplinary actions resulting from ethics violations; handles mandatory reporting to authorities. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination of the volunteer or contract assignment, permanent ban from the Organization, and legal action.
*   **Youth Protection Violations:** Any violation of Section 4.2 will result in immediate suspension pending investigation and potential referral to law enforcement.
*   **IP Violations:** Unauthorized use of third-party IP will result in the immediate removal of the infringing content and potential termination of the Instructor's engagement.
