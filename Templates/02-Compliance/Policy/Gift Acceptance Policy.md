### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Gift Acceptance Policy is to govern the acceptance of charitable contributions by Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy provides guidance to the Board of Directors, officers, and staff on which gifts can be accepted and how they should be handled. It ensures that all gifts align with the Organization’s charitable mission as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**, comply with IRS regulations, and do not expose the Organization to undue financial risk, legal liability, or reputational harm.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all forms of charitable contributions, including cash, tangible personal property, securities, real estate, and intangible rights, offered to the Organization by individuals, corporations, foundations, or government entities.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not govern the acceptance of government grants or contracts, which are subject to the *Grant Management Procedure* and *Procurement Policy*.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Unrestricted Gift** | A contribution given without any conditions or limitations on its use, allowing the Organization to use the funds for any purpose consistent with its mission. |
| **Restricted Gift** | A contribution designated by the donor for a specific purpose, project, or time period. |
| **In-Kind Contribution** | Non-cash donations of goods, services, or property (e.g., computer hardware, software licenses, consulting hours). |
| **Illiquid Asset** | Assets that cannot be easily converted into cash, such as real estate, closely held stock, or restricted securities. |
| **Gift Acceptance Committee (GAC)** | A sub-committee of the Finance Committee, or the Executive Committee acting in that capacity, responsible for reviewing non-standard gifts. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Guiding Principles
In accordance with **Bylaws Article I**, the Organization solicits and accepts gifts that are consistent with its mission to foster a community-driven GRC ecosystem and provide zero-cost resources.
*   **Mission Alignment:** The Organization will not accept gifts that obligate it to violate its core values, including its commitment to open-source principles (**Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**).
*   **No Private Benefit:** Per **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.4**, no gift will be accepted if it provides a private benefit or inurement to any Director, Officer, or private person, other than incidental recognition (e.g., naming rights).
*   **Conflict of Interest:** All gift acceptances must comply with the *Conflict of Interest Policy*.

#### 4.2 Authority to Accept Gifts
*   **Standard Gifts:** The Executive Director (CEO) and the Treasurer are authorized to accept standard gifts (Cash, Publicly Traded Securities, Checks) on behalf of the Organization.
*   **Non-Standard Gifts:** The following types of gifts require review by the Gift Acceptance Committee (GAC) and final approval by the Board of Directors:
    *   Real Estate.
    *   Closely held securities or business interests.
    *   Tangible personal property with an estimated value exceeding $5,000.
    *   Gifts with significant restrictions or "strings attached."
    *   Gifts that require the Organization to assume liability or ongoing maintenance costs.

#### 4.3 Criteria for Acceptance by Asset Type

##### 4.3.1 Cash and Cash Equivalents
Cash, checks, money orders, and online credit card payments are acceptable. Checks must be made payable to "Study GRC Inc."

##### 4.3.2 Publicly Traded Securities
The Organization accepts publicly traded securities. It is the general policy of the Organization to sell such securities immediately upon receipt to minimize market risk. The value of the gift is determined by the average of the high and low trading prices on the date the gift is received in the Organization’s brokerage account.

##### 4.3.3 Cryptocurrency
Cryptocurrency donations are accepted subject to the *Cryptocurrency Donation Policy (FIN-POL-060)*. Generally, all crypto assets will be liquidated into fiat currency (USD) immediately upon receipt to mitigate volatility.

##### 4.3.4 Tangible Personal Property (In-Kind)
The Organization will only accept in-kind gifts if the property can be used in the furtherance of the Organization’s mission or if it can be readily sold.
*   **Hardware/Software:** Must meet current technology standards and security requirements defined by the CISO.
*   **Valuation:** The Organization will not assign a monetary value to in-kind gifts for the donor. The donor is responsible for obtaining an appraisal for tax purposes (IRS Form 8283).

##### 4.3.5 Intellectual Property
Gifts of Intellectual Property (copyrights, patents, code) must be reviewed to ensure the Organization can legally accept the assignment.
*   **Open Source Mandate:** Donors must understand that per **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**, the Organization intends to make resources available as open-source. Gifts of IP requiring restrictive licensing that prohibits public distribution will generally be declined.

##### 4.3.6 Real Estate
Gifts of real estate may include developed property, undeveloped land, or gifts subject to a prior life interest. Due to the high risk, the GAC must require:
*   A Phase I Environmental Site Assessment.
*   A title search and title insurance policy.
*   A marketability review (how quickly can it be sold?).
*   A legal review of any zoning restrictions or liens.

#### 4.4 Restricted Gifts
The Organization encourages unrestricted gifts to provide maximum flexibility.
*   **Administrative Fee:** The Organization reserves the right to apply a standard administrative fee (up to 10% or as allowed by the donor/grantor) to restricted gifts to cover overhead and operational costs.
*   **Unfeasible Restrictions:** The Organization will not accept gifts with restrictions that are unlawful, discriminatory, unethical, or practically impossible to fulfill.
*   **Change of Circumstance:** The gift agreement for restricted gifts should contain a variance power clause, allowing the Board to re-designate the funds if the original purpose becomes obsolete or impossible to achieve.

#### 4.5 Unacceptable Gifts
The Organization reserves the right to refuse any gift. Gifts will be refused if:
1.  The gift is from a source that is inconsistent with the Organization’s ethical standards or could damage its reputation (e.g., sanctioned entities).
2.  The gift restricts the Organization’s control over its own content, curriculum, or governance.
3.  The cost of managing or liquidating the asset exceeds the value of the gift.
4.  The gift creates a conflict of interest for any Board member or Officer.

#### 4.6 Donor Privacy and Anonymity
The Organization respects the privacy of donors. Donors may request anonymity; however, the Organization must disclose donor information to the IRS and other government agencies where required by law (e.g., Schedule B of Form 990). The Organization will not sell or trade its donor list to other organizations.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Accepts standard gifts; refers non-standard gifts to the GAC; ensures donors receive proper acknowledgment. |
| **Treasurer / CFO** | Oversees the liquidation of securities/crypto; manages the financial recording of gifts; ensures compliance with IRS substantiation rules. |
| **Gift Acceptance Committee (GAC)** | Reviews non-standard gifts (Real Estate, complex IP, etc.) and makes recommendations to the Board. |
| **Board of Directors** | Has final authority to accept or decline any gift; approves changes to this policy. |
| **Donor** | Responsible for obtaining qualified appraisals for non-cash gifts >$5,000 and seeking their own tax/legal advice. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
*   **IRS Substantiation:** The Organization must provide a written acknowledgment for any single contribution of $250 or more, in compliance with IRS Section 170(f)(8).
*   **Form 8282:** If the Organization sells, exchanges, or disposes of donated property (other than money or publicly traded securities) within three years of receipt, it must file IRS Form 8282.
*   **Policy Violation:** Acceptance of a gift in violation of this policy may result in the return of the gift to the donor and disciplinary action against the responsible staff member or officer.
