### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this **Cybersecurity Governance Framework** is to establish the strategic direction, risk appetite, and oversight mechanisms for information security within Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy aligns with the **Govern (GV)** function of the **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0**, ensuring that cybersecurity risks are managed as a critical component of the Organization’s enterprise risk management strategy.

This framework fulfills the Board of Directors' fiduciary duty to protect the Organization's assets, reputation, and the privacy of its community—specifically the sensitive data associated with our K-12 youth programs and federal grant obligations.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the entire Organization, including:
*   **Personnel:** Board of Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors.
*   **Assets:** All information systems, networks, data (including donor data, youth participant data, and federal grant records), and intellectual property owned or leased by the Organization.
*   **Environment:** The Organization's remote-first operating environment, including cloud services (SaaS/IaaS) and Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) usage.

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Governance Structure and Authority
In accordance with **Bylaws Article III (Board of Directors)** and **Article V (Operational Leadership)**, the governance of cybersecurity shall follow a defined hierarchy to ensure accountability and separation of duties.

*   **4.1.1 Board Oversight:** The Board of Directors retains ultimate accountability for cybersecurity risk. The Board delegates detailed oversight to the **Audit and Risk Committee** (established under Bylaws Article VI), which must review the cybersecurity program status at least semi-annually.
*   **4.1.2 Executive Execution:** The **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** is granted the authority to implement this framework. The CISO reports operationally to the Executive Director (CEO) but retains a direct reporting line to the Board of Directors for matters of significant risk or ethical conflict.
*   **4.1.3 Independence:** To prevent conflicts of interest, the CISO function must remain independent from the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and the VP of Education & Training, ensuring security controls are not bypassed for financial or speed-to-market reasons.

#### 4.2 Organizational Risk Context and Appetite
The Organization adopts a risk-based approach to security, acknowledging that zero risk is unattainable. However, specific risk thresholds are established based on data sensitivity:

*   **4.2.1 Youth Data (Zero Tolerance):** The Organization maintains a **zero-tolerance** risk appetite for the unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of data regarding minors (under 18). Controls for this category must be prioritized above operational convenience.
*   **4.2.2 Federal Grant Data (Low Tolerance):** In compliance with **2 CFR 200** and federal award terms, the Organization maintains a **low** risk appetite for non-compliance or data loss regarding federal funds and associated reporting.
*   **4.2.3 Public Educational Content (Moderate Tolerance):** The Organization accepts a **moderate** risk appetite regarding the availability of open-source educational materials, provided that integrity (accuracy) is maintained.

#### 4.3 Cybersecurity Strategy Alignment
The cybersecurity strategy must align with the Organization's mission to foster a GRC community.

*   **4.3.1 Integration:** Cybersecurity requirements must be integrated into the strategic planning process, budget cycles, and new project initiations (e.g., new curriculum platforms or community events).
*   **4.3.2 Remote-First Posture:** Governance decisions must prioritize the security of a distributed workforce. Policies must assume a "Zero Trust" architecture where the network perimeter is identity-based, not physical.

#### 4.4 Policy Management and Standardization
*   **4.4.1 Framework Adoption:** The Organization formally adopts **NIST CSF 2.0** as its control framework. All sub-policies and standards (e.g., Access Control, Incident Response) must map to NIST CSF categories.
*   **4.4.2 Policy Lifecycle:** All cybersecurity policies must be reviewed annually. Material changes to Level 1 Policies require Board approval; Level 2 Standards and Procedures may be approved by the CISO or Executive Director.

#### 4.5 Supply Chain Risk Management (SCRM)
Recognizing the Organization's reliance on third-party SaaS providers:
*   **4.5.1 Vendor Due Diligence:** No third-party service provider may be engaged to process Sensitive Personal Data or Federal Grant Data without a completed Vendor Security Risk Assessment (VSRA).
*   **4.5.2 Contractual Protections:** All vendor contracts must include provisions for data confidentiality, breach notification, and right-to-audit, consistent with **Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)**.

#### 4.6 Performance Measurement and Reporting
*   **4.6.1 Metrics:** The CISO must define and track Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) to measure the effectiveness of the security program (e.g., Mean Time to Detect, Patching Cadence, Phishing Susceptibility).
*   **4.6.2 Board Reporting:** The CISO must present a "State of Security" report to the Board or Audit Committee no less than twice per fiscal year. This report must include:
    *   Current risk profile against the Risk Appetite.
    *   Summary of significant incidents.
    *   Status of compliance with federal regulations (e.g., 2 CFR 200).
    *   Resource adequacy.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Cybersecurity Governance Framework and Risk Appetite. Ensures adequate funding for the security program. |
| **Audit & Risk Committee** | Provides detailed oversight of the CISO and the security program. Reviews independent audit results. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Ensures the CISO has the authority and resources required to execute the program. Champions security culture across the organization. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Develops strategy, enforces policies, manages incidents, and reports on risk to the Board. Acts as the "Document Owner" for all IS policies. |
| **Department Heads** | Ensure their respective departments (Education, Finance, Ops) comply with security policies and participate in risk assessments. |
| **All Staff/Volunteers** | Adhere to Acceptable Use Policies and report suspected security incidents immediately. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from volunteer service, or termination of vendor contracts.

Legal action may be pursued in cases of malicious conduct, theft of intellectual property (violating **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.3**), or gross negligence resulting in a data breach.
