### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and procedures necessary for Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") to comply with the audit requirements of the Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards (2 CFR 200, Subpart F). This policy ensures the Organization accurately monitors federal expenditure thresholds, engages qualified independent auditors, and resolves audit findings to maintain eligibility for federal funding and tax-exempt status.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to the Board of Directors, the Audit Committee, the Chief Financial Officer (CFO), and all personnel involved in the administration, management, and financial reporting of federal awards.

**Inclusions:**
*   All federal financial assistance received directly from federal agencies.
*   All federal financial assistance received as a subrecipient through Pass-Through Entities (PTEs).

**Exclusions:**
*   Vendor relationships where the Organization provides goods or services on a commercial basis, which are not subject to Subpart F audit requirements (though other contract terms apply).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Single Audit** | An audit that includes both the entity's financial statements and the federal awards, required when federal expenditures exceed the statutory threshold. |
| **SEFA** | Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards. A financial schedule prepared by management listing total federal expenditures for the fiscal year. |
| **Uniform Guidance** | 2 CFR Part 200, specifically Subpart F regarding Audit Requirements. |
| **Cognizant Agency** | The federal agency that provides the predominant amount of direct funding to the Organization, responsible for audit oversight. |
| **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** | A document addressing each audit finding, stating the corrective action to be taken, the responsible person, and the anticipated completion date. |
| **ALN** | Assistance Listing Number (formerly CFDA). A unique number assigned to each federal program. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Audit Threshold Monitoring
In accordance with 2 CFR § 200.501, the Organization must conduct a Single Audit or Program-Specific Audit for any fiscal year in which it expends **$750,000 or more** in federal awards.

4.1.1 **Monthly Monitoring:** The CFO must monitor federal grant expenditures on a monthly basis.
4.1.2 **Year-End Determination:** Within 30 days of the fiscal year-end (December 31, per Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3), the CFO must formally certify whether the $750,000 threshold was met.
4.1.3 **Lower Thresholds:** If expenditures are below $750,000, the Organization is exempt from federal audit requirements for that year, but records must be available for review or audit by appropriate officials of the federal agency, pass-through entity, and Government Accountability Office (GAO).

#### 4.2 Auditor Selection and Procurement
If the threshold is met, the Organization must select an independent auditor in accordance with the procurement standards defined in 2 CFR § 200.509 and the Organization's *Procurement Policy (FIN-POL-010)*.

4.2.1 **Independence:** The auditor must meet the independence standards of the Government Auditing Standards (GAGAS/Yellow Book).
4.2.2 **Peer Review:** The Organization must request and review a copy of the audit firm’s most recent external quality control review (peer review) report.
4.2.3 **Rotation:** While mandatory rotation of the audit firm is not required by federal law, the Board of Directors shall review the engagement every five (5) years to ensure objectivity.

#### 4.3 Auditee Responsibilities
The Organization, led by the CFO, must perform the following duties to facilitate the audit:

4.3.1 **Financial Statements:** Prepare financial statements that reflect its financial position, results of operations, and cash flows for the fiscal year, in accordance with GAAP.
4.3.2 **Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA):** The CFO must prepare the SEFA for the period covered by the audit. Per 2 CFR § 200.510(b), the SEFA must include:
*   List of individual federal programs by federal agency.
*   Name of the Pass-Through Entity (if applicable) and the identifying number assigned by the PTE.
*   Total federal awards expended for each individual federal program and the ALN.
*   Total amount provided to subrecipients from each federal program.
*   Notes that describe the significant accounting policies used in preparing the SEFA.

4.3.3 **Internal Controls:** The Organization must maintain internal control over federal programs that provides reasonable assurance that the Organization is managing federal awards in compliance with statutes, regulations, and the terms and conditions of the federal award (refer to *Internal Controls Framework FIN-POL-005*).

#### 4.4 Audit Submission and Reporting
4.4.1 **Deadline:** The audit package and the Data Collection Form (SF-SAC) must be submitted to the Federal Audit Clearinghouse (FAC) within the earlier of:
*   30 calendar days after receipt of the auditor's report(s); or
*   Nine (9) months after the end of the audit period (September 30th).

4.4.2 **Public Transparency:** In alignment with Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.2, the audited financial statements and Form 990 shall be made publicly available on the Organization’s transparency webpage upon final acceptance by the Board.

#### 4.5 Audit Findings and Corrective Action
If the auditor identifies findings (material weaknesses, significant deficiencies, or instances of non-compliance):

4.5.1 **Corrective Action Plan (CAP):** The CFO must draft a CAP in accordance with 2 CFR § 200.511(c). The CAP must:
*   Address each finding included in the current year auditor's reports.
*   Provide the name(s) of the contact person(s) responsible for corrective action.
*   Indicate the corrective action planned and the anticipated completion date.
*   If the Organization disagrees with a finding, the CAP must include an explanation and specific reasons for the disagreement.

4.5.2 **Summary Schedule of Prior Audit Findings:** The CFO must prepare a summary schedule of prior audit findings showing the status of findings included in the prior audit's schedule.

#### 4.6 Access to Records
The Organization must provide the auditor with access to all personnel, accounts, books, records, supporting documentation, and other information as needed. Given the Organization's remote-first nature, the CFO must ensure all digital records are accessible via secure, read-only access for the auditors.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors (Audit Committee)** | Selects the independent auditor; reviews and approves the final audit report; oversees the implementation of the Corrective Action Plan. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Signs the management representation letter; ensures organizational cooperation with auditors; reports material risks to the Board. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Primary audit liaison; prepares Financial Statements and SEFA; monitors expenditure thresholds; drafts the CAP; submits reporting to the FAC. |
| **Program Managers** | Provide accurate grant expenditure data, subrecipient details, and programmatic compliance evidence to the CFO for SEFA preparation. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in the suspension or termination of federal funding, designation as a "high-risk" auditee, and potential legal action.

Internal violations of this policy (e.g., failure to prepare the SEFA, failure to cooperate with auditors) may result in disciplinary action against employees, up to and including termination of employment.
