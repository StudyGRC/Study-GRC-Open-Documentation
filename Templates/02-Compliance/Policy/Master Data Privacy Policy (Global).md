### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Master Data Privacy Policy is to establish the governing framework for how Study GRC Inc. ("the Organization") collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects Personal Data. As a remote-first, global non-profit organization, we are committed to the highest standards of data privacy. This policy ensures compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), while aligning with our Bylaws regarding transparency and member rights.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all "Personal Data" processed by the Organization, regardless of the media on which that data is stored.

*   **Applies to:** All Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, contractors, and third-party vendors acting on behalf of the Organization. It covers data related to members, donors, learners, event participants, and website visitors.
*   **Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to:
    *   Data that has been irreversibly anonymized or aggregated such that it can no longer identify a specific individual.
    *   Internal operational data that does not contain Personal Data (e.g., server logs without IP addresses).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Personal Data** | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("Data Subject"). This includes names, emails, IP addresses, and biometric data. Under TDPSA/CCPA, this includes data linked to a household or device. |
| **Sensitive Data** | A subset of Personal Data requiring higher protection, including racial/ethnic origin, religious beliefs, health data, biometric data, and data of known children (under 13). |
| **Processing** | Any operation performed on Personal Data, such as collection, recording, storage, adaptation, retrieval, use, disclosure, or destruction. |
| **Data Controller** | The entity that determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data (Study GRC Inc.). |
| **Data Processor** | An entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller (e.g., cloud service providers, payroll vendors). |
| **Consent** | A freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of the Data Subject's wishes. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Data Protection Principles
In accordance with GDPR Article 5 and global best practices, Study GRC Inc. adheres to the following principles:
1.  **Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency:** Data is processed lawfully and transparently.
2.  **Purpose Limitation:** Data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes (e.g., membership administration, education delivery) and not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes.
3.  **Data Minimization:** We collect only what is necessary.
4.  **Accuracy:** We take reasonable steps to ensure data is accurate and kept up to date.
5.  **Storage Limitation:** Data is kept only for as long as necessary (See *Records Retention Schedule*).
6.  **Integrity and Confidentiality:** Data is processed securely to prevent unauthorized access, loss, or damage.

#### 4.2 Lawful Basis for Processing
The Organization shall only process Personal Data when one of the following applies:
*   **Consent:** The individual has given clear consent (e.g., newsletter signup).
*   **Contract:** Processing is necessary for a contract (e.g., Membership Agreement, Bylaws Article II).
*   **Legal Obligation:** Processing is necessary to comply with the law (e.g., IRS reporting, Texas BOC record keeping).
*   **Legitimate Interests:** Processing is necessary for our legitimate interests (e.g., network security, fraud prevention), provided these are not overridden by the individual's rights.

#### 4.3 Data Subject Rights
Study GRC Inc. guarantees the following rights to all individuals, regardless of their geographic location (harmonizing GDPR, TDPSA, and CCPA rights):
1.  **Right to Access:** Individuals may request copies of their Personal Data.
2.  **Right to Rectification:** Individuals may request correction of inaccurate data.
3.  **Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"):** Individuals may request deletion of their data, subject to legal retention requirements (e.g., tax records).
4.  **Right to Restrict Processing:** Individuals may limit how we use their data.
5.  **Right to Data Portability:** Individuals may request their data in a structured, machine-readable format.
6.  **Right to Opt-Out:** Individuals may opt-out of the sale of personal data, targeted advertising, or profiling. **Note:** Study GRC Inc. does not sell data for monetary value.

#### 4.4 Children’s Privacy (Youth Protection)
Given the Organization's K-12 educational focus:
*   We strictly adhere to the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and GDPR-K.
*   **Under 13:** We do not knowingly collect Personal Data from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent.
*   **13-16:** We prohibit the sale or sharing of data for targeted advertising for users known to be between 13 and 16 years of age.
*   See *Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy* for detailed procedures.

#### 4.5 Member Records and Inspection
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article II, Section 2.6** and Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) § 22.351:
*   Voting Members have the right to inspect the membership list.
*   To protect privacy, the Organization requires a written demand stating a proper purpose.
*   **Prohibition:** Member data obtained via inspection **must not** be used for advertising, commercial solicitation, or marketing. Violations will result in immediate termination of membership and potential legal action.

#### 4.6 International Data Transfers
As a remote-first organization with a global community:
*   Personal Data may be transferred to, and processed in, the United States.
*   For transfers from the European Economic Area (EEA) to the US, we rely on the Data Privacy Framework (if applicable) or Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) to ensure adequate protection.

#### 4.7 Data Breach Notification
In the event of a Personal Data breach:
*   **Authority Notification:** We will notify relevant supervisory authorities (e.g., Texas Attorney General, EU DPAs) within **72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach, where feasible, if the breach is likely to result in a risk to rights and freedoms.
*   **Individual Notification:** We will notify affected individuals without undue delay if there is a high risk to their rights and freedoms, or as required by Texas Business & Commerce Code § 521.053.

#### 4.8 Vendor Management
The Organization shall only engage third-party vendors (Processors) that provide sufficient guarantees to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures. All vendors processing Personal Data must execute a Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this policy; ensures adequate resources for compliance; oversees high-level privacy risks. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Accountable for organizational compliance; authorizes Data Processing Agreements with vendors. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Acts as the Data Protection Officer (DPO); manages DSARs (Data Subject Access Requests); conducts Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs); manages breach response. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Ensures community outreach and member communications comply with consent and opt-out preferences. |
| **All Staff & Volunteers** | Must complete annual privacy training; report suspected breaches immediately; adhere to the "Clean Desk/Clear Screen" policy. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (for Cause per Bylaws Article III), or termination of volunteer assignment.

Civil and criminal penalties may also apply under GDPR, TDPSA, or CCPA for willful violations of data privacy laws.
