### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines for the accrual, usage, and management of Paid Time Off (PTO) for employees of Study GRC Inc. This policy is designed to promote work-life balance, support employee mental and physical health, and serve as a strategic retention tool. Furthermore, this policy ensures compliance with Texas Labor Code regarding wage payments and 2 CFR § 200.431 regarding the allowability of fringe benefits for federal grant recipients.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all regular, full-time employees of Study GRC Inc. working a minimum of 30 hours per week.

**Exclusions:**
*   Part-time employees working fewer than 30 hours per week (unless specified in their employment contract).
*   Temporary employees, interns, and seasonal staff.
*   Independent Contractors (1099 status).
*   Volunteers and Board Directors (uncompensated).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **PTO (Paid Time Off)** | An all-purpose bank of paid leave that can be used for vacation, personal illness, family care, mental health days, or personal business. |
| **Accrual** | The process of earning PTO hours over time based on hours worked or pay periods completed. |
| **Carryover** | The amount of unused PTO that may be transferred from one fiscal year to the next. |
| **Separation** | The termination of the employment relationship, whether voluntary (resignation/retirement) or involuntary (termination). |
| **Fiscal Year** | The calendar year (January 1 – December 31), as defined in Bylaws Article VIII, Section 8.3. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Accrual Methodology
Study GRC Inc. utilizes an accrual-based system to ensure financial liability is accurately tracked in accordance with GAAP and federal grant requirements.
*   **4.1.1 Start Date:** Accruals begin on the employee’s first day of employment.
*   **4.1.2 Availability:** Employees may begin using accrued PTO after completing thirty (30) days of employment.
*   **4.1.3 Accrual Rates:** To encourage retention, accrual rates increase with tenure:

| Tenure | Annual Accrual | Accrual per Pay Period (Bi-Weekly) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0 – 2 Years** | 15 Days (120 Hours) | ~4.62 Hours |
| **2 – 5 Years** | 20 Days (160 Hours) | ~6.15 Hours |
| **5+ Years** | 25 Days (200 Hours) | ~7.69 Hours |

*   **4.1.4 Non-Accrual Status:** PTO does not accrue during unpaid leaves of absence or while an employee is on long-term disability.

#### 4.2. Usage and Scheduling
*   **4.2.1 Increments:** PTO must be used in minimum increments of one (1) hour.
*   **4.2.2 Request Protocol:**
    *   **Planned Leave:** Requests for 3 or more consecutive days must be submitted to the direct manager at least two (2) weeks in advance.
    *   **Unplanned Leave:** In cases of illness or emergency, employees must notify their manager by 9:00 AM (local time) on the day of absence via the designated communication channel (e.g., Slack/Email).
*   **4.2.3 Approval:** All PTO requests are subject to manager approval based on business needs, team coverage, and project deadlines.
*   **4.2.4 Blackout Dates:** The Organization reserves the right to designate "blackout periods" during critical operational events (e.g., Annual Conference, Board Elections).

#### 4.3. Carryover and Cap
*   **4.3.1 Carryover Limit:** Employees may carry over a maximum of forty (40) hours of unused PTO into the next fiscal year.
*   **4.3.2 Forfeiture:** Any unused PTO in excess of 40 hours remaining on December 31st will be forfeited ("Use it or Lose it"), consistent with Texas law.
*   **4.3.3 Total Cap:** The total PTO balance may never exceed 1.5x the employee's annual accrual rate. Once this cap is reached, accruals cease until the balance is reduced.

#### 4.4. Separation and Payout
Pursuant to the Texas Payday Law, an employer is not required to pay out accrued PTO upon separation unless a written policy promises it. Study GRC Inc. adopts the following payout structure:
*   **4.4.1 Voluntary Resignation:** Employees who resign and provide at least two (2) weeks' written notice **and** work through the notice period will be paid for accrued, unused PTO, up to a maximum of eighty (80) hours.
*   **4.4.2 Involuntary Termination:** Employees terminated for cause (misconduct, violation of policy, etc.) are **not** eligible for PTO payout.
*   **4.4.3 Failure to Notice:** Employees who resign without providing two weeks' notice forfeit all accrued PTO.
*   **4.4.4 Grant Funding Restrictions:** If an employee's salary is 100% funded by a restricted grant that prohibits leave payouts upon termination, the grant terms supersede this policy.

#### 4.5. Federal Grant Compliance (2 CFR 200)
*   **4.5.1 Allowable Cost:** PTO is considered an allowable fringe benefit cost under 2 CFR § 200.431, provided it is reasonable and established in written policy.
*   **4.5.2 Equitable Allocation:** PTO costs are allocated equitably to all activities (grant and non-grant) based on the employee's salary distribution.
*   **4.5.3 Recording:** PTO taken must be accurately recorded on Time and Effort reports or Personnel Activity Reports (PARs) as required by HR-POL-046 (Time and Effort Reporting Policy).

#### 4.6. Borrowing PTO
Employees are not permitted to "borrow" PTO against future accruals (i.e., maintain a negative balance) without express written approval from the Executive Director.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Monitors own PTO balance; submits requests in a timely manner; ensures coverage for tasks during absence. |
| **Manager** | Reviews and approves/denies requests based on operational capacity; ensures team coverage; monitors for burnout. |
| **Payroll/HR** | Calculates accruals; maintains accurate records of balances; processes payouts upon separation in compliance with this policy. |
| **Executive Director** | Adjudicates exceptions to the policy; approves negative balance requests. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy, including falsification of time records or abuse of the PTO system (e.g., a pattern of calling in sick on Mondays/Fridays without medical documentation), may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
