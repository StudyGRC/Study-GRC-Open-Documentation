### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and protocols for the management of Study GRC Inc.’s official social media channels. As a community-driven 501(c)(3) organization, our digital presence is a primary vehicle for fulfilling our mission to foster a GRC ecosystem. This policy mitigates brand risk, ensures compliance with IRS restrictions on political activity, protects the privacy of our youth participants, and maintains the integrity of our open-source resources.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, Directors, Officers, volunteers, and contractors ("Authorized Users") who are granted access to manage, moderate, or post content on Official Organization Accounts.

**Inclusions:**
*   Social networking sites (e.g., LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Facebook, Instagram).
*   Video hosting platforms (e.g., YouTube, TikTok).
*   Community platforms (e.g., Discord, Slack, Reddit) when acting as an administrator or moderator.

**Exclusions:**
*   Personal social media accounts of staff or volunteers, provided they do not claim to speak on behalf of the Organization (covered separately in the *Employee Handbook*).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Official Organization Account** | Any social media account, page, or group created in the name of Study GRC Inc. or its programs, owned by the Organization. |
| **Authorized User** | An individual explicitly designated by the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG) to post, comment, or moderate on behalf of the Organization. |
| **Political Campaign Intervention** | Any statement favoring or opposing a candidate for public office, which is strictly prohibited for 501(c)(3) entities. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information (e.g., full names, addresses, photos of faces) that can identify an individual. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Account Authority and Security
4.1.1 **Ownership:** All social media accounts created for Study GRC Inc. are the exclusive property of the Organization. Login credentials must be registered using an organizational email address (e.g., `@studygrc.org`), never a personal email address.
4.1.2 **Access Control:** Access to Official Organization Accounts is restricted to Authorized Users.
4.1.3 **Security Protocols:**
*   **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** MFA must be enabled on all social media platforms where available, in accordance with *IS-POL-015 (MFA Mandate)*.
*   **Password Management:** Passwords must be unique, complex, and stored only in the Organization’s approved enterprise password manager. Sharing passwords via chat (Slack/Teams) or email is prohibited.
*   **Device Security:** Authorized Users must only access accounts from devices that comply with *IS-POL-022 (Bring Your Own Device Policy)*.

#### 4.2 Content Standards and Brand Voice
4.2.1 **Mission Alignment:** All content must advance the Organization’s purpose as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**: fostering GRC knowledge, removing financial barriers, and empowering learners.
4.2.2 **Tone of Kindness:** In alignment with the role of the Director of Kindness and Generosity, all interactions must be professional, inclusive, and constructive. Snark, hostility, or aggressive debate tactics are strictly prohibited.
4.2.3 **Accuracy:** Authorized Users must verify the accuracy of technical GRC information before posting. Misinformation must be corrected immediately and transparently upon discovery.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Content and Legal Restrictions
4.3.1 **Political Activity (Zero Tolerance):** As a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt entity, the Organization **must not** endorse or oppose any candidate for public office. Authorized Users are prohibited from posting, sharing, or "liking" partisan political content from Official Organization Accounts.
4.3.2 **Intellectual Property:**
*   **Respect for IP:** Content must not infringe on third-party copyrights or trademarks. Images must be stock (licensed), Creative Commons (with attribution), or original.
*   **Open Source Attribution:** When sharing community resources, proper credit must be given to the creator/contributor, aligning with our open-source values.
4.3.3 **Confidentiality:** No content may disclose confidential information, trade secrets, or internal Board deliberations (per **Bylaws Article IX, Section 9.2**).

#### 4.4 Youth Protection and Privacy
4.4.1 **Images of Minors:** Photos or videos of individuals under the age of 18 must **never** be posted without a signed *Parental Consent and Liability Waiver* on file.
4.4.2 **Identifiable Information:** The full names or locations of minor participants must never be published on public social media channels.
4.4.3 **Direct Messaging:** Authorized Users are prohibited from engaging in private Direct Messages (DMs) with minors through Official Organization Accounts. All communication with youth must occur in public channels or via official organizational email with a copy to a second adult (Two-Deep Leadership).

#### 4.5 Crisis Management
4.5.1 **Pause Protocol:** In the event of a significant national emergency, tragedy, or organizational crisis, all scheduled posts must be paused immediately.
4.5.2 **Escalation:** Negative viral trends, threats of violence, or legal threats received via social media must be reported immediately to the Executive Director and the CISO. Authorized Users must not engage in "flame wars" or defensive arguments.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG)** | Owner of this policy. Approves content strategy, designates Authorized Users, and monitors brand tone. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Enforces MFA and password security standards for all social accounts. Audits access logs. |
| **Executive Director** | Final authority on crisis communications and political neutrality interpretations. |
| **Authorized Users** | Adhere to this policy, draft compliant content, and secure their access credentials. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from volunteer service, and revocation of system access.

**Specific Violations:**
*   Posting partisan political content may result in immediate termination due to the risk posed to the Organization’s tax-exempt status.
*   Posting images of minors without consent will result in immediate suspension pending investigation.
