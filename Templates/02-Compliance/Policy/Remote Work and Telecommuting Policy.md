### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the standards, expectations, and liabilities associated with the "Remote-First" operating model of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). As a Texas Nonprofit Corporation operating without a central physical headquarters, this policy establishes the framework for:
1.  Ensuring business continuity and productivity in a distributed environment.
2.  Defining the boundaries of the "workplace" for OSHA compliance and Workers' Compensation purposes.
3.  Protecting organizational data outside of a controlled perimeter.
4.  Clarifying equipment ownership and expense reimbursement protocols.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, officers, and long-term contractors of the Organization.
*   **Inclusions:** Full-time employees, part-time employees, and stipend-based volunteers (e.g., Operational Officers defined in Bylaws Article V).
*   **Exclusions:** Short-term vendors or third-party service providers who do not access internal systems or possess Organization-issued hardware.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Remote-First** | An organizational model where the primary workflow, communication, and culture are designed for off-site participation, rather than retrofitted from an office-based model. |
| **Designated Workspace** | A specific, defined area within the employee's residence or remote location used for performing work duties. |
| **Core Hours** | Specific blocks of time during which an employee must be available for synchronous communication (meetings, Slack, phone). |
| **VPN** | Virtual Private Network; a secure tunnel used to access Organization resources. |
| **Approved Jurisdiction** | A state or country where the Organization is legally registered to employ staff (payroll tax nexus). |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Remote-First Operating Model
Study GRC Inc. operates primarily as a remote entity. Remote work is not a benefit or perk but a condition of employment for most roles. However, the Organization reserves the right to require physical attendance at specific events, annual retreats, or board meetings as defined in **Bylaws Article II (Meetings)** and **Article III (Board Governance)**.

#### 4.2 Jurisdictional Limitations
Employees may only work from an **Approved Jurisdiction**.
4.2.1 **Residency:** Employees must maintain their primary residence in a state where the Organization has established a payroll tax presence (currently: Texas).
4.2.2 **Relocation:** Employees must obtain written approval from the Executive Director *prior* to relocating to a different state or country. Failure to do so may result in termination due to tax and legal non-compliance.

#### 4.3 Workspace Safety and OSHA Compliance
In accordance with **OSHA Directive CPL 2-2.44D**, the Organization is not liable for the safety of an employee's home. However, to ensure a safe working environment and manage Workers' Compensation liability:
4.3.1 **Designated Workspace:** Employees must designate a specific area in their home as their workspace. This area must be kept free of recognized hazards (e.g., exposed wiring, tripping hazards).
4.3.2 **Self-Certification:** Employees must complete a *Remote Worksite Safety Self-Assessment* annually.
4.3.3 **Injuries:** Any injury that occurs within the Designated Workspace during working hours must be reported to the Manager and HR within 24 hours. The Organization is not liable for injuries to third parties (family members, visitors) in the employee's home.
4.3.4 **Right to Inspect:** While the Organization respects employee privacy, it reserves the right to inspect the Designated Workspace (virtually or in-person) to investigate a workplace injury or ensure security compliance, with 24-hour notice.

#### 4.4 Hours of Work and Availability
4.4.1 **Core Hours:** Unless otherwise specified in an employment contract, employees are expected to be available for synchronous collaboration between **10:00 AM and 2:00 PM Central Time**, Monday through Friday.
4.4.2 **Responsiveness:** During agreed-upon working hours, employees must respond to internal communications (Slack/Email) within a reasonable timeframe (generally 2 hours).
4.4.3 **Overtime (Non-Exempt):** Non-exempt employees must strictly record all hours worked. Working "off the clock" is prohibited. Overtime must be pre-approved in writing by a manager.

#### 4.5 Dependent Care
Remote work is not a substitute for childcare or dependent care.
4.5.1 **Focus:** Employees must ensure that dependent care arrangements are in place during working hours to allow for focused work.
4.5.2 **Flexibility:** While the Organization supports flexible scheduling to accommodate family needs, active caregiving must not occur simultaneously with active work duties.

#### 4.6 Equipment and Asset Management
4.6.1 **Organization Property:** Laptops, monitors, and peripherals provided by the Organization remain the sole property of Study GRC Inc. and must be returned upon termination.
4.6.2 **Personal Use:** Organization-issued devices are for business use only. Family members and non-employees are strictly prohibited from using Organization devices.
4.6.3 **Reimbursement:** The Organization will reimburse reasonable and necessary business expenses (e.g., portion of internet costs if required by law) only if pre-approved and substantiated in accordance with the **Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy**.

#### 4.7 Information Security and Data Protection
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)** and the **Master Information Security Policy**:
4.7.1 **Network Security:** Employees must use a secure internet connection. Use of public Wi-Fi (cafes, airports) is prohibited unless connected via the Organization-approved VPN.
4.7.2 **Confidentiality:** Employees must ensure that sensitive conversations cannot be overheard by smart devices (e.g., Alexa, Siri) or unauthorized individuals in the shared space.
4.7.3 **Physical Security:** When not in use, Organization devices must be locked (screen lock) and physically secured.

#### 4.8 Professionalism
4.8.1 **Video Conferencing:** Employees must maintain a professional appearance and a neutral or professional background (physical or virtual) during video calls with external stakeholders, partners, or members.
4.8.2 **Environment:** The Designated Workspace should be free from excessive noise or distractions during meetings.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Maintains a safe Designated Workspace; adheres to security protocols; accurately records time; reports injuries immediately. |
| **Manager** | Approves work schedules; focuses on results-based management rather than surveillance; ensures team connectivity. |
| **HR / Operations** | Manages state tax registrations; administers the *Remote Worksite Safety Self-Assessment*; handles Workers' Compensation claims. |
| **CISO / IT** | Provisions secure hardware; manages VPN and endpoint security; monitors for anomalous login activity. |
| **Executive Director** | Approves relocations to new jurisdictions; sets the tone for remote culture. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Security Violations:** Failure to secure remote devices resulting in a data breach will be treated as a severe violation of **Bylaws Article IX**.
*   **Fraud:** Falsifying hours worked (for non-exempt staff) constitutes wage theft and is grounds for immediate termination.
