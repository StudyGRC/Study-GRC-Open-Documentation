### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to provide compassionate support to employees of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") during times of loss. This policy establishes the guidelines for **Bereavement Leave**, ensuring employees are granted paid time off to grieve, attend funeral services, and manage personal affairs associated with the death of a family member or loved one, without fear of income loss or job insecurity. This policy aligns with the Organization’s commitment to mental well-being and its core value of kindness.

### 2. Scope
**Applies to:**
*   All full-time employees (30+ hours/week).
*   All part-time employees (20+ hours/week) on a pro-rated basis.
*   Remote and hybrid employees.

**Exclusions:**
*   Independent Contractors (1099), Temporary employees, and Volunteers. (Note: While not eligible for *paid* leave, these individuals may request unpaid time off or schedule adjustments for bereavement purposes).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Immediate Family** | Spouse, domestic partner, child (biological, adopted, foster, or step), parent (biological, adoptive, step, or in-law), sibling (biological, step, or in-law), grandparent, or grandchild. This definition explicitly includes pregnancy loss (miscarriage or stillbirth). |
| **Extended Family** | Aunts, uncles, nieces, nephews, cousins, or other relatives not listed under "Immediate Family." |
| **Affinity Relationship** | An individual with whom the employee has a significant, close personal relationship equivalent to a family tie (often referred to as "chosen family"), subject to approval by the Executive Director. |
| **Paid Bereavement Leave** | Time off from work where the employee receives their standard base pay. This is separate from and in addition to Paid Time Off (PTO) or Sick Leave. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Eligibility and Waiting Period
4.1.1 Employees are eligible for Bereavement Leave immediately upon their date of hire. There is no waiting period or tenure requirement to access this benefit.

#### 4.2 Leave Entitlement
4.2.1 **Immediate Family:** Employees are entitled to up to **five (5) consecutive business days** of Paid Bereavement Leave upon the death of an Immediate Family member.
4.2.2 **Extended Family:** Employees are entitled to up to **one (1) business day** of Paid Bereavement Leave upon the death of an Extended Family member.
4.2.3 **Affinity Relationships:** Requests for leave regarding Affinity Relationships will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis by the Executive Director or the Director of Kindness and Generosity (DKG). If approved, these will generally follow the allowance for Extended Family (1 day), unless significant circumstances warrant otherwise.

#### 4.3 Remote Work Considerations
4.3.1 Given the Organization's remote-first structure, Bereavement Leave is defined as a complete disconnection from work duties. Employees are not expected to monitor emails, Slack, or attend virtual meetings during this time.
4.3.2 If an employee is required to travel for funeral services, the leave days are intended to cover both the travel time and the grieving period.

#### 4.4 Requesting Leave
4.4.1 Employees must notify their direct supervisor as soon as possible regarding the need for Bereavement Leave.
4.4.2 Notification may be made via email, Slack, or phone. Formal approval is generally automatic, but the supervisor must be informed of the dates the employee will be offline to arrange coverage.
4.4.3 If the employee is unable to notify the supervisor personally due to the sudden nature of the loss, a family member or designee may contact the Organization on their behalf.

#### 4.5 Additional Time Off
4.5.1 If an employee requires additional time beyond the Bereavement Leave limits defined in Section 4.2, they may request to utilize accrued Paid Time Off (PTO).
4.5.2 If PTO is exhausted, the employee may request Unpaid Personal Leave, subject to approval by the Executive Director.

#### 4.6 Documentation and Verification
4.6.1 The Organization operates on a basis of trust and generally does not require documentation for Bereavement Leave.
4.6.2 However, the Organization reserves the right to request verification (e.g., an obituary, funeral program, or death certificate) if:
*   The employee requests leave extending beyond the standard limits.
*   There is a pattern of frequent bereavement leave requests that raises concerns of abuse.
*   The leave is requested during a period of critical business operations or restricted leave.

#### 4.7 Payroll Coding
4.7.1 Employees (or their supervisors) must record this time as "Bereavement Leave" in the Organization’s timekeeping system to ensure it is not deducted from the employee’s PTO balance.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employee** | Notify supervisor immediately of the loss and intended dates of absence. Ensure time is logged correctly in the payroll system. |
| **Supervisor** | Approve leave requests promptly and with compassion. Reassign critical tasks or deliverables during the employee's absence. Notify the DKG for potential internal support/condolences. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity (DKG)** | Coordinate organizational expressions of sympathy (e.g., flowers, cards) in accordance with the Gift Acceptance and Expense Policies. |
| **HR / Payroll** | Ensure Bereavement Leave is paid correctly and is not deducted from accrued PTO. Monitor usage for policy compliance. |
| **Executive Director** | Serve as the final approver for exceptions to this policy or requests regarding Affinity Relationships. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy, including the falsification of reasons for leave or misrepresentation of family relationships to claim benefits, constitutes a violation of the Organization's *Code of Ethics and Conduct*. Such violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment and a requirement to repay wages paid during the fraudulent leave period.
