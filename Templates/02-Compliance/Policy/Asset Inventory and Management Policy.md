### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards for the acquisition, tracking, protection, and disposal of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") assets. As a remote-first, federally funded non-profit, accurate asset management is critical to:
1.  Ensure compliance with **2 CFR 200.313** (Equipment) regarding property records and physical inventory.
2.  Safeguard the Organization’s resources against theft, loss, and misuse in accordance with the fiduciary duties outlined in **Bylaws Article VIII (Records, Transparency & Finance)**.
3.  Support cybersecurity objectives by maintaining visibility into the attack surface ("you cannot secure what you do not know").

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all tangible and intangible assets owned, leased, or managed by the Organization.

*   **Applies to:** All employees, Board Directors, contractors, and volunteers (collectively "Personnel") who are issued or have custody of Organization property.
*   **Includes:**
    *   **IT Hardware:** Laptops, servers, mobile devices, networking equipment, and peripherals.
    *   **Software:** Licenses, cloud subscriptions (SaaS), and intellectual property.
    *   **Capital Assets:** Furniture or equipment with a unit cost exceeding $5,000.
*   **Exclusions:** Consumable office supplies (e.g., pens, paper) and low-value peripherals (under $50) that do not process or store sensitive data.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Asset Tag** | A unique alphanumeric identifier (physical label and digital record) attached to an asset for tracking purposes. |
| **Capital Asset** | Tangible personal property having a useful life of more than one year and a per-unit acquisition cost which equals or exceeds the lesser of the capitalization level established by the Organization for financial statement purposes, or $5,000. |
| **Custodian** | The individual Personnel member who has physical possession of an asset and is responsible for its day-to-day care and security. |
| **FAIN** | Federal Award Identification Number. Required for tracking assets purchased with federal grant funds. |
| **Sanitization** | The process of removing data from media so that the data can no longer be retrieved, in compliance with NIST SP 800-88. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Asset Acquisition and Tagging
All assets meeting the scope criteria must be recorded in the Central Asset Registry immediately upon receipt.
*   **4.1.1 Tagging:** Physical assets must be affixed with a tamper-evident asset tag containing a unique ID and the Organization’s logo/contact info.
*   **4.1.2 Federal Funds:** Assets purchased with federal grant funds must be explicitly flagged in the registry to ensure compliance with **2 CFR 200.313(d)(1)**.

#### 4.2 Central Asset Registry Requirements
The Organization must maintain a centralized, digital Asset Registry. Per **2 CFR 200.313(d)(1)**, the registry must contain the following data points for every asset:
1.  Description of the property.
2.  Serial number or other identification number.
3.  Source of funding for the property (including the FAIN).
4.  Who holds title (Organization or Federal Government).
5.  Acquisition date.
6.  Cost of the property.
7.  Percentage of Federal participation in the project costs for the Federal award under which the property was acquired.
8.  Location (e.g., Remote Employee Home Address).
9.  Use and condition of the property.
10. Disposition data (date of disposal and sale price).

#### 4.3 Custody and Remote Work Stewardship
Due to the Organization's remote-first nature, strict custody chains must be maintained.
*   **4.3.1 Issuance:** Assets will not be shipped to Personnel until a **Remote Work Agreement** and **Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)** acknowledgment are signed.
*   **4.3.2 Stewardship:** Custodians must take reasonable care to protect assets from theft, damage, or environmental hazards. Assets must not be left unattended in vehicles or public spaces.
*   **4.3.3 Prohibition on Personal Use:** Organization assets are for business use only. Family members or housemates of the Custodian are prohibited from using Organization devices.

#### 4.4 Physical Inventory and Reconciliation
*   **4.4.1 Frequency:** A physical inventory of the property and the results must be reconciled with the property records at least once every **two (2) years**, as required by **2 CFR 200.313(d)(2)**.
*   **4.4.2 Remote Verification:** For remote Personnel, physical inventory verification may be conducted via video call with IT Staff or by submitting time-stamped photographs of the asset and its tag.

#### 4.5 Maintenance and Security
*   **4.5.1 Updates:** IT Hardware must be kept up-to-date with security patches in accordance with the **Patch Management Policy**.
*   **4.5.2 Encryption:** All mobile computing assets (laptops, tablets) must have full-disk encryption enabled.

#### 4.6 Lost, Stolen, or Damaged Assets
*   **4.6.1 Reporting:** Any loss, theft, or damage must be reported to the CISO and CFO within **24 hours** of discovery via the Incident Response procedure.
*   **4.6.2 Police Reports:** In cases of theft, a police report must be filed by the Custodian in their local jurisdiction, and a copy provided to the Organization.

#### 4.7 Disposal and Decommissioning
*   **4.7.1 Authorization:** Disposal of Capital Assets or assets purchased with Federal funds requires prior written approval from the CFO and Executive Director to ensure compliance with **2 CFR 200.313(e)**.
*   **4.7.2 Data Sanitization:** Prior to disposal, donation, or return to a vendor, all data storage media must be sanitized in strict accordance with **NIST SP 800-88 Guidelines for Media Sanitization**.
*   **4.7.3 Certificate of Destruction:** A record of the sanitization and disposal method must be retained in the Asset Registry.

#### 4.8 Return of Assets
Upon termination of employment, contract, or volunteer service, or upon request by the Organization, Custodians must return all assets within **five (5) business days**. Failure to return assets may result in legal action and the withholding of final payments where permitted by law.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Oversees the financial recording of assets, depreciation schedules, and compliance with federal grant property standards (2 CFR 200). Approves disposals. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Manages the Central Asset Registry, enforces security configurations (encryption), and oversees data sanitization procedures. |
| **IT Department** | Performs tagging, shipping, receiving, and technical maintenance of assets. Conducts the bi-annual physical inventory. |
| **Asset Custodian (User)** | Maintains physical control of the asset, prevents damage/loss, and reports status/location changes immediately. |
| **Human Resources** | Ensures assets are retrieved during the offboarding process and notifies IT of personnel changes. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
*   **Financial Liability:** To the extent permitted by applicable law, Custodians may be held financially liable for the replacement cost of assets lost or damaged due to gross negligence or willful misconduct.
*   **Legal Action:** Theft of Organization property will be referred to law enforcement.
