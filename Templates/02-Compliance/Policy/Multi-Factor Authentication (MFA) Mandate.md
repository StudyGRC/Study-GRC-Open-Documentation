### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a mandatory requirement for Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across all Study GRC Inc. information systems. As a remote-first organization, reliance on usernames and passwords alone is insufficient to protect our assets against credential theft, phishing, and brute-force attacks. This policy aligns with **CISA Cybersecurity Performance Goal (CPG) 2.1**, ensuring that the identity of all users—including Board Directors, employees, and volunteers—is rigorously verified before granting access to organizational resources.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Individuals:** All Board Directors, Officers, employees, contractors, volunteers, and interns (collectively "Users").
*   **Systems:** All cloud-based applications (e.g., Google Workspace, Slack, GitHub), financial systems, VPNs, and administrative portals owned or leased by Study GRC Inc.
*   **Access Types:** Remote network access, administrative access, and any access to Sensitive or Confidential data as defined in the *Data Classification Standard*.

**Exclusions:**
*   Public-facing website components intended for anonymous community consumption do not require MFA for read-only access.
*   Service Accounts (non-human) are governed by Section 4.6 of this policy.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** | An authentication method that requires the user to provide two or more verification factors to gain access to a resource. Factors include something you know (password), something you have (token/phone), and something you are (biometric). |
| **Phishing-Resistant MFA** | Authentication methods designed to prevent attackers from intercepting credentials via fake login pages. Examples include FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware keys (e.g., YubiKey) and Passkeys. |
| **TOTP (Time-based One-Time Password)** | A temporary passcode generated by an algorithm, typically via a mobile app like Google Authenticator or Authy. |
| **Privileged Account** | An account with elevated permissions (e.g., Super Admin, System Administrator) capable of altering system configurations, creating users, or accessing all data. |
| **CISA CPG** | Cybersecurity Performance Goals issued by the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Universal Mandate
In accordance with **Bylaws Article IX (Policies & Data Protection)** and the organization's commitment to zero-trust security, MFA is **mandatory** for all accounts accessing Study GRC Inc. resources.
*   Users must enroll in MFA immediately upon account activation.
*   Access to systems will be technically blocked until MFA is configured.
*   "Remember this device" settings shall not exceed 14 days for standard users and 24 hours for Privileged Accounts.

#### 4.2 Acceptable Authentication Methods
Study GRC Inc. enforces a tiered approach to acceptable MFA methods based on risk:

*   **Tier 1 (Preferred - High Security):** FIDO2/WebAuthn Hardware Security Keys (e.g., YubiKey) or device-bound Passkeys.
*   **Tier 2 (Standard - Acceptable):** Mobile App-based TOTP (e.g., Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password).
*   **Tier 3 (Restricted - Last Resort):** SMS or Voice Call verification.
    *   *Note:* SMS/Voice MFA is **prohibited** for Privileged Accounts due to the risk of SIM-swapping attacks. It may only be used by standard volunteers if no Tier 1 or Tier 2 option is viable, and requires CISO approval.

#### 4.3 Privileged Access Requirements
Users holding Privileged Accounts (e.g., IT Administrators, DevOps, Finance Directors) are held to a higher standard per **CISA CPG 2.1**:
*   Privileged Accounts **must** utilize Phishing-Resistant MFA (Tier 1) where technically supported by the platform.
*   If a platform does not support FIDO2/WebAuthn, a Tier 2 TOTP method is required.
*   Privileged users must not share MFA tokens or seeds.

#### 4.4 Remote Access and VPN
As a remote-first entity, all access is considered "remote."
*   Any connection to the organization's internal resources via VPN or Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA) solutions must be challenged with MFA at the time of connection.
*   There are no "trusted networks" or IP allow-lists that bypass MFA requirements for user interactive logins.

#### 4.5 Lost Devices and MFA Reset
To prevent social engineering attacks targeting the help desk:
*   Users who lose their MFA device must report the incident to the Security Team immediately.
*   MFA resets require **visual identity verification** (video call) with an Operational Officer or HR representative before the reset is processed.
*   Bypassing MFA (e.g., temporary disablement) for troubleshooting is prohibited unless authorized in writing by the CISO for a specific, time-bound duration (not to exceed 2 hours).

#### 4.6 Service Accounts (Non-Human Identities)
Where MFA cannot be implemented for automated service accounts (e.g., CI/CD pipelines, API integrations):
*   The account must utilize a complex, randomly generated password (minimum 25 characters) or API Key.
*   Access must be restricted by IP allow-listing or VPC peering.
*   The account must not have interactive login capabilities.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves this policy and ensures adequate budget for MFA hardware (e.g., YubiKeys) for critical staff. |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Defines acceptable MFA technologies; monitors compliance; authorizes exceptions; oversees the *MFA Enrollment and Config Procedure*. |
| **IT / Help Desk** | Assists users with MFA setup; performs identity verification prior to resetting MFA factors. |
| **Operational Officers** | Ensure their respective departments (Education, Finance, etc.) comply with this mandate. |
| **All Users** | Protect their MFA devices/tokens; report lost or stolen devices immediately; never approve an MFA push notification they did not initiate. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Compliance with this policy is mandatory.
*   **Technical Enforcement:** Systems will be configured to technically enforce MFA. Accounts without MFA will be locked automatically.
*   **Disciplinary Action:** Attempts to bypass, disable, or circumvent MFA mechanisms constitute a violation of the *Acceptable Use Policy* and *Professional Ethics*. Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, removal from the Board (per **Bylaws Section 3.6**), or revocation of volunteer status.
