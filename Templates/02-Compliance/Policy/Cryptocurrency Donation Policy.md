### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and procedures for the acceptance, processing, liquidation, and tax substantiation of cryptocurrency and digital asset donations to Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy ensures compliance with Internal Revenue Service (IRS) property regulations, mitigates financial volatility risks, and prevents the use of the Organization for money laundering or illicit financial activities.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all donations of virtual currency, cryptocurrency, stablecoins, non-fungible tokens (NFTs), and other digital assets offered to the Organization.
*   **Applies to:** Board of Directors, Officers, Finance Department, and Third-Party Payment Processors.
*   **Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to standard fiat currency donations (USD) made via credit card, check, or wire transfer.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cryptocurrency** | A digital representation of value that functions as a medium of exchange, a unit of account, and/or a store of value (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum). Treated as **property** by the IRS. |
| **Fiat Currency** | Government-issued currency that is designated as legal tender (e.g., US Dollar). |
| **Immediate Liquidation** | The process of converting cryptocurrency into fiat currency (USD) at the earliest possible moment upon receipt to lock in value. |
| **Wallet** | A digital interface used to store public and private keys for cryptocurrency transactions. |
| **Form 8283** | IRS form filed by donors to claim a deduction for non-cash charitable contributions greater than $500. |
| **Form 8282** | IRS form filed by the Organization (Donee) if it sells, exchanges, or disposes of charitable deduction property within three years of receipt. |
| **KYC (Know Your Customer)** | The mandatory process of verifying the identity of the donor to prevent fraud, money laundering, and financing of terrorism. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1. Acceptance of Digital Assets
The Organization accepts donations of cryptocurrency to support its charitable and educational purposes as defined in **Bylaws Article I, Section 1.3**.
*   **4.1.1 Approved Assets:** The Organization generally accepts liquid cryptocurrencies with high market capitalization (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, USDC) supported by our designated Third-Party Payment Processor (TPPP).
*   **4.1.2 Right to Refuse:** In accordance with the *Gift Acceptance Policy*, the Organization reserves the right to decline any digital asset donation that:
    *   Is illiquid or cannot be easily converted to USD.
    *   Is a "Privacy Coin" (e.g., Monero, Zcash) that prevents adequate source tracing/KYC.
    *   Generates unrelated business income tax (UBIT) liability.
    *   Originates from a wallet address associated with the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) sanctions list or known illicit activity.

#### 4.2. Custody and Security
To minimize security risks associated with private key management (e.g., hacking, loss of keys, internal theft):
*   **4.2.1 Third-Party Processing:** The Organization must utilize a reputable, SOC 2 compliant Third-Party Payment Processor (e.g., The Giving Block, Coinbase Commerce) to facilitate the receipt and custody of digital assets.
*   **4.2.2 Self-Custody Prohibition:** Operational Officers and Directors are prohibited from holding Organization assets in personal hardware wallets or personal exchange accounts.
*   **4.2.3 Access Control:** Access to the institutional exchange/processor account is restricted to the Treasurer and the CFO. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all access points.

#### 4.3. Immediate Liquidation (Volatility Mitigation)
The Organization is a non-profit entity, not a speculative investment fund. To protect the value of the donation for programmatic use:
*   **4.3.1 Automatic Conversion:** All cryptocurrency donations must be converted to Fiat Currency (USD) **immediately upon receipt**.
*   **4.3.2 No "HODLing":** The Organization shall not hold cryptocurrency as an investment asset. Exceptions to this rule require a specific resolution approved by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors, consistent with the *Investment Policy*.
*   **4.3.3 Transaction Fees:** Network fees (gas fees) and exchange fees incurred during liquidation will be deducted from the gross proceeds of the donation.

#### 4.4. Tax Substantiation and IRS Compliance
Per IRS Notice 2014-21, cryptocurrency is treated as **property**, not currency, for federal tax purposes.
*   **4.4.1 Acknowledgment:** The Organization will provide a contemporaneous written acknowledgment for donations valued at $250 or more. This receipt will state the **type and amount of cryptocurrency received** (e.g., "1.5 BTC") and the date of receipt.
*   **4.4.2 No Valuation:** The Organization **will not** assign a USD value to the donation in the acknowledgment letter. The burden of valuation/appraisal lies solely with the donor.
*   **4.4.3 Form 8283 (Donor Reporting):** For donations valued over $5,000, the donor must obtain a qualified appraisal. The Organization (via the CFO or Treasurer) will sign Part IV of IRS Form 8283 ("Donee Acknowledgment") to confirm receipt, but this signature does not represent agreement with the donor’s claimed value.
*   **4.4.4 Form 8282 (Donee Reporting):** Because the Organization liquidates assets immediately (within 3 years of receipt), the Organization must file **IRS Form 8282** ("Donee Information Return") with the IRS and provide a copy to the donor, disclosing the amount received upon sale (liquidation price).

#### 4.5. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Transparency
*   **4.5.1 Threshold for KYC:** For any cryptocurrency donation exceeding $5,000 USD in value, the Organization requires the donor to provide their legal name and address.
*   **4.5.2 Anonymous Donations:** Anonymous crypto donations are accepted only if they are below the $5,000 threshold and pass the TPPP’s automated blockchain analysis screening.
*   **4.5.3 Refund Prohibition:** To prevent money laundering (layering), cryptocurrency donations are generally **non-refundable**. If a refund is legally required, it will be remitted in USD, net of all conversion and processing fees, and only after rigorous identity verification of the original donor.

#### 4.6. Conflict of Interest
Pursuant to **Bylaws Article IX**, any Director or Officer donating cryptocurrency to the Organization must disclose if they are a creator, developer, or major holder (>5% supply) of the specific asset being donated, as this may impact liquidity and valuation.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Cryptocurrency Donation Policy; Reviews exceptions for holding assets (if any). |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Manages the TPPP account; Executes immediate liquidation; Signs IRS Form 8283; Files IRS Form 8282. |
| **Treasurer** | Provides oversight of the crypto-wallet/exchange account; Conducts monthly reconciliation of crypto-to-fiat conversions. |
| **Director of Kindness (DKG)** | Communicates policy requirements to donors; Issues contemporaneous written acknowledgments (without USD valuation). |
| **Donor** | Responsible for obtaining a qualified appraisal for gifts >$5,000 and determining the fair market value for their own tax deduction. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board.
*   **Tax Fraud:** Any employee or Officer found to be colluding with a donor to inflate the value of a crypto donation (e.g., signing a Form 8283 knowing the appraisal is false) will be subject to immediate termination and reported to the IRS under IRC § 6701 (Penalties for Aiding and Abetting Understatement of Tax Liability).
*   **Security Violations:** Use of personal wallets for Organization funds is considered misappropriation of assets.
