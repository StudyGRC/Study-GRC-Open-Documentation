### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish Study GRC Inc.'s commitment to maintaining a safe, healthy, and productive work environment free from the effects of substance abuse. As a federal grant recipient and an organization dedicated to youth education, Study GRC Inc. complies strictly with the **Drug-Free Workplace Act of 1988** and applicable Texas state laws. This policy outlines the prohibitions regarding drug and alcohol use and the mandatory reporting requirements for criminal drug convictions.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals representing Study GRC Inc., including:
*   **Personnel:** Full-time and part-time employees.
*   **Non-Employees:** Volunteers, interns, Board Directors, and independent contractors while performing work for the Organization.
*   **Locations:** This policy applies to any "Workplace" as defined in Section 3, including remote home offices during working hours, third-party event venues, and any location where Organization business is conducted.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Controlled Substance** | A controlled substance in schedules I through V of section 202 of the Controlled Substances Act (21 U.S.C. 812), including but not limited to marijuana, cocaine, opiates, amphetamines, and phencyclidine (PCP). |
| **Workplace** | Any location where Study GRC Inc. business is conducted. Due to the Organization's remote-first nature, this includes an employee’s or volunteer's home office, co-working space, or vehicle during scheduled work hours or while performing compensable work. |
| **Under the Influence** | A state of having a detectable level of alcohol or controlled substance in one's system, or exhibiting symptoms of impairment such as slurred speech, lack of coordination, or erratic behavior that affects safety or performance. |
| **Safety-Sensitive Position** | Any role involving the supervision of minors (K-12 participants), the operation of motor vehicles, or the oversight of critical IT security infrastructure (e.g., CISO). |
| **Conviction** | A finding of guilt (including a plea of *nolo contendere*) or imposition of sentence, or both, by any judicial body charged with the responsibility to determine violations of the Federal or State criminal drug statutes. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Prohibitions
The unlawful manufacture, distribution, dispensing, possession, or use of a controlled substance is strictly prohibited in the Study GRC Inc. Workplace.
4.1.1 **On-Duty Conduct:** No employee or volunteer may report to work or perform work-related duties while Under the Influence of alcohol or illegal drugs.
4.1.2 **Off-Duty Conduct:** Off-the-job illegal drug use or activity that negatively affects the employee’s performance, safety, or the Organization’s reputation is prohibited.

#### 4.2 Federal Grant Compliance (Drug-Free Workplace Act)
As a condition of employment under federal grant awards, all employees must:
4.2.1 Abide by the terms of this policy; and
4.2.2 Notify the Organization in writing of any criminal drug statute **Conviction** for a violation occurring in the workplace no later than **five (5) calendar days** after such conviction.

#### 4.3 Remote Work and Telecommuting
Given the Organization's remote-first structure, employees and volunteers acknowledge that the prohibition of substance use extends to their remote workspace during defined working hours.
4.3.1 **Video Conferencing:** Appearing visibly impaired during virtual meetings (internal or external) constitutes a violation of this policy.
4.3.2 **Availability:** Employees must remain unimpaired and available for communication during their scheduled work block.

#### 4.4 Prescription and Over-the-Counter Medications
The legal use of prescribed drugs or over-the-counter medications is permitted only if it does not impair the individual's ability to perform the essential functions of the job effectively and safely.
4.4.1 **Duty to Report:** Employees in Safety-Sensitive Positions must notify Human Resources or the Executive Director if they are taking medications that carry a warning label regarding the operation of machinery or mental alertness.
4.4.2 **Medical Marijuana:** Consistent with federal law and the Organization’s status as a federal contractor/grantee, the use of marijuana (including for medical purposes under state law) is prohibited in the Workplace and does not constitute a valid defense for a positive drug test under federal mandates.

#### 4.5 Alcohol Use
The consumption of alcohol is prohibited during working hours.
4.5.1 **Exceptions:** Limited alcohol consumption may be permitted at specific Organization-sponsored social events (e.g., post-conference networking) only with prior written approval from the Executive Director. In such instances, conduct must remain professional, and moderation is required.
4.5.2 **Zero Tolerance with Minors:** Alcohol consumption is strictly prohibited at any time during events where the primary audience is K-12 youth, regardless of whether the individual is "off the clock."

#### 4.6 Notification to Federal Agencies
Upon receiving notice of an employee's criminal drug statute conviction for a violation occurring in the workplace, the Organization will take the following actions in compliance with the Drug-Free Workplace Act:
4.6.1 **Agency Notification:** The Executive Director or designee will notify the granting Federal Agency within **ten (10) calendar days** after receiving notice of the conviction.
4.6.2 **Personnel Action:** Within **thirty (30) calendar days** of receiving notice of conviction, the Organization will take appropriate personnel action against the employee, up to and including termination, or require satisfactory participation in a drug abuse assistance or rehabilitation program.

#### 4.7 Searches and Inspections
To ensure the safety of the community and compliance with this policy, the Organization reserves the right to inspect Organization-owned property (e.g., laptops, digital accounts, files) at any time. While the Organization will not physically search private homes of remote workers, it reserves the right to require a visual inspection of the remote workspace environment via video if safety concerns arise.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Employees & Volunteers** | Adhere to this policy; report any personal criminal drug convictions within 5 days; report safety concerns regarding colleagues to leadership. |
| **Executive Director** | Ensure all staff receive a copy of this policy; manage the notification process to Federal Agencies regarding convictions; authorize disciplinary actions. |
| **Board of Directors** | Review and approve this policy; oversee the Executive Director's compliance with federal reporting requirements pursuant to Bylaws Article III. |
| **HR / Compliance Officer** | Maintain records of policy acknowledgment; facilitate drug-free awareness programs; manage confidential medical disclosures regarding prescription use. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy is a serious violation of the Organization's Code of Conduct and federal law.

6.1 **Disciplinary Action:** Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination of employment or volunteer assignment.
6.2 **Legal Action:** The Organization may refer evidence of illegal drug activity to law enforcement authorities.
6.3 **Rehabilitation:** At the discretion of the Board and Executive Director, an employee may be offered the opportunity to participate in a rehabilitation program in lieu of termination, provided this does not conflict with youth protection standards. Failure to successfully complete such a program will result in termination.
