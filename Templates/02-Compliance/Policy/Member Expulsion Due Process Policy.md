### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a fair, transparent, and legally defensible framework for the discipline, suspension, or expulsion of members of Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization"). This policy ensures that the Organization protects its community, reputation, and tax-exempt status while affording accused members appropriate procedural due process (notice and an opportunity to be heard) in accordance with Texas common law regarding non-profit membership rights.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all classes of membership defined in **Article II, Section 2.1** of the Bylaws:
*   Community Participants (Non-Voting)
*   Supporting Members (Voting)
*   Honorary Members (Voting)

**Exclusions:**
*   **Directors:** The removal of a Director from the Board is governed strictly by **Article III, Section 3.6** of the Bylaws. However, if a Director is also a Member, their *membership* status is subject to this policy. Loss of membership may disqualify an individual from Board service pursuant to **Article III, Section 3.3**.
*   **Employees:** Termination of employment is governed by the Employee Handbook and applicable employment laws.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Good Standing** | A status assigned to a member who is current on dues (if applicable) and not currently subject to disciplinary suspension. |
| **Cause** | Conduct that violates the Organization's Bylaws, Code of Ethics, policies, or laws, or conduct deemed detrimental to the Organization's mission or community safety. |
| **Summary Suspension** | An immediate, temporary suspension of membership rights invoked prior to a hearing when there is an imminent threat to safety, property, or reputation. |
| **Expulsion** | The permanent termination of membership and all associated rights and privileges. |
| **Notice** | Written communication sent to the member's address of record (email) as defined in **Article II, Section 2.6** of the Bylaws. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Grounds for Discipline
Membership in Study GRC Inc. is a privilege, not a right. Disciplinary action, up to and including expulsion, may be taken for "Cause," which includes but is not limited to:
1.  Violation of the **Bylaws**, **Code of Ethics**, or **Community Guidelines**.
2.  Violation of the **Child and Youth Safeguarding Policy** (e.g., inappropriate interaction with minors).
3.  Theft, fraud, or misuse of the Organization’s intellectual property or funds.
4.  Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of staff, volunteers, or other members.
5.  Conviction of a felony or a crime involving moral turpitude.
6.  Failure to pay required dues within 60 days of the due date (Administrative Termination).

#### 4.2 Levels of Discipline
The Board of Directors, or a designated Ethics Committee, may impose the following sanctions based on the severity of the offense:
*   **Written Reprimand:** A formal warning placed in the member's record.
*   **Probation:** A specific period during which the member acts under restricted rights.
*   **Suspension:** Temporary removal of all membership rights (voting, access to resources) for a set period.
*   **Expulsion:** Permanent removal from the Organization.

#### 4.3 Summary Suspension (Emergency Action)
Notwithstanding the hearing procedures below, the **Executive Director** or **President** may immediately suspend a member ("Summary Suspension") if:
1.  The member poses an immediate physical threat to any person.
2.  The member is accused of sexual misconduct, grooming, or abuse involving a minor.
3.  The member is actively compromising the Organization's cybersecurity infrastructure.

A Summary Suspension remains in effect until the Board concludes the formal review process defined in Section 4.4.

#### 4.4 Due Process Procedure
Except in cases of automatic administrative termination for non-payment of dues, the following process must be followed to ensure legal fairness:

**4.4.1 Notice of Intent**
The Organization must provide the member with a "Notice of Intent to Discipline" at least **fifteen (15) days** prior to the final decision date. The notice must include:
*   The specific rule(s) violated.
*   A summary of the evidence or conduct in question.
*   The proposed sanction (e.g., expulsion).
*   Instructions on how the member may respond.

**4.4.2 Opportunity to be Heard**
The member has the right to respond to the charges.
*   **Written Response:** The member may submit a written defense to the Board within **ten (10) days** of receiving the Notice.
*   **Hearing (Optional):** The Board may, at its sole discretion, grant a virtual hearing via video conference. If granted, the member may present their case personally. Legal counsel may attend but may not actively participate in the proceedings unless authorized by the Board.

**4.4.3 Board Review and Vote**
*   The Board (or a quorum thereof) shall review the evidence and the member's response.
*   A vote of **two-thirds (2/3)** of the Directors present is required to expel a Voting Member.
*   Directors with a Conflict of Interest regarding the member must recuse themselves pursuant to **Article IX, Section 9.1** of the Bylaws.

**4.4.4 Final Decision Notice**
The Secretary shall notify the member of the Board’s final decision in writing within **five (5) business days** of the vote. The decision of the Board is final and not subject to further appeal within the Organization.

#### 4.5 Effect of Expulsion
*   **Termination of Rights:** All rights, including voting rights and access to open-source repositories or community channels, cease immediately.
*   **No Refund:** Expelled members are **not** entitled to a refund of any dues or contributions previously paid, as these funds were committed to the charitable purpose.
*   **Re-Application:** Expelled members may not re-apply for membership for a period of **three (3) years**, and re-admission is subject to unanimous Board approval.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Acts as the final decision-making body for expulsions; ensures due process is followed. |
| **Secretary** | Issues all formal notices (Intent to Discipline, Final Decision) and maintains records of the proceedings. |
| **Executive Director** | Authorized to enact Summary Suspensions in emergency safety situations; reports incidents to the Board. |
| **Director of Kindness & Generosity** | May serve as the initial intake point for community complaints; attempts mediation prior to formal disciplinary proceedings where appropriate. |
| **Member** | Responsible for maintaining current contact information and responding to notices within the defined timelines. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to this policy by the Organization could result in legal liability for "wrongful expulsion." Failure by a member to adhere to the Code of Conduct cited herein results in the sanctions described above.

Records of disciplinary proceedings, including notices, evidence, and meeting minutes, shall be retained in a confidential legal file for a minimum of **seven (7) years** pursuant to the **Document Retention and Destruction Policy**.
