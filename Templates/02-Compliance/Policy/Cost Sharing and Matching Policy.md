### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and procedures for proposing, recording, valuing, and reporting cost sharing or matching funds for sponsored projects at Study GRC Inc. This policy ensures compliance with federal regulations, specifically **2 CFR § 200.306**, and prevents financial liability resulting from disallowed costs or unmet matching obligations.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, officers, and volunteers involved in the application for, administration of, or reporting on grants and cooperative agreements. It specifically governs:
*   **Mandatory Cost Sharing:** Required by the sponsor as a condition of the award.
*   **Voluntary Committed Cost Sharing:** Pledged voluntarily in the proposal and subsequently included in the award.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not apply to "Voluntary Uncommitted Cost Sharing" (cost sharing that is not included in the proposal budget or award and therefore not legally binding).

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Cost Sharing / Matching** | The portion of project costs not paid by Federal funds (unless otherwise authorized by Federal statute). The terms are often used interchangeably. |
| **Cash Match** | The Organization's own cash outlay allocated to the project (e.g., paying for a portion of an employee's salary to work on the grant). |
| **In-Kind Contribution** | The value of non-cash contributions (e.g., property, equipment, supplies, or volunteer services) provided by the Organization or third parties. |
| **Third-Party Contribution** | Cash or in-kind contributions provided by an entity other than Study GRC Inc. or the funding agency. |
| **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)** | Federal regulations governing the administration of federal awards (Office of Management and Budget). |
| **Fair Market Value (FMV)** | The price that property or services would sell for on the open market. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 General Principles
Study GRC Inc. shall only propose Cost Sharing when it is strictly required by the sponsor (Mandatory) or when the Board of Directors determines that the strategic value of the award outweighs the financial burden of the match (Voluntary Committed).

#### 4.2 Criteria for Allowability
In accordance with **2 CFR § 200.306(b)**, for any cost (cash or in-kind) to be accepted as part of a cost sharing or matching requirement, it must meet **all** of the following criteria:
1.  **Verifiable:** It must be verifiable from the Organization's records (e.g., general ledger, timesheets).
2.  **Not Double-Counted:** It must not be included as a contribution for any other Federal award.
3.  **Necessary and Reasonable:** It must be necessary and reasonable for the accomplishment of project or program objectives.
4.  **Allowable:** It must be allowable under **2 CFR 200 Subpart E (Cost Principles)**.
5.  **Not Federal Funds:** It must not be paid by the Federal government under another Federal award, except where the Federal statute authorizing a program specifically provides that Federal funds made available for such program can be applied to matching or cost sharing requirements of other Federal programs.
6.  **Budgeted:** It must be provided for in the approved budget when required by the Federal awarding agency.

#### 4.3 Valuation of In-Kind Contributions
The CFO is responsible for ensuring all in-kind contributions are valued correctly pursuant to **2 CFR § 200.306(d)-(k)**.

*   **4.3.1 Volunteer Services:**
    *   Volunteer services furnished by third-party professional and technical personnel, consultants, and other skilled and unskilled labor may be counted as cost sharing or matching if the service is an integral and necessary part of the project.
    *   **Valuation:** Rates for volunteer services must be consistent with those paid for similar work in the Organization's own labor market. In those instances in which the required skills are not found in the Organization, rates must be consistent with those paid for similar work in the labor market in which the Organization competes.
    *   **Documentation:** Volunteers must complete a "Volunteer Time & Effort Log" detailing hours worked and specific duties performed, signed by the volunteer and a supervisor.

*   **4.3.2 Donated Supplies and Equipment:**
    *   Donated supplies (expendable personal property) shall be valued at the Fair Market Value of the supplies at the time of donation.
    *   Donated equipment usage shall be valued at the fair rental rate of the equipment. Title to donated equipment generally does not pass to the project; only depreciation or use allowances may be claimed unless the grant specifically approves the full value.

*   **4.3.3 Donated Space:**
    *   The value of donated space shall not exceed the fair rental value of comparable space as established by an independent appraisal of comparable space and facilities in a privately-owned building in the same locality.

#### 4.4 Documentation and Tracking
*   **4.4.1 Segregation:** Cost sharing expenditures must be tracked separately within the accounting system (e.g., via specific class codes or project tags) to ensure they are not claimed on other awards.
*   **4.4.2 Third-Party Verification:** When a third party provides the match, Study GRC Inc. must obtain a letter of commitment at the proposal stage and a certification of actual contribution (after the fact) stating the fair market value and that the contribution was not funded by federal sources.

#### 4.5 Prohibited Matching Sources
The following may **never** be used as Cost Sharing/Matching:
*   Unallowable costs (e.g., alcoholic beverages, lobbying, bad debts).
*   The same cost used for two different projects.
*   Federal funds from another award (unless statutory exception exists).
*   Costs incurred outside the effective dates of the grant award.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves any grant application requiring a match greater than $50,000 or 25% of the total project budget. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Reviews and approves the valuation of all in-kind contributions. Ensures matching funds are verifiable in the General Ledger. Reports match status to the Treasurer. |
| **Grant Manager / Program Director** | Identifies matching sources during the proposal phase. Collects documentation (timesheets, donor letters) during the project. Ensures the match is "necessary and reasonable." |
| **Treasurer** | Provides oversight to ensure matching commitments do not jeopardize the Organization's liquidity or financial stability (per Bylaws Article IV). |
| **Volunteers** | Must accurately record time spent on grant-specific activities using the designated tracking system. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to meet a cost sharing or matching obligation may result in the Federal awarding agency reducing the total award amount, disallowing costs, or demanding repayment of funds.

Internal violations of this policy, such as falsifying matching documentation or committing the Organization to matching funds without authority, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board/Officer position.
