### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a centralized framework for the selection, retention, management, and evaluation of Outside Counsel. This policy ensures that Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") receives high-quality legal representation that is cost-effective, ethically sound, and aligned with the Organization’s mission and risk tolerance. It specifically mitigates the risks of unauthorized financial commitment, conflicts of interest, and waiver of attorney-client privilege.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Directors, Officers, employees, volunteers, and committees of the Organization.

**In Scope:**
*   Engagement of external law firms or solo practitioners for paid services.
*   Acceptance of Pro Bono legal services.
*   Management of legal disputes, litigation, and regulatory investigations.

**Exclusions:**
*   Standard commercial contracts (e.g., software terms of service) that do not require negotiation by counsel, subject to the *Signature Authority Policy*.
*   Personal legal matters of employees or Directors not related to their indemnification rights under Article VII of the Bylaws.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Outside Counsel** | Any external attorney, law firm, or legal service provider retained to represent the Organization. |
| **Engagement Letter** | A written contract between the Organization and Outside Counsel detailing the scope of work, fee structure, and terms of representation. |
| **Matter** | A specific legal project, lawsuit, transaction, or advisory request. |
| **Authorized Signatory** | An individual explicitly authorized by the Board or Bylaws to execute legal agreements on behalf of the Organization. |
| **Pro Bono** | Legal services provided without charge or at a substantially reduced rate for the public good. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Authority to Engage Counsel
To maintain financial control and privilege management, the authority to retain Outside Counsel is restricted.
*   **Exclusive Authority:** Only the **Executive Director (CEO)** or the **President of the Board** may authorize the engagement of Outside Counsel.
*   **Unauthorized Engagement:** No other officer, employee, volunteer, or committee member may contact an attorney to request services on behalf of the Organization without prior written approval from the Executive Director.
*   **Personal Liability:** Any individual who engages Outside Counsel in violation of this policy may be held personally liable for the resulting fees and expenses.

#### 4.2 Selection Criteria
When selecting Outside Counsel, the Organization must consider:
1.  **Expertise:** Demonstrated experience in the relevant area of law (e.g., Non-profit tax law, Intellectual Property, Employment, Federal Grants/2 CFR 200).
2.  **Jurisdiction:** Licensure and standing in the relevant jurisdiction (specifically State of Texas or Federal Courts).
3.  **Cost-Effectiveness:** Competitive billing rates or willingness to offer alternative fee arrangements (fixed fees, capped fees).
4.  **Diversity:** Commitment to diversity and inclusion within the firm's staffing.
5.  **Conflict of Interest:** The firm must clear a formal conflict check regarding the Organization, its Board members, and adverse parties.

#### 4.3 Engagement Letters
No legal work may commence (except in emergency exigent circumstances) without a fully executed Engagement Letter. All Engagement Letters must include:
*   **Scope of Services:** Clearly defined objectives and deliverables.
*   **Staffing:** Identification of the lead attorney and permissible support staff.
*   **Fee Structure:** Hourly rates, fixed fees, or contingency details.
*   **Expense Guidelines:** Prohibitions on markups for administrative tasks (e.g., copying, travel) unless pre-approved.
*   **Data Security:** Confirmation that the firm adheres to data protection standards compatible with the Organization's *Master Information Security Policy*.

#### 4.4 Pro Bono Legal Services
Pro Bono services are a valuable resource but must be managed with professional rigor.
*   **Standard of Care:** Pro Bono counsel must adhere to the same professional standards and engagement procedures as paid counsel.
*   **Written Agreement:** A zero-cost Engagement Letter is required to define the scope and confirm that the Organization is the client (not the individual contact).
*   **Expense Reimbursement:** The Organization may agree to reimburse out-of-pocket expenses (e.g., filing fees) for Pro Bono matters, provided these are approved in advance.

#### 4.5 Federal Grant Compliance
When legal services are procured using federal grant funds, the selection process must comply with **2 CFR 200.317-327** (Procurement Standards).
*   **Reasonableness:** Fees must be reasonable and consistent with market rates for similar services.
*   **Documentation:** The selection process, including price analysis and rationale for selection, must be documented in the *Procurement Document Retention Standard*.

#### 4.6 Management of Legal Costs
*   **Budgets:** For Matters anticipated to exceed $5,000, Outside Counsel must provide a preliminary budget.
*   **Billing Frequency:** Invoices must be submitted monthly.
*   **Block Billing Prohibition:** Invoices must itemize tasks individually. "Block billing" (grouping multiple distinct tasks into one time entry) is prohibited and will be rejected.
*   **Audit:** The Treasurer and Executive Director reserve the right to audit legal bills for compliance with the Engagement Letter.

#### 4.7 Waiver of Privilege
To protect the Organization, **Attorney-Client Privilege** must be strictly maintained.
*   **Distribution:** Legal advice and communications from Outside Counsel must not be forwarded to third parties, unauthorized employees, or external email addresses.
*   **Board Access:** Directors are entitled to access privileged information necessary for governance, provided no conflict of interest exists.

### 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Board of Directors** | Approves the Legal Services Engagement Policy. Authorizes legal action for Fundamental Actions (e.g., mergers, dissolution) or litigation involving Executive leadership. |
| **Executive Director (CEO)** | Primary point of contact for Outside Counsel. Negotiates fees, signs Engagement Letters, and approves invoices within budget authority. |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews legal expenses for budget compliance. Ensures proper allocation of legal costs (e.g., Program vs. Admin) for IRS Form 990 and grant reporting. |
| **Employees/Volunteers** | Must direct all potential legal issues to the Executive Director. Must preserve confidentiality of all legal communications. |

### 6. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or removal from the Board.
*   **Financial Liability:** The Organization reserves the right to refuse payment for legal services retained by unauthorized personnel.
*   **Privilege Waiver:** Employees who negligently waive attorney-client privilege through unauthorized disclosure may face immediate termination.
