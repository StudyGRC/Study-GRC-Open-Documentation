### 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish the standards and procedures by which Study GRC Inc. (the "Organization") will indemnify and advance expenses to Directors, Officers, employees, and volunteers who are made parties to legal proceedings because of their service to the Organization. This policy is designed to attract and retain qualified individuals to serve the Organization by providing financial protection against personal liability, consistent with **Article VII of the Bylaws** and **Chapter 8 of the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC)**.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
*   **Covered Persons:** Current and former Directors, Officers, Operational Officers, employees, committee members, and registered volunteers of the Organization.
*   **Covered Acts:** Actions taken (or omissions made) in good faith while performing duties within the scope of their role for the Organization.

**Exclusions:** This policy does not cover acts of gross negligence, intentional misconduct, knowing violations of the law, or transactions from which the individual derived an improper personal benefit.

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Indemnification** | The Organization’s reimbursement of a Covered Person for liabilities (judgments, settlements, fines) and reasonable expenses (attorney fees) incurred in a Proceeding. |
| **Advancement of Expenses** | Payment of legal fees and related expenses by the Organization on behalf of a Covered Person *before* the final disposition of a Proceeding. |
| **Proceeding** | Any threatened, pending, or completed action, suit, or proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative, arbitrative, or investigative. |
| **Good Faith** | Acting with a reasonable belief that the conduct was in the Organization’s best interests (or not opposed to them) and, in criminal cases, having no reasonable cause to believe the conduct was unlawful. |
| **Undertaking to Repay** | A written agreement by the Covered Person to repay advanced expenses if it is ultimately determined that they are not entitled to indemnification. |

### 4. Policy Statements

#### 4.1 Mandatory Indemnification
To the extent a Covered Person has been **wholly successful**, on the merits or otherwise, in the defense of any Proceeding, the Organization **must** indemnify them against reasonable expenses incurred in connection with the Proceeding.

#### 4.2 Permissive Indemnification
The Organization **may** indemnify a Covered Person who was, is, or is threatened to be made a respondent in a Proceeding if it is determined, per Section 5.2, that the person:
1.  Acted in **Good Faith**;
2.  Reasonably believed:
    *   In the case of conduct in an official capacity, that the conduct was in the Organization's best interests;
    *   In all other cases, that the conduct was not opposed to the Organization's best interests; and
3.  In the case of a criminal proceeding, had no reasonable cause to believe the conduct was unlawful.

#### 4.3 Prohibited Indemnification
The Organization shall **not** indemnify a Covered Person if:
1.  The person is found liable to the Organization (in a derivative suit) or is found liable on the basis that they received an improper personal benefit (breach of duty of loyalty); or
2.  The act or omission involved intentional misconduct or a knowing violation of the law.

#### 4.4 Advancement of Expenses
The Organization may pay or reimburse reasonable expenses incurred by a Covered Person in advance of the final disposition of the Proceeding. This requires:
1.  A written affirmation by the person of their Good Faith belief that they have met the standard of conduct necessary for indemnification; and
2.  A written **Undertaking to Repay** the amount paid if it is ultimately determined that they have not met that standard or are not entitled to indemnification.

#### 4.5 Insurance Priority
The Organization shall maintain Directors and Officers (D&O) Liability Insurance. Indemnification payments under this policy shall be secondary to insurance coverage. The Organization will pay the deductible or retention amount and any valid claims exceeding the policy limits, subject to the terms herein.

### 5. Procedures

#### 5.1 Notification of Claim
1.  **Immediate Notice:** Any Covered Person served with a lawsuit, subpoena, or notice of investigation related to their service must notify the **Board Secretary** and **President** in writing within **three (3) business days**.
2.  **Content:** The notice must include a copy of the legal documents served and a brief description of the circumstances.

#### 5.2 Determination of Eligibility
Before any indemnification payment is made (other than court-ordered), a determination must be made that the Covered Person met the standard of conduct defined in Section 4.2. This determination shall be made by:
1.  **Disinterested Directors:** A majority vote of Directors who are not parties to the Proceeding (even if less than a quorum); or
2.  **Special Legal Counsel:** Selected by the Disinterested Directors, providing a written legal opinion; or
3.  **Voting Members:** A vote of the Supporting and Honorary Members, excluding the vote of the person claiming indemnification.

#### 5.3 Authorization of Payment
Upon a favorable determination of eligibility:
1.  The **Treasurer** shall review the expenses to ensure they are reasonable and customary.
2.  The Board shall authorize payment.
3.  If the payment is an **Advancement of Expenses**, the Treasurer must first secure the signed **Undertaking to Repay** from the Covered Person.

#### 5.4 Reporting
Any indemnification or advance of expenses paid to a Director or Officer must be reported in writing to the Voting Members with or before the notice of the next meeting of members, or within 12 months, whichever is sooner (per TBOC § 8.152).

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Covered Person (Indemnitee)** | Promptly notifies the Organization of claims; cooperates fully with the defense; signs Undertaking to Repay for advancements. |
| **Board of Directors** | Selects D&O insurance; determines eligibility for indemnification (if disinterested); authorizes payments. |
| **Special Legal Counsel** | Provides an independent written opinion on whether the Covered Person met the standard of conduct (if requested by the Board). |
| **Treasurer** | Reviews legal invoices for reasonableness; manages insurance claims; collects Undertaking to Repay documents. |
| **Secretary** | Maintains records of all determinations, votes, and notifications regarding indemnification. |

### 7. Compliance and Enforcement
Failure to adhere to the notification procedures in Section 5.1 may jeopardize the Covered Person's right to indemnification. Falsification of information regarding the "Good Faith" nature of conduct will result in immediate termination of indemnification, a demand for repayment of all advanced funds, and disciplinary action up to and including removal from office and legal action for fraud.
